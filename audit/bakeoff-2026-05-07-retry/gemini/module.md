# Мій ранок: Прокидаюся, вмиваюся

Morning routines in Ukrainian rely heavily on a special class of verbs called reflexive verbs. These verbs describe actions you perform on yourself, like waking up or getting dressed. In this lesson, we will look at how these verbs work and how to describe your morning step-by-step.

## Діалоги

Let's start with a conversation between two roommates, Lina and Nastya, as they discuss their different morning habits.

Lina: Настю, коли ти зазвичай прокидаєшся?
Nastya: Я прокидаюся о сьомій годині. А ти?
Lina: Ого, так рано! А я прокидаюся о восьмій. Що ти робиш потім?
Nastya: Спочатку я вмиваюся і одягаюся. Потім снідаю.
Lina: А коли ти йдеш на роботу?
Nastya: Я йду на роботу о восьмій тридцять.

- Коли ти прокидаєшся? — When do you wake up?
- Я прокидаюся о сьомій. — I wake up at seven.
- Що ти робиш потім? — What do you do then?
- Вмиваюся, одягаюся і снідаю. — I wash up, get dressed, and have breakfast.
- А коли ти йдеш на роботу? — And when do you go to work?

Now, compare Nastya's busy workday morning with how she spends her Saturday.

Lina: Настю, ти завтра теж прокидаєшся рано?
Nastya: Ні, у суботу я не поспішаю. Я прокидаюся пізно.
Lina: Що ти робиш у ліжку?
Nastya: Я лежу і дивлюся телефон. Потім я неспішно снідаю і гуляю.

- У суботу я не поспішаю. — On Saturday I do not hurry.
- Прокидаюся пізно. — I wake up late.
- Дивлюся телефон. — I look at my phone.
- Я навчаюся вранці. — I study in the morning.

## Дієслова на -ся

The verbs you saw in the dialogues, like прокидатися (to wake up) or вмиватися (to wash up), are reflexive. In Ukrainian, these are recognized by the suffix -ся (or -сь) at the end.

As noted in the Karaman Grade 10 textbook (p. 176):
> Дієслова із суфіксом -ся(-сь), які виражають зворотну дію, називаються зворотними. Вони означають дію, спрямовану на самого її носія.

Basically, the suffix -ся functions like the English word "self."

- вмивати (to wash someone) → вмиватися (to wash oneself)
- одягати (to dress someone) → одягатися (to dress oneself)

### Conjugation Pattern

Reflexive verbs follow the standard conjugation patterns you already know, but you must add -ся (after consonants) or -сь (after vowels) at the very end of every form.

| Person | Ending | Example: Вмиватися |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Я (I) | -юся / -юся | Я вмиваюся |
| Ти (You) | -єшся | Ти вмиваєшся |
| Він/Вона (He/She) | -ється | Він/Вона вмивається |
| Ми (We) | -ємося | Ми вмиваємося |
| Ви (You all) | -єтеся | Ви вмиваєтеся |
| Вони (They) | -ються | Вони вмиваються |

### Pronunciation Alert

In Ukrainian, reflexive verbs are often spelled differently than they are pronounced. This is a common trap for learners. The Kravtsova Grade 4 textbook (p. 140) provides the following rule:

> Дієслова на -ться, -шся вимовляй і пиши так:
> ПИШЕМО: дивиться | ВИМОВЛЯЄМО: диви[ц´:а]
> ПИШЕМО: смієшся | ВИМОВЛЯЄМО: сміє[с´:а]

- -шся sounds like a long, soft [s]: [с':а]
- -ться sounds like a long, soft [ts]: [ц':а]

:::tip
Even though you see the letter "ш" in ти вмиваєшся, you should pronounce it as if it were a soft "с".
:::

## Мій ранок

To tell a story about your morning, you need both reflexive and non-reflexive verbs, along with sequence words to link your actions together.

### Essential Vocabulary

| Ukrainian | English | Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| прокидатися | to wake up | Reflexive |
| вмиватися | to wash up | Reflexive |
| одягатися | to get dressed | Reflexive |
| збиратися | to get ready | Reflexive |
| снідати | to have breakfast | Not reflexive |
| пити каву | to drink coffee | Not reflexive |

### The Irregular Verb: йти

The verb йти (to go) is irregular. You should memorize these three basic forms for now:

| Person | Ukrainian | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Я | я йду | I go |
| Ти | ти йдеш | You go |
| Він/Вона | він/вона йде | He/She goes |

### Sequence Words

Use these words to show the order of your actions:

- спочатку — first / at first
- потім — then / next
- після цього — after this
- нарешті — finally

Here is how a typical morning routine looks when put together:

Спочатку я прокидаюся о сьомій. Потім я вмиваюся і одягаюся. Після цього я снідаю. Нарешті я йду на роботу.

- Спочатку я прокидаюся. — First I wake up.
- Потім я вмиваюся. — Then I wash up.
- Після цього я снідаю. — After this I have breakfast.
- Нарешті я йду на роботу. — Finally I go to work.

### Глибший погляд на зворотні дієслова (A Deeper Look at Reflexive Verbs)

The particle **-ся** has a fascinating history in the Ukrainian language. Linguistically, it is a fossilized clitic, originally derived from the reflexive pronoun **себе** (oneself). In Old East Slavic, this pronoun was a separate word that followed the verb. Over centuries, it shortened and fused with the verb stem to become the suffix we use today.

As noted in linguistic studies of the Ukrainian language, this transition from a free-standing pronoun to a bound morpheme explains why **-ся** appears at the very end of the verb, even after the personal endings (like *-ю*, *-єш*, *-є*). Understanding this origin helps clarify why some verbs change their meaning so significantly when **-ся** is added. 

For example, consider the difference between transitive and reflexive meanings:
- **Вмивати** (to wash) requires an object, such as *вмивати дитину* (to wash a child).
- **Вмиватися** (to wash oneself) focuses the action back on the subject.

There is also a distinction in meaning that can be subtle for learners. Take the verb **вчити** (to teach):
- **Я вчу українську мову.** — I am teaching the Ukrainian language (or studying it).
- **Я вчуся в університеті.** — I am studying (literally "teaching myself") at the university.

If you say "Я вчу," native speakers will wait to hear *what* or *whom* you are teaching. If you say "Я вчуся," it is clear that you are the student in the process of learning.

### Фонетика та правопис (Phonetics and Spelling)

As we discussed earlier, the pronunciation of reflexive verbs often diverges from their written form. This is not a random occurrence but a systemic feature of Ukrainian phonetics known as assimilation. When the soft dental consonants **[т']** or **[ш]** meet the **-ся** suffix, they undergo a process of fusion.

1. **The -ться ending:** In verbs like *вмивається* or *прокидається*, the sequence **-ться** represents a long, soft **[ц':а]**. Historically, this reflects the softening of the **[т]** before the soft **[с']** of the reflexive particle. In modern standard Ukrainian, you should never pronounce the "т" and "с" as distinct sounds. Instead, imagine a very long "ts" sound, similar to the "zz" in the Italian word "pizza," but softened.

2. **The -шся ending:** In the second-person singular (the "ти" form), the ending **-шся** is written with a "ш," but it is pronounced as a long, soft **[с':а]**. This is often the most difficult rule for beginners to master because the visual cue of the letter "ш" is so strong. However, if you pronounce it as "sh-sya," it will sound unnatural and may even be difficult for native speakers to understand quickly. Practicing the transition from the vowel to a long, soft "s" sound is key to achieving a native-like accent.

### Розширений словниковий запас (Extended Vocabulary)

To describe a complete morning, we need a few more specific actions. Many of these are also reflexive, as they involve personal grooming. In the table below, pay attention to which verbs use the reflexive suffix and which do not.

| Ukrainian | English | Grammatical Category | Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| чистити зуби | to brush teeth | Verb + Object | Action on a specific part |
| розчісуватися | to comb one's hair | Reflexive Verb | General grooming |
| робити зарядку | to do morning exercises | Verb + Object | Non-reflexive activity |
| застеляти ліжко | to make the bed | Verb + Object | Housekeeping task |
| приймати душ | to take a shower | Verb + Object | Common alternative to *вмиватися* |
| голитися | to shave | Reflexive Verb | Action on oneself |

Note that "чистити зуби" (to brush teeth) is not reflexive in Ukrainian because you are acting upon a specific object (the teeth) rather than your whole self. However, "вмиватися" is reflexive because it implies washing your face and body as a general state of being. This logic is consistent throughout the language: if the action is directed at a specific body part mentioned by name, the verb is usually not reflexive.

### Культурний контекст: Український сніданок (Cultural Context: Ukrainian Breakfast)

What do Ukrainians typically eat in the morning? While modern life has introduced cereals and toast, many families still prefer traditional options that reflect the country's agricultural heritage. A popular choice is **канапка** or **бутерброд** (a sandwich), often made with a thick slice of bread, butter, and cheese or sausage (**ковбаса**). 

Another classic and beloved dish is **сирники** (fried curd cheese pancakes). These are usually served with a generous dollop of **сметана** (sour cream), honey, or fruit jam. For those who prefer a "heavier" or more nutritious start, **каша** (porridge) is a staple. Buckwheat (**гречка**) is particularly iconic in Ukraine, often eaten with a bit of butter or milk. Oatmeal (**вівсянка**) is also a common choice for health-conscious modern Ukrainians.

In terms of drinks, coffee (**кава**) has become the dominant morning beverage in cities. Lviv, in particular, is famous for its centuries-old coffee culture. However, tea (**чай**) remains a constant presence in many households, often served with a slice of lemon or a spoonful of honey.

### Розповідь: Ранок Олександра (Narrative: Oleksandr's Morning)

Read the following text to see how all these elements—reflexive verbs, sequence words, and morning activities—come together in a natural narrative.

Мене звати Олександр. Мій ранок завжди починається однаково. Я прокидаюся о пів на сьому. Це дуже рано, але я люблю ранкову тишу. Спочатку я роблю зарядку десять хвилин, щоб прокинутися остаточно. Потім я йду у ванну кімнату, де я вмиваюся холодною водою і чищу зуби. Після цього я голюся. 

О сьомій годині я йду на кухню. Я п'ю міцну каву і готую сніданок для сім'ї. Зазвичай я їм вівсянку з горіхами. Мій син Максим прокидається пізніше, о сьомій тридцять. Він не любить прокидатися рано, тому він часто поспішає. Максим вмивається, швидко одягається, і ми разом снідаємо. О восьмій годині ми нарешті виходимо з дому. Я йду на роботу, а Максим іде до школи.

**Translation for Reference:**
*My name is Oleksandr. My morning always starts the same way. I wake up at half past six. It is very early, but I love the morning silence. First, I do exercises for ten minutes to wake up completely. Then I go to the bathroom, where I wash up with cold water and brush my teeth. After that, I shave.*

*At seven o'clock, I go to the kitchen. I drink strong coffee and prepare breakfast for the family. Usually, I eat oatmeal with nuts. My son Maksym wakes up later, at seven-thirty. He does not like to wake up early, so he is often in a hurry. Maksym washes up, quickly gets dressed, and we have breakfast together. At eight o'clock, we finally leave the house. I go to work, and Maksym goes to school.*

## Підсумок

In this module, we covered the basics of describing a morning routine using reflexive verbs and sequence markers. 

To recap:
- Reflexive verbs end in -ся or -сь.
- They denote actions performed on oneself (e.g., вмиватися).
- The ending -шся is pronounced [с':а], and -ться is pronounced [ц':а].
- Sequence words like спочатку, потім, and нарешті help create a logical narrative.
