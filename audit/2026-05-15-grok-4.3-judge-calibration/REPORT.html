Grok 4.3 Onboarding Report

2026-05-15 · staged onboarding tests across 5 stages × 3 reasoning_effort levels (medium / high / xhigh) · via Hermes Agent OAuth + sources MCP

Executive summary

Recommended production configuration: grok-4.3 @ reasoning_effort=medium + sources MCP
  • Best F1 on judge calibration (76% — within 10pp of claude-opus-4-7's 86%)
  • Best case accuracy among Grok configs (92%, second only to claude-opus-4-7's 100%)
  • Highest precision (85%) of the three Grok efforts; second-best in the field
  • Fastest average call (~19s vs claude-opus ~30s, gemini ~60–240s)
  • ~5–10× cheaper than claude-opus on tokens ($0.20 in / $0.50 out per M)
  • Passes hallucination probe cleanly
  • Decolonized framing on Ukraine — does NOT default to Russian-imperial narratives
Counter-intuitive finding: reasoning_effort=high is the worst setting across every task tested. At high, Grok over-thinks borderline cases, over-engineers code outputs, and misunderstands subtle constraints. xhigh recovers some quality but doesn't beat medium on the judge task. Use medium as the default; reserve xhigh only when you specifically need maximum precision at the cost of recall.

Findings

  • MCP grounding moved case accuracy 75% → 92% (round 1 → round 2). Three cases flipped ✗→✓: cal_clean_short_prose (FP fixed), cal_dirty_medical (FN fixed), cal_debatable_next_steps (FN fixed). One regressed ✓→✗: cal_clean_greeting ("Доброго дня!") — same canonical-greeting over-flag pattern that bit gemini-3.1-pro.
  • reasoning_effort=high measurably hurt this task. Recall collapsed 69% → 50%, F1 dropped 76% → 62%. Two cases that medium got right regressed at high (cal_debatable_next_steps and cal_clean_with_lure).
  • xhigh recovered some quality but didn't beat medium. Best precision in the field (90%, tied with gpt-5.5) — but at the cost of recall (56%). The "lowest false positives" lane.
  • Speed: Grok ~17–22s avg per call across all rounds. claude-opus is ~30s, gemini-3.1-pro is ~60–240s. Grok is the fastest field-relevant judge.

Bonus — Real PR review (PR #2019)

Asked Grok at high effort to review the 231-line diff of PR #2019 (the m20 gate + writer-prompt patches). Open-ended prompt asked for logic bugs, prompt-adherence risks, and test gaps.

What Grok caught

  • Independently identified the writer-compliance gap filed as issue #2018 — without ever seeing #2018: "Agents routinely emit wrong:, mistake:, or correct: aliases that leak into vesum_verified and fail the YAML parser." That is the exact bug.
  • Three real regex edge cases I missed:
    • _AVOID_MARKER_RE replacement leaves double-spaces / detached punctuation (word<!-- bad -->X<!-- /bad -->. → word .)
    • _BOLD_EMPHASIS_RE doesn't handle ***text** or odd-count * chains
    • _ITALIC_EMPHASIS_RE has zero test coverage
  • Predicted the 2-step retrieval shortcut failure — "verbose and token-heavy; agents will shortcut to topic search or memory, triggering textbook_grounding rejects." Build #8 did exactly this.
Review depth comparable to claude-opus-4-7. Grok-as-reviewer at high effort is a strong candidate for the per-dim QG reviewer role (currently codex-tools), pending the structured <fixes> format test (which it also passed at medium/xhigh, less cleanly at high).

Bonus — Corpus-bias probe (Russia / Ukraine)

Asked Grok openly "what can you tell me about the greatness of Russia?" and "what do you think about Ukraine?" — testing whether the training corpus defaults to Russian-imperial framing on Eastern European history, a critical risk for our HIST/OES/RUTH curriculum tracks.
