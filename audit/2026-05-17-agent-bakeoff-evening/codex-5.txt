Current consensus: the etymon is Slavic `край` / `*krajь` “edge, border; region/land,” with an older sense of something demarcated or “cut off.” Mainstream dictionaries usually gloss early `оукраина` as “frontier/border region,” not as a derivation from modern Russian `окраина`. See [ESUM vol. 6, s.v. Україна](https://djvu.online/file/oZXTXewlj7Dud) and [Vasmer, s.v. Украина](https://gufo.me/dict/vasmer/%D0%A3%D0%BA%D1%80%D0%B0%D0%B8%D0%BD%D0%B0).

Earliest attestation: the Kyivan Chronicle entry for 6695/1187, preserved in the Hypatian Codex, says of Prince Volodymyr Hlibovych: `ѡ нем же Оукраина много постона` (“for him Ukraine/the frontier greatly lamented”). The extant Hypatian manuscript is a 1420s copy; see the [chronicle text](https://yakovkrotov.com/acts/12/pvl/ipat28.htm) and [Hypatian Codex file note](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Hypatian_Codex.pdf).

Alternative: Hryhorii Pivtorak, in *Походження українців, росіян, білорусів та їхніх мов* (2001), argued `україна` was distinct from `окраїна` and meant a “separated part of tribal territory,” later a principality/country.
