## Section-by-Section Research Notes

### Section 1: Вступ — Від реготу до сльози (625 words)

**Key points:**

- Contrast with Eneida: burlesque vs sentimentalism, body vs soul, male vs female
- Shakhovsky's "Козак-стихотворець" (1812) as provocation — Poltava audience outraged
- Kotliarevsky's response: dignified Ukrainian characters speaking real language
- Political dimension: Eneida hid behind humor mask; Natalka claimed equal status with "high" theatre
- Written 1819 for Poltava Theatre; published 1838 in Sreznevsky's collection
- Genre: "Опера малоросійська у двох діях"
- Two-act structure: Act I exposition, Act II resolution

### Section 2: Сентименталізм як літературний напрям (750 words)

**European context:**

- Sterne "Sentimental Journey" (1768) — origin of the term
- Richardson "Pamela" (1740) — poor servant vs rich master (structural parallel to Natalka)
- Rousseau "Julie" (1761) — "natural person" concept
- Goethe "Werther" (1774) — death from unrequited love
- Karamzin "Poor Liza" (1792) — peasant girl drowns; author looks down at subject

**Ukrainian specificity debate:**

- Chyzhevsky: denied separate Ukrainian sentimentalism (too few works)
- Zerov: first to identify it as separate phenomenon, compared to English sentimentalism
- Borzenko: nature cult + heart-over-reason + artistry
- Limborsky: "shift from life of reason to life of heart"
- Key difference: Ukrainian sentimentalism draws from folklore, not books

### Section 3: Наталка — архетип української нареченої (875 words)

**Character traits:**

- Faithful (4 years without news, like Penelope but in Ukrainian village)
- Dignified (refuses rich Vozny — "Better die than live with unloved")
- Hardworking ("Not rich but honest" — labor as honor source)
- Rebellious (defies mother and social norms of unequal marriage)
- First SUBJECT in Ukrainian drama (makes decisions, not passive object)

**Conflict "wealth vs dignity":**

- Skovoroda's "kindred souls" ideal
- "З милим рай і в курені" as ethical program
- "Moral capital" concept: Natalka poor in money, rich in honor
- Vozny rich in money, poor in love; finale = exchange of capitals

**Comparison with Marusya:**

- Kvitka's Marusya (1834) — dies; Natalka survives and wins
- Kotliarevsky more optimistic than Kvitka
- Genre difference: play needs resolution; prose can afford tragic ending

### Section 4: Музика важливіша за слово (875 words)

**Songs as psychological portraits:**

- "Віють вітри" — Natalka's depression aria; wind/tree metaphor; crying secretly
- "Всякому городу нрав і права" — Vozny's self-exposure; corrupted Skovoroda
- "Ой я дівчина Полтавка" — dignity manifesto; cumulative portrait through parallelisms
- "Гомін, гомін по діброві" — Mykola's heroic Cossack song; link to past
- "Сонце низенько" — Petro's elegy; sunset as metaphor for ending hope

**Opera transformation:**

- Lysenko 1889 Odessa premiere
- Preserved folk melodies + classical orchestration = unique synthesis
- Petrusenko's recording as audio document
- Opera internationalized the conflict; word → melody shift
- Theatre of Coryphées lineage

### Section 5: Другорядні персонажі (750 words)

**Vozny Tetervakovskyi:**

- Bureaucratic speech (канцеляризми, "язичіє") vs Natalka's living language
- Misused Latin phrases — parody of pseudo-education
- Rehabilitation in finale: "Насильно мил не будеш"
- Bureaucracy position ≈ district police officer; petty power corrupts

**Petro:**

- Mysterious 4-year absence; бурлака/wanderer archetype
- Passive: ready to give 40 rubles and surrender
- Natalka forces him to fight — gender inversion from Eneida
- 40 rubles as sacred object of sacrificial love

**Terpelykha:**

- Name = micro-narrative ("she who endures")
- Widow fallen from prosperity; fears poverty
- Generational conflict: marriage as economics vs love
- Not evil — driven by fear; launches gallery of Ukrainian literary mothers

### Section 6: Підсумок — Український театр народжується (625 words)

**Theatre significance:**

- Birth of Ukrainian professional theatre
- Zankovetska's legendary performance (Alexander III allegedly wept)
- 1936 paradox: Soviet Bolshoi production + diaspora film (Avramenko, NYC)
- Text-chameleon: safe enough for censors, Ukrainian enough for diaspora

**From burlesque to seriousness:**

- Natalka's victory = New Time over Old
- First Ukrainian work where author looks UP at characters, with respect
- From external (food, drink, battle) to internal (love, fear, decisions)
- Shevchenko's Kateryna = Natalka who lost

**Connection to Kvitka:**

- Direct influence on Kvitka-Osnovyanenko
- "Marusya" (1834) as first Ukrainian sentimental prose
- Shift from optimism (Natalka wins) to tragedy (Marusya dies)
- Lineage: burlesque → sentimentalism → romanticism
