## Section-by-Section Research Notes

### Section 1: Вступ — Чому перша частина є фундаментом? (625 words)

**Key facts:**

- Eneida has 6 parts (vs Virgil's 12 books)
- Written in 4-foot iamb (ямбічний тетраметр), 10-line stanzas
- Part 1 published 1798 (first 3 parts together) — sets tone for entire poem
- Part 1 = "камертон" (tuning fork) — establishes: (a) hero profile, (b) satirical method, (c) linguistic register, (d) allegorical framework

**Points to cover:**

- Structure of poem: 6 parts, each a self-contained narrative arc within larger journey
- Why Part 1 is foundational: introduces ALL main characters, establishes burlesque method, sets up Troy=Sich allegory
- Reader's first encounter with Kotliarevsky's method: shock of "low" language applied to "high" material

**Engagement hook:** Opening lines as cultural DNA — every Ukrainian knows "Еней був парубок моторний"

### Section 2: Еней — козак-мандрівник (875 words)

**Key facts from primary text (verified from existing content):**

- First lines: "Еней був парубок моторний / І хлопець хоть куди козак, / Удавсь на всеє зле проворний, / Завзятіший од всіх бурлак."
- "парубок" — young unmarried man (vs Virgil's mature _vir_)
- "моторний" — quick, agile, resourceful (козацький архетип)
- "хоть куди козак" — direct identification as Cossack (phraseologism = "top quality")
- "бурлак" — homeless wanderer, specifically post-1775 Zaporozhians displaced after Sich destruction
- Contrast with Virgil's "Arma virumque cano" — solemn invocation vs folk portrait

**Archetype analysis:**

- Eney as "шукач долі" (fortune-seeker) — Ukrainian cultural archetype
- Not a tragic hero (Achilles model) but a survival hero
- Carries "ладанку" (amulet) and money — pragmatist, not idealist
- Gathers "ватагу" (gang/band) — not regular army, partisans/outcasts

**Engagement hook:** Word-by-word analysis of first stanza as encoding of national identity

### Section 3: Олімп як петербурзька канцелярія (1000 words)

**Key facts:**

- Zeus: drinks horilka, eats herring, rules "скрізь пальці" (through fingers = negligently)
- Juno: called "суча дочка", "розкудкудакалась як квочка" — immediate desacralization
- Venus: uses feminine wiles to lobby Zeus, plays "бідна сирітка" — satirizes court favoritism
- Mercury: demands bribes for passports — satirizes passport system, police corruption
- Aeolus: takes bribe from Juno ("дівку чорнобриву") to send storm — nature itself is corrupt

**Bribery as plot engine:**

- Juno → Aeolus: "дівку чорнобриву" (sexual bribery)
- Eney → Neptune: "півкопи" (monetary bribery) — even calms sea for money
- Mercury: "здирав за пашпорти" — passport system corruption
- Reading: Nothing happens without a bribe. This mirrors 18th-century Russian Empire bureaucracy.

**Chancellery language:**

- Gods use bureaucratic terms: "приказ", "бумага", "протокол", "сенат", "резолюція"
- Olympus = Department, Juno writes "доноси" (denunciations), Venus "клопоче" (petitions)
- Satire on Petrine/Catherine bureaucratization of life

**Engagement hook:** Character chart — God = Imperial equivalent (Zeus=Emperor, Juno=russification policy, Venus=Ukrainian elite soft power, Eney=Ukrainian people)

### Section 4: Синонімічні ряди та "макаронічна мова" (875 words)

**Key facts:**

- 50+ synonyms for "бити" (motloshyty, hamselyty, luptsyuvaty, daty lyashcha, vshyty smal'tsyu, polataty rebra...)
- 100+ food/drink names in entire poem
- Swearing as verbal art: "суча дочка", "гад", "плюгавець" — not pornographic (like Russian mat) but "smachna" (flavorful), metaphorical
- Macaronic Latin-Ukrainian: "Енеус ностер магнус панус" (Eneus noster magnus panus)
- First Ukrainian dictionary: ~1000 words appended to 1798 edition
- Register mixing: chancellery language + folk speech + seminary Latin + military jargon

**Functions of synonymic series:**

1. Demonstrate language richness (counter imperial claim that Ukrainian is "poor")
2. Create comic effect through accumulation
3. Preserve dialectal and archaic words for posterity
4. Show social differentiation through language

**Functions of profanity:**

1. Realism — soldiers/Cossacks don't speak in salons
2. Carnivalization — calling Juno "суча дочка" strips her of power
3. Vitality — adds energy, rhythm, emotional charge

**Engagement hook:** Mini-anthology of Kotliarevsky's synonymic virtuosity

### Section 5: Структура першої частини (625 words)

**Plot of Part 1:**

1. Troy destroyed → Eney flees with "ватага"
2. Juno sends Aeolus → storm at sea
3. Neptune bribed → storm calms ("півкопи")
4. Venus appeals to Zeus → gets permission for Eney
5. Eney arrives at Carthage → meets Dido
6. Dido falls in love → feast/banquet scene
7. Zeus sends Mercury → Eney must leave
8. Eney flees → Dido self-immolates

**Authorial digressions:**

- Narrator breaks frame, addresses reader directly
- Comments on action, gives asides, moralizes ironically
- Creates intimacy between narrator and audience — barroom storytelling style

**Didona as character:**

- Strong woman, independent ruler ("розумна пані і моторна")
- Built Carthage — successful leader
- When abandoned: mask of cheerfulness falls, genuine lyrical pathos
- Self-immolation: pagan ritual, preservation of honor
- Only "real" (non-burlesque) moment in Part 1 — Kotliarevsky removes jester's cap

**Engagement hook:** Plot map showing parallel between Eney's journey and post-Sich Cossack diaspora

### Section 6: Підсумок — Гумор як захисний механізм (500 words)

**Key arguments:**

- Ukrainian humor = defense mechanism against imperial oppression
- Laughter makes oppressor (Juno/Empire) less terrifying — desacralization
- "Low" lexicon proves Ukrainian is richer than Russian
- Burlesque = trojan horse — looks harmless to censors, carries cultural revolution
- Resilience ("резильєнтність") — ability to recover after trauma through humor
