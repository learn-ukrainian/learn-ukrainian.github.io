## Key Themes & Engagement Hooks

### 1. Від Романтизму до Реалізму: Зміна мовної парадигми

- **Romanticism**: Idealized, poetic, elevated, historicized (Shevchenko, Kulish). Focus on "dukh".
- **Realism**: Concrete, precise, "grounded". Focus on "pravda" (truth).
- **Hook**: `[!context]` The shift from "painting with words" to "photographing with words".
- **Detail**: Realists rejected the "dead" bookish language in favor of the "living" spoken word of the peasantry and intelligentsia.

### 2. Маніфест Реалізму: Іван Нечуй-Левицький

- **Philosophy**: Literature must reflect the "real language of real people".
- **Battle**: Fought against "Halychyna influences" (perceived as artificial) and "Russianisms". Promoted the Central Dnieper dialect as the golden standard.
- **Style**: "Juicy" (соковита), colorful, full of idioms/phraseology.
- **Hook**: `[!quote]` "Мова — це лице народу, воно мусить бути чисте й гарне." (Нечуй-Левицький).
- **Myth**: `[!myth-buster]` Myth: Realists only wrote about peasants bc they didn't know other words. Truth: It was a conscious choice to legitimize the vernacular as a literary vehicle capable of describing psychological depth.

### 3. Діалект як інструмент (Dialect for Individualization)

- **Technique**: Using genuine dialectal features to distinguish characters (social marking).
- **Nechuy's usage**: Distinct speech patterns for Baba Paraska vs. Baba Palazhka.
- **Risk**: Too much dialect = unreadable ("kotliarevshchyna" in a bad sense). Realists balanced this carefully.
- **Hook**: `[!history-bite]` How Nechuy collected words like artifacts (ethnographic approach to lexicography).

### 4. Панас Мирний: Психологізм мови

- **Shift**: From external description (Nechuy) to internal monologue (Myrny).
- **Features**: "Melodious", "sincere". Focus on the "language of the soul".
- **Innovation**: Internal speech, streams of consciousness (rudimentary forms).
- **Legacy**: Paved the way for modernist psychologism (Kotsiubynsky).

### 5. Стандартизація в умовах заборон (The Struggle for a Norm)

- **Context**: Valuev (1863) & Ems (1876).
- **Resistance**: Writing in Ukrainian was a political act.
- **The Debate**: "Hulak-Artemovsky vs. Kostomarov vs. Nechuy" on what the standard should be.
- **Decolonization**: The refusal to accept the "dialect" status.

## Narrative Arc (The "Argument")

The module argues that Realism was not just a change in _topic_ (peasants vs. hetmans) but a revolution in _language_. By forcing literature to speak the actual language of the people, Realists like Nechuy and Myrny accomplished two things: 1) they preserved the language against imperial erasure, and 2) they proved its flexibility for high culture, paving the way for the Modernists.
