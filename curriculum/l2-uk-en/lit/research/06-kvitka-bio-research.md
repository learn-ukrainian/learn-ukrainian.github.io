## Key Biographical Facts (Verified)

- **Full name:** Григорій Федорович Квітка (pseudonym: Грицько Основ'яненко)
- **Born:** 18 (29) листопада 1778, село Основа (now within Kharkiv)
- **Died:** 8 (20) серпня 1843, Харків (age 64)
- **Family:** Father Федір Іванович Квітка (колезький радник), mother Марія Василівна (née Шидловська), older brother Андрій (born 1774). Old Cossack starshyna family of Slobozhanshchyna. Family hosted Skovoroda.
- **Childhood blindness:** Around age 5, from illness (золотуха/scrofula). Healed after pilgrimage to Озерянська ікона at Kuriazky Monastery.
- **Education:** Home education, then Kuriazky monastery school. Read Skovoroda, Hulak-Artemovsky, Kotlyarevsky's Eneida, Lomonosov, Molière, Zhukovsky, Cervantes.
- **Military service:** At 15, enrolled as vakhmistr in leib-guards horse regiment; 1796-1797 rotmistr in Siversky carabineer and Kharkiv cuirassier regiments.
- **Monastery period:** June 1804 to April 1805 (~10 months), novitiate at Kuriazky Monastery.
- **Civic career:** Povitovyi marshal of nobility (1817-1828), head of Kharkiv criminal court chamber.
- **Cultural achievements:** Co-founded Kharkiv professional theatre (1812, first director), Charitable Society (1812), Institute for Noble Girls (1812), Kharkiv Public Library (1832-1838), co-published "Украинский Вестник" journal (1816-1817) with Gonorsky and Filomatsky.
- **Pseudonym origin:** From family estate Основа (Osnova), now a district of Kharkiv. "Грицько" — common folk diminutive of Григорій, creating a mask of peasant narrator.
- **Key Ukrainian works:** Маруся (1832/1834), Конотопська відьма (1833), Козир-дівка (1838), Сватання на Гончарівці (1835), Сердешна Оксана (1841)
- **Key Russian works:** Пан Халявський (1840), Шельменко trilogy
- **Shevchenko connection:** Epistolary friendship; Shevchenko dedicated poem "До Основ'яненка" (1839) and created illustrations for Kvitka's works.
- **French translation:** "Сердешна Оксана" published in French in Paris, 1854.
- **Buried:** Холодногірське cemetery, Kharkiv; grave moved in 1930s when cemetery destroyed.

## Slobozhanshchyna Context (Verified)

- Settled by Cossacks fleeing Polish oppression in 17th century
- 5 Sloboda Cossack regiments: Kharkiv, Sumy, Okhtyrka, Izium, Ostrohozk
- Kharkiv University founded 1805 — local nobles raised 400,000 rubles
- Loss of Cossack autonomy under Catherine II (1765 — regiments converted to hussars)
- Kvitka family among largest landowners of the region
- Skovoroda connection: philosopher was guest of Kvitka family

## Literary Analysis Points

- **Two styles:** Burlesque satire (Конотопська відьма, Сватання на Гончарівці) vs. Sentimental prose (Маруся, Сердешна Оксана)
- **Pseudonym function:** "Грицько" = folk mask; "Основ'яненко" = geographic identity; nobleman writing AS simple person
- **Language advocacy:** Letter to Smorodsky — "wrote Marusya to prove our language can move to tears"
- **Polevoy debate:** Russian critic denied Ukrainian as literary language; Marusya was Kvitka's creative response
- **Kvitka vs Gogol:** Both from Slobozhanshchyna/Poltavshchyna, but Kvitka chose Ukrainian, Gogol chose Russian
- **Kvitka and Shevchenko:** Epistolary bridge Kharkiv-Petersburg, generational handoff
