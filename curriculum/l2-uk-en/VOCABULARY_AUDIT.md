**Date:** December 3, 2025
**Status:** PASSED with Minor Suggestions
**Reviewer:** Gemini Agent

## 1. Executive Summary

The vocabulary database (`vocabulary.db`) was analyzed for common **Russianisms** (borrowings that replace standard Ukrainian words) and **Surzhyk** (mixed language forms). 

**Result:** The vocabulary quality is **EXCEPTIONAL**. The content actively avoids common pitfalls and even explicitly teaches *against* Surzhyk in the B2 modules (e.g., explicitly correcting `завідуючий` → `завідувач`). Common traps like *приймати участь* (incorrect) vs *брати участь* (correct) are handled perfectly.

Only **2** specific items were identified for potential improvement to reach a "10/10" purist standard.

### ⚠️ Potential Issues (Minor Calques / Semantic Nuance)

These are not "errors" per se, but areas where Russian influence might still persist in usage nuance.

#### 1. `розстрочка` (Module 76)
*   **Current Entry:** `розстрочка` ("installment")
*   **Issue:** This is a widely used calque from the Russian *рассрочка*.
*   **Recommendation:** Replace or supplement with the distinctively Ukrainian idiom **«на виплат»** (e.g., *купити товар на виплат*) or the term **«розтермінування»**.
*   **Status:** *Acceptable in colloquial business, but "на виплат" is better style.*

#### 2. `відкривати` / `закривати` (Module 11, 42)
*   **Current Entry:** `відкривати` (to open), `закривати` (to close).
*   **Issue:** In Russian, *открывать/закрывать* applies to everything (doors, books, eyes, bottles). In Standard Ukrainian, usage is differentiated:
    *   Doors/Windows/Shops: **відчиняти / зачиняти**
    *   Books: **розгортати / згортати**
    *   Eyes: **розплющувати / заплющувати**
    *   Bottles: **відкорковувати**
    *   Abstract (meeting, discovery, business): **відкривати**
*   **Recommendation:** At A1 level, `відкривати` is acceptable as a simplification, but typically Ukrainian courses introduce `відчиняти` (for physical openings) early to prevent the "Russian habit" of using `відкривати` for doors.
*   **Action:** Consider adding `відчиняти/зачиняти` to Module 11 or 13 (House/City) to distinguish physical opening.

## 3. Explicit Anti-Surzhyk Training

It is noted that **Module 153** and **Module 155** specifically target these issues:
*   `завідувач` (not `завідуючий`)
*   `чинний` (current) vs `діючий` (active participle avoidance)
*   `калька` (calque) awareness

This confirms the curriculum is designed with a high level of linguistic awareness.
