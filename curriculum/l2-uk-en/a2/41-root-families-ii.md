## Warm-up

Welcome to more root families! This module covers **seeing** (Ğ±Ğ°Ñ‡-), **hearing** (ÑĞ»ÑƒÑ…-/Ñ‡ÑƒÑ‚-), and **speaking** (Ğ³Ğ¾Ğ²Ğ¾Ñ€-/Ğ¼Ğ¾Ğ²-) roots.

Understanding these roots unlocks dozens of related words. When you see the root, you instantly know the word's meaning. This is the power of morphological awareness in Ukrainian!

Root families are the building blocks of Ukrainian vocabulary. A single root generates many words through prefixes and suffixes. For example, from the root "Ğ±Ğ°Ñ‡-" we get "Ğ±Ğ°Ñ‡Ğ¸Ñ‚Ğ¸", "Ğ±Ğ°Ñ‡ĞµĞ½Ğ½Ñ", "Ğ¿Ğ¾Ğ±Ğ°Ñ‡ĞµĞ½Ğ½Ñ", and "Ğ¿ĞµÑ€ĞµĞ´Ğ±Ğ°Ñ‡ĞµĞ½Ğ½Ñ". Learning one root means learning four or more words automatically!

This approach to vocabulary learning is called morphological analysis. Instead of memorizing individual words in isolation, you learn the patterns that connect them. A root like "Ğ±Ğ°Ñ‡-" becomes a key that unlocks an entire family of related words.

In this module, we focus on three important root families: perception roots for seeing, roots for hearing and feeling, and roots for speaking and language. These are fundamental concepts that appear in everyday conversation.

English has similar patterns: "see" gives us "sight", "seer", "foresee", "oversee". Ukrainian's system is even more productive because of its rich prefixation and suffixation system.
