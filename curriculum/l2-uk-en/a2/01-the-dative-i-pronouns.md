## Introduction

Welcome to the sixth Ukrainian case: the **Dative** (–¥–∞–≤–∞–ª—å–Ω–∏–π –≤—ñ–¥–º—ñ–Ω–æ–∫). After mastering Nominative, Accusative, Genitive, Locative, and Vocative, the Dative case completes your core understanding of how Ukrainian nouns and pronouns function. The Dative case is primarily used to mark the **indirect object** ‚Äî the recipient of an action or the person experiencing a state.

In this module, you will learn how to use personal pronouns in the Dative case (to me, to you, to him, etc.). You will discover that many common Ukrainian expressions, such as ¬´I like,¬ª ¬´I need,¬ª and descriptions of physical or emotional states (¬´I am cold,¬ª ¬´I am bored¬ª), require the Dative case. This shift in perspective ‚Äî from being the active ¬´doer¬ª to the ¬´experiencer¬ª ‚Äî is a key milestone in reaching A2 proficiency.

## Presentation

### The Indirect Object and the Experiencer

In English, we often use prepositions like ¬´to¬ª or ¬´for¬ª to show who receives an action: ¬´I give a book **to him**.¬ª In Ukrainian, this relationship is shown by the Dative case. But the Dative also covers many situations where English uses a simple subject:
- **Likes:** ¬´**To me** is pleasing music¬ª (I like music)
- **Needs:** ¬´**To her** is necessary rest¬ª (She needs to rest)
- **States:** ¬´**To them** is cold¬ª (They are cold)

The question words for the Dative case are **–∫–æ–º—É?** (to whom?) and **—á–æ–º—É?** (to what?).
