The module states: *"Ми ніколи не кажемо «Привіт, мій друже!». Це помилка."* and *"Якщо ви скажете «мій друже», українці вас зрозуміють. Але це звучить як переклад з англійської мови."*

"Друже мій" is more common in emotional exclamations, but "мій друже" is natural Ukrainian, not an anglicism. Teaching learners that "мій друже" is a mistake will cause them to "correct" classic Ukrainian literature.

**Error B — CRITICAL: Claims only two declension groups exist (omits мішана група).**
The module states: *"Українська мова розділяє чоловічі професії на дві групи: тверда та м'яка."*

Ukrainian II declension has THREE groups: тверда, м'яка, and мішана. Every textbook consulted teaches all three (Litvinova Grade 6 p.159, Avramenko Grade 6 p.93-96, Zabolotny Grade 6 p.102). The mixed group has distinct vocative rules (стороже, Довбуше vs. товаришу, читачу). Claiming only two groups exist is factually wrong and will confuse learners when they encounter the mixed group later. The individual vocative FORMS taught are correct (лікарю = soft ✓, інженере = hard ✓), but the grammatical framework is incomplete.

**Error C — Minor: Overstatement about Russian losing the vocative.**
The module states: *"modern Russian has entirely lost this grammatical case."*
Russian retains vocative remnants (Боже, Господи) and has developed colloquial truncated vocatives (мам, пап, Кать). "Entirely lost" is an overstatement. More accurate: Russian has lost the vocative as a productive grammatical category.
