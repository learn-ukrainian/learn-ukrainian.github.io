**[LINGUISTIC ACCURACY / PEDAGOGICAL QUALITY] [MAJOR]**
Location: §1 — declension rules paragraph: "Masculine nouns ending in a soft consonant або «й» take the ending -ем або -єм. Наприклад, водій (driver) стає водієм, а лікар стає лікарем. [...] Some nouns ending in «-ар» have a soft declension, so секретар (secretary) becomes секретарем."
Issue: Presenting лікар as an example of "ending in a soft consonant" is misleading. The final -р in лікар is not audibly soft in the nominative form. Learners cannot predict whether an -ар noun takes -ом or -ем from the form alone (contrast: бухгалтер → бухгалтером [hard] vs. лікар → лікарем [soft group]). Kravtsova Grade 4, p.58 explicitly states these require dictionary verification. Grouping лікар with водій (where softness is obvious from й) creates a false analogy.
Fix: Separate лікар from the soft-consonant rule; present -ар/-яр nouns as a special note requiring memorization.
