## Findings

**[ENGAGEMENT] [MAJOR]**
Location: Multiple sections — "Це найпростіше правило в українській мові", "Це дуже легке правило, яке працює завжди", "Це дуже просте правило для всіх слів", "Їх дуже легко запам'ятати", "Тепер ви можете вільно говорити з друзями!"
Issue: Five instances of generic "it's so easy!" reassurance. This is patronizing, repetitive, and adds nothing. A learner struggling with case endings doesn't want to be told how simple it is — they want clear explanations and practice.
Fix: Remove or replace with substantive observations. Keep one light encouragement at most.

**[LINGUISTIC ACCURACY] [MAJOR]**
Location: Instrumental section — "Ці слова втрачають голосний звук перед закінченням."
Issue: Factually misleading. The irregular forms (дітьми, людьми, кіньми, гістьми) take a different archaic ending (-ми/-ьми) rather than the regular (-ами/-ями). This is not a vowel-loss process — it's a completely different morphological ending. Teaching A2 learners an incorrect mechanism creates confusion when they encounter other patterns.
Fix: Replace with accurate, simple explanation: these words take the special short ending -ми.

**[ENGAGEMENT] [MINOR]**
Location: Locative section — "Автомобілі їздять на вулицях."
Issue: Slightly unnatural. For movement through streets, Ukrainian prefers "по вулицях" (confirmed by Антоненко-Давидович's examples: "крокують по вулицях міста"). "На вулицях" works for static location, not vehicles driving. Since this example appears under the "на" subsection, a static-location example would be better.
Fix: Replace with a naturally "на"-governed example.
