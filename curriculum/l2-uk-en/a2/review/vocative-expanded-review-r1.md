### 6. Factual Errors in Grammar Claims

**CRITICAL — суддя vocative is WRONG:**
The module states: *«Ми кажемо «пане суддю» (Mr. Judge) або «пані суддю» (Madam Judge).»*
VESUM confirms the vocative of суддя is **судде** (for both masculine and feminine: `noun:anim:m:v_kly` = судде, `noun:anim:f:v_kly` = судде). The form суддю is the **accusative** (v_zna), NOT the vocative. This teaches learners a wrong form.

**MAJOR — донька → донечко conflation:**
The module states: *«Слово «донька» (daughter) у кличному відмінку стає формою «донечко» (daughter).»*
VESUM confirms the vocative of донька is **донько** (`noun:anim:f:v_kly` = донько). Донечко is the vocative of the *diminutive* донечка. The sentence as written implies донька's own vocative is донечко, which is factually wrong. The intended chain is донька → донечка (diminutive) → донечко (vocative), but the text skips the middle step.

**MAJOR — Soft/hard consonant rule is oversimplified and potentially confusing:**
The module states: *"If a masculine profession ends in a soft consonant, it takes the ending -ю"* (listing лікарю, секретарю) and *"If a masculine profession ends in a hard consonant, it takes the ending -е"* (listing інженере). Both лікар and інженер end in the consonant -р, yet take different endings. A learner cannot determine from the nominative form alone which group a noun belongs to. The added note "Nouns ending in -р that take soft endings follow this rule" is circular. This needs a clearer pedagogical framing — e.g., "These endings depend on the noun's declension group (hard or soft), which you learn with each word."
