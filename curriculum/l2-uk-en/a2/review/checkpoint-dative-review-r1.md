## Findings

**[LINGUISTIC ACCURACY] [MAJOR]**
Location: Part 1, "Дієслово «подобатися»" subsection — "The object that causes the sympathy is the grammatical subject in the nominative case"
Issue: English false friend. "Sympathy" in English means "співчуття" (compassion/pity), NOT "симпатія" (liking/attraction). English-speaking learners will misunderstand this sentence. The intended meaning is "the thing/person that is liked."
Fix: Replace "causes the sympathy" with "is liked."

**[PEDAGOGICAL QUALITY] [MAJOR]**
Location: Part 1, "Давальний чи Знахідний?" subsection — "The accusative case (**знахідний відмінок**) shows the direct object that receives the physical impact of an action."
Issue: "Physical impact" is misleading. Many accusative verbs involve no physical impact: `бачити кого` (to see), `чути кого` (to hear), `знати кого` (to know), `слухати кого` (to listen to). This overgeneralization will confuse learners when they encounter these verbs.
Fix: Replace "receives the physical impact of an action" with "is directly affected by the action."

## Verdict: REVISE

Two major English metalanguage errors that will mislead English-speaking learners. Ukrainian linguistic content is clean (10/10 on all VESUM-verified forms), but the English explanations contain a false friend ("sympathy" ≠ "симпатія") and a misleading claim ("physical impact" for accusative). Both are straightforward fixes.
