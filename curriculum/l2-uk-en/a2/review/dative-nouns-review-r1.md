## Findings

**[PEDAGOGICAL QUALITY] [MAJOR]**
Location: Feminine section, first paragraph — "Діти подарували квіти **вчительці**. *(The children gifted flowers to the teacher.)*"
Issue: вчителька → вчительці involves к→ц consonant alternation, but this example appears BEFORE the consonant alternation rule is explained (next paragraph). An A2 learner seeing this form without explanation creates a pedagogical gap — they may memorize it without understanding why к changed to ц, or be confused when the rule is taught next and realize an earlier example already used it silently.
Fix: Replace with a non-alternation feminine dative example (e.g., бабусі from бабуся — no velar alternation). Move вчительці to the consonant alternation section where it belongs.

**[PLAN ADHERENCE / STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY] [MAJOR]**
Location: End of feminine section — paragraph starting "The Dative case indicates the indirect object — the person or thing to whom or for whom an action is performed."
Issue: This paragraph teaches the Subject + Verb + Dative + Accusative pattern, which is §4's core content ("Давальний відмінок у реченні"). Placing it here creates redundancy — §4 opens with nearly identical content ("The most common use of the Dative case is to indicate the indirect object of a sentence."). The feminine section should focus on feminine forms, not general dative syntax.
Fix: Replace the theory paragraph with a brief transitional sentence that frames the examples as feminine dative practice, not a new grammar concept.

**[ENGAGEMENT & TONE] [MINOR]**
Location: Masculine section, -єві paragraph — "sounds very elegant and is widely used in both formal and everyday contexts to emphasize respect"
Issue: Florid and vague. "Elegant" and "emphasize respect" are subjective claims without evidence. The same paragraph later says "This creates a beautiful, rhythmic flow" — similarly promotional.
Fix: Trim to factual claims.

**[ENGAGEMENT & TONE] [MINOR]**
Location: Masculine section, first paragraph on -ові — "It sounds distinctly Ukrainian and clearly marks the Dative case, preventing confusion with the Genitive case."
Issue: While factually grounded (the -ові ending IS distinctly Ukrainian and does disambiguate from genitive -у), the phrasing "sounds distinctly Ukrainian" is promotional. Rephrase to be factual.
Fix: Rephrase to focus on the practical disambiguation benefit.

**[VOCABULARY COVERAGE] [MINOR]**
Location: Entire module
Issue: Recommended vocabulary "відміна" (declension) and "одержувач" (recipient) from the plan never appear in Ukrainian in the module. English equivalents "declension" and "recipient" are used instead.
Fix: Introduce "відміна" in §1 when first mentioning declension groups, and "одержувач" in §4 when discussing indirect objects.
