## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1 (CRITICAL — Factual Error in Activities): "на метро" Marked as Wrong

**Location:** Activities lines 24, 334, 606 (3 separate items)

**Problem:** The module teaches that for indeclinable transport words, only the bare form is correct (e.g., «Я їду метро»). It marks «на метро» as a wrong answer in multiple activities. However, «їхати на метро» is a perfectly standard, widely-used Ukrainian construction. Both «їду метро» and «їду на метро» are grammatically acceptable.

Specifically:
- Fill-in item (line 19-25): «Він швидко дістався на вокзал _____.» — marks "на метро" as incorrect
- Error-correction (line 327-336): «Він дуже часто їздить метром на роботу.» — "на метро" listed as wrong option
- Quiz (line 599-609): «Ми подорожуємо на метро.» — marked as wrong answer

**Impact:** A2 learners who correctly say «їду на метро» in real life will be told they're wrong. This directly undermines trust in the curriculum.

**Fix:** Accept both «метро» and «на метро» as correct for indeclinable transport. The ONLY incorrect forms are *метром* and *таксією* (false declension). Rewrite the table at line 84-89 to show both acceptable patterns.

### Issue 2 (CRITICAL — Naturalness): Questionable Collocations Taught as Standard

**Location:** Content lines 114-115, 120-121; Activity line 367

**Problem:** Two collocations overgeneralize the "bare instrumental = tool" pattern to contexts where native speakers use prepositions:

(a) «працювати потужним комп'ютером» (line 114) — Native speakers say «працювати на комп'ютері» (locative) or «працювати за комп'ютером» (behind). A computer is not a hand-wielded tool. The example «Мій старший брат цілий день ефективно працює новим комп'ютером» (line 120) would sound unnatural to most Ukrainians.

(b) «розмовляти мобільним телефоном» (line 115) — Native speakers overwhelmingly say «розмовляти по телефону». The bare instrumental form is bookish/unusual.

(c) The error-correction activity (line 367) asks learners to "fix" «Я щодня працюю з комп'ютером» → «комп'ютером», but the actually natural form is «працюю на комп'ютері».

**Impact:** Teaching these collocations makes learners sound unnatural while believing they sound native.

**Fix:** Replace «працювати комп'ютером» with a genuine instrumental-of-means verb like «користуватися комп'ютером» (which is already taught later and is correct). Replace «розмовляти телефоном» with «дзвонити телефоном» or simply note that «по телефону» is the everyday form while «телефоном» shows the grammatical pattern.

**Problem:** Across sections «Вступ: Без прийменника «з»», «Транспорт і рух», «Інструменти та знаряддя», and «Питання та відповіді», the same two-block pattern repeats identically:

6 Словник blocks and 7 Приклади blocks use this exact same format. The visual monotony is severe — reading the module feels like scrolling through an AI-generated template.

**Fix:** Vary presentation formats across sections. Use:

- Inline running text with embedded examples for section «Інструменти та знаряддя» 
- A paragraph-style narrative with tools woven in for section «Питання та відповіді» (the body parts subsection already hints at this)

### Issue 4 (SIGNIFICANT — Lesson Quality): Overly Verbose/Formal English for A2

**Location:** Lines 174, 160, 176-178, 216, 236

**Problem:** Multiple English paragraphs use academic, verbose prose inappropriate for A2 learners:

- Line 174: "As you naturally progress to building significantly more complex sentences in Ukrainian, it becomes absolutely crucial to clearly understand which specific questions trigger which underlying grammatical structures." — 34 words before reaching the actual teaching point.
- Line 160: "notably including nouns that uniquely only exist in the plural form (known grammatically as pluralia tantum)" — Latin grammatical terminology for A2 learners.
- Line 216: "Whenever you want to express the idea of 'to use an object,' 'to operate a device,' or 'to utilize a service,' Ukrainian grammar strictly requires this specific verb followed immediately by the Instrumental case." — Excessive formality.
- Line 236: "This conceptually simple, everyday story perfectly demonstrates just how foundational and ubiquitous the prepositionless Instrumental case is for accurately describing daily Ukrainian reality." — AI summary voice.

Excessive intensifiers throughout: "absolutely", "unequivocally", "uniformly", "fundamentally", "ubiquitous", "incredibly", "critically."

**Fix:** Rewrite English passages in simple, warm B1-level English. Cut sentences to <20 words where possible. Remove Latin terminology. Replace intensifiers with concrete examples.

### Issue 5 (SIGNIFICANT — Richness): Missing Cultural Callouts and Persona

**Location:** Module-wide

**Problem:** 
(a) Only 1 `[!culture]` callout (line 74, нічний поїзд). Richness audit requires 3. The section «Інструменти та знаряддя» is a natural home for Ukrainian craft culture (вишивка, різьба по дереву, гончарство) but has no cultural callout.

(b) The plan specifies `persona: Hutsul Craftsman` but the module contains zero Hutsul references. The Hutsul people are famous for woodcarving, embroidery, and metalwork — perfectly aligned with a "tools and instruments" module. This is a missed opportunity that would simultaneously fix the persona gap AND the cultural richness gap.

**Fix:** Add 2 `[!culture]` callouts: one in section «Інструменти та знаряддя» about Hutsul woodcarving tools (різець, стамеска) and one in section «Практика та підсумок» about Ukrainian вишивка tradition. Weave Hutsul Craftsman voice into section transitions.
