## Findings

### No critical or error-level issues found.

### SUGGESTION: "без кофеїну" — verify this is natural Ukrainian

**Location:** Section 3, point 3: "без кофеїну"

This is potentially a calque from English "decaf / caffeine-free." Ukrainian does use "без кофеїну" (VESUM confirms кофеїну as Gen. of кофеїн), but the more natural supermarket context might be "без цукру" (which is already listed). Keep both — just noting for the writer to verify naturalness with RAG/style guide during build.

### SUGGESTION: "від кашлю" vs "проти кашлю" — mention both

**Location:** Section 2, point 3: "ліки від кашлю"

Both "від кашлю" and "проти кашлю" are natural Ukrainian. The plan uses "від" consistently, which is correct and simpler for A2. Just noting that "проти" could appear in a note or activity as an alternative — learners will encounter both in pharmacies.

### SUGGESTION: Section 3 word target may be tight

**Location:** Section 3 "В аптеці та повсякденне здоров'я" — 550 words

This section covers pharmacy dialogue + health vocabulary + consolidation of both scenarios. 550 words for three distinct sub-topics may feel rushed. The writer should ensure the consolidation part isn't sacrificed. If needed, redistribute ~50 words from Section 1 (750→700) to Section 3 (550→600).

## Dialogue Situation Review

The three-scenario structure (ринок → лікар → аптека) is excellent:

**Strengths:**
- Natural progression: buying food → getting sick → visiting doctor → going to pharmacy
- Each scenario motivates different genitive patterns: quantity (кілограм помідорів), absence (немає температури), source/cause (від кашлю)
- Speakers have clear roles with realistic motivations
- The market scene includes natural touches: vendor offers tastes, suggests alternatives

**One concern:** The dialogue_situations field describes all three in one entry. For build clarity, consider whether this should be split into three separate entries (one per scenario). The current single-entry format works for planning but the writer will need to produce three distinct dialogues.
