# Дослідження: I Think That...

## State Standard Reference
§4.4.2: "складнопідрядні речення зі сполучником що: Викладач сказав, що я маю багато працювати. Я знаю, що ми зробили це правильно"
Alignment: Directly addresses the A2 requirement to form complex declarative sentences using "що", specifically highlighting the syntactic and punctuation requirement (the mandatory comma). For §2 (Catalogue A), it covers the communicative intention of expressing a personal stance, agreement, and disagreement.

## Cultural Hooks
1. Sincerity (щирість) is often prioritized over superficial politeness (ввічливість) in Ukrainian culture. This means Ukrainians may express opinions or disagreement more directly than English speakers, making the use of specific linguistic softeners essential to maintain harmony in formal contexts.
2. The misuse of the verb "рахувати" (to count) instead of "вважати" (to consider) is a widespread calque from the Russian "считать". Modern, decolonized Ukrainian instruction explicitly separates these concepts.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **"Я рахую, що..." instead of "Я вважаю, що..."**: A common calque. "Рахувати" is strictly for numbers (1, 2, 3), while "вважати" is for holding an opinion.
2. **Missing comma before "що"**: English speakers often drop the comma before "that" (e.g., "I know that..."), but in Ukrainian, the comma before "що" in complex sentences is an absolute syntactic rule (e.g., "Я знаю, що...").
3. **Gender mismatch with "згоден/згодна"**: Learners often forget that this short-form adjective must match the gender of the speaker. A female speaker must say "Я згодна", not "Я згоден".

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Immersion**: This is a Band 2 module (60-75% Ukrainian). Use English strictly for explaining abstract grammar concepts (like the conceptual difference between a mental process and holding an opinion), but keep all examples, dialogues, and practice instructions in Ukrainian.
- **Word Target**: 3000 words minimum. Ensure each H3 concept has sufficient depth, examples, and cultural context to reach this organically.
- **Sentences**: Keep Ukrainian sentences under 15 words to maintain A2 readability.
- **Decolonized Framing**: Teach the "рахувати/вважати" distinction as a matter of precise Ukrainian semantics and richness, without anchoring the explanation to Russian.
