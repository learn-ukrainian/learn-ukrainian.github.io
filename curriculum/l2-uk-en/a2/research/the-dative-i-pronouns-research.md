# Дослідження: The Dative I — Pronouns

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.2.3: "особа або предмет, для яких (на користь чи на шкоду) відбувається дія: Подарувати книжку студентові... суб’єкт кількісної вікової ознаки: Дідусеві вісімдесят років."
Alignment: This module introduces the Dative case for pronouns (recipient) and age expressions. Note: The standard classifies "Мені подобається" under §4.2.2.1 (Nominative) as "об’єкт зацікавлення", but this module teaches the *Dative pronoun* component of that structure.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Hospitality & Offering**: Ukrainian hospitality often involves urging guests to eat/drink. The phrase "Мені приємно" (It is pleasant to me) is a standard polite response to introductions or compliments.
2. **Gift Giving Taboos**: Giving gifts is huge, but never hand them across a threshold (через поріг) — it's bad luck. Also, flowers must be an odd number (3, 5, 7) for living people; even numbers are for funerals.
3. **"Bread and Salt"**: The ultimate symbol of welcome. You give bread and salt *to* guests (гостям).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Subject vs. Object with "Like"**: Learners say *"Я подобаю це"* (English structure) instead of *"Мені подобається це"* (It pleases me).
   *Correction*: Drill the inversion: Object is Subject, Person is Dative.
2. **Case Confusion (Acc vs Dat)**: Using Dative for direct objects (*"Я бачу тобі"*) or Accusative for indirect objects (*"Дай це мене"*).
   *Correction*: "Give TO me" = Dative. "See ME" = Accusative.
3. **"Help" + Case**: English "help me" (Direct Object). Ukrainian *"допомагати мені"* (Indirect Object/Dative).
   *Correction*: Treat "help" as "give help TO someone".

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Pedagogy Alert**: The Standard puts "I like" (Nom) and "I feel cold" (Impersonal/B1) in different buckets. For A2 learners, group them by the *Pronoun Form* (Мені...), not the syntactic structure.
- **Scaffolding**: Visual aid for "Subject -> Dative" transformation is critical for *подобатися*.
- **Persona**: Use the "Cultural Guide" persona to explain the "threshold" superstition when teaching "give to..." examples.
