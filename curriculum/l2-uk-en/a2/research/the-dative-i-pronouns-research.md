## Cultural Hooks

1. **Age expression idiom**: "Мені двадцять років" — English speakers find this backwards (literally "to me twenty years"). Every Ukrainian knows their age this way; it's the most common dative expression in daily life. Opens the module with a relatable surprise.
2. **Поріг threshold taboo**: "Не через поріг!" — Ukrainian superstition forbids shaking hands or passing objects across a doorstep (пов'язано зі смертю, межею між світами). Common in everyday interaction; a guest at the door will step inside before accepting anything. Grounded in real social behavior, not invented.
3. **Hospitality formula**: "Вам каву чи чай?" — offering coffee or tea to any guest is a strong Ukrainian norm. The formal Вам (vs. informal тобі) demonstrates the register distinction naturally. Learners can immediately use this in role-play.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Я подобаю» (Nominative SVO calque)** → Correct: «Мені подобається музика» — English "I like music" maps subject→verb→object; Ukrainian inverts to Dative experiencer + Nominative subject. The music does the pleasing.
2. **«Я треба» / «Я потрібно»** → Correct: «Мені треба», «Мені потрібно» — Learners treat "I need" as a verb with a subject; Ukrainian uses impersonal predicates with the needing person in Dative.
3. **«Дякую вас» (Accusative by English analogy)** → Correct: «Дякую вам» — "Thank you" takes a direct object in English but Dative in Ukrainian. Same for допомагати (дoпоможи їй, NOT її).
4. **Nominative/Dative pronoun confusion for 3rd person**: «він/вона» vs. «йому/їй» — learners sometimes use Nominative where Dative is needed: «дзвони він» → correct «дзвони йому».

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present 3 authentic Ukrainian sentences without explanation — «Мені холодно», «Їй подобається ця пісня», «Нам треба допомога». Ask: "What do мені, їй, нам have in common? What do you notice about word order?" Let learners notice the dative forms and the inversion before revealing the paradigm. Mirrors the textbook approach in Grade 5 Golub where learners identify patterns in invitation formulae (p. 202) before practicing them.
- **Best context**: A café/restaurant scene — ordering, offering, requesting. «Що вам принести?», «Мені каву, будь ласка», «Йому чай». Grounded, immediately useful, forces all pronouns naturally.
- **Contrastive pairs**: 
  - я люблю музику (I love music — verb takes Accusative) vs. мені подобається музика (I like music — Dative inversion)
  - Я дзвоню (I call — Nominative subject) vs. дзвони йому (call him — Dative object)
  - English: "I need" (subject verb) vs. Ukrainian: мені треба (Dative + predicate)

## Notes for Content Writing

- This is A2-001 — the learner's FIRST A2 module. Keep English scaffolding generous (45-65% Ukrainian per band rules). Every Ukrainian sentence needs translation support.
- The pronoun table (Nom → Dat) is the module's anchor; put it early and reference it repeatedly.
- Avoid abstract nouns (стан, почуття) per A2 constraints — say «мені холодно», not «стан холоду».
- Age expressions («Мені двадцять років») can serve as the discovery hook for Section 1 — it's surprising, memorable, and immediately testable.
- «Вам» for formal address must be flagged explicitly — learners use the café and service industry as their first real-world testing ground.
- Max 15 words per Ukrainian sentence throughout.
