# Дослідження: Introduction to Gerunds

## State Standard Reference

**A2 mapping has no gerund entry.** Gerunds (дієприслівниковий зворот) are formally introduced at **B1**, under §4.4.2 (Complex Simple Sentences):

§4.4.2: "просте речення з дієприслівниковим зворотом: *Повернувшись з екскурсії, ми смачно пообідали в ресторані*" (line 2375, State Standard 2024)

**Alignment:** This module (a2-075) is explicitly tagged `bridge_to: B1` and is the penultimate A2 module before the final exam. Introducing gerunds here as a *recognition-first* bridge topic is pedagogically sound and aligned with the State Standard's B1 placement. Content must emphasise passive recognition over active production — this is not a full B1 treatment.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **State Standard canonical example**: "Повернувшись з екскурсії, ми смачно пообідали в ресторані" — the B1 State Standard 2024 uses a past gerund (*повернувшись*) as its sole sentence illustration. This is the official, authoritative example for this construction in Ukrainian educational policy.

2. **Grade 7 textbook literary sources** (Litvinova 2024, §20): Authentic literary gerund examples include *"Читаю смакуючи"* (М. Дочинець), *"Відчувши небезпеку, кенгуру мчать як вітер"* (А. Шклярський), *"посміхнувшись відповіла пані Рута"* (О. Росич) — all from Ukrainian literature and well within A2 learner comprehension range.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Forming *прочитаючи* from a perfective verb** → correct form: *прочитавши*. Avramenko 2024 (Grade 7) uses exactly this error in a test question: "Неправильно утворено форму дієприслівника: прочитаючи" — confirming this is the #1 formation mistake. Cause: learner applies the more familiar -ючи suffix without checking verb aspect.

2. **Dangling gerund (subject mismatch)** → "Читаючи книгу, телефон задзвонив" is non-standard. Litvinova 2024 §20 confirms this rule. Cause: calque from English *"Having read the book, the phone rang"* and Russian equivalents. The self-check rule ("who does both actions?") is the standard pedagogical fix.

3. **Using *-ючий/-ачий* as noun modifiers** → e.g., *\*працююча людина* instead of *людина, яка працює*. These are Russianisms that entered Ukrainian via Soviet administrative language. The correct Ukrainian pattern is a relative clause with *який*. This is a language decolonisation point — must be framed as a Ukrainian-centric restoration, not as "different from Russian."

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Recognition-first framing is mandatory**: The plan correctly prioritises passive recognition. Phase B content should present gerunds in authentic Ukrainian texts first, then move to controlled production. Do not overload learners with production drills.
- **Textbook level**: Grade 7 (Litvinova 2024 §20–21, Avramenko 2024 §55) — these are the authoritative classroom treatments. Align examples and metalanguage accordingly.
- **VESUM tag is `advp`**: The Ukrainian grammatical term is *дієприслівник*, not *герундій*. Always use Ukrainian terminology.
- **Reflexive gerunds**: Litvinova 2024 explicitly notes that -ся is retained after the suffix: *насолодитися → насолодившись*. This is easy to forget — make the rule explicit.
- **Assimilation in past gerunds**: consonant-stem past gerunds undergo assimilation in speech: *занісши → [зан'іш:и]*, *завізши → [завіжши]*. Worth a brief phonetic note.
- **Immersion band**: Module 75 is in the "consolidation" band (M51–70+), target 70–90% Ukrainian. Treat grammar explanations as near-fully Ukrainian with English only in vocabulary tables.
- **Decolonisation framing**: The -ючий/-ачий ban section must not say "unlike Russian." Frame as: "Ukrainian has always preferred relative clauses; the *-ючий* modifier pattern entered through Soviet administrative texts and is being restored to its pre-Soviet norms."
