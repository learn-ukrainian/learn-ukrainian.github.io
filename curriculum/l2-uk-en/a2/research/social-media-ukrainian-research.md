## Cultural Hooks

1. **Telegram as Ukraine's primary information channel**: Since the full-scale Russian invasion (2022), Telegram became the dominant news and emergency alerts platform in Ukraine. Government ministries, regional administrations, and the air-raid alert system operate official Telegram channels. The phrase "читати в телеграмі" is a daily activity for millions of Ukrainians. *(Source: plan notes citing 75% news usage figure; widely attested in Ukrainian media coverage — e.g., RATING Group surveys 2023)*

2. **Instagram as Ukrainian micro-business hub**: "Пишіть у дірект" is a ubiquitous phrase in Ukrainian Instagram commerce. Ukrainian SMEs — artisans, food producers, clothing brands — routinely use Instagram as their primary storefront, replacing traditional websites. This is a documented economic phenomenon specific to Ukrainian small business culture, not universal across all Instagram markets. *(Source: plan notes; attested in Ukrainian business press coverage)*

3. **Textbook attestation of internet vocabulary**: Grade 6 Golub textbook (tier 1) uses "ставить лайки*" with an asterisk note treating "лайк" as a foreign borrowing requiring explanation — confirming this word is actively taught in Ukrainian schools as a known internet term. The same textbook covers "підписник" in a safety/digital literacy context (Grade 6, p. 239). This validates the vocabulary list and the pedagogical approach of treating these as "known but worthy of formal framing."

## Common Learner Errors

1. **сайт vs. сторінка confusion** → "Я знайшов її **сайт** в інстаграмі" should be "**сторінку**" — "сайт" (website) implies a standalone domain; a social media profile is "сторінка." Very common interference from English where both can loosely translate as "profile/page."

2. **Accusative after підписатися**: Learners often use Nominative/Genitive by analogy with other verbs: *"підписатися на **каналу**"* (Gen) or *"на **канал**"* (Acc — correct). Feminine nouns require ending change: "на сторінку" (not "сторінка"). Confusion arises because the target is inanimate and learners haven't consolidated the full Accusative inanimate paradigm for feminine nouns.

3. **Prepositional vs. Accusative with "в/у"**: "в інстаграмі" (location, Locative) vs. "в інстаграм" (direction, Accusative) — learners often default to Locative for all "in + platform" constructions. The difference: "Я бачила це **в інстаграмі**" (saw it there) vs. "Я написала **в інстаграм**" (posted to it).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **дірект**: Use in content as established internet slang with an explicit note that it is an informal borrowing not yet in the standard dictionary. The locative phrase "у дірект" functions as a fixed expression.
- **вподобайка**: GRAC shows only 11 occurrences — treat as a neologism/purist coinage rather than everyday speech. Present it as a localization/cultural curiosity, not the default term. лайк is the dominant everyday form.
- **стрічка**: When used in social media context, explicitly gloss as "стрічка новин / стрічка публікацій" to disambiguate from the general "tape/ribbon" meaning.
- **Immersion level**: Module is A2.6 Consolidation phase — target 70–90% Ukrainian. Vocabulary tables use English column; all prose is Ukrainian only. No mixed sentences.
- **Accusative drill priority**: The plan's core grammar point (підписатися на + Acc) is the highest-value error to address. Dedicate specific exercises to masculine inanimate (канал → на канал, no change) vs. feminine (сторінка → на сторінку, ending changes).
- **Avoid Russian framing**: Do not frame this vocabulary as "Ukrainian equivalents of Russian social media terms." Present Ukrainian digital culture on its own terms — Telegram's role in air-raid alerts and official communications is a distinctly Ukrainian context with no parallel elsewhere.
- **Register tension**: The plan correctly identifies the slang/purism tension. In content, frame вподобайка/світлина as conscious choices by Ukrainians who prefer native vocabulary in localized interfaces — a form of linguistic agency, not just "formal vs. informal."
