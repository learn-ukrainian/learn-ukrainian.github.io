**Suffix Productivity:**
- **-ння**: Extremely high productivity for verbal nouns (читання, життя).
- **-ість**: High productivity for abstract nouns (радість, незалежність).
- **-ач**: High productivity for active agents (слухач, глядач, викладач).

## Cultural Hooks
1.  **"Вороженьки"**: The Ukrainian anthem uses a diminutive suffix `-еньк` even for "enemies" (*вороженьки*), illustrating the language's tendency toward "softening" and emotional nuance, even in conflict.
2.  **Literary Neologisms**: The word **«мрія»** (dream) was coined by writer Mykhailo Starytsky in the 19th century. Before him, Ukrainians used foreign loans. This is a perfect example of conscious *word formation* shaping national identity.
3.  **"Паляниця"**: A shibboleth where the word formation (suffix *-иця*) combined with phonetics creates a unique cultural identifier.

## Common Learner Errors
1.  **Wrong Suffix Calques**: Using `-к` instead of `-ння` for processes (e.g., *розробка* vs *розроблення*).
    *   *Correction*: Focus on `-ння` for active processes.
2.  **Surzhyk Prefixes**: Using *само-* where Ukrainian prefers *авто-* or adjective structures, or Russian *со-* (spelled *спів-* in Ukr) incorrectly.
    *   *Correction*: *співробітник* (coworker) is correct, but *співпадати* is wrong -> *збігатися*.
3.  **Adjective Suffix Confusion**: Mixing `-ний` (general) and `-овий` (material/relation).
    *   *Example*: *лимонний* (lemon flavor) vs *лимоновий* (made of lemon - rare, usually synonymous but stylistically different).
