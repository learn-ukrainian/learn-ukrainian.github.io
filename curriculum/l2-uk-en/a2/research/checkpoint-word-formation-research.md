**Alignment note — IMPORTANT for content writer:** The A2 Standard's §4.3 covers only adjective comparison (§4.3.1) and aspect pairs (§4.3.2). The suffixes -ння, -ість, -ач and denominal adjectives (-ний, -ський) belong to B1 §4.3.3–4.3.6 (lines 2320–2334). The plan deliberately bridges A2→B1 as a checkpoint preview — this is pedagogically sound but the content must frame these B1 suffixes as *introduction/preview*, not A2 review. Label them clearly: "Заглядаємо у B1" or similar.

## Cultural Hooks

**1. Паляниця as shibboleth (VERIFIED — Wikipedia):**
"Паляниця" is a traditional Ukrainian flatbread (пшеничний хліб з горизонтальним надрізом). The word became a wartime shibboleth in 2022 (Russian invasion) because Russian speakers cannot correctly produce Ukrainian "и" — they substitute a soft "і", and pronounce "ц" hard or as "тс". The suffix **-иця** is linguistically significant: it forms feminine nouns denoting objects (паляниця, крамниця, вулиця, криниця). Etymology from "палити" — the baked crust that "burns" on top. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Паляниця

**Usage for class:** Break down паляниця → пал- (root, from палити) + -аниц- + -я. Demonstrate how morphological awareness both reveals meaning *and* is a cultural identifier. The suffix -иця appears in everyday words learners already know: вулиця, крамниця, лікарня... wait — only the -иця ones: вулиця, паляниця, синиця, криниця.

**2. Вороженьки in the Ukrainian national anthem (VERIFIED in VESUM):**
The verse of the Ukrainian anthem ("Ще не вмерла Україна") contains "Наші вороженьки, як роса на сонці, загинуть" — enemies (вороги) softened to "вороженьки" with the diminutive suffix **-еньк-**. This exemplifies how Ukrainian uses diminutive morphology to express irony, emotional distance, and even defiance — softening the word "enemies" while describing their defeat. The suffix -еньк- is highly productive in colloquial Ukrainian (рученьки, серденько, матусенька). VESUM confirmed "вороженько" (noun, diminutive form).

**3. Мрія coined by Михайло Старицький (PARTIALLY VERIFIED):**
Михайло Старицький (1839–1904) — Ukrainian playwright, poet, and cultural figure, one of the founders of professional Ukrainian theatre. He is credited in Ukrainian linguistic tradition with coining the word "мрія" (dream/aspiration) from the root "мріти" (to shimmer, to glimmer as in haze). This is one of the clearest examples of a writer consciously expanding the Ukrainian lexicon through morphological creativity. VESUM confirms "мрія" as a valid Ukrainian noun. **Note:** Wikipedia article on Старицький does not explicitly mention "мрія" — use this hook with pedagogical confidence but hedge slightly: "За народним переказом, Старицький утворив слово «мрія»..."

## Common Learner Errors

**1. співпадати → збігатися (Surzhyk calque)**
Error: "ці факти співпадають" (direct calque from Russian совпадают)
Correct: "ці факти збігаються"
Why: Russian prefix со- → Ukrainian prefix з-/зі- in many verbs, but the root also changes. "Спів-" exists in Ukrainian (співробітник, співпраця) but "співпадати" is a Surzhyk ghost word. VESUM confirms "збігатися" ✅, but "співпадати" is NOT in standard Ukrainian.

**2. -к suffix vs -ння suffix for process nouns**
Error: "розробка технології" in formal/process context
Correct: "розроблення технології" (formal register; -ння is preferred for active processes in literary Ukrainian)
Why: Both forms exist and VESUM accepts both, but учні overuse -к forms (calqued from Russian -ка collocations). The State Standard (B1 §4.3.4) specifically introduces -ння as the productive suffix for formal nominalization. At A2/checkpoint level: introduce the distinction, not enforce it.

**3. Prefix substitution errors (aspect confusion)**
Error: "я вийшов з дому, але не прийшов до школи" (mixing directional prefix semantics)
Correct: Prefixes carry directional meaning — ви- (out), при- (arrival), за- (beginning), від- (departure). Learners conflate prefix aspect function with directional function.
Additional: "переробити" ≠ "зробити знову" — prefix пере- signals repetition/transformation, not mere completion.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **B1 preview framing is essential.** Sections 2–4 of the content outline introduce material that belongs to B1 (§4.3.3–4.3.6). Frame these as "bridges" and "sneak peeks," not A2 review. Use metalanguage like "Це вже рівень B1 — але ви готові!"

2. **Metalanguage in Ukrainian:** The plan calls for introducing префікс, суфікс, корінь in Ukrainian. These are low-frequency GRAC terms (0.11–0.67 IPM). Treat as productive learning tools, not passive vocab — learners will use them to talk *about* language, not in daily communication.

3. **паляниця etymology** is genuinely useful: пали́ти (to burn) + -аниц- + -я. The morphological story (root → suffix → meaning) is one of the clearest demonstrations of Ukrainian word formation in action. Anchor the final workshop here.

4. **вороженьки / anthem hook:** Use the anthem diminutive to show -еньк- is not just "cute speech" but carries tonal/ironic meaning at the national level. Avoid saying "This doesn't exist in other languages" — instead frame it as "Ukrainian uses suffixes to layer emotion."

5. **No Surzhyk forms in examples:** Never include "співпадати," "получати," "любий" (as 'any') in positive examples. Only in error-correction activities.

6. **Immersion target 55–75% (A2 Applied Grammar band):** Grammar metalanguage (prefix/suffix definitions, §references) may be in English. All dialogue examples, cultural hooks, and practice sentences must be 100% Ukrainian.

7. **"Word Family Trees" visual:** The plan calls for root flower diagrams (корінь у центрі + похідні пелюстки). This is a visual learning anchor — reference it explicitly in content and ensure activity YAML can reference a diagram concept.
