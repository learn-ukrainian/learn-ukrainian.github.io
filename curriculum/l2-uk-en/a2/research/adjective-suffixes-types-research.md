## Cultural Hooks
1. **Яворівська забавка (Yavoriv Toy)**: Traditional wooden toys from the Lviv region, crafted primarily from «осика» (aspen wood). They are painted with traditional «вербівка» patterns and were historically believed to drive away evil spirits.
2. **Гуцульський ліжник (Hutsul Lizhnyk)**: A thick, woven blanket made from «овеча вовна» (sheep's wool) in the Carpathian Mountains. It is famous for its warmth, its ceremonial use in weddings, and its therapeutic "biting" texture.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Mutation Failure** → *Прагський* instead of *Празький* — Learners often simply attach the -ськ- suffix to city names without applying the mandatory consonant mutations (г/ж/з -> зьк).
2. **Material Suffix Confusion** → *деревовий* instead of *дерев'яний* — Misapplying the standard -ов- suffix to natural/traditional materials that require -ян-.
3. **Missing Apostrophe** → *деревяний* instead of *дерев'яний* — Forgetting the apostrophe after a labial consonant before 'я'.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize that the consonant mutation rules (like Прага -> Празький) are distinct phonetic features of Ukrainian. Present traditional crafts like the Yavoriv toy and Hutsul lizhnyk as living elements of Ukrainian identity, rather than "rustic" artifacts.
- **Immersion**: This is A2.3 (Band 2: 60-75% Ukrainian). English should ONLY be used to explain the abstract concepts (like the mutation rules and suffix logic). All dialogues, examples, and practice must be in Ukrainian.
