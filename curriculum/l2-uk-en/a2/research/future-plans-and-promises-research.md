# Дослідження: Future Plans and Promises

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.1: "Indicative — expanded: aspect pairs (доконаний/недоконаний), motion verbs (їхати/ходити)" (A2 verb morphology)
Alignment: This module operationalizes §4.2.3.1 by teaching both future formations — compound (буду + infinitive, imperfective) and simple (perfective conjugation) — and the semantic distinction between them in real communicative acts (plans, promises).

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Прислів'я «Обіцянка — цяцянка, а дурневі — радість»** — "A promise is a toy; only a fool rejoices in it." Use to open the module: makes empty promises feel culturally embarrassing, motivating learners to choose the right aspect for genuine commitments.
2. **Прислів'я «Обіцяного три роки ждуть»** — "You wait three years for what's promised." Directly exploits comedic recognition (learners know this pattern universally); excellent for the practice section contrasting буду робити (vague process) vs зроблю (firm deadline).
3. **«Тримати слово»** — the fixed phrase "to keep one's word." Appears in everyday speech (тримати слово / порушити слово). Grounds the aspect distinction in a real cultural value: perfective future = you're staking your word on it.

---

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Я буду зробити»** → **«Я зроблю»** — The single most common error: English speakers map "I will" onto «буду» universally. Must be flagged immediately: «буду» + perfective infinitive is ungrammatical in Ukrainian. Textbook exercises targeting aspect consistently use substitution drills that force learners to pick the correct paradigm.
2. **Aspect mismatch under negation** → Imperfective tends to replace perfective in negated future: «Я не буду читати» (correct for ongoing) but learners write «Я не прочитаю» when they mean process. Correct the direction of the error based on intended meaning.
3. **Motion verb confusion «піду» vs «йтиму»** → The synthetic imperfective future (йтиму, ходитиму) exists but is literary/high register; A2 learners should use compound future for imperfective motion verbs at this stage: «буду йти», not «йтиму».
4. **Overextending «буду»** — Learners avoid conjugating perfective verbs and lean on «буду» for all future contexts because it feels like "I will." The fix is contextual: when result is intended, only the perfective form works. Drills must reward switching.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present two mini-dialogues side by side. Dialogue A: «— Ти прочитаєш книгу? — Так, прочитаю до п'ятниці.» Dialogue B: «— Ти будеш читати книгу? — Так, буду читати щовечора.» Ask learners: *Which speaker is promising a finished result? Which is describing a habit/process?* Let them notice the one-word vs two-word pattern before the rule is stated.
- **Best context**: Weekend planning + making promises to a friend. «Що ти будеш робити у суботу? — Буду прибирати. — А ввечері? — Прийду до тебе.» Real stakes, familiar situation.
- **Contrastive pairs** (for disambiguation drills):
  - буду читати (reading in progress) ↔ прочитаю (will have finished)
  - буду писати (working on it) ↔ напишу (will deliver it)
  - буду чекати (waiting around) ↔ почекаю (wait once = polite request)

## Notes for Content Writing

- Lead with the proverb «Обіцянка — цяцянка» — it immediately frames *why* aspect matters in promises (cultural buy-in before grammar explanation).
- The «Ambitious Startup Founder» persona works well here: founders make pitches («Я зроблю це за три дні»), not vague plans (буду робити). Use business planning as secondary scaffolding for the perfective.
- Avoid «йтиму» form — synthetic imperfective future is out of scope at A2. Stick to compound future for all imperfective verbs.
- Immersion target 50-60% (core grammar band, M16). Grammar theory in English is permitted per A2 quick-ref.
- Max 15 words per Ukrainian sentence; no complex subordinate clauses.
