# Дослідження: Future Plans and Promises

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.1: "утворення складеної форми майбутнього часу дієслів недоконаного виду: буду боротися... майбутній час дієслів доконаного виду: скажу, закричите, поборють."
Alignment: This module addresses the A2 requirement to distinguish and correctly form both the compound future for imperfective verbs and the simple future for perfective verbs.

## Cultural Hooks
1. Proverb: «Обіцянка — цяцянка, а дурневі — радість» (A promise is a toy, but a joy to a fool) — Emphasizes the cultural importance of keeping promises over making empty ones.
2. Proverb: «Обіцяного три роки ждуть» (One waits three years for what is promised) — Highlights expectations and the reality of delayed fulfillment in daily life.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Aspect Mismatch: *Я буду зробити* → *Я зроблю* — Learners try to use "буду" with perfective infinitives because they treat it exactly like English "will".
2. Overuse of «буду» for promises: *Я буду дзвонити* (I will be calling) vs *Я подзвоню* (I will call) — Learners overuse the compound future as a crutch because it avoids conjugating perfective verbs.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Immersion is Band 1 (50-60%). English scaffolding is required for explaining the abstract aspectual distinction between simple and compound future.
- Decolonized framing: Treat the Ukrainian aspectual future system as an elegant mechanism for precision, not merely "different from other languages."
