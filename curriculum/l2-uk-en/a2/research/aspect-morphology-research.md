## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ранковий ритм (Morning Rhythm)**: Ukrainian diaries and social media often narrate mornings as a sequence of completed events: *Встав, вмився, поснідав — і на роботу.* This sequencing pattern is a natural entry point for perfectivity as closure.

2. **Hospitality routine**: The expression *Зараз приготую* (I'll make [it] right now — perfective future) vs *Я завжди готую борщ на свята* (I always make borscht for holidays — imperfective habitual) is a universally recognizable contrast. Ukrainian hospitality culture makes food preparation verbs extremely salient.

3. **Ukrainian saying**: *Взявся за гуж — не кажи, що не дуж* ("If you've taken up the harness, don't say you can't"). The perfective *взявся* (took up, committed) vs imperfective *береться* (is taking up) shows how aspect signals commitment and completion in folk idiom.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **з-/с- spelling before voiceless consonants** → *спитати* (not *зпитати*), *сказати* (not *зказати*) — happens because English has no analogous voicing-assimilation rule at prefix boundaries.
2. **Future tense: буду + infinitive always imperfective** → *Я буду читати* ✓ vs ~~*Я буду прочитати*~~ ✗ — English speakers transfer "will + verb" without aspect distinction; perfective future uses conjugated form *прочитаю*.
3. **Process vs. result confusion in past** → *Я читав цю книгу* (I was reading / read partway) vs *Я прочитав цю книгу* (I read [and finished] it) — English "I read the book" is ambiguous; Ukrainian forces the distinction.
4. **Imperative nuance** → *Читай!* (general advice / ongoing) vs *Прочитай!* (specific task to complete) — learners default to imperfective for all commands, missing urgency or task-specificity.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show a Ukrainian morning-routine mini-text with 8 verbs highlighted. Ask learner: "Which verbs describe finished actions? Which describe ongoing processes?" Let them sort BEFORE naming the grammar. Reveal: sorted groups = pf/impf. *Textbook model*: Zabolotnyi grade 5 p.51 uses exactly this sorting approach — two groups classify words by a hidden property, then a key reveals it.
- **Best context**: Morning routine (вставати/встати + вмиватися/вмитися) and daily task completion (закінчувати/закінчити + вирішувати/вирішити) are the most naturalistic frames for A2.
- **Contrastive pairs for drilling**: Use відкривати/відкрити as the clean template (suffix rule: відкри-ти → відкри-вати), then contrast with suppletive сідати/сісти to show the irregular class explicitly.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Immersion band**: This is A2 module 17 (Core grammar, M01-20) → target 45-65% Ukrainian. Grammar explanation sections may remain majority English.
- **з-/с- rule** needs a dedicated spelling box — it is the highest-frequency error and learners encounter it immediately when forming their first perfectives.
- The pair вмикати/увімкнути is genuinely suppletive (roots differ: вмик- vs увімкн-) — do not present it as a regular suffix derivation.
- Sentences must stay ≤15 words, max 2 clauses (A2 constraint). All example sentences in discovery should use vocabulary from the required list.
