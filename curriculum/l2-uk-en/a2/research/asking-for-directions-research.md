**Alignment:** This module directly satisfies all three standards simultaneously — §3.5 (direction adverbs + public transport), §3.6 (motion verb pairs іти/ходити, їхати/їздити), and §4.2.3.1 (conjugation of all four motion verbs). This is one of the most tightly State Standard-anchored modules in the A2 Practical phase.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Дерибасівська вулиця** — Odesa's famous pedestrian main street, named after Фелікс де Рібас (brother of the city's first governor, Хосе де Рібас). It is a landmark-by-name familiar to all Ukrainians and a natural navigation anchor for dialogues ("іди прямо до Дерибасівської"). Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia.

2. **Одеський театр опери та балету** — described as найстаріший оперний театр України (Ukraine's oldest opera theatre), located at the intersection of Ланжеронівська and Рішельєвська streets. Its address is given by cross-streets, not a number — a classic example of Ukrainian landmark-based navigation rather than numerical address navigation. Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia.

3. **⚠ CRITICAL UPDATE — Одеський трамвай**: As of 13 December 2025, the Odesa tram system has **suspended operations indefinitely**. Free replacement buses now cover tram routes. The plan's reference to "трамвай" as a culturally significant Odesa navigation tool is now outdated for Odesa specifically. **Content must not present Odesa tram as currently operating.** Трамвай remains valid vocabulary for other Ukrainian cities (Kyiv, Lviv, Vinnytsia, Dnipro). Suggested fix: use трамвай as general Ukrainian transport vocabulary, with Odesa dialogues using маршрутка/автобус as the active transport mode.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **йти vs їхати confusion** → Correct distinction: йти = on foot only; їхати = by any vehicle. Common error: "Я йду в Київ" (impossible unless walking). Correct: "Я їду в Київ (автобусом/потягом)." Happens because English "go" covers both, and Polish *iść/jechać* distinction isn't always known to learners.

2. **Unidirectional vs. habitual motion** → Error: "Я йду в школу кожен день." Correct: "Я ходжу в школу кожен день." Learners overgeneralise the unidirectional form because it's introduced first. Rule: іти/їхати = single trip happening now or soon; ходити/їздити = regular/repeated action.

3. **Prefix direction errors: по- vs при-** → Error: "Він приїхав до магазину" (when subject just left home). Correct: "Він поїхав до магазину" (departure). при- signals arrival; по- signals starting movement. Learners confuse these because both translate to English "went/left."

4. **Instrumental with transport** → Error: "їхати на автобусі" (locative — where you are sitting) vs correct "їхати автобусом" (instrumental — means of transport). Both exist but have different emphasis; "їхати автобусом" is the standard direction/transport answer.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Trамвай warning**: Do not write Odesa tram dialogues as if trams are running in 2026. Use маршрутка and автобус for Odesa scenes. Трамвай can appear in a vocabulary table with a note like "поширений в Києві, Львові, Вінниці."
- **Conjugation table placement**: State Standard §4.2.3.1 explicitly lists all four conjugation paradigms (іду/іді/ідеш... ходжу/ходиш... їду/їдеш... їжджу/їздиш...). A clear 4-column comparison table is required in Презентація — this is the central grammar deliverable of the module.
- **Phonetic confusion drill needed**: іду (іти, foot) vs їду (їхати, vehicle) look almost identical in print but differ by і vs ї. Worth a brief drill in Презентація.
- **Prepositions of motion**: до (direction toward, genitive) is the key pattern for asking directions — "Як пройти до театру? / Як доїхати до вокзалу?" Distinguish від preposition for departure ("від зупинки").
- **Register note**: маршрутка is colloquial (розмовний register, as the plan specifies). The formal term is маршрутне таксі (seen in Grade 1 textbook: "маршрутні таксі"), but маршрутка dominates in actual speech (GRAC: 3,896 occurrences).
- **Деколонізація framing**: Direction adverbs прямо, ліворуч, праворуч are fully Ukrainian with no Russian-derived alternatives needed. The motion verb system (іти/ходити, їхати/їздити) is a Ukrainian-specific aspectual distinction that parallels Polish iść/chodzić, jechać/jeździć — frame this as a Slavic feature of Ukrainian, not a comparison to Russian. The Karaman Grade 10 textbook warns explicitly against using відправлятися as a catch-all motion verb (Russian influence: отправляться) — prefer виїжджати, відбувати.
