# Research Notes: At the Doctor (A2 M59)

**Track**: Core A
**Module**: A2 M59 "At the Doctor" (У лікаря)
**Level**: A2 (Late Beginner)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference
According to the **Державний стандарт української мови (ДСТУ 2024)**, this module falls under:
- **§ 3.12. Здоров’я й особиста гігієна** (Health and personal hygiene).
- **Specific sub-themes**: 
  - *самопочуття* (well-being);
  - *хвороби й нездужання* (illnesses and ailments);
  - *візит до лікаря* (visit to the doctor);
  - *аптека* (pharmacy).
- **Grammar alignment**: Use of Imperative mood for medical advice (§ 4.4.2), Dative case for states (§ 4.2.1), and Genitive case with prepositions of movement/location (§ 4.2.1: *до лікаря*, *від лікаря*).

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency
### High-Frequency (Essential)
- **Nouns**: лікар (doctor), лікарня (hospital), аптека (pharmacy), ліки (medicine), рецепт (prescription), температура (temperature), кашель (cough), нежить (runny nose), грип (flu), біль (pain).
- **Body Parts**: голова (head), горло (throat), живіт (stomach), спина (back), рука (hand/arm), нога (foot/leg), зуби (teeth).
- **Verbs**: боліти (to hurt), хворіти/захворіти (to be/get sick), почуватися (to feel), приймати (to take [medicine]), викликати (to call [doctor/ambulance]).
- **Adjectives**: хворий (sick), здоровий (healthy), слабкий (weak).

### Common Collocations (A2 Level)
- **Мене болить...** (My ... hurts) — *Standard construction: Accusative of person + Nominative of body part.*
- **У мене болить...** (I have a pain in ...) — *Common alternative.*
- **Мені погано / Мені краще** (I feel bad / I feel better) — *Dative of state.*
- **Викликати швидку допомогу** (To call an ambulance).
- **Записатися на прийом** (To make an appointment).

## 3. Cultural Hook
- **Сімейний лікар (Family Doctor)**: Ukraine underwent a major healthcare reform in 2017. Now, every citizen signs a "declaration" with a specific family doctor who manages their health and provides referrals.
- **Pharmacies (Аптека)**: Pharmacies are ubiquitous in Ukrainian cities, often operating 24/7 (*Цілодобова аптека*). Pharmacists often provide initial advice for minor symptoms.
- **Traditional Remedies**: While modern medicine is the standard, Ukrainians frequently supplement treatment with herbal teas (lipa, kalyna) and honey, especially for colds.
- **Emergency Number**: **103** is the universal number for *Швидка допомога* (Ambulance).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes
- **"Мене болить" vs. "У мене болить"**: English speakers often struggle with the Accusative "Мене" in "Мене болить голова". It is helpful to explain it as "Head hurts me."
- **Aspect Distinction**: Reinforce the difference between *хворіти* (to be in the state of being sick, Impf.) and *захворіти* (the event of falling ill, Perf.).
- **Dative vs. Accusative**: Students already know Dative pronouns (*Мені*), which are used for general states (*Мені холодно, Мені погано*), but they must distinguish this from the physical pain construction (*Мене болить*).
- **Imperatives**: Since students have mastered the Imperative, this module is an excellent place to practice polite doctor’s orders: *"Пийте багато води," "Приймайте ліки двічі на день."*

## 5. Scope Boundaries
- **IN SCOPE**:
  - Use of **Dative** for subjective feelings (*Мені краще*).
  - Use of **Imperative** for medical advice.
  - **Genitive** with prepositions *до/від* (movement to/from the doctor).
  - **Perfective vs. Imperfective** past/future regarding illness.
  - **Reported speech** to explain symptoms: *"Він сказав, що його болить живіт."*
- **OUT OF SCOPE**:
  - Complex medical terminology (e.g., specialized names of diseases).
  - Passive voice constructions (e.g., "The medicine was prescribed").
  - Advanced anatomical terms (internal organs beyond *живіт, серце*).
  - Instrumental of means for complex medical procedures.
