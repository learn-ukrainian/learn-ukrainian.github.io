# Research Notes: Спорт та фітнес (Sports & Fitness)

**Track**: a2
**Module**: sports-fitness
**Level**: A1/A2 Transition
**Researched**: 2026-02-08

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (2024)**, the topic of "Sports & Fitness" is integrated into the thematic catalogs for the A1 and A2 levels.

### A1 Level Reference (§ 2.4.1.3. "Каталог Б. Тематичний каталог")
> **3.6. Дозвілля, відпочинок:**
> - уподобання (хобі);
> - види спорту і спортивних споруд (стадіон, спортзал, басейн).

### A2 Level Reference (§ 2.4.2.3. "Каталог Б. Тематичний каталог")
> **3.4. Дозвілля, відпочинок:**
> - способи проведення вільного часу;
> - хобі та зацікавлення;
> - види спорту, спортивних споруд і спортивне спорядження;
> - преса, радіо, телебачення, інтернет, соціальні мережі.

Additionally, the standard identifies specific linguistic competencies for describing these activities, particularly the use of the **Instrumental case** for hobbies and activities (§ 2.4.2.4.4.2.2.5.1. "об’єкт дії і стану: ... Вчимося розмовляти українською мовою. [Займатися спортом]").

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

### High-Frequency Items (Essential A1/A2)
*   **Nouns (Objects/Places):** спорт (sport), футбол (football), теніс (tennis), баскетбол (basketball), волейбол (volleyball), йога (yoga), фітнес (fitness), спортзал (gym), стадіон (stadium), басейн (swimming pool), м’яч (ball), команда (team), тренер (coach), змагання (competition), перемога (victory).
*   **Verbs (Actions):** бігати (to run), плавати (to swim), стрибати (to jump), вигравати/виграти (to win), програвати/програти (to lose), тренуватися (to train).

### Common Collocations at A2 Level
*   **займатися спортом** (to do sports/exercise) — *Uses Instrumental case.*
*   **грати в футбол / теніс** (to play football/tennis) — *Uses "в" + Accusative.*
*   **ходити в спортзал / басейн** (to go to the gym/pool) — *Uses "в" + Accusative for destination.*
*   **ходити на фітнес / йогу / тренування** (to go to fitness/yoga/training) — *Uses "на" + Accusative for events/activities.*
*   **робити зарядку** (to do morning exercises/warm-up).

## 3. Cultural Hook

### The Kyiv "Kachalka" (Hydropark)
One of the most unique fitness landmarks in the world is the **"Kachalka" gym** located on the Hydropark island in Kyiv. It is an enormous open-air gym built in the 1970s by volunteers using scrap metal, discarded machinery, and even tank parts. It is free for everyone, operating year-round (even in the snow), and represents a "hardcore" grassroots approach to fitness in Ukraine.

### National Sports Heroes
Sports is a major source of national pride and a key topic for decolonized narratives.
*   **Football:** Andriy Shevchenko (Ballon d'Or winner) and the success of clubs like Dynamo Kyiv and Shakhtar Donetsk.
*   **Boxing:** The Klitschko brothers (Vitaliy and Volodymyr) and contemporary champions like Oleksandr Usyk and Vasyl Lomachenko.
*   **Olympic Pride:** Ukraine consistently performs strongly in gymnastics, wrestling, and track & field.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
*   **"To Play" vs. "To Do":** English uses "play" for almost everything (play football, play tennis), but Ukrainian distinguishes between **грати в** (games with a ball/competitions) and **займатися** (process-based activities like "sport" in general, or yoga).
*   **Case Confusion:**
    *   *Accusative:* Used with "грати в" (грати в теніс).
    *   *Instrumental:* Used with "займатися" (займатися боксом).
    *   *Locative:* Used with "грати на" (playing instruments — a common interference error).
*   **Preposition Choice:** Students often struggle with **в** vs. **на**. As a rule of thumb: **в** is for buildings (спортзал, басейн), **на** is for activities or open spaces (стадіон, фітнес, тренування).

### Common Learner Errors
*   Using Nominative after "займатися" (*Я займаюся спорт* instead of *спортом*).
*   Using "грати" for sports that aren't games (*Я граю в йогу*).
*   Confusing the aspect of "тренуватися" (imperfective process) and "потренуватися" (single session).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### In-Scope (Based on Prior Knowledge)
*   **Cases:** All cases (especially Accusative for "грати в", Instrumental for "займатися", and Dative for "мені подобається").
*   **Tenses:** Present (habits), Past (completed actions), Future (plans).
*   **Aspect:** Using imperfective for regular training vs. perfective for winning a match.
*   **Comparatives:** "Швидше, вище, сильніше" (Faster, higher, stronger).
*   **Imperative:** Giving instructions for exercises (Дихайте! Стрибайте!).

### Out-of-Scope (Too Advanced)
*   **Technical Jargon:** Advanced sports terminology (offside, penalty shootout, set point).
*   **Passive Voice:** "The match was won by..." (Focus instead on active: "Команда виграла").
*   **Participles:** "Running man" or "Swimming athlete" (Use relative clauses instead: "людина, яка біжить").
*   **Advanced Morphology:** Prefixed motion verbs beyond the basic level (e.g., specific nuances of *забігати, оббігати* in a sports context).
