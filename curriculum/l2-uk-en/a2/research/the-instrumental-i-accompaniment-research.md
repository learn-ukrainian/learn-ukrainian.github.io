# Дослідження: The Instrumental I — Accompaniment

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.2.5: "Instrumental — means, manner, agent; with prepositions (з/із, за, між, над, перед, під)" (lines 1304–1322)
Sub-reference §4.2.2.5.2: спільність дії (joint action/accompaniment)
Alignment: This module covers the accompaniment sub-function of the instrumental — the з/із/зі + instrumental noun construction, euphony rules, and social verbs. Means/tools are deferred to a2-005.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Хліб-сіль**: "Зустріти з хлібом-сіллю" — welcoming guests with bread and salt. Bread = prosperity, salt = protection from evil. A concrete, memorable instrumental phrase that encodes core Ukrainian hospitality values. Directly usable as the opening cultural anchor.
2. **Friendship hierarchy** (друг / приятель / знайомий): In Ukrainian culture, calling someone "друг" signals deep, trusted closeness — not to be used lightly. Learners from English (where "friend" is casual) routinely overclaim this. Contrasting the three levels teaches both grammar and cultural literacy.
3. **Гуляти з собакою**: City dog-walking in Ukrainian parks (Трохи побачимо — "Let's walk") is ubiquitous in daily life. The phrase is grammatically simple, culturally authentic, and gives a concrete image that sticks.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Nominative overuse** → "Я йду з **друг**" instead of "Я йду з **другом**" — English has no case inflection so learners freeze at the dictionary form. Textbook exercise kravtsova p.56 (#153) directly targets this: blank-fill forcing ею/єю endings.
2. **Missing preposition з** → "Я розмовляю **мамою**" (implies using mother as instrument) vs correct "Я розмовляю **з мамою**" — extremely common; the verb feels complete without з to English speakers.
3. **Euphony default to з everywhere** → "**з** студентом" instead of "**зі** студентом" — learners learn з first and apply it universally. Zі is mandatory before consonant clusters (ст-, зв-, шк-, зн-) and before зі мною.
4. **Over-applying зі** → "зі **братом**" instead of "з **братом**" — after learning the rule, some learners apply зі before any consonant. Zі is only for clusters, not single consonants.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Show three paired sentences on screen:
  - "Я іду в кіно." / "Я іду з Оленою в кіно."
  - "Він живе в Києві." / "Він живе з батьками в Києві."
  - "Вона гуляє в парку." / "Вона гуляє з собакою в парку."
  Ask: *What did we add? What changed about the noun?* Let learners notice (1) the з and (2) the ending change before the rule is stated. Mirrors kravtsova p.56 step-by-step investigation pattern (Exercise 154: "Крок 1 — read, Крок 2 — identify base, notice ending").
- **Best context**: Planning an outing — "Хочеш піти з нами?" — is the ideal scenario. It requires з + person + activity, forces euphony choice, and is instantly recognizable as real speech.
- **Contrastive pairs**:
  - "Розмовляти **телефоном**" (phone as means, no з) vs "Розмовляти **з колегою**" (person, з required)
  - "Гуляти **парком**" (manner/route, no з) vs "Гуляти **з собакою**" (companion, з required)
  - "**з** братом" (single consonant → з) vs "**зі** студентом" (cluster ст → зі)

## Notes for Content Writing
- Persona is "Wedding Starosta" — use celebratory, social framing. "At a Ukrainian wedding, the starosta introduces families: 'Знайомтеся — це наречений з нареченою!'"
- Instrumental endings table must cover all four genders (masculine hard/soft, feminine hard/soft, neuter). Grade 4 kravtsova p.56 shows the -ею/-єю split for feminine — model this clearly.
- The "з/із/зі" euphony rule needs a short decision tree, not a prose paragraph. Learners at A2 need a rule they can apply in real-time.
- 50-60% immersion target for this band (M01-20). Grammar theory paragraphs stay in English; example sentences and dialogue are Ukrainian.
