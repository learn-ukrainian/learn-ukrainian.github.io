# Дослідження: Complete Imperative

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.2: "Форми 2-ї і 3-ї особи однини і множини наказового способу деяких дієслів: мріяти (мрій, мрійте, хай / нехай мріє, хай / нехай мріють), казати (кажи, кажіть, хай / нехай каже, хай / нехай кажуть), пити (пий, пийте, хай / нехай п’є, хай / нехай п’ють), боротися (борися, боріться, хай / нехай бореться, хай / нехай борються)."
Alignment: This module addresses the A2 requirement to form and use the 3rd person singular and plural imperative using the particles "хай / нехай", while reinforcing the 1st person plural ("let's") forms.

| нехай | High frequency (slightly formal) | нехай буде, нехай щастить |

## Cultural Hooks
1. "Будьмо!" is not merely a translation of "Cheers!" but historically functions as a call to collective presence and survival ("Let us be!").
2. The third toast is traditionally dedicated "за любов" or "за жінок" (for love / for women), during which men historically stand as a sign of respect.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The "Negative" Trap**: Believing that "нехай" is the negative form of "хай". Correct form: They are synonymous and interchangeable; the negative is "хай не" / "нехай не".
2. **Implicit "Let's"**: English speakers often try to use an infinitive after a direct translation of "let" instead of utilizing the 1st person plural imperative suffix "-мо/-імо" (e.g., ходімо, робімо).

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Avoid comparing "хай" to Russian "пусть". Focus on the intrinsic Ukrainian concept of shared willpower, wishing well, and collective action.
- Immersion Band 2 (60-75%): English is only allowed for explaining the abstract concepts of 3rd person imperative logic. Dialogues, examples, and practice must be entirely in Ukrainian.
