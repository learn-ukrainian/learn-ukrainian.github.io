## Cultural Hooks

**1. Домовик — the household spirit (verified via Ukrainian Wikipedia)**
Домовик is a figure from Ukrainian folk mythology: a protective household spirit (хатнє божество) who lives in the home and looks after its inhabitants. Wikipedia (uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Домовик) confirms:
- Ukrainian names: похатник, хатник, домашній дід — note the *Ukrainian* folk name Домовик, which is distinct from the Russian "домовой" (a separate folk tradition)
- Location in the home: "у запічку, під піччю, у комині чи біля нього" — in the stove area, the chimney, or attic
- Behavior: likes a tidy home (любить, коли в хаті прибрано), dislikes disorder; makes noise, hides things, or causes trouble if angered
- Ukrainian ethnologist Юлія Буйських notes the image combines beliefs from many historical eras and regional traditions
- Writer Дара Корній frames Домовик as a good household spirit whose power depends on family harmony
- **Pedagogical use:** Perfect anchor for the vocabulary of rooms and home layout — the spirit's "location" (у запічку, на горищі, в комині) naturally models Locative usage

**2. Поріг (The Threshold) taboo**
поріг has 10.12 IPM in GRAC — a genuinely high-frequency word. The threshold superstition (не вітатися і не передавати речі через поріг) is well-documented in Ukrainian folk culture and appears in contemporary Ukrainian daily life. This is an excellent cultural-etiquette moment:
- Shaking hands across the threshold is considered bad luck
- Passing objects (especially keys, money) over the threshold is avoided
- Sitting on the threshold is also avoided
- **Pedagogical use:** Provides a memorable location-based rule that makes поріг stick, and naturally introduces the vocabulary of home entry/exit

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Location vs. Direction case confusion:** Using Locative for motion — *Я йду в кімнаті* → correct: *Я йду в кімнату* (Accusative for direction). This is the single most common A2 error with home vocabulary because English "in" is case-neutral. Drill explicitly with minimal pairs: *він сидить у кімнаті* (static) vs. *він іде в кімнату* (motion).

2. **Consonant mutations in Locative (г→з, к→ц, х→с):** Textbook evidence (Grade 4, Kravtsova) confirms this is taught at Ukrainian school Grade 4 as a named phenomenon. Examples: книжка → на книжці, луг → у лузі, льох → у льосі. For this module: підлога → **на підлозі**, полиця → **на полиці**, стеля → **на стелі**. Learners tend to skip mutations and produce *на підлоги*, *на підлога* — both wrong. The mutations are predictable and can be presented as a table drill.

3. **Preposition на vs. у/в with rooms:** кухня is the primary idiomatic exception at this level — it requires **на кухні**, not у кухні. Compare: у вітальні ✓, у спальні ✓, у ванній ✓, **на кухні** ✓ (exception). Learners over-generalize у for all rooms. Secondary exception: на поверсі (floor/storey of a building) — worth a brief mention.

4. **ванна vs. ванна кімната:** In Ukrainian, "the bathroom" is typically expressed as *у ванній* (short for *у ванній кімнаті* — adjective substantivized). Learners confuse *ванна* (the bathtub itself) with the room. VESUM confirms ванна as a noun (the bath) and as an adjective form. Drill: *у ванній* = in the bathroom; *у ванні* = in the bathtub.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **меблі is plural-only (pluralia tantum):** Never use *одна меблі* or *меблів* as singular. Model: *меблі стоять*, *купити меблі*, *нові меблі*. Worth one explicit note.
- **Textbook anchor:** Grade 3 Vashulenko (trusted tier 2) has a "Моя кімната" exercise with the exact vocabulary list: *Кімната, двері, вікно, стеля, стіни, коридор, шафа, стіл, стілець, тумбочка, ліжко, підлога* — confirms this is core primary-school vocabulary, appropriate A2 scope.
- **Grade 6 Avramenko** (trusted tier 1) covers *інтер'єр* (interior description) as a formal text type — the module's Summary/Practice sections can draw on this framing for the "describe your home" task.
- **Domestic spirits framing:** Wikipedia confirms Домовик is the *Ukrainian* folk name (not the Russian "домовой"). Framing this as specifically Ukrainian folk culture, studied by Ukrainian ethnologists like Юлія Буйських and Володимир Галайчук, is both accurate and decolonized.
- **Sentence-length discipline:** Locative mutation examples work well as short demonstration pairs (≤10 words each). Keep Ukrainian sentence examples at ≤12 words as per A2 constraint.
- **на столі** — the canonical State Standard example — use it as the first Locative illustration; learners may already recognize it from §4.2.2.6.
