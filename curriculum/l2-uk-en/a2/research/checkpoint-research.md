# Research Notes: A2 M35 "Контрольна точка: Складні думки та здоров'я (Checkpoint A2.2)"

**Track**: Core A (General Curriculum)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (2024)**, this checkpoint synthesizes several key A2 competencies:

- **§3.10 Health (Здоров’я):** Focuses on "частини тіла" (body parts), "самопочуття" (well-being), "хворобливі стани" (painful states: болить, хворий, травма, висока температура), and "аптека" (pharmacy).
- **§4.3.2 Complex Sentences (Складні речення):** Specifically "складнопідрядні речення причини" using conjunctions **тому що** and **бо**. 
- **§1.4.1.1.1 (Monologue Competency):** Ability to "коротко висловлювати власну думку про певну тему, елементарно її обґрунтовуючи" (briefly express own opinion with basic justification).
- **§1.4.1.2.1 (Dialogue Competency):** Ability to "вступати в діалог й отримувати послуги в... лікарні" and "висловити... як себе почуває".

**Specific § Quote (Syntax/Complexity):**
> §4.3.2. Складні (складнопідрядні) речення з підрядною частиною причини (сполучники тому що, бо): Віктор не може говорити, тому що він хворий. Ми не гуляємо, бо холодно.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

At the A2.2 level, the vocabulary expands from simple naming to describing conditions and linking thoughts.

### Health & Symptoms (High Frequency)
- **Голова, спина, горло, живіт** (High frequency body parts for medical complaints).
- **Боліти** (To hurt/ache) — Essential verb. Used in the construction "У мене болить...".
- **Хворіти / Захворіти** (To be sick / To get sick) — Essential aspectual pair for health updates.
- **Температура, нежить, кашель** (Common symptoms).
- **Лікар, рецепт, таблетки, вітаміни** (Medical environment).

### Complex Thought Connectors (High Frequency)
- **Тому що / Бо** (Because) — "Бо" is more frequent in spoken language.
- **Я вважаю, що...** (I believe that...).
- **На мою думку...** (In my opinion...).
- **Мені здається, що...** (It seems to me that...).

### Relative Clauses
- **Який, яка, яке, які** (Which/Who) — High frequency for defining things (e.g., "Ліки, які я купив...").

## 3. Cultural Hook: The "Apteka" Culture

- **Pharmacists as Primary Consultants:** In Ukraine, people often visit an **Аптека** (pharmacy) as their first point of contact for minor health issues. Pharmacists often provide medical advice and suggest remedies without a prior doctor's visit for over-the-counter medicines.
- **24/7 Accessibility:** "Аптека 24/7" or "Цілодобова аптека" is a common feature in Ukrainian cities, reflecting the high priority placed on accessible healthcare.
- **Herbal "Grandmother" Remedies:** Traditional medicine remains strong. For a common cold, a Ukrainian is likely to recommend **липовий чай** (linden tea), **чай з малиною** (raspberry tea), or **калина** (viburnum) alongside modern medicine.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

- **"Боліти" vs. "Хворіти":** English speakers often confuse these. 
    - *Боліти* is used for specific pain (My head hurts).
    - *Хворіти* is used for the general state of being sick (I am sick).
- **The "U Mene" Construction:** Learners struggle with the Genitive "У мене болить..." versus the Dative "Мені болить...". While "У мене" is standard for health complaints, "Мені" is common in regional dialects (especially Western Ukraine) and colloquial speech.
- **Relative Clause Agreement:** The pronoun **який** must agree with the *antecedent* in gender/number, but its *case* is determined by its role in the subordinate clause. This is a major hurdle for English speakers who are used to "who/which/that".
- **Clause Separation:** In Ukrainian, subordinate clauses **must** be separated by a comma. English speakers frequently forget this punctuation rule.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN SCOPE (Student already knows / is practicing)
- **Cases:** All 6 cases in singular (Nom, Acc, Gen, Loc, Dat, Inst).
- **Aspect:** Basic usage of Imperfective vs. Perfective (e.g., *хворіти* vs. *захворіти*).
- **Complex Sentences:** Clauses using *що, щоб, бо, тому що, коли, який*.
- **Moods:** Imperative and simple Conditionals (*якби*).
- **Comparison:** Simple comparative and superlative adjectives.

### OUT OF SCOPE (Do NOT use)
- **Participles (Дієприкметники):** (e.g., "the broken leg" using a verbal adjective like *зламана* should be avoided or treated as a fixed vocabulary item).
- **Gerunds (Дієприслівники):** (e.g., "while walking..."). Use *коли я йшов...* instead.
- **Passive Voice:** Full passive constructions (e.g., "the patient was treated by..."). Use active constructions or simple "being/becoming" with the Instrumental.
- **Subjunctive with "аби":** Stick to *щоб*.
- **Complex Case Exceptions:** Nouns with highly irregular declensions (e.g., *мати*, *ім'я* in oblique cases) unless essential for the topic.
