# Дослідження: Plural Adjective Declension

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.1.2: "прикметники у формі множини: високі, дешеві, домашні, осінні, сині, українські"
Alignment: The module explicitly focuses on teaching the plural forms of both hard (-і) and soft (-і) group adjectives across all cases, directly fulfilling the A2 morphological requirement for the full adjective paradigm.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Ukrainian proverb**: "Старий друг краще нових двох" (An old friend is better than two new ones) — this beautifully showcases both Nominative Plural (старий друг) and Genitive Plural (нових двох) in a single, culturally resonant phrase.
2. **Ukrainian proverb**: "Одежа краща нова, а друзі — старі" (Clothes are better new, but friends are better old) — contrasting singular/plural adjective forms based on the noun being modified.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Russian Interference → Ukrainian Simplicity**: Learners often try to distinguish hard/soft endings in Nominative Plural based on Russian patterns (-ые/-ие). In Ukrainian, ALL adjectives simply take "-і" in Nominative Plural (нові, сині, великі).
2. **Mismatched Case**: Applying singular case endings to plural nouns (e.g., "великого будинки" instead of "великих будинків").
3. **Ghost Gender**: Trying to match gender in the plural. Plural adjectives elegantly collapse all genders into one form per case.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized framing**: Emphasize the simplicity and elegance of Ukrainian plural adjectives. The universal "-і" ending in the Nominative plural is a uniquely Ukrainian feature that makes learning straightforward. Frame this positively: "Ukrainian beautifully simplifies this..."
- **Immersion**: This is an A2 Band 1 module (50-60% immersion). Ensure English scaffolding is used for explicitly explaining the grammar rules and case declension logic.
- **Tone**: Maintain the warm, encouraging persona of the Ukrainian Teacher. Ensure all examples feel practical and rooted in daily Ukrainian life or culture.
