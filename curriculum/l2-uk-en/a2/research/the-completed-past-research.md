**Level**: A2 (Post-A1 Transition)

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

Key vocabulary for discussing completed actions and results at the A2 level.

### High-Frequency Verbs (Perfective)
- **зробити** (to do/make - finished)
- **купити** (to buy)
- **написати** (to write - finished)
- **прочитати** (to read - finished)
- **прийти** (to come/arrive)
- **поснідати / пообідати / повечеряти** (to finish a meal)
- **прибрати** (to clean up/tidy)
- **зателефонувати** (to make a phone call)
- **забути** (to forget)

### Common Collocations & Adverbs
- **вже** (already) — *Я вже зробив домашнє завдання.*
- **нарешті** (finally) — *Він нарешті прийшов.*
- **щощо** (just) — *Вона щойно купила квиток.*
- **результат** (result) — *Який результат?*

## 3. Cultural Hook: The Concept of "Толока" (Toloka)

**The Result of Collective Labor:**
In Ukrainian culture, **Толока** is a traditional form of mutual aid where a community gathers to help a neighbor with a large, urgent task—usually building a house (*збудувати хату*) or harvesting. 
The pedagogical hook is that the work is not just "happening"; it MUST be **finished** (*зроблено*) by the end of the day. Once the goal is reached (Perfective result), the owner hosts a feast for everyone. This highlights the cultural value of "finishing what you started" and celebrating the result.

**Fact:** In 2022-2024, the tradition of Toloka has seen a massive revival in Ukraine as volunteers gather to clear rubble and rebuild homes destroyed by the war, emphasizing the result: "We have cleared it" (*Ми прибрали*).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
- **English Tense Confusion:** English speakers often struggle to choose between the Ukrainian Perfective Past and English Present Perfect ("I have written") or Past Simple ("I wrote"). 
- **Focus Shift:** In Ukrainian, the distinction is binary: Did it reach the end? (Perfective) vs. Was it in progress? (Imperfective).
- **Adverbial Clues:** Words like *вже* (already) almost always trigger the perfective aspect in resultative contexts.

### Common Learner Errors
- Using the imperfective for one-time completed actions (e.g., saying *я читав книжку* when they mean they finished it).
- Mis-prefixation: Using the wrong prefix to form a perfective (e.g., *пописати* instead of *написати* for "to finish writing"). At A2, focus should be on the most common pairs.

### Teaching Sequence
1. Review the concept of aspect pairs (A1 intro).
2. Contrast: *Я писав лист* (process) vs *Я написав лист* (result).
3. Introduce "Narrative Sequences": *Я прийшов, побачив, переміг* (Veni, Vidi, Vici style chains of perfective actions).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope
- **Past Tense Endings:** -в, -ла, -ло, -ли.
- **Aspectual Pairs:** Simple prefixation (*робити/зробити*) and basic suffix shifts (*купувати/купити*).
- **Adverbs of time:** вже, ще не, нарешті, щойно.
- **Case Agreement:** Accusative for the object of the completed action (*Я купив хліб*).

### OUT Scope
- **Conditional Mood:** *Якби я зробив...* (Too advanced).
- **Passive Voice:** *Лист був написаний...* (Too advanced).
- **Gerunds:** *Зробивши роботу...* (Too advanced).
- **Suppletive Pairs:** *Брати / Взяти* (Introduce sparingly if at all in this specific module).
- **Complex Prefix nuances:** (e.g., the difference between *виписати*, *записати*, *переписати* beyond basic narrative use).
