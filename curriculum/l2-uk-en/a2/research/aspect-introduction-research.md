## Cultural Hooks

1. **Борщ і аспект**: The UNESCO 2022 listing of Ukrainian borsch culture is a concrete, verifiable hook. The pair **варити (process, imperfective)** vs. **зварити (result, perfective)** lets learners feel the difference through a culturally charged act: you can варити борщ for hours — but the meal only arrives when you зварили. Plan already incorporates this — reinforce it as the module's spine.

2. **Прислів'я**: *«Без труда нема плода»* — "Without labor there is no fruit." Process (праця, imperfective-world) produces result (плід, perfective-world). This is already in the plan and maps perfectly to the imperfective/perfective semantic distinction. Short, memorable, authentic.

3. **Everyday Ukrainian texting**: Ukrainians commonly use the perfective in texted check-ins: *«Ти вже зробив?»* (Did you finish?), *«Ще не написав»* (Haven't finished writing yet). This is a natural, motivating context for A2 — learners will encounter exactly these structures in real communication with Ukrainian friends or tutors.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Present tense perfective" error** → Using *написати* conjugated in present tense (*я напишу* ≠ "I am writing now"). English "-ing" maps onto Ukrainian present tense, but imperfective ONLY has a present. *Я пишу* = I am writing / I write; *я напишу* = I will write/finish writing. This is the #1 error at A2.

2. **Negation collapse** → *«Я не читав»* (I wasn't reading / I haven't been reading) vs. *«Я не прочитав»* (I didn't finish reading / I haven't read it through). English speakers collapse both to "I didn't read" and lose the distinction. Minimal pair drills are essential here.

3. **Suppletive pairs treated as predictable** → *говорити/сказати* and *брати/взяти* have unrelated stems. Learners apply the prefix rule (*на-, з-, ви-*) and produce *\*наговорити* with the wrong meaning (to talk a lot, not "to say"). Must explicitly flag that suppletive pairs require memorization, not derivation.

4. **Adverb trigger confusion** → *зараз* triggers imperfective (ongoing), but *уже* and *нарешті* trigger perfective (completion). English speakers often ignore adverbs as aspect cues. Textbook exercises from Grade 4 (sorting sentences by time markers) directly address this pattern — use the same sorting-by-adverb exercise format.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present three Ukrainian text messages side by side without labels:
  - *«Що ти робиш?»* — What are you doing?
  - *«Що ти зробив?»* — What did you (manage to) do?
  - *«Що ти будеш робити?»* — What will you be doing?
  Ask: "Which verb appears in all three? What changes?" Let learners notice that *робити* stays the same in #1 and #3, but *зробив* in #2 is different. Then introduce the pair. This parallels Grade 4 textbook exercises where students observe verb forms in context before labeling them.

- **Best context**: The kitchen / home chores — *«Ти вже зварила борщ?»* / *«Ні, я ще варю.»* Real-life check-ins about task completion are ubiquitous in Ukrainian daily communication.

- **Contrastive pairs for disambiguation**:
  - *Я писав лист* (I was writing / used to write a letter) vs. *Я написав лист* (I wrote / finished the letter)
  - *Він читав книгу* vs. *Він прочитав книгу*
  - *Щодня вона купує каву* vs. *Вона вже купила квиток*

## Notes for Content Writing

- Immersion: 45–65% Ukrainian (Core grammar band, module 14). Grammar theory sections may be mostly English; dialogue and example sentences should be Ukrainian-first.
- Max sentence length: 15 Ukrainian words; max 2 clauses.
- Grammar terms to introduce: **вид** (aspect), **недоконаний вид** (imperfective), **доконаний вид** (perfective). Use them in Ukrainian headings once introduced.
- Do NOT use abstract nouns like *почуття*, *відчуття* — forbidden at A2.
- Suppress suppletive pair formation rules from the "predictable" category — *говорити/сказати* and *брати/взяти* need memory flags, not pattern rules.
- The plan's five sections total 2000 words (325+500+475+375+325). Content writer should hit each budget ±10%.
- Persona is "Borsch Master Chef / Encouraging Cultural Guide" — warm, practical tone; borsch metaphor should reappear in the summary but not dominate every section.
