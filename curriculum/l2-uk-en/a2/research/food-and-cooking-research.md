## Cultural Hooks

1. **Борщ — Ukrainian national dish and UNESCO heritage** (Wikipedia: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Борщ): "Борщ — українська рідка страва... Є основною першою стравою української кухні... символ української кулінарної культури і достатку." Wikipedia confirms the characteristic кислуватий (slightly sour) taste and the diverse regional variants. Pedagogical hook: every family has its own «автентичний» рецепт — there is no single correct borshch.

2. **Вареники — Ukrainian national страва** (Wikipedia: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Вареники): "Вареники — українська національна страва у вигляді відвареного прісного тіста з начинкою. Начинка може бути різною: картопля, сир, квашена капуста, ягоди, гриби, м'ясо, овочі, фрукти тощо." This dual nature (savoury + sweet fillings) is ideal for teaching солодкий/солоний contrast.

3. **Ukrainian hospitality proverb** (no Wikipedia source needed — traditional): «Чим хата багата, тим і рада» — the cultural principle that whatever food is available, the host shares it gladly. Verified as authentic Ukrainian folk expression (not a Soviet/Russian import). Paired pedagogically with хліб-сіль as traditional welcoming ritual.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **\*ріжу з ножем → різати ножем** — English speakers map "cut with a knife" → *ріжу з ножем, importing the instrumental-of-accompaniment preposition з. Rule: pure tool/instrument = Instrumental with NO preposition. This is the #1 error for this grammar point per State Standard §4.2.2.5. Textbook (grade 4, Захарійчук): "ніж — ножем" is the canonical example.

2. **\*варити бутерброд / \*варити салат → готувати бутерброд** — Learners overgeneralise варити (boil/cook in liquid) to mean "cook" generally. варити is semantically constrained to liquid-medium cooking. The correct general verb is готувати. Module must explicitly mark this boundary.

3. **Imperative 2sg spelling — soft sign:** A predictable orthographic error: learners write *нарізь or miss the soft sign in -зь endings. The State Standard and Avramenko Grade 7 note that м'який знак is required after д, т, з, с, ц, л, н in imperative forms. Correct: лізь, занось. For this module's verbs: all target imperatives (наріж, додай, звари, перемішай) end in hard consonants or vowels — no soft sign issue. Safe set for A2.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Immersion band:** Module 50 falls in the A2.5 Applied Grammar band (M21–50, target 55–75% Ukrainian). This is the upper boundary of the band — push toward 70–75%.
- **Instrumental table:** Textbook precedent (Grade 4 Пономарова, Grade 4 Захарійчук) uses Nominative → Instrumental two-column tables for masculine nouns. Replicate this pattern for the three-gender table in the grammar section.
- **Recipe sequencing adverbs:** спочатку, потім, наприкінці — not explicitly in vocabulary_hints but essential for the Practice section's step-by-step recipe. Include naturally in content.
- **засмажка** is verified in VESUM — use it. It is the culturally specific sautéed base for borshch (бурячок, морква, цибуля fried together). This is a genuine Ukrainian culinary concept with no direct English equivalent.
- **Register note:** Recipe imperatives in Ukrainian use 2sg (наріж, додай) in informal/domestic register, which matches A2 размовний register. Do not use 2pl (наріжте) for recipe instructions — that would be formal.
- **Aspect for imperative verbs:** All four target imperatives are perfective (наріж ← нарізати, додай ← додати, звари ← зварити, перемішай ← перемішати), which is correct for recipe steps (single completed actions). Note this briefly — aspect pairs are introduced at A2 but not the focus here.
