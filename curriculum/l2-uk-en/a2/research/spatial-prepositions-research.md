## Cultural Hooks

1. **Піч (stove) as spatial anchor**: In a traditional Ukrainian хата, the піч was the organisational centre. Items were stored за піччю (behind the stove), dried above it (над піччю), and children slept на печі. This single object illustrates three different preposition+case combinations naturally.

2. **Покуття (sacred corner)**: The honoured guest corner, located навпроти печі (opposite the stove) and under icons. Guests were seated на покутті or за столом near it — real spatial vocabulary embedded in genuine hospitality culture, not a contrived exercise.

3. **Street directions as navigation**: Asking як дійти до… and using навпроти, біля, вздовж with urban landmarks (театр, університет, аптека) is a core real-life task for any Ukrainian city. This is the natural applied context for the full preposition set.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Location case on motion verbs** → "Я йду в школі" (Locative) → correct: "Я йду в школу" (Accusative). Happens because English "I'm going to school" does not mark motion morphologically. The куди?/де? question pair is the fix.

2. **Bare noun after Genitive prepositions** → "біля магазин" → correct: "біля магазину" (Gen. sg.). English has no case inflection so learners skip the ending. Pattern-recognition drills with fixed prepositions (біля, до, навпроти, навколо) help.

3. **Confusing в vs. на** → "на хаті" for "in the house" or "в концерті" for "at the concert." Rule: в/у for enclosed spaces, на for surfaces and event/open contexts. Textbook exercises often use physical diagrams (box/surface) to cement this — the "where is the cat?" quiz type directly targets this error.

4. **Instrumental endings on location prepositions** → "за стіл" (Accusative, motion) used when speaker means static "behind the table." The command drill ("Поставте лампу над столом" vs. "Покладіть книгу на стіл") from the plan directly mirrors how Ukrainian school textbooks use дієслова руху vs. стану to contrast cases.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show a labelled diagram of a traditional хата interior with 6 objects placed around a піч (a book, a lamp, a cat, a bag, a cup, icons). Ask: "Where is the book? Where is the cat?" — learners must choose from word banks. Then ask: "Now move the lamp — where is it going?" The change in answer form (над столом → над стіл) surfaces the location/motion split before any rule is stated.
- **Best context**: Interior arrangement + street directions. Both are concrete, spatial, and require learners to make the куди?/де? decision under communicative pressure.
- **Contrastive pairs for drilling**:
  - Він сидить **за столом** (Instr, location) vs. Він сів **за стіл** (Acc, motion)
  - Книга лежить **на столі** (Loc) vs. Поклади книгу **на стіл** (Acc)
  - Кіт сидить **під стільцем** (Instr) vs. Кіт заліз **під стілець** (Acc)

## Notes for Content Writing

- Enforce the А2 sentence cap: max 15 words per Ukrainian sentence, max 2 clauses. Spatial sentences are short by nature — stay concrete.
- The "Interior Designer" persona works well for command drills ("Поставте...", "Перемістіть...") — use imperative forms throughout the Practice section.
- Do not introduce поміж, всередині, ззовні, посеред, збоку in main exposition — reserve as "recommended" vocabulary in the vocab table with a brief note. Core load (в, на, під, за, між, біля, до, навпроти, перед, над) is already 10 prepositions for one module.
- Euphony reminder (§4.1.3 A2): у/в alternation must be used correctly throughout — у хаті not в хаті when preceded by a consonant.
- Immersion band: M07 = Core grammar band → 45–65% Ukrainian per A2 quick-ref.
