# Дослідження: Spatial Prepositions

Alignment: This module directly addresses the A2 requirement for expanded spatial relations, specifically focusing on the distinction between location (Locative/Instrumental) and motion (Accusative).

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Піч (The Stove)**: Historically the "heart" and "mother-nourisher" of the Ukrainian *khata*. It served as the primary heat source, cooking area, and even a warm bed for children and the elderly.
   - *Language Anchor*: Expressions like "спати **на** печі" (to sleep on the stove) or "ховатися **за** піччю" (to hide behind the stove) are deeply rooted in traditional life. (Source: soloxa.com.ua)
2. **Покуття (The Sacred Corner)**: The diagonal corner opposite the stove, decorated with icons and *rushnyky*. It is the spiritual center where the *didukh* is placed during Christmas.
   - *Language Anchor*: "Покуття розташоване **навпроти** печі" (The sacred corner is located opposite the stove). It represents the most honorable place in the house. (Source: rbc.ua)

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Static vs. Motion Confusion** → *Я йду в школі* (Wrong) vs. *Я йду в школу* (Correct). Learners often use the Locative case for motion or Accusative for static location because both use the same prepositions (в/на).
2. **Incorrect Case with Spatial Prepositions** → *Біля магазин* (Wrong) vs. *Біля магазину* (Correct). Learners forget that "біля" requires the Genitive case, or "під/над" requires the Instrumental for location.
3. **Misuse of В vs. На** → *В вулиці* (Wrong) vs. *На вулиці* (Correct). English speakers tend to translate "in" as "в" even for open spaces where Ukrainian requires "на" (square, street, island).

## Notes for Content Writing
- Emphasize the "куди?" vs. "де?" distinction using a decision table or flowchart.
- Ensure IPA is provided for all new vocabulary, especially for stressed vowels in prepositions like *напроти* [наˈпрɔти] and *навколо* [наˈўкɔлɔ].
- Include a "History Bite" about the *pich* to anchor the prepositions *на*, *під*, *за*.
