# Дослідження: If I Were...

## State Standard Reference

**Critical finding — scope mismatch**: The conditional mood (умовний спосіб) is formally a **B1** feature, not A2.

- **A2 verb moods covered** (§4.2.3.1–2, lines 1341–1365): Indicative (all tenses, aspect pairs, motion verbs) and Imperative (2nd + 3rd person). No conditional listed.
- **A2 complex sentences** (§4.4.2, lines 1403–1416): Includes якщо in subordinate clauses, but only for real conditions with indicative — no conditional mood forms.
- **B1 §4.2.3.3** (lines 2297–2298, verbatim): *"Умовний спосіб. Утворення форм умовного способу: говорив би, випила б, прочитали б."*

**Alignment note**: The plan's citation of §4.2.3.3 points to a B1 State Standard requirement, not A2. This is a pedagogical forward-reach: the curriculum intentionally introduces conditional at A2 because хотів би / хотіла б is extremely high-frequency in daily speech and polite register. This is defensible — many communicative curricula introduce хотів би at A2 for immediate utility — but content writers must treat this as an **introduction**, not mastery: basic formation and high-frequency fixed phrases only. Full conditional system (якби + complex conditions, fused якби, aspect in conditional) is B1 territory.

**A2-level anchor** (closest): §4.4.2 complex sentences include якщо for real conditions. This module can extend that to contrast якщо (real) vs. якщо б/якби (unreal).

**Note on хотів би**: GRAC doesn't parse multi-word phrases. Individual components: хотів (134 IPM) + б (683 IPM). The collocation хотів би / хотіла б is the most common real-world use of the conditional — textbooks and native speakers confirm this.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Proverb «Якби та кабі, то в роті росли б гриби»** — authenticated folk saying (equivalent of English "If ifs and buts were candy and nuts"). Suitable as cultural hook; note that **кабі is dialectal/archaic** (not in VESUM) — present it as a fixed proverb element, not a productive word. Context: the proverb mocks wishful thinking and overuse of hypotheticals.

2. **"Історія не знає умовного способу"** — Ukrainian cultural expression (Wikipedia verified) meaning historical analysis should not rely on hypotheticals. Useful cultural sidebar showing where the conditional mood is *excluded* — adds metalinguistic awareness. Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia, article «Умовний спосіб».

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Real/unreal contamination**: *\*Якщо б я буду мати гроші* → correct: *Якби я мав гроші* — learners who know якщо for real conditions pull the future tense into unreal conditions.

2. **Particle б/би phonological rule ignored**: *\*він би написав* (correct: він написав би) or *\*вона б написала* (correct since vowel: вона написала б). Rule: б after vowel, би after consonant — but particle position is also flexible (я б хотів = я хотів би), which adds confusion.

3. **Fixed phrase хотів би treated as verb phrase**: Learners sometimes try to inflect or separate *хотів би* in ways that break it: *\*я хотів дуже би* — the particle б/би cannot be separated from its clause by inserting adverbs between it and the verb.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Scope discipline**: Treat this module as a **controlled introduction** to the conditional, not a full survey. Prioritize: (1) formation rule (past tense + б/би), (2) хотів би / хотіла б as the flagship polite form, (3) real vs. unreal contrast with якщо vs. якби. Aspect in conditional (читав би vs. прочитав би) is valid at A2 but keep examples concrete and frequent.
- **кабі in the proverb**: Use the proverb as a cultural hook but explicitly gloss кабі as "archaic/dialectal variant of якби" — do not teach it as a productive word (not in VESUM).
- **б vs. би position**: The particle can float to other positions (я б хотів, він би знав) for emphasis. The textbooks (Avramenko 2024, §37) confirm: "Частки б / би з дієсловами пишемо окремо. Вони можуть стояти після будь-якого повнозначного слова." Mention this flexibility briefly.
- **Textbook source** (Grade 7, Litvinova 2024 / Avramenko 2024, Tier 1): Conditional mood taught in §12/§29-37 respectively. Good classroom-tested contrasts: *Я вам допомогла* vs. *Я б вам допомогла*. These are solid, simple examples appropriate for A2 presentation.
- **State Standard framing**: Do not cite §4.2.3.3 as an A2 requirement — it is B1. Frame the module as building toward the B1 standard, with a practical A2-level introduction driven by communicative need (politeness register, хотів би).
- **Immersion**: Module target is 55–75% Ukrainian (A2 Applied Grammar band). Grammar theory for real vs. unreal conditions may need English explanation; all examples, dialogues, and exercises should be 100% Ukrainian.
