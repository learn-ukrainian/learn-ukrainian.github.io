# Дослідження: In Order To

## State Standard Reference
§4.4.2: "складнопідрядні реченні зі сполучником щоб для вираження мети певної дії суб’єкта: Ми вчимо українську мову, щоб розмовляти з друзями."
Alignment: This module directly fulfills the A2 syntactic requirement to form complex sentences that express intentionality and purpose using «щоб» and its variants.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Тости (Toasts):** Ukrainian toast culture frequently uses «щоб» to express wishes. A classic example: «Щоб їлося і пилося, щоб хотілося і моглося!» This anchors the grammar in authentic, joyful social interaction.
2. **Народна мудрість (Folk Wisdom):** The proverb «На те коня кують, щоб не спотикався» (One shoes a horse so it doesn't stumble) beautifully illustrates the concept of intentionality and forward-planning.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The "For To" Calque** → *Я йду для купити* (Incorrect) vs *Я йду, щоб купити* (Correct). Learners directly translate "for" to «для» before an infinitive verb, failing to realize «для» strictly requires a noun/pronoun in the Genitive case.
2. **Subject Mismatch (Subjunctive Twist)** → *Я хочу тебе читати* (Incorrect) vs *Я хочу, щоб ти читав* (Correct). Learners fail to switch to the past tense when the subject of the main clause differs from the subordinate clause.
3. **Comma Omission** → *Він пішов щоб допомогти* (Incorrect) vs *Він пішов, щоб допомогти* (Correct). Ukrainian punctuation strictly requires a comma before «щоб» in complex sentences.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonization:** Emphasize the strict, logical elegance of the Ukrainian "Subject Logic" (infinitive vs past tense) without ever comparing it to Russian. Present it as an inherent, beautiful feature of Ukrainian syntax.
- **Immersion:** The module is A2.2 (Band 2: 60-75% Ukrainian). Use English scaffolding ONLY for explaining the abstract subject-shifting logic (the "subjunctive twist") and the "For To" calque error. Use Ukrainian for section intros, drills, examples, and cultural contexts.
- **Visuals:** Create a clear decision tree or flowchart for "Is the doer the same? → YES (Infinitive) / NO (Past Tense)".
