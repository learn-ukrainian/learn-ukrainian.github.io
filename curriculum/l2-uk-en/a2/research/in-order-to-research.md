# Дослідження: In Order To

## State Standard Reference

**§4.4.2** (lines 1415–1416): *"складнопідрядні речення зі сполучником щоб для вираження мети певної дії суб'єкта: Ми вчимо українську мову, щоб розмовляти з друзями."*

**Full §4.4.2 scope** (lines 1403–1416) — A2 complex sentences: і/та (coordination), але (contrast), де/куди/звідки (place), що (complement), тому що/бо (cause), **щоб (purpose)**. Purpose clauses are the final and most structurally complex item in A2 syntax.

**Alignment**: This module covers exactly the §4.4.2 requirement. The State Standard specifies щоб for same-subject purpose (infinitive); the different-subject variant (past tense) is a natural extension at A2.2. The module also introduces для того щоб (emphatic form) and distinguishes щоб from тому що — both directly serving the A2 communicative competency for expressing intentionality (State Standard §4.4.2 communicative application).

**Note on "для того щоб"**: GRAC does not index multi-word phrases, but all three component words are high-frequency and the construction is well-attested in textbooks. Use "для" (prep, top-50) and "щоб" (conj, top-100) as the anchors.

**Vocabulary recommendation**: At A2, anchor on щоб + аби. мета is useful for plan-talking vocabulary; задля/заради are passive recognition items only — do not drill production.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **The щоб Toast**: The Ukrainian toast *«Щоб їлося і пилося, щоб хотілося і моглося!»* is a widely known folk blessing using the exact щоб + past tense structure (different subject: speaker wishes for others). Ukrainian has a distinct word for toasts: **тост** (or archaic **заздоровниця / заздравиця**). This makes the toast a live grammar demonstration, not a decoration. Source: Wikipedia — *Тост* (confirmed article exists, definition confirmed).

2. **Proverb**: *«На те коня кують, щоб не спотикався.»* (One shoes a horse so it won't stumble.) — attested Ukrainian proverb illustrating purposeful action. The щоб clause here is same-subject + infinitive (negated), giving learners both the affirmative and negative form in one authentic sentence.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Для + infinitive" calque** → *Я йду для купити* vs. correct *Я йду, щоб купити* — Triggered by direct mapping from English "for + to-infinitive" or German "für + infinitive". In Ukrainian, **для** governs only nouns/pronouns in the Genitive; purpose with verbs always requires **щоб**.

2. **Subject mismatch / wrong verb form** → *Я хочу тебе прийти* vs. correct *Я хочу, щоб ти прийшов* — When the subject of the purpose clause differs from the main clause subject, the verb must shift to **past tense** (the subjunctive-like form). Learners default to infinitive regardless of subject.

3. **Comma omission** → *Ми вчимо мову щоб говорити* vs. correct *Ми вчимо мову, щоб говорити* — Ukrainian punctuation requires a comma before щоб in complex sentences. This is a hard rule with no exceptions, but learners from English backgrounds (where "in order to" needs no comma) consistently skip it.

## Decolonized Framing Note

The Voron Grade 9 textbook (2017) labels purpose clauses with a Russian gloss: *"Підрядні речення мети (рос. придаточные предложения цели)"* — a Soviet pedagogical legacy of using Russian as the metalanguage anchor. **Do not replicate this.** Frame Ukrainian purpose clauses independently:

- Compare to Portuguese *para que* + subjunctive, or German *damit* (different subject) vs. *um … zu* (same subject) — these are parallel structures that illustrate universal logic without centering Russian.
- Ukrainian's щоб has a broader semantic range than its nearest counterparts: it handles purpose, wish, and request simultaneously — a distinctive Ukrainian feature to highlight positively.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The State Standard example sentence (*Ми вчимо українську мову, щоб розмовляти з друзями*) should appear verbatim in the Introduction as the anchor example — it's the official curricular reference point.
- The "same subject → infinitive / different subject → past tense" decision tree is the cognitive core. Spend the most words here (§Презентація, 600 words).
- "Для того щоб" is an emphatic lengthening, not a grammatically separate construction — teach it as a stylistic intensifier, not a new grammar rule.
- аби: frequency is 190 IPM (legitimately common), but register is colloquial/spoken. Introduce it as recognition vocabulary with 1–2 examples maximum; do not drill production at A2.
- Immersion target for this module is 55–75% Ukrainian (A2 Applied Grammar band, M21–50). The grammar explanation sections (same/different subject logic) can use English; dialogues, examples, and cultural sections must be 100% Ukrainian.
- Avoid the noun **ціль** in A2 production — мета is more neutral and more common at this register.
