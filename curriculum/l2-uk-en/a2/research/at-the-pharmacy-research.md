# Дослідження: At the Pharmacy

## State Standard Reference
§3.12: "Здоров’я й особиста гігієна: ... самопочуття; хвороби й нездужання; візит до лікаря; аптека."
Alignment: This module directly addresses the "аптека" (pharmacy) requirement of the "Здоров’я й особиста гігієна" thematic catalogue, utilizing Genitive case structures (§4.2.2.2) for requesting medicines ("від кашлю") and Imperative mood (§4.2.3.2) for understanding dosage instructions.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Електронний рецепт (E-Recipe):** Since 2022, antibiotics and many other medicines in Ukraine require an electronic prescription ("е-рецепт"), which is sent via SMS. You cannot just buy antibiotics over the counter anymore.
2. **Herbal & Natural Focus:** Ukrainians often prefer herbal remedies (фітотерапія) for minor ailments like colds or stress (e.g., tea with viburnum/kalyna, herbal syrups) before turning to synthetic drugs.
3. **"Чергова аптека":** Many cities have designated "duty pharmacies" that remain open 24/7, marked with a green cross.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Wrong Word:** Using "лікарство" (Russianism/Surzhyk) instead of **ліки**.
   *   *Correction:* Always use "ліки".
2. **Grammar Number:** Treating "ліки" as singular.
   *   *Correction:* "Ліки" is **always plural** (pluralia tantum). "Ці ліки дорогі" (not "це ліки...").
3. **Preposition Error:** Using "для" (for) with ailments instead of "від" (from/against).
   *   *Correction:* "Ліки **від** голови" (Medicine *from* headache), not "для голови".
4. **Verb Confusion:** Using "пити таблетки" (to drink pills) vs "приймати ліки" (to take medicine).
   *   *Note:* "Пити" is colloquially common, but "приймати" is the correct standard form.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Crucial Distinction:** Ensure the dialogue clarifies that antibiotics require a prescription (realism), while painkillers/cold meds are OTC.
- **Genitive Drill:** The construction "щось від..." (something for/against...) is the core grammar point. Drill "від + Genitive" extensively.
- **Immersion:** Use standard "приймати" in instructions, but allow "пити" in colloquial patient dialogue for authenticity.
