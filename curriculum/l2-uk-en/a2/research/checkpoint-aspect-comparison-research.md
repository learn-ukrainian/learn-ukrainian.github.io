## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ukrainian proverb tradition — «Краще пізно, ніж ніколи»**: Ukrainian folk proverbs (прислів'я) are documented in *«Українські приказки, прислів'я і таке інше»*, collected by Матвій Номис and first published in 1864. This makes «Краще пізно, ніж ніколи» a verified element of Ukrainian folk wisdom, not a borrowing. The comparative *краще* here functions as a softening device in social apology — choosing the soft/gradable form over an absolute (добре/погано) is a genuine pragmatic feature. (Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Українські_приказки,_прислів'я_і_таке_інше)

2. **Aspect and social register in Ukrainian hospitality**: Ukrainian conversational norms assign different social weight to Imperfective vs Perfective imperatives. *Приходьте* (Imperfective) signals a warm, standing, open invitation ("come whenever you like"), while *Прийдіть* (Perfective) is a specific summons. This distinction is intrinsic to Ukrainian — not a borrowing, not a Russianism. It reflects a grammatical feature with real communicative stakes in everyday Ukrainian social life, making it ideal for a checkpoint consolidation.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Double comparative**: *\*більш кращий* → кращий (simple) OR більш добрий (compound) — never both. Learners mix the suppletive form кращий with the compound intensifier більш, treating it as an adverbial modifier rather than as an alternative paradigm.

2. **Numeral-noun agreement (the Numeral Collision)**: Using Genitive Plural for all numbers → *\*два столів* → два столи́ (Nom. Pl., 2–4) vs п'ять столі́в (Gen. Pl., 5+). This is the single highest-frequency numeral error at A2 because Genitive Plural *feels* like the default "after a number" form.

3. **Perfective in the present tense**: *\*Я зроблю зараз* → Я роблю зараз. Learners perceive "I'm doing it right now" as a completed/definitive act and reach for the perfective. The State Standard explicitly defines present tense as exclusively Imperfective (§4.2.3.1).

4. **якщо / коли confusion**: *\*Коли матиму гроші, куплю* (used for general hypothetical condition) vs the correct *Якщо матиму гроші, куплю*. коли = temporal ("when" as a time marker); якщо = conditional ("if"). Learners conflate them because English "when" covers both in informal speech.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The proverb «Краще пізно, ніж ніколи» is genuinely Ukrainian in origin (Номис collection, 1864) — use it confidently without hedging or Russian comparison.
- зробити is more frequent than робити in the GRAC corpus — worth noting in the lesson as a "surprising fact": the perfective is not the "harder" or rarer form.
- The якщо/коли section (Section 4) should include a minimal pair drill — both forms in near-identical sentences — since this error is highly predictable.
- більш is verified in VESUM as the adverb form of більше — both spellings (більш / більше) occur in compound comparatives; plan content should use більш for compound comparatives (більш важливий) per standard written norm.
