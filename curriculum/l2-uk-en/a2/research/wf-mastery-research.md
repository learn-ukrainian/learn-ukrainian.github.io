## Cultural Hooks

1. **12,000 roots, 300 active** — Ukrainian textbooks (Karaman, Grade 10) note that Ukrainian has ~12,000 roots, with an active core of ~300 around which 20,000 words cluster. These core roots cover life-essential concepts: бути, мати, нести, ходити, робити, земля. This is the empirical basis for the "LEGO" metaphor — mastering ~300 roots unlocks thousands of words.

2. **Cafe Ptakh mnemonic is attested in Ukrainian primary textbooks** — The mnemonic "Кафе «Птах»" (к, ф, п, т, х → prefix с-) appears in Grade 3 textbooks by Kravtsova (tier 2) and Ponomarova (tier 2), confirmed via RAG. This is a genuine Ukrainian pedagogical tradition, not an invention. Direct quote: *"Їх легко запам'ятати завдяки вислову Кафе «Птах»"* (Grade 3, Kravtsova).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **зфотографувати → сфотографувати** — English speakers apply voiced/voiceless logic inconsistently. The з-/с- alternation depends on the initial consonant of the root (к, п, т, ф, х → с-), not on the voicing of the prefix itself. Root cause: transfer from English spelling habits.

2. **реписати → переписати** — English speakers reach for the Latin-origin prefix *ре-* (re-write) which exists in Ukrainian (реформа, революція) but is non-productive for native verb derivation. The productive "again/repeat" prefix is пере-. This is a predictable L1 interference error.

3. **Vowel change masking roots** — learners fail to recognize кінець/кінця/закінчити as one root family because of the о/і alternation. Same with ходити/вихід (о→і in the noun). Root: Ukrainian vowel alternation in declension and derivation is a systematic phonological rule, not random spelling.

4. **захід polysemy** — learners learn захід as "west" (A1) and are surprised it also means "measure/event" (захід безпеки) and "sunset/entering" (за + хід). This is a teaching opportunity for the -хід- root family.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **LEGO metaphor is pedagogically valid** — it directly maps to the "synthesis" concept in word formation. Use it as the cultural hook for the intro section.
2. **Cafe Ptakh mnemonic must be presented as-is** — it is confirmed in primary school textbooks and is the standard Ukrainian classroom mnemonic. Attribute it naturally.
3. **захід polysemy is a teaching opportunity** — present захід explicitly as "west/sunset" (за+хід = going behind/into the horizon) to show the root -хід- in action.
4. **гідроелектростанція as wow-factor only** — use once to demonstrate the power of synthesis, do not drill it or include in vocabulary table.
5. **Keep metalanguage low-frequency terms (префікс, суфікс, корінь) in Ukrainian** — they are already known from prior modules in the word-formation phase; reinforce rather than re-introduce.
6. **Decolonized angle**: Ukrainian is a synthetic language with its own internal derivational logic. The prefixal aspect system (за-, ви-, пере-, з-/с-) is not a "quirk" — it is the primary mechanism of the lexicon. Contrast with English *phrasal verbs* (get up, get out), not with Russian.
7. **Word target 2000 words** — Module is at A2 Applied Grammar band (M21-50), immersion target 55-75%. Recommend 60% Ukrainian. Five sections total 325+500+550+400+225 = 2000 exactly — write to 2100-2200 to ensure gate passes after editing.
8. **первісний** — VESUM confirms ✅ but this adjective (meaning "original/primordial") carries a slight archaic tone. Use it only in the metalanguage context (первісна форма слова), not as general vocabulary.
