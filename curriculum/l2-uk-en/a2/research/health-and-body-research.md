## Cultural Hooks
1. **Бахіли (Shoe covers)**: It is culturally mandatory to wear blue plastic shoe covers over your street shoes when entering a clinic or hospital in Ukraine. Entering without them is considered extremely rude.
2. **Traditional remedies (Народна медицина)**: Ukrainians frequently use "чай з малиною" (raspberry tea) for fever and "калина" (viburnum) for colds, alongside modern medicine.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Transitive interference (English transfer)**: "Я болю голову" (I hurt my head) → Correct form: "У мене болить голова" (My head hurts). Learners must grasp that the body part is the grammatical subject.
2. **False friends**: "Ангіна" translating to angina (heart condition) → Correct meaning: "Ангіна" = tonsillitis/severe sore throat.
3. **Impersonal state matching**: "Я погано" (I am bad) → Correct form: "Мені погано" (I feel bad - Dative state).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Immersion level**: A2 Module 34 falls into Band 2 (60-75% immersion). Explanations for abstract grammar (like the intransitive nature of `боліти`) can use English scaffolding, but vocabulary, dialogues, and practice instructions must be strictly in Ukrainian.
- **Decolonized Framing**: Avoid comparing the impersonal `у мене болить` structure to Russian equivalents. Instead, present it natively as the standard way Ukrainian conceptualizes physical sensation (focusing on the locus of pain rather than the person as an active agent).
- Ensure explicit focus on the difference between `лікарня` (hospital/inpatient) and `поліклініка` (clinic/outpatient).
