## Cultural Hooks
1. **Melody of the Language**: Ukrainian is celebrated for its melodic flow, which is heavily driven by the rhythmic, vowel-rich endings produced by its 7-case declension system.
2. **Proto-Slavic Heritage**: The Vocative Case (Кличний відмінок) is a deeply rooted feature preserved from Proto-Slavic, unlike many neighboring languages that lost it. It adds intimacy, respect, and emotional depth to communication.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Locative without Preposition**: "Я працюю лікарні" → "Я працюю в лікарні". (Locative ALWAYS requires a preposition like в, у, на, по).
2. **Animate Accusative Confusion**: "Я бачу друг" → "Я бачу друга". (Animate masculine nouns share Genitive and Accusative endings).
3. **Age with Nominative/To Be**: "Я є 20 років" → "Мені 20 років". (Age requires the Dative case for the experiencer).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Focus on the logic of Ukrainian cases as an independent, complete system. Highlight the Vocative as a proud preservation of heritage. Do not use Russian grammar as a point of comparison.
- **Persona Context**: Use the "Court Translator" persona to emphasize that case endings are not just rules, but critical markers of precision (who did what to whom).
- **Immersion**: This is an A2.1 module (Band 1, 50-60%). Use English for the overarching grammar theory (explaining the 7-case system and syntactic roles), but use Ukrainian for dialogues, examples, and practice instructions.
