# Дослідження: Adjective Suffixes — Types

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.1.2: "Прикметник: прикметники чоловічого роду із закінченням -ий: високий, дешевий, український; ... -ій: домашній, осінній..."
Alignment: The Standard explicitly lists adjectives with suffixes `-ськ-` (український) and `-н-` (домашній, осінній) as core A2 morphology. While the *theory* of word formation is detailed in B1 (§4.3.6), the *usage* of these high-frequency derived adjectives is required for A2 communicative intentions (describing objects, origins) and themes (shopping, home, travel).

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Яворівська забавка (Yavoriv Toy):** Unique wooden toys from Lviv region, traditionally made of aspen (осика) which was believed to drive away evil. Decorated with "verbivka" (willow branch) patterns in green, red, blue, and yellow.
2. **Гуцульський ліжник (Hutsul Lizhnyk):** Heavy woolen blanket made of sheep's wool (овеча вовна). Used as bedding, clothing, and in ceremonies (weddings, funerals). Famous for "biting" texture which acts as a natural massage.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Mutation Failure (-ськ-):** Learners often simply add `-ськ-` without changing the stem consonant.
   *   *Error:* Прага -> *Прагський*
   *   *Correct:* Прага -> Празький (г -> зьк)
2. **Suffix Confusion (-ов- vs -ев-):** Difficulty choosing based on stress and stem softness.
   *   *Error:* *дубевий* or *польовий* (stress dependent)
   *   *Correct:* дубовий (hard stem), поле -> польовий (soft stem + stress)

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Pedagogy:** A2 learners don't need the full table of all mutations. Focus on the big three: `к/ч/ц` -> `цьк`, `г/ж/з` -> `зьк`, `х/ш/с` -> `ськ`.
- **Mnemonic:** Use "Prague -> Prazkyi" as the anchor for the `г -> з` mutation, as it's a common travel destination.
- **Material Bridge:** Explicitly contrast "Natural/Traditional" (-ян-, e.g., wooden, clay) vs "Standard" (-ов-, e.g., metal, plastic) as a heuristic, even if not 100% linguistically rigorous, it helps learners guess.
