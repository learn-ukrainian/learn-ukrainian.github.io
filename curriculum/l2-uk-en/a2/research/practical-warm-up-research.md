## Cultural Hooks

**1. Book Forum Lviv (Форум видавців / Book Forum Lviv)**
Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia, `Book_Forum_Lviv`
- Operating since **1994**, held annually in September in Lviv.
- Officially known as "Форум видавців у Львові" until 2018, now "Book Forum Lviv" (BFL).
- It is both a book fair and the Lviv International Literary Festival.
- Organizer: NGO "Форум видавців."
- Pedagogical hook: Browsing book blurbs (анотації) at a bookstore stall is a perfect skimming/scanning task — find the головна ідея without reading every word. This mirrors the §1.2.1.3 competency (finding specific information in brochures/advertisements).

**2. Всеукраїнський радіодиктант національної єдності**
Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia, `Радіодиктант_національної_єдності`
- Annual national event held on **День української писемності та мови** (27 жовтня, moved from 9 листопада in 2023 following the Orthodox calendar transition).
- First held: **May 2000** (near Day of Cyril and Methodius); regularised to autumn from 2001.
- Text: **100–120 words**, specially commissioned; no authorial punctuation ambiguities, but high density of challenging orthograms.
- Participants write by hand, submit online or by post. Results checked within 15 minutes since 2019.
- In 2024 (25th anniversary): text "Магія голосу" by Оксана Забужко, read by poet-soldier Павло Вишебаба. Estimated 1 million+ participants.
- Written in 30+ countries; soldiers of the Ukrainian Armed Forces participated from Kursk Oblast in 2024.
- Pedagogical hook: The dictation directly demonstrates §1.3.1.1 (transferring heard content to written form). The name "єдності" (unity) ties the writing act to national identity — a powerful motivational frame for diaspora and international learners.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **чути vs. слухати** → Learners use `чути` for all hearing contexts, including intentional listening. Correct form when actively attending: `слухати уважно`. Example: `Я не слухав тебе уважно` (I wasn't paying attention to you) vs. `Я не чув твого голосу` (I physically couldn't hear your voice). Root cause: `чути` has a higher frequency (34 IPM vs. 20 IPM) and feels more "universal," much like the false-friend pull from English "to hear."

2. **питання vs. запитання** → Learners use `питання` for all question contexts. `питання` = a topic, issue, or matter (`важливе питання`, `соціальне питання`); `запитання` = a specific inquiry directed at someone requiring a response (`ставити запитання`, `відповісти на запитання`). Root cause: near-synonyms with similar frequency (~111 IPM each) and same root. Error is reinforced by the fact that `питання` is used informally in questions too (`У мене є питання` — acceptable colloquially but less precise than `запитання`).

3. **Skimming/scanning confusion** → Learners either read everything (no skimming) or skip too much (no scanning). The strategy distinction: **оглядове читання** = skim for main idea (don't stop on unknown words); **пошукове читання** = scan for specific data (name, date, number). Root cause: these strategies are intuitive in a native language but must be explicitly activated in L2. Learners slow down at unknown words, losing the main idea entirely.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The **питання/запитання** distinction is a genuine high-value language point, not just vocabulary trivia — both words appear at ~111 IPM, meaning learners will encounter both frequently in the wild. The Learner Error Clinic treatment is well-placed.
- The **чути/слухати** distinction maps neatly to the §1.1.1.2 Standard requirement: "active listening" vs. passive hearing. Frame the clinic around the Standard's language ("активне сприйняття").
- **Book Forum Lviv** is a strong cultural anchor: it features brochures, blurbs, posters — exactly the text types listed in §1.2.2 (рекламні тексти, брошури та проспекти). Use it to make skimming/scanning concrete and context-specific.
- **Radio Dictation** is a deeply Ukrainian institution (25+ years, 30+ countries, 2024: ~1M participants). It validates the writing section emotionally: learners aren't doing a dry exercise — they're joining a global Ukrainian ritual. Mention the 100–120-word length as a concrete target for the mini-dictation integration task.
- The plan's immersion target is **70–90%** (A2.6 Consolidation band). Content should default Ukrainian-first; English appears only in vocabulary tables and the two Learner Error Clinic explanations.
- Grammar scope check: No participles. Simple subordinate clauses only. Aspect pairs may appear. All 7 cases allowed. Ukrainian sentences max 15 words / 2 clauses. ✓
- The A2.6 cultural frame (Lviv Book Forum + Radio Dictation) positions Ukrainian cultural life as rich, internationally connected, and inherently linked to language pride — decolonized by design.
