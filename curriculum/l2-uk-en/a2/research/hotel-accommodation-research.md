## Cultural Hooks

1. **Туристичний збір (Tourist tax):** Verified via Wikipedia. «Туристичний збір — це місцевий збір, кошти від якого зараховуються до місцевого бюджету. Місцеві ради самостійно приймають рішення про необхідність справляння даного збору на території громади.» This is a genuine municipal-level fee in Ukraine, not collected by the central government. Rates vary by city. In practice, it is often added separately to the hotel bill — a genuine learner surprise worth flagging.

2. **Passport requirement for hotel registration:** Ukrainian law (Закон України «Про туризм» + internal registration rules) requires hotels to register every guest with their original passport (паспорт) — a real administrative requirement unlike many Western European countries where a credit card suffices. This is an authentic cultural-legal hook that explains why check-in dialogues always include a passport request. Textbook source: Grade 9 Avramenko textbook mentions hotel creation and the travel context (chunk `9-klas-ukrainska-mova-avramenko-2017_s0114`).

3. **«Гостиниця» vs. «готель»:** The Wikipedia article notes «готель, заст. гостиниця» — «гостиниця» is an archaic/dated term. Modern Ukrainian uses «готель» exclusively in standard register. This is a decolonization-adjacent point: «гостиниця» was the Soviet-era term shared with Russian (гостиница); post-independence Ukrainian standardized around «готель» (from French/German etymology). Worth a brief mention without making it the focus.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Кімната» vs. «номер»** → Correct: «забронювати номер» — *Why it happens:* English-speaking learners transfer "room" to «кімната» (which they learned for domestic rooms). Hotel Ukrainian uses «номер» exclusively. «Кімната» in a hotel context sounds like Ukrainian spoken by a foreigner or someone describing their apartment.

2. **«Я в готель» (Accusative) vs. «Я у готелі» (Locative)** → Correct: «Я зупинився у готелі» — *Why it happens:* Learners at A2 know that «у/в» can take accusative for direction targets and often over-apply it. The locative for static location must be explicitly drilled. This is a high-frequency error because «готель» sounds like a motion destination to learners arriving at a hotel.

3. **Omitting formal «Ви» register** → Correct: «Ви можете показати паспорт?» not «Ти можеш…» — *Why it happens:* Learners default to «ти» forms learned in earlier social modules. Hotel interaction is a formal service register where «Ви» is mandatory with all staff. This is especially notable given that Ukrainian «ти/Ви» distinction functions differently from learners' expectations based on informal conversation practice.

4. **«Бронювати» vs. «забронювати» confusion** → Correct use: «Я хочу забронювати номер» (completed booking intention) vs. «Вони бронювали онлайн» (process) — *Why it happens:* Aspect pairs are new at A2; learners initially treat both verbs as interchangeable.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Locative drill priority:** The «у готелі» vs. «у готель» error is structurally important and recurs across many A2 modules. Make the distinction vivid with a concrete scenario (e.g., "phone call: де ти? — Я у готелі" vs. "arriving: Я їду у готель").
- **Dialogue authenticity:** The plan's passport dialogue is genuine. Ukrainian hotel staff say «Ваш паспорт, будь ласка» at check-in — not "ID or credit card." Building this into the check-in simulation adds real cultural value.
- **Gender agreement hook:** «Номер» masculine agreement («гарний номер», «вільний номер», «цей номер») should be shown across multiple collocations, not just stated as a rule — minimize explicit grammar, maximize authentic examples.
- **Туристичний збір framing:** Keep factual and brief — it's a cultural footnote to the payment section, not a legal lesson. «У деяких містах до рахунку додають туристичний збір» is the right register.
- **«Гостиниця» note:** One brief mention is sufficient — "you may see the older form «гостиниця» but modern Ukrainian uses «готель»" — frame positively as vocabulary awareness, not a correction.
- **Aspect pair «бронювати / забронювати»:** The plan correctly flags this as the key A2 aspect pair for this module. Present it in a booking scenario where the imperfective shows process (online browsing) and perfective shows the completed act (confirmation email).
