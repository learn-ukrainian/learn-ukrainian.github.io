# Дослідження: Adjective Suffixes — Qualities

## State Standard Reference

**Primary: §4.5.1** (lines 1420–1425) — Стилістичні засоби лексики:
> «антоніми: тут – там, весело – сумно... синоніми: водій, шофер... епітети: веселі птахи, золоті руки, висока оцінка; метафори: прийшла осінь, місто спить.»

**Alignment**: §4.5.1 covers expressive lexical means (epithets, stylistic vocabulary). Diminutive/augmentative adjective forms (-еньк-, -есеньк-, -езн-, -енн-) are expressive vocabulary devices that fall under this umbrella.

**⚠ Honest gap**: §4.5.1 does not explicitly list diminutive adjective suffixes — it lists synonyms, antonyms, epithets, metaphors. Пестливі forms are implied by "epithets" and "stylistic vocabulary," but not stated. The plan's claim of "§4.5.1 on emotional-expressive lexis" is defensible but requires careful framing: this module teaches the *morphological tools* that produce those stylistic effects.

**Supporting: word_formation (A2 §4.3)**: No dedicated A2 suffix-formation §exists for diminutive/augmentative adjectives — these are introduced explicitly at B1 (§4.3.6 denominal adjectives). This module is pedagogically slightly ahead of the formal standard grid for A2.3 position. Frame as "expressive vocabulary expansion" anchored to §4.5.1, not as new grammar structure.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Козаки and the -цьк- rule (Wikipedia-confirmed)**: The word *козак* traces to Turkic roots meaning "free person"; Cossack communities were documented in Ukraine from the 15th century. The word is the canonical textbook example of the к→ц mutation before -ськ- (козак + ськ = козацький). This grounds the spelling rule in culturally significant Ukrainian identity vocabulary — entirely organic, not invented.

2. **Diminutives as social register markers (textbook-confirmed, Grade 11 Avramenko)**: Ukrainian expressive suffixes (-еньк-, -есеньк-, -ісіньк-, -усіньк-) appear in artistic and conversational registers — flagged explicitly in Grade 10 (Karaman) and Grade 11 (Glazova, Avramenko) textbooks as markers of *художній та розмовний стиль*. The social register distinction (appropriate vs. inappropriate contexts) is authentic pedagogical content, confirmed by textbook framing.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Spelling mutation before -ськ-**: Writing "козакський" (wrong) instead of "козацький" (correct); "пражський" instead of "празький." Root cause: learners apply -ськ- directly without processing the mutation rule (к→ц, г/ж→з, х/ш→с). Canonical textbook rule confirmed: к, ц, ч + -ськ- = -цьк- (Grade 6 Litvinova, Avramenko).

2. **Confusing -енний (emphatic adj.) with -ений (past passive participle)**: e.g., *страшенний* (terrible — expressive adj.) vs. *страшений* (frightened — participle). Double-н is the marker. Learners frequently write single-н. Stress also differs: страше́нний vs. страше́ний. (Note: the participle -ений is forbidden at A2, but the *emphatic adjective* -енний is appropriate — this distinction must be explicit.)

3. **Overusing diminutives in formal contexts (register error)**: Using пестливі forms with authority figures or in professional writing, e.g., asking for «підписочок» instead of «підпис.» Authentic Ukrainian competence requires knowing *when not* to use these forms — a productive pragmatic skill.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **величезний is the high-frequency default** for "huge" — use it as the anchor; treat здоровенний/страшенний as expressive variants with register labels.
- **The -ськ-/-цьк-/-зьк- mutation rule** is Grade 5–6 curriculum content (tier 1 textbooks). At A2, this is revision + spelling drills, not new grammar — frame as "spelling alert," not "new structure."
- **Emphatic suffix -есеньк-** takes е (not и): малесенький, білесенький, тонесенький — the Grade 9 Voron textbook explicitly lists this e/и distinction as an orthographic rule.
- **Do NOT introduce -ісіньк-/-усіньк-** at A2 — textbooks place these at Grade 11 register level; they are beyond A2 scope. Stick to -еньк- and -есеньк-.
- **Avoid passive participle territory**: -ений forms are B1+. The module must explicitly contrast страшенний (adj., double-н) vs. participle forms without teaching the participle itself.
- **Decolonized framing**: Ukrainian's expressive suffix system is a *morphological richness indigenous to Ukrainian* — comparable to diminutive systems in Portuguese (bonitinho), Czech (hezoučký), or Polish (ładniutki). Do NOT frame as "similar to Russian" under any circumstances.
- **Section-level word allocation**: Вступ 275 + Презентація 1 500 + Презентація 2 500 + Практика 425 + Діалоги 300 = 2,000 words total — plan allocation is exact, leave no buffer. Content writing must hit minimum exactly; recommend targeting 2,100–2,150 as a 5% safety margin.
