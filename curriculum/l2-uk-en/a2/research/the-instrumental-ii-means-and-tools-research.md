**Ending patterns summary:**
- Hard masc/neut stem → **-ом** (автобус → автобусом)
- Soft/mixed masc stem (ц, ж, etc.) → **-ем** (олівець → олівцем, ніж → ножем)
- Soft masc stem (-ь) → **-ем** (пензель → пензлем)
- Fem -а/-я → **-ою/-єю** (ручка → ручкою, виделка → виделкою)
- Indeclinable → **no change** (метро, таксі)

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Нічний поїзд (The Night Train)**: Long-distance train travel is deeply embedded in Ukrainian daily life and culture. Overnight trains (купе, плацкарт) connect cities like Kyiv–Lviv–Odesa. The phrase *їхати нічним поїздом* is a natural vehicle (no pun intended) for practicing instrumental with modifiers.
2. **Їдальня на поїзді (Train dining)**: Eating on trains with cutlery — *їсти виделкою, різати ножем, тримати ложкою* — is a real scenario where three instrumental forms cluster naturally in a single context, making it an ideal micro-story setting.
3. **«Чим пишеш?»** (What are you writing with?): The classroom question *Чим ти пишеш? — Я пишу ручкою/олівцем* is a classic Ukrainian school-exercise frame (echoed in the textbook excerpts: "Склади і запиши речення"). Using it as a warm-up mirrors authentic classroom register.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Redundant preposition** → *\*з ручкою пишу* instead of *ручкою пишу* — English "with" triggers a з prepositional reflex; learners must learn that instrument use requires NO preposition (unlike accompaniment).
2. **Hard-stem ending on soft/mixed stems** → *\*олівецом*, *\*ножом* instead of *олівцем*, *ножем* — learners apply the default -ом to all masculines. The textbook suffix exercises (Grade 3, vashulenko p.89) directly target this pattern.
3. **Declining indeclinable nouns** → *\*метром*, *\*таксієм* instead of *метро*, *таксі* — borrowed words that don't decline are a persistent trap; needs an explicit table.
4. **Confusing Як? vs. Чим?** → Learners conflate manner (*Як? — повільно*) with instrument (*Чим? — олівцем*). The distinction is not natural in English.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Show three sentences without explanation:
  > *Я йду з другом.* / *Я їду автобусом.* / *Я пишу ручкою.*
  Ask: "In which sentence does 'with' appear in Ukrainian? In which does it NOT?" Let the learner notice the pattern: accompaniment gets з, instrument/means gets nothing. This mirrors the contrastive exercise frame from Grade 4 textbook (zaharijchuk p.61 — "У яких відмінках іменники вживають без прийменників?").
- **Best context**: Train journey scenario — works for transport (їхати поїздом/автобусом), eating (їсти виделкою/ложкою/ножем), and writing (записати ручкою). One continuous micro-narrative covers all three vocabulary clusters.
- **Contrastive pairs**:
  - *Я йду з ручкою* (I'm walking with a pen on me) vs. *Я пишу ручкою* (I write with a pen = instrument)
  - *їхати метро* (indeclinable) vs. *їхати автобусом* (regular -ом)
  - *Чим?* answer: *ножем* vs. *Як?* answer: *акуратно*

## Notes for Content Writing
- Keep the persona (Hutsul Craftsman) grounded in concrete craft/travel scenes — don't let it drift into metaphor (forbidden at A2).
- The indeclinable nouns table (метро, таксі) must appear early and visually distinct — learners will memorize it only if highlighted.
- *користуватися* + instrumental is a high-value collocation (*користуватися телефоном, користуватися комп'ютером*) — introduce it in the Questions section as a natural extension.
- A2 immersion: 45–65% Ukrainian target for this module (Core grammar band, M01-20). Grammar rule explanations may remain in English; example sentences Ukrainian-only.
- Max 15 words per Ukrainian sentence; no participles; no complex subordinate clauses.
- *очима* is a suppletive form (from *oko*) — flag this briefly; don't over-explain.
