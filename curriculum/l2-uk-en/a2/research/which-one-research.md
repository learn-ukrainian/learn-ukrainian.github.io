# Дослідження: Which One?

## State Standard Reference

**§4.2.1.4** (lines 1244–1254): Розширена система займенників на рівні A2 — особові, присвійні, вказівні, питальні. The standard explicitly includes питальні займенники (*який, котрий, хто, що, скільки*) at A2 morphology level.

**§4.4.2** (lines 1403–1416): Complex sentences at A2 — coordinating (і/й, але), subordinating with де/куди/звідки, що, тому що/бо, щоб. **Important caveat**: the verbatim text of §4.4.2 does NOT explicitly name "який" in this section. However, the project's mapping file description (which was presumably verified against curriculum practice) includes "який/яка/яке" as in scope. The Ukrainian Wikipedia article on Займенник confirms the dual function: *"якщо ж займенники хто, що, який, чий, котрий, скільки зв'язують підрядне речення з головним, то вони є відносними."* The module is curricular grounded via §4.2.1.4 (interrogative pronoun paradigm) + §4.4.2 (complex subordinate sentences) read jointly.

**Alignment**: Module teaches the full declension paradigm of який/яка/яке/які (established at §4.2.1.4) in their function as relative pronouns connecting subordinate clauses to antecedents (§4.4.2 scope). Both components are A2-appropriate.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ukrainian ринок (market) culture** — Informal open-air markets remain a central institution in Ukrainian daily life, where bargaining and precise specification are expected. The question «Який?» / «Яка?» is the fundamental tool for selecting specific items among visually similar options. VESUM confirms *ринок* as a productive noun. This is an authentic, non-folkloric cultural setting for A2 learners.

2. **Folk proverb «Який пан, такий жупан»** — A Ukrainian folk saying meaning "like master, like coat" (a garment reflects its wearer's status). Uses *який* in its correlative (не-підрядний) function to illustrate matching/agreement — directly models the grammatical concept of *який* reflecting the properties of its antecedent. Ukrainian Wikipedia's article on *Займенник* confirms the proverb tradition of using *який* in correlative structures.

3. **«Як ви човен назвете, так він і попливе»** — Plan's second hook. This is a Ukrainian variant of a pan-Slavic saying; it works well as a closing reflection on the importance of naming/defining through relative clauses. Use with care — the plan already includes it, no additional research needed.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Хто» vs. «який» for people** → *Чоловік, хто живе тут* ❌ → *Чоловік, який живе тут* ✅
   — Why: English speakers transfer relative "who" → they use *хто* (= "who" as interrogative) rather than *який*. Ukrainian *хто* in relative function is restricted to antecedents like *той, хто...* / *кожен, хто...* (no explicit noun antecedent). Grade 6 Litvinova (NUS tier 1) explicitly teaches this distinction.

2. **Case copied from antecedent rather than assigned by internal clause role** → *Я бачу дівчину, яка я знаю* ❌ → *Я бачу дівчину, яку я знаю* ✅
   — Why: Learners see "дівчину" (accusative) and carry accusative to *який*, but the pronoun's case is determined by its syntactic role *inside* the subordinate clause (direct object of *знаю* → accusative, but for animate feminine: *яку*). This is the core case confusion the plan addresses.

3. **Missing comma before relative clause** → *Чоловік який стоїть там...* ❌ → *Чоловік, який стоїть там...* ✅
   — Why: English has restrictive vs. non-restrictive distinction (comma optional for restrictive). Ukrainian requires the comma before all relative clauses introduced by *який*, with no restrictive/non-restrictive exception at this level. This is a punctuation rule with near-zero exceptions at A2.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The full declension table of *який* has 14 cells (7 cases × 2 numbers, with gender splitting singular) — do NOT present all at once. The plan's scaffolding (Nom → Acc → other cases) is pedagogically sound. Phase B should build the table incrementally.
- The plan's "Mirror analogy" (який reflects antecedent's gender/number) + "Case Detective" (який dresses for internal job) are strong pedagogical metaphors — keep both; they teach two different rules.
- GRAC frequency data shows *які* (plural) has the highest frequency of all forms — ensure plural forms appear in substantive examples, not just footnotes.
- **котрий** is a valid literary alternative to *який* for relative clauses — mention briefly as a variant at A2 without drilling it (full treatment belongs at B1+).
- Avoid any framing such as "Ukrainian uses *який* where Russian uses *который*" — this is both structurally false (Ukrainian *який* and *котрий* cover the same space; Russian *который* is not the baseline) and violates decolonized framing policy.
- State Standard textbook grounding: Grade 6 Litvinova (NUS tier 1, 2023) §54 teaches питальні/відносні займенники including *який*, with declension tables for *котрий, яка, чиє* as practice. Content writing can reference this pedagogical approach.
- Word target is 2000 — the five sections (300+475+375+550+300) sum to exactly 2000. Zero buffer. Phase B writer must hit every section target and should aim for slight overages in the practice section (which has most grammar density).
