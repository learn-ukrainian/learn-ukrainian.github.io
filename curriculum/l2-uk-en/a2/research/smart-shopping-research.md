## Cultural Hooks

1. **Базар vs. Ринок distinction** (Wikipedia, verified): *Базар* is attested in Ukrainian as a synonym of *ринок* for a marketplace, but carries the additional sense of "noisy, disorganized market" (*гамірний, неорганізований ринок*) and is used for Eastern markets specifically. The word is of Eastern origin. In contemporary Ukrainian: *ринок* is the neutral standard term; *базар* is colloquial/informal. This is worth foregrounding — the module's cultural hook should use *ринок* as the primary term while acknowledging *базар* as the colloquial variant learners will hear.

2. **Traditional measurement units at Ukrainian markets**: *Стакан* (glass ~200ml) and *пучок* (bunch) are genuine traditional measurement units used at Ukrainian outdoor markets — verified in VESUM and textbooks. Both words are common at produce stalls for berries/seeds and herbs respectively. This is a distinctive, authentic Ukrainian market feature that adds cultural texture and is directly listed in §3.9 ("одиниці ваги й об'єму").

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Consonant alternation in comparatives**: `г → жч` and `з → жч` clusters. Learners produce `*дорожший` (correct: **дорожчий**) and `*ближший` (correct: **ближчий**). Root: direct suffix attachment without applying the alternation rule `г/з + -ш- → жч`. Confirmed in Grade 6 textbooks (Litvinova 2023, p.199): "під час творення форм ступенів порівняння відбуваються звукові зміни: суфікси -к-, -ок-, -ек- випадають."

2. **Double comparison contamination**: `*більш дешевший`, `*більш кращий`. Root: mixing synthetic (-ший) and analytic (більш/менш +) forms. Ukrainian uses one OR the other. Grade 6 Avramenko is explicit: either `дешевший` OR `більш дешевий`. The plan correctly targets this in the Practice section.

3. **Russian superlative calque** `*самий кращий` → **найкращий**. Root: Russian uses *самый лучший* but Ukrainian superlatives always use prefix *най-*. Grade 6 Golub (tier 1) states explicitly: "Слово «самий» ніколи не вживаємо з прикметниками, натомість використовуємо префікс най-." This is a high-frequency Russianism that must be explicitly pre-empted.

4. **Comparison particle confusion**: `*дешевший як` → **дешевший ніж** or **дешевший за** + Accusative. Root: Ukrainian uses *ніж* (than) or preposition *за* + Acc for comparisons. The particle *як* is not used for direct comparisons in standard Ukrainian (it has other functions). This is a subtle trap for learners from Polish (*tańszy jak*) and Russian backgrounds.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Register split is real and teachable**: The ринок vs. магазин/онлайн-магазин contrast maps cleanly to register (розмовний negotiation vs. formal fixed prices) — lean into this as the structural backbone of the Production section.
- **`доставка` vs `доставлення`**: Present `доставка` as the practical digital shopping term (it IS what Ukrainians use on Rozetka, Nova Poshta, etc.) with a footnote that formal/documentary Ukrainian uses `доставлення`. Don't confuse learners with an overly formal form they won't encounter in real life.
- **Suppletive forms need their own treatment**: великий→більший, малий→менший, гарний→кращий, поганий→гірший must be listed explicitly — these cannot be derived by rule and learners must memorize them. Grade 6 textbooks treat them as a discrete list.
- **`більш/менш` + positive form only**: Always `більш дешевий` (positive), never `більш дешевший` (comparative). Content must model this correctly in every example.
- **Accusative after `за` in comparisons**: `дешевший за цей` — the noun/pronoun after `за` takes Accusative. Drill this case alongside the comparative form.
- **Decolonized framing**: Ukrainian market culture (ринок, бабусі selling garden produce, traditional units) is indigenous and has its own rich social logic. Present it on its own terms — not as a post-Soviet phenomenon or an echo of anything external.
- **Digital shopping vocabulary** (кошик, оформити замовлення, доставка) reflects contemporary Ukrainian digital commerce infrastructure — these are words learners need immediately for real-world use.
