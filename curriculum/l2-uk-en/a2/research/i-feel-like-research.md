# Дослідження: I Feel Like...

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.2.3: "особа або предмет, для яких (на користь чи на шкоду) відбувається дія... суб’єкт кількісної вікової ознаки"
Alignment: Covers the Dative case usage required for indirect objects and impersonal constructions describing subjective states and feelings ("мені холодно", "мені сумно") as outlined for the A2 level.

## Cultural Hooks
1. Метеозалежність (Meteosensitivity): In Ukraine, discussing how "магнітні бурі" (magnetic storms) and weather changes affect physical well-being (headaches, fatigue) is a very common and socially acceptable form of small talk and bonding.
2. Folk wisdom: The proverb "Ломить руки, або коліна – буде погоди переміна" (Joints ache - the weather will change) perfectly illustrates the cultural expectation that physical sensations are deeply tied to nature.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Я холодний" (I am a cold person) instead of "Мені холодно" (I feel cold) — Using the English Nominative subject structure instead of the Ukrainian Dative experiencer.
2. "Вона сумно" instead of "Їй сумно" — Forgetting to decline the pronoun into the Dative case (вона → їй).
3. "Я гарячий" instead of "Мені жарко" — Direct translation mistake that results in a sexual/attractive connotation rather than describing temperature.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized framing:** Present the Dative experiencer construction as a natural, core feature of Ukrainian logic (feelings happen *to* you), without defining it as a "deviation" from Russian or English. 
- **Immersion:** This is Band 2 (60-75% Ukrainian). Use English *only* to explain the abstract grammar logic of the Dative experiencer in the introduction. All examples, dialogues, and practice drills must be strictly in Ukrainian.
- **Complexity:** Strict adherence to a maximum of 15 words per sentence.
- **IPA:** Provide IPA only on the first occurrence of new vocabulary words (e.g., жарко, боляче).
