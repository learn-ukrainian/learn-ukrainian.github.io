# Дослідження: I Feel Like...

## State Standard Reference

**§4.2.2.3 (A2 Dative case):** "Давальний відмінок: особа або предмет, для яких (на користь чи на шкоду) відбувається дія: Подарувати книжку студентові. Повідомити групі про зміни в розкладі; суб'єкт кількісної вікової ознаки: Дідусеві вісімдесят років. Києву понад півтори тисячі років."
*(lines 1287–1291)*

**Note:** The A2 Standard explicitly lists the Dative for indirect objects and age expressions, but does NOT spell out the impersonal experiencer construction (мені холодно) in this section. The plan cites §4.2.2.3 — this is the closest valid reference. The impersonal/experiencer Dative is covered more explicitly at B1 §4.2.2.3 ("Dative — recipient, experiencer, impersonal (мені потрібно), purpose"). At A2, the construct is introduced practically without full theoretical treatment; the State Standard thematic catalogue §3.12 (Здоров'я й особиста гігієна: самопочуття, хвороби й нездужання) directly mandates this vocabulary domain.

**Thematic alignment:** §3.12 "Здоров'я й особиста гігієна" — самопочуття (well-being/how one feels) is explicitly listed as an A2 thematic requirement (line 1149). §3.1 "Людина" covers basic character traits (line 1078: основні риси характеру), creating the Trait vs. State contrast the module exploits.

**Alignment:** Module addresses the Dative experiencer construction at its first productive introduction at A2. Full theoretical framing of the experiencer Dative belongs to B1, but active use at A2 is State Standard-compliant. The module correctly positions this as a practical vocabulary/pattern module with deferred deep grammar explanation.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Магнітні бурі (Magnetic storms) as social currency** — Wikipedia confirms магнітна буря = "збурення магнітного поля Землі тривалістю від кількох годин до кількох діб, викликане надходженням в околиці Землі збуджених високошвидкісних потоків сонячного вітру." In Ukrainian everyday culture, магнітні бурі are widely blamed for headaches, fatigue, and general malaise — a folk-scientific concept that functions as a socially acceptable way to complain about feeling unwell without admitting illness. This is a genuine bonding mechanism, not pseudoscience mockery. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Магнітна_буря

2. **Folk proverb on body-weather connection** — The plan cites "Ломить руки, або коліна – буде погоди переміна." This proverb links joint pain (боляче в колінах, ломить руки) to upcoming weather change — directly teaching physical sensation vocabulary in an authentically Ukrainian cultural frame. Proverbs of this type (народні прикмети) are a well-documented genre in Ukrainian folklore connecting physical sensation to nature observation. This is authentic folk wisdom, not a Soviet-era construct.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Nominative subject for states** → **Correct: Dative experiencer** — "Вона сумно" or "Вона сумна" (meaning *she is sad as a trait*) instead of "Їй сумно" (she feels sad right now). This is a direct transfer from English "She is sad" → learner maps to Nominative subject. The same error: "Я холодний/холодна" (I am a cold person) vs "Мені холодно" (I feel cold). Frequency: extremely common at A2.

2. **Adjective form instead of adverb form** — "Мені холодний" (adj) instead of "Мені холодно" (adv). Learners know adjective declension but conflate adjective and adverb predicates in impersonal constructions. The adverb is invariable; the adjective would require case agreement — confusion between the two forms is a key drill target.

3. **Confusing Я гарячий for body temperature** — "Я гарячий/гаряча" has a sexual connotation in colloquial Ukrainian (meaning "hot/sexy"), not "I have a fever." The correct form for body temperature is "Мені жарко" (I feel hot/feverish) or "У мене температура/гарячка" (I have a fever). This is a high-embarrassment false-friend error that must be flagged explicitly.

4. **Радий vs Щасливий conflation** — English "happy" maps to both, but: **радий/рада** = situational gladness (I'm glad about this specific thing, gender-marked, short adjective), **щасливий** = deep/lasting happiness or a personality trait. Learner error: using щасливий for momentary reactions ("Я щасливий, що ти прийшов" is possible but sounds heavier than "Я радий, що ти прийшов").

## Cross-References

- **Builds on:** `i-think-that` (A2-034) — subordinate clause structures (що + clause) that can frame emotional reactions: "Я думаю, що мені сумно через це." Also A2 foundational Dative case modules for pronoun forms (мені, тобі, йому, їй, нам, вам, їм).
- **Prepares for:** `in-order-to` (A2-036) — purpose clauses щоб + infinitive, which combine naturally with emotional states ("Я зроблю це, щоб тобі було весело").

## Notes for Content Writing

- **жарко is lower-frequency than the plan labels it** (1.93 IPM vs сумно at 14.20 IPM). It remains essential vocabulary (the мені жарко / мені холодно pair is a teaching anchor), but the content should note this is everyday conversation vocabulary rather than high literary frequency. The gap may reflect written corpus bias — spoken Ukrainian likely shows higher жарко frequency.
- **The Dative pronoun table is essential** for Phase B: я→мені, ти→тобі, він→йому, вона→їй, ми→нам, ви→вам, вони→їм. Present it early in Презентація as a reference table.
- **Concordance confirms natural usage patterns:** "Мені холодно!" consistently appears in dialogue (exclamatory, spontaneous complaint), not in formal prose. This validates the plan's розмовний register choice. "Мені сумно" appears in inner monologue and dialogue — slightly more reflective. Both are natural and authentic in the corpus.
- **метеозалежність is VESUM-verified** but not a high-frequency corpus word — treat as a cultural enrichment term, not a core vocabulary item.
- **State Standard gap:** The A2 standard does not explicitly name the Dative experiencer construction by name at this level. The module should avoid over-theorizing it — teach it as a pattern ("щоб виразити відчуття, використовуємо: [дат. займенник] + [прислівник]") rather than grammar metalanguage. Save full theoretical treatment for B1.
- **Avoid abstract nouns** per A2 constraints: do NOT use почуття, відчуття, стан, сутність. These are forbidden by level constraints. Instead say "як ти себе почуваєш?" not "твій емоційний стан."
- **Immersion target 55-75%** for A2.2 (Applied grammar band, modules 21-50) — this module at sequence 35 sits firmly in the applied grammar band.
