Alignment: This module directly addresses all four bullet points of §3.4 — free-time activities, hobbies and interests, sports (via займатися спортом), and cultural leisure. The grammar focus on грати/займатися is grounded in the vocabulary of the standard.

**Supporting grammar references:**
- §4.2.2.5 (Instrumental case, A2, lines 1304–1322): covers "об'єкт дії і стану" — the standard example is "Ми захоплюємося сучасною музикою" (Instrumental without preposition for activity object), directly paralleling «займатися спортом / йогою».
- §4.2.2.4 (Accusative case, A2, lines 1293–1302): covers Accusative with prepositions у/в, which grounds «грати у футбол».
- §4.2.2.6 (Locative case, A2, lines 1324–1332): covers «на» for location/surface, relevant to «грати на гітарі».

## Cultural Hooks

**1. Тихе полювання (Mushroom Picking)**
No standalone Wikipedia article exists under "Тихе полювання." However, the phrase is a well-established folk metaphor for mushroom foraging. The activity (збирання грибів) is documented as a traditional practice particularly in the Polissia region and other forested areas of Ukraine — confirmed by Wikipedia search results referencing mushroom gathering traditions in Borshch z hrybamy, Polissia cuisine, and ethnographic group articles. The Wikipedia article on Polissia cuisine notes that geographic and climate conditions of the region shaped food traditions, with mushroom and berry gathering central to subsistence and culture. The label "тихе полювання" (silent hunting) reflects the meditative, solitary nature of the activity vs. active hunting — a culturally authentic collocation used in Ukrainian everyday speech.
**Pedagogical framing**: Present as a Ukrainian cultural tradition with roots in the forested north and west; avoid framing it as "like in Russia" — mushroom foraging exists across Eastern Europe and is not Russia-specific. Ukraine's Polissia region has its own deep tradition.

**2. Вишиванка (Embroidered Shirt)**
Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (verified):
> "Вишиванка — важлива складова українського національного вбрання... Традиційна вишивка сорочок має оберегове значення й різниться залежно від регіону."
The embroidery patterns differ by region (Hutsulshchyna, Poltava, Podillia, Volyn — each has distinct color and motif traditions). Since the 20th century it has been worn as festive, patriotic, and identity-affirming clothing.
**Decolonization note**: Wikipedia uses the term "руська сорочка" in its historical sense (referring to Kyivan Rus' / Ruthenian tradition), NOT "російська" (Russian). Тhe вишиванка is a distinctly Ukrainian cultural form; Soviet-era framing attempted to subsume it under "East Slavic" homogeneity. Frame it as: «Вишиванка — це символ української ідентичності та регіонального різноманіття» — not as a generic Slavic garment.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Я граю спорт"** → Correct: **"Я займаюся спортом"**
   Why it happens: Direct calque from English "I play sport" / "I do sport." The verb грати is NOT used for sports in Ukrainian — займатися + Instrumental is required. This is one of the most frequent intermediate-level errors for English speakers.

2. **"грати футбол"** → Correct: **"грати у футбол"** (or **"грати в футбол"**)
   Why it happens: Learners omit the preposition, again following English "to play football." Ukrainian requires «грати у/в + Accusative» for games with rules.

3. **"займатися йога / танці / фотографія"** → Correct: **"займатися йогою / танцями / фотографією"**
   Why it happens: Learners forget the Instrumental case requirement after займатися, using Nominative forms instead. Requires drilling of Instrumental endings across noun genders and plurals.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **грати conjugation for content**: грати is conjugation I (граю, граєш, грає, граємо, граєте, грають). займатися is reflexive: займаюся, займаєшся, займається, займаємося, займаєтеся, займаються. Both are common enough that full paradigm exposure in examples is valuable at A2.

2. **вільний час phrase**: GRAC does not handle multi-word phrases. Confirmed in textbook usage. The phrase «у вільний час» (in one's free time) is the standard prepositional idiom — content should drill both «мати вільний час» and «у вільний час».

3. **шахи grammar note**: шахи is plural-only (pluralia tantum) — «грати в шахи» uses plural Accusative. Worth one brief note in content to prevent «грати в шах».

4. **Register**: Module targets розмовний. Avoid formal synonyms like «проводити дозвілля» in spoken examples — reserve for written/formal register contrast. Dialogues should use «Що ти робиш у вільний час?» not «Як ви проводите своє дозвілля?»

5. **Immersion band**: A2.5 = Consolidation band (M51-70), target 70-90% Ukrainian. English restricted to vocabulary tables and one-line grammar notes only. Section headers can remain bilingual (Ukrainian title / English subtitle).

6. **Word count**: 2000-word minimum. Section allocation per plan: Вступ 275 + Лексика 400 + Граматика 600 + Практика 525 + Підсумок 200 = 2000 exactly. Write to 2100–2200 to provide safety margin.
