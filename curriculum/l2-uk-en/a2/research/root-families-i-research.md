# Дослідження: Root Families I

## State Standard Reference

**§4.3.2** (A2 Word Formation — Aspect Pairs, lines 1380–1381):
> "Видові пари дієслів: робити – зробити, ділити – поділити, **писати – написати, виходити – вийти**, забувати – забути."

The Standard directly names *писати* and *виходити* (from root *ход-*) as canonical examples. This module's root-family approach serves as the motivating structure behind why these aspect pairs work the way they do — prefix + root logic explains both meaning change and aspect formation.

**§4.2.3.1** (A2 Verb Indicative, lines 1341–1359):
> "Indicative — expanded: aspect pairs (доконаний/недоконаний), motion verbs (їхати/**ходити**)"

*Ходити* is explicitly an A2-required motion verb. Teaching the *ход-* family here directly fulfills this requirement.

**§4.1.1** (A2 Phonetics — Consonant Assimilation, lines 1176–1181):
> "Expanded phonetics — consonant assimilation, voiced/voiceless"

The з-/с- prefix rule (с- before к, п, т, ф, х) is confirmed by Glazova textbook (Grade 10): "Префікс с- пишуть перед літерами к, п, т, ф, х: **сказати, списати, стулити, сформулювати, схилити**." The *сходити* form directly exercises this rule.

**Alignment**: Module covers §4.3.2 (aspect pairs via root logic), §4.2.3.1 (ходити paradigm), and §4.1.1 (з-/с- assimilation before х). No dedicated §for "root families" exists at A2 — the topic spans these three sections.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Писанка and the пис- root**: Wikipedia confirms — *Писанка* is "яйце, декороване традиційними символами, які **пишуться** за допомогою воску й барвників." The verb *писати* is literally embedded in the practice — Easter eggs are *written* not painted. The Ukrainian word *писати* originally meant "to scratch/mark/decorate" (Proto-Slavic *pьsati*), making писанка a living fossil of the root's original meaning. This is a pedagogically powerful hook: the root пис- is not just about literacy but about marking the world. [Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Писанка]

2. **Побачення as "mutual vision"**: *По-* (reciprocal action) + *бач-* (seeing) = a date, a meeting where two people *see each other*. This etymology reveals how Ukrainian conceptualizes social connection through the act of mutual perception. Contrast with *до побачення* (goodbye = "until seeing"). Both expressions are deeply embedded in everyday A2 usage and make the бач- root memorable.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **\*зходити → сходити** — Learners transfer the default prefix form з- before х. The rule is с- before К, Ф, П, Т, Х (voiceless consonants). Error source: з- is the default prefix form drilled earlier; learners do not automatically apply the assimilation rule to new words. Fix: explicit rule + drill with *сходити, сказати, схилити*.

2. **Restricting записати to "writing down"** — Learners from English backgrounds use *записати* only for text (notebook). In Ukrainian, *записати* also covers audio/video recording (*записати відео, записати розмову*). This causes avoidance errors (learners say \*зафіксувати розмову or avoid the verb entirely). Fix: demonstrate both collocations explicitly.

3. **Confusing бачити / дивитися** — *Бачити* = passive perceptual state ("to see / be able to see"), *дивитися* = active intentional watching ("to watch/look at"). English uses "see" and "watch" similarly, but learners frequently substitute *бачити* for *дивитися* in contexts requiring directed attention (*\*Я бачу фільм* → correct: *Я дивлюся фільм*).

This confirms the pedagogical framing in the plan — ходити is an explicitly "life-essential" productive root in Ukrainian linguistics pedagogy. The "3 hundred core roots → 20 thousand words" statistic is a real textbook figure (Karaman, p.129) and can be used as a concrete teaching hook.

From **Glazova Grade 10** (tier 2): з-/с- rule formally stated with examples, confirms *сходити* correction is standard textbook content.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The **з-/с- phonetics section** is well-supported by textbooks and should be taught as an orthographic rule with clear mnemonic (КФПТХ = voiceless cluster, use С-). Avoid calling it "the Kafka-Ptakh rule" in content — use a clear Ukrainian mnemonic instead.
- **Frequency ordering for introduction**: бачити (50.69 ipm) → писати (36.89) → читати (28.90) → ходити (27.45). Consider introducing most-frequent first in warm-up, then organize pedagogically by logical grouping (motion / writing / reading / vision).
- **сходити ambiguity** must be handled carefully in activities — use context sentences that make meaning unambiguous, or limit to staircase context (*сходити сходами*).
- The aspect pairs in §4.3.2 (*писати–написати*, *виходити–вийти*) are the exact output targets. Frame the root-family lesson as "understanding *why* these aspect pairs work" — prefix meaning = semantic/aspectual change.
- A2 register: Avoid abstract etymological terms in Ukrainian-language examples. Use concrete contexts — library, post office, entering/exiting a building — to demonstrate all derivatives.
