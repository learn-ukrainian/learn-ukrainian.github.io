## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

The topic of "Root Families" (кореневі гнізда) falls under the **Словотвір (Word Formation)** competency. In the *Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (2024)*, this is explicitly introduced at the **A2 level** in **Section 4.3**.

> **§4.3. Словотвір.**
> "Особа вміє розпізнавати та використовувати граматично правильні мовні форми для вираження власних намірів у межах комунікативних потреб... Обсяг граматичних умінь рівня А2 охоплює... розширення лексичного матеріалу, зростанням діапазону синтаксичних структур."

Specifically, the standard highlights for A2:
- **4.3.1.** Degrees of comparison (requires understanding adjectival suffixes).
- **4.3.2.** Aspectual pairs (requires understanding prefixes and suffixes like *на-*, *з-*, *-ува-*).

At this stage, the student is expected to transition from learning isolated words to recognizing the **morphological structure** of the language to accelerate vocabulary acquisition.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Root Families

At the A1-A2 transition, students already possess a "passive" collection of words that belong to the same families. This module organizes them.

### High-Frequency Root Families for A1/A2:

| Root | Meaning | Key Vocabulary (A1/A2) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-мов-** | Speech / Language | **мова** (language), **розмова** (conversation), **розмовляти** (to speak), **вимова** (pronunciation) |
| **-пис-** | Writing | **писати** (to write), **написати** (to finish writing), **лист** (letter), **опис** (description) |
| **-чит-** | Reading | **читати** (to read), **прочитати** (to finish reading), **читання** (reading n.), **читач** (reader) |
| **-роб-** | Work / Do | **робити** (to do), **робота** (work), **робітник** (worker), **розробляти** (to develop) |
| **-вч-** | Learning | **вчити** (to teach/learn), **вчитися** (to study), **вчитель** (teacher), **учень** (student), **вивчити** (to master) |
| **-ход- / -ід-** | Motion (walk) | **ходити** (to walk), **вхід** (entrance), **вихід** (exit), **перехід** (crossing), **виходити** (to go out) |

### Common Suffix Patterns (A1/A2 Scope):
- **-тель / -ач**: Nouns indicating a profession or person doing an action (*вчитель, читач*).
- **-к(а)**: Diminutives or feminine versions (*мова -> мовка, вчителька*).
- **-нн(я)**: Abstract nouns from verbs (*читання, навчання*).

## 3. Cultural Hook: "The DNA of Language"

1.  **Etymology of "Ukraine"**: A powerful hook for students is exploring the root **-край-** (edge, land, border).
    - **Україна** (Ukraine), **країна** (country), **край** (edge/region), **кращий** (better - related via the concept of "the edge/prime").
    - This highlights how the name of the country itself is embedded in a "family" of words denoting land and belonging.
2.  **Diminutives as Emotional Root Families**: Ukrainian culture uses root-based diminutives extensively to express warmth.
    - Root **-вод-** (water): **вода** (water) -> **водичка** (sweet little water).
    - Root **-мам-**: **мама** -> **матуся**, **мамуся**.
    - This is not just grammar; it is the "emotional DNA" of how Ukrainians speak to loved ones and children.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English:
- **Synthetic vs. Analytic**: English often uses different roots for related concepts (e.g., *teach* vs. *professor*), whereas Ukrainian stays within the Slavic family (*вчити* vs. *викладач*).
- **Semantic Transparency**: Once a student knows the root **-роб-**, they can guess the meaning of *робітник*, *розробка*, and *безробіття*. English lacks this "transparency" for students who don't know Latin/Greek roots.

### Common Learner Errors:
- **Root Alternations (Чергування)**: Students often fail to recognize a root when the vowel changes (*о/і* or *е/і*).
    - Example: Failing to see that **рік** (year) and **року** (of the year) share the same root.
- **Over-application of Prefixes**: Trying to use prefixes like *re-* or *un-* as in English instead of Slavic prefixes (*пере-*, *не-*).

### Teaching Sequence:
1.  **Identify the Core**: Start with a known verb (e.g., *писати*).
2.  **Add Suffixes**: Show how the person (*письменник*) and the result (*лист/запис*) are formed.
3.  **Add Prefixes**: Show basic directional changes (*описати, підписати*).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (A1/A2):
- **Noun suffixes**: -тель, -ка, -ар/-яр (професії).
- **Verb prefixes**: на-, про-, ви-, за-, при- (basic meanings).
- **Alternations**: Basic o/i, e/i shifts in roots (*стіл/стола, шість/шостий*).
- **Word Classes**: Connecting a verb to its corresponding noun and adjective.

### OUT Scope:
- **Complex Abstract Nouns**: Suffixes like *-ість* (e.g., *свідомість*) or *-ство* (e.g., *мистецтво*) should be introduced sparingly as vocabulary, not as a productive rule yet.
- **Participles**: Words like *працюючий* or *написаний* are B1+ territory.
- **Archaic/Obsolete Roots**: Stick to high-frequency modern roots.
- **Deep Etymology**: Avoid Indo-European reconstructions; stay within Slavic commonalities.
