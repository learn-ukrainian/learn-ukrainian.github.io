# Дослідження: Root Families I

## State Standard Reference
§4.3.2: "Видові пари дієслів: робити – зробити, ділити – поділити, писати – написати, виходити – вийти, забувати – забути."
§4.2.3.1: "Дієвідмінювання дієслів у теперішньому часі: ... ходити (ходжу, ходиш, ходить...), ... пити (п’ю...)"
Alignment: This module directly addresses the A2 requirement for mastering aspectual pairs and high-frequency verb conjugations, specifically focusing on the productive roots *пис-* (*написати*) and *ход-* (*виходити*), which are explicitly cited in the Standard.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Root Family | Core Verb | Derivative Count | Key Collocations |
|-------------|-----------|------------------|------------------|
| **ход-** (motion) | ходити / йти | Very High (50+) | вхід (entrance), вихід (exit), перехід (crossing/transition), прихід (arrival), похід (campaign/hike) |
| **пис-** (writing) | писати | Very High (40+) | підпис (signature), опис (description), розклад (schedule - related concept), запис (record/entry), письменник (writer) |
| **чит-** (reading) | читати | High (20+) | читач (reader), читання (reading), прочитати (to read through), вичитати (to proofread) |
| **бач-** (vision) | бачити | High (15+) | побачення (date), бачення (vision/view), передбачити (to foresee), обачний (cautious) |

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Painted" Root**: The root *пис-* originally meant "to paint" or "to decorate" (Proto-Slavic *pьsati), which connects the verb *писати* (to write) directly to *писанка* (Easter egg). Writing was historically seen as a form of intricate decoration.
2. **The "Honored" Reader**: The root *чит-* comes from Proto-Slavic *čisti (to count, to honor). This suggests that "reading" was historically linked to "counting" or "reckoning" (making sense of things) and "honoring" the text.
3. **Greek Connection**: The root *ход-* is cognate with Ancient Greek *hodos* (way, path, journey), found in English words like *method* (meta + hodos) and *exodus* (ex + hodos).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Prefix Overload**: Confusing directional prefixes with resultative ones (e.g., *виходити* vs. *приходити*).
   *   *Correction*: Use physical motion analogies (in/out) to anchor meanings.
2. **Euphony Violations**: Struggling with *з-* vs. *зі-* vs. *с-* prefixes before roots (e.g., *зходити* instead of *сходити*).
   *   *Correction*: Teach the "Kafka-Ptakh" rule early for *с-* prefix.
3. **False Friends**: Assuming *записати* always means "to write down" (it can also mean "to record" audio/video) or *підписати* simply means "to sign" (it implies legal agency).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Visual Analogy**: Use the "Lego" metaphor strongly. The root is the "block," the prefix is the "connector."
- **Tone**: Use the etymology of *писанка* to make the *пис-* root feel magical rather than mechanical.
- **Simplification**: Do NOT introduce all prefixes. Stick to the "Big 4" for A2: *в-, ви-, при-, під-*.
