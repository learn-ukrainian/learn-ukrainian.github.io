## State Standard Reference

**Primary grammar reference — §4.2.2.3 (A2 Dative case):**
> "Давальний відмінок: особа або предмет, для яких (на користь чи на шкоду) відбувається дія: Подарувати книжку студентові. Повідомити групі про зміни в розкладі; суб'єкт кількісної вікової ознаки: Дідусеві вісімдесят років."
(Lines 1287–1291)

Note: The standard's A2 Dative entry covers indirect object and age constructions but does NOT explicitly list impersonal emotional constructions (мені весело). The impersonal pattern is covered more explicitly at B1 §4.2.2.3 (lines 2183–2192: "experiencer, impersonal — мені потрібно"). For A2 this means the emotional-dative introduction is a *forward* extension of the case's core meaning — pedagogically justified and expected, but the exact мені+adverb pattern should be introduced as a **new communicative function**, not as something "already taught."

**Secondary grammar reference — §4.2.1.2 (A2 Adjective declension):**
> "Прикметник: прикметники чоловічого роду із закінченням -ий: високий, дешевий, український; прикметники жіночого роду із закінченням -а: висока, дешева, українська; прикметники середнього роду із закінченням -е: високе, дешеве, українське; прикметники у формі множини: високі, дешеві..."
(Lines 1223–1233)

Alignment: The module's gender-agreement drilling (Він добрий / Вона добра) directly targets §4.2.1.2's full gender paradigm. The hard/soft distinction for personality adjectives (добрий/добра/добре; синій → not relevant here) is explicitly within scope.

**Thematic reference — §3.1 (A2 Тематичний каталог — Людина):**
> "основні риси характеру" (basic character traits)
(Line 1078)

This confirms personality adjectives are an explicit A2 thematic requirement. The module sits squarely within the standard's scope.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Кордоцентризм — "Philosophy of the Heart"** (Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia, Кордоцентризм)
The concept was named by philosopher Oleksandr Kulchytsky; the idea of "філософія серця" was coined by Dmytro Chyzhevsky. Key founders: Hryhorii Skovoroda, Pamfil Yurkevych, Taras Shevchenko. Core claim: Ukrainian philosophical tradition places the *spirit/heart* (серце) as the foundation of human existence — not just emotionalism but holistic personhood. For the A2 lesson, the accessible framing is: Ukrainians often say things like "що у тебе на душі?" ("what's in your soul?") because in Ukrainian culture, emotional authenticity (щирість) is valued over surface-level politeness.
**Important caveat for content writing**: the Wikipedia article notes that the common popular interpretation (emotions dominate reason) is "inadequate" — the scholarly meaning is about the primacy of *spirit in the whole person*. For A2, anchor the hook in the practical phrase "на душі" without over-claiming about national character.

2. **Прислів'я: "Не по одежині судять, а по розуму"** — This proverb is authentic Ukrainian folk material. It maps directly to the plan's §3 vocabulary section and reflects the cultural value placed on inner character (розум, доброта) over appearance. A cross-check: Grade 9 Avramenko textbook cites related material on "гідність" (dignity) and character — confirming this theme is taught in Ukrainian schools in the character-traits context.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Nominative instead of Dative for emotional states** → Incorrect: *Я нудний* / Correct: *Мені нудно* — Happens because English uses "I am bored" with the subject in nominative; learners map the nominative "I/Я" pattern onto Ukrainian. The adjective form Я нудний is grammatically possible but means "I am a boring person" (permanent trait), not "I feel bored right now" — a false cognate trap within Ukrainian itself.

2. **Masculine default for feminine subjects** → Incorrect: *Вона добрий* / Correct: *Вона добра* — Happens because learners often encounter masculine forms first in vocabulary lists and over-generalize. Particularly common with adjectives ending in -ий (добрий, спокійний, веселий). The -а feminine ending needs explicit drilling with female names/pronouns.

3. **False friend: симпатичний ≠ sympathetic** → Incorrect: *Вона дуже симпатична до нього* (meaning "she feels sympathy for him") / Correct usage: *Вона дуже симпатична* = "she is very attractive/pleasant." English "sympathetic" = *співчутливий* or *розуміючий* in Ukrainian. This is a high-stakes error because the word is very commonly used but the meaning gap is wide.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Avoid abstract nouns**: The plan's cultural hook (Кордоцентризм) must be introduced through the concrete phrase "на душі" — never use the word "кордоцентризм" in the student-facing text itself. Present it as a cultural note in a sidebar/engagement box, not in the main lesson prose.
- **Dative section is the grammatical core**: §4.2.2.3 supports the Dative case but the emotional impersonal pattern is not explicitly named there — content should frame it as "a new use of Dative you already know" (learners saw Dative for indirect object in earlier modules). Be explicit about the Nominative vs. Dative contrast.
- **Word target 2000**: This is A2 Consolidation band (M51-70), so 70–90% Ukrainian immersion is required. Grammar explanations can use English, but all examples, dialogues, and practice must be Ukrainian-only sentences.
- **Frequency-first ordering**: In vocabulary tables, list by GRAC frequency — добрий first, симпатичний later. This matches natural input frequency.
- **Proverb section**: "Не по одежині судять, а по розуму" is Grade 5-appropriate, confirmed as standard school material. Can be used as a reading hook without copyright concerns.
- **Gender agreement**: Grade 5 Avramenko textbook uses personality adjective tables for describing people — content can adopt the same "деталь портрета + означення" structure as a practical scaffold.
- **симпатичний false friend**: Must appear early in the module (introduction or Section 1 sidebar), not buried in Section 3. False friends discovered late cause learner frustration.
