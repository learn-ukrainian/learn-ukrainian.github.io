## Cultural Hooks

1. **Academic prestige in Ukrainian job culture** — Ukrainian higher education follows a degree-ladder (бакалавр → магістр → аспірантура → докторантура), regulated by the Law on Education. Diplomas (дипломи) and academic titles are prominently listed on CVs and carry significant social weight. Indicating your університет, факультет, and qualification is standard in a резюме, as modelled in the Avramenko Grade 9 textbook (sample resume exercise, p. 137). Source: Wikipedia "Вища освіта в Україні."

2. **Formal address register** — Professional Ukrainian culture requires `Ви` (formal you) throughout a job interview and workplace until explicitly invited to switch to `ти` with the phrase "можна на 'ти'?". The formal address `Пан / Пані + прізвище` (Mr./Ms. + surname) is the standard opening in an interview, e.g. "Добрий день, Пане Коваленку." This is a real-world register trap for English speakers who switch to first names immediately.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **праця vs. робота** — `шукати працю` → **Correct:** `шукати роботу`. `Праця` means labor/effort/work as an abstract concept; `робота` is the job or place of employment. "Шукати роботу" = to look for a job. This is explicitly flagged in the plan as a learner error.

2. **Literal "як + noun" for profession** — `Я працюю як менеджер` → **Correct:** `Я працюю менеджером` (Instrumental). English speakers transfer the "as a manager" construction literally. The State Standard §4.2.2.5 provides the exact corrective model: "Він працював менеджером."

3. **Register collapse: Ти instead of Ви** — Learners trained on conversational Ukrainian default to `ти` in all contexts. In a formal interview, this is a serious register error. Must use `Ви` + formal verb forms until given permission otherwise.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Instrumental case reinforcement** is the grammar spine of this module. Every profession example must use `працювати / бути + Instrumental` — not `як + Nom`.
- **заробітна плата** (formal/written) vs **зарплата** (spoken) — introduce both; label register difference explicitly.
- The Avramenko Grade 9 resume exercise (p. 137) confirms that резюме structure (мета, освіта, досвід роботи) is taught in Ukrainian schools — learners who attended Ukrainian school will recognize this. For L2 learners, this is new vocabulary.
- Dialogue section must model the `Ви`-register throughout: "Коли ви можете почати?" / "Яка бажана зарплата?" — these are fixed interview phrases worth memorizing as chunks.
- Avoid `отримувати зарплату` if tempted to use `получати` (Russianism) — the correct form is `отримувати` or `одержувати`.
- Module is A2 Consolidation band (M51-70): target 70-90% Ukrainian immersion. English only in vocabulary tables.
