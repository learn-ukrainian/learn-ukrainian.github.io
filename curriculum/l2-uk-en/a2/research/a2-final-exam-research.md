## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

The module corresponds to the completion of **II. Початковий рівень другого ступеня (А2)** in the Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (2024).

### Specific Sections (§):
- **§4.2 Morphology (Lines 1165–1440):** Mastery of the full case system (Nominative, Genitive, Dative, Accusative, Instrumental, Locative, and Vocative) for nouns, adjectives, and pronouns in singular and plural.
- **§4.2.3 Verbs (Lines 1400–1430):** Full conjugation in Present, Past, and Future (Imperfective composite and Perfective simple) tenses.
- **§4.3.2 Aspect Pairs (Lines 1450–1455):** Ability to distinguish and use perfective/imperfective pairs (e.g., *робити/зробити*, *писати/написати*).
- **§4.3.1 Comparison (Lines 1442–1448):** Simple and composite degrees of comparison for adjectives (*кращий*, *більш важливий*).
- **§4.4 Syntax (Lines 1460–1495):** Use of complex sentences with conjunctions *бо*, *тому що*, *що*, *щоб*, *який*, *коли*.

## 3. Cultural Hook: Exams and Assessment in Ukraine

1. **Grade System (12-point scale):** Ukraine uses a 12-point grading system in schools (12 being the highest, 1-3 being "unsatisfactory"). At the university level, the ECTS (European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System) 100-point scale is common, often converted to traditional grades: 90-100 (A/5), 82-89 (B/4), 74-81 (C/4), 64-73 (D/3), 60-63 (E/3).
2. **NMT (National Multi-Subject Test):** Since the full-scale invasion, Ukraine replaced the traditional ZNO (External Evaluation) with the NMT, a computer-based comprehensive test. Passing these exams is a rite of passage for every Ukrainian student, often accompanied by "Останній дзвоник" (Last Bell) traditions.
3. **Language Certification:** The National Commission for State Language Standards administers exams for government officials and candidates for citizenship. For foreign learners, the "Certificate of Proficiency in Ukrainian as a Foreign Language" (UMKI) is the standard benchmark.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

- **Case Interference:** At the end of A2, students often "mix and match" case endings (e.g., using Genitive *-а* where Dative *-ові* is needed). The exam should test high-contrast scenarios.
- **Aspect Trap:** The biggest challenge is choosing between *робив* (process) and *зробив* (result) in past narratives. Learners often default to imperfective because it's simpler.
- **Preposition Congruence:** Ensuring the preposition matches the case (e.g., *до* + Genitive vs. *на* + Accusative for movement).
- **Teaching Sequence:** The exam should move from **Recognition** (Multiple Choice) to **Transformation** (Change tense/aspect) to **Production** (Short dialogue/essay).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (Prior Knowledge):
- **Nouns/Adjectives:** All 7 cases (Singular/Plural).
- **Verbs:** Present, Past, Future; Imperative (Full paradigm); Aspect pairs.
- **Adjectives:** Comparative and Superlative degrees.
- **Numerals:** Cardinal (1–1000+) and Ordinal; Agreement with nouns.
- **Syntax:** Simple and Complex sentences (*який, бо, тому що, щоб*).
- **Mood:** Conditional (*якби*) and Imperative.

### OUT of Scope (Future B1/B2):
- **Participles & Gerunds:** (*читаючий, прочитавши*) — STRICTLY B1+.
- **Passive Voice (Participal):** (*написаний, зроблений*) — Only simple reflexive passive is known.
- **Complex Subjunctive:** Beyond simple *якби* + past.
- **Scientific/Technical Jargon:** Content remains within personal, social, and basic professional spheres.
- **Dialectal Variations:** Stick to Standard Literary Ukrainian.
