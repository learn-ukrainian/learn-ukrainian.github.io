## Cultural Hooks
1. **Surzhyk as a Stepping Stone**: Surzhyk (a sociolinguistic blend) is often stigmatized, but for learners, it represents a natural, brave transitional phase. Modern Ukrainian discourse emphasizes that speaking imperfectly is vastly better than not speaking at all.
2. **Vocative Case (Кличний відмінок)**: A deep historical feature of Ukrainian that survived Soviet standardization attempts. Using "Пане/Пані" + Vocative shows deep respect and cultural fluency, contrasting casual address.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Negation with Accusative** → **Negation with Genitive**: Learners use "Я не бачу книгу" instead of "Я не бачу книги". In Ukrainian, direct objects of negated verbs switch to the Genitive case.
2. **Perfective Present Trap** → **Imperfective Present**: Learners use perfective verbs for current actions ("я зараз напишу"), but perfective verbs lack a present tense—their present-conjugated forms carry a *future* result meaning.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized Framing: Frame the Vocative case as a resilient marker of Ukrainian identity. Frame Surzhyk without referencing "Russian dialects" but as a natural interlanguage phenomenon.
- Immersion: Band 3 (75-90% Ukrainian). Keep English scaffolding strictly to brief clarifications of abstract rules and vocabulary translations. Let the learner read mostly in Ukrainian.
