# Дослідження: Advanced Motion Prefixes

## State Standard Reference

**§4.2.3.1** (lines 1341–1359): Дійсний спосіб — explicitly lists motion verb conjugations (іти, їхати, ходити, їздити) and perfective future formation: *"майбутній час дієслів доконаного виду: скажу, закричите, поборють."*

**§4.3.2** (lines 1380–1381): Видові пари дієслів — includes *"виходити – вийти"* as a canonical aspect pair example, directly confirming that prefixed motion verbs are A2-scope content.

**Alignment**: This module expands on §4.2.3.1 (motion verb conjugation) and §4.3.2 (aspect pairs with prefixes). It bridges toward B1 §4.3.8 (lines 2336–2338: "Prefixed motion verbs — ви-, при-, за-, від-"), which extends the system. The A2 standard treats prefixed motion verbs as aspect pairs; this module teaches the spatial semantics of each prefix — a natural A2.3 "applied grammar" extension.

**Note on scope**: The State Standard does not list пере-, про-, об-, під-, до- as individual named prefixes at A2 — that granularity appears at B1 §4.3.8. However, the aspect pair principle (§4.3.2) and motion verb conjugation (§4.2.3.1) together fully justify this module. Frame the content as "learning aspect pairs of motion verbs through spatial meaning" rather than a prefix taxonomy.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Чорний кіт перейшов дорогу** — Ukrainian Wikipedia (uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Забобон) explicitly states: *"людина, що побачить перед собою чорного кота, може піти обхідною вулицею"* — confirming the superstition is documented in Ukrainian sources (not a Russian import). The article uses *обхідна вулиця* naturally, directly demonstrating об-prefix usage. The superstition uses пере- (the cat *crosses* the path) and the avoidance uses об- (going *around*). This is a pedagogically perfect double hook for two prefixes.

2. **Повернення додому — погана прикмета** — The belief that returning home shortly after leaving brings bad luck is a documented Ukrainian folk superstition (referenced in Українська мала енциклопедія, Буенос-Айрес 1958–1959). The remedy is to look in the mirror (*подивитися у дзеркало*) before leaving again. This hooks за- (*зайти* back home) and під- (approaching one's own door again). Note: this superstition is pan-Slavic in distribution — present it as "a Ukrainian folk belief" without attributing a specific ethnic origin.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Preposition mismatch with підійти/підходити** → Correct: *підійти ДО зупинки* (Genitive) — Why: Learners transfer the accusative destination pattern (іти В/НА + Acc) to approach verbs, producing *підійти в зупинку*. The verb підходити lexically requires "до + Genitive" because it encodes proximity, not arrival-inside.

2. **Compound future with perfective verbs** → Correct: *прийду* (not *буду прийти*) — Why: English speakers use "will + infinitive" for all future, mapping it to *буду + infinitive*. Prefixed perfective motion verbs form simple future directly; the State Standard §4.2.3.1 explicitly lists this: *"майбутній час дієслів доконаного виду."*

3. **пройти мимо** (Russianism) → Correct: *пройти ПОВЗ* — Why: Ukrainian uses *повз* (+ Accusative) for "past/by," while *мимо* is a borrowing from Russian that has infiltrated learner output. The Ukrainian form *повз* is standard and high-frequency. Similarly avoid *мимохідь* in favor of *проходячи повз*.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Textbook support (Grade 5, Заболотний 2023, Tier 1)**: The textbook teaches motion prefixes via a radial diagram — *бігти* at center with spokes for при-, за-, до-, по-, на-, в-, з-, від-, ви-, пере-, об-, під-. Content can reference this visual logic ("one engine, many steering wheels") without copying textbook content.
- **Aspect pair framing is mandatory**: At A2, all prefixed verbs must be presented as aspect pairs (перейти / переходити). Never introduce only the perfective without its imperfective partner.
- **Perfective future conjugation drill is essential**: *прийду, прийдеш, прийде, прийдемо, прийдете, прийдуть* — these forms must appear explicitly. This is a key §4.2.3.1 requirement.
- **"до + Gen" governs approach verbs**: підійти до, дійти до, зайти до — maintain consistent preposition drilling throughout.
- **перейти на vs перейти до**: distinction matters — *перейти на українську* (switch language = на + Acc), *перейти до наступного питання* (move on to next = до + Gen). Both are high-frequency and worth explicit contrast.
- **Decolonized framing**: The prefix system in Ukrainian has independent structure. Spatial semantics of пере-, про-, об- are described through Ukrainian-language visual metaphors and Ukrainian city contexts (Lviv Old Town, Kyiv Maidan area). No Russian comparisons.
- **55–75% Ukrainian immersion target** (applied grammar band, M42): English used only for abstract prefix semantics; all examples, dialogues, and cultural content in Ukrainian.
