# Дослідження: Advanced Motion Prefixes

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.1: "майбутній час дієслів доконаного виду: скажу, закричите, поборють." (Alignment: Future tense of perfective prefixed motion verbs like `прийду`, `перейду` without `буду`).
§4.2.2.4: "в, у, на (кінцевий пункт руху суб’єкта): Діти їдуть в Одесу." vs §4.2.2.2: "до (кінцевий пункт руху): Треба дістатися до готелю;" (Alignment: Teaching the distinction between preposition use with directional verbs vs approaching verbs).
§4.3.8: "Утворення префіксальних дієслів руху: іти – прийти, перейти, дійти, відійти, зайти, вийти..." (Alignment: A2 foundation bridging to B1 word formation).

## Cultural Hooks
1. "Чорний кіт перейшов дорогу" (A black cat crossed the path) — A common Ukrainian superstition about bad luck involving crossing spatial paths.
2. The "Mirror Ritual" for returning: In Ukrainian culture, returning home shortly after leaving (because you forgot something) is considered bad luck. To neutralize this, one must look in the mirror (подивитися у дзеркало) before leaving again.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Preposition Mismatch (Approaching vs Entering) → `Підійти в магазин` (Incorrect) vs `Підійти до магазину` (Correct) — Learners often use `в/у` (into) when the prefix `під-` specifically denotes approach (to/towards), requiring `до` + Genitive.
2. Coming vs Going (Arrival vs Departure) → `Пішов` (left) vs `Прийшов` (arrived) — Learners mix up the absolute direction relative to the speaker.
3. Perfective Future with `буду` → `Буду прийти` (Incorrect) vs `Прийду` (Correct) — Applying imperfective analytical future rules to perfective prefixed verbs.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Treat Ukrainian spatial logic as its own unique geometric system. Explain prefixes purely through visual mechanics (vectors, boundaries) rather than comparing them to Russian equivalents.
- Ensure sentences are kept short (max 15 words).
- Use Band 2 Immersion (60-75%): English should only be used for abstract grammatical concepts or bridging explanations, while Ukrainian handles all examples, dialogues, and practice instructions.
