# Research Notes: Орудний відмінок III — Бути та ставати (Instrumental Case III — Being and Becoming)

**Track**: Core A (Ukrainian for English Speakers)
**Module**: A2 M06
**Researched**: 2026-02-08
**Level**: A1+ (Transition to A2)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (рівні А1 – С2)**, approved by the National Commission on State Language Standards on August 29, 2024 (effective September 6, 2024), the Instrumental case without a preposition is used to express the characteristics of a person by profession or status when used with certain verbs.

Specifically, the standard includes:
> **§ 4.2.2.5.1. Без прийменника:**
> **характеристика особи за професією чи статусом:** Олег буде програмувальником. Микола хоче стати викладачем. Він працював менеджером. Вона мріє стати громадянкою України.

This module focuses on the use of the Instrumental case with copular and semi-copular verbs (to be, to become, to work as) to describe roles, professions, and social statuses.

The vocabulary for this topic centers on common professions and verbs of state and aspiration.

### Status and Roles (High Frequency)
*   **громадянин / громадянка** (citizen)
*   **друг / подруга** (friend)
*   **колега** (colleague)

### Key Verbs (High Frequency)
*   **бути** (to be) — Used in Past (*був/була*) and Future (*буду*) and Infinitive (*бути*).
*   **стати** (to become) — *став/стала*, *стану/станеш*.
*   **працювати** (to work [as]) — *працюю/працюєш*.
*   **хотіти** (to want [to be/become]) — *хочу бути*, *хочу стати*.
*   **мріяти** (to dream [of being/becoming]) — *мрію бути*, *мрію стати*.

## 3. Cultural Hook

### The Professional Identity Question: "Ким ви працюєте?"
In Ukrainian, when asking about someone's job, the question is literally "As whom do you work?" (*Ким ви працюєте?*) or "Who do you want to become?" (*Ким ти хочеш стати?*). This use of the Instrumental case (*Ким*) highlights that a profession is viewed as a "mode of being" or a "tool" through which one interacts with society.

### Prestige of the IT Sector
Ukraine has one of the fastest-growing IT sectors in Europe. Becoming a "програміст" (programmer) is a very common goal for young Ukrainians, often discussed using the verb *стати*. Phrases like *«Мій син хоче стати програмістом»* (My son wants to become a programmer) are ubiquitous in modern Ukrainian daily life.

### "Бути людиною" (To be a human)
A common Ukrainian moral idiom is *«Головне — бути людиною»* (The main thing is to be a [good] person/human). This reflects a deep cultural value placed on character over professional status, providing a perfect example of the Instrumental case used for an essential human status.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### The Present Tense "Zero Copula" Trap
The biggest hurdle for English speakers is the inconsistency between the Present and other tenses:
*   **Present:** *Я лікар.* (Nominative - "I am a doctor") — No "to be" verb is used, so the case is Nominative.
*   **Past/Future:** *Я був лікарем. / Я буду лікарем.* (Instrumental) — The presence of the verb *бути* triggers the Instrumental case.
*   **Interference:** Students often say *«Я є лікарем»* (incorrect in most contexts) or *«Я був лікар»* (grammatically weak or archaic).

### Hard vs. Soft Endings
This module is a critical point for practicing the masculine soft ending **-ем** vs. the hard ending **-ом**.
*   Hard: *директор* → *директором*, *лікар* (hard R in this declension group) → *лікарем* (Wait, *лікар* is soft/mixed, usually *лікарем*). 
*   *Correction:* In the Instrumental singular, masculine nouns ending in -р follow specific rules. *Лікар* ends in -ем (*лікарем*), while *інженер* ends in -ом (*інженером*). This nuance requires careful drilling.
*   Soft: *вчитель* → *вчителем*, *водій* → *водієм*.

### Feminine Nouns
Feminine nouns ending in **-а** change to **-ою** (*лікарка* → *лікаркою*), and those ending in **-я** change to **-ею** (*програмістка* → *програмісткою* - wait, *програмістка* is hard, so *програмісткою*). *Продавчиня* → *продавчинею*.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope:
*   **Singular nouns** (professions and status).
*   **Masculine and Feminine declension** (hard and soft).
*   **Verbs:** *бути* (Past/Future), *стати*, *працювати*, *хотіти/мріяти бути*.
*   **Interrogative pronoun:** *Ким?*

### OUT of Scope:
*   **Plural Instrumental** (e.g., *ми хочемо бути вчителями*).
*   **Adjectives in Instrumental** (e.g., *бути хорошим лікарем*). Focus should remain on the noun endings first.
*   **Other Instrumental verbs** like *керувати* (to lead/manage), *захоплюватися* (to be fascinated by), or *користуватися* (to use), even if they appear in the same DSTY section. This module focuses on "State of Being".
*   **Complex status** (e.g., *бути батьком трьох дітей* - Genitive construction).
*   **Instrumental of Time/Manner** (e.g., *весною*, *швидким кроком*).

By focusing on the "Present Nom vs. Past/Future Inst" contrast, students master a fundamental structural difference between English and Ukrainian.
