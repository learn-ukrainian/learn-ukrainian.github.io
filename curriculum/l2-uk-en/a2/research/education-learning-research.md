## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

The module aligns with **Державний стандарт української мови (ДСТУ 2024)**, specifically:

- **Thematic Catalog (Catalog B, Section 3.7):** "Навчання: основні канцелярські товари; дії, пов’язані зі здобуттям знань (вчити, вивчати, вчитися); навчальні дисципліни" (Lines 1113-1116).
- **Grammatical Competence (Catalog V):** Use of cases for educational context:
    - **Accusative:** Objects of study (вивчати математику).
    - **Locative:** Place of study (вчитися в університеті).
    - **Instrumental:** Tools for learning (писати ручкою).
    - **Dative:** Subjects of interest/liking (мені подобається історія).

> **Quote (Section 3.7):**

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Collocations

### High-Frequency Items (A2 Core)
- **Places:** школа, університет, клас, аудиторія, бібліотека.
- **People:** вчитель / вчителька, викладач / викладачка, учень / учениця, студент / студентка.
- **Subjects:** математика, історія, література, українська мова, англійська мова, біологія, географія.
- **Objects:** книга (підручник), зошит, ручка, олівець, дошка, розклад.
- **Events/Results:** урок, лекція, іспит (екзамен), оцінка, перерва.

### Common Collocations
- **вчитися в школі / в університеті**: (Locative for location).
- **вивчати [Accusative]**: вивчати мову, вивчати хімію.
- **складати іспит**: to take/pass an exam.
- **отримувати оцінку**: to get a grade.
- **йти на заняття**: to go to class (Accusative for direction).
- **виконувати домашнє завдання**: to do homework.

## 3. Cultural Hook

### Knowledge Day (День знань)
September 1st is a major cultural event in Ukraine. It marks the beginning of the school year with "The First Bell" (Перший дзвоник) ceremony. Students traditionally bring large bouquets of flowers for their teachers, and the day is celebrated as a festive occasion rather than just a return to work.

### The 12-Point Grading System
Unlike the 100-point or A-F systems common in the West, Ukrainian schools use a 1-12 scale. 12 is the highest (excellent), while 1-3 are considered failing. In higher education, this often maps to a 4-point scale (2=fail, 3=satisfactory, 4=good, 5=excellent) or the ECTS system.

### "Пара" (The Pair)
In Ukrainian universities, students don't talk about "classes" but about "пари" (pairs). This refers to a double-period block (usually 90 minutes). A student might say, "У мене сьогодні три пари" (I have three classes/pairs today).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### The "To Learn" Confusion
Ukrainian distinguishes between three verbs that English often collapses into "to learn" or "to study":
1.  **Вчитися (де? як?):** Intransitive. Refers to the process of being a student or gaining a general skill. *Я вчуся в університеті.*
2.  **Вивчати (що?):** Transitive. Refers to studying a specific subject or discipline in depth. *Я вивчаю програмування.*
3.  **Вчити (що?):** Transitive. Refers to memorizing something (like words or a poem) OR (informally) used similarly to *вивчати*. *Вчити* also means "to teach" when used with a Dative object (*Вчитель вчить нас*).

### Student vs. Pupil
English uses "student" broadly. In Ukrainian, **студент** is strictly for higher education (universities, colleges). Schoolchildren are **учні** (singular: **учень** / **учениця**). Calling a 7-year-old a "студент" sounds unnatural in Ukrainian.

### Case Reinforcement
- **Instrumental:** Reinforce "means/tools" (M05 knowledge) by asking students what they write with (*Я пишу ручкою*).
- **Dative:** Use with *подобатися* (M03 knowledge) to express favorite subjects (*Мені подобається географія*).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### In Scope
- **Grammar:** All A1 cases (singular) plus A2 additions (Dative/Instrumental).
- **Aspect:** Distinguishing between the process of studying (*я вчив*) and the result of learning something (*я вивчив*).
- **Prefixes:** Basic prefixes like *ви-* (вивчити - to learn fully) and *за-* (записати - to write down).
- **Clauses:** Using *що* (reported speech) and *щоб* (purpose). "Я вчуся, щоб знати мову."

### Out of Scope
- **Passive Voice:** "He was expelled" or "The exam was taken" are too complex.
- **Advanced Academic Jargon:** Terms like *дисертація*, *абревіатура*, or specific scientific terminology beyond basic school subjects.
- **Participles:** "The graduating student" or "The learned material" should be avoided.
- **Genitive Plural:** While some plural nouns might appear, complex plural declension is not the focus here.
