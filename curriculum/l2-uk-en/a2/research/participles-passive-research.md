# Дослідження: Passive Participles

## State Standard Reference

**Critical finding — incorrect §reference in plan:**

The plan cites `§4.2.3.1: participles required at A2 level`, but the actual A2 §4.2.3.1 (lines 1341–1359) contains **no mention of participles**. It covers indicative mood conjugation only (present/past/future verb forms: мріяти, казати, іти, їхати, пити, боротися).

Participles (дієприкметник) appear explicitly first at **B1 §4.2.3.1** (lines 2269–2291): *"Indicative — full conjugation, aspect in all tenses, participles (дієприкметник), gerunds (дієприслівник)"*.

**Correct A2 alignment:**

§4.2.1.2 (lines 1223–1233): *"Adjective declension — full paradigm"*
→ Teaching lexicalized participle-adjectives (відкритий, закритий, зроблений) AS adjectives within the A2 adjectival paradigm is the legitimate A2 hook. The plan's framing ("Scaffolding B1 morphological concepts through the lens of A2 adjectival declension §4.2.1.2") is correct in spirit — the §reference in the `sources` field should be corrected to **§4.2.1.2** for A2 compliance.

**Quote from actual A2 §4.2.3.1** (line 1341):
> *"4.2.3.1. Дійсний спосіб: дієвідмінювання дієслів у теперішньому часі: мріяти (мрію, мрієш, мріє, мріємо, мрієте, мріють)..."*

No participle content here. The module is pedagogically defensible as adjectival vocabulary enrichment, but should not claim to fulfil a State Standard participle requirement at A2. The formal participle standard is B1.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ukrainian proverb with dative participle**: *«Дарованому коню в зуби не заглядають»* — internationally attested proverb (confirmed via Wikipedia: Swedish equivalent *ska inte skåda given häst i munnen*). The word *дарованому* is a past passive participle (from *дарувати*) declined in dative singular masculine — a living example of a participle functioning exactly like an adjective in a fixed expression that A2 learners will encounter in everyday Ukrainian. The proverb is a natural anchor for the predicative/attributive section.

2. **Ukrainian street/public signage**: Past passive participles appear everywhere in urban life — shop signs (*ЗАЧИНЕНО / ВІДЧИНЕНО*), elevator panels (*ЗАЙНЯТО*), public notices (*ЗАКРИТО на ремонт*, *ВІДКРИТО*). These are not full participles in their -ний/-тий form but related short forms; however, signs such as *Зачинений магазин* (a closed shop) and *Відкритий урок* (an open lesson — a specific Ukrainian school tradition) use the full adjectival form. The *відкритий урок* (open lesson tradition in Ukrainian schools) is a culturally significant collocation worth a brief mention — source: Ukrainian school practice, well-documented. No Wikipedia article needed.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Agreement mismatch with grammatically plural nouns** → *двері відкритий* ❌ → *двері відкриті* ✅ — Why: English "the door is open" treats "door" as singular conceptually; Ukrainian *двері* is plural (plurale tantum), so the participle must be plural too. Same issue with *окуляри*, *гроші*, *штани*.

2. **Importing Russian active participle forms** → *читаючий студент* ❌ → *студент, який читає* ✅ — Why: Learners with Russian background or who have encountered Russian-influenced Ukrainian text bring -ючий/-ачий active present participle forms. Standard Ukrainian replaces these with relative clauses; Avramenko Grade 7 (2024, tier 1) and Zabolotnyi Grade 11 explicitly list this as a мовна норма violation. This is not a Ukrainian–Russian comparison framing — it is a Standard Ukrainian norming issue.

3. **Confusing -ний/-тий choice** → *відкритий* (from відкрити, monosyllabic stem → -тий) vs. *написаний* (from написати, -а- stem → -ний) — Why: The suffix alternation rule (-н- after -а-, -ен- after consonant/-и-/-і-, -т- from monosyllabic stems) is non-obvious and learners guess randomly. High-frequency lexical set approach (memorizing the forms as adjectives) sidesteps this productively at A2, deferring the generative rule to B1.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Pedagogical reframe**: Lead with the lexical approach — these are adjectives that happen to come from verbs. The "participial" grammar machinery is B1 content; A2 is about recognition and use of a high-frequency set as adjectives.
- **§reference correction needed**: The plan's `sources` note (`§4.2.3.1: participles required at A2 level`) is factually incorrect per the actual State Standard text. The A2 alignment is §4.2.1.2 (adjective declension). Phase B writer should frame this as adjectival enrichment, not as fulfilling a formal participle requirement.
- **Active participle ban**: Textbooks from Grade 7 (Litvinova 2024, tier 1) and Grade 11 (Avramenko 2024, Zabolotnyi) all confirm -ючий/-ачий forms are non-standard. Frame this as a Ukrainian language norm, not a contrast with any other language.
- **Suffix table**: Textbook sources (Litvinova 2024 Grade 7, Glazova Grade 11) provide clear formation tables: -н- (основа на -а-) → прочитаний; -ен-/-єн- (основа на приголосний/-и-/-і-) → побачений, озброєний; -т- (односкладові дієслова) → налитий, колотий. Use these at the recognition level only.
- **Proverb case forms**: *Дарованому коню* — дат. відм., однина, чоловічий рід — is a natural drill for the dative participle-adjective, but keep it light; it's a cultural hook, not a grammar drill.
- **Frequency priority**: Lead activities with відкритий, зроблений, закритий (highest ipm). зламаний is lower frequency but high communicative utility in daily life.
