# Дослідження: Preferences and Choices

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.2.3: "Давальний відмінок: особа або предмет, для яких (на користь чи на шкоду) відбувається дія"
§4.3.1: "Ступені порівняння якісних прикметників: проста форма вищого ступеня: солодший, важливіший"
Alignment: This module directly addresses the A2 requirements to express communicative intentions regarding likes/dislikes using the Dative experiencer constructions ("Мені подобається") and to compare options using comparative forms ("краще ніж").

## Cultural Hooks
1. Lviv Coffee Culture (Львівська кава): Choosing coffee in Lviv is a deeply rooted ritual. Locals often use the Galician word "філіжанка" (cup) and differentiate between traditional brewing methods like "кава на піску" (coffee brewed in hot sand).
2. Proverbial Wisdom: "На колір і смак товариш не всяк" (To each their own taste and color) highlights the Ukrainian cultural acceptance of differing personal preferences and politely handling disagreement.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Я подобається" → "Мені подобається" — Nominative overload: direct mapping of the English nominative subject "I" to Ukrainian, failing to recognize the Dative experiencer logic.
2. "Мені подобається цю книжку" → "Мені подобається ця книжка" — Object case error: incorrectly treating the liked object as an Accusative direct object, instead of the Nominative subject that is doing the pleasing.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized Framing: Explain the Dative experiencer construction as a fundamental feature of Ukrainian that emphasizes the effect of an object on a person, rather than framing it as "unlike English/Russian". Use positive, independent descriptions.
- Avoid Russianisms in examples (e.g., use "отримувати" not "получати", "їсти" not "кушати").
- IPA for new words on first occurrence only: ensure [ɔ] for о, [ɛ] for е, [t͡ʃ] for ч.
- Maintain Band 1 immersion (50-60%): Grammar explanations MUST be in English, but all dialogues, practice prompts, and examples must be in Ukrainian.
