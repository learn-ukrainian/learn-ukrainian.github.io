# Дослідження: Introduction to Gerunds

## State Standard Reference
§4.4.2 (B1 Level): "просте речення з дієприслівниковим зворотом: Повернувшись з екскурсії, ми смачно пообідали в ресторані"
Alignment: While the State Standard formally lists gerundial phrases (дієприслівникові звороти) as a B1 syntactical requirement, this curriculum introduces them in Module A2-75 as a bridging topic. This prepares learners for the heavy narrative texts and complex sentence structures they will immediately encounter at the B1 level, focusing first on passive recognition.

## Cultural Hooks
1. Folk Wisdom: The proverb "Скінчивши діло — гуляй сміло" (When the work is done, play freely) is a universally known Ukrainian idiom that perfectly demonstrates the past gerund (-вши) indicating a completed prior action.
2. Decolonisation of Grammar: The eradication of active present participles ending in *-ючий* (e.g., *працюючий*, *читаючий* as adjectives) is a major focus of modern Ukrainian language purification, rejecting Soviet-era grammatical russification in favour of native relative clauses (*який працює*).

## Common Learner Errors
1. Dangling Gerunds: "Читаючи книгу, задзвонив телефон" → "Коли я читав книгу, задзвонив телефон" OR "Читаючи книгу, я почув телефон". Learners often fail to match the gerund's implied subject with the main clause's subject.
2. Russian-style active participles: "*працююча людина*" → "людина, яка працює". Direct calques from Russian (*работающий*) or English (*working*).

## Cross-References
- Builds on: A2 verb conjugation (Conj I and Conj II), perfective/imperfective aspect.
- Prepares for: B1 narrative texts, B1 complex syntax.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Immersion target is 65-75%. Since this introduces a completely new abstract grammatical category, use English scaffolding to explain the core concept, but keep all examples and drills in Ukrainian.
- Frame Ukrainian grammar independently. Do not say "Unlike Russian, Ukrainian doesn't use -ючий as an adjective." Instead, say: "Authentic Ukrainian syntax prefers relative clauses over active participial adjectives."
