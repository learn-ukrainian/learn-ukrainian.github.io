# Дослідження: Numerals and Case Agreement

## State Standard Reference

Alignment: This module precisely addresses the A2 requirement for the declension and agreement of cardinal and ordinal numerals, specifically tackling the interaction between numbers and noun cases.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Dual Number (*Двоїна*)**: Historically, Ukrainian had a third grammatical number used specifically for pairs (e.g., eyes, hands). While officially suppressed during the 1933 Soviet linguistic reforms to force Ukrainian grammar closer to Russian, its legacy lives on! This is why the numbers 2, 3, and 4 trigger a different noun form (nominative plural) than 5+.
2. **Fossilized Duals**: Common words for paired body parts like *очі* (eyes) and *плечі* (shoulders), as well as the instrumental ending *-има* (*очима*, *дверима*), are direct descendants of this ancient grammatical feature.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Russian Transfer with 2, 3, 4**: *Два друга* (RU genitive singular) → **Два друзі** (UKR nominative plural). This happens because learners incorrectly apply the Russian rule.
2. **Missing Gender Agreement with 1 & 2**: *Один книга* / *Два пляшки* → **Одна книга** / **Дві пляшки**. Learners forget that Ukrainian "one" and "two" must agree with the noun's gender.
3. **The 11-14 Trap**: *Одинадцять рік* → **Одинадцять років**. Learners over-apply the "last digit rule" (1 triggers nominative) to 11-14, which should strictly follow the 5+ rule (genitive plural).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Explicitly present the 2-4 Nominative Plural rule as an elegant evolution of the Ukrainian dual number, highlighting its uniqueness and deep roots rather than treating it as "different from Russian."
- **Band 2 Immersion (M26)**: Use English ONLY for abstract grammatical concepts (e.g., explaining the concept of case agreement zones). All dialogues, examples, and practice must be in Ukrainian.
- **Tone**: The Patient & Supportive Ukrainian Tutor. Use practical scenarios like shopping for groceries or asking for age/time.
