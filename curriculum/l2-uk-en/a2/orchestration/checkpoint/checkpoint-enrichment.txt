## Cultural Hooks
1. **Tea with Raspberry & Viburnum**: Ukrainians traditionally treat colds with hot tea containing raspberry jam (*малина*) or viburnum (*калина*), believing in their natural antipyretic properties. Honey (*мед*) is often added instead of sugar.
2. **"Будьмо!"**: The universal Ukrainian toast "Будьмо!" literally means "Let us be!" (implied: healthy/alive), connecting social rituals directly to health.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Який Agreement**: Learners often fail to decline *який* to match the gender/case of the noun it replaces (e.g., using *який* for a feminine noun instead of *яка*).
2. **Щоб vs. Що б**: Confusing the conjunction *щоб* (in order to, one word) with the pronoun+particle *що б* (what would, two words).
3. **Тому що vs. Бо**: Overusing *тому що* in casual speech where *бо* is more natural, or using *бо* in very formal writing where *тому що* is preferred.
4. **Taking Medicine**: Using literal translations for "take medicine" instead of the correct *приймати ліки* (though *пити ліки* is common colloquially).

## Cross-References
- Builds on: A2 Modules covering basic case usage (Genitive/Accusative for body parts/pain), basic sentence structure.
- Prepares for: B1 Health modules (more specific diseases), B1 complex syntax (conditional *якби*).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Immersion**: Use Ukrainian for the dialogue and practical advice; keep grammar explanations for syntax in English/mixed as per A2 Band 2.
