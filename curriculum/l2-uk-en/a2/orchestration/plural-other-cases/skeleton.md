## Давальний множини: Кому? (~550 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): [Introduction to the Dative plural - the most "grateful" case in Ukrainian due to its extreme regularity across all genders. Explain the universal endings -ам (for hard stems like студентам, столам, вікнам) and -ям (for soft stems and -ь/-я endings like друзям, пісням, знанням).]
- P2 (~100 words): [Contrast the simplicity of Dative plural with the complexity of Genitive plural (which the learner just finished). Emphasize that in Dative plural, gender effectively disappears as a variable for ending choice, unlike in the singular.]
- P3 (~120 words): [Verbs of interaction and communication. Explain verbs that require the Dative case: давати (to give), допомагати (to help), телефонувати (to call), and дякувати (to thank). Use examples: "Ми дякуємо вчителям," "Я телефоную друзям."]
- P4 (~110 words): [Pedagogical Focus: Formal vs. Informal contexts. Using Dative plural to address groups: пояснювати дітям (explaining to children) vs. радити колегам (advising colleagues). Include the specific example: "Вчитель пояснює правила учням."]

- P5 (~100 words): [Common nouns with vowel shifts or specific stems in the Dative: батьки -> батькам, діти -> дітям. Note that the stem used for the Dative plural is almost always the same as the Nominative plural stem.]

## Орудний множини: З ким? Чим? (~600 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): [Introduction to the Instrumental plural endings -ами (hard) and -ями (soft). Explain its dual role: accompaniment (with 'з') and means/instrument (without preposition). Examples: студентами, книжками, мовами vs. друзями, вулицями.]
- P2 (~130 words): [High-frequency irregularities. Deep dive into the four essential irregular forms: діти -> дітьми, люди -> людьми, коні -> кіньми, гості -> гістьми. Explain that while "гостями" exists, "гістьми" is the classic form often found in literature and formal speech.]
- P3 (~110 words): [Accompaniment with friends and family. Using 'з/із' + Instrumental plural to describe social activities. Examples: "грати з дітьми" (playing with children), "гуляти з друзями" (walking with friends), "працювати з колегами" (working with colleagues).]
- P4 (~120 words): [Instrumental as a tool (means of action). Describing how we do things using plural nouns: писати олівцями (writing with pencils), їсти паличками (eating with chopsticks), прикрашати кімнати квітами (decorating rooms with flowers).]
- P5 (~120 words): [Dialogue: Organizing the school trip. A conversation between Вчитель and Учні where they discuss traveling "автобусами" (by buses), taking photos "камерами" (with cameras), and meeting "з місцевими жителями" (with local residents).]

## Місцевий множини: Де? На чому? (~500 words total)
- P1 (~100 words): [Introduction to Locative plural endings -ах (hard) and -ях (soft). Remind the learner that the Locative case NEVER appears without a preposition. Examples: у школах, на полицях, у містах.]
- P2 (~100 words): [Preposition 'у/в' for enclosed spaces and geographical locations. Focus on nouns like парках, лісах, готелях, театрах. Example: "Ми живемо в готелях під час подорожі."]
- P3 (~100 words): [Preposition 'на' for surfaces, events, and open spaces. Focus on nouns like столах (surfaces), заняттях (classes/events), вулицях (open spaces). Example: "На полицях стоять цікаві книжки."]
- P4 (~100 words): [The distributive 'по'. Explain how 'по' + Locative plural describes movement across or distribution among many points: ходити по магазинах (going shopping/among shops), гуляти по парках (walking through parks).]
- P5 (~100 words): [Comparison of Dative and Locative plural. Note the similarity in stems but the crucial difference in endings (-ам/-ям vs -ах/-ях). Use a contrastive example: "Я даю корм птахам (Dat)" vs "Птахи сидять на дахах (Loc)."]
- Exercise: [quiz, focus: Multiple choice choosing between у/в, на, and по with Locative plural nouns, 8 items]
