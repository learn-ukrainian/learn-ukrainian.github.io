## Чому дієслова потрібно вчити парами? (~450 words total)
- P1 (~100 words): Introduction to the concept of verb aspect (вид дієслова) as a fundamental feature of Ukrainian. Explain that learning one verb (e.g., *робити*) without its partner (*зробити*) is like learning only half a word because you won't know how to express a completed result.
- P2 (~100 words): Define the two aspects using official terminology from the Knowledge Packet: "недоконаний вид" (imperfective - process/habit) and "доконаний вид" (perfective - result/one-time action). Introduce the standard notation format used in dictionaries: *читати / прочитати*.
- P3 (~130 words): Dialogue: Cooking varenyky. Бабуся and Онучка in the kitchen. Бабуся uses aspect pairs to guide the process: "Ліпи (impf) вареники, поки тісто є. Ось, зліпи (pf) цей останній. Тепер вари (impf) їх 10 хвилин. Коли звариш (pf), поклич усіх." This illustrates the shift from process to completion.
- P4 (~120 words): Guide on using dictionaries to find aspectual pairs. Explain that in most Ukrainian dictionaries, the partner verb is listed immediately after the definition or in the header (e.g., *писати, -аю, -аєш; док. написати*). Mention that some digital dictionaries (like Slovnyk.ua) provide full paradigms for both.

## Спосіб 1: Додавання префікса (~650 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): Explain that prefixation is the most common way to create a perfective verb. A "pure" prefix changes only the aspect, not the core meaning of the action. Contrast *читати* (the act of reading) with *прочитати* (having finished the book).
- P2 (~90 words): Focus on the prefix **на-**. Provide examples like *писати / написати* (to write) and *малювати / намалювати* (to draw). Use a sentence example: "Я писав лист годину, і нарешті написав його."
- P3 (~90 words): Focus on the prefix **по-**. Use *бачити / побачити* (to see) and *чути / почути* (to hear). Explain that these often denote the sudden moment of perception.
- P4 (~90 words): Focus on the prefix **з- (зі-, с-)**. Provide *робити / зробити* (to do/make) and *готувати / приготувати* (to prepare/cook). Remind learners of the rule: use **с-** before *к, п, т, ф, х* (*сховати*).
- P5 (~90 words): Focus on the prefix **про-**. Use *читати / прочитати* and *співати / проспівати*. Explain that this prefix often implies going through the whole thing from start to finish.
- P6 (~70 words): Summary of prefix variety. Mention that while there are many prefixes (*при-, від-, ви-*), at the A2 level, learners should focus on memorizing the specific "partner" prefix for each core verb rather than guessing.

## Спосіб 2: Зміна в корені або суфіксі (~650 words total)
- P1 (~130 words): Introduce the concept of "imperfectivization." Explain that sometimes we start with a complex verb and change its suffix to make it imperfective. This often involves adding *-ува-* or *-юва-*. Contrast *купити* (pf) with *купувати* (impf).
- P2 (~120 words): Focus on the *-ува-* suffix pattern. Provide pairs: *вирішити / вирішувати* (to decide) and *запитати / запитувати* (to ask). Explain that the longer verb is usually the imperfective one.
- P3 (~100 words): Discuss suffix changes involving *-а-* or *-я-*. Use *відповісти / відповідати* (to answer) and *розповісти / розповідати* (to tell/narrate). Show how the addition of *-а-* turns a result-verb into a process-verb.
- P4 (~100 words): Explain internal vowel changes that happen during suffixation. The most common is **о -> а**. Use *допомогти / допомагати* (to help) and *перемогти / перемагати* (to win/overcome). This is a clear phonetic marker for the imperfective aspect.
- P5 (~100 words): Mention stress shifts as a subtle marker. In some pairs, only the stress changes: *виклика́ти* (impf) vs. *ви́кликати* (pf). Advise A2 learners to pay close attention to stress marks in their textbooks.
- Exercise: Find the Partner (Verb Matching). Focus: Matching imperfective verbs with their suffix-changed perfective partners (*купувати, вирішувати, відповідати, допомагати, розповідати, запитувати, зачиняти, вставати*). (match-up, 8 items)
- Exercise: Choose the Correct Aspect Partner. Focus: Given a sentence context, choose between the root-change pairs (e.g., "Він завжди [допомагає/допоможе] мені з домашнім завданням"). (fill-in, 8 items)

## Спосіб 3: Зовсім інші слова (суплетивізм) (~450 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): Introduce "suppletive" pairs (суплетивізм) — verbs that use completely different roots for their aspectual partners. While rare, these are among the most frequently used words in the language.
- P2 (~100 words): Detail the most important pair: *брати / взяти* (to take). Provide examples: "Я беру парасольку щодня" (habit) vs. "Я взяв парасольку сьогодні" (completed action).
- P3 (~100 words): Detail the pair *говорити / сказати* (to speak/say). Explain that *говорити* is for the process of talking, while *сказати* is for a specific utterance or result. "Він говорив довго, але нічого не сказав."
- P4 (~130 words): Introduce other essential A2 suppletive pairs: *класти / покласти* (to put/place) and *ловити / піймати* (to catch). Clarify the distinction between *шукати* (to search - process) and *знайти* (to find - result), noting that while they aren't a strict "grammatical" pair in all contexts, they function as a logical pair for learners.

## Summary (~150 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Recap of the three methods of aspect pair formation. Provide a final bulleted self-check list:
  1. Чому важливо вчити дієслова парами? (Because aspect defines the nature of the action—process vs. result).
  2. Який спосіб утворення найпопулярніший? (Adding a prefix like *на-, по-, з-*).
  3. Як змінюються слова типу "допомогти"? (Internal vowel change **о -> а**).
  4. Назвіть партнерів для *брати* та *говорити*. (*Взяти* та *сказати*).
  5. Де шукати пару, якщо ви її забули? (In the dictionary, usually right after the main entry).
