## State Standard Reference
§1.4.1.1.1: "створювати розповідь як просту послідовність подій;"
Alignment: This module provides the essential temporal conjunctions (коли, поки, після того як, перед тим як) required for learners to logically sequence events and build narrative monologues at the A2 level.

## Cultural Hooks
1. Проказка (Proverb): «Всьому свій час» (Everything has its time) — frames the Ukrainian cultural approach to patience and temporal logic.
2. Різдво (Christmas): The Christmas Eve (Свята Вечеря) tradition of fasting and waiting to eat «поки не зійде перша зірка» (until the first star appears) — serves as a memorable cultural anchor for the "поки не" grammar structure.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Missing Linkers ("Перед я пішов" → "Перед тим як я пішов") — English speakers drop the demonstrative pronoun anchors ("тим/того") because English "before/after" functions directly as a conjunction.
2. The "Until" Trap ("Я чекатиму, поки ти прийдеш" → "Я чекатиму, поки ти не прийдеш") — English uses affirmative logic for "until", whereas Ukrainian requires a negation in the limit-action clause.
3. The "If/When" Confusion ("Коли я матиму час, я допоможу" vs "Якщо я матиму час...") — English often uses "when" interchangeably with conditionals; Ukrainian strictly separates definite time (коли) from condition (якщо).

## Cross-References
- Builds on: Basic present/past/future tenses (A1), prepositional phrases of time (до, після, перед).
- Prepares for: Complex conditional sentences, detailed narrative sequences, and aspectual nuances in complex sentences.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Present Ukrainian temporal structures (especially the double negative in "поки не" and the demonstrative anchors in "перед тим як") as natural features of the language's internal logic, not as "exceptions" or in contrast to Russian.
- Immersion: The module is targeted at 60-75% Ukrainian (A2 Band 2). English should only be used for explaining the abstract mechanics (e.g., the double negative rule, the linker structure), while all examples, dialogues, and basic transitions must be in Ukrainian.
- Visuals: Use a timeline diagram or table to visually contrast «коли» (point in time) vs «поки» (continuous process).
