## Cultural Hooks
1. "Гроші" (money) is an exclusively plural noun in modern Ukrainian. It originates from the singular "гріш" (groschen), which was a physical coin used in medieval Ukraine (Kyivan Rus' and the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth era).
2. The robust preservation of the Vocative case in the plural (e.g., "друзі!", "панове!", "колеги!") highlights the Ukrainian cultural emphasis on direct, respectful personal address in group settings, standing out from languages that have lost this feature.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Overgeneralizing the "-ів" Genitive plural ending to all genders (e.g., saying "книгів" instead of "книг") → Zero ending is the standard for feminine and neuter nouns.
2. Forgetting consonant alternations in the Nominative plural (e.g., writing "други" instead of "друзі") → The г changes to з before і.
3. Ignoring the animate vs. inanimate distinction in the Accusative plural, leading to incorrect forms like "бачу студенти" instead of the correct "бачу студентів".

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized Framing: Present the Ukrainian noun declension system (such as consonant alternations and zero-ending plurals) as an inherent, elegant feature of the language. Do not use Russian as a baseline for comparison or present Ukrainian rules as "exceptions" from a generalized Slavic norm. Emphasize the historical depth of these forms.
- Avoid Russianisms in vocabulary and examples (e.g., use "отримувати гроші", not "получати гроші"; "квитки", not "білети" in some colloquial contexts).
