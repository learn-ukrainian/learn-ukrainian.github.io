## Куди ти йдеш? До + родовий для напрямку (Where Are You Going? До + Genitive for Direction) (~780 words total)
- P1 (~80 words): Intro to the preposition **до** as a primary tool for expressing movement toward a target. Explain its literal meaning "to" or "towards" and its requirement for the Genitive case.
- P2 (~150 words): Destination as a geographic place. Focus on cities (**до Києва, до Львова, до Одеси**) and countries (**до України, до Польщі, до Японії**). Explain the Genitive masculine ending **-а/-я** and feminine ending **-и/-і** for these proper nouns.
- P3 (~150 words): Destination as a person. Explain that **до + person** implies going to someone's home, office, or professional establishment. Examples: **до друга** (to a friend's place), **до лікаря** (to the doctor), **до бабусі** (to grandma's). Contrast this with the English "to the doctor's."
- P4 (~160 words): Detailed grammar breakdown of endings. Explain hard stems (**до будинку, до школи, до озера**) vs. soft stems (**до музею, до станції, до моря**). Provide a clear mapping of the Genitive case mutations needed for these directional phrases.
- P5 (~140 words): Contrast **до** vs. **в/на** + Accusative. Explain that while **іду в магазин** and **іду до магазину** are both used, **до** often emphasizes the journey or the approach to the entrance, whereas **в/на** emphasizes entering the interior. Note that in modern Ukrainian, they are frequently interchangeable for general direction.
- Dialogue (~100 words): A taxi ride scenario. Passenger giving instructions: "**До вокзалу, будь ласка.**" "**А тепер до аптеки.**" "**Потім до готелю 'Дніпро'.**" Driver confirming destinations using the same structures.
- Exercise: Fill-in-the-blanks (~150 words): Complete 12 sentences with the correct Genitive form of the destination noun (e.g., "Ми їдемо до (Київ)...").

## До якого часу? До + родовий для часу (Until When? До + Genitive for Time) (~680 words total)
- P1 (~180 words): Expressing a time limit ("until"). Explain how **до** marks the end point of a duration. Examples: **до вечора** (until evening), **до ранку** (until morning), **до літа** (until summer). Highlight the persistence of the action up to that point.
- P2 (~180 words): Expressing a deadline ("by" or "no later than"). Focus on tasks and appointments. Examples: **зробити до п'ятниці** (finish by Friday), **прийти до восьмої** (come by eight o'clock), **чекати до завтра** (wait until tomorrow). Explain that the event happens before or at the limit.
- P3 (~160 words): Using **до** in paired constructions with **від/з**. Explain how to describe full time ranges: **від ранку до вечора** (from morning to evening), **з понеділка до п'ятниці** (from Monday to Friday). Mention the Genitive case requirement for both prepositions.
- P4 (~160 words): Practical application in schedules and business hours. Examples: **працювати до шостої** (work until six), **музей відчинений до дев'ятої** (museum is open until nine). Provide examples with days of the week and specific hours.
- Exercise: Fill-in-the-blanks (~150 words): Complete 12 sentences involving time limits and deadlines (e.g., "Я буду вдома до (сьома година)...").

## До + родовий: решта значень та узагальнення (До + Genitive: Other Meanings and Summary) (~740 words total)
- P1 (~180 words): Abstract meanings and fixed purpose phrases. Explain common idioms like **до речі** (by the way) and the etymology of **до побачення** (literally "until seeing"). Discuss phrases like **готовий до екзамену** (ready for the exam) where **до** indicates preparation for a goal.
- P2 (~180 words): Addition and relation. Explain how **до** is used for adding items to a set or expressing an attitude. Examples: **додати до списку** (add to the list), **ставити питання до тексту** (ask questions about the text), **інтерес до мови** (interest in the language), **ставлення до роботи** (attitude toward work).
- P3 (~180 words): Consolidation and contrast with other prepositions. Compare **до** (toward/until) with **від** (away from/since) and **після** (after). Reiterate that all three take the Genitive, but define different temporal and spatial vectors. Example: **з ранку до вечора** vs. **після вечора**.
