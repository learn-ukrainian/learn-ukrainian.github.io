## До якого часу? До + родовий для часу (Until When? До + Genitive for Time) (~680 words total)
- P1 (~180 words): Expressing a time limit ("until"). Explain how **до** marks the end point of a duration. Examples: **до вечора** (until evening), **до ранку** (until morning), **до літа** (until summer). Highlight the persistence of the action up to that point.
- P2 (~180 words): Expressing a deadline ("by" or "no later than"). Focus on tasks and appointments. Examples: **зробити до п'ятниці** (finish by Friday), **прийти до восьмої** (come by eight o'clock), **чекати до завтра** (wait until tomorrow). Explain that the event happens before or at the limit.
- P3 (~160 words): Using **до** in paired constructions with **від/з**. Explain how to describe full time ranges: **від ранку до вечора** (from morning to evening), **з понеділка до п'ятниці** (from Monday to Friday). Mention the Genitive case requirement for both prepositions.
- P4 (~160 words): Practical application in schedules and business hours. Examples: **працювати до шостої** (work until six), **музей відчинений до дев'ятої** (museum is open until nine). Provide examples with days of the week and specific hours.

For example, you can say: — Я йду в магазин. *(I am going to the store.)* So, what is the difference between this and using **до**? Grammatically, both are correct. Conceptually, **до** often emphasizes the journey, the direction, or approaching the entrance of the place. Meanwhile, **в** or **на** strongly emphasizes the destination itself and the act of entering the interior. However, in modern, everyday Ukrainian, they are frequently interchangeable for general direction. You can use whichever fits the context or feels more natural. There is no strict hierarchy — both are standard. Note that with people, you must always use **до** (you cannot go *into* a person).
