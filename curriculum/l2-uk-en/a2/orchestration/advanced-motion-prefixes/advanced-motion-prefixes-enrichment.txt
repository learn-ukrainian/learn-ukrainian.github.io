## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Returning" Superstition**: It is considered bad luck to return home (`–њ–Њ–≤–µ—А—В–∞—В–Є—Б—П` / `–≤–µ—А–љ—Г—В–Є—Б—П`) shortly after leaving because you forgot something. If you must return, you should look in the mirror (`–њ–Њ–і–Є–≤–Є—В–Є—Б—П —Г –і–Ј–µ—А–Ї–∞–ї–Њ`) and smile/stick out your tongue to neutralize the negative energy. This is a perfect narrative hook for `–Ј–∞-` (popping back in) vs `–њ–Њ-` (leaving).
2. **Crossing the Path (`–Я–µ—А–µ–є—В–Є –і–Њ—А–Њ–≥—Г`)**: A black cat crossing your path (`—З–Њ—А–љ–Є–є –Ї—Ц—В –њ–µ—А–µ–є—И–Њ–≤ –і–Њ—А–Њ–≥—Г`) is a classic bad omen. To ward it off, superstitious people might wait for someone else to cross first or spit over their left shoulder. This anchors the verb `–њ–µ—А–µ–є—В–Є`.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Preposition Mismatch**: Using `–і–Њ` with all prefixes.
   *   *Error*: –Я—Ц–і—Ц–є—В–Є *–≤* –њ–∞—А–Ї.
   *   *Correction*: –Я—Ц–і—Ц–є—В–Є *–і–Њ* –њ–∞—А–Ї—Г (approach *to*).
   *   *Reason*: `–Я—Ц–і-` implies proximity, not entry.
2. **"Coming" vs "Going"**: Confusing `–њ—А–Є–є—В–Є` (arrive/come) with `–њ—Ц—В–Є` (go/leave).
   *   *Error*: –ѓ –њ—А–Є–є—И–Њ–≤ –љ–∞ —А–Њ–±–Њ—В—Г (when meaning "I left for work").
   *   *Correction*: –ѓ –њ—Ц—И–Њ–≤ –љ–∞ —А–Њ–±–Њ—В—Г.
   *   *Reason*: English "go" covers both start and end points depending on context; Ukrainian strictly separates departure (`–њ—Ц-`) and arrival (`–њ—А–Є-`).
3. **Perfective Future Confusion**: Trying to use `–±—Г–і—Г` with perfective verbs.
   *   *Error*: –ѓ –±—Г–і—Г –њ—А–Є–є—В–Є –Ј–∞–≤—В—А–∞.
   *   *Correction*: –ѓ –њ—А–Є–є–і—Г –Ј–∞–≤—В—А–∞.
   *   *Reason*: Prefixed motion verbs are usually perfective and form the future tense simply by conjugating (like `read` -> `will read`), without the auxiliary `–±—Г–і—Г`.
