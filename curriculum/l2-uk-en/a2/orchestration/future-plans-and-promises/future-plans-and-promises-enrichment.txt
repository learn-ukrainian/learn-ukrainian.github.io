# Дослідження: Future Plans and Promises

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.1: "утворення складеної форми майбутнього часу дієслів недоконаного виду: буду боротися... майбутній час дієслів доконаного виду: скажу, закричите, поборють."
Alignment: This module directly addresses the A2 requirement to master both the compound future (imperfective) and the simple future (perfective), focusing on the semantic distinction between process and result in future commitments.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Frequency / Source | Key collocations |
|------|-------------------|------------------|
| зроблю | High (Perfective) | зроблю все можливе, обов'язково зроблю |
| напишу | High (Perfective) | напишу листа, напишу повідомлення |
| скажу | High (Perfective) | скажу правду, скажу завтра |
| буду | Very High (Auxiliary) | буду чекати, буду працювати, буду там |
| піду | High (Motion) | піду в кіно, піду додому |

## Cultural Hooks
1. **"Обіцянка — цяцянка, а дурневі — радість"** (A promise is a toy, and a fool's joy). This classic Ukrainian proverb reflects a cultural caution against empty promises and the value placed on "keeping one's word" (тримати слово). Source: Ukrainian Folk Wisdom.
2. **"Обіцяного три роки ждуть"** (One waits three years for what is promised). A humorous but realistic observation that promised things often take a long time to materialize. Source: Ukrainian Folklore.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Aspect Mismatch** → *Я буду зробити* (Wrong) vs *Я зроблю* (Correct). Learners often try to use the auxiliary *буду* with perfective verbs because they haven't internalized that the perfective conjugation *is* the future tense.
2. **Process vs. Result** → *Я прочитаю книгу ввечері* (meaning "I will finish reading it") vs *Я буду читати книгу ввечері* (meaning "I will be spending time reading it"). Learners often use the simple future when they want to describe an ongoing plan, or vice versa.
3. **Overuse of "Буду"** → Using *буду + infinitive* for everything because it bypasses the need to remember perfective conjugations.

## Cross-References
- Builds on: a2-12 (Aspect introduction), a2-13 (Perfective past), A1 future tense basics.
- Prepares for: a2-15 (Aspect Morphology), a2-16 (Aspect Mastery Pairs), a2-22 (If I Were).

## Notes for Content Writing
- Emphasize that in Ukrainian, "I will do" (result) is a single word (*зроблю*), while "I will be doing" (process) requires two (*буду робити*).
- Integrate the proverb "Обіцянка — цяцянка" to add cultural depth to the "Promises" section.
