## –ѓ–Ї —Ж–µ –њ–Њ—П—Б–љ—О—О—В—М —Г —И–Ї–Њ–ї—Ц (How Schools Teach This)
The Instrumental case (**–Ю—А—Г–і–љ–Є–є –≤—Ц–і–Љ—Ц–љ–Њ–Ї**) is introduced in Ukrainian schools as early as Grade 4. It answers the questions **–Ї–Є–Љ?** (by whom?) and **—З–Є–Љ?** (with what?) (Source 40). The name of the case itself comes from the verb *–Њ—А—Г–і—Г–≤–∞—В–Є* (to handle, to wield a tool), so its primary meaning is that of an instrument. For example, `–њ–Є—И—Г —А—Г—З–Ї–Њ—О` (I write with a pen) (Source 153).

The second key meaning, and the focus of this brief, is **accompaniment**. This is almost always taught using the preposition **–Ј** (with). A core example provided in Grade 6 textbooks is `—Ц–і—Г (–Ј –Ї–Є–Љ?) –Ј –і—А—Г–≥–Њ–Љ` (I am going with a friend) (Source 12). This distinguishes the "accompaniment" use (with a preposition) from the "instrument" use (without a preposition).

Pedagogical materials for L2 learners, like the *Ukrainian Lessons Podcast*, explicitly connect the preposition `–Ј` with the Instrumental case for expressing "with." In an episode about Christmas dinner, the host explains that `–Ј` is used to list ingredients: `–Ї—Г—В—П –Ј –Љ–∞–Ї–Њ–Љ, –Љ–µ–і–Њ–Љ, —Ц –≥–Њ—А—Ц—Е–∞–Љ–Є` (kutia with poppy seeds, honey, and nuts) (Source 24). This context is highly effective for A2 learners as it's concrete and relatable.

Ukrainian schools systematically teach the endings for each declension:
1.  **Grade 4:** Introduction to the case and basic endings for masculine (`-–Њ–Љ`/`-–µ–Љ`) and feminine (`-–Њ—О`/`-–µ—О`) nouns (Sources 15, 44).
2.  **Grade 6:** A deep dive into all declensions, including the complex spelling rules for the 3rd declension (`–љ—Ц—З—З—О`) and the various ending patterns for 1st (`-–Њ—О, -–µ—О, -—Ф—О`) and 2nd declensions (`-–Њ–Љ, -–µ–Љ, -—Ф–Љ`) (Sources 5, 8, 15).
3.  **Grade 10:** Review and focus on complex cases, exceptions, and parallel forms like the plural `-–Љ–Є` ending (`–≥—А—Ц—И–Љ–Є`, `–Ї—Ц–љ—М–Љ–Є`) (Source 19).

The variants of the preposition `–Ј` (`—Ц–Ј`, `–Ј—Ц`, `–Ј–Њ`) are taught as a matter of euphony (–Љ–Є–ї–Њ–Ј–≤—Г—З–љ—Ц—Б—В—М). The rules are based on the sounds of the following word, for example, `–Ј—Ц –Љ–љ–Њ—О` (with me) or `–Ј—Ц –Ы—М–≤–Њ–≤–∞` (from Lviv) to avoid difficult consonant clusters (Source 43).

## –І–∞—Б—В–Њ—В–љ—Ц—Б—В—М —Ц –њ—А—Ц–Њ—А–Є—В–µ—В–Є (Frequency & Priorities)

**A2 Level Priority:**
The most crucial function of the Instrumental case at the A2 level is expressing **accompaniment** and **composition** with the preposition **`–Ј`**. Learners should master this pattern first, as it unlocks a vast range of descriptive sentences.

1.  **Accompaniment (People):** `–Ј –і—А—Г–≥–Њ–Љ`, `–Ј –њ–Њ–і—А—Г–≥–Њ—О`, `–Ј –±–∞—В—М–Ї–∞–Љ–Є`. This is fundamental for social communication.
2.  **Composition (Food & Things):** `–Ї–∞–≤–∞ –Ј –Љ–Њ–ї–Њ–Ї–Њ–Љ`, `–±—Г—В–µ—А–±—А–Њ–і –Ј —Б–Є—А–Њ–Љ`, `–Ї–љ–Є–≥–∞ –Ј –Ї–∞—А—В–Є–љ–Ї–∞–Љ–Є`. This is highly frequent in daily life. (Source `ext-ulp_youtube-242`)
3.  **Basic Instrument (No Preposition):** `–њ–Є—Б–∞—В–Є —А—Г—З–Ї–Њ—О`, `—Ч—Б—В–Є –ї–Њ–ґ–Ї–Њ—О`. This core meaning should also be introduced.

**Form Priorities for A2:**
*   **Singular nouns of 1st and 2nd declensions:** These are the most common. Master the `-–Њ—О`/`-–µ—О` and `-–Њ–Љ`/`-–µ–Љ` endings.
*   **Plural nouns with `-–∞–Љ–Є`/`-—П–Љ–Є`:** Essential for talking about groups (`–Ј –і—А—Г–Ј—П–Љ–Є`, `–Ј –±–∞—В—М–Ї–∞–Љ–Є`).
*   **Personal pronouns:** `–Ј—Ц –Љ–љ–Њ—О`, `–Ј —В–Њ–±–Њ—О`, `–Ј –љ–Є–Љ`, `–Ј –љ–µ—О`, `–Ј –љ–∞–Љ–Є`, `–Ј –≤–∞–Љ–Є`, `–Ј –љ–Є–Љ–Є`. These are non-negotiable for conversation. (Source `ext-article-1`)

**B1 Level and Beyond:**
*   **3rd Declension Spelling:** The consonant doubling (`–њ–Њ–і–Њ—А–Њ–ґ–ґ—О`) and apostrophe (`–ї—О–±–Њ–≤'—О`) are key spelling rules to master at the B1 level.
*   **Predicate Instrumental:** Using the Instrumental case for professions or temporary states after verbs like `–±—Г—В–Є` (e.g., `–Т—Ц–љ —Б—В–∞–≤ –ї—Ц–Ї–∞—А–µ–Љ` - He became a doctor).
*   **Parallel Plural Forms:** Recognizing and using forms like `–≥—Ц—Б—В—М–Љ–Є`, `–≥—А—Ц—И–Љ–Є` becomes relevant for a richer vocabulary.

| –ѓ –њ–Є—И—Г **–Ј —А—Г—З–Ї–Њ—О**. | –ѓ –њ–Є—И—Г **—А—Г—З–Ї–Њ—О**. | This is a classic "instrument" vs. "accompaniment" error. `–Ч —А—Г—З–Ї–Њ—О` implies the pen is a co-author. For tools, no preposition is used. (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2023_s0095`) |
| –Т–Њ–љ–∞ –њ'—Ф —З–∞–є **–Ј —Ж—Г–Ї—А–Њ—О**. | –Т–Њ–љ–∞ –њ'—Ф —З–∞–є **–Ј —Ж—Г–Ї—А–Њ–Љ**. | The noun `—Ж—Г–Ї–Њ—А` is masculine (2nd declension) and takes the `-–Њ–Љ` ending. The `-–Њ—О` ending is for feminine nouns (1st declension). |
| –Т–Њ–љ–∞ –њ–Њ–і—Ц–ї–Є–ї–∞—Б—П **—А–∞–і—Ц—Б—В—О**. | –Т–Њ–љ–∞ –њ–Њ–і—Ц–ї–Є–ї–∞—Б—П **—А–∞–і—Ц—Б—В—О**. | The noun `—А–∞–і—Ц—Б—В—М` (3rd declension) has a stem ending in a consonant cluster `-—Б—В-`. Therefore, the consonant `—В` is not doubled. Doubling occurs only after single consonants. (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2020_s0111`) |
| –Т—Ц–љ –њ–Њ–і–Њ—А–Њ–ґ—Г–≤–∞–≤ **–љ—Ц—З—О**. | –Т—Ц–љ –њ–Њ–і–Њ—А–Њ–ґ—Г–≤–∞–≤ **–љ—Ц—З—З—О**. | The noun `–љ—Ц—З` (3rd declension) has a stem ending in a single soft consonant (`—З`). Therefore, the consonant must be doubled before the `-—О` ending. (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-golub-2023_s0084`) |
| –ѓ –Ј—Г—Б—В—А—Ц–≤—Б—П **–Ј –є–Њ–≥–Њ –Љ–∞—В–Є**. | –ѓ –Ј—Г—Б—В—А—Ц–≤—Б—П **–Ј –є–Њ–≥–Њ –Љ–∞—В—Ц—А'—О**. | The noun `–Љ–∞—В—Ц—А` (3rd declension) is an exception. Its stem ends in `—А`, so it takes an apostrophe before the `-—О` ending, and there is no doubling. (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2023_s0170`) |

## –Ф–µ–Ї–Њ–ї–Њ–љ—Ц–Ј–∞—Ж—Ц–є–љ—Ц –Ј–∞—Б—В–µ—А–µ–ґ–µ–љ–љ—П (Decolonization Notes)
It is critical to teach Ukrainian grammar on its own terms, not as a variant of Russian. The Instrumental case provides several clear points of divergence.

1.  **Feminine Endings (`-–Њ—О`/`-–µ—О`):** Ukrainian preserves the full vowel endings `-–Њ—О` and `-–µ—О` for 1st declension nouns. This is a distinct feature.
    *   **Ukrainian:** `–≤–Њ–і–Њ—О`, `–Ј–µ–Љ–ї–µ—О`, `–њ—Ц—Б–љ–µ—О`.
    *   **Russian:** `–≤–Њ–і–Њ–є`, `–Ј–µ–Љ–ї—С–є`, `–њ–µ—Б–љ–µ–є`.
    The `–є` sound in the Russian endings is absent in Ukrainian, which uses a full vowel. Presenting this as a core feature of Ukrainian phonetics is essential.

2.  **Consonant Doubling in 3rd Declension:** The doubling of soft and sibilant consonants in the 3rd declension (`—Б—Ц–ї–ї—О`, `–љ—Ц—З—З—О`, `–њ–Њ–і–Њ—А–Њ–ґ–ґ—О`) is a hallmark of Ukrainian orthography and pronunciation.
    *   **Ukrainian:** `–љ—Ц—З—З—О` [ЋИnвБљ ≤вБЊitЌ° ГЋРu]
    *   **Russian:** `–љ–Њ—З—М—О` [ЋИnotЌ°…Хju]
    This feature is absent in Russian and should be taught as a fundamental rule of Ukrainian morphology, not an exception.

3.  **Plural Parallel Forms (`-–Љ–Є`):** The existence of parallel forms like `–≥—Ц—Б—В—М–Љ–Є`/`–≥–Њ—Б—В—П–Љ–Є` and `–Ї—Ц–љ—М–Љ–Є`/`–Ї–Њ–љ—П–Љ–Є` is a characteristic feature of the Ukrainian language. While Russian has `–ї—О–і—М–Љ–Є`, `–і–µ—В—М–Љ–Є`, the pattern is more restricted. In Ukrainian, these forms are part of the standard language for a wider set of nouns and represent an older grammatical layer preserved in Ukrainian (Source `10-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2018_s0248`).

4.  **Vocabulary:** Always use authentic Ukrainian vocabulary in examples. Avoid using Russianisms like `—Б–∞—Е–∞—А` (sugar) or `–Ї–Њ–љ—М–Ї–Є` (skates). The correct Ukrainian words are `—Ж—Г–Ї–Њ—А` and `–Ї–Њ–≤–Ј–∞–љ–Є`. Using Ukrainian words reinforces the distinct lexical system.

## –Я—А–Є—А–Њ–і–љ—Ц –њ—А–Є–Ї–ї–∞–і–Є (Natural Examples)
These examples are taken from textbook dialogues and real-world conversations to show natural usage.

**1. Accompaniment with People**
*   `–ѓ –Ј–∞–њ—А–Њ—И—Г –Ь–∞–Ї—Б–Є–Љ–∞, —П–Ї–Њ–≥–Њ —В–Є –±–∞—З–Є–ї–∞ –љ–∞ –Ї–Њ–љ—Ж–µ—А—В—Ц, —Ц –Д–≤–≥–µ–љ–∞, **—Й–Њ –њ—А–∞—Ж—О—Ф –Ј—Ц –Љ–љ–Њ—О** –≤ –Њ—Д—Ц—Б—Ц.` (I'll invite Maksym, whom you saw at the concert, and Yevhen, who works **with me** at the office.) (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-186`)
*   `–°–њ—Ц–ї—М–љ–Њ **–Ј (—А—Ц–і–љ—П)** –Љ–Є –≤–Є—А—Ц—И—Г—Ф–Љ–Њ —В–∞–Ї—Ц –њ–Є—В–∞–љ–љ—П...` -> `–°–њ—Ц–ї—М–љ–Њ **–Ј —А—Ц–і–љ–µ—О** –Љ–Є –≤–Є—А—Ц—И—Г—Ф–Љ–Њ...` (Together **with family** we decide such questions...) (Source: `10-klas-ukrmova-glazova-2018_s0260`)
*   `–Ф—А—Г–ґ–Є—В–Є **–Ј –Ь–∞—А—Ц—Ф—О**.` (To be friends **with Maria**.) (Source: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-varzatska-2021-1_s0057`)

**2. Composition of Food and Drinks**
*   `–Ъ—Г—В—П вАФ —Ж–µ –≤—Ц–і–≤–∞—А–µ–љ–∞ –њ—И–µ–љ–Є—Ж—П **–Ј –Љ–∞–Ї–Њ–Љ, –Љ–µ–і–Њ–Љ, –≥–Њ—А—Ц—Е–∞–Љ–Є —Ц —А–Њ–і–Ј–Є–љ–Ї–∞–Љ–Є**.` (Kutia is boiled wheat **with poppy seeds, honey, nuts, and raisins**.) (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-242`)
*   `–ѓ –≥–Њ—В—Г—О –≤–∞—А–µ–љ–Є–Ї–Є **–Ј –Ї–∞–њ—Г—Б—В–Њ—О —Ц –Ї–∞—А—В–Њ–њ–ї–µ—О**.` (I make varenyky **with cabbage and potatoes**.) (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-242`)
*   `–Э–∞ –і–µ—Б–µ—А—В вАФ –њ–Є—А—Ц–ґ–Ї–Є **–Ј –Љ–∞–Ї–Њ–Љ —Ц –≤–Є—И–љ—П–Љ–Є**.` (For dessert вАФ small pies **with poppy seeds and cherries**.) (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-242`)

**3. Describing things with `–Ј`**
*   `–Я–Њ–њ–µ—А–µ–і–љ—М–Њ –є–Њ–≥–Њ –Љ–∞—А–Є–љ—Г—О—В—М **–Ј —А—Ц–Ј–љ–Є–Љ–Є —Б–њ–µ—Ж—Ц—П–Љ–Є** –є **—Ж–Є–±—Г–ї–µ—О**.` (Beforehand, they marinate it **with various spices** and **onion**.) (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-186`)
*   `–¶–µ —Б–∞–ї–∞—В **–Ј –±—Г—А—П–Ї–∞ —Ц —Е—А–Њ–љ—Г**.` (This is a salad **of beet and horseradish**.) (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-78`)

**4. Using an Instrument (No Preposition)**
*   `–Ч–µ–Љ–ї—П —Ж—Ц–љ–Є—В—М—Б—П **–Ї–Њ–ї–Њ—Б–Њ–Љ**, –∞ –ї—О–і–Є–љ–∞ вАФ **–њ—А–∞—Ж–µ—О**.` (Land is valued **by its ear of grain**, and a person вАФ **by their labor**.) (Source: `10-klas-ukrmova-glazova-2018_s0260`)
*   `–ѓ –њ–Є—И—Г **–Њ–ї—Ц–≤—Ж–µ–Љ**, –∞ –љ–µ **—А—Г—З–Ї–Њ—О**.` (I am writing **with a pencil**, not **with a pen**.) (Source: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-kravtsova-2021-1_s0054`)

**5. 3rd Declension Spelling**
*   `–Ы—О–і–Є–љ–∞ –њ—А–µ–Ї—А–∞—Б–љ–∞ —Б–≤–Њ—Ф—О **–ї—О–і—П–љ—Ц—Б—В—О**, **–љ–µ–Ј–ї–∞–Љ–љ—Ц—Б—В—О** –і—Г—Е—Г...` (A person is beautiful for their **humanity**, **unbreakability** of spirit...) (Source: `10-klas-ukrmova-glazova-2018_s0283`)
*   `–°–≤–Њ—Ф—О **—А–∞–і—Ц—Б—В—О** –є **–њ–µ—З–∞–ї–ї—О**, —П–Ї **—Е–ї—Ц–±-—Б—Ц–ї—М**, –њ–Њ–і—Ц–ї–Є—Б—М.` (Share your **joy** and **sorrow** like **bread and salt**.) (Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2020_s0112`)

## –†–µ–Ї–Њ–Љ–µ–љ–і–∞—Ж—Ц—Ч –і–ї—П –≤–њ—А–∞–≤ (Activity Concepts)

**Phase 1: Recognition and Form Association**
*   **Drill 1 (Matching):** Provide two columns. Left column: nominative forms (`–і—А—Г–≥`, `–Ї–∞–≤–∞`, `—Б–µ—Б—В—А–∞`, `–Љ–Њ—А–µ`). Right column: instrumental forms (`—Б–µ—Б—В—А–Њ—О`, `–Ј –і—А—Г–≥–Њ–Љ`, `–Љ–Њ—А–µ–Љ`, `–Ј –Ї–∞–≤–Њ—О`). Students draw lines to match them.
*   **Drill 2 (Identification):** Give students a short dialogue (like from Source 1) and have them highlight all nouns in the Instrumental case.

**Phase 2: Controlled Practice**
*   **Drill 1 (Fill-in-the-blanks):** Focus on preposition `–Ј` + noun.
    *   `–ѓ –њ'—О —З–∞–є –Ј ___________ (–ї–Є–Љ–Њ–љ).` -> `–ї–Є–Љ–Њ–љ–Њ–Љ`
    *   `–Т–Њ–љ–∞ –≥–Њ–≤–Њ—А–Є—В—М –Ј ___________ (—Г—З–Є—В–µ–ї—М–Ї–∞).` -> `—Г—З–Є—В–µ–ї—М–Ї–Њ—О`
    *   `–Ь–Є —Ч–Љ–Њ –±–Њ—А—Й –Ј ___________ (—Б–Љ–µ—В–∞–љ–∞).` -> `—Б–Љ–µ—В–∞–љ–Њ—О`
*   **Drill 2 (Transformation):** Give a base sentence and a noun. The student must integrate it.
    *   `–¶–µ –Ї–∞–≤–∞. (–Љ–Њ–ї–Њ–Ї–Њ)` -> `–¶–µ –Ї–∞–≤–∞ –Ј –Љ–Њ–ї–Њ–Ї–Њ–Љ.`
    *   `–ѓ –є–і—Г –≤ –њ–∞—А–Ї. (–і—А—Г–Ј—Ц)` -> `–ѓ –є–і—Г –≤ –њ–∞—А–Ї –Ј –і—А—Г–Ј—П–Љ–Є.`

**Phase 3: Production and Spelling**
*   **Drill 1 (Question & Answer):**
    *   `–Ч –Ї–Є–Љ —В–Є –ґ–Є–≤–µ—И?` (Who do you live with?) -> `–ѓ –ґ–Є–≤—Г –Ј...`
    *   `–Ч —З–Є–Љ —В–Є –ї—О–±–Є—И —Ч—Б—В–Є –≤–∞—А–µ–љ–Є–Ї–Є?` (What do you like to eat varenyky with?) -> `–ѓ –ї—О–±–ї—О –≤–∞—А–µ–љ–Є–Ї–Є –Ј...`
*   **Drill 2 (Spelling Focus - 3rd Declension):** Provide 3rd declension nouns and have students write them in the Instrumental case.
    *   `–љ—Ц—З` -> `–љ—Ц—З—З—О`
    *   `–ї—О–±–Њ–≤` -> `–ї—О–±–Њ–≤'—О`
    *   `—А–∞–і—Ц—Б—В—М` -> `—А–∞–і—Ц—Б—В—О`
    *   `–њ–Њ–і–Њ—А–Њ–ґ` -> `–њ–Њ–і–Њ—А–Њ–ґ–ґ—О`

## –Ч–≤'—П–Ј–Ї–Є –Ј —Ц–љ—И–Є–Љ–Є —В–µ–Љ–∞–Љ–Є (Connections)

*   **Prerequisites:** A solid understanding of **noun gender** and the basic concept of **grammatical cases** is essential. The learner should be comfortable identifying the nominative form of a noun.
*   **Corequisites:** The Instrumental case of **personal pronouns** (`–Ј—Ц –Љ–љ–Њ—О, –Ј —В–Њ–±–Њ—О, –Ј –љ–Є–Љ`) should be taught alongside or immediately after this topic, as they are inseparable in conversation (Source `ext-article-1`).
*   **Next Steps:** Mastering the Instrumental of accompaniment is the gateway to understanding its other uses, which are typically B1-level topics:
    *   **Predicate Instrumental:** `–Т—Ц–љ —Е–Њ—З–µ —Б—В–∞—В–Є –ї—Ц–Ї–∞—А–µ–Љ.` (He wants to become a doctor).
    *   **Instrumental of Agent (Passive Voice):** `–Ы–Є—Б—В –љ–∞–њ–Є—Б–∞–љ–Є–є –Љ–љ–Њ—О.` (The letter was written by me).
    *   **Time Expressions:** `–њ—А–∞—Ж—О–≤–∞—В–Є –і–љ—П–Љ–Є —Ц –љ–Њ—З–∞–Љ–Є` (to work days and nights).

# –У—А–∞–Љ–∞—В–Є–Ї–∞ A2: –Э–∞–і, –њ—Ц–і, –Љ—Ц–ґ



## –ѓ–Ї —Ж–µ –њ–Њ—П—Б–љ—О—О—В—М —Г —И–Ї–Њ–ї—Ц (How Schools Teach This)

–Я—А–Є–є–Љ–µ–љ–љ–Є–Ї–Є –њ—А–Њ—Б—В–Њ—А–Њ–≤–Њ–≥–Њ –Ј–љ–∞—З–µ–љ–љ—П, –Ј–Њ–Ї—А–µ–Љ–∞ `–љ–∞–і`, `–њ—Ц–і`, —Ц `–Љ—Ц–ґ`, –≤–≤–Њ–і—П—В—М—Б—П –≤ —Г–Ї—А–∞—Ч–љ—Б—М–Ї—Ц–є —И–Ї—Ц–ї—М–љ—Ц–є –њ—А–Њ–≥—А–∞–Љ—Ц –і—Г–ґ–µ —А–∞–љ–Њ, –Ј–∞–Ј–≤–Є—З–∞–є –≤—Ц–Ј—Г–∞–ї—М–љ–Њ. –Т–ґ–µ —Г 1-2 –Ї–ї–∞—Б–∞—Е —Ч—Е —Ц–ї—О—Б—В—А—Г—О—В—М –Љ–∞–ї—О–љ–Ї–∞–Љ–Є –і–ї—П –і–µ–Љ–Њ–љ—Б—В—А–∞—Ж—Ц—Ч —А–Њ–Ј—В–∞—И—Г–≤–∞–љ–љ—П –њ—А–µ–і–Љ–µ—В—Ц–≤, –љ–∞–њ—А–Є–Ї–ї–∞–і, "–Ї–Њ—И–µ–љ—П –њ—Ц–і –Ї–ї—Г–±–Ї–Њ–Љ" –∞–±–Њ "–Ї–Њ—И–µ–љ—П –Љ—Ц–ґ –Ї–ї—Г–±–Ї–∞–Љ–Є" (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `2-klas-ukrmova-bolshakova-2019-2_s0066`). –Э–∞ —Ж—М–Њ–Љ—Г –µ—В–∞–њ—Ц –∞–Ї—Ж–µ–љ—В —А–Њ–±–Є—В—М—Б—П –љ–∞ —Ц–љ—В—Г—Ч—В–Є–≤–љ–Њ–Љ—Г —А–Њ–Ј—Г–Љ—Ц–љ–љ—Ц –њ—А–Њ—Б—В–Њ—А–Њ–≤–Є—Е –≤—Ц–і–љ–Њ—И–µ–љ—М, –∞ –љ–µ –љ–∞ –≥—А–∞–Љ–∞—В–Є—З–љ–Є—Е –њ—А–∞–≤–Є–ї–∞—Е.

## –І–∞—Б—В–Њ—В–љ—Ц—Б—В—М —Ц –њ—А—Ц–Њ—А–Є—В–µ—В–Є (Frequency & Priorities)

3.  **`–Ь—Ц–ґ` + Instrumental:** –°–µ—А–µ–і–љ—П —З–∞—Б—В–Њ—В–љ—Ц—Б—В—М. –Т–Є–Љ–∞–≥–∞—Ф –і–≤–Њ—Е –∞–±–Њ –±—Ц–ї—М—И–µ –Њ–±'—Ф–Ї—В—Ц–≤ (`–Љ—Ц–ґ —И–∞—Д–Њ—О —Ц —Б—В–Њ–ї–Њ–Љ`). –Т–∞–ґ–ї–Є–≤–Њ –і–ї—П –Њ–њ–Є—Б—Г –≤—Ц–і–љ–Њ—И–µ–љ—М –Љ—Ц–ґ –њ—А–µ–і–Љ–µ—В–∞–Љ–Є.

–Т–ґ–Є–≤–∞–љ–љ—П –Ј—Ц –Ч–љ–∞—Е—Ц–і–љ–Є–Љ –≤—Ц–і–Љ—Ц–љ–Ї–Њ–Љ (`–њ–Њ–Ї–ї–∞—Б—В–Є –њ—Ц–і —Б—В—Ц–ї`) –Љ–Њ–ґ–љ–∞ –≤—Ц–і–Ї–ї–∞—Б—В–Є –і–Њ —А—Ц–≤–љ—П B1, –Њ—Б–Ї—Ц–ї—М–Ї–Є –љ–∞ A2 –і–ї—П –љ–∞–њ—А—П–Љ–Ї—Г —З–∞—Б—В—Ц—И–µ –≤–Є–Ї–Њ—А–Є—Б—В–Њ–≤—Г—О—В—М—Б—П –њ—А–Є–є–Љ–µ–љ–љ–Є–Ї–Є `–≤/—Г` —В–∞ `–љ–∞`.
