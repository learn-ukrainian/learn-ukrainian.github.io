## Орудний відмінок: Знайомство (The Instrumental Case: Introduction) (~450 words total)
- P1 (~110 words): Introduction to the name of the case, "Орудний відмінок," explaining its derivation from the verb "орудувати" (to handle/manage/wield). Contrast its primary meaning of a tool (the instrument) with the social meaning of accompaniment (with someone), which is the focus of this lesson.
- P2 (~110 words): Presenting the essential questions "Ким? Чим?" (By whom? With what?). Explain that while "Чим?" can refer to a tool (instrument), when used with the preposition "з," it shifts to "with whom/what" (accompaniment).
- P3 (~115 words): Explain the conceptual "map" of where Instrumental fits in the learner's journey. It is the seventh case in the traditional Ukrainian system. Briefly contrast it with the Accusative (destination) and Genitive (possession/absence) to show how it adds the dimension of "togetherness" and "means."
- P4 (~115 words): Set the stage for the next few modules (M21-M25), clarifying that today we master the "social" use (with the preposition "з"), while later lessons will handle professions, tools without prepositions, and spatial locations (над/під). Use the foundational example: "Я іду з другом" (I am going with a friend).

## Закінчення орудного відмінка однини (Instrumental Singular Endings) (~600 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): Masculine nouns with hard stems. Explain the "-ом" ending using examples like "друг -> другом," "брат -> братом," and "стіл -> столом." Emphasize that most masculine nouns follow this simple pattern.
- P2 (~120 words): Masculine nouns with soft stems and sibilants. Introduce the "-ем" and "-єм" endings. Use examples like "вчитель -> вчителем" (soft), "ніж -> ножем" (sibilant), and "чай -> чаєм" (stem in [й]). Explain the spelling rule: soft stems take "e," and vowel stems take "є."
- P3 (~120 words): Feminine nouns with hard stems. Explain the "-ою" ending: "мама -> мамою," "сестра -> сестрою," "книга -> книгою." Crucial decolonization point: Highlight the full vowel "-ою" in Ukrainian, contrasting it with the Russian "-ой" (e.g., мамою vs. мамой) to establish authentic phonetic habits.
- P4 (~120 words): Feminine nouns with soft stems, sibilants, and vowel stems. Explain "-ею" and "-єю": "земля -> землею," "душа -> душею," "мрія -> мрією." Show how the soft stem "я" becomes "ею" and the vowel stem "я" (after another vowel) becomes "єю."
- P5 (~120 words): Neuter nouns. Explain that neuter nouns follow masculine patterns: "-ом" for hard stems ("вікно -> вікном," "молоко -> молоком") and "-ем/-ям" for soft stems ("море -> морем," "життя -> життям"). ## З/із/зі + орудний відмінок (Z/iz/zi + Instrumental) (~500 words total)
- P1 (~125 words): The primary meaning of accompaniment. Explain how "з + Instrumental" describes doing an action together with a person. Use examples: "гуляти з другом," "жити з батьками," "працювати з колегою."
- P2 (~125 words): The "attribute" or "ingredient" meaning. Explain how "з" describes the composition of items, especially food and drink. This is essential for survival Ukrainian. Use examples: "кава з молоком," "чай з лимоном," "піца з грибами," "хліб з маслом."
- P3 (~125 words): Phonetic variants (Милозвучність). Explain the rules for choosing between "з," "із," and "зі." "З" is the default; "із" is used before words starting with consonant clusters (із сестрою); "зі" is mandatory before words starting with "з," "с," "ш," "зв" (зі мною, зі сметаною, зі школи).
- P4 (~125 words): Practical phonetic table. Provide a list of "difficult" initial sounds and the correct preposition variant to use. Emphasize that "зі мною" is a fixed phrase every A2 learner must memorize.

## Практика: З ким? З чим? (Practice: With Whom? With What?) (~450 words total)
- P1 (~110 words): Answering the question "З ким?". Focus on people and relationships. Provide a short Q&A drill: "З ким ти ідеш у кіно? — З подругою." "З ким ти розмовляєш? — З братом."
- P2 (~110 words): Answering the question "З чим?". Focus on food, drinks, and descriptions. Examples: "Я люблю борщ зі сметаною." "Мені подобається кава з медом." Introduce "сметаною" as a specific 1st declension soft-stem example.
- P3 (~120 words): Dialogue: "В кафе" (At the Cafe). A multi-turn dialogue between two friends ordering drinks and snacks. Speaker A: "Я буду чай з лимоном." Speaker B: "А я хочу каву з холодним молоком і бутерброд з шинкою." Showcases mixed genders and stems in a natural setting.
- P4 (~110 words): Common verbal collocations. List and explain verbs that naturally pair with "з + Instrumental": "зустрічатися з..." (to meet with), "розмовляти з..." (to talk with), "погоджуватися з..." (to agree with).
