## Хтось, щось: невідоме, але конкретне (~550 words)
- P1 (~110 words): Introduction to the concept of indefinite pronouns as the "mystery markers" of Ukrainian. Explain that they are formed from question words (хто, що, де) by adding the suffix -сь, which indicates that the speaker knows something exists but doesn't know its exact identity. Use the specific contrast: "Хто?" (Who?) vs "Хтось" (Someone).
- P2 (~130 words): Detailed focus on хтось and щось. Explain their usage in affirmative sentences where the action is real but the subject/object is unnamed. Provide three concrete examples: "Хтось стукає у двері" (Someone is knocking), "Я бачу щось цікаве" (I see something interesting), and "Хтось забув парасольку" (Someone forgot an umbrella).
- P3 (~140 words): Expanding the -сь series to adverbs: десь (somewhere), колись (sometime/once), and якось (somehow). Explain that "десь" implies a specific but unknown location ("Він десь тут"), while "колись" can refer to both the past ("Колись я жив у Львові") and the future ("Колись ми зустрінемося знову").
- P4 (~120 words): Declension rules for the -сь series. Explain that the base pronoun (хто/що) declines normally while the suffix -сь stays at the very end. Show the transformation through three cases: Genitive (когось/чогось), Dative (комусь/чомусь), and Instrumental (кимось/чимось). Note that unlike the negative series, prepositions do NOT split the -сь series (e.g., "з кимось").
- P5 (~50 words): Brief warning against using Russian-style suffixes like -то or -либо. Emphasize that in Ukrainian, -сь is the universal equivalent for "specific unknown" entities.

## Будь-хто, дехто, абихто: різні відтінки (~550 words)
- P1 (~120 words): Introduction to the -небудь series (хто-небудь, що-небудь, де-небудь). Explain its specific usage in questions, commands, or hypothetical situations where any choice is acceptable. Use examples: "Ти бачив кого-небудь?" (Have you seen anyone?) and "Дай мені що-небудь почитати" (Give me anything to read).
- P2 (~130 words): Exploring the будь- prefix (будь-хто, будь-що, будь-який). Explain that "будь-" conveys a complete lack of restriction or selection—"it doesn't matter who/which." Contrast with -небудь: "будь-хто" is stronger ("anyone at all"). Examples: "Будь-хто може це зробити" and "Вибирай будь-яку книгу".
- P3 (~120 words): The де- prefix series (дехто, дещо, деякий). Explain that this prefix indicates "some" or "a part of a group." Use context: "Дехто з нас уже бачив цей фільм" (Some of us have already seen this movie) and "Я маю дещо сказати" (I have something to say/some things to tell).
- P4 (~100 words): The аби- prefix (абихто, абищо, абияк). Introduce the dismissive or "low-quality" nuance of this series. Explain that "абихто" often means "just anyone/any old person" and "абияк" means "carelessly." Example: "Він робить свою роботу абияк" (He does his job anyhow/carelessly).
- P5 (~80 words): Orthography and Prepositions. Explain the "sandwich rule" for будь-, -небудь, де-, and аби-. When a preposition appears, it breaks the pronoun apart: "будь з ким", "аби до кого", "де в чому". Remind learners that будь- and -небудь use hyphens, while де- and аби- are written as one word without a preposition.

## Ніхто, ніщо, ніколи: заперечення (~600 words)
- P1 (~120 words): Introduction to negative pronouns with the prefix ні- (ніхто, ніщо, ніякий, нічий) and adverbs (ніде, ніколи, ніяк). Explain that these are used to express the total absence of a person, object, or quality.
- P2 (~150 words): The Mandatory Double Negation Rule. This is the most critical syntax rule for this module. Explain that in Ukrainian, if you use a negative pronoun/adverb, the verb MUST also be negated with "не". Provide direct contrast with English: "I see nothing" (1 negation) vs "Я нічого не бачу" (2 negations: нічого + не). Provide three examples: "Ніхто не прийшов", "Ми ніколи не здаємося", "Вона нічого не знає".
- P3 (~130 words): Declension of ніхто and ніщо. Focus on the Genitive case which is used with "немає" to express non-existence. Show the paradigm: "Тут нікого немає" (There is nobody here), "Мені нічого не треба" (I don't need anything/nothing is needed to me). Note the stress patterns: ніко́го (nobody) vs ні́кого (no one to... [infinitive constructions]).
- P4 (~150 words): Preposition placement in negative pronouns. Explain that like the "any" series, prepositions split the ні- prefix from the pronoun. Give clear examples: "ні з ким" (with nobody), "ні про що" (about nothing), "ні в кого" (from/at nobody). Warn against common errors like "*з ніким".
- P5 (~50 words): Minimal pairs for recognition. Compare "колись" (once) vs "ніколи" (never), "десь" (somewhere) vs "ніде" (nowhere), and "хтось" (someone) vs "ніхто" (nobody). Explain how the prefix changes the entire world of the sentence from existence to non-existence.

## Практика: хтось чи ніхто? (~500 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Dialogue: The Mystery Party Game. Two speakers, Олена and Максим, discuss a strange noise in the kitchen. Олена: "Ти чув? Щось впало!" Максим: "Ніхто не міг туди зайти. Будь-хто з гостей зараз у залі." Олена: "Але десь тут має бути кіт. Може, це він?" Максим: "Я нікого не бачив."
- P2 (~180 words): Reading Story: "A Strange Evening". A short narrative about a man walking home. He hears someone (хтось) following him. He stops, but there is nobody (ніхто). He thinks he lost something (щось), but when he checks his pockets, there is nothing (нічого). He asks anyone (кого-небудь) on the street for the time, but nobody (ніхто) answers. Finally, he remembers he once (колись) had a similar dream.
- P3 (~120 words): Common Mistakes Recap. Address the three most frequent errors for A2 learners: 1) Forgetting the "не" in double negation, 2) Using Russian "-то" instead of "-сь", and 3) Misplacing prepositions (e.g., "з будь-ким" instead of "будь з ким"). Reiterate that "ніхто знає" is impossible in Ukrainian.
- P4 (~50 words): Final encouragement. Remind learners that mastering these pronouns makes their speech sound much more natural and precise, allowing them to handle uncertainty and negation like a native.
