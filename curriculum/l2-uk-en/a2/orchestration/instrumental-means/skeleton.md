## Їхати автобусом: Засіб пересування (Travel by Bus: Means of Transport) (~550 words total)

List essential transport nouns: автобус (автобусом), потяг (потягом), літак (літаком), трамвай (трамваєм), машина (машиною).]
- P2 (~120 words): [Conceptual contrast: Locative vs. Instrumental. Explain that "в автобусі" (Locative) describes where you are (location), while "автобусом" (Instrumental) describes how you travel (means). Use examples: "Я сиджу в автобусі" vs. "Я їду на роботу автобусом".]
- P3 (~110 words): [Dialogue: Two colleagues discussing their commute. Speaker A: "Я їду на роботу автобусом, а потім метро." Speaker B: "А я зазвичай їду власною машиною, або беру таксі." Note that "таксі" and "метро" are indeclinable but still function as Instrumental means.]
- P4 (~100 words): [The "On Foot" exception. Explain that "to go on foot" is "іти пішки". Even though it answers the "how" question, it uses an adverb "пішки" rather than a noun in the Instrumental case. Contrast: "Він їде потягом, а я йду пішки."]

## Орудний відмінок множини (Instrumental Plural) (~550 words total)
- P1 (~130 words): [Overview of Instrumental plural endings, which are unified across all genders. Explain the hard stem ending -ами (руками, столами, автобусами, вікнами) and the soft stem ending -ями (олівцями, паличками, морями). Stress that plural is simpler to remember than singular.]
- P2 (~120 words): [Practical application of plural tools. Provide examples: "малювати фарбами" (to paint with paints), "їсти паличками" (to eat with chopsticks), "працювати руками" (to work with hands), "писати олівцями" (to write with pencils).]
- P3 (~100 words): [Feminine III declension plural and sibilants. Explain the -ями ending for nouns like "ночі" (ночами) or "миші" (мишами). Mention that the spelling remains consistent with the soft/mixed stem rules.]

## Практика: Знаряддя чи супутник? (Practice: Tool or Companion?) (~500 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): [A deep dive into the "with" (з) vs. "no with" (bare Instrumental) distinction. Tool: "Я пишу ручкою" (The pen is the tool). Companion: "Я йду з ручкою" (I am walking while holding/having a pen with me). Provide clear visual or situational contrasts for both.]
- P2 (~100 words): [Applying the distinction to transport and people. "Я їду автобусом" (The bus is the means). "Я їду з другом" (The friend is my companion). Explain that adding "з" to transport ("їду з автобусом") sounds like you are traveling alongside a bus, not in it.]

## Підсумок (~150 words)
- P1 (~150 words): [Bulleted recap of the module's key rules:
  - Instrumental Case without a preposition expresses the **tool** (ручкою) or **means of transport** (автобусом).
  - Use ending **-ом** for hard masculine/neuter stems, **-ем** for soft/sibilant, and **-ою** for hard feminine.
  - **Plural** endings are -ами (hard) and -ями (soft) for all genders.
  - **NEVER use "з"** for tools or transport (unless you are literally traveling with a friend).
  - Exception: **Іти пішки** (on foot) is used instead of an instrumental noun.]
