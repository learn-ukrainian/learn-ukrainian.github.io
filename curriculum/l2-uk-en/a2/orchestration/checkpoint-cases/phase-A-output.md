## Cultural Hooks
1. **Digital Ukraine (Дія & Monobank)**: Ukraine is a global pioneer in digital state services. The "Diia" (Дія) app allows citizens to store official documents like passports and driver's licenses digitally, while banks like Monobank operate entirely without physical branches.
2. **Stamps as Currency (1918)**: During the Ukrainian People's Republic (УНР), there was a severe shortage of metal for coins. To solve this, the government printed special postage stamps on thick paper that circulated on par with coins (шаги).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Nom. for Instruments** → *Я пишу ручка* instead of *Я пишу ручкою* — Learners often forget that tools require the Instrumental case.
2. **Nom. after Negation** → *У мене немає гроші* instead of *У мене немає грошей* — The strict governance of the Genitive case after "немає" is often missed.
3. **Nom. instead of Vocative** → *Сергій, привіт!* instead of *Сергію, привіт!* — English speakers do not alter names when addressing people, leading to unnatural Ukrainian phrasing.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Frame the Ukrainian case system as a precise and logical tool for expressing meaning, avoiding any comparisons to Russian. Use positive, independent framing ("Ukrainian uses the Instrumental case to show the tool...").
- **Scaffolding**: This is an A2 Band 1 module (50-60% immersion). Ensure all grammar theory (especially case rules and preposition lists) is explained in English, while examples and dialogues are strictly in Ukrainian.
- **Tone**: Keep it encouraging. The case system is the biggest hurdle for English speakers; reinforce that they have already learned these in isolation and are now just putting the puzzle pieces together.
