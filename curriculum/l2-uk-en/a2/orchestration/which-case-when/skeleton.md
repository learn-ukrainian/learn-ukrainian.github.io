## Дієслово вирішує: Який відмінок після дієслова? (~600 words)
- P1 (~120 words): Dialogue: A "Grammar detective game" setting. A teacher and students read a Ukrainian newspaper article: "Президент зустрівся з прем'єром. Для журналістів підготували зал." They identify the Nominative, Instrumental, Genitive, and Accusative cases, analyzing why each was used based on the governing verb or preposition.
- P2 (~100 words): Explain verbs that take the Accusative case for direct objects. Most transitive verbs like "бачити", "знати", "любити", "читати", "купити", "шукати" require the Accusative. Provide examples: "Я читаю книгу", "Ми шукаємо ключі".
- P3 (~100 words): Explain verbs that take the Dative case for the recipient of an action. Emphasize "допомагати", "телефонувати", "дякувати", "радити", "заважати". Highlight that in Ukrainian, one helps or thanks *to* someone. Provide examples: "Я допомагаю сестрі", "Ми дякуємо вчителям".
- P4 (~100 words): Explain verbs that take the Instrumental case, often expressing a tool or management. Highlight "користуватися", "цікавитися", "займатися", "керувати". Provide examples: "Він користується комп'ютером", "Вона цікавиться історією".
- P5 (~100 words): Explain verbs and constructions that require the Genitive case, particularly for absence, fear, or need. Focus on "немає", "боятися", "потребувати". Provide examples: "У мене немає часу", "Вона боїться темряви".
- P6 (~80 words): Explain verbs that require a specific preposition followed by the Accusative case, specifically focusing on "думати про" and "мріяти про". Provide examples: "думати про майбутнє", "мріяти про подорож".

## Прийменник вирішує: Один прийменник — різні відмінки (~600 words)
- P1 (~100 words): Introduce the concept that prepositions dictate cases, but explain that some "dual-use" prepositions change the case they govern depending on their meaning, primarily distinguishing between direction and static location.
- P2 (~120 words): Detail the preposition "на" with the Accusative (direction/goal) versus the Locative (location). Compare examples: "Я йду на роботу" and "Поклади книгу на стіл" (Accusative) vs. "Я на роботі" and "Книга лежить на столі" (Locative).
- P3 (~120 words): Detail the preposition "у/в" with the Accusative (direction) versus the Locative (location). Compare examples: "Я йду в магазин" (Accusative) vs. "Я в магазині" (Locative). Briefly preview that this difference extends to time contexts.
- P4 (~80 words): Detail the preposition "по" with the Locative case to indicate surface or path. Provide examples of movement along a space: "бігати по кімнаті", "ходити по вулиці", "подорожувати по Україні".
- P5 (~90 words): Contrast the preposition "з/із" governing the Genitive (from/out of) versus the Instrumental (together with). Compare examples: "вийти з дому", "приїхати з Києва" (Genitive) vs. "піти з другом", "кава з молоком" (Instrumental).
- P6 (~90 words): Contrast the preposition "за" governing the Accusative (in exchange for) versus the Instrumental (behind/after). Compare examples: "дякувати за допомогу", "платити за квитки" (Accusative) vs. "за столом", "бігти за автобусом" (Instrumental).

## Особливі випадки: Час, характеристика, шлях (~550 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Explain using the Accusative case for days of the week and specific time periods with "у/в" and without. Provide examples: "у четвер", "у середу", "у п'ятницю", and "Цю неділю я відпочиваю". Contrast this with the Genitive used for next/last expressions: "Наступного тижня".
- P2 (~150 words): Explain using the Locative case to describe physical characteristics and clothing. Detail the pattern noun + у/в + Locative. Provide examples: "хлопець у червоному светрі", "дівчина в окулярах", "жінка у білому пальті".
- P3 (~100 words): Explain using the Locative case for years and broader time contexts. Provide examples: "у 2014 році", "у двадцять першому столітті", "у дитинстві".
- P4 (~150 words): Expand on expressing paths of motion using "по" + Locative. Detail how this structure communicates moving *across* or *around* a defined area, linking it to everyday actions. Provide examples: "бігати по кімнаті", "гуляти по парку", "їздити по місту".

## Алгоритм вибору відмінка (~450 words)
- P1 (~120 words): Introduce a practical 3-step Case Selection Algorithm. Step 1: Is there a preposition? (If yes, check which case it governs). Step 2: If no preposition, check which case the verb requires. Step 3: Still unsure? Ask the case question (Кого? Що? Кому? Ким?).
- P2 (~100 words): Walk through a decision tree using a mixed sentence to apply the steps in real-time, showing how to determine the Nominative for the subject and the dependent cases for objects based on the verb or preposition.
- P3 (~130 words): Highlight common pitfalls and typical L2 learner errors. Emphasize the confusion between "на + Acc." (direction) and "на + Loc." (location), forgetting that "думати" takes "про + Acc." rather than the Locative, and mistakenly using the Genitive instead of the Dative after "допомагати".
- P4 (~100 words): Provide a final encouraging recap. Emphasize that systematically applying the algorithm and checking verbs/prepositions removes the guesswork from case selection, leading to fluent and confident Ukrainian.
