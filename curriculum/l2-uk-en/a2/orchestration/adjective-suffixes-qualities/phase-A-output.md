## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Sweetness" of Ukrainian**: The widespread use of diminutive suffixes (-еньк-) to convey emotional warmth, respect, and intimacy, deeply embedded in folk songs and daily interactions (e.g., addressing parents as "рідненька матуся" rather than just stating physical size).
2. **Hyperbole in Folklore**: The use of augmentative suffixes (-енн-, -езн-) in Ukrainian storytelling and fairy tales to create vivid, larger-than-life imagery (e.g., "страшенний змій" - a terrifying dragon).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Over-reliance on "дуже" (very)**: English speakers often default to syntactic modifiers ("дуже малий") instead of utilizing the rich morphological system of Ukrainian suffixes ("малесенький").
2. **Orthographic mutations with -ськ-**: Failing to apply the K/Ch/Ts mutation rule, leading to spelling errors like «пражський» (instead of «празький») and «козакський» (instead of «козацький»).
3. **Register Mismatch**: Inappropriately using diminutives in formal or professional contexts (e.g., in a business meeting), which can sound patronizing or unprofessional.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Frame the richness of Ukrainian suffixes as an inherent feature of its poetic and emotional depth. Do not compare it to Russian. Present the morphological flexibility as a core strength of Ukrainian.
- **English Scaffolding**: Since this is A2 (Band 2: 60-75% Immersion), use English to explain the abstract concepts of word formation, derivation rules, and register nuances. Use Ukrainian for examples, tables, dialogues, and practice prompts.
- **Vowels & Orthography**: Ensure correct IPA for vowels (о = [ɔ], е = [ɛ]). Highlight the double "н" in emphatic adjectives (-енний) to distinguish them from participles (-ений) via stress shifts.
