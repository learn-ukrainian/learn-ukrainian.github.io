## Хай і нехай: Наказ для третіх осіб (3rd Person Imperatives) (~600 words)

> — **Шеф-кухар:** Увага, друзі! Наріжте цибулю дуже дрібно. *(Attention, friends! Chop the onion very finely.)*
> — **Студенти:** Ми вже нарізали! Що нам робити далі? *(We already chopped it! What should we do next?)*
> — **Шеф-кухар:** Дуже добре. Хай вода закипить. *(Very good. Let the water boil.)*
> — **Студентка:** Вода вже кипить! Вона дуже гаряча. *(The water is already boiling! It is very hot.)*
> — **Шеф-кухар:** Тоді помішаймо разом! Нехай соус настоїться п'ять хвилин. *(Then let's stir together! Let the sauce steep for five minutes.)*
> — **Студенти:** Зрозуміли, ми чекаємо. *(Understood, we are waiting.)*
> — **Шеф-кухар:** Хай усім смакує! *(May it taste good to everyone!)*

In the kitchen dialogue, the chef gives direct commands to the students, but he also commands objects to do things. When you want to give an indirect command, grant permission, or express a wish involving someone or something you are not directly addressing, you use the third-person imperative. This is a crucial part of the **наказовий спосіб** (imperative mood). To form it, you combine the particle **хай** (let — particle for 3rd person imperative) or **нехай** (let — formal variant) with the third-person present or future perfective form of a verb. The verb itself does not change its normal ending, making this construction relatively easy to master.

Ти використовуєш ці слова, коли об'єкт дії — це він, вона, воно або вони. Це дуже зручно для непрямих наказів. Наприклад, ти можеш сказати про друга: «Хай він читає». Або можна сказати про колегу: «Нехай вона прочитає весь документ».

> *You use these words when the object of the action is he, she, it, or they. This is very convenient for indirect commands. For example, you can say about a friend: "Let him read." Or you can say about a colleague: "Let her read the whole document."*

You might wonder about the stylistic difference between these two particles. The word **нехай** is slightly more formal and often appears in literature, official speeches, the news, or poetry. It carries a slightly elevated tone. The shorter word **хай** is commonly used in everyday speech, casual conversations, and text messages. Despite this stylistic difference, they are completely interchangeable in meaning. You can use whichever you prefer without changing the fundamental message of your sentence.

Обидва варіанти абсолютно правильні в українській мові. Ти можеш сказати «Хай іде!» або «Нехай іде!». Значення завжди буде однакове. Українці дуже часто використовують коротку форму в щоденному побуті, бо це швидше.

:::info
**Grammar box**
There is no special synthetic verb ending for the third-person imperative in Ukrainian. The verb always agrees with the subject in person and number, exactly as it does in standard present or future tense sentences.
:::

There are three primary situations where this construction truly shines. First, it is used for indirect commands. If you want someone else to perform an action, you tell a third party to make it happen. For instance, if you are waiting for a phone call, "Нехай вони подзвонять" means "Have them call." Second, these particles are used to grant permission. When you want to allow an action to take place or show that you do not object, you simply use the particle with the verb. "Хай іде, я не проти" translates perfectly to "Let him go, I don't mind." Third, they are essential for expressing a general **побажання** (wish, blessing). This is deeply rooted in Ukrainian culture. You will often hear phrases like "Хай щастить!" which means "Good luck!" or "May luck be with you." 

Дієслово завжди узгоджується з підметом в особі та числі. Якщо підмет стоїть у множині, дієслово теж має бути у множині. Хай вони прийдуть завтра вранці. Нехай діти вільно граються в парку.

> *The verb always agrees with the subject in person and number. If the subject stands in the plural, the verb must also be in the plural. Let them come tomorrow morning. Let the children play freely in the park.*

Finally, you must choose the correct verb aspect for your third-person commands. Just like direct commands to a second person, the aspect changes the focus of the action. Using an imperfective verb focuses on an ongoing process, a general action, or a repeated habit. "Хай пише" means "let him write" or "let him be writing." This is useful for activities that take time.

Доконаний вид показує результат або повністю завершену дію. Якщо ти хочеш побачити готовий результат, використовуй цей вид. Хай він напише цей лист сьогодні. Нехай вона прочитає цю книгу.

> *The perfective aspect shows a result or a completely finished action. If you want to see a finished result, use this aspect. Let him finish writing this letter today. Let her finish reading this book.*
