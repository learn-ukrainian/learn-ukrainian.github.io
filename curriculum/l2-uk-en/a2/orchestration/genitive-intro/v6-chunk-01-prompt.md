## Section Skeleton (follow this exactly)

## Родовий відмінок: Коли чогось немає (The Genitive Case: When Something Isn't There) (~650 words total)
- P1 (~110 words): Introduction to the Genitive Case (Родовий відмінок). Explain that after learning the Nominative (names of things) and Accusative (objects of action), we now look at the "Origin/Negation" case. Introduce the core questions: Кого? (of whom?) and Чого? (of what?).
- P2 (~130 words): The central construction of the module: `У мене немає...` (I don't have...). Explain the linguistic logic — in Ukrainian, we don't just "not possess" an item; rather, the item "is not there" in our personal space. Contrast this with English "I don't have a car" vs. Ukrainian "By me there is no car."
- P3 (~120 words): Contrast practice: `Є` (Nominative) vs. `Немає` (Genitive). Provide direct pairs: "У мене є брат" (Nominative) vs. "У мене немає брата" (Genitive). "Тут є телефон" vs. "Тут немає телефона." Emphasize that `немає` always triggers this case.
- Dialogue (~120 words): Moving into a new apartment. A conversation between a neighbor (Сусідка) and a new tenant (Нова мешканка). They walk through the kitchen and rooms noting what is missing: "Тут немає холодильника," "У ванній немає дзеркала," "На кухні немає плити."
- P4 (~110 words): Deep dive into the word `немає`. Briefly mention the distinction from `не має` (he/she does not have), as noted in the Zabolotnyi textbook reference, explaining that `немає` is an impersonal predicate meaning "there is no...".
- Exercise: Quiz, focus: Possession vs. Absence Drill (`Є` vs. `Немає`), 8 items. Focus on swapping between Nominative and Genitive based on the presence/absence of an object.
