## Родовий відмінок: Коли чогось немає (The Genitive Case: When Something Isn't There) (~650 words total)
- P1 (~110 words): Introduction to the Genitive Case (Родовий відмінок). Explain that after learning the Nominative (names of things) and Accusative (objects of action), we now look at the "Origin/Negation" case. Introduce the core questions: Кого? (of whom?) and Чого? (of what?).
- P2 (~130 words): The central construction of the module: `У мене немає...` (I don't have...). Explain the linguistic logic — in Ukrainian, we don't just "not possess" an item; rather, the item "is not there" in our personal space. Contrast this with English "I don't have a car" vs. Ukrainian "By me there is no car."
- P3 (~120 words): Contrast practice: `Є` (Nominative) vs. `Немає` (Genitive). Provide direct pairs: "У мене є брат" (Nominative) vs. "У мене немає брата" (Genitive). "Тут є телефон" vs. "Тут немає телефона." Emphasize that `немає` always triggers this case.
- Dialogue (~120 words): Moving into a new apartment. A conversation between a neighbor (Сусідка) and a new tenant (Нова мешканка). They walk through the kitchen and rooms noting what is missing: "Тут немає холодильника," "У ванній немає дзеркала," "На кухні немає плити."
- P4 (~110 words): Deep dive into the word `немає`. Briefly mention the distinction from `не має` (he/she does not have), as noted in the Zabolotnyi textbook reference, explaining that `немає` is an impersonal predicate meaning "there is no...".

## Закінчення родового відмінка однини (Genitive Singular Endings) (~750 words total)
- P1 (~100 words): General rule for Singular endings. Explain that the Genitive is "talkative" — it changes the endings of nouns significantly depending on gender and the "hardness" of the stem.
- P2 (~160 words): Masculine nouns: The `-а/-я` vs. `-у/-ю` puzzle. Explain that `-а/-я` is used for concrete objects, animate beings, and specific measurements (e.g., `брата`, `сина`, `стола`, `телефона`, `долара`). Use the specific example of `холодильника` from the dialogue.
- P3 (~160 words): Masculine nouns: The `-у/-ю` ending. Explain its use for abstract concepts, substances, and large locations (e.g., `часу`, `цукру`, `університету`, `болю`, `парку`). Mention that this is one of the most debated parts of Ukrainian grammar, so focus on the "Concrete vs. Abstract" logic.
- P4 (~130 words): Feminine nouns. Hard stems take `-и` (e.g., `мами`, `школи`, `книги`, `плити`). Soft stems and those ending in `-я` take `-і` (e.g., `землі`, `пісні`, `вулиці`, `Марії`). Point out the phonetic harmony here.
- P5 (~130 words): Neuter nouns. Most neuter nouns following the `-о` pattern take `-а` (e.g., `вікна`, `дзеркала`, `слова`). Soft neuter nouns in `-е` take `-я` (e.g., `моря`, `сонця`, `поле` -> `поля`).
- Exercise: Fill-in, focus: Genitive Singular Formation, 8 items. Practice forming the correct ending for nouns of all three genders in the context of "немає".

## Коли є багато або мало (When There Is a Lot or a Little) (~780 words total)
- P1 (~130 words): Introduction to Quantity words. Explain that words like `багато` (a lot), `мало` (little/few), `кілька/декілька` (a few), and `скільки` (how many) act like "Genitive magnets." When you have a quantity of something, that "something" must be in the Genitive Plural.
- P2 (~160 words): Genitive Plural for Masculine nouns. The dominant ending is `-ів` or `-їв` for vowel stems (e.g., `братів`, `столів`, `музеїв`, `доларів`). Mention the small group taking `-ей` like `гостей` or `друзів` (special case).
- P3 (~140 words): Genitive Plural for Feminine and Neuter nouns. Introduce the "Zero Ending" (нульове закінчення). This is where the vowel disappears: `книги` -> `багато книг`, `школи` -> `мало шкіл`, `вікна` -> `кілька вікон`.
- P4 (~140 words): Phonetic adjustments: The "Fleeting Vowel." Explain that when a zero ending creates a difficult cluster of consonants, Ukrainian inserts a helper vowel `о` or `е`. Use examples like `книжка` -> `книжок`, `сестра` -> `сестер`, `вікно` -> `вікон`.
- P5 (~120 words): Usage examples combining quantity and absence. "У мене багато друзів, але мало часу." "У кімнаті немає вікон." Explain how this allows for more complex descriptions of a scene.
- Exercise: Match-up, focus: Genitive Plural Formation with Quantity Words, 8 items. Match quantity words with correctly declined plural nouns.
