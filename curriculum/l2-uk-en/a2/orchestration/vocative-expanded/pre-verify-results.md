## Grammar Rules
- Vocative Endings (II Declension): Pravopys §87, §98 (as cited in Grade 6 textbooks) — Hard stem nouns take **-е** (пане, друже); soft stem nouns take **-ю** (лікарю, вчителю, добродію); nouns with suffixes -к, -ик, -ок and some others take **-у** (батьку, товаришу).
- Formal Address (Title + Name/Surname): Pravopys §141 — When combining a title (пан/пані) with a name, surname, or profession, both words MUST be in the vocative: **пане Петренку**, **пане лікарю**, **пані вчителько**.
- Emotional Vocative Word Order: General stylistic rule (Karaman, Grade 10) — The possessive pronoun *мій* can precede or follow the vocative noun, but placing it after (**друже мій**, **мамо моя**) adds emotional intimacy and is a common idiomatic pattern in Ukrainian.

## Calque Warnings
- пан лікар: Calque (Nominative error) — Should be **пане лікарю** (both words in Vocative).
- мій друже: OK — While **друже мій** is more emotional, **мій друже** is also grammatically correct and standard.
- добродійка: OK — Natural Ukrainian alternative for "madam/lady," though labeled as slightly literary or formal compared to *пані*.
