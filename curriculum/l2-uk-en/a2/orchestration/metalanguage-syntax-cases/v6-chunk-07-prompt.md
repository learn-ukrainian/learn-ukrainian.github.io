# Граматика A2: Синтаксис і відмінки



## Як це пояснюють у школі (How Schools Teach This)
The Ukrainian school system introduces syntax and cases gradually, building from simple components to complex relationships. The core idea is that cases (`відмінки`) are essential tools for connecting words into meaningful sentences (`речення`) (Source 16).

**Foundation (Grades 3-4):**
1.  **Sentence Members:** Learners first distinguish between **головні члени речення** (main members) and **другорядні члени речення** (secondary members) (Source 24).
    *   **Підмет** (Subject): Who or what the sentence is about (`хто? що?`).
    *   **Присудок** (Predicate): What the subject does or is.
2.  **Cases as Word Changes:** The concept of `відмінювання` (declension) is introduced as the changing of word endings to show relationships (Source 5, 12).
3.  **The Seven Cases:** Students memorize the seven case names and their questions. Mnemonics are often used, such as "Нашого Ромчика Дивує Зебра — Оця Маленька Красуня" (Source 5) or "Наша Рая Добре Знає Оцю Мову Калинову" (Source 16) to remember the order: Називний, Родовий, Давальний, Знахідний, Орудний, Місцевий, Кличний.

**Development (Grades 5-6):**
1.  **From Word to Phrase:** The concept of a **словосполучення** (word phrase) is formalized. It consists of a `головне слово` (main word) and a `залежне слово` (dependent word), connected by a question (Source 28, 36, 40). Crucially, the grammatical core of a sentence—підмет and присудок—is **not** a словосполучення (Source 28, 37).
2.  **Function of Cases:** The syntactic roles of cases become explicit.
    *   The **Називний відмінок** is the case of the **підмет** (Source 4, 14, 30).
    *   The other cases, called **непрямі відмінки** (indirect cases), are used for secondary sentence members (Source 4, 15).
3.  **Secondary Members Defined:**
    *   **Додаток** (Object): Answers the questions of indirect cases (`кого? чого?`, `кому? чому?`, etc.) and represents the object of an action (Source 7, 10, 11).
    *   **Означення** (Attribute): Answers `який? чий? котрий?` and describes a noun (Source 11, 17, 20).
    *   **Обставина** (Adverbial Modifier): Answers `де? коли? як? чому?` and describes the circumstances of an action (Source 1, 11).

The pedagogical progression is clear: identify sentence parts -> learn that endings change (cases) -> connect specific cases to specific sentence parts (Nominative to subject, indirect cases to objects).

## Повна парадигма (Full Paradigm)

In Ukrainian, nouns, pronouns, adjectives, and numerals change their endings to show their role in a sentence. This system is built on seven cases (`відмінки`). Each case answers specific questions and serves a distinct grammatical function (Source 1, 42, 49).

| Відмінок (Case) | Питання (Question) | Функція (Function) & English Equivalent |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Називний** (Nominative) | `хто? що?` | **Subject:** The "doer" of the action. The dictionary form. |
| **Родовий** (Genitive) | `кого? чого?` | **Possession** ('s, of); **absence** (e.g., with `немає`); quantity. |
| **Давальний** (Dative) | `кому? чому?` | **Indirect object:** The recipient of an action ("to whom/what"). |
| **Знахідний** (Accusative) | `кого? що?` | **Direct object:** The receiver of the action. |
| **Орудний** (Instrumental) | `ким? чим?` | **Means/tool** ("by/with what"); **accompaniment** ("with whom"). |
| **Місцевий** (Locative) | `на/у/по кому? чому?` | **Location** ("in/on/at"). Always used with a preposition. |
| **Кличний** (Vocative) | *(не відповідає)* | **Direct address:** Used when calling someone or something. |

## Частотність і пріоритети

For an A2 learner, not all cases and forms are equally urgent. The primary goal is to build simple, correct sentences.
1.  **The Core Triad (Highest Priority):**
    *   **Називний (Nominative):** To name the subject. This is non-negotiable.
    *   **Знахідний (Accusative):** To name the direct object. Mastering the Nominative-Verb-Accusative sentence pattern (`Я читаю книгу`) is the single most important syntactic skill at this level.
    *   **Місцевий (Locative):** To state location (`в/у`, `на`). This is fundamental for answering "where?" (`де?`).
2.  **The Functional Connectors (High Priority):**
    *   **Родовий (Genitive):** Essential for two high-frequency functions: showing possession (`мама студента`) and expressing absence with `немає` (`немає часу`).
    *   **Орудний (Instrumental):** Needed for expressing accompaniment (`з другом`) and using tools (`писати ручкою`). Both are very common in daily conversation.
3.  **The Relational Cases (Medium Priority):**
    *   **Давальний (Dative):** Crucial for indicating the recipient of an action (`дати студенту книгу`). While important, sentences can often be rephrased to avoid it at the earliest stages, making it slightly less urgent than the core triad.
    *   **Кличний (Vocative):** While grammatically simple, its use is a marker of natural, polite speech. It should be introduced early for common names and titles (`мамо`, `Андрію`, `пане`).

The priority for teaching should be function-driven. Instead of teaching "the genitive case," teach "how to show possession" (Source 49).

## Типові помилки L2 (Common L2 Errors)

English speakers rely on word order, whereas Ukrainian relies on case endings. This fundamental difference is the source of most errors.

| ❌ Помилково | ✅ Правильно | Чому |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `Я бачу **машина**.` | `Я бачу **машину**.` | English uses Subject-Verb-Object word order. Ukrainian uses the **Accusative case** for the direct object. The ending must change. (Source 49) |
| `Це **вчитель** книга.` | `Це книга **вчителя**.` | English uses `'s` or `of`. Ukrainian uses the **Genitive case** to show possession. The noun that possesses something must change its ending. |
| `Я живу **Київ**.` | `Я живу **в Києві**.` | The **Locative case** is required to express location and it **must** be used with a preposition like `в` or `на`. (Source 15, 49) |
| `Він дав мені **цікава** книга.` | `Він дав мені **цікаву** книгу.` | Adjectives must agree with their nouns in case. Since `книгу` is Accusative, `цікава` must also become Accusative (`цікаву`). (Source 6, 20) |
| `**Анна**, як справи?` | `**Анно**, як справи?` | Direct address requires the **Vocative case**. Using the Nominative sounds unnatural and robotic. It's a key feature of spoken Ukrainian. (Source 12, 16) |
| `Я їду з **мій друг**.` | `Я їду з **моїм другом**.`| The preposition `з` (with) requires the **Instrumental case**. Both the pronoun (`мій` -> `моїм`) and the noun (`друг` -> `другом`) must change. |

## Деколонізаційні застереження (Decolonization Notes)

When teaching Ukrainian grammar, it is critical to present it as a self-contained system, not as a deviation from Russian. Assuming Russian rules or using Russian examples is a common but harmful pedagogical shortcut.

1.  **Кличний відмінок (Vocative Case):** Emphasize that the Vocative is a living, breathing, and **mandatory** feature of standard Ukrainian for all applicable nouns. It is used in formal and informal speech, literature, and daily life (`пане`, `Ольго`, `земле`). This contrasts sharply with modern Russian, where it is an archaism largely restricted to religious texts (`Боже`, `Отче`). The vibrancy of the Ukrainian Vocative is a key distinguishing feature (Source 16).
2.  **Давальний відмінок (Dative Case Endings):** The masculine singular Dative endings `-ові, -еві` (`студентові`, `братові`) are fully standard and often preferred in formal writing to avoid ambiguity with other cases. While the `-у, -ю` endings also exist (`студенту`), the `-ові` forms are characteristic of Ukrainian and should be taught as a primary, correct option, not an afterthought (Source 9, 21, 42).
3.  **Чергування приголосних (Consonant Alternations):** The consonant mutations in the Dative and Locative singular (e.g., `рука` -> `в руці`, `нога` -> `на нозі`, `муха` -> `на мусі`) are a core phonological process in Ukrainian. They should be taught as a regular rule within the Ukrainian system, not as an "exception" (Source 15).
4.  **Синтаксичні конструкції (Syntactic Constructions):** Be mindful of constructions that differ. For example, the correct way to say "to laugh at someone" is `сміятися з когось` (Genitive), not `сміятися над кимось` (Instrumental), the latter being a common Russianism (Source 39). Always verify prepositions and the cases they govern against Ukrainian sources.

## Рекомендації для вправ (Activity Concepts)

The learning sequence should move from recognition to controlled production, then to free use.

*   **Phase 1: Identification & Matching.**
    *   **Concept:** Matching case questions to case names (`хто? що?` -> `Називний`).
    *   **Concept:** Given a simple sentence, identify the `підмет` and `додаток`. (`Хлопець читає книгу.` -> Підмет: `хлопець`, Додаток: `книгу`).
    *   **Concept:** Highlight a word in a sentence and have the learner identify its case. (e.g., "Я п'ю каву з **молоком**." -> Орудний).

*   **Phase 2: Controlled Production.**
    *   **Concept:** Fill-in-the-blanks with the correct ending. "Поставте іменники в дужках у правильній формі: `Я допомагаю (мама)`. -> `мамі`." (Inspired by Source 39).
    *   **Concept:** Sentence transformation. Provide a subject and object and have the learner build a sentence. "Ти / бачити / новий фільм" -> `Ти бачиш новий фільм?`.
    *   **Concept:** Choice drills. "Я йду в парк з (друг / другом / друга)."

*   **Phase 3: Communicative Practice.**
    *   **Concept:** Question-and-answer pairs that elicit specific cases.
        *   `Де ти працюєш?` -> `Я працюю в...` (Locative)
        *   `Що ти купив у магазині?` -> `Я купив...` (Accusative)
        *   `З ким ти розмовляв?` -> `Я розмовляв з...` (Instrumental)
    *   **Concept:** Picture description. "Що ви бачите на картинці?" Prompts learners to use Nominative (`На картинці є стіл.`), Locative (`На столі стоїть лампа.`), and Accusative (`Я бачу книгу.`).

## Зв'язки з іншими темами

Understanding cases is the gateway to Ukrainian syntax. It is not an isolated topic.

*   **Prerequisites:** A learner must first be able to identify a `noun`, `adjective`, and `verb`. A basic grasp of **noun gender** is essential, as declension patterns depend on it.
*   **Connects Directly To:**
    *   **Adjective Agreement:** Adjectives must match the noun's gender, number, and case. You cannot teach adjective declension without teaching noun cases.
    *   **Pronoun Declension:** Personal and possessive pronouns also change by case (`я` -> `мене`, `мені`; `мій` -> `мого`, `моєму`).
    *   **Verbs & Prepositions:** Many verbs and prepositions govern a specific case (e.g., `дякувати (кому?)` requires Dative; `цікавитися (чим?)` requires Instrumental; `чекати (на кого?)` requires Accusative).
*   **Enables:**
    *   **Flexible Word Order:** Once a learner understands that endings, not word position, determine grammatical role, they can understand and use the flexible word order of Ukrainian for emphasis (Source 49).
    *   **Building Complex Sentences:** Mastery of cases allows learners to move beyond simple S-V-O sentences to include indirect objects, locations, tools, and descriptive phrases, forming rich and complex thoughts.
