## Підсумок (~150 words)

The **родовий відмінок** (genitive case) in the plural form is the most complex aspect of Ukrainian noun declension. When expressing **кількість** (quantity, amount), we rely on three main pillars:

- **-ів / -їв**: The standard for most masculine nouns (*студентів*) and some neuter nouns (*морів*).
- **Нульове закінчення** (zero ending): Applies to most feminine nouns (*книг*) and neuter nouns ending in -о (*вікон*). Watch out for the fleeting vowels -о- or -е-!
- **-ей**: Used for feminine nouns of the third declension (*ночей*), some soft masculine nouns (*гостей*), and specific exceptions (*статей*).

This case is essential when you need to ask **скільки** (how many, how much). You will use it constantly to describe having **багато** (a lot, many) or **мало** (few, little) of something.

You will also need this grammar when counting your **гроші** (money), especially when paying in the local currency, the **гривня** (hryvnia).

Even if you only have **кілька** (a few, several) or **декілька** (a few, several) minutes each day, review these rules to build your confidence!

3. Which case do we use after the word *багато*? *(The Genitive plural)*
:::
