Medical conversations naturally require specific vocabulary and correct case usage to explain exactly how you feel. When a patient says «у мене болить голова» *(my head hurts)*, the word «голова» is in the Nominative case. It is the subject performing the action of hurting. The same rule applies to «болить горло» *(the throat hurts)* or «болить нога» *(the leg hurts)*. However, when we talk about the absence of a symptom, we must use the Genitive case. The patient says «немає температури» *(there is no fever)*. We also use the Genitive case with prepositions of location, like in the phrase «біля лікарні» *(near the hospital)*. The Dative case becomes essential when we indicate who receives help, advice, or instructions. For example, the doctor gives a prescription «пацієнтові» *(to the patient)* or recommends rest «хворому» *(to the sick person)*. The Instrumental case is very common when describing your physical symptoms to a doctor. We use the preposition «з» *(with)* to show what accompanies the illness. The patient explains that he came «з кашлем» *(with a cough)* and «з нежитем» *(with a runny nose)*. The Instrumental case is also used with prepositions of purpose, such as «йти за ліками» *(to go for medicine)* or «йти за порадою» *(to go for advice)*. The Locative case describes the setting where the action or conversation takes place. You wait «у лікарні» *(in the hospital)*, buy pills «в аптеці» *(in the pharmacy)*, or sit «на прийомі» *(at an appointment)*. Remember that the Locative case always requires a preposition to indicate a place. In medical contexts, you often need to switch between singular and plural forms of nouns. This is especially true when talking about quantities, doses, or time. When the doctor writes «один рецепт» *(one prescription)*, the word is in the Nominative or Accusative singular. But if you need «багато рецептів» *(many prescriptions)*, you must use the Genitive plural ending **-ів**. Body parts also change forms unpredictably. For instance, «одне око» *(one eye)* changes to «два ока» *(two eyes)* in the Nominative plural, but after words like «багато» or numbers five and above, it becomes «п'ять очей» *(five eyes)*.
