When the friends address each other, they use the Vocative case. You can hear this in the forms «Оксано!» and «Андрію!». The Vocative is only used to call someone or get their attention. It is never the subject of the sentence. The subject always takes the Nominative case. For example, look at the sentence «Друзі прийдуть о сьомій годині». The word «друзі» *(friends)* is in the Nominative plural because they are performing the action of coming. The distinction between addressing someone and talking about them is essential. Notice the difference between the Genitive and Accusative cases when talking about objects. Look at the phrase «я замовлю великий торт». The word «торт» *(cake)* is in the Accusative case because it is the direct object of the verb. However, the structure changes when expressing absence. In the sentence «у нас ще немає торта», the word «торта» is in the Genitive case. The word «немає» *(there is no)* always triggers the Genitive case. Additionally, the Genitive plural is used after words of quantity. In the phrase «багато гостей» *(many guests)*, the noun is in the Genitive plural because of the quantity word «багато». The Dative case is used to show the recipient of an action. The phrases «подарунок Олені» *(a gift for Olena)* and «напишу друзям» *(I will write to the friends)* use the Dative case because they receive the gift and the message. The Instrumental case shows accompaniment or the means of doing an action. We use the preposition «з» *(with)* for people, as in the phrase «з друзями» *(with friends)*. But notice the phrase «прикрасити кафе кулями» *(to decorate the cafe with balloons)*.
