## Section Skeleton (follow this exactly)

## Дієслова з давальним відмінком (Verbs That Take the Dative) (~650 words total)
- P1 (~60 words): Introduction to the logic of the Dative case as the "Case of Giving and Communication." Explain that while learners often associate Dative with the preposition "to," many Ukrainian verbs trigger this case directly to indicate the recipient or beneficiary of an action.
- P2 (~80 words): Focus on the two most common verbs: `допомагати` (to help) and `дякувати` (to thank). Contrast the English direct object ("help someone") with the Ukrainian Dative requirement ("help to someone"), emphasizing that the person being helped is a recipient.
- P3 (~90 words): A quick recap of Dative noun endings for this context: Masculine nouns ending in `-(о)ві / -(е)ві` (братові, вчителеві, другові) and Feminine nouns ending in `-і / -ї` (бабусі, мамі, подрузі). Use these in simple sentences like "Я допомагаю мамі."
- P4 (~90 words): Detailed look at `дзвонити` (to call/phone) and `відповідати` (to answer). Provide examples of calling a specific person versus an office, and answering a person versus answering a question.
- P5 (~80 words): Commands and Advice using `радити` (to advise) and `заважати` (to bother/disturb). Introduce the negative imperative "Не заважай мені!" (Don't bother me!) and "Я раджу тобі..." for giving suggestions.
- P6 (~110 words): Personal Pronouns in the Dative. Provide a clear chart-like explanation of `мені` (to me), `тобі` (to you), `йому` (to him), `їй` (to her), `нам` (to us), `вам` (to you pl.), `їм` (to them). Construct a sentence for each using the core verbs.
- Dialogue 1 (~120 words): A scene from a "Community Volunteer Day." The volunteer describes their actions: "Я допомагаю бабусі нести сумки," "Дзвоню другові," "Раджу сусідці нового лікаря."
- Exercise: Fill-in (8 items), focus on completing sentences with the correct dative form after verbs like `дякувати`, `допомагати`, and `дзвонити`.
