## Дієслова з давальним відмінком (Verbs That Take the Dative) (~650 words total)
- P1 (~60 words): Introduction to the logic of the Dative case as the "Case of Giving and Communication." Explain that while learners often associate Dative with the preposition "to," many Ukrainian verbs trigger this case directly to indicate the recipient or beneficiary of an action.
- P2 (~80 words): Focus on the two most common verbs: `допомагати` (to help) and `дякувати` (to thank). Contrast the English direct object ("help someone") with the Ukrainian Dative requirement ("help to someone"), emphasizing that the person being helped is a recipient.
- P3 (~90 words): A quick recap of Dative noun endings for this context: Masculine nouns ending in `-(о)ві / -(е)ві` (братові, вчителеві, другові) and Feminine nouns ending in `-і / -ї` (бабусі, мамі, подрузі). Use these in simple sentences like "Я допомагаю мамі."
- P4 (~90 words): Detailed look at `дзвонити` (to call/phone) and `відповідати` (to answer). Provide examples of calling a specific person versus an office, and answering a person versus answering a question.
- P5 (~80 words): Commands and Advice using `радити` (to advise) and `заважати` (to bother/disturb). Introduce the negative imperative "Не заважай мені!" (Don't bother me!) and "Я раджу тобі..." for giving suggestions.
- P6 (~110 words): Personal Pronouns in the Dative. Provide a clear chart-like explanation of `мені` (to me), `тобі` (to you), `йому` (to him), `їй` (to her), `нам` (to us), `вам` (to you pl.), `їм` (to them). Construct a sentence for each using the core verbs.
- Dialogue 1 (~120 words): A scene from a "Community Volunteer Day." The volunteer describes their actions: "Я допомагаю бабусі нести сумки," "Дзвоню другові," "Раджу сусідці нового лікаря."
- Exercise: Fill-in (8 items), focus on completing sentences with the correct dative form after verbs like `дякувати`, `допомагати`, and `дзвонити`.

## Мені подобається: Давальний відмінок досвідника (The Experiencer Dative) (~550 words total)
- P1 (~90 words): Explanation of the "Reversed Syntax" of the verb `подобатися`. Clarify that the person who "likes" is actually the Dative experiencer (to me, it is pleasing), while the thing liked is the Nominative subject that controls the verb.
- P2 (~100 words): Conjugation and Agreement logic. Contrast the singular `подобається` (used with a singular thing like `кава` or `книжка`) and the plural `подобаються` (used with plural things like `фільми` or `пісні`). Stress that the verb does NOT agree with the person (Мені/Тобі/Нам).
- P3 (~110 words): Past Tense Agreement. Explain how `подобатися` behaves in the past: `подобався` (masc), `подобалася` (fem), `подобалося` (neut), `подобалися` (plural). Use examples like "Мені подобалася та пісня" and "Тобі подобався той фільм?"
- P4 (~90 words): Comparison with English mental mapping. Explain why "Я подобаю..." is a common mistake for English speakers and how it sounds like "I am pleasing (to someone else)" rather than "I like." 
- P5 (~80 words): Adding nuance with adverbs like `дуже` (very much), `зовсім не` (not at all), and `більше` (more). Example: "Нам дуже подобаються ці гори."
- Exercise: Match-up (8 items), focus on matching `подобатися` sentences to their English equivalents, highlighting the reversed subject mapping.

## Скільки тобі років? Вік у давальному відмінку (Age in the Dative) (~500 words total)
- P1 (~90 words): The construction of age in Ukrainian. Explain that you don't "have" years or "are" a certain age; instead, the years "belong" to you in the Dative case. Formula: Dative Subject + Number + Year-word.
- P2 (~110 words): The triple rule for "Year/Years": `один рік` (1), `два/три/чотири роки` (2-4), and `п'ять і більше років` (5-20, round numbers). Explain the grammatical necessity of choosing the correct form based on the last digit of the number.
- P3 (~100 words): Question and Answer patterns. Teach "Скільки тобі років?" (How many years to you?) and how to ask about family members: "Скільки дідусеві років?" Mention that "років" is often dropped in casual speech: "Мені двадцять."
- P4 (~120 words): Practice with specific ages and family members. Use examples: "Дідусеві вісімдесят років," "Дитині три роки," "Сестрі двадцять п'ять." Include a note on asking "How old were you?" using the past tense of "to be" (було), which always stays neutral: "Скільки тобі було років?"
- Exercise: True-False (8 items), focus on judging whether age expressions use correct dative forms for nouns/pronouns and correct number agreement (рік/роки/років).

## Давальний чи знахідний? Порівняння (Dative vs. Accusative) (~480 words total)
- P1 (~90 words): Semantic distinction between the two cases. Accusative is for "Doing something TO a person" (Direct Object), while Dative is for "Doing something FOR/TO a person as a recipient" (Indirect Object).
- P2 (~100 words): Minimal Pair comparison with verbs. Show the difference between "Я бачу маму" (Accusative - physical sight) and "Я допомагаю мамі" (Dative - helping her). List other pairs like `любити/чекати` (Acc) vs `радити/дякувати` (Dat).
- P3 (~100 words): Verbs that take both: `давати` (to give), `казати` (to say), and `показувати` (to show). Explain the "Give SOMETHING (Acc) to SOMEONE (Dat)" structure. Example: "Я даю яблуко (Acc) братові (Dat)."
- P4 (~100 words): Pronoun contrast. Highlight the difference between `мене/тебе/його/її` (Accusative) and `мені/тобі/йому/їй` (Dative). Provide a "Decision Strategy" tip: ask if the person is a target (Acc) or a listener/recipient (Dat).
- Exercise: Quiz (8 items), focus on choosing between dative and accusative for the underlined noun or pronoun in sentences like "Він бачить (подругу/подрузі)" or "Він дзвонить (подрузі/подругу)."
