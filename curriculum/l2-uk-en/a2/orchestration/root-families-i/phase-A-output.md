## Cultural Hooks
1. The root *пис-* is deeply connected to Ukrainian culture through the word "писанка" (Easter egg), which historically comes from Proto-Slavic *pьsati (to paint or decorate), linking writing directly with art.
2. The word for a romantic date, "побачення", is literally a "mutual seeing" derived from the root *бач-*, showcasing how roots form conceptually beautiful vocabulary.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Confusing "бачити" and "дивитися" → "Бачити" is passive ability/perception (to see), while "дивитися" is active/intentional watching (to look/watch).
2. Misapplying the "з-" prefix to "ходити" → Learners often write "зходити", failing to apply the "Кафе Птах" euphony rule which mandates "сходити".
3. Over-restricting "записати" → Learners assume it only means "to write down", missing its critical modern meaning of "to record" (audio/video).

## Cross-References
- Builds on: A1 foundational vocabulary (basic verbs like читати, писати).
- Prepares for: Advanced A2/B1 modules on verbs of motion and deeper aspectual prefixing.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Do not explain the "Кафе Птах" rule or prefixes by comparing them to Russian. Present Ukrainian word formation as a native, elegant, Lego-like modular system.
- Ensure all example sentences clearly demonstrate the semantic shift caused by prefixes (e.g., хід vs вихід vs перехід).
- This is an A2 Band 2 module (60-75% immersion). English should only be used to explain the abstract concepts of word formation and euphony rules; all dialogues, examples, and practice must be in Ukrainian.
