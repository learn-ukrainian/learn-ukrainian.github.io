# Warm-up

> ğŸ’¡ **Why Aspect Matters:** English uses tense (past, present, future) to mark time. Ukrainian uses *both* tense AND aspect. Aspect tells you *how* the action unfolds: Is it a process? Is it finished? This is fundamental to Ukrainian thinking about actions.

## The Core Distinction

| Imperfective | Perfective |
|--------------|------------|
| Focus on **process** | Focus on **result** |
| **Ongoing** actions | **Completed** actions |
| **Repeated** actions | **Single** events |
| Duration matters | Completion matters |

## Cultural Insight: Aspect and Ukrainian Thought ğŸ‡ºğŸ‡¦

> [!important]
> **Philosophical Depth:** Aspect reflects how Ukrainians think about actions. Asking "Ğ¢Ğ¸ Ñ€Ğ¾Ğ±Ğ¸Ğ² Ñ†Ğµ?" (impf) asks about the *process* â€” Were you engaged in doing this? > [!tip]
> **Myth vs. Fact:** MYTH: "Aspect is just like English progressive tense." FACT: Aspect goes much deeper. Imperfective covers progressive, habitual, AND general actions.
