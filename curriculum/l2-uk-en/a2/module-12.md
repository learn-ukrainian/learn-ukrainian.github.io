# Presentation: Perfective Past Tense

## üéØ The Core Concept

When you use **perfective past**, you're saying: "The action is DONE. There's a RESULT." The focus is on completion, not process.

## Formation

## Cultural Insight: Result-Oriented Culture üá∫üá¶

> [!important]
> **Ukrainian Communication Style:** When Ukrainians ask "–¢–∏ –∑—Ä–æ–±–∏–≤?" (Did you do it?), using perfective, they're asking about RESULTS ‚Äî was it completed? > [!tip]
> **Myth vs. Fact:** MYTH: "Perfective means past tense." FACT: Perfective is an ASPECT, not a tense. Perfective verbs exist in past AND future. "–ü—Ä–æ—á–∏—Ç–∞–≤" (read/finished) is perfective past. "–ü—Ä–æ—á–∏—Ç–∞—é" (will read/finish) is perfective future. The aspect is about completion, not time.
