## Cultural Hooks

1. **Kyiv-Mohyla Academy etymology:** Ukrainian grammar terminology traces to Church Slavonic and Latin traditions brought to Kyiv by scholars at the Kyiv-Mohyla Academy (17th century). The word "відмінок" literally means "change/variation" — because the word form changes depending on its grammatical role. "Дієслово" is built from "дія" (action) + "слово" (word), a calque pattern mirroring Latin "verbum." Similarly, "займенник" = "за-іменник" (instead of a name), mirroring Latin "pro-nomen."
   - Source: Ukrainian linguistic tradition, confirmed by SUM etymological entries

2. **Кличний відмінок — unique to Ukrainian:** The vocative case term "кличний відмінок" was formalized by Ahatanhel Krymskyi in his "Українська граматика" (1907). Russian lost the vocative case, making it a distinctive feature of Ukrainian. The term was codified in the Ukrainian orthography of 1990 (Правопис 1990).
   - Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Кличний_відмінок, bukischool.com.ua

3. **Case mnemonic tradition:** "На Різдво Дід Загубив Орішки Між Ковбасками" (Н-Р-Д-З-О-М-К) — a genuine Ukrainian school mnemonic for remembering case order. Used in NUSh curriculum.
   - Source: Ukrainian school tradition, confirmed across educational sites (miyklas.com.ua, naurok.com.ua)
