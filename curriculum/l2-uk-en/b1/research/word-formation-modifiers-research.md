## Cultural Hooks

1. **незалежний — the most important не- word in Ukrainian** (Wikipedia: Відновлення державної незалежності України, 1990–1991). The word незалежний is formed transparently: не- + залежний (dependent). August 24, 1991: Акт проголошення незалежності України — the single most culturally weighted adjective in modern Ukrainian. The prefix не- that learners practice in grammar drills is the same morpheme that names Ukraine's statehood. This is a concrete, verifiable anchor.

2. **жовто-блакитний — compound adjectives encode identity** (Grade 7 Avramenko textbook, tier 1, confirmed). The Ukrainian flag's color description жовто-блакитний/жовто-синій is a hyphenated compound (рівноправні частини — equal components), contrasting with subordinate compounds like давньоукраїнський (written together). The hyphenation rule for color compounds is taught in Grade 7 alongside cardinal direction compounds (північно-західний). The flag's official heraldic color sequence (жовтий on top, блакитний below) is itself a point of public cultural literacy — the sky above the wheat fields.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **по-українськи vs. *по-українському** → Correct adverbial form is **по-українськи** (State Standard §4.3.7 explicitly lists both, but по-українськи is the primary adverb form; по-українському exists but is less standard as an adverb of manner). Root cause: interference from adjective declension (давальний/місцевий ends in -ому/-ій), learners treat the adverb as a declined form.

2. **к→ц / г→ж alternation errors in relational adjectives** → *козакський → correct: козацький; *паразький → correct: паризький. Root cause: learners apply the suffix -ськ- directly without applying the phonological alternation. The textbook (Karaman Grade 10, tier 2) explicitly notes: "При творенні відносних прикметників від географічних назв широко використовується суфікс -ськ- і його варіанти -зьк-, -цьк-." The alternation is obligatory and must be drilled.

3. **-ов- vs. -ев-/-єв- in material adjectives** → *грушовий → correct: грушевий (м'яка основа, ненаголошений суфікс). Grade 6 Avramenko (tier 1) explicitly teaches this: suffix choice depends on the noun's declension group (hard vs. soft) and stress pattern. Common error: applying -ов- universally as the default material suffix. Similarly, *деревяний (missing apostrophe) → correct: дерев'яний (апостроф required after губний приголосний before я).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Immersion**: Module is B1.7 (M57) — full Ukrainian immersion. No English in prose or callouts. English only in vocabulary table translation column.
- **Decolonized framing**: Frame Ukrainian's adjective suffixation positively as a rich, systematic, and highly productive mechanism. Do not use Polish as a baseline either — describe Ukrainian independently. The к→ц/г→ж alternations are a feature of Ukrainian phonology, not a "complication."
- **незалежний cultural moment**: The не- prefix section is the natural place to anchor the civic/cultural hook. Keep it brief (2–3 sentences) but emotionally resonant — this is a word that every adult Ukrainian knows viscerally.
- **жовто-блакитний**: The compound adjective section (Section 4) is the right place for the flag color note. The Grade 7 textbook contrast (жовто-синій with hyphen vs. давньогрецький without) maps exactly onto the module's content points.
- **Adverb section (Section 5)**: State Standard §4.3.7 explicitly lists по-українськи as the primary example. This is the culturally richest adverb formation example — speaking/writing Ukrainian as an act of identity. Use it as the opening example before moving to швидко, гарно, etc.
- **Word count strategy**: Sections 2 (Suffixation, 800w) and 5 (Adverbs, 800w) carry the most word load. Both have enough distinct productive patterns (6+ suffixes each) to support full 800-word treatment with paradigm tables, annotated examples, and practice prose. Section 4 (Compound Adjectives, 600w) needs the hyphenation rule table to reach its target.
- **Textbook sources confirmed**: Grade 6 Avramenko (tier 1) and Grade 6 Litvinova (tier 1) are authoritative primary sources for adjective formation rules. Grade 7 Avramenko (tier 1) for compound adjectives. Grade 10 Karaman (tier 2) for the -ськ-/-зьк-/-цьк- geographic suffix rule and кiosco/Paris/Baxmač examples.
