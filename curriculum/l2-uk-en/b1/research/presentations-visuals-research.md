## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

This module addresses specific communicative competencies defined in the **Ukrainian State Standard for Ukrainian as a Second Language (2024)**, specifically for Level B1 (Intermediate).

> **Annex 2. Communicative Competence (B1)**
>
> **Monologue Speech (Production):**
> *   "The user can maintain a monologue... can reasonably fluently narrate a simple story or **describe something**, presenting narrative points in a linear sequence."
> *   "Can briefly explain and justify their opinions and plans."
> *   "Can make a **short prepared presentation** on a topic from their field or area of interest, answering clearly articulated follow-up questions."
>
> **Relevant Grammar Topics (B1 Scope):**
> *   **Comparison:** Comparative and Superlative degrees of adjectives and adverbs (*більший за...*, *найвищий показник*).
> *   **Sequence & Logic:** Conjunctions and linking words (*по-перше*, *по-друге*, *отже*, *тому що*, *завдяки*).
> *   **Verb Aspect:** Use of Perfective verbs for completed results (*зріс*, *впав*) vs Imperfective for processes (*зростав*, *падав*).

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Analysis

### Core Vocabulary (High Frequency)
*   **презентація** (noun, fem.) - presentation. Ubiquitous in business/study.
*   **тема** (noun, fem.) - topic/theme.
*   **план** (noun, masc.) - plan/outline.
*   **слайд** (noun, masc.) - slide.
*   **екран** (noun, masc.) - screen.
*   **питання** (noun, neut.) - question.
*   **дякую** (verb) - thank you.
*   **увага** (noun, fem.) - attention. *Collocation: Дякую за увагу (Thank you for your attention).*

### Specific "Data Description" Vocabulary (Mid Frequency)
*   **графік** (noun, masc.) - graph (usually line graph) OR schedule. *Context is key.*
*   **діаграма** (noun, fem.) - chart/diagram (pie, bar).
*   **дані** (noun, plural only in this context) - data.
*   **відсоток** (noun, masc.) - percent.
*   **зростати / зрости** (verb) - to grow/increase.
*   **падати / впасти** (verb) - to fall/decrease.
*   **зменшуватися / зменшитися** (verb) - to decrease/shrink (more formal).
*   **стабільний** (adj.) - stable.

### Common Collocations (B1 Level)
*   **Робити / готувати презентацію** — To make/prepare a presentation.
*   **Зверніть увагу на...** (Accusative) — Pay attention to... / Please note...
*   **Як ви бачите на...** (Locative) — As you see on...
*   **Наступний слайд** — Next slide.
*   **Підбивати підсумки** — To sum up / summarize.
*   **Згідно з даними** — According to data.

## 3. Cultural Hook

*   **The "Diia" Effect & Modern Style:** Historically, Ukrainian presentations (especially in academia or government) were text-heavy and read aloud ("читати з листочка"). However, the boom of the Ukrainian IT sector and the "State in a Smartphone" (Diia) initiative has popularized a modern, minimalist, visual-first style. Apple-style keynotes are the new gold standard in Kyiv business hubs.
*   **Politeness Rituals:** Ukrainian presentations often start and end with specific ritual phrases.
    *   *Start:* "Шановні колеги! Дозвольте представити вам..." (Dear colleagues! Allow me to present to you...).
    *   *End:* "Дякую за увагу! Чи є у вас запитання?" (Thank you for attention! Do you have questions?). Failing to say "Дякую за увагу" is considered abrupt and slightly rude.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
*   **"Showing" vs "Demonstrating":** While English uses "show", Ukrainian often uses specific directional verbs or formal equivalents like *демонструвати* in business contexts.
*   **Data is Plural:** *Дані* is strictly plural. *Дані свідчать* (Data show/testify), NOT *Дані свідчить*.
*   **Graph vs. Schedule:** The word *графік* is a false friend/polyseme.
    *   *Графік роботи* = Work schedule.
    *   *Графік продажів* = Sales graph.
    *   *Діаграма* is safer for pie/bar charts.

### Common Learner Errors
*   **Prepositions with Trends:**
    *   Correct: *Продажі зросли **на** 10%* (Sales grew **by** 10%).
    *   Correct: *Продажі зросли **до** 100 одиниць* (Sales grew **to** 100 units).
    *   Error: Using *в* instead of *на* for difference, or mixing up *на* and *до*.
*   **Case after "На":**
    *   *На слайді* (Locative) - location.
    *   *Дивіться на слайд* (Accusative) - direction.
    *   Learners often confuse these.

### Teaching Sequence
1.  **Structure First:** Signposting words (*спочатку, потім, в кінці*).
2.  **Visual Nouns:** *Слайд, графік, таблиця*.
3.  **Trend Verbs:** *Зростати/падати* (Past/Present/Future).
4.  **Prepositions of Change:** *На / До*.
5.  **Summarizing:** *Отже, таким чином*.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1)
*   **Verb Aspect:** *Зростав* (process) vs *Зріс* (result).
*   **Future Tense:** *Ми будемо говорити про...*, *Ринок зросте...*
*   **Imperative (Polite):** *Подивіться*, *Зверніть увагу*, *Перейдімо*.
*   **Comparatives:** *Більший ніж*, *Менший за*.
*   **Complex Sentences:** *Це означає, що...*, *Тому що...*

### OUT of Scope (Reserved for B2/C1)
*   **Active Participles:** *Зростаючий ринок* (Market that is growing) -> Better to use *Ринок, що зростає* at B1 level to avoid clumsy morphology.
*   **Passive Constructions (Complex):** *Було презентовано*, *Буде розглянуто* -> B1 students should use Active voice (*Ми презентували*, *Ми розглянемо*) or simple reflexive (*Презентується*).
*   **Highly Technical Rhetoric:** Literary/Political rhetoric devices (metaphors, sophisticated stylistic inversions).
