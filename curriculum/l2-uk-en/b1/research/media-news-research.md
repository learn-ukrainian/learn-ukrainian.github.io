## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Collocations

### High Frequency (Core B1)
*   **Nouns**: новини (pl.), газета, журнал, радіо, телебачення, інтернет, сайт, стаття, програма, канал, відео, фото, журналіст, ведучий/ведуча.
*   **Verbs**: читати, писати, дивитися, слухати, знати, говорити, думати.
*   **Adjectives**: цікавий, важливий, новий, свіжий, популярний.

### Medium Frequency (Topic Specific)
*   **Nouns**: інтерв'ю, репортаж, подія, повідомлення, джерело, заголовок, блог, коментар, посилання (link), соцмережі.
*   **Verbs**: повідомляти (to report), публікувати (to publish), знімати (to film/record), перевіряти (to check/verify), поширювати (to share/spread).
*   **Adjectives**: офіційний, перевірений, фейковий, актуальний.

### Low Frequency (Passive/Contextual)
*   **Nouns**: редакція (editorial office), трансляція (broadcast), ефір (air/broadcast), передплатник (subscriber), маніпуляція.
*   **Verbs**: оприлюднювати (to release/make public), висвітлювати (to cover/highlight).

## 3. Cultural Hook

1.  **Telegram Channels**: In Ukraine, **Telegram** is a primary source of news for many people, often surpassing traditional TV or websites, especially for rapid updates on air raids or local events. Mentioning "читати новини в телеграмі" is extremely culturally relevant.
2.  **Suspilne (Суспільне)**: The transformation of the state broadcaster into a public broadcaster (*Суспільне Мовлення*) is a key democratic reform. It is known for neutral, standard Ukrainian and verified facts, making it a good recommendation for learners.
3.  **"Єдині новини" (United News)**: The 24/7 telethon phenomenon during the full-scale invasion, where major channels combined forces. While controversial now, it is a significant cultural marker of the 2022-2024 period.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
*   **"News" is Plural**: In English, "news" is singular ("The news **is** good"). In Ukrainian, *новини* is strictly plural ("Новини **були** цікаві"). This is a classic interference error.
*   **Case Governance**:
    *   *Слухати* + Accusative (*слухати музику*, NOT *слухати до музики*).
    *   *Дивитися* + Accusative (*дивитися телевізор*).
    *   *Дякувати* + Dative (often used in comments: *Дякую автору*).
*   **"Paper" vs "Newspaper"**: *Папір* is material; *газета* is the publication. Learners often confuse them.

### Common Learner Errors
*   Using "новина" (singular) when they mean the general concept of "news" (plural *новини*).
*   Misusing "article" (*стаття*) vs "item" (*річ/предмет*) vs "clause" (*пункт*).
*   Incorrect preposition use with media (*в інтернеті*, *на сайті*, *по телевізору/на телебаченні*, *у газеті*). The Prepositional case endings need reinforcement.

### Teaching Sequence
1.  **Sources**: Where do we get info? (TV, radio, internet). Focus on Prepositional case (*Де?*).
2.  **Content**: What do we consume? (Article, video, news). Focus on Accusative case (*Що?*).
3.  **Actions**: What do we do? (Read, watch, share, comment).
4.  **Quality**: Describing media (True, fake, interesting, boring).
5.  **Output**: Discussing a simple news item (Who? What? Where? When?).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1)
*   **Grammar**:
    *   Past/Present/Future tenses (Perfective/Imperfective aspects).
    *   All cases (singular and plural).
    *   Basic Complex sentences (*Я думаю, що...*, *Це стаття, яка...*).
    *   Reported speech basic patterns (*Він сказав, що...*).
    *   Imperative mood (for commands like "Click here", "Subscribe").
*   **Topics**:
    *   Describing daily media habits.
    *   Retelling a simple news story.
    *   Expressing simple opinions about an article.

### OUT of Scope (Save for B2/C1)
*   **Grammar**:
    *   Advanced Passive Voice constructions (passive participles are introduced in B1, but complex passive flows are B2).
    *   Stylistic nuances of "journalese" or archaic formal language.
    *   Complex Gerundial phrases (*Deepriykmestnyk* chains typical of formal news).
*   **Vocabulary**:
    *   Highly specific political/legal terminology (*ратифікація*, *імплементація*, *юрисдикція*).
    *   Slang or very informal internet jargon (unless very common like *лайк*, *пост*).
    *   Nuanced debate on media ethics or censorship (requires B2 argumentation).
