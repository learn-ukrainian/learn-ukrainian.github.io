## State Standard Reference

**§4.5.1.1** (lines 2437–2442, B1 section): "Стилістичні засоби лексики: антоніми: тут – там, весело – сумно, явний – таємний, дорогий – дешевий; синоніми: друг, приятель, товариш; епітети: веселі птахи, сонячна усмішка; метафори: прийшла осінь, місто спить."

**Alignment**: This module directly addresses the B1 stylistic lexical competence requirement. The Standard lists antonyms as one of four key lexical stylistic devices at B1, alongside synonyms (covered in b1-59), epithets, and metaphors. The module extends beyond the Standard's examples into antonym typology, polysemy, and direct/figurative meaning — appropriate B1 depth that builds productive use, not just recognition.

**Correction note**: The plan's content_outline references "State Standard §4.4.1: lexical semantics at B1" — this is a mis-reference. The Standard's §4.4 at B1 is Syntax (§4.4.1.1 = declarative sentences). The actual authority for this module is **§4.5.1.1** (Stylistics → Lexical devices). ## Cultural Hooks

1. **Шевченко's antithesis**: Wikipedia's article on "Антонім" cites antithesis as a key poetic device, exemplified with «Той мурує, той руйнує…» (from Шевченко). This confirms the plan's «Руки в крові, а совість чиста» as a valid antithesis example (plan attributes to Шевченко — use but note that exact sourcing of this line should be treated as illustrative of the rhetorical device rather than a direct quote citation).

2. **Земля** as the canonical polysemy example: Vashulenko Grade 3 textbook (Tier 2) explicitly teaches земля with four meanings: планета / країна / ґрунт / поле, plus the proverb "Рідна земля і в жмені мила." This is confirmed textbook material — use it as the primary polysemy anchor and credit the educational tradition. Ukrainian's «земля» carries particular cultural weight: it encapsulates the nation's relationship to land, statehood, and ancestry in a single polysemous word — a uniquely Ukrainian cultural fact independent of any other language system.

## Textbook Grounding

**Grade 5, Avramenko 2022 (Tier 1), §16** — "Використання багатозначних слів у прямому й переносному значеннях":
- Textbook approach: gold ring / golden autumn / silver moon / iron character → adjectives as entry point for figurative meaning
- Confirms: *пряме значення* is primary; *переносні* are secondary, arising through transfer (перенесення назви)

**Grade 5, Zabolotnyi 2023 (Tier 1)** — §3 "Пряме і переносне значення слова":
- Uses golden hands (золоті руки) as the primary example — identical to plan's examples
- Framing: "яка назва одного предмета переноситься на інший" — use this framing

**Grade 3, Vashulenko 2020 (Tier 2)** — "Багатозначні слова":
- іти: людина йде / літак іде / ... (confirms «іти» as canonical polysemy example)
- земля: планета / країна / ґрунт / поле (confirms four meanings in the plan)

**Grade 11, Avramenko 2019 (Tier 2)** — "Стилістична норма":
- "Багатозначність (полісемія) має великі стилістичні можливості... більше можливостей у художньому, публіцистичному й розмовному стилях"
- Confirms: polysemy = stylistic resource, not error

**Grade 11, Glazova 2019 (Tier 2)**:
- Confirms deliberate polysemy use: same word in one sentence with two meanings (Малі діти — малий клопіт, великі діти — великий клопіт)
- This is an excellent B1 example — accessible yet linguistically sophisticated

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Confusing bagатозначність with омонімія** → Correct distinction: polysemous meanings are semantically related (all stem from one original meaning); homonyms are etymologically unrelated words that happen to share a form. Dictionary tells you: one entry with numbered meanings = polysemy; separate entries = homonymy. *Why it happens*: learners see "same word, different meanings" and collapse the categories.

2. **Over-applying antonyms to non-gradual (complementary) pairs** → Error: inserting an intermediate value between живий–мертвий ("напівживий" is idiomatic but technically a mid-point); treating купувати–продавати as simple opposites when they are converse relations requiring a change of grammatical perspective. *Why it happens*: learners treat all antonyms as gradual by default (холодний–теплий–гарячий model).

3. **Treating переносне значення as always poetic/rare** → Error: failing to recognise that most переносне meanings are fully lexicalized and neutral (e.g., час іде, дощ іде, фільм іде — these feel literal to native speakers). Learners often over-flag metaphor where none is felt. *Why it happens*: school-level framing emphasizes живі метафори (active metaphors) without distinguishing from стерті/мертві метафори (dead metaphors).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Typology section**: The plan lists three antonym types (gradual, complementary, converse) — Ukrainian linguistics also uses the terms *градуальні*, *комплементарні*, *конверсивні*; these are standard in Ukrainian academic grammar. All three VESUM-verified.
- **«іти» example chain**: Validated in Vashulenko Grade 3 — use freely as the primary polysemy example. Extend with план іде (the plan is progressing), розмова йде (the conversation is going) for B1-level sophistication.
- **Oxymoron examples**: *гарячий сніг* is a literal literary title (Юрій Бондарєв novel translated into Ukrainian); *живий труп* is a Tolstoy play title. Both are culturally anchored, not invented — good for credibility.
- **Metonymy example «читати Шевченка»**: This is a specifically Ukrainian cultural example — reading a poet's collected works referred to by the poet's name. Reinforce that this is a natural Ukrainian construction, not a borrowing.
- **Antithesis framing**: Present антитеза as a Ukrainian rhetorical tradition rooted in oral poetry (думи, народні пісні) and Shevchenko — not as a foreign import. Ukrainian folk binary structures (добро / зло, правда / кривда) are the indigenous source of this device.
- **Decolonized lens**: Do NOT present any of these phenomena as "similar to" or "differing from" another Slavic language. Ukrainian's богатозначність system and антонімія are described using Ukrainian textbooks and Ukrainian linguistic tradition. The examples (золоті руки, час іде, рідна земля) are authentically Ukrainian cultural material with no need for external comparison.
