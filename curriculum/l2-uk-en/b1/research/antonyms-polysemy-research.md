## Cultural Hooks
1. **Precision of Ukrainian Verbs vs. Russian Calques**: Unlike Russian, which uses "открывать/закрывать" universally, Ukrainian uses precise, context-specific verbs that form elegant antonymic pairs: `зачиняти/відчиняти` (windows, doors), `відкривати/закривати` (discoveries, meetings), `розгортати/згортати` (books). 
2. **Proverbs as Antithesis**: Ukrainian folk wisdom heavily relies on antonyms for pedagogical contrast. Example: «Ранні пташки росу п'ють, а пізні слізки ллють» (Early vs. Late).
3. **The "Golden" Metaphor**: «Золоті руки» (golden hands) is one of the highest cultural compliments in Ukraine for a skilled craftsperson, serving as a perfect daily example of figurative meaning.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Prefix Misapplication for Antonyms** → Creating false pairs like `зачиняти` (to close a door) vs `розчиняти` (which actually means 'to dissolve in liquid', not 'to open'). Correct pair: `зачиняти — відчиняти`.
2. **Translating Polysemy Literally (Calques)** → Translating the English polysemous word "right" literally in all contexts. Correct Ukrainian splits the concept: `правий` (direction / being correct) vs `право` (legal right). 
3. **Literal Translation of Idioms** → Trying to interpret `дощ іде` literally ("the rain walks"), whereas in Ukrainian, `іти` is highly polysemous and applies to precipitation, time (`час іде`), and films (`фільм іде`).
