## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, closely aligned with CEFR descriptors for **Level B1**, the student is expected to master complex syntax to express relationships between ideas.

Relevant sections for **B1 Syntax**:
> **Складнопідрядні речення (Complex Sentences with Subordination):**
> *   **Означальні (Attributive):** Using сполучні слова *який, яка, яке, які* in all cases to define nouns.
> *   **З’ясувальні (Explanatory):** Using *що*, *хто*, *як* to explain verbs of speech/thought.
> *   **Обставинні (Adverbial):**
>     *   **Місця (Place):** *де, куди, звідки*.
>     *   **Часу (Time):** *коли* (basic correlation).
>     *   **Мети (Purpose):** *щоб* + інфінітив (same subject) / *щоб* + минулий час (different subjects).
>     *   **Умови (Condition):** *якщо* (real), *якби* (unreal/hypothetical).

This module serves as a **cumulative checkpoint** for these specific competencies introduced in Modules 26-33.

## 2. Vocabulary frequency

At the B1 level, the focus shifts from content words to **functional grammar words** (conjunctions and particles) that structure complex thought.

### High-Frequency Connectors (Active Mastery Required)
*   **який / яка / яке / які** (which/who/that) — *Top 50 frequency word*. Essential for almost all descriptions.
*   **що** (that/which) — Used as a relative pronoun and conjunction.
*   **щоб** (in order to / so that) — Critical for expressing motivation/intent.
*   **якщо** (if) — The standard "real" conditional.
*   **якби** (if only / would) — The standard marker for hypothetical situations.
*   **тому що / бо** (because) — Cause-and-effect basics.

### Common Collocations (Structure-Based)
*   **Той, хто...** (The one who...) — Very common generalized subject.
*   **Те, що...** (That which... / The fact that...) — Used to create noun phrases from clauses.
*   **Все, що...** (Everything that...)
*   **Робити все, щоб...** (To do everything in order to...)
*   **Якби я був...** (If I were...)

## 3. Cultural hook

**Proverbs as Syntactic Models**
Ukrainian folk wisdom often relies on complex sentence structures, making proverbs perfect authentic examples for this level.

*   **Relative Clause (який):** *"Не той друг, хто медом маже, а той, хто правду каже."* (Not the one who smears honey is a friend, but the one who tells the truth.)
    *   *Cultural Insight:* Ukrainians value blunt honesty ("truth") over superficial politeness ("honey").
*   **Conditional (якби):** *"Якби знав, де впадеш, то соломки б підстелив."* (If I knew where I'd fall, I'd spread some straw.)
    *   *Cultural Insight:* Fatalism and the unpredictability of life are common themes; this is the quintessential phrase for hindsight/regret.
*   **Purpose (щоб):** *"Треба нахилитися, щоб з криниці води напитися."* (One must bow down to drink water from the well.)
    *   *Cultural Insight:* Respect for work; nothing comes without effort.

## 4. Pedagogical notes

### Key Differences from English
1.  **"That" vs. "Which" (Що vs. Який):**
    *   English often uses "that" for people ("The man that I saw"). Ukrainian strict standard requires **який** (adjectival agreement) or **хто** (for pronouns). Learners often overuse *що*.
    *   *Teaching Tip:* Drill *який* agreement. It behaves exactly like an adjective.

2.  **The "Schob" Trap (Subjunctive of Purpose):**
    *   English: "I want **you to go**." (Infinitive construction).
    *   Ukrainian: "Я хочу, **щоб ти пішов**." (Subordinate clause with PAST tense).
    *   *Error:* "Я хочу тебе піти" (Direct translation) is a major fossilized error.
    *   *Rule:* Different subjects = *щоб* + past tense (subjunctive mood).

3.  **Tense in Conditionals:**
    *   Ukrainian "Real" conditionals often use Future tense in both parts (*Якщо я буду вільний, я допоможу*), whereas English uses Present in the if-clause (*If I am free...*).
    *   Ukrainian "Unreal" conditionals are simpler than English (no "would have been"): just *би* + past tense for both present and past hypotheticals.

### Recommended Teaching Sequence (Review)
1.  **Identification:** Can students distinguish a "real" plan (*якщо*) from a "dream" (*якби*)?
2.  **Transformation:** Convert simple sentences into complex ones (e.g., "Good student" -> "Student who studies well").
3.  **Correction:** Focus on the *Щоб* + Infinitive vs. Past distinction.

## 5. Scope boundaries

### IN SCOPE (B1 Checkpoint)
*   **Relative Clauses:** Defining nouns using *який* (declined in all cases).
*   **Place/Time:** Simple correlation (*місто, де я живу*; *день, коли ми зустрілися*).
*   **Purpose:** The *щоб* construction (both variants).
*   **Conditionals:** Standard *якщо* and *якби* patterns.
*   **Correlative pronouns:** *той... хто*, *те... що*.

### OUT OF SCOPE (Save for B2/C1)
*   **Participles:** Avoiding relative clauses by using active/passive participles (*читаюча дівчина* vs *дівчина, яка читає*). B1 prefers the clause.
*   **Complex Temporal nuances:** *ледве*, *щойно як*, *в міру того як*.
*   **Archaic/Literary Conditionals:** *коли б*, *аби*.
*   **Concessive Clauses:** *хоча*, *незважаючи на те, що* (This is Module 35 — the *next* module).
*   **Deep Stylistics:** Inversion or poetic word order in complex sentences.
