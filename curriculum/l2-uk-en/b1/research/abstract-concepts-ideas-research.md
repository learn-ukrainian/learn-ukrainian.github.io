## Cultural Hooks
1. Hryhorii Skovoroda and "Сродна праця" (congenial work): Explores the value (цінність) of inner freedom and philosophical reflection. Famous epitaph: «Світ ловив мене, та не спіймав» (Note: correcting the "скривав" typo in the plan).
2. Taras Shevchenko's "Думи мої, думи мої": Elevates the word "дума/думка" from a mere thought into a deep, burdensome philosophical reflection and personifies it.
3. Lesya Ukrainka's "Слово, чому ти не твердая криця": The abstract concept of a word functioning as a weapon, showing how ideas and convictions (переконання) drive national and personal struggle.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **"У мене є ідея про цей фільм"** → Correct: "У мене є думка про цей фільм". English speakers incorrectly use "ідея" (creative spark) for opinions. "Думка" must be used for opinions and thoughts.
2. **"Навести вагому суперечку"** → Correct: "Навести вагомий аргумент". "Аргумент" is a logical point in a debate, whereas "суперечка" is a verbal fight or quarrel.
3. **"У нього гарний погляд"** (meaning appearance) → Correct: "У нього гарний вигляд". "Погляд" means a view, opinion, or literal gaze, not physical appearance.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Treat Ukrainian philosophical discourse (Skovoroda, Shevchenko, Ukrainka) as an independent European intellectual tradition. Do not compare to Russian equivalents. Focus purely on Ukrainian agency and concepts.
- **Immersion**: B1.5 requires 100% Ukrainian in the prose. No English scaffolding is allowed in the lesson text, only in the vocabulary table translations.
- **Tone**: Since this bridges to abstract concepts, use modal hedging («ймовірно», «на мою думку») to reflect B1+ intellectual maturity.
