## Cultural Hooks

1. **Григорій Сковорода (1722–1794)** — Ukrainian philosopher-mystic, poet, pedagogue, theologian. Described as Ukraine's «Сократ». His concept of «Сродна праця» (congenial/kindred work) — the philosophical ідея that happiness comes from work aligned with one's innate nature — articulated in the dialogue «Алфавіт, або Буквар миру». His self-composed epitaph: «Світ ловив мене, та не спіймав» encapsulates his переконання in inner freedom over worldly success. He appears on the 500 hryvnia banknote. Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (Сковорода Григорій Савич); textbook Grade 9 (Mishchenko) and Grade 8 (Zabolotnyi literature).

2. **Тарас Шевченко's «Думи мої»** — The opening line «Думи мої, думи мої, лихо мені з вами!» (1839) personifies thoughts as burdensome companions, transforming «думка/дума» from an everyday cognitive word into a vehicle of existential philosophical weight. This is a canonical text taught in Grade 9 Ukrainian literature. Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (Шевченко Тарас Григорович).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **ідея used for opinions** → Use «думка» — English speakers map "idea" to both meanings; in Ukrainian, «ідея» = creative concept/plan/spark; «У мене є думка про цей фільм» (NOT «У мене є ідея про цей фільм»). The plan specifically flags this — ensure explicit drilling.

2. **погляд (view/opinion) confused with вигляд (appearance)** → «У нього гарний вигляд» (he looks good) vs «У нього власний погляд на проблему» (he has his own view on the issue). Learners mix these because both words contain «-гляд» root and English "look" covers both concepts.

3. **аргумент over-extended** → English "argument" covers both logical points AND quarrels. In Ukrainian: «аргумент» = strictly a logical reason/point to support a claim (вагомий аргумент); «суперечка» = quarrel/dispute (запекла суперечка, вступити в суперечку). Learners say «ми мали аргумент» meaning a fight — incorrect; should be «ми посварилися» or «у нас була суперечка».

## Notes for Content Writing

- **погляд frequency caveat:** The 73.50 IPM figure includes the literal sense (physical gaze). In intellectual discourse, the metaphorical «погляд» (view/opinion) is dominant. The погляд/вигляд contrast section should use paired examples showing physical context where вигляд is correct to make the distinction concrete.
- **істина is low-frequency (4.04 IPM):** It reads as philosophical/bookish. Position it explicitly in the Cultural Context section (Skovoroda searches for «істину»), not as an everyday word. Don't inflate it to same depth as думка/ідея.
- **Skovoroda's «Сродна праця»** does not have its own Wikipedia article; it appears as a concept inside the Skovoroda article. Use the philosopher's biographical entry for facts. Grade 8 literature textbook (Zabolotnyi tier 1) has classroom exercises on Skovoroda — consistent with pedagogical treatment.
- **Register contrast** (Practice section): The textbook evidence (Grade 10, Glazova) shows «полеміка як зіткнення різних поглядів» used in authentic formal Ukrainian. Grade 8 (Zabolotnyi) uses «сформулювати тезу (свою думку)» for academic writing — these are confirmed pedagogically appropriate collocations.
- **Deverbal noun formation** is explicitly in §4.3.4 — this is a confirmed B1 grammar gate. The module should present the formation pattern systematically (verb → noun with -ння/-ення or zero-suffix) before discussing the semantic properties.
- **Immersion level:** Module 63 is deep in B1.1+ full immersion phase. Zero English in prose/explanations. English permitted only in vocabulary translation column and false-friend disambiguation labels.
