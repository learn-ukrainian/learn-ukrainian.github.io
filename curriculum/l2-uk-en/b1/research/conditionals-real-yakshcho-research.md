# Дослідження: Умовні речення: реальна умова (якщо)

## State Standard Reference

§4.4.3 (lines 2414–2416): «складнопідрядні речення з підрядною частиною реальної умови (сполучник якщо): **Якщо ви вивчите слова з переліку, то легко зрозумієте цей текст**»

The same paragraph (lines 2417–2418) immediately contrasts the irreal condition: «ірреальної умови (якби, коли б): Якби ви вивчили слова з переліку, то зрозуміли б легко цей текст» — confirming the State Standard treats real and unreal conditionals as a paired system at B1, making real conditionals the prerequisite for b1-035.

Alignment: This module directly addresses §4.4.3's obstavynna-umovy clause type with `якщо` and the `якщо...то` correlative pair. The TTT structure maps well — the Standard's own exemplar sentence uses **future perfective** in the conditional clause («вивчите»), validating the plan's #1 friction point about Future tense requirement.

**Notes:**
- `якщо` (577 IPM) is a top-100 conjunction — extremely high frequency, core item.
- `коли` (1051 IPM) outranks `якщо` — learners encounter it constantly, which drives the confusion. The semantic fork (condition vs. certainty) is a real and frequent error.
- `наслідок` vs. `результат`: frequency gap (24 vs. 57 IPM) reflects that `результат` is neutral/positive and very common, `наслідок` slightly more formal/neutral-to-negative.
- Multi-word phrases (`за умови`, `у разі`) returned 0 from GRAC — GRAC doesn't support multi-word token queries in frequency mode. Both are verified via VESUM context; treat as formal register items (lower frequency in spoken text).
- Proverb forms: `зігрієшся` (109 hits, 0.05 IPM) and `спітнієш` (10 hits, ~0 IPM) — rare perfective future forms, which makes them pedagogically ideal: they demonstrate the rule in an authentic folk register where learners wouldn't expect to find it.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Proverb — resilience frame**: «Якщо боятися труднощів, то не варто й починати» — authentic folk proverb. Structurally clean `якщо...то` pattern, future perfective implied in the infinitive construction. Strong motivational hook appropriate to the TTT opening.

2. **Proverb — seasonal labour**: «Взимку не зігрієшся, якщо літом не спітнієш» — attested folk wisdom. Contains the plan's target forms: `зігрієшся` (pf. future 2sg) and `спітнієш` (pf. future 2sg) in a **reversed conditional** (result clause precedes condition). This structural inversion is pedagogically valuable — it shows that `якщо` can follow the main clause, not just precede it, which matches Section 4 of the plan.

3. **Wikipedia**: Ukrainian proverbs (*прислів'я*) are defined as «мала форма народної поетичної творчості, короткий, ритмізований вислів, що несе узагальнену думку» — framing them as *народна мудрість* (folk wisdom) is authentically Ukrainian and avoids the colonial framing of presenting them as a regional variant of anything else.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Future → Present tense transfer** (L1 English): «Якщо ти приходиш завтра...» → correct: «Якщо ти прийдеш завтра...» — English uses Present Simple in `if`-clauses for future events; Ukrainian requires Future (perfective preferred). This is the plan's stated #1 friction point and is well-supported by the textbook evidence (Авраменко Grade 9 §24 treats this as the defining characteristic of умовне підрядне).

2. **якщо / коли confusion**: Using «Коли буде дощ, я не піду» (temporal certainty — implies the rain *will* definitely happen) vs. «Якщо буде дощ, я не піду» (conditional possibility — rain may or may not happen). Avramenko Grade 9 (p. 86) uses the pair «Одягай пальто, бо буде холодно» / «Одягай пальто, якщо буде холодно» as a discovery exercise — textbook confirmation that this distinction is a taught, testable item.

3. **Omitting `то` incorrectly / inserting it incorrectly**: Learners either always use `якщо...то` (calquing English *if...then* too literally in formal contexts) or drop `то` when it adds necessary emphasis. The plan's treatment of the correlative pair as optional-but-emphatic is correct; the textbook (Litvinova Grade 7, p. 199) lists `то` under підрядні сполучники in the conditional cluster, confirming it is taught as an optional emphasizer.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **State Standard exemplar sentence** («Якщо ви вивчите слова з переліку, то легко зрозумієте цей текст») should be used or referenced directly in the grammar presentation — it's authoritative and models the `якщо...то` pair with future perfective in the protasis.
- **Reversed word order** (result clause first, then `якщо`-clause) must be covered — the proverb «Взимку не зігрієшся, якщо літом не спітнієш» is a natural vehicle for this.
- **`навіть якщо` concordance** from GRAC confirms authentic usage: the pattern consistently introduces a concessive condition that doesn't change the outcome. Five authentic literary/journalistic examples all show this usage — content section 2 can use these patterns directly.
- **Register note** for `у результаті` vs. `через це` / `тому`: `результат` (57 IPM) is extremely common; the bureaucratic `у результаті` (as a connector) is stylistically marked in casual speech. Section 4 of the plan handles this correctly.
- **Immersion level**: This is B1.3a — full immersion (no English in prose). Grammar metalanguage must be in Ukrainian. English disambiguation is allowed only in the vocabulary table translations column.
- **Avoid**: framing the `якщо`/`if` relationship as Ukrainian "doing it differently from" any other language. Present Ukrainian's future-in-conditionals as Ukrainian's own logical system — a feature of how Ukrainian encodes epistemic possibility vs. temporal certainty.
