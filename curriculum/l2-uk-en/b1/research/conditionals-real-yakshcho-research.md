# Research: Real Conditionals (Якщо)

**Module**: B1 M31 "Підсумок" (Real Conditionals)  
**Topic**: Real Conditionals with *якщо*  
**Level**: B1 (Intermediate)  
*Note: This research targets the B1 level as per the filename and curriculum sequence, superseding the erroneous 'A1' label in the prompt.*

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, modeled on CEFR descriptors for Level B1:

> **Розділ: Синтаксис. Складне речення.**
> "Учень розуміє та продукує складнопідрядні речення з підрядними умови, що виражають реальну умову, використовуючи сполучник **якщо** (рідше *коли*, *як*), та співвідносні слова **то**, **тоді**."
>
> *Descriptor*: Can connect phrases to form a coherent text using connecting words to express condition (*якщо*).

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Collocations

### High Frequency (Core)
*   **якщо** (if) - Top 100 frequency. Used in almost all conditional structures.
*   **то** (then) - Essential correlative in the main clause.
*   **тоді** (then/in that case) - Common alternative to *то*.
*   **коли** (when) - Often overlaps with *якщо* in real conditions (time/condition ambiguity).

### Medium Frequency (B1 Enrichment)
*   **в такому разі** (in that case)
*   **за умови, що** (on the condition that) - More formal, introduces B1 register.
*   **хіба що** (unless/except if) - Useful colloquial addition.

### Common Collocations
*   *Якщо чесно...* (If I'm being honest...) — Very common conversational opener.
*   *Якщо можна...* (If it is possible / If I may...) — Politeness marker.
*   *Що, якщо...?* (What if...?) — Speculating (real possibility).
*   *Якщо ні, то...* (If not, then...)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**Superstitions & Proverbs (Народна мудрість)**
Ukrainian folklore is full of conditional warnings ("primety") that use the *Якщо... то...* structure. This is a fun way to teach the grammar while introducing culture.

*   **Example 1**: "Якщо ластівки літають низько — буде дощ." (If swallows fly low, it will rain.) — A classic "real conditional" predicting weather.
*   **Example 2**: "Якщо не впевнений — не обганяй." (If you are not sure, don't overtake.) — A modern, practical road safety slogan that became idiomatic.
*   **Proverb**: "Якщо хочеш мати те, чого ніколи не мав, то зроби те, чого ніколи не робив." (If you want to have what you've never had, do what you've never done.)

**Cultural Context**: Ukrainians often use *Якщо* to soften requests or advice, making it less direct than an Imperative. "Якщо ти не проти..." (If you don't mind...) is crucial for polite interaction.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Contrast: English vs. Ukrainian Tenses
The **critical learner error** for English speakers involves the Future tense.
*   **English Rule**: *If* + Present, *Will* + Verb. (Future meaning expressed by Present in the condition).
    *   *English*: "If the weather **is** good, we **will go** to the park."
*   **Ukrainian Rule**: *Якщо* + Future, Future. (Future meaning expressed by Future in BOTH clauses).
    *   *Ukrainian*: "Якщо погода **буде** гарна, ми **підемо** в парк."
    *   *Error*: Students say "Якщо погода є гарна..." (Wrong).

### Common Errors
1.  **Omitting "то"**: While optional in English ("If A, B"), in Ukrainian *то* is often preferred for balance: "Якщо А, **то** Б."
2.  **Confusing *якщо* (if) and *чи* (whether)**:
    *   *Correct*: "Я не знаю, **чи** він прийде." (I don't know *if/whether* he will come.)
    *   *Wrong*: "Я не знаю, **якщо** він прийде."
3.  **Using *би* (would)**: Students confuse Real (*якщо*) and Unreal (*якби*) conditionals. Ensure they stick to *Real* possibilities here.

### Teaching Sequence
1.  **Formula**: *Якщо* [Condition], *то* [Result].
2.  **Tense Check**: Drill the "Future + Future" pattern specifically, as it fights English interference.
3.  **Position**: Show that clauses can swap. "[Result], *якщо* [Condition]" (comma usually remains or disappears depending on intonation, but comma is standard before *якщо*).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1 M31)
*   **Real Conditions**: Situations that are possible/likely in the present or future.
*   **Structure**: *Якщо + Present/Future + (то) + Present/Future/Imperative*.
*   **Complex Sentences**: Connecting two clauses with a comma.
*   **Tenses**: Present, Future (Imperfective/Perfective), Imperative.

### OUT of Scope (Save for M32+)
*   **Unreal Conditionals (Subjunctive)**: "If I *were* rich..." (Якби я був...). DO NOT introduce the particle **би** or past tense for present meaning.
*   **"Whether" (Чи)**: Indirect questions are a separate topic (though good to distinguish briefly).
*   **Past Real Conditions**: "If he did that yesterday..." (Possible, but sticking to Future/Present predicts maintains focus on the core "Future" difficulty).
*   **Formal Connectors**: *У випадку, якщо...* (Too formal for this intro to conditionals).
