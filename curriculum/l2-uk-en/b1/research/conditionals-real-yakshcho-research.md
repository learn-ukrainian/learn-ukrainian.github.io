## Common Learner Errors
1. **Tense mismatch (L1 transfer):** "Якщо ти приходиш завтра..." → "Якщо ти прийдеш завтра...". English uses Present Simple for future conditions, while Ukrainian strictly requires the Future tense (often Future Perfective) if the condition is in the future.
2. **Confusion of "коли" and "якщо":** "Коли буде дощ..." used to mean "If it rains...". "Коли" implies temporal certainty (when), while "якщо" implies conditionality (if).
3. **Using "якщо" for indirect questions:** "Я не знаю, якщо він прийде" → "Я не знаю, чи він прийде". Using "якщо" instead of the interrogative particle "чи" is a frequent syntactic error.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing:** Do not compare to Russian grammar. Explain Ukrainian logic as an independent system where tense directly reflects reality (future events take future tense).
- **100% Immersion:** No English in explanations or callouts. All theory, including the diagnostic quiz and error analysis, must be strictly in Ukrainian (B1.3a rule).
- **Visual Clarity:** Use explicit visual separation (tables, callouts) to show the semantic difference between "якщо" and "коли", and the necessity of "то" in correlative pairs.
- **Stylistic Polish:** Address the false use of "у результаті" (calque/bureaucratic) vs natural "через це", "тому".
