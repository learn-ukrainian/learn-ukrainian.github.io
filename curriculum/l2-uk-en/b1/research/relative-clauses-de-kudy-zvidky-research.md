# Research: Підрядні означальні: де, куди, звідки (B1-27)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, this topic falls under the **B1 Level (Independent User)** syntax requirements.

> **Section: Syntax (Синтаксис)**
> **Topic: Complex Sentences (Складнопідрядні речення)**
>
> *"На рівні В1 користувач повинен вміти будувати та розуміти складнопідрядні речення з підрядними означальними частинами (attributive clauses), що приєднуються до головної частини за допомогою сполучних слів **де, куди, звідки**."*
>
> *Reference ID: Syntax.B1.2.1 (Complex sentences with attributive clauses denoting place).*

The standard emphasizes the ability to distinguish between static location (*де*), direction of movement (*куди*), and origin of movement (*звідки*) within the structure of a complex sentence, correlating with the demonstrative adverbs *там, туди, звідти* in the main clause.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Collocations

### Core Connectors (High Frequency)
*   **де** (where) - *Very High Frequency*. Used for static location.
    *   *Collocations*: місто, де...; місце, де...; будинок, де...
*   **куди** (where to/whither) - *High Frequency*. Used for direction/destination.
    *   *Collocations*: країна, куди...; університет, куди...; їхати туди, куди...
*   **звідки** (where from/whence) - *Medium-High Frequency*. Used for origin/source.
    *   *Collocations*: село, звідки...; джерело, звідки...; повертатися туди, звідки...

### Associated Nouns (Contextual)
*   **місце** (place) - The most common antecedent noun.
*   **місто / село / країна** (city / village / country) - Common geographical antecedents.
*   **дім / будинок / квартира** (home / house / apartment) - Personal spaces.
*   **робота / офіс** (work / office) - Functional locations.

### Key Verbs (Motion vs. Stasis)
*   *Static (requires **де**)*: жити (to live), бути (to be), працювати (to work), стояти (to stand), знаходитися (to be located).
*   *Dynamic - Direction (requires **куди**)*: йти/ходити (to go), їхати/їздити (to drive/ride), бігти (to run), летіти (to fly), прямувати (to head).
*   *Dynamic - Origin (requires **звідки**)*: повертатися (to return), приїжджати (to arrive), походити (to originate).

## 3. Cultural Hook

### "De" vs. "Kudy" as a Mental Discipline
Unlike English, where "Where are you going?" uses the same word ("Where") as "Where are you?", Ukrainian culture strictly separates these spatial concepts.
*   **Cultural Reality**: Confusing *де* and *куди* sounds childish or uneducated to a native speaker. It reflects a worldview that cares deeply about the *vector* of movement versus the *state* of being.
*   **Example**: Asking "Де ти йдеш?" (Where are you walking?) implies you want to know the specific path or surface (e.g., "I'm walking in the park"), whereas "Куди ти йдеш?" asks for the destination.

### The Concept of "Small Homeland" (Мала Батьківщина)
The word **звідки** triggers deep associations with one's roots.
*   **Proverb**: *"Де народився, там і згодився"* (Where you were born, there you became useful/fit in). This highlights the connection between place of origin and destiny.
*   **Identity**: "Звідки ви?" is often the second question asked after "Як вас звати?". The answer usually implies not just a city, but a regional identity (Halychyna, Polissia, Slobozhanshchyna).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key English-Ukrainian Differences
1.  **The "Where" Ambiguity**: English speakers habitually use "Where" for both location and direction.
    *   *Error*: "Я не знаю, **де** він пішов." (Incorrect)
    *   *Correction*: "Я не знаю, **куди** він пішов."
2.  **Correlative Words**: Ukrainian often uses pairs (*там... де*, *туди... куди*). English often omits the first part.
    *   *Structure*: "Go [there] where you are happy." -> "Йди **туди**, **куди** хочеш." (Omitting *туди* is possible but less emphatic).

### Common Learner Errors
*   Using *куди* with static verbs (e.g., "Куди ти живеш?" - very wrong).
*   Using *де* with destination verbs (e.g., "Де ми їдемо?" instead of "Куди ми їдемо?").
*   Forgetting the comma before the relative clause (*де, куди, звідки*).

### Teaching Sequence Recommendations
1.  **Review Verbs of Motion**: Briefly refresh motion vs. non-motion verbs (from Modules 16-24).
2.  **Introduce Pairs**: Teach *там-де*, *туди-куди*, *звідти-звідки* as fixed magnetic pairs.
3.  **Static First**: Practice "This is the house **where** I live" (*де*).
4.  **Direction Second**: Practice "This is the city **where(to)** I want to go" (*куди*).
5.  **Origin Third**: Practice "This is the town **where** I came **from**" (*звідки*). Note that English puts "from" at the end, while Ukrainian encapsulates it in *звідки*.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (Target for B1)
*   **Relative Clauses**: Identifying and forming clauses with *де, куди, звідки*.
*   **Antecedents**: Common nouns denoting places (місце, край, сторона, дім).
*   **Correlative Pairs**: *там - де*, *туди - куди*, *звідти - звідки*.
*   **Verbs**: High-frequency verbs of position and motion.
*   **Tenses**: Present, Past, Future (all standard tenses are fair game at B1).

### OUT of Scope (Save for B2/C1)
*   **Oblique Cases of Relative Pronouns**: Complex structures involving *де* disguised as *в якому* (in which) where *де* would suffice but style dictates variety.
*   **Metaphorical Extensions**: Highly abstract uses of *звідки* in philosophical texts (though basic abstract uses like "звідки ти це знаєш?" - meaning source of information - are acceptable as set phrases).
*   **Archaic Forms**: Forms like *звідкіля* (variant of *звідки*) should be recognized but not necessarily produced.
