## Cultural Hooks

1. **Милозвучність as national identity marker.** Ukrainian textbooks (Glazova Grade 10, Karaman Grade 10) explicitly frame милозвучність as «одна з характерних рис української літературної мови» — a defining feature of Ukrainian literary language. The у/в, і/й alternations are presented as phonetic elegance unique to Ukrainian, not as arbitrary rules. This framing — Ukrainian euphony as an intentional design feature of the language — gives the B1 lesson a cultural anchor: learners are not just learning rules, they are inheriting an aesthetic tradition. Source: Grade 10 textbooks (Glazova 2018, Karaman 2018).

2. **Cluster simplification as a transparency mismatch.** Ukrainian textbooks (Litvinova Grade 5, Zabolotnyi Grade 5) treat the gap between spelling (написання) and pronunciation (вимова) as pedagogically central. The phrase «Спрощений у вимові приголосний, як правило, не пишемо» (Grade 5, Zabolotnyi) is the core rule — but crucially, there are exceptions (пестливий, хвастливий, кістлявий, шістнадцять, зап'ястний, контрастний) where the letter is retained in spelling. This exception class is an important teaching moment — "silent letter" conventions exist in Ukrainian just as in other European languages, but with clear, learnable patterns.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Cluster literalism" in спрощення** → Pronouncing [чесТний], [щасТливий] as spelled, adding the silent consonant back. Cause: L2 learners rely on grapheme-phoneme transparency; Ukrainian spelling preserves the etymological form (честь + -ний) while pronunciation drops it. Fix: minimal-pair contrast честь (full cluster) vs чесний (dropped); dictation drills from spelling to pronunciation, not the reverse.

2. **Overgeneralizing о/і alternation** → Applying стіл→столи to words that don't follow the open/closed syllable rule (e.g., treating ніж→ножа as an о/і alternation when it is actually ж/і from a different historical root, or failing to apply it where it does belong). Cause: the rule "о appears in open syllables, і in closed" is learnable, but requires correctly parsing syllable boundaries — a skill not automatic for English speakers. Fix: syllable-structure drills before the alternation rule itself.

3. **Wrong euphony choice after vowel+vowel boundaries** → Writing/saying «в Угорщині» (wrong) instead of «у Угорщині» — or conversely, «у вересні» instead of correct «у вересні» (actually correct here). The у/в rule depends on the *preceding* phonetic environment, not the following word's initial sound — a common point of confusion. Fix: present the rule as "look back, then choose," with frequent exposure to the full rule chart (preceding vowel → в; preceding consonant or pause → у; but exceptions for власні назви and іншомовні слова like Угорщина, увертюра).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Decolonized framing ready:** Glazova Grade 10 provides the exact quote framing vowel alternation as characteristic of Ukrainian and distinguishing it from other East Slavic languages — use this as the opening anchor. Do NOT use "unlike Russian" framing; instead "вирізняє українську серед інших слов'янських мов."
- **Exception class for simplification:** Four exceptions where letters are retained in spelling despite pronunciation dropping the consonant (шістнадцять, зап'ястний, контрастний, баластний) — and three words where simplification does NOT occur at all (пестливий, хвастливий, кістлявий). These must appear in the module as a discrete exception callout.
- **Syllable polysemy warning:** склад (51.48 IPM) is highly polysemous. Every use in the module must be contextually disambiguated: відкритий/закритий склад clearly signals the phonetic meaning.
- **Consonant alternation scope:** The State Standard §4.1.1 lists г/к/х → ж/ч/ш (vocative + diminutive formation) AND г/к/х → з'/ц'/с' (dative/locative of nouns) AND д/т/з/с/к/х/г → дж/ч/ж/ш/ч/ш/ж in verbs. The plan covers the first set but should mention the verb paradigm connections (link to aspect module) as a preview.
- **у/в exceptions:** У/в do NOT alternate in: власні назви starting with У/В (Угорщина, Вдовенко), іншомовні слова (увертюра, утопія), words where change alters meaning (вправа/управа, вдача/удача). These exceptions belong in the euphony section.
- **Word target:** 4000 words. Six sections with targets 500+800+800+800+600+500 = 4000. No redistribution needed — plan is well-balanced.
