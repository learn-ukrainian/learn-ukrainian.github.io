**Module**: B1 M71

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, B1 proficiency involves the ability to maintain interaction and discuss abstract topics like relationships and feelings.

> **Section: Communicative Intentions (B1)**
> "The user can initiate, maintain, and end a conversation; express their own opinion and ask for the interlocutor's opinion; express agreement or disagreement; express sympathy, advice, and support."
>
> **Grammar Competence (B1)**
> - Use of the Conditional Mood (Умовний спосіб) for polite advice (*Я б радив...*, *Тобі варто було б...*).
> - Use of Complex Sentences with *щоб* to express purpose (*Я слухаю, щоб зрозуміти*).
> - Government of verbs related to interpersonal interaction (*довіряти* + Dat, *вибачати* + Dat/Acc).

## 3. Cultural Hook

**Advice Culture ("Країна Рад")**
Ukrainian culture has historically been "high-advice." It is common for friends, family, and even strangers to give direct unsolicited advice (*Тобі треба...* "You need to...").
*   **Context for Learners**: Explain that direct advice is often seen as *caring*, not intrusive. However, modern urban culture is shifting towards more boundaries.
*   **Softening**: Teach learners how to soften advice (*Я б радив...*, *Можливо, варто...*) to sound polite but culturally competent.

**Sympathy vs. Action**
When someone shares a problem, Ukrainians often jump to *solutions* or *active help* rather than just "holding space."
*   **Phraseology**: "Тримайся!" (Hold on! / Hang in there!) is the universal phrase of support. It implies resilience and strength.

**"Виливати душу" (Pouring out one's soul)**
As noted in M64, the concept of deep, honest conversation is central to friendship. "Surface-level" friendships are often viewed as insufficient. Emotional intelligence in Ukraine implies *authenticity* (щирість) more than just "politeness."

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Common Learner Errors:**
1.  **False Friend "Sympathy"**: English *Sympathy* $
eq$ Ukrainian *Симпатія* (liking someone/attraction). The correct word for "sympathy/compassion" is **співчуття**. *Я маю до тебе симпатію* = I like you (romantically/personally). *Я тобі співчуваю* = I sympathize with you.
2.  **Forgive vs. Apologize**: Confusion between *вибачатися* (to apologize - literally "to exculpate oneself") and *вибачати* (to forgive).
    *   *Я вибачаюся* (I apologize - active).
    *   *Я вибачаю тебе* (I forgive you).
    *   *Note*: Purists argue *вибачаюся* is incorrect and prefer *прошу вибачення*, but *вибачаюся* is extremely common in spoken language.
3.  **Case Governance**:
    *   *Довіряти* (to trust) + **Dative** (кому? мені). *NOT Accusative*.
    *   *Дякувати* (to thank) + **Dative** (кому? тобі). *NOT Accusative*.
    *   *Вибачати* (to forgive) + **Dative** (person) OR **Accusative** (person - increasingly common). Standard: *Вибач мені* (Dat).

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  Start with **Active Listening**: *Так, розумію, справді?* (Minimal verbal response).
2.  Introduce **Support Phrases**: *Не хвилюйся, все буде добре, я з тобою*.
3.  Teach **Advice Patterns**: *Тобі варто...*, *Спробуй...*, *Я б на твоєму місці...*.
4.  Move to **Conflict Resolution**: *Я відчуваю..., коли ти...* ("I statements").

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (B1):**
- **Conditional Mood for Advice**: *Я б зробив...* (I would do...).
- **Imperative**: *Послухай, не кажи, зроби*.
- **Object Clauses**: *Він сказав, що...* (He said that...).
- **"I-statements"**: *Мені неприємно, коли...* (It is unpleasant to me when...).
- **Reciprocal Pronouns**: *Один одного* (each other) - introduction/practice.

**OUT OF SCOPE (Save for B2/C1):**
- **Complex Psychological Analysis**: Freud, Jung, cognitive distortions terminology.
- **Subjunctive with conjunctions**: *Якби ж то...* (If only...).
- **Dialectal/Slang conflict words**: *розбірки, наїзд* (too informal/slangy for this module).
- **Archaic forms of politeness**: *Вельмишановний...* (Highly respected...).
