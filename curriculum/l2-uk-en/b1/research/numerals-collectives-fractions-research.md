# Research: Numerals - Collectives and Fractions (B1 M49)

> **Note on Level**: The prompt indicated "A1 Level" and "Alphabet only" prior knowledge, which contradicts the module ID (B1 M49) and topic (Collective Numerals/Fractions). This research targets the **B1 Level** per the official curriculum plan (`curriculum/l2-uk-en/plans/b1/49-numerals-collectives-fractions.yaml`).

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard of Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)** (derived from the Standard for B1 level):

*   **Section**: Morphology. Numerals. (Морфологія. Числівник.)
*   **Requirement**: "Student recognizes and uses collective numerals (*двоє, троє, четверо*) and fractional numerals (*пів, половина, третина, півтора, півтори*)."
*   **Specific Rules**:
    *   **Collectives**: Usage with masculine nouns denoting persons (*двоє студентів*), nouns denoting young animals (*троє кошенят*), and pluralia tantum nouns (*четверо дверей*, *двоє окулярів*).
    *   **Fractions**: Construction of fractional expressions (Cardinal + Ordinal in Genitive). Usage of *пів* with nouns in Genitive case (*пів години*, *пів яблука*).

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency (B1)

### High Frequency (Active)
*   **Collectives (2-5 are most common)**: `двоє` (two), `троє` (three), `четверо` (four), `п'ятеро` (five).
    *   *Note*: Collectives above 7-10 are rare in speech; usually cardinal numbers are used instead.
*   **Fractions**:
    *   `половина` (half)
    *   `пів` (half - prefix/particle)
    *   `півтора` (one and a half - masc/neut), `півтори` (one and a half - fem)
    *   `третина` (a third)
    *   `чверть` (quarter)
*   **General**: `відсоток` (percent), `пара` (pair/couple), `дюжина` (dozen - receptive).

### Lower Frequency (Passive/Contextual)
*   **Higher Collectives**: `шестеро`, `семеро`, `восьмеро`, `дев'ятеро`, `десятеро`.
*   **Math terms**: `плюс`, `мінус`, `дорівнює` (equals), `дріб` (fraction).

### Common Collocations
*   **People**: `нас було троє` (there were three of us), `двоє друзів` (two friends).
*   **Plural-only objects**: `двоє дверей` (two doors), `троє окулярів` (three pairs of glasses), `двоє штанів` (two pairs of trousers).
*   **Time/Quantity**: `півтора року` (year and a half), `півтори години` (hour and a half), `одна друга` (1/2).

## 3. Cultural Hook

1.  **"Three" in Folklore**: The number three (`троє`) is archetypal in Ukrainian folklore, similar to other Slavic cultures. Tales often feature `троє братів` (three brothers) or `три спроби` (three attempts). The collective `троє` is standard when referring to a group of people in storytelling (*Було у батька троє синів...*).
2.  **"Piv" (Half) in Time**: Ukrainians heavily use `пів` + Ordinal for telling time, which learners often find confusing. *О пів на сьому* (Half to seven = 6:30). While this module focuses on quantifiers, reinforcing `пів` connects to the daily ritual of time-telling.
3.  **Market Culture**: Buying specific quantities. `Пів кіло` (half a kilo) is a standard market unit. `Десяток` (ten) is used for eggs (unlike "dozen" in the US/UK).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
*   **Restrictive Usage of Collectives**: In English, "two" works for everything. In Ukrainian, `двоє` CANNOT be used with feminine nouns (usually). You cannot say `двоє дівчат` (debated, but standard is `дві дівчини`). You strictly use it with male persons, mixed groups, children, animals, and plural-only objects.
*   **Case Governance**:
    *   Collectives govern the **Genitive Plural**: `двоє братів` (NOT `брати`).
    *   Fractions govern specific cases: `півтора` + Genitive Singular (*півтора метра*), `півтори` + Genitive Singular (*півтори тонни*).
*   **Pluralia Tantum**: Learners struggle with "two pairs of scissors". In Ukrainian, you must use the collective: `двоє ножиць`.

### Common Learner Errors
*   Using collectives with feminine nouns: *двоє жінок* (Incorrect -> *дві жінки*).
*   Wrong case after fractions: *півтора годин* (Incorrect -> *півтора години*).
*   Confusing `півтора` (m/n) and `півтори` (f).

### Teaching Sequence
1.  **Concept**: Introduce "Group Concept" (The "Gang" of numbers).
2.  **Form**: Show patterns `дв-оє`, `тр-оє`, `четв-еро`.
3.  **Usage Rule 1**: "The Men & Mixed Group" (Students, brothers, friends).
4.  **Usage Rule 2**: "The Little Ones" (Kittens, children).
5.  **Usage Rule 3**: "The Weird Plurals" (Doors, glasses, scissors).
6.  **Fractions**: Focus on `пів`, `півтора/півтори` first as they are high frequency. Then formal fractions (`одна п'ята`).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1)
*   Collective numerals 2-10 (*двоє* through *десятеро*).
*   Case governance of collectives (Genitive Plural).
*   Using collectives with masculine persons, mixed groups, young animals/children.
*   Using collectives with *pluralia tantum* nouns (doors, scissors).
*   Basic fractions: `половина`, `третина`, `чверть`.
*   `Півтора/півтори` usage.
*   Percentages: `50 відсотків` (Genitive Plural governance).

### OUT of Scope (Save for B2/C1)
*   Declension of collective numerals (changing `двоє` to `двох`, `двом` etc. is advanced; stick to Nominative/Accusative mostly for B1, or simple Genitive contexts).
*   Complex mathematical fractions (*три цілих і чотирнадцять сотих*).
*   Stylistic nuances of collectives with inanimate objects (rare/poetic).
*   Archaic forms like `обоє` (both - acceptable but `обидва` is more common).
