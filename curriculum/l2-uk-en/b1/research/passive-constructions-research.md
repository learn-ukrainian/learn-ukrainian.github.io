# Research: Passive Constructions (B1 M47)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

This module aligns with the **B1 (Середній рівень першого ступеня)** requirements of the *Ukrainian State Standard 2024*, specifically regarding complex simple sentences and voice.

> **§ 4.4.2. Просте ускладнене речення**
> "просте речення з дієприкметниковим зворотом: Три години тому ми повернулися з екскурсії, **організованої для учасників конференції**;"
> *(Simple sentence with a participial phrase)*

> **§ 4.2.4.1. Дієслово**
> Reflexive verbs (-ся) are introduced earlier (A2/B1) and are essential for forming the present passive (e.g., *будинок будується*).

While the "Full System" of passive voice is often consolidated at B2 (§ 4.4.1.1 in the B2 catalog explicitly lists passive constructions), B1 requires the ability to understand and use participial phrases and impersonal forms (like *-но/-то*), which are high-frequency in the target text types for this level (news, basic reports).

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

At the B1 level, passive vocabulary centers on "process" and "result" verbs.

**High Frequency (Core):**
- **Verbs of Creation/Completion:** *зроблено* (done), *відкрито* (opened), *зачинено* (closed), *написано* (written), *збудовано* (built).
- **Official Status:** *заборонено* (forbidden), *дозволено* (allowed).
- **Metalanguage:** *пасивний стан* (passive voice), *активний стан* (active voice), *суб’єкт* (subject), *об’єкт* (object).

**Common Collocations:**
- *Ким це зроблено?* (By whom was this done?)
- *Магазин відкрито / зачинено.* (Store is open / closed.)
- *Ця книжка написана відомим автором.* (This book is written by a famous author.)
- *Будинок будується.* (The house is being built.)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**The "Bureaucratic Passive" (Oficijno-dilovyj styl):**
Ukrainian official culture leans heavily on impersonal passive forms (*-но/-то*) to sound objective and authoritative. Unlike English, which might say "We closed the store," a Ukrainian sign will say "**Магазин зачинено**" (The store [is] closed-result). This reflects a cultural preference for focusing on the *result* or *state* rather than the specific person responsible, especially in public spaces.

**Historical Plaques:**
Walking through Kyiv or Lviv, students will constantly see plaques starting with: "**У цьому будинку жив...**" (active) but also "**Цей пам'ятник встановлено...**" (This monument was established...). The passive voice is the voice of history and permanence in the urban landscape.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Key Differences from English:**
1.  **Three Passives, One Goal:** English mostly uses "to be + V3". Ukrainian has three distinct tools:
    -   **Reflexive (-ся):** Focus on process ("Building is *building itself*").
    -   **Participle (Short -но/-то):** Focus on result/impersonal ("It is done").
    -   **Participle (Adjectival):** Focus on description ("The *written* letter").
2.  **The Agent Problem:** In English, "by him" is standard. In Ukrainian, the agent (Instrumental case) is possible but often awkward or avoided in favor of the active voice. *Only teach the Instrumental agent with caution.*

**Common Learner Errors:**
-   Overusing *бути* as an auxiliary in the present tense (e.g., saying *Я є зроблений* instead of *Це зроблено*).
-   Confusing the "Reflexive Passive" with "Reflexive Action" (e.g., *Я миюся* = I wash myself, vs. *Вікно миється* = The window is being washed).

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  Start with **Result** (*-но/-то*) – easiest to grasp as "status" (open/closed).
2.  Move to **Process** (*-ся*) – connect to known reflexive verbs.
3.  End with **Full Participles** – connecting to gender agreement.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**Clarification on Prerequisites:**
*Note: The task prompt listed "A1 / Alphabet Only" as prerequisites. This module (B1 M47) is functionally impossible for a student with only alphabet knowledge. The scope below assumes a student entering B1.*

**IN SCOPE (B1):**
-   **Forms:** Impersonal *-но/-то* forms (result); Reflexive *-ся* verbs (process); Passive Participles (short and long).
-   **Tenses:** Past (perfective passive), Present (imperfective reflexive).
-   **Cases:** Instrumental case for the agent (passive construction agent).

**OUT OF SCOPE (Deferred to B2/C1):**
-   **Complex Archaic Passives:** Rare forms found in religious or poetic texts.
-   **Stylistic Nuances of Agent:** Detailed stylistics on when *not* to use the passive agent (Instrumental) vs Active voice is a C1 editing skill. At B1, we just teach *how* to form it.
-   **Action vs. State ambiguity:** Deep linguistic analysis of aspectual pairs in passive.
