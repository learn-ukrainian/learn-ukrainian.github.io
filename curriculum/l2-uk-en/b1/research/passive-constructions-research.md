## State Standard Reference

**§4.2.3.1** (lines 2269–2291): Дійсний спосіб — covers verb conjugation in all tenses, aspect pairs, and is the anchor for participial forms (дієприкметник/дієприслівник) at B1. The B1 mapping description explicitly includes "participles (дієприкметник), gerunds (дієприслівник)" under this section. Verbatim from Standard:
> *"дієвідмінювання дієслів у теперішньому часі: запрошувати... митися..."* — reflexive conjugation is modelled from the start.

**§4.4.2** (lines 2367–2378): Просте ускладнене речення — directly names the participial phrase construction:
> *"просте речення з дієприкметниковим зворотом: Три години тому ми повернулися з екскурсії, організованої для учасників конференції"*

**Note on -но/-то scope**: The State Standard gives -но/-то full systematic treatment at B2 (§4.3.3 one-member sentences, lines 3409–3418). At B1, this construction is introduced as part of §4.4.2 complex-simple sentences. The plan is deliberately front-loading the complete system at B1 (module 48, phase B1.4a Participles) — pedagogically justified as synthesis after the two preceding participle modules (b1-46/47), though the formal standard anchoring is B2.

**Alignment**: This module synthesises §4.2.3.1 (participial forms) + §4.4.2 (participial phrases in sentences) + B1 stylistics §4.5.1 (formal vs informal register), with preview of B2 §4.3.3 scope.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Канцелярит as Soviet linguistic legacy** (Wikipedia verified): *"Канцелярит — особлива мова (жаргон) канцелярських працівників (бюрократів), що представляє собою спотворену модифікацію літературної мови, що базується на канцеляризмах."* Ukrainian linguists (notably Олександр Авраменко and Оксана Глазова in Grade 11 textbooks) treat канцелярит eradication as part of linguistic decolonization — Soviet bureaucratic Ukrainian was systematically saturated with passive constructions calqued from Russian officialese. The prescriptive Ukrainian norm explicitly prefers active voice.

2. **Textbook prescriptive stance** (Глазова, Grade 11, verified): *"Українська мова уникає пасивних конструкцій. Тому перевагу, де це можливо, слід надавати дієсловам активного стану."* This is a direct, learner-facing cultural signal that the passive in Ukrainian is marked — it carries register weight, and native-sounding Ukrainian defaults to active constructions far more than English does. The contrast is particularly vivid in public signage: «Вхід заборонено» (fact-stating -но/-то, normal) vs. «Вхід є забороненим» (adjectival passive, stilted) vs. «Вхід заборонений» (adjectival, acceptable in context).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Agent + -но/-то construction**: `*Мною було зроблено` → Correct: `Я зробив` або просто `Зроблено` — happens because English passive freely takes agents ("It was done by me"), and because Russian also permits this structure. Ukrainian -но/-то is categorically agentless: it states a fact/result with no performer.

2. **Process/result confusion (-ся vs -но/-то)**: `двері зачиняються` (ongoing process, habitual) ≠ `двері зачинено` (completed result, current state as fact). English conflates both as "the door is closed/closing." Minimal pair drills are critical: *Магазин зачиняється о 18:00* (closes at 6pm daily) vs. *Магазин зачинено* (it is closed — the result is here now).

3. **Overusing participle passive in spoken register**: `Лист написаний мною` sounds bookish/unnatural in conversation; native speakers say `Я написав листа`. The adjectival passive (written/closed/locked as property) is for describing states of things, not reporting actions — learners calque English "the letter was written by me" directly into Ukrainian participial passive.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **The -но/-то frequency advantage**: Lead with the high-frequency argument — зроблено (31.51 ipm) and заборонено (22.56 ipm) are among the most common verb forms learners see in real life. This motivates the grammar.
- **Three-column contrast table** will be essential: Adjectival / -но/-то / -ся passive — same base verb (зачинити/зачиняти), three different surface forms, three distinct pragmatic functions.
- **Авраменко's naturalness test** (Grade 11 textbook, verified): The side-by-side "which sounds more natural?" observation exercise is a proven pedagogical entry point — adapt this format for the diagnostic section.
- **Глазова's reflexive verb caveat** (verified): Distinguish true reflexive (-ся meaning self-directed, reciprocal, benefactive) from passive -ся — students confuse «дитина вмивається» (reflexive: washes herself) with «рецепт читається учнями» (passive: is being read). These are formally identical but functionally opposite.
- **Decolonized framing**: Frame канцелярит as a documented sociolinguistic phenomenon in Ukrainian (not as "unlike Russian") — Ukrainian has its own native preference for active voice, codified in textbooks and State Standard stylistics §4.5.1.
- **Register ladder for dialogues**: Official announcement → formal document → conversational speech. -но/-то is natural in official signage; adjectival passive suits descriptions; -ся passive fits process descriptions; active voice dominates everyday speech.
- **Suffix formation reminder** (Заболотний, Gr.11): -н- (from -а- stem), -ен-/-єн- (from consonant/-и-/-і- stem), -т- (monosyllabic verbs) — brief recap needed since b1-046 covered this; link back without repeating full drills.
