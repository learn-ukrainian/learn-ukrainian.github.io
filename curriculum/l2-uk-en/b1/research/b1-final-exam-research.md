# Research: B1 Final Exam (Фінальний іспит B1)

**Level**: B1 (Intermediate)
**Module**: B1 M92
**Topic**: Comprehensive B1 Level Assessment

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

The relevant standard is **«Українська мова як іноземна. Рівні загального володіння А1 – С2»**, approved by the National Commission on State Language Standards on **August 29, 2024 (Decision No. 279)**.

**Section for B1 (Independent User / Рубіжний рівень):**
> "Кандидат розпізнає та використовує граматично правильні мовні форми для вираження власних намірів у межах визначених тематичних контекстів. Знання граматики може бути спорадичним, однак демонструється розуміння основних морфологічних та синтаксичних категорій (родові форми, відмінювання, дієвідмінювання, часи, узгодження)."

The standard defines B1 competence through three catalogs:
*   **Catalog A**: Communicative Intentions (asking/giving info, expressing opinion).
*   **Catalog B**: Thematic Range (Daily life, Work, Travel, Current events).
*   **Catalog C**: Linguistic Competence (Grammar inventory).

## 3. Cultural Hook

**The State Certificate (Державний сертифікат):**
In Ukraine, passing a B1/B2 level exam is not just academic—it is often a legal requirement. The **National Commission on State Language Standards** administers exams for:
1.  **Citizenship**: Foreigners seeking Ukrainian citizenship must pass the exam (typically B1+ level required).
2.  **Public Office**: Civil servants (derzhsluzhbovtsi) must certify their fluency (C1/C2).

**Exam Culture**:
Ukrainian exams traditionally emphasize **grammatical precision** (correct endings) more heavily than some Western communicative exams. A "spelling mistake" in an ending (e.g., *пишу ручком* instead of *ручкою*) is often penalized strictly as a grammar error.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Key Differences from English:**
*   **Testing Focus**: Unlike English exams which may accept "understandable" errors, Ukrainian B1 assessments rigorously test **Case Government** (Keruvannia) and **Verbal Aspect** (Vyd).
*   **Structure**: The exam includes a distinct "Grammar" section (lexico-grammatical test), whereas modern English exams often integrate grammar into reading/writing.

**Common Learner Errors at B1:**
*   **Aspect Confusion**: Using Imperfective for a completed action in the past (*Я писав тест і здав його* vs *Я написав тест...*).
*   **Motion Verbs**: Confusing unidirectional (*йти*) and multidirectional (*ходити*) in narrative contexts.
*   **Complex Syntax**: Incorrect use of linking words (*тому що* vs *через те, що*).
*   **Stress Panic**: Freezing when encountering unknown vocabulary in Reading texts instead of deducing meaning from context.

**Teaching Sequence Recommendations:**
1.  **Review Strategies**: Teach students to identify keywords in questions *before* reading the text.
2.  **Time Management**: Drill the concept of "moving on" if a question is too hard.
3.  **Error Checking**: Encourage checking written work specifically for "Agreement" (Gender/Number) and "Government" (Case endings) before submitting.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (Expected Knowledge for B1 Exam):**
*   **Cases**: All 7 cases for Nouns, Adjectives, and Pronouns (Singular & Plural).
*   **Verbs**: Present, Past, Future (Perfective/Imperfective).
*   **Aspect**: Clear distinction in usage (Process vs Result, Repeated vs One-time).
*   **Motion Verbs**: Base pairs (іти/ходити, їхати/їздити) + Basic prefixes (при-, пі-, ви-, за-).
*   **Syntax**: Complex sentences with *який*, *що*, *коли*, *якщо*, *тому що*.
*   **Topics**: Biography, Plans, Description of events, Expressing simple opinions.

**OUT OF SCOPE (Do NOT Test):**
*   **Participles**: Active/Passive Participles (used as adjectives is okay, but not constructing complex participle phrases - this is B2).
*   **Gerunds (Diyepryslivnyk)**: Active usage is B2+. Passive recognition is borderline B1.
*   **Stylistics**: Archaic words, Dialects, or highly specific Professional Jargon.
*   **Nuanced Modality**: Subtle shades of "might have been" (Subjunctive beyond simple "би").
