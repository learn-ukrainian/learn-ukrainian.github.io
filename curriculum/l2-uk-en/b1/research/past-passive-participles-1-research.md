# Research: Past Passive Participles (Пасивні дієприкметники минулого часу)

**Module**: B1 M45
**Topic**: Past Passive Participles (-ний/-тий suffixes)
**Level**: B1 (Intermediate)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard of Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, Level B1 candidates are expected to master the formation and usage of participles.

> **Section: Морфологія. Дієслово (Morphology. Verb)**
> "Кандидат уміє утворювати та вживати: ...дієприкметники пасивного стану минулого часу (форми на -ний, -тий); узгоджувати їх з іменниками у роді, числі та відмінку; використовувати їх у ролі означення та частини складеного іменного присудка."
> *(Candidate can form and use: ...past passive participles (forms ending in -ний, -тий); agree them with nouns in gender, number, and case; use them as attributes and parts of a compound nominal predicate.)*

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

At the B1 level, passive participles act functionally as adjectives. Mastery of high-frequency forms is prioritized over the ability to generate them from every possible verb.

### High-Frequency Items (Must Know)
These are used daily, often as fixed attributes or states.

*   **відкритий / закритий (відчинений / зачинений)** — open / closed (extremely common for signs, doors, shops).
*   **зроблений** — done/made.
*   **написаний** — written.
*   **зайнятий** — occupied/busy (e.g., "Це місце зайняте?").
*   **одружений** — married (specifically: "married man" or general status, literally "encircled/wifed").
*   **стомлений** — tired (often functions as an adjective "fatigued").

### Common Collocations (A2/B1 Bridge)
*   *Зачинені двері* (Closed doors)
*   *Зроблена робота* (Done work)
*   *Забуті речі* (Forgotten things)
*   *Приготована вечеря* (Prepared dinner)
*   *Розбите серце* (Broken heart) - classic idiomatic usage of -тий suffix.

## 3. Cultural Hook

### "Тіні забутих предків" (Shadows of Forgotten Ancestors)
The title of Mykhailo Kotsiubynsky's masterpiece (and Parajanov's film) perfectly illustrates the grammar:
*   **Забутих** (Forgotten) — Past Passive Participle, Plural, Genitive Case.
*   It modifies "Ancestors" (Предків).
*   *Significance:* A cornerstone of Ukrainian magical realism and cinema.

### "Розстріляне відродження" (The Executed Renaissance)
A historical term referring to the generation of Ukrainian artists and writers destroyed by Stalin in the 1930s.
*   **Розстріляне** (Executed/Shot) — Past Passive Participle, Neuter, Nominative.
*   It describes the "Renaissance" (Відродження - neuter noun).
*   *Significance:* Essential historical context for B1/B2 cultural literacy.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
1.  **Agreement:** Unlike English "done" or "broken" (invariant), Ukrainian participles **change** like adjectives (червоний -> зроблений). They must agree in Gender, Number, and Case with the noun.
    *   *The letter is written* -> Лист написан**ий**.
    *   *The book is written* -> Книга написан**а**.
2.  **Transitivity Requirement:** You can only form these from **transitive** verbs (verbs that take a direct object). You cannot make a passive participle from "to sleep" or "to go".

### Common Learner Errors
*   **Confusion with Adjectives:** Treating them as pure adjectives and failing to recognize their verbal governance (e.g., trying to use them with objects in the wrong case).
*   **Suffix Confusion:** Using -ний where -тий is required (e.g., "миний" instead of "митий").
    *   *Rule of thumb:* Monosyllabic stems (ми-, би-, пи-) often take -тий.
*   **Calquing English Passive:** "Я є народжений" vs "Я народився". While "Я народжений" (I am born) exists, usage differs slightly from English passive voice.

### Teaching Sequence
1.  Start with **Attributive Use** (as adjectives): "Це *зачинений* магазин." (This is a closed shop).
2.  Move to **Predicative Use** (states): "Магазин *зачинений*." (The shop is closed).
3.  Introduce **Creation** rules:
    *   Infinitives in -ати -> -аний (писати -> написаний).
    *   Infinitives in -ити -> -ений (робити -> зроблений).
    *   Monosyllabics -> -тий (пити -> випитий).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1 Level)
*   **Formation:** Standard suffixes -ний, -ений, -тий from common transitive verbs.
*   **Declension:** Full adjectival declension (Hard group).
*   **Short Forms:** Mentioning that short forms (написан, зроблен) are **archaic/dialectal** in Ukrainian and should be avoided (unlike Russian where they are mandatory for predicates). Use full forms for predicates: "Він зроблений" (It is done).
*   **Usage:** Describing results of actions (Resultative).

### OUT of Scope (Save for B2/C1)
*   **Active Participles:** (robliachiy/pishovshiy) - stylistically controversial and often replaced by clauses.
*   **Verbal Adverbs (Дієприслівники):** (robliachy/zrobyvshy) - distinctly different category ("doing/having done").
*   **Complex consonant mutations:** in formation (beyond basic с/ш, д/дж), unless high frequency.
*   **Passive Voice Constructions with "було":** "Книгу було прочитано" (Impersonal passive) - this is a separate B1/B2 topic (Forms in -но/-то). Focus strictly on the *adjectival* participle here.
