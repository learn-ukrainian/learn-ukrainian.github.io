## Cultural Hooks

1. **«Зроблено в Україні»** — Національний бренд товарів українського виробництва (торгівельна марка, кампанія підтримки виробників). Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Зроблено_в_Україні. A living, high-profile example of a passive participle (-но form) anchored in Ukrainian national identity — ideal for the §5 lesson on -но/-то forms. The phrase appears on product labels across Ukraine, making it instantly relatable.

2. **Standard §4.4.2 example**: "екскурсія, організована для учасників конференції" — already built into the State Standard, suggesting textbook authors treat conference/cultural tourism as the canonical participial context at B1. This anchors the module in familiar academic-civic settings.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Agreement failure** → *Лист написана* → **Лист написаний** — Learners match the participle to a perceived generic "feminine" form rather than agreeing with the actual noun gender. Particularly common when the sentence topic noun comes before the participle (predicative use).

2. **-мий trap (systematic Russian interference)** → *любимий, бажаний, відомий* treated as passive participles → replace with **улюблений**, **бажаний** (legitimate but check meaning), **відомий** (is a genuine adjective, not a participle). The form *любимий* is a direct calque of Russian любимый; Ukrainian uses **улюблений**. Source: Avramenko Gr.11 §18, which explicitly contrasts natural vs unnatural Ukrainian forms.

3. **Applying passive participles to intransitive/reflexive verbs** → *\*розмовлений, \*ходжений* (intransitive — no passive possible); *\*розвиненийся* (reflexive — cannot form passive). Root cause: learners transfer the rule "verb → participle" without filtering for transitivity. Textbook rule (Glazova Gr.11): "утворюються від основи неозначеної форми **перехідних** дієслів."

4. **Suffix confusion: -н- vs -ен-** → *\*читаний* (wrong; stem ends in consonant after dropping infinitive ending -ти → **читан-** → needs -ений → **читаний** — actually this one IS correct). More problematic: *\*садений* → **саджений** (missing the [д//дж] alternation). Formation rules require attention to what follows the stem vowel drop.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Frequency asymmetry** is the most important finding: impersonal -но/-то forms (zроблено 31.51 IPM) swamp the adjectival forms (зроблений 7.13 IPM). Phase B content should state this clearly with numbers rather than just asserting "formal contexts only."
- **прочитаний** (0.26 IPM) is extremely rare in corpus — good for practice but not a high-frequency production target. Consider foregrounding **відкритий** (15.40) and **зроблений** (7.13) as the highest-priority production forms.
- **Decolonized framing**: The plan correctly identifies -мий forms as Russian interference. Phase B should frame this as "Ukrainian has developed its own system" — note that Ukrainian built the -ений/-ний/-тий distinction around its own phonological and morphological logic, which is distinct from how Polish, Portuguese, and other inflected languages handle this.
- **Alternation patterns** ([д//дж], [с//ш]) are a continuation of the euphony thread from A1 (§4.1.7). Connecting these explicitly will help learners see them as part of a broader system, not isolated exceptions.
- The §4.4.2 canonical example ("екскурсії, організованої для учасників конференції") uses the **genitive case** agreement, which is a non-trivial agreement case. Phase B should include agreement drills in genitive, not just nominative.
