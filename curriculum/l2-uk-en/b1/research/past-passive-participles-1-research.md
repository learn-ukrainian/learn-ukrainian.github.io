## Cultural Hooks
1. **«Зроблено в Україні» (Made in Ukraine)**: A highly prevalent cultural and commercial phrase, especially post-2014, used to promote domestic products and economic resilience. It perfectly illustrates the -но/-то phenomenon derived from passive participles.
2. **Action vs. State**: Ukrainian culture strongly prefers expressing active agency. Using the passive voice with an agent ("Книга написана мною") sounds unnatural and bureaucratic; passive forms are reserved almost exclusively for emphasizing the *result* or *state* of the object, not the actor.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Agreement Failure**: "Лист написана" instead of "Лист написаний". Learners often forget that passive participles function as full adjectives and must agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.
2. **The "-мий" Trap (Russian Interference)**: Creating pseudo-participles like "любимий" (beloved) or "читаємий" (readable). Ukrainian firmly rejects these Russian-style present passive participles, preferring adjectives like "улюблений" or descriptive clauses.
3. **Overusing the Agentive Passive**: Saying "Обід приготований мамою" instead of the natural active "Мама приготувала обід".

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize that Ukrainian uses participles primarily for states and results, actively avoiding the Russian tendency to use them with instrumental agents. Highlight the unique -но/-то impersonal forms as a distinct feature of Ukrainian syntax.
- **Immersion**: B1.1+ requires 100% Ukrainian immersion. All grammar explanations must be in Ukrainian. Do not use English in parentheses.
- **Word Target**: Expand on the euphony/alternation rules and syntactic word order to comfortably meet the 4000-word target.
