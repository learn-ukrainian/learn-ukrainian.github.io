## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

### High Frequency (Core B1)
*   **бізнес** (business) - Ubiquitous. Used in collocations: *малий бізнес* (small business), *робити бізнес* (to do business).
*   **компанія** (company) - Generic term for any business entity.
*   **робота** (work/job) - Basic but essential foundation.
*   **гроші** (money) - Essential.
*   **клієнт** (client/customer) - Standard in all service industries.
*   **директор** (director/manager) - Common title.

### Medium Frequency (Topic Specific)
*   **підприємство** (enterprise/undertaking) - Slightly more formal than *компанія*, often refers to manufacturing or larger state entities.
*   **прибуток** (profit) - Key financial term.
*   **збитки** (losses) - Antonym to profit.
*   **постачальник** (supplier) - Essential for supply chain discussions.
*   **інвестиція** (investment) - Internationalism, easily recognized.
*   **ринок** (market) - Both physical market and abstract economic market.

### Common Collocations
*   **відкрити справу/бізнес** - to open a business (case/business).
*   **підписати контракт/договір** - to sign a contract.
*   **отримати прибуток** - to receive/make a profit.
*   **працювати на себе** - to work for oneself (be self-employed).
*   **шукати інвесторів** - to look for investors.

## 3. Cultural Hook

*   **The "FOP" Phenomenon (ФОП)**: A crucial cultural concept in modern Ukraine is the **ФОП** (*Фізична особа-підприємець* - Individual Entrepreneur). It is the standard legal status for most freelancers, IT specialists, and small business owners. Understanding "ФОП" is key to understanding the Ukrainian middle class and IT sector.
*   **IT Sector Influence**: Ukraine is a major global IT hub. Business vocabulary often heavily borrows from English ("мітинг", "дедлайн", "офер"), but standard Ukrainian terms are preferred in formal documentation and polished speech. Using pure Ukrainian equivalents (*нарада* instead of *мітинг*) signals high education and respect for the language.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

*   **False Friends & Nuances**:
    *   **Компанія** vs **Фірма**: *Фірма* can sound slightly dated or colloquial/small-scale compared to *компанія* in modern corporate speak, though both are used.
    *   **Економіка** vs **Економія**: *Економіка* is "economy" (the system), *економія* is "saving/thriftiness".
*   **Terminology Confusion**:
    *   Learners often confuse **робітний** (working - rare/specific) with **робочий** (working - e.g., day, hour). *Робочий день* (work day), not *робітний день*.
*   **Formal Address**: Business requires the strict use of **Ви** (formal You) and the Vocative case for names (*Пане Андрію*, *Пані Олено*), which should be reinforced even if colleagues switch to *Ти* later.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1 Level)
*   **Topic Vocabulary**: General business structure, profit/loss, employment, basic negotiation.
*   **Grammar**:
    *   **Future Tense**: Essential for planning (*ми підпишемо*, *компанія буде розвиватися*).
    *   **Conditional**: Hypotheticals (*якщо ми інвестуємо...*).
    *   **Aspect (Perfective/Imperfective)**: Vital for "signing" (completed) vs "negotiating" (process).
    *   **Numerals (Money)**: Complex case government with large numbers (thousands, millions).

### OUT Scope (To Avoid)
*   **Specialized Legal Jargon**: Terms like *юридична особа*, *оподаткування* (taxation details), *статутний капітал* are too complex for general B1 business overview.
*   **Complex Financial Instruments**: *Акції*, *облігації*, *дивіденди* (unless very surface level).
*   **Bureaucratic Style**: Avoid the heavy "kancelaryt" style of official state documents. Stick to modern, clear business Ukrainian.
