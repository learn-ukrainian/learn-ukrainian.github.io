# Research: Дієприслівники доконаного виду (Perfective Adverbial Participles)

**Module**: B1 M43
**Level**: B1 (Intermediate)
**Topic**: Formation and usage of perfective adverbial participles (-вши/-ши)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (Level B1)**, the learner is expected to master unchanging verb forms, specifically the Adverbial Participle (*Дієприслівник*).

**Relevant Section (Morphology - Verb):**
> "Учень розуміє та вживає дієприслівники доконаного виду для вираження додаткової дії, що передує основній дії дієслова-предиката."
> *(The student understands and uses perfective adverbial participles to express an additional action that precedes the main action of the predicate verb.)*

The standard emphasizes the **temporal relationship** (anteriority) – the action expressed by the participle must be completed *before* the main action starts (e.g., *Прочитавши книгу, він віддав її другу* – Having read the book, he gave it to a friend).

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

At the B1 level, students should focus on forming participles from high-frequency perfective verbs used in daily narratives.

**High-Frequency Forms (Core A):**
These forms appear frequently in spoken and written narratives:

| Infinitive (Perfective) | Participle (-вши/-ши) | Translation | Frequency |
| ----------------------- | --------------------- | ----------- | --------- |
| **зробити** | **зробивши** | having done | High |
| **сказати** | **сказавши** | having said | High |
| **побачити** | **побачивши** | having seen | High |
| **прийти** | **прийшовши** | having come/arrived | High |
| **почути** | **почувши** | having heard | High |
| **прочитати** | **прочитавши** | having read | Med |
| **написати** | **написавши** | having written | Med |
| **дізнатися** | **дізнавшись** | having found out | Med |
| **повернутися** | **повернувшись** | having returned | Med |

**Common Collocations:**
- *Подумавши, він відповів...* (Having thought, he answered...)
- *Недовго думаючи...* (Without thinking long... - though typically imperfective, idiomatic usage often blends)
- *Зробивши паузу...* (Having made a pause...)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**The Virtue of Conciseness**
Ukrainian discourse often favors conciseness. Instead of using a full subordinate clause (*Коли він зробив роботу, він пішов додому*), a native speaker often condenses this information using a participle (*Зробивши роботу, він пішов додому*). This structure makes the narrative flow faster and highlights the sequence of events more elegantly.

**Proverbs & Wisdom:**
- **"Зробивши діло — гуляй сміло."**
  *(Having done the deed, walk [party/relax] boldly.)*
  This is the quintessential example of the perfective participle. It encapsulates the Ukrainian work ethic: complete your obligations first, then enjoy your leisure time without guilt.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Key Differences from English:**
- **Register:** In English, "Having done..." is often formal or literary. In Ukrainian, *зробивши* is standard and neutral, used widely in both conversation and writing.
- **Subject Requirement:** The "Same Subject Rule" is strict. The doer of the participle action MUST be the same as the doer of the main verb.

**Common Learner Errors:**
1.  **Dangling Participle (Subject Mismatch):**
    *   *Error:* *Читаючи книгу, задзвонив телефон.* (Reading the book, the phone rang.)
    *   *Correction:* *Коли я читав книгу, задзвонив телефон.* (Because "phone" cannot read).
2.  **Wrong Suffix:** Confusing *-ючи* (imperfective) with *-вши* (perfective).
    *   *Error:* *Роблячи домашнє завдання, я пішов гуляти.* (Doing homework [simultaneously], I went for a walk.)
    *   *Correction:* *Зробивши домашнє завдання, я пішов гуляти.* (Having done homework [finished first], I went for a walk.)
3.  **Stress Placement:** Stress usually remains on the same syllable as in the past tense masculine form (*зроб**и**в* -> *зроб**и**вши*), but learners often shift it.

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  Review **Past Tense Masculine** forms (students know this from A2).
2.  Formula: Take Past Masculine (*читав*), remove *-в*, add *-вши* (*читавши*).
    *   *Note:* For consonant stems (rare in perfective), usage is *-ши* (e.g., *приніс* -> *принісши*), but sticking to the "Past stem + suffix" rule usually works for the vast majority of regular verbs.
3.  Practice transforming "Коли..." sentences into participle phrases.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**CRITICAL NOTE ON PREREQUISITES:**
The provided task constraint ("Student knows alphabet only") is incompatible with this module. This module **requires** B1-level knowledge of:
- **Verbal Aspect** (Perfective vs. Imperfective) - Covered in B1 M06-15.
- **Past Tense** - Covered in A2.
Without these, forming *Дієприслівники доконаного виду* is impossible. The scope below assumes these prerequisites.

**IN SCOPE:**
- **Formation:** Suffixes **-вши** (standard) and **-ши** (after consonants).
- **Reflexive Forms:** Suffix **-шись** (e.g., *повернувшись*).
- **Usage:** Expressing anteriority (completed action before main action).
- **Transformation:** Converting *Коли + Past Perfective* clauses into participle phrases.
- **Negative Forms:** *Не + participle* (written separately: *не зробивши*).

**OUT OF SCOPE:**
- **Imperfective Participles:** (*читаючи*, *роблячи*) - Covered in B1 M42.
- **Active Adjectival Participles:** (*читаючий*, *зробивший*) - These are adjectival, declinable, and controversial/less common in standard Ukrainian.
- **Passive Participles:** (*прочитаний*, *зроблений*) - Covered in B1 M45-46.
- **Complex Literary Constructions:** Archaic forms or highly stylized poetic usage.
