**Alignment**: Module b1-051 addresses both §4.2.3.1 (morphological formation of verb-derived forms) and §4.4.2 (use of дієприслівниковий зворот in просте ускладнене речення). The State Standard's model sentence uses a perfective adverbial participle (*Повернувшись*) — exactly the form this module teaches.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **State Standard model sentence** (primary hook): «*Повернувшись з екскурсії, ми смачно пообідали в ресторані*» — the official B1 example from §4.4.2 uses a perfective adverbial participle. This anchors the module directly to the standard and gives an immediately memorable, lived example.

2. **Proverb «Далеко сховавши, близько не знайдеш»** — frequency data confirms *сховавши* exists in corpus (1,278 occurrences). The proverb is authentic Ukrainian folk wisdom; Wikipedia's article on Дієприслівник confirms it as a canonical form (*міркувавши, писавши, читавши* listed as examples). Proverb is pedagogically sound as an introduction to the concept of попередність.

3. **Wikipedia definition** (source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Дієприслівник): «невідмінювана форма дієслова, яка, пояснюючи присудок, називає додаткову дію. Відповідає на питання *що зробивши?*» — the perfective question form is explicitly *що зробивши?*, not *що роблячи?*. This question-form distinction is a clean pedagogical hook.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Dangling participle — subject mismatch** → «*Прийшовши додому, почався дощ*» ❌ → «*Прийшовши додому, я побачив дощ*» ✅  
   *Why*: Ukrainian grammar requires that the subject of the participial phrase is identical to the subject of the main verb. Learners transfer L1 English patterns where dangling participles are common in informal speech. Textbook (Avramenko, Grade 7) uses «*Фотографуючи, супутник літає навколо Землі*» as the classic error model — the satellite cannot be taking photos.

2. **Using imperfective (-ючи) for completed sequential actions** → «*Читаючи книгу, я дізнався правду*» ❌ (implies simultaneous) → «*Прочитавши книгу, я дізнався правду*» ✅ (completed first, then learned)  
   *Why*: Learners often default to the more familiar imperfective form. The aspectual distinction between simultaneous process (-ючи) and completed prior action (-вши) is not yet intuitive at B1.

3. **Confusion with active participle form (-ший)** → «*сказавший*», «*привівша*» ❌ — Glazova (Grade 11) explicitly labels these as «грубе порушення мовної норми». Learners occasionally construct a hybrid form mixing the -ший active participle suffix with past-tense stems.  
   *Why*: Cross-contamination from the active participle paradigm (taught in adjacent modules b1-45, b1-50). Must distinguish: adverbial participle → *зробивши* (indeclinable); active participle → *зроблений* (declinable adjective form).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Frequency priority for examples**: Lead with `побачивши` (15 IPM) — the most frequent form in real Ukrainian text. Use it as the canonical first example. `зробивши` (5.45 IPM) and `повернувшись` (5.25 IPM) are the second tier.
- **State Standard sentence is gold**: «*Повернувшись з екскурсії, ми смачно пообідали в ресторані*» — use this as the introductory model sentence. It is officially sanctioned, B1-appropriate, and uses a reflexive form (*повернувшись*), which demonstrates the -вши + -сь pattern.
- **Textbook source**: Litvinova Grade 7 (tier 1) and Avramenko Grade 7 (tier 1) both dedicate a full chapter (§21 in Litvinova, §54–55 in Avramenko) to дієприслівник. This topic is taught at Grade 7 in Ukrainian schools — B1 learners are working at native Grade 7 level for this grammar point.
- **The -ши vs -вши rule**: Litvinova explicitly notes the phonological conditioning — *занісши* → [зан'íш:и], *завізши* → [зав'íжши] — assimilation in consonant-stem forms. This is worth a sidebar note in the morphology section.
- **Immersion**: Module is B1.4a — full Ukrainian immersion required (M06+). No English in prose. English only in vocabulary table translations.
- **Word count**: Target 4,000 words. Plan allocates: Вступ 500 + Граматика 900 + Помилки 1,000 + Лексика 1,000 + Практика 600 = 4,000. This is tight; recommend 5,500–6,000 word draft to ensure post-editing meets minimum.
