# Дослідження: Рух: початок і повернення

## State Standard Reference
§4.3.8: "Утворення префіксальних дієслів руху: іти – прийти, перейти, дійти, відійти, зайти, вийти; їхати – приїхати, переїхати, доїхати, від’їхати, заїхати, поїхати, виїхати, об’їхати."
Alignment: This module directly addresses the B1 State Standard requirement to master prefixed verbs of motion, focusing specifically on spatial semantics of initiation (`по-`), visiting/deviation (`за-`), and dispersion (`роз-`) for both pedestrian (`іти`) and vehicular (`їхати`) movement.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **«Присісти на дорогу»**: A traditional pre-departure ritual performed before one is ready to `поїхати`. Originally rooted in folklore (appeasing the "домовик" or house spirit so it wouldn't follow the traveler), it functions today as a crucial psychological pause to gather thoughts and transition into a travel mindset.
2. **«На коня»**: The traditional final toast before guests `розходяться` (disperse). Historically the "stirrup cup" for Cossacks departing on horseback, it is now a staple of Ukrainian hospitality marking the definitive end of a social gathering.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Overuse of «увійти» for social visits** → Correct form: `зайти`. Learners often literally translate "enter" as formal `увійти` (physical entry) instead of using the natural, socially appropriate `зайти до друга` (to drop by/visit).
2. **Ignoring transport distinctions for long distances** → Correct form: `поїхати до Львова`. Learners frequently say `піти до Львова` (literally walking to Lviv), failing to apply the strict `по-` + vehicle root distinction required for intercity travel.
3. **Preposition mismatch with «зайти»** → Confusing `зайти в кімнату` (enter), `зайти за кут` (go behind), and the critical social phrase `зайти за другом` (pick up a friend).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Frame Ukrainian motion prefixes as an elegant, logical system of spatial geometry. Emphasize active Ukrainian agency ("Ми вирішили поїхати"). Avoid comparisons to Russian prefixed verbs; establish the Ukrainian system internally.
- **Immersion**: 100% Ukrainian. No English scaffolding in the prose. Use explicit formatting (tables, Mermaid flowcharts) to clarify the `іти` vs `їхати` split and prefix logic.
- **Tone**: Professional, analytical, but grounded in real-world urban and social contexts.
