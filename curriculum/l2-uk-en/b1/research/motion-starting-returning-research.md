## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

**Section:** Annex 2.2. Grammar Competence (Level B1)
**Topic:** Дієслово (Verb) — Verbs of Motion with Prefixes
**Standard Citation:**
> "The learner distinguishes and uses prefixed verbs of motion to express the start of an action (*піти*, *поїхати*), movement with a brief stop (*зайти*, *заїхати*), and multidirectional dispersal (*розійтися*, *роз’їхатися*)."

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

### High Frequency (Core A1/A2 Review + B1 Focus)
*   **піти** (to go/set off on foot) — *Very High*. Used constantly to initiate movement (e.g., "Я пішов додому").
*   **поїхати** (to go/set off by vehicle) — *Very High*.
*   **зайти** (to enter; to stop by) — *High*. Frequent in both "come in" (generic) and "stop by" (social) contexts.

### Medium Frequency (B1 Target)
*   **заїхати** (to drive in; to stop by vehicle) — *Medium*. Common for logistics/travel ("We will stop by the gas station").
*   **розійтися** (to disperse/split up) — *Medium*. Used for endings of events ("After the concert, everyone dispersed").
*   **зупинка** (stop/halt) — *High*.
*   **напрямок** (direction) — *Medium*.

### Common Collocations
*   *Піти додому* (To set off for home)
*   *Зайти в гості* (To drop in for a visit)
*   *Зайти на каву* (To stop by for coffee)
*   *Поїхати на море* (To set off for the sea)
*   *Наші шляхи розійшлися* (Our paths diverged - idiomatic)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**"Заходь!" (Come in / Drop by)**
In Ukrainian culture, the concept of *зайти* (stopping by) is deeply embedded in hospitality. Unlike some Western cultures where visits are strictly scheduled, Ukrainian "visiting culture" (*гостини*) often involves spontaneous or semi-spontaneous dropping in. The phrase "Заходь на каву/чай" is a standard friendly closer, implying a low-pressure invitation to visit briefly.

**"На коня" (For the horse)**
When guests *start to leave* (*збираються йти*), there is a cultural tradition called "на коня" — a final drink or toast at the door before departure. While the guests *вже пішли* (have technically started leaving), the departure process can stretch out, highlighting the transition from *бути* (being there) to *піти* (setting off).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
*   **Multi-functionality of Prefixes:** English uses phrasal verbs ("set off", "drop by", "break up"). Ukrainian encodes this directionality/aspect into the prefix (*по-*, *за-*, *роз-*).
*   **The "Start" vs. "Process" Confusion:** Learners often confuse *йти* (process: "I am walking") with *піти* (initiation: "I have started walking" / "I am leaving").
    *   *Error:* "Я йду в кіно завтра" (Process - acceptable as future plan) vs "Я пішов" (Result/Start - "I'm off/I'm leaving now").
*   **Transitivity with *За-***: *Зайти* often takes *до* + Genitive (person) or *в/на* + Accusative (place), similar to other motion verbs, but the "short duration" meaning is context-dependent.

### Teaching Sequence Recommendations
1.  **Contrast *Йти* vs. *Піти*:** Solidify that *по-* denotes the *start* of the motion or the intent to leave.
2.  **Introduce *За-***: Frame it as a detour or a short visit. Use the map analogy: Point A -> Stopover (За-) -> Point B.
3.  **Introduce *Роз-***: Use visuals of fireworks or a crowd leaving a stadium. Use the reflexive *ся* marker as a cue for "self/each other" (they moved themselves apart).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (For this module)
*   **Prefixes:** *по-* (start), *за-* (visit/stop), *роз-* (disperse).
*   **Stems:** *ходити/йти*, *їздити/їхати* (and their fused forms like *піти*, *розійтися*).
*   **Aspect:** Focus on the Perfective meaning (completed act of starting/stopping).
*   **Cases:**
    *   *Accusative* (Destinations: *в парк*, *на роботу*).
    *   *Genitive* (Persons/Origins: *до друзів*).

### OUT of Scope (Saved for other modules)
*   **Complex Prefixes:** *пере-* (crossing - Module 18), *до-* (reaching limit), *об-* (circumnavigating).
*   **Figurative Meanings:** *Сонце зайшло* (The sun set), *Час пішов* (Time started). Keep strictly to physical motion for now to avoid overload.
*   **Imperative Nuances:** While *Пішли!* (Let's go!) is common, complex imperative formations should be limited to set phrases.
