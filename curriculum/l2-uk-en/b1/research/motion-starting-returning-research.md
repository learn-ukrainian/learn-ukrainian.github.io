# Дослідження: Рух: початок і повернення

## State Standard Reference

§4.3.8: "Утворення префіксальних дієслів руху: іти – прийти, перейти, дійти, відійти, **зайти, вийти**; їхати – приїхати, переїхати, доїхати, від'їхати, **заїхати, поїхати**, виїхати, об'їхати."
*(Source: UKRAINIAN-STATE-STANDARD-2024.txt, lines 2336–2338)*

**Alignment note**: The Standard's §4.3.8 enumerates the full paradigm of prefixed motion verbs but does not group them by semantic category. The module imposes a pedagogically motivated 3-cluster grouping: **по-** (starting movement), **за-** (stopping by / entering), **роз-** (dispersing). This grouping is consistent with the Standard's scope — поїхати, зайти, заїхати, вийти are all explicitly listed. The роз- dispersal cluster (розійтися, роз'їхатися) is a natural extension of the same paradigm, semantically justified as the terminal counterpart to по- initiation. No fabrication: the Standard lists the lexemes; the semantic framing is pedagogical design.

**Important gap**: The Standard §4.3.8 explicitly lists по- only in поїхати and виїхати — it does not frame по- as a dedicated "starting movement" prefix at the §4.3.8 level. The broader semantics of по- as inceptive are part of the aspect/prefix system covered in §4.2.3.1 (indicative, aspect in all tenses). Content writing should ground the по- starting-movement explanation in the aspect system (perfective pairs like йти → піти mark inception of action) rather than claiming the Standard explicitly codifies it as a separate rule.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Маршрутне таксі (маршрутка)** — Wikipedia verified: "Маршрутне таксі або «маршрутка» — один із видів комерційного громадського транспорту, що здійснює перевезення пасажирів на міському чи приміському автобусному маршруті загального користування за розкладом руху... з висадкою та посадкою пасажирів чи громадян на їхню вимогу." The маршрутка is explicitly identified as a near-universal feature of large and medium cities in Ukraine. This validates the diagnostic scenario (boarding a full маршрутка) as authentic Ukrainian urban context. The driver-passenger dynamic (shouting "виходиш?" / "заходьте!") is a lived daily-life situation for most Ukrainian urban dwellers.

2. **"Спочатку виходять, потім заходять"** — A social norm on Ukrainian public transport (buses, trams, маршрутки, metro). This is not Wikipedia-sourced as a distinct article but is culturally salient and behaviorally verified: violation of this norm is a recognized social faux pas in Ukrainian city life. It directly encodes the вийти/зайти pair in a memorable, authentic context. Use as a mnemonic rule — the transport etiquette maps precisely onto the grammar.

3. **"Присісти на дорогу" (sitting before a journey)** — Wikipedia search did not return a dedicated article. The tradition is real (pre-departure, everyone sits briefly in silence before leaving the home) but the pedagogical framing should be attributed to Ukrainian folk custom generally, not cited with a specific Wikipedia source. Treat as cultural context, not a sourced fact. Possible origin note: the practice is widespread across Slavic cultures and is linked to folk beliefs about travel safety; in Ukrainian context it emphasizes the gravity of departure (піти / поїхати as a threshold moment).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Overuse of увійти for social visits** → Correct: зайти до друга / зайти в гості
   — Why: English speakers map "enter" → увійти for all entry contexts. Ukrainian distinguishes formal/physical entry (увійти до зали) from casual stopping by (зайти до сусіди). Using увійти in social contexts sounds overly formal or bureaucratic.

2. **піти до Львова for long-distance travel** → Correct: поїхати до Львова
   — Why: піти implies foot travel. English has no systematic foot/vehicle distinction in "go to"; Ukrainian encodes it lexically. Learners overgeneralize піти as the default perfective of "go."

3. **Preposition confusion: зайти в vs. зайти за** → These are minimal pairs with distinct meanings:
   - зайти **в** магазин = enter the shop
   - зайти **за** ріг = go behind the corner / go round the corner
   - зайти **за** кимось = pick someone up on foot (collect them)
   — Why: "за" is polysemous; learners who know "за" as "behind" (locative) fail to recognize its directional/purposive uses.

4. **Confusion of прийти (arrival, expected) vs. зайти (drop in, unannounced or casual)**
   — Why: Both translate as "come" in English. прийти на вечерю = coming to dinner (invited, purposeful); зайти на хвилинку = pop in briefly. The distinction maps onto register and intentionality, not physical direction.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Aspect framing for по-**: The content should explain по- as forming the perfective of motion verbs (йти → піти, бігти → побігти), where the prefix shifts the verb from ongoing action to the moment of departure/initiation. This is accurate Ukrainian grammar and sidesteps any framing that resembles Russian aspectual pedagogy.
- **роз- spelling rule**: As confirmed by Grade 6 Litvinova textbook (GRAC result 4): роз- always written with з, even before voiceless consonants in writing (though devoiced in speech: ро[сп]овісти). Content should mention this only if the spelling is a natural teaching moment — it's not the focus of this module.
- **Register layering**: заскочити (0.74 IPM) is low-frequency and colloquial. Mark explicitly as розмовний стиль so learners understand it is not neutral register.
- **Collocation "розійтися по"**: GRAC confirms freq=4,002 for the "розійтися + по" pattern, validating "розійтися по домівках" as authentic. Also productive for metaphorical use: думки розійшлися (opinions diverged) — worth one example.
- **Diagnostic scenario**: The маршрутка scene is culturally grounded. Useful verbs for the dialogue: заскочити в кіоск, вийти на зупинці, зайти через задні двері — all VESUM-verified.
- **Transport etiquette rule as mnemonic**: "Спочатку виходять, потім заходять" encodes the вийти/зайти perfective pair in a socially memorable context. Structure the cultural section around this rule.
