# Дослідження: Вид у минулому: результат і процес

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.1: "утворення форм роду і числа дієслів минулого часу недоконаного і доконаного виду: хотів, хотіла, хотіло, хотіли; побачив, побачила, побачило, побачили"
Alignment: This module explicitly targets the distinction between imperfective (хотів) and perfective (побачив) forms in the past tense, focusing on the semantic difference between process and result as required by the standard's indicative mood specification.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **"Зробив діло — гуляй сміло"**: This classic proverb perfectly encapsulates the **Perfective (Result)** aspect. It emphasizes that leisure is justified only *after* the completion of an action.
2. **"Тихіше їдеш — далі будеш"**: Represents the **Imperfective (Process)** focus. The emphasis is on the manner and duration of the journey rather than just the arrival.
3. **The "Toloka" Tradition**: Historically, huge communal work parties (toloka) were focused on the *result* (building a house in a day), but the social aspect was a shared *process*. This contrast can be used to explain: "Ми будували хату (process, all day) і збудували її (result, by sunset)."

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Process for Result**: "Я писав тест" (I was writing) instead of "Я написав тест" (I wrote/finished) when reporting an achievement.
   *   *Correction*: Focus on the outcome marker ("написав").
   *   *Why*: Learners transfer English "I wrote" (Simple Past) to Imperfective, not realizing Simple Past can be Perfective in Ukrainian.
2. **Overuse of "був"**: "Я був ходив" instead of "Я ходив".
   *   *Correction*: Remove auxiliary.
   *   *Why*: Attempting to mirror English "was walking" or "had gone".
3. **Gender Mismatch**: "Я зробив" (said by a female student).
   *   *Correction*: "Я зробила".
   *   *Why*: Phonetic oversight or over-reliance on the dictionary form.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Persona Flavor**: Use the **Crime Scene Investigator** persona as hinted in the plan ("Detective" logic). Analyze "evidence" of completed actions (perfective) vs "traces" of ongoing actions (imperfective).
- **Visuals**: Use a timeline metaphor. Process = a wavy line with no end point visible. Result = a point or a checkmark on the timeline.
- **Context**: In the "Work Report" dialogue, emphasize that bosses usually ask for Perfective (What did you *accomplish*?), while friends might ask for Imperfective (What were you *doing* all day?).
- **Immersion**: Ensure all grammar explanations are 100% Ukrainian. Use clear, simple terms like «дія завершена» vs «дія тривала».
