## Cultural Hooks
1. **Толока (Toloka)**: A traditional Ukrainian form of mutual voluntary aid where the community gathers to execute large-scale work (e.g., building a house). It perfectly encapsulates the grammar point: the shared, ongoing community effort (process: "ми будували") culminating in a tangible achievement and feast (result: "ми збудували").
2. **Proverbs of Action**: «Зробив діло — гуляй сміло» (Leisure is justified only by the *result* / perfective aspect) vs. «Тихіше їдеш — далі будеш» (Focus on the *process* and manner of action / imperfective aspect).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Process for Result (Transfer Error)**: English speakers rely on context ("I wrote the test" meaning it's finished). In Ukrainian, using "Я писав тест" implies the ongoing process without guaranteeing completion. Learners must actively switch to "Я написав тест" to report a result.
2. **False Auxiliary "був"**: Mimicking English past continuous/perfect ("I was doing / I had done") by saying "Я був робив". This must be corrected to the standard synthetic past tense ("Я робив").
3. **Gender Mismatch**: Phonetic blurring where female learners use the masculine "-в" ending ("Я зробив") instead of "-ла".

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize Ukrainian as an independent system with its own logical elegance regarding aspect. Do not use Russian as a comparative baseline. Use the active voice to emphasize Ukrainian agency ("Ми збудували", not "Було збудовано").
- **Immersion**: B1.1 requires 100% Ukrainian immersion in prose. Ensure all explanations and metalanguage are in Ukrainian.
