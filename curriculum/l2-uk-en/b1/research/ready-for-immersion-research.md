## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Forbidden Letter 'Ґ'**: Banned in 1933 during the Soviet orthography reform ("The Skrypnykivka" purge) as "nationalistic." Reinstated only in 1990. Its return is a symbol of reclaiming Ukrainian identity and *правопис* (orthography).
2. **"Слово — не горобець, вилетить — не спіймаєш"**: A classic proverb emphasizing precision and responsibility in speech, relevant to the module's focus on accuracy and metalanguage.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Letter vs. Sound**: Confusing *буква* (letter) and *звук* (sound).
   *   *Error*: Thinking "щ" is two letters or "дз" is two sounds.
   *   *Correction*: "Щ" = 1 letter, 2 sounds ([шч]). "Дз" = 2 letters, 1 sound ([dz]).
2. **False Friends**: Confusing *правило* (grammar rule) with *право* (legal right).
   *   *Error*: "Я маю правило це робити" (incorrect for "I have the right").
   *   *Correction*: *Правило* is for regulation/grammar; *право* is for entitlement/law.
3. **Stress Shift**: Ignoring *наголос* which changes meaning.
   *   *Example*: *пла́чу* (I am crying) vs. *плачу́* (I am paying).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Tone Shift**: This module is the "airlock." It must explicitly warn learners that the "English safety net" is being removed in the next module (M06). Use the "Immersion Warning" section to frame metalanguage as their survival kit.
- **Pedagogy**: When introducing *вид* (aspect), do NOT teach the full grammar yet. Teach the *labels* `доконаний` and `недоконаний` so they can recognize them in dictionaries and rules.
- **Visuals**: Use a "Tree of Speech" (`Частини мови`) analogy to categorize the 10 parts of speech (Self-standing vs. Service parts).
