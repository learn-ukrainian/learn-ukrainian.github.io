## Cultural Hooks

1. **The 1933 Orthography Purge and Letter Ґ** (Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia — Харківський правопис): The Kharkiv Orthography (Скрипниківка), adopted in 1928 after the All-Ukrainian Orthographic Conference of 1927, was rewritten in 1933 by a commission under A. Khvylya, which declared the 1928 norms "nationalist." The letter Ґ was eliminated in 1933 *"без жодних обговорень"* (without any discussion) as "nationalist." Most linguists who drafted the 1928 code were subsequently repressed by the Stalinist regime (Krymsʹky, Kuryla, Synyavsʹky, Hnatsov, and others). The letter was restored at the start of the 1990s. This is documented, verifiable, and directly relevant to the правопис vocabulary and the module's identity/resilience framing.

2. **Харківський правопис and the Diaspora** (Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia): The 1928 Kharkiv Orthography continues to be used by much of the Ukrainian diaspora — including the oldest Ukrainian-language newspaper *Svoboda* — meaning students who encounter historical or diaspora Ukrainian texts may see Ґ used differently. The 2019 edition of Ukrainian Orthography restored several elements of the 1928 code. This creates a living connection: the "forbidden letter" is not just history but an ongoing orthographic debate.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **буква vs звук** → Learners conflate written letters with spoken sounds. Example: the letter *я* is ONE letter but TWO sounds [j] + [a]; the letter *щ* is ONE letter but a cluster [ʃtʃ]. Drills should use: «Скільки звуків у слові «яма»?» (4 sounds, not 3 letters). Root cause: L1 English conflates letter-names with sounds in spelling.

2. **Stress-shifting minimal pairs**: пла́чу (I am crying, < плакати) vs плачу́ (I am paying, < платити). Also: за́мок (castle) vs замо́к (lock), о́рган (organ/body part) vs орга́н (musical organ/institution). Root cause: English stress is relatively fixed within words; learners do not expect lexical-semantic stress to shift between paradigm forms.

3. **правило (rule/norm) vs право (right/entitlement/law)**: Learners say «У мене є правило робити це» when they mean *«У мене є право»*. Root cause: These are partial false friends across European languages (правило resembles "rule" semantically but право = "right"). The confusion is compounded because both words appear in academic texts.

4. **Verb label reading**: Learners encountering «дієслово доконаного виду» in a Ukrainian grammar text assume «доконаний» relates to «закінчення» (ending) rather than «вид» (aspect). Explicit alignment with the labels seen in dictionary notation (нд, д) is needed.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **The "airlock" metaphor is pedagogically sound** — use it explicitly and early. The transition from B1.0 (English scaffold) to B1.1+ (full Ukrainian) is the module's structural purpose; the tone should be warm but firm.

2. **Proverb «Слово — не горобець, вилетить — не спіймаєш»**: This is an authentic Ukrainian proverb. Learners should encounter it first in Ukrainian, then unpack it — do NOT pre-translate. The metalanguage analysis of a Ukrainian proverb is itself a demonstration of the module's goal.

3. **Parts of speech taxonomy**: Ukrainian school grammar recognizes 10 parts of speech (частин мови): іменник, прикметник, числівник, займенник, дієслово, прислівник (самостійні — 6), + прийменник, сполучник, частка, вигук (службові/вигук — 4). The "tree" analogy (коріння = самостійні, гілки = службові) maps cleanly onto the Litvinova Grade 6 textbook definition.

4. **Minimal pair drills**: пла́чу/плачу́ are both real paradigm forms from different verbs — ensure the context sentences are unambiguous (avoid context that could be read either way without stress marking).

5. **Abbreviations scope**: The §4.1.5 list (вул., м, кг, 1-й, об 11 год) is the canonical B1 set. The fill-in-the-blank activity should use именно these institutional abbreviations (ООН, ЗСУ, вул., пор., напр., тощо, ін., с., ст.) — not invented ones.

6. **Russicism watch**: The vocabulary list is clean. Watch for: «буква» is correct (not Russian «буква» used as a false cognate — they are identical, fine). «Значення» (not «смисл» — the latter is acceptable but «значення» is the standard academic term). Avoid «правильно/неправильно» as sole evaluative frame — prefer «відповідно до норми / поза нормою».

7. **Decolonized framing for the Ґ section**: Frame the 1933 purge as Soviet linguistic repression — not "simplification" or "standardization." The letter's restoration in the 1990s is part of Ukrainian linguistic sovereignty. Ukrainian Ґ represents a distinct phoneme [ɡ] in native and borrowed vocabulary (ґанок, ґрунт, ґудзик, Ґонта) — present these as authentic Ukrainian words, not exceptions.
