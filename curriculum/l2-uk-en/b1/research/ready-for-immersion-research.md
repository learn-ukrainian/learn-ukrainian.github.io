## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

This module aligns with the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a State Language (2024)** regarding linguistic competence at the B1 level. Specifically, it addresses the ability to use language resources to discuss language itself (metalinguistic function).

> **Section II. Linguistic Competence (B1):**
> "The user understands sentences and frequently used expressions related to areas of most immediate relevance... Can describe in simple terms aspects of his/her background, immediate environment and matters in areas of immediate need."
>
> *Specific alignment with Metalanguage:*
> "Здатність усвідомлювати структуру мови, розпізнавати граматичні категорії та використовувати термінологічний апарат для обговорення мовних явищ у процесі навчання." (Ability to realize the structure of language, recognize grammatical categories, and use terminological apparatus to discuss linguistic phenomena during the learning process.)

*Note: The standard emphasizes "conscious learning" (свідоме навчання), where the learner acts as an active analyzer of the language.*

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

This module focuses on the "Language of Learning".

**High Frequency (Essential for daily classwork):**
*   **слово** (word)
*   **речення** (sentence)
*   **значення** (meaning)
*   **приклад** (example)
*   **помилка** (mistake)
*   **правило** (rule)
*   **вправа** (exercise)

**Mid Frequency (Grammar specific):**
*   **іменник** (noun)
*   **дієслово** (verb)
*   **прикметник** (adjective)
*   **рід** (gender) — *Collocations*: чоловічий/жіночий/середній рід.
*   **число** (number) — *Collocations*: однина/множина.
*   **час** (tense) — *Collocations*: теперішній/минулий/майбутній час.

**Lower Frequency (Passive recognition recommended):**
*   **відмінювання** (declension)
*   **дієвідмінювання** (conjugation)
*   **виняток** (exception) — Critical concept, even if the word is less frequent in basic speech.
*   **наголос** (stress/accent)

**Common Collocations:**
*   *Наведіть приклад* (Give an example)
*   *Яке значення цього слова?* (What is the meaning of this word?)
*   *Зробити помилку* (To make a mistake)
*   *Виняток з правила* (Exception to the rule)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**The "Literary Language" Ideal (Літературна мова):**
Ukrainian culture places a high value on *literary norms* (літературна норма). Unlike some Anglophone cultures where "usage dictates rule", in Ukraine, the "Slovnyk" (Dictionary) and "Pravopys" (Orthography) are viewed as authorities.
*   **Fact**: The "Radio Dictation of National Unity" (Радіодиктант національної єдності) is a massive annual holiday where heavily immersed Ukrainians, from schoolchildren to the President, write a dictation to test their grammar and spelling. It celebrates *correctness* and *literacy* as acts of patriotism.
*   **Cultural Context**: Discussing "correct Ukrainian" vs. "Surzhyk" (mixed Ukr-Ru) is a national pastime. Knowing metalanguage allows the learner to participate in these frequent cultural conversations about "how we should speak."

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Differences from English:**
*   **Aspect vs. Tense**: English speakers obsess over Tense (12+ forms). Ukrainian focuses on Aspect (Perfective/Imperfective). The terminology (*доконаний/недоконаний вид*) is crucial because the concept doesn't map 1:1.
*   **Case Anxiety**: English speakers lack a mental category for "Case" (*Відмінок*). They often treat endings as optional decorations. The metalanguage must reinforce that *Ending = Function*.

**Common Learner Errors:**
*   Confusing **звук** (sound) and **буква** (letter).
*   Using "tense" logic for aspect (e.g., trying to find "continuous" markers).
*   Ignoring **наголос** (stress), which can change meaning (e.g., *плачу* - I pay vs. *плачу* - I cry).

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  **Labeling**: Ensure they can tag words in a sentence (Noun, Verb).
2.  **Functional Analysis**: "What is this word doing?" (Subject, Object).
3.  **Rule Reading**: Guided reading of a simple dictionary definition or grammar rule in Ukrainian.
4.  **Meta-Communication**: Roleplay asking for clarification ("How do you spell...?", "Is this correct?").

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN Scope (B1.0 Bridge):**
*   **10 Parts of Speech**: Recognition and basic labeling.
*   **Sentence Parts**: Subject (*підмет*), Predicate (*присудок*).
*   **Basic Phonetics**: Vowels/Consonants, Stress.
*   **Instructional Verbs**: *читайте, пишіть, з'єднайте, виберіть* (imperatives used in tasks).
*   **Question Words**: *Хто? Що? Який? Чий?* (as tools for analysis).

**OUT Scope (Too Advanced):**
*   **Complex Syntax Trees**: Clauses within clauses (keep analysis to simple/compound sentences).
*   **Phonology**: Phonemes, allophones, palatalization mechanics (unless practical).
*   **Stylistics**: Registers, archaisms, dialects (wait for B2/C1).
*   **Obscure Exceptions**: Focus on the *system*, not the weird outliers (e.g., heteroclite nouns).
