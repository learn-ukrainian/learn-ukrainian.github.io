# Дослідження: Синонімія I: Дієслова мислення

## State Standard Reference
§4.5.1.1: "Стилістичні засоби лексики: [...] синоніми"
Alignment: This module directly addresses lexical stylistics and synonymy differentiation, teaching learners to select the appropriate "thinking verb" based on register and context. It also fulfills §3.1 "Людина" (риси характеру, взаємини з іншими людьми) by giving learners tools to express opinions and cognitive states accurately.

## Cultural Hooks
1. Taras Shevchenko's "Думи мої, думи мої...": The concept of "дума" is deeply ingrained in Ukrainian culture, representing not just a passing thought, but a profound, often sorrowful reflection on national destiny and identity.
2. René Descartes' "Я мислю, отже я існую" (Cogito, ergo sum): Connects to the highest register verb "мислити", illustrating the philosophical depth and abstract reasoning available in Ukrainian vocabulary.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Я рахую, що..." → "Я вважаю, що...": This is a pervasive calque from Russian ("считать"). In Ukrainian, "рахувати" is strictly used for mathematics and money.
2. "Циганка гадала мені" → "Циганка ворожила мені": Learners confuse the mental act of supposing ("гадати") with fortune-telling ("ворожити").
3. "Думати над тебе" → "Думати про тебе": Preposition mismatch. "Над" + instrumental is for intellectual effort over a problem; "про" + accusative is for general subjects.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Treat "вважати" as the authentic Ukrainian standard for opinion, directly debunking the Russified "рахувати" as an unnatural intrusion.
- English is strictly forbidden in the prose (100% immersion for B1.6). Only use it in the vocabulary table translations.
- Emphasize the active agency of the Ukrainian speaker when formulating thoughts and opinions.
