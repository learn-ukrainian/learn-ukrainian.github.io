## Cultural Hooks

1. **Шевченко «Думи мої, думи мої» (1840)** — Verified via Wikipedia (uk.wikipedia.org). This poem is the lyrical introduction to *Кобзар* (1840) — «літературний маніфест молодого автора, який представляє загалові свою першу книжку, а з нею — свій голос як національного поета України.» The word *думи* functions simultaneously as "thoughts" (cognitive noun) and the folk genre of *duma* (epic lyric) — making it a perfect entry point into the semantic depth of the thinking-verb family. Note: a second poem with the same title (1847) also exists; use the 1840 version for the module.

2. **Рене Декарт — «Я мислю, отже я існую»** — Verified via Wikipedia (uk.wikipedia.org). Декарт described as «автор методу радикального сумніву в філософії» — the Cogito is his most famous formulation. The Ukrainian translation uses *мислити* (not *думати*), which naturally anchors the distinction between the abstract philosophical verb (*мислити*) and its everyday counterpart (*думати*). This is authentic and pedagogically productive.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Я рахую, що...»** → **«Я вважаю, що...»** — Calque from Russian *я считаю, что*. In Ukrainian, *рахувати* is strictly numerical/monetary. Grade 6 Avramenko textbook (tier 1) explicitly names калькування as a lexical error class. This is the central error-correction focus of Section 3.

2. **«Гадати» confused with fortune-telling** → Correct: «ворожити» for divination — *гадати* = mental supposition (я гадаю = I suppose/guess); fortune-telling = *ворожити* (ворожити на картах, ворожити долю). The confusion arises from folk expressions where the boundary between "guessing" and "foretelling" blurs; the module must draw this line explicitly.

3. **«Мислити» as generic synonym for «думати»** → *мислити* is elevated/philosophical (suitable for Descartes, intellectual discourse); in everyday speech it sounds unnatural where *думати* is expected. Learners over-apply it after encountering the Cogito.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Frequency gap is pedagogically significant:** *думати* (38 IPM) and *вважати* (36 IPM) dominate; *міркувати* (2 IPM) and *гадати* (2.4 IPM) are genuinely lower-register. Content should reflect this asymmetry — these are not equal-frequency alternatives.
- **«Осягати» is very rare (0.22 IPM)** — treat as literary/advanced flourish in Section 4 only; do not present as a productive B1 item.
- **«Мислити» vs «думати» distinction** is the most philosophically interesting point but must be kept grounded — one focused paragraph, not an extended digression.
- **Decolonized framing:** Present the *рахувати* error as a *calque* (калькування) — a general category of lexical borrowing — without framing Ukrainian as "correcting Russian influence." The textbook (Avramenko gr.6) already uses this neutral framing; follow it.
- **Шевченко section (Section 4):** The word *думи* bridges cognitive and cultural meaning organically. Avoid over-explaining — let the polysemy emerge from the text analysis itself.
- **B1.6 immersion rule is in effect (module 58 >> module 6):** Zero English in prose, headings, callouts. English only in the vocabulary table translation column.
