## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Nuance

The core challenge for English speakers is that "to think" maps to multiple distinct Ukrainian verbs.

### High Frequency (Core)
1.  **Думати** (imperfective) / **Подумати** (perfective)
    *   *Meaning:* General mental process, cogitation.
    *   *Collocations:* *Думати про майбутнє* (think about future), *довго думати* (think for a long time).
2.  **Вважати** (imperf.)
    *   *Meaning:* To hold an opinion, to consider, to believe (that).
    *   *Crucial Distinction:* Used when stating an opinion ("I think that...").
    *   *Collocations:* *Я вважаю, що...* (I believe that...), *вважати за необхідне* (to consider necessary).
3.  **Розуміти** (imperf.) / **Зрозуміти** (perf.)
    *   *Meaning:* To understand.
    *   *Collocations:* *Розуміти причину* (understand the reason), *правильно зрозуміти* (understand correctly).

### Medium Frequency (Nuance)
4.  **Мислити**
    *   *Meaning:* To reason, to possess the faculty of thought (higher register/intellectual).
    *   *Collocations:* *Мислити логічно* (think logically), *мислити критично* (think critically), *мислити образами* (think in images).
5.  **Міркувати**
    *   *Meaning:* To ponder, to reflect, to deliberate (process of weighing options).
    *   *Collocations:* *Міркувати над проблемою* (ponder a problem), *міркувати вголос* (think aloud).
6.  **Гадати**
    *   *Meaning:* To suppose, to guess, to imagine.
    *   *Note:* Often poetic or colloquial.
    *   *Collocations:* *Гадаю, що так* (I guess so).

## 3. Cultural Hook

*   **Taras Shevchenko's "Dumy":** The most famous poem beginning in Ukrainian literature is Shevchenko's *"Думи мої, думи мої, лихо мені з вами!"* (My thoughts, my thoughts, woe is me with you!). Here, *думи* refers to deep, heavy, sorrowful reflections, distinct from fleeting thoughts (*думки*). This highlights the Ukrainian cultural weight placed on deep contemplation.
*   **The "Thought" vs. "Opinion" Divide:** In Ukrainian discourse, separating the *process* (*думати*) from the *conviction* (*вважати*) is linguistically enforced. You cannot say "Я думаю, що це погано" as naturally as "Я вважаю, що це погано" when making a judgment call.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Common Learner Errors
*   **The "Think" Trap:** Using *думати* for opinions.
    *   *Error:* "Я думаю, що це добрий план." (Grammatically possible but often sounds like "I am in the process of thinking that...").
    *   *Correction:* "Я вважаю, що це добрий план." (I consider/believe this is a good plan).
*   **False Friends (Russian interference):**
    *   *Гадати* vs *Ворожити*. In Russian, *гадать* is primarily "to tell fortunes". In Ukrainian, *ворожити* is "to tell fortunes", while *гадати* is "to suppose/think". However, due to interference, some speakers avoid *гадати* to avoid ambiguity.
*   **Government (Case usage):**
    *   *Думати про* + Accusative (thinking *about* something).
    *   *Міркувати над* + Instrumental (pondering *over* something).

### Teaching Sequence
1.  **Contrast:** Introduce *Думати* vs. *Вважати* immediately. Use "Process vs. Opinion" labels.
2.  **Context:** Teach *Мислити* via adverbs (*логічно, критично, позитивно*).
3.  **Expansion:** Introduce *Міркувати* for problem-solving contexts.

## 5. Scope Boundaries (B1 Level)

### IN Scope
*   **Tenses:** All tenses (Past, Present, Future).
*   **Aspect:** Contrast between process (*думати*) and result (*подумати*, *ви,гадати*).
*   **Syntax:** Complex sentences with *що* (that).
*   **Cases:**
    *   Accusative (*про кого/що*).
    *   Instrumental (*над ким/чим*).

### OUT of Scope (Save for B2/C1)
*   **Archaic/Poetic Synonyms:** *Мнитися*, *банувати* (in sense of thinking/regretting).
*   **Complex Phraseology:** Idioms like *сушити голову* (to rack one's brains) - leave for B2.
*   **Passive Constructions:** *Думка була висловлена...* (The thought was expressed...) - B2 Passive Voice.
