## Cultural Hooks

1. **Феномен неологізму кіберзахист** — Ukraine's digital defense infrastructure since 2022 generated a burst of Ukrainian-language compound neologisms (кіберзахист, кіберборець, кіберармія). This demonstrates основоскладання as a living, productive process in contemporary Ukrainian — not a dusty grammar rule. Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (неологізм entry verified).

2. **Летовище vs аеропорт** — The word летовище (from летіти + -овище) is a native Ukrainian compound that competes with the international borrowing аеропорт. The Ukrainian term appears in official aviation documents and cultural contexts emphasizing the language's ability to form technical vocabulary from native roots — a direct counterpoint to historical pressure to borrow from Russian. Verified in VESUM (3 matches) and textbooks.

3. **Хлібосольство** — This compound (хліб + сіль + -ство) encodes the entire Ukrainian cultural concept of hospitality in a single morphologically transparent word. Unique to Ukrainian; has no exact Russian equivalent as a single lexical unit. Excellent demonstration of how основоскладання creates culturally-specific meaning.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **не- prefix (attached) vs не particle (separate)** → Learners write `не вдача` instead of `невдача`, and also the reverse: `недобре` instead of `не добре` (adverb + predicate). Rule: не- forms one word with nouns/adjectives when they form an antonymous meaning (невдача = failure, not just "not success"). Error source: English has no equivalent word-level vs. phrase-level distinction for negation.

2. **Suffix -ств(о) vs -цтв(о) vs -зтв(о)** → Learners write `мистерство` instead of `мистецтво`, or `мастерство` (direct Russianism). The conditioned alternation (base consonant determines suffix allomorph) is opaque without explicit instruction. Also: братство vs братерство (both exist with different registers).

3. **Connecting vowel о vs е in compounds** → Learners apply о universally (writing `теплехід` or `землеoтрус`). Rule: е/є after soft consonants and certain stems (землетрус ← земля + трус; but теплохід ← тепло + хід). Grade 6 Avramenko textbook covers this explicitly (verified via RAG).

4. **Agent noun suffix selection** → Choosing -ник when -ач or -ець is correct: `читальник` instead of `читач`, `бориць` instead of `борець`. Suffix-stem compatibility is not obvious; requires exposure to the full table with stems.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Word chain drill** (писати→письмо→письменник→письменність→записка) is pedagogically strong AND builds on the highest-frequency anchor word. Lead with this chain in the intro.
- **Textbook grounding**: Grade 6 Avramenko (tier 1, NUS 2022+) covers основоскладання with сполучний голосний о/е — use its rule formulation as the basis for the compound nouns section.
- **Grade 10 Karaman** (tier 2) notes that -ння/-ття suffixes "belong to книжного забарвлення" and are "продуктивними в офіційно-діловому, науковому та публіцистичному стилях" — register distinction worth making explicit.
- **Feminitives** (студентка, майстриня, поетеса): Grade 11 Avramenko notes not all profession names allow feminitive formation, and suffix selection varies. Worth a brief note to avoid overgeneralizing -ка to all professions.
- **Decolonized framing**: When introducing words like перебудова (restructuring), anchor it to Ukrainian historical usage — Ukrainian перебудова predates Soviet Gorbachev usage as a native word meaning renovation/reconstruction of buildings. Do not reference the Russian political term.
- **Modern compounds** (кіберзахист, євроінтеграція): These are authentic contemporary Ukrainian — anchor word formation in current events to show the language is living and growing, not a museum artifact.
- **No compound section should mention Russian** — the connecting vowel rule (о/е) is taught via Ukrainian internal logic (твердий/м'який приголосний), not by comparison to another language.
- All 38 vocabulary items are VESUM-verified: safe to use as examples throughout the module.
