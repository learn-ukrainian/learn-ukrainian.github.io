## Cultural Hooks

1. **Говерла — 2 061 м, вік ~25 млн років** (Wikipedia: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Говерла)
   Найвища вершина Українських Карпат і найвища точка України. Розташована в масиві Чорногора на межі Івано-Франківської та Закарпатської областей, за 17 км від кордону з Румунією. Strong anchor for vocabulary "найвища точка, підкорити вершину."

2. **Піп Іван Чорногірський — 2 028,5 м** (Wikipedia: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Піп_Іван_(Чорногора))
   Розташована на південно-східному кінці головного хребта Чорногори. На вершині стоїть довоєнна польська метеорологічна обсерваторія (т. зв. "Білий слон") — abandoned in 1939, returned to use in recent years as a restoration project. The building's dramatic ice-encrusted winter appearance has become a cultural landmark and social-media phenomenon, making it a compelling hook for contemporary learners.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **йти vs. ходити (habitual)** → "Я йду в гори щоліта" ❌ → "Я ходжу в гори щоліта" ✅
   Why: `йти` is unidirectional (single trip in progress now); `ходити` expresses habitual/repeated action. English has no morphological distinction, so learners default to the more frequent `йти`.

2. **Euphony violation: "в гори" after consonant** → "похід в гори" ❌ → "похід у гори" ✅
   Why: The rule is triggered by the preceding word's final sound and the following word's initial sound. "Похід" ends in a consonant, "гори" begins with a consonant → preposition `у` required. Learners often default to `в` by analogy with Russian.

3. **Russian loanwords in mountain vocabulary:**
   - `палатка` → correct: `намет`
   - `костєр` → correct: `вогнище`
   - `рюкзак` (widely used, not wrong) → purist preferred: `наплічник`
   - `бутилка` → correct: `пляшка`
   These errors are predictable from surzyk interference and should be addressed explicitly in Section 4 of the module.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Immersion level**: Module 90 is firmly in B1.1+ territory — full Ukrainian immersion required. No English in prose, titles, or explanations. English only in vocabulary table translations.
- **Polarity of motion verbs**: Section 5 should include a clear paradigm table showing йти (present, one direction) vs. ходити (present, habitual) side by side with mountain examples, not abstract ones.
- **Euphony rule framing**: Present as a positive Ukrainian phonetic feature ("милозвучність — краса мови"), not as a rule to avoid errors. This reframes the learner mindset.
- **"Білий слон" hook**: The Піп Іван observatory is a strong contemporary cultural hook — restoration work ongoing, social media visibility high among Ukrainian hikers. Use it in Section 2 as the "cultural surprise" moment.
- **Polонина definition**: It is a Carpathian alpine meadow above the treeline, central to Hutsul pastoral culture (вівчарство). Define within the text — learners from outside the region will not know it.
- **трейлраннінг**: Introduce as "гірський біг, або трейлраннінг" — this normalizes both terms and signals that the Ukrainian term exists.
- **Chornohora Sky Marathon**: No Wikipedia article found under that title. Content writers should reference it as a known event (60 km, 6 двотисячники) based on plan data but avoid invented statistics. If specific race details are needed, the discover phase should locate an authoritative Ukrainian source.
- **Decolonized framing**: Frame Carpathian hiking culture as distinctly Ukrainian — Hutsul traditions, Ukrainian trail-marking system, Ukrainian mountain rescue services. Do not reference Soviet-era mountaineering heritage as the frame; instead connect to post-2014/post-2022 renaissance of active Ukrainian identity.
