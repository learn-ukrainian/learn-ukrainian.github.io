## Cultural Hooks
1. **Chornohora Sky Marathon**: A legendary race that traverses all six "two-thousanders" (peaks over 2000m) in Ukraine, including Hoverla and Pip Ivan. It covers approx. 60km with massive elevation gain.
2. **"White Elephant" (Білий Слон)**: The old astronomical observatory on the summit of Pip Ivan Chornohirskyi. In winter, it gets completely covered in snow and ice, resembling a giant white animal.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Motion Verbs**: *Я йду в гори щоліта* (Incorrect) → *Я ходжу в гори щоліта* (Correct). **Reason**: Confusion between uni-directional (*йти* - now/once) and multi-directional/habitual (*ходити* - regularly).
2. **Prepositions (Euphony)**: *Похід в гори* (after consonant) → *Похід у гори*. **Reason**: Violation of euphony rules (avoiding consonant clusters).
3. **Lexical Interference**: *Палатка* → *Намет*; *Костєр* → *Вогнище*; *Бутилка* → *Пляшка*. **Reason**: Russisms common in casual speech but incorrect in standard Ukrainian.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Tone**: The "Carpathian Trail Guide" persona should use professional yet inspiring language. Use "наплічник" preferably, but "рюкзак" is acceptable if needed for variety (State Standard allows synonyms).
- **Safety First**: Emphasize safety (registration with rescuers, proper gear) as this is a key part of Ukrainian mountain culture (`гірські рятувальники`).
- **Geography**: Clearly distinguish between "Chornohora" (highest ridge) and lower ranges like "Gorgany" (rocky fields).
