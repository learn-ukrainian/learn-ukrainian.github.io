## 3. Cultural hook

1.  **Hoverla Tradition:** Climbing Mount Hoverla (2061 m), the highest point in Ukraine, is a significant bucket-list item for many Ukrainians. It is especially popular on Independence Day (August 24th), when thousands of people ascend the mountain, often creating "human traffic jams" on the trails.
2.  **Chornohora Sky Marathon:** This is one of the most famous and challenging trail running events in Ukraine. It spans the entire Chornohora ridge, crossing all six "two-thousanders" (peaks over 2000m). It represents the growing popularity of trail running culture in Ukraine, blending extreme sport with the traditional Hutsul landscape.
3.  **Polonyna Culture:** The Carpathians are unique for their *polonynas* (alpine meadows above the treeline). Unlike rocky alpine peaks elsewhere, these are grassy and traditionally used for sheep herding. Understanding the word *polonyna* is essential for navigating Ukrainian mountains.

## 4. Pedagogical notes

### Key Differences from English
- **Go vs. Walk/Run:** English speakers often overuse generic "go" (*йти*). In this context, distinguishing between **йти** (hiking/walking) and **бігти** (trail running) is crucial.
- **Prepositions of Direction:** The distinction between **в** (into the forest) and **на** (onto the mountain) is strict.
    - *в гори* (to the mountains - general direction)
    - *на гору* (up the mountain / to the top)

### Common Learner Errors
- **Confusion of Aspect:** Using imperfective *підніматися* when the result *піднятися* (reached the top) is meant.
- **Case Errors:** Mixing Locative (*де?* - на горі) with Accusative (*куди?* - на гору).
- **False Friends:** *Турист* in Ukrainian often means "hiker/backpacker" (active), not just a sightseer (passive).

### Teaching Sequence Recommendations
1.  **Geography & Places:** Establish the setting (Carpathians, Hoverla) and basic nouns.
2.  **Gear & Preparation:** Introduce instrumental case for "equipped with" or simple accusative "taking X".
3.  **Action (Motion Verbs):** Focus on the specific verbs for movement in terrain (*підніматися, спускатися, бігти, йти*).
4.  **The Narrative:** Read/listen to a story about a specific hike or race to see the grammar in context.

## 5. Scope boundaries

### IN Scope (B1 Level)
- **Grammar:**
    - Verbs of motion with prefixes (*під-, з-, пере-*).
    - Future tense for planning trips.
    - Imperative mood for safety advice (*не йдіть, візьміть*).
    - Locative case for location (*у Карпатах, на Говерлі*).
    - Accusative case for direction (*у Карпати, на Говерлу*).
    - Instrumental case for satisfaction/interest (*милуватися природою, займатися спортом*).
- **Topics:** Basic hiking gear, weather conditions, feelings/physical state.

### OUT of Scope (Save for B2/C1)
- **Grammar:**
    - Complex participle constructions (*гори, вкриті снігом* -> use *гори, де лежить сніг*).
    - Stylistic nuances of archaic/poetic nature descriptions.
- **Vocabulary:**
    - Specific geological terms (ridge types, rock formations).
    - Specialized botanical names (rare flowers/trees).
    - Technical mountaineering jargon (carabiners, belaying, knots).
    - Dialectal Hutsul vocabulary (except culturally ubiquitous words like *полонина* or *трембіта*, which should be explained).
