# Research: Reported Questions & Commands (B1 Module 40)

**Level**: B1 (Intermediate)
**Module**: 40-reported-questions-commands
**Topic**: Indirect Speech: Transforming Questions and Imperatives

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

This module covers complex sentences with explanatory clauses (складнопідрядні речення з з'ясувальною частиною), specifically transforming direct speech into indirect speech.

**Relevant Section in ДСТУ 2024 (Annex A - B1 Level):**

> **Синтаксис. Складне речення.**
> *   Складнопідрядні речення з підрядними з'ясувальними (сполучники *що*, *як*, *щоб*, *ніби*; сполучні слова *хто*, *що*, *який*, *чий*, *де*, *куди*, *звідки*, *коли*, *скільки*, *як*; частка *чи*).
> *   Пряма і непряма мова. Заміна прямої мови непрямою.
> *   Вираження прохання, наказу, поради в непрямій мові (сполучник *щоб*).

**Key Rules:**
1.  **Yes/No Questions**: Use the particle **чи** (equivalent to English *if/whether*).
    *   Direct: «Ти голодний?» → Indirect: «Вона запитала, **чи** я голодний».
2.  **Wh- Questions**: Retain the question word (*хто, де, коли, чому*) as the connector.
    *   Direct: «Де ти живеш?» → Indirect: «Він запитав, **де** я живу».
3.  **Commands/Requests**: Use **щоб** + **Past Tense** (Subject Change) OR **Infinitive** (No Subject Change/General).
    *   Direct: «Читай!» → Indirect: «Вона сказала, **щоб** я **читав**» (She told me to read).
    *   *Contrast with English*: English uses "told me **to** do". Ukrainian often uses a full clause "said **that** I did" (subjunctive meaning).

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

**High-Frequency Reporting Verbs (B1 Core):**
*   **питати / запитати** (to ask) - *Top 100 verbs*
*   **цікавитися / поцікавитися** (to be interested/wonder) - *Common in B1*
*   **дізнаватися / дізнатися** (to find out)
*   **просити / попросити** (to ask/request)
*   **казати / сказати** (to say/tell) - *Used for commands*
*   **радити / порадити** (to advise)
*   **вимагати** (to demand) - *Lower frequency but important for strength*

**Connectors:**
*   **чи** (if/whether) - *Essential*
*   **щоб** (in order to / that) - *Essential for commands*

**Common Collocations:**
*   Він поцікавився, чи... (He wondered if...)
*   Мама попросила, щоб я... (Mom asked me to...)
*   Лікар порадив, щоб він... (The doctor advised him to...)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**"Telephone" Game & Rumors:**
Ukrainians often joke that news travels faster via "Kum" (godparent/close friend) than via the internet. Using reported speech accurately is the linguistic engine of social bonding and "sharing news" (plitky/chutky).

**Politeness Hierarchy:**
In Ukrainian culture, the distinction between a direct command («Зроби це!») and a reported request («Він попросив, щоб ти це зробив») is crucial for politeness. Direct commands can sound harsh from strangers. Using the reported structure *просити, щоб...* softens the imperative, making it a key tool for social diplomacy.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Difference from English:**
1.  **NO Tense Backshift (The "Golden Rule"):**
    *   *English*: "He said he **was** busy" (Past implies present reality).
    *   *Ukrainian*: «Він сказав, що він **зайнятий**» (Keep the Present Tense of the original thought).
    *   *Student Error*: Students often apply English logic: «Він сказав, що був зайнятий» (This means he was busy *before* he said it, not *at the time*).
2.  **Commands with *Щоб*:**
    *   Learners try to translate "She told me **to buy** milk" literally with an infinitive: *«Вона сказала мені купити молоко»*. While understandable, the more natural B1 structure involves a clause: *«Вона сказала, **щоб я купив** молоко»*.
3.  **Word Order in Questions:**
    *   In indirect questions, standard declarative word order is preferred, though Ukrainian is flexible.
    *   *Error*: Keeping the auxiliary "do/does" or inversion from English mental habits.

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  **Review**: Yes/No questions (using *чи*) vs. Wh- questions.
2.  **Step 1**: Reported Questions (Focus on *чи* and lack of tense shift).
3.  **Step 2**: Reported Commands (Focus on *щоб* + past tense verb).
4.  **Practice**: "Roleplay Gossip" – Student A says something, Student B tells Student C what A said.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (B1):**
*   Transforming Present, Past, and Future direct questions.
*   Using *чи* for Yes/No questions.
*   Using *щоб* + past tense for reported imperatives (*сказав, щоб...*).
*   Basic reporting verbs (*питати, просити, радити*).

**OUT OF SCOPE (Save for B2/C1):**
*   Complex "Free Indirect Discourse" (literary style).
*   Subtle modal nuances in reported speech (e.g., distinguishing "He claimed that..." vs "He alleged that...").
*   Complex subjunctive nuances beyond the standard *щоб* pattern.
*   Reporting verbs governing non-standard cases (unless already known).
