**Alignment assessment:** This module is correctly placed at B1.7 as **foundational lexicology preparation** for B2 stylistic usage. The plan's note "§4.4.1: homonyms and paronyms at B1" is slightly misaligned — B1 §4.4.1 = declarative syntax. The correct upstream framing is: B1 builds the lexicology foundation; B2 §4.4.1.2 applies it stylistically. Content writing should reference the B2 gate explicitly as the "next step."

## Cultural Hooks

1. **«Коса»** — canonical Ukrainian triple homonym: коса (braid/plait), коса (scythe), коса (sand spit/cape). All three appear in Ukrainian folklore, folk songs, and idioms. Textbooks use this as the prime example. Well-anchored in Ukrainian cultural landscape (harvest, Cossack imagery).

2. **«Мати»** — uniquely Ukrainian morphological homonym/homoform: мати (mother, noun) / мати (to have, verb) / Мати (poetic address). This pair appears in both B2 §4.4.1.2 and Grade 11 (Glazova). Ukrainian literary tradition (Shevchenko's "Мати") gives this pair enormous cultural resonance. Frame as a specifically Ukrainian linguistic phenomenon — not a mere coincidence.

3. **«Ключ»** cited in Maksym Rylsky's poetry (Grade 11 textbook): "А ключ прозоро б'є з-під косогору, і рине, і дробиться по піску" — authentic literary hook for the lesson.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **компанія → кампанія confusion** — English "campaign" → learners write *компанія виборів* instead of *кампанія виборів*. Root: both borrowed words, English phonology of "company" bleeds into "campaign." GRAC ratio (10:1) shows компанія dominates learner exposure, reinforcing the error.

2. **ефективний ↔ ефектний mix-up** — Writing *ефектний менеджер* ("impressive manager") when meaning *ефективний менеджер* ("effective manager"). Both are internationalisms from French; semantic distinction (impressive vs. producing results) requires active memorization. Frequency ratio 12:1 suggests ефектний is significantly under-used.

3. **замóк/зáмок homograph** — Spoken Ukrainian distinguishes these by stress; in writing, learners (and native speakers) occasionally confuse contexts. For L2 learners: the absence of stress marks in standard orthography makes this particularly treacherous. Mnemonic: the castle (зáмок) is big and old; the lock (замóк) clicks when it closes (stress falls on the final syllable, like a click).

4. **адреса vs адрес** — Learners default to *адреса* for both "address of location" and "formal greeting address," since *адрес* (greeting document) is rare in everyday use. Grade 7 Avramenko (tier 1) covers this in practical postal writing context.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Homonym vs. polysemy distinction is the hardest conceptual hurdle.** Both Litvinova and Avramenko devote explicit attention to this distinction with the "спільне значення" test. The content must replicate this: if the meanings share any semantic core → полісемія; if completely unrelated → омонімія. Use *ключ* (три різні омоніми) vs. *серце* (кілька значень, але всі пов'язані) as the contrast pair.

- **Dictionary representation as anchor.** The distinction is operationalized in dictionaries: омоніми = separate entries (КЛЮЧ¹, КЛЮЧ²…); полісемія = numbered sub-meanings in one entry. This is a precise, teachable rule — use it early in Section 1.

- **«Мати» homoform is specifically Ukrainian.** Do NOT frame as "similar to other Slavic languages." Ukrainian мати (to have) comes from a distinct Proto-Slavic root and does not have the same homoform collision in most other Slavic languages. Frame positively.

- **Paronym register matters for formal writing.** The адрес/адреса and особовий/особистий pairs are especially relevant for official documents (документи). Tie to formal register skills — learners will encounter these in actual life situations (writing applications, filling forms).

- **Frequency asymmetry as pedagogy.** The GRAC data showing 10:1 and 12:1 ratios for paronym pairs is itself a teaching point: "the less frequent word is the one you need for precision." Use this to motivate learners — knowing the rare word marks educated, precise usage.

- **Stress-marked homographs need audio context.** зАмок/замОк and мУка/мукА are indistinguishable in standard written Ukrainian (no stress marks). Content should flag this explicitly and suggest reading aloud as disambiguation strategy.
