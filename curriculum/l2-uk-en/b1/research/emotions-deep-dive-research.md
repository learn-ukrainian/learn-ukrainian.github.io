# Research: B1 M64 "Емоції: глибоке занурення" (Emotions: Deep Dive)

**Module**: B1 M64
**Level**: B1 (Intermediate)
**Topic**: Advanced emotional vocabulary, nuance, and intensity modifiers.

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, Level B1 proficiency requires the ability to express personal feelings, emotions, and reactions in detail.

> **Section: Communicative Intentions (B1)**
> "The user can express and justify their feelings, such as surprise, happiness, sadness, interest, and indifference, and can react to the feelings of others with appropriate expressions of sympathy or shared joy."
>
> **Grammar Competence (B1)**
> - Use of impersonal predicative constructions with the Dative case (Мені сумно, їй весело).
> - Use of adjectives and participles to describe states (захоплений, розчарований).
> - Use of intensity modifiers (надзвичайно, трохи, досить) with emotional adjectives.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

At B1 level, students move beyond basic happy/sad/angry into nuanced emotional states.

**High Frequency (Must Know):**
- **радість** (joy) / **радісний** (joyful)
- **смуток** (sadness) / **сумний** (sad)
- **страх** (fear) / **страшно** (scary/scared)
- **гнів** (anger) / **сердитий** (angry)
- **тривога** (anxiety/alarm) / **тривожний** (anxious)
- **щастя** (happiness) / **щасливий** (happy)
- **захоплення** (admiration/delight)
- **розчарування** (disappointment)

**Medium Frequency (B1 Targets):**
- **натхнення** (inspiration)
- **відчай** (despair)
- **заздрість** (envy)
- **співчуття** (compassion/sympathy)
- **ревнощі** (jealousy)
- **образа** (resentment/offense)

**Common Collocations (B1):**
- **Відчувати (емоцію)**: відчувати радість, відчувати страх (To feel joy/fear).
- **Бути у стані...**: бути у стані шоку/ейфорії (To be in a state of shock/euphoria).
- **Глибоке...**: глибоке розчарування, глибока вдячність (Deep disappointment/gratitude).
- **Стримувати...**: стримувати сльози/сміх/гнів (To hold back tears/laughter/anger).
- **Виражати...**: виражати свої почуття (To express one's feelings).

## 3. Cultural Hook

**The Concept of "Душа" (Soul)**
In Ukrainian culture, emotions are often located in the "soul" (душа) rather than the "heart" or "mind". Common idioms reflect this:
- **"Душа болить"** (The soul hurts) – Deep emotional pain or empathy.
- **"Душа співає"** (The soul sings) – Extreme happiness or inspiration.
- **"Вкласти душу"** (To put one's soul into something) – To do something with great passion and care.

**Public vs. Private Expression**
Ukrainians tend to value sincerity (*щирість*) in private relationships, often sharing deep personal troubles (*виливати душу* - to pour out one's soul) with close friends. However, in formal or public settings, excessive emotional display is less common than in some Western cultures, though more permissible than in others.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Key Differences from English:**
1.  **Impersonal Constructions**: English says "I am sad" (Subject + Adjective). Ukrainian often uses the Dative construction: "**Мені сумно**" (To me [it is] sad). Learners often incorrectly say "Я сумний" which means "I am a sad person (by nature)" rather than "I feel sad right now".
2.  **Transitive vs. Intransitive Verbs**: *Хвилюватися* (to worry - intransitive) vs. *Турбувати* (to bother/worry someone - transitive).
3.  **Adjective vs. Adverb**: Confusing *сумний* (adjective) with *сумно* (adverb/predicative).

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  Review basic "Мені + adverb" constructions.
2.  Introduce nouns for emotions (радість, гнів) and the verb *відчувати* + Accusative.
3.  Introduce "being" states: *бути* + Instrumental (бути щасливим).
4.  Practice intensity modifiers (*дуже, надзвичайно, трохи*) with both adverbs and adjectives.
5.  Work on collocations (verbs that go with specific emotions).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (B1):**
- Dative case for experiencer (*Мені весело*).
- Instrumental case with *бути/ставати* (*Він став серйозним*).
- Accusative case for direct objects of feeling (*Я відчуваю біль*).
- Complex sentences with *тому що*, *через те що* to explain reasons for emotions.
- Participles as adjectives (*хвилюючий, вражаючий* - though purists prefer *що хвилює/вражає*, at B1 passive/active participles are introduced).

**OUT OF SCOPE (C1/C2):**
- Archaic or poetic terms for emotions (*туга, журба* - usually Literature/C1).
- Highly specific psychological terminology (*когнітивний дисонанс, апатія, фрустрація* - unless common loanwords).
- Complex idiomatic phraseology that requires historical context (*сміх крізь сльози* is fine, but obscure ones are out).
