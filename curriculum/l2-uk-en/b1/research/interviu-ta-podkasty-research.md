**Stylistics reference** §4.5.1 (lines 2435–2450): register distinction — formal vs informal, lexical and syntactic markers — directly supports the module's host/guest register contrast.

**Alignment**: This module directly addresses the mandated B1 listening competence: understanding publicistic audio texts (interviews, programmes about social events), global vs. selective comprehension, and speaker intent. The plan's four-stage listening cycle (prediction → listening → notes → summary) maps cleanly onto the Standard's distinction between глобальне розуміння (§1.1.1.1) and вибіркове/детальне розуміння (§1.1.1.2).

**Notes**:
- `інтерв'ю` (95.73 IPM) is one of the highest-frequency loanwords in Ukrainian media vocabulary — high priority.
- `власне` (68.22 IPM) is extremely common as a discourse particle — essential for B1 listening comprehension.
- `отже` (46.34 IPM) — among the top discourse markers for summarizing/concluding; correct mastery is measurable.
- `подкаст` (0.57 IPM) is a modern loanword with low corpus frequency (GRAC corpus is older); real-world usage post-2022 is significantly higher. Note Wikipedia confirms the normative spelling is **подкаст** (not *підкаст*).
- `ведучий` functions as both adj and noun — learners frequently use the adj form only, missing the lexicalized noun meaning.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Громадське радіо** — Ukrainian non-state public radio organization, founded as part of the Громадське media network. Focuses on independent journalism, analysis, and documentary storytelling. Post-2022 expanded its podcast offerings significantly; hosts topical interviews and current-affairs programs in normative literary Ukrainian — making it ideal as a "standard language model" source. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Громадське_радіо

2. **Подкаст як жанр** — Wikipedia Ukraine defines *подкаст* as a digital media file distributed over the internet, covering formats that include interviews, lectures, and radio-show-style content ("усний жанр"). The article explicitly notes the normative Ukrainian term is **подкаст** (not the common hypercorrect *підкаст*). The podcast boom among Ukrainian creators post-2022 became a documented cultural phenomenon — Ukrainian-language podcasts on history, decolonization, identity, and social commentary grew sharply as creators shifted from Russian-language content. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Подкаст

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Word-for-word translation panic** → strategy of global comprehension — Learners at B1 attempt to translate every word and lose the thread entirely when encountering an unknown item. Correct approach: identify keywords (опорні слова) and use context to reconstruct meaning. This is directly addressed in §1.1.1.1's "глобальне розуміння" requirement.

2. **Ignoring discourse markers** → treating markers as noise — `власне`, `до речі`, `отже` signal topic shifts, clarifications, and conclusions. Learners who skip them miss structural cues, losing the ability to follow spoken argument structure (вибіркове розуміння). These markers need explicit training.

3. **Register confusion: host vs. guest** — Learners may adopt guest register (colloquial, sometimes суржик-inflected) rather than the host's literary standard as their model. This is a real-world risk: authentic Ukrainian podcasts feature guests with variable registers. Explicit instruction to distinguish ведучий (literary norm) from гість (variable register) prevents fossilization of non-standard forms.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Terminology note**: Ukrainian Wikipedia confirms **подкаст** (not *підкаст*) as normative. Use consistently.
- **Register section is pedagogically central**: The host/guest register contrast (literary vs. colloquial/суржик) is a distinctive authentic-listening challenge specific to Ukrainian media post-2022. Phase B should build a dedicated mini-analysis around a realistic podcast scenario.
- **Discourse markers trio** (`власне` / `до речі` / `отже`) should be introduced with а) frequency data b) functional contrast (clarification / digression / conclusion) and c) short authentic-style example dialogues — not abstract definitions.
- **Four-stage listening cycle** (predict → listen → note → summarize) should be presented as a numbered workflow with Ukrainian metalanguage names for each stage — learners at B1 need to know how to *talk about* their own listening process.
- **Production section** (Section 4) should include a concrete чек-лист (self-assessment checklist) as a YAML-style or table callout — high engagement value, directly measurable progress.
- **Word count note**: 4000-word target for B1 skills module is generous for this topic but achievable by ensuring the Practical Interview Analysis section (1200 words) includes annotated authentic-style transcript excerpts and multi-example analysis tables.
- **Avoid**: framing Ukrainian podcast culture as "emerging" or "developing" — it is established and growing. Frame confidently: Ukrainian audio media is a living, expanding ecosystem with distinctive editorial voice.
