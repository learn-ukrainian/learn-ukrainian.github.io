**Module**: B1 M88

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (Level B1)**, the student must demonstrate the following competencies relevant to this topic:

> **Аудіювання (Listening Comprehension)**:
> "Розуміє основний зміст нормативного мовлення на знайомі теми (робота, навчання, дозвілля тощо). Розуміє головні думки багатьох радіо- та телепрограм про поточні події або на теми, що стосуються особистих чи професійних інтересів, якщо мовлення відносно повільне й чітке."
>
> **Говоріння (Speaking - Interaction)**:
> "Може без попередньої підготовки вступати в діалог з носієм мови, брати участь в обговоренні знайомої теми, висловлювати своє ставлення, аргументувати думку."

**Relevant Grammar Focus for B1 M88:**
*   **Indirect Speech (Непряма мова)**: Reporting what was said in an interview ("Він сказав, що...", "Вона запитала, чи...").
*   **Interrogative Intonation & Structures**: Diverse question forms beyond simple "Що/Де/Коли" (e.g., "Як ви ставитеся до...", "Чи не могли б ви...").
*   **Active vs. Passive (Introduction)**: Focus on Active voice as primary in speech, but recognizing passive constructions in formal intro/outros.
*   **Discourse Markers**: Fillers and transitions common in spontaneous speech (*власне, до речі, чесно кажучи, так би мовити*).

## 3. Cultural Hook

*   **The Podcast Boom**: Since roughly 2018, and accelerating massively after 2022, Ukrainian-language podcasts have exploded. This is part of the broader linguistic shift away from Russian content.
*   **Key Examples**:
    *   *The Ukrainians Audio*: Storytelling and social reporting.
    *   *Radio Skovoroda*: Modern urban culture.
    *   *Minkultpryvit* (Nariman Aliev): Informal, deep cultural conversations.
    *   *Bez Ogoloshennya Viyny*: History and psychology.
*   **Role in Society**: Podcasts in Ukraine often serve as "therapy" or deep dives into history/identity, replacing the vacuum left by Russian superficial pop-culture content. They are a primary vehicle for "Lahitna Ukrainizatsiya" (Gentle Ukrainization).
*   **Tech Literacy**: Ukrainians are highly digital; referencing Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and YouTube (which is treated as a podcast platform in Ukraine) is culturally authentic.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

*   **Listening Strategies (The "Gist" vs. Detail)**: At B1, students panic when they miss a word. Teach them to listen for *keywords* and *intonation* to grasp the main idea of the interview segment.
*   **Register Shifting**:
    *   Interviews often start with formal **Ви** (You formal).
    *   Podcasts often explicitly switch to **ти** (you informal) to create intimacy: *"Ми перейшли на 'ти'?"* (Did we switch to 'ty'?).
    *   *Error Alert*: Students mix these up. Explicitly pointing out the transition is helpful.
*   **"Stavyty Pytannya" vs. "Zadaty Pytannya"**: While *задати питання* is common in colloquial speech (surzhyk influence), the literary norm promoted in B1 should be **ставити питання**.
*   **Verb Aspect in Interviews**:
    *   **Imperfective**: Describing processes, habits ("Як ви працювали над цим?").
    *   **Perfective**: Single achievements ("Коли ви написали цю книгу?").

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (B1):**
*   **All Cases**: Full usage of noun/adjective declensions.
*   **Aspect Pairs**: Essential for storytelling in interviews.
*   **Conditional Mood**: "Що б ви зробили, якби...?" (What would you do if...?).
*   **Complex Sentences**: Connecting thoughts with *тому що, хоча, якщо, щоб*.
*   **Imperative**: Requests to subscribe/listen (*підпишіться, слухайте*).

**OUT OF SCOPE (Deferred to B2/C1):**
*   **Stylistic Nuances**: Irony, sarcasm, heavily idiomatic speech (unless explained as a fixed phrase).
*   **Dialects**: Heavy regional dialects (Hutsul, Transcarpathian) often found in raw interviews.
*   **Active Participles**: Forms like "працюючий", "ведучий" (as participle vs noun) – stick to standard relative clauses (*той, хто веде*).
*   **Professional Jargon**: Specific tech/medical/legal terminology unless central to the specific podcast topic (keep the topic general: culture, life, biography).
