# Дослідження: Невизначені та заперечні займенники

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.1.4: "Займенник... питально-відносні займенники: хто, що, який, яка, яке, чий, чия, чиє, котрий, котра, котре."
**Alignment**: The State Standard introduces the base interrogative pronouns at A1/A2. At B1, the standard requires "Expanded pronouns — indefinite (хтось, щось, дехто), negative (ніхто, ніщо)" (Mapping §4.2.1.4). Although explicit declension tables for indefinite pronouns appear in the B2 text (§4.1.1.4), B1 usage is confirmed by standard examples like "ніхто не зможе забути" (§4.4.2, line 2378).

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Precision of Uncertainty"**: Ukrainian culture values semantic precision even in uncertainty. Unlike English, which often switches between "someone" and "anyone" based on grammatical rules (questions vs. statements), Ukrainian distinguishes by *meaning*: Is the person real but unknown (`-сь`), or purely hypothetical (`-небудь`)?
2. **"Ніхто" in Folklore**: The concept of `ніхто` appears in riddles and proverbs, often personified as a tricky force or absolute absence (e.g., "Ніхто не знає, що нас чекає").

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Missing Double Negation** → *Ніхто прийшов* (Wrong) vs *Ніхто **не** прийшов* (Correct). learners transfer English logic ("Nobody came") where the negative pronoun is sufficient. In Ukrainian, the verb MUST also be negated.
2. **Preposition Merging** → *Нізким* (Wrong) vs *Ні з ким* (Correct). Learners treat the pronoun as a fixed block. The preposition must "split" the negative particle from the root.
3. **Misusing -небудь in Past Tense** → *Хто-небудь подзвонив* (Unnatural) vs *Хтось подзвонив* (Correct). Since a past event implies a specific agent existed, `-сь` is required. `-небудь` sounds strangely hypothetical for a completed fact.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Persona Flavor**: Use the "Logic Puzzle Host" voice to explain the `-сь` vs `-небудь` distinction as a logic game (Real vs. Hypothetical).
- **Visuals**: Use a "Splitting the Atom" visual metaphor for `ні з ким` (Preposition splitting the negative pronoun).
- **Pedagogy**: Focus heavily on the "Double Negation" rule as a mandatory feature, not a stylistic choice.
