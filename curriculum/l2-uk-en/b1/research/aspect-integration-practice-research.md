## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Collocations

### High-Frequency Connectors (Specific to Aspect Integration)
These connectors dictate aspect choice and are critical for B1 narrative flow.

| Connector | Translation | Aspect Tendency | Usage Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **коли** | when | Both | Context dependent (sequence vs background). |
| **поки** | while | Imperfective | Indicates simultaneous duration. |
| **доки** | until / while | Imperfective | Often implies a limit to the action. |
| **після того як** | after | Perfective | Usually precedes the main clause action. |
| **перед тим як** | before | Both | Often focuses on the *fact* of the action. |
| **як тільки** | as soon as | Perfective | Emphasizes immediate sequence. |

### High-Frequency Aspect Pairs (Recap from M12-M13)
*   **робити / зробити** (to do)
*   **читати / прочитати** (to read)
*   **писати / написати** (to write)
*   **говорити / сказати** (to speak/say)
*   **брати / взяти** (to take)
*   **давати / дати** (to give)
*   **починати / почати** (to start)
*   **закінчувати / закінчити** (to finish)

### Common Collocations
*   **довго чекати** (wait for a long time - Imp.)
*   **раптом зрозуміти** (suddenly understand - Perf.)
*   **весь день працювати** (work all day - Imp.)
*   **відразу відповісти** (answer immediately - Perf.)
*   **щодня зустрічатися** (meet every day - Imp.)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**Concept: "Linear vs. Cyclical Time"**
Ukrainian linguistic culture places a strong emphasis on the *result* versus the *process*. In storytelling (e.g., recounting a weekend or a holiday), Ukrainians often switch rapidly between:
*   **The Vibe (Process/Imperfective):** "Ми сиділи, пили каву, розмовляли..." (We were sitting, drinking coffee, talking...) -> Sets the scene.
*   **The Plot (Event/Perfective):** "І раптом він зайшов і сказав..." (And suddenly he walked in and said...) -> Moves the timeline.

This "background vs. foreground" distinction is not just grammar; it's the rhythm of Ukrainian narrative. Failing to switch aspects makes a story feel either static (all process) or frantic (all result).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key English-Ukrainian Differences
*   **Sequence vs. Simultaneity:** English uses "When I arrived, he was sleeping" (Past Simple + Past Continuous). Ukrainian uses Aspect: "Коли я **прийшов** (Perf), він **спав** (Imp)."
*   **"After doing X":** English learners love Gerunds ("After finishing work..."). Ukrainian MUST use a finite clause: "Після того як я **закінчив** (Perf) роботу..."

### Common Learner Errors
1.  **The "After" Trap:** Using *після* + Noun instead of *після того як* + Clause (e.g., "Після роботи я пішов..." is correct, but "Після я зробив..." is wrong).
2.  **Overusing Imperfective for Sequence:** "Вчора я вставав, снідав і йшов на роботу." (Sounds like a routine, not a specific single day's sequence).
3.  **Ignoring the "Result" in Negation:** "Я не чув це" (I wasn't listening/didn't hear - Process) vs "Я не почув це" (I failed to hear - Result).

### Teaching Sequence
1.  **Review:** Quick blitz of Aspect formation (pairs).
2.  **Simultaneity:** Focus on *поки* + Imp + Imp ("Поки я читав, вона писала").
3.  **Sequence:** Focus on *після того як* + Perf + Perf.
4.  **Interruption:** The classic "Imp background + Perf interruption" pattern ("Коли я йшов, я зустрів друга").
5.  **Integration:** Mixing all three in a paragraph.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (What they know & use)
*   **Tenses:** Past, Future (composite & simple), Present.
*   **Aspect Forms:** All standard pairs introduced in M06-M12.
*   **Connectors:** Basic temporal conjunctions (*коли, що, як*).
*   **Mood:** Imperative, Indicative.

### OUT Scope (Do NOT use yet)
*   **Participles:** Active (*читаючий*), Passive (*прочитаний*), Adverbial (*читаючи/прочитавши*). These are B1.2 topics (M42+).
*   **Passive Voice:** "Книжка була написана..." (M47).
*   **Stylistic Nuances:** Archaic or highly literary aspect usage.
*   **Complex Double-Aspect Verbs:** (e.g., *веліти*, *ранити* - treat as context-dependent later).
