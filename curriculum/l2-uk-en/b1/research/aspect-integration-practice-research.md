# Дослідження: Інтеграція виду: практика

## State Standard Reference

**§4.2.3.1** (lines 2269–2291): Aspect forms across tenses — past tense gender/number forms (НДВ vs ДВ): *хотів/хотіла/хотіли* vs *побачив/побачила/побачили*; synthetic future imperfective (*боротимуся, питимеш*); compound future imperfective (*буду боротися*); perfective future (*скажу, закричите, поборють*).

**§4.4.3** (lines 2380–2431): Complex sentences — including explicitly:

> «складнопідрядні речення із часовою підрядною частиною (сполучне слово **коли**):  
> вираження значення **одночасності**: Коли ми **йшли** до університету, викладач повідомив... [Imp + Imp]  
> вираження значення **різночасовості**: Коли ми **прийшли** до університету, викладач повідомив... [Perf + Imp]»

**Alignment**: Module b1-016 directly addresses the §4.4.3 requirement for temporal subordinate clauses with коли and the одночасність/різночасовість distinction. The plan extends beyond the Standard minimum by adding поки, доки, як тільки — these are not explicitly listed in §4.4.3 but fall under the broader B1 complex-sentence scope. Content must ground the extension in the State Standard's коли example and treat additional connectors as enrichment.

**⚠ Gap note**: §4.4.3 only explicitly names **коли** as the temporal connector. Connectors поки, доки, як тільки are pedagogically essential for B1 but are curriculum extensions beyond the Standard's explicit B1 listing. This should be noted in content framing ("будуємо на основі §4.4.3, розширюємо систему...").

## Cultural Hooks

1. **«Не кажи "гоп", поки не перескочиш»** — attested in Grade 9 Avramenko textbook (tier 1, 2024) as a live pedagogical example of поки не + Perfective marking a boundary event. Ukrainian proverb, not a calque. Ideal anchor for the поки не construction.

2. **«Поки грім не вдарить, мужик не перехреститься»** — authentic Ukrainian folk proverb (from Номис's collection *Українські приказки*, 1864). Uses поки не + Perfective (вдарить) to mark the boundary condition. The plan also references a variant with «доки грім не гряне» — the грякне/вдарить variant is more common in written sources. Either is valid; content should note both variants exist.

3. **Народна творчість as aspect laboratory**: Ukrainian folk proverbs systematically exploit the НДВ/ДВ contrast for rhetorical effect (ongoing process vs boundary event). This is a distinctly Ukrainian narrative tradition — not borrowed from any other language, rooted in pre-literary oral culture documented by Номис, Франко, and Українка.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Habitual vs one-time confusion**: *«Кожного дня він прочитав новини»* → correct: *читав*. Caused by L1 English not marking this distinction morphologically; learners default to Perfective (which "sounds more completed").

2. **Future Tense Mixing**: *«Я буду написати листа»* → correct: *напишу листа*. The compound future буду + infinitive is exclusively НДВ. Perfective future is synthetic only. High-frequency error; English "will write" gives no signal.

3. **коли ambiguity ignored**: Learners write *«Коли я прийшов, вона читала книгу»* correctly, but then also write *«Коли я приходив, вона прочитала книгу»* — mismatching aspect directions. The rule (Imp = simultaneous background; Perf = sequential trigger) needs explicit drilling with minimal pairs.

4. **доки vs поки conflation**: Native speakers use both interchangeably in informal speech, but for B1 learners the semantic distinctions matter: *поки* (while / as long as) vs *доки не* (until — strict limit). Overcorrection risk: learners may avoid доки entirely after learning поки.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The State Standard §4.4.3 only explicitly mentions **коли** as a B1 temporal connector. Content should acknowledge this anchor and frame поки/доки/як тільки as systematic extensions, not surprises.
- GRAC confirms **потім** (309 IPM) and **поки** (237 IPM) as truly high-frequency words — worth emphasizing in vocabulary presentation as "words you will see constantly."
- The Grade 9 Avramenko textbook uses «Не кажи "гоп", поки не перескочиш» as a live example — safe to reuse with attribution to народна творчість.
- **зрештою** (32 IPM) vs **врешті-решт** (10 IPM): frequency data supports the plan's preference for зрештою. Also note **зрештою** functions as both a temporal adverb and a parenthetical (вставне слово) — a useful double function for B1 learners.
- The «Logic Switch» metaphor in the plan (simultaneous vs sequential decision tree) aligns perfectly with the §4.4.3 одночасність/різночасовість binary — use the Standard's exact terminology to anchor the metaphor.
- Avoid any framing that presents Ukrainian aspect as "similar to Russian aspect" — describe it as a feature of Ukrainian's Slavic heritage shared with related languages (Polish *aspekt*, Portuguese *aspecto verbal*).
- The folk proverb cultural hook is strong; it demonstrates that the НДВ/ДВ distinction is not a classroom abstraction but is encoded in living oral tradition.
