# Дослідження: Згода і незгода

## State Standard Reference

**Каталог А (В1), рядки 1846–1849:**

> «запитувати про згоду й відмову: Ти (не)погоджуєшся зі мною? Ти (не)згоден / (не)згодна? Ти за чи проти?;
> подавати, приймати й відхиляти пропозиції: Пропоную піти на концерт. На жаль, не можу сьогодні. А я залюбки піду з вами;»

The plan cites **§2.4.3.2**, which corresponds to these two consecutive items in Catalogue A of the B1 State Standard (lines 1846–1849). The §-numbering in the plan does not appear verbatim in the document — the Standard lists these as unnumbered bullet items under "2. Каталог А. Перелік комунікативних намірів:" (line 1777). The functional match is exact.

**Alignment:** This module directly addresses both catalogue items: expressing/querying agreement and refusal (*згоден/згодна, погоджуватися, заперечувати*) and the full register of accepting/rejecting proposals in formal and informal contexts.

Adjacent supporting entries in the same Catalogue A (B1) include:
- рядок 1814: «виражати свої думки й переконання: Я думаю / вважаю / переконаний, що...»
- рядок 1841–1843: «виражати зв'язки між частинами тексту... покликатися на чиюсь думку: По-перше. По-друге.»
- рядок 1803–1804: «обґрунтовувати твердження, аргументувати свою думку»

Stylistics alignment: **§4.5.1 (lines 2435–2450)** — register distinction formal vs. informal — is the primary linguistic-competence anchor.

**Frequency insight:** The *правильно* vs. *вірно* distinction is quantitatively supported: *правильно* (45.00 IPM) outfrequents *вірно* (5.77 IPM) by 8:1. In agreement contexts specifically, *вірно* is a calque from Soviet-era usage meaning "loyally/faithfully." This drill is pedagogically well-grounded.

**Aspect pair note:** *погоджуватися* (2.10 IPM) vs. *погодитися* (11.40 IPM) — the perfective form is 5× more frequent. Content must teach both and clarify when each is used: *погоджуватися* for process/ongoing stance, *погодитися* for completed act of agreement.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Руїна (1657–1687):** Verified via Wikipedia. The Ruina was the period of Ukrainian state collapse between Bohdan Khmelnytsky's death (1657) and Ivan Mazepa's hetmanship (1687), marked by civil war between rival hetmans, foreign interventions (Poland, Muscovy, Ottoman Empire), and the fragmentation of the Hetmanate. The proverb *«Де два українці, там три гетьмани»* — though not documented in Wikipedia — is a folk reflection of this era's political fragmentation and the culture of individual leadership claims. The hook is culturally sound: the Ruina directly produced the individualist political culture this proverb captures. **Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Руїна**

2. **Яблуко незгоди as living idiom:** Corpus evidence (10 authentic GRAC examples) shows this idiom is actively used in Ukrainian political discourse (Crimean Fleet dispute), literary commentary, and everyday speech — not archaic. Modern usage attested: *«Присутність у погоджувальній комісії буде тим яблуком незгоди, яке розсварить нас усіх»* (political context). The idiom entered Ukrainian via Greek mythology (the apple of discord thrown by Eris) and is fully integrated. **Source: GRAC concordance**

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Я є згоден»** → **«Я згоден»** — English calque *"I am agree"* mapped onto *«Я є згоден»*. Ukrainian short-form adjectives serve as predicates directly without a copula. The copula *є* is absent in present tense for adjective predicates. This is the single most common agreement-expression error for English speakers.

2. **Gender mismatch: «Я згоден»** (said by a woman) → **«Я згодна»** — English speakers do not have grammatical gender for self-reference, so they default to the masculine form *згоден* regardless of speaker gender. Requires explicit drilling: ♂ *згоден*, ♀ *згодна*. The same applies to other short-form predicates used in this module (*задоволений/задоволена, переконаний/переконана*).

3. **«Вірно» for logical agreement** → **«Правильно»** — Calque from Soviet administrative language where *вірно* means "correct/right." In Ukrainian, *вірно* means "faithfully/loyally" (*вірний друг* = loyal friend). In dialogue confirming factual accuracy or opinion: *«Правильно!»* / *«Ви правильно сказали»* — never *«Вірно!»* in this sense.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The **aspect pair *погоджуватися/погодитися*** must be explicitly presented with usage contrast, not just mentioned. The 5:1 frequency ratio (pf dominant) means learners will encounter *погодитися* far more in authentic texts.
- **Register layering** is the core pedagogical challenge: *безперечно* (14.79 IPM) and *безумовно* (21.62 IPM) are near-synonyms — content should clarify that *безумовно* is slightly more formal/written while *безперечно* is more natural in spoken debate.
- **Яблуко незгоди idiom**: use only 1 authentic corpus example in the module; resist over-explaining the Greek mythology origin — one sentence is enough.
- The *правильно/вірно* distinction (Section 3) is a decolonization point: *вірно* as agreement marker is a Soviet administrative calque. Frame this as linguistic precision, not rule-following — *правильно* is the word that Ukrainian actually uses.
- For the digital/messenger section (Section 5): «+1» as agreement marker is attested in Ukrainian online discourse; *«Лайк»* (loanword from "like") is also used but should be noted as informal/youth register.
- **Concessive structures** (*хоча, але, однак, проте*) need contrast in Section 4: *однак* and *проте* are more formal than *але*; this distinction supports the register thread running through the whole module.
- Word budget: the 900-word Section 4 (Polite Discussion Strategies) is the richest section — ensure it contains actual dialogue examples demonstrating the diplomatic patterns, not just explanations of them.
