# Дослідження: Умовні речення: змішані та складні умови

## State Standard Reference

**§4.2.3.3** (B1, lines 2297–2298):
> «Умовний спосіб. Утворення форм умовного способу: говорив би, випила б, прочитали б.»

**§4.4.3** (B1, lines 2415–2418) — first matching the module's core content:
> «складнопідрядні речення з підрядною частиною:
> реальної умови (сполучник якщо): Якщо ви вивчите слова з переліку, то легко зрозумієте цей текст;
> ірреальної умови (якби, коли б): Якби ви вивчили слова з переліку, то зрозуміли б легко цей текст.»

**Alignment:**
The module addresses the *intersection* of these two standards: §4.2.3.3 provides the morphological base (умовний спосіб formation), and §4.4.3 provides the syntactic frame (conditional clauses — real vs unreal). The module extends both by teaching their *combination* in mixed and chained structures — a natural B1.3a progression once both types are mastered separately.

Note: The Standard does not explicitly enumerate "mixed conditionals" as a separate subtype. This module represents a pedagogically motivated synthesis of the two Standard-sanctioned types.

## Cultural Hooks

**1. Прислів'я «Якби знав, де впадеш, соломки підстелив би»**
Source: Підтверджено у шкільному підручнику — Авраменко, 7 клас (tier 1, §81): «Як би знав, де впаду, то й соломки б підстелив» (orthographic variant). This is a canonical Ukrainian proverb illustrating *past unreal condition + timeless/habitual result* — the module plan's "Type 1" mixed conditional. The makohon (макогін) proverb in the dialogues section draws from the same folk register.

**2. «Історія не знає умовного способу»**
Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia, article «Умовний спосіб». This well-known Ukrainian aphorism states that history doesn't tolerate the conditional mood — meaning speculative "what if" reasoning is unacceptable in historical analysis. This is the *perfect paradox* for the dialogues section: learners use the conditional mood precisely to engage in historical "what if" debates. The Wikipedia article also notes this forms the basis of the *альтернативна історія* (alternative history) literary genre — directly usable as a discussion prompt.

## Common Learner Errors

**1. Conjunction contamination: «Якщо я був би» → «Якби я був»**
Why it happens: English "if I would / if I were" maps incorrectly to якщо + умовний спосіб simultaneously. Learners apply якщо (real) with the conditional particle, producing a hybrid that is ungrammatical in Ukrainian. In Ukrainian, якби *already encodes* unreality — adding би after якщо creates a redundant/false structure.
Fix: якщо + indicative (real) | якби + умовний спосіб (unreal). Never mix.

**2. Russian interference: «Єслі» → «якщо»**
Why it happens: Russian «если» is the learner's stored conditional conjunction. Transfer is direct. Also: «в случае если» → «у випадку якщо» (acceptable) vs «за умови що» (preferred formal). The Standard (§4.4.3) uses якщо exclusively.
Fix: systematic replacement + de-Russification checklist in the plan's final section.

**3. Omission of comma before якщо / якби in clause-initial position**
Why it happens: English does not mark the subordinate clause boundary with a comma when the conditional follows the main clause: "I would have come *if* you had called" (no comma). Ukrainian obligatorily marks: «Я б прийшов, якби ти зателефонував». Learners trained on English comma rules omit it.
Fix: explicit comma rule with paired examples contrasting correct/incorrect punctuation.

**Bonus — particle placement error:**
«Я це б зробив» (wrong word order) → «Я б це зробив» / «Я це зробив би».
The particle би/б is enclitic and follows the first stressed element in the clause. This is a subtle but frequent error at B1.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **"Mixed" is a pedagogical label, not a Standard term.** The Standard names only "реальна умова" and "ірреальна умова." Phase B should introduce "змішаний тип" as a curriculum term for combining both types, with explicit cross-labelling to the Standard.

2. **Conditional particle position is teachable at B1** — the biti/b enclitic rule is consistent and can be stated as a simple positional rule. Include it in the explanation section.

3. **«За умови» vs «якщо»** — register distinction is real and textbook-confirmed: за умови is formal/written; якщо is universal. Good for the formal register focus in the plan.

4. **The «Якби не + Noun» pattern** (dialogues section) is confirmed by GRAC concordance: «якби не годованець наш Петро…» — this is a productive elliptical pattern worth drilling separately.

5. **Doky (доки)** — 37 IPM suggests medium-frequency word; teachable at B1. Appears in temporal/conditional overlap contexts: «доки не зроблю → якщо не зроблю» — the semantic boundary between temporal and conditional conjunctions is a useful B1 discussion point.

6. **Immersion constraint (B1.3a):** Module 36 is well past bridge zone (M01-05). Full Ukrainian immersion required. The plan's "English only for disambiguation of false friends" rule means якщо/якби contrast with Russian "если" gets one line of English in the vocabulary table, not in prose.

7. **Proverb orthography note:** Авраменко uses «Як би знав…» (two words — adverb + particle) while other sources render «Якби знав…» (one word — conjunction). The plan uses the conjunction form (якби), which is correct for the unreal conditional conjunction. Phase B should note this як би / якби distinction explicitly — Авраменко §81 makes exactly this point.
