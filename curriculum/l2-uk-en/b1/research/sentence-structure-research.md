## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, Level B1 requires the ability to analyze and construct both simple and complex sentences.

**Relevant Section:** § **Grammatical Competence: Syntax**

The user can identify and use basic punctuation marks (comma, period, question mark, exclamation mark) and connect parts of a sentence using conjunctions (i, a, ale, bo, shcho, koly)."

## 3. Cultural Hook

**Logical Stress vs. Word Order:**
Unlike English, which relies on strict SVO (Subject-Verb-Object) word order to determine meaning, Ukrainian uses **case endings** to define roles. This allows for "Free Word Order," which is actually driven by **Logical Stress (Логічний наголос)**.
*   **Fact:** In Ukrainian culture/speech, the most new or important information often comes at the *end* of the sentence.
*   *Example:* "Я кохаю тебе" (I love you - neutral) vs "Тебе я кохаю" (It is YOU I love - emotional/specific).
*   **Education:** "Syntactic Analysis" (Синтаксичний розбір речення) is a rigorous drill in Ukrainian schools. Knowing the terms *підмет* and *присудок* connects the learner to the native educational experience.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Key Differences from English:**
1.  **Subject Position:** In English, the subject is almost always before the verb. In Ukrainian, it can follow the verb (*Мені подобається кава* - Coffee pleases me). Students often incorrectly identify "Мені" (Me) as the subject because it comes first.
2.  **Comma Usage:** Ukrainian is "comma-heavy." Commas are strictly required before most subordinate conjunctions (*що, який, коли, бо*), unlike the optional nature in informal English.

**Common Learner Errors:**
*   Ignoring commas before *що* (that).
*   Identifying the logical object as the grammatical subject in Dative constructions (e.g., confusing *мені* for the subject).
*   Overusing pronouns (dropping *я/ти* is natural in Ukrainian when the verb ending is clear, but English speakers tend to keep them).

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  Review SVO in English vs Ukrainian.
2.  Introduce terms *Підмет* (Subject) and *Присудок* (Predicate).
3.  Drill identification in "reversed" sentences (OVS).
4.  Introduce *Головне* vs *Підрядне* clauses to explain comma rules.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (B1.0 Bridge):**
*   **Terminology:** Naming parts of the sentence (Subject, Predicate, Object).
*   **Identification:** Finding these parts in A2/B1 texts.
*   **Basic Punctuation:** Commas before *а, але, що, бо*.
*   **Sentence Types:** Distinguishing Simple vs. Complex.

**OUT OF SCOPE (Save for later B1/B2):**
*   **Complex Syntax:** Deep analysis of multi-level subordination (B2).
*   **Advanced Punctuation:** Dashes (—) for elliptical sentences or appositions (unless basic).
*   **Stylistics:** Inversion for poetic effect (C1).
*   **Passive Constructions:** Formal passive voice (B1.5+).
