**§4.4.1.1** (lines 2344–2352): Declarative sentences — стверджувальне, заперечне, подвійне заперечення (baseline context for the module).

**Alignment**: This module (b1-004) directly prepares learners for §4.4.2 and §4.4.3 by teaching the metalanguage (підмет, присудок, граматична основа, ускладнене речення, складнопідрядне/складносурядне) required to understand and produce these sentence types throughout the B1 curriculum.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ukrainian as a free-word-order language** — Ukrainian syntax has historically been described through the lens of Slavic comparative grammar. Grade 11 textbook (Voron, p.110) states directly: "Українська мова належить до мов із **вільним порядком слів**." This is a genuine structural feature of Ukrainian independent of other Slavic languages — free word order enables rich topicalization and stylistic inversion. This can anchor the lesson: Ukrainian's syntactic flexibility is a feature, not a source of confusion.

2. **The "underlining ritual" in Ukrainian schools** — The visual system of underlining sentence parts (one line for підмет, two lines for присудок, wavy for означення, dotted for додаток, dash-dot for обставина) is a standardized pedagogical practice taught from grade 3 (confirmed: Kravtsova grade 3, p.109; Ponomarova grade 3, p.131). This is a distinctly Ukrainian school tradition — every Ukrainian student learns this visual metalanguage. For adult L2 learners, framing this as "the system every Ukrainian schoolchild learns" creates cultural connection and practical utility for helping children with homework or discussing grammar with native speakers.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Part of speech ≠ Sentence part** — Learners conflate grammatical category (іменник/noun) with syntactic function (підмет/subject). Example: in "Книгу читає студент", "студент" (noun) is the **підмет** but "книгу" (noun) is the **додаток**. The same word class can fill different syntactic roles. Textbooks address this explicitly (Litvinova grade 5, p.256: "Чому підмет і присудок називаємо головними членами речення, а інші — другорядними?"). Fix: teach role identification via question-asking (хто? що? — підмет; кого? що? — додаток).

2. **Pro-drop panic** — English speakers expect an explicit subject in every sentence. Ukrainian is a pro-drop language: "Люблю каву" (I love coffee) is grammatically complete — the subject is encoded in the verb ending. Learners often label these as "broken sentences" or try to insert a pronoun. State Standard §4.4.1.1 lists "Люблю ранкову тишу" as a valid declarative sentence. Fix: present pro-drop as a feature of Ukrainian grammar, not an ellipsis or error — the граматична основа here consists of a **односкладне речення** with предикатив only.

3. **Object vs. Adverbial confusion with prepositional phrases** — Learners struggle to distinguish додаток from обставина when both appear with prepositions. "Думати **про роботу**" (про кого? що? → додаток) vs. "Прийти **через годину**" (коли? → обставина часу). The key diagnostic is question-type: object questions (кого? чого? кому? чим?) vs. circumstantial questions (де? куди? коли? як? чому?). Grade 11 Avramenko and Voron both address this in sections on sentence parts.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Multi-word NOT FOUND terms** are all pedagogical metalanguage confirmed in textbooks and the State Standard. Use them as planned — VESUM is a lemma dictionary, not a phrase dictionary.
- **Gramatical basis framing**: Grade 5 textbooks (Avramenko §64–65, Litvinova p.196) define граматична основа identically: "підмет і присудок = граматична основа." Use this exact school definition.
- **Inversiya** (0.21 IPM) — attested in general corpus and grade 11 textbooks. Grade 11 Glazova (p.100): "При інверсії найпомітнішим є той член речення, який стоїть або на початку, або в кінці речення." This is excellent material for the word-order section.
- **Pro-drop coverage in plan** correctly identifies this as a learner error area. The State Standard examples ("Люблю ранкову тишу") validate its inclusion.
- **Однорідні члени** — Confirmed in §4.4.2 as the primary ускладнення type. Grade 11 Voron (p.106) gives the exact definition: "Ускладнюються за рахунок однорідних і відокремлених членів речення, звертань і вставних слів." Use this for the section on ускладнене речення.
- **B1.0 Bridge immersion**: This module is module 4 of the metalanguage bridge (modules 1–5 use mixed Ukrainian/English). English scaffolding for abstract syntactic concepts is permitted per curriculum design. Prioritize Ukrainian terms with parenthetical English equivalents rather than full English explanations.
- **Avoid**: Any framing that presents Ukrainian word order as "similar to Russian." Frame Ukrainian's free word order as an inherent feature shared with many European languages (Polish, Latin, Portuguese) — not as a Slavic family property.
