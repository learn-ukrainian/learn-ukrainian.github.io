# Дослідження: Вид у наказовому способі

## State Standard Reference

**§4.2.3.2** (lines 2293–2295 of UKRAINIAN-STATE-STANDARD-2024.txt):

> "Наказовий спосіб. Утворення форм наказового способу: мрій, мріймо; кажіть, скажіть, скажімо; пий, пиймо, пийте; борися, борімося, боріться, хай / нехай бореться, хай / нехай борються."

**Critical observation**: The Standard's examples directly embed the aspect distinction without stating it explicitly. The pair **кажіть** (НДВ) vs. **скажіть** (ДВ) and the pair **мрій/мріймо** (НДВ) encode the core B1 learning goal. The Standard covers formation; this module's job is to teach the *functional* (pragmatic) distinction between aspects — which is *why* кажіть and скажіть are both listed.

**Alignment**: Module b1-013 directly fulfills §4.2.3.2 by teaching the НДВ/ДВ choice in imperatives. The collective forms (мріймо, скажімо, пиймо) cited in the Standard support the "collective imperatives" point in section 2 of the content_outline.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Прислів'я "Вік живи — вік учись"** — Confirmed authentic Ukrainian proverb (attested in comparative folklore literature, equivalent to Latin *nunquam discere seris*). Both verbs **живи** and **учись** are НДВ imperatives — ideal illustration that ongoing, lifelong processes demand Imperfective aspect. This single proverb encodes the НДВ=process/duration rule in a culturally rich, memorable form.

2. **"Давай + future" as norm violation** — Grade 11 Avramenko (tier 1 NUS textbook) uses contemporary Ukrainian pop songs (А. Кузьменко / Скрябін, гурт «Антитіла», А. Підлужний) as examples of morphological norm *violations* to analyse: "Давай виключим світло...", "Давай поговорим...". This is a powerful cultural hook: widely used in pop music and everyday speech, yet classified as non-standard in school Ukrainian. The correct synthetic forms are **вимкнімо, поговорімо**. This tension between colloquial reality and normative standard is authentic content for B1 register discussion.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Wrong aspect for single-instance commands** → *Зачиняй вікно* (НДВ, process of closing) when the request means "close it once" → correct: **Зачини вікно** (ДВ). Root cause: learners default to НДВ as the "safe" option.

2. **Perfective for general prohibitions** → *Не зроби цього* (sounds like a warning: "don't accidentally do it") when intending a general ban → correct: **Не роби цього** (НДВ). Root cause: confusion between prohibition (НДВ) and warning (ДВ) — the negation flip logic is not intuitive.

3. **Colloquial "давай + future/infinitive"** used in formal writing → *Давай підемо* (spoken) → standard form: **Ходімо / ходьмо**. Root cause: extremely high colloquial frequency of давай normalises the pattern, but it fails the morphological norm. Grade 11 textbooks explicitly flag this.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **The §4.2.3.2 examples кажіть/скажіть are the module's backbone** — use this pair early and repeatedly.
- **Norm vs. colloquial tension** (давай + future) is a genuinely rich register discussion for B1 — treat it as descriptive pragmatics, not just "this is wrong."
- **Negation flip** (Не роби = prohibition НДВ; Не впади = warning ДВ) is the hardest concept — allocate adequate space; plan's 900-word section is appropriate.
- All activity vocabulary verified in VESUM; зачини imperative is valid (зачинити → зачини) but double-check in sandbox phase.
- **Decolonization framing**: aspect is a shared Slavic feature — frame НДВ/ДВ distinction through Ukrainian literary and folk examples (proverbs, Франко, Лесь Українка) rather than any contrastive framing with other languages.
- **"Politeness Paradox" (Сядьте vs. Сідайте)**: сідайте has 3.55 IPM in real corpus — good frequency data to cite as evidence that НДВ invitation forms are genuinely in use.
