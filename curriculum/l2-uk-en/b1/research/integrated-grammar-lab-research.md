Alignment: This module directly addresses §4.5.1 — formal vs. informal communication, register-specific lexical and syntactic markers. The module extends the Standard by adding register-switching practice (one content item → three styles), active-voice preference, and anti-канцелярит training, all of which operationalise the Standard's requirement for learners to distinguish and deploy appropriate stylistic means.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Синонімічне багатство: слово «горизонт»** — Ukrainian has over 10 native synonyms for горизонт: *обрій, небокрай, виднокруг, видноколо, виднокрай, крайнебо, круговид, кругозір, кругогляд, небозвід, небосхил, овид.* (Source: Grade 6 textbook, Литвинова 2023, tier 1). This illustrates the plan's point about synonymic rows calibrating register from poetic (*обрій*) to scientific (*горизонт*) — a concrete, textbook-grounded example for the Explanation section.

2. **Боротьба з канцеляритом** — The term *канцелярит* was coined by Russian writer Korney Chukovsky in 1962 ("Живой как жизнь"), and popularized in Ukrainian discourse through the translator Nora Gal's "Слово живе і мертве" (1972). **Decolonized framing note**: the Ukrainian preference for active constructions predates Chukovsky — it is rooted in Ukraine's own 1920s language purism movement (the *Розстріляне відродження* era), when linguists such as Mykola Skrypnyk actively championed verb-rich, agent-centered Ukrainian prose against Soviet administrative creep. The module should frame the rejection of канцелярит as a positive Ukrainian language value, not as "correcting Russian influence." Key teaching contrast: *Комісія ухвалила рішення* (active, Ukrainian norm) vs. *Рішення було ухвалено комісією* (passive, канцелярит pattern). (Source: Wikipedia — Канцелярит; Grade 11, Глазова 2019, tier 2 — "Українська мова уникає пасивних конструкцій. Тому перевагу, де це можливо, слід надавати дієсловам активного стану.")

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Verbalization (відієслівний іменник → дієслово)** — Канцелярит pattern: *здійснити купівлю* → correct: *купити*; *прийняти рішення* → correct: *вирішити*. Learners trained in formal English academic writing tend to nominalise verbs and carry this into Ukrainian, producing unnatural bureaucratic stacking.

2. **Overuse of passive** — *Книга читається учнем* → Ukrainian norm: *Учень читає книгу*. Learners (especially those with Russian or Latin-language backgrounds) default to passive constructions which, while grammatically correct in Ukrainian, violate register expectations in casual and journalistic styles.

3. **Register bleeding: "даний" and "здійснити" in casual contexts** — These words are not grammatically wrong but carry strong офіційно-діловий register markers. Using *даний* (замість *цей/той*) or *здійснити* (замість *зробити*) in family conversation is a register mismatch that sounds unintentionally formal or Soviet-bureaucratic. This is the most productive error type for the module's diagnostic and editing tasks.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Горизонт synonymic row** is a perfect opening hook for the Explanation section — it is textbook-grounded (Grade 6, Литвинова 2023), memorable, and directly illustrates the register-calibration concept without being abstract.
- **Grade 8, Зболотний 2025 (tier 1)** contains an explicit table of офіційно-діловий features (written: закони, постанови, накази, розпорядження, довідки, заяви, протоколи, контракти; oral: промови, доповіді, переговори, дискусії, бесіди). This can ground Scenario 1 in real document types learners will encounter.
- **Grade 11, Зболотний 2019** confirms that синоніми are used *sparingly* in official-business and scientific styles — important counter-intuitive insight for advanced learners who assume richer vocabulary = better formal style.
- **Decolonized framing**: frame Ukrainian active-voice preference as a *positive feature of the language's own expressive tradition*, not as a corrective to external influence. The 1920s purism movement provides a strong Ukrainian-centric historical grounding.
- **Word frequency gap**: `регістр` (0.25/million) and `канцелярит` (0.01/million) are specialist metalanguage terms. The module should introduce them explicitly with definition boxes, not assume passive recognition.
- **"Даний" note**: VESUM confirms it is a valid Ukrainian adjective — but the module should teach its register *restriction* (formal/official only), which is the actual competency gap.
- **Section word budget check**: Total plan = 4,000 words across 8 sections. The two scenario sections (700 words each) need to include multiple worked examples, not just explanations, to hit their targets.
