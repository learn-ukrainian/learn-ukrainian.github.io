## State Standard Reference

**§4.4.2** (lines 2367–2378): *"Просте ускладнене речення: ... просте речення зі вставними словами мабуть, можливо, здається: Мабуть, ніхто не зможе забути нашу зустріч."*

**§4.5.1** (lines 2435–2450): *"Офіційне й неофіційне спілкування: ... стилістичні засоби лексики — антоніми, синоніми, епітети, метафори; стилістичні засоби синтаксису — звертання, еліпсис, повтор, порівняння."*

**Alignment:** §4.4.2 introduces вставні слова at B1 with three basic epistemic markers. This module extends that foundation into the full functional taxonomy of advanced discourse markers (sequencing, addition, contrast, clarification, result, conclusion), which is consistent with §4.4.3's complex-sentence conjunctions and §4.5.1's register differentiation mandate. The plan's §4.4.2/§4.5.1 references are accurate; the extension to 25+ markers is pedagogically appropriate overfulfillment of the standard, not scope creep.

## Cultural Hooks

**1. Києво-Могилянська академія (1632–1817) — VERIFIED**
Founded by Metropolitan Petro Mohyla as the Kyiv-Bratsk Collegium; gained academy status in the second half of the 17th century. It was *"one of the greatest and most distinguished learning centers in Eastern Europe"* (uk.wikipedia.org). Rhetoric — the art of structured, persuasive speech — was a core discipline. The academy produced the first systematic Ukrainian rhetoric curricula, teaching exactly the kind of logical sequencing this module targets. This is an authentic, decolonized cultural anchor. ✅

**2. Григорій Савич Сковорода (1722–1794) — VERIFIED**
Ukrainian philosopher-humanist, poet, pedagogue, called the "Ukrainian Socrates." A Kyiv-Mohyla alumnus, he wrote in clear, structured philosophical dialogues where transitional logic was essential to his method. His famous dictum "Пізнай себе" ("Know thyself") exemplifies how clear rhetorical structure conveys depth. ✅

**⚠️ DECOLONIZATION FLAG — Феофан Прокопович:**
Wikipedia describes him as *"Ukrainian and Russian theologian"* who in 1721 *"suggested to Tsar Peter I a new name for the Muscovite Tsardom"* (i.e., helped rebrand it as the Russian Empire). He served as Archbishop in Russian cities from 1718 onward. He is a collaborator with Russian imperial expansion, not a defender of Ukrainian intellectual tradition. **Do not use him as a cultural hero for this module.** Replace with Skovoroda or the institution itself (Kyiv-Mohyla Academy's collective rhetoric tradition).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Punctuation omission** → По-перше я думаю … → **По-перше, я думаю …** — Discourse markers (вставні слова) require comma separation; learners treat them as sentence-initial adverbs. Textbook source: Zabolotnyi Grade 8 §38; Glazova Grade 11 §28.

2. **нарешті vs наприкінці confusion** → *"Нарешті сторінки"* (final pages) is wrong — нарешті = temporal climax ("at last, we agreed"); for location/position, use **наприкінці** (at the end of) or **насамкінець** (in conclusion). The plan correctly flags нарешті/накінець, but note накінець is archaic; the active confusion is нарешті vs наприкінці.

3. **Russianisms in discourse structure** — surzhyk patterns:
   - *во-пєрвих / во-вторих* → **по-перше / по-друге**
   - *короче* (Russian slang summary marker) → **словом / підсумовуючи**
   - *таком образом* → **таким чином**
   - *і так далі* misused as a filler instead of conclusion marker → **отже / таким чином**

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **GRAC multi-word limitation**: таким чином, крім того, з одного боку/з іншого боку are real, common phrases but return 0 in GRAC because it indexes single tokens. Do not interpret this as rare — these are core formal connectors.
2. **Frequency-informed teaching order**: Present markers in descending frequency order within each cluster so learners acquire the highest-utility items first.
3. **Прокопович must be removed**: Replace with Skovoroda or the Kyiv-Mohyla Academy rhetoric tradition collectively. Prокопович enabled Russian imperial infrastructure and is not an appropriate decolonized cultural anchor.
4. **словом as discourse marker**: VESUM finds it only as the instrumental of *слово* (noun), but its discourse-marker function (*словом, це було важко*) is well-established. Teach it as a lexicalized adverbial, not a noun form.
5. **Register table**: The formal/conversational split is clearly grounded in §4.5.1. A table contrasting formal (підсумовуючи, таким чином, отже) vs. semi-formal (загалом, проте) vs. conversational (словом, до речі) will satisfy the register mandate.
6. **State Standard scope**: §4.4.2 only names мабуть, можливо, здається explicitly at B1. The 25-marker scope in this module is an academically justified extension; frame it as *building* the introductory word system, not replacing it.
