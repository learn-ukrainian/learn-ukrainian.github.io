§4.2.3.1 (B1): *«Дійсний спосіб: утворення форм роду і числа дієслів минулого часу недоконаного і доконаного виду: хотів, хотіла, хотіло, хотіли; побачив, побачила, побачило, побачили; дієвідмінювання дієслів недоконаного виду в майбутньому часі — складена форма: буду боротися, будеш пити; дієвідмінювання дієслів недоконаного виду в майбутньому часі — синтетична форма: боротимуся, питимеш, кричатиме; дієвідмінювання дієслів доконаного виду в майбутньому часі: скажу, закричите, поборють.»* (lines 2269–2291)

**Alignment**: The Standard mandates B1 learners form both perfective and imperfective tenses correctly across all three tenses. This module addresses the Standard directly: it drills the imperfective/perfective distinction in past and future (the two tenses where aspect choice is active), and enforces the Present = Imperfective ONLY rule implicit in the Standard's paradigm structure (no perfective present forms listed).

Secondary alignment — A2 §4.3.2 (lines 1380–1381): aspect pair formation patterns introduced at A2; B1 extends this to systematic use across 40 high-frequency pairs.

**Key pedagogical insight from corpus data**: Many high-frequency pairs show the **perfective is MORE frequent** overall (зробити, сказати, дати, побачити, взяти, вивчити, почати). Content writing should foreground this empirical fact.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **«Зробив діло — гуляй сміло»** — Widely attested Ukrainian proverb (not retrievable via Wikipedia article; attested in Ukrainian proverb collections). Encodes the perfective aspect's core meaning: completion unlocks freedom. The perfective «зробив» (did/completed) is the gatekeeper — only after completion does the imperfective «гуляй» (enjoy yourself, process) become possible. Perfect hook for the Perfective = result, Imperfective = ongoing contrast.

2. **«Крапля камінь точить»** — Ukrainian form of the persistence proverb (Wikipedia search confirms «крапля камінь точить» is the attested Ukrainian form; the plan's «вода камінь точить» appears to be a less standard variant — use «крапля камінь точить» for accuracy). Imperfective «точить» (wears away, ongoing process) illustrates the imperfective's core: repetitive, habitual action without defined endpoint. Source: attested in Panchen literary sources, Kitaiskyi sad article references on Wikipedia.

3. **«Куй залізо, поки гаряче»** — Confirmed Ukrainian proverb (equivalent in Swedish, attested cross-linguistically via Wikipedia Swedish proverbs article). Imperative «куй» (imperfective command = ongoing process) highlights timing and aspect choice in instructions. Relevant to Section 5 on common errors: imperative aspect selection connects directly to b1-11 prerequisites.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Perfective Present» trap**: Using perfective form where only imperfective exists — e.g., *«Я зроблю це зараз»* when meaning "I'm doing this right now." Correct: «Я роблю це зараз». Error cause: English "I'll do it now" maps onto what feels like present-tense immediacy; learners conflate future + immediacy with present. Textbook confirmation: Avramenko Grade 7, p.57 — «Усі дієслова у формі теперішнього часу завжди недоконаного виду» (ALL present-tense verbs are always imperfective).

2. **Compound future + perfective infinitive**: *«Я буду прочитати»* mixing auxiliary «буду» with perfective infinitive «прочитати». Rule: compound future (буду + infinitive) takes **imperfective infinitives only** → «буду читати». Perfective future is simple: «прочитаю». Textbook source: Litvinova Grade 7, p.52 — explicit paradigm showing compound future = imperfective only.

3. **Past tense process/result conflation**: English "I read a book yesterday" translates both «Вчора я читав книгу» (process, habitual, possibly unfinished) and «Вчора я прочитав книгу» (completed, reached the end). English speakers default to the imperfective; corpus data shows the perfective past is often more informative in context. Drill needs situational anchors: «читав годину» (was reading for an hour) vs «прочитав за годину» (finished in an hour).

## Decolonized Framing Notes

- **Never use Russian as baseline**: Describe Ukrainian aspect as a feature Ukrainian developed on its own terms. Parallels with Portuguese/Spanish (perfectivo/imperfectivo) are more neutral and equally illustrative if cross-linguistic comparison is needed.
- **Suppletive pairs are authentically Ukrainian**: говорити/сказати, брати/взяти — these reflect Ukrainian's own lexical history, not borrowings or derivatives.
- **Soviet linguistics distortion**: Soviet-era grammars often presented Ukrainian aspectual pairs as mirror images of Russian ones, erasing Ukrainian-specific collocational and register differences. Content should present Ukrainian pairs through Ukrainian corpus data (provided above), not through borrowed Russian metalanguage.
- **Frequency data reframes the narrative**: The corpus shows perfectives often exceed imperfectives in frequency — this is a fact about how Ukrainian is actually used, derived from Ukrainian texts alone.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The diagnostic intro should use the «Perfective Present» trap as its hook — it's immediately relatable and sets up the whole module's central rule.
- The GRAC frequency data should be explicitly mentioned in the 40-pair overview section — it grounds the selection in empirical evidence and signals to learners which pairs to prioritize.
- Proverb «крапля камінь точить» (not «вода камінь точить» as in the plan) — verify with content writer to use the attested form.
- The 40-pair overview (1000-word section) should use a structured table with 🔄/✅ symbols as planned, but **group by formation pattern** (prefix, suffix, suppletive) with corpus frequency rank noted per pair.
- «Двовидові дієслова» (атакувати, гарантувати, організувати) — mention briefly as edge case/footnote in the morphology section; do not expand since they're out of scope for B1.1 frequency focus.
- Both future forms for imperfective (synthetic: читатиму; compound: буду читати) should be contrasted — Grade 7 Litvinova provides the exact paradigm to mirror.
