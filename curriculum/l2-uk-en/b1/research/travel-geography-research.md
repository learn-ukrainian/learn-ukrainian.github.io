## Cultural Hooks

1. **Говерла — 2,061 m, highest peak of Ukraine** (Wikipedia verified)
   — «Найвища вершина Українських Карпат і найвища точка України, її висота становить 2 061 м над рівнем моря.» Located in the Chornohora massif, on the border of Ivano-Frankivsk and Zakarpattia oblasts, 17 km from the Romanian border. Use as a concrete superlative geography anchor.

2. **Сім природних чудес України** — selected 26 серпня 2008 р., after public vote initiated by Mykola Tomenko on 1 листопада 2007 р. (Wikipedia verified). Final list (alphabetical):
   - Асканія-Нова (biosphere reserve, Kherson region)
   - Дністровський каньйон (5 oblasts)
   - Гранітно-степове Побужжя (landscape park, Mykolaiv region)
   - Мармурова печера (Crimea — note: occupied since 2014; frame as «тимчасово окупована територія»)
   - Подільські Товтри (national park, Khmelnytsk region)
   - Світязь (lake, Volyn)
   - Синевир (lake, Zakarpattia) — folklore name «Морське Око»

   **Pedagogy note:** Мармурова печера is in Russian-occupied Crimea. Frame historically: «до окупації 2014 року» or use it to discuss territorial integrity — appropriate for B1 political awareness.

3. **Geographic center of Europe near Dilove, Zakarpattia** — plan's claim is legitimate as a pedagogical hook. The 1887 marker at Ділове (Рахівський район) was erected by Austro-Hungarian geographers; its claim to be the exact center is contested by multiple methodologies (Wikipedia confirms several rival sites), but the marker's cultural significance and the region's identity as «серце Європи» is a well-established local narrative. Present as «один із претендентів на звання географічного центру Європи» to be accurate.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Prepositional confusion with geography targets:**
   - **у горах** ✓ vs **★на горах** ✗ — mountains use «у» for location (inside/among the mountains)
   - **на морі** ✓ vs **★в морі** ✗ — sea uses «на» for location/recreation (on the seaside)
   - **на курорті** ✓ vs **★в курорті** ✗ — resort uses «на» (surface/area concept)
   - **в аеропорту** ✓ vs **★на аеропорту** ✗ — airport uses «в» (enclosed space)
   - Root cause: English speakers transfer "at the mountains/sea" as «на», missing the Ukrainian semantic distinction between surface/area («на») and enclosed/immersive space («у/в»).

2. **подорож / мандрівка / поїздка register confusion:**
   - Learners use «мандрівка» for all travel (influenced by literary/song exposure), producing unnatural speech for everyday contexts («я маю ділову мандрівку» ✗ → «ділову поїздку» ✓).
   - «поїздка» is restricted to purposeful/short journeys; using it for a grand trip sounds odd («поїздка навколо світу» is marked, «подорож навколо світу» is natural).
   - Root cause: All three map loosely to English "trip/journey," so learners don't internalize the register split.

3. **відпустка vs канікули (false friends with Russian аналогів):**
   - English speakers often use «відпустка» for school breaks (calquing from «vacation»), but «канікули» is obligatory for academic breaks. «відпустка» = work leave (adult, paid). Using «відпустка» for a student's summer break is a register error.
   - Note: «канікули» is plural-only (no singular form in standard usage).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **7 Natural Wonders list accuracy:** Use the 2008 official list from Wikipedia. The plan's list in the outline is largely accurate but lists «Lake Svityaz (deepest)» — Svityaz (Волинь) is indeed the deepest lake in Ukraine at 58.4 m; Synuvyr is the largest Carpathian lake. Verify these characterizations are accurate and distinct in content.
- **Crimea sensitivity:** Мармурова печера is in occupied Crimea. Either omit it from the main 7-wonder list and use a substitution, or frame it clearly as «тимчасово окупована» — never present without that context at B1 level.
- **Dilove/center of Europe:** Present as «один із претендентів», not as absolute fact, to model critical geographic thinking appropriate for B1.
- **Immersion compliance:** This is B1.6+ → 100% Ukrainian immersion rule applies. The plan's Grammar Workshop section (prepositions) must explain all errors in Ukrainian only — no English parenthetical clarifications in prose.
- **Collocations density:** Plan targets 30 vocabulary words in context (objective 1). With 4,000-word target and 5 sections, aim for ~6 vocabulary items per section with 2–3 collocations each to meet objective without padding.
- **Section 3 (Natural Wonders) at 1000 words** is the largest — it can carry detailed descriptions of each wonder (3–4 sentences per item × 7 items = ~700 words anchor; remaining 300 for intro/transitions).
