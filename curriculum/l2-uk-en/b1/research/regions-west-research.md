## Cultural Hooks

1. **Юрій-Франц Кульчицький (1683)** — [Wikipedia verified] Rutheнian nobleman (герб Сас), interpreter and intelligence agent for King Jan Sobieski, hero of the Battle of Vienna. After the battle he reportedly used captured Ottoman coffee supplies to open one of Vienna's first coffee houses. Wikipedia credits him as "автором рецепту віденської кави та популяризатором у Європі цього напою." This is the authentic Ukrainian origin story for Viennese coffee culture — a powerful hook connecting Lviv's coffee identity to a specific historical moment.

2. **Закарпаття borders 4 countries** — [Wikipedia verified] Закарпатська область borders Romania (south), Hungary (southwest), Slovakia (west), Poland (northwest). The administrative center is Уж**город**. This unique quadruple border status is the concrete evidence for calling it a "mozaic of cultures" — Hungarian, Romanian, Slovak, and Ukrainian influences are geographically grounded, not abstract.

3. **Галичина coverage** — [Wikipedia verified] In current usage, Галичина refers primarily to the territories of Lvivska, Ternopilska, and Ivano-Frankivska oblasts (55,700 km²). The name derives from the city of Halych (Галич), once capital of the Galician principality. Important: **Галичина is a historical region, not an administrative unit** — this distinction is pedagogically significant.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"в Галичині" → "на Галичині"** — Territorial historical regions in Ukrainian use the preposition `на` (not `в/у`): *на Галичині, на Закарпатті, на Волині, на Буковині, на Поліссі*. The rule: `на` for historical/geographical territories; `у/в` for enclosed spaces and modern cities. Learners transfer the English "in Galicia" → `в Галичині` (wrong). This is directly flagged in the plan and is a high-priority error to address.

2. **Adjective agreement with "кава" (feminine)** — English "coffee" has no gender; learners default to neuter agreement. Drill: *смачна кава* (not *смачне кава*), *гаряча кава, міцна кава, ароматна кава, чорна кава, львівська кава*. The plan correctly identifies this agreement exercise.

3. **Conflating "Галичина" with "Закарпаття"** — B1 learners often treat "Western Ukraine" as homogeneous. Key distinctions: Galicia (former Habsburg empire, Greek Catholic majority, dialect markers філіжанка/файно) vs Zakarpattia (multi-ethnic, Hungarian/Slovak influence, Orthodox + Catholic mix, бограч/виноробство). These are culturally and linguistically distinct — the plan's decolonized framing of internal diversity is correct.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Kулічицький framing**: Wikipedia gives his full name as Юрій-Франц Кульчицький (герб Сас). The plan's reference to "Під синьою пляшкою" (Zur Blauen Flasche) is historically attested. Use this full name on first mention for accuracy.
- **UNESCO status**: Lviv's historic centre was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1998. The official Ukrainian name: "Ансамбль історичного центру Львова." Confirm this framing is used rather than vague references to "UNESCO heritage."
- **Ethnonym precision**: гуцули, бойки, лемки — these are distinct sub-Carpathian ethnographic groups. Lемки are now largely outside Ukraine (Lemkivshchyna was depopulated via Operation Vistula, 1947). Content should present them sensitively — as historically present groups whose culture endures, without erasing the forced deportation context. At B1 level, a brief factual mention suffices; do not over-explain or avoid the topic entirely.
- **Decolonized framing**: The Habsburg/Austro-Hungarian historical context of Western Ukraine should be presented as Ukrainian cultural history in a European context — *not* as "non-Russian Ukraine" or defined against Moscow. The region's European orientation is intrinsic, not reactive.
- **Register note**: The plan specifies `розмовний` register. Galician dialectal words (філіжанка, файно) should appear as **authentic cultural markers** in dialogue/example contexts, clearly labeled as dialectal, not introduced as active target vocabulary.
- **Textbook validation**: Grade 5 history textbook (Schupak 2022, NUS tier 1) explicitly teaches "Історико-географічні регіони України" using the same conceptual framing (territory, history, customs). This confirms the module's approach is grade-appropriate and curriculum-aligned.
