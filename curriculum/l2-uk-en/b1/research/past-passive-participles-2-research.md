# Research: Passive Participles: Short Form (-но/-то)

**Module**: B1 M46 "Пасивні дієприкметники: коротка форма"
**Topic**: Impersonal verb forms ending in -но/-то (Past Passive Participles Short Forms)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

These forms are classified under the **Morphological Competence** section of the Ukrainian State Standard (Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної).

> **Reference**: "Морфологія. Дієслово. Безособові форми на -но, -то. Їх творення та вживання у безособових реченнях для позначення результату дії без вказівки на виконавця."

*(Morphology. Verb. Impersonal forms ending in -no, -to. Their formation and usage in impersonal sentences to denote the result of an action without indication of the performer.)*

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

These forms are extremely high-frequency in official communications, signage, and public announcements. They often function as "lexical chunks" for A1/A2 learners before the grammar is fully understood at B1.

**High Frequency (Lexicalized - Memorize as Fixed Expressions):**
*   **Заборонено** (Forbidden/Prohibited) - *Ubiquitous on signs.*
*   **Дозволено** (Allowed/Permitted) - *Common in rules/regulations.*
*   **Зачинено** (Closed) - *Store signs.*
*   **Відчинено** (Open) - *Store signs.*
*   **Зайнято** (Occupied/Busy) - *Restrooms, fitting rooms.*
*   **Зроблено** (Done) - *Task completion.*
*   **Написано** (Written) - *Reference to texts ("Як тут написано?").*

**Medium Frequency (Productive Grammar usage):**
*   **Сказано** (Said/It is said)
*   **Завершено** (Completed/Finished)
*   **Прибрано** (Cleaned)
*   **Вирішено** (Decided)
*   **Побудовано** (Built)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**The Language of Public Space (Visual Culture)**
In Ukraine, the impersonal nature of authority is codified in these forms. You rarely see "Ми не працюємо" (We are not working) on a shop door; you see "**Зачинено**" (It is closed). This creates a detached, objective reality—the state of "closed-ness" exists independent of the shopkeeper.

**"Made in Ukraine"**
The phrase "**Зроблено в Україні**" (Made in Ukraine) is a prime example of this grammar used as a badge of pride and origin. It emphasizes the *result* (production) and the *location*, rather than the specific manufacturer.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Key Differences from English:**
*   **The "Neuter" Trap**: English speakers often confuse the short form (ending in **-о**) with the Neuter Gender Adjective/Participle (ending in **-е**).
    *   *Correct*: Це **зроблено**. (Impersonal state)
    *   *Incorrect*: Це зроблене. (Attributive - implies "This is a created [thing]")
*   **Subject Disappearance**: English uses "It is done" (Subject "It"). Ukrainian uses a subject-less construction. Learners try to force a subject where none exists.

**Common Learner Errors:**
*   Using **-е** instead of **-о** for resultative states (*Магазин зачинене* vs *Магазин зачинено*).
*   Trying to conjugate them (they are invariable).
*   Using them with a nominative subject when referring to a person (*Я зайнято* - Incorrect; *Я зайнятий* - Correct full form).

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  **Lexical**: Teach *Зачинено/Заборонено* as fixed "traffic light" words (A1/A2).
2.  **Structural (B1)**: Show derivation from Past Passive Participles (Module 45).
    *   *Зроблений* (adj) $	o$ Drop *-ий*, add *-о* $	o$ *Зроблено*.
3.  **Transformation**: Practice transforming "Active Sentence" $	o$ "Passive Full" $	o$ "Impersonal Short".

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN Scope (Focus for Module 46):**
*   **Formation**: Deriving -но/-то forms from full participles (ending in -ний/-тий).
*   **Usage**: Expressing result/state without an active agent.
*   **Signage/Status**: Using these forms for labels and status updates.
*   **Contrast**: Distinguishing *Він зачинений* (Adjective describing 'He/It') vs *Зачинено* (Impersonal state).

**OUT of Scope (Do not confuse learners yet):**
*   **The "Instrumental Agent" Debate**: Avoid teaching "Зроблено **мною**" (Done by me). While grammatically possible in some views, modern style guides prefer active voice (*Я зробил*) or strict impersonal without agent. Keep it agent-less.
*   **Complex Object Case Government**: The shifting of the object from Nominative to Accusative (*Книгу (Acc) прочитано* vs *Книга (Nom) прочитана*) is a B2 nuance. Stick to simple intransitive or clear contexts where case isn't the main friction point.
