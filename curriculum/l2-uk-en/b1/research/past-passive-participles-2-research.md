Alignment: The module introduces short passive participles (-но/-то) as a specific non-finite verb form conveying resultative meaning, satisfying the B1 requirement for participle comprehension and usage within the verbal paradigm and simple sentences.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Architecture of Bureaucracy**: Ukrainian official-business style (офіційно-діловий стиль) relies heavily on -но/-то forms to project bureaucratic objectivity and inevitability, stripping agency to emphasize the absolute fact of an action («Рішення прийнято»).
2. **Historical Resonance**: The Universals of the Ukrainian Central Rada (1917-1918) extensively utilized these solemn, resultative forms for state-building pronouncements («Акт проголошено», «Універсал підписано»), giving the grammar a uniquely Ukrainian historical weight.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Syntactic Mismatch (Nominative Object)**: Learners often use the Nominative case for the object (*Книга написано*) instead of the mandatory Accusative (*Книгу написано*), falsely equating the short form to an English/Russian full passive adjective.
2. **Aspect Error (Imperfective Stem)**: Forming resultative -но/-то suffixes from imperfective stems (saying *роблено* instead of *зроблено*, *писано* instead of *написано*).
3. **The "Було" Redundancy**: Inserting the auxiliary verb «було» in present-tense contexts (*Це було зроблено зараз* → *Це зроблено*), mimicking the English "it is done".

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize that the Accusative object assignment with -но/-то is a distinct and highly productive syntactic feature of the Ukrainian language. Do not compare it to Russian equivalents; instead, frame it as a native tool for emphasizing the result of an action objectively.
- **Immersion**: B1.4 module requires 100% Ukrainian immersion. English is only permitted in the vocabulary YAML translation column. Use clear, simple Ukrainian for all grammar explanations.
- **Visuals**: A mermaid flowchart or a clean Markdown table is essential for the "Syntax Shift" section to visually demonstrate the transition from Subject (Nom) -> Active Verb -> Object (Acc) to None -> Impersonal Verb (-но) -> Object (Acc).
