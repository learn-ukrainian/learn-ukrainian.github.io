**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)

**Note:** This research targets B1 level proficiency, correcting the erroneous "A1" designation in the task prompt. M60 is a late-stage B1 module where students possess solid knowledge of cases, verb aspects, and complex sentences.

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, Level B1 (Independent User):

> **Lexical Competence (B1):** The user commands a sufficient vocabulary to express him/herself on most topics pertinent to his/her everyday life such as family, hobbies and interests, work, travel, and current events.
>
> **Sociocultural Competence (Section 2.3):** The student understands and uses language related to:
> *   **Social and political life:** Basic terminology of the political system, elections, and civic duties.
> *   **Media and Information:** Ability to understand the main points of radio news bulletins and simple TV interviews on familiar topics.

**Specific Grammar Focus for M60:**
*   **Abstract Noun Formation:** Suffixes *-ство* (суспільство, членство), *-ість* (незалежність), *-ція* (корупція, революція).
*   **Government of Political Verbs:** *голосувати за* (Acc.), *брати участь у* (Loc.), *обирати / бути обраним*.

## 3. Cultural Hook

1.  **Diia (Дія) — "State in a Smartphone":**
    Ukraine is a world leader in digital governance. The **Diia** app allows citizens to hold digital passports (legally equivalent to physical ones), pay taxes, sign petitions, and even pay traffic fines instantly. It represents a modern, transparent relationship between the citizen and the state, minimizing bureaucracy.

2.  **The Role of the "Maidan" (Майдан):**
    For Ukrainians, "politics" isn't just what happens in parliament; it's what happens on the square (*maidan*). The tradition of direct democracy and public assembly (Revolution on Granite 1990, Orange Revolution 2004, Revolution of Dignity 2013-2014) is central to the concept of **громадянське суспільство** (civil society). It emphasizes personal responsibility for the country's fate.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

*   **"Politics" vs "Policy":**
    English speakers often distinguish "politics" (the game) from "policy" (the plan). Ukrainian uses **політика** for both (*зовнішня політика* - foreign policy; *займатися політикою* - to do politics). Use context to differentiate.
*   **Plural Only:**
    **Вибори** (elections) is grammatically plural (*вибори почалися*, not *вибори почався*). Similar to *гроші* (money) or *двері* (door).
*   **Preposition Pitfalls:**
    *   *Vote FOR* -> **голосувати ЗА + Accusative** (not dative/genitive). *Я голосую за кандидата.*
    *   *Participate IN* -> **брати участь У + Locative** (not "in" + Accusative). *У виборах*, not *в вибори*.
*   **False Friends:**
    *   **Мітинг** (rally/protest), NOT usually a business meeting (which is *зустріч* or *нарада*). If a Ukrainian says "I am going to a meeting" (*я йду на мітинг*), they are going to protest, not to the office.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1 Level)
*   **Passive Constructions:** *Закон прийнятий* (The law is passed), *Депутат обраний* (The MP is elected).
*   **Complex Sentences:** *Я вважаю, що...* (I consider that...), *Тому що...* (Because...), *Незважаючи на...* (Despite...).
*   **Participles:** Passive and active participles commonly found in news headlines (*діючий президент*, *заплановані зміни*).
*   **Abstract Nouns:** Understanding suffix derivation (*демократ* -> *демократія*).

### OUT of Scope (Reserved for B2/C1)
*   **Legal Jargon:** *Юрисдикція, ратифікація, імпічмент, презумпція*.
*   **Stylistic Nuances:** Irony, political satire, specialized idiomatic expressions used in political punditry.
*   **Complex Gerunds:** Heavy bureaucratic phrasing (*з метою забезпечення виконання...*). Keep it simpler: *щоб виконати*.
