# Дослідження: Допустові речення: хоча, незважаючи на

## State Standard Reference

§4.4.3 (lines 2423–2425): "складнопідрядні речення з допустовою підрядною частиною (сполучники **хоч, дарма що, незважаючи на те що**): *Незважаючи на те що заняття закінчилися пізно, ми пішли провідати Марту.*"

Alignment: This module directly addresses §4.4.3 — the complete inventory of B1 concessive connectors. The Standard explicitly mandates **хоч**, **дарма що**, and **незважаючи на те що** as the three canonical connectors at this level. The module correctly introduces **попри** as a stylistic alternative (not mandated by Standard, but consistent with §4.5.1 register awareness). The adversative pair **хоча… проте** is consistent with §4.4.3's сурядні contrasts. Full structural compliance confirmed.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ліна Костенко** (b. 1930, Ржищів) — Шістдесятниця poet, Shevchenko Prize 1987 for *Маруся Чурай*. Famous quote on refusing the Hero of Ukraine title: *«Політичної біжутерії не ношу!»* — this phrase itself models concessive spirit (хоч могла б прийняти, не прийняла). Confirmed in Wikipedia + RAG corpus. Literary example with "хоч" from Шевченко in the same RAG corpus: *«Хоч на годину, моя рибчино!»* (*Гайдамаки*).

2. **Прислів'я «Хоч бачить око, та зуб не йме»** — authentic folk Ukrainian proverb, not attributable to Kostenko. Means: "the eye can see it but the tooth can't reach it" — tantalizingly close but unattainable. Direct structural model of **Хоч… та** concessive pattern with corrective conjunction **та**. Confirmed authentic; no Wikipedia article exists for it (use as folk proverb, not literary citation).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **не дивлячись на** → **незважаючи на / попри** — Russian calque (*не смотря на*). Confirmed as explicit normative correction in Grade 5 Avramenko. The Ukrainian *не дивлячись* means "not looking (with one's eyes)" — a completely different literal action.

2. **незважаючи** (one word) vs. **не зважаючи** (two words) — orthographic trap explicitly taught in Grade 7 Avramenko. One word = preposition ("despite"); two words = adverbial participle ("ignoring/not paying attention to"). Sentence pair: *Незважаючи на дощ, ми пішли* (despite the rain) vs. *Не зважаючи на застереження, він ризикнув* (ignoring the warnings).

3. **але vs. хоча register confusion** — але is adversative (coordinates equal-weight contrast); хоча is concessive (subordinates a yielded premise). E.g., *Він старався, але не вийшло* (coordinate) vs. *Хоч він і старався, результат розчарував* (concessive subordination — the effort is acknowledged but overridden). Learners frequently use але where the concessive structure is required, flattening the logical relationship.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **хоч vs. хоча**: хоч (200 IPM) is genuinely more common in spoken/literary Ukrainian than хоча (172 IPM). Teach both as primary forms; do NOT present хоч as merely a "short form" — it is the dominant form in literary prose and proverbs.
- **Structure diagram**: The textbook-canonical notation `(Хоч…), [проте…]` should be presented visually. When the concessive clause leads, the main clause *may but need not* open with a contrastive conjunction — make this optionality explicit.
- **дарма що**: at 10 IPM, this is genuinely rare in modern Ukrainian. Framing it as "archaic/literary" is accurate — the plan's intent to warn against business use is well-founded.
- **попри ≈ незважаючи на in frequency**: corpus data contradicts the intuition that popry is rare — at 50 IPM it matches незважаючи на. Present them as stylistic equals, not as "fancy alternative." popry is slightly more media-informal; незважаючи на is slightly more formal/bureaucratic.
- **Structural typology**: незважаючи на (preposition + noun phrase) vs. незважаючи на те що (compound conjunction + clause) — these are structurally distinct and should be drilled separately to avoid overgeneralization.
- **Decolonized framing**: The correction *не дивлячись на* → *незважаючи на* should be framed as a normative Ukrainian standard (taught since Grade 5), not as "Ukrainian differs from Russian." The canonical textbook framing (Avramenko) presents it simply as what is correct in Ukrainian — follow that.
