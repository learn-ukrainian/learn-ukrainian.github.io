## Cultural Hooks
1. **Качалка в Гідропарку (Київ):** An iconic, free outdoor gym on the Dnipro river islands, created entirely from scrap metal and tank chains in the 1970s. It stands as a symbol of grassroots fitness culture and Ukrainian ingenuity.
2. **Карпатський хайкінг:** Hiking in the Chornohora or Gorgany mountain ranges is a deeply rooted tradition of Ukrainian active leisure, blending physical endurance with a profound connection to native landscapes and Hutsul heritage.

## Common Learner Errors
1. *Займатися спорт (Accusative)* → Займатися спорт**ом** (Instrumental) — Literal translation of "to do sports" ignores the required Instrumental case for "займатися".
2. *Я йду в спортзал щодня (йти)* → Я **ходжу** в спортзал щодня (ходити) — Confusing unidirectional motion (happening right now) with multidirectional/habitual motion (regular routine).
3. *В стадіон / На спортзал* → **На** стадіон / **В** спортзал — Misapplication of prepositions 'в' and 'на' for specific sports facilities.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing:** Contrast the modern, joyful, and community-driven Ukrainian wellness culture (like the grassroots Kachalka) with the rigid, state-mandated, militarized "фізкультура" (fizkultura) of the Soviet era. Emphasize Ukrainian agency: people creating their own spaces and exploring their own mountains.
- Ensure 100% Ukrainian immersion in all prose, explanations, and dialogues. English is strictly reserved for the vocabulary table's translation column.
- Use the 'Wellness Coach' persona to keep the tone encouraging and focused on small steps and consistency (регулярність).
