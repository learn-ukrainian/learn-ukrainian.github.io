## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard of Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)** (aligned with CEFR B1):

*   **Sphere of Communication:** Personal and Public (Особистісна та публічна сфери).
*   **Topic:** "Leisure, interests, hobbies" (Дозвілля, інтереси, захоплення) and "Health and physical condition" (Здоров'я та фізичний стан).
*   **Grammatical Competence (B1):**
    *   Use of **Instrumental Case** with verbs of interest/occupation (`займатися спортом`, `цікавитися футболом`).
    *   Use of **Imperfective Aspect** for habits/routines (`щоранку я бігаю`) vs **Perfective Aspect** for achievements (`вчора я пробіг 5 км`).
    *   **Motion Verbs** (Multi-directional) for general activities (`ходити в зал`, `їздити на велосипеді`).

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

**High Frequency (Active B1):**
*   **Verbs:** `займатися` (to practice/do), `тренуватися` (to train), `бігати` (to run), `плавати` (to swim), `відпочивати` (to rest).
*   **Nouns:** `спорт` (sport), `зал` (gym), `тренування` (training/workout), `здоров'я` (health), `активність` (activity), `м'язи` (muscles), `тіло` (body).
*   **Adjectives:** `активний` (active), `здоровий` (healthy), `корисний` (useful/beneficial), `спортивний` (sports/athletic).

**Medium Frequency (Contextual B1):**
*   `воркаут` (workout - street workout), `фітнес-клуб` (fitness club), `пробіжка` (jog), `розминка` (warm-up), `дієта` (diet), `спорядження` (gear/equipment), `абонемент` (subscription/membership).

**Common Collocations:**
*   `вести здоровий спосіб життя` (to lead a healthy lifestyle)
*   `займатися спортом` (to do sports)
*   `ходити в зал` (to go to the gym)
*   `бути у формі` (to be in shape)
*   `активний відпочинок` (active rest/recreation)

## 3. Cultural Hook

1.  **Hydropark "Kachalka" (Kyiv):** A legendary open-air gym in Kyiv's Hydropark, created in the 1970s from scrap metal and industrial chains. It is free, open to everyone, and a unique symbol of Kyiv's "hardcore" fitness culture. It represents the accessibility of sport in Ukraine.
2.  **"Pohid" Culture (Carpathians):** Going for a "pohid" (hike/trek) in the Carpathian mountains is a quintessential Ukrainian active holiday. It often involves gathering mushrooms/berries, sleeping in tents, and cooking "bograch" or "banosh" on a fire. It's more than just walking; it's a connection to nature and tradition.
3.  **Modern Running Boom:** Since 2010s, running events like the **Nova Poshta Half Marathon** have exploded in popularity in all major cities (Kyiv, Lviv, Dnipro), marking a shift from Soviet-era mandatory PE to modern, voluntary, community-driven fitness.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

*   **"Sport" vs "Exercise":** Ukrainians often use `займатися спортом` for any physical activity, even just going to the gym, whereas English speakers might distinguish between "playing sports" and "working out".
*   **Case Government:** The verb `займатися` strictly requires the **Instrumental case** (`займатися йог**ою**`, `займатися біг**ом**`). This is a common error source for learners who might try to use Accusative.
*   **"Active Rest":** The term `активний відпочинок` is a specific concept in Ukrainian (and Slavic languages) denoting leisure that involves physical activity (hiking, kayaking, skiing), distinct from `пасивний відпочинок` (lying on the beach).
*   **Teaching Sequence:**
    1.  Introduce activity verbs (`бігати`, `плавати`).
    2.  Introduce `займатися` + Instrumental.
    3.  Connect with frequency adverbs (`часто`, `рідко`, `щодня`) for routines.
    4.  Discuss "Why?" using `для здоров'я`, `щоб бути сильним` (Purpose constructions).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN Scope (B1):**
*   **Cases:** All cases, with specific focus on **Instrumental** (hobbies) and **Locative** (places: `у парку`, `на стадіоні`).
*   **Verbs:** Motion verbs (unidirectional/multidirectional distinction is relevant: `бігти` vs `бігати`).
*   **Aspect:** Habits (Imp) vs One-time actions (Perf).
*   **Topics:** Health, daily routine, hobbies, nature.

**OUT of Scope (Too Advanced/Specific):**
*   Complex medical terminology related to injuries (C1/C2).
*   Highly technical sports rules or professional athlete jargon.
*   Archaic or very dialectal terms for exercises.
*   Deep physiological descriptions (biochemistry of muscles).
