# Research: News Reading Strategies (B1)

**Module**: B1 M87 "Новини: як читати"  
**Level**: B1 (Intermediate)  
**Topic**: Media Literacy & Reading Strategies  

> **Note**: This research assumes the learner has completed B1 Modules 01-86, consistent with the module plan, despite the prompt's A1 label. Reading authentic news requires B1+ competency.

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

The Ukrainian State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024) and CEFR alignment for **B1 Reading** emphasizes the ability to understand main points of clear standard input on familiar matters.

**Relevant Sections (B1 Level):**
*   **Reading (Читання)**: "Can understand texts that consist mainly of high frequency everyday or job-related language. Can understand the description of events, feelings and wishes in personal letters."
*   **Grammar Focus**:
    *   **Passive Constructions (Пасивні конструкції)**: Essential for news reports (e.g., *було вирішено*, *повідомляється*).
    *   **Participles (Дієприкметники)**: Used heavily in descriptive news (e.g., *зроблений*, *підписаний*).
    *   **Impersonal Sentences (Безособові речення)**: *Важливо знати*, *Варто зазначити*.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Collocations

### High Frequency (Core B1)
*   **новини** (news) — *останні новини* (breaking/latest news)
*   **подія** (event) — *важлива подія* (important event)
*   **світ** (world) — *у світі* (in the world)
*   **війна** (war) — *почалася війна* (war started)
*   **президент** (president) — *заява президента* (president's statement)
*   **уряд** (government) — *рішення уряду* (government decision)
*   **джерело** (source) — *з перевірених джерел* (from verified sources)

### Medium Frequency (Topic-Specific)
*   **ЗМІ (Засоби масової інформації)** (Mass Media)
*   **заголовок** (headline) — *кричущий заголовок* (screaming headline)

*   *Читати новини* (to read news)

*   *Стежити за подіями* (to follow events)
*   *Оголошувати тривогу* (to announce an alert - specific to current context)

## 3. Cultural Hook

*   **The "Telegram" Phenomenon**: Unlike many Western countries, Ukrainians heavily rely on **Telegram channels** for real-time news consumption, especially for air raid alerts and rapid updates from officials (e.g., the President, mayors, governors). This shifts the reading style from long articles to short, rapid-fire posts with emojis and heavy use of "Infinitive Writing" (shortened sentences).
*   **Information Hygiene (Інфогігієна)**: Due to the ongoing war, the concept of "information hygiene" is a daily reality. Ukrainians are culturally conditioned to ask "Who is the source?" (*Хто джерело?*) and check for "IPS/PsyOps" (*ІПСО*). Teaching students to be skeptical of emotional headlines is culturally authentic.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

*   **"Headlinese" Syntax**: Explain that headlines often drop the verb "to be" (*є*) or omit the subject if it's clear.
    *   *English*: "The President is visiting Kyiv."
    *   *Ukrainian Headline*: "Президент відвідав Київ" (Past) or "Президент у Києві" (Locative, verb omitted).
*   **Word Order Inversion**: In news, the *new information* often comes at the end.
    *   *Normal*: "Іван купив хліб."
    *   *News*: "Хліб купив Іван" (Emphasizing WHO bought it, or strictly following Topic-Comment structure).
*   **Passive vs. Active**: English speakers overuse active voice. Ukrainian news prefers "Повідомляється" (It is reported) over "We report".

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (B1):**
*   **Reporting Verbs**: *повідомляти*, *заявляти*, *писати*, *розповідати*.
*   **Passive Participles (Short form basics)**: *зачинено*, *відкрито*, *зроблено*.
*   **Complex Sentences**: Using *що*, *який*, *коли* (Relative clauses are assumed known from B1 M26-28).
*   **Genitive Plural**: Crucial for quantities (*багато людей*, *тисячі гривень*).

**OUT OF SCOPE (Save for B2/C1):**
*   **Active Participles (Active forms ending in -учий/-ачий)**: These are often stylistically poor in Ukrainian anyway; focus on *який...* clauses instead.
*   **Deep Political Jargon**: Terms like *інавгурація*, *імпічмент*, *вето* (unless cognates).
*   **Archaic/Literary Forms**: Found in literature but not in standard news.
*   **Gerunds (Дієприслівники)**: While common in news, deep analysis of their formation is a separate advanced topic; treat them as vocabulary/set phrases if they appear.
