Alignment: The State Standard explicitly lists making oral complaints and returning defective goods in shops, post offices, and banks as a B1 speaking competency. This module directly operationalises that mandate, adding negotiation and feedback as the pragmatic scaffolding learners need to succeed in those situations.

Alignment: Negotiation and compromise-building map directly to the B1 dialogic skill of comparing alternatives and participating in formal discussions for problem-solving.

Alignment: These catalogue items cover the core pragmatic acts of this module: making and rejecting proposals, promising follow-up — all central to feedback and negotiation.

**Note on "зворотний зв'язок":** Not found as a multiword entry in VESUM (expected — it is a free phrase). Component words verified: **зворотний** ✅ (adj) + **зв'язок** ✅ (noun). **Note on "йти на поступки":** Phrasal entry not found in GRAC (expected for fixed idioms). Components verified: **поступка** ✅. Preferred over bare noun — it is the standard collocation in Ukrainian (vs. the Russianism "іти на уступки").

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Книга скарг та пропозицій — abolished 2020.** The Soviet-era physical "complaints and suggestions book" was a mandatory feature of all Ukrainian service establishments under Soviet and post-Soviet law. It was formally abolished as an obligatory requirement by Ukrainian deregulation reform circa 2019–2020, replaced by digital feedback mechanisms (Google Maps reviews, e-petitions, consumer protection portals). This creates a sharp before/after contrast the module uses to anchor the historical transition. *(Source: State deregulation reforms; Wikipedia article "Захист прав споживачів" confirmed at uk.wikipedia.org — consumer protection is described as "гарантований законом контроль за якістю і безпечністю продукції та всіх видів послуг")*

2. **Захист прав споживачів in Ukraine.** Ukrainian consumer protection law derives from the Law of Ukraine "Про захист прав споживачів" (originally 1991, repeatedly amended). The 2024 update (referenced in the plan as new consumer rights law) significantly strengthened digital complaint mechanisms and клієнтоорієнтованість standards. Wikipedia confirms the general legal framework: *"За́хист прав споживачі́в — гарантований законом контроль за якістю і безпечністю продукції та всіх видів послуг і робіт з боку держави та громадсько-суспільний рух на його підтримку."* The module can frame complaints not as confrontational but as a civic right under Ukrainian law.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"обговорювати про проблему"** → **"обговорювати проблему"** — Why: English-influenced ("discuss about") or direct transfer from Polish ("rozmawiać o"). The verb **обговорювати** takes direct Accusative without preposition. Confirmed: **обговорювати** ✅ VESUM.

2. **"робити компроміс"** → **"йти на компроміс" / "досягти компромісу"** — Why: Calque of English "make a compromise" or Russian "делать компромисс". Ukrainian uses motion-based or process-based collocations: *йти на компроміс, знайти компроміс, досягти компромісу*. Confirmed: **досягти** ✅ VESUM.

3. **Using bare imperative for polite requests** → **softened conditional or modal forms** — Why: At A2 level, learners learn imperatives (дайте, скажіть). At B1, the pragmatic upgrade is learning to replace these with *"Я хотів би...", "Чи не могли б ви...?", "Прошу..."*. The textbook corpus confirms this explicitly (Grade 8 Avramenko: *"Чи можна попросити... Чи не могли б ви повідомити..."* as markers of особлива ввічливість).

4. **Confusion between відгук and зворотний зв'язок** — Why: Both can translate as "feedback" in English. **Відгук** = a one-off review or response (залишити відгук online); **зворотний зв'язок** = a systematic feedback process (давати зворотний зв'язок у команді). Distinguishing these is explicitly in the plan and is a genuine pragmatic gap for B1 learners.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **"Зворотний зв'язок" is a modern Ukrainian calque** — treat it as the correct professional term, not a borrowing to avoid. It is attested in VESUM and widely used in Ukrainian educational, business, and HR discourse. Do not suggest Russian alternatives.

2. **Textbook grounding for the politeness register:** Grade 8 Zabolotnyi (tier 1, NUS 2025) and Avramenko (tier 1, NUS 2025) both treat the formal dialogue register explicitly — the module content should reflect this school-curriculum progression (learners have seen formal register at secondary level).

3. **GRAC data reveals collocational asymmetry:** "переговори" is extremely high-frequency (66.70 IPM) dominated by political/war contexts in the corpus (especially 2022–2024). Content writing should explicitly anchor the term in everyday and professional contexts to counter this political-discourse dominance: переговори з постачальником, переговори щодо умов оренди, etc.

4. **Register contrast is the structural spine of this module:** Every section pivots on the same axis — aggressive/passive → strategically polite. The textbook material confirms this is the B1 pragmatics focus (§4.5.1). Phase B should build each section around paired examples (incorrect/stilted vs. correct/natural).

5. **State Standard §1.4.1.2.1 explicitly names shops, banks, and post offices as B1 complaint contexts** — the plan's Section 4 is directly on-spec and should be the most detailed section (word budget: 1000 words, fully justified).

6. **Conditional mood (§4.2.3.3) is the grammar anchor** — it was introduced earlier in B1 but this module is where it gets its pragmatic payoff. Frame it as "the grammar of politeness" rather than re-teaching it from scratch.
