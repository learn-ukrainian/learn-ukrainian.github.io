## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

**Core Topics**: Music Genres, Performance, Emotions/Impressions.

### High-Frequency (Essential B1)
*   **гурт** (m) — band (Note: *не* "банда", *не* "група" in musical context usually)
*   **співак / співачка** — singer (m/f)
*   **пісня** — song
*   **виконавець** — performer / artist
*   **альбом** — album
*   **концерт** — concert
*   **звучати** — to sound (verb)
*   **голос** — voice
*   **сцена** — stage

### Medium-Frequency (Contextual)
*   **перемога** — victory (context: Eurovision)
*   **сучасний** — contemporary / modern
*   **народний** — folk / people's
*   **стиль** — style
*   **шанувальник** — fan / admirer

### Common Collocations
*   **слухати музику** — to listen to music
*   **ходити на концерт** — to go to a concert
*   **виступати на сцені** — to perform on stage
*   **зайняти перше місце** — to take first place
*   **писати пісні** — to write songs

## 3. Cultural Hook

1.  **Eurovision as National Sport**: Ukraine takes Eurovision extremely seriously and is one of the most successful countries in the contest's history (Wins: Ruslana 2004, Jamala 2016, Kalush Orchestra 2022). It is a major platform for cultural diplomacy (soft power).
2.  **"Oii u luzi chervona kalyna"**: A WWI-era Sich Riflemen song that became the global anthem of resistance in 2022 after Andriy Khlyvnyuk (Boombox) sang it in Kyiv via Instagram. Pink Floyd later sampled it. It bridges history (1914) and modernity (2022).
3.  **The "Bayraktar" Phenomenon**: The rapid creation of war-time folk/pop songs (e.g., "Bayraktar", "Stefania") reflects the Ukrainian tradition of documenting history through song in real-time.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
*   **"Band" vs "Group"**: While "група" exists, **гурт** is the specific Ukrainian term for a musical band. "Група" can imply a generic group of people.
*   **Instrumental Case for Interests**: "I am interested in music" -> "Я цікавлюся музикою" (Instrumental case, no preposition). This is a common error (students often try to translate "in").
*   **"To play"**:
    *   Play an instrument: грати **на** гітарі (Locative + preposition *на*).
    *   Play a game: грати **у** футбол (Accusative + preposition *у*).
    *   *Different from English where preposition usage varies or is absent.*

### Common Learner Errors
*   Using "любити" for all preferences. Encourage: **подобатися** (dative subject), **обожнювати** (adore), **захоплюватися** (be into).
*   Declining foreign names: "Я слухаю *Linkin Park*" (often indeclinable if treated as a quote/label, or strictly transliterated and declined). Beginners struggle with whether to decline foreign brand/band names.

## 5. Scope Boundaries (B1 Level)

### IN Scope (Assumed Known)
*   **Cases**: Full declension system (Genitive, Dative, Accusative, Instrumental, Locative).
*   **Tenses**: Past, Present, Future (Imperfective/Perfective aspects).
*   **Complex Sentences**: Using *тому що*, *який*, *коли*.
*   **Government**: Verbs like *слухати (кого? що?)*, *грати на (чому?)*.

### OUT Scope (Avoid)
*   **Archaic/Poetic forms**: Often found in folk songs (*зелененький*, *козаченько* - diminutives are fine, but obscure dialect words should be glossed).
*   **Deep Musical Terminology**: Solfeggio terms, complex production slang.
*   **Complex Gerunds**: Active participles (*співаючий*) are rare in spoken Ukrainian; use relative clauses instead (*той, хто співає*).
