## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

Adverbial participles are more common in written/literary styles but appear in high-frequency phrases in speech.

### High-Frequency (Essential for B1)
*   **читаючи** (while reading) — *Читаючи книгу, я заснув.*
*   **йдучи** (while walking) — *Йдучи додому, я зустрів друга.*
*   **знаючи** (knowing) — *Знаючи правду, він мовчав.*
*   **думаючи** (thinking) — *Він сидів, думаючи про неї.*
*   **кажучи** (saying/speaking) — *Чесно кажучи (Honestly speaking...)* — **Fixed Phrase**

### Medium-Frequency (Contextual)
*   **роблячи** (doing/making)
*   **співаючи** (singing)
*   **дивлячись** (looking/watching) — *Note: Often functions almost as a preposition "depending on" or just "watching".*

### Collocations (A1/A2 Primitives transformed to B1)
*   **сидіти, читаючи** (to sit reading)
*   **іти, слухаючи музику** (to walk listening to music)
*   **лежати, мріючи** (to lie dreaming)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**Shevchenko's Legacy:**
Adverbial participles give Ukrainian a melodic, flowing quality often associated with Taras Shevchenko's poetry.

*   **Fact:** The famous lines typically use these forms to chain actions without breaking the rhythm.
*   **Quote:** *"Реве та стогне Дніпр широкий, сердитий вітер завива..."* (Not a participle here, but the *style* is iconic).
*   **Better Example:** *"Ідучи, не оглядайся"* (While walking, don't look back) — Common folk wisdom about moving forward in life.
*   **Proverb:** *"Не знаючи броду, не лізь у воду"* (Not knowing the ford, don't climb into the water). This is the most culturally ubiquitous use of the imperfective participle *знаючи* (knowing). It teaches caution.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Concept: Simultaneity
*   **English Equivalent:** "Doing X, I did Y" or "While doing X..."
*   **Difference:** Ukrainian strictly separates Imperfective (Simultaneous) from Perfective (Sequential). This module focuses ONLY on Simultaneous.

### The "Golden Rule" (Common Error)
*   **Same Subject Rule:** The action in the participle MUST be performed by the **Subject** of the main sentence.
    *   *Correct:* Йдучи додому, **я** побачив парк. (Walking home, **I** saw a park.)
    *   *Incorrect (Typical Error):* Йдучи додому, **почався** дощ. (Walking home, the rain started.) -> The rain was not walking home.

### Teaching Sequence
1.  **Recall Present Tense:** Start with *вони читають*, *вони знають*.
2.  **Derivation:** Show the stem extraction (*чита-ють* -> *чита-*).
3.  **Suffixation:** Add *-ючи* (*читаючи*).
4.  **Sentence Integration:** Add main clause.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1 Level)
*   **Derivation:** From Present Tense Stem (3rd Person Plural).
    *   I Conjugation (-уть/-ють) -> **-учи/-ючи**.
    *   II Conjugation (-ать/-ять) -> **-ачи/-ячи**.
*   **Usage:** Simultaneous actions only (*недоконаний вид*).
*   **Reflexive verbs:** *сміючись* (laughing) - *requires attention to -сь particle*.
*   **Fixed expressions:** *чесно кажучи* (honestly speaking), *незважаючи на* (despite - treat as preposition).

### OUT of Scope (Save for later)
*   **Perfective Participles:** *прочитавши*, *зробивши* (Sequential action / "Having done").
*   **Passive Participles:** *зроблений*, *написаний*.
*   **Adjectival Participles (Active):** *читаючий* (the reading man) - Stylistically discouraged in modern Ukrainian anyway.
*   **Complex Syntax:** Deeply nested participle clauses (Keep sentences simple: Main Clause + Participle Phrase).
