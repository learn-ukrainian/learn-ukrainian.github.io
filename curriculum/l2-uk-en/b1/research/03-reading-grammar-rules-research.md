## Cultural Hooks
1. **Meletiy Smotrytsky (1619)**: Written in 1619, his "Grammar" was the standard textbook for Slavic languages for over 150 years. He was the first to systematically codify the letter **ґ** and the system of cases (vidminky) used today.
2. **Ivan Ohiienko (Metropolitan Ilarion)**: A crusader for the "Purity" of the language in the 20th century, he established norms to purge "surzhyk" and Russian calques, emphasizing that grammar reflects the nation's soul.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **"Закінчення" vs "Кінець"**: Learners often use "кінець" (end of a movie/stick) when talking about word endings.
   * *Correct*: "У цього слова нульове закінчення." (Not "нульовий кінець").
2. **Ignoring "Винятки" (Exceptions)**: Learners often apply rules rigidly (overgeneralization), missing the instruction "крім" (except) or "виняток".
   * *Fix*: Highlight instruction keywords like "за винятком".
3. **Misinterpreting "Утворіть" (Form/Create)**: Learners confuse this with general "make" (робити).
   * *Context*: "Утворіть минулий час" (Form the past tense).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Tone**: The persona is a "Law Student" / "Senior Specialist" - the tone should be structured, logical, and slightly analytical, treating grammar like a system of laws/rules.
- **Visuals**: Use "textbook excerpts" as visual aids — simulate a page from a Ukrainian grammar book and have the user "decode" it.
- **Key Distinction**: Ensure clear differentiation between "розмовна мова" (colloquial) and "літературна мова" (standard/literary), as this is a key B1 stylistic competence.
