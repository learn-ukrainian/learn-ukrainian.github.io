# Дослідження: Вид у минулому: одного разу vs щодня

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.1: "утворення форм роду і числа дієслів минулого часу недоконаного і доконаного виду: хотів, хотіла, хотіло, хотіли; побачив, побачила, побачило, побачили"
Alignment: Systematizes the formation and functional contrast between imperfective past tense (process/habitual) and perfective past tense (single completed event) in narrative contexts.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Folk Tale Opener**: «Одного разу...» (Once upon a time) acts as a canonical trigger that immediately shifts Ukrainian language into a narrative storytelling mode, anchoring the sentence in the perfective aspect.
2. **Habitual Hospitality**: Cultural traditions denoted by time markers like «щонеділі» (every Sunday) or «на свята» (on holidays) are deeply associated with recurring rituals (gathering, cooking borscht, visiting relatives) and fundamentally pair with the imperfective aspect.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The "Perfective for Habituals" Trap** → Incorrect: «Він прочитав книгу щодня». Correct: «Він читав книгу щодня». — Learners mistakenly prioritize the "completion" of the daily act over the "frequency marker" (щодня), which demands the imperfective.
2. **English "Used to" Mismapping** → Attempting to translate "I used to do X" literally (e.g., using a separate verb) instead of understanding that the Ukrainian past imperfective natively expresses habitual past actions («Я робив»).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Frame the Ukrainian aspectual system independently. Describe how Ukrainian natively handles habits and events. Do NOT use Russian as a baseline or for comparisons ("Unlike Russian...").
- **Language Purity**: Ensure 100% immersion (B1.1 requires all Ukrainian explanations). Completely avoid Russianisms (e.g., use «їсти» not «кушати», «отримувати» not «получати»).
- **Vowel Pronunciation**: Maintain strict adherence to Ukrainian phonetic rules (о = [ɔ], е = [ɛ]).
