## Cultural Hooks

**1. Культ протягу (The Draft Cult) — verified cultural pattern**
The belief that a cross-breeze (протяг) causes illness is widespread in Ukrainian everyday culture. In public transport, at workplaces, and in homes, Ukrainians routinely close windows/doors citing протяг as a health risk even in warm weather. The phenomenon is attested pan-Slavic but particularly intense in Ukrainian and related cultures; linguistically, боятися протягу and зачинити через протяг are fixed collocations. This is a reliable cultural hook with strong learner engagement potential (surprise/relatability).
Source: attested in GRAC corpus (frequent collocates of протяг in domestic contexts), culturally documented.

**2. Калина як символ здоров'я та культури**
Калина звичайна (Viburnum opulus) is simultaneously a national cultural symbol and a folk medicine staple. Its berries are used for чай з калини for colds and as a source of Vitamin C. Wikipedia confirms it as a "high shrub, 2–4m, family Adoxaceae." Unlike many folk remedies, kalyna-based tea is supported by modern pharmacology (high ascorbic acid content). This dual cultural-medicinal status makes it ideal for a B1 module combining vocabulary and culture.
Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Калина_звичайна

**3. Фітотерапія як медична практика**
Wikipedia confirms: "Траволікування (фітотерапія) — в народній медицині система специфічних методів лікування й профілактики захворювань з використанням фітопрепаратів." Ukrainian pharmacies (аптеки) routinely stock herbal preparations (фітопрепарати) alongside conventional medicine — a concrete, observable fact learners can connect to real-world experience.
Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Траволікування

## Common Learner Errors

**1. Case error: *хворіти грипом* → хворіти на грип**
- Pattern: English speakers (and learners influenced by other Slavic languages) may use Instrumental case by analogy ("to be sick *with* the flu").
- Ukrainian requires: **хворіти на** + Accusative: *хворіти на грип, на кір, на ангіну, на застуду.*
- Why it happens: Instrumental case for accompaniment is heavily drilled; learners over-generalize it. Some Slavic languages do use Instrumental here.
- Drill strategy: Present the preposition **на** as a fixed collocation marker with хворіти, not as a directional preposition.

**2. Verb confusion: *відчувати* vs. *почуватися***
- Error: *Я відчуваю добре / Я відчуваю себе добре* (calque of "I feel good")
- Correct: **Я почуваюся добре** (internal state, reflexive)
- Distinction: **відчувати** = to sense/perceive an external stimulus (відчувати біль = to feel pain as a sensation; відчувати запах = to smell something). **Почуватися** = to be in a state of well-being.
- Both are correct verbs — the error is using відчувати for the general state of feeling.

**3. Agreement error: боліти + body part**
- Error: *У мене болять голова / У мене болить зуби*
- Rule: **боліти** agrees with the body part (the grammatical subject in Nominative), not with the experiencer.
  - Singular: У мене **болить** голова / живіт / горло
  - Plural: У мене **болять** зуби / вуха / очі / ноги
- Why it happens: The "у мене" frame leads learners to treat мене as the subject; English "I have a headache" reinforces this misparse.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **"народна медицина" as a phrase:** VESUM didn't find it as a single entry (expected — it's a noun phrase, both constituent words are verified). Use freely in prose.

2. **Протяг homograph risk:** The word протяг has two meanings: (a) draft/draught [noun, cultural] and (b) temporal use in *на протязі* (but note: *на протязі* is non-normative; *протягом* is correct). Phase B content should explicitly gloss протяг as "протяг [повітря]" (air draft) at first use to prevent confusion.

3. **Register differentiation (§4.5.1):** The formal doctor register features: ставити діагноз, проходити обстеження, здавати аналізи, скаржитися на (+ Accusative), призначати лікування. The informal register: нездужати, кепсько почуватися, підхопити застуду, прихворіти. Both need explicit labeling in content.

4. **Decolonized framing:** Ukrainian народна медицина is an independently documented tradition rooted in Slavic botanical knowledge — frame it as a self-sufficient cultural practice. Avoid framing it as shared with or derivative of any neighbor's tradition. The kalyna's role is distinctively documented in Ukrainian ethnobotany.

5. **Word target strategy:** At 4000 words for B1, the five sections (600 + 1000 + 800 + 1000 + 600) are tight. Phase B should write toward 10–15% overshoot on each section (~660 / 1100 / 880 / 1100 / 660) to ensure the total gate passes after editing.

6. **Textbook evidence:** Avramenko Grade 7 corpus (search result 2) confirms *почуваємося* in a health context (Grade 5 Golub: "Завдяки корисній їжі ми почуваємося енергійними") — usable as a model sentence pattern. Grade 7 Avramenko has лікування очей, підтверджуючи botanical medicine register in educational Ukrainian.
