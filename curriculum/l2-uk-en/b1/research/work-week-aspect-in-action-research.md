## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

**Core Vocabulary (High Frequency):**
*   **працювати** (Imperfective) - to work (process).
*   **зробити** (Perfective) - to do/finish (result).
*   **писати / написати** (Imp/Perf) - to write / to finish writing (often used with "звіт" or "лист").
*   **завдання** (Neut, n) - task.
*   **звіт** (Masc, n) - report.
*   **зустріч** (Fem, n) - meeting.
*   **офіс** (Masc, n) - office.
*   **колега** (Masc/Fem, n) - colleague.
*   **начальник / керівник** (Masc, n) - boss / manager.

**Contextual Vocabulary (B1 Specific):**
*   **дедлайн** (Masc, n) - deadline (Loanword, extremely common).
*   **проект** (Masc, n) - project.
*   **графік** (Masc, n) - schedule.
*   **план** (Masc, n) - plan.
*   **нарада** (Fem, n) - meeting (more formal than зустріч, e.g., board meeting).
*   **відпустка** (Fem, n) - vacation/leave.

**Common Collocations:**
*   *Писати звіт* (Process - "I was writing the report all day") vs. *Написати звіт* (Result - "I have written the report").
*   *Робити завдання* (Process) vs. *Зробити завдання* (Result).
*   *Дотримуватися дедлайну* (To meet the deadline).
*   *Планувати тиждень* (To plan the week).

## 3. Cultural Hook

1.  **The "Zvit" (Report) Culture:** In Ukrainian work culture, especially in traditional sectors and government, the concept of **звіт** (report) and **звітний період** (reporting period) is significant. It implies a formal finalization of work. Using the *Perfective* aspect (*я написав звіт*, *ми здали проект*) is crucial here—your boss cares about the *result* (Perfective), not just that you *were working* (Imperfective) on it.
2.  **"Deadline":** The English word "deadline" (**дедлайн**) has been fully assimilated into modern Ukrainian business language, especially in IT and corporate sectors. It declins like a masculine noun (*до дедлайну*, *після дедлайну*). It represents the pressure point where *Perfective* verbs become mandatory (*Треба закінчити до дедлайну*).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Key Challenges for Learners:**
*   **English Interference:** English speakers often try to use "Present Perfect" logic. In Ukrainian, "I have done it" is simply **Past Perfective** (*Я зробив*).
*   **Process vs. Result:** Students struggle to decide between *Я писав звіт* (I was writing - describing the process/time spent) and *Я написав звіт* (I wrote - focusing on the completed object). In a work status meeting, confusing these can sound like you didn't finish the job.
*   **"Present" Mistake:** Learners often try to conjugate Perfective verbs in the Present tense (*Я зроблю*), not realizing that strictly forms the **Future** tense in Ukrainian.
*   **Repetition:** The distinction that *Imperfective* is used for repeated actions (*Кожного понеділка ми проводимо нараду*) is a key B1 concept.

**Teaching Recommendations:**
1.  **Contrast Pairs:** Always teach the pair (*робити/зробити*) together in context.
2.  **Timeline Visuals:** Show a line (Process/Imp) vs. a Dot/Checkmark (Result/Perf).
3.  **Context Triggers:** Drill with trigger words: *вчора цілий день* (Imp), *вже* (Perf), *щотижня* (Imp), *нарешті* (Perf).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN Scope (B1 Level):**
*   **Tenses:** Past (Imp vs Perf), Future (Imp vs Perf).
*   **Aspect Pairs:** Common work verbs (*писати/написати*, *робити/зробити*, *читати/прочитати*, *надсилати/надіслати*).
*   **Imperative:** Basic commands (*Напишіть звіт!* - Do it once/result vs. *Пишіть звіти вчасно* - Do it regularly).
*   **Infinitive:** After *хочу*, *треба*, *можу* (*Треба написати звіт*).

**OUT of Scope (Too Advanced):**
*   **Participles:** Active/Passive participles (*написаний*, *зроблений*) are B2 grammar. Use "SVO" structure instead (*Я написав звіт* instead of *Звіт написаний мною*).
*   **Verbal Adverbs (Diyepryslivnyk):** (*Працюючи над проектом...*) - B2 grammar.
*   **Complex Bureaucratic Style:** Avoid overly formal "kanselyaryzm" (officialese) constructions. Stick to standard professional communication.
