## Cultural Hooks

1. **40-годинний робочий тиждень — законодавче закріплення.** The Ukrainian Labour Code (Кодекс законів про працю України) regulates working time: the standard is 40 hours/week (5×8h). This is codified statutory law, not convention — directly relevant for the "Ділові ситуації" section when discussing scheduling. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Кодекс_законів_про_працю_України.

2. **Дедлайн vs. реченець — мовна дискусія.** The word `дедлайн` (English "deadline") entered Ukrainian primarily via IT culture in the 2000s–2010s. Language purists advocate `реченець` (attested in older Ukrainian, IPM 0.28 — nearly obsolete in modern usage) and `кінцевий термін` (official register). The GRAC corpus confirms `дедлайн` is the living form in both casual and official contexts. This registers as a genuine sociolinguistic tension — excellent for the plan's "Authentic Dialogue" scenario.

3. **Планірка — радянський канцеляризм.** The word `планірка` (a short planning meeting) is a borrowing from Soviet Russian office culture (`планёрка`). Its Ukrainian equivalent is `нарада` (GRAC: 11,675 occurrences, far more frequent) or more precisely `оперативна нарада`. Teaching learners to replace `планірка` with `нарада` is a genuine decolonization move grounded in contemporary usage data.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Perfective in the present tense** → *Я напишу звіт зараз* (incorrect — perfective has no present tense form) → **Я пишу звіт зараз** (НДВ). *Why it happens:* English present continuous ("I am writing") has no formal aspect distinction; learners map English simple present to Ukrainian perfective by association with "pointedness."

2. **Imperfective future confusion (складена vs. синтетична форма)** → Learners mix register: `буду писати` (neutral, both written/spoken) vs. `писатиму` (slightly more literary/formal). *Why it happens:* The synthetic future is taught late and underdrilled; learners default to the analytic form in all contexts. B1 Standard explicitly requires BOTH forms (§4.2.3.1, lines 2284–2289).

3. **Process/result confusion in past tense** → *Я робив звіт* (spent time on it, possibly unfinished) vs. *Я зробив звіт* (it is complete). *Why it happens:* English "I wrote the report" is ambiguous about completion; Ukrainian encodes this distinction obligatorily. Without explicit drilling, learners default to НДВ for all past events.

4. **Gender agreement with `дедлайн`** → *жорстка дедлайн* (incorrect, feminine agreement) → **жорсткий дедлайн** (masculine). *Why it happens:* English "deadline" is gender-neutral; Ukrainian loanword assignment is unpredictable for learners. VESUM confirms masculine.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Synthetic future is mandatory:** §4.2.3.1 explicitly lists both `буду складати` (analytic) and `складатиму` (synthetic) as B1 requirements. The section on planning the work week must drill BOTH forms explicitly — not just mention one.
- **Frequency-graded vocabulary:** `завдання` should appear with highest density; `реченець` should appear once as a cultural aside only — do not teach it as active vocabulary.
- **`нарада` collocations confirmed from GRAC:** провести нараду, бути на нараді, під час наради — all high-frequency patterns safe to use as examples.
- **Textbook framing of aspect:** Grade 7 Литвинова (tier 1, NUS 2022) and Авраменко (tier 1) both frame aspect via the завершеність/незавершеність opposition with questions `що зробити? / що робити?` — content writing should echo this framing for internal consistency.
- **Деколонізація — планірка:** Treat explicitly as a Soviet borrowing. Frame `нарада` as the native Ukrainian term with deep attestation, not as a "replacement" — it predates `планірка`.
- **`дедлайн` masculine:** Agreement examples must use masculine adjectives throughout.
- **Register table:** The plan calls for нарада (formal) vs. планірка (colloquial) — extend this to кінцевий термін (official documents) vs. дедлайн (office/IT) vs. реченець (purist/archaic). A 3-row register table would serve the content well.
