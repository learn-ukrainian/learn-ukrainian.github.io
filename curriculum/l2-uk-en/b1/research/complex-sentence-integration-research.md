# Research: Integration of Complex Sentences (B1 M38)

**Module**: B1 M38 "Підсумок" (Complex Sentence Integration)  
**Topic**: Combining multiple clause types (Coordination + Subordination)  
**Level**: B1 (Intermediate)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, aligned with CEFR B1 descriptors, the learner is expected to demonstrate the following syntactic competencies:

> **Syntax of the Complex Sentence (B1 Level):**
> *   The ability to combine simple sentences into complex structures to express logical relationships (cause, effect, condition, purpose, time).
> *   Mastery of both **Compound Sentences (Складносурядні речення)** using coordinating conjunctions (*і, а, але, та*) and **Complex Sentences (Складнопідрядні речення)** using subordinating conjunctions and allied words (*що, який, де, коли, тому що, якщо, хоча*).
> *   The capacity to produce multi-clause sentences (3+ clauses) that integrate different types of connections (e.g., a relative clause embedded within a conditional sentence).
> *   Correct usage of punctuation marks (commas) between clauses in complex structures.

This module specifically addresses the integration of these skills, moving beyond isolated clause practice to fluid, multi-component sentence construction suitable for B1 narrative tasks.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

This topic relies heavily on **connectors (сполучники)** and **conjunction words (сполучні слова)** rather than content vocabulary.

### High-Frequency (Core B1 Tools)
*   **і / й / та** (and) — Ubiquitous.
*   **а / але** (but/and contrast) — Essential for coordination.
*   **що** (that/which) — The most frequent subordinator.
*   **який / яка / яке / які** (which/who) — Essential relative pronoun.
*   **тому що / бо** (because) — Standard causality.
*   **коли** (when) — Time.
*   **якщо** (if) — Condition.
*   **щоб** (in order to / that) — Purpose.
*   **де / куди** (where/whither) — Location.

### Mid-Frequency (B1 Enhancement)
*   **хоча** (although) — Concession.
*   **через те що** (due to the fact that) — Emphatic causality.
*   **як тільки** (as soon as) — Precise timing.
*   **перед тим як** (before doing...) — Temporal sequence.
*   **після того як** (after doing...) — Temporal sequence.
*   **для того щоб** (in order to - emphatic) — Explicit purpose.
*   **так, що** (so that) — Result.

### Collocations & Phrases
*   **Я думаю, що...** (I think that...)
*   **Людина, яка...** (The person who...)
*   **Місце, де...** (The place where...)
*   **Якщо ти хочеш, то...** (If you want, then...)
*   **Зробив це, щоб...** (Did this to...)

## 3. Cultural Hook

### The "Endless Sentence" of Classical Literature
Ukrainian classical literature (19th-early 20th century) often features incredibly long, multi-layered complex sentences that can span an entire paragraph. Writers like **Ivan Franko** or **Oksana Zabuzhko** (contemporary) are famous for their "syntactic architecture."
*   **Cultural Fact**: In Ukrainian schools, the ultimate test of literacy is the **dictation (диктант)**. These texts are often chosen specifically for their complex syntactic structure, requiring students to intuitively "feel" where the clauses end and begin. The placement of commas in complex sentences is a rigorous indicator of an educated person in Ukraine.
*   **Comparison**: Unlike modern English, which favors short, punchy sentences (Subject-Verb-Object), high-style Ukrainian values the "period" (період) — a long, rhythmic sentence structure with balanced clauses. Mastering this "flow" distinguishes a B1 learner from an A2 learner who speaks in staccato bursts.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
1.  **Punctuation (The Comma Rule)**: In Ukrainian, **commas are mandatory** before almost all subordinating conjunctions (*що, який, коли, якщо*). English is loose ("I think that..." - no comma); Ukrainian is strict (*Я думаю, що...* - comma required). This is the #1 error for English speakers.
2.  **Word Order Flexibility**: In complex sentences, the subordinate clause can often precede the main clause for emphasis (*Коли я прийшов, вона вже спала*), which is similar to English, but the internal word order of the clauses is more flexible in Ukrainian.
3.  **Gender/Number Agreement**: Relative pronouns (*який/яка*) must agree with the antecedent. English "which" is invariable. This adds a layer of morphological difficulty to the syntactic task.

### Common Learner Errors
*   **Missing Commas**: *Я знаю що ти тут* (Incorrect) vs *Я знаю, що ти тут* (Correct).
*   **Wrong "Which" Case**: Using *який* (Nominative) when the role in the sub-clause requires Accusative or Locative.
    *   *Error*: *Книга, яка я читаю...* (The book which I am reading...)
    *   *Correction*: *Книга, яку я читаю...* (Accusative).
*   **Overloading**: Trying to translate English participle phrases ("Being tired, he went home") directly, instead of using the safer B1 strategy of a full clause (*Оскільки він був втомлений, він пішов додому*).

### Teaching Sequence Recommendations
1.  **Review**: Quick check of individual clause types (Relative, Conditional, Temporal).
2.  **Integration**: Start with "Chain" sentences (A + B + C). *Я подзвонив другу, який живе у Києві, щоб запитати про новини.* (Main + Relative + Purpose).
3.  **Punctuation Drill**: Explicit drills on where to put the comma.
4.  **Production**: Give learners 3 simple sentences and ask them to combine them into one elegant complex sentence using specific connectors.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1 Target)
*   **Combining 2-3 Clauses**: Main clause + 1-2 subordinate clauses.
*   **Standard Connectors**: *і, але, що, який, коли, якщо, щоб, тому що, хоча*.
*   **Case Agreement in Relative Clauses**: Essential skill.
*   **Correlative Words**: *той, хто... / те, що...* (He who... / That which...).
*   **Mixed Types**: Combining a conditional with a relative clause (*Якщо ти побачиш людину, яка...*).

### OUT of Scope (Save for B2/C1)
*   **Deeply Nested Participle Phrases**: Replacing clauses with active/passive participles (*читаючий*, *зроблений*) is a B2 skill. At B1, we prefer full clauses (*який читає*, *який зробили*).
*   **Archaic/Literary Connectors**: *позаяк* (since/as), *мов* (as if), *начебто* (allegedly/as though), *даремно що* (despite).
*   **Complex Subjunctive Moods**: Beyond standard *якби*.
*   **Stylistic Inversion**: Highly poetic word order that disrupts standard clause boundaries.
