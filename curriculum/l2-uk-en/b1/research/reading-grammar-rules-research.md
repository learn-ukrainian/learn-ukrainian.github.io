# Research: B1 M03 "Читаємо граматичні правила" (A1 Adaptation)

**Level:** A1 (Beginner)
**Topic:** Basic Metalanguage & Understanding Instructions
**Prior Knowledge:** Alphabet & Pronunciation Only

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (2024)**, Level A1 (Початковий рівень):

> **Розділ IV. Мовленнєві вміння. Читання.**
> "Здобувач розуміє дуже короткі, прості тексти, поодинокі фрази та слова, знайомі імена, слова та дуже прості речення, наприклад, у оголошеннях, плакатах або каталогах."

Specifically relevant to this module is the ability to understand **basic instructions** in an educational context:
> "Розуміння простих інструкцій та завдань у навчальному процесі (наприклад: 'читайте', 'слухайте', 'пишіть')."

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

At the absolute beginner level (A1.1), students need only the highest-frequency "classroom interaction" words to navigate future grammar lessons.

**High Frequency (Must Know):**
*   **Слово** (word) — Essential for pointing at language.
*   **Речення** (sentence) — Basic unit of grammar.
*   **Питання** (question) — Visualized with "?".
*   **Відповідь** (answer) — Visualized with instructions.
*   **Правило** (rule) — The concept of a grammar rule.
*   **Приклад** (example) — Often marked as "Наприклад".
*   **Вправа** (exercise) — Header for activities.
*   **Сторінка** (page) — For navigation.

**Common Collocations (A1):**
*   **Нове слово** (new word)
*   **Це правило** (this is a rule)
*   **Дивись приклад** (look at the example)
*   **Номер вправи** (exercise number)

**Lower Frequency (Passive/Contextual):**
*   **Помилка** (mistake)
*   **Виняток** (exception) — Too complex for A1, teach as "Special case".
*   **Граматика** (grammar) — The word itself is international but needs recognition.

## 3. Cultural Hook

*   **Formal Education Culture:** Ukrainian education traditionally places high value on structured learning and "correctness" (грамотність). Textbooks often use formal imperatives even for children. Understanding instructions like "Увага!" (Attention!) reflects this respect for rules.
*   **The "Rule Box" (Рамка):** In Ukrainian textbooks, grammar rules are almost always enclosed in a border or colored box (often yellow or blue). Teaching students to visually identify "The Box" = "The Rule" is a cultural shortcut to navigating Ukrainian materials.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Differences from English:**
*   **Instructional Imperatives:** Ukrainian uses plural imperatives (Читайте, Пишіть) for politeness/groups, whereas English just uses the base verb (Read, Write).
*   **Gendered Terms:** Even metalanguage words have gender (*слово* is neuter, *речення* is neuter, *питання* is neuter — good cluster for teaching neuter gender later).

**Common Learner Errors:**
*   Confusing **слово** (word) with **світ** (world) due to phonetic similarity.
*   Ignoring the **header/instruction** and jumping straight to the text (cultural reading habit).
*   Trying to translate every word in a rule instead of scanning for keywords.

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  **Visual Recognition:** Identify "Rule Boxes" vs. "Exercises" without reading text.
2.  **Keywords:** Match icons to words (Ear -> Слухайте, Pen -> Пишіть).
3.  **Basic Concepts:** Distinguish "Letter" (Буква) -> "Word" (Слово) -> "Sentence" (Речення).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (A1 Beginner):**
*   Basic nouns for language parts: *буква, слово, речення, текст*.
*   Basic instructional verbs (imperative): *читайте, слухайте, пишіть, говоріть*.
*   Visual markers: *! (Увага), ? (Питання)*.
*   Cognates: *граматика, старт, фініш*.

**OUT OF SCOPE (Do NOT Teach):**
*   **Case Names:** Do not use "Nominative", "Genitive", etc. Use "Form 1", "Form 2" or visual changes.
*   **Parts of Speech terms:** Avoid *іменник* (noun), *дієслово* (verb), *прикметник* (adjective). Use "Item words", "Action words" or color coding.
*   **Complex Syntax:** No "Subject", "Predicate", "Clause".
*   **Explanation Text:** Do not expect students to read *definitions* of rules. They should only identify *where* the rule is.
