## Cultural Hooks
1. **UNESCO Recognition:** On July 1, 2022, UNESCO inscribed the culture of Ukrainian borscht cooking on the List of Intangible Cultural Heritage. This was a significant decolonization victory, officially recognizing borscht as distinctly Ukrainian rather than a generic "Soviet" or "Russian" soup.
2. **The "New Ukrainian Cuisine":** Spearheaded by modern chefs like Ievhen Klopotenko, there is a massive movement to revive authentic, pre-Soviet Ukrainian recipes, rejecting the standardized "GOST" catering menus imposed during the Soviet era.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Accusative vs. Partitive Genitive:** Using the Accusative for unspecified quantities of food (e.g., "Я хочу борщ" instead of "Я хочу борщу"). *Correction:* Use the Genitive case (Partitive) when asking for "some" of a dish or drink.
2. **Gender of "Кава":** Assuming "кава" is masculine or neuter due to foreign associations. *Correction:* "Кава" is strictly feminine in Ukrainian ("смачна кава", not "смачний кава").

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing:** Frame Ukrainian cuisine as an independent, rich, and historically rooted culinary tradition. Do not compare dishes to Russian equivalents. Focus on regional diversity (Poltava, Lviv, Odesa) to showcase the breadth of the culture.
- **Agency:** Ensure Ukrainians are the active subjects in historical notes (e.g., "Українці зберегли ці рецепти", not "Рецепти були збережені").
- **No English:** This is a B1 module. Explanations of Partitive Genitive and etiquette must be 100% in Ukrainian.
