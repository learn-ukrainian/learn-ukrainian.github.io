# Дослідження: Колокації та вирази

## State Standard Reference

§4.5.1.1 (B1, lines 2437–2442): **«Стилістичні засоби лексики: антоніми: тут – там, весело – сумно, явний – таємний, дорогий – дешевий; синоніми: друг, приятель, товариш; епітети: веселі птахи, сонячна усмішка; метафори: прийшла осінь, місто спить.»**

Alignment: The module addresses §4.5.1.1 (stylistic lexical means) and §4.5.1 (formal/informal communication) by developing learners' lexical competence through collocations — a more applied dimension of synonym-choice and precise lexical combination. The focus on calque-correction maps directly to the "lexical error" (лексична помилка) concept taught in school curricula at grades 5–6 (Avramenko). Case government in complex collocations (e.g., genitive after набувати) aligns with §4.2.2.2 (B1 genitive usage, lines 2135–2181). Note: the State Standard does not use the word «колокація» explicitly; the closest official framing is «сталі словосполучення» within stylistic lexicon.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ukrainian synonym richness — "Horizon"**: Ukrainian Wikipedia article on «Горизонт» lists **14 native Ukrainian synonyms** for the single concept: *горизонт, обрій, овид, видноколо, видокрай, виднокруг, небокрай, небосхил, небозвід, крайнебо, круговид, кругозір, кругогляд, пруг*. This is a verifiable, striking demonstration of Ukrainian's lexical wealth — directly supports the plan's cultural hook. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Горизонт. Use this list in the introduction: the existence of 14 words for a single concept illustrates that Ukrainian is not "word-poor" but demands precision in choosing the right word for the right context — exactly what collocations are about.

2. **Суржик as a pedagogical framing**: Wikipedia defines суржик as «елементи двох або кількох мов, об'єднані штучно, без дотримання норм літературної мови.» Originally an agricultural term (mixed grain of wheat + rye), it was repurposed to name mixed Ukrainian-Russian speech. The plan's "linguistic decolonization" framing is fully supported: surzyk arises primarily from calquing Russian structures into Ukrainian. This is taught explicitly in Ukrainian school textbooks from Grade 4 onward (Zaharijchuk Grade 4, Avramenko Grade 5 §21, Avramenko Grade 6 §13).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Приймати участь»** → **«Брати участь»** — Avramenko Grade 6 §13 exercise explicitly marks «приймати участь» as a lexical error (test question). This is a calque from «принимать участие». The Ukrainian collocation verb is «брати» (to take), not «приймати» (to receive/accept).

2. **«Вірна відповідь»** → **«Правильна відповідь»** — Confirmed by Avramenko Grade 5 §21: «вірна відповідь (правильна відповідь)» is the canonical example of "a word used in a meaning inappropriate to it." «Вірний» means *loyal/faithful* (вірний друг, вірний пес). «Правильний» means *correct*. This is also a calque from «верный» which covers both meanings in the source language.

3. **«Рахувати» (to count numbers)** → **«Вважати» (to consider/think)** — Zabolotnyi Grade 11 appendix ("Поширені помилки слововживання") explicitly lists: *«рахувати за потрібне» → «вважати за потрібне»* as a common error. The confusion arises because in colloquial surzyk, «рахувати» absorbs the cognitive/opinion function. Frequency data confirms «вважати» (35.82/M) is the normal register verb for opinions.

**Bonus error to include**: «Відкривати очі» → «Розплющувати очі» — «відкривати» is for doors, bottles, discoveries; physical eye-opening uses «розплющувати». This distinction is not well-documented in the textbooks found, but is a genuine learner error worth the plan's inclusion as precision vocabulary.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **The §4.5.1.1 reference in the plan is correct** but note that the State Standard names it twice at lines 2437 and 2444 (apparent numbering error in the source document — both sub-subsections are labeled §4.5.1.1). The first covers lexical devices, the second covers syntactic devices. This module maps to the first (lexical).
- **"Горизонт" hook is stronger than the plan anticipated**: 14 synonyms (not "10+") — use the full Wikipedia list for impact.
- **VESUM confirms all 20 key vocabulary items exist** in the dictionary. «Видноколо» (3 VESUM entries), «небокрай» (2 entries), «обрій» (2 entries) — all verified.
- **Genitive government**: «Набувати досвіду» — genitive is the correct case. This is an important B1 case governance teaching point (§4.2.2.2). Expand this into a brief explanation in the content.
- **Register gradient**: «Ухвалювати рішення» (4.86/M, official/parliamentary) vs. «приймати рішення» (42.93/M, everyday) — this frequency gap is pedagogically valuable; show learners that both are correct but register-marked.
- **Frequency-based priority**: Focus most drill time on проводити (49.75/M), приймати (42.93/M), вважати (35.82/M) — these appear most often in natural text. «Гаяти» and «розплющувати» are contrastive/enrichment items, not core production targets.
- **Textbook anchor**: Avramenko Grade 5 §21 and Grade 6 §13 are the strongest textbook sources for error correction content. These can be cited to show learners the corrections are not arbitrary — they follow established school grammar norms.
- **Decolonized framing**: All errors in §3 of the plan originate from calquing — describe this as "preserving Ukrainian's own logic" not as "avoiding Russian." The linguistic argument is positive: Ukrainian has its own verb-collocate system that is precise and native.
