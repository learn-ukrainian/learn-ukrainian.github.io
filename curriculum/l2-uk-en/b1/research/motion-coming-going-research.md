# Дослідження: Рух: Прибуття та вихід

## State Standard Reference
§4.3.8: "Утворення префіксальних дієслів руху: іти – прийти, перейти, дійти, відійти, зайти, вийти; їхати – приїхати... виїхати."
Alignment: This module directly fulfills the requirement to form and use prefixed motion verbs, specifically focusing on arrival (при-), exit (ви-), and entry (в-/у-).

## Cultural Hooks
1. "Посидіти на дорогу" — A pre-departure ritual where everyone sits in silence for a moment before leaving the house, believed to ensure a calm and safe journey.
2. "Зустріч з хлібом-сіллю" — The traditional welcoming of guests (arrival) with a loaf of bread (korovai) and salt on a rushnyk (embroidered towel), symbolizing profound hospitality and prosperity.
3. "На коня" — A traditional farewell toast (literally "for the horse"), drunk right before departure.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Prefix confusion ("при-" vs "по-") → Using "Я приїхав" (I arrived) when meaning "I set off" (Я поїхав) — caused by translating the generic English "go" without considering the physical outcome.
2. Preposition mismatch ("приїхати з" + destination) → Using "з" (from) instead of "в/на" (to/in/on) for destination — a spatial logic error.
3. Aspectual misuse ("приходив" vs "прийшов") → Using imperfective for a single completed arrival — misunderstanding that "прийшов" strictly focuses on the permanent result of being at the destination.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize that Ukrainian strictly encodes the *physical result* of motion in its prefixes. Do not use Russian as a comparative baseline. Frame explanations around the spatial logic of the Ukrainian worldview.
- **Euphony**: Highlight the unique Ukrainian у/в alternation in prefixes (e.g., увійти vs в'їхати), which is a distinct feature of Ukrainian phonetics.
- **Agency**: Use active constructions ("Ми вирушаємо", "Українці кажуть").
- **Immersion**: B1.2 requires 100% Ukrainian in prose. English is restricted strictly to the vocabulary YAML.
