# Research Notes: B1 M17 "Рух: Прибуття та вихід"

**Level**: B1 (Intermediate)
**Topic**: Verbs of Motion (Arrival & Departure)
**Focus**: Prefixes `при-` (arrival) and `ви-` (exit)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (Level B1)**:

> **Annex A (Lexical and Grammatical Competence)**
> **Section: Morphology. The Verb.**
> "Formation of perfective verbs using prefixes... Verbs of motion with prefixes expressing spatial meanings: direction of action (`в-`, `у-`, `ви-`), approaching (`при-`, `під-`), moving away (`від-`)..."

> **Section: Syntax. Expressing Spatial Relations.**
> "Expression of the starting point of motion (`з`, `із` + Genitive), destination (`до` + Genitive; `в`, `на` + Accusative)."

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

**High Frequency (Core):**
*   **приходити / прийти** (to arrive/come on foot) — Essential.
*   **виходити / вийти** (to go out/leave on foot) — Essential.
*   **приїжджати / приїхати** (to arrive by vehicle) — High.
*   **виїжджати / виїхати** (to leave/drive out) — High.

**Common Collocations:**
*   *Прийти додому* (come home)
*   *Вийти з дому* (leave the house)
*   *Приїхати в Україну* (arrive in Ukraine)
*   *Вийти з автобуса* (get off the bus)
*   *Вихід* (exit - noun) / *Вхід* (entrance - noun)

**Lower Frequency (Contextual):**
*   *Прибувати / прибути* (formal arrival, e.g., trains/flights).
*   *Вилітати* (fly out - unless focusing on airports).

## 3. Cultural Hook

1.  **"Посидіти на дорогу" (Sit for the road)**: A superstitious tradition where everyone sits down in silence for a minute before leaving on a long journey (leaving the house). It’s believed to ensure a safe trip and trick evil spirits into thinking you aren't leaving.
2.  **Greeting Guests**: "Приходьте ще!" (Come again!). Ukrainian hospitality culture emphasizes the joy of *arrival* (`при-`) and the reluctance of *departure* (`ви-`). Guests are often walked to the door or gate ("проводжати").

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

*   **Prefix Logic**: English uses particles ("go **out**", "come **in**"). Ukrainian uses prefixes attached to the root (`**ви**ходити`, `**за**ходити`). Students often forget the prefix and just say "йти" + preposition.
*   **Deictic Confusion**: "Come" vs "Go" in English depends on the speaker's location. In Ukrainian, `при-` focuses strictly on the *result of reaching a goal*, while `ви-` focuses on *leaving a container/space*.
*   **Aspect Trap**: This is usually the first time students encounter the strict Imperfective/Perfective distinct stems for motion (e.g., `приходити` vs `прийти`). The stem change (`-ход-` vs `-й-`) is tricky.
*   **Preposition Check**: `Виходити` demands `з` (from/out of) + Genitive. `Приходити` demands `в/на` (into/on) + Accusative.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (B1 Target):**
*   Prefixes `при-` and `ви-` ONLY.
*   Formation of aspect pairs (`приходити` / `прийти`).
*   Usage with `в/на` (Acc) and `з` (Gen).
*   Contrast between "foot" (`-ход-/-й-`) and "vehicle" (`-їзд-/-їх-`).

**OUT OF SCOPE (Save for later):**
*   Complex prefixes (`пере-`, `за-`, `під-`, `об-`) — usually B1+ or B2.
*   Abstract motion (e.g., "time comes", "conclusion comes") — keep it physical.
*   Figurative meanings of `виходити` (e.g., "it turned out well").
*   Determinate/Indeterminate nuance for *prefixed* verbs (at this stage, treat prefixed forms as standard Imp/Perf pairs, ignoring the multi-directional nuance of the base).
