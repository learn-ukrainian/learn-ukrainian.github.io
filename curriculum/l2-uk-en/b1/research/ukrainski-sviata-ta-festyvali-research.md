## Cultural Hooks
1. **Календарна реформа 2023 року**: Ukraine officially shifted to the Revised Julian calendar (celebrating Christmas on Dec 25, St. Nicholas on Dec 6), marking a definitive cultural break from the Russian Orthodox Church and reclaiming historical Ukrainian and European timelines.
2. **Дідух (Didukh)**: A pre-Christian, traditional Ukrainian sheaf of wheat placed in the home for Christmas. It represents the spirit of ancestors and Ukraine's deep agricultural roots, serving as an authentic counterpart to the Soviet-imposed "Novy God" tree (Yolka) which was historically designed to erase distinct national traditions.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Nominative instead of Instrumental for greetings**: Learners often say «Вітаю з свято!» or «З Новий рік!» instead of «Вітаю зі святом!» and «З Новим роком!». This happens due to direct translation without applying the preposition 'з/зі' + Instrumental case rule.
2. **Generic vs. Specific Greetings**: Using a generic "З Різдвом!" instead of the traditional call-and-response «Христос народився! — Славімо Його!» which is specifically expected during the Christmas period.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Frame the 2023 calendar shift as an act of agency—"Ukraine rejecting Russian colonial influence over its timeline"—rather than merely "matching the West." Emphasize that practices like the Didukh and Malanka are authentic traditions that survived Soviet homogenization efforts.
- **Agency Pass**: Describe Ukrainians actively preserving and reviving folk traditions, treating them as a living, dynamic culture rather than just static history.
- **Language Requirements**: Zero English in the prose (100% immersion for B1.7). Ensure all grammatical explanations for the Instrumental case (з/зі + Орудний відмінок) are structured clearly using tables, highlighting the word endings.
