## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Collocations

For B1 learners, focus on high-frequency metaphorical uses of core motion verbs (*іти, бігти, нести, вести*).

### High Frequency (Must Know)
These are essential for daily communication.

*   **Час іде / біжить / летить** (Time goes / runs / flies)
    *   *Usage*: Universal time expression.
*   **Дощ / сніг іде** (Rain / snow is coming/falling)
    *   *Usage*: Weather phenomena (standard phrasing, not "falling").
*   **Йти на компроміс** (To compromise / "go onto compromise")
    *   *Usage*: Professional and personal negotiations.
*   **Справи йдуть добре/погано** (Things are going well/badly)
    *   *Usage*: Standard answer to "Як справи?".
*   **Вести переговори** (To conduct negotiations / "lead negotiations")
    *   *Usage*: Business/Formal contexts.
*   **Нести відповідальність** (To bear responsibility / "carry responsibility")
    *   *Usage*: Formal/Legal contexts.

### Medium Frequency (Enrichment)
*   **Ходять чутки** (Rumors are circulating / "walking")
*   **Думка промайнула / прийшла** (A thought crossed my mind / came)
*   **Плисти за течією** (To go with the flow / "swim with the current")

## 3. Cultural Hook

**Idiom**: *"Лізти поперед батька в пекло"* (To climb into hell before one's father).
*   **Meaning**: To be overly hasty; to act rashly without waiting for elders/superiors; to interrupt.
*   **Cultural Insight**: This reflects the deep-seated traditional Ukrainian respect for hierarchy and age. Even in a disastrous destination ("hell"), the elder has the right of way. It is used humorously or sternly to check impatience.

**Metaphor**: *"Плисти за течією"* (To swim with the current).
*   **Cultural Insight**: While universal, in Ukrainian literature and discourse (e.g., Ivan Franko's "Каменярі" implies the opposite—breaking rock), passivity is often contrasted with the "struggle" (*боротьба*). This phrase is commonly used to criticize lack of agency or initiative.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
1.  **Weather**: English uses "it is raining" (state/action). Ukrainian uses "rain goes" (*дощ іде*). This is a conceptual shift: weather is an active agent moving through space.
2.  **Abstract Carriers**: In English, you "hold" a meeting or "have" a responsibility. In Ukrainian, you "lead" (*вести*) a meeting and "carry" (*нести*) responsibility. The metaphors are more dynamic/weight-bearing.

### Common Learner Errors
*   Translating "Time passes" as *Час проходить* (technically possible but less idiomatic than *Час іде/летить*).
*   Using *падати* (to fall) for rain instead of *іти*.
*   Confusion between *вести* (to lead/conduct) and *водити* (to drive/guide generally) in phrases like *вести блог* (to run a blog).

### Teaching Sequence
1.  **Weather & Time**: Introduce *Дощ іде* and *Час іде* immediately as fixed phrases.
2.  **Abstract "Going"**: *Справи йдуть*, *Йде війна* (War is going on), *Йде фільм* (Movie is playing).
3.  **Abstract "Carrying/Leading"**: *Нести відповідальність*, *Вести спосіб життя* (Lead a lifestyle).
4.  **Idioms**: Save complex idioms (*Лізти поперед батька...*) for the end of the module as "flavor".

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (B1 Level)
*   **Core Verbs**: *іти/ходити, їхати/їздити, бігти/бігати, нести/носити, вести/водити, пливти/плавати, летіти/літати*.
*   **Tenses**: Present, Past, Future (imperfective).
*   **Cases**: Accusative (objects), Locative (places), Instrumental (with whom/how).

### OUT of Scope (Too Advanced)
*   **Prefix Nuances**: Complex prefixed distinctions like *прибувати* vs *доходити* in metaphorical senses (leave for B2).
*   **Archaic/Literary Motion**: *Брести* (to trudge), *чалапати* (to shuffle) in metaphorical uses.
*   **Complex Participles**: Avoid structures like *спливаючий* (elapsed).
*   **Highly Specific Slang**: e.g., *наїхати на когось* (to bully/press someone) — keep it professional/standard for now.
