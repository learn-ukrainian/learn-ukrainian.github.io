## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2024)**, Level B1, Section **Morphology: The Verb**, the student must master the following conceptual categories, which requires understanding the associated terminology:

> **Вимоги до володіння дієсловом (Рівень B1):**
> *   **Загальні відомості**: Інфінітив як початкова форма.
> *   **Категорія виду**: Розуміння та вживання доконаного та недоконаного виду (досягнення результату vs процес). Двовидові дієслова.
> *   **Категорія часу**: Теперішній, минулий, майбутній (проста і складена форми).
> *   **Категорія способу**: Дійсний та наказовий спосіб (імператив).
> *   **Зворотні дієслова**: Вживання частки -ся.
> *   **Дієвідмінювання**: Особові форми І та ІІ дієвідмін.

This module specifically targets the *metalanguage* (terminology) required to discuss these rules in the target language, enabling "Grammar in Grammar" (teaching grammar using Ukrainian).

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

This module focuses on linguistic terminology. While the concepts are fundamental, the *labels* vary in frequency.

### High Frequency (Core A1/A2 words used in B1 context)
*   **Час** (Time/Tense) - Extremely high frequency. Used in daily life ("мало часу") and grammar ("теперішній час").
*   **Дія** (Action) - High frequency. Used in general contexts ("бойові дії", "план дій").
*   **Слово** (Word) - Very high frequency.
*   **Кінець** (End) - High frequency (root for "Закінчена дія").

### Mid Frequency (Educational/Grammar Context)
*   **Дієслово** (Verb) - Common in school/learning contexts. "Word of action".
*   **Минулий / Майбутній** (Past / Future) - High frequency adjectives.
*   **Результат** (Result) - Common in professional/general usage.
*   **Процес** (Process) - Common loanword.

### Low Frequency (Specialized Terminology - The Target of this Module)
*   **Вид** (Aspect/View) - High frequency as "view/type", but specialized as "grammatical aspect".
*   **Доконаний** (Perfective) - Exclusively grammatical term.
*   **Недоконаний** (Imperfective) - Exclusively grammatical term.
*   **Дієвідміна** (Conjugation class) - Specialized.
*   **Спосіб** (Mood/Way) - High frequency as "way/method", specialized as "mood".

**Common Collocations (B1 Level):**
*   *Який це час?* (What tense is this?)
*   *Це дієслово недоконаного виду.* (This is an imperfective verb.)
*   *Дія відбувається зараз.* (The action is happening now.)
*   *Завершена дія.* (Completed action.)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**"Дієслово" - The Word of Action**
In Ukrainian, the word for "verb" is **дієслово**, literally meaning "action-word" (дія + слово). This transparency helps learners understand the function of the part of speech immediately, unlike the Latin-derived "verb".

**The "Result" Mindset (Aspect)**
Ukrainian culture (and Slavic languages in general) encodes a sharp distinction between "doing" and "getting it done" directly into the grammar. Using the wrong aspect isn't just a grammar error; it can sound culturally jarring. For example, inviting someone to *drink* coffee (пити - process) is a social activity; inviting someone to *drink up* coffee (випити - result) sounds like a rush or a challenge. This module gives students the *words* (доконаний/недоконаний) to discuss this nuance.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Key Differences from English:**
*   **No Continuous Tenses**: English speakers look for "-ing" equivalents. Ukrainian uses *Imperfective Aspect* covers both Simple and Continuous meanings. Students often confuse "Imperfective" with "Continuous".
*   **Strict Binary**: Aspect is binary (Perfective/Imperfective), whereas English has a matrix of Tense x Aspect (Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous).
*   **Terminology Barrier**: "Perfective" sounds like "Perfect Tense" (Have done), but they are not identical.

**Teaching Recommendations:**
1.  **Concept First**: Ensure they understand *Process vs. Result* before attaching the labels *Недоконаний vs. Доконаний*.
2.  **Transparent Labels**: Use the etymology. *Не-доконаний* (Not-completed/done).
3.  **Tense vs. Aspect**: Clarify that *Tense* is "When", *Aspect* is "How".
4.  **Meta-Discussion**: The goal is for students to be able to ask *"Який це вид?"* (What aspect is this?) when learning new vocabulary.

**Common Errors:**
*   Confusing *Час* (Time/Tense) with *Раз* (Time/Occasion).
*   Applying "Present Tense" to Perfective verbs (which creates Future meaning).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (B1.0 Bridge):**
*   **Terminology**: vid, chas, sposib, diyeslovo, infinitiv.
*   **Concept Mapping**: Connecting "Process" to "Imperfective" and "Result" to "Perfective".
*   **Identification**: Looking at a verb form and naming its tense/aspect.
*   **Basic Conjugation types**: Knowing Type I vs Type II exist (terminology), but not mastering all exceptions.

**OUT OF SCOPE (Saved for B1.1 - B2):**
*   **Advanced Moods**: Conditional mood nuances (B1.5).
*   **Passive Voice**: Full passive participles (B2).
*   **Motion Verbs**: Detailed prefixes for motion verbs (B1.2).
*   **Aspect Pairs**: Memorizing irregular aspect pairs (Continuous process throughout B1/B2).
*   **Stylistics**: Using Imperfective for "annulled result" (C1).

**Prerequisite Check:**
*   Students must know A2 basic grammar (Cases, Basic Conjugation).
*   Students must be able to read Cyrillic fluently.
*   Students effectively know "Past/Present/Future" conceptually, but may lack the Ukrainian metalanguage labels.
