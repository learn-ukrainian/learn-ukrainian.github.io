# Research: Підрядні означальні (Relative Clauses with "який")

**Module:** B1 M26
**Topic:** Relative Clauses (Qualitative/Attributive)
**Focus:** The relative pronoun *який* (which/that/who)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard Reference

According to the **State Standard for Ukrainian as a Foreign Language (2018/2024)**, this topic falls under **B1 Syntax** (Complex Sentences).

> **Синтаксис. Складне речення.**
> *Складнопідрядні речення з означальними підрядними частинами.*
> Студент повинен вміти будувати та розуміти речення зі сполучним словом **«який»** (у різних відмінкових формах), що узгоджується з означуваним іменником у роді та числі.

*Note: While introduced simply in A2, B1 requires mastery of "який" in oblique cases (e.g., "The city **in which** I live").*

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

At the B1 level, examples should use high-frequency antecedents (nouns) to ensure the focus remains on the grammar structure.

**High-Frequency Antecedents (Nouns):**
*   **Людина, чоловік, жінка** (Person, man, woman) — for "who".
*   **Книга, фільм, телефон, автобус** (Book, film, phone, bus) — for inanimate "which".
*   **Місто, країна, університет** (City, country, university) — often used with locative "in which" (в якому).
*   **Друг, колега, сусід** (Friend, colleague, neighbor).

**Common Collocations (B1):**
*   *Це той фільм, який я бачив.* (That is the film that I saw.)
*   *Людина, яка працює тут...* (The person who works here...)
*   *Місто, в якому я живу...* (The city in which I live...)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**The "Poetic Precision" of Ukrainian Description:**
Ukrainian culture, particularly in literature and folk songs, places high value on descriptive precision. Unlike English, which often omits the relative pronoun (e.g., "The book I read" vs. "The book *that* I read"), Ukrainian **must** retain the connector (*який/що*).

*   **Cultural Reference:** Ukrainian definitions often function through description rather than categorization. In folk riddles (*загадки*), objects are described via clauses: *"Той, хто..."* (He who...) or *"Те, що..."* (That which...).
*   **Usage:** In formal Ukrainian address or toasts, it is common to use elaborate relative clauses to compliment someone: *"Шановний Іване, який завжди допомагає..."* (Dear Ivan, who always helps...).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

**Key Concept: The "Split Allegiance" of *Який***
This is the most common stumbling block for English speakers. The word *який* functions as a bridge with two anchor points:
1.  **Backward Anchor (Gender/Number):** It looks *back* at the noun it describes (Antecedent) to determine its **Gender** and **Number**.
2.  **Forward Anchor (Case):** It looks *forward* into the relative clause to determine its **Case** (based on its role in that specific clause: Subject, Object, or Prepositional object).

**Common Learner Errors:**
*   **English Interference:** Omitting the pronoun. (*Це книга, я читаю* instead of *Це книга, **яку** я читаю*).
*   **Case Confusion:** Using Nominative *який* when Accusative is needed. (*Дівчина, **яка** я бачу* — Incorrect. Should be *яку*).
*   **Confusion with *Що*:** Using *що* universally (like English "that"), which is colloquial and sometimes incorrect in formal B1 writing compared to the declinable *який*.

**Teaching Sequence:**
1.  **Subject Focus (Nominative):** "The man **who** is standing." (*який стоїть*) — Case matches antecedent.
2.  **Object Focus (Accusative):** "The book **which** I read." (*яку я читаю*) — Case differs from antecedent.
3.  **Prepositional Usage:** "The house **in which** I live." (*в якому я живу*).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**Prerequisites (Expected B1 Entry Knowledge):**
*   **Gender & Number:** Essential for agreement.
*   **Case System:** Mastery of Nominative, Accusative, and Locative forms of adjectives (since *який* declines exactly like an adjective).
*   **Verbal Aspects:** Understanding that the action in the clause might be continuous or finished.

**In Scope (B1 M26):**
*   Defining relative clauses.
*   *Який* in Nominative, Accusative (Inanimate/Animate), and Locative cases.
*   Simple Genitive usages (e.g., "The author **whose** book..." - *автор, книгу якого...*).

**Out of Scope (Save for B2/C1):**
*   Complex instrumentals ("The pen **with which** I write").
*   Participle phrases replacing relative clauses (*Дієприкметникові звороти*) — this is the formal alternative taught later.
*   Stylistic nuances between *який*, *котрий*, and *що*. Focus strictly on *який* as the universal standard.
