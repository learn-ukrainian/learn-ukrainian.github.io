## Cultural Hooks

**1. Кум/кума — spiritual kinship (Wikipedia: Кум (рідня))**
The terms кум/кума denote the godfather/godmother in relation to the biological parents of the baptized child. Etymologically from Latin *commater* (co-mother) via Balkan Latin dialects. Historically, кумівство bonds were used "to confirm and strengthen friendships" — the verb *покуматися* once meant simply "to become friends" (заприятелювати/затоваришувати). The proverb "кум королю і сват міністру" describes someone who presents themselves as having powerful connections. In Ukrainian folktales, "кум" is the traditional epithet for the wolf, "кума" for the fox. This establishes кумівство as a rich cultural-linguistic node with multiple registers.

**2. Прислів'я «Рідня до півдня, а куми — довіку»**
This proverb directly encodes the cultural primacy of spiritual kinship over blood relatives in certain contexts. "Родичі — до полудня" (relatives help only while it's convenient), but godparents are bound for life. This reflects the deep Slavic-Christian tradition of куми as a parallel family structure that carries lifelong moral obligations, not merely ceremonial roles.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Женився на ній»** → **«Одружився з нею»** — Direct calque from the neighbouring Eastern Slavic pattern using на + Locative. Ukrainian requires одружитися з + Instrumental. This is the single most documented Russianism in this semantic field.

2. **«Я кохаю маму / Я кохаю борщ»** → **«Я люблю маму / Я люблю борщ»** — English speakers map both кохати and любити onto "to love" and default to the phonetically salient кохати. кохати is semantically restricted to exclusive romantic passion between partners. Using it for family or food creates an unintentionally comic or inappropriate register.

3. **Confusing стосунки / відносини / взаємини / ставлення** — All four translate as "relations/relationship" in English. Grade 9 textbook (Воронь) explicitly addresses this: *відношення* = logical/mathematical correspondence; *ставлення* = one-directional attitude/treatment; *взаємини* = reciprocal warm connection; *стосунки* = personal/emotional interactions in general. Learners who rely on translation alone will consistently choose відносини (higher GRAC frequency) in contexts that require стосунки.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Textbook anchor (Воронь, Grade 9, p.22):** The four-way стосунки/взаємини/відношення/ставлення distinction is directly taught in the Ukrainian school curriculum at Grade 9. This gives the module authoritative grounding — quote or paraphrase this distinction in the introductory section.
- **Frequency paradox for content:** відносини outpaces стосунки in corpus frequency overall, but this is register artifact (political journalism). The Phase B content should make this corpus skew explicit so learners understand why they will encounter відносини more often in text but should default to стосунки in personal speech.
- **кохати frequency (1.86 ipm):** The very low corpus frequency of кохати vs. любити is itself pedagogically useful data — it demonstrates the word's restricted domain. This can be surfaced explicitly in the Linguistic Nuance section.
- **Decolonized framing for одружитися:** Frame the preposition pattern positively — "Ukrainian uses з + Instrumental to express partnership symmetry" — not as "unlike the neighbouring Eastern Slavic pattern." This is intrinsically interesting as a window into how the language encodes the relationship.
- **Proverb «Рідня до півдня, а куми — довіку»:** Well-suited for the Reading/Culture section as a productive analysis anchor. The temporal contrast (південь = limited time; довіку = forever) is linguistically accessible at B1.
- **VESUM gate:** All 15 vocabulary_hints items verified. зв'язок matches two lemmas in VESUM (зв'язка and зв'язок) — content must consistently use зв'язок (m.) for the "connection" meaning, not зв'язка (f., "bundle/tie").
