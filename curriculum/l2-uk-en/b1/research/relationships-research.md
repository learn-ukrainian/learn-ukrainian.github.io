## Cultural Hooks
1. **Інститут кумівства (Godparenthood):** In Ukrainian culture, "куми" (godparents) form a lifelong spiritual bond, often considered as close or closer than blood relatives. The proverb "Рідня до півдня, а куми — довіку" (Relatives are until noon, but godparents are forever) reflects this deep social priority.
2. **Egalitarian Marriage Construction:** Ukrainian uses the reciprocal construction "одружитися з кимось" (to marry with someone, Instrumental case), reflecting a partnership, in stark contrast to the Russian patriarchal construction "жениться на ком-то" (to marry on someone, Locative case).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Overusing "кохати"** → "Я кохаю борщ / маму". Correct form: "Я люблю борщ / маму". Why: English learners map "love" universally; in Ukrainian, "кохати" is strictly for romantic passion.
2. **Calquing Russian marriage phrases** → "Він женився на ній". Correct form: "Він одружився з нею". Why: Interference from Russian syntax; learners must use "з" + Instrumental.
3. **Misusing "відносини" for romance** → "У нас романтичні відносини". Correct form: "У нас романтичні стосунки". Why: "Відносини" sounds formal, like a diplomatic treaty or business contract.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing:** Explicitly contrast the egalitarian nature of "одружитися з" against imperial/patriarchal constructions. Highlight the richness of Ukrainian vocabulary for connections (стосунки/відносини/взаємини/зв'язок) as a mark of a highly developed social language.
- **Immersion:** 100% Ukrainian. Explain semantic nuances (like the difference between "любити" and "кохати") using Ukrainian definitions and situational examples, not English translations.
- **IPA:** Use IPA in the vocabulary YAML, but ensure no transliteration or Latin letters appear in the prose.
