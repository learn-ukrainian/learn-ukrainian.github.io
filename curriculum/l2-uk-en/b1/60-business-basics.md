### Cultural Context: Working with Ukrainians

Understanding the local business culture is crucial for success. Here are some key differences you might notice compared to Western Europe or the USA.

**1. Relationship-Oriented:**
In Ukraine, business is personal. People prefer to do business with those they know and trust. Building a relationship often comes before signing a contract. Don't be surprised if your partner asks about your family or personal interests during a meeting.

**2. Direct Communication:**
Ukrainians tend to be quite direct. They value honesty ("—á–µ—Å–Ω—ñ—Å—Ç—å") and may express their opinions openly, even if they disagree. This is not rude; it's a sign of trust.

**3. Hierarchical Structures:**
While the IT sector is very modern and horizontal, traditional Ukrainian companies often have a strong hierarchy. Decisions are usually made by the top management ("–¥–∏—Ä–µ–∫—Ç–æ—Ä"). It's important to identify who the decision-maker is.

**4. Flexibility:**
Ukrainians are known for their adaptability. Plans can change quickly, and people are generally good at problem-solving in unpredictable situations.

**5. Hospitality:**
If you visit a Ukrainian office, you will likely be offered coffee or tea multiple times. Refusing might be seen as impolite. Sharing a meal is also a common way to build trust.
