## Що таке чергування приголосних? (~550 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): [Definition and Bridge]: Revisit vowel alternations from M08 (стіл-стола). Introduce the concept of consonant alternation (чергування приголосних) as a systematic shift at the root/stem boundary. Explain that while vowel changes often happen inside the root, consonant changes respond to suffixes or case endings, like a "phonetic handshake."
- P2 (~100 words): [The Three-Way Pattern]: Present the core velar grouping [г], [к], [х]. Explain that each has two possible "partners" depending on the grammatical context. Introduce the primary sets: [г] — [з'] — [ж], [к] — [ц'] — [ч], [х] — [с'] — [ш]. Use the classic example: друг (base) — друзі (plural) — дружба (noun) to show the root's flexibility.
- P3 (~110 words): [Historical Logic]: Briefly touch on the history of the "front vowels" (і, е) that triggered these changes in Old East Slavic. Explain that this isn't just a list of random rules but a natural evolution of sounds moving from the back of the mouth (velars) to the front to match the following vowel. Compare to English "electric" vs "electricity" to show the concept exists in other languages.
- P4 (~120 words): [Productivity and Regularity]: Emphasize that these rules are productive in modern Ukrainian. If a new word ends in -к, it will likely follow the [к] -> [ц'] or [к] -> [ч] pattern. Contrast this with English irregular plurals (foot-feet) to show that Ukrainian alternations are actually more predictable and "math-like."
- P5 (~100 words): [Visualizing the Shift]: Describe the physical production of the sounds—how [г] (back) moves to [ж] (middle) or [з'] (front). Provide a small introductory list: рука, нога, вухо to set the stage for the next sections.

## Перша палаталізація: [г/к/х] -> [ж/ч/ш] (~820 words total)
- P1 (~130 words): [Core Rule]: Explain the first palatalization pattern: [г] -> [ж], [к] -> [ч], [х] -> [ш]. Specify that this is the "hardest" shift, often appearing in word formation (creating new nouns or adjectives) and the Vocative case. Use examples: нога -> ніжка (diminutive), рука -> ручка, вухо -> вушко.
- P2 (~120 words): [Vocative Case (Кличний) Context]: Deep dive into how masculine nouns of the 2nd declension (hard group) change their final consonant before the ending -е. Provide examples: козак -> козаче, пастух -> пастуше, ворог -> вороже. Explain that this makes the address sound more natural and traditional.
- P3 (~110 words): [Word Formation - Suffixes]: Show how the suffixes -к-, -н-, and -ськ- trigger this shift. Examples: книга -> книжка (diminutive), молоко -> молочний (adjective), Прага -> празький (geographic adjective). Note that [г] before -ськ- results in [з'], which is a slight variation but follows the logic of the group.
- P4 (~110 words): [Diminutives and Endearment]: Explain the emotional weight of these alternations. By changing [к] to [ч] in "донька -> донечка" or "рука -> рученька," the speaker expresses affection. This demonstrates that grammar is tied to emotion in Ukrainian.
- P5 (~130 words): [Cultural Reading: Proverbs]: Introduce the proverb "Терпи, козаче, отаманом будеш." Explain the meaning (endurance leads to leadership) and point out the [к] -> [ч] shift in "козаче." Provide a second example: "Береженого Бог береже," showing the verb-noun relationship (берегти-береже).
- Exercise 1: [fill-in]: Write the correct кличний відмінок form of masculine nouns (e.g., козак -> козач___, друг -> друж___). 8 items.
- Exercise 2: [match-up]: Match base nouns with their diminutive forms involving first palatalization (e.g., дорога <-> доріжка, книга <-> книжка). 8 items.
- P6 (~120 words): [Common Exceptions]: Briefly mention nouns where the alternation is resisted or follows a different path, like modern loanwords (маркетинг -> маркетингу, vocative usually takes -у). Reinforce that for traditional Ukrainian roots, the rule is ironclad.

## Друга палаталізація: [г/к/х] -> [з'/ц'/с'] (~830 words total)
- P1 (~130 words): [Core Rule]: Present the second palatalization: [г] -> [з'], [к] -> [ц'], [х] -> [с']. Explain that this is the "soft" shift, specifically triggered by the ending -і in the Dative and Locative cases of feminine nouns (1st declension). Examples: нога -> на нозі, рука -> на руці, муха -> на мусі.
- P2 (~120 words): [The Dative/Locative Trigger]: Explain why this only happens in these two cases. The ending -і is a "weakening" sound that pulls the back consonant forward. Contrast this with the Genitive case (ноги) or Instrumental (ногою), where the consonant stays hard because the ending isn't -і.
- P3 (~130 words): [Proper Names in Address/Gifts]: Focus on feminine names: Ольга -> Ользі, Марійка -> Марійці, Наталка -> Наталці. Explain that if you are giving a gift "to Olga," you must say "це Ользі." This is a frequent area of error for learners.
- P4 (~110 words): [Geographic Applications]: Apply the rule to maps. Прага -> у Празі, Рига -> у Ризі, Америка -> в Америці. Note the difference between "Африка -> в Африці" (standard) and why some foreign names might resist (covered later).
- P5 (~120 words): [Avoiding Overgeneralization]: Crucial distinction: [з'/ц'/с'] is for Dative/Locative, while [ж/ч/ш] is for Vocative/Word-formation. Explain that saying *на ножі instead of *на нозі changes the meaning from "on the leg" to "on the knife."
- Exercise 3: [quiz]: Identify which palatalization type is present: first ([ж/ч/ш]) or second ([з'/ц'/с']), given a word pair (друг-друже vs. нога-нозі). 8 items.
- Exercise 4: [fill-in]: Write the correct dative/locative form of feminine nouns (e.g., нога -> на ноз___, рука -> руц___, муха -> мус___). 6 items.
- P6 (~120 words): [Phonetic Drill]: Provide a pronunciation guide for [з'], [ц'], [с']. Remind the learner to keep these sounds soft (palatalized) to ensure the grammatical meaning is clear to a native speaker.

## Чергування [ц'] -> [ч] та інші (~550 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): [The Soft Alternation]: Introduce the specific shift [ц'] -> [ч]. Explain that this primarily occurs in the Vocative case for nouns ending in -ець. Examples: хлопець -> хлопче, молодець -> молодче, швець -> шевче. Note that the soft [ц'] hardens and changes its quality to a hushing [ч].
- P2 (~110 words): [Linguistic Context]: Explain that this [ц'] to [ч] shift is distinct from the [к] to [ч] shift. It highlights the unique behavior of the -ець suffix in Ukrainian, which is very common in names of professions and social roles.
- P3 (~100 words): [Minor Consonant Shifts]: Mention less common alternations like [з] -> [ж] in specific historical nouns: князь -> княже (Vocative). Explain that while rare, these are important for reading historical literature or addressing figures in formal contexts.
- P4 (~110 words): [Summary of Hushing/Sibilant Relationships]: Recap how these sounds are interconnected. Hushing sounds (ж, ч, ш) are often the "alternation result" of back consonants or soft sibilants. This creates a phonetic balance in the language.
- Exercise 5: [match-up]: Match masculine nouns ending in -ець with their correct Vocative forms (e.g., жнець <-> женче, кравець <-> кравче). 8 items.
- P5 (~110 words): [Interactive Reflection]: Ask the learner to look at the name of the module's target speaker/topic. If someone's name ends in -ець, how would you address them? This bridges the gap to real-world usage.

## Чергування у відмінюванні іменників II відміни (~600 words total)
- P1 (~130 words): [The Masculine Paradigm]: Systematically look at how alternations appear (or don't) in the 2nd declension (masculine/neuter). Explain that for masculine nouns, the only case that typically triggers alternation is the Vocative. Contrast: Р.в. друга, Д.в. другу, but Кл.в. друже.
- P2 (~120 words): [Locative Case Exceptions]: Explain that while feminine nouns alternate in the Locative (на руці), masculine nouns usually avoid it by using the ending -у or -ові: на другу, на порозі (but порогу is also possible). Note that [г] -> [з'] can still happen in specific masculine words: поріг -> на порозі, берег -> на березі.
- P3 (~120 words): [Group Classification]: Introduce how the "Hard Group" (тверда група) is the primary site for these alternations. Soft and Mixed groups have their own rules but often don't require the same [г/к/х] shifts because their stem-final consonants are already different.
- P4 (~110 words): [Consistency with Plurals]: Discuss the plural forms. друг -> друзі (2nd palatalization logic). Note that while most masculine alternations are singular Vocative, the [г] -> [з'] shift in the nominative plural (друзі) is a vital exception to learn early.
- P5 (~120 words): [Reference Table: 2nd Declension]: Provide a clear breakdown for a few model words: ворог, чоловік, пастух. Show the Vocative shift clearly against the rest of the hard-consonant paradigm.

## Чергування у власних назвах i географічних іменах (~550 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): [The Rule of Names]: Reiterate that Ukrainian names are not exempt. Olga becomes Ользі, and Marichka becomes Марічці. Explain that this is a sign of respect—using the correct inflected form shows the speaker is attentive to the grammar of the person's name.
- P2 (~120 words): [Geography and Locative]: Focus on cities and countries ending in -ка or -га. Америка -> в Америці, Африка -> в Африці, Прага -> у Празі. Explain that even modern place names follow this ancient rule because it is deeply embedded in the "ear" of the language.
- P3 (~130 words): [Foreign Resistance]: Mention that some foreign or non-Slavic names might not alternate to preserve their recognizable root: Пенелопа -> Пенелопі, Джессіка -> Джессіці (wait, Джессіці DOES alternate [к]->[ц']). Clarify: [г/к/х] usually DOES alternate in foreign names if they end in -а, but some very rare stems might not. Stick to the rule: "If it ends in -а, it probably alternates."
- Dialogue (~100 words): [At a Львівська книгарня]: A conversation between a Книгар (bookseller) and a Покупець. They discuss books (книги) and specific titles. The bookseller says: "У цій книжці (книжка) ви знайдете відповіді..." and "Ользі Кобилянській присвячено цей розділ." The buyer responds using the Vocative: "Друже, підкажіть ціну."
- Exercise 6: [error-correction]: Find and fix consonant alternation errors in sentences (e.g., *на ножі -> на нозі, *козаже -> козаче, *Ольгі -> Ользі). 6 items.
