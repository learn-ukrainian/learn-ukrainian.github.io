## Різночасність: дія до основної (~825 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Introduction to the concept of the perfective gerund (дієприслівник доконаного виду). Explain the core question "що зробивши?" and its primary function: expressing an additional action that was completed BEFORE the main action. Use the textbook example from Zabolotnyi: "Прочитавши книгу, я віддав її другу" (First: finished reading; Then: gave it back).
- P2 (~160 words): Explaining the concept of "різночасність" (non-simultaneity). Contrast the logical flow of time in Ukrainian compared to simple coordination. Example: "Дізнавшись новину, мати зателефонувала." Break down the sequence: 1. Mother learned the news (completion). 2. Mother called. Highlight that the gerund acts as a "bridge" between two finished events.
- P3 (~180 words): Contrast with Imperfective Gerunds (M61). Detail the difference between "одночасність" (simultaneity) and "попередність" (precedence). Compare "Відчиняючи двері, я побачив кота" (While opening...) vs. "Відчинивши двері, я побачив кота" (Having opened...). Explain how changing the aspect changes the entire scene's timing.
- P4 (~120 words): Dialogue: Detective recounting events. Detective: "Увійшовши в кімнату, я побачив сліди." Colleague: "Оглянувши місце, ви зателефонували нам?" Detective: "Так, дізнавшись правду, ми почали діяти." Focus on the natural flow of sequential completed actions in a formal/narrative context.
- P5 (~110 words): Analysis of the dialogue's grammar. List the gerunds used (увійшовши, оглянувши, дізнавшись) and verify that in each case, the subject (the Detective) is the one performing both the additional and the main action.

## Творення дієприслівників доконаного виду (~935 words)
- P1 (~170 words): Basic formation rule from Litvinova: Infinitive stem of a perfective verb + suffix -вши (if the stem ends in a vowel). Provide a clear paradigm: прочита-ти → прочитавши, написа-ти → написавши, зроби-ти → зробивши. Note that the "v" of the past tense (він зробив) is effectively replaced or the infinitive stem is used directly.
- P2 (~160 words): Formation from stems ending in a consonant. Introduce the suffix -ши. Examples: принести → принісши, привезти → привізши. Explain the phonetic assimilation in pronunciation: [прин'ішши], [зав'іжши]. Mention that these forms are less frequent in speech but essential for literature and formal reports.
- P3 (~170 words): Reflexive perfective gerunds. Explain how the postfix -ся/сь behaves. Rule: it becomes -сь after the vowel of -вши (повернутися → повернувшись, дізнатися → дізнавшись) and stays -ся after the consonant of -ши (though rarely used this way). Focus on common reflexive verbs: прокинувшись, зібравшись, зустрівшись.
- P4 (~150 words): Stem changes and irregular-looking forms. Explain "знайти" → "знайшовши" (using the past tense stem) and verbs with the -ну- suffix that stays (розгорнути → розгорнувши). Warn against the "Russianism" trap of trying to use -учи/-ючи for perfective verbs (e.g., *прочитаючи is wrong).
- P5 (~150 words): Stylistic warning: Verbs that don't form gerunds easily. Explain that some consonant-stem verbs (випекти → ?випекши) sound awkward or archaic. Teach the "Rule of Thumb": if the gerund form feels heavy, Ukrainian speakers naturally pivot to a subordinate clause (після того як...).

## Порівняння двох видів дієприслівників (~825 words)
- P1 (~180 words): Systematic Side-by-Side Comparison. Construct a mental table: Aspect (Imperfective vs. Perfective), Question (що роблячи? vs. що зробивши?), Timing (одночасність vs. різночасність), and Suffixes (-учи/-ючи vs. -вши/-ши). Use "читаючи" (while reading) vs. "прочитавши" (having read) as the anchor example.
- P2 (~170 words): Case Study: The Morning Routine. Explain how a single paragraph uses both types to create a vivid sequence. Example: "Прокинувшись (having woken up), Андрій снідав, переглядаючи (while reviewing) пошту." Show how one action is the "pre-condition" and the other is the "accompanying process."
- P3 (~160 words): Semantic nuances. Discuss how choosing the wrong aspect can create logical absurdities. Example: "Умиваючись, я витерся рушником" (impossible - washing and drying simultaneously) vs. "Умившись, я витерся рушником" (correct - washed, then dried).
- P4 (~160 words): Identifying the logical subject. Reinforce that regardless of aspect, the gerund's action MUST belong to the main sentence subject. Contrast "Закінчивши роботу, я пішов додому" (Correct) with the common error "Закінчивши роботу, почався дощ" (The rain didn't finish the work).

## Дієприслівник vs підрядне речення (~715 words)
- P1 (~170 words): Transformation logic. Every perfective gerund phrase can be expanded into a clause with "після того як" or "коли". Example: "Повернувшись, я зателефонував" = "Після того як я повернувся, я зателефонував." Explain that the gerund is a tool for "compression."
- P2 (~160 words): Register and Style. Explain that gerunds are the hallmark of "книжна мова" (literary/official style). In daily "розмовна мова", Ukrainians prefer clauses. However, using a gerund correctly adds elegance and professional polish to B1+ writing.
- P3 (~180 words): The "Dangling Participle" (висячий дієприслівник). Deep dive into why "Повернувшись додому, почався дощ" is a grave error. Explain that the main verb "почався" (started) takes "дощ" (rain) as its subject, making the rain the one who returned home. Teach the fix: always convert to a clause if the subjects differ.
- P4 (~140 words): Decolonization: Avoiding Russianisms. Warn against active participles like *прочитавший* (the one who read). Explain that Russian uses these as adjectives, while Ukrainian uses the gerund "прочитавши" as an adverbial or a clause "який прочитав."
