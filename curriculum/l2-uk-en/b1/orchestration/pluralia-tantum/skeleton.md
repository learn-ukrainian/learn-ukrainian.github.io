## Що таке іменники, які вживаються тільки у множині? (~600 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Introduction based on Литвінова Grade 6 p.134. Explain that while most Ukrainian nouns have both singular (однина) and plural (множина) forms (e.g., стіл/столи, книга/книги), a specific group of nouns exists only in the plural. These are called pluralia tantum. Contrast the flexibility of "regular" nouns with the fixed state of words like "двері" (door) or "окуляри" (glasses).
- P2 (~150 words): Define the grammatical status based on Заболотний Grade 6 p.96. Explain why these nouns do not belong to any of the four standard declension groups (відміни): because declension groups are defined by the ending and gender of the noun in the singular nominative case. Since these nouns lack a singular form, they are "outside" the system of відміни. Use examples: "таксі" (indeclinable) vs. "двері" (plural-only).
- P3 (~150 words): Discuss the semantic logic behind pluralia tantum. Why do they exist? Explain that many refer to paired or composite objects (ножиці — scissors, сани — sleigh) or collective states/actions (канікули — holidays). Mention that this is a cross-linguistic phenomenon but has unique Ukrainian manifestations.
- P4 (~150 words): Introduction of geographical pluralia tantum. Explain that many Ukrainian cities and regions are grammatically plural: Карпати (Carpathians), Черкаси, Суми, Чернівці. Mention the international parallels like Афіни (Athens) or Гімалаї (Himalayas) and how they function in Ukrainian sentences.

## Семантичні групи (~700 words)
- P1 (~175 words): Detailed look at Group 1 — Paired objects (Парні предмети). Focus on "окуляри" (glasses), "ножиці" (scissors), and "штани" (trousers). Introduce the linguistic "test": can you say "одна"? For "шкарпетки" (socks), you can say "одна шкарпетка," so it is NOT pluralia tantum. For "ножиці," there is no "одна ножиця" in standard Ukrainian. 
- P2 (~175 words): Group 2 — Collective and mass concepts (Збірні та масові поняття). Reference Авраменко Grade 10 p.164. Teach "гроші" (money), "дріжджі" (yeast), "парфуми" (perfume), "ліки" (medicine), and "меблі" (furniture). Note that "меблі" is always plural in Ukrainian, unlike "furniture" in English.
- P3 (~175 words): Group 3 — Time periods, events, and games (Часові періоди, події, ігри). Cover "канікули" (holidays), "іменини" (name day), "жнива" (harvest), and "шахи" (chess). Explain the cultural context of "іменини" vs "день народження" (which is singular).
- P4 (~175 words): Dialogue Situation — Packing for a move. A conversation between a resident and a neighbor helping them pack. They discuss finding the "окуляри" in a box, placing "гроші" in an envelope, and checking if the "двері" are locked. This dialogue reinforces the plural agreement of these nouns in a natural setting.

## Відмінювання (~700 words)
- P1 (~175 words): Presentation of the full declension paradigm from Заболотний Grade 6 p.112. Show the forms for "окуляри" (hard stem), "гроші" (soft stem/hissing), and "ворота" (neuter-like plural). Contrast the endings across cases (Н., Р., Д., Зн., Ор., М., Кл.).
- P2 (~175 words): Deep dive into the Genitive (Родовий відмінок) patterns. Explain the three main ending types: -ів/-їв (окулярів, сходів), -ей (грошей, дверей, саней), and zero ending (воріт, Карпат). Emphasize that learners must memorize which word takes which ending as there is no single rule for pluralia tantum.
- P3 (~175 words): Special Instrumental (Орудний відмінок) forms. Introduce the distinctively Ukrainian -ми endings: "грішми" vs "грошима", "ворітьми" vs "воротами", "дверима" vs "дверями". Explain the stylistic nuance: the -ми forms are more archaic, poetic, or characteristic of traditional literary Ukrainian, while the -ами forms are standard modern variants.
- P4 (~175 words): Focus on "гроші" and "люди". Explain that "люди" (people) effectively functions as a pluralia tantum noun (singular is "людина," but the plural paradigm is distinct). Show the forms "людей," "людям," "людьми." Mention the "грішми" vs "грошима" choice again as a key marker of natural speech.

## Чергування в pluralia tantum (~550 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Vowel alternations (о → і). Reiterate the rule from M08: in closed syllables, [о] often changes to [і]. Apply this to "ворота" (nominative, open) → "воріт" (genitive plural, closed). This helps learners predict the genitive forms of nouns with [о] in the stem.
- P2 (~150 words): Fleeting vowels (e/o). Explain how vowels appear or disappear in the genitive zero-ending forms. Contrast "ножиці" (Н.) with "ножиць" (Р. — zero ending, no change) vs a hypothetical noun like "сукні" → "суконь." Discuss "гроші" → "грошей" (vowel retention).
- P3 (~150 words): Consonant shifts and stress in geographical names. Explain how "Чернівці" changes to "Чернівців" and how "Суми" remains "Сумах" in the Locative. Provide a small table of city names and their Locative forms (essential for "I live in..." sentences).
- P4 (~100 words): Short travel narrative about the "Карпати". A 100-word text describing a vacation where the narrator brings "окуляри," pays "гроші" for "ліки," and stays in a house with "дерев'яні двері." Learners identify the cases used for each pluralia tantum noun.

## Singularia tantum — протилежна група (~550 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Contrast with nouns that exist ONLY in the singular (singularia tantum). Reference Литвінова Grade 6 p.134. Group these into semantic categories: abstract concepts (добро, щастя), substances (молоко, золото, залізо), and collective nouns (студентство, листя).
- P2 (~150 words): Why this contrast matters for learners. Explain the "trap" of trying to pluralize abstract nouns (like *дружби) or singularize pluralia tantum (like *один гроші). Highlight that these nouns reflect a specific Ukrainian way of categorizing the world (e.g., "молодь" — youth — is always singular).
- P3 (~150 words): Stylistic exceptions. Mention that in literature, abstract nouns *can* be used in the plural for effect (красоти природи, радості життя), but for B1 learners, the rule is to treat them as singular-only.
- P4 (~100 words): Proper names (Київ, Оксана) vs Plural geographical names (Суми, Черкаси). Reinforce that while "Київ" is singular-only, "Суми" is plural-only, even though both are single cities.

## Узгодження з pluralia tantum (~450 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Grammatical agreement (узгодження). Explain that adjectives and verbs MUST always be plural when used with pluralia tantum nouns, regardless of whether the noun refers to one object. Example: "великі двері" (big door), "мої окуляри зламалися" (my glasses broke). Correct the common L2 error of using singular adjectives (*великий двері).
- P2 (~150 words): Numerals with pluralia tantum. Introduce "одні" for "one" (одні двері, одні ножиці). Explain the use of collective numerals (збірні числівники) for numbers 2-4: "двоє дверей," "троє ножиць," "четверо саней." Mention that "два двері" is less common/stylistically different than "двоє дверей."
- P3 (~150 words): Word order and predicates. Explain that the plural status controls the verb even if the verb comes before the noun. "На столі лежали гроші." Contrast this with singularia tantum: "На столі лежало масло." This reinforces the inherent "plural-ness" of these words.

## Підсумок (~450 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Key Takeaways Recap: 1. Pluralia tantum nouns have no singular form and belong to no declension group. 2. They cover paired objects, collective concepts, events, and many geographical names. 3. Their genitive endings (-ів, -ей, zero) vary and must be learned individually. 4. Agreement with adjectives, verbs, and numerals is always plural.
- P2 (~200 words): Self-check Questions:
  * 1. Які іменники називаються pluralia tantum? (Ті, що мають тільки форму множини).
  * 2. Чому "двері" не належать до I чи II відміни? (Бо відміна визначається за формою однини, якої у слова "двері" немає).
  * 3. Яка форма родового відмінка у слів: "окуляри", "гроші", "ворота"? (Окулярів, грошей, воріт).
  * 4. Як правильно сказати: "one pair of scissors"? (Одні ножиці).
  * 5. Яке чергування відбувається у слові "ворота" в родовому відмінку? (о → і: воріт).
