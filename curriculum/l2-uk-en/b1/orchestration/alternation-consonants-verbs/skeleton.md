## Від іменників до дієслів (~550 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Introduction to the concept of systematic sound change (morphophonemics) as a natural evolution of Ukrainian phonology. Bridge from M09 (noun alternations) by explaining that the same consonants [г, к, х] are involved, but we are moving from the declension of objects to the conjugation of actions.
- P2 (~200 words): Historical explanation based on Заболотний Grade 7. Discuss the ancient suffix *j* and its role in triggering palatalization in the transition from Proto-Slavic to Ukrainian. Explain how this "invisible j" still shapes modern verb endings like -у/-ю.
- P3 (~200 words): Explanation of why alternation is restricted to the 1st person singular (я-form) in II conjugation verbs. Contrast the phonological environment of "я воджу" (historically followed by a palatalizing vowel) with "ти водиш" (where the vowel -и prevented the shift). Use this to set expectations: alternation is a "local" phenomenon in the conjugation table.

## Чергування зубних і свистячих (~850 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Detailed breakdown of the [д] -> [дж] alternation. Provide specific examples like водити — воджу and сидіти — сиджу. Emphasize the unique Ukrainian affricate [дж] and warn against the Russian-influenced [ж] (e.g., *сижу is incorrect).
- P2 (~150 words): The [т] -> [ч] alternation. Explain the shift with examples: крутити — кручу, летіти — лечу, світити — свічу. Introduce the minimal pair contrast between платити — плачу (to pay) and плакати — плачу (to cry), noting that both reach the same form [ч] but from different paths ([т] vs [к]).
- P3 (~150 words): The [з] -> [ж] and [с] -> [ш] shifts. Use high-frequency verbs: возити — вожу, морозити — морожу, носити — ношу, просити — прошу. Explain how these sibilant changes improve the "flow" (euphony) of the verb stem.
- P4 (~200 words): Complex consonant clusters. Explain the transition of [зд] -> [ждж] (їздити — їжджу) and [ст] -> [щ] (мостити — мощу, простити — прощу, чистити — чищу). Clarify that the letter "щ" represents the combined sounds [шч], following the logic of [с]->[ш] and [т]->[ч].
- P5 (~200 words): Full conjugation paradigm for a "dental" verb like "водити". Show the "Alternation Island" effect: я воджу (alternation), ти водиш, він водить, ми водимо, ви водите, вони водять (no alternation). This visual structure helps learners memorize the scope.

## Чергування задньоязикових у дієсловах (~650 words)
- P1 (~250 words): Transition to I conjugation verbs with stems in [г, к, х]. Explain that unlike II conjugation, these verbs often carry the alternation through all persons. Examples: могти — можу/можеш, пекти — печу/печеш, берегти — бережу/бережеш. Reference Глазова Grade 10 for the rule of "stem consistency" in this group.
- P2 (~200 words): Direct comparison with noun alternations from M09. Show that [г] -> [ж] in "друг -> друже" is the same phonological process as "стерегти -> стережу". Explain that the "brain" of the learner should recognize the sound-shift targets [ж, ч, ш] regardless of the part of speech.
- P3 (~200 words): Dialogue: "The Cooking Show Competition." A host narrates the frantic actions of participants: "Я ходжу (д->дж) між столами. Зараз я печу (к->ч) пиріг. Учасник махає рукою, я колишу (х->ш) сковорідку." This anchors the grammar in active narration.

## Чергування губних + [л]: робити — роблю (~650 words)
- P1 (~200 words): Introduction to the "epenthetic L" (insertive L). Explain that for labial consonants [б, п, в, м, ф], the alternation doesn't replace the sound but adds [л] after it. Quote Заболотний Grade 7 on this unique "labial-lateral" partnership.
- P2 (~250 words): Catalog of examples across the labial group. [б] -> [бл]: любити — люблю; [п] -> [пл]: купити — куплю; [в] -> [вл]: ловити — ловлю; [м] -> [мл]: ломити — ломлю. Mention the rare [ф] -> [фл] in words like "графити — графлю."
- P3 (~200 words): Conjugation scope for labials. Highlight that the [л] appears in the 1st person singular (я люблю) AND the 3rd person plural (вони люблять) for II conjugation verbs. This is a crucial distinction from the "dental" group where alternation is only in the 1st person.

## Чергування при утворенні недоконаних дієслів (~600 words)
- P1 (~200 words): Explain the role of consonant alternation in word formation (derivation). When moving from perfective to imperfective using the -ати/-увати suffix, the same shifts occur. Examples: народити (perf) -> народжувати (impf), запросити -> запрошувати.
- P2 (~200 words): The "Decoding Strategy." Teach learners how to find the root of an unfamiliar imperfective verb by "reversing" the alternation. If you see "прощати," you can suspect a root in [ст] like "простити." This builds B1-level linguistic intuition.
- P3 (~200 words): Practical list of common pairs with alternations: скоротити — скорочувати, знизити — знижувати, погасити — погашати, зарядити — заряджати. Emphasize that these changes are permanent in the new word, not just a conjugation shift.

## Повна парадигма: від інфінітива до всіх форм (~600 words)
- P1 (~200 words): "The Alternation Map." A comprehensive check of where alternations *do not* happen. Remind students that alternations are absent in the past tense (носив, а не *ношив), the infinitive (носити), and the future with -му (носитиму).
- P2 (~200 words): Contrast with the Imperative mood (Наказовий спосіб). Explain that despite the 1st person having [ш] in "прошу," the imperative remains [с]: "проси!", "просіть!". This clarifies that the trigger is purely morphological, not a "permanent" change to the verb root.
- P3 (~200 words): Decision Flowchart. Step 1: Identify conjugation (I or II). Step 2: Identify the stem consonant. Step 3: Check if you are in the 1st person present or forming a new aspect. Step 4: Apply the shift. Use "сидіти" and "могти" as test cases for the flowchart.

## Підсумок: таблиця дієслівних чергувань (~450 words)
- P1 (~200 words): Recap of the module. Consonant alternation is not a set of exceptions, but a "phonetic signature" of the Ukrainian verb system. Reiterate the three main groups: dentals, velars, and labials with their respective "partners."
- P2 (~150 words): The Master Reference Table.
  * [д] -> [дж] (ходити — ходжу)
  * [т] -> [ч] (летіти — лечу)
  * [з] -> [ж] (возити — вожу)
  * [с] -> [ш] (писати — пишу)
  * [ст] -> [щ] (мостити — мощу)
  * [б, п, в, м] -> [бл, пл, вл, мл] (люблю, куплю, ловлю, ломлю)
