## Зворотний займенник себе (~650 words total)

When you perform an action that affects yourself, you need a special word to indicate that the subject and the object are the same entity. In Ukrainian, this is the job of the **зворотний** (reflexive) pronoun **себе** (oneself). A fascinating and entirely logical feature of this pronoun is that it completely lacks a Nominative case form, and it does not have gender or number distinctions. Why? The Nominative case is reserved exclusively for the subject of a sentence—the doer of the action. However, the reflexive pronoun **себе** only exists to be the *receiver* or target of that action. It always points back to the subject, regardless of who that subject is. Whether you are talking about yourself, another person, or a group of people, the pronoun remains exactly the same.

Зворотний займенник завжди вказує на те, що дія спрямована на самого виконавця. Наприклад: «Я бачу себе в дзеркалі» або «Вони бачать себе на фотографії». Форма слова залишається незмінною, незалежно від того, хто виконує дію.

> *The reflexive pronoun always indicates that the action is directed at the performer themselves. For example: "I see myself in the mirror" or "They see themselves in the photograph." The form of the word remains unchanged, regardless of who is performing the action.*

Since it has no Nominative form, its declension starts directly from the Genitive case. The paradigm is quite compact and easy to memorize: Genitive is **себе**, Dative is **собі**, Accusative is **себе**, Instrumental is **собою**, and Locative is **на собі**. You will frequently encounter the Dative form **собі** when someone performs an action for their own personal benefit. For instance, if you buy something for yourself, you say «купити собі». Similarly, the phrase «уявити собі» means to imagine something vividly in your own mind. The Instrumental form **собою** is heavily used to express personal states, particularly satisfaction or pride. When you achieve a difficult goal, you can proudly say that you are «задоволений собою» (satisfied with yourself).

Кожен відмінок має свою специфічну функцію в реченні. Якщо ви хочете сказати, що покладаєтесь виключно на власні сили, ви використаєте родовий відмінок: «Я покладаюся тільки на себе». Коли дія приносить вам користь, знадобиться давальний: «Я замовив собі смачну каву».

> *Each case has its specific function in a sentence. If you want to say that you rely solely on your own strength, you will use the genitive case: "I rely only on myself." When an action brings you benefit, the dative is needed: "I ordered myself a delicious coffee."*

:::info
**Grammar box**
The pronoun **себе** never changes for gender or number. «Він береже себе» (He protects himself), «Вона береже себе» (She protects herself), and «Вони бережуть себе» (They protect themselves) all use the exact same form.
:::

Beyond basic grammatical functions, this pronoun is deeply embedded in numerous everyday Ukrainian idioms and fixed expressions. One of the most important verbs you will use to discuss your physical health or emotional well-being is «почуватися». When someone acts independently, without external help or influence, we say they are «сам по собі». If you ask a friend how a new movie was, and they reply «так собі», it means the movie was mediocre. To express that you are speaking on your own behalf, use the phrase «від себе». Finally, if a situation feels eerie or awkward, Ukrainians describe this internal state as «не по собі».

Українська мова має багато сталих виразів із цим займенником, які роблять мовлення природним. Наприклад, коли людина потрапляє в незнайому компанію і нікого не знає, їй може стати не по собі. А коли політик виступає з промовою, він часто додає кілька слів від себе, щоб здаватися більш щирим.

> *The Ukrainian language has many fixed expressions with this pronoun that make speech natural. For example, when a person finds themselves in an unfamiliar company and knows no one, they might feel uncomfortable. And when a politician gives a speech, they often add a few words from themselves to appear more sincere.*

A critical conceptual distinction for learners is understanding the difference between the reflexive pronoun **себе** and the possessive adjective **свій**. While both words point back to the subject of the sentence, they occupy entirely different grammatical slots and serve distinct purposes. The pronoun **себе** acts as the *object* of an action. For example, in the sentence «Він любить себе» (He loves himself), the man is the direct target of his own love. On the other hand, **свій** indicates reflexive *ownership*. In the sentence «Він любить свою роботу» (He loves his own work), the object of his love is the work, and the word **свій** simply confirms that the work belongs to him.

Завжди пам'ятайте про різницю між об'єктом дії та належністю. Займенник «себе» приймає дію на себе і відмінюється як іменник. Натомість слово «свій» завжди супроводжує інший предмет і відмінюється як прикметник, узгоджуючись із цим предметом у роді, числі та відмінку.

> *Always remember the difference between the object of an action and possession. The pronoun "себе" takes the action upon itself and declines like a noun. Instead, the word "свій" always accompanies another object and declines like an adjective, agreeing with that object in gender, number, and case.*
