## Означальні займенники (~550 words total)

The State Standard for B1 introduces another critical category of words: definitive, or universal, pronouns. These words help you generalize about a group, identify a specific entity within a set, or emphasize a noun's identity. The core set includes the word **кожен** (every) and the variations **весь** or **увесь** (all, entire). You will also learn to distinguish **сам** (self, alone) from its emphatic counterpart. You already use some of these intuitively, but understanding their precise grammatical behavior allows you to build more complex and accurate sentences.

Unlike a **неозначений** (indefinite) pronoun which points to an unknown object, definitive pronouns clarify the scope of something already known. A definitive **займенник** (pronoun) acts very much like an adjective, agreeing with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. This group also includes words like **інший** (other) and **самий** (the exact same).

Кожна людина має свої переконання, і весь наш досвід формує наше бачення світу. Іноді ми повинні діяти самі, без допомоги інших людей. Коли ми зустрічаємо ту саму проблему знову, ми починаємо шукати інший підхід. Означальні займенники дозволяють нам робити такі загальні висновки чіткими та зрозумілими.

> *Every person has their own beliefs, and our entire experience shapes our vision of the world. Sometimes we must act alone, without the help of other people. When we encounter the exact same problem again, we start looking for another approach. Definitive pronouns allow us to make such general conclusions clear and understandable.*

When it comes to declension, most of these pronouns follow standard adjectival patterns, but the word meaning "all" or "entire" has an irregular paradigm that you must memorize. The masculine and neuter singular forms share the same oblique cases: the Genitive is **всього** (or усього), the Dative is **всьому**, the Instrumental is **всім**, and the Locative is **на всьому**. The feminine singular paradigm uses **вся**, **всієї**, **всій**, **всю**, **всією**, and **на всій**.

In the plural, you will use the form **всі** or **усі**. The plural cases are **всіх** for Genitive and Accusative, **всім** for Dative, **всіма** for Instrumental, and **на всіх** for Locative.

:::info
**Милозвучність (Euphony) with Pronouns**
The choice between **весь** and **увесь** follows the standard Ukrainian rules for avoiding consonant clusters. After a consonant, you should use **увесь**, and after a vowel, you use **весь**. This same principle applies to the preposition **з** (with). To avoid a harsh sound before the Instrumental form **всіма**, you must use the variant **зі**.

**Я згоден зі всіма.** — *I agree with everyone.*
:::

Усі студенти успішно склали іспит. Ми розіслали запрошення всім учасникам конференції. Керівник проекту був задоволений усіма результатами нашої роботи. Важливо звертати увагу на милозвучність, коли ми спілкуємося зі всіма нашими колегами.

> *All students successfully passed the exam. We sent out invitations to all conference participants. The project manager was satisfied with all the results of our work. It is important to pay attention to euphony when we communicate with all our colleagues.*

A common point of confusion for learners is the distinction between **сам** and **самий**. While they look similar and share a root, they serve entirely different functions. The pronoun **сам** (alone, unaided) emphasizes that the subject performed the action independently, and that **ніхто** (nobody) else helped them. It declines exactly like a hard-stem adjective: **самого**, **самому**, **самим**.

In contrast, the word **самий** is used for emphasis to mean "the very" or "the exact same" when paired with the demonstrative pronoun **той**. It identifies an exact match or specifies a strict limit, such as "in the very center". The pronouns **інший** and **кожен** also decline predictably like standard adjectives, giving us forms like **кожного** and **кожному**.

Він сам побудував цей будинок. Це той самий чоловік, якого ми бачили вчора. Ми зустрілися в самому центрі міста. Кожного разу він використовує інший інструмент.

> *He built this house by himself. This is the exact same man whom we saw yesterday. We met in the very center of the city. Every time he uses another tool.*
