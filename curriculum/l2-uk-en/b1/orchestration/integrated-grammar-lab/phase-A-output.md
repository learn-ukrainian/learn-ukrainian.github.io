## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Fight Against "Kantseliaryt" (Канцелярит)**: Coined originally in the 1960s to criticize bureaucratic Soviet-era language, the concept is deeply relevant to modern Ukrainian. Contemporary Ukrainian style guides strongly advocate for eradicating artificial passive constructions and nominalizations in favor of dynamic, active verbs.
2. **The Richness of Synonymic Rows (Синонімічні ряди)**: Ukrainian boasts vast synonymic variation that allows precise stylistic calibration (e.g., *друг* [neutral] vs. *товариш* [formal/contextual] vs. *приятель* [casual]). Choosing the right synonym is key to natural register switching.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Passive Voice Calques** → Learner says "Ця книга читається мною" instead of "Я читаю цю книгу". *Why it happens*: Direct translation from English or Russian passive structures. Ukrainian strongly prefers the active voice (активний стан).
2. **Register Mismatch** → Learner uses "Я здійснив покупку" in a text to a friend instead of "Я купив". *Why it happens*: Over-reliance on formal dictionary definitions without understanding stylistic context.

- **100% Immersion**: This is B1.4. Absolutely no English scaffolding in the prose. All explanations, meta-language, and scenario descriptions must be in Ukrainian.
- Use explicit visual tables to compare the same message across 3 different registers (official, neutral, casual).
