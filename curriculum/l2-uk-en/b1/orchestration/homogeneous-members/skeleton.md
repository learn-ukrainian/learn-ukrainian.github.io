## Сполучники при однорідних членах (~1150 words total)
- P1 (~140 words): [Classification of Conjunctions: Based on Avramenko (Grade 7), categorize coordinating conjunctions into 'єднальні' (additive), 'протиставні' (adversative), and 'розділові' (disjunctive). Explain that the choice of conjunction changes the logic of the relationship between items.]
- P2 (~160 words): [Єднальні (Additive) Logic: Focus on 'і (й)', 'та' (=і), and 'також'. Explain the single conjunction rule (no comma: 'степ і легіт') versus the repeated conjunction rule (comma before each repetition: 'І яблука, і груші, і сливи'). Mention euphony (і vs й).]
- P3 (~160 words): [Протиставні (Adversative) Logic: Focus on 'а', 'ле', 'проте', 'зате', 'однак'. Emphasize the 'Golden Rule' of Ukrainian punctuation: ALWAYS place a comma before these conjunctions. Example: 'Підніматися важко, але радісно'. Contrast 'а' (opposition) vs 'але' (concession).]
- P4 (~160 words): [Розділові (Disjunctive) Logic: Focus on 'або', 'чи', 'то...то', 'чи...чи'. Explain that a single 'або/чи' requires no comma, but repeated ones like 'То дощ, то сонце' require a comma before the second item. Discuss the nuance of choosing between options.]
- P5 (~120 words): [Dialogue: Friends planning a weekend trip. 'Ірина: Поїдемо і на Синевир, і до Мукачева. Дмитро: А мені хочеться або в гори, або на узбережжя.' Use this to demonstrate natural repeated enumeration in speech with correct commas.]
- P6 (~150 words): [Euphony and Conjunctions: Deep dive into the choice between 'і', 'й', and 'та'. Explain when 'та' is preferred for rhythm (e.g., 'батько та мати') and how 'й' avoids hiatus (vowel clashes). Reference 'правила милозвучності' from A1/A2.]
- P7 (~130 words): [L2 Common Error — The English 'Oxford Comma': Explain that in Ukrainian, unlike some English styles, a comma is NEVER used before a single 'і/та/або' that connects the last item in a list. 'Я люблю каву, чай і сік' — No comma after 'чай'.]
