## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Реченець" Debate**: While *дедлайн* is widely used in IT and modern business, linguistic purists and official style guides advocate for **кінцевий термін** or the revival of the archaic **реченець**. This is a great C1-level nuance to introduce at B1 as a "fun fact" about language purity.
2. **Standard vs. Flexible**: The legal standard is a 40-hour work week (Mon-Fri, 9-18), but the IT sector (a major employer for English speakers) heavily favors flexible hours (*гнучкий графік*) and remote work (*віддалена робота*), creating a split in work culture vocabulary.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Perfective in Present**: Learners often try to use perfective verbs for present actions (e.g., *Я напишу звіт зараз* meaning "I am writing it now") → Correction: *Я пишу звіт зараз*. Perfective = Future result or Past completion.
2. **Process vs. Result Confusion**: *Я робив звіт вчора* (I worked on it, maybe didn't finish) vs. *Я зробив звіт вчора* (I finished it). Learners often default to Imperfective (*робив*) when they mean the result (*зробив*).
3. **"Deadline" Gender**: *Дедлайн* is masculine, but learners might confuse it based on ending sounds or native language.
