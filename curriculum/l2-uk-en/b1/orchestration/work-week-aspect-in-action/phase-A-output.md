## Cultural Hooks
1. Modern vs. Traditional Terminology: The transition in modern Ukrainian corporate culture from Soviet-era bureaucratic terms ("нарада", "планірка") to globalized loanwords ("мітинг", "дедлайн", "колл"), especially driven by Ukraine's massive IT sector.
2. The standard Ukrainian work week is 40 hours according to the Labor Code (КЗпП), but the concept of "віддалена робота" (remote work) and "гнучкий графік" (flexible schedule) became deeply embedded in the work culture long before global shifts, largely due to the tech industry.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Using Perfective in the Present**: Constructing phrases like "*Я напишу звіт зараз*" to describe current action. → Correct form: "Я пишу звіт зараз" (НДВ). English speakers confuse their intention (will write) with the Ukrainian grammatical requirement for present processes (imperfective).
2. **Process vs. Result in the Past Tense**: Saying "Я робив звіт" to mean the report is finished. → Correct form: "Я зробив звіт" (ДВ). The imperfective only indicates that time was spent on the task, not that it was completed.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Treat modern Ukrainian business culture as inherently integrated with European/global norms. Avoid comparisons to Russian corporate structures or Soviet hangovers unless explicitly discussing archaic terms (like "планірка" vs "мітинг"). 
- Ensure all vowel notations use correct IPA: о = [ɔ], е = [ɛ].
- Keep all explanations strictly in Ukrainian (100% immersion) to meet B1 requirements.
