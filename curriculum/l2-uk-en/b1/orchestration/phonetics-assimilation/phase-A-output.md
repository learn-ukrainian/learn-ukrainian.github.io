## Cultural Hooks
1. **Співоча мова (Singing language)**: Ukrainian is fundamentally structured around "милозвучність" (euphony). Phonetic rules exist to prevent harsh, unpronounceable consonant clusters, resulting in the melodic rhythm characteristic of traditional Ukrainian folk songs.
2. **Harmony over Restriction**: Unlike languages that tolerate heavy consonant clusters, Ukrainian actively "cleans up" its phonetics (simplification, alternating у/в and і/й) to ensure a smooth flow of breath and sound.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Devoicing Final Consonants** → Correct form: [хл'іб], [дід] — Russian and German speakers often erroneously devoice final consonants (e.g., saying "хліп" or "діт"). Ukrainian strictly preserves final voiced consonants.
2. **Mispronouncing Reflexive -ться** → Correct form: [ц'ц'а] — Learners often read it literally as [т'-с'а] or [ца]. It must be articulated as a long, soft [ц'].
3. **Cluster Literalism** → Correct form: [чесний], [сонце] — Attempting to pronounce silent letters that have been simplified out (e.g., trying to say the 'т' in чесний or the 'л' in сонце).
