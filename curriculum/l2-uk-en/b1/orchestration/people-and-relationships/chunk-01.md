## Зовнішність людини: портретна лексика (~770 words total)

When you read a Ukrainian novel, study a historical text, or simply listen to a friend describe someone they met, you are engaging with a linguistic portrait. In Ukrainian grammar and literature studies, this is formally called an "опис зовнішності людини" (description of a person's appearance). This is a foundational skill that bridges basic vocabulary with advanced narrative abilities.

Опис зовнішності людини — це відтворення її індивідуального вигляду за допомогою засобів мови. Зазвичай такий опис є важливою частиною розповіді або великого художнього тексту. У ньому обов'язково називають три основні елементи: частини тіла, риси обличчя та особливості одягу.

> *A description of a person's appearance is the recreation of their individual look using language tools. Usually, such a description is an important part of a narrative or a large literary text. It necessarily names three main elements: body parts, facial features, and clothing features.*

This structured approach helps build a complete, vivid image of a person's **зовнішність** (appearance — a person's outward look) in the reader's or listener's mind. To do this naturally, you need a specific set of adjectives known as portrait vocabulary.

The foundation of any verbal portrait begins with general characteristics like age and height. Ukrainian offers precise adjectives to categorize these stages of life and physical dimensions accurately.

Для опису віку ми використовуємо такі слова, як юний, підлітковий, молодий, зрілий та літній. Юний стосується ранньої молодості, тоді як підлітковий описує тінейджерів. Слово зрілий вказує на людину середнього віку з багатим життєвим досвідом, а літній — це дуже ввічливий спосіб сказати про старшу людину.

> *To describe age, we use words like youthful, adolescent, young, mature, and elderly. Youthful refers to early youth, while adolescent describes teenagers. The word mature indicates a middle-aged person with rich life experience, and elderly is a very polite way to speak about an older person.*

When discussing height, the categories are relatively straightforward. A person can be "високий" (tall), "середнього зросту" (of average height), or "невисокий" (short). Notice that the word "зріст" (height) is a noun, so "середнього зросту" is a genitive construction literally meaning "of average height". You will often see these terms combined with age to set the initial scene.

Moving beyond simple height, a true portrait captures how a person holds themselves and their overall physical frame. This involves their **постава** (posture/bearing — how a person holds their body) and their general build. 

Статура людини може бути дуже різною, і українська мова має багатий словник для цього. Наприклад, дорослий чоловік може бути кремезний або широкоплечий. З іншого боку, молода дівчина часто буває струнка або має дуже тендітний вигляд.

> *A person's build can be very different, and the Ukrainian language has a rich vocabulary for this. For example, an adult man can be stocky or broad-shouldered. On the other hand, a young girl is often slender or has a very delicate look.*

The adjective **кремезний** (stocky/sturdy — of strong build) implies physical strength and solidity, often associated with a broad chest and a robust frame. In stark contrast, **тендітний** (slender/delicate — of light build) suggests fragility, elegance, and physical lightness. Other highly descriptive terms include "приземкуватий" (squat/stocky), which indicates someone who is short and thickset, and "стрункий" (slender/graceful), which is universally used to describe a well-proportioned, lean figure for any gender.

The most expressive part of any portrait, however, is the face and hair. The details here are endless, but let us focus on the most common descriptive categories used in Ukrainian literature and daily conversation.

Коли ми описуємо волосся, ми завжди звертаємо увагу на його колір та структуру. Волосся може бути русяве, каштанове, темне або світле. За своєю структурою воно буває кучеряве або зовсім пряме. Очі можуть бути карі, блакитні, зелені або сірі.

> *When we describe hair, we always pay attention to its color and texture. Hair can be light brown, chestnut, dark, or light. By its texture, it can be curly or completely straight. Eyes can be brown, blue, green, or gray.*

The word "русяве" specifically refers to the natural, light-brown or dark-blond hair color that is very common in Eastern Europe. "Каштанове" is a warm, rich chestnut brown. When describing the face itself, a person might be called **вродливий** (beautiful/handsome — describing a person). A face can also be "смагляве" (swarthy/dark-skinned) from spending time in the sun, "рум'яне" (rosy-cheeked), or simply "бліде" (pale). 

:::tip
**Внутрішній світ через зовнішність**
In Ukrainian literature and school pedagogy, describing appearance is never just a cold list of physical facts. According to standard curriculum guidelines, a writer shows the character's inner world, their life history, and their personality through elements of external characterization. A "привітний погляд" (welcoming gaze) or a "задумане обличчя" (thoughtful face) tells the reader immediately about the person's soul, not just their anatomy.
:::

To master this vocabulary, observe how Ukrainian authors combine these elements. They rarely list everything; instead, they select a few striking features to create a lasting impression. Notice how the adjectives change endings to agree with masculine and feminine subjects.

Це був чоловік середнього віку та високого зросту. Він мав кремезну статуру та дуже засмагле обличчя. Його русяве волосся було трохи кучеряве, а погляд — завжди теплий і привітний.

> *It was a man of middle age and tall height. He had a sturdy build and a very tanned face. His light brown hair was a bit curly, and his gaze was always warm and welcoming.*

Поруч з ним стояла юна дівчина. Вона була невисока, неймовірно тендітна та струнка. Її довге каштанове волосся падало на плечі, а великі карі очі дивилися на світ з цікавістю.

> *Next to him stood a youthful girl. She was short, incredibly delicate, and slender. Her long chestnut hair fell on her shoulders, and her large brown eyes looked at the world with curiosity.*

In these short excerpts, we easily identify the portrait vocabulary categorized by its function: age and height ("юна", "високого зросту"), physical build ("кремезну", "тендітна"), facial appearance ("засмагле обличчя"), hair color and texture ("русяве", "каштанове"), and the emotional impression of the eyes ("карі очі", "привітний").
