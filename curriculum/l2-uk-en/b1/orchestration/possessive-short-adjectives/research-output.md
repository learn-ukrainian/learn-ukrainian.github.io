## Common Learner Errors
1. *батьківський дім vs. батьків дім → Learners often confuse relational adjectives (-ськ-) with true possessive adjectives (-ів/-ин). Why it happens: English uses "father's" for both general relation and specific ownership.
2. *рад хлопчик vs. хлопчик рад → Using short-form adjectives in an attributive position before a noun. Why it happens: Learners assume short forms can be placed before nouns just like full adjectives, ignoring their strictly predicative nature in modern Ukrainian.
3. Overuse of genitive (книга батька) instead of possessive (батькова книга) → Why it happens: The genitive case is introduced earlier (A1/A2) and feels like a safer, universal fallback, leading to less natural-sounding phrasing in contexts where a possessive adjective would be more idiomatic.
