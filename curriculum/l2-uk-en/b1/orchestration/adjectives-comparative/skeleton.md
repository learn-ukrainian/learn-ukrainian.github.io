Ukrainian comparatives are not just adverbs; they are full adjectives that must agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Examples: сильніший (m.), сильніша (f.), сильніше (n.), сильніші (pl.). Contrast this with the invariable English "stronger".]
- P3 (~150 words): [Qualitative vs. Relative Adjectives: Review of adjective types from A2. Explanation that only "якісні" (qualitative) adjectives can form degrees because they represent traits that can exist in varying intensities (e.g., теплий, розумний). Mention that "відносні" (relative, e.g., дерев'яний) and "присвійні" (possessive, e.g., батьків) are logically excluded.]

## Проста форма вищого ступеня (~950 words total)
- P1 (~150 words): [The Suffix -іш-: The most productive and standard pattern. Applied to stems ending in a cluster or specific consonants. Examples: холодний → холодніший, цікавий → цікавіший, красивий → красивіший. Note on stress: the stress often shifts to the suffix -іш-.]
- P2 (~150 words): [The Suffix -ш-: Used with a smaller, more restricted group of adjectives, often with monosyllabic roots or those ending in -к-, -ок-, -ек- (which drop during formation). Examples: старий → старший, молодий → молодший, тонкий → тонший.]
- P3 (~150 words): [Consonant Alternations (The [г/з/к] + -ш- Pattern): Detailed explanation of phonetic shifts based on Avramenko Grade 6. [г] + -ш- = [жч] (дорогий → дорожчий); [з] + -ш- = [жч] (вузький → вужчий); [к] + -ш- = [чч] (written as -щ- in light of historical spelling) (легкий → легший [exception], but note the general rule for others like тяжкий → тяжчий).]
- P4 (~150 words): [Consonant Alternations (The [с] + -ш- Pattern): Focusing on the shift [с] + -ш- = [шч] (written as -щ-). Primary example: високий → вищий. Secondary example: красний → кращий (noting this as a historical root for "beautiful/good"). Warning against the common error *високіший*.]

## Складена форма вищого ступеня (~750 words total)
- P1 (~180 words): [Formation Mechanics: The Analytic form. Rule: більш (more) or менш (less) + the base (nominative singular masculine) form of the adjective. Examples: більш складний, менш яскравий, більш відомий. Crucial point: the adjective after більш/менш must remain in its base dictionary form before declension, not the comparative suffixal form.]
- P2 (~150 words): [Register and Usage: When to choose Складена over Проста. Explanation that the analytic form is preferred in formal, scientific, and official-business styles (e.g., "більш об'єктивний аналіз"). Also, it is used for adjectives where the synthetic form is phonetically awkward (e.g., більш синій instead of ?синіший).]
- P3 (~170 words): [The "Anti-Russicism" Warning: Zero-tolerance rule for mixing forms. Explanation from Avramenko Grade 11 that *більш сильніший* is a hybrid error (Russicism). One must use either сильніший OR більш сильний. Examples of corrected sentences: "Він став більш розумним" (correct) vs "Він став більш розумнішим" (wrong).]
- P4 (~150 words): [Declension of the Analytic Form: Explanation that 'більш' and 'менш' are invariable particles. Only the following adjective declines. Examples: "з більш цікавим фільмом" (Instr.), "у більш зручному кріслі" (Loc.).]

## Порівняльні конструкції (~700 words total)
- P1 (~160 words): [Construction 1: 'за' + Accusative (Знахідний відмінок). The most frequent colloquial form. "Він вищий за мене." "Ця книга цікавіша за ту." Explanation that the object of comparison follows the preposition 'за' in the Accusative case.]
- P2 (~160 words): [Construction 2: 'від' + Genitive (Родовий відмінок). Slightly more formal or literary. "Він вищий від мене." "Цей шлях коротший від попереднього." Note that the meaning is identical to the 'за' construction.]
- P3 (~160 words): [Construction 3: 'ніж' + Nominative (Називний відмінок). Used for complex comparisons or when comparing actions/qualities. "Він вищий, ніж я (є)." "Сьогодні тепліше, ніж було вчора." Explanation of the comma usage before 'ніж'.]
- P4 (~120 words): [Degree Modifiers: Using adverbs to intensify the comparison. Introduction of 'набагато' (much), 'значно' (significantly), 'трохи' (a bit), and 'куди' (way - informal). Examples: "набагато кращий", "значно дорожчий", "трохи легший".]
