## Cultural Hooks
1.  **Didukh vs. Christmas Tree**: Before the Christmas tree (yalynka) became popular, the central decoration in Ukrainian homes was the **Didukh** (дідух) — a sheaf of wheat symbolizing the spirit of ancestors and the harvest. It is believed to bring prosperity and protection to the family.
2.  **Malanka Carnival**: On "Old New Year" (Jan 13-14), Ukrainians celebrate **Malanka**, a folk carnival featuring costumed characters like the Goat (Koza), Malanka (a man dressed as a woman), and the Gypsy. It involves skits, pranks, and caroling, distinct from the solemnity of Christmas.
3.  **Modern Scale**: **Atlas Festival** (formerly Atlas Weekend) in Kyiv is one of the largest music festivals in Eastern Europe, showing that Ukrainian "festivals" are not just folk traditions but massive modern events with international headliners.

## Common Learner Errors
1.  **Case Error in Greetings**: Learners often translate "Happy [Holiday]" literally or use Nominative.
    *   *Error*: "Вітаю з свято!" / "З Новий рік!"
    *   *Correct*: "Вітаю зі **святом**!" / "З **Новим роком**!" (Must use **Instrumental** case with preposition **з/зі**).
2.  **Greeting Specificity**: Using generic greetings when specific ones are required.
    *   *Context*: On Christmas, the greeting is "Христос народився!" (Response: "Славімо його!"). On Easter: "Христос Воскрес!" (Response: "Воістину Воскрес!"). Using "Привіт" or just "Вітаю" can seem culturally unaware in these specific religious contexts.
3.  **Date Confusion**: Mixing up the "Old" (Julian) and "New" (Gregorian) dates. While Ukraine officially moved to Dec 25 for Christmas in 2023, learners often confuse the "Old New Year" (Jan 14) concept, which is culturally celebrated but not an official state holiday.
