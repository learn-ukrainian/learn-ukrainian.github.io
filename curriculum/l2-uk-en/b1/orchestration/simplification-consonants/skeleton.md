## Що таке спрощення? (~650 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): [Intro — Setting the stage by bridging from M08-M10. We have seen sounds change (alternation), but now we see them vanish. Define "спрощення" (simplification) as the dropping of a consonant from a cluster to achieve "милозвучність" (euphony). Use the example of "щастя" (happiness) vs "щасливий" (happy) where the [т] disappears.]
- P2 (~150 words): [Phonetic logic — Explaining the "Cluster of Three." When word formation creates a sequence like [стн] or [здн], it is heavy for the tongue. Ukrainian prefers a flow of two-consonant clusters. Use the visual from Litvinova Grade 5: "проїЗД + Н = проїЗДНий -> проїзний." Describe the physical feeling of trying to say [здн] versus the smooth [зн].]
- P3 (~130 words): [Morphophonemic distinction — Crucial comparison between alternation (чергування) and simplification (спрощення). In alternation, the sound transforms (г -> ж in "друг -> друже"). In simplification, it is deleted (т -> zero in "користь -> корисний"). Both serve the same master: the musicality of the language.]
- Dialogue (~120 words): [Teacher (Вчитель) and students (Студенти) in a Dnipro classroom. The teacher asks why we don't write "серДце." A student tries to pronounce it with the [д] and laughs because it sounds like a hiccup. They conclude that "серце" sounds "lighter" and "more Ukrainian."]
- P4 (~130 words): [The "Rule of the Middle Man" — Explaining that it is almost always the middle consonant in a triple-cluster that gets sacrificed. In [стн], [т] is between [с] and [н], so it drops. This establishes a predictable pattern for the learner to look for when they see three consonants in a row.]

## Спрощення, закріплене на письмі (~950 words total)
- P1 (~100 words): [Defining "закріплене на письмі" — Unlike English where we keep silent letters (like the 'k' in 'knife'), Ukrainian spelling is phonetic. If we don't say it, we usually don't write it. This section covers the core rules where the letter is officially removed from the dictionary form.]
- P2 (~130 words): [Pattern 1: [ждн] -> [жн]. The classic example: "тиждень" (week) becoming "тижневий" (weekly). Note how the [д] vanishes completely in both sound and ink. Compare this to Russian "еженедельный" to show the Ukrainian-specific preference for simplification.]
- P3 (~130 words): [Pattern 2: [здн] -> [зн]. Examples: "проїзд" (passage) -> "проїзний" (travel card), "виїзд" (exit) -> "виїзний" (outbound). Explain that these are essential words for transport and travel, making this rule high-priority for daily life.]
- P4 (~130 words): [Pattern 3: [стл] -> [сл]. Examples: "щастя" -> "щасливий" (happy), "лестощі" (flattery) -> "улесливий" (fawning). Mention that "щасливий" is a high-frequency word and knowing why the 'т' is gone helps with root identification.]
- P5 (~160 words): [Pattern 4: [стн] -> [сн]. The most productive group. List examples: "радість -> радісний" (joyful), "якість -> якісний" (high-quality), "честь -> чесний" (honest), "вість -> вісник" (herald). This pattern is the "workhorse" of adjective formation from abstract nouns.]
- P6 (~100 words): [Pattern 5: [скн] -> [сн]. Verb-focused. Examples: "бризки" (splashes) -> "бризнути" (to splash), "тиск" (pressure) -> "тиснути" (to press). Show how the [к] disappears in the verb form to simplify the action-word.]
- P7 (~100 words): [Pattern 6: [слн] -> [сн]. A specific culinary/daily example: "масло" (butter/oil) -> "масний" (greasy/buttery). This completes the table of common phonetic casualties.]
- Exercise (~100 words): [match-up — Match base words with their simplified derivatives. Items: проїзд/проїзний, щастя/щасливий, тиждень/тижневий, радість/радісний, користь/корисний, честь/чесний, масло/масний, область/обласний.]

## Винятки: спрощення у вимові, але не на письмі (~750 words total)
- P1 (~120 words): [Intro to Exceptions — Just as the learner gets comfortable dropping letters, we introduce the "stubborn" words. Some words simplify in speech (you don't hear the middle sound) but keep the letter in writing to preserve the visual link to the root. This is "орфографічна пам'ять."]
- P2 (~160 words): [Group 1: The "No-Simplification" Trio. These words simplify neither in speech nor writing: "пестливий" (affectionate), "хвастливий" (boastful), "кістлявий" (bony). You must pronounce and write the [стл] cluster fully. Explain that these are descriptive adjectives where the cluster adds a certain "sharpness" to the word.]
- P3 (~140 words): [Group 2: The "Silent T" in Numbers. The most famous example: "шістнадцять" (sixteen). We pronounce it [шіс:нац':ат'], dropping the [т] sound, but we MUST write the 'т' because of "шість" (six). If we removed it, the connection to the number six would be visually lost.]
- P4 (~130 words): [Group 3: Medical and Foreign Terms. "зап'ястний" (carpal/wrist-related) keeps the 'т' to stay close to "зап'ястя." Also, loanwords like "контрастний" (contrast), "баластний" (ballast), and "аванпостний" (outpost) keep the letters to match their international forms.]
- P5 (~100 words): [Mnemonic for the "Silent T" — How to remember: If the word is a number (16, 600) or a technical term borrowed from another language, keep the 'т'. If it's a "natural" Ukrainian feeling word like "happy" or "joyful," drop it.]
- Exercise (~100 words): [quiz — Decide: does the derived word keep or drop the consonant? Items: шість + надцять (шістнадцять - keep), радість + -ний (радісний - drop), пестити + -ливий (пестливий - keep), тиждень + -ний (тижневий - drop), контраст + -ний (контрастний - keep).]

## Спрощення [сонце], [серце] та інші особливі випадки (~650 words total)
- P1 (~150 words): [Etymological Ghosts — Words like "сонце" (sun) and "серце" (heart) contain historical simplifications. In "сонце," there was once an [л] (related to "солоній" - old sun root), and in "серце," there was a [д] (related to "сердитий" - angry/heart-felt). We teach these as fixed spelling rules (орфограми) because the base form is no longer used in modern grammar.]
- P2 (~130 words): [Cultural framing — Connecting these words to the concept of "милозвучність." Ukrainian doesn't just simplify for speed; it simplifies for beauty. Clusters of 4 consonants are avoided at all costs. "Сонце" and "серце" are the "poster children" of this phonetic grooming.]
- P3 (~150 words): [Complex families — Look at the word "тиждень" again. It's a morphophonemic puzzle. "Тиждень" (nominative) -> "тижня" (genitive, vowel [е] drops) -> "тижневий" (adjective, consonant [д] drops). Showing how multiple rules from B1.1 converge on a single word family.]
- P4 (~120 words): [Diagnostic Tip — If you aren't sure if a word has simplified, try to find a related word where the consonants are separated by a vowel. For "серце," think of "сердечний." The [д] appears there, proving it is "hiding" in the simplified form.]
- Exercise (~100 words): [error-correction — Find spelling errors in a short paragraph. Items: *щастливий (щасливий), *тиждневий (тижневий), *шіснадцять (шістнадцять), *радістний (радісний), *контрасний (контрастний).]

## Спрощення у дієслівних формах та прикметниках (~650 words total)
- P1 (~150 words): [Adjective formation — Focus on the suffix "-ний." When adding this suffix to a noun ending in a consonant cluster, simplification is almost guaranteed. Example: "область" (region) -> "обласний" (regional). Note that "обласний" is essential for understanding Ukrainian geography and administration.]
- P2 (~150 words): [The "Momentary" Verbs — Simplification in verbs ending in "-нути." These usually describe a quick, sudden action. Examples: "блиск" (glitter) -> "блиснути" (to flash/glance), "тріск" (crack) -> "тріснути" (to snap/crack). The [к] drops to make the word sound as fast as the action it describes.]
- P3 (~150 words): [Syntactic application — Using these simplified words in sentences. "Це був чесний і корисний вибір" (It was an honest and useful choice). Showing that these aren't just spelling rules; they are the building blocks of natural B1-level prose.]
- P4 (~100 words): [Review of the three tools — Now the learner has: vowel alternation (M08), consonant alternation (M09-10), and simplification (M11). Explain that these three together explain 90% of why Ukrainian words "change shape" during declension and derivation.]
- Exercise (~100 words): [fill-in — Form adjectives or verbs from nouns. Items: совість (совісний), область (обласний), ненависть (ненависний), тиск (тиснути), бризки (бризнути), виїзд (виїзний), радість (радісно - adverb).]

## Спрощення vs. чергування: порівняння (~450 words total)
- P1 (~150 words): [The Diagnostic Table — Analyzing the side-by-side comparison. Rule 1: Does it change (A->B)? Rule 2: Does it disappear (A->0)? Use "козак -> козацький" (к->ц alternation) versus "проїзд -> проїзний" (д->0 simplification) to illustrate.]
- P2 (~100 words): [Reversibility — Both processes are reversible when you go back to the base word. This is the key for checking your work. If you "undo" the simplification of "щасливий," you get "щастя." If you "undo" the alternation of "друже," you get "друг."]
- P3 (~100 words): [Authentic Text Peek — A three-sentence snippet from a Ukrainian news report about a "тижневий фестиваль" (weekly festival) that was "радісний" (joyful) for the "друже" (friend). Learners must spot and label the processes.]
