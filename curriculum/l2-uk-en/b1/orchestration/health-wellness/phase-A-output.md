# Дослідження: Здоров'я та самопочуття

## State Standard Reference
§3.7: "Здоров’я й особиста гігієна: частини тіла; особиста гігієна; самопочуття; хвороби й нездужання; візит до лікаря; аптека; діагностика (аналізи й обстеження)."
Alignment: This module directly addresses §3.7 by teaching terminology for "самопочуття", "хвороби й нездужання", and equipping learners with functional language for a "візит до лікаря", "аптека", and medical "діагностика".

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Cult of "Protyah" (Draft)**: In Ukrainian folk belief, a draft is considered a primary cause of illness, even in summer. This leads to social phenomena like strictly closing windows in public transport to avoid "протяг". (Source: Ministry of Health myths & facts).
2. **Kalyna in Folk Medicine**: Viburnum (калина) is an iconic home remedy in Ukraine, containing double the vitamin C of lemons. It is traditionally mashed with honey to lower fever and boost immunity during a "застуда". (Source: fitomarket.com.ua, simya.com.ua).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **"Я відчуваю добре" → "Я почуваюся добре"** — Learners confuse "відчувати" (perception/sensing, takes Accusative object) with "почуватися" (internal state, takes adverbs).
2. **"Хворіти грипом" (Instr.) → "Хворіти на грип" (на + Acc.)** — The instrumental case is a Russism. Ukrainian strictly uses "на" + Accusative for illnesses.
3. **"Я маю біль голови" → "У мене болить голова"** — Direct English translation is unnatural; Ukrainian uses the locative possessive construction with the verb agreeing with the body part.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonization**: Teach "хворіти на" positively as an authentic Ukrainian construction without centering the explanation on "why Russian is different".
- **Tone**: Maintain the "Active Patient" persona, encouraging agency over one's health rather than a passive medical narrative.
- **Immersion**: B1.6 requires 100% Ukrainian immersion in prose. No English scaffolding is permitted.
