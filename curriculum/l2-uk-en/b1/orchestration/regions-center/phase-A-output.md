## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Ethnographic Encyclopedia**: Ivan Kotlyarevsky's "Eneida" (1798), written in the Poltava dialect, is not just a poem but an exhaustive catalog of Ukrainian traditions, food, and daily life, solidifying the dialect as the basis for the modern literary language.
2. **The Proto-Sich**: Almost 20 years before the famous Zaporozhian Sich was founded, Ostafiy Dashkevych, a starosta of the Cherkasy lands, proposed the first defensive fortifications on the Dnipro, laying the geographic and conceptual groundwork for the Cossack republic.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Russianisms in Toponyms** → Learners often write "Черкасси" (double 'с') or "Полтавщіна" (incorrect vowel). Must drill the Ukrainian forms: *Черкаси*, suffix *-щина*.
2. **Case Mismatch with Destinations** → Confusing motion vs. location prepositions. Saying "їхати в Черкасах" instead of "їхати до Черкас" (Genitive for destination), and "бути до Черкас" instead of "бути в Черкасах" (Locative for location).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Frame Kyiv's history as a continuous Ukrainian European tradition; explicitly reject the imperial "cradle of three brotherly nations" myth. Frame Kotlyarevsky's work not as a "reaction" to empire, but as a vibrant, organic expression of an unbroken cultural continuum.
- **Agency**: Ensure Ukrainians are the active subjects (e.g., "Козаки будували державу", not "Держава була збудована").
