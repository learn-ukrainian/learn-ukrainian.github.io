## Cultural Hooks
1. **Cordocentrism (Philosophy of the Heart)**: Ukrainian culture is historically "cordocentric" (Hryhorii Skovoroda, Pamfil Yurkevych), emphasizing the "heart" as the center of emotional life and cognition, unlike the Western "head/mind" focus. Emotional intelligence is thus an organic concept for Ukrainians.
2. **Proverb**: "Поділена радість — подвійна, а поділене горе — вдвічі менше." (Shared joy is double, shared grief is half). Perfect anchor for the "Empathy" section.
3. **Proverb**: "Добрий чоловік — надійніше кам'яного мосту." (A kind man is more reliable than a stone bridge).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Вибачатися vs. Просити вибачення**:
   - *Error*: "Я вибачаюся" (I apologize myself).
   - *Correction*: "Я перепрошую" or "Я прошу вибачення".
   - *Reason*: Reflexive verbs with -ся often imply doing something to oneself. "Вибачаюся" technically means "I forgive myself".
2. **Sympathy vs. Empathy**:
   - *Confusion*: Using them interchangeably.
   - *Distinction*: **Емпатія** implies feeling *with* someone (sharing the emotion), while **Співчуття** implies feeling *for* someone (pity/compassion from outside).
3. **Calques**:
   - *Error*: "Приносити вибачення" (Russian calque).
   - *Correction*: "Просити вибачення".

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Tone**: The "Conflict Mediator" persona should guide the student through delicate situations (apologies, awkward moments).
- **Nuance**: Explicitly teach the "feeling with" vs "feeling for" distinction for empathy/sympathy.
- **Immersion**: Use the "Cordocentrism" concept to explain why Ukrainians often use "heart" idioms (брати до серця, від щирого серця) where English might use "mind".
