## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Experiencer Focus**: Ukrainian syntax heavily favors describing the world through the lens of how it affects a person (Dative experiencer + impersonal state, e.g., «Мені холодно», «Йому пощастило») rather than the English nominative «I am cold». This reflects a worldview where states happen *to* people.
2. **Literary Scene Setting**: Ukrainian literature and news media extensively use nominal sentences (Називні речення) for dramatic, immediate scene-setting. A classic example is the atmospheric opening «Ніч. Тиша. Місяць.» which creates instant immersion without active verbs.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The "Dummy Subject" Intrusion**: English speakers often try to insert a direct translation of "it" into impersonal sentences.
   *Error*: *Воно холодно.* → *Correct*: Холодно. — Ukrainian does not use dummy pronouns for states or weather.
2. **Pronoun Overuse**: Using explicit personal pronouns when the verb ending already conveys the subject.
   *Error*: *Я йду додому, а ти що робиш?* (sounds overly emphatic) → *Correct*: Іду додому, а ти що робиш? (Означено-особове).
