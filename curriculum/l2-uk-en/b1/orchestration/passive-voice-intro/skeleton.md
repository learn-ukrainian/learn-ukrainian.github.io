## Активний і пасивний стан (~750 words)
- P1 (~130 words): Introduction to the grammatical concept of "стан" (voice). Explain that voice describes the relationship between the "підмет" (subject) and the "дія" (action). Contrast English "I read the book" vs "The book is read" to set a familiar stage before moving into Ukrainian terminology.
- P2 (~140 words): Defining "Активний стан" (Active Voice). Explain that the subject is the active doer of the action. Provide clear transitive examples: "Студенти (subject) читають (verb) книгу (object)." Emphasize that in Ukrainian, this is the default and most natural way to express almost any idea.
- P3 (~135 words): Defining "Пасивний стан" (Passive Voice). Explain that the subject "зазнає дії" (undergoes the action). Introduce the structural shift: "Книга читається студентами." Note that the focus shifts from the doer to the object being acted upon.
- P4 (~115 words): The "Naturalness Rule" (Заболотний Grade 8). Explain why Ukrainian is stylistically different from English. While English uses passive voice extensively in academic and formal writing, Ukrainian strictly avoids it wherever possible. Introduce the concept of "канцелярит" (bureaucratese) — the cold, unnatural sound of excessive passive usage.
- P5 (~130 words): Legitimate use cases for the passive. Detail when passive IS appropriate: 1. The agent (doer) is unknown ("Місто було засновано"), 2. The result is the focus ("Завдання виконано"), 3. Scientific/Technical contexts, 4. Official legal declarations.

## Пасив через зворотні дієслова (~700 words)
- P1 (~120 words): Formation of passive voice using the suffix "-ся". Explain that transitive verbs (перехідні дієслова) become passive when "-ся" is added: "будувати" (to build) -> "будуватися" (to be built). Use the example: "Цей дім будується вже два роки."
- P2 (~110 words): Expressing the agent (doer) in "-ся" passives. Introduce the "Орудний відмінок" (Instrumental Case) for the agent: "Книга читається учнями." Explain that "учнями" is the instrument/agent, but reiterate that this structure is often considered "awkward" or "heavy."
- P3 (~120 words): The Avramenko warning (Grade 11). Contrast the passive "-ся" sentence with its active counterpart. Show why "Робітники будують міст" is superior to "Міст будується робітниками." Explain that the latter sounds like a translation from Russian and should be avoided in fluid speech.
- P4 (~125 words): Distinguishing passive "-ся" from other categories learned in M22. Contrast reflexive ("Він миється" - he washes himself) with passive ("Товар продається" - the item is sold). Provide a "semantic test": if the subject is inanimate and cannot act upon itself, it is passive.
- P5 (~125 words): Lexicalized and common "-ся" passive verbs. List verbs where the passive form is so standard it doesn't feel forced: "називатися" (to be called), "знаходитися" (to be located), "вважатися" (to be considered), "використовуватися" (to be used).

## Форми на -но/-то: українська спеціальність (~850 words)
- P1 (~140 words): Introduction to the "Ukrainian Speciality" — impersonal forms ending in "-но" and "-то". Explain that this is the most idiomatic and "Ukrainian" way to express a passive result. Use the punchy example: "Зроблено!" (Done!).
- P2 (~120 words): Morphological origin. Explain that these forms are derived from passive participles (пасивні дієприкметники): "зроблений" -> "зроблено", "прочитаний" -> "прочитано", "закритий" -> "закрито". Mention that unlike participles, these are "незмінні" (invariable).
- P3 (~150 words): The "Impersonal Rule" and Case agreement. Explain that these forms appear in sentences without a nominative subject. Crucial point: the object remains in the "Знахідний відмінок" (Accusative). Contrast: "Книга (Nom.) була прочитана" (passive adj.) vs "Книжку (Acc.) прочитано" (impersonal form).
- P4 (~130 words): Tense variations. Explain how to express past and future results using "було" and "буде": "Проєкт було завершено" (The project was completed) and "Закон буде ухвалено" (The law will be passed).
- P5 (~140 words): The "Forbidden Agent" Rule (Авраменко Grade 7). Explain a major difference from Russian: you CANNOT use an agent in the instrumental case with -но/-то. "Лист написано мною" is a Russicism. Correct: "Я написав лист" or simply "Лист написано."
- P6 (~70 words): Formation practice logic. Provide a list of stem-to-suffix transformations: "писати -> написаний -> написано", "відкрити -> відкритий -> відкрито", "забути -> забутий -> забуто".

## Порівняння трьох конструкцій (~600 words)
- P1 (~120 words): The "Voice Triangle". Present three ways to say the same thing: 1. Active: "Робітники збудували дім," 2. Passive -ся: "Дім будувався робітниками," 3. Passive -но: "Дім збудовано."
- P2 (~130 words): The Hierarchy of Naturalness. Rank the three options for a learner: Active is #1 (default), "-но/-то" is #2 (for result without doer), and "-ся" passive is #3 (rare, mostly for scientific processes or lexicalized verbs like "знаходитися").
- P3 (~120 words): Decision Tree logic. Ask: Is the doer known? If yes -> use Active. Is the result the only thing that matters? -> use -но/-то. Is it a continuous state of an object? -> use -ся.
- P4 (~115 words): Semantic nuances. Explain that "-ся" often implies a process ("дім будується" - the house is being built), while "-но/-то" implies a final, completed result ("дім збудовано" - the house is [now] built).
- P5 (~115 words): Summary of common pitfalls. Warn about over-translating English "is/was" into Ukrainian passive forms. Remind students that when in doubt, "active is always correct."

## Практика: пасив у контексті (~750 words)
- Dialogue (~120 words): A tour of a renovated "Палац культури" in Dnipro. The architect uses passive forms to describe the work: "Цей зал було відкрито минулого року," "Стіни були пофарбовані (participle as adj)," "Ремонт завершено." The journalist asks active questions.
- P1 (~130 words): Setting the cultural context for the dialogue. Mention Dnipro as a hub of modern Ukrainian architecture and the importance of public "cultural spaces."
- P2 (~150 words): Reading passage — a formal news report about a newly established museum or a passed law. Examples: "Закон ухвалено," "Експозицію відкрито," "Перші квитки продано."
- P3 (~130 words): Analysis of the reading passage. Explain why the author chose the passive forms (focus on the public event result, not the specific administrative workers).
- P4 (~110 words): Production exercise guidelines. Instruct learners to imagine they have finished a big project (moving house, finishing a course) and write 5 results using -но/-то: "Речі зібрано," "Квитки куплено," "Екзамен складено."
- P5 (~110 words): Final vocabulary review. Focus on technical terms like "перехідне дієслово" (transitive verb) and how it relates to the ability to form a passive.

## Підсумок (~450 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Summary of the main rules.
  * Passive voice shifts focus to the object.
  * Active voice is the stylistic king in Ukrainian.
  * -но/-то is the most natural passive tool for results.
  * Avoid the agent (mною/нами) with -но/-то forms.
- P2 (~150 words): Self-check list:
  * Чи можу я відрізнити "Активний стан" від "Пасивного"? (Can I distinguish active from passive?)
  * Чи вмію я утворювати форми на -но/-то від інфінітивів? (Can I form -no/-to from infinitives?)
  * Чи розумію я, чому "Лист написано мною" — це помилка? (Do I understand why "The letter was written by me" [passive form] is an error?)
  * Чи знаю я, що об'єкт у реченнях на -но/-то стоїть у знахідному відмінку? (Do I know the object is in Accusative?)
- P3 (~150 words): Preview of Module 24. Explain that we have finished the logic of how verbs relate to subjects (Voice). In M24, we will look at how verbs themselves are built (prefixes and suffixes) to change meaning, such as "писати" -> "підписати" (to sign) or "записати" (to record).
