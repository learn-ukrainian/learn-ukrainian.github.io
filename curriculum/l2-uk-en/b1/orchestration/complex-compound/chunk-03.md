## Протиставні та розділові сполучники (~800 words total)

While **єднальний** (copulative — і, й, та) conjunctions link ideas that flow together in perfect **одночасність** (simultaneity) or chronological **послідовність** (sequence), another crucial group highlights the boundaries, differences, or conflicts between them. These are the **протиставний** (adversative — але, а, зате, проте) conjunctions, a category that includes «а», «але», «та» (when it means 'but'), «зате», «проте», and «однак». Their primary grammatical function is to introduce a clear contrast between independent clauses within a **складносурядне речення** (compound sentence). In Ukrainian, this contrast forms a nuanced semantic bridge ranging from a direct, sharp **протиставлення** (opposition/contrast) to a milder, purely descriptive **зіставлення** (comparison/juxtaposition). When you use an adversative **сполучник сурядності** (coordinating conjunction), you signal that the second part of the sentence will diverge from expectations. The distinction between «а» and «але» is a common stumbling block, primarily because English often translates both as "but". The conjunction «а» is used for comparison. It places two facts side by side to highlight differences without implying conflict: «Сестра читає, а брат малює.» *(The sister is reading, and the brother is drawing.)* The sister reading does not prevent the brother from drawing. Conversely, «але» introduces a direct contradiction or an unexpected outcome: «Я хотів прийти, але не зміг.» *(I wanted to come, but I couldn't.)* Here, the inability to arrive directly opposes the initial desire.

Сполучник «а» чудово підходить для створення виразних паралелей у літературі та повсякденному спілкуванні. Відомий український режисер Олександр Довженко писав: «Людина створила культуру, а культура — людину». У цьому реченні немає жодного конфлікту між першою та другою частинами. Навпаки, вони гармонійно доповнюють одна одну через м'яке зіставлення, показуючи глибокий взаємозв'язок між творцем і його творінням. Використання сполучника «але» в такому контексті зруйнувало б усю красу фрази, перетворивши її на грубе протиріччя.

> *The conjunction "а" is perfectly suited for creating expressive parallels in literature and everyday communication. The famous Ukrainian director Oleksandr Dovzhenko wrote: "Humanity created culture, and culture [created] humanity." There is no conflict between the first and second parts in this sentence. On the contrary, they harmoniously complement each other through soft juxtaposition, showing the deep interconnectedness between the creator and their creation. Using the conjunction "але" in such a context would destroy the entire beauty of the phrase, turning it into a harsh contradiction.*

Beyond «а» and «але», Ukrainian offers several other adversative conjunctions with refined meanings. The conjunction «зате» is incredibly useful when you want to introduce a compensatory positive fact. It acknowledges a negative aspect in the first clause but immediately counters it with a positive trait in the second clause, essentially meaning "but on the other hand". For instance: «Він мало говорить, зате багато робить.» *(He speaks little, but on the other hand, he does a lot.)* The lack of speech is directly compensated by a strong work ethic; you are balancing a perceived weakness with a distinct strength. Similarly, the conjunctions «проте» and «однак» introduce a counter-argument or an unexpected result, much like "however" or "nevertheless". While «проте» is common in both spoken and written Ukrainian, «однак» tends to feel slightly more formal. It is frequently employed in academic writing, journalistic registers, or official speeches to pivot a discussion or present a contrasting viewpoint.

:::info
**Grammar box**
You already know «та» as a copulative conjunction meaning "and". However, it can also act as an adversative conjunction meaning "but". Because the word itself does not change, you must rely entirely on the context of the sentence. If the clauses describe flowing, parallel events, «та» means "and". If they present a contrast, like in the phrase «Світить, та не гріє» *(It shines, but does not warm)*, «та» functions identically to «але».
:::

The third major group of coordinating conjunctions consists of the **розділовий** (disjunctive — або, чи, то...то) conjunctions. This category includes words and pairs such as «або», «чи», «або...або», «чи...чи», «то...то», and «не то...не то». While adversative conjunctions contrast events, disjunctive conjunctions separate them. They indicate that the actions or states described in the **рівноправний** (equal, coordinate — parts of equal status) clauses cannot happen in the exact same way at the exact same time. The most straightforward semantic relationship they create is **взаємовиключення** (mutual exclusion). This means that only one of the presented options can be realized; the occurrence of one prohibits the occurrence of the other. The conjunctions «або» and «чи» are the standard tools for expressing this choice. When you want to add dramatic emphasis or absolute certainty to this mutual exclusion, you use the paired version «або...або». A classic example of this uncompromising choice is the well-known Ukrainian proverb: «Або рибку з'їсти, або на дно сісти.» *(Either eat the fish or sink to the bottom.)* You cannot have it both ways; you must choose one single path and accept the consequences.

Another fascinating semantic relationship expressed by disjunctive conjunctions is **чергування** (alternation). Unlike strict mutual exclusion, alternation describes a scenario where multiple events actually happen, but they take turns in a cyclical pattern. The paired conjunction «то...то» is designed for this precise purpose, painting a vivid picture of a changing environment. For example, consider the poetic line: «То сонце усміхнеться нам крізь хмари, то дощик рясно землю полива.» *(Now the sun smiles at us through the clouds, now the rain abundantly waters the earth.)* The sun and the rain are not mutually exclusive forever; they take turns dominating the landscape, creating a beautiful rhythm of shifting events. You can contrast this dynamic alternation with the paired conjunction «чи...чи». While «то...то» describes events definitely happening in turns, «чи...чи» implies a deep sense of uncertainty strictly from the observer's perspective. It suggests that the speaker is unsure which of the two possibilities is actually occurring, leaving the situation entirely unresolved.
