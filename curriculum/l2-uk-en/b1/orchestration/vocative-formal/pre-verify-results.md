## Grammar Rules
- **Name + Patronymic**: Правопис (§ 87, 93) & Textbooks (Litvinova Gr. 6, p. 141) — Both components MUST be in the vocative case: *Олександре Миколайовичу*, *Маріє Степанівно*.
- **Title + Name/Surname**: Textbooks (Zabolotnyi Gr. 8, p. 175) — For "Pan/Pani" + Name, both are in vocative: *пане Євгене*. For "Title + Surname", the title is in vocative, while the surname typically remains in nominative (especially for men): *пане професоре Бойчук*, *лікарю Онищук*.
- **Adjectives in Addresses**: Textbooks (Avramenko Gr. 10, p. 176) — Adjectives and pronouns in addresses remain in the nominative case: *шановний пане*, *вельмишановна пані*.
- **Plural Vocative**: Textbooks (Avramenko Gr. 10, p. 176) — Matches nominative plural, except for *панове* (singular *пан*, plural nom. *пани*).
- **Punctuation**: Textbooks (Avramenko Gr. 8, p. 133) — Vocative addresses are always set off by commas: *Доброго дня, пане Петре!* or an exclamation mark if emphasized.

## Calque Warnings
- **Звертання на ім'я**: Avoid using Nominative for address (Russianism). Correct: *Іване!* (Voc), not *Іван!* (Nom).
- **По батькові**: The phrase is correct Ukrainian; ensure both parts are declined in official address.
- **Добрий день vs Доброго дня**: Both are used, but "Добрий день" is the traditional greeting, while "Доброго дня" is often used as a wish (Genitive). In formal correspondence, "Доброго дня, [Name]!" is common and acceptable.
