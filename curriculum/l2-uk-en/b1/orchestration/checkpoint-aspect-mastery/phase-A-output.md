## Cultural Hooks
1. **Folk wisdom and outcomes**: The proverb "Зробив діло — гуляй сміло" perfectly encapsulates the perfective aspect's absolute focus on completed results rather than ongoing actions.
2. **Timeless truths**: The saying "Хто не робить, той не їсть" illustrates how the imperfective aspect naturally serves to state universal, timeless realities and habitual necessities in Ukrainian society.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Prohibitions**: Using perfective aspect for negative commands (e.g., "Не скажи!" instead of "Не кажи!"). Ukrainian strictly requires the imperfective aspect for direct prohibitions to mean "don't do the action at all."
2. **Future confusion**: Mixing up the synthetic future ("читатиму") with present perfective forms describing future intent ("прочитаю"). 
3. **Past tense negation**: Failing to distinguish between "не робив" (the process never happened) and "не зробив" (engaged in it, but failed to complete the intended result).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize the unique elegance of the Ukrainian synthetic future tense (боротимуся, читатимеш). Present Ukrainian aspect as an independent, highly logical system of semantic precision, not through comparisons with Russian. Avoid framing Ukrainian syntax as an "alternative."
- **Immersion**: This is B1.1 (M15), requiring 100% Ukrainian immersion in all explanations, instructions, and prose. English is restricted strictly to the "Переклад" column of the vocabulary table.
- **Tone**: Professional, encouraging evaluator. Guide the learner through self-assessment rigorously but positively. Ensure Ukrainians are the active subjects in narrative examples.
