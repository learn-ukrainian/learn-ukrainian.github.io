| додати | High (Dialogues) | додати, що... (added that) |

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Oral History (Усна історія)**: In Ukrainian culture, especially during Soviet times, "what people said" (перекази) was often more trusted than official written records. The phrase «Люди кажуть...» implies a shared, unofficial truth.
2. **Citation Culture**: It is common in daily speech to reference literature or folk wisdom using «Як казав Шевченко...» or «Як кажуть у народі...» to add weight to one's own statement.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Tense Backshift (The "English Trap")** → *Error:* Він сказав, що він **був** зайнятий (He said he was busy - meaning now). *Correction:* Він сказав, що він **зайнятий** (He said that he [is] busy). *Reason:* Ukrainian retains the original tense of the direct speech; English backshifts.
2. **Missing Conjunction "що"** → *Error:* Він сказав, він прийде. *Correction:* Він сказав, **що** він прийде. *Reason:* English often omits "that"; in Ukrainian, «що» is mandatory in reported statements.
3. **Pronoun Retention** → *Error:* (Direct: "Я хочу їсти") → Він сказав, що **я** хочу їсти. *Correction:* Він сказав, що **він** хоче їсти. *Reason:* Forgetting to shift the perspective from 1st to 3rd person.

## Cross-References
- Builds on: b1-38 (Complex Sentence Integration), A2 grammar (Conjugation).
- Prepares for: b1-40 (Reported Questions & Commands), B2 Indirect Speech (stylistics).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **CRITICAL PEDAGOGY**: Devote a significant section to "No Tense Backshift". This is the hardest concept for Anglophones. Use a "Time Travel" metaphor: "Reported speech acts like a time machine that keeps the original moment frozen."
- **Reporting Verbs**: Introduce variety early (`відповісти`, `пояснити`, `зауважити`) so students don't repeat `сказати` 50 times.
- **Register**: Distinguish between `сказати` (neutral) and `заявити` (formal/assertive).
