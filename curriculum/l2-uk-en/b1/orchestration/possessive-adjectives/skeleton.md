## Присвійні прикметники: що це і навіщо (~1100 words total)
- P1 (~150 words): Introduction to the concept of possessive adjectives (присвійні прикметники). Define them as adjectives answering the question "чий? чия? чиє? чиї?" (whose?) and indicating belonging to a specific person or animal. Contrast with the general descriptive question "який?".
- P2 (~150 words): Categorization based on Zabolotnyi (Grade 6). Briefly distinguish between quality (якісні — великий), relative (відносні — дерев'яний), and possessive (присвійні — батьків) adjectives. Use a concrete example: "старий" (quality) vs "міський" (relative) vs "дідусів" (possessive) house.
- P3 (~120 words): Dialogue: Family gathering. Онука and Мама discuss belongings using possessive forms: "Це батьків годинник чи дідусів? — Дідусів. А ця хустка — бабусина чи материна? — Бабусина, ще з війни." Focus on the immediate agreement with the possessed object.
- P4 (~180 words): The stylistic preference in Ukrainian. Explain why "батьків дім" is often more natural and frequent in speech and literature than the genitive construction "дім батька". Mention that possessive adjectives are a "living" and productive category in Ukrainian, unlike the fossilized forms in some other Slavic languages.
- P5 (~180 words): Contrast with English structures. English uses the possessive 's (father's house) or "of" phrases, but lacks a specific category of derived adjectives for this. Clarify that "мій, твій" are possessive pronouns (присвійні займенники), which are a separate group from these noun-derived adjectives.
- P6 (~170 words): Decolonization note: Emphasize that in Ukrainian, forming adjectives from names (Сергіїв олівець, Маріїн портфель) is standard and high-frequency, whereas in Russian, such forms are often perceived as colloquial or archaic, leading learners to mistakenly default to the genitive case.

## Творення від II відміни: суфікси -ів/-їв (~1100 words total)
- P1 (~200 words): Formation from masculine nouns of the 2nd declension (II відміна). Explain the use of the suffix -ів for hard stems: батько → батьків, брат → братів, сусід → сусідів. Use the example of "батьків дім" to show the suffix attachment to the noun stem.
- P2 (~180 words): Rules for soft stems and stems ending in -й. Explain the shift to -їв: Андрій → Андріїв, Сергій → Сергіїв, Олексій → Олексіїв. Highlight that the -й of the stem is dropped before adding -їв (Марі[й] → Маріїн, but here for Masc: Андрій -> Андріїв).
- P3 (~180 words): The behavior of proper names ending in -о (Шевченко, Франко). Explain that these are treated as 2nd declension masculine nouns: Шевченко → Шевченків, Франко → Франків. Note the capitalization rule: if it's direct belonging, it's capitalized (Шевченкова поезія).
- P4 (~160 words): Lack of consonant alternation in the -ів suffix. Contrast "Олег → Олегів" with the feminine "Ольга → Ольжин" to show that g→zh does NOT happen here. This is a common point of confusion for learners who over-generalize alternations.
- P5 (~160 words): Specific cases: nouns like "коваль" or "учитель". Formation: ковалів, учителів. Explain that the "softness" of the stem is preserved through the choice of forms in further declension (учителева), but the nominative Masc remains -ів.
- P6 (~120 words): Practice examples: [Тарас] + книжка = Тарасова книжка; [директор] + кабінет = директорів кабінет. Show how the gender of the adjective depends on the *possessed* noun, not the owner.

## Творення від I відміни: суфікси -ин/-ін (-їн) (~1100 words total)
- P1 (~180 words): General rule for 1st declension (I відміна) nouns (mostly feminine). Use of suffix -ин: сестра → сестрин, мама → мамин, бабуся → бабусин, Леся → Лесин. Note that even masculine nouns in this declension follow this rule: Микола → Миколин.
- P2 (~200 words): The "Big Three" consonant alternations: г → ж. Explain with examples: Ольга → Ольжин, подруга → подружин. Emphasize that this is an obligatory phonetic rule in Ukrainian, not an optional stylistic choice.
- P3 (~200 words): The "Big Three" consonant alternations: к → ч. Detailed examples: тітка → тітчин, дочка → доччин, Даринка → Даринчин, Наталка → Наталчин. Explain that the "k" sound shifts to "ch" to facilitate the transition to the "y" sound in -ин.
- P4 (~150 words): The "Big Three" consonant alternations: х → ш. Use examples: Солоха → Солошин, муха → мушин, сваха → свашин. Explain the historical root of these changes (the first palatalization).
- P5 (~180 words): Suffixes -ін and -їн. Explain that -їн is used after vowels (stem ending in [j]): Марія → Маріїн, Софія → Софіїн, Наталія → Наталіїн. Contrast "Наталія" (Маріїн type) with "Наталка" (Наталчин type) based on which name form is used as the base.
- P6 (~190 words): Common pitfalls: "бабуся" (soft stem) becomes "бабусин" (the softness is absorbed/lost in the -ин suffix in the nominative). Explain why we say "бабусин", not "*бабусін".

## Відмінювання і вживання (~1100 words total)
- P1 (~200 words): Introduction to "Mixed Declension" (змішане відмінювання). Explain that possessive adjectives have "short" forms in the Nominative (батьків, материн) but switch to "full" adjective-like endings in oblique cases (батькового, материної).
- P2 (~220 words): Vowel alternation in declension (-ів vs -ов/-ев). Explain the [і]/[о] shift: Nom. Masc: батьків (closed syllable) -> Gen. Masc: батькового (open syllable). Provide examples: Сергіїв -> Сергієвого, ковалів -> ковалева.
- P3 (~250 words): Detailed declension table for "батьків" (Masc/Neut/Fem/Pl) in all cases: Nominative, Genitive, Dative, Accusative, Instrumental, Locative. Point out the unique Masc. Accusative (depends on animacy: бачу батьків дім vs бачу батькового брата).
- P4 (~200 words): Comparison table for "-ин" type declension: материн, материної, материній, материну, материною, (на) материній. Highlight the difference from regular adjectives (regular adjectives have -ої in Gen. Fem, these have it too, but the stem remains short in Nom).
- P5 (~180 words): Stylistic and Orthographic rules. Capitalization: "Шевченкові поезії" (from the person) vs "шевченківські читання" (relative adjective with -івськ-). Explain that -івськ- adjectives are NOT possessive and are never capitalized unless starting a sentence.
- P6 (~50 words): Transition to the final summary and check-out.
