Before we move to the final assessment, we need a **комплексний** (comprehensive) overview of how all these grammatical pieces fit together in real life. This kind of **повторення** (review) helps solidify your understanding. Reading analytical texts is an excellent way to see advanced cases and prepositions in action. The following article discusses the current state of the real estate market in Ukraine's largest cities. Pay close attention to how the author describes prices, reasons for market changes, and the characteristics of buyers and renters.

Житловий ринок сучасної України переживає трансформації через вплив різних економічних факторів. Протягом останніх років ми спостерігаємо цікаві тенденції в найбільших містах, зокрема в Києві та Львові. Завдяки внутрішній міграції попит на оренду квартир у західних регіонах зріс, що вплинуло на ціноутворення. Наприклад, середня вартість оренди однокімнатної квартири у Львові сьогодні може сягати п'ятнадцяти тисяч гривень на місяць. Для багатьох молодих спеціалістів стало викликом знайти житло поблизу центру міста.

> *The residential market of modern Ukraine is undergoing transformations due to the influence of various economic factors. Over the last few years, we have been observing interesting trends in the largest cities, particularly in Kyiv and Lviv. Thanks to internal migration, the demand for apartment rentals in the western regions has grown, which affected pricing. For example, the average cost of renting a one-room apartment in Lviv today can reach fifteen thousand hryvnias per month. For many young specialists, it has become a challenge to find housing near the city center.*

У столиці ситуація має інший характер. Багато людей прагнуть бути власниками нерухомості, а не просто орендарями. Заради цієї мети вони готові брати іпотечні кредити або заощаджувати кошти. Через високі ціни на квартири в престижних районах, інвестори часто звертають увагу на передмістя. Життя за містом стає популярним завдяки кращій екології та розбудові інфраструктури. Після завершення будівництва нових комплексів, туди переїжджають сім'ї з дітьми. Для комфортного проживання їм потрібні школи та парки, які забудовники намагаються інтегрувати в проєкти.

> *In the capital, the situation has a different character. Many people strive to be owners of real estate, rather than just tenants. For the sake of this goal, they are ready to take mortgage loans or save money. Because of high prices for apartments in prestigious areas, investors often pay attention to the suburbs. Living outside the city is becoming popular thanks to better ecology and the development of infrastructure. After the completion of the construction of new complexes, families with children move there. For comfortable living, they need schools and parks, which developers try to integrate into the projects.*

Протягом наступного року експерти прогнозують стабілізацію ринку, хоча багато залежатиме від загальної ситуації в країні. Аналітики вважають, що перед початком ділового сезону восени ціни можуть зрости. Проте, внаслідок нових державних програм підтримки, більше українців отримають шанс придбати житло. Купівля квартири вимагає ретельного аналізу. Потрібно уважно читати договори перед підписанням і консультуватися з юристами, щоб уникнути ризиків. Таким чином, ринок нерухомості залишається динамічним середовищем, де діють суворі правила попиту та пропозиції.

> *Over the next year, experts predict market stabilization, although much will depend on the overall situation in the country. Analysts believe that before the start of the business season in the autumn, prices may rise. However, as a result of new state support programs, more Ukrainians will get a chance to purchase housing. Buying an apartment requires careful analysis. One must carefully read contracts before signing and consult with lawyers to avoid risks. Thus, the real estate market remains a dynamic environment where strict rules of demand and supply operate.*

In academic and professional settings, discussing data requires precision and a consistently formal tone. The following dialogue takes place at a university seminar where a student is presenting detailed research on urban development to a faculty member. Notice how the student respectfully uses the formal Vocative case to address the professor, employs the experiencer Dative to express an impersonal need of the research group, and utilizes collective numerals to accurately describe the composition of the research team. These elements elevate the spoken language from a casual chat to academic discourse.

> — **Студент:** Пане професоре, дозвольте розпочати нашу презентацію про розвиток інфраструктури Києва. *(Professor, please allow me to begin our presentation on the infrastructure development of Kyiv.)*
> — **Професор:** Будь ласка, Олександре. Які основні висновки вашого дослідження? *(Please, Oleksandr. What are the main conclusions of your research?)*
> — **Студент:** Нам важливо зрозуміти, як змінилися транспортні потоки протягом останнього десятиліття. *(It is important for us to understand how traffic flows have changed over the last decade.)*
> — **Професор:** Хто збирав ці статистичні дані? *(Who collected these statistical data?)*
> — **Студент:** Над цим проєктом працювали двоє дослідників з нашого факультету. Завдяки їхній роботі ми маємо точні цифри. *(Two researchers from our faculty worked on this project. Thanks to their work, we have exact numbers.)*
> — **Професор:** Дуже добре. Продовжуйте, ми вас уважно слухаємо. *(Very well. Continue, we are listening to you carefully.)*

One of the biggest challenges at the B1 level is eliminating "linguistic ghosts" — grammatical structures from other languages, particularly Russian, that haunt a learner's Ukrainian. Because the two languages share a lot of vocabulary, it is incredibly easy to assume they also share the exact same case government rules. They do not. Using the wrong case with a common verb instantly flags your speech as unnatural. Let's examine three of the most persistent calques and how to fix them.

The first ghost appears when you want to express gratitude. In English, you say "thank you" using a direct object, and in Russian, you use the Accusative case. However, in Ukrainian, the verb **дякувати** (to thank) absolutely requires the Dative case. You are giving your thanks *to* someone. Therefore, you must say **дякую вам** or **дякуємо лікарю**, never the grammatically incorrect form that uses the Accusative.

:::info
**The «Дякую» Rule**
Never translate the English direct object "thank you" directly into Ukrainian. The verb **дякувати** always requires the Dative case (to thank *to* someone): **дякую вам**, **дякуємо мамі**.
:::

The second ghost involves the verb **сміятися** (to laugh) when it means to laugh *at* someone or something. A common mistake is to use the preposition **над** with the Instrumental case. This is a direct calque. Authentic Ukrainian requires the preposition **з** followed by the Genitive case. You laugh "out of" something. The correct form is **сміятися з брата** or **ми сміялися з цієї ситуації**.

The third ghost is found with verbs of learning, such as **навчатися** (to learn or to study a specific discipline). Learners frequently default to the Dative case here, as if they are dedicating themselves "to" a subject. In standard Ukrainian, however, this verb strictly governs the Genitive case. You study *of* a subject. Thus, you must say **навчатися музики** or **навчатися української мови**, completely avoiding the Dative forms. Mastering these specific verb-case pairs is essential for producing truly authentic and elegant Ukrainian sentences.

Now it is time to put everything you have reviewed into practice through a highly realistic writing simulation. Imagine you have recently relocated to a new city for work and urgently need to find a long-term apartment. You have found a promising listing online and decide to write a formal email to the landlord, Pan Sydorenko, to inquire about the property, discuss the terms, and schedule a viewing.

Your task is to draft a polite and comprehensive message that clearly communicates your situation, your housing needs, and your financial boundaries. To do this successfully, you must integrate the advanced grammar concepts from this phase. Your email must begin with a proper formal greeting using the Vocative case. You need to explain the reason for your relocation using the compound conjunction **через те що** (because of the fact that). Describe the intended purpose of the rental using the preposition **для** (for) with the Genitive case, and specify the exact duration of your planned stay using **протягом** (throughout) alongside the correct time expression.

:::tip
**Formal Greetings**
When writing to a landlord you don't know well, always use the formal Vocative. «Шановний пане Сидоренку!» sets the right tone for a respectful and serious negotiation.
:::

Furthermore, you must demonstrate your ability to decline numerals accurately in a business context. Clearly state your maximum monthly budget in hryvnias or dollars, ensuring that the currency noun perfectly agrees with the preceding number. Finally, mention the specific address or floor number you are interested in, which will require the correct use of ordinal numerals.
