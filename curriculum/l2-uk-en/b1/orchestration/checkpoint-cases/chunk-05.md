## Самооцінка та підготовка до Фази 7 (~550 words total)

To finalize your **повторення** (review) of Phase 6, it is time to measure your practical control over the Ukrainian case system. This phase was not about memorizing isolated endings, but about mastering the semantic nuances that make your speech sound authentic. A truly **комплексний** (comprehensive) understanding means you can effortlessly choose the right preposition and the correct case based on the specific context of your sentence. To help you evaluate your readiness for the next level, we have designed a mental **самооцінка** (self-assessment) matrix. Go through the following questions and answer them honestly.

Чи можете ви правильно утворити форму родового відмінка для абстрактних понять та речовин? Якщо ви випадково кажете «склянка воду» замість «склянка води», вам потрібно ще раз переглянути першу частину цього модуля. Чи знаєте ви різницю між прийменниками причини та мети? Якщо ви все ще плутаєте їх у повсякденному спілкуванні, зверніть увагу на розділ про прийменники. Чи вмієте ви вітатися в офіційному листуванні? Якщо ви пишете «шановний пан» замість правильної форми кличного відмінка «шановний пане», вам варто закріпити правила звертання.

> *Can you correctly form the Genitive case for abstract concepts and substances? If you accidentally say "склянка воду" instead of "склянка води", you need to review the first part of this module again. Do you know the difference between prepositions of cause and purpose? If you still confuse them in everyday communication, pay attention to the prepositions section. Do you know how to greet someone in formal correspondence? If you write "шановний пан" instead of the correct Vocative case form "шановний пане", you should consolidate the rules of address.*

Identifying a gap in your knowledge is the first step toward true fluency. If you find yourself hesitating when choosing between the **-а** and **-у** endings for masculine nouns in the Genitive case, do not simply stare at grammar tables. Memorizing lists of abstract rules is rarely effective at this stage. Instead, you need a strategy of drilling with context. 

:::info
**Context is King**
When reviewing masculine Genitive endings, remember that meaning dictates the form. Abstract concepts, processes, and games generally take the **-у** ending, while tangible objects, living beings, and specific measurements take the **-а** ending.
:::

Create your own sentences using the words that give you trouble, tying them to your daily life or personal interests. If prepositions trip you up, practice them as fixed phrases rather than isolated words. Write down combinations and repeat them until they feel natural.

**Піти по хліб.** — *To go for bread.*

**Сміятися з ситуації.** — *To laugh at the situation.*

Contextual drilling builds muscle memory, transforming conscious grammar translation into instinctive language production.

As we prepare to cross the bridge into Phase 7, your mastery of these case nuances will be put to the ultimate test. The next phase introduces **дієприкметники** (participles), which act as adjective-verb hybrids. These complex structures allow you to compress entire relative clauses into a single, elegant modifier. However, a participle must perfectly agree with its head noun in gender, number, and case. If you do not have a solid grasp of the Instrumental case, you will not be able to identify the agent of a passive construction. If your Genitive case is weak, you will struggle to negate participial phrases.

У наступній фазі ви будете будувати дуже складні речення. Ви навчитеся використовувати пасивні конструкції з орудним відмінком. Наприклад, ви зможете сказати, що ця цікава стаття була написана відомим українським журналістом. Ваше глибоке розуміння відмінків стане надійним фундаментом для цих нових граматичних тем.

> *In the next phase, you will build very complex sentences. You will learn to use passive constructions with the Instrumental case. For example, you will be able to say that this interesting article was written by a famous Ukrainian journalist. Your deep understanding of cases will become a reliable foundation for these new grammar topics.*

Use it to solidify your confidence before advancing to the next level.
