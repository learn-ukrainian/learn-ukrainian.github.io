## Сполучник vs сполучне слово (~660 words total)
- P1 (~165 words): Explain the critical grammatical distinction: A сполучник (like "що" in most object clauses) has NO syntactic role in the subordinate clause. It functions purely as a link. Analyze the example: "Я знаю, що він прийде." Show that "він" is the subject, "прийде" is the predicate, and "що" is just a structural connector.
- P2 (~165 words): Explain what a сполучне слово is: IT IS an active member of the subordinate clause. Analyze the example: "Я знаю, що ти читаєш." Here, "що" acts as a relative pronoun representing the thing being read, making it the direct object of the verb "читаєш".
- P3 (~165 words): Provide practical tests to distinguish the two. Test 1: Can you replace "що" with "те, що"? If yes, it's a relative word. Test 2: Can you drop "що" and the subordinate clause still makes complete grammatical sense on its own? If yes, it's a conjunction.
- P4 (~165 words): Discuss other relative words that always function as clause members and analyze their roles. Examples: "хто" as subject ("Я не знаю, хто це зробив"), "де" as adverbial of place ("Скажи, де ти був"), "куди" as direction ("Я не пам'ятаю, куди поклав ключі"), and "коли" as time.

## Пунктуація та побудова (~715 words total)
- P1 (~180 words): State the main punctuation rule for complex sentences in Ukrainian: always place a comma before the subordinate conjunction or relative word. Provide clear examples demonstrating this: "Я думаю, що це правильно" and "Він не знає, де я живу." Emphasize that unlike English, the comma is mandatory before "що".

- P3 (~180 words): Discuss the typical position of з'ясувальні clauses. Explain that they naturally come AFTER the main clause (specifically after the пояснюване слово). Contrast this with обставинні (adverbial) clauses, which are much more flexible in their placement within the sentence.
