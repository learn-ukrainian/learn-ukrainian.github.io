## Підсумок та перехід до M68 (~495 words total)

Let us take a moment to consolidate everything we have learned about complex syntax in this section. You now know that a **складнопідрядне речення** (complex sentence with subordination) is an essential tool for expressing your thoughts clearly. Every such sentence contains a **головна частина** (main clause) that sets the stage, alongside a **підрядна частина** (subordinate clause) that provides the necessary details. These elements form the absolute foundation of advanced communication.

In this module, we focused specifically on explanatory clauses. A **з'ясувальне речення** (explanatory/object clause) acts exactly like a direct or indirect object in a simple sentence. It always attaches to a specific **пояснюване слово** (explained word — verb in main clause), such as a verb of speech, thought, perception, or feeling. Once you identify that main verb, you can simply ask questions of the indirect cases, such as «кого?», «чого?», «кому?», «чому?», «що?», «ким?», or «чим?». If the dependent part answers one of these questions, you are looking at an object clause. This is exactly how Ukrainians naturally report speech, share their opinions, and express their desires in daily life.

We also learned to distinguish the connecting means that join these two parts together. Most often, we use a **сполучник підрядності** (subordinating conjunction), which simply attaches new information without performing any grammatical role. The most popular of these is **що** (that — conjunction or relative word), which is perfect for conveying facts and reporting statements. If you want to express a request, a command, or a strong desire, you will need **щоб** (in order to/that — conjunction of desire/purpose). 

To convey indirect questions, we rely on **чи** (whether — conjunction for questions). On the other hand, sometimes we need a **сполучне слово** (relative/connective word), for example, «хто», «де», «куди», «коли», «як», or «скільки». They not only connect parts of the sentence but also act as full grammatical members of the dependent clause. Remember the main punctuation rule: a comma is strictly mandatory before each such connecting means. 

Це маленьке правило робить ваше письмо грамотним і професійним. Коли ви ставите кому перед сполучником, ви показуєте глибоке розуміння структури мови. Українці завжди звертають увагу на правильну пунктуацію в текстах. Тому ніколи не забувайте про цей важливий розділовий знак!

> *This small rule makes your writing literate and professional. When you place a comma before a conjunction, you show a deep understanding of the language structure. Ukrainians always pay attention to correct punctuation in texts. Therefore, never forget about this important punctuation mark!*

As you master these structures, you will notice that your Ukrainian sounds much more natural and sophisticated. Now that you can report what people say and think, you are ready to expand your descriptive abilities even further. In the next module, M68, we will explore relative clauses, known in Ukrainian as «Означальні речення». Instead of acting like an object, these subordinate clauses act like complex adjectives. They always describe a specific noun or pronoun in the main clause, answering the questions «який?», «яка?», «яке?», or «які?». This means you will learn how to say things like "the book that I read" or "the city where I was born". Before you move on to that exciting topic, use this quick self-check to verify your understanding of everything we have covered today.

:::info
**Self-Check: З'ясувальні речення**

- Чи можу я знайти головну та підрядну частини? Так, головна частина містить опорне слово, а підрядна відповідає на питання.
- Чи можу я побудувати з'ясувальне речення зі сполучником «що»? Так: «Я знаю, що він вдома».
- Чи знаю я, коли використовувати «щоб»? Так, для вираження бажання або прохання: «Я хочу, щоб ти читав».
- Чи можу я сформулювати непряме питання? Так, за допомогою «чи» або питального слова: «Запитай, чи є квитки».
- Чи пам'ятаю я про кому? Так, перед засобом зв'язку завжди ставиться кома!

> *Can I find the main and subordinate clauses? Yes, the main clause contains the explained word, and the subordinate clause answers the question.*
> *Can I build an explanatory clause with the conjunction "що"? Yes: "I know that he is at home."*
> *Do I know when to use "щоб"? Yes, for expressing a desire or request: "I want you to read."*
> *Can I formulate an indirect question? Yes, using "чи" or a question word: "Ask if there are tickets."*
> *Do I remember the comma? Yes, a comma is always placed before the connecting word!*
:::
