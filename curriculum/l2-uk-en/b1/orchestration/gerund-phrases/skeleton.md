## Правила відокремлення (~990 words)
- P1 (~180 words): [The "Golden Rule" of Ukrainian gerunds from Lytvinova Grade 7: The *дієприслівниковий зворот* is ALWAYS set off by commas, regardless of its position in the sentence. This is the primary distinction from participle phrases (M59). Examples: *Прочитавши листа, Оленка задумалася* (Start), *Оленка, прочитавши листа, задумалася* (Middle), *Оленка задумалася, прочитавши листа* (End).]
- P2 (~150 words): [Handling multiple phrases and homogeneous members. Explain that two phrases joined by *і* are treated as a single unit for punctuation: *Відчинивши двері і ввімкнувши світло, він оглянув кімнату*. Contrast this with sequential phrases that require individual commas: *Дізнавшись правду, зрозумівши все, він почав діяти*.]
- P3 (~150 words): [Exception Group 1: Phraseological units (фразеологічні звороти). Explain why these do NOT take commas based on Avramenko Grade 7. Use core examples: *працювати не покладаючи рук* (to work tirelessly), *слухати затамувавши подих* (to listen with bated breath), *бігти не чуючи ніг* (to run very fast).]
- P4 (~180 words): [Exception Group 2: The intensifying particle *і/й*. Explain that if the phrase is preceded by this particle, the comma is dropped. Example from Zabolotnyi Grade 8: *Це можна виконати й не знаючи правил*. Contrast this with the conjunction *і* in compound sentences to avoid confusion.]
- P5 (~180 words): [Exception Group 3: Adverbialization of single gerunds. Explain that a single gerund standing AFTER the verb that indicates manner (how?) is not set off. Examples: *стояв похилившись* (stood leaning), *сидів задумавшись* (sat lost in thought), *читати лежачи* (to read lying down). Clarify that if it has even one dependent word, the comma returns!]
- P6 (~150 words): [Stylistic and rhythmic pauses. Explain that in oral speech, commas correspond to logical pauses. Contrast the flow of a standard phrase with the "fused" flow of an idiom like *склавши руки*. Provide a quick reference list of "Comma" vs "No Comma" scenarios.]

## Порівняння зворотів: дієприкметниковий vs дієприслівниковий (~715 words)
- P1 (~150 words): [Syntactic function: *означення* (attribute) vs *обставина* (modifier). Explain that the participle phrase (M59) describes a noun (*який?*), while the gerund phrase describes an action (*як? коли?*). Side-by-side example: *Студент, прочитавши книгу...* (When?) vs *Книга, прочитана студентом...* (Which book?).]
- P2 (~150 words): [Modification targets. A participle phrase modifies an *іменник* (noun) or *займенник* (pronoun). A gerund phrase modifies a *дієслово-присудок* (main verb). Show how this change in target changes the logic of the sentence: *Лист, написаний другом...* (Target: лист) vs *Написавши листа другу...* (Target: відправив).]
- P3 (~150 words): [Punctuation divergence. Recall the "Position Rule" for participles (commas only after the noun) and contrast it with the "Absolute Rule" for gerunds (always commas). Example: *Написана другом книга* (No comma) vs *Написавши книгу, друг відпочив* (Always comma).]
- P4 (~150 words): [Logical transformation. Explain that a participle phrase can be replaced by a *який*-clause (*книга, яку написали*), while a gerund phrase is replaced by a *коли*-clause (*коли написав книгу*). This is a key test for learners to distinguish the two.]

## Помилки та стилістика (~715 words)
- P1 (~150 words): [Prevention of the "Dangling Gerund" (Review from M61-62). Reiterate that the subject of the main verb MUST be the performer of the gerund phrase action. Example of error: **Перейшовши дорогу, мені стало легше* (Who crossed? Not 'it'). Correction: *Перейшовши дорогу, я відчув полегшення*.]
- P2 (~150 words): [Semantic mismatches. Avoid sentences where different subjects perform the two actions. Example: **Готуючи вечерю, кіт забіг на кухню* (The cat didn't cook!). Correct to a clause: *Коли я готував вечерю, кіт забіг на кухню*.]
- P3 (~150 words): [Stylistic "Heaviness" (*канцелярит*). Warn against overloading sentences with too many phrases. Show a "bad" example: *Прокинувшись, вмившись, поснідавши, зібравшись, я вийшов...*. Show the "natural" Ukrainian fix using multiple verbs: *Я прокинувся, вмився і поснідав. Потім зібрався і вийшов*.]
- P4 (~150 words): [Register awareness. Explain that gerund phrases are hallmarks of *художній* (literary), *науковий* (scientific), and *публіцистичний* (journalistic) styles. In *розмовний* (colloquial) style, prefer simple coordinate clauses or *коли*-subordination.]
- P5 (~115 words): [Decolonization note: Avoid the Russian habit of using gerunds for everything. In Ukrainian, the "Action + Action" structure (*Я сидів і читав*) is often more natural than "Action + Gerund" (*Я сидів, читаючи*). Emphasize the beauty of Ukrainian coordinating conjunctions.]
