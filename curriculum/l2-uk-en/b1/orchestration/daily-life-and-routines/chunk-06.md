## Підсумок — Summary (~400 words)

Throughout this module, we have brought together all the essential grammatical tools from Phase 3 to describe our **розпорядок дня** (daily routine) with nuance. We utilized the conditional mood to imagine hypotheticals, like planning a **пошук роботи** (job search). The imperative mood allowed us to offer polite advice to a **колега** (colleague).

We saw how verbal nouns elevate our speech when discussing formal concepts such as cleaning or studying. Reflexive verbs proved essential, allowing us to describe how we **прокидатися** (to wake up) in the morning. They also help us explain how we **займатися** (to engage in) sports during the **вихідні** (weekend).

To report that a task is already finished, we applied the passive voice using the *-но* and *-то* endings. Furthermore, verb prefixes subtly change the meaning of our actions; without them, you might ignore your **будильник** (alarm clock). This could cause you to **запізнюватися** (to be late) for a crucial **співбесіда** (interview).

If you want to **встигати** (to manage in time) with all your goals, you must never **відкладати** (to postpone) your assignments. A structured day ensures you can handle your professional **обов'язки** (duties) without feeling overwhelmed. This proactive approach makes every week significantly more productive.

When managing your home, you have to tackle various **хатні справи** (household chores) effectively. You must actively **прибирати** (to clean up) your living space on a regular basis. It is also important to **пилососити** (to vacuum) the carpets so your environment remains pleasant.

It is necessary to **прасувати** (to iron) your clothes before heading to the office. Every week, you will also need to **прати білизну** (to do laundry) to ensure you always have clean garments. Remembering to **виносити сміття** (to take out the trash) becomes effortless with a good routine.

A solid schedule allows you to calmly **снідати** (to have breakfast) and **обідати** (to have lunch) without rushing. In the evening, you will have plenty of time to **вечеряти** (to have dinner) with your family. Consistent habits truly transform how you experience the entire week.

You will easily focus at your **робоче місце** (workplace) and complete your tasks efficiently. Afterwards, you will find enough energy to **готувати їжу** (to cook food) instead of ordering delivery. This balance helps you maintain a healthy lifestyle.

Щоденна рутина — це не лише обов'язки, але й мистецтво знаходити час для себе. Коли ви плануєте свій день українською мовою, ви починаєте думати як носій. Ваша лексика стає багатшою, а граматичні конструкції звучать природно. Продовжуйте практикувати ці навички щодня, і ви побачите неймовірний прогрес.

> *A daily routine is not just about duties, but also the art of finding time for yourself. When you plan your day in Ukrainian, you start thinking like a native speaker. Your vocabulary becomes richer, and your grammatical structures sound natural. Keep practicing these skills every day, and you will see incredible progress.*

:::info
**Grammar box**
Remember that the verb **прати** is used strictly for washing clothes or laundry. Using the Russianism *стирати* in this context is a common mistake that you should actively avoid to keep your Ukrainian natural and authentic.
:::

If your answer is "yes", you are ready to move on.*
