## Що таке III відміна? (~600 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Introduction to the Third Declension (III відміна). Definition from Grade 6 textbooks: feminine nouns ending in a consonant in the Nominative (zero ending). Contrast with I declension (жінка, земля) to establish the structural difference.
- P2 (~150 words): The linguistic logic of the "zero ending" (нульове закінчення). Explanation of how words like *ніч, сіль, любов* are identified as feminine despite the lack of an -а/-я ending. Historical note on the ancient *i*-stem origin of these words.
- P3 (~150 words): The unique case of the word *мати* (mother). Why it belongs to the III declension even though its paradigm involves stem expansion. Contrast with *мама* (I declension).

## Відмінювання III відміни в однині (~850 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Overview of the singular paradigm. Focus on the dominance of the ending **-і** in the Genitive, Dative, and Locative cases. Examples: *ночі, солі, радості*.
- P2 (~150 words): The Vocative case (Кличний відмінок). Explain the ending **-е**. Usage examples in literature and high style: *ноче, радосте, любове*. Note that *мати* remains *мати* in the Vocative.
- P3 (~150 words): The Genitive case variation. Detailed explanation of the parallel endings **-і / -и** for nouns ending in *-ть* after a consonant (*радости*) and words like *крови, любови, соли*. Reference to the 2019 Pravopys.
- P4 (~150 words): Accusative and Dative cases. Accusative usually matches Nominative for inanimate nouns (*бачу ніч*). Dative uses **-і**. Contrast with I declension to show the uniformity of III declension endings.
- P5 (~150 words): Vowel Alternation (Чергування [і] → [о/е]). Explain the pattern where the closed syllable [і] opens and changes back to its original vowel: *ніч — ночі (і→о), піч — печі (і→е), річ — речі (і→е)*.
- P6 (~100 words): Practical tips for remembering the singular forms. Focus on the "triple-i" rule (Gen/Dat/Loc all end in -і).

## Подвоєння в орудному відмінку (~900 words)
- P1 (~150 words): The core rule of the Instrumental case (Орудний відмінок). Explain the doubling (*подвоєння*) of the final consonant before the ending **-ю**. Examples: *сіль → сіллю, подорож → подорожжю, тінь → тінню*.
- P2 (~150 words): The phonetic principle of doubling. Explain that the doubling occurs between two vowels (the root vowel and the -ю suffix). Decolonization note: Ukrainian uses doubling (*ніччю*), while Russian uses a soft sign (*ночью*).
- P3 (~150 words): The "Cluster Exception." Explain why doubling does NOT occur if the stem ends in a consonant cluster. Examples: *радість → радістю, честь → честю, мудрість → мудрістю*.
- P4 (~150 words): The "Labial and R Exception." Explain why *б, п, в, м, ф* (губні) and *р* do not double and instead take an apostrophe. Examples: *любов'ю, кров'ю, матір'ю*.
- P5 (~150 words): Common traps and historical spelling. Why *жовч* becomes *жовчю* (sh-sounds) and why *нехворощ* follows specific rules. Contrast *річчю* vs *радістю*.
- P6 (~150 words): Detailed breakdown of the apostrophe usage. Emphasis on the hard pronunciation of the labial before the soft [й] of the suffix **-ю**.

## Відмінювання у множині (~700 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Nominative and Accusative plural. The standard ending **-і**. Examples: *ночі, печі, радості, подорожі*.
- P2 (~150 words): Genitive plural. The characteristic ending **-ей**. Examples: *ночей, печей, речей, мишей, подорожей*. Note that this is more common than the zero ending found in other declensions.
- P3 (~150 words): Dative, Instrumental, and Locative plural. Standard endings **-ам, -ами, -ах**. Examples: *ночам, ночами, на ночах*. Contrast with the singular Instrumental doubling rule (no doubling in plural).
- P4 (~150 words): The full paradigm of *мати*. Stem expansion with **-ер-**. Detailed list of forms: *матері, матерів, матерям, матерів, матерями, на матерях*. Note the unique Genitive plural *-ів*.

## III відміна vs інші відміни (~550 words)
- P1 (~150 words): The "Masculine Trap." Differentiating between feminine III declension nouns (*ніч, осінь*) and masculine II declension nouns ending in a soft consonant (*гість, олень, біль, степ*). The importance of checking the gender in the dictionary.
- P2 (~150 words): Contrast with I Declension. Feminine nouns with endings (*жінка, пісня*) vs zero-ending nouns (*тінь, річ*). Why this distinction is critical for the Instrumental case (*-ою/-ею* vs *-ю* with doubling).
- P3 (~150 words): Diagnostic algorithm. Three questions to ask: 1. Is it feminine? 2. Is there a zero ending? 3. Is it *мати*? Examples of categorization: *піч, день, міць, путь*.
- P4 (~100 words): Summary of the main differences in case endings across I, II, and III declensions for feminine/soft-stem nouns.

## Підсумок (~400 words)
- P1 (~400 words): Final summary of the module.
  - **Key rules:**
    1. III відміна = feminine nouns + zero ending + *мати*.
    2. Instrumental singular: Doubling (*ніччю, сіллю*), NO doubling for clusters (*радістю*) or labials (*кров'ю*).
    3. Genitive singular: Parallel endings **-і / -и** for specific words.
    4. Vowel alternation: [і] → [о/е] (*ніч/ночі, піч/печі*).
