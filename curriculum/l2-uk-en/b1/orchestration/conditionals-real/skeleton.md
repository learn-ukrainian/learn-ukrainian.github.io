## Що таке спосіб дієслова? (~550 words)
- P1 (~150 words): Introduction to the concept of "спосіб" (mood) in Ukrainian grammar as a way to express the speaker's relation to reality. Contrast "дійсний спосіб" (indicative), which covers past, present, and future facts, with "умовний спосіб" (conditional), which deals with potential or desired actions. Provide initial examples: "Я читаю" (I am reading) vs "Я читав би" (I would read).
- P2 (~150 words): Deep dive into the three-way system based on Litvinova Grade 7. Explain that only the Indicative mood has tenses. Define the Conditional mood as expressing an action that is desirable or possible only under certain conditions. Briefly mention the Imperative mood (наказовий) to complete the set.
- P3 (~150 words): Why B1 learners need this now. Moving from A2 (stating facts) to B1 (nuancing plans and desires). Explain the psychological shift from "I want" (Я хочу) to "I would like" (Я хотів би), highlighting how this changes the tone of communication from direct to sophisticated.
- P4 (~100 words): Setting the scope for the module: Real vs. Unreal conditions. Explain that this module focuses on situations that *can actually happen* (e.g., plans depending on weather), while the next module (M19) will cover the "alternative history" of impossible or contrary-to-fact scenarios.

## Утворення умовного способу (~700 words)
- P1 (~150 words): The mechanical formula for formation from Zabolotnyi Grade 7. Explain that the conditional is formed using the past tense form of the verb plus the particle "б" or "би". Emphasize the crucial orthographic rule: the particle is ALWAYS written separately (окремо). Example: "сказав + би = сказав би".
- P2 (~150 words): The Law of Euphony (Милозвучність) from Avramenko Grade 7. Explain when to use "б" vs. "би". After a vowel, use "б" (вона прийшла б, ми поїхали б). After a consonant, use "би" (він прийшов би, я хотів би). This mirrors the "у/в" and "і/й" rules learners already know.
- P3 (~150 words): The unique flexibility of the particle "б/би" in Ukrainian. Unlike English "would", the particle can follow any significant word in the sentence to shift emphasis. Use the literary quote: "Я б навіть сонцю одягнула вінок" vs "Я одягнула б навіть сонцю вінок".
- P4 (~150 words): Full conjugation paradigm breakdown. Present a table for the verb "читати" (masculine: читав би, feminine: читала б, neuter: читало б, plural: читали б). Point out that since it's based on the past tense, it changes by gender in the singular and by number, but has no person-specific endings.
- P5 (~100 words): Special cases: Reflexive verbs (дієслова на -ся). Show how the particle interacts: "він вчився би", "вона вчилася б". Clarify that the particle still follows the euphony rule regardless of the -ся suffix.

## Реальна умова з якщо (~700 words)
- P1 (~150 words): The logic of real conditional sentences from Zabolotnyi Grade 8. Define the structure: "Якщо [умова], (то) [наслідок]". Explain that both parts use the Indicative mood (Present or Future) because the condition is real and achievable. Use the Nestor the Chronicler quote: "Якщо пошукаєш у книгах мудрості, то знайдеш велику користь для душі своєї."
- P2 (~150 words): Conjunction varieties: "якщо", "коли", and "як". Explain that "якщо" is standard, "коли" is often used in poetic or general contexts (Коли прийде весна, зацвітуть сади), and "як" is more colloquial (Як прийдеш, зателефонуй).
- P3 (~150 words): The role of the correlative "то" in the main clause. Explain that it is optional but adds logical clarity and weight to the consequence. Discuss punctuation: a comma always separates the condition from the consequence, regardless of whether "то" is used.
- P4 (~150 words): Aspect choice in real conditionals. Compare "Якщо ти вчитимеш слова щодня" (process/imperfective) with "Якщо ти вивчиш ці слова до вечора" (result/perfective). Explain how the choice of aspect signals the speaker's focus on the action's duration vs. its completion.
- P5 (~100 words): Position of the clauses. Demonstrate that the condition can come first or second: "Якщо буде дощ, ми залишимося" vs "Ми залишимося, якщо буде дощ". Note that the comma remains mandatory.

## Умовний спосіб для ввічливості (~600 words)
- P1 (~150 words): The social function of the conditional mood in Ukraine. Explain how changing a direct imperative (Дайте!) to a conditional (Чи не могли б ви дати?) acts as a "politeness softener". Contrast the registers: informal/direct vs formal/sophisticated.
- P2 (~150 words): Using "хотіти" (to want) in the conditional. Compare "Я хочу кави" with "Я хотів би кави". Introduce the impersonal construction "Мені хотілося б..." as a way to sound even more humble and polite when expressing desires.
- P3 (~150 words): Giving advice and suggestions. Show how to use "На вашому місці я б..." (In your place I would...) or "Варто було б..." (It would be worth it...). Provide examples related to language learning: "Я б порадив вам читати більше вголос."
- P4 (~150 words): Polite questions and inquiries. Use "Чи не підказали б ви...?" (Wouldn't you suggest...?) or "Я хотів би запитати...". Explain that this is the standard way to approach strangers or colleagues in a professional setting.

## Типові помилки (~400 words)
- P1 (~150 words): The "would + go" calque. Correct the English-speaking learner's tendency to say "Я був би піти" (I would go). Explain that Ukrainian needs only ONE verb in its past form: "Я пішов би". Contrast this with the English auxiliary system.
- P2 (~150 words): Confusing "якщо" with "якби". Explain the error: "*Якщо я мав би час..." (mixing real conjunction with unreal grammar). Rule: Real condition = якщо + indicative. Unreal condition (next module) = якби + conditional.
- P3 (~100 words): Orthography and Euphony errors. Remind learners about writing particles separately (*зробивби -> зробив би) and the "б/би" selection (вона сказала би -> вона сказала б).
