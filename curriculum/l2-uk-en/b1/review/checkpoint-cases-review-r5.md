## Findings
[2] [critical]
Location: `When using professional titles alongside a surname, the title takes the Vocative case, but an invariable surname like **Петренко** remains unchanged: **лікарю Петренко**.`
Issue: Male surnames ending in `-ко` are not invariable in Ukrainian; they decline like corresponding nouns. The vocative form for a male doctor named Петренко is "лікарю Петренку". Female surnames in `-ко` are invariable, but presenting "Петренко" as an inherently "invariable surname" teaches a false grammatical rule.
Fix: Update the text to explain that the surname declines for males but remains unchanged for females.

[2] [critical]
Location: `Найбільша проблема — це постійні затори. Через високу кількість автомобілів, мешканцям важко дістатися до центру.`
Issue: 1) "Висока кількість" is a calque from English ("high amount") or Russian ("высокое количество"); in Ukrainian, "кількість" must be paired with "велика" or "мала". 2) The comma after "автомобілів" is grammatically incorrect, as prepositional phrases at the start of a sentence should not be separated here.
Fix: Change to "Через велику кількість автомобілів мешканцям важко дістатися до центру."
