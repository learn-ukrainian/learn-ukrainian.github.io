## Issues Found

### Issue 1: Oversimplified Latin vs Ukrainian Comparison
**Location:** Content, line 31
**Original:** «замість сухих назв на кшталт "Nominative" ми маємо «Називний» (той, що називає), що робить граматику більш описовою та зрозумілою»
**Problem:** Latin *Nominativus* also derives from *nominare* (to name). The comparison frames Latin as "dry" when the etymological logic is actually parallel. The advantage of Ukrainian terms is their *internal transparency* for native speakers, not inherent superiority.
**Severity:** Minor
**Fix:** Reframe as: «Перевага українських назв у тому, що їхнє значення прозоре для носія мови: ви чуєте "називати" у слові "називний"»

### Issue 2: Aspect Oversimplification
**Location:** Content, line 43
**Original:** «Вид показує, чи дія є завершеною, а час вказує на момент її виконання»
**Problem:** Reducing aspect to "completion" is a common textbook simplification. Aspect also involves boundedness, resultativity, and iterativity. For B1 this may be acceptable as a first approximation, but the teacher dialogue could acknowledge this complexity.
**Severity:** Minor
**Fix:** Add a teacher note: «Це спрощене пояснення — вид має багато нюансів, які ми детально розглянемо у наступних модулях»

### Issue 3: Structural Repetition in Content Categories
**Location:** Content, Parts of Speech section
**Original:** Every part of speech follows identical pattern: term (English) → definition → question → 3 examples with (part of speech) labels
**Problem:** The rigid template creates an encyclopedic rhythm that reduces engagement. After the 4th part of speech, the reader can predict the exact structure. This is a mild LLM fingerprint — human teachers vary their approach.
**Severity:** Minor
**Fix:** Vary the presentation: use a mini-dialogue for some terms, a comparison table for others, a discovery exercise for at least one.

### Issue 4: "Кмета" Etymology
**Location:** Content, line 59 (tip box)
**Original:** «прикметник пов'язаний з "кметою" (ознакою) предмета, а прислівник стоїть "при слові"»
**Problem:** The word "кмета" is archaic and may confuse B1 learners. The standard linguistic explanation traces прикметник to "прикмета" (feature, sign). Using the more common form would be clearer.
**Severity:** Minor
**Fix:** Change «"кметою" (ознакою)» to «"прикметою" (ознакою, рисою)»

### Issue 5: Mark-the-Words Activity Density
**Location:** Activities, activity #13 "Пошук частин мови"
**Original:** Single sentence with 6 nouns to identify
**Problem:** One sentence provides minimal practice. A paragraph of 3-4 sentences with 10-12 target nouns would better reinforce the skill. Current item count meets the 6-item minimum but sits at the floor.
**Severity:** Minor
**Fix:** Expand to a short paragraph with more nouns embedded in varied syntactic positions.
