## Linguistic Scan
- **Linguistic Error (Stylistic):** "приймає закінчення", "приймають закінчення", "приймає нульове закінчення". In Ukrainian grammar terminology, the natural and standard form (as seen in textbooks like Zabolotnyi and Litvinova) is **"має закінчення"** (has an ending) or **"набуває форми"** (acquires the form). "Приймати" (to take/accept) for abstract properties like endings is often a calque from Russian "принимать".
- **Linguistic Error (Stylistic):** **"хранителями"** (хранитель) is described as "rare" (рідко) in SUM-11 and is often associated with religious contexts (ангел-хранитель) or Russian-influenced bookish style. In the context of preserving national memory, **"охоронцями"** (guardians) or **"носіями"** (carriers/bearers) is more natural and preferred in decolonized Ukrainian.
- **Linguistic Error (Stylistic):** **"вгадувати значення"**. "Вгадувати" means to guess (like a riddle). When deducing the meaning of words based on morphology, **"здогадуватися про значення"** is more idiomatic and precise.
- **Linguistic Consistency:** The text uses "полтавчанин" (to fit the -ин suffix topic) but later states that "полтавець" is the traditional/correct form. While "полтавчанин" is valid, the contradiction should be handled carefully. Since the goal is teaching the -ин suffix, using "полтавчанин" as a variant is acceptable, but the stylistic preference for "полтавець" is correctly noted.

- **[LINGUISTIC] [MINOR]**
