### –ß–æ–º—É —Ü–µ –≤–∞–∂–ª–∏–≤–æ

These three prefixes describe motion relative to a reference point:
- **–ø—ñ–¥-** = movement TOWARD something (approaching)
- **–≤—ñ–¥-** = movement AWAY from something (departing)
- **–¥–æ-** = movement UNTIL reaching something (completion)

English expresses these with separate verbs or phrases: "approach," "walk away from," "reach." Ukrainian uses prefixes on the same verb stem.

> üí° **Did You Know**
>
> The prefix **–ø—ñ–¥-** comes from the preposition "–ø—ñ–¥" (under, below), but when used with motion verbs, it means "approach." This is because historically, approaching someone meant coming "under" their attention or sphere. Similarly, **–≤—ñ–¥-** comes from "–≤—ñ–¥" (from) and **–¥–æ-** from "–¥–æ" (to, until).

> üîó **Language Link**
>
> English uses completely different verbs: "approach," "leave," "reach." Ukrainian uses the SAME verb with different prefixes. This means once you know the base verb, you can express all three movements by changing the prefix.
