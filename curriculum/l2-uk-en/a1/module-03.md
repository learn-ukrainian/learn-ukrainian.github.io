# The Gender Code

> **Note:** Welcome to the most important concept in Ukrainian grammar. Every noun has a "gender". This isn't about biology; it's about **sound**. Words have a musical "family" they belong to, and this determines how they dance (change) in a sentence.

## Warm-up: The Three Boxes
Imagine three boxes on a table. Every object in the universe fits into one of them.
1. **Ğ’Ñ–Ğ½ (He):** Masculine
2. **Ğ’Ğ¾Ğ½Ğ° (She):** Feminine
3. **Ğ’Ğ¾Ğ½Ğ¾ (It):** Neuter

In English, "Table" is "It". In Ukrainian, **Ğ¡Ñ‚Ñ–Ğ»** (Table) is "He". **ĞšĞ½Ğ¸Ğ³Ğ°** (Book) is "She". **Ğ’Ñ–ĞºĞ½Ğ¾** (Window) is "It".
Why? Look at the **end** of the word.

## Presentation: The 4 Families (Declensions)

> [!tip] ğŸ’¡ **Thinking Trick:** Don't ask "Is a table male?" Ask "Does the word end in a consonant?" If yes, it's usually Masculine.

### 1. Masculine (The Consonants)
If a word ends in a **Consonant** (Hard or Soft), it is likely Masculine.
- **Ğ¡Ñ‚Ñ–Ğ»** (Table) - Ends in L. Masculine.
- **Ğ‘Ğ°Ğ½Ğº** (Bank) - Ends in K. Masculine.
- **Ğ§Ğ°Ğ¹** (Tea) - Ends in Y. Masculine.

### 2. Feminine (The A-Team)
If a word ends in **-Ğ** or **-Ğ¯**, it is likely Feminine.
- **ĞœĞ°Ğ¼Ğ°** (Mom) - Ends in A. Feminine.
- **ĞšĞ½Ğ¸Ğ³Ğ°** (Book) - Ends in A. Feminine.
- **Ğ—ĞµĞ¼Ğ»Ñ** (Earth) - Ends in Ya. Feminine.

### 3. Neuter (The O-E Circles)
If a word ends in **-Ğ** or **-Ğ•**, it is Neuter.
- **Ğ’Ñ–ĞºĞ½Ğ¾** (Window) - Ends in O. Neuter.
- **ĞœĞ¾Ñ€Ğµ** (Sea) - Ends in E. Neuter.
- **ĞœÑ–ÑÑ‚Ğ¾** (City) - Ends in O. Neuter.

### 4. The 4 Declension Families (Advanced Theory)
To speak perfect Ukrainian, we group these genders into "Families" (Declensions) based on how they change.

| Family | Description | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Family 1** | **Feminine** ending in -Ğ/-Ğ¯ | Ğ–Ñ–Ğ½ĞºĞ°, Ğ—ĞµĞ¼Ğ»Ñ |
| **Family 2** | **Masculine** (Consonant) + **Neuter** (-Ğ/-Ğ•) | Ğ¡Ñ‚Ñ–Ğ», Ğ’Ñ–ĞºĞ½Ğ¾ |
| **Family 3** | **Feminine** ending in Consonant/Soft Sign | ĞÑ–Ñ‡, Ğ¡Ñ–Ğ»ÑŒ |
| **Family 4** | **Neuter** ending in -Ğ/-Ğ¯ (Baby words) | Ğ†Ğ¼'Ñ, Ğ¦ÑƒÑ†ĞµĞ½Ñ |

> [!warning] **The Trap:** A word ending in **-Ğ** is usually Feminine (Family 1). But **Ğ¢Ğ°Ñ‚Ğ¾** (Dad) ends in -O, so he is Family 2 (grammatically acting like a noun in -O, though biologically male). Wait, actually **Ğ¢Ğ°Ñ‚Ğ¾** usually acts like Family 1 in endings! Correction: **Ğ¢Ğ°Ñ‚Ğ¾** (Dad) is Family 1 because it ends in -a/-o but declines like Mama. **Ğ”ÑĞ´ÑŒĞºĞ¾** (Uncle) is Family 2.
> *Simplication for A1:* Just look at the ending for now!

# Summary
- **Masculine:** Ends in Consonant (Ğ¡Ñ‚Ñ–Ğ»).
- **Feminine:** Ends in -Ğ / -Ğ¯ (ĞœĞ°Ğ¼Ğ°).
- **Neuter:** Ends in -Ğ / -Ğ• (Ğ’Ñ–ĞºĞ½Ğ¾).
- **Exceptions:** Words like *Ğ¢Ğ°Ñ‚Ğ¾* (Dad) are Masculine but act like Family 1. Words like *ĞÑ–Ñ‡* (Night) are Feminine (Family 3).
