## Діало́ги (Dialogues)

**Де** (where) — one small word, and suddenly you can find anyone and anything. Where is Olena? Where do you work? Where do you live? Every answer needs the same grammar: the locative case.

<div class="dialogue">


**Марія:** Де Оле́на? *(Where is Olena?)*


**Тарас:** Вона́ в шко́лі. *(She is at school.)*


**Марія:** А де Андрі́й? *(And where is Andriy?)*


**Тарас:** Він на робо́ті. *(He is at work.)*


**Марія:** А ді́ти? *(And the kids?)*


**Тарас:** Вони́ в парку. *(They are in the park.)*


**Марія:** А кі́шка? *(And the cat?)*


**Тарас:** Вона на дива́ні! *(She is on the couch!)*


</div>



Notice the pattern: **Де?** → preposition (**в** or **на**) + a noun with a changed ending. The noun **школа** (school) becomes **в школі**. The noun **робота** (work) becomes **на роботі**. The noun **парк** (park) becomes **в парку**. These changed forms are the locative case — **місцевий відмі́нок**.

Now a second dialogue — this time about where someone lives and works:

<div class="dialogue">


**Оленка:** Де ти живе́ш? *(Where do you live?)*


**Богдан:** Я живу́ в Ки́єві. *(I live in Kyiv.)*


**Оленка:** А де ти працю́єш? *(And where do you work?)*


**Богдан:** Я працю́ю в о́фісі. *(I work in an office.)*


**Оленка:** На які́й ву́лиці? *(On which street?)*


**Богдан:** На вулиці Хреща́тик. *(On Khreshchatyk Street.)*


*(На якій — locative of яка, "which" — you'll learn adjective forms soon; for now, treat it as a fixed phrase.)*


</div>



Here **місто** (city) becomes **в місті**, **вулиця** (street) becomes **на вулиці**, and the city name **Київ** becomes **в Києві**. Every answer to **Де?** follows this same locative pattern.

The key vocabulary from these dialogues:

- **школа** → **в школі** (school → at school)
- **робота** → **на роботі** (work → at work)
- **парк** → **в парку** (park → in the park)
- **місто** → **в місті** (city → in the city)
- **вулиця** → **на вулиці** (street → on the street)
- **офіс** → **в офісі** (office → in the office)

## Місцевий відмінок (The Locative Case)

Ukrainian has seven grammatical cases. You have already seen the nominative case (хто? що? — who? what?), which names things. Now meet the locative — **місцевий відмінок**. Ukrainian Grade 4 textbooks teach a helper-word method for each case (Захарійчук, Grade 4, p. 74). For the locative, the helper questions are:

**М. (на, у) — на/у ко́му? на/у чому́?**

This tells you two things. First, the locative always answers **Де?** — where something is. Second, it always requires a preposition: **в/у** (in, inside) or **на** (on, at). You can never use the locative form alone without one of these prepositions.

### Basic Locative Endings

The most common locative ending is **-і**. Here are the patterns for familiar nouns:

| Nominative | Locative | English |
|---|---|---|
| **школа** | **в школі** | at school |
| **робота** | **на роботі** | at work |
| **вулиця** | **на вулиці** | on the street |
| **лікарня** | **в лікарні** | in the hospital |
| **магазин** | **в магазині** | in the shop |
| **місто** | **в місті** | in the city |

Most feminine nouns (ending in **-а**, **-я**) and many masculine nouns take the ending **-і** in the locative. But some masculine nouns take **-у** instead:

| Nominative | Locative | English |
|---|---|---|
| **банк** | **у банку** | at the bank |
| **парк** | **в парку** | in the park |

For now, learn each place as a fixed phrase — **банк** → **у банку**, **магазин** → **в магазині**. The rules behind which masculine nouns take **-у** and which take **-і** are complex. At this stage, memorizing common places is more effective than memorizing rules.

Some nouns do not change at all. The word **кафе** (café) is borrowed from French and stays the same: **в кафе**.

## В чи на? (В or На?)

Both **в/у** and **на** trigger the locative case, but they mean different things. Choosing the wrong preposition sounds as odd as saying "at the park" instead of "in the park" in English — people understand you, but it sounds off.

### В/у — inside, enclosed spaces

Use **в** (or **у** — remember euphony from the previous module!) for buildings and enclosed spaces:

- **в школі** — at school (inside the building)
- **в магазині** — in the shop
- **у банку** — at the bank
- **в лікарні** — in the hospital
- **в кафе** — in the café

### На — surfaces, open spaces, events

Use **на** for open areas, surfaces, and certain conventional locations:

- **на вулиці** — on the street
- **на площі** (square) — in the square
- **на роботі** — at work
- **на вокзалі** (train station) — at the train station
- **на пошті** (post office) — at the post office

Some places are conventional — you simply learn them with their preposition, just like English uses "at the post office" but "in the hospital." The word **пошта** always takes **на**: **на пошті**, never "в пошті." Similarly, **вокзал** always takes **на**: **на вокзалі**.

### Countries and cities: always в/у

For countries and cities, use **в** or **у**:

- **в Україні** — in Ukraine
- **у Києві** — in Kyiv
- **у Львові** — in Lviv

:::caution
**В Україні** — always. Never "на Україні." The form "на Україні" was imposed by Russian imperial and Soviet usage to frame Ukraine as a territory, not a sovereign country. Modern Ukrainian grammar is clear: **в Україні**. This is not just grammar — it is a matter of respect and sovereignty.
:::

## Підсумок — Summary

The locative case — **місцевий відмінок** — answers one question: **Де?** (Where?). It always appears with a preposition: **в/у** for enclosed spaces or **на** for open spaces, surfaces, and conventional locations.

The helper-word method from Ukrainian Grade 4 textbooks: **М. (на, у) — на/у кому? на/у чому?**

Here are the essential places from this module:

| Place | Locative | Preposition rule |
|---|---|---|
| **школа** (school) | **в школі** | в — building |
| **робота** (work) | **на роботі** | на — conventional |
| **банк** (bank) | **у банку** | у — building |
| **магазин** (shop) | **в магазині** | в — building |
| **вулиця** (street) | **на вулиці** | на — open space |
| **місто** (city) | **в місті** | в — large place |
| **парк** (park) | **в парку** | в — open but bounded |
| **лікарня** (hospital) | **в лікарні** | в — building |
| **кафе** (café) | **в кафе** | в — building (no change!) |
| **площа** (square) | **на площі** | на — open space |
| **пошта** (post office) | **на пошті** | на — conventional |
| **вокзал** (train station) | **на вокзалі** | на — conventional |

The most common ending is **-і**. Some masculine nouns take **-у** (**банк** → **у банку**, **парк** → **в парку**). Indeclinable nouns like **кафе** do not change.
