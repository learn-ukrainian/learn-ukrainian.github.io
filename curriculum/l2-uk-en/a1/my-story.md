## Dialogues

When you meet someone new, the conversation inevitably moves beyond simple greetings and asking how the weather is. Eventually, someone will want to know more about you. They might ask you to share your life story. To do this naturally, you need to seamlessly blend the past, present, and future tenses. 

In this module, we will bring together everything you have learned about Ukrainian verbs to help you narrate your life journey. Let us start by looking at how a natural conversation flows through different periods of time.

<div class="dialogue">


**Марко:** Розкажи́ про себе́! *(Tell about yourself!)*


**Девід:** Я народи́вся в Кана́ді, у Торо́нто. *(I was born in Canada, in Toronto.)*


**Марко:** А за́раз ти живе́ш тут? *(And now do you live here?)*


**Девід:** Так, зараз я живу́ в Ки́єві. *(Yes, now I live in Kyiv.)*


**Марко:** Чо́му ти переї́хав? *(Why did you move?)*


**Девід:** Я хоті́в вивча́ти украї́нську. *(I wanted to study Ukrainian.)*


**Девід:** Мої́ бабу́ся і діду́сь з Украї́ни. *(My grandma and grandpa are from Ukraine.)*


**Марко:** А що ти бу́деш роби́ти да́лі? *(And what will you do next?)*


**Девід:** Я бу́ду працюва́ти тут і вчи́ти мо́ву. *(I will work here and study the language.)*


**Марко:** Чудо́во! У́спіхів! *(Great! Success!)*


</div>

Notice how David shifts effortlessly between timelines. He uses the verb **народитися** (to be born) and **переїхати** (to move) in the past tense to establish his background. Then, he switches to the present tense to describe his current situation, using **жити** (to live). Finally, he looks ahead with the future tense construction **буду працювати** (I will work). This conversation feels dynamic because it connects his **дитинство** (childhood) to his current life and his future **мрія** (dream).

Now, let us listen to Anna sharing her journey. She describes her education, her career, and her plans to see the world.

<div class="dialogue">


**Анна:** Я народи́лася у Льво́ві. *(I was born in Lviv.)*


**Анна:** Там я вчи́лася в шко́лі. *(There I studied at school.)*


**Анна:** По́тім я переї́хала в Ки́їв. *(Then I moved to Kyiv.)*


**Анна:** Я закі́нчи́ла університе́т. *(I finished university.)*


**Анна:** Зараз я працю́ю в школі. Я вчи́телька. *(Now I work at school. I am a teacher.)*


**Анна:** Я живу в це́нтрі мі́ста. *(I live in the city center.)*


**Максим:** А що далі? *(And what next?)*


**Анна:** Я буду подорожува́ти! *(I will travel!)*


**Анна:** Я хо́чу поба́чити Япо́нію. *(I want to see Japan.)*


**Максим:** І ти будеш вчити япо́нську? *(And will you study Japanese?)*


**Анна:** Мо́же! *(Maybe!)*


**Анна:** Але́ споча́тку — украї́нська для тебе́! *(But first — Ukrainian for you!)*


</div>

Anna provides a very clear, step-by-step narrative. She uses the verb **вчитися** (to study) and **закінчити** (to finish/graduate) to talk about her past education. Notice that she uses the feminine past tense ending **-ла**, saying **народилася** and **вчилася**, because she is a woman. Her story is a perfect example of a logical past-to-present-to-future flow.

## Три часи́ ра́зом (Three Tenses Together)

Telling your story requires structure. A good biography acts like a bridge connecting three distinct points in time: the past (мину́лий час), the present (тепе́рішній час), and the future (майбу́тній час). When you want **розповідати** (to tell/narrate) your life events clearly, you must signal to your listener which timeline you are entering. 

### The Past (Минулий час)

Your origin story always begins in the past. To form the past tense, we use the specific gender endings: **-в** for masculine, **-ла** for feminine, **-ло** for neuter, and **-ли** for plural. For reflexive verbs, we add the suffix **-ся** (or **-сь** after vowels).

- **Я народився в Канаді.** (I was born in Canada. — *masculine speaker*)
- **Я народилася у Львові.** (I was born in Lviv. — *feminine speaker*)
- **Я жив у мі́сті.** (I lived in the city. — *masculine*)
- **Вона жила́ там.** (She lived there. — *feminine*)
- **Я вчився в школі.** (I studied at school. — *masculine*)
- **Я працюва́ла там.** (I worked there. — *feminine*)

To help your listener, you should use signal words that explicitly point to the past. These include **раніше** (before/earlier) and the phrase **у дитинстві** (in childhood). Sometimes, native speakers will also say **коли я був маленьким** (when I was little) or **коли я була мале́нькою**, but **у дитинстві** is the most direct and simple way to express this idea.

### The Present (Теперішній час)

After establishing your origins, you bring the listener to the current moment. The present tense uses the standard person endings that match the subject (я, ти, він, ми, ви, вони́). 

- **Зараз я живу в Києві.** (Now I live in Kyiv.)
- **Я працюю тут.** (I work here.)
- **Я вивча́ю українську.** (I am studying Ukrainian.)
- **Я люблю́ му́зику.** (I love music.)

The signal words for this timeline are very immediate. You will frequently hear **зараз** (now), **сьогодні** (today), and **цього року** (this year). These words tell the listener that the action is happening right at this very moment in your life.

### The Future (Майбутній час)

Finally, you share your aspirations. The most common way to express future plans is to use the future forms of the verb "to be" plus the infinitive of the main verb (**буду** + infinitive).

- **Я буду працювати там.** (I will work there.)
- **Я буду вивчати англі́йську.** (I will study English.)
- **Вона бу́де жити в місті.** (She will live in the city.)
- **Ми бу́демо подорожувати.** (We will travel.)

When shifting into your future plans, native speakers use transitional signal words like **потім** (then), **далі** (further/next), and **наступного року** (next year). These words act like road signs, preparing the listener for a shift in perspective.

## Моя́ істо́рія (My Story)

To truly master this skill, you need to see all of these pieces working together in a complete, unified paragraph. Let us look at a model story written by Taras. 

As you read his biography, pay close attention to the verbs. Watch how he establishes his past, transitions smoothly into his current life, and then confidently states his future goals.

> **Я народився в Оде́сі у ти́сяча дев'ятсо́т дев'яно́сто п'я́тому ро́ці. Я жив там з батьками і сестро́ю. Я ходи́в у шко́лу і люби́в матема́тику. Потім я переїхав у Київ і вчився в університеті. Зараз я живу в Києві. Я працюю програмі́стом. Я люблю свою́ робо́ту. У ві́льний час я гра́ю у футбо́л і чита́ю книжки́. Далі я буду подорожувати. Я буду вивчати англійську. І я буду жити в Києві — це моє́ мі́сто!**

*(I was born in Odesa in 1995. I lived there with my parents and sister. I went to school and loved math. Then I moved to Kyiv and studied at the university. Now I live in Kyiv. I work as a programmer. I love my work. In my free time I play football and read books. Next I will travel. I will study English. And I will live in Kyiv — this is my city!)*

:::tip
When you want to state the specific year you were born, you use a fixed phrase format. For example, **у тисяча дев'ятсот дев'яносто п'ятому році** means "in 1995". This phrase always starts with the preposition **у** (in) and ends with the word **році** (year). 
:::

Taras uses a highly effective structure. He begins with his **батьки** (parents) in Odesa, describing his early life with past tense verbs (**народився**, **жив**, **ходив**, **любив**, **переїхав**, **вчився**). 

Next, he anchors us in the present moment with the signal word **зараз**. The verbs immediately shift to present tense forms (**живу**, **працюю**, **люблю**, **граю**, **читаю**). Notice that he says he works as a **програміст** (programmer), an excellent profession for a modern city. 

Finally, he looks ahead with the signal word **далі** (further/next), shifting cleanly into the future tense (**буду подорожувати**, **буду вивчати**, **буду жити**). This progression creates a natural, easy-to-follow narrative. 

### Your Turn — Tell YOUR Story

Now it is time to build your own biography. You can use the template below to structure your thoughts. When you want to give a reason for studying Ukrainian, you might want to use the conjunction **тому що** (because). However, a very natural and simple way to express this is to use two short, clear sentences.

**Start with the Past:**
- **Я народився / народилася в...** (I was born in...)
- **Я жив / жила...** (I lived...)
- **Я вчився / вчилася...** (I studied...)
- **Я працюва́в / працювала...** (I worked...)

**Move to the Present:**
- **Зараз я живу...** (Now I live...)
- **Я працюю...** (I work...)
- **Я вивчаю українську.** (I am studying Ukrainian.)
- **Це ду́же ціка́во.** (It is very interesting.)

**Look to the Future:**
- **Далі я буду...** (Next I will...)
- **Я хочу...** (I want...)

By practicing this simple sequence, you will be prepared to share your background with anyone you meet in Ukraine.

## Summary

Telling your life story requires you to comfortably navigate three different time periods. Let us review the key mechanical rules for combining all three tenses in a single narrative.

**1. The Past Tense Mechanics**
The past tense relies exclusively on gender and number endings. You drop the infinitive ending and add the appropriate marker:
- Masculine: **-в** (Він жив, Я народився)
- Feminine: **-ла** (Вона жила, Я народилася)
- Neuter: **-ло** (Воно́ жило́)
- Plural: **-ли** (Ми жили, Вони вчи́лися)

**2. The Present Tense Mechanics**
The present tense focuses on the person performing the action. The endings change based on the pronoun (я, ти, він/вона, ми, ви, вони). 
- **Я живу.** (I live.)
- **Ти працю́єш.** (You work.)
- **Вона вивча́є.** (She studies.)

**3. The Future Tense Mechanics**
The compound future tense is built using a conjugated form of the helper verb **бути** (to be) followed by the unconjugated infinitive of the action verb. 
- **Я буду працювати.** (I will work.)
- **Вона буде жити.** (She will live.)
- **Ми будемо подорожувати.** (We will travel.)

**4. Signal Words**
To help your conversation partner follow your timeline, always use clear signal words. 
- To indicate the past: **раніше** (before/earlier).
- To indicate the present: **зараз** (now).
- To indicate the future: **далі** (further/next).

**5. Life Story Vocabulary**
Mastering a few specific verbs will allow you to tell a rich and detailed biography. Ensure you are comfortable with:
- **народитися** (to be born)
- **жити** (to live)
- **вчитися** (to study)
- **закінчити** (to finish/graduate)
- **переїхати** (to move)
- **працюю** (I work)
- **подорожувати** (to travel)

For your self-check, write your life story in 8-10 sentences using all three tenses. Remember to start in the past, anchor yourself in the present, and share your goals for the future.
