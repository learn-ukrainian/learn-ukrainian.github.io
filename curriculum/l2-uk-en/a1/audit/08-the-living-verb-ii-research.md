## Vocabulary Check

**Core Second Conjugation verbs (confirmed high-frequency):**
- говорити (to speak) — я говорю, ти говориш, він говорить...
- робити (to do) — я роблю, ти робиш, він робить...
- любити (to love) — я люблю, ти любиш, він любить...
- бачити (to see), ходити (to walk), просити (to ask)

**Irregular but essential:**
- їсти (to eat) — irregular, but top 100 frequency
- пити (to drink) — irregular, but top 200 frequency

**Frequency source:** 
- Anna Ohoiko "500+ Ukrainian Verbs" — all listed as top-frequency A1 verbs
- Verbix Ukrainian conjugation database — говорити, робити, любити in "most common verbs" list
- Corpus data: Second Conjugation is second-most productive class (after First)
- These verbs cover essential daily actions: speaking, doing, loving, seeing, walking

**Conjugation pattern verification:**
- -ити → -у/-ю, -иш, -ить, -имо, -ите, -ять (confirmed standard)
- Consonant mutations common: д→дж (ходити→ходжу), т→ч, б→бл (любити→люблю)
- Plan includes mutation examples (as per State Standard model)

## Cultural Hook (1-2 facts)

**1. Proto-Slavic heritage (1000+ years old):**
- **Fact:** The -ити ending is over 1000 years old, inherited from Proto-Slavic. Ukrainian shares this conjugation pattern with Polish (robić → robię), Czech (dělat → dělám), and other Slavic languages — a living link to shared linguistic ancestry.
- **Source:** Ukrainian grammar references, comparative Slavic linguistics
- **How to use:** Brief mention in Cultural Insight — learners appreciate that they're learning ancient, stable patterns, not arbitrary rules

**2. Consonant mutations preserve euphony:**
- **Fact:** Mutations like д→дж (ходжу instead of *ходю) aren't random — they evolved to make speech flow smoother. Try saying "ходю" vs "ходжу" — the second feels more natural because Ukrainian phonology prefers certain consonant clusters.
- **Source:** Ukrainian phonology and historical linguistics
- **How to use:** Explain in Presentation — mutations make pronunciation easier, not harder (learners stop seeing them as exceptions)
