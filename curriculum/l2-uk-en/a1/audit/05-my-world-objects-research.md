## Cultural Hook (1-2 facts)

### Hook 1: Ukrainian Living Spaces
- **Fact:** Traditional Ukrainian apartments (хрущовки/khrushchyovky - named after Khrushchev) built in 1960s-80s are still the most common housing type in Ukrainian cities. These small flats (30-50 m²) typically have a kitchen (кухня), bathroom (ванна), and 1-2 rooms (кімнати), requiring multi-functional furniture like складані дивани (folding sofas) and шафи-купе (sliding wardrobes).
- **Source:** Ukrainian Housing Census data, architectural history references
- **How to use:** Explain in Cultural Insight why Ukrainian apartment vocabulary emphasizes compact, multi-functional furniture (диван that converts to ліжко, шафа that stores everything)

### Hook 2: Demonstrative це Dual Function
- **Fact:** The demonstrative це has TWO distinct functions in Ukrainian: (1) neuter form "this" matching neuter nouns (це вікно = this window), and (2) general pointing word "this is" regardless of gender (це стіл = this is [a] table, це книга = this is [a] book). This dual role is inherited from Old East Slavic and creates a unique pattern not found in English.
- **Source:** §4.2.2 State Standard examples, historical grammar references
- **How to use:** Address the confusion English speakers face: "Why does це work with masculine стіл if it's neuter?" Answer: Because in "це стіл" the це isn't agreeing with gender—it's the general pointing word.

## Pedagogical Notes

### Why This Module Now (Sequence Logic)
- **Prerequisites:** M03 (Gender) provides the foundation—learners already know masculine/feminine/neuter
- **Next step:** Adding demonstratives turns static gender knowledge into functional pointing/describing
- **Connection:** M04 introduced це as "this is"—now we expand to full demonstrative system with gender agreement

### Challenge for English Speakers
- English "this/that" don't change by gender
- English speakers must think: "What gender is the noun? → Choose matching demonstrative"
- Extra cognitive load: цей vs ця vs це requires gender recall every time

### Pattern Recognition
- **Near forms (цей/ця/це/ці):** All start with ц-
- **Far forms (той/та/те/ті):** All start with т-
- Parallel structure helps memory: цей-той, ця-та, це-те, ці-ті

### Room Vocabulary Strategy
- Choose words across all three genders to practice demonstrative agreement
- Masculine: стіл, стілець, диван (furniture)
- Feminine: книга, лампа, шафа (objects)
- Neuter: вікно, ліжко, крісло (furniture/architecture)
- Ensures balanced practice of all demonstrative forms
