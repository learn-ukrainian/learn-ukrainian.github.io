## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Scope Creep (Grammar)
- **Location**: Line 80 / Section "Presentation"
- **Original**: "4. **Ми дивимося фільм.**"
- **Problem**: The verb `дивитися` is reflexive (-ся). Reflexive verbs are explicitly Module 09. The "Reflexive Verbs Preview" box appears *after* this example. This confuses learners who haven't seen -ся yet.
- **Fix**: Replace with a known verb. "Ми любимо цей фільм." (We love this movie).

### Issue 2: Scope Creep (Vocabulary in Activities)
- **Location**: Activity `quiz` / Item "Ми зазвичай ___ вдома ввечері."
- **Original**: Answer `вечеряємо` (from `вечеряти`).
- **Problem**: `вечеряти` (to have dinner) is not in the vocabulary list and likely not taught yet.
- **Fix**: Change context to reading. "Ми зазвичай **читаємо** вдома ввечері." (Answer: `читаємо`).

### Issue 3: Awkward Phrasing
- **Location**: Activity `quiz` / Last Item
- **Original**: "Що вони роблять? Вони ___." (Answer: `бачать`).
- **Problem**: "Вони бачать" (They see) is linguistically incomplete as an activity description without an object (See what?). It sounds odd.
- **Fix**: Change verb to `стоять` (intransitive is fine) or add object.
    - Option A: "Що вони роблять? Вони **стоять**." (They are standing).
    - Option B: "Що вони роблять? Вони **бачать** птаха." (They see a bird).
    - Recommendation: Option A is simpler.

### Issue 4: Minor Naturalness
- **Location**: Activity `quiz` / Item "Що ці люди роблять? Вони ___ обід."
- **Original**: "Вони **їдять** обід."
- **Problem**: While grammatically correct, Ukrainians usually say `вони обідають`. `їсти обід` is a bit of an English calque (eat lunch).
- **Fix**: Change `обід` to specific food. "Вони **їдять** піцу."
