## Issues Found

### CRITICAL (blocks deployment)

None.

### HIGH (should fix before deployment)

1. **Missing alphabet chart** — Plan objective 1 says "Show the full 33-letter alphabet chart as a reference map." The content mentions 33 letters but never displays them. Add a 33-letter chart table to the Вступ section.

2. **Missing vowel/consonant lists** — Plan asks for "6 base (А О У Е И І) + 4 iotated (Я Ю Є Ї)" in the Голосні та приголосні section. Content says "10 vowel letters" and "22 consonant letters" but never lists them. Add category tables.

### MEDIUM (fix if possible)

3. **Watch-and-repeat undercount** — 4 items vs 10 planned. Each of the 10 practice letters should have its own watch-and-repeat item with the corresponding video.

4. **Match-up concept mismatch** — Plan wanted letter→sound matching for false friends (Н≠H, С≠C). Built match-up does word→translation. Add a second match-up or replace with the false-friend version.

5. **Three sections of pure exposition before practice** — Sections 1-3 (Вступ, Букви і звуки, Голосні та приголосні) are ~550 words of English explanation before the learner sees any practice. Consider adding a mini-exercise (e.g., "Can you spot which of these letters look like English letters?") earlier.

### LOW (informational)

6. **Formal voice** — "Let us" (4x), "We will" (3x), "Do not" (1x) instead of contractions. Warmer tutor voice should use "Let's", "You'll", "Don't".

7. **Only 1 "Don't worry" moment** — Tier 1 rubric asks for ≥2. Add one more reassuring moment (e.g., in Голосні та приголосні when introducing the 33-letter count).

8. **Quiz tests recall, not reading** — Q1-Q2 ("How do you say hello?", "Which word means thank you?") test English→Ukrainian translation, not the reading skill this module focuses on. Better: show a Ukrainian word and ask "What does this say?"

## Grade Justification

**Grade: B — Good, minor issues only**

The module delivers solid A1.1 content with correct Ukrainian, appropriate pacing, encouraging tone, and good variety of activities (9 types). All 23 vocabulary words are present and VESUM-verified. The pedagogical arc (Welcome→Preview→Present→Practice→Celebrate) works well. The two HIGH issues — missing alphabet chart and missing vowel/consonant lists — are significant visual references the learner needs, but they don't break the module's core learning path. The match-up concept mismatch and watch-and-repeat undercount are activity-level gaps that reduce practice variety but don't prevent learning. Overall, this is a functional, encouraging first module that needs a patch (add charts, expand watch-and-repeat, fix match-up) rather than a rebuild.
