## Checkpoint Philosophy (TTT vs PPP)

### Test-Teach-Test Approach
- **Definition:** Checkpoints use TTT (Test-Teach-Test) pedagogy, NOT PPP (Present-Practice-Produce)
- **Difference:** 
  - PPP: Teach new content → Practice it → Use it productively
  - TTT: Test what learners know → Address gaps → Retest
- **Purpose:** Checkpoints REVIEW and CONSOLIDATE existing knowledge from previous modules, they DON'T introduce new grammar/vocabulary
- **State Standard alignment:** Checkpoints verify learners have achieved level descriptors from §2.4.1

### What Makes M10 a Checkpoint
- **Scope:** Tests modules 01-09 (all of A1.1 "First Contact" phase)
- **Skills tested:**
  1. Cyrillic reading fluency (M01-M02)
  2. Gender identification (M03)
  3. Verb conjugation (M06, M08, M09)
  4. Question formation (M07)
  5. Negation (M07)

## A1.1 Phase Coverage (Modules 01-09)

### Cyrillic Mastery
- **M01:** First 15 letters (True Friends, False Friends, New Letters)
- **M02:** Complete alphabet (33 letters), special letters (Ґ, Є, І, Ї)
- **Checkpoint task:** Read words without transliteration, identify common errors (и/і, е/є, г/ґ)

### Gender System
- **M03:** Three genders (m/f/n), gender by ending rules
- **Checkpoint task:** Quickly classify nouns by gender, identify exceptions (тато, батько)

### Verb System Foundation
- **M06:** First Conjugation (-ати verbs)
- **M08:** Second Conjugation (-ити verbs)
- **M09:** Reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)
- **Checkpoint task:** Choose correct conjugation pattern, avoid common errors (mixing conjugations)

### Question and Negation
- **M07:** Question words (хто, що, де, коли), чи particle, не negation
- **Checkpoint task:** Form questions and negative statements accurately

### Integration Scenario
- **Final task:** Café dialogue using ALL skills—reading menu (Cyrillic), ordering (verbs), asking questions (де кава?), describing preferences (gender agreement)

## Common A1.1 Errors (What to Test)

### Error 1: и/і Confusion
- **Problem:** English speakers confuse и [ɪ] and і [i]
- **Common mistakes:** *"сістра"* instead of "сестра", *"мий"* instead of "мій"
- **Checkpoint focus:** Test recognition of both vowels in context

### Error 2: Conjugation Mixing
- **Problem:** Applying First Conjugation endings to Second Conjugation verbs (or vice versa)
- **Common mistakes:** *"говораю"* instead of "говорю", *"читию"* instead of "читаю"
- **Checkpoint focus:** Present mixed verbs, require correct conjugation

### Error 3: Gender Agreement Failure
- **Problem:** Using wrong demonstrative or possessive for noun gender
- **Common mistakes:** *"моя стіл"* (f. with m. noun), *"цей книга"* (m. with f. noun)
- **Checkpoint focus:** Gender agreement in demonstratives and possessives

### Error 4: Adding English "Do"
- **Problem:** Translating English question structure literally
- **Common mistakes:** *"Чи ти робиш читати?"* (trying to say "Do you read?")
- **Checkpoint focus:** Test correct question formation (Чи ти читаєш? or Ти читаєш?)

## Cultural Hook (Checkpoint Context)

### Hook 1: Ukrainian Checkpoints vs English "Tests"
- **Fact:** Ukrainian educational system uses "контрольна робота" (checkpoint work) as formative assessment—it's seen as DIAGNOSTIC, not punitive. The goal is to identify gaps and address them, not to rank/grade students. This aligns with European CEFR philosophy: checkpoints verify readiness to progress, not measure against peers.
- **Source:** Ukrainian pedagogy tradition, CEFR assessment guidelines
- **How to use:** Frame checkpoint positively—"This helps YOU see what you've mastered and what needs review"

### Hook 2: Café as Integration Context
- **Fact:** Ukrainian café culture is central to daily life, especially post-2014. Cafés (кав'ярні) became gathering spaces for civil society, with Wi-Fi and work-friendly environments. Ordering at a café requires: reading menu (literacy), asking questions (де туалет?), using verbs (я хочу), understanding gender (одну каву, один чай), and being polite (будь ласка, дякую).
- **Source:** Ukrainian cultural studies post-Euromaidan
- **How to use:** Justify café scenario as authentic integration task—real-world application of all A1.1 skills

### Self-Assessment Focus
- Checkpoints should include self-evaluation rubrics
- "Can you...?" format: Can you read Cyrillic without transliteration? Can you conjugate -ати verbs?
- Encourages metacognition and learner autonomy
- Aligns with CEFR "can-do" descriptors

### What Happens If Learners Fail?
- **Checkpoint failure → Review specific modules**
- Failed Cyrillic? → Review M01-M02
- Failed conjugation? → Review M06, M08
- Checkpoints identify gaps; learners revisit modules before continuing to A1.2
