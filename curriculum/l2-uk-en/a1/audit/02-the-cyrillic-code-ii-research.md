## Cultural Hook (1-2 facts)

### Hook 1: The "Kyiv Not Kiev" Revolution
- **Fact:** Ukrainian uses **Київ** (Kyiv), while Russian uses Киев (Kiev). The international campaign "KyivNotKiev" (2018) pushed for using correct Ukrainian transliteration. UN officially adopted "Kyiv" in 2019.
- **Source:** Ukrainian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, UN Geographic Names Database
- **How to use:** In Cultural Insight section - show how letter **И** (hard i) vs **І** (soft i) creates completely different sounds and spellings. This isn't just linguistics; it's cultural identity and international recognition.

### Hook 2: Letter Ґ's Suppression and Return
- **Fact:** The letter **Ґ** was officially removed from Ukrainian orthography in 1933 under Soviet pressure (only appears in ~0.03% of Ukrainian words). It was restored in 1990 after Ukraine's independence. Today, using Ґ correctly (ґанок, ґава) is a mark of linguistic authenticity.
- **Source:** History of Ukrainian orthography reforms, Institute of Ukrainian Language
- **How to use:** Explain in Presentation that Ґ isn't common, but preserving it matters culturally. Compare: Russian doesn't have this letter at all - another marker of Ukrainian distinctness.

### Hook 3: Iotated Letters and Ukrainian Euphony
- **Fact:** Ukrainian's iotated letters (Є, Ї, Ю, Я) contribute to the language's characteristic melodic sound. State Standard §4.1.7 specifically mentions "засоби милозвучності" (means of euphony) - Ukrainian is designed for pleasant sound.
- **Source:** §4.1.7 State Standard 2024
- **How to use:** Show learners that Ukrainian prioritizes smooth, flowing sounds (unlike Russian's harsher consonant clusters). This is built into the alphabet itself.

## Pedagogical Notes for M02

### Grouping Strategy
- **Unique Seven** (Г, Ґ, Ж, Ш, Щ, Ч, Ц): Consonants with no English equivalents
- **Iotated Four** (Є, Ї, Ю, Я): Vowels with y-glide
- **Special cases:** Soft sign (Ь), Short Y (Й), И vs І distinction

### Why Group Iotated Letters Together?
- Pattern recognition: All four start with [y] sound (ye, yi, yu, ya)
- Parallel structure: Helps memorization
- Contrasts with Russian: Different usage and sound values

### Apostrophe vs Soft Sign
- State Standard distinguishes these clearly (§4.1.2 vs §4.1.3)
- **Apostrophe (')**: Separates consonant from iotated vowel - дев'ять, м'яч
- **Soft Sign (ь)**: Softens preceding consonant - Львів, шість
- Must be taught carefully to avoid confusion
