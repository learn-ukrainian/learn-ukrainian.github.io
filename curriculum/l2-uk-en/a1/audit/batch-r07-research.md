## M25: My Daily Routine (Reflexive Verbs & Daily Activities)

### State Standard Reference
- **§3.1.2**: Daily life topics (routine, time management)
- **§4.2.3**: Reflexive verbs in present tense
- **Level**: A1 (Can describe basic daily activities)

### Vocabulary Analysis
**Required (8 words)**:
- прокидатися, вмиватися, одягатися, снідати, обідати, вечеряти, лягати, спати

**Recommended (6 words)**:
- спочатку, потім, нарешті, зазвичай, щодня, відпочивати

**Total**: 14 words (appropriate for A1)
**Domain**: Daily routine, time sequencing

### Cultural Hooks
1. **Ukrainian meal traditions**: Three main meals (сніданок, обід, вечеря) with обід traditionally being the largest meal
2. **Work-life balance**: Ukrainian 9-to-6 work culture with lunch break
3. **Weekday vs weekend routines**: Different patterns for робочі дні vs вихідні
4. **Ukrainian breakfast foods**: Каша, сирники, яєчня as typical morning foods
5. **Evening rituals**: Tea drinking (пити чай) as social/family time

### Grammar Integration
- Reflexive verbs with -ся/-сь suffix
- Sequence adverbs (спочатку, потім, нарешті)
- Time expressions with "о котрій годині?"

### Activity Notes
- 4 activity types planned (unjumble, fill-in, match-up, fill-in)
- 61 total items
- Focus: ordering activities, time matching, personal production

## M26: Describing Things - Adjectives (Gender Agreement)

### State Standard Reference
- **§4.3.1**: Adjective declension basics
- **§4.3.2**: Gender and number agreement
- **Level**: A1 (Can use basic adjectives with correct endings)

### Vocabulary Analysis
**Required (8 words)**:
- новий, старий, гарний, великий, малий, добрий, поганий, цікавий

**Recommended (6 words)**:
- синій, червоний, молодий, дорогий, дешевий, смачний

**Total**: 14 words (appropriate for A1)
**Domain**: Basic descriptive adjectives (size, quality, age)

### Cultural Hooks
1. **Ukrainian home descriptions**: Traditional focus on затишок (coziness) vs size
2. **Adjective usage patterns**: Ukrainians often use diminutives more than adjectives (будиночок vs малий будинок)
3. **Quality over quantity**: Cultural preference in describing possessions
4. **Ukrainian architecture**: Describing typical Ukrainian хата, квартира
5. **Color symbolism**: Traditional Ukrainian color preferences (blue/yellow national symbolism subtle)

### Grammar Integration
- Hard stem adjectives: -ий/-а/-е/-і
- Soft stem adjectives: -ій/-я/-є/-і
- Adjective placement: before noun (attributive) or after бути (predicative)
- Antonym pairs for contrast learning

### Activity Notes
- 4 activity types planned
- 68 total items
- Focus: gender changing, noun-adjective matching, picture description

## M27: Colors & Clothing (Describing What You Wear)

### State Standard Reference
- **§3.1.3**: Personal belongings and clothing
- **§4.3.1**: Color adjectives with agreement
- **Level**: A1 (Can describe clothing and colors)

### Vocabulary Analysis
**Required (8 words)**:
- білий, чорний, червоний, синій, зелений, сорочка, штани, плаття

**Recommended (6 words)**:
- жовтий, коричневий, куртка, светр, взуття, носити

**Total**: 14 words (appropriate for A1)
**Domain**: Colors + clothing vocabulary

### Cultural Hooks
1. **Ukrainian traditional clothing**: Вишиванка (embroidered shirt) as cultural symbol - brief mention at A1 level
2. **Color preferences**: Blue and yellow in traditional embroidery (national colors)
3. **Seasonal clothing**: Four distinct seasons = distinct wardrobes (especially warm зимовий одяг)
4. **Shopping culture**: Ukrainian ринок (market) vs магазин для shopping
5. **Pluralia tantum**: Ukrainian штани, джинси, окуляри (always plural) - grammatical quirk

### Grammar Integration
- Color adjectives with gender agreement
- Носити + accusative case (Я ношу джинси)
- Pluralia tantum items (pants, glasses)
- Demonstrative це/ця/цей with clothing

### Activity Notes
- 4 activity types planned
- 66 total items
- Focus: color-item matching, outfit description, shopping dialogues

## M28: Description - Adverbs (How We Do Things)

### State Standard Reference
- **§4.4.1**: Adverbs of manner
- **§4.4.2**: Adverbs of frequency
- **Level**: A1 (Can use basic adverbs to describe actions)

### Vocabulary Analysis
**Required (8 words)**:
- добре, погано, швидко, повільно, завжди, часто, іноді, ніколи

**Recommended (6 words)**:
- голосно, тихо, рідко, зазвичай, легко, важко

**Total**: 14 words (appropriate for A1)
**Domain**: Manner and frequency adverbs

### Cultural Hooks
1. **Ukrainian pace of life**: Contrast between urban швидко and rural повільно lifestyle
2. **Frequency expressions**: Ukrainian habits with завжди (always being on time, hospitality)
3. **Politeness and manner**: Speaking тихо in public spaces (cultural norm)
4. **Learning Ukrainian**: Context of легко vs важко for different aspects
5. **Work ethic**: Cultural values around doing things добре (quality consciousness)

### Grammar Integration
- Adverb formation: adjective stem + -о (швидкий → швидко)
- Irregular adverbs: добрий → добре (not добро)
- Frequency adverb scale and positioning
- Intensifiers: дуже, трохи, зовсім, майже

### Activity Notes
- 4 activity types planned
- 63 total items
- Focus: adjective→adverb transformation, frequency ordering, habit description

## Production Notes

### Common Pitfalls (A1 level)
1. **Reflexive verbs**: Forgetting -ся/-сь alternation
2. **Adjective endings**: Mixing up m/f/n endings
3. **Color agreement**: Treating colors as invariable
4. **Adverb position**: Placing frequency adverbs incorrectly
5. **Pluralia tantum**: Saying "штан" instead of "штани"

### Success Metrics
- Can describe daily routine with 5+ activities in sequence
- Can use 10+ adjectives with correct gender agreement
- Can name 8+ colors and 6+ clothing items
- Can distinguish adjectives from adverbs in usage
