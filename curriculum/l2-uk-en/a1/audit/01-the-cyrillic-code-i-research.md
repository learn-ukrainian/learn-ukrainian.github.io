## Cultural Hook (1-2 facts)

### Hook 1: Ukrainian Cyrillic Uniqueness
- **Fact:** Ukrainian has four letters that don't exist in Russian Cyrillic: **Ґ**, **Є**, **І**, **Ї** (ґринджоли, є, і, ї). These letters were restored after Ukraine's independence in 1991, having been suppressed during Soviet times.
- **Source:** Ukrainian State Standard 2024, Cultural context in alphabet reforms
- **How to use:** Emphasize in "Cultural Insight" section that learners are learning Ukrainian alphabet, not "Russian letters." This builds pride and cultural awareness from day 1.

### Hook 2: Є as a distinctly Ukrainian sound
- **Fact:** The letter **Є** (ye) represents the sound [je] and is one of Ukraine's unique letters. Example: Європа (Europe - Yevropa). This letter doesn't exist in Russian, which uses different spelling.
- **Source:** §4.1.1 examples from State Standard
- **How to use:** Show learners that Ukrainian spelling often preserves historical and etymological connections to European languages, reinforcing Ukraine's European identity.

## Pedagogical Notes for M01

### Why "True Friends, False Friends, New Letters" Strategy
- **Cognitive load management:** Grouping by visual familiarity reduces overwhelm
- **Quick wins:** True Friends (А, Е, О) give immediate success
- **Explicit warning:** False Friends (В=v, Н=n, Р=r) prevent frustration
- **Foundation:** New letters build confidence after initial success

### Module 01 vs Module 02 Split
- **M01 focus:** Visual recognition, basic decoding (~15 letters)
- **M02 focus:** Complete alphabet, all special letters (Ґ, Є, І, Ї), digraphs
- State Standard §4.1.1 covers entire alphabet, but pedagogically we split across two modules for digestibility

### Transliteration Strategy for M01
- **Full transliteration** for all words (bank -> банк (bank))
- Per A1 quick-ref: M01-10 use full transliteration
- Builds phonetic awareness and reduces anxiety
