## Ukrainian Question Formation Simplicity

### No Auxiliary "Do/Does" Required
- **Linguistic fact:** Ukrainian forms questions WITHOUT auxiliary verbs. English requires "do/does/did" (Do you read? Does he work?), but Ukrainian simply adds чи or uses rising intonation: Ти читаєш? (You read?)
- **Comparison:** English: "Do you work?" (3 words, auxiliary needed). Ukrainian: "Працюєш?" (1 word, just verb with question intonation) or "Чи працюєш?" (2 words, particle + verb)
- **Source:** Comparative Slavic-Germanic syntax (Shevelov, 1963)
- **How to use:** Emphasize in Presentation that Ukrainian questions are SIMPLER than English—no need to learn complex auxiliary systems

### Чи as Register Marker
- **Sociolinguistic fact:** Using чи signals formal/careful speech, while omitting it (just rising intonation) signals casual/colloquial register. Compare: "Чи ти студент?" (formal) vs "Ти студент?" (casual). This register distinction is taught implicitly through examples.
- **Source:** Ukrainian sociolinguistic studies
- **How to use:** Note in Cultural Insight that younger Ukrainians often skip чи in casual conversation, but it's safer for learners to use it initially

## Negation Simplicity

### Single Particle не
- **Fact:** Ukrainian negation uses ONLY не (not) placed directly before the verb. English has complex negation with auxiliaries (don't, doesn't, didn't, won't, can't), but Ukrainian uses one particle for all tenses/persons: не читаю (don't read), не читав (didn't read), не читатиму (won't read).
- **Source:** §4.3.1 State Standard examples
- **How to use:** Highlight that Ukrainian negation is ONE pattern—"не + verb"—versus English's 10+ negative auxiliary forms

## Cultural Hook (1-2 facts)

### Hook 1: Question Intonation Pattern
- **Fact:** Ukrainian yes/no questions without чи use a distinctive RISING intonation on the VERB, not at the end of sentence like English. "Ти ЧИТАЄШ?" (Are you reading?) has the pitch peak on читаєш. This inherited Slavic intonation pattern contrasts with English's sentence-final rise.
- **Source:** Ukrainian phonetics textbooks (Kononenko, 2004)
- **How to use:** Explain intonation pattern in Presentation—Ukrainian rising tone comes EARLIER in the sentence than English speakers expect

### Hook 2: Negative Adverbs ніколи, ніщо
- **Fact:** Ukrainian uses "double negative" constructions that would be considered incorrect in English: "Я ніколи не читаю" (I never not read = I never read). The не is required even though ніколи already means "never." This is a preserved Proto-Slavic feature shared with Russian, Polish, Czech.
- **Source:** Slavic syntax comparative studies
- **How to use:** Preview this pattern briefly (full lesson comes later), noting that "double negatives" are CORRECT and required in Ukrainian, unlike English

## Pedagogical Notes

### Why Questions After Verbs
- **Sequence logic:** M06 taught verb conjugation—now learners can form questions ABOUT actions
- **Functional need:** Can't have conversations without questions
- **State Standard:** Каталог А requires ability to "ставити прості запитання" and "відповідати на прості запитання"

### Challenge for English Speakers
- Must UNLEARN adding "do/does" in questions
- Common error: *"Ти робиш читати?" trying to say "Do you read?"
- Solution: Just verb + question intonation or чи + statement

### Wh-Question Flexibility
- Ukrainian wh-questions don't require inversion: "Де ти живеш?" (Where you live?) not *"Де живеш ти?"
- Subject-verb order stays same as declarative sentences
- Simpler than English: "Where do you live?" (subject-auxiliary inversion required)
