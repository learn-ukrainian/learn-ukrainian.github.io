## Pedagogical Quality

**Lesson Quality Score:** 8/10

**Strengths:**
- Progressive structure: affricates -> iotated vowels -> modifiers -> complete alphabet
- The "dual nature" concept is well-explained: "At the beginning of a word, or after another vowel, these letters represent two sounds... when they appear right after a consonant, they only make their vowel sound, but they soften the consonant."
- Ї exception rule stated clearly: "It ALWAYS makes two sounds, no matter where it stands... It never softens a preceding consonant."
- Soft sign vs apostrophe contrast is elegantly summarized: "the soft sign softens the letter that comes before it, while the apostrophe acts as a wall that blocks softening completely."
- Strong cultural hooks: Ґ repression (1933-1990), Ї as resistance graffiti, Shevchenko poetry
- Bonus texts ("Моє місто", "Моя сім'я") provide meaningful reading practice using the full alphabet
- Self-check questions reinforce the three most critical concepts

**Weaknesses:**
- Iotated vowels section is dense: 5 letters + dual nature rules in one section is heavy cognitive load
- Example sentences are repetitive: "Це X." and "Там X." dominate (13 of ~20 examples)
- No explicit Г vs Ґ sound contrast, though both appear in the content
- The Shevchenko excerpt uses complex vocabulary (плугатарі, хрущі, гудуть) that may overwhelm -- but the instruction says "Do not worry about meaning; just focus on decoding the sounds" which mitigates this

### Image-to-Letter Issues

1. **Door emoji (🚪) for ґанок (porch).** A door is not a porch. Learners seeing 🚪 would think "двері" (door, starts with Д), not "ґанок". The mapping is misleading. **HIGH**
2. **City emoji (🏙️) for центр (center).** A cityscape more naturally suggests "місто" (М), not "центр" (Ц). Requires unintuitive mental leap. **MEDIUM**
3. **Man emoji (👨) for юнак (young man).** Generic man emoji does not clearly convey "young man." A learner might think "чоловік" or "тато". **MEDIUM**
