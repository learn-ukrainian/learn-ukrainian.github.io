# Lesson Content

## warm-up
**The Art of Cynicism**

In Module 11, we learned to love things (Accusative: "I love pizza").
In Module 13, we learned to find things (Locative: "Pizza is on the table").
In Module 14, we claimed things (Possessive: "It is MY pizza").

Now, we face the harsh reality.
Sometimes, there is **no pizza**.
When something is gone, missing, or absent, we use the **Genitive Case**.
Also, when we want "Coffee **without** sugar".
Or when we come **from** Ukraine.

The Genitive is the "OF" case.
- Cup **of** tea.
- Resident **of** Kyiv.
- Absence **of** problems.

### Section 4: Having and Not Having.

Ukrainian doesn't have a simple verb "To Have".
We use a structure: **"By me is [Subject]"**.

### Section 6: Summary of Endings (Singular)

Let's recap the patterns.
1.  **Hard Masculine:** -a (Pasport -> Pasporta).
2.  **Soft Masculine:** -ya (Likar -> Likarya).
3.  **Hard Feminine:** -y (Mama -> Mamy).
4.  **Soft Feminine:** -i (Kukhnya -> Kukhni).
5.  **Neuter:** -a/-ya (Misto -> Mista).

It basically sounds like "A" or "Y/I".
If it sounds like "A", it's probably Masculine or Neuter.
If it sounds like "Y/I", it's probably Feminine.
(Rough heuristic).
