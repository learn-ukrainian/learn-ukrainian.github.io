### Example 1: Family Tree Structure (lines 42-78)
> **Immediate Family**
>
> **Grandparents (Бабуся і Дідусь):**
> - бабуся (grandmother)
> - дідусь (grandfather)
>
> **Parents (Батьки):**
> - мама (mother)
> - тато (father)
> - батько (father, formal)
>
> **Children (Діти):**
> - син (son)
> - донька (daughter)
> - брат (brother)
> - сестра (sister)
>
> **Siblings:**
> - старший брат (older brother)
> - молодша сестра (younger sister)

**Why it works:** Organizes vocabulary by generation hierarchy. Shows three levels (grandparents → parents → children). Includes both formal (батько) and informal (тато) terms. Demonstrates age-marked sibling terms (старший, молодша). Complete family tree framework. Practical for introducing family members.

### Example 3: Possessive Adjectives Table (lines 100-122)
> **Possessive Adjectives**
>
> | Possessive | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
> |------------|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
> | my | мій | моя | моє | мої |
> | your (sg) | твій | твоя | твоє | твої |
> | our | наш | наша | наше | наші |
> | your (pl) | ваш | ваша | ваше | ваші |
> | his | його | його | його | його |
> | her | її | її | її | її |
> | their | їхній | їхня | їхнє | їхні |
>
> **Examples:**
> - **Мій** брат працює. (My brother works.) — masculine
> - **Моя** сестра вчиться. (My sister studies.) — feminine
> - **Мої** батьки живуть у Києві. (My parents live in Kyiv.) — plural

**Why it works:** Complete possessive paradigm. Shows gender agreement for each possessive. Notes його/її don't change (invariable). Provides three examples demonstrating usage. Creates reference table for possessive selection. Connects to adjective agreement from Module 26.

### Example 6: Vocative Case Introduction (lines 146-156)
> **Calling Family Members (Vocative Case)**
>
> When calling someone, Ukrainian uses a special form called the Vocative case:
> - мама → **Мамо!** (Mom!)
> - тато → **Тату!** (Dad!)
> - бабуся → **Бабусю!** (Grandma!)
> - дідусь → **Дідусю!** (Grandpa!)
> - сестра → **Сестро!** (Sister!)
> - брат → **Брате!** (Brother!)
>
> **Note:** This is your first introduction to the Vocative case. You'll learn more at A2!

**Why it works:** Introduces Vocative case naturally with family terms. Shows six common transformations. Provides rule preview (direct address). Notes A2 full coverage (manages expectations). Creates immediate practical competence (calling family members). Pattern evident (о-ending common). Pedagogically sound introduction.

### Example 7: "Only Child" Expression (lines 158-162)
> **Only Child**
> - Я **єдина** дитина. (I'm an only child.) — feminine speaker
> - Я **єдиний** син. (I'm an only son.) — masculine speaker

**Why it works:** Provides essential family structure vocabulary. Shows gender agreement (єдина/єдиний). Teaches useful expression for describing siblings. Explains meaning ("only, sole"). Short, practical, memorable.

### Example 8: Dialogue - Introducing Family (lines 218-236)
> — Розкажи про свою сім'ю.
> — У мене **велика** сім'я. У мене є **мама**, **тато**, **старший брат** і **молодша сестра**.
> — А бабуся і дідусь?
> — Так, вони живуть з нами.
> — Де працює твій **тато**?
> — Він інженер. А **мама** — вчителька.
> — А твій **брат**?
> — Він вже студент. Вчиться в університеті.

**Why it works:** Natural family introduction conversation. Shows possessives (свою сім'ю, твій тато, твій брат). Demonstrates age order terms (старший брат, молодша сестра). Includes multigenerational household (живуть з нами). Shows occupation descriptions (інженер, вчителька, студент). Bilingual translation aids comprehension.

### Example 10: Ukrainian Family Note (lines 356-360)
> **Ukrainian Families**
>
> Ukrainian culture emphasizes close family ties. Multigenerational households are common, with grandparents often living with their children and grandchildren. Ukrainians traditionally use **patronymics** (middle names from father's name) in formal settings: Іван Петрович (Ivan, son of Petro).

**Why it works:** Provides cultural context for family vocabulary. Explains multigenerational household prevalence (relevant for "живуть з нами" phrases). Introduces patronymic tradition (prepares for formal name usage). Shows Ukrainian family values (close ties). Cultural literacy enhances language understanding.
