### Example 2: Greeting Formula (lines 78-88)
> [!observe] Pattern Discovery
>
> Look at the greeting pattern:
> - З **Новим роком**! (Happy New Year!)
> - З **Різдвом**! (Merry Christmas!)
> - З **Днем народження**! (Happy Birthday!)
>
> **Formula:** З + Instrumental case
>
> The preposition **з** (with) + holiday in Instrumental case creates the greeting!

**Why it works:** Reveals systematic greeting formula. Shows three examples demonstrating pattern. Identifies grammatical construction (З + Instrumental). Connects to Instrumental case from earlier learning. Creates productive competence (learners can generate greetings for any holiday). Pattern discovery format encourages observation.

### Example 3: Wish Expressions (lines 90-110)
> **Making Wishes — Бажаю + Genitive**
>
> When wishing someone something, use: **Бажаю** (I wish) + Genitive case
>
> **Common Wishes:**
> - Бажаю **щастя**! (I wish [you] happiness!)
> - Бажаю **здоров'я**! (I wish [you] health!)
> - Бажаю **успіхів**! (I wish [you] success!)
> - Бажаю **любові**! (I wish [you] love!)
> - Бажаю **удачі**! (I wish [you] luck!)
> - Бажаю **миру**! (I wish [you] peace!)
> - Бажаю **добра**! (I wish [you] goodness!)
> - Бажаю **радості**! (I wish [you] joy!)

**Why it works:** Shows wish formula (Бажаю + Genitive). Provides 8 common wishes covering key values (happiness, health, success, love, luck, peace, goodness, joy). Demonstrates Genitive case usage. Creates practical competence for holiday cards and greetings. Shows Ukrainian cultural values through wish vocabulary.

### Example 4: Christmas Date Update (lines 112-116)
> **Christmas — Important Change!**
>
> Historically, Ukraine celebrated Christmas on **January 7** (following the Julian calendar). Since 2023, many Ukrainians have moved to celebrating on **December 25** (Gregorian calendar), aligning with Western Europe. Both dates are still observed by different communities.

**Why it works:** Provides current cultural information. Explains historical practice (January 7, Julian calendar). Notes recent change (2023 to December 25). Shows calendar difference (Julian vs Gregorian). Acknowledges both dates still observed. Prepares learners for variation. Demonstrates Ukrainian cultural shift toward European practices.

### Example 5: New Year Traditions (lines 118-128)
> **New Year Traditions**
>
> - **12 grapes at midnight** — Make a wish with each grape!
> - **Дід Мороз і Снігурочка** (Grandfather Frost and Snow Maiden) — Ukrainian gift-givers
> - **Soviet legacy**: New Year became the main winter holiday during the Soviet era when religious Christmas was suppressed
> - **Champagne toast** at midnight with "З Новим роком!"

**Why it works:** Provides four key traditions. Explains 12 grapes custom (wish-making ritual). Introduces Дід Мороз characters (gift-giving tradition). Notes Soviet historical context (New Year primacy over Christmas). Shows typical celebration (champagne toast). Cultural depth beyond vocabulary. Historical awareness (Soviet influence).

### Example 6: Easter Traditions (lines 130-140)
> **Великдень (Easter) Traditions**
>
> - **Писанки (Pysanky)** — Intricately decorated eggs using wax-resist dyeing technique
> - **Паска** — Special braided Easter bread
> - **Greeting**: "Христос воскрес!" (Christ is risen!) → Response: "Воістину воскрес!" (Truly risen!)
> - **Blessing basket**: Bringing food to church for blessing (eggs, paska, sausage, horseradish)

**Why it works:** Shows four Easter traditions. Explains pysanky art (wax-resist technique). Introduces paska bread (special food). Teaches Easter greeting exchange (call-and-response pattern). Describes blessing basket ritual. Cultural depth (food, art, religious practice). Demonstrates Orthodox traditions.

### Example 7: Independence Day (lines 142-146)
> **День Незалежності (Independence Day) — August 24**
>
> Celebrates Ukraine's independence from the Soviet Union (1991). Many people wear **вишиванки** (traditional embroidered shirts) as a symbol of national pride and cultural identity.

**Why it works:** Provides historical context (1991 independence). Introduces vyshyvanka tradition (embroidered shirts). Shows national identity expression. Connects clothing to patriotism. Cultural literacy about modern Ukraine. Demonstrates post-Soviet national consciousness.

### Example 8: Birthday and Name Day (lines 148-154)
> **День народження (Birthday) and Іменини (Name Day)**
>
> Traditionally, Ukrainians celebrated **іменини** (name day) — the feast day of the saint you're named after. Modern Ukrainians celebrate both birthdays and name days, though birthdays are more common now.

**Why it works:** Explains two celebration types. Defines іменини (saint's feast day). Notes traditional vs modern practice shift. Shows cultural evolution (name days → birthdays). Creates awareness of dual celebration possibility. Orthodox tradition context.
