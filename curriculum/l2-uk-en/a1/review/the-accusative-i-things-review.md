## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Untaught Vocabulary in Activities
- **Location**: Activities File / `fill-in` "Список покупок" & `match-up` "Дія і предмет"
- **Original**: «сир», «ковбаса», «участь», «помилку»
- **Problem**: These words appear in the activities but are **never introduced** in the text or the vocabulary list. A beginner cannot complete the "Shopping List" activity (items 7-8) or the "Match-up" (items 7-8) without guessing or external tools.
- **Fix**: Either add these words to the "Ситуація: У магазині" section or replace them in the activities with taught words (e.g., *хліб*, *вода*, *яблуко*).

### Issue 2: Surprise Verbs in Examples
- **Location**: Section "Практика: Вчимося змінювати закінчення" / Step 4
- **Original**: «Я їм вишню.»
- **Problem**: The verb «їсти» (to eat) / form «їм» is used here for the first time. The "Key Verbs" section only listed *бачити, чути, брати, мати, хотіти, купувати*. This breaks the "safe harbor" promise.
- **Fix**: Change to a taught verb: «Я хочу вишню» or «Я бачу вишню». Or gloss it: «Я їм (I eat) вишню.»

### Issue 3: Low Immersion / Excessive English Fluff
- **Location**: Throughout the module
- **Original**: "Language lives in context. Let's see how these forms appear in real life." (and similar transitions)
- **Problem**: The module is very heavy on English meta-commentary. For A1.2, we aim for 40-60% immersion. Currently, it feels closer to 25%. The content explains *about* the language rather than just *using* it.
- **Fix**: Remove "cheerleader" sentences.
    - Cut: "This is where the main action happens."
    - Cut: "Now for the good news."
    - Cut: "Expressing desire is a primary human need."

### Issue 4: Grammatically awkward English
- **Location**: Section "Культурний погляд"
- **Original**: "Both диня, груша, капуста, and сметана are feminine..."
- **Problem**: "Both" cannot be used with a list of 4 items.
- **Fix**: "The words диня, груша, капуста, and сметана are all feminine..."
