**Gender/case/factual checks:**

1. **"не ділати"** — The module states: «неділя historically means a day when you "do nothing" (не ділати)». The verb **ділати** is archaic/dialectal. The standard modern explanation uses **не діяти** (not to act/do). However, etymologically the word does derive from the Old East Slavic *не дѣлати*, so "не ділати" is defensible as an etymology note. Minor quibble — marking as minor finding below for precision.

2. **"червень — from червоний (red), when the red berries ripen"** — More precisely, червень derives from **червець** (cochineal insect / kermes), not directly from червоний. Both share the root, but the standard etymological explanation references the червець insect (used for red dye) that was harvested in June. This is a factual inaccuracy.

## Findings

**[DIM 2: Linguistic accuracy] [SEVERITY: minor]**
Location: Місяці і пори року section — "**червень** (June) — from **червоний** (red), when the red berries ripen."
Issue: Standard Ukrainian etymology derives червень from **червець** (kermes insect / cochineal), not directly from червоний. Both share the PIE root for "red/worm," but the month is named for the insect harvest, not the color.
Fix: Change to reference червець.

**[DIM 2: Linguistic accuracy] [SEVERITY: minor]**
Location: Дні тижня section — "The word **неділя** historically means a day when you 'do nothing' (не ділати)"
Issue: The verb form **ділати** is non-standard. The etymology is from Old East Slavic *не дѣлати* → modern **не діяти** (not to act). Using "не ділати" could confuse learners looking for this verb.
Fix: Use the standard modern form "не діяти" in the parenthetical.

**[DIM 6: Engagement & tone] [SEVERITY: minor]**
Location: Opening paragraph of Діалоги — "Time shapes our daily routines. Whenever you want to arrange a meeting with a friend, plan a study session, or ask about an upcoming celebration, you rely on the vocabulary of the calendar. Mastering the names of the days and months is an essential step toward full independence in a new language."
Issue: Generic motivational opening. The second sentence is LLM-style filler — it tells the learner why calendars matter, which is obvious. A stronger opening would jump into the situation directly.
Fix: Tighten the opening.

**[DIM 6: Engagement & tone] [SEVERITY: minor]**
Location: Summary final paragraph — "If you can answer all five questions, you have mastered the Ukrainian calendar. You are ready to plan your week, talk about your birthday, and describe the seasons like a native speaker."
Issue: Over-promising. An A1 learner who can name days and months has not "mastered the calendar like a native speaker." This is LLM-style false encouragement.
Fix: Tone down the claim.
