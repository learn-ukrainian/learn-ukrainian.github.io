> [!observe] Pattern Discovery

> Look at these sentences:

### Example 3: Levels of Certainty (lines 78-106)
> **Levels of Certainty**
>
> Different verbs express different levels of commitment:
> - **обов'язково буду** — definitely will
> - **планую** — I plan to
> - **збираюся** — I'm going to
> - **сподіваюся** — I hope to
> - **мрію** — I dream of

**Why it works:** Organizes planning verbs by commitment strength. Shows pragmatic usage (strong intention → planning → hoping → trying). Provides 12+ examples showing each verb in context. Teaches appropriateness, not just grammar. Empowers nuanced expression of future intentions.

### Example 5: Useful Adverbs Section (lines 136-149)
> **вже** (already) — something happened or will happen sooner than expected:
> - Я **вже** вирішив. (I have already decided.)
> - Завтра він **вже** буде тут. (Tomorrow he will already be here.)
>
> **ще** (still/yet) — something continues or hasn't happened:
> - Я **ще** не знаю. (I don't know yet.)
> - Вона **ще** буде працювати. (She will still be working.)
>
> **ніколи** (never) — negation of future:
> - Я **ніколи** не забуду. (I will never forget.)

**Why it works:** Explains nuanced adverbs with clear definitions. Provides two examples for each showing different contexts. Shows вже with both past and future. Demonstrates ще with negation (ще не). Teaches sentence-level fluency, not just vocabulary.

### Example 6: Dialogue - Weekend Plans (lines 184-196)
> — Що ти **будеш** робити **завтра**?
> — **Завтра** я **буду** прибирати квартиру. А **потім** я **планую** піти в кіно.
> — Добре! А що **післязавтра**?
> — **Післязавтра** я **збираюся** відвідати бабусю.
> — Чудово! Я **сподіваюся**, погода **буде** гарна.

**Why it works:** Natural casual conversation. Uses three different planning expressions (буду, планую, збираюся, сподіваюся). Shows three time markers (завтра, потім, післязавтра). Demonstrates question formation (Що ти будеш робити?). Bilingual translation aids comprehension.

**Why it works:** Connects grammar to Ukrainian culture and resilience. Shows future tense used for hope/optimism. References famous anthem known to Ukrainians. Creates emotional connection to grammatical structure.

### Example 8: Two Futures Insight (lines 225-227)
> **Two Futures**
>
> Ukrainian actually has two ways to express future! The compound future (буду + infinitive) we learned today is for ongoing/repeated actions. For single completed actions, Ukrainian uses the perfective future (which looks like present tense of perfective verbs). You'll master both as you progress!

**Why it works:** Previews aspect system without overwhelming. Explains compound future usage scope (ongoing/repeated). Mentions perfective future existence. Reassures learners ("You'll master both as you progress"). Prevents confusion when encountering perfective forms later.

**Why it works:** Provides immediately practical phrases. Shows shortened appointment language ("Я буду о третій"). Demonstrates perfective future in natural context (Зателефоную, Побачимось). Teaches functional competence for scheduling.
