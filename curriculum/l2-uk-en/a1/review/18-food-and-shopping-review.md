## Pedagogical Highlights

### Example 1: Grammar Integration Table (lines 82-88)
> | Case | Usage | Example |
> |------|-------|---------|
> | **Nominative** | Subject | **Я** візьму каву. (I'll take coffee.) |
> | **Accusative** | Direct object | Я візьму **каву**. (I'll take coffee.) |
> | **Genitive** | Quantity | Склянку **води**, будь ласка. (A glass of water, please.) |
> | **Locative** | Location | Ми в **кафе**. (We're at the café.) |

**Why it works:** Shows all three cases in one sentence context. Demonstrates how cases work together in real communication. Reinforces M11-16 learning through practical application.

### Example 3: Mini-Dialogue at Store (lines 159-168)
> — Добрий день! Скільки коштує **хліб**? (Good day! How much is the bread?)
> — 25 гривень. (25 hryvnias.)
> — Добре. Ще **молоко** та **сир**. (Good. Also milk and cheese.)
> — Разом 120 гривень. (120 hryvnias total.)

**Why it works:** Natural flow, realistic prices. Uses accusative (хліб, молоко, сир unchanged). Shows разом (total) usage. Includes cultural tip: greet salesperson even if not buying.

### Example 4: Food as Identity (lines 281-285)
> For Ukrainians, food is deeply connected to **identity** and **home**. Борщ isn't just soup—it's a symbol of family, tradition, and survival. During the Holodomor (1932-33 famine-genocide), millions of Ukrainians starved while grain was exported. Today, every bowl of borsch carries that historical memory.
>
> When you order Ukrainian food, you're participating in a living tradition that survived empires, wars, and attempts to erase it.

**Why it works:** Elevates food from vocabulary to cultural identity. Explains Holodomor context with appropriate gravity. Shows food = resistance and survival. Prepares learners for cultural depth behind simple menu items.

---

### Example 5: UNESCO Borsch Recognition (line 248)
> During Soviet times, Ukrainian dishes were often rebranded as "Russian," but they originated in Ukrainian villages and Cossack kitchens. Today, UNESCO recognizes Ukrainian **borsch culture** as endangered heritage (2022), protecting its authenticity.

**Why it works:** Addresses historical erasure directly. Shows UNESCO recognition as recent (2022). Connects food to cultural preservation. Empowers learners to recognize Ukrainian vs Russian claims.

---

### Example 6: Bread as Sacred (lines 297-299)
> **Folklore Corner: Bread as Sacred**
>
> In Ukrainian tradition, **хліб** (bread) is sacred. Guests are greeted with **хліб-сіль** (bread and salt) as a sign of hospitality. Dropping bread on the floor requires picking it up and kissing it as an apology. This reverence comes from centuries of agricultural culture where grain was life itself.

**Why it works:** Reveals deep cultural practice. Explains хліб-сіль tradition. Shows bread = sacred, not just food. Connects to agricultural heritage. Prevents cultural faux pas (dropping bread).

### Example 7: Common Mistakes Section (lines 216-221)
> **Поширені помилки (Common mistakes):**
> - ❌ "Я хочу кава" → ✅ "Я хочу **каву**" (знахідний відмінок! / accusative!)
> - ❌ "Скільки є хліб?" → ✅ "Скільки **коштує** хліб?" (використовуйте "коштує" для ціни / use "коштує" for price)
> - ❌ "Одна вода" → ✅ "Склянку **води**" (родовий відмінок для кількості / genitive for quantities)

**Why it works:** Shows exact errors with corrections. Provides bilingual explanations. Prevents common A1 mistakes. Reinforces case usage rules.
