## Pedagogical Highlights

### Example 1: могти vs вміти Pattern Discovery (lines 56-63)
> [!observe] Pattern Discovery
>
> Look at these sentences:
> - Я **можу** говорити українською. (I can speak Ukrainian.) — general ability
> - Я **вмію** плавати. (I know how to swim.) — learned skill
>
> **могти** = physical/circumstantial ability
> **вміти** = acquired skill or knowledge

**Why it works:** Shows critical distinction many learners confuse. Provides clear rule (physical/circumstantial vs skill). Two examples demonstrate difference. Pattern discovery encourages observation before explicit teaching. This prevents common errors.

---

### Example 2: могти Conjugation Table (lines 66-74)
> | Person | могти | Example |
> |--------|-------|---------|
> | я | можу | Я можу бігти. (I can run.) |
> | ти | можеш | Ти можеш допомогти? (Can you help?) |
> | він/вона | може | Він може прийти. (He can come.) |
> | ми | можемо | Ми можемо зробити це. (We can do this.) |
> | ви | можете | Ви можете почекати? (Can you wait?) |
> | вони | можуть | Вони можуть грати. (They can play.) |

**Why it works:** Complete conjugation paradigm. Shows all six persons systematically. Examples demonstrate practical usage. Includes questions (Ти можеш...? Ви можете...?). Empowers learners to conjugate for any subject.

### Example 3: повинен Gender Agreement (lines 97-106)
> **повинен/повинна — Must (Personal)**
>
> This is an adjective that agrees in gender:
>
> | Gender | Form | Example |
> |--------|------|---------|
> | masculine | повинен | Він **повинен** вчитися. (He must study.) |
> | feminine | повинна | Вона **повинна** працювати. (She must work.) |
> | neuter | повинне | — (rare) |
> | plural | повинні | Вони **повинні** прийти. (They must come.) |

**Why it works:** Explains повинен as adjective (not verb). Shows gender agreement explicitly. Marks neuter as rare (saves learning time). Provides examples for each form. Prevents error of using повинен with female subjects.

---

### Example 4: Impersonal Modals (lines 108-114)
> **треба / потрібно — Need To (Impersonal)**
>
> These are impersonal — the "subject" is optional:
>
> - **Треба** працювати. (One needs to work. / It's necessary to work.)
> - **Потрібно** вчитися. (One needs to study.)
> - **Необхідно** відповісти. (It's essential to answer.)

**Why it works:** Explains impersonal construction (subject optional). Shows three synonyms with formality levels (треба = casual, необхідно = formal). Provides double translation ("one needs" vs "it's necessary"). Teaches grammatically distinct modal type.

---

### Example 5: можна Permission System (lines 131-137)
> [!observe] Pattern Discovery
>
> - **Можна** увійти? (May I enter?)
> - **Не можна** палити тут. (Smoking is not allowed here.)
>
> **можна** = allowed, permitted
> **не можна** = not allowed, forbidden

**Why it works:** Shows можна dual usage (requesting permission + stating permission). Demonstrates negation (не можна). Provides clear English equivalents. Pattern discovery format encourages observation. Essential pragmatic skill for asking permission politely.

---

### Example 6: Swimmer Analogy (lines 267-269)
> **могти vs вміти**
>
> Remember: **могти** is about possibility ("I can do it now"), while **вміти** is about skill ("I know how to do it"). A swimmer might say "Я вмію плавати" (I know how to swim) but "Я не можу плавати сьогодні — вода холодна!" (I can't swim today — the water is cold!)

**Why it works:** Perfect real-world analogy. Shows skill (вмію плавати) exists independently of circumstance (не можу today). Creates memorable scenario (cold water preventing swimming). Prevents common learner confusion. Teaches pragmatic distinction, not just translation.

### Example 7: Trying Verbs Section (lines 157-165)
> | Verb | Meaning | Example |
> |------|---------|---------|
> | намагатися | to try, attempt | Я **намагаюся** зрозуміти. (I'm trying to understand.) |
> | старатися | to try hard | Він **старається** вчитися. (He tries hard to study.) |
> | пробувати | to try, taste | **Спробуй** цю страву! (Try this dish!) |
> | вдаватися | to succeed | Це не **вдається**. (This isn't working out.) |
> | встигати | to manage (in time) | Я не **встигаю**! (I don't have time!) |

**Why it works:** Expands beyond basic modals to attempt/success expressions. Shows five verbs with nuanced meanings. пробувати dual meaning (try/taste) noted. вдаватися teaches success/failure expression. встигати introduces time management concept. Builds pragmatic competence.

### Example 8: Modal Adjectives Table (lines 169-177)
> | Adjective | Meaning | Example |
> |-----------|---------|---------|
> | здатний/здатна | capable | Він **здатний** зробити це. (He is capable of doing this.) |
> | готовий/готова | ready | Я **готовий** почати. (I'm ready to start.) |
> | згодний/згодна | willing, agree | Вона **згодна** допомогти. (She agrees to help.) |
> | впевнений/впевнена | sure | Я **впевнений**. (I'm sure.) |
> | певний/певна | certain | Він **певний** у своєму рішенні. (He's certain of his decision.) |

**Why it works:** Teaches modal meaning through adjectives. Shows gender forms for each (m/f). Five essential adjectives for expressing readiness/capability/certainty. Examples demonstrate usage patterns. Expands expressive range beyond verb modals.

### Example 9: Dialogue - Asking Permission (lines 219-233)
> — **Можна** увійти?
> — Так, **можна**. Заходь!
> — Дякую. **Можна** сісти тут?
> — Звичайно. Сідай.
> — А **можна** відкрити вікно? Тут тепло.
> — Так, будь ласка.

**Why it works:** Natural permission-seeking sequence. Shows можна used three times (enter, sit, open window). Demonstrates polite acceptance (Так, можна / Звичайно / Так, будь ласка). Teaches imperative responses (Заходь! Сідай.). Realistic social interaction. Bilingual translation aids comprehension.

### Example 10: Regional Variation Note (lines 275-277)
> **Regional Variation**
>
> **мусити** is more common in Western Ukraine and formal contexts. In everyday speech, **повинен** or **треба** are more typical in central Ukraine.

**Why it works:** Teaches sociolinguistic awareness. Explains мусити regional distribution (West + formal). Shows повинен/треба as central Ukraine preference. Prepares learners for dialectal variation. Demonstrates language geography knowledge.

---

### Example 11: Permission Signs Note (lines 271-273)
> **Permission in Practice**
>
> In Ukraine, you'll often see signs with **заборонено** (forbidden): "Палити заборонено" (Smoking forbidden), "Вхід заборонено" (Entry forbidden). For polite requests, use "Можна...?" — it's universal!

**Why it works:** Prepares for real signs learners will see. Shows typical заборонено phrases. Reinforces Можна...? as universal polite request. Teaches receptive and productive competence. Practical cultural knowledge.
