## Pedagogical Highlights

### Example 1: Class II Pattern Table (lines 56-72)
> **Conjugation formula:** Drop **-ити/-іти**, add these endings:
>
> | Person | Singular | Plural |
> |--------|----------|--------|
> | 1st (I, we) | **-ю** / **-у** | **-имо** |
> | 2nd (you) | **-иш** | **-ите** |
> | 3rd (he/she/they) | **-ить** | **-ять** / **-ать** |

**Why it works:** Tabular format, systematic, shows both singular and plural, sets up говорити example.

### Example 3: Consonant Mutation Explanation (lines 179-188)
> Notice how Class II verbs change their stem slightly:
> - **робити** → роб**лю** (not "робю")
> - **ходити** → ход**жу** (not "ходю")
> - **любити** → люб**лю** (not "любю")
>
> This is called **consonant mutation** — certain letters change before the **-ю** ending. You'll pick up these patterns naturally as you practice!

**Why it works:** Names the phenomenon, gives rule (consonant changes before -ю), reassures learners they'll acquire it naturally.

---

### Example 4: Cultural Insight on Verb Precision (lines 212-223)
> Ukrainian verbs are incredibly precise. Notice how **ходити** (to walk/go on foot) and **їздити** (to ride/go by vehicle) are two different verbs? In English, we just say "go," but Ukrainian forces you to think: *How are you traveling?*
>
> This precision extends to posture verbs too:
> - **сидіти** (to sit) — you're seated
> - **стояти** (to stand) — you're standing
> - **лежати** (to lie) — you're lying down

**Why it works:** Shows Ukrainian's semantic precision, contrasts with English, gives practical examples (Де ти? — Я сиджу в кафе).

---

### Example 5: Common Mistake Box (lines 189-195)
> **Common Mistake**
> Don't confuse **Class I** and **Class II** endings! Class I uses **-аю/-ають**, Class II uses **-ю/-ять**. Compare:
> - **читати** (Class I): читаЮ, читаЄш, читаЄ, читаЮТЬ
> - **говорити** (Class II): говорЮ, говорИШ, говорИТЬ, говорЯТЬ
>
> The vowels are different!

**Why it works:** Side-by-side comparison, highlights vowel differences with capitalization, prevents common learner error.
