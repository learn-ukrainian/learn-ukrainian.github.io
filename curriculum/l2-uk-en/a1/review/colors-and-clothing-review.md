## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Impossible "Wrong/Right" Example Logic
**Location**: `Практичне застосування — Describing Outfits`
**Problem**: The module tries to show a common error but uses the exact same text for both versions: `- **Ця вишиванка — біла й синя.** — Wait — that's wrong! Let's fix it: **Ця вишиванка — біла й синя.**` This is logically broken and deeply confusing for a learner trying to spot the difference.
**Fix**: Introduce an actual grammatical error in the first sentence (e.g., using masculine endings `білий й синій`) so the correction makes sense.

### Issue 2: Fake-out Pedagogical Pattern Ruins Beginner Safety
**Location**: `Презентація кольорів та граматика узгодження (Colors & Grammar of Agreement)`
**Problem**: The text uses a red X and strikethrough for a grammatically correct form: `❌ ~~синя сорочка~~ ... wait, this IS correct! The feminine form is **синя**`. Beginners rely heavily on visual cues (✅/❌); deliberately misleading them, even as a joke, breaks learner confidence and creates cognitive friction.
**Fix**: Remove the fake-out. Show a genuine mistake (like `синій сорочка`) and correct it straightforwardly.

### Issue 3: Grammatically Mismatched and Nonsensical Riddle
**Location**: `Практичне застосування — Describing Outfits` (in the `[!challenge]` block)
**Problem**: The riddle asks: `«Червоне, але не сонце. Жовте, але не лимон. Синє, але не небо.»` and gives the answer as `прапор and a field of poppies!`. First, adjectives in riddles must match the gender of the implied answer—here they are neuter (`Червоне`), but the answers are masculine (`прапор`) and plural (`маки`). Second, mashing a flag and poppies into one guess is semantic word salad.
**Fix**: Rewrite the riddle so the adjective genders logically match a single, coherent answer (e.g., `«Синій, але не океан. Жовтий, але не лимон. Що це? Це прапор!»`).

### Issue 4: Contextual Mismatch in Activity
**Location**: `Activities` (fill-in: "Describe the Outfit")
**Problem**: The activity is explicitly titled "Describe the Outfit", yet it randomly includes an item that is not clothing: `sentence: "Це _____ небо."`
**Fix**: Replace `"небо"` with an actual item of clothing taught in the module, such as `"плаття"`.

### Issue 5: Mischaracterization of "Плаття" (Russicism check)
**Location**: `Одяг — Clothing Vocabulary`
**Problem**: The text claims: `**Плаття** is the everyday, conversational word.` In modern Ukrainian, `сукня` is standard for both everyday and formal use. Teaching `плаття` purely as the "everyday" equivalent without noting it is an older calque/Russian influence (which many modern speakers avoid) is poor pedagogical framing.
**Fix**: Clarify that `сукня` is the standard preferred choice, and `плаття` is a conversational variant with Russian influence that they might still hear.
