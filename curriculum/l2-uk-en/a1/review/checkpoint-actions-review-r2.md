## Findings
[LINGUISTIC ACCURACY] [SEVERITY: critical]  
Location: `Грама́тика (Grammar Summary)` — “in Ukrainian, we say **ста́вити пита́ння** (to ask a question), never the direct translation "задава́ти".”  
Issue: This teaches a false absolute rule. `задавати` exists in standard Ukrainian; the problem here is preference/register, not impossibility.  
Fix: Reword this as a usage preference, e.g. recommend `ставити запитання/питання` or `запитувати`, without saying `задавати` is “never” Ukrainian.

[PLAN ADHERENCE] [SEVERITY: major]  
Location: `Діало́г (Connected Dialogue)` — “The context is a meeting between two friends, Olena and Viktor. Olena is busy with her tasks, but Viktor wants to hang out.”  
Issue: The plan specifies a new-flatmate conversation about daily schedules and chores. The generated dialogue changes the situation entirely.  
Fix: Rewrite the dialogue as a flatmate exchange about who cooks, who studies in the morning, and who can/must do which chore.

[PLAN ADHERENCE] [SEVERITY: major]  
Location: whole module, especially `Що ми зна́ємо?` and `Грама́тика (Grammar Summary)`  
Issue: Section budgets are badly off. The self-check and grammar sections are padded with English exposition instead of staying near the planned 200-word summaries.  
Fix: Cut the meta-introductory English paragraphs and keep each section close to the planned scope with short explanations plus examples.

[PEDAGOGICAL QUALITY] [SEVERITY: major]  
Location: `Чита́ння (Reading Practice)` — “We can analyze the lexical choices in Pavlo's story.”; `Діало́г (Connected Dialogue)` — “Several pedagogical mechanics are at play in this conversation.”  
Issue: The module spends too much time explaining the exercise in English after the learner has already seen it. For an A1 checkpoint, that is teacher-talk bloat rather than practice-oriented review.  
Fix: Replace these analytical paragraphs with either a short task prompt or 1-2 concise takeaways, then spend the space on controlled Ukrainian input/examples.

[VOCABULARY COVERAGE] [SEVERITY: major]  
Location: whole module, especially reading/dialogue prose  
Issue: Recommended action verbs from the plan are underused. Search confirms no occurrences of `готувати`, `прибирати`, `допомагати`, `танцювати`, `співати`, `грати`, or `писати`.  
Fix: Integrate several of these verbs naturally into the reading and the flatmate dialogue so the checkpoint reviews a broader slice of A1.3 action vocabulary.

[PLAN ADHERENCE] [SEVERITY: major]  
Location: `Діало́г (Connected Dialogue)` — actual questions are “Що ти ро́биш?”, “Коли ти ві́льний?”, “Де ми зустріча́ємося?”  
Issue: The module names all seven question words in grammar, but connected usage only models `що`, `коли`, and `де`. `хто`, `куди`, `чому`, and `як` are not practiced in dialogue.  
Fix: Expand the dialogue or self-check examples so all seven question words appear in actual questions, not just in a list.

[ENGAGEMENT & TONE] [SEVERITY: minor]  
Location: `Підсумок — Summary` — “You have officially reached the end…”, “This is a massive step forward…”, “Keep practicing your verbs…”  
Issue: These lines add encouragement but not much instructional value, and they push the module further into filler.  
Fix: Trim the congratulatory phrasing and replace it with a concrete recap of what the learner can now say and ask.

## Verdict: REJECT
The module has one critical language-teaching error and multiple major plan/pedagogical failures. The dialogue scenario is wrong, the section proportions are far off the plan, and too much of the word count is spent on English meta-commentary instead of checkpoint content. This needs a rebuild of the reading, grammar, and dialogue sections, not a light revision.
