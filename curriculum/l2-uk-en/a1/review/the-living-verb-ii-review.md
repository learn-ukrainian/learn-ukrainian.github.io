## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Missing Spelling Rule for "бачити"
- **Location**: Section "Велика різниця: Тип 1 і Тип 2", Line 110
- **Original**: «This is a reliable rule of thumb: Type 1 loves **Ю**, Type 2 loves **Я**.»
- **Problem**: The verb **бачити** is a key verb in this lesson, but its "they" form is **вони бачать** (ending in **-ать**), not **-ять**, because of the spelling rule after **ч**. A student following the rule blindly will write **бачять** (error).
- **Fix**: Add a `[!note]` or brief mention: "Note: After **ч**, **ж**, **ш**, we write **-ать** instead of **-ять** (e.g., **вони бачать**)."

### Issue 2: Ghost Word "йду" in Dialogue
- **Location**: Section "Простий діалог", Line 275
- **Original**: «**Андрій:** Привіт! Я йду в кафе. Я хочу їсти.»
- **Problem**: The verb **йти** (to go, unidirectional) has not been introduced, only **ходити** (multidirectional/habitual). **Йду** looks like a Type 1 verb (ends in -у), but its infinitive isn't clear to the learner. Using it without explanation invites confusion with **ходити**.
- **Fix**: Add a gloss or parenthesis: `(I am going [now])` or change to `Я хочу йти в кафе` (I want to go to a cafe - uses infinitive, though `йти` is still new). Better: Keep `йду` but add a small margin note or gloss that it means "going right now".

### Issue 3: Misleading Linguistic Metaphor
- **Location**: Section "Вступ: Світ дієслів", Line 38
- **Original**: «While **читати** (to read) and **слухати** (to listen) are passive and receptive, the core verbs of the Second Conjugation often describe fundamental human interactions...»
- **Problem**: Linguistically, **слухати** (to listen) is active (vs **чути** - to hear). **Бачити** (to see) is often stative/receptive. Labeling Type 1 as "passive" and Type 2 as "Doers" is a false dichotomy that might confuse learners about aspect later.
- **Fix**: Rephrase to focus on the "sound" or "vibe" rather than semantic passivity. "Think of these as the 'Action Family'..." (without demoting Type 1 to 'passive').
