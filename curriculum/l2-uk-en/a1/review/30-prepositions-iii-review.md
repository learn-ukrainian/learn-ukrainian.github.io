## Pedagogical Highlights

### Example 1: Three-Question Framework (lines 42-54)
> [!observe] Pattern Discovery
>
> Ukrainian organizes prepositions around three key questions:
>
> - **Де?** (Where?) — location
>   - Я в **парку**. (I'm in the park.)
>
> - **Куди?** (Where to?) — direction/destination
>   - Я йду в **парк**. (I'm going to the park.)
>
> - **Звідки?** (Where from?) — origin
>   - Я йду з **парку**. (I'm coming from the park.)

**Why it works:** Presents organizing principle for entire preposition system. Shows three questions with examples using same noun (парк in three cases). Demonstrates case changes based on question type. Creates framework for understanding spatial expressions. Pattern discovery format encourages observation before rules.

### Example 3: Location Prepositions - Де? (lines 68-92)
> **Location — Де? (Where?) + Locative Case**
>
> - **в/у** (in) — Я в **парку**. (I'm in the park.)
> - **на** (on, at) — Книга на **столі**. (The book is on the table.)
> - **біля** (near) — Він біля **будинку**. (He's near the house.)
> - **під** (under) — Кіт під **столом**. (The cat is under the table.)
> - **над** (above) — Лампа над **столом**. (The lamp is above the table.)
> - **за** (behind) — Стілець за **дверима**. (The chair is behind the door.)
> - **перед** (in front of) — Я перед **будинком**. (I'm in front of the house.)
> - **між** (between) — Він між **нами**. (He's between us.)

**Why it works:** Shows eight location prepositions systematically. Each with Locative case example. Covers all spatial relationships (in, on, near, under, above, behind, in front, between). Creates complete location description framework. Examples use diverse nouns demonstrating Locative endings.

### Example 4: Direction Prepositions - Куди? (lines 94-118)
> **Direction — Куди? (Where to?) + Accusative Case**
>
> - **в/у** (to, into) — Я йду в **парк**. (I'm going to the park.)
> - **на** (to, onto) — Він йде на **роботу**. (He's going to work.)
> - **до** (to, towards) — Вона йде до **школи**. (She's going to school.)
> - **за** (behind, beyond) — Ми йдемо за **будинок**. (We're going behind the house.)
> - **під** (under) — Собака біжить під **стіл**. (The dog is running under the table.)
> - **через** (across, through) — Я йду через **вулицю**. (I'm going across the street.)
> - **додому** (home) — Я йду **додому**. (I'm going home.) — special adverb

**Why it works:** Shows seven direction prepositions with Accusative case. Each example uses motion verb (йду, йде, біжить). Demonstrates в/на usage for destinations. Includes через for crossing/passing through. Notes додому as special adverb (doesn't require case). Creates complete motion description framework.

### Example 5: Origin Prepositions - Звідки? (lines 120-140)
> **Origin — Звідки? (Where from?) + Genitive Case**
>
> - **з/із/зі** (from, out of) — Я з **України**. (I'm from Ukraine.)
> - **від** (from) — Я йду від **лікаря**. (I'm coming from the doctor.)
> - **з-за** (from behind) — Кіт вийшов з-за **дивана**. (The cat came out from behind the sofa.)
> - **з-під** (from under) — Він дістав книгу з-під **столу**. (He got the book from under the table.)
> - **додому→з дому** (from home) — Я їду **з дому**. (I'm leaving from home.)

**Why it works:** Shows five origin prepositions with Genitive case. Demonstrates з variants (з/із/зі based on following sound). Includes compound prepositions (з-за, з-під). Explains від usage (from person/place). Notes додому→з дому transformation. Complete origin expression framework.

---

### Example 6: з vs від Distinction (lines 142-148)
> **з vs від — Important Difference**
>
> - **з** (from) — origin from inside or surface
>   - Я з **парку**. (I'm from the park.) — was inside
>   - Я з **України**. (I'm from Ukraine.) — origin country
>
> - **від** (from) — origin from person or away from something
>   - Я йду від **лікаря**. (I'm coming from the doctor.) — from person
>   - Він від **будинку**. (He's away from the house.) — distance

**Why it works:** Explains confusing distinction clearly. Shows з = from inside/surface with two examples. Shows від = from person/away from with two examples. Prevents common error (mixing з and від). Teaches appropriateness based on spatial relationship.

### Example 8: Spatial Opposites (lines 179-185)
> **Preposition Pairs**
>
> Some prepositions form natural pairs:
> - в (into) ↔ з (out of)
> - на (onto) ↔ з (off of)
> - до (towards) ↔ від (away from)
> - під (under going) ↔ з-під (from under)
> - за (behind going) ↔ з-за (from behind)

**Why it works:** Shows directional opposites. Five pairs demonstrate motion-reversal relationships. Helps learners understand preposition logic. Creates memorable associations. Demonstrates systematic thinking in Ukrainian spatial expressions.

### Example 9: Dialogue - Asking Directions (lines 235-251)
> — Вибачте, **де** тут магазин?
> — Магазин **біля** парку. Ви йдете **до** парку?
> — Так, я йду **в** парк.
> — Добре. Магазин **перед** парком, **з** лівого боку.
> — **З** якого боку?
> — **З** лівого. Не можна помилитися!
> — Дуже дякую!

**Why it works:** Natural directions conversation. Uses five prepositions (де біля, до, в, перед, з). Shows Де? question (де тут магазин?). Demonstrates direction giving with location prepositions. Includes clarification request (З якого боку?). Bilingual translation aids comprehension.
