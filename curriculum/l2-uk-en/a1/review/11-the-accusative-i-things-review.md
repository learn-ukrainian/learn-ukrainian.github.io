## Pedagogical Highlights

### Example 1: Key Insight Box (line 63)
> **Key insight:** Only feminine nouns ending in -а/-я change their endings in accusative. Masculine inanimate and neuter nouns stay the same!

**Why it works:** Reduces cognitive load. Learners can ignore masculine/neuter and focus on feminine -а → -у pattern.

---

### Example 2: Mini-Dialogue at Post Office (lines 110-116)
> — Добрий день! Я хочу відправити **посилку**.
> — Будь ласка. Покажіть **паспорт**.
> — Ось, будь ласка. Я також маю **лист**.
> — Добре. Ви берете **конверт**?
> — Так, дайте один **конверт**, будь ласка.

**Why it works:** Natural flow, uses taught vocabulary (посилка, паспорт, лист, конверт), shows accusative in real post office scenario. Note mix of feminine changing (посилку) and masculine unchanging (паспорт, лист, конверт).

---

### Example 3: Gift-Giving Cultural Insight (lines 203-206)
> When giving gifts in Ukraine, there are unwritten rules:
> - Always use **both hands** when giving or receiving
> - Flowers are common, but remember: **odd numbers only** (3, 5, 7...) for celebrations. Even numbers are for funerals.
> - When you say **"Я даю подарунок"**, you're participating in a deeply cultural act.

**Why it works:** Provides practical etiquette guidance, prevents cultural faux pas (even numbers at birthday = funeral flowers), connects grammar to lived experience.

---

### Example 4: Common Mistakes Section (lines 141-149)
> **❌ Wrong:** Я бачу книга.
> **✅ Right:** Я бачу книгу.
>
> **❌ Wrong:** Вона читає газета.
> **✅ Right:** Вона читає газету.

**Why it works:** Shows most common learner error (forgetting to change feminine nouns), provides correct/incorrect pairs, reinforces pattern with reminder: "Feminine -а/-я nouns ALWAYS change to -у/-ю in accusative!"
