## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: LLM Fingerprint / Structural Monotony
- **Location**: Throughout the module (Intro, Pharmacy, Doctor sections)
- **Original**:
    - Line 8: «Уявіть: ви хворі. Ви у лікаря. Треба знати слова.»
    - Line 67: «Уявіть: ви гуляєте. Ви бачите знак. Ви йдете всередину.»
    - Line 95: «Отже, ви знаєте симптоми. Час діяти. Українці хворіють.»
    - Line 146: «Ви знаєте симптоми. У вас є ліки. А як щодо роботи?»
- **Problem**: Extremely repetitive, staccato sentence structure (Short sentence. Short sentence. Short sentence.). It sounds like a robotic tutor and creates a monotonous reading experience.
- **Fix**: Rewrite these introductions to be more natural and varied. Use connecting words and varied sentence lengths. E.g., "When you get sick in Ukraine, you will likely need to visit a pharmacy."

### Issue 2: Plan Deviation (Missing Grammar)
- **Location**: Section "В аптеці та у лікаря: Лікування"
- **Original**: «Лікар каже: ми відпочиваємо.» / «Advice: Лежіть. Пийте багато води.»
- **Problem**: The Plan explicitly required: "Grammar connection: Using 'треба' (need) + infinitive for advice ('Треба пити чай')". The text uses Imperatives instead. The learner is not taught the "Треба + Inf" construction as planned.
- **Fix**: Add a dedicated explanation block for "Giving Advice with Треба". Change examples to: «Треба пити багато води», «Треба лежати».

### Issue 3: Overwhelming Word Count
- **Location**: Entire Module
- **Original**: Word Count 2112
- **Problem**: The target is 820 words. The module is 2.5x the target size. For A1 students, wading through 2000 words of text (even with English support) is exhausting and discourages "Quick Wins".
- **Fix**: Cut the "Imagine..." narrative setups significantly. condense English explanations. Aim for <1200 words.

### Issue 4: Stereotypical/Odd Phrasing
- **Location**: Line 146 / Section "Практика"
- **Original**: «Українці відповідальні. Ми хворі? Ми телефонуємо на роботу.»
- **Problem**: This is a weird cultural generalization. Calling work when sick is standard employment behavior, not a specific trait of "responsible Ukrainians". It feels artificial.
- **Fix**: Remove the generalization. Just say: "When you are sick and cannot go to work, you need to call your manager."
