## Findings
[LINGUISTIC ACCURACY] [SEVERITY: critical]  
Location: `Хотіти (To Want)` — “The second pattern connects desire directly to a noun instead of an action. The noun automatically takes the accusative case ending.”  
Issue: This teaches the wrong rule. Ukrainian nouns take the accusative case here, but they do not always show a new ending; `кава → каву` changes, while inanimate masculine nouns like `борщ` and `чай` usually stay formally unchanged.  
Fix: Replace “accusative case ending” with “accusative case,” and explicitly note that some nouns change form and some do not.

[LINGUISTIC ACCURACY] [SEVERITY: critical]  
Location: `Могти і мусити (Can and Must)` — “It signifies that you have to do something immediately, rather than it being a mild recommendation.”  
Issue: This assigns an incorrect meaning to `мусити`. The verb expresses strong obligation or necessity, not immediate timing.  
Fix: Replace “immediately” with “as a strong obligation or necessity.”
