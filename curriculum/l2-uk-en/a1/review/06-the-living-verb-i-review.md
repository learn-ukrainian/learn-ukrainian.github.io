## Pedagogical Highlights

### Example 1: Pattern Presentation (lines 60-79)
> **The Pattern: -ати → -аю, -аєш, -ає...**
>
> Class I verbs are verbs whose infinitive (dictionary form) ends in **-ати** or **-яти**. To conjugate them in the present tense, we:
>
> 1. Drop the **-ти** ending
> 2. Add personal endings based on **who** is doing the action

**Why it works:** Simple 2-step rule, no jargon, concrete example follows immediately.

---

### Example 2: Fun Fact on Pronoun Dropping (lines 80-82)
> In Ukrainian, you often **drop the pronoun** because the verb ending already tells you who's doing the action! So instead of "Я читаю", Ukrainians often just say "Читаю" — the ending **-ю** makes it clear it's "I". This makes Ukrainian sound efficient and rhythmic.

**Why it works:** Explains a key Ukrainian feature (pro-drop), gives learners confidence to understand pronoun-less sentences they'll encounter.

---

### Example 3: Daily Life Context (lines 112-133)
> **Ранок (Morning):**
> - Я **снідаю**. (I have breakfast.)
> - Ти **починаєш** роботу. (You start work.)
>
> **День (Day):**
> - Вона **працює**. (She works.)
> - Ми **обідаємо**. (We have lunch.)

**Why it works:** Shows verbs in real daily routine, not isolated sentences. Learners see practical application immediately.

---

### Example 4: Lviv Café Cultural Insight (lines 134-136)
> Walk into any Ukrainian café in Lviv and you might hear: "Ти **працюєш** сьогодні?" (Are you working today?). The barista asks this to decide if they'll pour you a strong espresso or a relaxing cappuccino. One verb — працюєш — and they know your whole vibe.

**Why it works:** Specific, vivid scene. Makes grammar memorable through cultural context. Shows how one verb carries social meaning.
