## Findings

```
[PEDAGOGICAL QUALITY] [MAJOR]
Location: Opening of "Діалоги (Dialogues)" section — "In Ukrainian, every noun belongs to a grammatical category called **рід** (gender)... Three different forms of the same word, because three different genders demand them."
Issue: The first ~150 words are an abstract grammar explanation BEFORE the first dialogue. In PPP methodology, the "Present" phase should let the learner encounter the language naturally first, THEN explain the pattern. Front-loading the explanation undermines the discovery moment. The plan explicitly says "Gender emerges naturally through мій стіл (m), моя кімната (f), моє ліжко (n)."
Fix: Move the abstract explanation to AFTER both dialogues. Let the dialogues come first, then the paragraph starting "These are very simple, everyday conversations, but they contain a massive structural secret" serves as the bridge to explicit teaching.
```

```
[ENGAGEMENT & TONE] [MINOR]

Issue: Slightly preachy/motivational — tells the learner what they're achieving rather than letting them experience it. Borders on LLM filler.
Fix: Cut or rephrase to something actionable: "Practice saying these aloud without translating — go straight from the object to the Ukrainian word."
```

```
[PLAN ADHERENCE] [MINOR]

Fix: Change "exactly" to "almost" — "functions almost like a grammatical barcode."
```
