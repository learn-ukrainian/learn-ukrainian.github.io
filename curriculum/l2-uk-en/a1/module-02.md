# The Cyrillic Code II

> **Note:** Now we leave the safe harbor of "Friends". Today we meet the letters that make Ukrainian unique: the buzzing insects (Å½, Å ), the hidden Y's (Ya, Yu), and the ghost that isn't a sound at all (Soft Sign).

## Warm-up: The Insect Zoo
Ukrainian has a rich set of "hushing" sounds. These are separate letters, not combinations like "sh" or "ch". They are efficient. One symbol = one complex sound.

Imagine a zoo of insects:
- **Ğ– (Zh)**: A beetle buzzes. *Zhzhzh*. (Like in "Mea**s**ure").
- **Ğ¨ (Sh)**: A snake hisses. *Shhh*. (Like in "**Sh**oe").
- **Ğ§ (Ch)**: A train chugs. *Ch-ch-ch*. (Like in "**Ch**air").
- **Ğ© (Shch)**: You are frying bacon. A long, rich hiss. It's actually two sounds: Sh + Ch together. (Like in "Fre**sh ch**eese").

## Presentation: The Hidden Y's (Iotation)

These letters are the secret to Ukrainian melody. They soften the consonant before them.

### 3. The Ghost Letter: Soft Sign (Ğ¬)
This letter has **NO SOUND**. It is a modifier. It tells you to pronounce the previous consonant softly.
- **Ğ”** = Hard D.
- **Ğ”ÑŒ** = Soft D (like D in "Dew").
- It's like a musical notation mark.

## Cultural Insight: The Letter G (Ğ“ vs Ò) ğŸ‡ºğŸ‡¦

> ğŸ’¡ **Did You Know?**
>
> Ukrainian has TWO "G" sounds.
> 1. **Ğ“ (He)**: The standard sound. It's a soft, voiced H (like "H" in "Hello" but buzzier). This is the hallmark of Ukrainian speech.
> 2. **Ò (Ge)**: The hard G (like "Go"). It was banned by the Soviets in 1933 to make Ukrainian look more like Russian. It was restored in 1991! Using **Ò** properly is a small act of linguistic independence.
