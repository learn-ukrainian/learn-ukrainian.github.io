## Cultural Hooks
1. "Солов'їна мова" (nightingale language): Ukrainian's reputation for being highly melodic and musical is structurally rooted in its preference for open syllables (ending in vowels), creating a fluid, staccato-free rhythm.
2. Handwriting tradition: Ukrainian schools place heavy emphasis on cursive handwriting (каліграфія), making strict adherence to "переніс" (hyphenation/transfer rules) a deeply ingrained cultural habit for educated native speakers.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Splitting consonant clusters English-style: *сес-тра* instead of the correct Ukrainian *се-стра* (maximal onset principle).
2. Stranding phonetic modifiers: *пал-ьці* or *сім-'я* instead of keeping ь/' with their preceding consonants (*паль-ці*, *сі-м'я*).
3. Leaving a single letter isolated: *У-країна* instead of *Укра-їна* or *Украї-на*.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Contrast Ukrainian's fluid open-syllable preference with English's closed-syllable tendency, or compare its flow to Italian/Spanish. Do NOT use Russian as a comparative baseline.
- English Scaffolding: Since this is A1 M05, the immersion target is 10-15%. Grammar theory (what is a syllable, what is an open/closed syllable, transfer rules) must be explicitly explained in English, using clear Ukrainian examples.
- IPA Usage: Do not use IPA for entire sentences, only for first occurrences of new words.
