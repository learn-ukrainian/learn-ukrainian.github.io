## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Rule Contradiction (Feminine Nouns)
- **Location**: Section "М’який знак і закінчення -ї"
- **Original**: «Sometimes a word ends with ... the letter -я (like кухня). ... Then we cannot write -і. We need another letter ... We use -ї. ... Кухня (kitchen) $\rightarrow$ **на кухні**»
- **Problem**: The text states that words ending in `-я` use `-ї`, but the example correctly shows `кухні` (ending in `-і`). This is confusing. Words ending in a consonant + `я` (like `кухня`, `їдальня`) use `-і`. Only vowel + `я` (like `лінія`) uses `-ї`.
- **Fix**: Correct the rule to state that `-ї` is used for `-й` and `-ія` endings. Move `кухня` to the `-і` category or explain it as a soft stem using `-і`.

### Issue 2: Mnemonic Mismatch ("Hand-Leg-Ear")
- **Location**: Section "Магічна зміна звуків (К, Г, Х)"
- **Original**: «We call this "Hand-Leg-Ear" rule. ... | Парк | Аптека | Книга | Поверх | Дорога | Бібліотека |»
- **Problem**: The rule is named after specific Ukrainian words (рука, нога, вухо) that are completely absent from the examples table. This prevents the learner from making the connection between the rule name and the language.
- **Fix**: Include `рука` (hand), `нога` (leg), and `вухо` (ear) at the top of the table to justify the mnemonic.

### Issue 3: Content Bloat (A1 Threshold)
- **Location**: Global
- **Original**: 2959 words
- **Problem**: The word target is 1331. At 222% of target, this single A1 module is longer than some B2 modules. This violates the A1 "small wins" pedagogical principle and will likely lead to learner fatigue.
- **Fix**: Trim the Practice section (reduce the length of the reading story) and move the "Questions for you" and "Yes or No?" sections to a separate practice module.
