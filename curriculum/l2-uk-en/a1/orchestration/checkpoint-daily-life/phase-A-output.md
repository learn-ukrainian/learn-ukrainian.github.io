# Дослідження: Checkpoint: Daily Life

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "утворення форм минулого часу дієслова: читав, читала, читало, читали... утворення складеної форми майбутнього часу дієслів недоконаного виду: буду читати, будеш сидіти, буде сміятися..."
Alignment: This checkpoint directly tests and consolidates the formation and usage of past and future tenses, which are core requirements for the A1 verb paradigm according to the State Standard.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Frequency / Source | Key collocations |
|------|-------------------|------------------|
| вчора | High (spoken) | вчора вранці (yesterday morning), вчора ввечері (yesterday evening) |
| завтра | High (spoken) | завтра я буду... (tomorrow I will...) |
| ранок | High (general) | доброго ранку, кожного ранку |
| коштувати | Medium (functional) | скільки це коштує? (how much is this?) |
| тепло / холодно | High (spoken) | сьогодні дуже тепло (today is very warm) |

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Daily Meals**: Ukrainians typically have a hot, substantial meal for lunch ("обід") often consisting of "перше" (soup like borscht) and "друге" (main dish), whereas dinner ("вечеря") is usually lighter.
2. **Weather Small Talk**: Discussing the weather ("Сьогодні така гарна погода" / "Сьогодні холодно") is a universal and polite icebreaker in daily Ukrainian interactions.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Gender Agreement in Past Tense**: Forgetting to match the verb ending to the subject's gender (e.g., saying "Вона робив" instead of "Вона робила").
2. **Auxiliary Overuse**: Trying to literally translate English past continuous by adding "бути" before the verb (e.g., "Я був робити" instead of the correct "Я робив").
3. **Direction Cases**: Using Locative instead of Accusative for "Куди?" (e.g., "Я йду на роботі" instead of "Я йду на роботу").

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Scaffolding**: As an A1 module, use English to clearly explain the rules and instructions, but ensure all examples are in Ukrainian.
- **Tone**: Maintain the patient, supportive tutor voice. Frame the checkpoint not as a stressful exam, but as a "let's see how much you've learned" celebration.
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize the simplicity and elegance of the Ukrainian past tense suffix system independently.
- **Structure**: Give each tense and topic its own clear `###` header to prevent cognitive overload.
