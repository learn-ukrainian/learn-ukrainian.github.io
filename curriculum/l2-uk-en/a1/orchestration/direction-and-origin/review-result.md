## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Out-of-Scope Grammar (Imperative)
**Location**: `## До + Родовий (To + Genitive)` -> `> [!cultural-note] **Ukrainian hospitality**`
**Problem**: The text uses the imperative form `Ходіть` (`If someone says **«Ходіть до нас»**`). Imperatives are an advanced topic not taught until M47, violating beginner safety and progression rules as flagged by the pre-screen.
**Fix**: Reword the cultural note to avoid the imperative. E.g., `If someone invites you **до себе** (to their place)...`

### Issue 2: English Calque / Unnatural Phrasing
**Location**: `## Практика (Practice)` -> `> **(У кафе / At a café)**`
**Problem**: The dialog includes `— А звідки ти зараз?` as a translation for "And where are you coming from now?". This is an awkward, literal translation. Native speakers would typically use a verb of motion here.
**Fix**: Change to a more natural phrasing like `— А звідки ти йдеш зараз?`

### Issue 3: Unnatural Motion Verb Usage
**Location**: `## До + Родовий (To + Genitive)` -> `> [!example] **Quick travel phrase**`
**Problem**: The example `Як іти до центру?` uses the imperfective "іти", which is understandable but unnatural for asking directions to a specific destination. Native speakers use the perfective `дійти` or `пройти`.
**Fix**: Change the phrase to `Як дійти до центру?`

### Issue 4: Deterministic Parser Failures (Agreement & Metalanguage)
**Location**: `## Підсумок` and `### До + Genitive for people and places`
**Problem**: The text contains `**до + Родовий відмінок**` which caused a parser agreement failure (`Родовий` vs `до`). Additionally, `до друга` was flagged as a mismatch because `друга` was misparsed as a feminine ordinal. While humans understand it, it breaks deterministic checks.
**Fix**: Standardize metalanguage to English (`**до** + Genitive`). Replace `до друга` with an unambiguous noun like `до мами` or `до брата`.
