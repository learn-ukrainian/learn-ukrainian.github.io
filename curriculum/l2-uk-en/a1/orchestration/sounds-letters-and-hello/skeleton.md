## Звуки і літери — Sounds and Letters (~275 words total)
- P1 (~90 words): Open with the golden rule from Большакова Grade 1 p.24: "Ми чуємо і вимовляємо звуки, а бачимо і пишемо літери." Frame sounds vs letters as hearing vs seeing. Ukrainian has 33 letters (літери) in its alphabet but 38 sounds (звуки) — more sounds than letters, because some letters can represent different sounds. Letters are symbols on paper; sounds are what your mouth makes and your ear hears. Introduce the Ukrainian term абетка (alphabet).
- P2 (~95 words): Голосні (vowels) — made with voice alone, mouth open, no obstruction. Introduce with Большакова's poem: "Голосні почуєш в пісні." Six vowel sounds: [а], [о], [у], [е], [и], [і]. Ten vowel letters: А, О, У, Е, И, І, Я, Ю, Є, Ї. Why more letters than sounds? Because Я, Ю, Є, Ї are special — they can represent two sounds at once (more in M02). For now, just know the six pure vowel sounds. Say each one aloud — feel how air flows freely, no tongue or lip blockage.
- P3 (~90 words): Приголосні (consonants) — made when lips, teeth, or tongue create an obstruction. Voice + noise or noise only. Большакова's poem: "Приголосні деренчать, і тихенько шелестять." 32 consonant sounds from just 22 consonant letters (plus Ь, which has no sound of its own). Try saying [м] — lips close. Try [с] — tongue creates a narrow gap. That obstruction is what makes a consonant. Use the Grade 1 textbook notation: [•] for vowels, [–] for consonants.
- Exercise: **Quiz** — Distinguish between звуки and літери. 6 items: "Що ми чуємо і вимовляємо?" → звуки; "Що ми бачимо і пишемо?" → літери; "Скільки літер в абетці?" → 33; "Скільки звуків в українській мові?" → 38; "Голосні утворюються за допомогою..." → голосу; "Приголосні утворюються за допомогою..." → голосу і шуму.

## Перші слова — First Words (~330 words total)
- P1 (~85 words): Friendly letters — Cyrillic letters that look AND sound similar to Latin ones. Start reading immediately with А [а], О [о], К [к], М [м], Т [т]. Your first Ukrainian words: мама (mother), тато (father), кома (comma), атом (atom), мак (poppy), око (eye). Sound out each letter left to right, blend them. Important pronunciation note: Ukrainian Т is dental (tongue touches upper teeth, not gum ridge like English T), and К and Т are unaspirated (no puff of air after them). Close enough for now — refine later.
- P2 (~100 words): False friend letters — the biggest trap. These letters LOOK like Latin letters but sound completely different. В sounds [в] (like English "v", NOT "b"). Н sounds [н] (like English "n", NOT "h"). Р sounds [р] (a rolled/trilled "r", NOT "p"). С sounds [с] (like English "s", NOT "c/k"). У sounds [у] (like "oo" in "moon", NOT "y"). Х sounds [х] (like "ch" in Scottish "loch", NOT "x"). Practice reading with false friends: вода (water) — start with [в] not [б]; рука (hand) — roll that [р]; сон (dream); ніс (nose); хата (house). Read slowly, catching yourself on each false friend.
- P3 (~90 words): New shapes — letters with no Latin lookalike. Б [б], Г [г], Ґ [ґ], Д [д], Ж [ж], З [з], И [и], Й [й], Л [л], П [п], Ф [ф], Ц [ц], Ч [ч], Ш [ш], Щ [шч]. Meet them through words: банк (bank), дім (home), зима (winter), книга (book), школа (school). Special cases: Щ always makes TWO sounds [шч]. Ь (soft sign) has NO sound — it softens the consonant before it, as in день [ден']. Ї always equals [йі] and is unique to Ukrainian.
- P4 (~55 words): Preview of Я, Ю, Є — these letters have a double life. Sometimes they represent two sounds (я = [йа]), sometimes they soften the consonant before them (ля = [л'а]). Full exploration comes in M02. For now, just recognize them when you see them in words like яблуко, юнак, Європа.
- Exercise: **Match-up** — Match false friend Cyrillic letters to their real Ukrainian sounds. 6 pairs: В ↔ [в], Н ↔ [н], Р ↔ [р], С ↔ [с], У ↔ [у], Х ↔ [х].

## Привіт! — Hello! (~275 words total)
- P1 (~80 words): Your first Ukrainian conversation. Привіт! means "Hi!" — use it with friends, family, classmates, anyone you'd address informally. Follow up with Як справи? (How are you?). Answers: Добре (Fine/Good), Чудово (Great/Wonderful), Нормально (Okay/Not bad). To ask back: А у тебе? (And you?). These phrases are your survival kit for day one in Ukraine.
- Dialogue (~100 words): Model conversation between two friends meeting on the street. Full dialogue with stage directions. Speaker A: "Привіт!" Speaker B: "Привіт! Як справи?" A: "Добре, дякую. А у тебе?" B: "Чудово!" Then a second variation: A: "Привіт!" B: "О, привіт! Як справи?" A: "Нормально. А у тебе?" B: "Добре." Introduce Рада тебе бачити! (female speaker) / Радий тебе бачити! (male speaker) — "Glad to see you!" Note: Ukrainian has gendered forms. Women say рада, men say радий. This is the learner's first encounter with grammatical gender — a major topic starting M08.
- P2 (~95 words): Reading practice with the greeting word Привіт. Break it down letter by letter: П (new shape — no Latin equivalent), р (false friend — rolled, not "p"), и (new vowel — between English "i" and "e"), в (false friend — [в] not "b"), і (vowel — like "ee"), т (familiar shape). Blend into syllables: При-віт. This single word uses letters from ALL three groups you just learned — familiar, false friends, and new shapes. Try saying it aloud. Roll that р, remember в is [в]. Привіт!
- Exercise: **Fill-in** — Complete a greeting dialogue with blanks. 4 blanks in pattern: "— {Привіт}! Як {справи}?" / "— {Добре}. А у {тебе}?" Options: Привіт, справи, Добре, тебе, Чудово, Нормально.

## Читаємо — Reading Practice (~275 words total)
- P1 (~100 words): Environmental reading — signs you would see walking through a Ukrainian city. Sound out each word letter by letter, then blend into syllables: Аптека (pharmacy) — А-пте-ка; Банк (bank) — Банк; Кафе (cafe) — Ка-фе; Метро (metro) — Мет-ро; Пошта (post office) — Пош-та; Школа (school) — Шко-ла; Зупинка (bus stop) — Зу-пин-ка. Notice how many of these words are recognizable — банк, метро, кафе look and sound close to their English equivalents. Others are fully Ukrainian — пошта, зупинка. Either way, you can now READ them in Cyrillic.
- P2 (~80 words): Ukrainian city names — read the names of real places on the map. Київ (Kyiv) — Ки-їв (notice Ї!). Львів (Lviv) — Львів (consonant cluster at the start — three consonants before the vowel). Одеса (Odesa) — О-де-са. Харків (Kharkiv) — Хар-ків (Х is [х], remember). Дніпро (Dnipro) — Дніп-ро. Полтава (Poltava) — Пол-та-ва. You are now reading real Ukrainian geography in Cyrillic.
- P3 (~95 words): First sentences with Це (this is). Це is one of the most useful words in Ukrainian — it means "this is" and lets you identify anything around you. Це мама. (This is mom.) Це банк. (This is a bank.) Це Київ. (This is Kyiv.) Це школа. (This is a school.) To ask: Що це? (What is this?) and Хто це? (Who is this?). Що for things, Хто for people — Ukrainian distinguishes what from who, just like English. Try pointing at objects around you and saying: Це... [word].
- Exercise: **Group-sort** — Sort Cyrillic letters into Голосні (vowels) and Приголосні (consonants). 8+ items to sort. Голосні: А, О, У, Е, И, І. Приголосні: К, М, Т, В, Н, Р, С, Х.

## Підсумок — Summary (~165 words total)
- P1 (~165 words): Recap what you learned. Ukrainian has 33 літери (letters) and 38 звуків (sounds) — you hear and pronounce sounds, you see and write letters. Sounds split into two families: голосні (6 vowel sounds, 10 vowel letters) and приголосні (32 consonant sounds). You met three groups of Cyrillic letters: friendly ones (А, О, К, М, Т), false friends (В, Н, Р, С, У, Х — look familiar but sound different!), and new shapes (Б, Г, Д, Ж, Ш, Щ...). You read your first words — мама, тато, вода, книга, школа — and real Ukrainian signs and city names. You learned to say Привіт!, ask Як справи?, and answer Добре, Чудово, or Нормально. Self-check: How many letters? (33) How many sounds? (38) Why the difference? What are голосні? What are приголосні? Next up in M02: Reading Ukrainian — where we go deeper into how letters combine into syllables.
