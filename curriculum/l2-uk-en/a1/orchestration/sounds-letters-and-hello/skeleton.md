## Звуки і літери (~330 words total)
- P1 (~100 words): [Intro to Phonetics: The Golden Rule] Explain Zabolotnyi’s "Golden Rule" (5th Grade): "Звуки ми чуємо й вимовляємо, а букви бачимо й пишемо." Contrast the physical act of hearing the sound [м] in "мама" versus the visual act of writing the character "М". Establish that sounds are the "living flesh" of the language, while letters are just symbols.
- P2 (~120 words): [The Numbers Game: 33 vs 38] Detail why there are 33 letters but 38 sounds. Introduce the concept that some letters (Я, Ю, Є, Ї) represent two sounds [й+а, etc.], while the soft sign (Ь) represents zero sounds. Address Litvinova’s pedagogical trap: explain why we cannot say "vowel letter" (голосна літера), because vowel/consonant is a property of the sound, not the ink on the page.
- P3 (~110 words): [Phonetic Integrity] Introduce the Ukrainian alphabet (Абетка) and its phonetic nature. Explain that, unlike English with its silent "k" in "know" or multiple sounds for "c", Ukrainian is "what you see is (mostly) what you get." Introduce the names of letters vs. their sounds.

## Голосні звуки (~280 words total)
- P1 (~100 words): [The Music of Vowels] Present Bolshakova’s (1st Grade) poetic definition: "Голосні почуєш в пісні... Легко вимовляються, весело співаються!" Define vowels as sounds made purely with voice, where air passes freely through the mouth. Contrast this with the English "schwa" reduction; explain that Ukrainian vowels remain clear and "musical."
- P2 (~100 words): [The 6 Sounds vs 10 Letters] List the 6 basic vowel sounds: [а], [о], [у], [е], [и], [і]. Mention that while there are 10 vowel letters, the extra four (Я, Ю, Є, Ї) are "iotated" (Й-variants). Focus on the distinctness of [и] (high-mid, retracted) vs [і] (high-front) using examples like "мило" (soap) vs "міло" (to sweep).
- P3 (~80 words): [Visual Models] Introduce Zakhariichuk’s notation: the dot [•] for vowels. Walk through a simple sound analysis of "мА-мА" (two dots) and "мО-лО-кО" (three dots), showing how vowels form the "heart" of every syllable.

## Приголосні звуки (~280 words total)
- P1 (~100 words): [Obstacles and Noise] Present Bolshakova’s "hiss and whistle" concept: "Приголосні деренчать і тихенько шелестять." Define consonants as sounds created by obstacles (lips, teeth, tongue). Challenge the student to try singing [к] or [п] to prove the presence of an obstacle that breaks the musical flow.
- P2 (~90 words): [Hard vs Soft] Introduce the 32 sounds from 22 letters. Explain the concept of hard [–] and soft [=] pairs (e.g., [т] vs [т’]). Note that this distinction doesn't exist in English phonology in the same way. Use "стан" (condition) vs "стань" (stand up!) as a minimal pair to show how softness changes meaning.
- P3 (~90 words): [Specific Ukrainian Sounds] Explain unique phonetic markers: Ґ [g] (plosive) vs Г [ɦ] (frictional/h-like). Explain that Щ always represents two sounds [шч]. Clarify that Ь (м’який знак) has no sound of its own but "melts" the previous consonant.
