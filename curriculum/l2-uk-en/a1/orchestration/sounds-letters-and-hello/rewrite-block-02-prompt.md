## Голосні звуки (Vowel Sounds)

The textbook by Большакова for Grade 1, page 24, teaches vowels through a poem: «Голосні почуєш в пісні, і у темному у лісі, і коли дивуєшся, і коли милуєшся. Легко вимовляються, весело співаються!» A **голосний** (vowel sound) is made with your voice only. The air flows freely through your mouth with no obstruction from your teeth or tongue. You can sing these sounds. You can shout them across a field. Try saying a loud [а] or a long [о]. Your mouth remains open and the sound travels easily.

:::tip
A simple trick to identify a vowel sound is to try singing it. If you can hold the sound indefinitely without any physical obstruction in your mouth, it is a **голосний** (vowel sound).
:::

There are exactly six vowel sounds in Ukrainian: [а], [о], [у], [е], [и], and [і]. However, there are ten vowel letters: А, О, У, Е, И, І, Я, Ю, Є, and Ї. The extra four letters (Я, Ю, Є, Ї) are called iotated vowels because they can represent two sounds simultaneously. Future lessons cover their full details. For now, remember that every Ukrainian word has at least one vowel sound. Vowels form the heart of every syllable. A word cannot exist without a vowel sound.

The textbook by Захарійчук for Grade 1, page 13, uses a specific notation for sound models. Vowel sounds are marked with a circle [•]. You can practice hearing the vowels in simple words. For example, the word **мама** (mother) has two [а] sounds. The word **молоко** (milk) has three [о] sounds. The name Уля has one [у] sound. Your **ніс** (nose) contains one [і] sound. A **сон** (dream) contains one [о] sound. Watch the Anna Ohoiko videos for each vowel letter. Observe the speaker carefully, listen to the pronunciation, and repeat the sounds aloud.
