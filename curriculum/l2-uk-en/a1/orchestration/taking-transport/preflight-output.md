# Taking Transport — Навігація містом

Приві́т! Welcome back to our city adventure. Today, we are stepping away from the ticket office and onto the streets. My name is your **Marshrutka Veteran**, and I’m here to make sure you don't just survive the Ukrainian transit system—you master it. Whether you are descending into the depths of the Kyiv **метро́** or squeeze into a crowded **маршру́тка**, this module will give you the confidence to move through any Ukrainian **місто** (city) like a local.

By the end of this lesson, you’ll be able to:
- Identify different types of **грома́дський тра́нспорт** (public transport).
- Distinguish between a **зупи́нка** (stop) and a **ста́нція** (station).
- Navigate the social "etiquette" of Ukrainian buses.
- Understand metro announcements and ask for directions.

## Вступ (Introduction)

In a big Ukrainian **місто**, life moves fast. People are always on the move, rushing to work or university. But before we jump on a bus, we need to fix one of the most common mistakes English speakers make.

In English, we say "I am going to the library" regardless of whether we are walking or driving. In Ukrainian, the distinction is vital. You already know **іти́** (to walk), but when wheels are involved, you must use the verb **ї́хати** (to go by vehicle).

> [!warning]
> **Learner Error:** **Я йду́ на метро́** means you are walking on top of the metro station building.
> **Correct:** **Я ї́ду на метро́** (I am going by/on the metro).

How do you get to work or school? Let's look at some patterns:

- **Я ї́ду на робо́ту.** — I am going (driving/riding) to work.
- **Ти ї́деш в університе́т?** — Are you going (riding) to the university?
- **Ми ї́демо додо́му.** — We are going (riding) home.
- **Ви ї́дете в центр?** — Are you going (riding) to the center?
- **Вони́ ї́дуть у па́рк.** — They are going (riding) to the park.

> **(At the Metro Entrance / Біля вхо́ду в метро́)**
> — Ви ї́дете в це́нтра?
> — Так, я ї́ду на ста́нцію «Хреща́тик».
> — А як туди́ дої́хати?
> — Це пря́мо, си́ня лі́нія.
> — Дя́кую!
>
> *Gloss:*
> — Are you going to the center?
> — Yes, I am going to the station "Khreshchatyk".
> — And how to get there?
> — It is straight, the blue line.
> — Thanks!

## Презентація (Presentation)

Ukrainian cities offer a variety of ways to get around. You have the heavy hitters: the **авто́бус**, the **троле́йбус**, and the **тра́мвай**. But the king of the city is the **метро́**.

There is a small linguistic trap here. For buses, trams, and trolleys, you look for a **зупи́нка** (stop). However, for the metro or trains, you look for a **ста́нція** (station).

> [!culture] **The Deepest Station in the World**
> The Kyiv Metro is legendary. **Ста́нція** «Арсена́льна» (Arsenalna) is the deepest in the world, sitting 105.5 meters underground. The escalators are so long that people often finish entire podcast episodes or chapters of a book before they reach the platform!

### Grammar: Direction vs. Location

When talking about transport, we use two different cases depending on whether we are moving toward a destination or describing where we currently are.

1. **Куди́?** (Where to? / Direction) + **Accusative**: Used with **ї́хати**.
   - **Я ї́ду на ста́нцію.** (I am going to the station.)
2. **Де?** (Where? / Location) + **Locative**: Used with **бути** (to be) or **чека́ти** (to wait).
   - **Я на ста́нції.** (I am at the station.)
   - **Я чека́ю на зупи́нці.** (I am waiting at the stop.)

### How are you traveling? (The Case Choice)

To say *how* you are traveling, you have two options. Both are correct, but the second one is more common in casual speech.

- **Instrumental Case (No preposition):** **ї́хати авто́бусом**, **ї́хати метро́м**, **ї́хати таксі́**.
- **Locative Case (with «на»):** **ї́хати на авто́бусі**, **ї́хати на метро́**, **ї́хати на трамва́ї**.

> [!note]
> Never say ~~**ї́ду авто́бус**~~. You must use a case ending to show the vehicle is your "instrument" of travel!

- **Авто́бус** → **Я ї́ду авто́бусом.**
- **Метро́** → **Ми ї́демо метро́м.**
- **Маршру́тка** → **Ти ї́деш маршру́ткою?**
- **По́тяг** → **Вони́ ї́дуть по́тягом.**

## Практика (Practice)

Now, let's talk about the most unique Ukrainian experience: the **маршру́тка**. This is a yellow minibus that follows a fixed route but operates with its own social rules.

### "Переда́йте за про́їзд!"

In a crowded **маршру́тка**, you might not be able to reach the **воді́й** (driver) to pay. You simply hand your money to the person in front of you and say: «**Переда́йте за про́їзд, будь ла́ска!**» (Please pass [this] for the fare!). The money travels hand-to-hand to the front, and your change travels back the same way.

> **(In a Marshrutka / У маршру́тці)**
> — Переда́йте за про́їзд, будь ла́ска!
> — За одного́?
> — Так, за одного́. Оплата готівкою.
> — Про́шу, ось ва́ша ре́шта.
> — Дя́кую!
>
> *Gloss:*
> — Please pass for the fare!
> — For one [person]?
> — Yes, for one. Payment in cash.
> — Here, here is your change.
> — Thanks!

### Modern Urban Reality

In modern cities like Kyiv, we now use the **Київ Цифрови́й** (Kyiv Digital) system. You carry a transit card or a QR code on your phone. To pay, you find the **валіда́тор** and **прикласти ка́ртку** (tap the card). If it works, it beeps; if it doesn't, you might hear a local grumble that the **валіда́тор** is broken.

### Essential Transport Phrases

When the transport is crowded, you need to be polite but firm. The most vital phrase is: **«Ви вихо́дите?»** (Are you getting off?). This is how you ask the person blocking the door if they are moving so you can get through.

- **Насту́пна зупи́нка — ву́лиця Хреща́тик.** — Next stop is Khreshchatyk Street.
- **Обере́жно, две́рі зачиня́ються.** — Caution, the doors are closing.
- **Ста́нція переса́дки.** — Transfer station.
- **Прокомпостува́ти квито́к.** — To validate/punch a ticket (traditional).
- **Купи́ти проїзни́й.** — To buy a transit pass.

> [!practice] **Asking for Help**
> If you are lost, use the phrase **«Як дої́хати до...?»** (How to get to...?).
> — Скажі́ть, будь ла́ска, як дої́хати до па́рку?
> — Вам тре́ба авто́бус но́мер два.
> — Де зупи́нка?
> — Зупи́нка он там, напра́во.
