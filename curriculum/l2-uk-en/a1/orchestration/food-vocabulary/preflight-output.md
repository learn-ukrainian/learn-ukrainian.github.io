Welcome back! By now, you’ve mastered your **щоде́нний роз́клад** (daily routine), and you know how to talk about your morning and evening. But there is one thing that truly brings a day to life in Ukraine: the food.

In this module, we are stepping into the heart of the Ukrainian home — the **ку́хня** (kitchen). You will learn to name the essentials, understand why bread is considered sacred, and discover the "secret word" that became a national symbol. Whether you are ordering a **ка́ва** in a Lviv cafe or being welcomed with **хліб-сіль** (bread and salt), this vocabulary will make you feel right at home.

## Їжа (Food)

In Ukrainian, we don't just "eat" — we celebrate the flavors of the land. Before we dive into grammar, let's look at our main food categories. To help your brain organize these, we group them by their type.

### The Foundation: Bread and Grains
Bread isn't just a side dish in Ukraine; it is the center of the meal.
*   **хліб** (bread) — **сві́жий хліб** (fresh bread)
*   **ка́ша** (porridge/cereal) — **гаря́ча ка́ша** (hot porridge)

### The Main Course: Meat, Fish, and Soup
Ukrainian cuisine is famous for its hearty soups and rich proteins.
*   **борщ** (borscht) — **смачни́й борщ** (delicious borscht)
*   **суп** (soup) — **дома́шній суп** (homemade soup)
*   **м’я́со** (meat) — **сві́же м’я́со** (fresh meat)
*   **ри́ба** (fish) — **сма́жена ри́ба** (fried fish)

### From the Garden: Vegetables and Fruits
*   **о́вочі** (vegetables) — **сві́жі о́вочі** (fresh vegetables)
*   **фру́кти** (fruits) — **соло́дкі фру́кти** (sweet fruits)

### Dairy and More
*   **сир** (cheese/cottage cheese) — **бі́лий сир** (white cheese)
*   **молоко́** (milk) — **холо́дне молоко́** (cold milk)

> **Tutor’s Note:** Be careful with the word **сир**! In Russian, this word often specifically means hard yellow cheese, but in Ukrainian, **сир** covers both hard cheese and traditional white cottage cheese. It’s the ultimate versatile ingredient!

### Gender Matters: Adjective Agreement
Just like the objects in your room, every food item has a gender. This matters because your adjectives (like "fresh" or "hot") must change their endings to match.

| Masculine (m) | Feminine (f) | Neuter (n) | Plural (pl) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **хліб** | **ка́ша** | **м’я́со** | **о́вочі** |
| **борщ** | **ри́ба** | **молоко́** | **фру́кти** |
| **суп** | **ка́ва** | **я́блуко** | **цуке́рки** |
| **сир** | **вода́** | | |

Look at how the adjective **сві́жий** (fresh) changes:
*   **сві́жий хліб** (m)
*   **сві́жа ка́ша** (f)
*   **сві́же м’я́со** (n)
*   **сві́жі о́вочі** (pl)

## Напої (Drinks)

Whether you prefer the buzzing energy of a city cafe or a quiet moment with a traditional home-cooked meal, knowing your **напо́ї** (drinks) is essential.

### Your Daily Drinks
*   **вода́** (water) — **холо́дна вода́** (cold water)
*   **чай** (tea) — **гаря́чий чай** (hot tea)
*   **ка́ва** (coffee) — **мі́цна ка́ва** (strong coffee)
*   **сік** (juice) — **апельси́новий сік** (orange juice)
*   **компо́т** (compote) — **соло́дкий компо́т** (sweet compote)

### Ordering Like a Native
In Module 26, you learned the **Знахі́дний відмі́нок** (Accusative case) for direct objects. When you want to order a drink, you use the verb **хоті́ти** (to want) or simply say "Please, a...". Remember that feminine nouns ending in **-а** change to **-у**.

*   **Я хо́чу ка́ву, будь ла́ска.** (I want coffee, please.)
*   **Да́йте, будь ла́ска, во́ду.** (Give [me], please, water.)
*   **Я п'ю чай з лимо́ном.** (I drink tea with lemon.)

### Cultural Note: Tea and Coffee Culture
Ukraine has a vibrant and diverse drinking culture. In the west, especially in **Львів** (Lviv), coffee is king. You will hear people talk about **ка́ва по-льві́вськи** — often brewed in sand or served with a piece of chocolate. 

In most homes, however, **чай** is the default hospitality offering. It’s rarely just plain tea; it’s usually **чай з ме́дом** (tea with honey) or **чай з лимо́ном** (tea with lemon). And don’t forget **компо́т**! This isn't juice; it's a traditional drink made by boiling seasonal fruits (like cherries or apples) with water and a bit of sugar. It’s the taste of a Ukrainian childhood.

## Паляниця (The Shibboleth Bread)

In 2022, a simple word for a traditional loaf of bread became a global symbol of Ukrainian identity: **паляни́ця**.

### What is it?
A **паляни́ця** is a large, round wheat bread with a characteristic horizontal cut across the top, which creates a "lip" as it bakes. Traditionally, it was baked for holidays or special guests.

### The "Secret" Pronunciation
Why did it become a "shibboleth" (a word used to identify people)? It’s all about the phonetics. The word ends in the soft combination **-ця**. Ukrainian speakers pronounce the **ц** softly by touching the tongue to the lower teeth. For Russian speakers, this specific sequence of sounds (**пал-я-ни-ця**) is extremely difficult to mimic without years of practice. In the early days of the full-scale invasion, Ukrainians used this word to distinguish between neighbors and intruders.

### Bread Traditions: «Хліб — усьо́му голова́»
In Ukraine, bread is more than food; it is a sacred object.
*   **Hospitality:** You might see guests welcomed with **хліб-сіль** (bread and salt) on a traditional embroidered towel (**рушни́к**).
*   **Respect:** There is an old saying: **«Хліб — усьо́му голова́»** (Bread is the head of everything). You will rarely see a Ukrainian throw away bread. If a piece falls on the floor, some older generations might even pick it up and kiss it as an apology to the grain.

## Мені подобається / Я не їм (Preferences)

Now that you know the words, let's talk about what you actually like to eat! 

### Expressing Likes
To say you like something, we use a construction you’ve seen before: **Мені́ подо́бається...** (literally "To me is pleasing...").

*   **Мені́ подо́бається борщ.** (I like borscht.)
*   **Тобі́ подо́бається ка́ва?** (Do you like coffee?)
*   **Нам подо́баються фру́кти.** (We like fruits.)

If you want to be more direct, use the verb **люблю́** (I love/like) + the Accusative case:
*   **Я люблю́ ка́ву з молоко́м.** (I love coffee with milk.)
*   **Він лю́бить сві́жий хліб.** (He loves fresh bread.)

### Expressing Dislikes and Restrictions
If you are a vegetarian or have allergies, these phrases are your survival kit.

> **Pattern Box: Negation**
> To say you don't eat or drink something, just add **не** before the verb.
> **Я не їм...** (I don't eat...)
> **Я не п'ю...** (I don't drink...)

*   **Я не їм м’я́со.** (I don't eat meat.)
*   **Я вегетаріа́нець / вегетаріа́нка.** (I am a vegetarian.)
*   **Я не п'ю молоко́.** (I don't drink milk.)
*   **Я не мо́жу ї́сти глюте́н.** (I cannot eat gluten.)
*   **У ме́не алергі́я на горі́хи.** (I have an allergy to nuts.)
