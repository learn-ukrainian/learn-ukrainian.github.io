## Cultural Hooks
1. **Кава по-львівськи**: Lviv has a rich, centuries-old coffee culture that predates the Russian Empire, deeply rooted in Central European traditions and figures like Yuriy Kulchytsky.
2. **Борщ**: Recognized by UNESCO as an intangible cultural heritage of Ukraine. It is a symbol of Ukrainian culinary identity, not just a "Slavic soup."
3. **Хліб-сіль**: The tradition of greeting honored guests with bread and salt. Bread is considered sacred in Ukrainian culture («Хліб — усьому голова»).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Accusative case for feminine nouns**: Saying "Я хочу вода" instead of "Я хочу воду". — *Why it happens*: Forgetting that feminine nouns ending in -а change to -у when they are the direct object.
2. **Verb choice for soup**: Saying "пити суп" (drink soup) instead of "їсти суп" (eat soup). — *Why it happens*: In English and many other languages, liquids are drunk, but in Ukrainian, soup is "eaten" with a spoon.
3. **Gender of loanwords and specific endings**: Assuming "кава" is neuter or "м'ясо" is feminine. — *Why it happens*: Lack of attention to noun endings (-а = feminine, -о = neuter).
