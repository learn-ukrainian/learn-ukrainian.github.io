## Граматика (Grammar Summary)

It is time to organize everything you know about places and movement. Ukrainian relies on a beautiful, consistent logic. The prepositions you use change depending on your intention: are you describing a static location, a dynamic direction, or an origin?

Let's review the core patterns that form the foundation of urban navigation.

**1. Euphony (Милозвучність)**
Ukrainian avoids clashing consonants and clashing vowels. This is why we alternate short words to maintain a musical rhythm.
*   **у** / **в** (in, into, at): **Він у Києві** (He is in Kyiv) vs. **Вона в кімнаті** (She is in the room). 
*   **і** / **й** (and): **Брат і сестра** (Brother and sister) vs. **Мама й тато** (Mom and dad).
*   **з** / **із** / **зі** (from): **Я з України** (I am from Ukraine), **Ми зі США** (We are from the USA), **Вона зі школи** (She is from school).

**2. Location: Де? (Where?)**
To say where something is located, we use the locative case with **в** / **у** (inside) or **на** (on/at open spaces and events). 
*   **в школі** (at school)
*   **в аптеці** (at the pharmacy)
*   **в центрі** (in the center)
*   **на роботі** (at work)
*   **на площі** (on the square)
*   **на вокзалі** (at the station)

**3. Direction: Куди? (Where to?)**
To say where you are going, we use the accusative case with **в** / **у** or **на**. The preposition matches the one you would use for the static location.
*   **у школу** (to school)
*   **в аптеку** (to the pharmacy)
*   **у центр** (to the center)
*   **на роботу** (to work)
*   **на площу** (to the square)
*   **на вокзал** (to the station)

**4. Origin: Звідки? (Where from?)**
To say where you come from or where you are returning from, we use **з** / **із** / **зі** plus the genitive case.
*   **з України** (from Ukraine)
*   **з Канади** (from Canada)
*   **з роботи** (from work)
*   **зі школи** (from school)

**5. Transport and Directions**
When navigating the city streets, we use fixed phrases that you can easily memorize.
*   Transport: **на метро** (by metro), **на автобусі** (by bus) or simply **автобусом** (by bus).
*   Directions: **прямо** (straight), **направо** (to the right), **наліво** (to the left), **далеко** (far), **близько** (close).

### Step 4: Output raw scores

Output ONLY the raw 1-10 scores in the table below. Do NOT calculate the weighted total — the pipeline script handles the math deterministically. Just write your per-dimension scores.

### Step 5: List findings

### Step 6: Verdict

Base your verdict on the **severity of findings** — the pipeline calculates the weighted score separately.

| Verdict | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **PASS** | Zero critical findings, at most minor issues |
| **REVISE** | Has major findings but no criticals — fixable without rewrite |
| **REJECT** | Has any critical finding — fundamental problems requiring rewrite |

### Step 7: Fix it yourself (REVISE only)

Rules for fixes:
- Each fix is a YAML entry with `find:` (exact text from the module) and `replace:` (corrected text)
- The `find` string must be an EXACT substring of the module content — copy-paste it
- Keep fixes minimal — change only what's wrong, preserve surrounding text
- Do NOT fix словник/vocabulary tables (those are generated by a downstream tool)
- For word count issues: add content as a new `insert_after:` entry instead of find/replace
