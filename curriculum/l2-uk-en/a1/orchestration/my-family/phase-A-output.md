# Дослідження: My Family

## State Standard Reference
§3: "людина", §4.2.3.4: "Vocative case — address forms"
Alignment: This module fulfills the foundational thematic vocabulary for "людина" (family members) and introduces basic communicative intentions for addressing people directly using the vocative case, as well as expressing possession ("У мене є...").

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|-------|------------------|
| мама / тато | Highest frequency. Essential to teach vocative forms immediately (мамо, тату). | моя мама, рідний тато |
| сім'я / родина | Crucial cultural distinction: nuclear (сім'я) vs. extended lineage (родина). | велика сім'я, вся родина |
| чоловік / дружина | "чоловік" has dual meaning (man/husband). "дружина" is the standard neutral term for wife. | мій чоловік, моя дружина |

## Cultural Hooks
1. The deep semantic difference between «сім'я» (the nuclear family unit) and «родина» (extended family, lineage, clan). "We gather with the whole *rodyna*."
2. The highly active role of grandparents (дідусь, бабуся) in daily childcare and the common tradition of multi-generational support networks.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Я маю..." instead of "У мене є..." → English calque for possession. Must establish "У мене є" as the natural framework early on.
2. "Мій сестра" instead of "Моя сестра" → Failing to align possessive pronoun gender with the noun. Needs explicit drilling on the matching "-я / -а" sounds.
3. Translating "wife" simply as "жінка" → While used colloquially, "дружина" must be taught as the standard neutral term to avoid confusion with "woman."

## Notes for Content Writing
- Remember the A1 constraint: NO imperative forms (Слухайте!, Читайте!) can be used in the text or instructions, as this is before M47. Use indirect phrasing or English for instructions.
- Keep sentences short (max 8-10 words). Use English scaffolding for all grammar explanations, particularly for introducing the Vocative case and the "У мене є" construction.
