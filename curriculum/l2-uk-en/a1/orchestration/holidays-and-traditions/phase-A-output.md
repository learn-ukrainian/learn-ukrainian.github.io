# Дослідження: Holidays & Traditions

## State Standard Reference
§3: "традиції" (Thematic catalogue topics)
Alignment: This module addresses the thematic requirement to discuss cultural traditions and holidays, while introducing essential vocabulary for greetings, celebrations, and well-wishes.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|-------|------------------|
| свято | High frequency (top 500) | державне свято, релігійне свято |
| вітати | Act of greeting/congratulating | вітати зі святом, вітати з днем народження |
| бажати | Expressing wishes; requires Genitive | бажати щастя, бажати здоров'я, бажати успіху |
| подарунок | High frequency noun | дарувати подарунок, отримати подарунок |

## Cultural Hooks
1. On Christmas Eve (Святвечір), Ukrainians traditionally prepare 12 meatless dishes, with the sweet wheat berry pudding "Кутя" (Kutia) at the center of the table.
2. The world-famous "Carol of the Bells" is actually an ancient Ukrainian New Year's carol called "Щедрик", arranged by Mykola Leontovych.
3. On birthdays, there is a playful tradition of gently pulling the birthday person's ears ("тягнути за вуха")—one pull for each year of life, plus one more to "grow big."

## Common Learner Errors
1. Using Nominative after 'З' for greetings (e.g., "З Новий рік" instead of "З Новим роком") — Learners often don't realize that 'З' in this context requires the Instrumental case.
2. Using Accusative for abstract wishes (e.g., "Бажаю успіх" instead of "Бажаю успіху") — Learners translate "I wish success" directly, missing that abstract wishes take the Genitive case.
3. Confusing "вітати" and "бажати" — Learners use them interchangeably, but "вітати" is the act of congratulating someone, while "бажати" introduces the specific things wished for.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Module M50 allows past, present, future, and imperative, but general A1 rules restrict deep Instrumental/Dative paradigms. Present "З Новим роком", "З Різдвом", and "Зі святом" purely as fixed, lexicalized formulas rather than teaching the full Instrumental paradigm.
- Maintain high English scaffolding (~40% immersion) to explain the case requirements conceptually before providing Ukrainian examples.
- Emphasize the verb "отримати" (to receive a gift) to establish correct, natural Ukrainian vocabulary early on.
