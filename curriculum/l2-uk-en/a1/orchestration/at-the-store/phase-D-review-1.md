## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Unverified Silpo Etymology in Callout Box
- **Location**: Line 43 / Section «Український супермаркет: більше ніж просто покупки»
- **Original**: «"Silpo" comes from the Soviet-era abbreviation "сільське споживання" (rural consumption)»
- **Problem**: This etymology claim is not grounded in the research notes (`at-the-store-research.md`). Research notes mention Silpo as a cultural hook for themed stores but provide no etymology. The commonly cited derivation is "сільське постачання" (rural supply) or "сільське споживче товариство" (rural consumer cooperative), not "сільське споживання" (rural consumption). This appears to be an LLM-fabricated etymology presented as fact in a [!culture] callout box.
- **Fix**: Either remove the etymology claim entirely, or replace with a hedged version: "The name 'Silpo' is believed to echo Soviet-era rural cooperative terminology, but the modern brand..." — avoiding presenting an unverified specific derivation as fact.

### Issue 2: IPA Inconsistency in Loanwords
- **Location**: Lines 26, 30 / Section «Український супермаркет: більше ніж просто покупки»
- **Original**: «**Супермаркет** [superˈmɑrket]» and «**Гіпермаркет** [ɦiperˈmɑrket]»
- **Problem**: The Ukrainian е is rendered as [e] in these loanwords, but elsewhere in the same module, е is consistently rendered as [ɛ] — e.g., «Цукерки» [t͡suˈkɛrkɪ] (line 100), «Печиво» [ˈpɛt͡ʃɪʋɔ] (line 101), «Вибачте» [ʋɪˈbɑt͡ʃtɛ] (line 150). This internal inconsistency undermines IPA reliability for A1 learners.
- **Fix**: Change to [supɛrˈmɑrkɛt] and [ɦipɛrˈmɑrkɛt] for consistency with the module's own transcription convention.

### Issue 3: Unjumble Answers Missing Mandatory Punctuation
- **Location**: Lines 180, 184 / Activities file
- **Original**: «Скажіть будь ласка де молоко» (line 180) and «Я плачу карткою будь ласка» (line 184)
- **Problem**: In Ukrainian, "будь ласка" as an interjection MUST be set off by commas. Teaching A1 learners to construct sentences without these commas reinforces incorrect writing. The correct forms are: "Скажіть, будь ласка, де молоко?" and "Я плачу карткою, будь ласка."
- **Fix**: Add commas to unjumble answers: `answer: "Скажіть, будь ласка, де молоко"` and `answer: "Я плачу карткою, будь ласка"`. Add comma tokens to the word lists if the unjumble format supports punctuation tokens, or restructure as fill-in activities instead.

### Issue 4: Missing Learning Preview for Beginner Safety
- **Location**: Lines 11-17 / Section «Український супермаркет: більше ніж просто покупки»
- **Original**: The module opens with «Чому це важливо?» blockquote → then «Ласкаво просимо у світ українських магазинів!» immediately jumping into content.
- **Problem**: No explicit "Today you'll learn to..." preview section. A1 learners need clear expectations before content begins. The "Чому це важливо?" blockquote provides motivation but not a concrete roadmap of what the learner will be able to do.
- **Fix**: Add a brief learning preview after the blockquote: "In this lesson, you'll learn to: (1) navigate a supermarket using Ukrainian department names, (2) politely ask for help finding items, (3) handle the checkout conversation, (4) understand promotions and receipts."

### Issue 5: Plan-Required Error Drills Missing
- **Location**: Content-wide gap / Missing from Section «Практика: У супермаркеті» and «Відділи та товари»
- **Problem**: The plan's Practice section specifies three learner error drills: (1) Case confusion between Acc and Loc ("іти в магазин" vs "бути в магазині"), (2) Gender agreement practice ("ця ціна" not "цей ціна"), (3) Inanimate Masculine Over-correction ("я купую хліб" Acc=Nom, not "хліба"). None of these drills appear in the content. The dialogues in section «Практика: У супермаркеті» provide communicative practice but no error-focused drilling.
- **Fix**: Add a brief "Common Mistakes" subsection before the dialogues with 2-3 paired correct/incorrect examples highlighting these error patterns.
