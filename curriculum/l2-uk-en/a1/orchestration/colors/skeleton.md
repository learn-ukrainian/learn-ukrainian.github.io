## Синій ≠ блакитний (~330 words)
- P1 (~120 words): Clearly distinguish between the two separate basic blue colors in Ukrainian: `синій` (dark/deep blue) and `блакитний` (light/sky blue). Anchor them with strong, standardized visual examples: `синє море` and `синє чорнило` versus `блакитне небо`. Mention the colors of the Ukrainian flag: `синьо-жовтий прапор` (explaining that `синє — небо, жовте — жито`). Note that if learners encounter `голубий` outside the module, it simply means light blue, but `блакитний` is the active vocabulary target here.

## Підсумок (~330 words)
- P1 (~130 words): Recap the two morphological groups for colors. Remind learners that the Hard group uses standard endings (`червоний стіл`, `червона книга`, `червоне вікно`), while the Soft group specifically applies to `синій` (`синій стіл`, `синя книга`, `синє вікно`). Reiterate that `синій` is a crucial exception to memorize for accurate gender agreement. 
- P2 (~100 words): Summarize the main communication tool: the question `Якого кольору...?` and the importance of matching the short answer's gender to the object being discussed. Quickly restate the fixed appearance phrases (`карі очі`, `русяве волосся`, `сиве волосся`) as non-literal translations. (Author note: Do not introduce comparative forms like `зеленіший` or `синіший`).
- P3 (~100 words): Self-check exercises:
  * Поставте 3 запитання: Look around your environment and form 3 questions using the formula `Якого кольору...?` about objects you see.
  * Опишіть 3 речі: Give short descriptive answers for 3 items in your room, ensuring the color adjective matches the noun's gender (e.g., `Моя книга червона`).
  * Природний опис: Describe the appearance of yourself or a friend using the new fixed chunks: `карі очі`, `русяве волосся`, or `сиве волосся`.
