## Прогноз і клімат

Now that we know the seasons, let’s talk about planning your day. Knowing if it is winter or summer is good, but you really need to know what happens tomorrow. Will it rain? Do you need a jacket? In the mountains, the weather can change in one hour, so checking the forecast is a daily habit.

### Завтра буде... (Tomorrow will be...)

To talk about the future, we use a very simple helper word: **буде** [ˈbudɛ]. This means "will be." You do not need to conjugate it for the weather. You just say "Tomorrow will be" and add the weather word (adverb or noun). It works for temperature and for phenomena like rain or snow.

*   **Завтра** [ˈzɑu̯trɑ] — Tomorrow
*   **Буде** [ˈbudɛ] — Will be

We can combine this with our temperature words. Notice that we do not use "it." We just say "Tomorrow will be warm."

_Приклади:_
*   **Завтра буде тепло.** (Tomorrow [will be] warm.)
*   **Завтра буде холодно.** (Tomorrow [will be] cold.)
*   **Завтра буде сніг.** (Tomorrow [there will be] snow.)

> [!tip]
> **Simple Future**
> You can use **буде** for almost any weather prediction. It is your magic key for the future tense.
> *   **Завтра буде гарна погода.** (Tomorrow [will be] good weather.)

### Небо і вітер (Sky and Wind)

When you look at the sky, you see more than just rain or snow. Often, we describe the sky itself. Is the sun shining? Are there clouds? Is the air moving? We use specific adverbs and nouns for this.

*   **Сонячно** [ˈsɔnʲɑtʃnɔ] — Sunny (adverb)
*   **Хмарно** [ˈxmɑrnɔ] — Cloudy (adverb)
*   **Вітер** [ˈvʲitɛr] — Wind (noun)

For "sunny" and "cloudy," we use the adverb form ending in **-о**, just like **тепло**. But for wind, we usually use the noun **вітер**. We say "Today [is] wind" or "Tomorrow will be wind."

_Приклади:_
*   **Сьогодні хмарно.** (Today [it is] cloudy.)
*   **Влітку часто сонячно.** (In summer [it is] often sunny.)
*   **Завтра буде вітер.** (Tomorrow [there will be] wind.)

### Погода в Карпатах (Weather in the Carpathians)

Here in the mountains, the climate is special. It is not like the city. The four seasons are very distinct, but nature has its own schedule.

**Взимку** [ˈwzɪmku] here is very cold and has a lot of snow.
**Навесні** [nɑvɛsˈnʲi] it is fresh, but often **хмарно** [ˈxmɑrnɔ].
**Влітку** [ˈwlʲitku] it is usually **тепло** [ˈtɛplɔ], but rarely **спекотно** [spɛˈkɔtnɔ] (hot) high in the mountains.

There is also a special time in early autumn. We call it **бабине літо** [ˈbɑbɪnɛ ˈlʲitɔ] (Indian summer). It is a warm, sunny period in September or October when nature rests before winter.

_Приклади:_
*   **У Карпатах взимку холодно.** (In the Carpathians in winter [it is] cold.)
*   **Восени буває бабине літо.** (In autumn [there] happens Indian summer.)
*   **Влітку в горах не спекотно.** (In summer in the mountains [it is] not hot.)

> [!culture]
> **Бабине літо (Babyne lito)**
> Literally "Grandmother's summer." This is a beloved time in Ukraine. It is the golden period of autumn when the sun is gentle and warm. People go to parks or forests to enjoy the last warm days.
