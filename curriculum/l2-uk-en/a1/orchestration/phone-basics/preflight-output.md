Віта́ю! Welcome to another step in your Ukrainian journey! You have already learned how to navigate transport and talk about your daily life. Today, you are going to master one of the most practical skills for living and working in Ukraine: handling phone calls and digital messages. Whether you are ordering a delivery or calling a friend, these phrases will help you feel confident and connected. By the end of this module, you will be able to start and end calls professionally, leave messages, and handle those common "wrong number" situations like a pro.

## Вступ та етикет (Introduction and Etiquette)

When your **телефо́н** (phone) rings in Ukraine, the first word you will likely say or hear is **алло́** (hello). It is the universal greeting for answering a call, much like in many other languages. However, you should also know a more professional and polite way to answer, especially in an office or a service context. This word is **слу́хаю** (I am listening) or **слу́хаю вас** (I am listening to you). Using **слу́хаю** signals that you are ready to help and are giving the caller your full attention. It is a wonderful way to sound more like a native speaker in professional settings.

> **(Office / Офіс)**
> — Алло́? (Hello?)
> — Добри́й день! Слу́хаю вас. (Good day! I am listening to you.)
> — Це Макси́м. Чи можу я поговорити з Оленою? (This is Maksym. May I speak with Olena?)
> — Зачека́йте хвили́нку. (Wait a moment.)

Choosing between **ти** (informal "you") and **Ви** (formal "you") is very important in Ukrainian phone etiquette. You should default to **Ви** when you are talking to strangers, such as a **кур'є́р** (courier), a receptionist, or a shop assistant. Even if you cannot see the person, your voice carries your respect through the language you use. You can use **ти** only when you are calling a close friend or a family member. Being overly informal too early can sometimes be seen as rude, so if you are not sure, **Ви** is always your safest and most polite bet!

One of the most unique sounds in the Ukrainian language is the affricate sound **дз**. You will find it in words like **дзвони́ти** (to call) and **дзвіно́к** (a call). It is very important that you do not pronounce this as two separate sounds (**д** + **з**) or just a simple **з**. Instead, try to pronounce it as a single, quick sound, like the "ds" in the English word "seeds" but at the start of the word. Mastering this sound will immediately make your Ukrainian sound more authentic!

> **Phonetic practice / Практика вимови:**
> * **дзвони́ти** (to call) — **Я дзвоню́ ма́мі.** (I am calling mom.)
> * **дзвіно́к** (a call) — **Це важли́вий дзвіно́к.** (This is an important call.)
> * **передзвони́ти** (to call back) — **Будь ласка, передзвоніть пізніше.** (Please call back later.)
> * **дзво́нить** (is calling) — **Хто це дзво́нить?** (Who is calling?)

[!tip]
When you answer the phone at work, try saying **Слу́хаю вас** instead of just **Алло**. It sounds very professional and polite!

## Презентація: Основні структури (Presentation: Core Structures)

When you identify yourself on the phone, you might be tempted to use the English-style "I am Elena." In Ukrainian, however, we use different structures. The most natural way to say who you are is **Це...** (This is...) or simply stating your name followed by **слу́хає** (is listening/speaking). Avoid saying «Я є Олена», as it sounds quite unnatural and stiff in modern speech.

* **Це Олена.** (This is Olena.)
* **Говорить Іва́н.** (Ivan speaking.)
* **Макси́м слу́хає.** (Maksym speaking/listening.)
* **Алло́, це Тара́с.** (Hello, this is Taras.)
* **Це менеджер.** (This is the manager.)

Asking for the person you want to talk to is your next step. You can use a very simple and polite phrase: **Чи можу я поговорити з...?** (May I speak with...?). Another very useful tool is the phrase **скажіть, будь ласка** (tell me, please), which helps you bridge the gap between your greeting and your request. Remember, your goal is to be clear and polite.

> **(Call to a friend / Дзвінок другу)**
> — Алло́, Приві́т! (Hello, hi!)
> — Приві́т, Окса́но! Це Тара́с. (Hi, Oksana! This is Taras.)
> — Чи можу я поговорити з Вікторим? (May I speak with Viktor?)
> — Віктор зараз за́йнятий. (Viktor is busy now.)
> — Добре, я передзвоню́ пізніше. (Good, I will call back later.)

Managing the conversation requires you to use polite commands, known as imperatives. If you need someone to wait, say **зачека́йте, будь ласка** (wait, please). If you didn't hear something clearly, you can ask **повторі́ть, будь ласка** (repeat, please). These small additions of **будь ласка** go a long way in making your requests sound soft and friendly.

* **Зачека́йте хвили́нку.** (Wait a moment.)
* **Повторіть, будь ласка, но́мер.** (Repeat the number, please.)
* **Говоріть повільніше.** (Speak slower.)
* **Будь ласка, передзвоніть.** (Please call back.)
* **Почекайте тут.** (Wait here.)

Finally, ending the call correctly is just as important as starting it. While **До побачення** (Goodbye) is standard, a very modern and popular way to end a call or a message thread is **До зв'язку́!** (Talk soon! / Until we connect again!). It bridges the gap between the very formal "Goodbye" and the very informal **Бувай** (Bye). It is perfect for both friends and colleagues!

* **До зв'язку́!** (Talk soon!)
* **До побачення!** (Goodbye!)
* **Бувай!** (Bye! - informal)
* **Па-па!** (Bye-bye! - informal)
* **Дякую, до зв'язку!** (Thank you, talk soon!)

[!note]
The phrase **До зв'язку!** is extremely common in business emails and Telegram messages too. It's a great "all-purpose" sign-off!

## Практика: Життєві ситуації (Practice: Real-life Situations)

Sometimes you might **помили́тися но́мером** (dial the wrong number). Don't worry, it happens to everyone! If you realize you have called the wrong person, or if someone calls you by mistake, use the fixed formula: **Вибачте, я помили́вся но́мером** (Sorry, I made a mistake with the number). It is polite, quick, and resolves the situation immediately.

> **(Wrong number / Помилка номером)**
> — Алло́? Хто це? (Hello? Who is this?)
> — Це Олена. Можна Олега? (This is Olena. May I [speak to] Oleh?)
> — Тут немає Олега. (There is no Oleh here.)
> — Вибачте, я помили́лася но́мером. (Sorry, I made a mistake with the number.)
> — Нічого. До побачення. (Nothing [no problem]. Goodbye.)

In modern Ukraine, one of the most common calls you will receive is from a **кур'є́р** (courier). They often call to let you know they are nearby. In these short calls, you will use phrases like **я буду через...** (I will be [there] in...) or **зали́ште біля двере́й** (leave it by the door). Practice these, as you will hear them often!

* **Це кур'є́р.** (This is the courier.)
* **Я буду через п'ять хвили́н.** (I will be [there] in five minutes.)
* **Зали́ште паке́т біля двере́й.** (Leave the package by the door.)
* **Ваш но́мер телефо́ну?** (Your phone number?)
* **Я за́йнятий, зачека́йте.** (I am busy, wait.)

While calling is great, digital messaging via apps like Telegram or Viber is huge in Ukraine. You should know how to say you will **написа́ти повідо́млення** (write a message) or **зали́шити повідо́млення** (leave a message). Often, if a **лі́нія за́йнята** (the line is busy), leaving a text message is the best alternative.

* **Я напишу́ повідо́млення.** (I will write a message.)
* **Можна зали́шити повідо́млення?** (Can I leave a message?)
* **Лі́нія за́йнята.** (The line is busy.)
* **Відповіда́ти на повідо́млення.** (To answer a message.)
* **Переда́ти повідо́млення.** (To pass on a message.)

> **(Leaving a message / Залишити повідомлення)**
> — Олега немає. Що переда́ти? (Oleh is not here. What should I pass on?)
> — Передайте, будь ласка, но́мер. (Please pass on the number.)
> — Добре, я переда́м. (Good, I will pass [it] on.)
> — Дякую. До зв'язку́! (Thank you. Talk soon!)
