## Практика: Покупки та ціни

### Скільки це коштує?
Now that you can count your hryvnias correctly, let's go to the market! I always say: a good accountant knows the price of everything. Let's walk to your local neighborhood bazaar. You see fresh bread. You also smell hot coffee. It is time to talk to the sellers. **Ми йдемо́ на ри́нок.** (We go to the market). **Ми ба́чимо ка́ву.** (We see coffee). To ask for the **ціна́** (price), we use a very simple question. You say: **Скільки це коштує?** (How much does this cost?). This phrase works in any shop or bazaar. The verb **коштува́ти** (to cost) is essential for every shopper. We use it to find out if an item is **до́рого** (expensive) or **де́шево** (cheap). This helps you manage your budget. It also helps you compare different options. You can use it for anything you see.

_Приклади:_
- Скі́льки кошту́є ка́ва? (How much does the coffee cost?)
- Ка́ва кошту́є со́рок гри́вень. (The coffee costs forty hryvnias.)
- Це до́рого чи де́шево? (Is this expensive or cheap?)
- Ціна́ — сто гри́вень. (The price is one hundred hryvnias.)
- Скі́льки кошту́є цей хліб? (How much does this bread cost?)

**Нові слова:**
- **ціна́** — [tsʲiˈna] — price
- **скі́льки** — [ˈsʲkʲilʲkɪ] — how much
- **коштува́ти** — [koʃtuˈʋatɪ] — to cost
- **до́рого** — [ˈdoroɦo] — expensive
- **де́шево** — [ˈdɛʃɛʋo] — cheap

### Рахунок та Здача
Are you ready for the payment? You need to know the total amount. **Ми раху́ємо гро́ші.** (We count the money). **Гро́ші — це важли́во.** (Money is important). A friendly way to ask for the total is **Скільки з мене?** (How much from me?). This phrase is common at bazaars and small shops. It shows that you are ready to pay. Sometimes you pay with a large bill. You might use **сто гри́вень** (one hundred hryvnias). In this case, you will receive **зда́ча** (change). Another word for this is **ре́шта** (change/the rest). Sellers usually count the bills out loud for you. I always check my change too! It is a good idea to count along. This practice helps you recognize the numbers quickly. It also builds trust with the seller.

_Приклади:_
- Скі́льки з ме́не? (How much is my total?)
- Ва́ша зда́ча — де́сять гри́вень. (Your change is ten hryvnias.)
- Ось ва́ша ре́шта. (Here is your change.)
- Да́йте, будь ла́ска, зда́чу. (Give the change, please.)
- Ми раху́ємо гро́ші. (We count the money.)

**Нові слова:**
- **зда́ча** — [ˈzdatʃa] — change
- **ре́шта** — [ˈrɛʃta] — change/the rest
- **сто** — [sto] — hundred
- **гро́ші** — [ˈɦrɔʃʲi] — money

### Рольова гра: На ринку
Let's practice a real conversation between a buyer and a seller. You want to buy bread. You also want to buy water. A loaf of bread often costs around 35 UAH. A bottle of water is about 20 UAH. You use simple sentences to communicate your needs. The seller is patient. The seller is also helpful during the transaction. They appreciate your effort to speak Ukrainian in their shop. This small interaction makes your day better. It also improves your language skills. **Прода́вець ка́же ціну́.** (The seller says the price). **Поку́пець дає́ гро́ші.** (The buyer gives the money).

**Діалог:**
— Скі́льки кошту́є хліб? (How much does the bread cost?)
— Хліб кошту́є три́дцять п'ять гри́вень. (The bread costs thirty-five hryvnias.)
— Добре. А скі́льки кошту́є вода́? (Good. And how much does the water cost?)
— Вода́ кошту́є два́дцять гри́вень. (The water costs twenty hryvnias.)
— Скі́льки з ме́не? (How much from me?)
— П'ятдеся́т п'ять гри́вень. (Fifty-five hryvnias.)
— Ось сто гри́вень. (Here is one hundred hryvnias.)
— Ва́ша зда́ча — со́рок п'ять гри́вень. (Your change is forty-five hryvnias.)
— Дя́кую. До поба́чення! (Thank you. Goodbye!)

**Нові слова:**
- **хліб** — [xlʲib] — bread
- **вода́** — [ʋoˈda] — water
- **дя́кую** — [ˈdʲakʊjʊ] — thank you

[!fact]
The word **зда́ча** comes from the verb **дава́ти** (to give). It literally means «giving back». In modern supermarkets, you often see the word **ре́шта** on the digital screen. Both words are correct and common!
