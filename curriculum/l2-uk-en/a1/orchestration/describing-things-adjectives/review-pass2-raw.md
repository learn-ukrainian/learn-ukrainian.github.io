## Вступ: Світ прикметників — Introduction: The World of Adjectives

Welcome to your first step into the world of Ukrainian adjectives — **прикметники** (adjectives)! You already know the three-gender system from the Gender Code module: masculine, feminine, and neuter nouns. Now you'll discover how adjectives change their endings to match the noun they describe.

In Ukrainian, every adjective answers one of four questions depending on the noun's gender and number:

- **Який?** (which? / what kind?) — for masculine nouns
- **Яка?** — for feminine nouns
- **Яке?** — for neuter nouns
- **Які?** — for plural nouns

Let's see this in action right away. Look at the word **рідний** (native, dear). Watch how it transforms:

- **рідний край** — native land (masculine)
- **рідна мова** — native language (feminine)
- **рідне слово** — native word (neuter)
- **рідні люди** — dear people (plural)

<!-- adapted from: Вашуленко, Grade 3 -->

One root — **рідн-** — and four different endings! This is the heart of adjective agreement in Ukrainian. You'll master this pattern by the end of this module.

> [!culture] **Софійський собор** (St. Sophia's Cathedral) in Kyiv is one of Ukraine's most treasured landmarks. Ukrainians describe it as **старий** (ancient) and **великий** (grand). To talk about Ukraine's history and culture, you need descriptive words — **прикметники**. By the end of this module, you'll be able to describe anything around you in Ukrainian!

You already know three genders from the Gender Code. Now you'll see how adjectives follow the same gender system. Think of it this way: the noun is the boss, and the adjective is the loyal assistant — it always matches the boss's gender. Get the noun's gender right, and the adjective ending follows automatically.

## Презентація: Тверда група — Presentation: Hard Stem Adjectives

Most Ukrainian adjectives belong to the **тверда група** (hard group). These adjectives have stems ending in a hard consonant, and their endings follow a clear, predictable pattern:

| Gender | Question | Ending | Example |
|--------|----------|--------|---------|
| Masculine 🔵 | Який? | **-ий** | **новий дім** (new house) |
| Feminine 🔴 | Яка? | **-а** | **нова книга** (new book) |
| Neuter 🟡 | Яке? | **-е** | **нове місто** (new city) |
| Plural 🟢 | Які? | **-і** | **нові друзі** (new friends) |

<!-- adapted from: Вашуленко, Grade 3, p. 130 -->

Let's practice with several high-frequency adjectives. Notice how the ending always follows the noun's gender:

**гарний** (beautiful, nice):
- **гарний день** — a beautiful day
- **гарна погода** — nice weather
- **гарне небо** — a beautiful sky
- **гарні дні** — beautiful days

**великий** (big, grand) and **малий** (small):
- **великий будинок** — a big building
- **мала кімната** — a small room
- **велике вікно** — a big window
- **малі двері** — small doors

### Adjective Position

In Ukrainian, adjectives usually come before the noun: **гарна погода** (nice weather). This is the most common pattern. But you can also place the adjective after the noun using the zero copula you already know:

- **Це гарна погода.** — This is nice weather.
- **Погода гарна.** — The weather is nice.

In both positions, the ending stays the same. You don't need to worry about the position changing anything — just match the gender!

> [!warning] **Common Mistake: Gender Mismatch**
> English doesn't have gender agreement, so beginners often use the masculine dictionary form (-ий) for everything. Watch out for this trap:
> - ❌ *новий машина* — wrong! **машина** is feminine
> - ✅ **нова машина** — a new car
>
> Always check the noun's gender first, then pick the matching adjective ending.

### Opposites — Your Descriptive Toolkit

Opposites are a powerful way to double your vocabulary quickly. Here are essential pairs you'll use every day:

**добрий / поганий** (good / bad):
- **добрий день** — good day
- **поганий настрій** — bad mood
- **добра людина** — a good person
- **погана погода** — bad weather

**великий / малий** (big / small):
- **великий дім** — a big house
- **малий дім** — a small house

**дорогий / дешевий** (expensive / cheap):
- **дорогий телефон** — an expensive phone
- **дешевий телефон** — a cheap phone

> [!tip] **Quick Check**: Before you add an adjective to a noun, ask yourself: «Який? Яка? Яке? Які?» The question tells you which ending to use!

## Презентація 2: М'яка група та Специфіка (Presentation 2: Soft Stem and Nuances)

Some adjectives belong to the **м'яка група** (soft group). Their stem ends in a soft consonant, and the endings look slightly different from the hard group:

| Gender | Question | Ending | Example |
|--------|----------|--------|---------|
| Masculine 🔵 | Який? | **-ій** | **синій** (blue) |
| Feminine 🔴 | Яка? | **-я** | **синя лінія** (blue line) |
| Neuter 🟡 | Яке? | **-є** | **синє небо** (blue sky) |
| Plural 🟢 | Які? | **-і** | **сині** (blue — plural) |

The word **синій** (blue) is your key example for the soft group. Here's a practical way to remember it: the Kyiv Metro has a **синя лінія** (Blue Line) and a **червона лінія** (Red Line). Notice: **синя** (soft, -я) vs. **червона** (hard, -а) — two different groups describing the same noun **лінія**!

> [!warning] **Watch the Spelling!**
> A common error is writing *синий* instead of the correct **синій**. Notice the difference carefully: the soft group masculine ending is **-ій** (with **ї**), not **-ий** (with **и**). This one letter makes all the difference between correct and incorrect Ukrainian.

Here's a side-by-side comparison of hard and soft endings:

| | Hard: **новий** | Soft: **синій** |
|---|---|---|
| Masculine | нов-**ий** | син-**ій** |
| Feminine | нов-**а** | син-**я** |
| Neuter | нов-**е** | син-**є** |
| Plural | нов-**і** | син-**і** |

### Plural — One Ending for All

Here's great news for you: in the plural, both hard and soft adjectives take the same ending — **-і**. And this ending is the same regardless of the noun's gender:

- **гарні дні** — beautiful days (masculine noun)
- **гарні дівчата** — beautiful girls (feminine noun)
- **гарні вікна** — beautiful windows (neuter noun)
- **сині олівці** — blue pencils (masculine noun)

No gender-matching needed in the plural — it's always **-і**! That's one less thing to worry about.

> [!note] **Hard vs. Soft — Quick Summary**
> - Hard group (most adjectives): **-ий, -а, -е, -і**
> - Soft group (like синій): **-ій, -я, -є, -і**
> - Plural is the same for both: always **-і**!
