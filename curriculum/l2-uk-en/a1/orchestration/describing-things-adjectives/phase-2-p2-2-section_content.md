## Характеристики: Опис предметів і простору

And because the rule never changes, we can now describe *everything* around us. You are not just naming objects anymore. You are painting a picture with words. Let’s look at the most common adjectives you will use every day to describe your home, your city, and your friends.

### Великий і малий (Size)

These are the first two words you need when you look at an apartment or a building. In Ukrainian, size is very important for description.

*   **Великий** [ʋɛˈlɪkɪj] — Big / Large
*   **Малий** [mɑˈlɪj] — Small

Remember our matching rule? The ending must match the noun. If you see a house (**дім**, masculine), you use the masculine form. If you see a window (**вікно**, neuter), you change the ending.

> [!culture]
> **A Symbol of Kyiv**
> Look at **Софійський собор** [sɔˈfijʲsʲkɪj sɔˈbɔr] (St. Sophia's Cathedral) in Kyiv. It is a perfect example for us.
> *   **Це старий собор.** [tʃɛ stɑˈrɪj sɔˈbɔr] (This is an old cathedral.)
> *   **Він дуже великий.** [ʋin ˈduʒɛ ʋɛˈlɪkɪj] (It is very big.)
> Ukrainians are very proud of this architecture. It stands in the center of the city.

### Новий і старий (Age)

When we talk about things, we often look at their age. Are they brand new or do they have a history?

*   **Новий** [nɔˈʋɪj] — New
*   **Старий** [stɑˈrɪj] — Old

Imagine you are a real estate agent. You show a client different options. You point to a modern building and then to an antique chair.

*   **Це новий дім.** [tʃɛ nɔˈʋɪj dʲim] (This is a new house.)
*   **Тут старе вікно.** [tut stɑˈrɛ ʋiˈknɔ] (Here is an old window.)
*   **Це стара книга.** [tʃɛ stɑˈrɑ ˈknɪɦɑ] (This is an old book.)

**Note:** You can use **старий** for people too (an old man), but for young people, we use a special word: **молодий** [mɔlɔˈdɪj].

### Гарний, добрий і поганий (Quality)

Be careful with these words. In English, "good" covers many meanings. In Ukrainian, we separate them.

*   **Гарний** [ˈɦɑrnɪj] — Beautiful / Nice / Good quality (visual or functional)
*   **Добрий** [ˈdɔbrɪj] — Kind / Good (personality or specific quality like "good morning")
*   **Поганий** [pɔˈɦɑnɪj] — Bad

If a student is smart and behaves well, he is **добрий студент**. If a dress looks amazing, it is **гарна сукня**.

*   **Сьогодні гарна погода.** [sʲɔˈɦɔdʲnʲi ˈɦɑrnɑ pɔˈɦɔdɑ] (Today is beautiful weather.)
*   **Він добрий чоловік.** [ʋin ˈdɔbrɪj tʃɔlɔˈʋik] (He is a kind man.)
*   **Це поганий настрій.** [tʃɛ pɔˈɦɑnɪj ˈnɑsʲtʲrʲij] (This is a bad mood.)

### Дорогий і дешевий (Value)

Now, let's go shopping. You look at the price tag. Is it a bargain or a luxury item?

*   **Дорогий** [dɔrɔˈɦɪj] — Expensive / Dear
*   **Дешевий** [dɛˈʃɛʋɪj] — Cheap

*   **Цей телефон дорогий.** [tʃɛj tɛlɛˈfɔn dɔrɔˈɦɪj] (This phone is expensive.)
*   **Це дешевий квиток.** [tʃɛ dɛˈʃɛʋɪj kʋɪˈtɔk] (This is a cheap ticket.)
*   **Це дорога річ.** [tʃɛ dɔrɔˈɦɑ rʲitʃ] (This is an expensive thing.)

### Опис людей (People)

We also use adjectives to describe personality and appearance. We already met **добрий** (kind). Here are two more essential traits.

*   **Цікавий** [tʃiˈkɑʋɪj] — Interesting
*   **Молодий** [mɔlɔˈdɪj] — Young

*   **Це молода жінка.** [tʃɛ mɔlɔˈdɑ ˈʒinkɑ] (This is a young woman.)
*   **Наш студент цікавий.** [nɑʃ stuˈdɛnt tʃiˈkɑʋɪj] (Our student is interesting.)
*   **Вона добра мама.** [ʋɔˈnɑ ˈdɔbrɑ ˈmɑmɑ] (She is a kind mom.)

### Mnemonic: Bricks and Pillows

How do you remember which ending to use? Think about the "Hard" and "Soft" groups we discussed earlier.

**The Hard Group (-ий)** is like a solid brick wall. Words like **новий**, **гарний**, **старий** are strong and simple. They follow the standard pattern.

**The Soft Group (-ій)** is like a soft pillow. Words like **синій** (blue) or **вечірній** (evening) feel softer.

| Type | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hard (Brick)** | **-ий** (новий) | **-а** (нова) | **-е** (нове) | **-і** (нові) |
| **Soft (Pillow)** | **-ій** (синій) | **-я** (синя) | **-є** (синє) | **-і** (сині) |

Most adjectives you learn now are "Bricks". They are hard and reliable.
