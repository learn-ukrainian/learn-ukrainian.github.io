## Основи: Рід і число прикметників

### Який це предмет?

Imagine you are walking down a street in Kyiv with me. We see a building. You want to ask me about it. In English, you simply say: "What kind of building is it?" or "Which building?"

In Ukrainian, even the question word **«який»** (which / what kind of) must change its form. It acts like a chameleon. It changes to match the gender of the noun you are asking about. This is the **Gender Code** in action again.

If you ask about a masculine noun, use the masculine question form. If you ask about a feminine noun, the question word changes shape.

*   **Який?** — [jɑˈkɪj] — for Masculine nouns (house, park).
*   **Яка?** — [jɑˈkɑ] — for Feminine nouns (room, book).
*   **Яке?** — [jɑˈkɛ] — for Neuter nouns (window, city).
*   **Які?** — [jɑˈkʲi] — for Plural nouns (houses, books).

> [!tip]
> **Matching Endings**
> Notice a pattern? The question word often rhymes with the noun ending or the adjective ending you expect. **Яка** кімната? (Feminine `-а`). **Яке** місто? (Neuter `-о/-е`). This "rhyme" helps your brain connect the words.

### Тверда група: Правило «Новий»

Most adjectives in Ukrainian belong to the **Hard Group**. Think of these as the standard building blocks. They have "hard" endings. The dictionary form always ends in **-ий**.

Let's look at the word **новий** [nɔˈʋɪj] (new). Just like the question word, the adjective takes a different ending for each gender. It agrees with the noun.

| Gender | Ending | Example | IPA | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Masculine** | **-ий** | Новий дім | [nɔˈʋɪj dʲim] | New house |
| **Feminine** | **-а** | Нова квартира | [nɔˈʋɑ kʋɑrˈtɪrɑ] | New apartment |
| **Neuter** | **-е** | Нове вікно | [nɔˈʋɛ ʋʲikˈnɔ] | New window |
| **Plural** | **-і** | Нові меблі | [nɔˈʋʲi ˈmɛblʲi] | New furniture |

See how the stem **нов-** stays the same? Only the tail changes. This applies to most adjectives you know, like **гарний** [ˈɦɑrnɪj] (beautiful/good), **старий** [stɑˈrɪj] (old), and **цікавий** [tʲsʲiˈkɑʋɪj] (interesting).

*   **Це гарний парк.** [tʃɛ ˈɦɑrnɪj pɑrk] (This is a beautiful park.)
*   **Тут стара школа.** [tut stɑˈrɑ ˈʃkɔlɑ] (Here is an old school.)
*   **Там цікаве місто.** [tɑm tʲsʲiˈkɑʋɛ ˈmʲistɔ] (There is an interesting city.)

### М'яка група: Правило «Синій»

Some adjectives are "soft." They feel different in your mouth. Their stem ends in a soft sound, so they cannot take the hard **-ий** ending. Instead, they take the soft **-ій**.

The most common example for beginners is **синій** [ˈsɪnʲij] (blue). Compare the endings carefully.

*   **Masculine:** синій [ˈsɪnʲij] (ends in **-ій**, not -ий)
*   **Feminine:** синя [ˈsɪnʲɑ] (ends in **-я**, not -а)
*   **Neuter:** синє [ˈsɪnʲɛ] (ends in **-є**, not -е)
*   **Plural:** сині [ˈsɪnʲi] (ends in **-і**, same as hard group)

Notice the "soft" vowels: **я** instead of **а**, **є** instead of **е**. The logic is the same (Gender Agreement), but the vowels shift to accommodate the soft stem.

*   **Це синій зошит.** [tʃɛ ˈsɪnʲij ˈzɔʃɪt] (This is a blue notebook.)
*   **Тут синя ручка.** [tut ˈsɪnʲɑ ˈrutʃkɑ] (Here is a blue pen.)
*   **Там синє море.** [tɑm ˈsɪnʲɛ ˈmɔrɛ] (There is a blue sea.)

### Порядок слів: Де стоїть прикметник?

You can use adjectives in two places.

**1. Before the noun (Descriptive)**
This is the standard position. You are simply naming the object with its quality.
*   **Це великий стіл.** [tʃɛ ʋɛˈlɪkɪj sʲtʲil] (This is a big table.)
*   **Це добрий студент.** [tʃɛ ˈdɔbrɪj stuˈdɛnt] (This is a good student.)

**2. After the noun (Statement)**
Here, you are making a specific claim about the object. In English, you use "is" (The table *is* big). In Ukrainian, we skip "is", but the meaning is clear from the word order and intonation.
*   **Цей стіл великий.** [tʃɛj sʲtʲil ʋɛˈlɪkɪj] (This table [is] big.)
*   **Цей студент добрий.** [tʃɛj stuˈdɛnt ˈdɔbrɪj] (This student [is] good.)

In both cases, the ending matches the noun. The rule never changes!
