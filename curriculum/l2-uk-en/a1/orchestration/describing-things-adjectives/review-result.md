## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Ambiguous Match-Up Activity
**Location**: Activities section, `type: match-up`
**Problem**: The activity asks learners to match adjectives (`новий`, `старий`) with nouns (`дім`, `телефон`). Since `новий телефон` and `старий дім` are completely valid Ukrainian phrases, a strict 1-to-1 matching system will penalize learners for grammatically correct combinations simply because they didn't guess the exact pairs written in the YAML.
**Fix**: Change the match-up pairs to link the English translation to the Ukrainian phrase (e.g., `new house` -> `новий дім`), ensuring exactly one unambiguous correct answer per item.

### Issue 2: LLM Fingerprint and Unnatural Phrasing
**Location**: Section "Практика та Культурний контекст" and Section "Агентство нерухомості"
**Problem**: The sentences `Мавка — молода. Мавка — гарна. Мавка — цікава. Це зелена Мавка.` are robotic and highly repetitive. Additionally, `Це зелена Мавка` ("This is green Mavka") is unnatural in Ukrainian—Mavka is a mythological being, not an object with color variants. The text also includes LLM filler: "The adjective смачний (tasty) works beautifully across all three genders!"
**Fix**: Combine the Mavka sentences into a natural description: `Мавка — молода і гарна.` Remove `Це зелена Мавка.` Change the filler sentence to a direct instruction: "Notice how **смачний** changes its ending to match the food:".

### Issue 3: Pedagogical Contradiction Regarding Colors
**Location**: Section "Підсумок" and Vocabulary
**Problem**: The module explicitly states "In the next module, you'll learn color vocabulary", but it actively teaches `зелений` and `червоний` in the current module's text and vocabulary list. This dilutes the focus of the current lesson and contradicts the signposting.
**Fix**: Remove `зелений` from the text and the vocabulary list. Keep `синій` as the core example of the soft group, but do not frame colors as general vocabulary to memorize until the next module.
