## Культурний контекст: «Чи» та Альф

As we saw in the dialogues, asking a question is more than just grammar. It is about your relationship with the person you are talking to. Let’s look closer at how a tiny word and a funny alien changed how Ukrainians speak.

### The Politeness Scale
You already know two ways to ask "Yes/No" questions. But when should you use which? Think of the particle **чи** as a formal tie. It dresses up your sentence.

When you speak to a stranger, a boss, or a barista, use **чи**. It shows respect. It signals that you are polite. But with close friends or family, you can take off the tie. You can drop the **чи** and just use your voice (intonation).

> [!tip]
> **Safe Harbor Rule**
> If you are unsure, use **чи**. It is never wrong to be too polite. But dropping it with a stranger might sound too casual.

*   **Чи ви тут працюєте?** [tʃɪ vɪ tut prɑtsʲˈujɛtɛ] — Do you work here? (Polite)
*   **Ви тут працюєте?** [vɪ tut prɑtsʲˈujɛtɛ] — Do you work here? (Casual)
*   **Чи це ваш кіт?** [tʃɪ tsɛ vɑʃ kit] — Is this your cat? (Polite)

### The Alien Who Taught Us
Language learning often comes from surprising places. For many Ukrainians, the best teacher of natural questions was a TV show from the 90s: *ALF*.

The Ukrainian dubbing of this show is legendary. It was not just a translation. It was a masterpiece of conversational Ukrainian. The voice actors used perfect intonation and natural sentence structures. They showed that Ukrainian can be funny, modern, and cool.

### The Famous Cat Quote
There is one line from *ALF* that every Ukrainian knows. It perfectly demonstrates how to use **не** (not) to make a negative statement.

Alf is surprised that someone does not like cats. He explains why.

*   **Ти не любиш котів?** [tɪ nɛ lʲˈubɪʃ kotˈiw] — You do not like cats?
*   **Ти просто не вмієш їх готувати!** [tɪ prˈosto nɛ vmˈijɛʃ jix hotuvˈɑtɪ] — You just do not know how to cook them!

Look at the grammar here. The particle **не** stands strictly before the verb **любиш** (like) and **вмієш** (know how). It blocks the action. This simple structure is the key to saying "no" to any action.

*   **Я не готую.** [jɑ nɛ hotˈuju] — I do not cook.
*   **Ми не знаємо.** [mɪ nɛ znˈɑjɛmo] — We do not know.
*   **Він не тут.** [vin nɛ tut] — He is not here.
