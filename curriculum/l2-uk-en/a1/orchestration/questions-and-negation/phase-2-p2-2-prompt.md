### –Ъ–ї—О—З—Ц –і–Њ —А–Њ–Ј–Љ–Њ–≤–Є
Imagine we are neighbors chatting over the fence. You want to ask if I am home, or if I have a hammer. Statements like "I am here" are useful, but questions unlock real conversation. Today, we invite you to the world of interaction. The good news is that Ukrainian questions are much simpler than in English. You do not need "do", "does", or "did". We build questions with simple blocks.

> [!tip]
> **No "Do" Gymnastics**
> In English, you say "You read" but "**Do** you read?".
> In Ukrainian, you just say "**–Ґ–Є —З–Є—В–∞—Ф—И**" [t…™ t Г…™tЋИ…Сje Г] (You read) vs "**–Ґ–Є —З–Є—В–∞—Ф—И?**" [t…™ t Г…™tЋИ…Сje Г] (Do you read?).
> The words stay the same. Only the music of your voice changes.

### –Ж–љ—Б—В—А—Г–Љ–µ–љ—В вДЦ1: –І–∞—Б—В–Ї–∞ ¬Ђ–І–Є¬ї
To make a clear Yes/No question, we often use the small word **—З–Є** [t Г…™]. It acts like a flag at the start of a sentence. It signals: "Attention! A question is coming." Think of it as an upside-down question mark at the beginning. It is polite and very common in books.

_–Я—А–Є–Ї–ї–∞–і:_
*   **–Ґ–Є –Ј–љ–∞—Ф—И.** [t…™ znЋИ…Сje Г] вАФ You know. (Statement)
*   **–І–Є —В–Є –Ј–љ–∞—Ф—И?** [t Г…™ t…™ znЋИ…Сje Г] вАФ Do you know? (Question)
*   **–І–Є —Ж–µ –Ї—Ц—В?** [t Г…™ ts…Ы kit] вАФ Is this a cat?

### –Ж–љ—Б—В—А—Г–Љ–µ–љ—В вДЦ2: –Ж–љ—В–Њ–љ–∞—Ж—Ц—П
In casual talk, neighbors often drop **—З–Є** [t Г…™]. We just raise our voice at the end of the sentence. This is the "lazy" but friendly way to ask. Your voice goes up, like a slide.

_–Я—А–Є–Ї–ї–∞–і:_
*   **–Т—Ц–љ —В–∞–Љ.** [vin t…Сm] вАФ He is there. (Flat voice)
*   **–Т—Ц–љ —В–∞–Љ?** [vin t…Сm] вАФ Is he there? (Voice goes up вЖЧ)

### –Ж–љ—Б—В—А—Г–Љ–µ–љ—В вДЦ3: –Ч–∞–њ–µ—А–µ—З–µ–љ–љ—П ¬Ђ–Э–µ¬ї
Finally, to say "not", we use the particle **–љ–µ** [n…Ы]. It is strong and simple. You place it strictly before the word you want to negate. Usually, it stands right before the action (the verb).

_–Я—А–Є–Ї–ї–∞–і:_
*   **–ѓ –љ–µ –Ј–љ–∞—О.** [j…С n…Ы znЋИ…Сju] вАФ I do not know.
*   **–Т—Ц–љ –љ–µ —В—Г—В.** [vin n…Ы tut] вАФ He is not here.
