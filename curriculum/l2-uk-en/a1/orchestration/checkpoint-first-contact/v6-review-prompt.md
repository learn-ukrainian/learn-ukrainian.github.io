## Граматика (Grammar Summary)

A1.1 gave you six core patterns. You do not need to memorize grammar tables — you need to recognize these patterns and use them without hesitation. Here they are, all in one place.

**The six patterns of A1.1:**

1. **Це** (this is) + noun — identification:
   - **Це мама.** — This is mom.
   - **Це брат.** — This is a brother.

2. Subject — Noun (no "is") — description:
   - **Я — студент.** — I am a student.
   - **Вона — лікарка.** — She is a doctor.

3. **У мене є** (I have) + noun — possession:
   - **У мене є сестра.** — I have a sister.
   - **У мене є брат.** — I have a brother.

4. **Як тебе/вас звати?** — asking and giving names:
   - **Як вас звати?** — What is your name?
   - **Мене звати Оксана.** — My name is Оксана.

5. **Мій/моя/моє** + noun — possessive with gender:
   - **мій тато** — my dad (masculine)
   - **моя мама** — my mom (feminine)
   - **моє ім'я** — my name (neuter)

6. **Звідки ти?** — **Я з...** — origin:
   - **Звідки ви?** — Where are you from?
   - **Я з Канади.** — I am from Canada.

These six patterns are building blocks. Real conversation chains them together naturally. Look: **Привіт! Мене звати Тарас. Я зі Львова. Я — студент. У мене є сестра. Моя сестра — Ганна.** Every sentence uses one of the six patterns. Fluency means chaining these patterns without stopping to think about which one you are using.

One cultural note before we finish. Ukrainians typically use **Добрий день!** (Good day!) — not **Привіт!** (Hi!) — with strangers and older people. The **ви**-form (**Як вас звати? Звідки ви?**) is the default until someone suggests switching to **ти** (you, informal). Among university students or young people, **Привіт** + **ти** is perfectly fine from the start. After exchanging names, the standard closer is **Приємно познайомитися!** (Nice to meet you!). Handshakes are common in Ukraine, and Ukrainians value eye contact during greetings — it shows respect and sincerity.

## Review Protocol

### Step 1: Scan for linguistic errors

Scan the Ukrainian text for errors. Report ONLY problems found — do not echo correct text.

**Four SEPARATE checks (these are four different problems):**
1. **Russianisms** — Russian words used instead of Ukrainian (кот→кіт, хорошо→добре, конечно→звичайно, сейчас→зараз)
2. **Surzhyk** — mixed Russian-Ukrainian forms (шо→що, чо→чому, тіпа→типу)
3. **Calques** — literal translations from Russian that sound wrong in Ukrainian (приймати душ→брати душ, приймати рішення→ухвалювати рішення)
4. **Paronyms** — similar-sounding words with different meanings used incorrectly (тактична≠тактовна, ефектний≠ефективний)

**Also check:**
- Russian characters (ы, э, ё, ъ) — these must NEVER appear
- Incorrect gender assignment or case endings
- Factually wrong claims about Ukrainian phonetics, grammar, or culture

**Authority hierarchy for verification (check in this order):**
1. VESUM — does this word/form exist? POS? Gender? (415K lemmas)
2. Правопис 2019 — is it spelled correctly? Orthography rules.
