## Діалоги (Dialogues)

In Ukrainian, every noun belongs to a grammatical category called **рід** (gender). Tables, lamps, windows, ideas — each one is either masculine, feminine, or neuter. This is not about biological sex. It is a grammatical category that determines how words behave together in a sentence. The word for "my" changes depending on the object: **мій** стіл (my table), **моя** кімната (my room), **моє** вікно (my window). Three different forms of the same word, because three different genders demand them.

You will see this immediately in real conversation. Imagine you are on a video call with a friend in Ukraine. You have just moved, and you are showing them your new apartment.

<div class="dialogue">


**Марко:** Привіт! Дивись, це моя кімната. *(Hi! Look, this is my room.)*


**Анна:** Класно! У тебе є стіл? *(Cool! Do you have a table?)*


**Марко:** Так, у мене є стіл і ліжко. *(Yes, I have a table and a bed.)*


**Анна:** А вікно? *(And a window?)*


**Марко:** Ось моє вікно. *(Here is my window.)*


</div>



Consider a different scenario. You are meeting a classmate at a cafe to study for an upcoming exam. You are sitting down and comparing what supplies you brought in your bags.

<div class="dialogue">


**Олена:** Що у тебе є? *(What do you have?)*


**Дмитро:** У мене є книга, телефон і фото. *(I have a book, a phone, and a photo.)*


**Олена:** А у мене є ручка і зошит. *(And I have a pen and a notebook.)*


</div>



These are very simple, everyday conversations, but they contain a massive structural secret of the Ukrainian language. Look closely at the very first dialogue. Marko says **моя кімната** (my room). But a few moments later, he says **моє вікно** (my window). If he were to talk about his table in the same way, he would confidently say **мій стіл** (my table). 

Why does the word for "my" keep changing? Why can't we just use one word for everything? It changes because the objects themselves belong to different grammatical categories. The room is inherently feminine, so it demands the feminine form of the word "my", which is **моя**. The window is inherently neuter, so it demands the neuter form, which is **моє**. The table is inherently masculine, so it takes the masculine form, which is **мій**. 

This is one of the most important principles in Ukrainian grammar: words must agree with each other. You cannot put a masculine "my" next to a feminine "room". The words must match perfectly to sound correct.

## Він, вона, воно (The Gender Test)

In Ukrainian grammar, every noun belongs strictly to one of three categories: **чоловічий рід** (masculine), **жіночий рід** (feminine), or **середній рід** (neuter). You cannot form a correct Ukrainian sentence without knowing the gender of the nouns you are using.

Ukrainian schoolchildren learn a highly intuitive test to determine the gender of any word. They ask themselves a simple question: Can I logically replace this noun with the pronoun **він** (he), **вона** (she), or **воно** (it)?

If you can point to an inanimate object and comfortably replace its name with **він** (he), the object belongs to the masculine gender. If you can point to it and replace its name with **вона** (she), it belongs to the feminine gender. If you use **воно** (it), it belongs to the neuter gender.

As an English speaker learning the language, you do not have to guess or memorize the gender of every single object blindly. You just have to look at the end of the word. The final letter of a Ukrainian noun functions exactly like a grammatical barcode. It tells you exactly what gender the word is. This spelling rule covers the vast majority of nouns in the entire language, with very few exceptions.

### Чоловічий рід (Masculine)
Masculine nouns almost always end in a consonant — no trailing vowel, just a hard stop. That absence of a vowel is your clue.

*   **стіл** (table) → ends in **-л** → він → **мій стіл**
*   **телефон** (phone) → ends in **-н** → він → **мій телефон**
*   **зошит** (notebook) → ends in **-т** → він → **мій зошит**

The pattern is consistent: consonant ending = masculine. Try saying **він** with each word — стіл? він. Телефон? він. Зошит? він. It fits naturally.

### Жіночий рід (Feminine)
Feminine nouns almost always end in **-а** or **-я** — an open vowel sound at the end of the word.

*   **книга** (book) → ends in **-а** → вона → **моя книга**
*   **лампа** (lamp) → ends in **-а** → вона → **моя лампа**
*   **кімната** (room) → ends in **-а** → вона → **моя кімната**
*   **ручка** (pen) → ends in **-а** → вона → **моя ручка**

Say **вона** with each: книга? вона. Лампа? вона. Кімната? вона. The **-а** ending and **вона** go hand in hand.

### Середній рід (Neuter)
Neuter nouns generally end in **-о** or **-е** — round, open sounds.

*   **вікно** (window) → ends in **-о** → воно → **моє вікно**
*   **ліжко** (bed) → ends in **-о** → воно → **моє ліжко**
*   **крісло** (armchair) → ends in **-о** → воно → **моє крісло**
*   **місто** (city) → ends in **-о** → воно → **моє місто**

Notice how **воно** and **моє** pair with every **-о** ending. The sound pattern is your guide.

### У мене є... (I have...)

In a previous module about family, we learned the powerful structure **У мене є** (I have). Literally, this phrase translates to "By me there is". This is the standard, universal way to express possession in Ukrainian. We used it to state relationships, saying things like **У мене є брат** (I have a brother) or **У мене є сестра** (I have a sister).

The same structure works for inanimate objects. Combine **У мене є** with any noun you have learned:

*   **У мене є стіл.** (I have a table.)
*   **У мене є книга.** (I have a book.)
*   **У мене є вікно.** (I have a window.)
*   **У мене є комп'ютер.** (I have a computer.)
*   **У мене є сумка.** (I have a bag.)
*   **У мене є дзеркало.** (I have a mirror.)

Whether the object is masculine, feminine, or neuter, **У мене є** stays the same — gender does not change this phrase.

| 9 | **Dialogue & conversation quality** | 10% | Dialogues natural and culturally appropriate? Real situations, real responses? Speaker roles clear? Not stilted or textbook-robotic? |

**Note:** Vocabulary tables (словник), video embeds, and external resource links are added by a downstream ENRICH step — do NOT penalize their absence or quality. If you see a vocabulary table with wrong translations, missing words, or formatting issues, that content was NOT written by the writer — it was generated deterministically by the ENRICH step. Do NOT flag словník problems as linguistic errors or deduct points from the writer. Report them separately under "ENRICH issues" in your findings (informational only, not scored).

### Step 4: Output raw scores

Output ONLY the raw 1-10 scores in the table below. Do NOT calculate the weighted total — the pipeline script handles the math deterministically. Just write your per-dimension scores.

### Step 5: List findings

For every issue found, provide:
```
[DIMENSION] [SEVERITY: critical/major/minor]
Location: [specific section/paragraph — quote the actual text]
Issue: [what's wrong]
Fix: [exactly how to fix it]
```

Critical = module cannot ship. Major = quality below standard. Minor = polish item.

### Step 6: Verdict

Base your verdict on the **severity of findings** — the pipeline calculates the weighted score separately.

| Verdict | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **PASS** | Zero critical findings, at most minor issues |
| **REVISE** | Has major findings but no criticals — fixable without rewrite |
| **REJECT** | Has any critical finding — fundamental problems requiring rewrite |

### Step 7: Fix it yourself (REVISE only)

If your verdict is **REVISE**, you MUST output a `<fixes>` block with exact find/replace pairs. The pipeline applies these deterministically — no LLM regeneration, no rewriting from scratch.

Rules for fixes:
- Each fix is a YAML entry with `find:` (exact text from the module) and `replace:` (corrected text)
- The `find` string must be an EXACT substring of the module content — copy-paste it
- Keep fixes minimal — change only what's wrong, preserve surrounding text
- Do NOT fix словник/vocabulary tables (those are generated by a downstream tool)
- For word count issues: add content as a new `insert_after:` entry instead of find/replace
