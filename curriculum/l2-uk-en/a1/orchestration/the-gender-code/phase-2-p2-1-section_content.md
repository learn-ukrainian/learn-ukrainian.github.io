## Вступ: Родовий код іменників

Hello there! It is so wonderful to meet you. I am your neighbor, and I am so excited to help you start your journey into Ukrainian. **Добрий день!** [ˈdɔbrɪj dɛnʲ] (Good day!) Today we are going to learn the «gender code». This is the secret key to understanding how our nouns work. Once you know this code, you can unlock almost every sentence in our beautiful language.

### The Three Grammatical Families
Every Ukrainian noun lives in a specific family. We call these families «genders». There are three genders in our language: masculine, feminine, and neuter. This is a bit different from English. In English, we usually call objects «it». However, in Ukrainian, every object is either «he», «she», or «it». A house is a «he». A book is a «she». A window is an «it». This is because of grammatical gender. Sometimes gender matches biological sex. For example, a brother is masculine and a sister is feminine. But for objects like tables or cities, the gender is just a grammatical rule. Imagine you are visiting a neighbor on our street. Look at their house and their family. Everything you see has a name and belongs to a specific gender family. In Ukrainian, we even use different words for «my» to match these families. We use **мій** [mij] for masculine, **моя** [mоˈjа] for feminine, and **моє** [mоˈjе] for neuter.

- **брат** [brat] — brother (masculine). **Мій брат.** [mij brat] — My brother.
- **сестра** [seˈstrɑ] — sister (feminine). **Моя сестра.** [mɔˈjɑ seˈstrɑ] — My sister.
- **вікно** [ʋikˈnɔ] — window (neuter). **Моє вікно.** [mɔˈjɛ ʋikˈnɔ] — My window.

> [!context]
> Do not think about the object's personality or physical traits. Just look at the grammar rule. A table is masculine. A house is masculine. In Ukrainian, we call them «he» (**він** [ʋin]). **Я розумію.** [jɑ rɔzuˈmiju] (I understand.)

### Cracking the Code: The Power of Endings
You might think that memorizing the gender of every word is hard. But I have a secret for you! You can crack this code very easily. You do not need to memorize every single noun. You just look at the end of the word. The ending reveals the gender 95% of the time. To help you remember, we often use colors as visual anchors. We use Blue for masculine, Red for feminine, and Yellow for neuter. Masculine words usually end in a consonant. This is a «stop» sound like «t» or «m». Feminine words usually end in the vowels -а or -я. Neuter words usually end in the vowels -о or -е. This is our «round» rule. **Це правда!** [tsɛ ˈprɑu̯dɑ] (This is true!) **Це дуже просто** [tsɛ ˈduʒɛ ˈprɔstɔ] (this is very simple).

- **дім** [dim] — house (masculine). **Це мій дім.** [tsɛ mij dim] — This is my house.
- **книга** [ˈknɪɦɑ] — book (feminine). **Це моя книга.** [tsɛ mɔˈjɑ ˈknɪɦɑ] — This is my book.
- **місто** [ˈmisto] — city (neuter). **Це моє місто.** [tsɛ mɔˈjɛ ˈmisto] — This is my city.

**Usage Note:**
Biological sex is the only rule that overrides the endings. For example, a father is a male person. Even though the word ends in -о, it belongs to the masculine family because he is a man. This is why **тато** [ˈtɑtɔ] (father) is masculine. **Це книга?** [tsɛ ˈknɪɦɑ] (Is this a book?) **Так, це книга.** [tɑk tsɛ ˈknɪɦɑ] (Yes, this is a book.)

### The Instinct Test: Guessing New Words
Let's try a small test of your new intuition. Imagine you are at a local cafe in Kyiv. You are looking at a menu with new words. You can use the «ending rule» to guess their gender immediately. Take a look at the ending first. What is the gender of «піца» [ˈpitsɑ] (pizza)? It ends in -а, so it must be feminine! What is the gender of «таксі» [tɑkˈsi] (taxi)? This is a loanword from another language. Most words you encounter will follow our simple code. You will soon start to «feel» the language. It feels like meeting new neighbors. You see their faces and you instinctively know their names. In Ukrainian, you see the ending and you know the gender. This code helps you speak better because it affects your adjectives and pronouns later. **Успіхів!** [ˈuspixʲiu̯] (Good luck!)

- **мама** [ˈmɑmɑ] — mother (feminine). **Це моя мама.** [tsɛ mɔˈjɑ ˈmɑmɑ] — This is my mother.
- **тато** [ˈtɑtɔ] — father (masculine). **Це мій тато.** [tsɛ mij ˈtɑtɔ] — This is my father.
- **серце** [ˈsɛrtse] — heart (neuter). **Це моє серце.** [tsɛ mɔˈjɛ ˈsɛrtse] — This is my heart.

> [!tip]
> Think of the word endings as a uniform. Masculine words wear a consonant. Feminine words wear the letter -а. Neuter words wear the letter -о.
