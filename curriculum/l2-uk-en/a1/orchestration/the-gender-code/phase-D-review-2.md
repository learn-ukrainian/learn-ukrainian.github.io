## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Dense Theory Block Without Practice Breaks (Pedagogy/Beginner Safety)
- **Location**: Lines 142-216 / Section «Практика: Чотири сім'ї та винятки»
- **Problem**: This section presents 4 declension families with multiple exceptions across 74 lines of continuous theory. Between the interactive self-check on line 135 («Спробуйте зараз!») and the Production section on line 221, the learner encounters ~1000 words of new grammatical concepts without any practice opportunity. For A1 module 3, this violates the "≤2 concepts before practice" guideline — the section presents 4 families plus 4 named exceptions (Микола/собака, день, тато, ім'я).
- **Fix**: Insert a mini-exercise after Family 2 (line 189) before continuing to Families 3-4. Something like: "Quick check — which family does **сестра** belong to? What about **вікно**?" This breaks the theory block into two manageable halves.

### Issue 2: Misleading "Doubled Consonant + -я = Neuter" Pattern
- **Location**: Line 285 / Section «Культурний контекст: Жива мова»
- **Original**: «Укриття [uˈkrɪtːʲɑ] (Shelter) — Ends in '-я' after a double consonant. It is **Neuter** — a place, a state of safety.»
- **Problem**: Lines 285 and 295 present "doubled consonant + -я" as a neuter indicator. However, this pattern is not reliable — «суддя» (judge) has doubled дд + -я but is masculine. At A1, introducing an unreliable pattern as if it were a rule creates future confusion. The actual pattern is that deverbative nouns with the -ття/-ззя suffix are neuter, not all "doubled consonant + -я" words.
- **Fix**: Change line 285 to: «**Укриття** [uˈkrɪtːʲɑ] (Shelter) — Ends in '-тя', a Neuter ending for this type of noun. It is **Neuter** — a place, a state of safety.» On line 295, change to: «**Запоріжжя** (Zaporizhzhia) is Neuter (-жжя ending, like *укриття*)» — removing the generalized "doubled consonant" claim.

### Issue 3: Quiz Activity Overlap
- **Location**: Activities file, activities 3 (lines 62-198) and 9 (lines 494-630)
- **Problem**: Quiz "Теорія роду" and quiz "Пастки та винятки" share 5 overlapping target nouns: собака (Q in act 3 line 74 not directly, but in act 9 line 507), ім'я (act 3 line 119, act 9 line 518), ніч (act 3 line 131, act 9 line 529), день (act 9 line 540), тато (act 3 line 108). Both quizzes use identical 4-option MCQ format. The second quiz adds чоловік, жінка, брат, сестра, серце, дім — mostly standard nouns without real "traps." This dilutes the "Пастки та винятки" (Traps and Exceptions) concept.
- **Fix**: Remove the standard-gender items from quiz 9 (брат, мама, сестра, дім, серце) and replace with genuinely tricky items: «любов» (F despite consonant ending), «кімната» → already standard, «Микола» (M despite -а ending), «теля» (N despite -я ending). Keep the quiz focused on actual exceptions.

### Issue 4: Missing Plan-Mandated Adjective Collocations
- **Location**: Section «Презентація: Три кити роду» (lines 58-140)
- **Problem**: The plan's content_outline explicitly requires: "Syntactic Agreement: How gender dictates the form of adjectives and pronouns — examples with 'великий стіл' (M), 'цікава книга' (F), and 'чисте вікно' (N)." None of these three collocations appear anywhere in the content. The content uses other adj+noun phrases (добрий день, велике місто, тиха ніч, рідна земля, добре серце, рідний брат, ясне сонце) but skips the plan's specific triple that demonstrates agreement across all three genders with the core vocabulary.
- **Fix**: Add to section «Презентація: Три кити роду» after the comparison table (line 133): "Notice how adjectives also follow the code: **великий стіл** (big table — masculine), **цікава книга** (interesting book — feminine), **чисте вікно** (clean window — neuter). The adjective ending changes to match the noun's gender!"
