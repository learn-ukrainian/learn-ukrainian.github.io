## Де це? (Where Is It?)

When someone asks you where a building is, giving the exact street address is not always necessary. Often, people just want to know the general direction or distance. You can provide this information using specific location words.

Here are the most essential location words you need to know:

*   **тут** (here)
*   **там** (there)
*   **далеко** (far)
*   **близько** (near / close)
*   **у центрі** (in the center)
*   **на розі** (on the corner)

The first four — тут, там, далеко, близько — are adverbs that never change their form. The last two — у центрі and на розі — are prepositional phrases (preposition + noun) that work the same way in a sentence. You can use any of them after a noun to state its location.

*   Аптека **тут**. *(The pharmacy is here.)*
*   Вокзал **далеко**. *(The train station is far.)*
*   Магазин **близько**. *(The shop is close.)*
*   Кафе **на розі**. *(The café is on the corner.)*
*   Музей **у центрі**. *(The museum is in the center.)*

### Using "Біля" (Near)

If you want to say that one place is near another specific place, you must use the word **біля**. 

However, **біля** is a demanding preposition. It requires the noun that follows it to take the genitive case. You will learn the rules for the genitive case later. For now, it is best to memorize these common combinations as fixed chunks of vocabulary.

Memorize these useful chunks:

*   **біля парку** (near the park)
*   **біля дому** (near the house)
*   **біля університету** (near the university)
*   **біля станції** (near the station)
*   **біля метро** (near the subway)

You can use these chunks to give very helpful directions:

*   Бібліотека **біля парку**. *(The library is near the park.)*
*   Магазин тут, **біля дому**. *(The shop is here, near the house.)*
*   Ресторан **біля університету**. *(The restaurant is near the university.)*

### Describing Your City with "Є"

When you want to tell someone what exists in your city or neighborhood, use the word **є**. You have seen this word before when talking about possession (У мене є...), but it also translates perfectly to the English concept of "there is" or "there are". 

Place **є** right after the location to emphasize what can be found there.

*   У моєму місті **є** великий парк. *(In my city there is a large park.)*
*   У моєму місті **є** два музеї. *(In my city there are two museums.)*
*   Тут **є** хороший ресторан. *(There is a good restaurant here.)*
*   Біля дому **є** супермаркет. *(Near the house there is a supermarket.)*
*   Там **є** стара церква. *(There is an old church there.)*

By combining the word **є** with your new vocabulary, you can give a complete tour of your environment in Ukrainian!

| **REJECT** | Has any critical finding — fundamental problems requiring rewrite |

### Step 7: Fix it yourself (REVISE only)

If your verdict is **REVISE**, you MUST output a `<fixes>` block with exact find/replace pairs. The pipeline applies these deterministically — no LLM regeneration, no rewriting from scratch.

Rules for fixes:
- Each fix is a YAML entry with `find:` (exact text from the module) and `replace:` (corrected text)
- The `find` string must be an EXACT substring of the module content — copy-paste it
- Keep fixes minimal — change only what's wrong, preserve surrounding text
- Do NOT fix словник/vocabulary tables (those are generated by a downstream tool)
- For word count issues: add content as a new `insert_after:` entry instead of find/replace
