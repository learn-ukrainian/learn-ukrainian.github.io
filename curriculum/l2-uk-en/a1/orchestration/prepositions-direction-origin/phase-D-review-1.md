## Dimension Scoring

### 1. Language Quality

**Score: 7/10**

**Evidence:**

**Issue 1 (HIGH) — Line 386: Unnatural/confusing preposition usage in practice example.**
The morning route narrative reads:
«Я виходжу **з дому**. Я йду **на автобус**. Автобус їде **в центр**.»

The phrase «Я йду на автобус» is problematic at A1. Throughout this module, "на" is taught exclusively for surfaces (`на столі`) and events/activities (`на концерті`, `на роботі`). A bus is neither a surface nor an event. While «на автобус» exists in colloquial speech (meaning "to catch the bus"), it contradicts every rule the module just taught and will confuse A1 learners. Should be «Я йду на зупинку» (to the bus stop) or restructured entirely.

**Issue 2 (MEDIUM) — Line 134: Euphony rule in Ukrainian only without English support.**
«Запам'ятайте евфонію: **у** парку (після приголосної), **в** офісі (після голосної).»

This is a grammar rule compressed into one Ukrainian sentence with no English explanation. At A1, a learner encountering "після приголосної" (after a consonant) for the first time will not understand what this means without English scaffolding. The rule itself is correct, but it needs context: "Choose **у** when the previous word ends in a consonant, **в** when it ends in a vowel."

**Issue 3 (LOW) — IPA discrepancy for "звідки".**
Content line 26 shows `[ˈzʋʲidkɪ]` while vocabulary sidecar line 12 shows `[ˈzʲʋʲidkɪ]`. The consonant "з" before "в" is not palatalized in standard pronunciation; the content version is correct. The vocab sidecar needs correction.

**Positive:** No Russianisms detected. No colonial framing. Ukrainian grammar is accurate across all case forms. The в/на + Locative and в/на + Accusative distinctions are well-explained. Euphony variants з/із/зі are correctly presented (line 258-262).

## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1 (HIGH): «Я йду на автобус» contradicts taught rules
- **Location:** Content line 386, section «Практика: Маршрут по місту»
- **Problem:** «Я йду **на автобус**» uses "на" with a vehicle, which contradicts the module's own container/surface/event framework. A bus is not a surface or event. While colloquially acceptable, this will confuse A1 learners who just learned when to use "на".
- **Fix:** Replace with «Я йду на зупинку» (I go to the bus stop) — "зупинка" is an open/functional space that fits the "на" rule.

### Issue 2 (HIGH): «студенти ... до школи» — wrong word
- **Location:** Activities file line 300, quiz "Йти чи Їхати?" item 4
- **Problem:** «Вранці всі студенти ... до школи на уроки.» In Ukrainian, «студенти» attend university; «учні» attend school. This is an unnatural collocation.
- **Fix:** Change «студенти» to «учні».

### Issue 3 (MEDIUM): Internal inconsistency about Куди? case mapping
- **Location:** Content line 46 vs line 70
- **Problem:** Line 46 says «**Куди?** -> **Accusative Case** or **Genitive Case**» but the Decision Table at line 70 maps Куди → Accusative only (omitting до + Genitive). Learners using the table as reference will miss the до + Gen pattern.
- **Fix:** Either (a) add a row to the Decision Table for до + Genitive, or (b) remove the Genitive mention from line 46 and add a footnote when до is introduced in section «Напрямок до людини» that до is a special case.

### Issue 4 (MEDIUM): Missing Slipper Culture cultural hook
- **Location:** Content line 159-166 (section «Місце: Де? (Локатив)», subsection «Культурний аспект: Вдома»)
- **Problem:** Both the plan and meta specify slipper culture as a key cultural hook for «вдома». The content treats «вдома» as pure grammar without any cultural context.
- **Fix:** Add 2-3 sentences about the shoes-off culture: "In Ukraine, «вдома» means more than just 'at home'. When you enter a Ukrainian home, you take off your shoes — the host will offer you slippers (капці). This boundary between outside and inside is reflected in the language."

### Issue 5 (MEDIUM): Euphony rule without English support
- **Location:** Content line 134 (section «Місце: Де? (Локатив)»)
- **Problem:** «Запам'ятайте евфонію: **у** парку (після приголосної), **в** офісі (після голосної).» is entirely in Ukrainian with no English gloss. A1 learners encountering "після приголосної" for the first time won't understand.
- **Fix:** Add English: "Remember the euphony rule: use **у** when the previous word ends in a consonant, **в** when it ends in a vowel."
