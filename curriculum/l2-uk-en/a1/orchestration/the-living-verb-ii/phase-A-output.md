# Дослідження: The Living Verb II

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Indicative mood — present, past, future tenses; conjugation I/II; reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)"
Alignment: Introduces Class II (-ити) verb conjugation in the present tense, covering regular endings, consonant mutations, and high-frequency irregulars.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|-------|------------------|
| говорити | Top 100 frequency. Core to 'Hospitality Triad'. | говорити українською, говорити правду |
| робити | Top 50 frequency. Mutation: я роблю. | робити домашнє завдання, робити покупки |
| любити | Shares PIE root (*lewdh-) with 'люди'. | я люблю тебе, любити природу |
| їсти | Irregular: я їм, ти їси, він їсть. | хотіти їсти, смачно їсти |

## Cultural Hooks
1. The Triad of Hospitality: In Ukrainian culture, social connection is built around "їсти" (eating), "пити" (drinking), and "говорити" (speaking).
2. The word "любити" (to love) shares an ancient Indo-European root with "люди" (people), reflecting the idea that love is linked to belonging to a community.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Conjugation Mixing → ти робиш (NOT ти робеш) — Applying Class I (-ати) endings to Class II (-ити) verbs.
2. Mutation Omission → я ходжу, я сиджу (NOT я ходю, я сидю) — Forgetting to mutate the consonant in the first person singular form.
3. Irregular Overgeneralization → я їм (NOT я їджу/їстю) — Attempting to regularize the highly irregular verb 'їсти'.

## Cross-References
- Builds on: a1-15 (The Living Verb I)
- Prepares for: a1-17 (Reflexive Verbs), a1-21 (Past Tense)

## Notes for Content Writing
- Remember A1 constraints: DO NOT use imperative forms yet (they are introduced in M47). Use indirect requests or English for instructions.
- Ensure clear visual comparison between Class I (-ати) and Class II (-ити) endings.
- Keep the distinction between "бачити" (faculty of seeing) and "дивитися" (process of watching) simple and practical.
