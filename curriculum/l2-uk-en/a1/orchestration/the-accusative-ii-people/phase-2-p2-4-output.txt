## Культурний погляд

### Друг чи просто знайомий?

In Ukrainian culture, the word **друг** [druɦ] (friend) has a very special weight. It is not just someone you know from Facebook or work. A **друг** is a person you trust completely. It is a deep relationship.

For people you know but are not close with, we use the word **знайомий** [znaˈjɔmɪj] (acquaintance). If you call a casual contact a "friend," it might sound unnatural or too intense.

Think of it like circles of trust:
1.  **Сім’я** (Family) — мама, тато, брат, сестра.
2.  **Друзі** (Friends) — your close circle.
3.  **Знайомі** (Acquaintances) — neighbors, colleagues, people you met once.

> [!culture]
> **The Inner Circle**
> Ukrainians are very warm and hospitable, but we protect our inner circle. When you say **«Я маю друга»** [ja ˈmaju ˈdruɦa], it means you have a brother-in-spirit. For everyone else, say **«Я маю знайомого»** [ja ˈmaju znaˈjɔmoɦo].

Grammatically, both words are animate nouns. They describe people. So they follow the rule we just learned:
*   **Я бачу друга.** [ja ˈbatʃʊ ˈdruɦa] (I see a friend.) — *Masculine Change (-а)*
*   **Я бачу знайомого.** [ja ˈbatʃʊ znaˈjɔmoɦo] (I see an acquaintance.) — *Masculine Change (-ого)*

### The "Name Game"

In Ukraine, we rarely use full official names in daily life. Official names like **Олександр** [olekˈsandr] or **Катерина** [kateˈrɪna] are for passports and documents.

In conversation, friends and family use **diminutives** (short, affectionate forms). This signals closeness. And here is the important grammar point: **names change too**. When you talk about a person, their name goes into the Accusative case!

Let's look at two popular names:

**1. Олександр (Alexander) → Сашко (Alex)**
*   **Сашко** [saʃˈkɔ] ends in **-о**. It acts like a Masculine noun.
*   In the Accusative, we add **-а**.
*   **Я знаю Сашка.** [ja ˈznaju saʃˈka] (I know Sashko.)
*   **Я бачу Сашка.** [ja ˈbatʃʊ saʃˈka] (I see Sashko.)

**2. Катерина (Katherine) → Катя (Kate)**
*   **Катя** [ˈkatʲa] ends in **-я**. It acts like a Feminine noun.
*   In the Accusative, we change **-я** to **-ю**.
*   **Я знаю Катю.** [ja ˈznaju ˈkatʲu] (I know Katya.)
*   **Я люблю Катю.** [ja lʲuˈblʲu ˈkatʲu] (I love Katya.)

### How to Ask About People

When you want to ask about someone, you usually use the verb **знати** [ˈznatɪ] (to know). This is polite and direct. Remember to use the Accusative case for the name or title of the person.

Imagine you are at a party or a meeting. You can ask:

*   **Ти знаєш Андрія?** [tɪ ˈznajeʃ anˈdrija] (Do you know Andriy?)
*   **Ти знаєш лікаря?** [tɪ ˈznajeʃ ˈlʲikarʲa] (Do you know the doctor?)
*   **Ти знаєш маму?** [tɪ ˈznajeʃ ˈmamʊ] (Do you know Mom?)

If you ask "Ти знаєш Андрій?" (Nominative), it sounds like "Do you know Andriy (the word)?" or it sounds like a mistake. Using the Accusative shows you respect the grammar and the person!
