## Практика: Робота з формою

### Хто? чи Що? (Animate vs Inanimate)
Just like cats and dogs, all people are Heart Words. In Ukrainian, we first ask a simple question to sort our vocabulary. We ask: **Хто це?** — [xtɔ tsɛ] (Who is this?). If the answer is a person or an animal, it is an **істота** (animate). If we ask **Що це?** — [ʃtɔ tsɛ] (What is this?), it is a **неістота** (inanimate). This distinction is vital because animate masculine nouns change their form to match the Genitive case.

**Розрізняємо слова** (Distinguishing words).
Think of your family. Your **мама** (mother) and **брат** (brother) have hearts. They are alive. But your **дім** (house) or **стіл** (table) are "Stone Words". They do not have feelings or hearts. They do not change when you see them or know them. For people, you must always look for the heart. **Це істота? Так.** — [tsɛ iˈstɔtɑ tɑk] (Is this an animate? Yes.)

_Приклад:_
- **Хто це? Це брат.** — [xtɔ tsɛ tsɛ brɑt] (Who is this? This is the brother.)
- **Що це? Це стіл.** — [ʃtɔ tsɛ tsɛ stil] (What is this? This is the table.)
- **Я бачу брата.** — [jɑ ˈbɑtʃʊ ˈbrɑtɑ] (I see the brother.)
- **Я бачу стіл.** — [jɑ ˈbɑtʃʊ stil] (I see the table.)

`[!tip]` **Запитуємо правильно** (Asking correctly).
Use **«Хто?»** for every person. Use **«Що?»** for every thing. This helps you choose the right ending. It is a simple logic for your brain.

### Від суб'єкта до об'єкта (From Subject to Object)
To talk about people correctly, we must change the subject into an object. **Ми змінюємо форму.** — [mɪ ˈzmʲinʲʊjɛmɔ ˈfɔrmʊ] (We change the form.) For masculine people like a **брат** (brother) or a **друг** (friend), we usually add the **-а** or **-я** ending. This is the same "Heart Word" rule we saw with animals earlier. If the base word ends in a hard consonant, we add **-а**. If it ends in a soft sound or a «-ь», we use **-я**.

**Робимо об'єкт** (Making an object).
This rule works for family members and professional jobs. A **лікар** (doctor) or a **вчитель** (teacher) is a person. So they change form to show they are the target of your action. Feminine people like **сестра** (sister) or a **дитина** (child) are different. They always use the **-у** or **-ю** ending. This is the same standard pattern as for objects like «книга».

_Приклад:_
- **Вчитель** (teacher) → **Я знаю вчителя.** — [jɑ ˈznɑjʊ ˈʋtʃɪtɛlʲɑ] (I know the teacher.)
- **Лікар** (doctor) → **Ти бачиш лікаря.** — [tɪ ˈbɑtʃɪʃ ˈlikɑrʲɑ] (You see the doctor.)
- **Студент** (student) → **Ми чекаємо студента.** — [mɪ tʃɛˈkɑjɛmɔ stʊˈdɛntɑ] (We are waiting for the student.)

| Суб'єкт (Хто?) | Об'єкт (Кого?) | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Тато** | **Тата** | Father |
| **Брат** | **Брата** | Brother |
| **Друг** | **Друга** | Friend (m.) |
| **Лікар** | **Лікаря** | Doctor |
| **Сестра** | **Сестру** | Sister |

### Дієслова та люди (Verbs and People)
Now we can combine our new forms with high-frequency verbs. **Ми використовуємо дієслова.** — [mɪ ʋɪkɔrɪˈstɔʋʊjɛmɔ dʲijɛˈslɔʋɑ] (We use verbs.) Some verbs are like magnets for people. We use **бачити** (to see), **знати** (to know), and **любити** (to love) every day. In Ukrainian, we also use the verb **чекати** (to wait for) with the direct object. This is a common point where English speakers get confused.

**Будуємо речення** (Building sentences).
When you use these verbs, the person is the target. You must use the "Heart Word" ending to be correct. If you forget, your sentence sounds like you are talking about a piece of furniture! Remember that **чекати** does not need the English word "for". The object comes right after the verb in its Accusative form.

_Приклад:_
- **Я люблю маму і тата.** — [jɑ lʲʊˈblʲʊ ˈmɑmʊ i ˈtɑtɑ] (I love mom and dad.)
- **Ми знаємо сусіда.** — [mɪ ˈznɑjɛmɔ sʊˈsidɑ] (We know the neighbor.)
- **Вона чекає подругу.** — [ʋɔˈnɑ tʃɛˈkɑjɛ ˈpɔdrʊɦʊ] (She is waiting for a friend.)

`[!warning]` **Не забувайте закінчення!** (Do not forget the ending!)
Always check if the person is an object. If you say «Я бачу лікар», it sounds strange. You must say **«Я бачу лікаря»**. This shows you respect the "Heart Word"!
