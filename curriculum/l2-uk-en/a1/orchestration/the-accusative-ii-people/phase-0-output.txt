## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Name Game (Diminutives)**: Ukrainians rarely use full official names in close relationships. "Олександр" becomes "Сашко" or "Саша", "Володимир" becomes "Володя", "Катерина" becomes "Катя". Using a diminutive signals closeness.
2. **Friend vs. Acquaintance**: The word **друг** carries significant weight in Ukrainian culture, implying a deep bond of trust and long history. Casual contacts are strictly **знайомий** (acquaintance). Calling a casual acquaintance "друг" can sound unnatural or overly familiar.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The "Inanimate Trap" (Masculine)**: Saying "Я бачу брат" instead of "Я бачу брата". Learners apply the inanimate rule (Nominative = Accusative) to people because they learned "стіл/телефон" first.
2. **The "Universal No-Change" Error**: Forgetting that feminine nouns change (-а → -у) *regardless* of animacy, or confusing the rules and thinking feminine animate nouns behave differently than feminine inanimate ones (they don't; both take -у).
3. **Pronoun Case Failure**: Using Nominative pronouns as objects (e.g., "Я знаю він" instead of "Я знаю його").
