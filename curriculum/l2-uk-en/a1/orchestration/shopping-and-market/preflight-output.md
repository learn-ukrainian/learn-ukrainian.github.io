Welcome back! In our last module, we explored the delicious world of **борщ** (borscht) and **картопля** (potatoes). But before you can cook that feast, you need to know how to get your ingredients! Today, we are heading to the **магазин** (store) and the **ринок** (market). You will learn how to ask for prices, use Ukrainian currency, and order exactly the right amount of everything you need. Let’s get shopping!

## Скільки коштує? (How much?)

The most important question in any shopping trip is, of course, "How much does it cost?" In Ukrainian, we use the verb **коштувати** (to cost). Whether you are looking at a single **яблуко** (apple) or a whole bag of groceries, the pattern is simple and consistent.

To ask for the price of a specific item, use the phrase **Скільки коштує...?** (How much does... cost?). If you are just pointing at something, you can say **Скільки це коштує?** (How much does this cost?).

> **Скільки коштує цей хліб?** — How much does this bread cost?
> **Скільки коштує молоко?** — How much does the milk cost?
> **Скільки це коштує?** — How much does this cost?

When answering, we use the same verb. For a single price, we say **Це коштує...** (It costs...). Prices in Ukraine are measured in **гривня** (hryvnia). Just like in our previous lesson on numbers, the word **гривня** changes its ending depending on how many you have!

| Number | Pattern | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **гривня** | **одна гривня** (1 hryvnia) |
| **2, 3, 4** | **гривні** | **дві гривні** (2 hryvnias) |
| **5 – 20** | **гривень** | **п'ять гривень** (5 hryvnias) |

Remember, the numbers 11 through 14 always follow the **гривень** pattern, even though they end in 1, 2, 3, or 4! Once you hit 21, the pattern resets based on the last digit. Let's look at some full sentences you might hear or say:

* **Хліб коштує п'ятнадцять гривень.** — Bread costs fifteen hryvnias.
* **Це коштує сорок дві гривні.** — This costs forty-two hryvnias.
* **Борщ коштує сімдесят гривень.** — Borscht costs seventy hryvnias.
* **Молоко коштує тридцять одну гривню.** — Milk costs thirty-one hryvnias.

## У магазині (In the Store)

When you enter a **магазин** (store) or walk up to a stall on the **ринок** (market), you will want to be polite. The most common way to ask for something is to use the phrase **Дайте, будь ласка...** (Give me, please...). If you have a specific goal in mind, you can say **Я хочу купити...** (I want to buy...).

If you aren't sure if the store has what you need, use the word **є** (there is/are). You might ask **У вас є...?** (Do you have...?) or simply **Тут є...?** (Is there... here?). Be careful! Many learners try to use the verb **мати** (to have), but in a shopping context, Ukrainians almost always use the **є** construction to check availability.

> **— Добрий день! У вас є яблука?** — Good day! Do you have apples?
> **— Так, є. Які ви хочете?** — Yes, we do. Which ones do you want?
> **— Дайте, будь ласка, ці червоні яблука.** — Give me, please, those red apples.
> **— Скільки вони коштують?** — How much do they cost?
> **— Двадцять гривень.** — Twenty hryvnias.

Shopping in Ukraine has a very specific flow. It starts with a greeting, followed by checking for your item, asking the price, paying, and finally, a warm thank you. Here is how a full transaction looks:

1. **Greeting:** **Добрий день!** (Good day!)
2. **The Request:** **Я хочу купити сир.** (I want to buy cheese.)
3. **The Price:** **Скільки він коштує?** (How much does it cost?)
4. **The Payment:** **Ось сорок гривень.** (Here is forty hryvnias.)
5. **The Change:** **Ваша решта — п'ять гривень.** (Your change is five hryvnias.)
6. **The Closing:** **Дякую! До побачення!** (Thank you! Goodbye!)

You can often pay with a **картка** (card) in most modern shops. Just ask: **Можна карткою?** (May I [pay] by card?).

## Кількість та одиниці (Quantities and Units)

Sometimes "one" isn't enough. You might need a **кілограм** (kilogram) of potatoes or a **літр** (liter) of milk. When we use these units of measurement, the product itself usually goes into the **Genitive** case (the "of" case).

Think of it as "a bottle **of** water" or "a packet **of** sugar." Here are the most common containers and units you will find in a Ukrainian **магазин**:

* **кілограм** (kilogram) — **кілограм яблук** (a kg of apples)
* **літр** (liter) — **літр молока** (a liter of milk)
* **пачка** (packet/pack) — **пачка цукру** (a pack of sugar)
* **пляшка** (bottle) — **пляшка води** (a bottle of water)
* **штука** (piece/item) — **одна штука** (one piece)

Just like with the currency **гривня**, these measurement units change their endings depending on the number. 

| Amount | Ending for Masculine (**кілограм**) | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **(base form)** | **один кілограм** |
| **2, 3, 4** | **-и** | **два кілограми** |
| **5+** | **-ів** | **п'ять кілограмів** |

For feminine units like **пачка** and **пляшка**, the pattern is:
* **одна пачка / пляшка**
* **дві пачки / пляшки**
* **п'ять пачок / пляшок**

When you combine these, you get very precise shopping requests. Practice saying these patterns out loud:
* **Я хочу купити два літри соку.** — I want to buy two liters of juice.
* **Дайте, будь ласка, три пачки масла.** — Give me, please, three packs of butter.
* **Мені потрібна одна пляшка води.** — I need one bottle of water.

## Засоби гігієни (Hygiene Products)

Shopping isn't just about food! Sometimes you need to find the hygiene aisle. Whether you are at a large supermarket or a smaller pharmacy, these words are essential for your daily routine.

If you are looking for these items, you can use the same location questions we learned earlier: **Де можна купити...?** (Where can one buy...?) or **Де тут магазин?** (Where is a store here?).

* **мило** (soap) — **Це мило коштує десять гривень.** (This soap costs 10 hryvnias.)
* **шампунь** (shampoo) — **У вас є цей шампунь?** (Do you have this shampoo?) *Note: shampoo is masculine in Ukrainian!*
* **рушник** (towel) — **Я хочу купити гарний рушник.** (I want to buy a beautiful towel.)
* **зубна паста** (toothpaste) — **Мені потрібна пачка зубної пасти.** (I need a pack of toothpaste.)
* **туалетний папір** (toilet paper) — **Де тут туалетний папір?** (Where is the toilet paper here?)

If you can't find a big store, look for an **аптека** (pharmacy). They often carry **мило**, **шампунь**, and other hygiene essentials. 

> **— Вибачте, де можна купити мило?** — Excuse me, where can one buy soap?
> **— Там є магазин "Космо".** — There is a "Kosmo" store there.
> **— Дякую!** — Thank you!

## Практика (Practice)

Let's put everything together in a few real-world scenarios. Imagine you are at the **ринок** (market). The atmosphere is loud, colorful, and very friendly!

**At the Market (На ринку)**
> **— Добрий день! Скільки коштують ці яблука?** — Good day! How much do these apples cost?
> **— Двадцять п'ять гривень за кілограм.** — Twenty-five hryvnias per kilogram.
> **— Дайте, будь ласка, два кілограми.** — Give me, please, two kilograms.
> **— Ось, прошу. Це п'ятдесят гривень.** — Here you go. That's fifty hryvnias.
> **— Дякую. А у вас є картопля?** — Thanks. And do you have potatoes?
> **— Так, є. Ось вона.** — Yes, we do. Here they are.

Now, imagine you are at a modern supermarket **магазин**. You need a few household items and you want to pay with your **картка**.

**At the Store (У магазині)**
> **— Вибачте, де тут шампунь?** — Excuse me, where is the shampoo here?
> **— Ось тут, поруч із милом.** — Right here, next to the soap.
> **— Дякую. Я хочу купити цей шампунь і рушник.** — Thanks. I want to buy this shampoo and a towel.
> **— Скільки це коштує?** — How much does this cost?
> **— Сто п'ятнадцять гривень.** — One hundred and fifteen hryvnias.
> **— Можна карткою?** — Can I pay by card?
> **— Так, звичайно.** — Yes, of course.
