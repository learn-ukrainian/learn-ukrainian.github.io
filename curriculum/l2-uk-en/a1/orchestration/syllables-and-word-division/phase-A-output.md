# Дослідження: Syllables and Word Division

## State Standard Reference
§4.1.4: "Vowel and consonant sounds"
Alignment: Understanding vowels is the fundamental prerequisite for Ukrainian syllable division, as every syllable must contain exactly one vowel. This module bridges phonetic awareness into structural reading.

## Cultural Hooks
1. Calligraphy and handwriting (каліграфія) are still traditionally emphasized in Ukrainian schools, making correct word division (переніс) an important everyday skill for native speakers.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Splitting consonant clusters like English** → Correct form: *се-стра* (not *сес-тра*). English speakers naturally try to close syllables, but Ukrainian pushes consonants forward to maximize open syllables.
2. **Leaving single letters at the end of a line** → Correct form: keeping the word together or moving more letters, e.g., *Ук-раїна* (not *У-країна*). English hyphenation rules allow some single-letter syllables, whereas Ukrainian strictly forbids leaving or transferring a single letter.
3. **Separating the soft sign (ь) or apostrophe** → Correct form: *паль-ці*, *сі-м'я* (not *пал-ьці*, *сім-'я*). Learners often treat these as standalone markers rather than modifiers attached to the preceding consonant.
