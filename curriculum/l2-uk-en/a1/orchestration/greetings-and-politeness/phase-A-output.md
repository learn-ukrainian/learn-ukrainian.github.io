## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Ви" Boundary**: In Ukrainian culture, switching from formal "Ви" to informal "ти" is a significant relational step, often requiring explicit permission ("Давай на ти?"). Using "ти" with elders or strangers is viewed as highly disrespectful.
2. **Handshake Etiquette**: In formal settings, handshakes are standard, but cultural superstition dictates that one should never shake hands across a threshold (через поріг), as it is believed to bring bad luck or arguments.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Using "Привіт" formally** → "Добрий день" — Learners often overuse "Привіт" with strangers or in shops, which sounds overly familiar or rude.
2. **Literal Translation of "I am"** → "Мене звати..." — Beginners say "Я є Джон" instead of the natural Ukrainian "Мене звати Джон" (literally: "They call me John").
3. **Confusing "Вибачте" and "Перепрошую"** → Learners may struggle with nuances; "Вибачте" is standard for quick attention/minor apology, while "Перепрошую" can feel slightly more formal or sincere.
