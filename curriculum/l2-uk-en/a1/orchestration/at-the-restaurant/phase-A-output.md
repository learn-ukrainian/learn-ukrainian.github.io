# Дослідження: At the Restaurant

Alignment: This module fulfills A1 thematic requirements for dining out, while practically applying the Accusative case for direct objects and the Future tense of "бути" for placing orders.

|------|-------|------------------|
| замовити | High frequency verb | замовити страву, замовити столик |
| рахунок | Essential noun; watch for Russian interference | попросити рахунок, оплатити рахунок |

| перше / друге | Nouns used as meal categories | перша страва, друга страва, гарнір |

## Cultural Hooks
1. Traditional Ukrainian dining structures meals into three courses: «Перше» (soups like borshch), «Друге» (meat/fish + гарнір), and «Третє» (drinks/dessert/uzvar).
2. The universal Ukrainian toast is «Будьмо!» (Let us be!), frequently met with the rhythmic response «Гей!». It is used across all settings and beverages.
3. Tipping («чайові») is universally 10%; while QR code payments (like Expirenza) are very common, leaving cash is still appreciated by staff.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Nominative instead of Accusative → «Я буду кава» ❌ becomes «Я буду каву» ✅ — English speakers often forget to decline the direct object when ordering.
2. Russian interference with vocabulary → using «счёт» ❌ instead of the correct Ukrainian «рахунок» ✅ for the bill.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Focus heavily on the «Я буду + [Accusative]» structure as the practical anchor of the module.
- Keep dialogues short and practical; use English scaffolding as needed for A1 grammar explanations.
- Include modern dietary vocabulary («без м'яса», «без глютену») as they are highly useful for foreigners in urban Ukraine.
