# Plurals and Alternation

> **Чому це важливо? — Why does this matter?**
>
> Welcome to the next big step in your journey. So far, we have talked about one thing at a time. To describe the world accurately, you need to know how to talk about multiple items. Let us see how Ukrainian words adapt when there is more than one.

## Множина іменників (Noun Plurals)

Це студе́нт. (This is a student.)
А це студе́нти. (And these are students.)

In Ukrainian, changing a noun from singular to plural depends entirely on its gender. The rules are very consistent and logical. We simply look at the last letter of the singular word to know exactly how to form the plural. Let us start by looking at masculine nouns.

Masculine nouns usually end in a hard consonant. To make them plural, we simply add the ending **-и**. If the word ends in a soft sign or a soft consonant, we add the letter **-і** instead. This is the most common and predictable pattern you will see.

| Singular | Plural | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| студе́нт | студе́нти | students |
| хло́пець | хло́пці | boys |

Feminine nouns usually end in the vowels **-а** or **-я**. To make them plural, we must remove the final vowel completely. We replace the letter **-а** with **-и**. We replace the letter **-я** with **-і**. Notice how the final vowel simply changes to a new plural vowel.

| Singular | Plural | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| кни́га | кни́ги | books |
| земля́ | зе́млі | lands / soils |

> [!tip]
> A helpful trick for masculine and feminine words: if the original word sounds «hard», the plural ending is **-и**. If the original word sounds «soft», the plural ending is **-і**. They actually share the exact same plural endings!

Neuter nouns behave differently. They usually end in **-о** or **-е**. To form the plural, we swap their characteristic ending. We replace **-о** with **-а**. We replace **-е** with **-я**. You must pay attention, because notice how neuter plurals look a lot like singular feminine nouns!

| Singular | Plural | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| мі́сто | міста́ | cities |
| мо́ре | моря́ | seas |

Some very common words do not follow the regular rules at all. These are irregular plurals. You will hear them every single day, so you just need to memorize them. The singular and plural forms can look completely different from each other.

| Singular | Plural | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| дити́на | ді́ти | children |
| люди́на | лю́ди | people |
| о́ко | о́чі | eyes |

Там гра́ють ді́ти. (Children are playing there.)
Це до́брі лю́ди. (These are good people.)

Let us practice these new patterns. Look at the word and identify its gender. Then apply the correct rule carefully. We drop the ending vowel if there is one. We add the new plural vowel. It takes a little practice to remember the neuter endings. Always remember that the letter **-о** changes to **-а**.

## Чергування (Vowel Alternation)

Sometimes, forming the plural is not just about changing the ending of the word. The letters inside the word can change too. In Ukrainian, vowels inside the word stem sometimes alternate. The most famous example of this is called the «fleeting і».

When a syllable is closed, meaning it ends in a consonant, it very often contains the vowel **і**. When we add a plural ending, we add a brand new vowel to the word. This opens the syllable. When the syllable finally opens, the vowel **і** often changes back to **о** or **е**.

Let us look at the shift from **і** to **о**.

Це мій кіт. (This is my cat.)
Там сидя́ть коти́. (Cats are sitting there.)

Here, the singular word **кіт** has a closed syllable. When we add the plural ending **-и**, the syllable becomes open. The vowel **і** immediately changes to **о**. This is a historical vowel change that happens very often.

| Singular | Plural | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| кіт | коти́ | cats |
| рік | роки́ | years |

Now let us look at the shift from **і** to **е**. The underlying logic is exactly the same as before. The vowel changes simply because the syllable opens up when we add the plural ending.

| Singular | Plural | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| піч | печі́ | ovens |
| ніж | ножі́ | knives |

> [!warning]
> English speakers often make a critical mistake here. They naturally want to simply add an ending to the base word without changing the inside. They often say «кіти» instead of the correct word «коти». Always watch out for the vowel **і** in short words!

How can you confidently predict this? Here is a simple and effective strategy. Check monosyllabic masculine nouns carefully. If a short masculine word has the vowel **і** in the middle, there is a very high chance it will alternate in the plural form. You will learn to spot these specific words quickly as you read more Ukrainian texts. It becomes second nature over time.

## Множина прикметників (Adjective Plurals)

We have learned how to make nouns plural. But what about adjectives? There is excellent news here. Plural adjectives are much simpler than plural nouns. You will learn this very quickly.

In the singular form, adjectives have different endings for masculine, feminine, and neuter genders. In the plural form, all three genders completely collapse into one single ending. All plural adjectives end in **-і**. If the adjective has a soft stem, it ends in **-ї**.

| Singular | Plural | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| нови́й / нова́ / нове́ | нові́ | new |
| си́ній / си́ня / си́нє | си́ні | blue |

Це нова́ кни́га. (This is a new book.)
Це нові́ кни́ги. (These are new books.)

You must always match the adjective directly with the noun. If the noun is plural, the adjective must be plural too. You cannot use a singular adjective with a plural noun under any circumstances. They must always agree in number.

Ті вели́кі міста́. (Those large cities.)
Там молоді́ студе́нти. (There are young students.)

Let us practice forming simple and correct sentences. We will combine a plural adjective with a plural noun. This is how you build longer descriptions.

У ме́не є нові́ кни́ги. (I have new books.)
Це вели́кі буди́нки. (These are large buildings.)

> [!culture]
> Ukrainian is fundamentally a language of agreement. Words work together like a cohesive team. When one word becomes plural, its describing words must also wear their plural uniforms! This rule makes the entire sentence sound harmonious and perfectly complete.

## Винятки та особливості (Special Cases)

Every language has special cases, and Ukrainian is certainly no exception. Some nouns only exist in the singular form. These are usually uncountable nouns. You simply cannot count them one by one. They represent a mass or a general concept.

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| молоко́ | milk |
| цу́кор | sugar |
| вода́ | water |
| пові́тря | air |

Other nouns exist exclusively in the plural form. They do not have a singular version at all. These unique words are called pluralia tantum. A very important and common word in this group is the word for money.

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| гро́ші | money |
| две́рі | door |
| но́жиці | scissors |

Де мої́ гро́ші? (Where is my money?)

Notice how the concept of money is completely plural in Ukrainian, but it is singular in English. You literally talk about «moneys» when speaking Ukrainian.

Finally, you must watch out for stress shifts. Sometimes the stress unexpectedly moves to a different syllable in the plural form.

Це моя́ рука́. (This is my hand.)
Це мої́ ру́ки. (These are my hands.)

## Підсумок і практика (Summary and Practice)

Let us quickly review the basic rules for plural nouns and adjectives that we just covered.

Masculine and feminine nouns usually take the endings **-и** or **-і**. Neuter nouns usually take the endings **-а** or **-я**. This covers the vast majority of regular nouns you will encounter. All plural adjectives end in **-і** or **-ї**, regardless of gender.

Always watch out for the fleeting **і**. It often changes to **о** or **е** when the syllable opens in plural forms.

Now, test your new knowledge. Try to answer these self-check questions carefully.

1. Make these basic nouns plural: студе́нт, кни́га, мі́сто.
2. What exactly happens to the word **кіт** in the plural form? Why does it change?
3. Combine the adjective **нови́й** and the noun **студе́нти** in the plural form correctly.
4. Which of these common words is only used in the plural: молоко́, гро́ші, цу́кор?

Remember to practice these patterns regularly. Read the Ukrainian examples aloud to get used to the exact sound of plural endings. Learning plurals opens up a whole new world of daily expression!
