# Дослідження: The Accusative I: Things

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.2.1: "Без прийменника (об’єкт дії): Віра читає цікаву книжку."
Alignment: This module directly addresses the requirement to teach the Accusative case for the direct object (об’єкт дії) without prepositions, specifically focusing on inanimate nouns at the A1 level.

## Cultural Hooks
1. The checkout question: «Пакет потрібен?» (Do you need a bag?). This is a universal anchor for the modern Ukrainian supermarket experience, contrasting with the social hub of the traditional «базар».
2. Staple foods: «хліб» (bread) and «гречка» (buckwheat). Bread has profound historical and cultural significance in Ukraine, and buckwheat is the ultimate comfort food and staple, perfectly illustrating the feminine -а → -у pattern (гречка → гречку).

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Conceptual Nominative" → Learners say «Я бачу книга» instead of «Я бачу книгу». Why: Forgetting that objects receive actions and must change their endings.
2. "Masculine Inanimate Confusion" → Learners over-apply endings to masculine inanimate nouns, saying «бачу стола» instead of «бачу стіл», confusing it with the animate rule.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **English Scaffolding:** Required (Immersion target 10-50%). Use English to explicitly define "Direct Object" vs "Subject" before introducing Ukrainian examples.
- **Syntactic Roles:** Introduce the terms *підмет* (subject), *присудок* (predicate), and *додаток* (object) clearly.
- **Decolonized Framing:** Present the Ukrainian case system as logical and elegant. Do not compare it to Russian. Show that while English uses strict word order to show who does what to whom, Ukrainian uses the beautiful flexibility of word endings.
- **IPA:** Provide IPA only on the first occurrence of each new vocabulary word. Do not transcribe full sentences.
