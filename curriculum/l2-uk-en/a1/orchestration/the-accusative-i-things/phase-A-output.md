# Дослідження: The Accusative I: Things

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.2: "Accusative — direct object, with prepositions (в/у, на, за, через, про)"
Alignment: Introduces the foundational use of the Accusative case for direct objects (without prepositions) focusing on inanimate nouns.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|-------|------------------|
| бачити | High frequency core verb | бачити проблему, я бачу... |
| мати | Very high frequency (Core A1) | мати рацію, мати час, мати можливість |
| книга | Paradigm example for Feminine Accusative | читати цікаву книгу |
| хліб | Masculine inanimate safe harbor (no change) | купувати хліб |

## Cultural Hooks
1. The ubiquitous "Пакет потрібен?" (Do you need a bag?) phrase heard at all Ukrainian supermarket checkouts.
2. The contrast between impersonal, modern shopping at a "супермаркет" and the lively, social atmosphere of a traditional "базар".

## Common Learner Errors
1. Conceptual Nominative → Using "Я читаю книга" instead of "Я читаю книгу" — English relies on strict word order for direct objects, so learners often forget to change the ending.
2. Masculine Inanimate Confusion → Saying "бачу стола" instead of "бачу стіл" — mistakenly applying animate accusative or genitive endings to inanimate masculine nouns, which should not change.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Imperative Ban**: As this is module 25 (before M47), do NOT use Ukrainian imperative forms like Запам'ятайте, Подивіться, or Прочитайте. Use English instructions instead (e.g., "Remember that...", "Look at...").
- Ensure the grammatical distinction between Subject (підмет) and Direct Object (додаток) is clearly scaffolded in English before introducing the Ukrainian transformations.
