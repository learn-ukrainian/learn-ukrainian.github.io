## Розповідь про день (Narrating a Day)

To tell a compelling story, you need more than just a list of verbs. You need a structure that helps your listener follow along. In Ukrainian, we use time markers and sequencing words to connect our thoughts into a smooth, natural narrative.

### Time Markers

A typical day is divided into four main parts. These words anchor your story and answer the question **«Коли?»** (When?).

* **Зранку** means "in the morning".
* **Вдень** means "in the afternoon" or "during the day".
* **Ввечері** means "in the evening".
* **Вночі** means "at night".

These time markers structure a sequence of events:
* **Зранку я пив каву.** (In the morning I drank coffee.)
* **Вдень я багато працював.** (In the afternoon I worked a lot.)
* **Ввечері я читав книгу.** (In the evening I read a book.)
* **Вночі я добре спав.** (At night I slept well.)

### Sequencing Words

When you want to emphasize the order of your actions, use sequencing words. These turn isolated sentences into a cohesive story.

* **Спочатку** means "first" or "at first".
* **Потім** means "then" or "after that".
* **Після цього** means "after this".
* **Нарешті** means "finally".

These markers link actions naturally:
* **Спочатку я поснідав.** (First I had breakfast.)
* **Потім я пішов на роботу.** (Then I went to work.)
* **Після цього я обідав.** (After this I had lunch.)
* **Нарешті я пішов додому.** (Finally I went home.)

### Daily Routine Verbs

When narrating a day, a few core verbs do most of the heavy lifting. In the past tense, you must remember that the ending changes based on the gender of the speaker. 

Male speakers use endings with **-в** or **-вся**. Female speakers use endings with **-ла** or **-лася**.

The verb **прокинутися** (to wake up) is a reflexive verb, which is why it has the special ending **-ся** or **-лася**.
* **я прокинувся** (I woke up — male speaker)
* **я прокинулася** (I woke up — female speaker)

The verb **поснідати** (to have breakfast) comes from the noun for breakfast.
* **я поснідав** (I had breakfast — male speaker)
* **я поснідала** (I had breakfast — female speaker)

The verb **обідати** (to have lunch) comes from the noun for lunch.
* **я обідав** (I had lunch — male speaker)
* **я обідала** (I had lunch — female speaker)

The verb **піти** (to go / to set off) is a common action word.
* **я пішов** (I went — male speaker)
* **я пішла** (I went — female speaker)

The verb **повернутися** (to return) is also reflexive.
* **я повернувся** (I returned — male speaker)
* **я повернулася** (I returned — female speaker)

The phrase **лягти спати** (to go to bed) literally translates as "to lie down to sleep". The word **лягти** (to lie down) has an irregular past tense root.
* **я ліг спати** (I went to bed — male speaker)
* **я лягла спати** (I went to bed — female speaker)

## Summary

A compelling story relies on a strong foundation of vocabulary, grammar, and structural markers. Here is the complete toolkit for narrating a day.

**1. Time Structure**
To give your story a clear timeline, break the day into natural segments. These markers let your listener know exactly when an action happened.
* **зранку** (in the morning)
* **вдень** (in the afternoon)
* **ввечері** (in the evening)
* **вночі** (at night)

**2. Sequencing Words**
To show the order of events and move your story forward smoothly, use connecting words. They build a bridge from one sentence to the next.
* **спочатку** (first / at first)
* **потім** (then)
* **після цього** (after that)
* **нарешті** (finally)

**3. Core Daily Routine Verbs (Past Tense)**
Your essential toolkit for describing daily actions includes these highly frequent verbs.
* **прокинувся / прокинулася** (woke up)
* **поснідав / поснідала** (had breakfast)
* **пішов / пішла** (went)
* **обідав / обідала** (had lunch)
* **повернувся / повернулася** (returned)
* **ліг / лягла спати** (went to bed)

**4. Gender Consistency**
The most important grammatical rule when talking about yourself in the past tense is maintaining gender consistency. Your listener relies on these verb endings.

* **Male speakers:** You must use the **-в** or **-вся** ending for every action you performed. If you woke up, ate, and went to work, you say: **Я прокинувся, я поснідав, я пішов.**
* **Female speakers:** You must use the **-ла** or **-лася** ending for every action you performed. If you woke up, ate, and went to work, you say: **Я прокинулася, я поснідала, я пішла.**

:::tip
Always match your past tense verbs to your own gender when narrating a personal story. This consistency is one of the most important markers of natural Ukrainian speech!
:::

| 9 | **Dialogue & conversation quality** | 10% | Dialogues natural and culturally appropriate? Real situations, real responses? Speaker roles clear? Not stilted or textbook-robotic? |

**Note:** Vocabulary tables (словник), video embeds, and external resource links are added by a downstream ENRICH step — do NOT penalize their absence or quality. If you see a vocabulary table with wrong translations, missing words, or formatting issues, that content was NOT written by the writer — it was generated deterministically by the ENRICH step. Do NOT flag словník problems as linguistic errors or deduct points from the writer. Report them separately under "ENRICH issues" in your findings (informational only, not scored).

### Step 4: Output raw scores

Output ONLY the raw 1-10 scores in the table below. Do NOT calculate the weighted total — the pipeline script handles the math deterministically. Just write your per-dimension scores.

### Step 5: List findings

For every issue found, provide:
```
[DIMENSION] [SEVERITY: critical/major/minor]
Location: [specific section/paragraph — quote the actual text]
Issue: [what's wrong]
Fix: [exactly how to fix it]
```

Critical = module cannot ship. Major = quality below standard. Minor = polish item.

### Step 6: Verdict

Base your verdict on the **severity of findings** — the pipeline calculates the weighted score separately.

| Verdict | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **PASS** | Zero critical findings, at most minor issues |
| **REVISE** | Has major findings but no criticals — fixable without rewrite |
| **REJECT** | Has any critical finding — fundamental problems requiring rewrite |

### Step 7: Fix it yourself (REVISE only)

If your verdict is **REVISE**, you MUST output a `<fixes>` block with exact find/replace pairs. The pipeline applies these deterministically — no LLM regeneration, no rewriting from scratch.

Rules for fixes:
- Each fix is a YAML entry with `find:` (exact text from the module) and `replace:` (corrected text)
- The `find` string must be an EXACT substring of the module content — copy-paste it
- Keep fixes minimal — change only what's wrong, preserve surrounding text
- Do NOT fix словник/vocabulary tables (those are generated by a downstream tool)
- For word count issues: add content as a new `insert_after:` entry instead of find/replace
