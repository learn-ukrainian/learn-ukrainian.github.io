One of the most common questions you will hear in any language is "What did you do?". Whether you are catching up with a friend after the weekend, talking to a colleague on Monday morning, or just making small talk, narrating past events is an essential daily skill.

**Антон:** Він **гуляв** у парку. *(He walked in the park.)*


**Софія:** А Олена? *(And Olena?)*


**Антон:** Вона **працювала**. *(She worked.)*

## Минулий час (Past Tense)

In Ukrainian textbooks for grades 3 and 4, students learn a foundational rule about the **минулий час** (past tense). The rule is quite elegant and surprisingly simple: the past tense in Ukrainian focuses on **gender**, not on the person speaking. 

To form the past tense, you start with the infinitive of the verb (the dictionary form). All regular verbs end in **-ти**. You simply remove this **-ти** ending to find the stem, and then you add a specific ending based on the gender of the subject.

Let's take the verb **читати** (to read). We drop the **-ти** and we are left with the stem **чита-**. Now we add the gender endings:

*   For a masculine subject (**він**), we add **-в**: **читав** (he read).
*   For a feminine subject (**вона**), we add **-ла**: **читала** (she read).
*   For a neuter subject (**воно**), we add **-ло**: **читало** (it read).
*   For a plural subject (**вони**), we add **-ли**: **читали** (they read).

This leads to a KEY INSIGHT: the past tense shows GENDER, not person! 

Think about the English sentence "I was reading." In English, the form is the same whether the speaker is a man or a woman. In Ukrainian, it matters. 

*   **Я читав** = I (male) was reading. 
*   **Я читала** = I (female) was reading. 

It is the exact same person (**я**), but a completely different gender ending. The verb must agree with the physical gender of the person performing the action. If you are a man, you will always use the **-в** ending for yourself. If you are a woman, you will always use the **-ла** ending for yourself.

This is a major difference from the present tense. In the present tense, verbs mark the *person* (I, you, he/she/it). 
*   Present: **я читаю**, **ти читаєш**, **він читає** (person endings).

In the past tense, the person does not matter. Only the gender matters.
*   Past: **я / ти / він читав** (masculine gender).
*   Past: **я / ти / вона читала** (feminine gender).

Let's look at another verb: **працювати** (to work).
*   **Він працював.** (He worked.)
*   **Вона працювала.** (She worked.)
*   **Воно працювало.** (It worked. — often used for machines, like a radio or a phone)
*   **Вони працювали.** (They worked.)

Notice that the plural form (**вони**) always takes the **-ли** ending. When you are talking about multiple people, the gender distinction disappears. It does not matter if "they" are a group of men, a group of women, or a mixed group. Plural is always plural: **вони завжди -ли** (they are always -ли). 

At this A1 stage, we do not need to worry about the concept of aspect (completed vs. ongoing actions). For now, we are just learning how to build these fundamental forms.

## Практика (Practice)

Now that we understand the mechanics, let's look at how to form the past tense for the core verbs you already know. Remember the pattern: remove the **-ти**, then add **-в**, **-ла**, **-ло**, or **-ли**.

Here is the complete transformation for the verbs we use every day:

*   **читати** (to read) → **читав** / **читала** / **читало** / **читали**
*   **працювати** (to work) → **працював** / **працювала** / **працювало** / **працювали**
*   **гуляти** (to walk) → **гуляв** / **гуляла** / **гуляло** / **гуляли**
*   **готувати** (to cook) → **готував** / **готувала** / **готувало** / **готували**
*   **говорити** (to speak) → **говорив** / **говорила** / **говорило** / **говорили**

There is one small exception when dealing with verbs that have a reflexive particle (**-ся**) at the end. The verb **дивитися** (to watch) is a perfect example. You still apply the gender endings, but they sit *before* the reflexive part. The forms look like this:
*   **дивитися** (to watch) → **дивився** / **дивилася** / **дивилося** / **дивилися**

Now let's build some complete sentences about the past. To give context to our sentences, we often use specific time words. The most important word for the past is **учора** (yesterday). We can also talk about a previous time period, like **минулого тижня** (last week).

Let's look at how these pieces come together in simple, clear statements:

*   **Учора я читав цікаву книжку.** (Yesterday I [male] read an interesting book.)
*   **Вона працювала в офісі.** (She worked in the office.)
*   **Ми гуляли в парку.** (We walked in the park.)
*   **Вони готували вечерю разом.** (They cooked dinner together.)
*   **Ти дивилася фільм учора?** (Did you [female] watch a film yesterday?)
*   **Він говорив дуже тихо.** (He spoke very quietly.)

When you construct these sentences, the order is very flexible, just like in the present tense. You can put the time word at the beginning for emphasis, or at the end. 

*   **Я гуляла учора.** (I walked yesterday.)
*   **Учора я гуляла.** (Yesterday I walked.)

Both are perfectly correct and natural. The key is ensuring that the verb ending matches the subject. If you are talking about your brother, use **-в**. If you are talking about your mother, use **-ла**. If you are talking about your parents, use **-ли**.

## Summary

The Ukrainian **минулий час** (past tense) is straightforward once you grasp its core logic. The most important takeaway is that past tense formation is entirely based on **gender**.

The formula is consistent: take the infinitive stem of the verb (by dropping **-ти**) and add the appropriate gender ending:
*   **-в** for masculine (**він**)
*   **-ла** for feminine (**вона**)
*   **-ло** for neuter (**воно**)
*   **-ли** for plural (**вони**)

Remember that gender matters for the speaker and the listener as well. If you are a male speaker, you will say **Я читав**. If you are a female speaker, you will say **Я читала**. It does not matter if you are talking about yourself (**я**), the person in front of you (**ти**), or someone else (**він** / **вона**); if the subject is masculine, the verb ends in **-в**. If the subject is feminine, the verb ends in **-ла**. 

Plural forms are the easiest because they ignore gender completely. When referring to "we" (**ми**), "you all" (**ви**), or "they" (**вони**), the ending is always **-ли**. 

The fundamental question for this topic is:
*   **Що ти робив?** (What did you do? — asked to a man)
*   **Що ти робила?** (What did you do? — asked to a woman)

And the fundamental answer is:
*   **Я читав / читала книжку.** (I read a book.)
