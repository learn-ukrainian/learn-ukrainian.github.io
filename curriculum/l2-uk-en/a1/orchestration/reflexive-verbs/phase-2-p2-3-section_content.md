## Практика: Відмінювання та вправи

Just as **сміятися** — [sʲmʲiˈjɑtɪsʲɐ] (to laugh) or **подобатися** — [poˈdɔbɐtɪsʲɐ] (to please) describe internal states, other reflexive verbs describe actions we perform on ourselves. To master these **дієслова** (verbs), we must focus on how the **суфікс** (suffix) behaves during **відмінювання** (conjugation). When we change the person, the suffix always stays at the very end. Think of it as a permanent attachment that travels with the verb throughout the sentence.

### Відмінювання: Практика з «я», «ти», «він»
The process of **відмінювання** (conjugation) for reflexive verbs follows the standard patterns you already know. You first conjugate the base **дієслово** (verb) according to the person, and then you attach the reflexive part. For example, look at the **дієслово** «одягатися» — [odʲɐˈɦɑtɪsʲɐ] (to dress oneself). Even when the verb ending changes for «ти» or «він», the suffix remains firmly attached. This **правило** (rule) is the key to accuracy in your **мовлення** (speech).

**Приклад** (Example):
- **Я одягаюся.** — I am dressing myself.
- **Ти одягаєшся.** — You are dressing yourself.
- **Він одягається.** — He is dressing himself.
- **Вона одягається.** — She is dressing herself.
- **Ми одягаємося.** — We are dressing ourselves.

Note how the stem conjugates normally like any Class I verb, and then we simply attach the reflexive part based on the vowel or consonant rule we learned earlier.

### Пари дієслів: Перехідні та зворотні
One of the most powerful features of the Ukrainian language is how a small suffix can transform the entire focus of a sentence. We call the verbs without the suffix **перехідні дієслова** (transitive verbs) because the action "crosses over" to someone else. When we add the **зворотний суфікс** (reflexive suffix), the action returns to the subject. This changes the **дієслово** (verb) from an outward force to an inward one (**дія на себе** — action on oneself).

Compare these pairs to see the difference in **значення** (meaning):
- **Вона вмиває дитину.** — She is washing the child.
- **Вона вмивається.** — [wmɪˈwɑjɛtsʲɐ] — She is washing herself.
- **Я одягаю дитину.** — I am dressing the child.
- **Я одягаюся.** — I am dressing myself.
- **Я дивлюся фільм.** — [dɪˈwlʲusʲɐ] — I am watching a movie.

In the first **речення** (sentence), the action is directed outward. In the second, the subject and the object are the same person. The verb **дивитися** — [dɪˈwɪtɪsʲɐ] (to watch) is always reflexive because the act of looking is considered an internal sensory experience.

### Вибір суфікса: -ся чи -сь?
As we have seen, the choice between **-ся** and **-сь** depends on the sound that comes right before it. A consonant likes **-ся**, while a vowel prefers the shorter **-сь**. This **фонетичне правило** (phonetic rule) creates a rhythmic pattern within a single conjugation that helps the language flow smoothly. When you conjugate (**відмінюєте**), always check the last letter of your base verb form.

Let's look at the verb **вчитися** — [ˈwtʃɪtɪsʲɐ] (to study/learn):
- **Я вчуся.** — I study (vowel + **-сь**).
- **Ми вчимося.** — We study (vowel + **-сь**).
- **Вони вчаться.** — They study (consonant + **-ся**).
- **Ти вчишся.** — You study (consonant + **-ся**).

[!observe] Soft Buzzing Sounds
The written cluster **-ться** sounds like a long soft **[ts:a]**. Similarly, the combination **-шся** in conversational speech sounds like a soft **[s:a]**. Listen carefully to these sounds to make your speech sound more natural and authentic.

This phonetic switch ensures that two vowels don't clash. Always listen to the ending before you add the reflexive part.

### Знайомство: «Називатися» та «знайомитися»
Now we can use these tools to build a simple **діалог** (dialogue). The verb **називатися** — [nɐzɪˈwɑtɪsʲɐ] (to be called) is perfect for objects or groups, while **знайомитися** — [znɐˈjɔmɪtɪsʲɐ] (to get acquainted) is essential for social life. These verbs help us navigate new environments (**нове середовище**) and meet new people in a polite and clear way (**ввічливо та ясно**).

**Речення** (Sentences):
- **Як називається це місто?** — What is this city called?
- **Ми знайомимося.** — We are getting acquainted.
- **Вони знайомляться.** — They are getting acquainted.
- **Я знайомлюся.** — I am getting acquainted.

[!warning] Common Suffix Loss
Learners often forget to keep the suffix when they conjugate. Remember: the **-ся** is part of the verb's identity. Without it, the meaning changes or becomes incomplete.
- Incorrect: *Я вмиваю* (I wash...)
- Correct: **Я вмиваюся** (I wash myself)
This is a very common mistake because many English equivalents don't use the word "self."
