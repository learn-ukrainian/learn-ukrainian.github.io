## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Pedagogy / Unfair Testing
- **Location**: Activities / True-False / Item 8
- **Original**: "The form 'я дивлюсь' shows a consonant mutation (див → дивл). [True]"
- **Problem**: The content text **never mentions** consonant mutation or Class II conjugation patterns. It only shows the table for `вмиватися` (Class I). A student reading the text cannot know this rule, making the quiz question unfair and the usage of `дивлюсь` in dialogues confusing if they try to apply the `вмиватися` pattern (`диваюсь`?).
- **Fix**: Add a subsection or a `> [!note]` in the Presentation section explaining that Class II verbs (like `дивитися`, `вчитися`) change their ending slightly (vowel disappears) and 1st person adds `-л-`.

### Issue 2: English Phrasing (Calque risk)
- **Location**: Line 22
- **Original**: "When you ask "How is this called?", you are using..."
- **Problem**: In standard English, we ask "What is this called?". "How is it called" is a common error by speakers of European languages. While understandable, the instruction should be in standard English.
- **Fix**: "When you ask "What is this called?" (literally "How is it named?"), you are using..."

### Issue 3: Phantom Vocabulary
- **Location**: Activities / Anagram & Group-Sort
- **Original**: `зупинятися` (to stop), `цілуватися` (to kiss)
- **Problem**: These words appear in the activities but are not introduced in the text, lists, or examples within the module content.
- **Fix**: Either add them to the "Three Types" lists in the text or remove them from activities to ensure alignment.
