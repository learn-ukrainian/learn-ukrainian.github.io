## Граматика (Grammar Summary)

Grammar provides the framework that holds your vocabulary together. The core mechanisms of Ukrainian verbs dictate how actions relate to the speaker. In the A1.3 phase, we covered the essential conjugation patterns and functional words. The essential patterns of Ukrainian verbs operate on strict rules.

**1. The Infinitive**
The dictionary form of a verb is the infinitive. You cannot use this form directly with a subject like **я** or **ти**. It must be conjugated. Almost all infinitives end in **-ти**.
*   **читати** (to read)
*   **говорити** (to speak)
*   **хотіти** (to want)

**2. Group I Verbs**
Group I verbs typically have an **-ати** or **-яти** ending in the infinitive. Their endings feature the vowels **-є-** or **-е-**.
*   **я** + **-ю** (я читаю)
*   **ти** + **-єш** (ти читаєш)
*   **він/вона/воно** + **-є** (він читає)
*   **ми** + **-ємо** (ми читаємо)
*   **ви** + **-єте** (ви читаєте)
*   **вони** + **-ють** (вони читають)

**3. Group II Verbs**
Group II verbs typically have an **-ити** or **-іти** ending in the infinitive. Their endings feature the vowels **-и-** or **-і-**.
*   **я** + **-ю** / **-у** (я говорю)
*   **ти** + **-иш** (ти говориш)
*   **він/вона/воно** + **-ить** (вона говорить)
*   **ми** + **-имо** (ми говоримо)
*   **ви** + **-ите** (ви говорите)
*   **вони** + **-ять** / **-ать** (вони говорять)

**4. Modal Verbs**
Modal verbs express desire, ability, or obligation. They act as the primary conjugated verb in the sentence and are always followed immediately by an infinitive.
*   **Я хочу гуляти.** (I want to walk.)
*   **Я можу працювати.** (I can work.)
*   **Я мушу йти.** (I must go.)

**5. Question Words**
Asking questions is crucial for active communication. These seven words allow you to extract specific information from a conversation partner.
*   **Хто** (Who)
*   **Що** (What)
*   **Де** (Where - location)
*   **Куди** (Where to - direction)
*   **Коли** (When)
*   **Чому** (Why)
*   **Як** (How)

**6. Negation**
To make a sentence negative, place the particle **не** directly before the verb. Ukrainian also uses double negation, which means words like **ніхто** (nobody) still require **не** before the verb.
*   **Я не знаю.** (I do not know.)
*   **Ніхто не знає.** (Nobody knows.)

**7. Reflexive Verbs**
Reflexive verbs show that the subject is performing the action on themselves. They add the particle **-ся** to the end of the conjugated verb.
*   **Я прокидаюся.** (I wake up.)
*   **Він вмивається.** (He washes himself.)

Testing these conjugations trains your ability to select the right form quickly.

## Діалог (Connected Dialogue)

Real conversations require you to ask questions, respond with statements, use modals to suggest plans, and negate verbs when necessary. A natural exchange rarely relies on a single grammar point. You must switch between different verb groups and perspectives seamlessly. 

Read the following dialogue. It represents a typical interaction where two friends meet and try to coordinate their schedules. Observe the mechanics driving this exchange.

<div class="dialogue">


**Максим:** Привіт! Що ти робиш? *(Hi! What are you doing?)*


**Олена:** Привіт! Я читаю книгу. А ти? *(Hi! I am reading a book. And you?)*


**Максим:** Я хочу гуляти. Можеш піти зі мною? *(I want to walk. Can you go with me?)*


**Олена:** Не можу, мушу працювати. *(I can't, I must work.)*


**Максим:** Коли ти повертаєшся? *(When do you return?)*


**Олена:** О шостій. *(At six.)*


**Максим:** Добре, тоді гуляємо ввечері! *(Good, then we walk in the evening!)*


</div>

First, Максим initiates the conversation with a clear question using the word **що** (what). He applies the **ти** (you) form of the Group II verb **робити** (to do). Олена answers using the **я** (I) form of the Group I verb **читати** (to read). 

The conversation then shifts to making plans. Максим uses the modal verb **хотіти** (to want) followed by an infinitive to state his desire. He immediately asks about her ability using another modal verb, **могти** (can, to be able to). 

Олена declines the invitation. She applies the negative particle **не** (not) directly before the modal verb. She justifies her refusal using a third modal verb, **мусити** (must), indicating an obligation. 

Максим extracts a specific detail about her schedule using the question word **коли** (when) and a reflexive verb. Once they establish a time, they confirm their new plans.
