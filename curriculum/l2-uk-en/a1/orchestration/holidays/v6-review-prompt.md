## Українські свята (Ukrainian Holidays)

Traditional Ukrainian celebrations are deeply woven into the agricultural calendar and spiritual life. The two most significant religious and cultural markers of the year are Christmas and Easter.

### Різдво (Christmas)
**Різдво** (Christmas) is a time of profound symbolism. For many years, due to the influence of the Russian Orthodox church, Christmas was officially marked on January 7. However, in 2023, Ukraine officially moved Christmas to **двадцять п'ятого грудня** (December 25) to align with Europe and the rest of the Christian world.

The celebration begins the evening before, on December 24, with **Свята вечеря** (Holy Supper). When the first star appears in the sky, families gather around a table that traditionally holds exactly **дванадцять страв** (12 dishes), symbolizing the twelve apostles. All dishes are vegan, containing no meat or dairy. The centerpiece of the table is always **кутя** (kutia) — a sweet porridge made of boiled wheat, poppy seeds, honey, and walnuts. Eating **кутя** is the very first thing everyone does at the table. 

After dinner, the singing begins. **Колядки** (carols) echo through homes and streets. Groups of **колядники** (carolers), often dressed in traditional costumes and carrying a large handmade star, go from door to door to sing **колядки** and bless the household for the coming year.

### Великдень (Easter)
In the spring, Ukrainians celebrate **Великдень** (Easter, m), which literally translates to "Great Day." It is considered the most important religious holiday of the year. The exact date changes each year according to the lunar calendar, but it always falls in the spring. 

The weeks leading up to **Великдень** are filled with preparation, specifically the creation of **писанки** (decorated Easter eggs, f). A **писанка** is not simply dyed; it is an intricate piece of art created using a wax-resist method, covered in ancient geometric and floral symbols that predate Christianity. 

On Easter morning, families wake up very early to go to church and **святити кошик** (bless the basket). This wicker basket is covered with an embroidered cloth and filled with a rich, sweet bread called **паска** (Easter bread, f), along with sausage, cheese, butter, salt, and eggs. 

When greeting people during this time, Ukrainians do not simply say "Hello." They say:
- **Христос воскрес!** *(Christ is risen!)*
And the required response is:
- **Воістину воскрес!** *(Indeed risen!)*

## Державні свята (National Holidays)

Alongside ancient traditions, modern Ukraine observes several powerful national days that honor the country's statehood, resilience, and identity. 

### День Незалежності (Independence Day)
The most important **державне свято** (national holiday) is **День Незалежності** (Independence Day). On August 24, 1991, Ukraine formally declared its independence from the Soviet Union. Today, the country marks this date with immense pride. 

Cities transform into seas of blue and yellow. People gather in central squares to watch a **парад** (parade) and listen to outdoor **концерти** (concerts). In the evening, the sky often lights up with a **салют** (fireworks). Balconies and cars fly the national **прапор** (flag, m). 

To greet someone on this day, you say:
- **З Днем Незалежності!** *(Happy Independence Day!)*
And you will frequently hear the patriotic exchange:
- **Слава Україні!** *(Glory to Ukraine!)*
- **Героям слава!** *(Glory to the heroes!)*

### Other Important Holidays
The Ukrainian calendar has several other key dates that define the modern cultural rhythm.

**Новий рік** (New Year) is celebrated on January 1. It remains the biggest secular celebration of the winter season, marked by midnight toasts, exchanging gifts, and decorating a festive tree. 

**Вишиванковий день** (Vyshyvanka Day) occurs on the third Thursday of May. On this day, everyone — from news anchors and politicians to students and bus drivers — wears a **вишиванка** (embroidered shirt, f). The **вишиванка** is a profound symbol of Ukrainian identity and resistance.

**День Конституції** (Constitution Day) is observed on June 28, honoring the adoption of the modern Ukrainian constitution.

**День захисників і захисниць** (Defenders' Day) falls on October 1. This solemn and respectful day honors all the men and women who serve in the armed forces to protect Ukraine's freedom.

## Підсумок — Summary

When you want to **вітати** (to congratulate/greet) someone on a holiday, Ukrainian uses a very specific grammatical pattern. You cannot simply translate the English word "Happy." Instead, Ukrainians wish you joy *with* the holiday.

To form a holiday greeting, you use the preposition **з** (with) followed by the name of the holiday in the **Instrumental case**. 

You already know the preposition **з** from talking about food and drink, like **кава з молоком** (coffee with milk) or **борщ з м'ясом** (borscht with meat). The logic here is identical: you are greeting someone "with" the arrival of the occasion.

Here is the pattern in action:
- **З + [holiday in instrumental] + !**

Let us look at how the endings change for the major holidays:
- **Різдво** (Christmas) → **З Різдвом!** *(Merry Christmas!)*
- **Великдень** (Easter) → **З Великоднем!** *(Happy Easter!)*
- **Новий рік** (New Year) → **З Новим роком!** *(Happy New Year!)*
- **День Незалежності** (Independence Day) → **З Днем Незалежності!** *(Happy Independence Day!)*

Notice that for phrases with two words, like **Новий рік**, both the adjective and the noun take the instrumental ending: **Новим роком**. For **День Незалежності**, only the word **День** changes to **Днем**; the word **Незалежності** is already in the genitive case (Day *of* Independence) and remains locked in that form.

You use this exact same pattern for personal celebrations as well. The phrase for "birthday" is **день народження** (day of birth). To say "Happy birthday," you apply the instrumental case to the word "day":
- **З днем народження!** *(Happy birthday!)*

- **весна** — Великдень
- **серпень 24** — День Незалежності
- **червень 28** — День Конституції
- **жовтень 1** — День захисників і захисниць

| 8 | **Cultural accuracy** | 5% | Decolonized (Ukrainian on its own terms, never "like Russian but...")? Factually correct claims about Ukrainian? Respectful representation? |
| 9 | **Dialogue & conversation quality** | 10% | Dialogues natural and culturally appropriate? Real situations, real responses? Speaker roles clear? Not stilted or textbook-robotic? |

**Note:** Vocabulary tables (словник), video embeds, and external resource links are added by a downstream ENRICH step — do NOT penalize their absence or quality. If you see a vocabulary table with wrong translations, missing words, or formatting issues, that content was NOT written by the writer — it was generated deterministically by the ENRICH step. Do NOT flag словník problems as linguistic errors or deduct points from the writer. Report them separately under "ENRICH issues" in your findings (informational only, not scored).

### Step 4: Output raw scores

Output ONLY the raw 1-10 scores in the table below. Do NOT calculate the weighted total — the pipeline script handles the math deterministically. Just write your per-dimension scores.

### Step 5: List findings

For every issue found, provide:
```
[DIMENSION] [SEVERITY: critical/major/minor]
Location: [specific section/paragraph — quote the actual text]
Issue: [what's wrong]
Fix: [exactly how to fix it]
```

Critical = module cannot ship. Major = quality below standard. Minor = polish item.

### Step 6: Verdict

Base your verdict on the **severity of findings** — the pipeline calculates the weighted score separately.

| Verdict | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **PASS** | Zero critical findings, at most minor issues |
| **REVISE** | Has major findings but no criticals — fixable without rewrite |
| **REJECT** | Has any critical finding — fundamental problems requiring rewrite |

### Step 7: Fix it yourself (REVISE only)

If your verdict is **REVISE**, you MUST output a `<fixes>` block with exact find/replace pairs. The pipeline applies these deterministically — no LLM regeneration, no rewriting from scratch.

Rules for fixes:
- Each fix is a YAML entry with `find:` (exact text from the module) and `replace:` (corrected text)
- The `find` string must be an EXACT substring of the module content — copy-paste it
- Keep fixes minimal — change only what's wrong, preserve surrounding text
- Do NOT fix словник/vocabulary tables (those are generated by a downstream tool)
- For word count issues: add content as a new `insert_after:` entry instead of find/replace
