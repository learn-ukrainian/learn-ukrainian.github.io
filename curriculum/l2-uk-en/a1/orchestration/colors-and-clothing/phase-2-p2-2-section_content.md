## Український одяг: Що ми носимо?

### From Colors to Cloth

You know the colors, so let's use them to paint a picture of ourselves. When we step out of the house, we describe what we see and what we wear. Let's start with the basics of your **гардероб** (wardrobe).

Here are the essential items you will see every day:
*   **сорочка** [sɔˈrɔtʃkɑ] — shirt
*   **сукня** [ˈsuknʲɑ] — dress
*   **светр** [swɛtr] — sweater
*   **куртка** [ˈkurtkɑ] — jacket

In Ukraine, a shirt is often more than just fabric. It can be a story.

> [!history-bite]
> **Вишиванка як оберіг (The Talisman)**
> The traditional embroidered shirt, the **вишиванка** [ʋɪʃɪˈʋɑnkɑ], is not just beautiful. Historically, it was an **оберіг** [ɔbɛˈrʲiɦ] (talisman). The patterns on the collar and cuffs were believed to protect the person wearing it. **Червоний** (red) symbolized energy, and **чорний** (black) represented the earth.

Now, look at these items with their colors. Remember our gender rules?
*   **Це біла сорочка.** (This is a white shirt.) — Feminine
*   **Це теплий светр.** (This is a warm sweater.) — Masculine
*   **Це чорна куртка.** (This is a black jacket.) — Feminine

### The Art of Wearing

Knowing the names of clothes is good, but wearing them is better. To say "I wear" or "I am wearing," we use the verb **носити** [nɔˈsɪtɪ].

*   **Я ношу...** [jɑ ˈnɔʃu] — I wear...
*   **Ти носиш...** [tɪ ˈnɔsɪʃ] — You wear...
*   **Він/Вона носить...** [win / ʋɔˈnɑ ˈnɔsɪtʲ] — He/She wears...

But here is the trick. When you wear something, you do something *to* it. The object changes. In Grammar terms, this is the **Знахідний відмінок** (Accusative Case).

For masculine non-living things (like a sweater), nothing changes. It stays the same.
*   **Це светр.** (This is a sweater.) $\rightarrow$ **Я ношу светр.** (I wear a sweater.)

But feminine nouns are different. They change their ending from **-а** to **-у**.
*   **Це сукня.** (This is a dress.) $\rightarrow$ **Я ношу сукню.** (I wear a dress.)
*   **Це сорочка.** (This is a shirt.) $\rightarrow$ **Я ношу сорочку.** (I wear a shirt.)
*   **Я ношу червону куртку.** (I wear a red jacket.)

Notice that the adjective changes too! **Червона** becomes **червону**.

### The "Always Many" Rule

Some things in your closet are never alone. Just like in English, words like "pants" or "glasses" are always plural. We call these **Pluralia Tantum**. You cannot count them as "one."

Here are the most common ones:
*   **штани** [ʃtɑˈnɪ] — pants / trousers
*   **джинси** [ˈdʒɪnsɪ] — jeans
*   **окуляри** [ɔkuˈlʲɑrɪ] — glasses

Because they are plural, you cannot use **це** (this). You must use **ці** [tsi] (these). And your adjectives must have the plural ending **-і**.

*   **Ці штани сині.** (These pants are dark blue.)
*   **Ці джинси нові.** (These jeans are new.)
*   **Ці окуляри чорні.** (These glasses are black.)

When you use **носити** with these words, they do not change form because they are inanimate plurals.
*   **Я ношу джинси.** (I wear jeans.)
*   **Він носить окуляри.** (He wears glasses.)
*   **Ми носимо чорні штани.** (We wear black pants.)
