# Дослідження: Colors & Clothing

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.1.2: "Adjective declension — hard/soft groups, gender agreement"
Alignment: Introduces core color adjectives and reinforces gender agreement in the Nominative case. Also touches on §3 themes ("купівля", "побут").

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|-------|------------------|
| білий | High Core A1; symbolizes purity | біла сорочка, білий світ |
| чорний | High Core A1; symbolizes fertile earth | чорні штани, чорна кава |
| червоний | High Core A1; symbolizes life and love | червона сукня, червона калина |
| сорочка | Foundation of traditional dress | вишита сорочка, біла сорочка |
| штани | Pluralia tantum (always plural) | нові штани, сині штани |
| носити | Verb High; requires Accusative object | носити одяг, носити окуляри |

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Vyshyvanka Color Symbolism**: In traditional Ukrainian embroidery, *червоний* (red) represents life, love, and energy, while *чорний* (black) stands for the fertile earth and wisdom. *Білий* (white) is for purity.
2. **Protective Patterns**: Embroidery on collars and cuffs historically served as a talisman (оберіг) to protect the wearer's energy zones.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Ignoring Agreement** → *червоний сорочка* (Incorrect) vs *червона сорочка* (Correct) — English speakers often forget that adjectives must match the noun's gender.
2. **Singular Pluralia Tantum** → *цей штани* (Incorrect) vs *ці штани* (Correct) — Words like *штани* and *окуляри* are always plural, requiring plural demonstratives/adjectives.
3. **Accusative Feminine Missing** → *Я ношу червона сукня* (Incorrect) vs *Я ношу червону сукню* (Correct) — Missing the -у/-ю ending shift for feminine objects of the verb *носити*.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Grammar Constraint**: Accusative case with *носити* must be taught strictly as a lexical pattern (chunking: *носити сукню*, *носити штани*) before formal Accusative instruction in seq 25. Do not explain the case rules deeply.
- Keep sentences short (max 8-10 words). 
- Use English scaffolding for grammar explanations.
