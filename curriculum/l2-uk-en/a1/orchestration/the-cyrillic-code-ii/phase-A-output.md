# Дослідження: The Cyrillic Code II

## State Standard Reference
§4.1.1: "Український алфавіт. Друковані та писані літери. Великі і малі літери: Київ..."
§4.1.4: "Голосні та приголосні звуки: вимова голосних у наголошеній і ненаголошеній позиціях... тверді та м’які приголосні: лист – ліс, Рим – Рівне..."
Alignment: This module introduces 7 new letters (К, И, Р, Б, В, Д, І) plus О, teaching alphabet recognition (§4.1.1) and directly addressing the phonetic distinction between vowels and consonants, specifically targeting the hard 'И' vs soft 'І' distinction highlighted in §4.1.4.

## Cultural Hooks
1. The dotted letter 'І' is a distinct visual marker of the Ukrainian alphabet and is frequently used in art, literature, and branding as a proud symbol of Ukrainian linguistic identity.
2. The letter 'И' represents a uniquely deep and relaxed sound in Ukrainian. It is almost never used at the very beginning of a native Ukrainian word, acting instead as a core sound within roots and suffixes.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Confusing visual "trap letters": Reading 'Р' as the English /p/ and 'В' as the English /b/. → Explicitly drill the visual contrast with Latin letters.
2. Pronouncing 'И' too sharply like the English "ee" or too gutturally. → Teach the relaxed jaw position (lower than "ee").
3. Reducing unstressed 'О' to an 'A' sound (a common error caused by exposure to Russian). → Emphasize that Ukrainian unstressed 'О' remains a pure /o/ in all positions.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Rely heavily on English scaffolding (A1 requirement) to explain the phonetics, ensuring the learner feels safe.
- Frame the pronunciation rules completely independently of Russian. Describe how the mouth moves rather than comparing it to neighbouring Slavic languages.
- Ensure the 'И' vs 'І' contrast is framed positively as a beautiful feature of Ukrainian euphony.
