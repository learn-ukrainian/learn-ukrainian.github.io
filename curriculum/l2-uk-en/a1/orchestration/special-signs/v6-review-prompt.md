## М'який знак (The Soft Sign — Ь)

Say the word **день** (day) out loud. Now say the word **ден**. Feel the difference at the end? That tiny shift — the consonant becoming lighter, thinner — is the work of the soft sign, **Ь**.

Here is the most important fact about **Ь**: it has no sound of its own. Zero. It is not a vowel, not a consonant. Its only job is to soften the consonant that comes before it. Ukrainian distinguishes between hard consonants (**тверді**) and soft consonants (**пом'якшені**). Ukrainian Grade 1 textbooks mark this difference: a hard consonant gets the symbol [–], while a soft consonant gets [=]. When you see **Ь** after a letter, that letter becomes soft.

Where does **Ь** appear? Only after consonants, never at the beginning of a word. It shows up in very common patterns:

- **-нь**: **день** (day), **кінь** (horse), **осінь** (autumn)
- **-ль**: **сіль** (salt), **біль** (pain)
- **-ть**: **мить** (moment)
- **-зь**: **мазь** (ointment)

Look at longer words too. **Учитель** (teacher) — the **Ь** softens the final **Л**. **Батько** (father) — the **Ь** softens the **Т** before **К**. **Маленький** (small) — the **Ь** softens the **К**.

Notice a pattern: **Ь** frequently appears at the end of a word or before another consonant. It never appears before a vowel in these positions — that job belongs to a different sign, which you will meet next.



Practice reading these words slowly, focusing on making the final consonant soft and light. Imagine the **Ь** as a gentle feather touch at the end of the word. **Сіль**... **день**... **мить**. It takes time to train your tongue to make these sounds if they do not exist in your native language, so be patient with yourself. Listen closely to native speakers and try to mimic that airy quality.

## Апостроф (The Apostrophe)

Look at the word **сім'я** (family). See that small mark between **м** and **я**? That is the apostrophe — **апостроф**. It does the opposite of what **Ь** does.

The rule, straight from Захарійчук Grade 1 (p. 97): the apostrophe appears after the consonants **б, п, в, м, ф, р** — and only before the vowels **я, ю, є, ї**. Its job? It keeps the consonant hard and lets the vowel keep its full two-sound value.

Here is what that means in practice. Without an apostrophe, a vowel like **я** softens the consonant before it. In **пісня** (song), the **н** before **я** is soft. But with an apostrophe, the consonant stays hard, and **я** sounds like two sounds together — "й" + "а".

Compare these:

| Without apostrophe | With apostrophe |
|---|---|
| **ря** — soft **р** + "а" | **р'я** — hard **р** + "й" + "а" |
| **мя** — soft **м** + "а" | **м'я** — hard **м** + "й" + "а" |

Now look at real words:

- **сім'я** (family) — hard **м**, then "й" + "а"
- **м'ясо** (meat) — hard **м**, then "й" + "а" + "со"
- **п'ять** (five) — hard **п**, then "й" + "а" + "ть"
- **комп'ютер** (computer) — hard **п**, then "й" + "у"
- **дев'ять** (nine) — hard **в**, then "й" + "а"

Reading practice — say these words out loud: **п'ять**, **дев'ять**, **м'який** (soft), **м'яч** (ball), **об'єкт** (object).



When reading, treat the apostrophe almost like a tiny pause or a 'speed bump' that stops the consonant from sliding into the vowel. It forces you to pronounce them as distinct elements. This is why a word like **м'ясо** sounds energetic and bouncy, compared to words where consonants and vowels blend smoothly. Getting this right is a huge step toward sounding like a native Ukrainian speaker.

## Дзвінкі і глухі (Voiced and Voiceless)

Place your hand on your throat and say "ББББ." Feel the vibration? Now say "ПППП" — the vibration stops. That is the difference between a voiced consonant (**дзвінкий**) and a voiceless one (**глухий**).

Ukrainian consonants come in neat voiced-voiceless pairs:

| Дзвінкий (voiced) | Глухий (voiceless) |
|---|---|
| **Б** | **П** |
| **Д** | **Т** |
| **Г** | **Х** |
| **Ґ** | **К** |
| **З** | **С** |
| **Ж** | **Ш** |
| **ДЗ** | **Ц** |
| **ДЖ** | **Ч** |

The throat test works for every pair. Try it: **Д** — vibration. **Т** — no vibration. **З** — vibration. **С** — no vibration.

Now, one critical difference from Russian that makes Ukrainian sound like Ukrainian. In Russian, voiced consonants lose their voice at the end of a word — the word for "oak" is written «дуб» but pronounced with a **П** sound at the end. Ukrainian does NOT do this. **Дуб** (oak) is "дуб" — the **Б** stays voiced. **Мороз** (frost) is "мороз" — the **З** stays voiced, not "морос." This is authentic Ukrainian pronunciation.



If you are coming from Russian, or if you speak German or Polish (which also devoice final consonants), you must consciously train yourself to keep your vocal cords vibrating all the way to the end of the word. This makes Ukrainian sound rich, resonant, and melodic. Practice holding the final voiced sound for an extra second: **дуббб**, **мороззз**. It might feel exaggerated at first, but it is exactly what you need to build the right habit.

Try these minimal pairs — words that differ by only one sound:

- **Балка** (beam) — **палка** (stick): Б vs П
- **Коза** (goat) — **коса** (braid): З vs С

## Важкі звуки (Tricky Sounds)

Some Ukrainian sounds have no close English equivalent. These are the sounds learners ask about most.

### И

**И** is not English "ee" and not English short "i." It lives between them. To find it: smile as if you are about to say "ee," but pull your tongue slightly back and lower your jaw just a bit. The result is a sound unique to Ukrainian. Practice with these words: **бик** (bull), **лист** (leaf), **зима** (winter), **тихо** (quietly), **синій** (blue). Listen for **И** in every one — it is the same sound each time.

### Г and Ґ

Ukrainian has two letters that English speakers confuse. **Г** is a voiced sound made in the throat — like saying "h" but adding voice to it. Think of the "h" in "behind" said very emphatically, with your throat vibrating. Words with **Г**: **гарно** (nicely, beautifully), **гори** (mountains), **голова** (head).

**Ґ** is completely different — it is the hard "g" sound, like "g" in "go." But **Ґ** is rare in Ukrainian. It appears in only a handful of native words: **ґанок** (porch), **ґудзик** (button), **ґречний** (polite).

:::tip
Most Ukrainian words use **Г**, not **Ґ**. When in doubt, **Г** is almost always correct.
:::

### Р

Ukrainian **Р** is rolled or trilled, similar to Spanish "rr." The tongue tip vibrates against the ridge behind the upper teeth. Practice words: **рука** (hand), **робота** (work), **ранок** (morning), **риба** (fish). Even an imperfect **Р** is perfectly understood — do not stress about mastering the trill immediately.



A great trick for practicing the trilled **Р** is to say the English word 'butter' very quickly. The way your tongue taps the roof of your mouth for the 'tt' is exactly the motion you need for the Ukrainian **Р**. Try making that sound repeatedly, and then gradually add a vowel: 'ra-ra-ra'. With time and practice, the vibration will come naturally.

| 3 | **Pedagogical quality** | 15% | PPP (Present→Practice→Produce) applied? Textbook pedagogy used (Большакова, Захарійчук)? Grammar scope respected (no A2 in A1)? |
| 4 | **Vocabulary coverage** | 10% | All required vocab from plan used naturally in prose? Recommended vocab included? New words introduced in context, not as lists? |
| 5 | **Exercise quality** | 15% | Placeholders specific enough? Test the right skills? Placed after relevant teaching? Match plan's activity_hints? Sufficient items? |
| 6 | **Engagement & tone** | 10% | Interesting for teens/adults? Authoritative but warm (like a skilled teacher)? No LLM filler ("Good news!", "Don't panic!", "Fun fact!")? Cultural hooks? |
| 7 | **Structural integrity** | 5% | All H2 headings from plan present? Word count in range? No duplicate sections? No meta-commentary? Clean markdown? |
| 8 | **Cultural accuracy** | 5% | Decolonized (Ukrainian on its own terms, never "like Russian but...")? Factually correct claims about Ukrainian? Respectful representation? |
| 9 | **Dialogue & conversation quality** | 10% | Dialogues natural and culturally appropriate? Real situations, real responses? Speaker roles clear? Not stilted or textbook-robotic? |

**Note:** Vocabulary tables (словник), video embeds, and external resource links are added by a downstream ENRICH step — do NOT penalize their absence or quality. If you see a vocabulary table with wrong translations, missing words, or formatting issues, that content was NOT written by the writer — it was generated deterministically by the ENRICH step. Do NOT flag словník problems as linguistic errors or deduct points from the writer. Report them separately under "ENRICH issues" in your findings (informational only, not scored).

### Step 4: Output raw scores

Output ONLY the raw 1-10 scores in the table below. Do NOT calculate the weighted total — the pipeline script handles the math deterministically. Just write your per-dimension scores.

### Step 5: List findings

For every issue found, provide:
```
[DIMENSION] [SEVERITY: critical/major/minor]
Location: [specific section/paragraph — quote the actual text]
Issue: [what's wrong]
Fix: [exactly how to fix it]
```

Critical = module cannot ship. Major = quality below standard. Minor = polish item.

### Step 6: Verdict

Base your verdict on the **severity of findings** — the pipeline calculates the weighted score separately.

| Verdict | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **PASS** | Zero critical findings, at most minor issues |
| **REVISE** | Has major findings but no criticals — fixable without rewrite |
| **REJECT** | Has any critical finding — fundamental problems requiring rewrite |

### Step 7: Fix it yourself (REVISE only)

If your verdict is **REVISE**, you MUST output a `<fixes>` block with exact find/replace pairs. The pipeline applies these deterministically — no LLM regeneration, no rewriting from scratch.

Rules for fixes:
- Each fix is a YAML entry with `find:` (exact text from the module) and `replace:` (corrected text)
- The `find` string must be an EXACT substring of the module content — copy-paste it
- Keep fixes minimal — change only what's wrong, preserve surrounding text
- Do NOT fix словник/vocabulary tables (those are generated by a downstream tool)
- For word count issues: add content as a new `insert_after:` entry instead of find/replace
