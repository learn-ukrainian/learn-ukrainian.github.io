## 3. Cultural Hook

### The Formal/Informal Divide ("–í–∏" vs "–¢–∏")
In Ukrainian culture, the distinction between formal (*–í–∏*) and informal (*–¢–∏*) address is strictly observed. Even among young people, *–í–∏* is the default for strangers, service staff, and teachers. A key cultural "fail" for foreigners is using *–¢–∏* with a waiter or a bus driver, which can be perceived as patronizing.

### Patronymics and Respect
In formal settings (work, official institutions), Ukrainians traditionally use the **Name + Patronymic** (*–Ü–º'—è —Ç–∞ –ü–æ –±–∞—Ç—å–∫–æ–≤—ñ*) construction (e.g., *–û–ª–µ–Ω–æ –ü–µ—Ç—Ä—ñ–≤–Ω–æ*, *–Ü–≤–∞–Ω–µ –ú–∏–∫–æ–ª–∞–π–æ–≤–∏—á—É*). While modern urban environments and international companies are shifting toward Pan/Pani + Name, the patronymic remains the standard for showing high respect to elders and superiors.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Core Challenges for English Speakers
- **Gender Agreement**: Unlike English where only people have gender, every object in Ukrainian has a gender that dictates the ending of adjectives and possessive pronouns. Learners often forget to change *–º—ñ–π* to *–º–æ—è* for feminine nouns.
- **Stress Mobility**: Stress in Ukrainian is not fixed. When a word changes case or number, the stress can shift (e.g., *—Ä—ÉÃÅ–∫–∏* Nom. Pl. vs *—Ä—É–∫–∏ÃÅ* Gen. Sg.).
- **Omitting "To Be"**: English speakers struggle with the zero-copula in the present tense (e.g., *–Ø —Å—Ç—É–¥–µ–Ω—Ç* instead of *I am a student*).

### Teaching Sequence Recommendation
The checkpoint should focus on **functional application** rather than abstract tables. 
1. **Identity**: Combining gender + identity nouns + adjectives.
2. **Possession**: Using Genitive for absence (*–£ –º–µ–Ω–µ –Ω–µ–º–∞—î...*) and possession.
3. **Action**: Mixing tenses (What I did yesterday vs. what I will do tomorrow).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (Consolidation Targets)
- **Gender**: Masc, Fem, Neut.
- **Nouns**: Nominative (subject), Accusative (direct object/destination), Locative (location), Genitive (possession/negation).
- **Verbs**: Present (both groups), Past, Future (compound).
- **Modals**: –•–æ—á—É, –º–æ–∂—É, –º—É—à—É.
- **Pronouns**: Personal and Possessive.
- **Numbers**: 1‚Äì100.

### OUT Scope (Do Not Introduce)
