## Warm-up

If you want to experience the "soul" of a Ukrainian city, you must visit the **ринок** (market) or **базар** (bazaar). In Ukraine, markets are not just places to buy food; they are social hubs where people meet, talk, and exchange news. From the famous **Бессарабський ринок** (Bessarabsky Market) in Kyiv to the sprawling **Привоз** (Pryvoz) in Odesa, each market has its own character.

In this module, you will learn how to ask for prices, discuss the quality of products, use weights and measures, and even try your hand at the ancient art of haggling!

> [!myth-buster]
> **Myth:** Markets are only for old people or those looking for cheap food.
>
> **Fact:** Ukrainian markets are becoming trendy hubs! Many young people and top chefs visit markets to find the freshest **домашні продукти** (home-grown products). Farmers bring organic, high-quality honey, cheese, and vegetables directly from their villages.

## Presentation

### Market Basics (Основи на ринку)

When you walk through the market, you will see many **продавці** /prɔdɑˈvt͡sʲi/ (sellers) displaying their goods.

- **Скільки коштує?** (How much does it cost?)
- **Яка ціна?** (What is the price?)
- **Це свіже?** (Is it fresh?)
- **Це домашнє?** (Is it home-grown/homemade?)
- **Можна спробувати?** (Can I try/taste it?)

> [!tip]
> **Try Before You Buy!**
>
> In Ukrainian markets, it is very common to ask **«Можна спробувати?»** (Can I taste?). Sellers are usually proud of their products and will happily cut a slice of cheese, fruit, or even sausage for you. If you taste it and like it, buying a small amount is a polite way to say thank you!

### Weights and Measures (Вага та міри)

Ukrainians use the metric system. You will often hear:

| Measure | IPA | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Кілограм** | /kʲilɔˈɦrɑm/ | Kilogram / kg |
| **Півкілограма** | /piwkʲilɔˈɦrɑmɑ/ | Half a kilogram |
| **Грам** | /ɦrɑm/ | Gram |
| **Літр** | /litr/ | Liter |
| **Штука** | /ˈʃtukɑ/ | Item / Piece |

**Examples:**
- **Дайте один кілограм помідорів.** (Give me one kilogram of tomatoes.)
- **Мені три штуки, будь ласка.** (For me three pieces, please.)
- **Півкілограма сиру.** (Half a kg of cheese.)

### Quality and Freshness (Якість та свіжість)

You want the best products! Use these adjectives to describe what you see:

| Phrase | IPA | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Свіжий хліб.** | /ˈswʲiʒɪj xlib/ | Fresh bread. |
| **Стиглий кавун.** | /ˈstɪɦlɪj kɑˈvun/ | Ripe watermelon. |
| **Солодке яблуко.** | /sɔˈlɔdkɛ ˈjɑblukɔ/ | Sweet apple. |
| **Кисла слива.** | /ˈkɪslɑ ˈslɪvɑ/ | Sour plum. |
| **Смачний сир.** | /smɑt͡ʃˈnɪj sɪr/ | Tasty cheese. |
| **Великий огірок.** | /vɛˈlɪkɪj ɔɦiˈrɔk/ | Big cucumber. |
| **Зелений чай.** | /zɛˈlɛnɪj t͡ʃɑj/ | Green tea. |

> [!history-bite]
> **Pryvoz: The Soul of Odesa**
>
> If you visit Odesa, you must go to **Привоз** (Pryvoz). Built in 1827, it is one of the largest and oldest markets in the world. There is a famous Odesan saying: «If you haven't been to Pryvoz, you haven't been to Odesa.» It is known for its humor, fresh seafood from the Black Sea, and the unique Odesan dialect of Ukrainian and Russian. ### Scenario 1: Buying Vegetables (Купівля овочів)

You are at a vegetable stall.

**Ви:** Доброго дня! У вас є свіжі помідори?
**Продавець:** Так, звісно! Ось дуже гарні помідори. Вони тільки з городу.
**Ви:** Скільки коштує кілограм?

**Продавець:** Прошу. Ось ваші помідори. Сімдесят гривень.
**Ви:** Дякую. Ось гроші.

### Scenario 2: Testing the Fruit (Спроба фруктів)

You see some beautiful plums (**сливи**).

## 3. Cultural Hook

**The Ukrainian "Bazar" Atmosphere:**
In Ukraine, the *базар* (market) is more than just a place to shop; it is a sensory and social experience.
- **Tasting Tradition ("Спробуйте!")**: It is perfectly normal, and often expected, for a vendor to offer a slice of apple, a piece of cheese, or a spoonful of honey for you to taste before you buy. Refusing can sometimes be seen as lack of interest.
- **"Домашнє" (Homemade/Local)**: The highest praise for a product is that it is *домашнє*. This implies it was grown in a private garden or made in a village kitchen rather than produced industrially.
- **The "Bessarabka" and "Privoz"**: Iconic markets like the *Бессарабський ринок* in Kyiv or *Привоз* in Odesa are historic landmarks where the art of "talking" (and occasionally polite bargaining) is part of the transaction.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
- **Countable vs. Mass Nouns**: While English uses "fruit" as often uncountable, Ukrainian uses **фрукти** (plural) almost exclusively in a shopping context.
- **Quantities and Cases**: Quantities trigger the **Genitive case**. 
  - *1 кілограм яблук* (Genitive plural).
  - *Пакет молока* (Genitive singular).
- **Formal vs. Informal**: Shopping at the market is usually conducted in the formal *Ви* (You), even if the atmosphere is friendly.

### Common Learner Errors
- **Agreement with Numbers**: Learners struggle with *кілограм* (1), *кілограми* (2-4), and *кілограмів* (5+). At A1, focus on the singular *кілограм* and teach plural forms as fixed chunks.
- **Gender Confusion**: Thinking *картопля* (fem.) is plural because it ends in -я, or forgetting that *яблуко* is neuter.
- **Request Phrasing**: Using "Я хочу..." too abruptly. Encourage the softer "Дайте, будь ласка" or "Можна...?" (May I [have]...?).

### Teaching Sequence
1. Review fruit/vegetable names from M18.
2. Introduce units of measurement (кг, г, штука).
3. Teach the formula: **[Number] + [Unit] + [Genitive of noun]**.
4. Practice the full dialogue cycle: Greeting -> Asking price -> Requesting amount -> Paying/Change.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope
- **Accusative Case**: For the items requested (Я хочу капусту, моркву).
- **Genitive Case**: For quantities (кілограм яблук) and prices (п’ять гривень).
- **Numbers**: 1-100 for weights and currency.
- **Modal Verbs**: *Можу, хочу* (I can, I want).
- **Basic Adjectives**: *Свіжий, солодкий, стиглий*.

### OUT of Scope
- **Instrumental Case**: Paying *with* card/cash (*карткою/готівкою*). Stick to Nominative or simple "картка/готівка".
- **Dative Case**: "Give *to me*" (*Дайте мені*).
- **Verbal Aspect**: Stick to Imperfective (*купувати, коштувати, вибирати*).
- **Complex Bargaining**: Beyond simple "А дешевше можна?" or "Яка ціна?".
- **Complex Plural Genitive**: Stick to common items where the ending is regular or taught as a block.
