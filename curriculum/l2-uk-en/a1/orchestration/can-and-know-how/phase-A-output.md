# Дослідження: Can and Know How

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Дійсний спосіб: дієвідмінювання дієслів у теперішньому часі: ... могти (можу, можеш, може, можемо, можете, можуть)"
Alignment: This module directly addresses the A1 requirement to conjugate the irregular verb "могти" in the present tense, expanding to related modal expressions of ability and permission ("вміти", "можна") for communicative competence.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Frequency / Source | Key collocations |
|------|-------------------|------------------|
| могти | Very High (Top 50) | можу допомогти, можу прийти |
| вміти | High | вмію плавати, вмію готувати, вмію читати |
| можна | Very High (Top 100) | можна увійти?, можна запитати?, не можна |

## Cultural Hooks
1. The one-word question "Можна?" (May I?) is culturally ubiquitous in Ukraine for seeking permission to enter a room, sit at a shared table, or squeeze past someone in public transport.
2. Navigating public spaces in Ukraine requires recognizing impersonal prohibition signs like "Не можна курити" (No smoking) or "Не можна фотографувати" (No photography).

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Я можу плавати" vs "Я вмію плавати" → English speakers overuse "могти" for learned skills because "can" covers both. "Вміти" must be explicitly tied to learning/training.
2. Conjugating "можна" → Learners often try to conjugate it like a verb (e.g., "я можна"). It must be taught strictly as an impersonal adverb + infinitive.
3. Translating "I can not" directly as "Я не можна" → Confusion between personal inability ("не можу") and impersonal prohibition ("не можна").

## Notes for Content Writing
- Maintain the "Patient Supportive Tutor" persona.
- Use English scaffolding to clearly distinguish circumstantial ability (могти) from learned skill (вміти) — this is a critical conceptual gap for English native speakers.
- Ensure decolonized framing: present Ukrainian modal concepts on their own terms, not as a deviation from English patterns. Avoid Russianisms in examples.
- Include IPA for the first occurrence of new verbs like *плавати*, *готувати*.
- Do not exceed 8-10 words per Ukrainian example sentence to keep within A1 constraints.
