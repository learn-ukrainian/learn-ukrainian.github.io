# Дослідження: Can and Know How

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Дійсний спосіб: дієвідмінювання дієслів у теперішньому часі: ... могти (можу, можеш, може, можемо, можете, можуть)..."
Alignment: The module directly fulfills the State Standard A1 requirement for present tense conjugation of the irregular verb `могти`.

## Cultural Hooks
1. "Можна?" as a standalone question: In Ukraine, it's very common to just say "Можна?" (May I?) while pointing at an empty chair at a cafe or when asking to pass by someone in a crowded space. It acts as an incredibly efficient social lubricant.
2. Direct prohibitions: Signs like «Не можна курити» (No smoking) or «Не можна фотографувати» (No photography) are standard in Ukrainian public spaces, museums, and churches. They represent clear, impersonal rules.

## Common Learner Errors
1. *Я можу плавати* instead of *Я вмію плавати* — English speakers frequently conflate circumstantial ability and learned skills because English uses "can" for both.
2. *Я можна* instead of *Мені можна* or just *Можна* — Beginners try to conjugate the impersonal `можна` as if it were a regular verb with a nominative subject.
3. *Я могю* instead of *Я можу* — Failing to apply the required stem consonant mutation (г → ж) in the first person singular.
