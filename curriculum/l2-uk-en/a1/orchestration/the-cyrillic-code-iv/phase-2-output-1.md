## Злиті звуки та рідкісні букви — Affricates, Digraphs & Ґ

Now we will look at some unique consonants. Some of these represent blended sounds, while others are quite rare in the language. Learning these will help you recognize some of the most interesting words in Ukrainian. Це ціка́во! (This is interesting!) Два спеціа́льні зву́ки. (Two special sounds.)

### Літера Ц

Це лі́тера Ц. (This is the letter Ц.) The letter **Ц** represents an affricate. Це оди́н звук. (This is one sound.) This means it is a blend of the T and S sounds, pronounced together smoothly as one single sound. It sounds exactly like the "ts" at the end of the English word "cats" or "boots". It is crucial that you do not pronounce it as two completely separate consonants. Це ду́же важли́во запам'ята́ти. (This is very important to remember.)

Диві́ться ві́део: (Watch the video:) [Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ц](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u44eCjR2Oz8)

You will see the letter **Ц** very often in word endings and right in the middle of words.

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **це** | this is |
| **це́нтр** | center |

### Літера Щ

Це лі́тера Щ. (This is the letter Щ.) The letter **Щ** is uniquely Ukrainian and represents a fascinating sound. Це два зву́ки ра́зом. (These are two sounds together.) It represents two sounds crammed perfectly into one single letter: a firm SH sound followed immediately by a CH sound. This is a visual and phonetic trap! You must never confuse it with the letter **Ш**, which is just the plain, simple SH sound. 

Слу́хайте: (Listen:) [Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Щ](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QmBLieIuf6Q)

Listen carefully to the distinct double sound when you read these high-frequency words.

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **ще** | more, still |
| **ща́стя** | happiness |

> [!warning]
> Обере́жно! (Careful!) Be careful not to pronounce the letter **Щ** as a simple "sh" sound. It is always a distinct double sound: sh+ch. Saying the word **ще** should sound distinctly like "sh-che".

### Літера Ф

Це лі́тера Ф. (This is the letter Ф.) The letter **Ф** sounds exactly like the English F sound. It is the voiceless partner to the letter В. Interestingly, **Ф** is quite rare in native Ukrainian words. You will find it mostly in words that have been borrowed from other foreign languages, which actually makes them incredibly easy to recognize! Це ле́гко! (This is easy!)

|------|---------|
| **факт** | fact |
| **ка́фе** | cafe |

### Digraphs ДЖ and ДЗ

ДЖ і ДЗ — це дигра́фи. (ДЖ and ДЗ are digraphs.) A digraph is a pair of letters that combine to make one single unified sound. Ukrainian has two very important digraphs that you need to know. Вони́ познача́ють оди́н звук. (They represent one sound.)

The combination **ДЖ** sounds exactly like the English J sound you hear in words like "jungle" or "jump". Це оди́н звук. (This is one sound.)

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **джаз** | jazz |

The combination **ДЗ** is a tight blend of D and Z into one single sound. There is no exact English equivalent, but you can think of the sound directly in the middle of the word "pads". Це украї́нський звук. (This is a Ukrainian sound.) This sound is uniquely Ukrainian.

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **дзвін** | bell |

### Літера Ґ

Це лі́тера Ґ. (This is the letter Ґ.) The letter **Ґ** represents the hard G sound, exactly like the G in the English word "go" or "good". Вона́ ду́же рідкісна. (It is very rare.)

Слу́хайте ува́жно: (Listen carefully:) [Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ґ](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gNjHqjTW9WQ)

> [!history-bite]
> The letter **Ґ** is widely known as the repressed letter. It was officially removed from the Ukrainian alphabet by Soviet authorities in the year 1933 to artificially bring the Ukrainian language closer to Russian. It was proudly and officially restored to the alphabet in 1990! Це на́ша істо́рія. (This is our history.)

Because of this specific history, **Ґ** is extremely rare. There are only about four hundred native words that use it today.

|------|---------|
| **ґа́нок** | porch |

## Йотовані голосні — Iotated Vowels Я, Ю, Є, Ї, Й

Я, Ю, Є, Ї — це йотова́ні голосні́. (Я, Ю, Є, Ї are iotated vowels.) Now we meet the fascinating "iotated" vowels. These specific letters are special because they closely follow a strict "dual nature" principle. At the very beginning of a word or directly after another vowel, they represent TWO separate sounds (a distinct Y-sound plus a vowel). Вони́ ма́ють два зву́ки. (They have two sounds.) However, when they come directly after a consonant, they represent ONE single sound and simply soften the consonant right before them. Але́ пі́сля при́голосного — оди́н звук. (But after a consonant — one sound.) Це пра́вило. (This is a rule.)

### Літера Я

Це лі́тера Я. (This is the letter Я.) The letter **Я** sounds exactly like "ya" (as in "yacht") when it starts a word. 

Диві́ться ві́део: (Watch the video:) [Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Я](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yhSAf41LX8I)

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **я** | I |
| **я́блуко** | apple |

When **Я** follows a consonant, it does not make the "y" sound at all. Instead, it softens the preceding consonant and just makes a simple "a" sound.

### Літера Ю

Це лі́тера Ю. (This is the letter Ю.) The letter **Ю** sounds exactly like "you" (as in "youth") when it begins a word.

Слу́хайте: (Listen:) [Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ю](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9JdIBYCTWGw)

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **юна́к** | young man |
| **суп-пюре́** | puree soup |

Just like the letter **Я**, when **Ю** follows a consonant, it immediately softens that consonant and simply makes an "oo" sound.

### Літера Є

Це лі́тера Є. (This is the letter Є.) The letter **Є** sounds exactly like "ye" (as in "yellow") at the beginning of a word.

Диві́ться ві́део: (Watch the video:) [Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Є](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=O0bwRyyBQSc)

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Євро́па** | Europe |

> [!culture]
> The letter **Є** is very important for a significant cultural touchstone. The concept of **Євро́па** (Europe) is central to modern Ukrainian identity and political direction, especially seen in powerful movements like the Euromaidan. Це Євро́па! (This is Europe!)

It is relatively rare to find **Є** immediately after a consonant, but when you do, it softens the consonant and makes a simple "e" sound.

### Літера Ї

Це лі́тера Ї. (This is the letter Ї.) The letter **Ї** is the strict exception to the dual nature rule. Це ви́няток. (This is an exception.) Вона́ за́вжди ма́є два зву́ки. (It always has two sounds.) It ALWAYS makes two distinct sounds: "yi" (as in "yield"). It NEVER softens a consonant under any circumstances.

> [!fact]
> The letter **Ї** is beautifully and uniquely Ukrainian. During the full-scale invasion, drawing the letter **Ї** in occupied territories became a powerful, defiant symbol of Ukrainian resistance and national identity. Украї́на перемо́же! (Ukraine will win!)

### Літера Й

Це лі́тера Й. (This is the letter Й.) Це коро́ткий при́голосний. (This is a short consonant.) The letter **Й** is a very short, quick consonant sound. It sounds exactly like the "y" in the English words "boy" or "toy".

Unlike the vowels we just learned, **Й** never forms a syllable on its own. You will find it very frequently at the absolute end of words or sitting comfortably right next to other vowels.

| **чай** | tea |
| **мій** | my |

## М'який знак та апостроф — Soft Sign & Apostrophe

We have talked extensively about consonants becoming "soft" or palatalized in Ukrainian. Ukrainian actually has two special symbols that perfectly control this softening process: the soft sign and the apostrophe. І оди́н си́мвол. (And one symbol.) Це ду́же важли́во! (This is very important!)

### М'який знак (Ь)

Це м'яки́й знак. (This is the soft sign.) The soft sign **Ь** is not actually a letter with its own distinct sound. It is a powerful modifier. М'яки́й знак не ма́є зву́ку. (The soft sign has no sound.) Він пом'я́кшує при́голосний. (It softens the consonant.) Its only job in the entire language is to soften the consonant that comes directly before it.

Слу́хайте ува́жно: (Listen carefully:) [Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ь](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cJlal8XKBxo)

Think of it as a silent, helpful partner that changes the quality of the preceding consonant, making it sound significantly lighter and closer to the roof of the mouth.

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **день** | day |
| **Львів** | Lviv |

In the word **день**, the **Ь** smoothly makes the final N sound soft. In the famous city name **Львів**, the **Ь** effectively makes the L sound soft. 

### Апостроф (')

Це апо́строф. (This is an apostrophe.) The apostrophe serves the exact opposite purpose in Ukrainian writing. Апо́строф блоку́є пом'я́кшення. (The apostrophe blocks softening.) It acts as a strong blocker or a protective shield. It clearly separates a hard consonant from a following iotated vowel (**Я**, **Ю**, **Є**, **Ї**). 

Why do we need this special shield? Because normally, an iotated vowel would automatically soften the consonant directly before it. The apostrophe completely prevents this softening from happening and forces the iotated vowel to stubbornly keep its full "y" sound. 

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **сім'я́** | family |
| **м'яч** | ball |

In the word **сім'я́**, the apostrophe strictly stops the **М** from becoming soft and perfectly ensures the **Я** is pronounced fully as "ya". 

> [!tip]
> Memorize this incredibly simple core rule: The soft sign (**Ь**) softens what comes BEFORE it. The apostrophe (**'**) BLOCKS softening and fiercely keeps the "y" sound in the following iotated vowel. Це пра́вило. (This is a rule.)
