## Cultural Hooks
1. "–•–ї—Ц–±-—Б—Ц–ї—М" (bread and salt) is a traditional Ukrainian symbol of hospitality, making "—Е–ї—Ц–±" a culturally significant first word to learn.
2. The letter –Ц physically resembles a beetle (–ґ—Г–Ї), which is also its keyword and a very helpful visual mnemonic device for learners.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Pronouncing –Я as /r/ вЖТ –Я is /p/ вАФ Learners confuse it with the Cyrillic –† (/r/) from Code II, requiring explicit visual contrast and minimal pair practice.
2. Pronouncing –У as a hard English "g" вЖТ –У is a soft glottal /…¶/ вАФ English speakers naturally default to a hard 'g', missing the unique throaty characteristic of the Ukrainian letter.
3. Over-aspirating –Я вЖТ Ukrainian –Я is unaspirated вАФ English speakers tend to puff air on 'p', whereas Ukrainian consonants are typically crisper.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decodability constraint:** Only 23 letters are known. Avoid words using –Щ, –©, –ѓ, –Ѓ, –Д, –ђ, –З, –¶, –§, “Р, –Ф–Ц, –Ф–Ч unless an English translation is explicitly provided.
- **Grammar ban:** Strictly no verbs and no imperative forms. Use English for all instructional text. Only bare nouns and noun phrases are allowed.
- Emphasize the tactile "hand on the throat" trick for teaching voiced and voiceless pairs, as it is highly effective for A1 learners.
