## Cultural Hooks
1. "–•–ї—Ц–±-—Б—Ц–ї—М" (Bread and salt) is a traditional Slavic welcoming ceremony, symbolizing hospitality and friendship. "–•–ї—Ц–±" is perfectly introduced here alongside the letter –•.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Visual Trap: Confusing the new letter –Я (looks like a platform, sounds like /p/) with the previously learned letter –† (sounds like /r/). вЖТ Minimal pair visual practice is essential to break this habit early.
2. English interference on –У: Pronouncing the Ukrainian –У as a hard English 'g' (like in "go"). вЖТ Emphasize that it is a soft, throaty glottal fricative (/…¶/), and explicit articulatory instruction is needed.
3. Voicing errors: Struggling to differentiate sibilant pairs like –Ц /zh/ and –® /sh/ since the voiced equivalent /zh/ does not have its own distinct single letter in the English alphabet.
