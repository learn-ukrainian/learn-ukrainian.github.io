# The Cyrillic Code III

> **Why does this matter?**
>
> You are about to cross a major threshold in your learning journey. By adding these nine consonants, you will know twenty-three letters in total, unlocking the ability to decode nearly all common street signs, menus, and basic words you encounter.

## Вступ — Introduction

Welcome to your third step in cracking the Cyrillic alphabet. You have already mastered the vowels and a solid foundation of essential consonants. Now, we are expanding your decodability even further. 

In this lesson, we will introduce nine new consonants. This brings your total to twenty-three letters. You will quickly notice that the more letters you learn, the faster you can recognize international words and essential everyday vocabulary. This progression transforms abstract symbols into meaningful language that you can immediately apply.

A key concept we will explore today is the difference between voiced and voiceless sounds. Ukrainian consonants often come in matched pairs. One sound vibrates in your throat, which is called a voiced consonant. The other sound uses only air and no vibration, which is called a voiceless consonant. Understanding this mechanical difference is the secret to developing a natural and authentic accent. 

> [!tip]
> **Check your vibration**
> Place your hand lightly on your throat. When you make a voiced sound, you will feel a distinct buzz. When you make a voiceless sound, you will only feel air moving. This physical feedback is a powerful tool for improving your pronunciation.

Let us dive into the first group of new letters and see how many words you can already read.

## Приголосні — Consonants Б, Д, П

> — Це **банан**? (Is this a banana?)
> — Так, це **банан**. (Yes, this is a banana.)
> — А це **парк**? (And is this a park?)
> — Ні, це **дім**. (No, this is a home.)
> — Де **парк**? (Where is the park?)
> — **Парк** там. (The park is there.)
> — Ось **парк**. (Here is the park.)

These three consonants are extremely common and will help you form many essential words used in daily life. Let us look at them closely and understand their unique characteristics.

The letter **Б** is a voiced consonant. Visually, it looks a bit like the number six, but it sounds exactly like the English equivalent. It is a foundational sound that you will encounter frequently. It is the first letter in the word **банан**. Watch the pronunciation video below to hear it clearly and practice repeating it aloud.

Now we will look at three more consonants that expand your range of expression. These include a sharp hissing sound and two sounds that are formed deeper in your mouth and throat. Mastering these will significantly improve your overall pronunciation.

The letter **З** is a voiced consonant. It looks strikingly similar to the number three. It makes a clear buzzing sound, completely identical to the first sound in the English word "zebra." We find it in the everyday word **зуб**. Focus on sustaining the vibration when you pronounce it.

## Дзвінкі та глухі — Voiced and Voiceless Pairs

> — Що це? (What is this?)
> — Це **шапка**. (This is a hat.)
> — А це? (And this?)
> — Це **школа**. (This is a school.)
> — Де **школа**? (Where is the school?)
> — Та **школа** там. (That school is there.)

We have extensively mentioned paired sounds throughout this lesson. This phonetic concept means that your mouth, lips, and tongue are in the exact same physical position, but one variant uses active vocal cord vibration while the other uses only a stream of air. We will now learn three distinctive symbols collectively called "hushers" that perfectly demonstrate this system.

The letter **Ж** is voiced. Visually, it looks quite a bit like a six-legged beetle, and appropriately, it makes a rich buzzing sound similar to a flying insect. The sound is very close to the middle consonant in the English word "measure." Ensure your lips are slightly rounded when you produce it.

### Літера Ж
[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ж](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dIrGVcqPwqM)

The letter **Ш** is the unvoiced companion. It looks somewhat like a pitchfork pointing squarely upwards. It produces a strong hissing noise, much like the English "sh" in "ship," but your tongue must be pulled back slightly for a more authentic tone. It appears very frequently, such as in the word **школа**.

### Літера Ш
[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ш](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1D-6MIw3OXY)

The letter **Ч** is also voiceless. It visually resembles an upside-down chair or a handwritten number four. It makes a sharp "ch" sound, similar to the beginning of the English word "church." It appears frequently in basic vocabulary, such as in the word **час** and the universally recognized word **чай**. 

### Літера Ч

Let us summarize the vital sound combinations we have learned so far. Notice how elegantly they organize perfectly within the phonetic system.

- **Б** and **П** form a matched pair.
- **Д** goes with the letter for the "T" sound.
- **З** links with the letter for the "S" sound.
- **Ж** and **Ш** are direct partners.
