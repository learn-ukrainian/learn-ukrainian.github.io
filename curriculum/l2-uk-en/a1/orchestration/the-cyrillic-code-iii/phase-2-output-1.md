# The Cyrillic Code III

> **Why does this matter?**
>
> Learning consonant pairs unlocks the structure of the Ukrainian alphabet. Once you know these letters, you can read almost anything around you.

## Вступ — Introduction

Welcome back to your journey through the Cyrillic alphabet. Let us start by looking at some familiar words from our previous lessons. Take a deep breath and read these aloud: 

- **Це молоко́** (This is milk).
- **Там кіт** (There is a cat).
- **Тут соба́ка** (A dog is here).
- **Це мі́сто** (This is a city).

You already know fourteen letters, which is a fantastic achievement. This provides a strong foundation for the next steps. **Чудо́во!** (Great!)

In this specific module, your main objective involves meeting nine new consonant letters. These characters are **Б**, **Д**, **П**, **З**, **Г**, **Х**, **Ж**, **Ш**, and **Ч**. After learning these, you unlock twenty-three letters in total. This means that almost every common text you see on the street becomes readable. For example, you will soon recognize words like **кафе́** (cafe), **рестора́н** (restaurant), and **суперма́ркет** (supermarket). 

We also introduce a key phonetic concept called voiced and voiceless pairs. This concept helps you understand how Ukrainian consonants relate to each other. By grasping this idea early, you build a solid foundation for excellent pronunciation. Do not worry about memorizing everything instantly. Take your time, read aloud, and enjoy the process of unlocking these new characters.

## Приголосні — Consonants Б, Д, П

We begin with three very common letters. Two of these are voiced consonants, and one represents a voiceless consonant. They form the building blocks of many everyday words. 

First, we look at the letter **Б**. The symbol sounds exactly like the English letter B. You find it in many familiar international words, which makes the character quite easy to remember. 

Відео для літери Б (Video for letter Б):
[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Б](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V1hxBE_JbGg)

Напри́клад:
- **бана́н** (banana) → **Це бана́н** (This is a banana).
- **буди́нок** (building) → **Там вели́кий буди́нок** (There is a large building).
- **брат** (brother) → **Там брат** (There is a brother).
- **ба́ба** (grandma) → **Це ба́ба** (This is grandma).

This character acts as the voiced partner of another letter we cover shortly.

Next is the letter **Д**. The shape looks unfamiliar at first, somewhat like a small house resting on little feet. However, the pronunciation remains simple. The sound resembles the English letter D, but in Ukrainian, you must place your tongue against the back of your top teeth. This dental articulation makes the letter sound slightly softer and clearer than the English equivalent. 

Відео для літери Д (Video for letter Д):
[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Д](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g4Bh-lqzd48)

Напри́клад:
- **дім** (house / home) → **Це дім** (This is a house).
- **день** (day) → **До́брий день** (Good day). 
- **до́бре** (good) → **Це до́бре** (This is good).
- **дере́во** (tree) → **Там дере́во** (There is a tree).

The third letter in this group is **П**. The pronunciation matches the English letter P. However, there is a crucial difference for your speaking habits. In English, you often release a small puff of air when you say P. In Ukrainian, **П** produces no puff of air. The sound comes out completely unaspirated and very crisp. 

Відео для літери П (Video for letter П):
[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — П](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JksSjjxyW5Y)

Напри́клад:
- **парк** (park) → **Там парк** (There is a park).
- **паву́к** (spider) → **Це паву́к** (This is a spider).
- **пробле́ма** (problem) → **Це пробле́ма** (This is a problem).

> [!warning] 
> Ува́га (Attention)! Visual Trap Alert! The new letter **П** looks like a small table or a platform. The pronunciation equals the English P. Do not confuse the shape with the letter **Р**, which you learned earlier. The letter **Р** sounds like a rolled R. Look at this comparison: **па́лка** (stick) uses the new P sound, while **раке́та** (rocket) uses the R sound. Always pay attention to the square shape.

The letters **Б** and **П** form an important pair. The first is voiced, and the second is voiceless. 

## Приголосні — Consonants З, Г, Х

Now we move to three more important letters. These require a bit of focus on your throat and mouth position. They bring a lot of character to the language.

Let us start with the letter **З**. The symbol looks like the number three. The pronunciation equals the English letter Z. The sound acts as the voiced partner of the letter **С**, which you already know from the first module. 

Подивіться на літеру З (Look at letter З):
[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — З](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BhASNxitC1A)

Напри́клад:
- **зе́бра** (zebra) → **Там зе́бра** (There is a zebra).
- **зуб** (tooth) → **Це зуб** (This is a tooth).
- **зима́** (winter) → **Тут зима́** (It is winter here).

Another great example is the word **газе́та** (newspaper).

This brings us to the letter **Г**. We consider this a very special sound in the language. You should never pronounce this as the hard English G. Instead, the character represents a soft, throaty sound. The pronunciation feels similar to the H in the English word "ahead" but with your voice added. Linguists call this a voiced glottal fricative. You make the sound deep down in your throat. 

Слухайте літеру Г (Listen to letter Г):
[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Г](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVnclpSI0DU)

Напри́клад:
- **гора́** (mountain) → **Там гора́** (There is a mountain).
- **газе́та** (newspaper) → **Це газе́та** (This is a newspaper).
- **гра** (game) → **Це гра** (This is a game).

> [!note] 
> The hard G sound does exist in Ukrainian, but a different letter represents it: **Ґ**. We preview that the hard sound is coming in the next module. For now, whenever you see the normal **Г**, keep the sound very soft and throaty.

Finally, we introduce the letter **Х**. The symbol looks exactly like an English X, but the sound differs completely. The letter represents a voiceless sound. You never say "ks". Instead, the pronunciation resembles the "ch" in the Scottish word "loch" or the German word "ach". Forming the sound requires a gentle friction in the back of your mouth.

Ось літера Х (Here is letter Х):
[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Х](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vpr58zJSJKc)

Напри́клад:
- **хліб** (bread) → **Це хліб** (This is bread).
- **хо́лодно** (cold) → **Тут хо́лодно** (It is cold here).
- **ха́та** (house) → **Там ха́та** (There is a house).

> [!culture]
> The word **хліб** (bread) remains a cultural staple. Bread plays an extremely important role in Ukrainian culture and history. The traditional phrase **хліб-сіль** (bread and salt) represents warm hospitality to guests. You often hear collocations about **бі́лий хліб** (white bread) or **сві́жий хліб** (fresh bread).

This symbol serves as the voiceless partner of the throaty letter **Г**. 

## Дзвінкі та глухі — Voiced and Voiceless Pairs

You have probably noticed a distinct pattern by now. Many Ukrainian consonants come in perfectly matched pairs. One is voiced, and the other remains voiceless. Understanding this concept proves fundamental for clear speech. 

Let us try an interactive physical test. Place your hand flat against the front of your throat. Say the English letter Z aloud and hold the sound. Your fingers detect a strong vibration in your throat. This vibration means the sound is voiced. In Ukrainian, we call this characteristic **дзвінки́й** (voiced). Now say the English letter S and hold the sound. Only air leaves your mouth, with zero vibration. This absence of vibration means the sound is voiceless. We call this feature **глухи́й** (voiceless).

Let us meet the remaining consonants for this module. Linguists know these as sibilants and affricates, and they add a beautiful texture to the words.

First is the letter **Ж**. The character represents a strong, voiced sound. There is no single English letter for this, but the pronunciation matches the "s" in the English word "measure" or "pleasure". Your lips should be slightly rounded and your tongue raised toward the roof of your mouth.

Ось як звучить Ж (This is how Ж sounds):
[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ж](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dIrGVcqPwqM)

Напри́клад:
- **жук** (beetle / bug) → **Там жук** (There is a beetle).
- **жа́ба** (frog) → **Це жа́ба** (This is a frog).
- **жі́нка** (woman) → **Там жі́нка** (There is a woman).

> [!tip]
> Пора́да (Tip): The letter **Ж** actually looks exactly like a little beetle with many legs. The keyword is **жук** (beetle). This visual trick makes the symbol very easy to remember! 

Its voiceless partner is the letter **Ш**. The pronunciation sounds like the English "sh" in the word "ship". Remember to use hard articulation by pulling your tongue slightly back.

[Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ш](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1D-6MIw3OXY)

Напри́клад:
- **шко́ла** (school) → **Це шко́ла** (This is a school).
- **ша́пка** (hat) → **Тут ша́пка** (Here is a hat).
- **шарф** (scarf) → **Там шарф** (There is a scarf).

A common collocation is a **зимо́ва ша́пка** (winter hat) or **те́плий шарф** (warm scarf). 

The last letter we introduce today is **Ч**. The character acts as a voiceless affricate, and the sound matches the "ch" in the English word "church". The symbol appears very frequently.

Напри́клад:
- **черепа́ха** (turtle) → **Це черепа́ха** (This is a turtle).
- **чай** (tea) → **Тут чай** (Here is tea).
- **час** (time) → **Це час** (This is time).

Here is a summary of the voiced and voiceless pairs you know so far: 
- **Б** is voiced and pairs with **П** which is voiceless.
- **Д** is voiced and pairs with **Т** which is voiceless.
- **Г** makes a soft throaty noise, while its voiceless counterpart is **Х**.
- **Ж** provides a strong sibilant vibration, contrasting with the airy **Ш**.
- **З** buzzes like a bee, whereas **С** merely hisses.

We cover the **В** and **Ф** pair in the next module. **Ду́же до́бре!** (Very good!)

## Практика читання — Reading Practice

Now you need to practice your reading skills. You know twenty-three letters. This knowledge gives you an incredible advantage. You possess the power to read signs, menus, and basic texts.

Let us build your immediate confidence by reading some internationalisms. Read these words aloud slowly: 

- **банк** (bank)
- **парк** (park)
- **тра́нспорт** (transport)
- **студе́нт** (student)
- **до́ктор** (doctor)
- **па́спорт** (passport)

These words look and sound very familiar. They use the exact letters you just learned, making them perfect for your first real reading steps. 

Next, let us practice distinguishing between voiced and voiceless pairs. This exercise provides excellent ear training and helps your mouth muscles adapt. Look at these minimal pairs and read them carefully:

- **зуб** (tooth) versus **суп** (soup). The first word starts with a voiced **З**, while the second word starts with a voiceless **С**.
- **жар** (heat) versus **шар** (layer). Notice how the initial vibration changes the entire meaning from heat to layer.

Let us continue with practical, everyday vocabulary. These words appear extremely often. All of these examples are fully readable using only your twenty-three letters:

- **шко́ла** (school)
- **по́шта** (post office). Try using this in a short phrase like **на по́шті** (at the post office).
- **чай** (tea). This features the letter **Ч** you just learned. 
- **брат** (brother) and **сестра́** (sister).

Words like **газе́та** (newspaper) and **буди́нок** (building) are now within your reach. Try reading all of these examples multiple times. The more you read, the faster your brain connects the Cyrillic shape to the correct sound. **Так трима́ти!** (Keep it up!)

## Підсумок — Summary

Congratulations on your amazing progress. You now know twenty-three of the thirty-three letters in the alphabet. This achievement means you understand six vowels and seventeen consonants. Most importantly, you mastered the essential concept of voiced and voiceless consonant pairs. You are past the halfway point, and nearly all common text is readable. 

- **Чи можете ви відчути різницю між Б і П? (Can you feel the difference in your throat between Б and П?)** — Place your hand on your throat. The letter **Б** vibrates, but the letter **П** does not.
- **Яка візуальна різниця між літерами П і Р? (What is the visual difference between the letters П and Р?)** — The letter **П** looks like a platform and sounds like "p". The letter **Р** looks like a "p" but sounds like a rolled "r".
- **Як ви вимовляєте Г і Х? (How do you pronounce Г vs Х?)** — The letter **Г** is a soft, voiced throaty sound. The letter **Х** is a voiceless sound like the "ch" in "loch".
