# Дослідження: Accusative Prepositions

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.2: "Із прийменниками: в, у, на (указівка на кінцевий пункт руху або переміщення): Степан заходить у кімнату. Я кладу книжку на твій стіл; у (в) (позначення часу дії (дні тижня)): До зустрічі в понеділок. У середу, мабуть, буде сонячна погода. У суботу офіс не працює; про (з дієсловами думати, мріяти): Думати про відпочинок. Мріяти про вечірку."
Alignment: This module precisely aligns with the A1 standard by explicitly teaching destination markers (в/у, на) and topic markers (про), while systematically extending to the foundational spatial and temporal meanings of "за" and "через".

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Nova Poshta (Нова пошта)**: Postal services, specifically "Nova Poshta", are an integral, highly efficient part of daily Ukrainian life. Going "на пошту" (to the post office) is a frequent and culturally relatable errand.
2. **The Culture of Gratitude**: In Ukrainian communication, expressing specific gratitude ("Дякую за...") is essential for politeness. Generically saying "thank you" is common, but explicitly stating what you are thankful for (e.g., "дякую за каву", "дякую за допомогу") shows warmth and sincerity.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Translating "for" as "для" for gratitude** → *Дякую для допомогу* (Incorrect) instead of *Дякую за допомогу* (Correct). Learners directly translate the English "for" without realizing "за" governs exchange/gratitude.
2. **Using "в" for events** → *Я іду в концерт* (Incorrect) instead of *Я іду на концерт* (Correct). Learners default to "в/у" for all destinations.
3. **Confusing temporal "за" and "через"** → Both translate to "in" in English ("in an hour"), but "за годину" means the task takes an hour to complete, while "через годину" means the event happens an hour from now.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Explain the `в/у` alternation as a beautiful, rhythmic feature of the Ukrainian language (euphony), designed to avoid awkward consonant clusters, not as a rigid rule. Present Ukrainian independently.
- **English Scaffolding**: Since this is A1, use English clearly in the grammar explanations to draw the cognitive distinction between static location ("where") and dynamic destination ("where to").
- **Strict Verification**: Ensure no Russianisms slip into vocabulary examples (e.g., use "їсти", never "кушати"; "отримувати", never "получати").
