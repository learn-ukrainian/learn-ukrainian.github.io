## Діалоги (Dialogues)

How do you start your day? Describing your daily routine is one of the most common conversation topics in any language. In Ukrainian, talking about your morning involves a special group of verbs that show you are performing an action on yourself.

Before we look at the grammar rules, let's observe how people talk about their mornings in real life. Pay attention to the verbs that end in **-ся**. 

Here is a conversation between two colleagues discussing their typical weekday mornings. Notice how they ask about time and sequence.

<div class="dialogue">


**Олена:** Привіт! Коли ти прокидаєшся? *(Hi! When do you wake up?)*


**Максим:** Я прокидаюся о сьомій. *(I wake up at seven.)*


**Олена:** Що ти робиш потім? *(What do you do then?)*


**Максим:** Вмиваюся, одягаюся і снідаю. *(I wash up, get dressed, and have breakfast.)*


**Олена:** А коли ти йдеш на роботу? *(And when do you go to work?)*


**Максим:** О восьмій. *(At eight.)*


</div>



In this short exchange, you can already see a pattern. Words like **прокидаюся** (I wake up) and **вмиваюся** (I wash up) describe actions the speaker does to themselves. Meanwhile, **снідаю** (I have breakfast) is a standard action. We also see the phrase **о сьомій** (at seven) and **о восьмій** (at eight). When talking about routines, simply adding **о** before the number tells us *at what time* an action happens.

## Дієслова на -ся (Reflexive Verbs)

Now that we have seen these verbs in action, let's understand how they work. In Ukrainian, verbs with the suffix **-ся** or **-сь** at the end are called reflexive verbs. 

As explained in Ukrainian textbooks (such as Караман Grade 10, p.176), these verbs denote an action directed at oneself. The suffix **-ся** actually comes from an old, shortened form of the word "self". Therefore, when you add it to a verb, you change the target of the action from someone else to yourself.

Let's look at a direct comparison:
*   **вмивати** (to wash someone) → **вмиватися** (to wash oneself)
*   **одягати** (to dress someone) → **одягатися** (to dress oneself)

If a mother washes her child's face, she uses the verb **вмивати**. But when you wash your own face in the morning, you use **вмиватися**. 

### Conjugating Reflexive Verbs

The beauty of reflexive verbs is that they do not require you to learn a completely new conjugation system. You take the verb stem and add special reflexive endings. These endings combine the standard conjugation pattern with **-ся** built right in.

Let's conjugate the verb **вмиватися** (to wash up). The stem is **вмива-**, and the reflexive endings are:

*   **я** вмива + **юся** = **я вмиваюся** (I wash up)
*   **ти** вмива + **єшся** = **ти вмиваєшся** (You wash up)
*   **він/вона** вмива + **ється** = **він/вона вмивається** (He/she washes up)

Notice: for **я** and **ти**, you can think of it as adding **-ся** to the regular form (вмиваю + ся, вмиваєш + ся). But for **він/вона**, the ending is **-ється** as a whole unit — do not try to add **-ся** to вмиває, because that would give you the wrong form.

:::tip
You will sometimes see the shorter suffix **-сь** used after vowels, like **я вмиваюсь**. Both **я вмиваюся** and **я вмиваюсь** are absolutely correct and mean exactly the same thing. For now, focus on the full **-ся** ending to recognize the pattern clearly.
:::

Another essential morning verb is **прокидатися** (to wake up). It conjugates exactly the same way.

*   **я прокидаюся** (I wake up)
*   **ти прокидаєшся** (You wake up)
*   **він прокидається** (He wakes up)
*   **вона прокидається** (She wakes up)

### Pronunciation Rules

When we add **-ся** to the **ти** and **він/вона** forms, we create consonant combinations that change how the word sounds. Ukrainian spelling preserves the grammatical structure, but the spoken pronunciation blends the sounds together smoothly. 

As noted in Кравцова Grade 4 (p.113), the spelling and pronunciation differ, and you must learn both!

1.  **The -шся ending (ти form):** When **ш** and **с** meet, they blend into a long, soft "s" sound. It sounds like a prolonged, gentle hiss. 
    *   Spelling: **ти вмиваєшся**
    *   Pronunciation: sounds like [вмиваєс':а]
2.  **The -ться ending (він/вона form):** When **т** and **с** meet, they blend into a long, soft "ts" sound.
    *   Spelling: **він вмивається**
    *   Pronunciation: sounds like [вмиваєц':а]

Do not try to pronounce the "ш" or the "т" individually. Let them melt into the following "с". This smooth blending is a hallmark of natural, fluent Ukrainian speech.

## Мій ранок (My Morning)

To describe a complete morning routine, we need to combine our reflexive verbs with some regular verbs and, most importantly, words that organize our story into a logical sequence. 

Let's review the essential vocabulary for your morning routine. Notice which actions are reflexive and which are not.

Reflexive morning verbs:
*   **прокидатися** (to wake up)
*   **вмиватися** (to wash face/hands)
*   **одягатися** (to get dressed)
*   **збиратися** (to get ready)
*   **повертатися** (to return home)

Non-reflexive morning verbs for contrast:
*   **снідати** (to have breakfast)
*   **пити каву** (to drink coffee)

### The Irregular Verb "Йти"

There is one crucial action missing from our list: leaving the house! The verb **йти** (to go, to walk) is essential for daily routines, but it is irregular. It does not follow the standard Group I or Group II conjugation patterns you have learned so far. You must memorize its forms.

*   **я йду** (I go)
*   **ти йдеш** (You go)
*   **він/вона йде** (He/she goes)

We use this verb when we are talking about walking or moving in a specific direction right now or as a regular habit, such as going to work or going to school.

*   Я **йду** на роботу. (I go to work.)
*   Він **йде** в університет. (He goes to the university.)

### Sequence Words

When we tell a story, even a simple story about waking up, we need connecting words. If you only use "and" (і), your story will sound like a robotic list. To make your narrative flow naturally, Ukrainian uses sequence words. These words help the listener understand the order of events.

*   **спочатку** (first, at first)
*   **потім** (then, next)
*   **після цього** (after this)
*   **нарешті** (finally)

You usually place these words at the beginning of the sentence. They set the stage for the action that follows. 

Let's put everything together. Here is a complete, natural description of a morning routine using reflexive verbs, the irregular verb **йти**, and sequence words to connect the thoughts.

> **Мій ранок**
> **Спочатку** я прокидаюся о сьомій. 
> **Потім** вмиваюся і одягаюся. 
> **Після цього** снідаю і п'ю каву. 
> **Нарешті** йду на роботу.

Notice how the sequence words guide the reader step-by-step through the morning. The actions flow logically from waking up to leaving the house. The verb **снідати** does not have a **-ся** ending because you do not "breakfast yourself"; it is a standard action. However, **прокидатися** and **вмиватися** clearly show that you are the one waking up and washing yourself.

By mastering these few sequence words and understanding which verbs require the reflexive ending, you can easily describe your daily habits. This structure works for any routine, whether you are talking about your busy Monday morning or your relaxed Sunday afternoon.

| Verdict | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **PASS** | Zero critical findings, at most minor issues |
| **REVISE** | Has major findings but no criticals — fixable without rewrite |
| **REJECT** | Has any critical finding — fundamental problems requiring rewrite |

### Step 7: Fix it yourself (REVISE only)

If your verdict is **REVISE**, you MUST output a `<fixes>` block with exact find/replace pairs. The pipeline applies these deterministically — no LLM regeneration, no rewriting from scratch.

Rules for fixes:
- Each fix is a YAML entry with `find:` (exact text from the module) and `replace:` (corrected text)
- The `find` string must be an EXACT substring of the module content — copy-paste it
- Keep fixes minimal — change only what's wrong, preserve surrounding text
- Do NOT fix словник/vocabulary tables (those are generated by a downstream tool)
- For word count issues: add content as a new `insert_after:` entry instead of find/replace
