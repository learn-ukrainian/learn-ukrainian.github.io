# Дослідження: Imperative and Requests

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.2: "Imperative mood — 2nd person only at A1"
Alignment: This module adheres exactly to the standard by teaching only the 2nd person singular (ти) and plural/formal (ви) imperative forms, deliberately excluding 1st person plural and 3rd person forms. It introduces the core required vocabulary for directions and requests.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|-------|------------------|
| читай(те) | Regular stem, highly frequent classroom command. | Читайте текст. |
| скажи(те) | Perfective. Irregular consonant mutation (к→ж). | Скажіть, будь ласка. |
| дай(те) | Perfective. Irregular short form stem. | Дайте, будь ласка. |
| іди(те) | High frequency motion command. | Ідіть сюди. |

## Cultural Hooks
1. Using the formal "Ви" form (e.g., *читайте*, *дайте*) is culturally mandatory when addressing strangers, service workers, or elders, to show respect. 
2. Direct commands in Ukrainian are common but can sound abrupt to English speakers; adding "будь ласка" (*please*) is the essential social lubricant that transforms an order into a polite request.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Confusing imperative with infinitive → Saying "Читати!" instead of "Читай!" — English speakers often rely on dictionaries and think the infinitive is the base command form, not realizing infinitives are for public signs, not personal dialogue.
2. Forgetting the plural/formal endings → Saying "Дай, будь ласка" to a barista — Learners default to the simplest imperative form and forget to add "-те" to match the polite "Ви" register.
3. Over-regularizing irregular stems → Saying "Дайти" instead of "Дайте" — Learners attempt to apply the standard "drop the -ти" rule to irregular verbs like "дати" or "сказати".

## Notes for Content Writing
- Perfective aspect verbs (like сказати, дати, взяти) are uniquely ALLOWED here for imperative forms, despite general A1 imperfective rules.
- Maintain the strict A1 limit of max 10 words per Ukrainian sentence.
- Ensure thorough English scaffolding for all grammar rules; use structural containers (tables, bullet lists) for the Ukrainian examples.
- Include IPA only on the first occurrence of a new word form.
