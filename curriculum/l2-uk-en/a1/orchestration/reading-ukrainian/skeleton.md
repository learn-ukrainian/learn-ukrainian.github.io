## Склади (Syllables) (~275 words total)

- P1 (~80 words): Hook — you already know the letters from M01, now learn to read whole words. The secret: Ukrainian has one unbreakable rule for reading. Introduce the syllable rule directly from Большакова Grade 1 p.25: "У слові стільки складів, скільки голосних звуків." Count vowels = count syllables. Demonstrate: ма-ма (2 vowels А, А = 2 syllables), мо-ло-ко (3 vowels О, О, О = 3 syllables), банк (1 vowel А = 1 syllable), сон (1 vowel О = 1 syllable).
- P2 (~90 words): Step-by-step method for reading any new word, following Большакова p.29 звуковий аналіз. Four steps: (1) Find the vowels — circle them mentally, (2) Split at syllable boundaries — consonants prefer starting new syllables (open-syllable principle), (3) Sound out each syllable slowly, (4) Blend at natural speed. Walk through аптека: vowels А, Е, А → а-пте-ка → read aloud. Then університет: vowels У, І, Е, И, Е → у-ні-вер-си-тет.
- P3 (~55 words): Practice set with scaffolding. Words grouped by syllable count: 1 syllable (дім, ліс, хліб), 2 syllables (ка-ша, во-да, ру-ка), 3 syllables (мо-ло-ко, лю-ди-на, ву-ли-ця). Encourage the learner to find vowels first, then split, then read aloud.
- Exercise: quiz — "How many syllables?" Count the vowels in 8 words (столиця, шоколад, бібліотека, банк, оса, ананас, книга, університет). From activity_hints item 2.
- P4 (~50 words): Why this matters — Ukrainian children learn to read this exact way. Unlike English, Ukrainian spelling is consistent: every letter makes the same sound every time. Once you master syllable-by-syllable reading, you can read ANY Ukrainian word, even ones you've never seen before.

## Голосні літери (Vowel Letters) (~330 words total)

- P1 (~70 words): Recap from M01 — Ukrainian has 6 vowel sounds but 10 vowel letters. Now meet all 10. The simple six first: А [а], О [о], У [у], Е [е], И [и], І [і]. Each letter = one consistent sound, no exceptions. These are the straightforward ones — what you see is what you hear. Examples: мама (А twice), молоко (О three times), музика (У, И, А).
- P2 (~100 words): The iotated vowels — Я, Ю, Є, Ї. These are clever letters that do double duty. At word start or after a vowel, they contain a hidden [й]: Я = [йа] (яблуко, маяк), Ю = [йу] (юнак, мою), Є = [йе] (єнот, моє). After a consonant, they soften it instead: пісня (Н softened + [а]), люблю (Л softened + [у]), синє (Н softened + [е]). Reference Большакова Grade 1 p.66 and Grade 2 p.35 sound analysis exercises.
- P3 (~60 words): Ї — the unique one. Буква Ї ALWAYS means [йі], no exceptions. It never softens a consonant. It appears only at word start (їжак, їсти), after a vowel (мої, твої), or after an apostrophe (з'їсти). Ї is uniquely Ukrainian — no other Slavic language has it. Examples: їжак (hedgehog), їсти (to eat), мої (my, plural).
- Exercise: match-up — Match 4 iotated vowels to their sound components: Я↔[й]+[а], Ю↔[й]+[у], Є↔[й]+[е], Ї↔[й]+[і]. From activity_hints item 3.
- P4 (~100 words): Critical minimal pairs — И vs І. These two sounds don't exist in English, and confusing them changes meaning entirely. кит (whale) vs кіт (cat), дим (smoke) vs дім (house), лис (fox) vs ліс (forest), сир (cheese) vs сір (grey). И is a back-central vowel (tongue pulled back), І is a front vowel (tongue forward, like "ee" but shorter). Reference Anna's pronunciation videos for each pair. Practice: read кит, then кіт — feel how your tongue position shifts. This distinction is essential; native speakers hear it instantly.

## Читання слів (Reading Words) (~330 words total)

- P1 (~80 words): Now combine everything — letters, vowel knowledge, syllable splitting — to read real Ukrainian words fluently. Key mindset shift: stop reading letter-by-letter. Read syllable-by-syllable. Your eyes should jump from vowel to vowel, building syllables around them. Demonstrate with книга: spot vowels И, А → кни-га → say it. Then столиця: О, И, Я → сто-ли-ця. Then шоколад: О, О, А → шо-ко-лад. Speed comes from recognizing syllable patterns, not individual letters.
- P2 (~90 words): Common word patterns — train your eye to recognize shapes. CVCV (consonant-vowel-consonant-vowel): мама, тато, каша, вода, рука, хата, коза, нога — these are the easiest, always two syllables, always predictable. CVC (one syllable, closed): дім, сон, ліс, дуб, хліб, банк. CVCCV: школа, книга, парта — the consonant cluster in the middle looks intimidating but just belongs to the second syllable (шко-ла, кни-га, пар-та). The more patterns you see, the faster you'll read without thinking.
- P3 (~80 words): Special combinations to watch for — three letters that behave differently. Щ always sounds [шч]: що, ще, щастя — it's one letter but two sounds, like "shch" compressed. Ь (soft sign) has NO sound of its own — it softens the preceding consonant: день, сіль, кінь — the Н in день is softer than in дно. Апостроф (') separates a consonant from an iotated vowel: сім'я, м'ясо, п'ять — without it, the consonant would be softened instead of separated. All three explored fully in M03.
- Exercise: fill-in — Divide 8 words into syllables using the vowel-counting method: молоко → мо-ло-ко, аптека → а-пте-ка, людина → лю-ди-на, вулиця → ву-ли-ця, бібліотека → бі-блі-о-те-ка, столиця → сто-ли-ця, фотографія → фо-то-гра-фі-я, університет → у-ні-вер-си-тет. From activity_hints item 1.
- P4 (~80 words): Reading tip — when you encounter a long unfamiliar word, don't panic. Apply the method mechanically: find vowels, split into syllables, sound out each syllable, blend. Even бібліотека (5 syllables!) becomes manageable: І, І, О, Е, А → бі-блі-о-те-ка. Speed will come with practice. Native speakers don't think about this process anymore — it's automatic. Your goal: make syllable reading so automatic that you forget you're doing it.

## Читаємо разом (Reading Together) (~220 words total)

- P1 (~70 words): Progressive reading ladder — start where you're comfortable, push one level higher. Level 1 (2 syllables): мама, тато, вода, рука, хата, каша, коза, нога. Level 2 (3 syllables): аптека, молоко, людина, вулиця, столиця, музика. Level 3 (4+ syllables): університет, бібліотека, фотографія, енциклопедія. Read each word aloud. Time yourself — by the end of this module, Level 1 should feel instant.
- P2 (~80 words): Your first Ukrainian text — read this passage aloud, syllable by syllable, then again at natural speed. "Це Київ. Це столиця. Тут аптека і банк. Там школа. Що це? Це кафе. А це пошта." Every word here uses only what you've learned. Це (this is), тут (here), там (there), і (and), що (what), а (and/but). You just read seven Ukrainian sentences. You're reading Ukrainian.
- Exercise: quiz — Read the word and choose its meaning from four options. 6 items: яблуко, молоко, людина, столиця, каша, пісня. From activity_hints item 4.
- P3 (~70 words): Challenge round — read these slightly longer sentences: "Це моя книга. Тут мама і тато. Там каша і молоко. Що це? Це яблуко." Notice how words you practiced individually now flow together in sentences. Reading Ukrainian is syllable by syllable at first, then word by word, then thought by thought. You're already on step two.

## Підсумок — Summary (~165 words total)

- P1 (~165 words): Recap the four key skills from this module. First: the syllable rule — count vowels, count syllables. It never fails. Second: the 10 vowel letters — 6 simple (А, О, У, Е, И, І) and 4 iotated (Я, Ю, Є, Ї), each with predictable behavior. Third: the reading method — find vowels, split into syllables, sound out, blend. Fourth: special signs Щ, Ь, and apostrophe (preview for M03). Self-check questions: How many syllables in бібліотека? (5 — count the vowels: І, І, О, Е, А.) What sound does Ї always make? ([йі].) What's the difference between кит and кіт? (Whale vs cat — И vs І.) What does Ь do? (Softens the consonant before it.) If you answered all four, you're ready for M03: Special Signs. Practice reading any Ukrainian text you can find — signs, menus, social media. Sound out every word. Speed will follow.
