## Склади (Syllables) (~275 words total)
- P1 (~90 words): Hook — you already know the alphabet from M01, now learn to read any word. Introduce the golden rule from Большакова Grade 1 p.25: «У слові стільки складів, скільки голосних звуків.» Demonstrate with ма-ма (2 vowels = 2 syllables), мо-ло-ко (3 vowels = 3 syllables), банк (1 vowel = 1 syllable), о-са (2 vowels = 2 syllables). This rule never breaks — it works for every Ukrainian word.
- P2 (~100 words): Step-by-step reading method based on Большакова p.29 звуковий аналіз: (1) Find the vowels — they are the syllable cores, (2) Split at syllable boundaries — Ukrainian follows the open-syllable principle (складоподіл), consonants prefer starting new syllables, (3) Sound out each syllable, (4) Blend into the full word at natural speed. Walk through аптека: а-пте-ка (vowels А, Е, А → 3 syllables). Then університет: у-ні-вер-си-тет (5 vowels = 5 syllables).
- P3 (~85 words): Practice counting without splitting — just count the vowels. Words: сон (1), сало (2), ламана (3), смола (2), ананас (3), бібліотека (5). Emphasize that ь and apostrophe are NOT vowels, never count them. Mention that наголос (stress) falls on one syllable — covered fully in M03, but for now just focus on splitting and blending.
- Exercise: quiz — "How many syllables?" Count the vowels in 8 words (сон, слон, оса, молоко, книга, школа, університет, шоколад).

## Голосні літери (Vowel Letters) (~330 words total)
- P1 (~80 words): Recall from M01: Ukrainian has 6 vowel sounds but 10 vowel letters. Now meet all 10 individually. The simple six — each letter makes ONE consistent sound, no surprises: А [а] (аптека), О [о] (око), У [у] (рука), Е [е] (село), И [и] (кит), І [і] (кіт). Unlike English, these sounds never change — А is always [а], never [ei] or [ə].
- P2 (~120 words): The iotated vowels — four letters that can represent TWO sounds. Я = [йа] at word start or after a vowel: яблуко [йаблуко], моя [мойа]. After a consonant, Я softens it + [а]: пісня (Н is softened). Same pattern for Ю = [йу] at start (юнак), softening + [у] after consonant (люблю). Є = [йе] at start (єнот), softening + [е] after consonant (синє). Then the unique one: Ї ALWAYS = [йі] — it never softens a consonant. Only appears at word start, after a vowel, or after apostrophe: їжак, мої, з'їв. Ї is uniquely Ukrainian — Russian doesn't have it.
- P3 (~70 words): Critical minimal pairs where one vowel letter changes meaning entirely. И vs І: кит (whale) vs кіт (cat), дим (smoke) vs дім (house), лис (fox) vs ліс (forest), рик (roar) vs рік (year), сир (cheese) vs сір (grey). These are different sounds — И is a back vowel (tongue pulled back), І is a front vowel (tongue forward). Practice hearing the difference.
- Exercise: match-up — Match 4 iotated vowels to their sound components: Я=[й]+[а], Ю=[й]+[у], Є=[й]+[е], Ї=[й]+[і].
- Exercise: fill-in — Divide 8 words into syllables marking vowels: яб-лу-ко, мо-ло-ко, лю-ди-на, ву-ли-ця, пі-сня, є-нот, ї-жак, сто-ли-ця.

## Читання слів (Reading Words) (~330 words total)
- P1 (~100 words): Now apply everything to read real words fluently. Key strategy: don't read letter-by-letter — read syllable-by-syllable. Start with the vowels, then build outward. Demonstrate with книга: find vowels И, А → кни-га → blend → книга. Then шоколад: О, О, А → шо-ко-лад. Then вулиця: У, И, Я → ву-ли-ця. The more you practice this vowel-first approach, the faster you'll read any new word without hesitation.
- P2 (~110 words): Common word patterns for building reading speed. CVCV (simplest): мама, тато, каша, вода, рука, хата, коза, нога — two syllables, alternating consonant-vowel. CVCCV: школа, книга, парта — a consonant cluster before the second vowel. CVC (one syllable): дім, сон, ліс, дуб, хліб, банк. Longer patterns: аптека (V-CCV-CV), молоко (CV-CV-CV), столиця (CCVCVCV). Read each group aloud — start slow, then speed up. Pattern recognition is what makes reading feel natural rather than mechanical.
- P3 (~70 words): Special letter combinations to watch for while reading. Щ is always [шч]: що, ще, щастя. Ь (soft sign) has no sound of its own — it softens the consonant before it: день, сіль, кінь. The apostrophe (') separates a consonant from an iotated vowel: сім'я [с'імйа], м'ясо [мйасо], п'ять [пйать]. These will be explored fully in M03 — for now, just recognize them when reading.
- Exercise: quiz — Read the word and choose its meaning, 6 items (каша, вулиця, столиця, яблуко, людина, бібліотека — match to English meanings).

## Читаємо разом (Reading Together) (~220 words total)
- P1 (~80 words): Progressive reading ladder — start simple, build up. Level 1 (2 syllables): мама, тато, вода, рука, хата, каша, книга, школа. Level 2 (3 syllables): аптека, молоко, людина, вулиця, аптека, столиця. Level 3 (4+ syllables): університет, бібліотека, фотографія, шоколадний. Read each level until it feels comfortable, then move to the next. Speed comes from practice, not rushing.
- P2 (~80 words): Reading a simple connected text using only Це + nouns and location words (no verb conjugation yet). «Це Київ. Це столиця. Тут аптека і банк. Там школа. А це що? Це кафе. Поруч пошта. Тут вулиця, там парк.» Read it aloud three times: first slowly syllable-by-syllable, then at normal pace, then try to read without pausing between words. You just read Ukrainian sentences.
- P3 (~60 words): Challenge reading — try these longer words using the vowel-first method: бібліотека (і, і, о, е, а → 5 syllables), університет (у, і, е, и, е → 5 syllables), фотографія (о, о, а, і, я → 5 syllables). If you can read these, you can read any Ukrainian word. The syllable rule and vowel-first strategy work every time.

## Підсумок — Summary (~165 words total)
- P1 (~90 words): Recap the three key skills from this module. First: the syllable rule — count vowels to count syllables, it never fails. Second: the 10 vowel letters — 6 simple (А, О, У, Е, И, І) and 4 iotated (Я, Ю, Є, Ї) that can represent two sounds. Third: the reading strategy — find vowels first, split into syllables, sound out, blend. These three tools together let you read any Ukrainian word you encounter, even if you've never seen it before.
