## Склади (Syllables) (~275 words total)

- P1 (~70 words): Hook — you already know the alphabet from M01, now learn to read any word. Introduce the one rule that never breaks: "У слові стільки складів, скільки голосних звуків" (Большакова Grade 1 p.25). Demonstrate with ма-ма (2 vowels = 2 syllables), мо-ло-ко (3 vowels = 3 syllables), банк (1 vowel = 1 syllable). Emphasize: this works for EVERY Ukrainian word, no exceptions.

- P2 (~90 words): Step-by-step method for reading any new word, following Большакова p.29 звуковий аналіз: (1) Find the vowels — they're the syllable cores. (2) Split at syllable boundaries — Ukrainian follows the open-syllable principle (складоподіл), meaning consonants prefer to START new syllables: а-пте-ка (not ап-те-ка). (3) Sound out each syllable slowly. (4) Blend at natural speed. Walk through у-ні-вер-си-тет step by step, marking vowels У, І, Е, И, Е first, then dividing.

- P3 (~55 words): Quick-count practice block. Present 6-8 words and model counting vowels to predict syllables before reading: сон (1), о-са (2), со-сна (2), а-на-нас (3), бі-блі-о-те-ка (5). Reference the Grade 2 textbook exercise "Визначте кількість складів у словах, не поділяючи їх на склади."

- Exercise: **quiz** — "How many syllables?" Count the vowels in 8 words (шоколад, книга, молоко, дім, вулиця, аптека, хліб, університет). Matches activity_hint #2.

- P4 (~60 words): Reinforce that syllable-counting is the foundation for everything that follows. Preview: once you can split into syllables, reading becomes syllable-blending, not letter-guessing. Compare a child sounding out "мо-ло-ко" vs an adult reading "молоко" — same process, just faster. This is exactly how Ukrainian first-graders learn (Большакова method).

## Голосні літери (Vowel Letters) (~330 words total)

- P1 (~80 words): Recall from M01: Ukrainian has 6 vowel sounds but 10 vowel letters. Now meet all 10 individually. The simple six first — each letter makes ONE consistent sound, no surprises: А [а] (мама), О [о] (молоко), У [у] (рука), Е [е] (село), И [и] (дим), І [і] (дім). Unlike English, these never change pronunciation based on position. А always sounds like А.

- P2 (~110 words): The iotated vowels — four letters that do double duty. Explain the two-sound rule: at word start or after a vowel, they produce [й] + vowel. Я = [йа]: яблуко, яма, маяк (after vowel А). Ю = [йу]: юрта, юнак. Є = [йе]: єнот. Ї = ALWAYS [йі], never softens a consonant — їжак, їдять, мої. Ї is unique to Ukrainian. Then the softening rule: after a consonant, Я/Ю/Є soften it and produce just the vowel sound. Пісня — Н is softened + [а]. Люблю — Л is softened + [у]. Reference Grade 2 textbook (p.35): "Букви я, ю, є на початку складу позначають два звуки."

- P3 (~70 words): Critical minimal pairs that show why vowels matter. И vs І changes meaning: кит (whale) vs кіт (cat), дим (smoke) vs дім (house), лис (fox) vs ліс (forest), сік (juice) vs рік (year). These pairs come directly from the Большакова буквар cover page. Stress that И and І are different sounds — not interchangeable. Mention Anna's pronunciation videos for hearing the difference.

- Exercise: **match-up** — Match iotated vowels to their sound components: Я=[й]+[а], Ю=[й]+[у], Є=[й]+[е], Ї=[й]+[і]. 4 items. Matches activity_hint #3.

## Читання слів (Reading Words) (~330 words total)

- P1 (~80 words): Transition from letters to fluent reading. Key mindset shift: don't read letter-by-letter, read syllable-by-syllable. Strategy: find vowels first (you just learned them), divide into syllables (you just learned that too), then blend. Walk through книга: find vowels И, А → кни-га → blend → книга. Then столиця: О, И, Я → сто-ли-ця → столиця. Each new word uses the same two-step method.

- P2 (~90 words): Common word patterns for building reading speed. Present grouped examples: Two-syllable CVCV: мама, тато, каша, вода, рука, хата, коза, нога. Two-syllable CVCCV: школа, книга, парта. One-syllable CVC: дім, сон, ліс, дуб, хліб, банк. Explain that recognizing patterns speeds up reading — once you've read каша, вода, рука, you can read хата instantly because it follows the same pattern. The more patterns your eye learns, the less sounding-out you need.

- Exercise: **fill-in** — Divide 8 words into syllables with dividers: мо|ло|ко, ап|те|ка, лю|ди|на, ву|ли|ця, шо|ко|лад, бі|блі|о|те|ка, фо|то|гра|фі|я, у|ні|вер|си|тет. Matches activity_hint #1.

- P3 (~80 words): Special letter combinations to watch for while reading. Щ always reads as [шч]: що, ще, щастя — it's one letter but two sounds. Ь (soft sign) has no sound of its own — it softens the preceding consonant: день, сіль, кінь. The apostrophe (') separates a consonant from an iotated vowel, blocking softening: сім'я (not *сімя), м'ясо, п'ять. Flag these as previews — M03 covers them in full detail.

- P4 (~80 words): Put it together with real words from everyday life. Read these words using the full method (find vowels → split → blend): аптека (3 syllables — а-пте-ка), людина (3 — лю-ди-на), вулиця (3 — ву-ли-ця), столиця (3 — сто-ли-ця), шоколад (3 — шо-ко-лад), фотографія (5 — фо-то-гра-фі-я). Note how людина uses Ю after Л (softening, not two sounds) while юнак uses Ю at word start (two sounds [йу]).

## Читаємо разом (Reading Together) (~220 words total)

- P1 (~70 words): Progressive reading ladder — start simple, build confidence. Level 1 (2 syllables): мама, тато, вода, рука, хата, каша. Level 2 (3 syllables): аптека, молоко, людина, вулиця, столиця. Level 3 (4+ syllables): університет, бібліотека, фотографія. Challenge: read each level aloud, first slowly (syllable by syllable), then at natural speed. Time yourself — you'll get faster with each pass.

- P2 (~80 words): First reading passage — a simple text using only Це + noun structures (no verb conjugation needed, keeping to A1.1 scope). Present: "Це Київ. Це столиця. Тут аптека і банк. Там школа. Що це? Це кафе. А це пошта." Then guide comprehension: point out familiar words, show how Це introduces things, how Тут (here) and Там (there) indicate location. This is real Ukrainian reading — simple, but real.

- Exercise: **quiz** — Read the word and choose its meaning. 6 items using words from the passage and reading ladder: столиця, аптека, бібліотека, школа, пошта, кафе. Matches activity_hint #4.

- P3 (~70 words): Encouragement and reading tips. You can now sound out ANY Ukrainian word — even ones you've never seen. The method always works: vowels → syllables → blend. Speed comes with practice, not memorization. Suggest: pick any Ukrainian sign, menu, or webpage and try reading aloud. You won't understand every word yet, but you CAN pronounce them — and that's a huge step.
