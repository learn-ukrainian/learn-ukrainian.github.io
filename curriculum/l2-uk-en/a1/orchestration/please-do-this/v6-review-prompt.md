## Наказовий спосіб (The Imperative Mood)

In Ukrainian grade 5 textbooks, students learn about the **наказовий спосіб** (imperative mood). The word **наказ** means "order" or "command," and **спосіб** means "mood" or "method." This form of the verb is used whenever you want someone to do something. You use it for commands, requests, instructions, and invitations. 

At the A1 level, we focus on two main forms of the imperative mood: the **ти** (informal, one person) form and the **ви** (formal or plural) form. 

### Is it rude to use the imperative?

English speakers often worry that using a direct command sounds rude or bossy. In English, we often soften requests by saying "Could you please read?" or "Would you mind opening the door?". 

In Ukrainian, using the imperative mood is normal in daily speech. Telling a friend **Читай!** (Read!) is not rude — it is simply direct. It is how teachers talk to students, parents to children, and friends to each other. 

To make any command polite, you simply add the magic phrase **будь ласка** (please). 

*   **Дай!** (Give! — informal) → **Дай, будь ласка.** (Please give. — informal)
*   **Дайте!** (Give! — formal/plural) → **Дайте, будь ласка.** (Please give. — formal/plural)

You can also make a command sound friendly and warm by adding the person's name in the vocative case, along with a pleasant tone of voice.

*   **Олено, прочитай, будь ласка.** (Olena, please read.)
*   **Тарасе, скажи, будь ласка.** (Taras, please tell me.)

Here are a few more examples of requests using the imperative with **будь ласка**:

*   **Слухай, будь ласка.** (Please listen. — to a friend)
*   **Слухайте, будь ласка.** (Please listen. — to a group or a formal contact)
*   **Дивись, будь ласка.** (Please look. — to a friend)
*   **Дивіться, будь ласка.** (Please look. — to a group or a formal contact)

When you hear a teacher say **пишіть** (write), they are not yelling at you; they are simply giving an instruction using the correct grammatical form.

## Як утворити? (How to Form It)

Forming the imperative mood is usually quite simple. The pattern involves taking the stem of the verb and adding a specific ending. Let's look at how to build the **ти** (informal, singular) form and the **ви** (formal/plural) form. Most imperative forms are short — often just one or two syllables.

### The Ти-form (Informal, Singular)

To form the imperative for a friend, a family member, or a child, we create the **ти** form. 

**Group I verbs** often drop the infinitive ending **-ти** and replace it with **-й**.
*   **читати** (to read) → **читай** (read!)
*   **слухати** (to listen) → **слухай** (listen!)
*   **запитати** (to ask) → **запитай** (ask!)

**Group II verbs** often drop the infinitive ending **-ти** and replace it with **-и** or sometimes just leave a soft consonant.
*   **говорити** (to speak) → **говори** (speak!)
*   **ходити** (to walk/go) → **ходи** (walk!)
*   **дивитися** (to look/watch) → **дивись** (look!) — notice the **-ся** becomes **-сь**.

Some **-ати** verbs have a consonant change in the stem, so their imperative looks different from the simple **-й** pattern:
*   **писати** (to write) → **пиши** (write!) — the consonant changes from **с** to **ш**.

**Irregular and common short verbs** have special forms that you should memorize. They are used very frequently.
*   **дати** (to give) → **дай** (give!)
*   **сказати** (to say/tell) → **скажи** (say/tell!)
*   **іти** (to go) → **іди** (go!)
*   **сісти** (to sit down) → **сядь** (sit down!)
*   **відкрити** (to open) → **відкрий** (open!)

### The Ви-form (Formal or Plural)

Once you know the **ти** form, creating the **ви** form is very easy. The general rule is: **add -те to the ти-form**.

If the **ти** form ends in **-й** or a soft consonant:
*   **читай** → **читайте** (read!)
*   **слухай** → **слухайте** (listen!)
*   **дай** → **дайте** (give!)
*   **відкрий** → **відкрийте** (open!)
*   **сядь** → **сядьте** (sit down!)
*   **запитай** → **запитайте** (ask!)

If the **ти** form ends in **-и**, you change the **-и** to **-іть** for the **ви** form. Note the spelling change: **-и** becomes **-і** before **-ть**.
*   **пиши** → **пишіть** (write!)
*   **говори** → **говоріть** (speak!)
*   **скажи** → **скажіть** (say/tell!)
*   **іди** → **ідіть** (go!)
*   **ходи** → **ходіть** (walk/go!)

If the verb has the reflexive particle **-ся / -сь**, it attaches at the very end:
*   **дивись** → **дивіться** (look!)

Let's see these in full sentences:

*   **Олено, читай текст.** (Olena, read the text.)
*   **Діти, читайте текст.** (Children, read the text.)
*   **Тарасе, пиши речення.** (Taras, write the sentence.)
*   **Пане Іване, пишіть речення.** (Mr. Ivan, write the sentence.)
*   **Дай мені підручник, будь ласка.** (Give me the textbook, please. — informal)
*   **Дайте мені підручник, будь ласка.** (Give me the textbook, please. — formal/plural)

| 5 | **Exercise quality** | 15% | Placeholders specific enough? Test the right skills? Placed after relevant teaching? Match plan's activity_hints? Sufficient items? |
| 6 | **Engagement & tone** | 10% | Interesting for teens/adults? Authoritative but warm (like a skilled teacher)? No LLM filler ("Good news!", "Don't panic!", "Fun fact!")? Cultural hooks? |
| 7 | **Structural integrity** | 5% | All H2 headings from plan present? Word count in range? No duplicate sections? No meta-commentary? Clean markdown? |
| 8 | **Cultural accuracy** | 5% | Decolonized (Ukrainian on its own terms, never "like Russian but...")? Factually correct claims about Ukrainian? Respectful representation? |
| 9 | **Dialogue & conversation quality** | 10% | Dialogues natural and culturally appropriate? Real situations, real responses? Speaker roles clear? Not stilted or textbook-robotic? |

**Note:** Vocabulary tables (словник), video embeds, and external resource links are added by a downstream ENRICH step — do NOT penalize their absence or quality. If you see a vocabulary table with wrong translations, missing words, or formatting issues, that content was NOT written by the writer — it was generated deterministically by the ENRICH step. Do NOT flag словník problems as linguistic errors or deduct points from the writer. Report them separately under "ENRICH issues" in your findings (informational only, not scored).

### Step 4: Output raw scores

Output ONLY the raw 1-10 scores in the table below. Do NOT calculate the weighted total — the pipeline script handles the math deterministically. Just write your per-dimension scores.

### Step 5: List findings

For every issue found, provide:

Critical = module cannot ship. Major = quality below standard. Minor = polish item.

### Step 6: Verdict

Base your verdict on the **severity of findings** — the pipeline calculates the weighted score separately.

| Verdict | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **PASS** | Zero critical findings, at most minor issues |
| **REVISE** | Has major findings but no criticals — fixable without rewrite |
| **REJECT** | Has any critical finding — fundamental problems requiring rewrite |

### Step 7: Fix it yourself (REVISE only)

If your verdict is **REVISE**, you MUST output a `<fixes>` block with exact find/replace pairs. The pipeline applies these deterministically — no LLM regeneration, no rewriting from scratch.

Rules for fixes:
- Each fix is a YAML entry with `find:` (exact text from the module) and `replace:` (corrected text)
- The `find` string must be an EXACT substring of the module content — copy-paste it
- Keep fixes minimal — change only what's wrong, preserve surrounding text
- Do NOT fix словник/vocabulary tables (those are generated by a downstream tool)
- For word count issues: add content as a new `insert_after:` entry instead of find/replace
