## Діалог у кафе: Як зробити замовлення

Ukraine has a deep and historic love for coffee. The city of Lviv is the center of this beautiful culture. Walking its streets feels like being in Vienna or Paris. Many years ago, a Ukrainian man named Yuriy Kulchytsky opened one of the first cafes in Europe. Today, you can try something special: **ка́ва на піску́** (coffee on sand). The barista brews it in a small copper pot on hot sand. It is strong, thick, and truly magical. It is the perfect drink for a cold morning in Ukraine.

### Вітаємо офіціанта: The Rule of Politeness
In a Ukrainian café, the relationship between the customer and the **офіція́нт** (waiter) — [ofitsijˈɑnt] — is built on mutual respect. While you might use informal language with friends, you should always use the formal **Ви** (You) when speaking to staff. This is the standard of polite service culture across the country. A warm greeting like **«До́брий день!»** (Good day!) is the perfect way to start your interaction.

When the waiter approaches your table, they will usually ask: **«Що бажа́єте?»** (What would you like?). This is your cue to look at the **меню́** (menu) — [mɛˈnʲu] — and decide. Remember, being polite is easy: just add **«Бу́дь ла́ска»** (please) — [budʲ ˈlɑskɑ] — to your requests. It makes a huge difference in how you are perceived!

«До́брий день! Мо́жна меню́?» (Good day! Can I have the menu?)
«Що ви бажа́єте замо́вити?» (What do you wish to order?)
«Я чека́ю на офіція́нта.» (I am waiting for the waiter.)

> [!warning]
> **Polite Addressing**: Never use the informal «ти» (you) with a waiter or barista, even if they look younger than you. In Ukraine, «Ви» is the essential mark of respect in a professional setting. Using «ти» can sound rude or overly familiar.

### Як зробити замовлення: Beyond "I Want"
When you are ready to **замовля́ти** (to order) — [zɑmɔuˈlʲɑtɪ] — you might be tempted to say «Я хо́чу...» (I want...). While grammatically correct, it can sound a bit demanding or "childish" to Ukrainian ears. Instead, the most natural and polite way to order is using the phrase **«Мені́, бу́дь ла́ска...»** (For me, please...).

Another great option is to use the phrase **«Я ві́зьму...»** (I will take...). This uses a simple future form of the verb «брати» (to take). It sounds confident and decisive. Whether you are ordering a **ка́ва** (coffee) — [ˈkɑwɑ] — or a **чай** (tea) — [tʃɑj] — these patterns will make you sound like a local.

| English Phrase | Ukrainian Pattern | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| For me, please... | Мені́, бу́дь ла́ска... | Мені́, бу́дь ла́ска, чай. |
| I will take... | Я ві́зьму́... | Я ві́зьму́ чорну каву. |
| Can I have... | Мо́жна... | Мо́жна капучі́но? |

«Мені́, бу́дь ла́ска, вели́ке капучі́но.» (For me, please, a large cappuccino.)
«Я ві́зьму́ міцне́ еспре́со.» (I will take a strong espresso.)
«Принесі́ть, бу́дь ла́ска, чай.» (Bring, please, some tea.)

### Уточнення замовлення: Getting the Details Right
Once you choose your drink, the waiter might need to clarify a few things. They often ask about size or additions. For example, they might ask: **«Вели́кий чи мале́нький?»** (Large or small?). If you are ordering coffee, a very common question is **«З молоко́м?»** (With milk?).

If you want something sweet to accompany your drink, you can look for a **круаса́н** (croissant) — [kruɑˈsɑn] — or a **ті́стечко** (pastry) — [ˈtʲistɛtʃkɔ]. Ukrainian pastries are famous for being fresh and not too sweet. If you are unsure what to pick, you can always ask the waiter for a recommendation by saying: **«Що ви пора́дите?»** (What do you recommend?).

«Вам вели́кий чи мале́нький чай?» (Do you want large or small tea?)
«Я хо́чу ка́ву з молоко́м.» (I want coffee with milk.)
«Цей круаса́н ду́же смачни́й.» (This croissant is very tasty.)

> [!tip]
> **Coffee Additions**: If you want sugar, ask for «цу́кор» [ˈtsukɔr]. If you want it without sugar, say «без цу́кру» [bɛz ˈtsukru]. Ukrainians love their coffee, and many prefer it "black" to taste the quality of the beans.
