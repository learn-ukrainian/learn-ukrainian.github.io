## Розрахунок: Рахунок та оплата

### Як попроси́ти раху́нок?
After you order what the waiter recommends and enjoy your **ті́стечко** (pastry) — [ˈtʲistɛtʃkɔ] — it is time to wrap up your visit. **В Украї́ні ви сидите́ до́вго.** (In Ukraine you sit for a long time.) **Ви відпочива́єте в кафе́.** (You rest in the café.) However, when you are ready to leave, you need to attract the attention of the **офіція́нт** (waiter) — [ɔfʲitsʲiˈjɑnt]. **Офіція́нт не несе́ раху́нок відра́зу.** (The waiter does not bring the bill immediately.) This is a sign of respect for your time. **Ви просто підніма́єте ру́ку.** (You simply raise your hand.) Then, you can use a very polite phrase: **«Мо́жна раху́нок, будь ла́ска?»** (Can I have the bill, please?).

**Діало́г у кафе́:**
— Мо́жна раху́нок? (Can I have the bill?)
— Так, ось ваш раху́нок. (Yes, here is your bill.)
— Скі́льки з ме́не? (How much from me?)
— Сто гри́вень, будь ла́ска. (One hundred hryvnias, please.)

**Це дуже приємна фраза.** (This is a very pleasant phrase.) The phrase **«Скі́льки з ме́не?»** (How much from me?) — [ˈsʲkʲilʲkɪ z ˈmɛnɛ] — is a friendly and natural way to ask for the total amount. **Ви часто чуєте цю фразу.** (You often hear this phrase.) It uses the **Genitive case** for the pronoun **«ме́не»** (me). This form is used after the preposition **«з»** (from/out of). It sounds warm and polite to the staff. You will hear this phrase often in cozy Lviv cafes!

### Раху́нок чи чек: Яка різни́ця?
There is a small but important difference between a **раху́нок** (bill) — [rɐˈxunɔk] — and a **чек** (receipt) — [tʃɛk]. **Студенти часто плутають ці слова.** (Students often confuse these words.) You ask for the **раху́нок** when you want to see the total and prepare to pay. **Офіція́нт несе́ раху́нок у па́пці.** (The waiter brings the bill in a folder.) After you pay, the waiter gives you the **чек**. **Це офіці́йний докуме́нт.** (This is an official document.) This is the paper from the cash machine that confirms your payment is successful.

| Сло́во (Word) | Коли́? (When?) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Раху́нок** | За́раз пла́тимо. (Now we pay.) |
| **Чек** | Опла́та вже є. (Payment already is.) |

**Ви використову́єте знахідни́й відмі́нок.** (You use the Accusative case.) When we talk about the bill or receipt as a direct object, we use the **Accusative case**. For these masculine nouns, the form does not change. **Форма не зміню́ється.** (The form does not change.) It stays the same as the **Nominative** case. For example, in the sentence **«Я ма́ю раху́нок»** (I have the bill), the word **«раху́нок»** is in the Accusative. This makes it very easy to use in your daily conversations at the café! You are making great progress with these cases!

### Опла́та та чайові́
You will be happy to know that **Украї́на — це ду́же цифрова́ краї́на.** (Ukraine is a very digital country.) Most cafes, even very small ones, have a **терміна́л** (terminal) — [tɛrmʲiˈnɑl]. **Ви використову́єте ка́ртку ма́йже всю́ди.** (You use a card almost everywhere.) **Це дуже зручно.** (It is very convenient.) If you prefer to use **готівка** (cash) — [ɦɔˈtʲiu̯kɐ], you can simply place the money in the folder. **Ви кладете́ готівку в па́пку.** (You put cash in the folder.) If you have a card, you can ask the waiter: **«Ви ма́єте терміна́л?»** (Do you have a terminal?).

«Я ма́ю ка́ртку.» (I have a card.)
«Я ма́ю готі́вку.» (I have cash.)
«У вас є терміна́л?» (Do you have a terminal?)

> [!fact]
> **Tipping Culture**: In Ukraine, it is standard practice to leave **чайові́** (tips) — [tʃɐjɔˈʋʲi]. **Лю́ди залиша́ють чайові́ в кафе́.** (People leave tips in the café.) Usually, people leave about 10% of the total bill. **Ви кладете́ готівку на стіл.** (You put cash on the table.) Many modern cafes also allow you to add a tip directly on the card terminal. This is a great way to say **«Дя́кую!»** (Thank you!) — [ˈdʲɑkuju] — to your waiter for the good service and tasty coffee.
