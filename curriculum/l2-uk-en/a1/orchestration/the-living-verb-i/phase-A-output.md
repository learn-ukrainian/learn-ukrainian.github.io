# Дослідження: The Living Verb I

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Indicative mood — present, past, future tenses; conjugation I/II; reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)"
Alignment: Introduces indicative mood conjugation in the present tense focusing exclusively on Class I (-ати/-яти) verbs for high-frequency daily actions.

## Cultural Hooks
1. The "Apostol" (1574) by Ivan Fedorov: Ukraine's first printed book, highlighting a deep-rooted literacy tradition (connects to *читати*).
2. Proverb: "Птицю пізнати по пір'ю, а людину по мові" (A bird is known by its feathers, a person by their speech) — linking grammar accuracy to personal character.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Infinitive Abuse ("Я читати" → "Я читаю") — English speakers often rely on the infinitive form because English uses the bare infinitive for the present tense.
2. Pronoun Overuse ("Я читаю. Я працюю" → "Читаю. Працюю.") — English requires subject pronouns, but Ukrainian is a pro-drop language where the verb ending already indicates the subject.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Strict Constraint**: Imperative forms (Слухайте!, Читайте!, Пишіть!) are NOT taught until M47. Use indirect requests or English for instructions.
- Ensure only imperfective verbs are used (A1 level rule).
- Use inanimate masculine and neuter objects (e.g., журнал, радіо, лист) for initial SVO practice because their Nominative equals Accusative, preventing early case confusion.
- Emphasize the concept of imperfective aspect (ongoing or habitual actions).
