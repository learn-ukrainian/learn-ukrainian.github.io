# Дослідження: The Living Verb I

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Indicative mood — present, past, future tenses; conjugation I/II; reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)"
Alignment: This module introduces indicative mood conjugation in the present tense (Class I / First Conjugation -ати verbs).

## Vocabulary Frequency

|------|-------|------------------|
| читати | Top 100 verb, high frequency | читати книгу, читати новини |
| знати | Top 20 verb, very high frequency | знати все, знати правду |
| працювати | Top 50 verb, high frequency | працювати вдома, працювати багато |
| слухати | High frequency | слухати музику, слухати радіо |

## Cultural Hooks
1. The *Apostol* (1574), printed by Ivan Fedorov, is the first printed book in Ukraine, symbolizing the deep-rooted literacy tradition connected to the verb *читати*.
2. Proverb: «Птицю пізнати по пір'ю, а людину по мові» (A bird is known by its feathers, a person by their speech), emphasizing the importance of grammatical accuracy.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Infinitive Abuse**: Saying "Я читати" instead of "Я читаю" — English speakers often forget to conjugate because English verb forms change very little.
2. **Pronoun Overuse**: Saying "Я читаю. Я працюю." — English requires subjects, but Ukrainian verb endings already contain the subject, making constant pronouns unnatural.
3. **Case Confusion**: Overcomplicating objects too early — using inanimate masculine/neuter objects (e.g., журнал) prevents early accusative case confusion since Nominative = Accusative.

## Notes for Content Writing
- A1 Level Constraints: Use English for grammar explanations (scaffolding). Only use Ukrainian for examples, with translations.
- Imperative verbs (Слухайте!, Читайте!) are BANNED. Use indirect requests in English like "Read...", "Notice that...", "Compare...".
- Do not introduce the Dative or Instrumental cases.
- Emphasize the "Master Key" metaphor: this single pattern unlocks hundreds of everyday verbs.
