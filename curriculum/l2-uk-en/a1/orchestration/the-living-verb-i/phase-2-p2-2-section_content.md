## Практика: Життя в русі

Now that the verb is awake and out of the dictionary, let us put it to work. You have the "objects" from our previous lessons—books, radio, letters. Now you have the "engine" to make them move. We will build simple, strong sentences that describe your reality right now.

### Building Your First Sentences

You already know the pattern. Now we simply connect the **Subject** (who), the **Verb** (action), and the **Object** (what). In Ukrainian, this feels very similar to English, but with more rhythm.

Let us look at **слухати** (to listen). This is a perfect Class I verb. It describes what you do with music, news, or a friend.

| Who? | Action | What? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Я** (I) | **слухаю** | **радіо** (radio) |
| **Ми** (We) | **слухаємо** | **музику** (music) |

Notice the harmony. The ending **-ю** matches **Я**. The ending **-ємо** matches **Ми**.

*   **Я слухаю радіо.** [jɑ ˈsluxɑju ˈrɑdʲijo] — I listen to the radio.
*   **Ти слухаєш музику.** [tɪ ˈsluxɑjɛʃ ˈmuzɪku] — You listen to music.
*   **Студент слухає текст.** [stuˈdɛnt ˈsluxɑjɛ tɛkst] — The student listens to the text.

> [!observe]
> **The Hidden "About"**
> In English, you listen **to** music. In Ukrainian, you just "listen music" (**слухати музику**). There is no extra word like "to" in the middle. The verb connects directly to the object. It is faster and more direct.

### The Art of Knowing

The verb **знати** (to know) is your anchor. You use it to confirm facts or express familiarity with a place. It follows the exact same rhythm as **читати**.

*   **Я знаю це.** [jɑ ˈznɑju tsɛ] — I know this.
*   **Ми знаємо місто.** [mɪ ˈznɑjɛmɔ ˈmistɔ] — We know the city.
*   **Ви знаєте все.** [vɪ ˈznɑjɛtɛ wsɛ] — You know everything.

Use this when you are confident. It is a strong statement.

### Asking and Working

Let us look at **питати** (to ask) and **працювати** (to work). Imagine you are in an office or a university. These actions happen all around you.

**Питати** works well with the word **про** (about).
*   **Я питаю про текст.** [jɑ pɪˈtɑju prɔ tɛkst] — I ask about the text.
*   **Ти питаєш про це?** [tɪ pɪˈtɑjɛʃ prɔ tsɛ] — Do you ask about this?

**Працювати** describes your labor or your job.
*   **Він працює тут.** [win prɑˈtsʲujɛ tut] — He works here.
*   **Ми працюємо вдома.** [mɪ prɑˈtsʲujɛmɔ ˈwdɔmɑ] — We work at home.
*   **Вони працюють багато.** [wɔˈnɪ prɑˈtsʲujutʲ bɑˈɦɑtɔ] — They work a lot.

### The Dropped Pronoun

You will often hear Ukrainians speak without the words "I", "you", or "we". This is not lazy. It is efficient. Because the ending of the verb **already tells you** who is doing the action, the pronoun is extra information.

If I say **«Читаю»**, the ending **-ю** screams "I am doing it!". We do not need **Я**.

*   **Знаю це.** [ˈznɑju tsɛ] — (I) know this.
*   **Слухаємо радіо.** [ˈsluxɑjɛmɔ ˈrɑdʲijo] — (We) listen to the radio.
*   **Чекаєш маму?** [tʃeˈkɑjɛʃ ˈmɑmu] — (Are you) waiting for mom?

This sounds very natural. It shows you are comfortable with the language. You are focusing on the *action*, not on yourself.
