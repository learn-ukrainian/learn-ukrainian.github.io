## Культурний контекст: Українське слово

This focus on the action tells us something deep about the Ukrainian worldview. In English, we often define ourselves by who we are ("I am a worker"). In Ukrainian, the emphasis often shifts to the contribution itself—to what is being done.

### Дія важливіша за особу

When you drop the pronoun, you are not being vague. You are shining a spotlight on the verb. This structure reflects a culture where the *process* often matters more than the *person* claiming credit for it.

If you say **«Працюю»**, the listener hears dedication. The ending **-ю** carries the "I", but the word itself carries the effort.

*   **Знаємо це місто.** [ˈznɑjɛmɔ tsɛ ˈmʲistɔ] — (We) know this city.
*   **Слухаю радіо.** [ˈsluxɑju ˈrɑdʲijo] — (I) listen to the radio.
*   **Питають про театр.** [pɪˈtɑjutʲ prɔ tɛˈɑtr] — (They) ask about the theater.

> **Note:** The word **про** [prɔ] means "about". It is a very useful little word!

### Ключ до тисячі дверей

You might feel that you have only learned a handful of verbs today, like **читати** [tʃɪˈtɑtɪ] (to read) or **знати** [ˈznɑtɪ] (to know). But actually, you have acquired a "Master Key."

Ukrainian verbs are built on patterns. The pattern you learned today (the **-ати** group) is the most common and stable one. If you see a new word like **вивчати** [wɪwˈtʃɑtɪ] (to study) or **відпочивати** [widpɔtʃɪˈwɑtɪ] (to rest), you already know exactly how to use it. You do not need a dictionary. You just apply the ending.

| I do (Я) | You do (Ти) | We do (Ми) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **вивча-ю** | **вивча-єш** | **вивча-ємо** |
| **відпочива-ю** | **відпочива-єш** | **відпочива-ємо** |

Try these sentences with the new verbs:

*   **Ми вивчаємо місто.** [mɪ wɪwˈtʃɑjɛmɔ ˈmʲistɔ] — We study the city.
*   **Він відпочиває тут.** [win widpɔtʃɪˈwɑjɛ tut] — He rests here.
*   **Вивчаєш це?** [wɪwˈtʃɑjɛʃ tsɛ] — (Do you) study this?

### Нація читачів

Historically, the written word has held a sacred place in Ukraine. The first book printed in Ukraine, the "Apostol," appeared in Lviv in 1574. Literacy has always been highly valued, and you will often see people reading books in parks, on the metro, or in cafes.

The verb **читати** [tʃɪˈtɑtɪ] (to read) is one of the most frequently used words in the language. It connects you to the history and the daily life of the country.

*   **Я читаю журнал.** [jɑ tʃɪˈtɑju ʒurˈnɑl] — I read a magazine.
*   **Ми читаємо вдома.** [mɪ tʃɪˈtɑjɛmɔ ˈwdɔmɑ] — We read at home.
*   **Вони читають швидко.** [wɔˈnɪ tʃɪˈtɑjutʲ ˈʃwɪdkɔ] — They read quickly.

> [!culture]
> **The Bird and the Word**
>
> There is a famous Ukrainian saying:
> **«Птицю пізнати по пір'ю, а людину по мові.»**
> [ˈptɪtsʲu pʲizˈnɑtɪ pɔ ˈpʲirju ɑ lʲuˈdɪnu pɔ ˈmɔwi]
> *"You know a bird by its feathers, and a person by their language."*
>
> This reminds us that how we speak — our choice of words and our grammar — shows our character. By learning these verb endings, you are showing respect for the language structure.
