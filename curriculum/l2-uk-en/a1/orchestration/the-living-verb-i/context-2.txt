### The Dropped Pronoun

You will often hear Ukrainians speak without the words "I", "you", or "we". This is not lazy. It is efficient. Because the ending of the verb **already tells you** who is doing the action, the pronoun is extra information.

If I say **¬ę–ß–ł—ā–į—é¬Ľ**, the ending **-—é** screams "I am doing it!". We do not need **–Į**.

*   **–ó–Ĺ–į—é —Ü–Ķ.** [ňązn…Ďju ts…õ] ‚ÄĒ (I) know this.
*   **–°–Ľ—É—Ö–į—Ē–ľ–ĺ —Ä–į–ī—Ė–ĺ.** [ňąslux…Ďj…õm…Ē ňąr…Ďd ≤ijo] ‚ÄĒ (We) listen to the radio.
*   **–ß–Ķ–ļ–į—Ē—ą –ľ–į–ľ—É?** [t Éeňąk…Ďj…õ É ňąm…Ďmu] ‚ÄĒ (Are you) waiting for mom?

This sounds very natural. It shows you are comfortable with the language. You are focusing on the *action*, not on yourself.
