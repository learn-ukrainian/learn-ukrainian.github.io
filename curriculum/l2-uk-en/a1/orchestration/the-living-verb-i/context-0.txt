Welcome back! You already know many Ukrainian words. You see a **телефон** [tɛlɛˈfɔn] (phone) or a **журнал** [ʒurˈnɑl] (magazine). But naming things is not enough. You want to speak. To speak, we need action. We need the **дієслово** [dijɛˈslɔwɔ] (verb). The verb is the engine of the sentence. It turns a static picture into a movie. Without verbs, we just point at objects. With verbs, we truly communicate.

### The Master Key
You might worry about complex grammar. Please, do not worry. Ukrainian verbs follow a beautiful logic. Today, we discover a "Master Key". This is the **-ати** pattern. Many essential verbs share this pattern. You learn one simple rule, and you unlock hundreds of words immediately. It is like magic. You will confidently say "I read", "I know", or "I listen". Trust this pattern.

### Your Day in Ukrainian
Think about your daily life. You do many things every day. You work. You listen to news. You ask questions. Now you can say these things in Ukrainian.

*   **Я працюю.** [jɑ prɑˈtsʲuju] — I work.
*   **Я слухаю радіо.** [jɑ ˈsluxɑju ˈrɑdijo] — I listen to the radio.
*   **Він знає все.** [win ˈznɑje wsɛ] — He knows everything.

> [!tip]
> **Where is "I"?**
> In Ukrainian, the verb ending often tells us *who* is doing the action. You can say **Я читаю** [jɑ tʃɪˈtɑju] (I read) or just **Читаю** [tʃɪˈtɑju]. Both are correct! We will see this logic in action very soon.
