## Dialogues

When you travel to a new country, learning how to handle an emergency is the most important step you can take for your safety. In Ukraine, the universal emergency number is **112** (**один один два**). You can call this number from any mobile phone, even if you do not have a local SIM card or if your phone is locked. The operator can connect you to the police, the fire department, or an ambulance.

In an emergency, your goal is not to have perfect grammar or to speak in long, complicated sentences. Your goal is to communicate three things quickly and clearly: what happened, where you are, and who you are. The operator will guide you with questions, but knowing the basic Ukrainian phrases will save critical time.

Let us look at a real situation. Imagine you see a traffic accident on the street. Someone is hurt. You dial the emergency number. Listen to how this conversation happens.

## Екстрені ситуації (Emergencies)

The most crucial word you can learn for your safety is the word for help. In Ukrainian, the noun is **допомога** (help). But when you need to shout for assistance, you must use the imperative verb form.

*   **Допоможіть!** (Help!)

This is the formal and plural command form. You use it when you are speaking to a stranger, to an authority figure, or to a crowd of people. It is the best and safest form to use in any public emergency. If you only remember one word from this module, remember **допоможіть**.

When you call the operator, you need to tell them exactly who to send. You will use another formal command: **викличте** (call / summon). This verb specifically means to call an official service or person to a location.

*   **Викличте швидку!** (Call an ambulance!)
*   **Викличте поліцію!** (Call the police!)

The word **швидка** literally means "fast," but it is the common everyday word for an ambulance (it is short for **швидка допомога** — fast help). The word **поліція** means police.

Next, you need to state the problem. Use the word **тут** (here) to show that the emergency is happening right where you are. This creates a simple, effective sentence structure.

*   **Тут аварія!** (There is an accident here!)
*   **Тут пожежа!** (There is a fire here!)
*   **Людині погано!** (Someone is feeling bad! / A person is unwell!)
*   **Мені потрібна допомога!** (I need help!)

The phrase **людині погано** is very common. It literally means "it is bad to a person," and you use it when someone faints, has a heart attack, or suddenly becomes ill on the street.

Once you state the problem, the operator will ask: **Де ви?** (Where are you?). You must give your location clearly. You can use vocabulary from previous modules to describe your surroundings.

*   **Я на вулиці Хрещатик.** (I am on Khreshchatyk street.)
*   **Я біля метро.** (I am near the metro.)
*   **Я в парку.** (I am in the park.)
*   **Адреса: вулиця Франка, будинок десять.** (Address: Franko street, building ten.)

Remember that the word **будинок** means building or house. When giving an exact address, state the street name first, then the building number.

## Допомога (Getting Help)

Not all emergencies require a dramatic phone call to the rescue service. Sometimes, you might need to walk into a hospital (**лікарня**) or a pharmacy yourself. In these situations, you need to explain what is wrong with your body.

In a previous module, you learned how to talk about health. When you enter a clinic or hospital, you can use the phrase **мені потрібен** (I need). This phrase is a fixed chunk. You do not need to analyze the grammar right now; just memorize how to use it. Because Ukrainian is a gendered language, the word for "needed" changes depending on the thing you need. If you need a male person or a masculine object, use **потрібен**. If you need a feminine object, use **потрібна**.

*   **Мені потрібен лікар.** (I need a doctor.)
*   **Мені потрібна допомога.** (I need help.)
*   **Мені потрібна швидка.** (I need an ambulance.)

Once you see the doctor, you can use the phrase **у мене болить** (my ... hurts) to point to the problem.

*   **У мене болить голова.** (My head hurts.)
*   **У мене болить живіт.** (My stomach hurts.)

It is also critical to tell the medical staff if you have any allergies. You can do this by saying **у мене алергія на** (I have an allergy to).

*   **У мене алергія на антибіотики.** (I am allergic to antibiotics.)

If the doctor or police officer is speaking too quickly, do not panic. Ask them to repeat themselves, or ask if they speak English.

*   **Я не розумію.** (I do not understand.)
*   **Повторіть, будь ласка.** (Repeat, please.)
*   **Ви говорите англійською?** (Do you speak English?)

When reporting a crime or checking into a hospital, you will be asked to provide your personal details. The authorities need to fill out a **форма** (form / document). They will ask you for several pieces of information.

*   **Мене звати Адам.** (My name is Adam.)
*   **Моє прізвище Сміт.** (My surname is Smith.)
*   **Мій номер телефону...** (My phone number is...)
*   **Я з Канади.** (I am from Canada.)
*   **Мій паспорт...** (My passport...)
*   **Мій готель — «Дніпро».** (My hotel is "Dnipro".)

If you are at the police station because you lost something, you will use the past tense verb **загубити** (to lose). Remember that past tense verbs change based on your gender. If you are a man, say **я загубив**. If you are a woman, say **я загубила**.

*   **Я загубив паспорт.** (I lost my passport. — male speaker)
*   **Я загубила телефон.** (I lost my phone. — female speaker)

## Summary

Let us review everything you have learned in this module. Being prepared for an emergency means knowing exactly what to say without having to think about it. You should practice these phrases until they become automatic.

Your survival kit starts with the universal emergency number: **112**. This is your lifeline for any critical situation in Ukraine. Whether you need medical help, police assistance, or fire rescue, this single number is all you need to remember.

The most important commands you can give are:

*   **Допоможіть!** (Help!)
*   **Викличте швидку!** (Call an ambulance!)
*   **Викличте поліцію!** (Call the police!)

When you are on the phone, immediately state the problem. Use simple, direct sentences. Do not worry about being overly polite; focus on being clear.

*   **Тут аварія!** (There is an accident here!)
*   **Тут пожежа!** (There is a fire here!)
*   **Людині погано!** (Someone is feeling bad!)

Next, be ready to answer the question **Де ви?** (Where are you?). Provide your exact location. Remember to use prepositions correctly to give accurate directions.

*   **Я на вулиці...** (I am on ... street.)
*   **Я біля...** (I am near...)
*   **Адреса: вулиця... будинок...** (Address: street... building...)

If you need medical attention at a **лікарня** (hospital), use these phrases to explain your situation to the staff.

*   **Мені потрібен лікар.** (I need a doctor.)
*   **У мене болить...** (My ... hurts.)
*   **У мене алергія на...** (I am allergic to...)

If you need to speak to the authorities because you lost a document, state the problem clearly using the past tense. Choose the correct ending based on your gender.

*   **Я загубив паспорт.** (I lost my passport. — male speaker)
*   **Я загубила паспорт.** (I lost my passport. — female speaker)

Finally, always be prepared to give your personal information when asked. You will need to provide your **ім'я** (name), **прізвище** (surname), **номер телефону** (phone number), country of origin, and your local **адреса** (address). If you do not understand a question, you can always ask them to repeat it by saying **повторіть, будь ласка** (repeat, please). Having this information ready will speed up any bureaucratic process, whether at a police station or a hospital admissions desk.
