# Дослідження: Emergencies

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.2: "Наказовий спосіб. Форми 2-ї особи однини і множини наказового способу деяких дієслів..."
§4.2.3.4: "Кличний відмінок (адресат мовлення – потенційний суб’єкт дії)..."
Alignment: The module teaches how to form urgent imperative requests (допоможіть, скажіть, дайте) for safety and use the vocative case for addressing emergency personnel (лікарю, поліціє), directly fulfilling the linguistic competencies of the A1 standard for addressing others and giving commands.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Єдиний номер 112**: In July 2023, Ukraine launched the unified emergency number 112, scaling it nationwide throughout 2024. It operates on the European "single window" model, allowing callers to dispatch multiple services (police, fire, medical) at once, symbolizing Ukraine's European integration.
2. **Digital Resilience**: The «112 Ukraine» application and services like «Дія» allow citizens to seek help even in challenging conditions (like calling via Wi-Fi from shelters without cellular signal), demonstrating Ukraine's advanced digitalization in safety.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **"Дзвонити" vs "Викликати"** → Learners often say "дзвонити поліцію" (literal translation of "call the police"). Correct form: "викликати поліцію". "Дзвонити" is dialing a phone; "викликати" is summoning a service.
2. **Russism "Визивати"** → Using the Russian-derived "визивати швидку". Correct form: "викликати швидку". 
3. **Nominative for Address** → Saying "лікар!" instead of the vocative "лікарю!".

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized Framing: Present the 112 system and digitalization of emergency services as a proud achievement of modern Ukraine. 
- Ensure English scaffolding is explicitly included for introducing the Vocative case and Imperative mood, given the A1.4 phase (10-50% immersion).
- The focus is highly practical; avoid deep theoretical grammar tangents. Keep imperative forms limited to 2nd person formal/plural ("Допоможіть!", "Скажіть!").
