## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
- **The Zero-Copula Trap**: In "Combined Practice," students often try to insert a verb for "to be" in sentences like "Where is the station?" (*–î–µ –∑—É–ø–∏–Ω–∫–∞?*). Reiterate that at A1, "is/am/are" is usually invisible in the present tense.
- **Gendered Requests**: Unlike the neutral English "Give me a ticket," the Ukrainian request depends on the gender of the object (–ö–≤–∏—Ç–æ–∫ - Masc, –í–æ–¥–∞ - Fem). Learners must synthesize gender recognition with the Accusative case endings (-—É for feminine).

### Common Learner Errors
- **Cognitive Overload**: When combining scenarios (e.g., buying a ticket then finding a platform), students often revert to Nominative instead of Accusative for destinations (*–Ø —ó–¥—É –ö–∏—ó–≤* instead of *–Ø —ó–¥—É –≤ –ö–∏—ó–≤*).
- **"–ë—É–¥—å –ª–∞—Å–∫–∞" Overuse**: English speakers use "please" at the end of every sentence. While polite, in Ukrainian, *–±—É–¥—å –ª–∞—Å–∫–∞* can also mean "you're welcome" or "here you go."

### Teaching Sequence Recommendations
1. Warm-up with rapid-fire "Survival Phrases."
2. Multi-step roleplays (e.g., at the station: check schedule -> buy ticket -> find platform).
