## Звуки і літери (Sounds and Letters)

Welcome to Ukrainian. Before you learn a single word, Ukrainian schoolchildren learn a golden rule about their language — and so will you:

> Ми чуємо і вимовляємо звуки, а бачимо і пишемо літери.

This means: *We hear and pronounce SOUNDS (звуки), but we see and write LETTERS (літери).* This distinction is the foundation of Ukrainian phonetics, and Ukrainian teachers drill it from the very first day of Grade 1.
<!-- adapted from: Большакова, Grade 1, p.3 -->

Why does this matter? Because Ukrainian has **33 letters** (літери) but **38 sounds** (звуки). Letters and sounds are not the same thing. Some letters represent two sounds. Some letters change the sound of the letter before them. One letter has no sound at all. You will meet all of these in this module.

Ukrainian sounds divide into two families:

- **Голосні** (vowels) — made with voice alone. Your mouth stays open, the air flows freely, nothing blocks it. You can sing a vowel sound. Ukrainian schoolchildren learn: *"Голосні почуєш в пісні"* — you'll hear vowels in a song.
<!-- adapted from: unknown, Grade 1, p.24 -->

- **Приголосні** (consonants) — made with voice AND noise. Your lips, tongue, or teeth create an obstruction. You cannot sing a pure consonant — try holding just the sound [к] or [т].
<!-- adapted from: unknown, Grade 2, p.34 -->

Every Ukrainian syllable has exactly one vowel sound at its core. No vowel, no syllable. This rule never breaks.

## Голосні звуки (Vowel Sounds)

Ukrainian has **6 vowel sounds**: [а], [о], [у], [е], [и], [і].

But it has **10 vowel letters**: А, О, У, Е, И, І, Я, Ю, Є, Ї.
<!-- adapted from: unknown, Grade 2, p.34 -->

Why ten letters for six sounds? Because four letters — **Я, Ю, Є, Ї** — are "double-duty" letters. Each one can represent TWO sounds at once.

### The iotated vowels (Я, Ю, Є, Ї)

At the beginning of a word or after another vowel, these letters represent two sounds:

| Letter | Two sounds | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Я** | [й] + [а] | **яблуко** (apple) — the Я at the start = [йа] |
| **Ю** | [й] + [у] | **юрта** (yurt) |
| **Є** | [й] + [е] | **єнот** (raccoon) |
| **Ї** | [й] + [і] | **їжак** (hedgehog) — ALWAYS two sounds |

<!-- adapted from: unknown, Grade 2, p.10 -->

After a consonant, **Я, Ю, Є** work differently — they soften the consonant before them and contribute only the vowel sound. But **Ї** is unique: it ALWAYS represents two sounds [йі], no matter where it appears. **Ї** never softens a consonant. It only appears at the start of a word, after a vowel, or after an apostrophe. No other Slavic language has this letter — it belongs to Ukrainian alone.
<!-- adapted from: unknown, Grade 1, p.66 -->

### И vs І — a critical distinction

These two vowels look similar but sound different, and mixing them up changes meaning:

| Word with И | Meaning | Word with І | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **кит** | whale | **кіт** | cat |
| **бик** | bull | **бік** | side |

<!-- adapted from: Большакова, Grade 1 (буквар cover pairs: кит/кіт, дим/дім) -->

**И** is a lower, more relaxed sound — your tongue sits in the middle of your mouth. **І** is higher and tighter — your tongue pushes forward and up. Listen carefully to each video below.

## Приголосні: знайомі та фальшиві друзі (Consonants: Familiar and False Friends)

### Letters that look AND sound familiar

Some Ukrainian letters look like Latin letters and have similar sounds. These are your friends — they let you start reading immediately:

**А, О, К, М, Т, Е** — with just these letters, you can already read **мама**, **тато**, **метро**.

One important detail: Ukrainian **Т** is *dental* — your tongue touches the back of your teeth, not the gum ridge as in English. And Ukrainian **К** and **Т** have no puff of air (they are unaspirated). Native speakers notice the difference, but for now, close enough — you will refine this later.

### The false friends — the biggest trap

These letters LOOK like Latin letters you know — but they make completely different sounds. This is where English-speaking learners stumble hardest:

| Ukrainian letter | Looks like | Actually sounds like |
|-----------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **В** | Latin B | [в] — like English "v" in "van" |
| **Н** | Latin H | [н] — like English "n" in "no" |
| **Р** | Latin P | [р] — a rolled "r" (like Spanish) |
| **С** | Latin C | [с] — always "s" (never "k") |
| **Х** | Latin X | [х] — like Scottish "loch" |

Practice these words aloud. If you say [в] for **В** and [р] for **Р**, you are breaking your Latin reflexes — and that is exactly what you need to do:

> **вода** — water. **ніс** — nose. **рука** — hand. **сон** — dream. **хата** — house.

### New Cyrillic shapes — consonants with no Latin lookalike

These letters have shapes you have never seen. Each one represents a single consonant sound:

- **Б** [б] — like English "b"
- **Г** [г] — a soft, breathy "h" (NOT a hard "g")
- **Ґ** [ґ] — a hard "g" (rare in Ukrainian — only in words like **ґанок**, **ґудзик**)
- **Д** [д] — like English "d" (dental)
- **Ж** [ж] — like the "s" in English "pleasure"
- **З** [з] — like English "z"
- **Й** [й] — a short "y" sound (like English "y" in "yes")
- **Л** [л] — like English "l"
- **П** [п] — like English "p" (unaspirated)
- **Ф** [ф] — like English "f"
- **Ц** [ц] — like "ts" in "ca**ts**"
- **Ч** [ч] — like "ch" in "**ch**urch"
- **Ш** [ш] — like "sh" in "**sh**ip"
- **Щ** [шч] — ALWAYS two sounds: [ш] + [ч] together, like "fre**sh ch**eese"

Practice words with new shapes: **банк**, **дім**, **зима**, **книга**, **школа**.

## М'який знак (The Soft Sign — Ь)

The 33rd letter of the Ukrainian alphabet is **Ь** — the м'який знак (soft sign). It is unique: **it has no sound of its own**. You cannot pronounce Ь alone.

Its job is to *soften* the consonant before it — making that consonant sound lighter and gentler, as if the middle of your tongue briefly rises toward the roof of your mouth. Compare:

| Without Ь | With Ь |
|-----------|--------|
| **кон** (beginning of a word) | **кінь** (horse) — the Н is softened |

Common words with Ь:

- **день** (day) — the Н is soft
- **сіль** (salt) — the Л is soft
- **кінь** (horse) — the Н is soft
- **мідь** (copper) — the Д is soft
<!-- adapted from: unknown, Grade 1, p.15 — Приголосні тверді та м'які -->

This is part of why Ukrainian has 33 letters but 38 sounds: many consonants have both a hard and a soft version. The soft sign creates those soft versions. You will explore Ь in depth in Module 2 — for now, just know it exists and recognize it when you see it.

## Читаємо вивіски (Reading Signs)

You know all 33 letters. Time to read. Ukrainian schoolchildren start by reading words around them — signs, labels, names. This is the **буквар** tradition: sound out each letter, blend into syllables, then read the whole word.
<!-- adapted from: unknown, Grade 2, p.3 — school sign reading exercise -->

### Вивіски (Signs)

Read each sign aloud. Go letter by letter, then blend:

| Sign | Sounds out as | Meaning |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Аптека** | А-п-т-е-к-а | pharmacy |
| **Банк** | Б-а-н-к | bank |
| **Кафе** | К-а-ф-е | café |
| **Метро** | М-е-т-р-о | metro |
| **Пошта** | П-о-ш-т-а | post office |
| **Школа** | Ш-к-о-л-а | school |
| **Зупинка** | З-у-п-и-н-к-а | bus stop |

Watch for the false friends: **Банк** starts with **Б** [б], not Latin "B." **Метро** has **Р** [р] — a rolled "r," not English "p."

### Ukrainian cities

City names exercise every letter combination you have learned. Read them aloud:

> **Київ** — the capital. Notice **К**, then the false friend **В** at the end: [в], not "v" written as Latin "v" — but here, actually, В IS [в]. Your reflex is right this time!

> **Львів** — the western jewel. Two **В** letters: Л-ь-в-і-в. The Ь softens the Л.

> **Одеса** — the Black Sea port. All friendly letters: О-д-е-с-а.

> **Харків** — eastern Ukraine. Starts with **Х** [х], not a Latin "X" sound.

> **Дніпро** — the river city. **Д** [д], **Н** [н] (not "H"!), **Р** [р] (not "P"!).

> **Полтава** — central Ukraine. The **В** near the end: [в].

> **Чернігів** — northern Ukraine. Starts with **Ч** [ч], has **Г** [г] (the soft breathy "h").

### Your first sentences

Using the simplest structure in Ukrainian — **Це** (this is) + a noun — you can now make complete sentences:

> Це аптека. Це банк. Це метро.

> Це Київ. Це Україна.

> Що це? — Це школа. Це пошта.

These are your first complete Ukrainian sentences. No verbs, no grammar — just identification. Point at a sign, say **Це** + the word, and you are speaking Ukrainian.

## Summary

You have learned the foundation of the Ukrainian writing system:

- Ukrainian has **33 літери** (letters) but **38 звуків** (sounds) — letters and sounds are not the same thing.
- Vowel sounds (голосні) are 6, but vowel letters are 10 — because **Я, Ю, Є, Ї** can each represent two sounds. **Ї** is uniquely Ukrainian and ALWAYS represents [йі].
- The false friend consonants — **В** [в], **Н** [н], **Р** [р], **С** [с], **Х** [х] — look like Latin letters but sound completely different. Breaking your Latin reflexes here is the single most important step.
- **Ь** (м'який знак) has no sound — it softens the consonant before it.
- You can already read Ukrainian signs and say your first sentences: **Це аптека. Це Київ. Це Україна.**

4. What does the soft sign **Ь** do?
