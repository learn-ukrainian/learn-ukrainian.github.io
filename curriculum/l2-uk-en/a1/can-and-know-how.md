Welcome to the pool! In Ukrainian, just like in your daily life, there are different ways to say "can." Sometimes you talk about a skill you've learned, sometimes about whether you're free to do something right now, and sometimes about whether you're allowed to do it. Today, you'll learn how to navigate these three situations with confidence.

## Могти (Can/Be able to)

The verb **могти** is one of the most common words in Ukrainian. It describes physical ability or circumstantial possibility—whether something is possible right now or in a specific situation.

One thing to watch out for is the stem change. Notice how the **г** in the infinitive **могти** changes to a **ж** in most forms, except for the "they" form.

| Person | Conjugation | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Я** | **можу** | I can |
| **Ти** | **можеш** | You can (informal) |
| **Він / Вона / Воно** | **може** | He / She / It can |
| **Ми** | **можемо** | We can |
| **Ви** | **можете** | You can (formal/plural) |
| **Вони** | **можуть** | They can |

Use **могти** when you want to say you are free to do something or have the physical capacity right now. 

> — Ти **можеш** прийти завтра? (Can you come tomorrow?)
> — Так, я **можу** прийти о п'ятій. (Yes, I can come at five.)
> — Я не **можу** зараз розмовляти. (I cannot talk right now.)

You can also use it for polite requests, similar to "May I...?" in English:

- **Можу** я запитати? (May I ask?)
- **Можу** я подивитися? (May I look?)
- **Можу** я увійти? (May I enter?)

## Вміти (Know how to)

While **могти** is about circumstances, **вміти** is all about acquired skills and talents. If you've learned how to do something—like drive, cook, or speak a language—you use **вміти**.

This verb follows a regular pattern for verbs ending in **-іти**, where the first-person singular ends in **-ію**.

| Person | Conjugation | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Я** | **вмію** | I know how to |
| **Ти** | **вмієш** | You know how to |
| **Він / Вона / Воно** | **вміє** | He / She / It knows how to |
| **Ми** | **вміємо** | We know how to |
| **Ви** | **вмієте** | You know how to |
| **Вони** | **вміють** | They know how to |

Think of **вміти** as the "skill verb." Here are some classic examples:

- Я **вмію** **плавати**. (I know how to swim.)
- Ти **вмієш** **готувати**? (Do you know how to cook?)
- Мій брат **вміє** **водити** машину. (My brother knows how to drive a car.)
- Вона дуже добре **вміє** **співати**. (She knows how to sing very well.)

The contrast is key. If you say «Я **вмію** плавати», it means you have the skill. If you say «Я **можу** плавати», it might mean the pool is open and you are free to go in!

## Можна / Не можна (It is allowed / Not allowed)

When you see a sign or ask for permission, you'll use the impersonal word **можна**. It never changes its form—no conjugation needed!

- **Можна** — It is allowed / May I?
- **Не можна** — It is not allowed / Prohibited

This is very practical for navigating public spaces in Ukraine. You'll see these words on signs everywhere.

> — Тут **можна** **фотографувати**? (Is it allowed to photograph here?)
> — Ні, тут **не можна**. (No, it's not allowed here.)

Use **можна** with an infinitive to ask for permission politely. It's often more common than **можу я** in daily situations.

- **Можна** увійти? (May I enter?)
- **Можна** подивитися меню? (May I look at the menu?)
- Тут **можна** їсти? (Is it allowed to eat here?)

You will also see **не можна** on prohibition signs:

- **Не можна** **курити**. (No smoking.)
- **Не можна** паркуватися. (No parking.)
- **Не можна** голосно розмовляти. (No loud talking.)

## Не можу vs Не вмію vs Не можна (Contrasting Negations)

Choosing the right way to say "cannot" depends on *why* you can't do it. Mixing these up is a common mistake for learners, but the distinction is simple once you see the logic.

1. **Не можу** (Circumstance): "I am unable right now." Maybe you're busy, tired, or the door is locked.
2. **Не вмію** (Knowledge): "I never learned." You lack the skill.
3. **Не можна** (Rule): "It's against the rules." Someone else said no.

Compare these situations:

- Я **не можу** прийти сьогодні. (I am busy or something happened—circumstance.)
- Я **не вмію** **кататися** на ковзанах. (I never learned how to skate—permanent gap.)
- Тут **не можна** **курити**. (It's a library or a hospital—external rule.)

Another example:
- Я **не вмію** **малювати**. (I'm not an artist.)
- Я **не можу** **малювати** зараз. (I don't have any paper or my hand hurts.)
- Тут **не можна** **малювати**. (It's a wall in a museum!)

Remember: If someone asks "Can you swim?", they might mean "Do you have the skill?" (**Ти вмієш?**) or "Are you able to go to the beach now?" (**Ти можеш?**).

## Практика (Practice)

Let's look at how these words work in real-life conversations. Notice which modal is chosen based on the context.

**У школі (At school)**
> — **Можна** запитати? (May I ask?)
> — Так, звичайно. (Yes, of course.)
> — Я **не можу** прочитати це слово. (I cannot read this word—circumstance/difficulty.)
> — Ти **вмієш** читати по-українськи? (Do you know how to read in Ukrainian?—skill.)

**У спортзалі (At the gym)**
> — Ти **вмієш** **грати** на гітарі? (Do you know how to play the guitar?)
> — Так, але я **не можу** **грати** сьогодні. (Yes, but I cannot play today.)
> — Чому? (Why?)
> — Тут **не можна** голосно **грати**. (Loud playing is not allowed here.)

**У кафе (At the cafe)**
> — Ви **можете** принести рахунок? (Can you bring the bill?)
> — Так, звичайно. (Yes, of course.)
> — Тут **можна** платити карткою? (Is it allowed to pay by card here?)
> — Так, **можна**. (Yes, it is.)

# Підсумок
In this module, you've mastered the three faces of "can" in Ukrainian:
1. **Могти** for circumstantial or physical ability (I can come).
2. **Вміти** for learned skills and talents (I know how to swim).
3. **Можна** for permission and rules (May I? / It's allowed).

**Self-check questions:**
1. How does the stem of **могти** change when you conjugate it?
2. Which verb would you use to say you know how to drive a car?
3. What is the difference between «Я **не можу** готувати» and «Я **не вмію** готувати»?
4. How do you ask "May I enter?" using **можна**?
