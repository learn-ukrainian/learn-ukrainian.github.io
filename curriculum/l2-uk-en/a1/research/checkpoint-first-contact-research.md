## Cultural Hooks
1. **Юрій Кульчицький (1683)** — Ukrainian Cossack who used captured Ottoman coffee after the Battle of Vienna to open one of Europe's first coffeehouses, "Під синьою пляшкою," in Vienna. This is documented history linking Ukrainian identity to European coffeehouse culture. Concrete hook: the café section persona (Hostel Host) can reference this tradition.
2. **Lviv as Ukraine's coffee capital** — Lviv's historic coffeehouses (Вірменська кав'ярня, the modern Копальня кави) make «кава» a real cultural touchstone, not just vocabulary. Learners visiting Ukraine will encounter this immediately.
3. **«Смачного!»** — Ukrainian dining ritual phrase with no exact English equivalent. Every café interaction requires it. Contrast with Russian "Приятного аппетита" — Ukrainian form is distinctly shorter and more common in daily speech.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **тато gender** → masculine (великий тато), not neuter despite -о ending. English speakers expect -о = neuter like місто, село. Drill: «Це тато. Він великий» (when verbs arrive) vs «Це місто. Воно нове».
2. **И /ɪ/ vs І /i/ confusion** → Київ contains both (Ки- vs -їв). Grade 5 Avramenko §38-39 targets exactly this: stress + vowel quality pairs. Minimal pair drill: ти/ті, дим/дім, кит/кіт.
3. **Adjective agreement default to masculine** → *великий книга instead of велика книга. English has no gender agreement, so learners default to the dictionary form. Fix: teach ending as part of the noun phrase, not a separate rule.
4. **Це misread as conjugated verb** → «Це кіт» is a nominal sentence (no verb). Learners want to say *"Це є кіт." Clarify early: Це is a demonstrative particle, not "is."

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Show a mini café menu image with 6 items: «кава» (F), «торт» (M), «молоко» (N), «сік» (M), «борщ» (M), «вода» (F). Ask learners to write «Це ___» for each item, then add one adjective (великий/маленький). Before explaining why endings change, let them notice the pattern across their answers — which ending goes with which noun ending. TTT: Test first → reveal pattern → retest.
- **Best context**: Hostel check-in / café order. Real scenario: learner arrives at a Lviv hostel, host points to items: «Це ваша кімната. Це ваш ключ. Це нова карта міста.» — forced gender agreement without verb conjugation.
- **Contrastive pairs for gender**: великий дім (M -ий) / велика книга (F -а) / велике місто (N -е). Also: тато (M, misleading -о) vs місто (N, regular -о). Grade 5 Avramenko §38-39 uses minimal pair drills as model exercises for this type of phonetic/morphological contrast.

## Notes for Content Writing
- This is a CONSOLIDATION module — no new grammar. All content must review M1-M13 material, not introduce.
- Verb-free constraint is strict: Це + noun/adj only. Hostel host persona works perfectly because pointing at items ("Це ваш номер") requires zero verbs.
- The integration task must be cumulative: combine Cyrillic reading fluency, gender ID, adjective agreement, and plural forms — ideally in one short café scenario.
- Word target 1200 is achievable without padding: the TTT structure naturally generates diagnostic examples, error analysis boxes, and self-check questions.
