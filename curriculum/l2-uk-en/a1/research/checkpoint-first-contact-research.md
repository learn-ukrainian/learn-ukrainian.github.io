## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

At the M10 Checkpoint, students focus on foundational verbs and survival vocabulary.

**High-Frequency Items (Essential):**
*   **Verbs**: знати (to know), говорити (to speak), читати (to read), розуміти (to understand), хотіти (to want - though usually taught later, used in "First Contact" phrases).
*   **Questions/Interrogatives**: хто (who), що (what), де (where), куди (where to), як (how).
*   **Particles/Adverbs**: так (yes), ні (no), не (not), тут (here), там (there).
*   **Basic Nouns**: меню (menu), кава (coffee), борщ (borscht), вода (water), чек (bill/receipt).

**Lower-Frequency/Academic Items (Functional for the Checkpoint):**
*   перевіряти (to check), відповідати (to answer), питати (to ask), помилка (mistake).

## 3. Cultural Hook: First Contact Etiquette

1.  **Greeting Register**: In Ukraine, the choice between "Добрий день" (Good day) and "Привіт" (Hi) is strictly governed by social distance. Even in a casual "First Contact" at a café, students should default to the polite "Ви" (formal you) and formal greetings unless speaking to a peer in a very informal setting.
2.  **The "Handshake" Barrier**: While handshakes are the standard "First Contact" for men in Ukraine, it is less common for men to initiate a handshake with women they have just met unless a hand is offered first. A polite nod and "Дуже приємно" (Nice to meet you) are the safer bets for a successful first interaction.
3.  **Café Culture**: Ordering "First Contact" often happens at the counter in modern Ukrainian cities. Knowing "Можна...?" (May I [have]...?) is more common and polite than just "Я хочу" (I want).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

*   **Gender Interference**: English speakers struggle with assigning gender to inanimate objects. They often use "it" (воно) for everything, but in Ukrainian, objects are "він" or "вона". This is a critical skill to verify in M10.
*   **Conjugation Over-Generalization**: Students often try to apply Group 1 endings (-аю, -аєш) to Group 2 verbs (-ю, -иш). The checkpoint needs to isolate these two patterns.
*   **Cyrillic False Friends**: Learners frequently confuse Cyrillic **и** (pronounced like 'i' in 'sit') with **і** (pronounced like 'ee' in 'see'), and **е** with **є**.
*   **Reflexive Placement**: The suffix **-ся** / **-сь** is often forgotten or misplaced by learners who treat it as a separate word (like in some other Slavic languages).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (Verified at this Checkpoint):**
*   **Nouns**: Nominative case only. Recognition of Gender (m/f/n) by endings.
*   **Verbs**: Present Tense (Group 1 & 2). Reflexive verbs in present tense.
*   **Syntax**: Negation with "не". Word order in questions.
*   **Interrogatives**: Хто, Що, Де, Куди, Як.

**OUT OF SCOPE (Do NOT test):**
*   **Cases**: Accusative (M11), Genitive (M16), etc. *Crucial: Avoid sentences where the object changes form (e.g., "I see the woman") unless using masculine inanimate/neuter words.*
*   **Tenses**: Past (M21) and Future (M22) are not yet learned.
*   **Adjectives**: Gender agreement for adjectives is taught in M26. Keep descriptions limited to nouns and "Це..." (This is...).
*   **Plurals**: Nominative Plural is typically introduced later (M13-15 range depending on the track).
