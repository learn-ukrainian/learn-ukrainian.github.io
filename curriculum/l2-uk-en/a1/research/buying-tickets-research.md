# Дослідження: Buying Tickets

## State Standard Reference
§3 (A1 Thematic Areas, lines 479–556): Topics include **«подорожі»** (travel) and **«купівля»** (purchasing transactions) — both directly served by this module.
§4.2.3.1 (Genitive): Genitive case at A1 — basic forms only; `до + Genitive` for direction is lexicalized at this level.
§4.2.4.2 (Imperative): 2nd-person imperative allowed at A1 — supports `Дайте, будь ласка`.
Alignment: Module covers купівля + подорожі thematic areas using genitive direction chunks and imperative request forms, fully within A1 grammar scope.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Підсклянник культура** — Ukrainian trains (Укрзалізниця) have провідники who bring hot tea in iconic silver підсклянники (glass holders). This is a genuinely Ukrainian rail tradition that learners encounter immediately on overnight trips. Use as context for the провідник/підсклянник vocabulary introduction.
2. **Плацкарт як соціальний простір** — Плацкарт carriages are open dormitory-style sleepers where it's customary to share food (especially варена курка, яйця, помідори) and converse with strangers. This is a known cultural phenomenon distinct from Western train travel. Contrasts with закрите купе.
3. **Укрзалізниця QR-квиток** — Modern Ukrainian rail has nearly fully migrated to QR-code e-tickets via the UZ app and website. Learners traveling to Ukraine will almost certainly use електронний квиток rather than a physical ticket from a каса — a practical, real-world hook.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Gender agreement on квиток** → `один квиток` (not `одна квиток`) — квиток is masculine. English speakers default to treating all nouns as neutral and often apply feminine `одна` (perhaps by analogy with "one"). The plan explicitly flags this.
2. **Wrong preposition for destination cities** → `їхати до Львова` (not `їхати в Львів`) — English speakers transfer the English "to" directly as `в`, which is wrong for destinations at A1. Teach `до` as the fixed travel preposition chunk.
3. **Туди** without **й назад** → the full fixed phrase is `туди й назад` (round trip); dropping `й назад` produces awkward partial phrase. Teach as an indivisible chunk.
4. **Вокзал vs автовокзал vs автостанція confusion** — English has one word ("station") for all transit hubs. Ukrainian distinguishes залізничний вокзал (rail), автовокзал (intercity bus hub), автостанція/зупинка (local/regional stop). Drill the distinction early to prevent navigation errors.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present a short dialogue at a каса without explaining grammar first:
  > — Один квиток до Львова, будь ласка.
  > — Туди й назад чи в один бік?
  > — В один бік. Нижнє місце.
  Ask the learner: *What is being bought? Where is the person going? What kind of seat?* Then draw attention to `до Львова` — "what does `до` attach to? What changes about the city name?" Let them notice the genitive pattern before the rule is stated.

- **Best context**: Ticket office (каса) transaction — a tightly scripted, real-world dialogue with predictable turn-taking. PPP structure works well: Present the kaса dialogue → Practice with fill-in variations (different cities, seat types) → Produce their own купівля-квитка dialogue.

- **Contrastive pairs**:
  - `до Львова` ↔ `в Львів` (correct vs. common English-transfer error)
  - `один квиток` ↔ `одна квиток` (masculine gender agreement)
  - `туди й назад` ↔ `в один бік` (round trip vs. one-way)
  - `плацкарта` ↔ `купе` (open social vs. private compartment)
  - `вокзал` ↔ `автовокзал` ↔ `автостанція` (three levels of station)

## Notes for Content Writing

- Genitive city endings (`до Ки́єва`, `до Льво́ва`, `до Оде́си`, `до Харко́ва`) should be presented as fixed lexical chunks with stress marks — **do not explain genitive declension rules** at A1.
- `О котрій відправлення?` / `Коли прибуття?` use nominalized forms — present these as fixed question phrases, not as a grammar lesson.
- The провідник/підсклянник cultural section belongs in the "Поїздка та комфорт" section to give it narrative context, not as a disconnected fact.
- All dialogues max 10 words per Ukrainian sentence per A1 constraints. No subordinate clauses.
- Use blockquote format for dialogue containers; tables for paradigm chunks; bullet lists for vocabulary sets.
