# Research Notes: Buying Tickets (M39)

**Track**: Core A1
**Module**: buying-tickets
**Level**: A1 (Travel Arrangements)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

This module aligns with the **Державний стандарт української мови (ДСТУ 2024)** requirements for Level A1, specifically focusing on functional communication in public spaces and travel.

**Key Sections:**
- **§2.4.1.2 Каталог А (Перелік комунікативних намірів):** 
  > "прохання зробити щось: Дайте, будь ласка;"
- **§2.4.1.3 Каталог Б (Тематичний каталог):**
  > "3.7. Подорожі: вокзал, автовокзал, аеропорт (летовище); основні дії на позначення руху (іти, їхати – у базових фразах);"
- **§1.2.1.2 Читання (Загальний перелік умінь):**
  > "знаходити і вибирати потрібну інформацію, наприклад у чеку, на квитку, у меню."

The module utilizes the **Accusative Case** for objects of purchase (§4.2.3.2.1) and destination (§4.2.3.2.2), and the **Genitive Case** for directions with "до" (§4.2.3.4 - though usually taught earlier for absence).

## 3. Cultural Hook

1. **The "Platzkart" Experience:** In Ukraine, long-distance trains often feature the "плацкарт" (platzkart) carriage — an open-plan dormitory-style car. It is a unique social space where strangers share food and stories. While modern "Intercity" trains are popular, the "platzkart" remains a culturally iconic way to travel.
2. **Ukrzaliznytsia (UZ):** The state-owned railway operator, Ukrzaliznytsia, is the backbone of Ukrainian transport. Tickets are increasingly purchased via the official mobile app or website, but the station "каса" (ticket window) remains a primary point of interaction for many, especially for regional travel.
3. **Student Discounts:** Holders of a "студентський квиток" (student ID) are entitled to a 50% discount on regional and long-distance trains (excluding certain luxury classes), making "Buy a student ticket" a top priority for younger learners.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

- **Politeness vs. Directness:** English learners often try to translate "Can I have...?" literally. In Ukrainian, "Дайте, будь ласка" (Give, please) or "Я хочу купити..." (I want to buy...) is more direct and perfectly polite.
- **24-Hour Clock:** Ukrainian tickets and station boards use the 24-hour time format (e.g., 18:45 instead of 6:45 PM). A1 students need to practice reading these numbers fluently.
- **Numbers and Cases:** While students know numbers, the "Buying Tickets" context is a great place to reinforce the Accusative Case (Buying *what?* - квиток) and Genitive Case (To *where?* - до Києва).
- **Phonetic Note:** The word "квиток" (kvy-tok) contains the [и] sound, which English speakers often confuse with [і].

## 5. Scope Boundaries

**IN SCOPE (Prior Knowledge):**
- **Numbers:** Prices (e.g., 250 грн) and platforms/times.
- **Accusative Case:** Objects of purchase (купити квиток, студентський квиток).
- **Genitive Case:** Destination with "до" (до Львова, до Харкова) and absence (немає квитків).
- **Locative Case:** Location (на вокзалі, на платформі).
- **Modal Verbs:** "Хочу купити" (I want to buy).
- **Questions:** "Де каса?", "Скільки коштує?".

**OUT OF SCOPE:**
- **Dative Case:** Buying *for* someone (e.g., "купити братові").
- **Instrumental Case:** Traveling *by* a certain means (e.g., "їхати поїздом"). Use "на поїзді" (Locative) or focus on the destination.
- **Complex Aspect:** Avoid the perfective/imperfective struggle (купувати vs купити) unless introduced as a fixed pair for "I want to buy".
- **Advanced Classifications:** Specific train categories beyond "поїзд" and "автобус" (e.g., "електричка", "швидкісний").
