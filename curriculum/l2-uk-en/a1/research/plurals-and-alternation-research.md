## Cultural Hooks

1. **Market vendor scenario**: At a Ukrainian market (ринок), vendors display quantity labels: «яблука», «огірки», «помідори» — all plurals. A learner encounters plurals immediately when shopping. The Grade 4 textbook (p. 53) uses food vocabulary (вареники, борщі, котлетки) in a plural context — directly usable as a cultural hook.

2. **Pluralia tantum в побуті**: Ukrainian has several everyday objects that are plural-only and have no singular: «ножиці», «окуляри», «двері», «гроші». The Grade 3 textbook (Vashulenko, p. 116) explicitly contrasts these with countable nouns in a discovery exercise — ideal model for learners.

3. **Stress-shifting plurals**: Ukrainian folk songs and children's rhymes often depend on stress shifts that accompany pluralization (рукА → рУки, ногА → нОги). This connects to the a1-06 stress module and shows learners that Ukrainian pronunciation changes predictably at word boundaries.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **English plural "s" substitution** → Trying «студентс», «книгс» — corrected to «студенти», «книги». English speakers have one suffix; Ukrainian has gender-dependent endings (-и/-і for masc/fem, -а/-я for neut).

2. **Ignoring vowel alternation** → Writing «кіти» instead of «коти»; «ніжи» instead of «ножі». The alternation і → о/е is invisible to learners who copy the stem unchanged. The Grade 3 textbook exercise (p. 114: зірка → зірки, місто → міста) models the correct approach — compare stem + ending.

3. **Treating pluralia tantum as singular** → Saying «один гроші» or trying to make «одна двері» — these nouns have no singular form. The Vashulenko (p. 116) discovery exercise directly addresses this by asking whether certain words can form a singular.

4. **Wrong adjective plural ending** → Using «нові книга» (mixing singular noun with plural adjective) or «нова книги» (keeping singular adjective). Agreement in number must be drilled: adjective MUST match the noun's number.

## Teaching Strategy

**Discovery exercise**: Present a market scene in English with Ukrainian labels. Show a table with two columns — single item vs. multiple items on a market stall:

| One (однина) | Many (множина) |
|---|---|
| **яблуко** | **яблука** |
| **огірок** | **огірки** |
| **помідор** | **помідори** |

Ask: "What changes? Does the same thing happen every time?" Let the learner notice the gender-based variation before the rule is stated. Then reveal the rule in English with bolded Ukrainian forms inline.

**Best context**: The market/shopping scenario from register «розмовний» is perfect — vendors and customers naturally use plural forms: «Скільки огірків?», «Де яблука?», «Це гроші.» The plan assigns the Market Vendor persona, so this is ready-made.

**Contrastive pairs for discrimination**:
- Regular vs. alternating: **хліб → хліби** (no alternation) vs. **кіт → коти** (і → о)
- Singular-only vs. plural-only: **молоко** (no plural) vs. **гроші** (no singular)
- Regular vs. suppletive: **кішка → кішки** (predictable) vs. **дитина → діти** (suppletive)

**Textbook model**: Vashulenko Grade 3, p. 116 — the "Я — дослідник/дослідниця" discovery task for pluralia tantum is a direct model for the exceptions section. The two-column comparison format (однина/множина) is textbook-validated and can be replicated.

## Notes for Content Writing

- This is a **pre-verbal module** (M13 is the first module to introduce Locative, which also appears in this plan as a preview for consonant alternation). Keep all Ukrainian examples in Nominative plural — no verb conjugation.
- The consonant alternation preview (к → ц, г → з, х → с before -і) should be labeled as a **preview only** — brief mention with a note "this pattern returns in the Locative case module." Don't teach it deeply here.
- Stress shifts in plurals (рукА → рУки) should be shown with stress marks (рукá → рýки) — reinforce habit from a1-06.
- The adjective plural section is a **simplification message**: three genders collapse to one plural form (-і/-ї). Frame it as good news for the learner.
- Avoid introducing genitive case forms (багато грошей) in explanatory text — when listing pluralia tantum, use «Це гроші» constructions only.
