## Cultural Hook (1-2 facts)
- **Fact 1**: The most common street name in Ukraine is "вулиця Шевченка" (Shevchenko Street), named after the national poet Taras Shevchenko. Almost every city and village has one.
- **Fact 2**: The usage of "на" (on/at) vs "в/у" (in) follows specific patterns for public spaces. In Ukrainian, you are "на пошті" (at the post office), "на вокзалі" (at the station), and "на роботі" (at work), whereas you are "в аптеці" (in the pharmacy) or "в магазині" (in the store).
- **Source**: [rbc.ua](https://www.rbc.ua/rus/styler/pochemu-kiev-nazvali-kievom-lvov-lvovom-interesnye-1621592634.html), [ogo.ua](https://ogo.ua/articles/view/2016-02-15/72810.html).
- **How to use**: Incorporate "вулиця Шевченка" in location dialogues. Explain the "на роботі / на вокзалі" pattern as a cultural-linguistic tip in the Presentation section.
