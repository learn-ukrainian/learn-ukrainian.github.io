## Cultural Hooks
1. **Паляниця**: A traditional Ukrainian hearth-baked round wheat bread. It serves as a profound symbol of Ukrainian phonetic identity because its sequence of sounds—especially the uniquely soft Ukrainian 'ц' [t͡sʲ]—relies on native phonetic rules.
2. **Хліб-сіль**: Bread and salt. A foundational symbol of Ukrainian hospitality. Bread is deeply respected, reflected in the cultural proverb «Хліб — усьому голова» (Bread is the head of everything).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Feminine Accusative Omission**: "Я хочу кава" → "Я хочу каву". Beginners often forget to apply the -а to -у shift when ordering feminine food/drink items.
2. **Russianisms (Surzhyk)**: "кушати" → "їсти". Learners exposed to Russian might incorrectly use "кушати". In standard Ukrainian, "їсти" is the universal and correct verb for eating.
3. **Gender Agreement Mismatches**: "смачний кава" → "смачна кава". Failing to match the adjective to the noun's gender, especially since 'кава' is feminine.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonization & Framing**: Highlight Ukrainian's distinct phonetics regarding *паляниця* positively (focusing on the unique soft 'ц' [t͡sʲ] and vowel harmony), rather than using Russian speaker's inability as the baseline.
- **Scaffolding**: Provide English scaffolding for introducing noun genders of food items and the Dative "Мені подобається" construction.
- **Banned Words**: Ensure *кушати* does not appear; strictly enforce *їсти*.
