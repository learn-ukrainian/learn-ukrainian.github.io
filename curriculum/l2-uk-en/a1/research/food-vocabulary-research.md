## Cultural Hooks
1. **Паляниця as a Shibboleth**: The round wheat bread became a prominent symbol of Ukrainian linguistic identity in 2022. The specific phonetic features of the word (particularly the soft 'ц') highlight the distinctness of the Ukrainian language.
2. **Bread and Salt (Хліб-сіль)**: The traditional Ukrainian welcoming ceremony using bread and salt, reflecting the deep reverence for bread captured in the proverb «Хліб — усьому голова».
3. **Lviv Coffee Culture**: The historical depth of coffee culture in western Ukraine (кава по-львівськи) contrasts with the traditional tea culture.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Gender of borrowings**: Assuming "кава" is masculine or confusing the gender of "суп" (m). -> Practice collocations (гаряча кава, смачний суп) to reinforce gender.
2. **Drinking vs. Eating soup**: English speakers often say "I drink soup". -> Emphasize that in Ukrainian, we *eat* soup (їсти суп).
3. **Negation with Accusative vs Genitive**: At A1, keep it simple with Nominative/Accusative (Я не їм м'ясо) before introducing the Genitive of negation.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize Ukrainian phonetics (like the 'ц' in паляниця) as independent features, not as "differences from Russian". Highlight unique cultural elements without Soviet-era comparisons.
- **Scaffolding**: Since this is A1 (approx. 35-40% immersion target), use English scaffolding to clearly explain gender categories and the "Мені подобається" Dative structure. 
- **Vocabulary**: Provide IPA for all new food words on their first occurrence.
