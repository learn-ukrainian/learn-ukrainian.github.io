# Research Notes: Describing Things - Adjectives (A1 M26)

**Track**: Core A1
**Module**: 26-describing-things-adjectives
**Researched**: 2026-02-08
**Level**: A1 (Beginner)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

The grammar covered in this module aligns with the requirements of the **Державний стандарт української мови (ДСТУ 2024)** for the A1 level (Початковий рівень першого ступеня).

**Relevant Section**: §4.2.1.2. Прикметник (Adjective)
**Specific Requirement**: 
> "форми називного відмінка однини прикметників чоловічого, жіночого, середнього роду; узгодження прикметників з іменниками в роді й числі"
*(Nominative singular forms of masculine, feminine, and neuter adjectives; agreement of adjectives with nouns in gender and number)*

**Examples from the Standard**:
- чоловічий рід: іноземний студент, домашній одяг
- жіночий рід: зелена сумка, літня погода
- середній рід: велике вікно, останнє запитання
- множина: іноземні студенти, зелені сумки, великі вікна, останні запитання

This module specifically focuses on these base forms (Nominative) and the concept of agreement (Mirroring), which is a foundational skill for A1 students.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

At the A1 level, the "Thematic Catalog" (Каталог Б, §3) emphasizes describing people (appearance), housing (furniture), and the city. The following adjectives are high-frequency and essential for this stage:

### High Frequency (Essential)
- **великий** (big) / **малий** (small)
- **новий** (new) / **старий** (old)
- **гарний** (good/beautiful) / **поганий** (bad)
- **добрий** (good/kind) / **злий** (mean/angry)
- **цікавий** (interesting)
- **смачний** (tasty)
- **дорогий** (expensive) / **дешевий** (cheap)

### Medium Frequency (Expansion)
- **високий** (tall/high) / **низький** (low)
- **чистий** (clean) / **брудний** (dirty)
- **швидкий** (fast) / **повільний** (slow)
- **світлий** (bright) / **темний** (dark)
- **синій** (blue) - *Note: Used to introduce soft stems.*

### Common A1 Collocations
- **велике місто** (big city)
- **нова квартира** (new apartment)
- **цікава книга** (interesting book)
- **смачний борщ** (tasty borscht)
- **іноземний студент** (foreign student)

## 3. Cultural Hook

### The Dual Meaning of "Гарний"
In Ukrainian, the adjective **гарний** is exceptionally versatile. It translates as both "beautiful/handsome" and "good/nice." For example, "гарна погода" is "good weather," but "гарна дівчина" is a "beautiful girl." This reflects a cultural tendency to equate aesthetic beauty with moral or functional quality.

### Adjectives in Folklore: Epithets
Ukrainian folklore frequently uses constant epithets. Adjectives like **зелений** (green - for groves/gardens), **білий** (white - for light/homes), and **чистий** (pure/clean - for fields) carry deep symbolic weight. In A1, mentioning "зелена вулиця" or "світла кімната" connects the student to these traditional descriptive patterns.

### "Солов'їна мова" (Nightingale Language)
A common cultural descriptor for the Ukrainian language is **солов'їна** (like a nightingale). While the noun "nightingale" might be A2+, the concept that the language itself is "красива" and "мелодійна" is a strong motivational hook for A1 learners.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### The "Mirror" Analogy
Students should be taught that an adjective is like a **mirror** reflecting the noun's properties. Because the student already knows Noun Gender (M2) and Number, they simply need to learn how to change the "reflection" (the ending).

### Common Learner Errors (English Speakers)
- **Omission of Endings**: English adjectives are static. Students often try to use the dictionary form (masculine) for all genders (e.g., *великий книга* instead of *велика книга*).
- **Hard/Soft Confusion**: Students may apply hard endings to soft stems (*синий* instead of *синій*). Soft stems (-ій) should be introduced as a small, specialized group.
- **Word Order**: While Ukrainian is flexible, A1 should focus on **Adjective + Noun** to mirror English and build confidence.

### Teaching Sequence
1. Masc (-ий) — the dictionary form.
2. Fem (-а) and Neut (-е) — the gender counterparts.
3. Plural (-і) — the universal plural marker.
4. Soft stems (-ій/-я/-є) — introduced with a few common examples (синій, літній).

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope
- **Nominative Case Agreement**: Matching masculine, feminine, neuter, and plural nouns.
- **Qualitative Adjectives**: Describing color, size, quality, and appearance.
- **Hard and Soft Stems**: Primary patterns (-ий vs -ій).
- **Basic Predicative Use**: "Будинок великий" (The house is big).

### OUT of Scope (A1 M26)
- **Case Declension**: Adjectives in Accusative, Locative, or Genitive. Although nouns in these cases are known, adjective declension is usually reserved for A2 or later A1 stages to avoid cognitive overload.
- **Comparison**: Superlatives (найкращий) and comparatives (кращий) are B1+ topics.
- **Short Forms**: Archaisms like "певен" or "варт" are out of scope.
- **Relative Adjectives**: Complex derivations (e.g., "залізобетонний") should be avoided.
- **Possessive Adjectives**: (e.g., "материн") are distinct from possessive pronouns and are out of scope.
