# Research Notes: A1 M23 "What Time Is It?"

**Module**: A1 M23 "What Time Is It?"
**Subtitle**: Telling Time, Days, and Months
**Level**: A1 (Beginner)

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (2024)**, the topics of time, days, and months are core components of the A1 (Початковий рівень першого ступеня) level.

Relevant sections include:

- **Section 3.4. Побут (Everyday Life)**: Includes requirements for "окреслення часу (пори року, місяці, дні тижня, години і хвилини – телеграфний стиль)" (outlining time: seasons, months, days of the week, hours and minutes – telegraphic style).
- **Section 4.2.3.2.2 (Accusative Case with Prepositions)**: Specifies the use of **у/в** for days of the week: "у (в) (позначення часу дії (дні тижня)): До зустрічі в понеділок. У середу, мабуть, буде сонячна погода."
- **Section 4.2.3.3 (Locative Case with Prepositions)**: Specifies the use of **у, в** for months: "у, в (позначення моменту часу (коли?) в поєднанні зі словами – назвами місяців): Марія народилася у травні."
- **Section 4.2.3.4 (Vocative Case)**: Provides the standard question for time: "Пане / пані, скажіть, будь ласка, котра година?"

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency & Collocations

### High-Frequency Nouns (A1)
- **Time units**: час (time), година (hour/o'clock), хвилина (minute), секунда (second).
- **Parts of day**: ранок (morning), день (day), вечір (evening), ніч (night).
- **Days of the week**: понеділок, вівторок, середа, четвер, п’ятниця, субота, неділя.
- **Seasons**: зима, весна, літо, осінь.
- **Relative terms**: сьогодні (today), вчора (yesterday), завтра (tomorrow), зараз (now), потім (later).

### High-Frequency Collocations
- **Asking time**: Котра година? (Which hour is it? / What time is it?).
- **Time of day (adverbs)**: вранці (in the morning), вдень (during the day), ввечері (in the evening), вночі (at night).
- **Week/Month**: цього тижня (this week), минулого місяця (last month), наступного року (next year). Note: While Genitive is known, A1 students focus on basic usage.

## 3. Cultural Hooks

### Etymological Months
Unlike English or many European languages that use Latin-based names, Ukrainian month names are descriptive of nature and agricultural cycles. This is a significant cultural point:
- **Січень** (January) – from "сікти" (to chop), referring to biting frost or wood-cutting.
- **Квітень** (April) – from "квіти" (flowers), the month of blooming.
- **Серпень** (August) – from "серп" (sickle), the month of harvest.
- **Листопад** (November) – "leaf fall".

### Time Format
In Ukraine, the 24-hour clock is the official standard for schedules, transportation, and media. However, in casual speech, the 12-hour clock is frequently used, often supplemented by phrases like "дня" (of the day) or "вечора" (of the evening) to avoid ambiguity.

### Greetings
Greetings change based on the time of day, and the distinction between "Добрий день" (Nominative) and "Доброго дня" (Genitive) is a common point of discussion, though A1 focuses on the standard Nominative forms found in the ДСТУ.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### The "Hour" vs "Time" Confusion
Learners often confuse **година** (a specific hour or "o'clock") and **час** (the general concept of time). English uses "time" for both "What time is it?" and "I have no time." In Ukrainian, these must be distinguished: "Котра година?" vs "У мене немає часу."

### Case Usage for "When"
A significant hurdle for English speakers is the preposition/case shift:
- **Days of the week**: Use **в/у + Accusative**. (e.g., *у понеділок*).
- **Months/Years**: Use **в/у + Locative**. (e.g., *у травні*).
- **Seasons**: Can be used as nouns or adverbs (e.g., *взимку*), but for A1, starting with the adverbs or simple "у + Locative" is common.

### Ordinal Numbers for Time
Ukrainian tells time by asking "Which hour is it?" (Котра година?), requiring **ordinal numbers** (перша, друга, третя...). Since students already know cardinal numbers, the transition to ordinals for time-telling is a logical next step.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope
- **Telegraphic Time**: Using cardinal/ordinal numbers to say 10:15 (десята п'ятнадцять) or 8:30 (восьма тридцять).
- **Days of the Week**: Recognition and use with *у/в + Accusative*.
- **Months**: Recognition and use with *у/в + Locative*.
- **Basic Questions**: Котра година? О котрій годині? (At what hour?).
- **Past/Future Tense Integration**: "Вчора була субота" (Yesterday was Saturday), "Завтра буде неділя" (Tomorrow will be Sunday).

### OUT Scope
- **Complex Time Expressions**: Phrases like "за чверть до..." (quarter to) or "пів на..." (half past) involve complex Genitive/Accusative constructions that are too advanced for A1.
- **Instrumental Case**: Telling time using "перед/після" (before/after) with the Instrumental case.
- **Dative Case**: Using time for appointments (на яку годину?) if it requires Dative constructions (though some Accusative forms overlap).
- **Aspect in Time**: Distinguishing between perfective/imperfective verbs in the context of specific time durations (though basic past/future are known).
