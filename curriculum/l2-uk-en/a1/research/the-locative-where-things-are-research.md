## Cultural Hooks

1. **The "Shoes Off" Rule** — Entering a Ukrainian home requires removing street shoes and wearing **капці** (house slippers). This mandatory domestic ritual makes the locative phrase **вдома** / **у кімнаті** immediately culturally grounded. Every learner will encounter this on first visit.

2. **"Де козак, там і слава"** — Real Ukrainian proverb ("Where there's a Cossack, there's glory"). Shows the **де** construction in an authentic, memorable context. Connects grammar to national identity at a surface level without over-framing.

3. **Kyiv landmarks as location anchors** — Asking "Де Майдан?" or "Де Хрещатик?" is a realistic tourist/navigation scenario. Ukrainian city vocabulary (вокзал, пошта, парк, школа) matches everyday survival communication needs.

---

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Accusative for static location**: *Я в парк → Я в парку* — Learners carry over accusative from M25–26 and use direction forms for static location. Textbook contrast exercises (Locative = "where you are" vs Accusative = "where you go") target this directly.

2. **Euphony violation**: *В Львові → У Львові* — English speakers apply в before consonant clusters without the Ukrainian euphony rule (§4.1.7). Особливо tricky before proper nouns starting with consonant clusters.

3. **Missing velar mutation**: *на ногі → на нозі* (г→з), *на руці* correct, *на нозі* correct — English has no consonant mutations before case endings, so these feel arbitrary. "Hand and Leg" mnemonic (рука→у руці, нога→на нозі, вухо→у вусі) helps.

4. **Activity/building confusion**: *в роботі → на роботі* — English "at work" maps to **на роботі** (activity), not **в роботі** (inside the building). Similarly банк triggers **у банку** (building interior), while роботи, концерті, уроці use **на**.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show a simple image of a Ukrainian kitchen/room (or describe it in English): "A book is on the table. A cat is in the room. Olena is at work." Then show the Ukrainian equivalents: *Книга на столі. Кіт у кімнаті. Олена на роботі.* Ask learners: "What happened to стіл, кімната, робота?" — they notice the endings changed. Only then introduce the rule.

- **Best context**: Domestic space (Ukrainian apartment) + city navigation. Both are A1 survival situations. The shoes-off cultural hook grounds the first context immediately.

- **Contrastive pairs** (critical disambiguation):
  - Static vs. directional: *Я в парку* (I'm in the park) vs. *Я іду в парк* (I'm going to the park)
  - на activity vs. в building: *він на роботі* vs. *він у банку*
  - Euphony: *у Львові* vs. *в Одесі* (demonstrate with both cities)

## Notes for Content Writing

- The plan's "Kyiv City Guide" persona fits perfectly — frame the production section as a mini city-guide scenario.
- Velar mutations (к/г/х→ц/з/с) are the hardest morphological feature here. Budget extra example sentences here — at least 4–5 mutation examples in the practice section.
- Keep the в/у alternation rule clearly explained in English; do not assume learners will infer it from examples alone at A1.
- **на роботі** is the single most useful activity-exception to memorize; consider bolding it and giving a memorable mini-dialogue.
- Housing types (Stalinka, Khrushchevka, Novobudova) are concrete cultural vocabulary for the production section — keep descriptions in English with bolded Ukrainian terms.
