## State Standard Reference

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Бессарабський ринок (Kyiv, 1912)** — Art Nouveau covered market with Ukraine's first refrigerators, still called "the most expensive in the city." Concrete detail: the cast-iron facade by architect Гай. The name itself is a cultural lesson — Bessarabia, southern Ukraine.
2. **Привоз (Odesa, 1827)** — Ukraine's most legendary market, famous for vendor wit and theatrical bargaining. Colorful idioms include "тільки з грядки" (just from the garden, meaning ultra-fresh). Folk legend claims a circus elephant named Мурза was sold here. Useful phrase: **«А дешевше можна?»** (Can I get a discount?) — authentic Privoz register.
3. **"Спробуйте!" tradition** — Offering a taste of cheese, honey, or fruit before buying is a genuine Ukrainian market custom. It's not a sales trick — it builds trust (довіра) between buyer and vendor. Learners can role-play this before any transaction.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Number-noun agreement with кілограм**: English speakers say "два кілограм" (zero plural). Correct: **два кіло́грами** (Nom.Pl, 2–4) vs **п'ять кіло́грамів** (Gen.Pl, 5+). Teach the 2-4 / 5+ split as two frozen phrases first; don't introduce all cases of numerals at once.
2. **Nominative instead of genitive after weight units**: "кілограм картопля" → **кілограм карто́плі**. English has no genitive, so learners default to the dictionary form. Teach "кілограм ___" as a slot that always takes the genitive ending — practice with 5–6 produce words.
3. **"Я хочу" for requests** (too direct / rude in Ukrainian market register): Correct polite form is **«Дайте, будь ласка»** or **«Можна...»**. "Я хочу" sounds blunt; vendors may react with raised eyebrows. This maps to real-world failure learners will encounter immediately.
4. **«Здача» vs «Решта»**: Russicism alert — «здача» exists in Ukrainian but is 23× less frequent than **«решта»** (GRAC). Learners who've heard Russian will default to «здача»; present «решта» as the natural Ukrainian word, note «здача» as a recognized but non-preferred synonym.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present the short dialogue cold, no vocabulary pre-teaching:
  > *Продавець: — Що бажаєте?*
  > *Покупець: — Дайте, будь ласка, кілограм яблук і два кілограми помідорів.*
  > *Продавець: — Будь ласка! Скільки з вас? Тридцять гривень.*
  > *Покупець: — Ось, без решти.*
  
  Ask: "What did the buyer ask for? How do you know it's about weight?" Learners notice **кілограм** and deduce the genitive slot from context before any rule is stated. This matches PPP pedagogy stated in the plan.

- **Best context**: Outdoor produce stall — vendor/buyer dynamic with weight units, prices, and politeness is fully authentic. The market context motivates *all* grammar in this module: you need genitive for quantities, imperative for requests, and numbers for prices.
- **Contrastive pairs**:
  - «Дайте, будь ласка» vs «Я хочу» (polite request vs rude demand)
  - «один кілограм» / «два кілограми» / «п'ять кілограмів» (number-agreement triple)
  - «без решти» / «візьміть решту» (exact change vs take the change)
  - «тільки з грядки» / «свіжий» (idiomatic vs literal "fresh")

## Notes for Content Writing

- Treat «кілограм + genitive» as **lexical chunks**, not full case theory. The plan is explicit: "without deepening into case theory." Give 5–6 frozen examples, label them, let patterns emerge.
- The plan specifies **розмовний register** and persona: Privoz vendor. Dialogue section should have Odessa-flavored humor ("А дешевше можна?") without caricature.
- **Privoz** roleplay works well as culminating task: buyer negotiates, vendor uses "тільки з грядки" and similar color. Keep Ukrainian sentences ≤10 words per A1 constraint.
- «пучок» (bunch) appears in plan §3.8 quote but is not in vocabulary_hints — include it in body as supplementary without requiring mastery.
- Numbers: cover 1–10 for prices + quantities since a1-39 introduced basic numbers; this module reinforces them in transactional context.
