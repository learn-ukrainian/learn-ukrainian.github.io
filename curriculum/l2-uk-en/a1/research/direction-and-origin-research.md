# Дослідження: Direction and Origin

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Train travel saying**: "Їду до Київа" — Ukrainian railways connect every region; the phrase "Де ти їдеш?" / "Я їду до Львова" is a standard conversation opener on Ukrzaliznytsia (Ukrainian Railways). Direction language has immediate practical relevance for anyone visiting Ukraine.
2. **Folk proverb (origin)**: *"Де народився, там і пригодився."* (Where you were born, there you are useful.) — natural context for з + Genitive origin: "Я з Полтави," tying language learning to national identity and regional belonging.
3. **Practical daily context**: Directions to/from the market — "Куди ти йдеш? — Я іду на ринок. Звідки ти? — Я з ринку." Markets (ринок) are central to Ukrainian urban life; the ринок vocabulary gives immediate real-world anchoring.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Де? vs Куди? case confusion** → *в магазині* (Loc) vs *в магазин* (Acc) — English uses "in/at" for both location and direction; learners default to Locative for all в/у/на phrases. Textbook-style contrast drills (two-column: location vs destination) directly target this.
2. **До + Genitive vs у/на + Accusative conflation** → "Я іду до магазин" ❌ → *до магазину* — learners forget до always takes Genitive, not Accusative. Animate (до лікаря) vs inanimate (до магазину) Genitive endings add another layer.
3. **З/із/зі selection errors** → "я із Київ" ❌ → *я з Києва* — English speakers don't have preposition alternation; the з/із/зі euphony rule (Pravopys §25) needs concrete pattern drilling: з before open consonants, із before clusters, зі before з-/с-/ш-.
4. **від vs з confusion** → "лист з мами" ❌ → *лист від мами* — both mean "from" in English; learners must learn the з (from inside a place) vs від (from a person) semantic split.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present three dialogue bubbles about the same place (school):
  - *Де Марія?* — *Вона в школі.* (already known: Locative)
  - *Куди йде Марія?* — *Вона іде в школу.*
  - *Звідки йде Марія?* — *Вона іде зі школи.*
  Have the learner spot what changes (the noun ending and preposition) before naming the rule. The "same noun, three forms" pattern makes the paradigm visual and memorable.
- **Best context**: Train station or bus stop scenario — "Куди ти їдеш? Звідки ти приїхав/приїхала?" — mirrors the Avramenko grade-7 Metro dialogue exercise (Grade 7, p.136) which uses direction language in a transit setting.
- **Contrastive pairs**: в лікарні / в лікарню / з лікарні and до лікаря / від лікаря — same root, contrasting prepositions and cases. The в лікарню (into the hospital building) vs до лікаря (towards the doctor person) distinction is a productive minimal pair showing semantic + case interaction.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Use the Train Conductor persona to frame the module: "Every journey has three questions — where are you? where are you going? where are you from?" This gives the three-question paradigm a narrative hook.
- The з/із/зі rules should be presented as a pronunciation-driven choice (not arbitrary spelling), with a clear 3-row table: з | most cases | з Києва; із | cluster/sibilant | із Львова; зі | з-/с-/ш-/щ- initial | зі школи, зі мною.
- Past tense (A1.4 phase) allows: *Вчора я ходив у магазин. Він приїхав з Одеси.* — use sparingly for variety.
- Keep all Ukrainian example sentences ≤10 words, single clause.
