## Cultural Hooks
1. **Phytotherapy (Траволікування)**: Ukrainians often use herbal teas (chamomile/ромашка, calendula/нагідки, linden/липа) as a first response to minor ailments. It's common to hear "Випий чаю з малиною" (Drink tea with raspberries) for a cold. (Source: Ukrainian Folklore/Traditional Medicine studies).
2. **Traditional "Egg Rolling" (Викачування яйцем)**: In folk medicine, a common ritual involves rolling a raw egg over the body to "extract" the illness. While less common in cities, it remains a known cultural motif for "healing." (Source: Ivan Honchar Museum research on folk medicine).
3. **Proverb**: "У здоровому тілі — здоровий дух" (In a healthy body — a healthy spirit) — a fundamental Ukrainian proverb emphasizing holistic well-being.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Agreement mismatch** → *У мене болять голова* (Wrong) vs. *У мене болить голова* (Correct). Learners forget that "болить" must agree with the body part (singular) or "болять" (plural).
2. **Russian Influence (Surzhyk)** → *Я болію* (Wrong for illness) vs. *Я хворію* (Correct). In modern Ukrainian, "боліти" is for the part that hurts, while "хворіти" is for the person who is sick.
3. **Dative Confusion** → *Я погано* (Wrong) vs. *Мені погано* (Correct). English speakers often try to use "I am bad" literally instead of the impersonal dative construction.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Immersion level**: Keep explanations of the "У мене болить" construction in English to ensure clarity, as case usage (Nominative for the subject of pain) can be counter-intuitive for English speakers.
- **Visuals**: A table comparing "болить" (singular parts) and "болять" (plural parts like eyes/очі, ears/вуха, hands/руки) is highly recommended.
- **Causal links**: Integrate "бо" and "тому що" in the practice dialogues (e.g., "Я не прийшов, бо я хворий").
