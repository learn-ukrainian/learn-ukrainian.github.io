## Cultural Hooks

1. **Де/куди contrast in Ukrainian street navigation** — In Ukrainian cities, asking for directions uses a strict де (location) vs. куди (direction) split that English "where" blurs: *Де зупинка?* vs. *Куди йде автобус?* This is a natural real-life hook learners encounter immediately in Kyiv, Lviv, or any Ukrainian city.
2. **Proverb with Genitive absence**: *Немає лиха без добра* ("No evil without good" — every cloud has a silver lining). Demonstrates немає + Genitive in a memorable, authentic sentence. Well-known Ukrainian saying.
3. **н-prefix pronoun rule in everyday speech**: *Він чекає на тебе* (no н) vs. *Він чекає на нього* (н required after preposition). The rule is purely phonetic — the н restores the /n/ lost when the original *jь* contracted. Present as a sound trick, not a grammar rule, at A1.

---

## Common Learner Errors

1. **де vs. куди confusion** → *Де ти йдеш?* ✗ / *Куди ти йдеш?* ✓ — English "where are you going?" maps to куди, not де. English has one word; Ukrainian has two.
2. **Accusative = Nominative for feminine nouns** → *Я бачу нова книга* ✗ / *Я бачу нову книгу* ✓ — English has no case marking, so learners copy the Nominative form into object position.
3. **Forgetting н-prefix on pronouns after prepositions** → *у його* ✗ / *у нього* ✓ — Very frequent error because learners learn його as a standalone form first and forget the preposition-triggered variant.
4. **в/на confusion for Locative** → *у горі* ✗ / *на горі* ✓ (на for open surfaces/elevated places); *в місті* ✓ / *на місті* ✗ — the в vs. на choice is lexically fixed, not rule-derived, and must be memorized per noun.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Open with a short city map dialogue — two characters are lost. Show 4 lines of speech mixing де, куди, немає, and в/на + correct case. Ask learners: *Which questions ask about location? Which ask about movement? What happens to the noun endings after "немає"?* Let them notice the pattern before any rule box.
- **Best context**: City navigation — asking for a café, bus stop, park. Also: shopping (немає цукру, немає хліба). Both contexts appear naturally in A1 Thematic Area §3: місто, побут.
- **Contrastive pairs** (most useful for this module):
  - де *парк*? → у па́рку (Loc) vs. йду в па́рк (Acc)
  - бачу *кни́гу* (Acc inan) vs. бачу *студе́нта* (Acc anim = Gen)
  - *його́* (standalone) vs. *у ньо́го* (after preposition)
  - *нова кімна́та* (Nom) → *нову кімна́ту* (Acc) → *у новій кімна́ті* (Loc)

## Notes for Content Writing

- This is a **TTT checkpoint** — Test, Teach, Test. Open with a diagnostic task, then teach/review gaps, then close with a confirmation task. Do not teach new grammar.
- The де/куди/звідки triad is the vocabulary spine of this module — build the integration scenario around these three adverbs.
- Adjective paradigm table (новий: Nom/Acc/Loc across genders) should appear as a reference box — learners have seen individual forms but never side-by-side.
- н-prefix rule (його → нього) should be framed as a "sound trick," not a grammar rule — keep register розмовний.
- 30-item quiz specified in `activity_hints` — ensure variety: gap-fill, multiple choice preposition, case-end selection, dialogue correction.
