## Cultural Hook (2 facts)
- **Fact 1:** The Cyrillic alphabet was created in the 9th century in Bulgaria by followers of Saints Cyril and Methodius — NOT by Russians. Ukraine adopted Cyrillic independently through Kyivan Rus'. The Russian language didn't exist as a distinct language when Cyrillic was created.
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyrillic_script, https://uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Кирилиця
  - How to use: In the Cultural Insight section — decolonization point, already integrated as "Did You Know?" callout

- **Fact 2:** Ukrainian has 4 unique letters not found in Russian (Ґ, Є, І, Ї), and Russian has 4 letters not found in Ukrainian (Ё, Ъ, Ы, Э). These alphabets are distinct systems adapted for different languages.
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ukrainian_alphabet
  - How to use: In the Cultural Insight section about unique Ukrainian letters, already integrated
