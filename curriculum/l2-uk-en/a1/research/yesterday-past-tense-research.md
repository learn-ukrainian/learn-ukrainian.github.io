## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Indicative mood — present, past, future tenses; conjugation I/II; reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)" (lines 703–719)
Alignment: This module directly implements the past tense component of §4.2.4.1 — L-participle formation (основа інфінітива + суфікси -в/-ла/-ло/-ли), gender agreement, and past tense time expressions. The plan references §4.2.4.1 explicitly.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ukrainian folk greeting pattern** — Як справи? / Що нового? often elicits a mini past-tense narrative: "Учора ходив у кіно. Було цікаво." This is the most natural real-life trigger for the past tense at A1.
2. **Ivan Fedorov's first printed book** — 15 лютого 1574: "Федоров надрукував «Апостол» у Львові." A concrete historical sentence using one past-tense verb; the plan already includes this as motivation hook. Keeps pedagogy grounded in Ukrainian cultural memory.
3. **Ukrainian proverb** — "Що було, те минуло" (What was, has passed). Simple, all past-tense words, perfect as a closing cultural note tying time expressions to folk wisdom.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Gender mismatch on first-person forms** — An English speaker doesn't think of themselves as gendered: they write "я писав" regardless of their gender. → Female learners must use "я писала." Why: English has no grammatical gender on past tense; this is the single biggest A1 error per Grade 7 textbook (avramenko §29 error exercise targets this directly).
2. **Calque "я був працював" for "I was working"** → Correct: "я працював." Ukrainian imperfective past covers both English simple and progressive; no auxiliary needed. Why: English progressive tense misleads learners into adding "був/була."
3. **Adding прийменник "в/у" to time expressions** — learners say "в минулому тижні" → Correct: "минулого тижня" (genitive, no preposition). Why: direct calque from English "last week" → "in last week."
4. **Irregular root confusion: їсти/йти** — "він їв" vs. "він йшов" (not "ходив" for a single trip). The ішов/ішла forms cause confusion because learners expect a regular -ити ending pattern. The avramenko Grade 7 exercise drills доконаний vs. недоконаний aspect in past — profilist at determining form correctness.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present a short dialogue between two friends meeting in the morning. All verbs are present tense EXCEPT two which appear in past tense (yesterday context):
  > — Привіт! Що ти зараз робиш?  
  > — Читаю книгу. А вчора я дивився фільм.  
  > — Я вчора теж була вдома. Їла піцу і спала.
  
  Ask the learner: "Find the verbs. Two groups — what's different?" They notice: вчора → дивився / була / їла / спала. Then ask: "What changes between він/вона?" The gender ending pattern emerges from data before the rule is stated.

- **Best context**: Daily routine reporting — "What did you do yesterday?" This maps perfectly to real L2 use (telling a friend, writing to a language partner). Museum curator role (plan persona) works well for historical sentences alongside personal anecdotes.

- **Contrastive pairs for disambiguation**:
  - він читав / вона читала (gender agreement — most important)
  - він ішов / він ходив (unidirectional vs. multidirectional — brief note, full treatment at A2)
  - я працював (imperfective past) vs. ≠ "я був працював" (calque to reject)

## Notes for Content Writing

- Imperfective aspect ONLY (per A1 quick-ref) — do NOT introduce perfective verbs (прочитав, з'їв). The plan drills imperfective past throughout.
- Reflexive verbs: дивитися → дивився/дивилася — explain -ся stays attached; this is a first encounter with reflexive past forms.
- ішов/ішла: introduce as a lexical pair (два слова), not as a conjugation. Full motion-verb system comes later.
- The persona is "Museum Curator" — use this for historical hook sentences (Федоров надрукував) but keep the core practice context personal/daily (вчора я...).
- Avoid subordinate clauses (що, коли, бо) — banned at A1. All example sentences must be single clause.
- Max 10 words per Ukrainian sentence (strict A1 constraint).
