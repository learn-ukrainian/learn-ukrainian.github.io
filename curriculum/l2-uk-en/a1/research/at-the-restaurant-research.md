# Дослідження: At the Restaurant

## State Standard Reference
§3.9: "Ресторан, кафе: страви; напої; посуд і столові прибори."
Alignment: This module provides the practical vocabulary and communicative structures for the "Restaurant" thematic area. It also implements §4.2.3.2 (Accusative case for direct objects) and §4.2.4.1 (Indicative verbs, specifically future composite/simple future for ordering).

## Cultural Hooks
1. **"Ресторан" vs. "Кафе"**: In Ukraine, a *ресторан* usually implies a more formal, high-class establishment often reserved for special occasions or evening dining. For a casual meal, lunch, or coffee, Ukrainians typically look for a *кафе*.
2. **Tipping Culture**: Tipping is common and appreciated in Ukrainian urban centers. A tip of 5-15% is standard for good service. It is often preferred to leave tips in cash, even if the main bill is paid by card.
3. **Toasting**: Sharing a meal with locals often involves toasts. The most universal Ukrainian toast is "Будьмо!" ([ˈbudʲmɔ] — "Let us be!").

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Case Mismatch (Accusative)**: Learners often use the Nominative case when ordering (e.g., *Я буду кава* ❌ instead of *Я буду каву* ✅). In Ukrainian, the item you "will have" is a direct object in the Accusative.
2. **Over-formality**: Using overly stiff or complex sentences instead of the natural *Мені, будь ласка...* (To me, please...) or *Можна...?* (Is it possible... / Can I have...?).
3. **Russian Intrusion**: Using the Russian word for "bill" (*счёт*) instead of the Ukrainian **рахунок**.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Focus on the structure of a traditional Ukrainian menu: **перше** (soups), **друге** (mains/sides), **десерт**.
- Emphasize the polite "Я буду..." structure as the standard way to order at the A1 level.
- Include dietary requirement phrases early (без м'яса, без глютену) as these are highly practical for modern travelers.
- Use IPA for "смачного" and "будь ласка" as they are the most frequent polite markers.
