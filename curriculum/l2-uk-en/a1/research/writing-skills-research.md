## Cultural Hooks
1. **Modern Address Format:** Ukraine uses the European standard address format (Name → Street → City → Postcode), a deliberate shift away from the old Soviet "general-to-specific" model (Country → City → Street → Name).
2. **Patronymics (По батькові):** These are an authentic, deeply rooted Slavic and Ukrainian tradition dating back to Kyivan Rus, used universally on official forms. 

## Common Learner Errors
1. *Translating age directly* → **Мені 20 років** (not "Я є 20 років"). Learners often forget the Dative case for age.
2. *Forming gender in documents* → **чоловіча/жіноча** (adjectives). Learners mistakenly write "чоловік/жінка" (nouns) in the "Стать" field.
3. *Postcard greetings* → **Привіт з Києва** (Genitive). Learners often use the Nominative: "Привіт з Київ".

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing:** Emphasise that the address structure matches modern European standards. Explain patronymics as an integral part of Ukrainian heritage, independent of Soviet/Russian bureaucratic practices.
- Keep English scaffolding clear and encouraging, as writing tasks can be intimidating for A1 learners.
- Use visual tables to represent forms and envelopes.
- Explicitly connect previously learned grammar (Dative for age, Genitive with "з") to these practical writing tasks.
