# Research Notes: Emergencies (A1 M42)

**Track**: Core A1
**Module**: 42-emergencies
**Subtitle**: Help and Safety
**Level**: A1 (Beginner)
**Researched**: 2026-02-08

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (2024)**, the topic of emergencies and basic safety is categorized under "Survival" communication and health needs at the A1 level (§2.4.1).

The standard explicitly mentions emergency communication in the following sections:

> **§1.1.2.2. Діалогічне мовлення (Listening):**
> "...дуже прості вказівки про можливість отримати їжу, воду, першу допомогу тощо: ... **Мені треба лікаря / ліки / швидку допомогу. Де поліція / лікарня? Який номер поліції? Швидка допомога – 103.**"

> **§1.3.2. Типи текстів (Writing):**
> "окремі слова та словосполучення, що є назвами комунікативно важливих понять: Вокзал. **Допоможіть!** Замовити таксі;"

Additionally, the thematic catalog (§3.10) includes:
- **Здоров’я:** частини тіла; самопочуття; хворобливі стани (**болить, хворий, травма**, висока температура); **аптека**.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

### High Frequency (Survival Essentials)
- **Допоможіть!** (Help!) — The absolute priority for safety.
- **Поліція** (Police) — Universal cognate, easily recognized.
- **Швидка допомога** (Ambulance) — Often shortened to just "Швидка" in speech.
- **Лікар / Лікарня** (Doctor / Hospital) — Core health-related locations.
- **Аптека** (Pharmacy) — Essential for minor medical emergencies.
- **Пожежа** (Fire) / **Пожежники** (Firefighters).
- **Небезпека** (Danger) — Frequently seen on signs.
- **Обережно!** (Careful! / Watch out!) — Standard warning.

### Emergency Verbs & Phrases
- **Викличте...** (Call [the]...) — Imperative of *викликати*.
- **Загубив / Загубила** (Lost) — Past tense of *загубити*. Used for passports/phones.
- **Вкрали** (Stolen / They stole) — Plural past. Used for "My phone was stolen" (*У мене вкрали телефон*).
- **Мені треба...** (I need...) — The most versatile modal construction for requesting aid.

### Common Collocations
- **Викличте поліцію / швидку!** (Call the police / ambulance!)
- **Де найближча лікарня?** (Where is the nearest hospital?)
- **У мене проблема.** (I have a problem.)
- **Я не розумію.** (I don't understand.) — Crucial for clearing up confusion with authorities.

## 3. Cultural Hook

- **Unified Emergency Number (112)**: While the traditional Soviet-era numbers are still active (101-Fire, 102-Police, 103-Ambulance), Ukraine has transitioned to the unified European **112** system. Knowing both is critical for a foreigner.
- **Air Raid Protocol (Повітряна тривога)**: In the current context of Ukraine, "Safety" includes understanding air raid sirens. The words **Тривога** (Alert) and **Укриття** (Shelter) are part of daily survival vocabulary. Signs pointing to the nearest "Укриття" are ubiquitous in cities.
- **The "Diia" App (Дія)**: In interactions with police, Ukrainians often show digital documents in the *Diia* app. Foreigners should be prepared to show their physical passport or "Posvidka" (residency permit).

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

- **Imperative Stress**: English speakers often struggle with the stress in *Допоможіть* (do-po-mo-ZHIT'). Misplacing the stress can make the plea unrecognizable in a high-stress situation.
- **"У мене вкрали" vs "Мій ... вкрали"**: Students often try to translate "My phone was stolen" literally. They should be taught the idiomatic *У мене вкрали [Object]* construction, which utilizes the Genitive of possession they learned in M22.
- **Gendered Past Tense**: Remind students to use the correct gender for *загубив* (m) vs *загубила* (f) when reporting a lost item, as this reinforces the past tense rules from M37.
- **Phonetics of "Швидка"**: The cluster *швд* is difficult. Practice *швид-ка* to avoid it sounding like "shvyd-ka" with a silent 'd'.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope
- **Grammar**: 
  - **Accusative Case**: Direct objects for "need" and "call" (*Викличте швидку*, *Мені треба допомогу*).
  - **Genitive Case**: Possession and absence (*У мене немає паспорта*, *У мене вкрали гроші*).
  - **Locative Case**: Locations (*Я в лікарні*, *Де в аптеці?*).
  - **Past Tense**: Reporting what happened (*Я загубив гаманець*).
  - **Modal Verbs**: *Мушу* (must), *можу* (can).
- **Vocabulary**: Basic emergency services, lost items, simple injury terms (pain, blood, help).

### OUT of Scope
- **Instrumental Case**: Explaining *how* something happened using Instrumental nouns (e.g., "by accident", "with a knife").
- **Dative Case**: Declining nouns to say "Give the doctor..." (unless taught as a formulaic chunk).
- **Conditional Mood**: "If I had seen the thief..." (Too complex for A1).
- **Passive Voice**: "The door was broken." Stick to active or impersonal past (*Зламали двері*).
- **Complex Legal/Medical Jargon**: Terms like "perpetrator", "laceration", or "litigation".
