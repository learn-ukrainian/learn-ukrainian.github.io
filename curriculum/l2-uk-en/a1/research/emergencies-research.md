## Cultural Hooks
1. **The 112 System**: Ukraine has actively transitioned to the European "112" single emergency number (starting in 2023), reflecting its European integration and moving away from exclusively relying on the legacy Soviet-era fragmented system. It includes accessible features like sign-language video calls.
2. **Digital Resilience (Дія)**: The "Дія" (Diia) app acts as a digital life-saver, allowing Ukrainians to store digital documents (useful when physical ones are lost) and access state services, showcasing Ukraine's high level of digitalization.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Дзвонити поліцію" → "Викликати поліцію" — Learners often directly translate "call the police" using "дзвонити" (to ring/make a phone call), but in Ukrainian, you "summon" emergency services («викликати»).
2. "Визивати швидку" → "Викликати швидку" — A very common Russism (surzhyk). "Викликати" is the authentic Ukrainian verb.
3. Nominative vs. Vocative — Shouting "Лікар!" instead of the correct "Лікарю!" in an emergency. The Vocative case is mandatory for addressing people.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize Ukraine's modern, integrated 112 emergency response and digital infrastructure (Дія) as signs of a modern European state. Contrast the correct Ukrainian «викликати» with the Russian-influenced «визивати» without presenting the Russian form as a "baseline" – frame it simply as an incorrect habit to avoid.
- **Scaffolding**: Provide clear English scaffolding (immersion is ~35-40% at A1) when explaining the Vocative case and Imperative mood, but keep dialogues and examples purely in Ukrainian with English translations.
