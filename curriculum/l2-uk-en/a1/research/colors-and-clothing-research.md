# Дослідження: Colors & Clothing

## State Standard Reference
**§4.2.1.2**: "Adjective declension — hard/soft groups, gender agreement" (lines 633–650)
Alignment: This module applies adjective gender agreement (introduced in M11) to color adjectives + clothing nouns. Colors provide a concrete vocabulary set to practice hard-stem (-ий/-а/-е) and soft-stem (-ій/-я/-є) paradigms. The thematic area falls under A1 §3 topic **«купівля»** and **«побут»** (lines 479–556).

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Вишиванка color symbolism**: Central Ukrainian embroidery pairs **червоний** (life, love, energy) with **чорний** (earth, wisdom, fertility) — never random. The combination is a recognized traditional code. Learners can say: «Це вишиванка. Вона червона й чорна.» — immediately applying two colors and gender agreement in a culturally grounded sentence.

2. **Ukrainian flag — синє та жовте**: The flag provides the cleanest mnemonic for two key colors: **синє небо** (soft-stem adj!) + **жовте поле** (hard-stem adj). Use as a discovery pair: *Why does синє look different from жовте?* This naturally surfaces the hard/soft distinction before the rule is stated.

3. **Загадка for color review**: A simple Ukrainian folk riddle pattern learners can encounter:
   *«Червоне, але не сонце. Жовте, але не пісок. Синє, але не море.»*
   (No answer required — the pattern itself is the exercise.) Verb-free, purely adj + noun. Source: folk oral tradition, no copyright.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Gender agreement default to masculine**: `*червоний сорочка` → `червона сорочка` — English has no gender, so learners apply the masculine form (-ий) universally. Most common error with F nouns (сорочка, куртка, сукня). Textbook models: contrast pairs explicitly (червоний светр M / червона куртка F).

2. **Синій soft-stem confusion**: `*синій`→`*синья` (F) or `*синьо` (N) → correct: **синя** (F), **синє** (N). Синій is the only common basic color with a soft stem. Learners tend to regularize it to *-а/-е. Needs flagging early with the flag example (синє небо, not *синьо небо).

3. **Pluralia tantum — wrong numeral**: `*один штани` or `*одна штани` → correct: **одні штани**. English "a pair of pants" gives no Ukrainian intuition. Same issue with джинси and окуляри. No singular form exists in normal usage (VESUM confirms джинси as standard plural).

4. **Invariable loanwords get gender endings added**: `*бордова сукня` → correct: **сукня бордо** (or: **сукня — бордо**). Learners over-apply the agreement rule they just learned. The лoancolor follows the noun, takes no ending, regardless of gender.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show 3 clothing images side-by-side — a red shirt (F), a blue sweater (M), a white dress (N). Label only in Ukrainian: «Це червона сорочка», «Це синій светр», «Це біле плаття». Ask: *What does the ending on the color word change between the three?* Let learners notice -а / -ий / -е before any rule is stated. Then contrast синій vs. зелений in the same exercise to surface the hard/soft distinction.
- **Best context**: Getting dressed, shopping for clothes. The vyschankov context works well because it uses only colors + nouns in nominative — no verbs needed yet.
- **Contrastive pairs for disambiguation**:
  - Hard vs. soft: жовтий/жовта/жовте vs. синій/**синя**/**синє** (flag example)
  - Invariable vs. inflecting: червона куртка vs. куртка бордо
  - Pluralia tantum: ці штани сині (not «цей штан синій»)
- **Textbook model**: The Grade 3/4 textbooks use identification exercises (name + match) before production — align activity sequence: match-up first, fill-in second.

## Notes for Content Writing

- M12 is pre-verbal: ALL Ukrainian example sentences must be verb-free (Це + noun/adj, noun phrase labels, Adj + noun). No «Я ношу», no «Вона одягла». All classroom directions in English.
- Синій is structurally exceptional among the 6 basic colors — flag mnemonic is the most reliable hook; highlight in a dedicated pattern box.
- Pluralia tantum section should introduce «одні» as the numeral pairing (одні штани, одні джинси, одні окуляри) even if not drilled deeply — learners need to hear it once to avoid «один штани».
- Invariable loancolors (бордо, беж, хакі) — mention briefly as "bonus advanced colors"; do not drill heavily. One example per word is enough.
- VESUM confirms all 18 vocabulary items. Светр verified (2 morphological entries, standard noun).
