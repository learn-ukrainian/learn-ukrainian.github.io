# Дослідження: Questions & Negation

## State Standard Reference
§2.1.1: "Formulate simple questions and respond to them using basic affirmative and negative structures."
Alignment: This module introduces fundamental question words, yes/no question formation via intonation and the particle "чи", and basic verbal/nominal negation using "не" and "ні" for A1 learners.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|-------|------------------|
| хто  | Animate question word | Хто це? Хто там? |
| що   | Inanimate question word | Що це? Що ти робиш? |
| де   | Location question word | Де ти? Де він живе? |
| чи   | Yes/no question marker | Чи ти знаєш? |
| не   | Verbal/adjectival negator | не знаю, не розумію, не добре |
| ні   | Absolute negative response | Ні, дякую. Ні, це не так. |

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Folk Saying:** "Не кажи «гоп», поки не перескочиш" (Don't say 'hop' until you jump) — excellent early exposure to "не" placed directly before the verb.
2. **Everyday Interaction:** The classic "Хто там?" (Who is there?) used when someone knocks on the door, deeply ingrained in Ukrainian daily life and pop culture.
3. **Double Negation Preview:** While deep double negation is for later, a simple phrase like "Нічого не знаю" (I know nothing) is a very common, natural phrase that introduces the concept early without overwhelming the learner.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Using English Auxiliaries** → English speakers often try to use "do" to form questions (e.g., "Робиш ти розуміти?"). *Correct form:* "Ти розумієш?" — questions are formed simply by intonation or "чи".
2. **Confusing "Ні" and "Не"** → Using "ні" before a verb (e.g., "Я ні знаю"). *Correct form:* "Я не знаю." "Ні" is the standalone "no", "не" is "not".
3. **Misplacing "Не"** → Placing the negator after the verb like in German or English "is not" (e.g., "Він є не студент"). *Correct form:* "Він не студент." "Не" always immediately precedes the word it negates.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Present a short, illustrated dialogue of someone looking for their keys. "Де мої ключі? Це мої ключі? Ні. Це не ключі, це телефон." Let the learner deduce that "Ні" stands alone to answer a question, while "не" negates the specific item or action.
- **Best context**: A lost-and-found scenario, or a playful interrogation game (20 Questions style) asking "Is it big? No, it's not big." This grounds the grammar in an immediate, practical reality.
- **Contrastive pairs**: Highlight the difference between responding to a question and negating a fact: "Ти студент?" -> "Ні." vs. "Я не студент." Show them together: "Ні, я не студент."

## Notes for Content Writing
- Keep explanations of "чи" very light. Emphasize that in spoken Ukrainian, intonation is the primary way to ask a yes/no question. "Чи" is good for clarity in writing or formal speech, but not strictly required for A1 daily conversation.
- Do not introduce complex cases for "хто/що" (кого, кому, чому, etc.) yet. Stick strictly to the nominative case forms.
