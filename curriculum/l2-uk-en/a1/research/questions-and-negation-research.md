# Дослідження: Questions & Negation

## State Standard Reference
§4.3.1: "питальне речення: Чи студент відповідає? Чи це наш автобус?; питальне речення зі спеціальними словами де, звідки, куди, що, хто: Де ви були вчора? Хто вам це сказав? Що тобі купити? Куди ви їдете?; стверджувальні й заперечні речення: Він чекає. Він не чекає."
Alignment: This module directly implements the A1 requirement for simple interrogative sentences (Yes/No with 'чи', special questions with 'хто/що/де') and simple negation with 'не'.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Iconic Dubbing**: The Ukrainian dub of "ALF" is legendary. A perfect example of question+negation is the famous line: "Ти не любиш котів? Ти просто не вмієш їх готувати!" (You don't like cats? You just don't know how to cook them!).
2. **The "Чи" Register Check**: In strict literary Ukrainian, "Чи" is standard for Yes/No questions. In casual speech, it is often dropped, relying solely on rising intonation. Using "Чи" makes a learner sound more polite and educated, while dropping it sounds more conversational.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The "Do" Trap** → *Робиш ти знати?* (Wrong) vs *Ти знаєш?* (Correct) — Learners try to translate English auxiliary "do/does" literally.
2. **"No" vs "Not"** → *Я ні хочу* (Wrong) vs *Я не хочу* (Correct) — Confusion between the answer "ні" (no) and the particle "не" (not).
3. **Flat Intonation** → Failing to raise pitch at the end of a question without "чи", making it sound like a statement.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Intonation Visuals**: Use arrows (↗) in the text or explicit instructions to help learners visualize the rising intonation for Yes/No questions, especially when 'чи' is omitted.
- **"Що" vs "Шо"**: Briefly mention that while "що" is standard, learners will hear "шо" constantly in daily life. Stick to teaching "що" but validate "шо" as a recognized spoken variant so they aren't confused by input.
