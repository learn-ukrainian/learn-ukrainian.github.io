## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **Державний стандарт української мови (2024)** for Level A1:

*   **§ 2.4.1.2. Каталог А. Перелік комунікативних намірів**:
    > "Виразити просторові значення: тут, там, далеко, близько, на столі."
*   **§ 2.4.1.3. Каталог Б. Тематичний каталог (3.3 Місто)**:
    > "Орієнтування в місті: основні заклади (ресторан, кафе, ринок, лікарня, банк, пошта), транспорт і місця."
*   **§ 1.1.2.1.2. Короткі тексти розмовного стилю**:
    > "Вказівки, наприклад, щодо напрямку руху: Ідіть прямо. Треба йти прямо. Ми йдемо прямо."

The checkpoint focuses on the student's ability to combine spatial adverbs, locative case for positions, and simple imperatives to navigate basic city scenarios.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

### High-Frequency Items (Essential for A1.2)
*   **Question Words**: Де? (Where?), Куди? (Where to?)
*   **Directions**: Прямо (Straight), Ліворуч (Left), Праворуч (Right), Тут (Here), Там (There).
*   **Nouns (Locations)**: Вулиця (Street), Площа (Square), Центр (Center), Метро (Metro), Зупинка (Stop), Вокзал (Station), Парк (Park), Музей (Museum), Банк (Bank).
*   **Verbs**: Іти (To go), Їхати (To go by transport), Знаходитися (To be located), Повернути (To turn).

### Common Collocations
*   **Де знаходиться...?** — Where is ... located?
*   **На зупинці** — At the stop (Locative).
*   **Ідіть прямо** — Go straight.
*   **Поверніть ліворуч** — Turn left.
*   **Як дійти до...?** — How to get to...? (Note: uses Genitive).

## 3. Cultural Hook

*   **The "Marshrutka" Protocol**: In Ukrainian cities, yellow minibuses (*маршрутки*) are ubiquitous. A cultural staple is the "human chain" of passing money to the driver and asking for a stop: *"На зупинці, будь ласка!"* (At the stop, please!). Navigation often involves knowing the informal names of stops (e.g., "Near the Tank" or "At the Old Market").
*   **Meeting Points**: Ukrainians frequently use landmarks rather than street addresses. A famous meeting point in Kyiv is "under the clock" (*під годинником*) on Maidan, or "near the monument" (*біля пам'ятника*). Understanding navigation often means recognizing these specific cultural landmarks.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

*   **Де vs. Куди**: One of the most common errors for English speakers is using *Де* (location) when they mean *Куди* (direction/destination). At A1, students must distinguish between *"Де банк?"* (Where is the bank?) and *"Куди ви йдете?"* (Where are you going to?).
*   **Imperative as a Formula**: While the full imperative mood is a B1 topic, A1 students learn *ідіть* (go) and *поверніть* (turn) as functional "formulaic" chunks for navigation.
*   **The "In/On" Distinction**: Choosing between *в* and *на* for city locations (e.g., *в університеті* vs. *на площі*) is a point of difficulty. The rule of thumb for "open spaces" using *на* is introduced here.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope (Prior Knowledge)
*   **Locative Case**: Expressing location (*в університеті*, *на вокзалі*).
*   **Accusative Case**: Expressing destination with *в/на* (*я йду в парк*).
*   **Genitive Case**: Used for possession or absence (*тут немає банку*), and with simple prepositions like *до* (to/towards).
*   **Numbers**: Bus/tram numbers, walking distances (5 хвилин).
*   **Present Tense**: Group 1 and 2 verbs (*я йду*, *він їде*).

### OUT of Scope (Do Not Use)
*   **Dative Case**: Giving directions *to* someone (*мені треба...* is okay as a formula, but full Dative is too much).
*   **Instrumental Case**: Expressing movement *between* or *past* objects (*між*, *повз*).
*   **Complex Motion Verbs**: Prefixed verbs like *перейти* (cross), *дійти* (reach), *зайти* (stop by). Stick to *іти/їхати*.
*   **Passive Voice**: "The street is located..." should be simplified to *"Це вулиця..."* or *"Тут знаходиться..."*.
*   **Comparative Adverbs**: "Faster", "Further", "Closer" are usually saved for later A1 or A2.
