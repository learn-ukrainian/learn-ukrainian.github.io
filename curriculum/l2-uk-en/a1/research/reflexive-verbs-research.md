# Дослідження: Reflexive Verbs (-ся)

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Indicative mood — present, past, future tenses; conjugation I/II; reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)"
Alignment: This module directly addresses the §4.2.4.1 requirement for reflexive verb conjugation in the present tense. Past tense forms (сміявся/сміялася) are mentioned briefly for pattern recognition only — not drilled (past tense taught at M36).

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Вибачаюсь vs. Вибачте**: Ukrainian speakers often debate this — "Вибачаюсь" literally means "I excuse myself" (self-directed -ся), which is logically odd when apologizing to another person. The prescriptively correct form is "Вибачте мені" (forgive me). This is a live folk linguistic controversy that shows how -ся semantics are felt by native speakers.

2. **Morning routine proverb**: "Хто рано встає, тому Бог дає" (The early bird gets the worm — literally "God gives to those who rise early"). Ukrainian mornings are culturally tied to routines: вмиватися, одягатися, снідати. The reflexive verb cluster maps naturally onto the Ukrainian morning routine script that learners recognize from any culture.

3. **-ться pronunciation as a shibboleth**: In Ukrainian, -ться is pronounced with a long soft [ц'а] ("мию-ть-ся"), distinguishing it sharply from Russian [ца] ("мое-тся"). This is a real-world pronunciation marker that Ukrainians notice — useful framing for learners who may also have Russian exposure.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Redundant себе**: Saying "Я мию себе" instead of "Я миюся" — direct calque from English "I wash myself." The -ся suffix already encodes себе; adding it is redundant and unnatural.

2. **Wrong allomorph (-ся/-сь)**: Using -ся after vowels (❌ миюся instead of ✅ миюсь) or -сь after consonants (❌ миєтьсь instead of ✅ миється). Rule: -ся after consonants, -сь after vowels — but note that both forms are acceptable in colloquial speech; the distinction is primarily orthographic/standard register.

3. **Conjugation stem errors in 1sg**: дивитися → ❌ дивіюся instead of ✅ дивлюся. The -л- insertion in Class I verbs surprises learners. Highlight дивлюся, дивишся pattern explicitly.

4. **Treating lexicalized reflexives as transitive**: Attempting ❌ "Я сміюся тебе" (I laugh you) instead of ✅ "Я сміюся з тебе." Сміятися has no transitive counterpart — the -ся is fused to the verb, not removable.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show two columns of sentences without labels:
  | Column A | Column B |
  |---|---|
  | Оля миє тарілку. | Оля миється. |
  | Він одягає дитину. | Він одягається. |
  | Я слухаю музику. | — |
  Ask: "What is Olia doing in each sentence? Who does the action affect?" Let learners notice that Column B always ends in -ся and the subject is also the recipient. Then reveal: the -ся is like a mirror — the action bounces back to the doer.

- **Best context**: Morning routine (bathroom/bedroom) — вмиватися, одягатися, голитися are universally relatable and self-contained. A yoga class (persona: Yoga Instructor) also works well — займатися йогою, розминатися (warm up), розслаблятися (relax) add variety.

- **Contrastive pairs to drill**:
  - мити (щось) ↔ митися
  - одягати (когось) ↔ одягатися
  - називати (щось) ↔ називатися
  - вчити (когось) ↔ вчитися
