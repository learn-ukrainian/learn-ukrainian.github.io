## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Letter Ф**: In native Ukrainian phonology, the /f/ sound is practically non-existent. Words containing "Ф" are almost exclusively borrowings from other languages (фото, факт, футбол). Historically, Ukrainians substituted foreign /f/ with "ХВ" (e.g., Хведір instead of Федір) or "П" (Пилип).
2. **Хліб (Bread)**: Bread holds profound cultural and historical significance in Ukraine. It is a symbol of life and hospitality, embodied in the traditional "хліб-сіль" (bread and salt) greeting for esteemed guests.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Unstressed О** → Pronouncing it as "А" (Russian influence). Correct form: In Ukrainian, "О" must always be pronounced clearly as /o/, regardless of stress.
2. **П vs. Р** → Reading Cyrillic "Р" as English "P". Correct form: Cyrillic "Р" is a rolled /r/, while "П" is the /p/ sound.
3. **Дж/Дз split** → Pronouncing digraphs as two separate sounds (D + Zh). Correct form: They are single, unified affricate sounds.
