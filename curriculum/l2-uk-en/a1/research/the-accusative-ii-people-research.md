# Дослідження: The Accusative II: People

Alignment: This module focuses specifically on the "Accusative = Genitive" rule for animate masculine nouns (§4.2.1.1) and the general object function (§4.2.3.2.1).

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Друг vs. Знайомий**: In Ukrainian culture, a "друг" is a deeply trusted, long-term soulmate, whereas "знайомий" is a casual acquaintance. Using "друг" implies a high level of intimacy.
2. **Diminutives in the Family**: Ukrainians frequently use affectionate forms (матуся, татусь, братик, сестричка) even in adult conversation to express warmth. These follow the same Accusative patterns (матусю, татуся).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The Masculine Animate Trap** → *Я бачу брат* instead of *Я бачу брата*. Learners often apply the inanimate rule (Nom = Acc) to people.
2. **Animal Animacy Confusion** → *Я маю кіт* instead of *Я маю кота*. Learners forget that pets/animals are grammatically "alive" and follow the animate rule.
3. **Feminine Overthinking** → Learners sometimes think feminine animate nouns (сестра) follow the genitive pattern (сестри), but they actually follow the standard -у/-ю Accusative rule (сестру).

## Notes for Content Writing
- Use the "Heart Words" (Animate) vs "Stone Words" (Inanimate) visual metaphor to explain the logic.
- Emphasize that masculine animate nouns are "greedy" and take the Genitive ending to distinguish themselves from objects.
- Ensure the contrast between "брат → брата" (Masc) and "сестра → сестру" (Fem) is clear, as the rules for Masc/Fem are different.
