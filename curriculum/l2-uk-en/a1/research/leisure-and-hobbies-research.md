# Дослідження: Leisure and Hobbies

## State Standard Reference
§3: Thematic area "дозвілля" (leisure) and §4.2.3.2: Accusative case with prepositions (в/у, на).
Alignment: Introduces vocabulary for hobbies, sports, and leisure activities, and practically applies the Accusative case for destinations (ходити в кіно, йти на стадіон) and games (грати в футбол).

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|-------|------------------|
| грати | Takes 'в + Accusative' for sports, 'на + Locative' for instruments. | грати в футбол, грати на гітарі |
| хобі | Indeclinable neuter noun. | моє хобі, яке хобі |
| ходити | Habitual motion verb. Highly frequent. | ходити в кіно, ходити в гості |
| кіно | Indeclinable neuter noun. | ходити в кіно, нове кіно |
| велосипед | Common leisure transport. Uses bare verb phrase. | кататися на велосипеді |

## Cultural Hooks
1. Playing chess (грати в шахи) is a traditional and highly visible outdoor activity in Ukrainian parks, heavily enjoyed across generations.
2. "Ходити в гості" (visiting someone's home) is a cornerstone of Ukrainian social life, often preferred over meeting at restaurants or cafes for close friends and family.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Грати футбол" instead of "грати в футбол" → English speakers often omit the preposition "в" because English uses a bare object ("play football").
2. Declining indeclinable nouns → Trying to add case endings to foreign borrowings like "хобі" or "кіно" (e.g., в кіні). Both must remain unchanged.
3. Confusing location with destination → Using "бути в басейн" instead of "ходити в басейн" (Accusative for destination) vs. "бути в басейні" (Locative for static location).

## Notes for Content Writing
- This is module 51 (Phase A1.5). Present, past, and future tenses are known. Imperatives were introduced in M47, so they can be safely used in instructions and invitations (Ходімо!, Давай!).
- The plan explicitly uses Instrumental for "зустрічатися з друзями" and "захоплююся...". Treat these strictly as fixed lexical phrases. Do not teach the Instrumental case paradigm, as that is reserved for A2.
- English scaffolding is mandatory when explaining the conceptual difference between Accusative (destination) and Locative (location).
- Sentences must remain short and accessible (maximum 8-10 Ukrainian words per sentence) to maintain the A1 Safe Harbor.
