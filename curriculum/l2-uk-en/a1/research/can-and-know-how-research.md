# Дослідження: Can and Know How

## State Standard Reference
No specific § — modal verbs могти/вміти/можна are foundational communicative competence at A1, enabling expression of ability and permission (essential for survival communication). They align with the A1 communicative goal of expressing personal capabilities and navigating public rules.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Народне прислів'я**: *"Не вмієш — не берись."* (Don't take on what you can't do.) — Perfect introduction to вміти: inability as a permanent state. Authentic, short, memorable. Source: widely documented folk proverb.
2. **Public signs**: Ukrainian public spaces use не можна prominently — не можна курити, не можна паркуватися, не можна з їжею. Learners encounter these immediately in real life; treating them as a "sign-reading" skill adds practical value.
3. **Swimming ability as cultural touchstone**: *Я вмію плавати* is the most frequently cited textbook example for вміти across Ukrainian school grammar books (confirmed via RAG: Grade 7 Litvinova uses могти→можу alternation explicitly). Swimming pools and rivers (Дніпро) are part of Ukrainian summer culture — the example lands naturally.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **могти vs вміти conflation** → *Я можу плавати* (said for "I know how to swim") — The English "can" covers both; Ukrainian strictly distinguishes circumstance (можу) from acquired skill (вмію). This is the #1 error.
2. **можна subject agreement** → *Він можна увійти* — Learners try to conjugate the impersonal можна. It never inflects. Reference: Grade 5 Golub p.240 imperative ad-text shows impersonal constructions in signs (Купуйте!, Не зволікайте!) — same impersonal logic.
3. **Stem alternation in могти** → *Я мочу* (calque from Russian мочь→могу) — The correct Ukrainian is *можу* (г→ж alternation). Grade 7 Litvinova p.48 explicitly teaches this alternation table and lists могти→можу as the canonical example.
4. **Infinitive after modal omission** → *Я можу танець* instead of *Я можу танцювати* — English speakers sometimes omit the infinitive, using a noun instead. All three modals (могти, вміти, можна) require infinitive, not noun.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Present a dialogue between a swimming instructor and student:
  > *— Ти вмієш плавати?*
  > *— Так, я вмію плавати.*
  > *— Ти можеш прийти в п'ятницю?*
  > *— Ні, не можу. У мене урок.*
  > *— Тут можна фотографувати?*
  > *— Так, тут можна.*
  Ask learners: "Which word means 'I know how'? Which means 'I'm able to right now'? Which is a rule?" — Let them notice the three-way split before explaining it.
- **Best context**: Swimming pool / sports complex — naturally combines all three modals: вміти (do you know how?), могти (can you come today?), можна/не можна (pool rules posted on signs).
- **Contrastive pairs to drill**:
  - *Я вмію готувати* (I learned to cook — skill) vs *Я можу готувати зараз* (I'm free to cook now — circumstance)
  - *Я не вмію кататися на ковзанах* (never learned) vs *Я не можу кататися сьогодні* (circumstances prevent it) vs *Тут не можна кататися* (prohibited)

## Notes for Content Writing
- The persona is a Swimming Instructor — use the pool as the anchor environment throughout. Post signs on the wall (не можна бігати, не можна їсти, можна фотографувати), have instructor model вміти sentences.
- Avoid subordinate clauses (тому що, коли, щоб) — A1 constraint. All examples must be ≤10 words, single clause.
- грати needs both preposition patterns introduced: *грати на* (instrument: гітара, піано) vs *грати у* (sport/game: футбол, теніс).
- водити машину is the fixed collocation — never водити авто at A1 (less frequent for this level).
- The three-way negation contrast (не можу / не вмію / не можна) is the pedagogical climax — give it its own section with a clear comparison table.
