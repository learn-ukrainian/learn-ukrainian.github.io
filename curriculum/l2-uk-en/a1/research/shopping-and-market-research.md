Supporting: §4.2.3.2 Accusative (direct objects: купити хліб), §4.2.1.3 Numerals (cardinal forms for prices), §4.2.3.1 Nominative (subject: Це коштує...).
Alignment: This module directly addresses the §3 "купівля" theme at A1 with transaction vocabulary, price questions, and quantity expressions grounded in formulas learners can use immediately.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Textbook замок/замок homophones gag** (Grade 5, Avramenko p. 88): "Скільки коштує відремонтувати замок? — Три з половиною мільйони гривень. / — Триста п'ятдесят гривень." The punchline is word stress distinguishing за́мок (castle) from замо́к (lock). Perfect discovery hook: same spelling, wildly different prices.

2. **Language rights in the market** (Grade 5, Avramenko p. 7): A dialogue shows a store seller responding in Russian. The textbook asks: "Яку норму порушила продавчиня?" (ethical? legal? both?) — referencing Article 10 of the Constitution. For the module: use a correct-model dialogue (seller responds in Ukrainian) and note that Ukrainian is the law in service settings. Avoids lecturing; textbook models it.

3. **Трембіта as price anchor** (Grade 6, Avramenko p. 18): A dialogue in a music-instrument shop — "Чи є у вас трембіта? — Так, є. — Скільки вона коштує? — Дев'ять тисяч гривень." Familiar dialogue structure (Є? / Скільки коштує?) applied to a distinctly Ukrainian folk instrument. Good for the "у магазині" section as a cultural-flavored model dialogue.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **мати vs. є for availability** → Use **Є у вас...?** / **У вас є...?**, not **Ви маєте...?** — мати is for personal possession; store availability uses existential є. Textbook p. 7 implicitly contrasts this in store dialogue.

2. **гривня number agreement** → одна **гривня** / дві **гривні** / п'ять **гривень**. English speakers apply one rule ("five dollars" = just add s) and produce "п'ять гривня." This overlaps with a1-17 (Numbers and Money) — brief review needed.

3. **шампунь gender** → Masculine, not feminine. Learners guess feminine (шампунь ends in soft sign, which is feminine in many nouns). Impact: adjective agreement errors.

4. **Forgetting preposition switch** → **у магазині** (в/у + Locative) but **на ринку** (на + Locative). English has just "at the store / at the market." This paired contrast (у vs. на) is worth a dedicated example box.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show three price tags: "1 гривня / 2 гривні / 5 гривень" with images. Ask learners to spot the pattern before naming it. Then show 11–14 гривень (same Gen.pl. as 5+) to deepen. Source: Grade 6 Litvinova p. 244 models this numeral-noun agreement focus.
- **Best context**: Market (ринок) and store (магазин) dialogues are the perfect container — they have a natural start (greeting), middle (ask/answer), and close (payment/thanks). The Grade 5 Avramenko p. 7 buyer-seller dialogue models the exact structure.
- **Contrastive pairs**: у магазині / на ринку (preposition switch); Є? (availability) / Скільки коштує? (price) — two questions that go together in every transaction; кілограм яблук (2–4 = Gen.sg.) / п'ять кілограмів (5+ = Gen.pl.).
- **Дайте, будь ласка note**: The plan explicitly includes imperative shopping phrases as grammar. Treat **Дайте, будь ласка** as a frozen shopping formula — teach it as a chunk, not as a productive imperative rule. Do NOT teach imperative conjugation patterns (that belongs to M47). One fixed phrase, learned as vocabulary.

## Notes for Content Writing

- купити (perfective) is plan-sanctioned — present it as the "shopping verb" for a single completed purchase. No need to contrast aspect deeply; just show "Я хочу купити хліб" as a natural pattern.
- платити карткою: mention it as a cultural note (Ukrainians pay by card widely) but frame as a frozen phrase — do not teach Instrumental case.
- The plan's Практика section (250w) should contain at least one market dialogue and one store dialogue — different prepositions, slightly different register.
- Word budget is tight at 1200. Keep hygiene section lean (it's vocabulary-listing, not grammar-heavy).
