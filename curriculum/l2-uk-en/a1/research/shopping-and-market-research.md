# Дослідження: Shopping and Market

## State Standard Reference
§3.8: "Купівля (закупи): магазини і ринки; споживчі товари (овочі, фрукти, м’ясо і молочні продукти, хліб, яйця); засоби гігієни (мило, шампунь, зубна паста); одиниці ваги й об’єму; гроші."
Alignment: This module directly fulfills the thematic requirements of Catalogue B (3.8) for A1 by covering stores, hygiene products, units of measurement, and currency. Note: While "одиниці ваги й об'єму" is required here, the formal Genitive case is an A2 requirement (§4.2.2.2). Therefore, Genitive forms for quantities (води, цукру) should be taught formulaically as fixed vocabulary chunks.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Hryvnia's Roots**: The currency name «гривня» (hryvnia) dates back to Kyivan Rus' in the 11th century, originally referring to a measure of weight or a valuable necklace. Emphasizing this ancient, independent origin supports a decolonized framing.
2. **Ринок vs Магазин**: The traditional «ринок» (market), such as the historic Zhytniy Market in Kyiv, remains a vibrant part of Ukrainian culture where personal interaction, haggling, and buying fresh, home-grown produce («домашнє») are prioritized over sterile supermarkets.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Number-Gender Disagreement with Currency**: Using "один гривня" instead of "одна гривня". Hryvnia is feminine, so it strictly requires "одна", "дві".
2. **"У вас є...?" vs "Ви маєте...?"**: Learners often default to literal translations from English like "Ви маєте...?" when checking store availability. The existential "У вас є...?" or simply "Є...?" is much more natural and common in Ukrainian shops.
3. **Overcomplicating the Genitive**: Trying to apply full case endings instead of learning formulaic phrases for A1 (e.g., getting confused by "пляшка води" vs "пачка цукру").

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Introduce Ukrainian markets and currency as self-standing cultural phenomena. Avoid comparisons to rubles or Soviet-era shopping habits. Let Ukrainian stand on its own.
- **Pedagogy**: Keep the Genitive case strictly lexical. Do not explain the Genitive singular/plural paradigms here. Provide the specific phrases (кілограм яблук, літр молока) and have learners practice them as fixed units.
- **English Scaffolding**: Since this is A1, use English to explain the "Скільки коштує" structure and the concept of formulaic quantity phrases, but keep the shopping dialogues in Ukrainian with English translations.
