# Дослідження: Greetings and Politeness

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.1.4: "Personal pronouns — basic declension" 
Alignment: This module introduces essential daily communication formulas and the foundational cultural concept of the T-V distinction (Ти/Ви) prior to formal pronoun declension. It relies on memorized chunks for introductory phrases.

## Cultural Hooks
1. The T-V distinction (Ти vs Ви) is strictly observed in Ukraine. Using "Ти" with a stranger or elder is considered disrespectful. "Добрий день" and "Ви" is the safest default.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Translating "My name is" literally → Мене звати... (Correct form). English speakers often try to construct "Моє ім'я є," which sounds unnatural.
2. Overusing "Привіт" → "Добрий день" (Correct for strangers). Learners often use informal greetings in formal situations.
3. Looking for a separate word for "you're welcome" → Будь ласка (Correct form). It functions as both "please" and "you're welcome."

## Notes for Content Writing
- Keep the explanation of "Ти і Ви" simple and focused on social distance rather than grammatical plural/singular forms, since verb conjugation is not yet taught.
- Emphasize the pairing: "Привіт" goes with "Ти", "Добрий день" goes with "Ви".
- Highlight the dual use of "Будь ласка" (both "please" and "you're welcome").
- Treat phrases like "Мене звати" and "Як вас звати" as fixed lexical chunks, avoiding explanations of case endings at this stage.
