## Cultural Hooks

1. **The «Ви» safety net** — Ukrainian maintains a strict T-V distinction (ти/Ви) absent in English. Starting with Ви for any unknown adult is non-negotiable. The social ritual of shifting to ти — called «Давай на ти?» — is a meaningful social milestone, often accompanied by a toast (Bruderschaft). This is a real, everyday moment every learner will encounter in Ukraine.
2. **Identity riddle (загадка)** — Folk riddles of the «Хто це?» / «Що це?» pattern appear in Ukrainian children's tradition. Example: *«Хто це? — Мій друг. Що це? — Мій підручник.»* The very structure of this module's grammar echoes the folk tradition of naming and identifying.
3. **«Мене звати...»** — Ukrainian uses a reflexive-adjacent naming formula where English uses «My name is». This is a lived Ukrainian reality: at every formal introduction, in schools and offices, the formula «Мене звати [Name]» is expected. Contrasting it with the English structure grounds the grammar in real life.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **The Phantom «є»** → *\*Я є студент* instead of «Я студент» — Caused by L1 transfer from English "I am a student." The verb «є» exists in Ukrainian but is archaic/emphatic; modern identification drops it entirely. Plan labels this the "Phantom Is" error.
2. **The «It» Trap** → *\*Воно — стіл* instead of «Він — стіл» — English uses "it" for all inanimate objects; Ukrainian assigns grammatical gender. Learners from a1-07 know gender for nouns, but applying він/вона as pronouns for inanimates is a new step.
3. **Register collapse** → *\*Ти вчитель?* to an unknown teacher — English has no T-V distinction, so learners default to informal. Must be drilled explicitly: any adult stranger = Ви until invited otherwise.
4. **Article insertion** → *\*Я a студент* — English article "a" has no equivalent in Ukrainian. Learners instinctively want to insert something between pronoun and noun predicate. The «Ø» visualization from the plan is the correct corrective tool.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show the learner a "passport card" with a name, photo, and role label: *«Це Ірина. Ірина — вчителька. Вона вчителька.»* Then show a second card: *«Це Тарас. Тарас — студент. Він студент.»* Then ask: where is "is"? Learners spot the gap themselves before the rule is named. This mirrors the Grade 5 textbook exercise (Голуб, p. 173): "Я — Софія Амундсен. Ти — це я." — a literary text that uses exactly the zero copula pattern.
- **Best context**: First-day classroom introductions, passport control (the plan's «Passport Officer» persona is perfect), or name tags at a conference. These force the Ви register and the identity statement structure simultaneously.
- **Contrastive pairs**: English "I **am** a student" vs. Ukrainian «Я **Ø** студент» (use Ø symbol visually); «ти» vs. «Ви» (informal/formal); «він» (masculine noun) vs. «вона» (feminine noun) vs. «воно» (neuter noun — limited use).

## Notes for Content Writing

- Module is pre-verbal: no conjugated verbs in Ukrainian examples, no imperatives. All Ukrainian appears in structural containers (tables, blockquote dialogues, pattern boxes) per A1 immersion rules.
- Section headers must be bilingual: «Хто це? Що це? — Who Is This? What Is This?» etc. (module 9, pre-M15 rule applies).
- The Ø / — dash visualization for zero copula should appear as a visual table or pattern box, not inline prose.
- Gender pair nouns (студент/студентка, українець/українка, вчитель/вчителька) should be introduced as pairs in a table — this reinforces a1-07 gender knowledge.
- Keep «воно» minimal — acknowledge it exists for neuter nouns, but don't drill it heavily here; that belongs to a1-10.
- «Мене звати...» is §4.2.3.1-adjacent (State Standard), include as a bonus formula with a note it's distinct from zero copula but equally foundational.
