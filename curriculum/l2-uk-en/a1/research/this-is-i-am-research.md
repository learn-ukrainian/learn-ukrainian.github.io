## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Bruderschaft Ritual**: The transition from formal «Ви» to informal «ти» is marked by a traditional "brotherhood" toast (Bruderschaft). In modern times, a simple verbal agreement («Перейдімо на „ти“?») is more common, but the cultural weight remains.
2. **The Rebuff**: "Ми з вами на брудершафт не пили!" is a classic cultural phrase used to put someone in their place if they use «ти» uninvited, reinforcing the strict boundary between registers.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The Phantom 'Is'** → *Я є студент (Incorrect) vs. Я студент (Correct) — English interference causes learners to insert 'є' where Ukrainian efficiently uses a direct subject-predicate link (zero copula).
2. **The 'It' Trap** → *Стіл - воно (Incorrect) vs. Стіл - він (Correct) — English speakers use "it" for all objects, forgetting that Ukrainian maps pronouns strictly to grammatical gender.
3. **Register Mix-up** → Using «ти» with officials, elders, or strangers due to the lack of a T-V distinction in modern English.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Present the zero copula as a feature of Ukrainian efficiency, not as something "missing". Do not reference Russian grammatical similarities. 
- **Immersion**: This is an A1 module. English scaffolding is MANDATORY for explaining grammatical concepts like the zero copula and T-V distinction, followed by clear Ukrainian examples.
- **IPA**: Remember to include IPA only on the *first occurrence* of new vocabulary words (я, ти, він, студент, etc.).
