## Cultural Hooks

1. **Hospitality Triad**: In Ukrainian culture, hosting guests means offering їжу (food), напої (drinks), and розмову (conversation). Refusing to eat what a host prepares — "я не їм" — can carry an unintended social message of rejection. The verbs їсти, пити, говорити are not just high-frequency words; they are the grammar of belonging.

2. **Etymology of любити**: The root *lewdh- connects любити (to love) → люди (people) → людство (humanity). This is a concrete, memorable etymological hook: to love is literally "to be drawn toward people." Useful as a cultural-linguistic aside in the production section.

3. **Прислів'я about speaking**: "Говори мало, слухай багато, думай ще більше." — common Ukrainian folk saying that introduces говорити in a memorable cultural frame. All words are within A1 scope except більше (comparative), which can be glossed.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Conjugation mixing** — applying 1st conj. endings to 2nd conj. verbs: *ти робеш* → **ти робиш**. Cause: -ити verbs also have infinitive -ти like 1st conj. verbs; learners over-generalize.
2. **Mutation omission in я-form** — *я ходю* / *я сидю* → **я ходжу** / **я сиджу**. Cause: mutation feels arbitrary without phonetic framing; the д→дж / с→ш / т→ч pattern needs explicit naming.
3. **Irregular їсти** — *я їджу* / *я їстю* → **я їм**. Cause: learners assume regular -ити endings apply; їсти is a suppletive outlier requiring rote memorization.
4. **бачити vs дивитися confusion** — using *я бачу* to mean "I am watching" → **я дивлюся**. Cause: English "see/watch" distinction doesn't map cleanly; bачити = involuntary/faculty, дивитися = deliberate action.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Present a short scene without explanation — a table at a Ukrainian kitchen: "Бабуся готує. Онук сидить. Мама говорить. Тато їсть борщ. Дід п'є чай." Ask the learner: what are the endings on all these verbs? What vowel do you see? The learner notices -ить, -ить, -ить, -ть — a pattern emerges before the rule is named. Then contrast with First Conjugation: читає, пише, знає — different vowel. This two-column contrast (е/є vs и/і) is the discovery anchor.
- **Best context**: A Ukrainian kitchen/meal scene (hospitality triad: їсти, пити, говорити) is the most natural and culturally resonant frame. Supports практика section activities.
- **Contrastive pairs**: говорити / читати (2nd vs 1st, same -ти infinitive); бачити / дивитися (see vs watch, state vs process); я люблю / я любиш (mutation vs no-mutation form).

## Notes for Content Writing
- Labial mutation (люблю, роблю) should be introduced as a **phonetic phenomenon** — "your lips need a buffer before ю" — not just a spelling rule.
- їсти paradigm must appear in a dedicated box; do not bury it in running text.
- стояти ending вони стоять is an irregular-looking form worth noting as a pattern (стояти, кричати belong to -а stem group, but their endings are identical to -ити group for most persons).
- Keep mutation inventory manageable: focus on д→дж (ходжу, сиджу), с→ш (прошу), т→ч (плачу), labials +л (люблю, роблю). Do not introduce б→бл, п→пл, в→вл separately — treat as "labials all get +л".
