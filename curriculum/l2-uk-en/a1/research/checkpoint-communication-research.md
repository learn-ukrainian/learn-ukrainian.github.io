## Cultural Hooks
1. **Directness vs. Politeness**: In Ukrainian, using the imperative form with "будь ласка" (e.g., "Дайте, будь ласка") is the standard, natural way to make polite requests. Over-relying on questions like "Could you give me..." sounds unnecessarily formal or foreign.
2. **Skill vs. Circumstance**: Ukrainian culture makes a sharp distinction between learned skills ("вміти", e.g., speaking a language, swimming) and circumstantial ability or permission ("могти", e.g., having the time or physical capacity to do it right now). 

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Confusing вміти / могти**: "Я не можу читати українською" (incorrect for "I don't know how to read") → "Я не вмію читати українською" (correct).
2. **Impersonal verbs with Nominative**: Using "треба" with the nominative pronoun ("Я треба") instead of the Dative experiencer ("Мені треба").
3. **Overusing повинен**: Relying on "повинен" for daily necessities (translating "must") instead of the much more natural and common "треба" / "потрібно".

## Notes for Content Writing
- Highlight the Dative requirement for "треба" to prevent early fossilization of "Я треба".
- Frame Ukrainian imperatives as naturally polite and direct. Avoid comparing them negatively to English indirect requests.
- Ensure all example sentences and vocabulary avoid Russianisms (no "кушати", "получати").
