## Cultural Hooks
1. **Public Transport Etiquette**: In Ukraine, offering your seat to elders, pregnant women, or children is a strict social norm, making the imperative «Сідайте, будь ласка!» a crucial real-world survival phrase.
2. **Guest Culture**: When invited somewhere («Ходімо до нас»), it is a strong tradition to bring a small token (like sweets or cake), combining invitation structures with social expectations.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Я треба" → "Мені треба" — English speakers often default to Nominative subjects for necessity instead of the required Dative case.
2. Translating "can" indiscriminately → Failing to distinguish "могти" (circumstantial ability) and "вміти" (learned skill, know-how).
3. Overusing "повинен" → Learners often translate "must" directly to the strong personal obligation "повинен", when native speakers use the softer, impersonal "треба" for everyday necessities.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Present Ukrainian impersonal constructions (`мені треба`) as an independent, elegant feature of the language, avoiding any reference to Russian grammar.
- Scaffolding: Use English to explain the conceptual differences between modals (ability vs. learned skill), but keep dialogues and examples fully in Ukrainian. Ensure strict separation.
- Limit IPA: Provide IPA only for the first occurrence of newly reviewed vocabulary items, avoiding IPA for full sentences.
