## Cultural Hooks

1. **"Дайте, будь ласка"** — In Ukrainian shops and cafés, this imperative + будь ласка is the completely normal, polite way to order. English speakers often hesitate to use imperative forms thinking they sound rude; in Ukrainian, this phrasing is courteous and expected.
2. **Ходімо! / Давай зустрінемось!** — Two standard Ukrainian invitation patterns with different registers: *Ходімо!* (Let's go — spontaneous, immediate) vs *Давай зустрінемось* (Let's meet — scheduled, planning ahead). Both are high-frequency in everyday speech.
3. **Weekend поїздки** — Short weekend trips (до Карпат, на озеро, у місто) are a common leisure activity referenced in Ukrainian school textbooks and everyday conversation. The integration task scenario (планувати поїздку) mirrors a real social script learners will encounter.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **могти vs вміти conflation** → Use могти for circumstance ("I can come today"), вміти for skill ("I can swim") — English "can" covers both; learners default to могти for everything.
2. **треба + verb (NOT pronoun + треба)** → Мені треба купити ❌ *Я треба купити* — the impersonal construction drops the personal subject pronoun; English "I need to buy" misleads learners into inserting я before треба as a subject.
3. **Ви-form imperative ending** → Читайте / Скажіть ❌ *Читайтеся / Скажіте* — learners add wrong endings or omit -те pluralization entirely.
4. **хотіти stem change** → хочу/хочеш ❌ *хотю/хотиш* — the ти-form хотіти → хоч- stem is irregular and often misspelled from analogy with regular -ити verbs.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise (TTT)**: Open with a mini-diagnostic. Show 6 sentences using all A1.5 modals and 3 imperatives in a travel-planning dialogue. Ask the learner: "Which sentences express ability? Which express necessity? Which are commands?" This activates prior knowledge before any review content appears.
- **Best context**: Weekend trip planning — "You and a friend are deciding what to do on Saturday." This scenario naturally requires all target forms: *Хочеш поїхати до Львова? Треба купити квитки. Подивись розклад! Я вмію водити машину.*
- **Contrastive pairs**:
  - *Я можу прийти* (I'm able to come / circumstances allow) vs *Я вмію плавати* (I know how to swim / learned skill)
  - *Дай* (ти-form, casual) vs *Дайте* (Ви-form, formal/plural)
  - *Треба йти* (impersonal necessity) vs *Я повинен іти* (personal obligation)

## Notes for Content Writing
- This is a **checkpoint** with TTT pedagogy — open each skill section with a short diagnostic, then reinforce gaps, don't re-teach from scratch.
- The plan's integration task scenario is the anchor — reference it early in the overview so learners know the goal.
- Imperative module (M47) is just 7 modules back; imperative recall should be strong. Focus diagnostic effort on modal discrimination (могти/вміти/треба/повинен) which is the hardest A1.5 area.
- A1 grammar rule: no Instrumental case explanation for транспортом — present the pattern (їхати автобусом) as a fixed phrase at A1 level.
