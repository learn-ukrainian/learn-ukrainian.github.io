# Дослідження: My World: Objects

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.2: "Pronoun gender and number forms" (A1, lines 659–667)
Alignment: This module operationalises §4.2.2 by teaching the four-way gender/number paradigm of цей/ця/це/ці and той/та/те/ті as applied to nominative-case household nouns. Gender agreement with demonstratives is the core competency tested here.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Прислів'я**: «В гостях добре, а вдома краще» — exact Ukrainian equivalent of "East or West, home is best." Introduces **дім** as the module's thematic anchor without any verb required (bare proverb form).
2. **Покуття** (verified in VESUM: `noun:inanim:n`) — the sacred "red corner" of a traditional Ukrainian хата where icons and rushnyky (embroidered cloths) were displayed. Even modern apartments often retain a decorative corner echoing this tradition. Useful for discussing object vocabulary in a culturally grounded room layout.
3. **хата / квартира / дім** — three genuinely distinct words for dwelling in Ukrainian. хата = rural/traditional cottage (high cultural resonance); квартира = urban apartment (everyday modern); дім = house or home (general/affective). English speakers map all three to "house/home" and need explicit contrast.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Gender mismatch on feminine nouns ending -а**: *цей книга* → **ця книга** — English has no grammatical gender, so learners default to цей for everything. Textbook exercise model: Grade 2 Bolshakova p. 43 uses minimal-pair adjective agreement with двері/вікно/стіна — same agreement logic, different target.
2. **Identification vs. Specification confusion**: *Це стіл* (This is a table — predicate) vs. *Цей стіл* (This table — attributive). English "this" covers both; Ukrainian splits them across two structures. Must be explicitly contrasted.
3. **двері treated as singular**: *цей двері* or *це двері* → **ці двері** — двері is a pluralia tantum (plural-only noun). Learners must memorise it with its fixed form ці двері as a lexical chunk rather than applying gender agreement rules.
4. **Proximity confusion**: Using цей for distant objects and той for near ones — the opposite of the intended system. A visual mnemonic ("T for There/That") resolves this quickly.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present a hand-drawn or illustrated Ukrainian кімната with 6 objects labelled (стіл, книга, вікно, шафа, лампа, стілець). Ask the learner to sort them into three columns by their цей/ця/це ending — before any rule is given. The rhyme between demonstrative ending and noun ending (-а/ця, -о/це, consonant/цей) should emerge from sorting. Model: Bolshakova Grade 2, p. 43 — adjective-noun agreement drill using дах, двері, вікно, стіна — identical discovery logic.
- **Best context**: Interior design / home tour — "Point to the near object, point to the far object." Natural proximity contrast without verbs: *Це стіл. Цей стіл. Той стіл.*
- **Contrastive pairs**: (1) *Це стіл* vs. *Цей стіл* — identification vs. specification; (2) *цей стілець* / *ця лампа* / *це ліжко* — masc/fem/neut minimal set; (3) *цей стіл* / *той стіл* — proximity near/far.

## Notes for Content Writing

- Module is pre-verbal (M10), so ALL Ukrainian instruction language must be English. "Look at the room" not «Дивіться на кімнату».
- The rhyme association цЯ книгА / цЕ вікнО / цЕЙ стіл is a reliable mnemonic — emphasise ending sounds in examples.
- двері must be introduced as a frozen chunk: always «ці двері», no gender analysis.
- Keep all Ukrainian sentences ≤ 10 words; nominative only; no prepositions needed at this stage.
- Use blockquote containers for Ukrainian example dialogues: «Що це? — Це книга. — Ця книга? — Так, ця книга.»
