# Дослідження: Pronoun Declension

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.1.4: "Personal pronouns — basic declension" (lines 656–657)
Alignment: This module delivers the core personal pronoun oblique paradigm (Acc, Loc, Dat, Gen) required by §4.2.1.4. Dative forms (мені, тобі, йому/їй, нам, вам, їм) are introduced as chunk-based preview per scope_note — full Dative paradigm with nouns deferred to A2. The н-prefix rule for 3rd-person pronouns after prepositions is the key A1-level distinction not covered elsewhere.

## Vocabulary Frequency

| Form | Case(s) | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|---------|-------|------------------|
| мене | Acc / Gen | Most frequent oblique 1sg form; homophonous across Acc + Gen | бачить мене, для мене, у мене є, без мене |
| тебе | Acc / Gen | 2sg; Acc = Gen (same form) | знаю тебе, без тебе, у тебе є |
| його | Acc / Gen (no prep) | Bare form, NO н-; н-prefix form нього used after prepositions | бачу його / у нього є |
| її | Acc / Gen (no prep) | Bare form; after prepositions → неї | знаю її / у неї є |
| нас | Acc / Gen / Loc | Plural 1p; same form in three cases | бачить нас, для нас, у нас є |
| вас | Acc / Gen / Loc | Plural 2p; also formal singular (Вас) | бачить вас, для вас, у вас є |
| мені | Dat / Loc | Already known from "Мені подобається" (M19) — now systematized | мені подобається, на мені, дай мені |
| тобі | Dat / Loc | Mirrors мені pattern | тобі подобається, на тобі, скажи тобі |
| йому | Dat | 3sg masc.; н-prefix in Loc: на ньому | скажи йому, дай йому |
| їй | Dat | 3sg fem.; н-prefix in Loc: на ній | напиши їй, дай їй |
| нам | Dat | 1pl Dative | дай нам, скажи нам |
| ньому | Loc | н-prefix obligatory after preposition: на ньому, у ньому | на ньому, у ньому |
| нього | Gen / Acc (after prep) | н-prefix form of його | у нього, для нього, без нього |
| них | Loc / Gen / Acc (after prep) | н-prefix plural 3p | на них, для них, у них |
| їм | Dat | 3pl Dative | скажи їм, дай їм |

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Everyday hospitality phrase**: "У нас є кава, чай, і вода" — the construction **у + Gen pronoun + є** is how Ukrainians signal what they have available. Learners encounter this immediately at a café or host's home. Grade 6 Avramenko (p. 91) models у-constructions with nouns; this module extends them to pronouns.
2. **Text/message conventions**: Ukrainian SMS culture uses short imperative + pronoun patterns: "Напиши мені", "Зателефонуй мені", "Дай мені знати" — all Dative. Learners already want to send these messages; the pronoun forms unlock them.
3. **Folk rhyme pattern**: Children's counting rhymes exploit pronoun pairs (я / мене, ти / тебе) — the contrast between Nominative subject and oblique object is made audible before it is explained. A simple pair: *"Я тебе бачу — ти мене бачиш"* is structurally clean and memorable.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **н-prefix confusion: його → \*його (after preposition)** → correct: **нього**. Why: English has no such rule; learners treat pronouns as invariant. Grade 6 Zabotnyi (p. 94) targets this with preposition–noun placement; the same confusion transfers to pronouns. Contrastive drilling: *його книга* (no prep) vs *у нього є* (after prep).
2. **Using Nominative where Accusative is required**: "Я бачу \*він" → "Я бачу **його**". Happens because English "I see him" feels like a different word class than "He sees me." The Acc/Gen homophony (мене = тебе's both Acc and Gen) adds confusion.
3. **Conflating Dative мені with Nominative я**: Learners produce "Мені є студент" (calque of "I am a student") instead of "Я студент." The real mені construction is "Мені подобається" — emphasize the **non-subject** role of Dative pronouns.
4. **Dropping н- in complex preposition phrases**: "для \*його" → "для **нього**." Textbook exercises (Grade 5, Litvinova p. 213–214) show noun forms in prepositional structures; the н-prefix for pronoun 3rd person requires its own explicit attention.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show a dialogue in a block-quote box:
  > "Де Олег?" / "Я не знаю **його**."
  > "Ти бачив **її**?" / "Так, я бачив **її** вчора."
  > "Вони чекають **на нас**?"
  Then: "What happened to він/вона/ми when they followed a verb or preposition?" Learners notice the form changed — this is the hook before the rule. Mirrors Grade 5 Litvinova (p. 213) contrastive sentence pairs: Катерина запросила подругу / Подруга запросила Катерину.

- **Best context**: Phone/chat messages — "Напиши мені", "Я чекаю на тебе", "У нього є квиток?" These are immediately useful sentences that learners want to produce. The messaging scenario makes Dative feel functional, not abstract.

- **Contrastive pairs** (display as table in module):
  | Nominative (subject) | Accusative (object) | After preposition |
  |---|---|---|
  | він | його | у/для **нього** |
  | вона | її | у/для **неї** |
  | вони | їх | у/для **них** |

  The н-prefix column is the key "aha" moment — make it visually prominent.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The scope_note in the plan is critical: Dative forms are **chunk-based preview**, not systematic case instruction. Don't introduce new Dative-taking verbs beyond давати, говорити, писати, допомагати listed in the plan.
- Acc = Gen homophony (мене, тебе, нас, вас) is pedagogically useful — mention it explicitly to reduce confusion, not add it. Framing: "good news — you already know this form."
- Locative pronouns (мені, тобі, ньому, ній, нас/нам confusion) are tricky — use physical location examples (Він сидить на **мені**? Що на **тобі** зараз?) to anchor Loc vs Dat.
- Keep н-prefix as the ONE structural rule that gets repeated emphasis. Everything else is paradigm memorization helped by tables.
