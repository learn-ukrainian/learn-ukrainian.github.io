# –Я–µ–і–∞–≥–Њ–≥—Ц–Ї–∞ A1: What Time



## –Ь–µ—В–Њ–і–Є—З–љ–Є–є –њ—Ц–і—Е—Ц–і (Methodological Approach)

The native pedagogical approach to teaching time in Ukrainian is rooted in distinguishing between *identity* and *sequence*. This is immediately visible in the core questions taught to first and second graders (Source: `2-klas-ukrmova-vashulenko-2019-1_s0089`, `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-ponomarova-2021-1_s0082`).

1.  **Question for Time Identity: `–Ъ–Њ—В—А–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞?`**
    *   This translates to "Which hour is it?" and conceptually treats the hours on a clock as items in an ordered set. The answer requires a **feminine ordinal numeral** (`–њ–µ—А—И–∞`, `–і—А—Г–≥–∞`, `—В—А–µ—В—П`). This is the foundational concept (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-236`, `ext-other_blogs-42`). Ukrainian pedagogy emphasizes that `–≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞` is a feminine noun, so the ordinal number must agree with it (Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-golub-2023_s0167`).

2.  **Question for Events: `–Ю –Ї–Њ—В—А—Ц–є –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ—Ц?`**
    *   This means "At what time?" and is used for scheduling. The answer requires the preposition **`–Њ`** (or `–Њ–±` before a vowel) followed by the **locative case** of the feminine ordinal numeral (`–Њ –њ–µ—А—И—Ц–є`, `–Њ –і—А—Г–≥—Ц–є`, `–Њ–± –Њ–і–Є–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—Ц–є`) (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-236`, `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-zaharijchuk-2021-1_s0084`).

3.  **Question for Quantity (Minutes): `–°–Ї—Ц–ї—М–Ї–Є —Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ?`**
    *   Minutes are treated as a simple quantity, not a sequence. Therefore, they use **cardinal numerals** (`–њ'—П—В—М`, `–і–µ—Б—П—В—М`, `–і–≤–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М`) (Source: `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0057`, `6-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2023_s0180`). This distinction between ordinal hours and cardinal minutes is a critical pedagogical point.

Ukrainian textbooks for young native speakers break down the hour into halves and quarters, introducing colloquial phrases early on. The models are presented visually with clocks and tables, showing multiple correct ways to express the same time (Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2023_s0252`, `2-klas-ukrmova-vashulenko-2019-1_s0089`). This multi-option approach (e.g., `—И–Њ—Б—В–∞ —Б–Њ—А–Њ–Ї`, `–Ј–∞ –і–≤–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М —Б—М–Њ–Љ–∞`, `–і–≤–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М –і–Њ —Б—М–Њ–Љ–Њ—Ч`) is standard and should be taught to L2 learners to equip them for real-world conversation (Source: `5-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2022_s0197`).

## –Я–Њ—Б–ї—Ц–і–Њ–≤–љ—Ц—Б—В—М –≤–≤–µ–і–µ–љ–љ—П (Introduction Sequence)

This sequence progresses from the simplest structures to more complex colloquial forms, mirroring the logic in Ukrainian school materials.

1.  **Step 1: The Core Question & Full Hours**
    *   **Concept:** Asking "What time is it?" and answering for times exactly on the hour.
    *   **Question:** `–Ъ–Њ—В—А–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞?` (Source: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-zaharijchuk-2021-1_s0083`)
    *   **Answer Structure:** Ordinal Numeral (Feminine, Nominative) + `–≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞`.
    *   **Examples:** `–Я–µ—А—И–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞.` (1:00), `–°—М–Њ–Љ–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞.` (7:00), `–Ф–≤–∞–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞.` (12:00) (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-236`).
    *   **Why:** This establishes the core principle of using ordinal numbers for hours and ensures correct gender agreement from the start.

2.  **Step 2: Scheduling Events on the Hour**
    *   **Concept:** Stating when an event happens.
    *   **Question:** `–Ю –Ї–Њ—В—А—Ц–є –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ—Ц?` (Source: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-ponomarova-2021-1_s0082`)
    *   **Answer Structure:** `–Ю` + Ordinal Numeral (Feminine, Locative).
    *   **Examples:** `–£—А–Њ–Ї –њ–Њ—З–Є–љ–∞—Ф—В—М—Б—П –Њ –і–µ–≤'—П—В—Ц–є –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ—Ц.` (9:00), `–Ч—Г—Б—В—А—Ц–љ–µ–Љ–Њ—Б—М –Њ —В—А–µ—В—Ц–є.` (3:00) (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-236`).
    *   **Why:** Introduces the locative case in a high-frequency, practical context. The preposition `–Њ` is fundamental for scheduling.

3.  **Step 3: The Half-Hour (`–њ—Ц–≤ –љ–∞ ...`)**
    *   **Concept:** Expressing "__:30". This is the most common and idiomatic way.
    *   **Structure:** `–њ—Ц–≤ –љ–∞` + Ordinal Numeral (Feminine, **Accusative** case, which looks like Nominative for this form).
    *   **Examples:** `–њ—Ц–≤ –љ–∞ —Б—М–Њ–Љ—Г` (6:30, literally "half towards the seventh"), `–њ—Ц–≤ –љ–∞ –і–≤–∞–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—Г` (11:30) (Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0164`, `6-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2023_s0252`).
    *   **Why:** This is a fixed, highly frequent chunk. Teaching it as a single unit is more effective than deconstructing its grammar at A1. It logically follows full hours.

4.  **Step 4: Minutes Past the Hour (First Half)**
    *   **Concept:** Expressing minutes from 1 to 29.
    *   **Structure 1 (Official):** Hour (Ordinal) + `–≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞` + Minutes (Cardinal) + `—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ`.
        *   Example: `–°—М–Њ–Љ–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞ –њвАЩ—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М —Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ.` (7:15) (Source: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-ponomarova-2021-1_s0083`).
    *   **Structure 2 (Colloquial `... –њ–Њ ...`):** Minutes (Cardinal) + `(—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ) –њ–Њ` + Hour (Ordinal, **Locative**).
        *   Example: `–Я'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М (—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ) –њ–Њ —Б—М–Њ–Љ—Ц–є.` (7:15) (Source: `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0047`).
    *   **Structure 3 (Colloquial `... –љ–∞ ...`):** Minutes (Cardinal) + `(—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ) –љ–∞` + Next Hour (Ordinal, **Accusative**).
        *   Example: `–Я'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М (—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ) –љ–∞ –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ—Г.` (7:15, literally "15 minutes onto the eighth hour") (Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0164`).
    *   **Why:** Introduce the official form first for clarity, then the common colloquial variants. The concept of "quarter" (`—З–≤–µ—А—В—М`) can be introduced here as a substitute for `–њ'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М —Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ` (e.g., `—З–≤–µ—А—В—М –њ–Њ —Б—М–Њ–Љ—Ц–є`, `—З–≤–µ—А—В—М –љ–∞ –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ—Г`) (Source: `2-klas-ukrmova-vashulenko-2019-1_s0089`).

5.  **Step 5: Minutes To the Hour (Second Half)**
    *   **Concept:** Expressing minutes from 31 to 59.
    *   **Structure 1 (Official):** Hour (Ordinal) + `–≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞` + Minutes (Cardinal) + `—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ`.
        *   Example: `–°—М–Њ–Љ–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞ —Б–Њ—А–Њ–Ї –њвАЩ—П—В—М —Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ.` (7:45) (Source: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-ponomarova-2021-1_s0083`).
    *   **Structure 2 (Colloquial `–Ј–∞ ...`):** `–Ј–∞` + Minutes Missing (Cardinal) + `(—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ)` + Next Hour (Ordinal, **Nominative**).
        *   Example: `–Ч–∞ –њ'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ–∞.` (7:45, literally "in 15 minutes, it's the eighth") (Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0164`).
    *   **Structure 3 (Colloquial `... –і–Њ ...`):** Minutes Missing (Cardinal) + `(—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ) –і–Њ` + Next Hour (Ordinal, **Genitive**).
        *   Example: `–Я'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М (—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ) –і–Њ –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ–Њ—Ч.` (7:45) (Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2023_s0252`).
    *   **Why:** This is often the most confusing part for learners. Teaching `–Ј–∞ ...` first is often easier as the hour remains in the nominative case. `... –і–Њ ...` requires the genitive, adding complexity. Again, `—З–≤–µ—А—В—М` can be used here (`–Ј–∞ —З–≤–µ—А—В—М –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ–∞`) (Source: `12-klas-ukrmova-vashulenko-2019-1_s0089`).

## –Ф–µ–Ї–Њ–ї–Њ–љ—Ц–Ј–∞—Ж—Ц–є–љ—Ц –Ј–∞—Б—В–µ—А–µ–ґ–µ–љ–љ—П (Decolonization Notes)

This topic is a critical area for decolonization in language teaching, as Russian-influenced forms are common among non-native speakers and even some legacy dialects.

1.  **Forbid the Preposition `–С–µ–Ј`:** The construction `*–±–µ–Ј –і–µ—Б—П—В–Є —Б—Ц–Љ` (for 6:50) is the single most common Russianism in this topic. It must be explicitly marked as incorrect and foreign to the Ukrainian grammatical system. The teacher must insist on the native forms: `–Ј–∞ –і–µ—Б—П—В—М —Б—М–Њ–Љ–∞` or `–і–µ—Б—П—В—М (—Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ) –і–Њ —Б—М–Њ–Љ–Њ—Ч` (Source: `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0060`, `5-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2022_s0199`). Do not present it as a "colloquial" or "acceptable" alternative; it is a grammatical error stemming from another language.

2.  **Reinforce `–Ъ–Њ—В—А–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞?`:** The standard question is `–Ъ–Њ—В—А–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞?`. While a learner might be understood asking `–°–Ї—Ц–ї—М–Ї–Є –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ?` or `–ѓ–Ї–Є–є —З–∞—Б?`, these are not the idiomatic, native questions taught in Ukrainian schools (Source: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-zaharijchuk-2021-1_s0083`). Correcting this from Day 1 establishes a native grammatical foundation and avoids habits from Russian (`—Б–Ї–Њ–ї—М–Ї–Њ –≤—А–µ–Љ–µ–љ–Є?`).

3.  **Teach Forms Holistically:** Ukrainian offers multiple correct ways to state the time (e.g., 8:15 can be `–≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ–∞ –њ'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М`, `–њ'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М –њ–Њ –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ—Ц–є`, or `–њ'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М –љ–∞ –і–µ–≤'—П—В—Г`) (Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0164`). Teach all common native forms. Do not simplify the system by teaching only the "official" format or a single colloquialism, as this impoverishes the learner's fluency and makes them unable to understand native speakers. Avoid presenting one form as "better" than another; they are simply different registers (official vs. conversational).

## –Я—А–Є–Ї–ї–∞–і–Є –Ј –њ—Ц–і—А—Г—З–љ–Є–Ї—Ц–≤ (Textbook Examples)

1.  **Matching Clocks to Written Times (from Ponomarova, Grade 4)**
    *   **Task:** The textbook shows several clock faces. The student must match them to the correct written description.
    *   **Example options:**
        1.  `–°—М–Њ–Љ–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞ –њвАЩ—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М —Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ, –∞–±–Њ —З–≤–µ—А—В—М –љ–∞ –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ—Г.`
        2.  `–°—М–Њ–Љ–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞ —Б–Њ—А–Њ–Ї –њвАЩ—П—В—М —Е–≤–Є–ї–Є–љ, –∞–±–Њ –Ј–∞ —З–≤–µ—А—В—М –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ–∞.`
        3.  `–Ф–µ—Б—П—В–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞.`
    *   **(Source: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-ponomarova-2021-1_s0083`)** This exercise reinforces the equivalence of official and colloquial forms.

2.  **Dialogue Practice (from Ponomarova, Grade 4)**
    *   **Task:** Students work in pairs to ask and answer questions about their daily routine.
    *   **Example questions:**
        *   `–Ю –Ї–Њ—В—А—Ц–є –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ—Ц —В–Є –њ—А–Њ—Б–Є–љ–∞—Ф—И—Б—П –≤ –±—Г–і–љ—Ц?` (At what time do you wake up on weekdays?)
        *   `–Ф–Њ –Ї–Њ—В—А–Њ—Ч –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–Є —В–Є —Б–њ–Є—И —Г –≤–Є—Е—Ц–і–љ—Ц?` (Until what time do you sleep on weekends?)
        *   `–Ъ–Њ—В—А–∞ –Ј–∞—А–∞–Ј –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞?` (What time is it now?)
    *   **(Source: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-ponomarova-2021-1_s0083`)** This grounds the grammar in a practical, communicative context.

3.  **Table Completion: Digital to Words (from Betsa, Grade 6)**
    *   **Task:** Students fill in a table, converting digital time into written Ukrainian for both `–Ъ–Њ—В—А–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞?` and `–Ю –Ї–Њ—В—А—Ц–є –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ—Ц?`.
    *   **Example Row:**
        | –У–Њ–і–Є–љ–Є | –Ъ–Њ—В—А–∞ –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ–∞? | –Ю –Ї–Њ—В—А—Ц–є –≥–Њ–і–Є–љ—Ц? |
        | :--- | :--- | :--- |
        | 07:30 | `–њ—Ц–≤ –љ–∞ –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ—Г` | `–Њ –њ—Ц–≤ –љ–∞ –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ—Г` |
        | 09:15 | `–і–µ–≤'—П—В–∞ –њ'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М` / `—З–≤–µ—А—В—М –њ–Њ –і–µ–≤'—П—В—Ц–є` | `–Њ –і–µ–≤'—П—В—Ц–є –њ'—П—В–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—М` / `–Њ —З–≤–µ—А—В—М –њ–Њ –і–µ–≤'—П—В—Ц–є` |
    *   **(Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0164`)** This exercise systematically drills the different forms and cases required.

4.  **Error Correction (from Litvinova, Grade 6)**
    *   **Task:** The student is given a list of time expressions, some of which are incorrect, and must write the correct versions.
    *   **Example incorrect forms to fix:**
        *   `*–±–µ–Ј —И–µ—Б—В–Є –≤—Ц—Б—Ц–Љ` -> `–Ј–∞ —И—Ц—Б—В—М –≤–Њ—Б—М–Љ–∞`
        *   `*–њ–Њ–ї–Њ–≤–Є–љ–∞ –Њ–і–Є–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В–Є` -> `–њ—Ц–≤ –љ–∞ –Њ–і–Є–љ–∞–і—Ж—П—В—Г`
        *   `*–±—Ц–ї—П —Б—М–Њ–Љ–Њ—Ч` -> `–±–ї–Є–Ј—М–Ї–Њ —Б—М–Њ–Љ–Њ—Ч` or `–Њ —Б—М–Њ–Љ—Ц–є`
    *   **(Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2023_s0253`)** This directly targets common mistakes and reinforces correct usage.

# –Я–µ–і–∞–≥–Њ–≥—Ц–Ї–∞ A1: What Is It Like



## –Ь–µ—В–Њ–і–Є—З–љ–Є–є –њ—Ц–і—Е—Ц–і (Methodological Approach)

The core of teaching descriptive language at the A1 level is to establish the **–њ—А–Є–Ї–Љ–µћБ—В–љ–Є–Ї (adjective)** as a word that answers the questions **—П–Ї–ЄћБ–є? —П–Ї–∞ћБ? —П–Ї–µћБ? —П–Ї—ЦћБ?** (what kind of?) and describes an **–Њ–Ј–љ–∞ћБ–Ї—Г –њ—А–µ–і–Љ–µћБ—В–∞ (an attribute of an object)** (Source `3-klas-ukrainska-mova-vashulenko-2020-1_s0120`, Source `2-klas-ukrmova-kravcova-2019-1_s0075`). The native Ukrainian pedagogy, evident in early grade textbooks, avoids dense grammatical tables. Instead, it builds an intuitive understanding of agreement through question-and-answer pairings.

The primary method is to always present adjectives in tight connection with the noun they modify. Exercises in Grade 2 and 3 textbooks consistently model this relationship (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `2-klas-ukrmova-kravcova-2019-1_s0081`, `3-klas-ukrainska-mova-vashulenko-2020-1_s0123`). For example, a teacher would ask, "–Ю–≥—Ц—А–Њ–Ї (—П–Ї–Є–є?)" and expect the answer "–Ј–µ–ї–µ–љ–Є–є" (Source `2-klas-ukrmova-kravcova-2019-1_s0075`). This constant questioning reinforces the concept of gender and number agreement organically before the formal case system is introduced.

The initial focus is on **—П–Ї—ЦћБ—Б–љ—Ц –њ—А–Є–Ї–Љ–µћБ—В–љ–Є–Ї–Є (qualitative adjectives)**вАФthose describing a quality that can exist in degrees (e.g., big, small, good, bad)вАФas they are the most frequent and intuitive for beginners (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `6-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2020_s0135`). The concept of comparative (`–≤–Є—Й–Є–є —Б—В—Г–њ—Ц–љ—М`) and superlative (`–љ–∞–є–≤–Є—Й–Є–є —Б—В—Г–њ—Ц–љ—М`) is introduced with only the most common, irregular forms (`–Ї—А–∞—Й–Є–є`, `–±—Ц–ї—М—И–Є–є`) at first, mirroring how they appear in natural A1-level conversation (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `ext-ulp_youtube-199`).

## –Я–Њ—Б–ї—Ц–і–Њ–≤–љ—Ц—Б—В—М –≤–≤–µ–і–µ–љ–љ—П (Introduction Sequence)

The introduction of descriptive language must be systematic and build from the concrete to the abstract.

1.  **Step 1: Core Concept & Basic Vocabulary.** Introduce the `–њ—А–Є–Ї–Љ–µ—В–љ–Є–Ї` as a "describing word." Start with a small set of high-frequency, concrete adjectives related to size, quality, and color.
    *   **Size:** `–≤–µ–ї–ЄћБ–Ї–Є–є` (big), `–Љ–∞–ї–ЄћБ–є` (small) (Source `ext-ulp_youtube-251`)
    *   **Quality:** `–≥–∞ћБ—А–љ–Є–є` (good/nice), `–њ–Њ–≥–∞ћБ–љ–Є–є` (bad), `–љ–Њ–≤–ЄћБ–є` (new), `—Б—В–∞—А–ЄћБ–є` (old) (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0034`)
    *   **Color:** `–±—ЦћБ–ї–Є–є` (white), `—З–ЊћБ—А–љ–Є–є` (black), `—З–µ—А–≤–ЊћБ–љ–Є–є` (red), `—Б–ЄћБ–љ—Ц–є` (blue) (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `4-klas-ukrmova-zaharijchuk_s0107`)

2.  **Step 2: Nominative Case Agreement (Gender & Number).** This is the most critical A1 skill. Teach the pattern of endings `-–Є–є, -–∞, -–µ, -—Ц` through examples, not rules.
    *   `–≥–∞—А–љ–Є–є —Б—В—Ц–ї` (masculine)
    *   `–≥–∞—А–љ–∞ —А—Г—З–Ї–∞` (feminine)
    *   `–≥–∞—А–љ–µ –≤—Ц–Ї–љ–Њ` (neuter)
    *   `–≥–∞—А–љ—Ц –Ї–љ–Є–≥–Є` (plural)
    *   This pattern is consistently drilled in early-grade textbooks (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0034`).

3.  **Step 3: Expanding Vocabulary & Simple Phrases.** Introduce adjectives for weather, feelings, and taste. Practice them in simple phrases like `–Ь–µ–љ—Ц –њ–Њ–і–ЊћБ–±–∞—Ф—В—М—Б—П...` or `–¶–µ...`.
    *   **Weather/Temp:** `—Е–Њ–ї–ЊћБ–і–љ–Є–є` (cold), `—В–µћБ–њ–ї–Є–є` (warm)
    *   **Feelings:** `–≤–µ—Б–µћБ–ї–Є–є` (happy), `—Б—Г–Љ–љ–ЄћБ–є` (sad)
    *   **Taste:** `—Б–Љ–∞—З–љ–ЄћБ–є` (tasty), `—Б–Њ–ї–ЊћБ–і–Ї–Є–є` (sweet) (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2023_s0154`)

4.  **Step 4: Introduction to Simple Comparatives.** Introduce only the most essential, suppletive (irregular) forms that are unavoidable in A1 conversation.
    *   `–≥–∞—А–љ–Є–є вЖТ –Ї—А–∞ћБ—Й–Є–є` (good вЖТ better)
    *   `–њ–Њ–≥–∞–љ–Є–є вЖТ –≥—ЦћБ—А—И–Є–є` (bad вЖТ worse)
    *   `–≤–µ–ї–Є–Ї–Є–є вЖТ –±—ЦћБ–ї—М—И–Є–є` (big вЖТ bigger)
    *   `–Љ–∞–ї–Є–є вЖТ –Љ–µћБ–љ—И–Є–є` (small вЖТ smaller)
    *   This is explicitly supported by multiple grammar guides (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `6-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2023_s0206`, `6-klas-ukrmova-golub-2023_s0134`). The form `–±—Ц–ї—М—И/–Љ–µ–љ—И + adjective` should be delayed until A2, as it is a more formal, "bookish" construction (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0022`).

## –Ґ–Є–њ–Њ–≤—Ц –њ–Њ–Љ–Є–ї–Ї–Є L2 (Common L2 Errors)

English-speaking learners often struggle with agreement and transfer habits from English.

| вЭМ –Я–Њ–Љ–Є–ї–Ї–Њ–≤–Њ | вЬЕ –Я—А–∞–≤–Є–ї—М–љ–Њ | –І–Њ–Љ—Г |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `—Ж–µ *–≥–∞—А–љ–Є–є* –Ї–љ–Є–≥–∞` | `—Ж–µ *–≥–∞—А–љ–∞* –Ї–љ–Є–≥–∞` | **Gender Agreement Failure.** English adjectives are invariable. Learners must be drilled to match the adjective's ending to the noun's gender. The question `–Ї–љ–Є–≥–∞ (—П–Ї–∞?)` helps correct this (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0034`). |
| `–Љ—Ц–є *–љ–Њ–≤–Є–є* –і—А—Г–Ј—Ц` | `–Љ–Њ—Ч *–љ–Њ–≤—Ц* –і—А—Г–Ј—Ц` | **Number Agreement Failure.** The learner forgets to make the adjective plural to match the plural noun. The question `–і—А—Г–Ј—Ц (—П–Ї—Ц?)` reinforces the correct form (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `3-klas-ukrainska-mova-vashulenko-2020-1_s0120`). |
| `*—Б–∞–Љ–Є–є –Ї—А–∞—Й–Є–є* –і–µ–љ—М` | `*–љ–∞–є–Ї—А–∞—Й–Є–є* –і–µ–љ—М` | **Russian Calque.** This is a direct translation of the Russian superlative construction (`—Б–∞–Љ—Л–є –ї—Г—З—И–Є–є`). Ukrainian uses the prefix `–љ–∞–є-`. This error is a critical one to correct, as it is a hallmark of Surzhyk. Textbooks for natives explicitly forbid using `—Б–∞–Љ–Є–є` (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0121`, `6-klas-ukrmova-golub-2023_s0134`). |
| `–Т–Њ–љ–∞ —Б–њ—Ц–≤–∞—Ф *–≥–∞—А–љ–Є–є*.` | `–Т–Њ–љ–∞ —Б–њ—Ц–≤–∞—Ф *–≥–∞—А–љ–Њ*.` | **Adjective/Adverb Confusion.** In English, the distinction between "good" (adjective) and "well" (adverb) can be fluid. Ukrainian maintains a strict distinction between `–≥–∞—А–љ–Є–є` (describes a noun) and `–≥–∞—А–љ–Њ` (describes a verb). This must be taught explicitly (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-ponomarova-2021-1_s0118`). |
| `–Т—Ц–љ *–≤–µ–ї–Є–Ї–Є–є* –Ј–∞ –Љ–µ–љ–µ.` | `–Т—Ц–љ *–±—Ц–ї—М—И–Є–є* –Ј–∞ –Љ–µ–љ–µ.` | **Using Base Adjective for Comparison.** English uses "bigger than," not "big than." The learner attempts a literal translation without using the comparative form (`–≤–Є—Й–Є–є —Б—В—Г–њ—Ц–љ—М`). It's crucial to teach that comparisons require a special form (`–±—Ц–ї—М—И–Є–є`, `–Ї—А–∞—Й–Є–є`, etc.) (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0023`). |

## –Ф–µ–Ї–Њ–ї–Њ–љ—Ц–Ј–∞—Ж—Ц–є–љ—Ц –Ј–∞—Б—В–µ—А–µ–ґ–µ–љ–љ—П (Decolonization Notes)

Teaching Ukrainian on its own terms from day one is non-negotiable.

1.  **NO Russian Analogies:** Never teach Ukrainian phonetics or letters by comparing them to Russian (e.g., "Ukrainian `–Є` is like Russian `—Л`"). Teach the sounds of Ukrainian independently, using native audio and phonetic descriptions relevant to an English speaker's palate. The learner must build a new, separate phonetic system for Ukrainian.

2.  **Color Terminology:** Be precise with color names that are false friends with Russian.
    *   `—Б–ЄћБ–љ—Ц–є` in Ukrainian is a deep, dark blue. The lighter, sky-blue color is `–±–ї–∞–Ї–ЄћБ—В–љ–Є–є` or `–≥–Њ–ї—Г–±–ЄћБ–є`. Historical linguistic sources show that `—Б–Є–љ—Ц–є` historically meant "dark" or even "black," which explains its modern usage for dark shades (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `ext-istoria_movy-78`). Confusing it with Russian `—Б–Є–љ–Є–є` (which covers a broader blue spectrum) leads to unnatural phrasing.
    *   `—З–µ—А–≤–ЊћБ–љ–Є–є` is the standard word for "red." The word `–Ї—А–∞ћБ—Б–љ–Є–є` is archaic/poetic for "beautiful" and should not be taught as "red," which is its primary meaning in Russian.

3.  **Source of Vocabulary:** When discussing shared Slavic words (e.g., `—Б—В–Њ–і–Њ–ї–∞`, `–≥—А—Г–±–∞`), present them as part of a shared heritage or as Ukrainian words that influenced neighboring languages like Romanian, rather than defaulting to a narrative of Russian influence (–Ф–ґ–µ—А–µ–ї–Њ: `ext-istoria_movy-10`). This correctly positions Ukrainian as a historically significant and independent language.

## –°–ї–Њ–≤–љ–Є–Ї–Њ–≤–Є–є –Љ—Ц–љ—Ц–Љ—Г–Љ (Vocabulary Boundaries)

This vocabulary is foundational for A1 learners to describe their immediate world.

**–Я—А–Є–Ї–Љ–µ—В–љ–Є–Ї–Є (Adjectives):**
*   вШЕвШЕвШЕ (Essential): `–≥–∞ћБ—А–љ–Є–є` (good, nice), `–њ–Њ–≥–∞ћБ–љ–Є–є` (bad), `–≤–µ–ї–ЄћБ–Ї–Є–є` (big), `–Љ–∞–ї–ЄћБ–є`/`–Љ–∞–ї–µћБ–љ—М–Ї–Є–є` (small), `–љ–Њ–≤–ЄћБ–є` (new), `—Б—В–∞—А–ЄћБ–є` (old), `–і–Њ–±—А–Є–є` (kind), `—Ж—Ц–Ї–∞ћБ–≤–Є–є` (interesting), `—Е–Њ–ї–ЊћБ–і–љ–Є–є` (cold), `—В–µћБ–њ–ї–Є–є` (warm). Colors: `–±—ЦћБ–ї–Є–є`, `—З–ЊћБ—А–љ–Є–є`, `—З–µ—А–≤–ЊћБ–љ–Є–є`, `—Б–ЄћБ–љ—Ц–є`, `–Ј–µ–ї–µћБ–љ–Є–є`, `–ґ–ЊћБ–≤—В–Є–є`.
*   вШЕвШЕ (Useful): `—Б–Љ–∞—З–љ–ЄћБ–є` (tasty), `—И–≤–Є–і–Ї–ЄћБ–є` (fast), `–њ–Њ–≤—ЦћБ–ї—М–љ–Є–є` (slow), `–≤–∞–ґ–Ї–ЄћБ–є` (heavy, difficult), `–ї–µ–≥–Ї–ЄћБ–є` (light, easy), `–і–µ—И–µћБ–≤–Є–є` (cheap), `–і–Њ—А–Њ–≥–ЄћБ–є` (expensive), `–≤–µ—Б–µћБ–ї–Є–є` (cheerful), `—Б—Г–Љ–љ–ЄћБ–є` (sad).
*   вШЕ (Can wait): `—З–ЄћБ—Б—В–Є–є` (clean), `–±—А—Г–і–љ–ЄћБ–є` (dirty), `–≤–Є—Б–ЊћБ–Ї–Є–є` (tall/high), `–љ–Є–Ј—М–Ї–ЄћБ–є` (short/low), `—И–Є—А–ЊћБ–Ї–Є–є` (wide), `–≤—Г–Ј—М–Ї–ЄћБ–є` (narrow).

**–Ж–Љ–µ–љ–љ–Є–Ї–Є (Nouns to pair with):**
*   вШЕвШЕвШЕ: `–і–µ–љ—М`, `–і—Ц–Љ`, `—Б—В—Ц–ї`, `–і—А—Г–≥`; `–Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥–∞`, `—А–Њ–±–ЊћБ—В–∞`, `–≤–Њ–і–∞ћБ`, `—Ч–ґ–∞`; `–≤—Ц–Ї–љ–ЊћБ`, `—Б–ї–ЊћБ–≤–Њ`, `–Љ—ЦћБ—Б—В–Њ`; `–ї—ОћБ–і–Є`, `–і—ЦћБ—В–Є`, `–ЊћБ—З—Ц`.

**–Ф—Ц—Ф—Б–ї–Њ–≤–∞ (Verbs to use in sentences):**
*   вШЕвШЕвШЕ: `–±—ГћБ—В–Є` (to be), `–Љ–∞ћБ—В–Є` (to have), `—Е–Њ—В—ЦћБ—В–Є` (to want), `–ї—О–±–ЄћБ—В–Є` (to love), `–±–∞ћБ—З–Є—В–Є` (to see), `–Ј–љ–∞ћБ—В–Є` (to know), `–њ–Њ–і–ЊћБ–±–∞—В–Є—Б—П` (to like).

## –Я—А–Є–Ї–ї–∞–і–Є –Ј –њ—Ц–і—А—Г—З–љ–Є–Ї—Ц–≤ (Textbook Examples)

These exercises from Ukrainian textbooks are the gold standard for A1 activities. They are simple, repetitive, and build intuition for agreement.

1.  **Question-based Completion (Source: `2-klas-ukrmova-kravcova-2019-1_s0075`)**
    *   **Format:** The student is given a noun and a question word to prompt the correct adjective form.
    *   **Example:**
        *   `–Ю–≥—Ц—А–Њ–Ї (—П–Ї–Є–є?) ______________`
        *   `–Ф–Є–љ—П (—П–Ї–∞?) ______________`
        *   `–°–Њ–љ—Ж–µ (—П–Ї–µ?) ______________`
        *   `–Ъ–∞–±–∞—З–Ї–Є (—П–Ї—Ц?) ______________`
    *   **Pedagogical Value:** Directly links the noun's gender/number to the adjective's ending through the question word.

2.  **Identifying Nouns by Attribute (Source: `2-klas-ukrmova-kravcova-2019-1_s0075`)**
    *   **Format:** The student fills in the blank with a noun that matches the given adjective.
    *   **Example:**
        *   `–Ъ–Њ–ї—О—З–Є–є ...` (—Ч–ґ–∞–Ї)
        *   `–Т–µ–ї–Є–Ї–Є–є ...` (–≤–µ–і–Љ—Ц–і—М)
        *   `–•–Є—В—А–∞ ...` (–ї–Є—Б–Є—Ж—П)
        *   `–Я—Г—Е–љ–∞—Б—В–µ ...` (–Ї—Г—А—З–∞—В–Ї–Њ)
    *   **Pedagogical Value:** Reinforces adjective-noun collocations and vocabulary.

3.  **Pattern Drill for Agreement (Source: `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0034`)**
    *   **Format:** The student applies a single adjective to a list of nouns with different genders and numbers.
    *   **Example:** `(–≥–∞—А–љ–Є–є) —И–∞—А—Д вАФ –і—Ц–≤—З–Є–љ–∞ вАФ –Њ–Ј–µ—А–Њ вАФ –Ї–≤—Ц—В–Є.`
    *   **Expected Output:** `–≥–∞—А–љ–Є–є —И–∞—А—Д, –≥–∞—А–љ–∞ –і—Ц–≤—З–Є–љ–∞, –≥–∞—А–љ–µ –Њ–Ј–µ—А–Њ, –≥–∞—А–љ—Ц –Ї–≤—Ц—В–Є.`
    *   **Pedagogical Value:** Isolates and drills the core A1 skill of changing adjective endings to match the noun.

4.  **Fill-in-the-blank from a Word Bank (Source: `4-klas-ukrmova-zaharijchuk_s0089`)**
    *   **Format:** Students complete a short poem or text by choosing appropriate adjectives from a provided list (`–Ф–Њ–≤—Ц–і–Ї–∞`).
    *   **Example:**
        ```
        –Ж —Ж–µ–є ... —В–∞ ... –Ј–∞–њ–∞—Е
        –Я—А–Є–є—И–Њ–≤ –і–Њ –Љ–µ–љ–µ —Г–≤—Ц —Б–љ—Ц.
        –Р —А–∞–љ–Ї–Њ–Љ –Ї—Ц—В –њ—А–Є–љ—Ц—Б –љ–∞ –ї–∞–њ–∞—Е
        ... ... –њ–µ—А—И–Є–є —Б–љ—Ц–≥!
        –Ф–Њ–≤—Ц–і–Ї–∞: –њвАЩ—П–љ–Ї—Г, —В–Њ–љ–Ї–Є–є, –љ—Ц–ґ–љ–Є–є, –њ—Г—Е–љ–∞—Б—В–Є–є, –±—Ц–ї–Є–є.
        ```
    *   **Pedagogical Value:** Combines reading comprehension with adjective agreement in a meaningful context.
