## Cultural Hooks
1. **Kyiv Metro Depth**: Arsenalna is the deepest metro station in the world at 105.5 meters. The nearly 5-minute escalator ride has fostered a unique commuter culture of reading and reflection.
2. **Transit Evolution**: The cooperative social contract of passing cash fare in a crowded minibus ("Передайте за проїзд") exists alongside the modern, tech-forward "Київ Цифровий" app used on municipal transport.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "Я йду на метро" → Correct: "Я їду на метро" — English speakers often default to a generic "go" without distinguishing between walking (іти) and riding a vehicle (їхати).
2. "Я їду автобус" → Correct: "Я їду на автобусі" (Locative) or "автобусом" (Instrumental) — Learners frequently omit the required preposition or case ending for means of transport.
3. Destination vs. Location → Confusing "Я в метро" (I am in the metro - Locative/Where) with "Я їду в метро" (I am traveling into the metro - Accusative/Where to).

## Notes for Content Writing
- Frame the Ukrainian transit system independently; highlight modern digital infrastructure (Київ Цифровий) alongside traditional elements to avoid presenting a solely post-Soviet stereotype.
- Strict English scaffolding required for explaining the 'іти' vs 'їхати' distinction (40-50% immersion for A1).
- Prevent Russianisms: reinforce 'зупинка' (not остановка) and 'квиток' (not билет).
