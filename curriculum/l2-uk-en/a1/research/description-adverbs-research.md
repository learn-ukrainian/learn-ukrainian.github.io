# Дослідження: Description: Adverbs

## State Standard Reference
**§4.3.1**: "Simple sentences — SVO, questions (хто? що? де? коли?), negation (не, ні)" (lines 729–739)
**Catalog A** (lines ~374, 436): Spatial/temporal markers (тут, там, сьогодні, завтра) — communicative intentions.
Alignment: Adverbs are used within simple SVO sentences (§4.3.1). The formation rule (-ий → -о) is formally scoped to B1 §4.3.7, but basic adverb usage (frequency, manner, place, time) is foundational to Catalog A communicative intentions. This module teaches usage patterns expected at A1, using discovery-first before the formation rule.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Proverb**: «Повільно їдеш — далі будеш» (lit. "You'll go farther by going slowly" → Slow and steady wins the race). Directly uses **повільно**, the antonym of **швидко**. Usable as a discovery anchor: show the proverb first, ask learners what the bolded word might mean from context.
2. **«Добре» as a social lubricant**: In everyday Ukrainian, **добре** functions as "OK / got it / agreed" — far beyond its literal "well." A simple dialogue like *— Зустрінемось о шостій. — Добре!* demonstrates this before grammar rules appear.
3. **Café/restaurant context**: Ukrainian speakers naturally use manner adverbs in service settings — *Говоріть повільніше, будь ласка* (A2 form, but the pattern is observable). At A1, the Food Critic persona (plan: describes how someone cooks — *дуже смачно, занадто солоно*) grounds the grammar in a real scenario learners can picture.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Adjective form used as adverb**: *Він говорить хороший* → correct: *Він говорить добре*. Caused by English "He speaks good" (informal) and failure to see that Ukrainian mandates the adverb form categorically.
2. **Missing double negation with ніколи**: *Я ніколи йду туди* → correct: *Я ніколи не йду туди*. English "I never go" has no second negative; Ukrainian requires **ніколи не** — the negation is obligatory, not optional emphasis.
3. **Дуже placed after the modified word**: *Це добре дуже* → correct: *Це дуже добре*. English allows end-placement ("That's great, really"). Ukrainian intensifiers must precede.
4. **добрий/добре confusion**: Learners write *Він добрий говорить* (adjective, wrong form, wrong position) instead of *Він говорить добре*. The exception (добрий → добре, not добро) needs explicit flagging.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Present 4 mini-dialogues without explanation:
  - *— Як ти їдеш? — Швидко.*
  - *— Як він співає? — Добре.*
  - *— Як вона говорить? — Тихо.*
  - *— Як ти читаєш? — Повільно.*
  Ask: "What question word appears in all four? What do the answer words have in common?" Learners notice (a) Як? is the trigger question, (b) all answers end in -о (except добре). Discovery precedes the rule.
- **Best context**: Daily routine (I always eat quickly, I usually work slowly) + café/food critic (he cooks very well, she speaks too quietly). These give frequency adverbs and manner adverbs a real home.
- **Contrastive pairs for drill**: добре ↔ погано | швидко ↔ повільно | голосно ↔ тихо | завжди ↔ ніколи. Pair-based production is faster than isolated recall.

## Notes for Content Writing
- The -ий → -о rule has ONE high-frequency exception: добрий → **добре** (not *добро*). Teach this exception immediately alongside the rule — delay causes fossilized error.
- Frequency scale (завжди → зазвичай → часто → іноді → рідко → ніколи) should appear as a visual spectrum/table, not prose. Learners retain scales better spatially.
- Spatial/temporal markers (тут, там, сьогодні, завтра) are State Standard Catalog A requirements — include 2-3 example sentences using them alongside manner adverbs to fulfill the standard.
- Keep examples using present/past tense only (phase A1.4 — past tense introduced at M36, future at M37, both available).
- No imperative constructions (banned until M47).
