## Cultural Hooks
1. **Скоромовка**: «На дворі трава, на траві дрова» — 6 two-syllable words in rapid sequence. Clapping out syllables (На-дво-рі / тра-ва) makes syllable boundaries tactile and memorable. Used in Ukrainian schools exactly as the Zabotnyi textbook exercises model (counting syllables by vowels).
2. **Україна as the model word**: Every Ukrainian child learns у-кра-ї-на as their first 4-syllable word. The *и* with і-diacritic (ї) makes it iconic for counting — four vowels, four syllables. Natural cultural anchor point.
3. **Word division in handwriting**: Ukrainian handwritten text uses переніс visibly in copybooks. The Grade 5 Zabolotnyi textbook (textbook excerpt p.90) explicitly models «о-лі-вець» and «книж-ка» as canonical division examples — real words from Ukrainian school culture.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Confusing phonetic syllables with orthographic hyphenation** → Phonetically, it is «се-стра» (maximal onset). Orthographically, Pravopys 2019 allows multiple hyphenations («се-стра», «сес-тра», «сест-ра»). Learners should learn the phonetic «се-стра» for pronunciation, but recognize the flexible writing rules. Zabolotnyi exercise (p.90) targets this: "observe whether vowel count = syllable count."
2. **Not counting ї as its own vowel** → thinking «Україна» has 3 syllables instead of 4 (у-кра-ї-на). (Note: «Украї-на» is a valid orthographic hyphenation, but phonetically it has 4 syllables). ї is always a full vowel. Also й is NOT a vowel — learners confuse й/ї.
3. **Splitting single letters at word edges** → «У-країна» — forbidden; a single letter cannot be carried over alone. This mirrors English "don't leave one letter" but learners still make it, especially with У- prefixed words.
4. **Splitting дж/дз digraphs** → «ґуд-зик» instead of «ґу-дзик» — learners see two letters and split them; digraphs representing one sound are inseparable.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Present 5 words with vowels highlighted in red: **мо**л**о**к**о**, к**і**т, **У**кр**а**їн**а**, **ав**т**о**б**у**с, сестр**а**. Ask: "Count the red letters. Count the syllables. What do you notice?" Learner discovers: vowel count = syllable count. Exactly mirrors Zabolotnyi p.90 task A (observe, conclude, then read the rule). Rule comes AFTER the discovery.
- **Best context**: Word-clapping — a physical, classroom-style activity. Show a word, clap each syllable, count. Молоко → 3 claps. Natural kindergarten/school context that learners will recognize as universal.
- **Contrastive pairs for word division errors**: «се-стра» vs ❌«сес-тра»; «ґу-дзик» vs ❌«ґуд-зик»; «паль-ці» vs ❌«пал-ьці» — show correct and incorrect side-by-side as the Vashulenko Grade 3 p.68 stress lesson does (rule box → examples).

## Notes for Content Writing
- This is module 5, pre-verbal phase. ALL Ukrainian sentence examples must be static noun phrases or «Це + noun» — no verbs.
- «ї» deserves a dedicated callout box: it is always a vowel, always its own syllable. Same for «і» (not to be confused with й).
- The «Автобус — ав-то-бус» pattern (loanword + division) is great for engagement: learners recognize the word from English.
- Word division rules section (400 words target) is the heaviest — sequence: can't split (single letter → digraph → ь → apostrophe group) THEN can split (V-C boundary, CC boundary). Don't reverse this order.
- Metalanguage terms (склад, голосний, приголосний, перенос) should appear in English-first format: "syllable (склад)", "vowel (голосний)".
