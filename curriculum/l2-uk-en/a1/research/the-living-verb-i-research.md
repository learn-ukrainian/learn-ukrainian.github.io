## Cultural Hooks
1. The "Apostol" (1574) by Ivan Fedorov, printed in Lviv, is Ukraine's first known printed book, serving as a powerful symbol of a deep-rooted literacy tradition connected to the verb "читати".

## Common Learner Errors
1. Infinitive Abuse → Я читаю (not Я читати) — English speakers often rely on the infinitive form because English verbs barely change, whereas Ukrainian verbs must always be conjugated.
2. Pronoun Overuse → Читаю. Працюю. (not Я читаю. Я працюю.) — English syntax requires subject pronouns, but Ukrainian verb endings already indicate the subject (pro-drop), making constant pronouns sound unnatural.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Crucial M15+ Constraint:** Do NOT use imperative forms (e.g., Запам'ятайте, Зверніть увагу, Прочитайте) for instructions. Use indirect requests or English (e.g., "Remember that...", "Notice that...", "Read...").
- Keep aspect explanations light: frame imperfective simply as ongoing actions ("what you are doing now or usually do").
- To avoid early accusative case confusion, strictly use inanimate masculine and neuter objects for SVO practice (e.g., журнал, радіо, лист) where Nominative equals Accusative.
