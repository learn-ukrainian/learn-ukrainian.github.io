**Morphological note**: `працювати` and `відпочивати` follow the `-овувати → -ую` stem contraction pattern (regular but looks different). Flag this clearly — it trips up learners who expect *працюваю*.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Proverb** (plan-specified): *Птицю пізнати по пір'ю, а людину по мові* — "A bird is known by its feathers, a person by their speech." Directly links verb mastery to identity and character. Immediately deployable as a lesson-opener.
2. **The Apostol (1574)**: Ivan Fedorov printed the first book in Ukraine — the word *читати* literally built Ukrainian literate culture. This gives the most basic verb a historical weight that resonates.
3. **Chalkboard скоромовка**: *Саша швидко читає, Саша гарно пише* — a minimal tongue-twister that drills читає/пише in third person without requiring new vocabulary. Textbook-style rhythm exercise.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Infinitive abuse**: *Я читати книгу* → *Я читаю книгу* — direct transfer from English "I read" without conjugating. The infinitive feels "safe" so learners freeze there.
2. **Constant pronoun use**: *Я читаю. Я пишу. Я знаю.* → *Читаю. Пишу. Знаю.* — English requires subject pronouns; Ukrainian does not. Overuse sounds robotic and non-native.
3. **-овувати stem confusion**: *я працюваю* → *я працюю* — learners apply the full infinitive stem to present tense instead of the contracted `-ую` form.
4. **слухати vs. чути conflation**: Using *слухати* for involuntary hearing — в мене болить вухо, я погано *слухаю* should be *чую*. The listen/hear distinction maps poorly from English.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present three mini speech bubbles without explanation:
  - Іван: *Читаю книгу.*
  - Марія: *Читаєш швидко!*
  - Вони: *Читають разом.*
  Ask learners: "What changes? What stays the same?" They should notice the `чита-` stem is constant and the endings shift. Only then reveal the full paradigm table. This mirrors the inductive exercise style in Авраменко grade 7 (§1-style pattern discovery before rule statement).

- **Best context**: Daily routine narration — a learner describing their own morning (*Я читаю новини. Слухаю музику. Пишу повідомлення.*) is immediately authentic and personal. The pro-drop feature becomes natural here: they quickly learn to drop *Я* for flow.

- **Contrastive pairs**:
  - *читати* (infinitive) vs. *читаю* (I read/am reading) — the core paradigm entry point
  - *слухати* vs. *чути* — volitional vs. involuntary hearing
  - *Я читаю.* vs. *Читаю.* — with vs. without pronoun, both correct

- Use SVO frames with inanimate masculine/neuter nouns only (журнал, лист, радіо, повідомлення) — Nominative = Accusative for these, so no case confusion is introduced before M25.

## Notes for Content Writing

- Keep the paradigm table as the visual anchor — return to it after each new verb introduced.
- The `-овувати` verbs (працювати, відпочивати) need a dedicated callout box or the learner will over-apply the full stem.
- Do not introduce чекати's preposition *на* as a grammar rule — just model it in fixed phrases.
- `грати на гітарі` requires Locative (M13+ scope) — present it as a fixed collocation, not an analyzed phrase. Same for `грати в ігри` (Accusative, M25). Use only `грати в ігри` collocations in practice drills if needed, but safest to hold грати for illustration only.
- Plan specifies "Sports Commentator" persona role — this can motivate the present-tense narration angle: commentators narrate ongoing actions exactly as imperfective present does.
