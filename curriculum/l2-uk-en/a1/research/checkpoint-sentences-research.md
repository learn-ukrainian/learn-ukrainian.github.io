## Cultural Hooks

1. **Скоромовка for verb fluency**: "Мама мила Милу милом" — a well-known Ukrainian tongue-twister that drills 3rd-person verb forms. Use it as a fun pattern-drill for `мила` (was washing, past) — note this is past tense, so use it purely as a pronunciation/rhythm hook, not a grammar model.
2. **Real-life context — Kyiv café**: Ordering at a café uses all A1.2 skills in one interaction: `Що ти хочеш?` / `Я хочу каву.` / `Мені подобається цей торт.` — a dialogue scaffold that mirrors real cafés in Kyiv where Ukrainian has become the dominant language since 2022.
3. **Folk question-and-answer pattern**: Ukrainian folk riddles (загадки) are structured as Що це? + answer. Example: `Що це? Це не будинок, але тут живуть люди.` — playfully reviews `що/це/це не` patterns.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **II conjugation 3rd plural**: Learners write `говорять` correctly but default to `*говорують` (I-conjugation pattern). → Correct: `говорять`, `бачать`, `сидять`. Textbook-style contrast drills (Avramenko pattern: contrastive pairs) work well here.
2. **Dative `мені подобається` vs. Accusative `я люблю`**: Learners say `*я подобаюся музику` mixing constructions. → The subject of `подобатися` is the thing liked; the person is Dative. Model: `Мені подобається ця пісня.` vs. `Я люблю музику.`
3. **Gender agreement with possessives**: English has no gender, so learners write `*мій книга` instead of `моя книга`. → Every possessive/demonstrative needs a gender-agreement check before checkpoint passage.
4. **`де` vs. `куди`**: Learners use `де` for both location and direction. → `Де ти?` (static location) vs. `Куди ти йдеш?` (motion toward). A directional context pair is essential.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present a short 4-line café dialogue with deliberate mixed examples of all four skill areas, then ask: "Which line uses a Dative construction? Which verb is II conjugation? Which word tells us direction vs. location?" — learner diagnoses the patterns rather than being told.
- **Best context**: The café/meet-up scenario covers all skills organically — verb conjugation (Що ти хочеш?), questions (Де ти зараз?), preferences (Мені подобається цей торт), possessives (Це моя кава).
- **Contrastive pairs**: `говорять` vs. `читають`; `мій` vs. `моя`; `де` vs. `куди`; `мені подобається` vs. `я люблю`. The Avramenko textbook consistently uses sentence-level contrastive exercises (pairs of minimal sentences differing by one feature) — replicate this structure for the verb conjugation and demonstrative sections.

## Notes for Content Writing

- This is TTT (Test-Teach-Test) pedagogical format — open with a self-diagnostic, teach gaps, then retest. The overview section should frame it as "Let's see what you already know, then fill any gaps."
- Reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь) appear in scope (§4.2.4.1) — a brief example (`подобатися`, `називатися`) is appropriate without a full section.
- Do NOT introduce perfective aspect — imperfective only at A1 (quick-ref constraint).
- The integration task should use a single cohesive dialogue or scenario (café, market, or phone call) that authentically requires all four skills together — not a list of isolated sentences.
- Use Ukrainian section headers with English in parentheses (M15+ rule applies here at M24).
