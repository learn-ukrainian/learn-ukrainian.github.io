## Cultural Hooks

1. **Маршрутка etiquette**: In Ukrainian cities, minibuses (маршрутки) have no automatic stops — passengers must shout "На зупинці, будь ласка!" to request their stop. This is essential survival Ukrainian, not just a grammar drill. A learner who doesn't know this phrase will miss their stop.

2. **Landmark-based navigation**: Ukrainian urban communication strongly favors landmark orientation over cardinal directions. Textbook exercise (Zabolotny Grade 5, §127): "Уявіть, що вам потрібно пояснити комусь, як дістатися до школи (поліції, лікарні, магазину, вокзалу тощо). Опишіть детально маршрут: якою вулицею іти, куди повернути, де перейти дорогу." This mirrors exactly how Ukrainians give directions in real life — "до банку, потім наліво, біля аптеки."

3. **Майдан as civic concept**: "Майдан" is not just a word for "square" — it is deeply embedded in Ukrainian civic identity (Майдан Незалежності, Revolution of Dignity). Distinguishing "Майдан" (central civic hub) from "площа" (generic square) is culturally significant.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Wrong preposition: "в/у" vs "на"** → *в пошті* → **на пошті**; *в площі* → **на площі**. Happens because English uses "at/in" uniformly. The plan already targets this explicitly.
2. **"Де" for movement** → *Де ти йдеш?* → **Куди ти йдеш?** Learners calque English "Where are you going?" onto "Де," not realizing Ukrainian requires "Куди" for motion.
3. **Nominative after "біля/навпроти"** → *біля аптека* → **біля аптеки**. Prepositions requiring Genitive are unfamiliar to English speakers (English has no case). Teach as fixed chunks, not as case grammar at this stage.
4. **Confusing formal/colloquial direction pairs** → *ліворуч* (signs, formal) vs *наліво* (spoken directions). Not an error per se, but registers clash. Textbook exercise model (Zabolotny §127) uses dієслова з префіксами: розвернутися, доїхати — keep these passive at A1.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show a simple city map (Kyiv's Khreshchatyk area or a generic Ukrainian street grid). Present 4 short sentences: *Аптека на вулиці Хрещатик. Пошта навпроти парку. Зупинка біля банку. Майдан прямо.* Ask: "What form does 'аптека' take after 'на'? Why 'навпроти парку' and not 'парк'?" Learner notices the pattern before the rule is stated.
- **Best context**: Street dialogue — asking a passerby for directions to the pharmacy (Де аптека? / Як дістатися до пошти?). Real-life urgency makes the grammar feel necessary, not academic.
- **Contrastive pairs**: Де ти? / Куди ти йдеш? — and — в аптеці / до аптеки — position vs destination. Textbook model: Zabolotny Grade 5 §127 treats the full route description as one authentic communicative task.

---

## Notes for Content Writing

- Teach "біля" and "навпроти" as **lexical chunks** (+Genitive), not as case grammar. Save the Genitive explanation for a1-32. The plan explicitly says "вчити як фіксований лексичний блок."
- The dialogue persona is a Taxi Driver — use first-person navigation instructions ("Їдьте прямо, потім направо...") as the core dialogue container.
- IMPERATIVE BAN applies (M47). Use present tense only: "ти йдеш прямо" not "йди прямо." Reinforce this for the content writer — the route explanation must use non-imperative forms.
- Section 3 (Practice) should include the "Де vs Куди" gap-fill as the primary drill — this is the highest-frequency error and the plan's key discriminator.
