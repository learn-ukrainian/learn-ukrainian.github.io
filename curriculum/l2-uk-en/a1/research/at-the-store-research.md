# Дослідження: At the Store

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.3.2: "Accusative — direct object, with prepositions (в/у, на, за, через, про)" — primary grammar focus.
§4.2.3.3: "Locative case — location (в/у, на)" — destination vs location contrast.
§3 Thematic area: "купівля" — explicitly listed as an A1 thematic requirement.
Alignment: This module directly addresses §3 купівля, reinforces §4.2.3.2 Accusative with shopping items, and contrasts §4.2.3.3 Locative (магазині) with Accusative destination (магазин). "Карткою/готівкою" are taught as frozen Instrumental chunks (plan-level exception to A1 Instrumental restriction — communicative survival priority).

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Silpo themed supermarkets**: Ukraine's Silpo chain built branches themed around GTA, Steampunk, and seasonal concepts — a real-world cultural contrast to standardized Western big-box stores. Concrete hook: "In Kyiv, you can shop in a supermarket designed like a video game." Use as Вступ hook to establish engagement.
2. **"Пакет потрібен?"** — The Bag Ritual: Every Ukrainian cashier asks this. Since 2019, single-use plastic bags cost ~2 UAH. This is a near-universal interaction every learner will encounter; it anchors the checkout script and doubles as a pronunciation target.
3. **"Передбачення на чеках"** — Some Ukrainian stores (including Сільпо) print short motivational or fortune-style messages on the bottom of receipts. Learnable as a playful reading activity at checkout.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Accusative vs Locative confusion**: *іти в магазин* (direction → Acc) vs *бути в магазині* (location → Loc) — English "in the store" doesn't distinguish, so learners write *в магазин* for both. Drill with motion/static verb pairs (іти/бути, купити/знаходитися).
2. **Feminine agreement failure**: *це ціна / це каса / це знижка* — learners write *це* for all genders following English "this." Drill: *ця ціна, ця каса, ця знижка* (all feminine).
3. **Russicism "покупка"**: "покупка" as a process word (❌ *робити покупки*) is a calque from Russian. Correct Ukrainian for the process: *купувати*, *скуповуватися*, *купівля*. (Note: "покупка" as the physical item purchased IS acceptable.)
4. **Inanimate masculine accusative = nominative**: Learners over-apply animate-accusative patterns and write *купую магазина* instead of *купую магазин* (inanimate Masc Acc = Nom).

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show a short two-line supermarket checkout dialogue in a blockquote — cashier says "Пакет потрібен?" / customer says "Так, один, будь ласка." Ask learner: what is the cashier asking? What does the customer need? THEN present the question structure. This grounds the grammar in a recognizable real-world moment before any rule explanation.
- **Best context**: Store map visual (label the відділи — молочний, хлібний, м'ясний, кондитерський) with Locative "у якому відділі?" questions attached to each zone. This makes the Locative feel spatial and concrete.
- **Contrastive pairs**: *іти в магазин* (goal, Acc) ↔ *бути в магазині* (location, Loc); *ця ціна* (fem) ↔ *цей магазин* (masc) — gender agreement via store vocabulary.
- Use tables for відділ names + Locative forms — the A1 immersion rule permits full Ukrainian in tables.

## Notes for Content Writing

- Imperatives are available (A1.6, module 61 — M47 established imperative). Use for store directions: "Йдіть до молочного відділу."
- Frozen instrumental chunks (карткою, готівкою) are plan-approved exceptions to A1 Instrumental restriction — present them explicitly as fixed expressions, not productive grammar.
- Max 10 words per Ukrainian sentence — keep all in-text Ukrainian strictly within this limit.
- Section headers use Ukrainian + English in parentheses (M15+ rule applies at M61).
