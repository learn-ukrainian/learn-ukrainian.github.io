# Research Notes: At the Café - Practical (A1 M35)

**Track**: Core A
**Module**: M35 - At the Café (Ordering Drinks and Snacks)
**Level**: A1 (Beginner - Practical Scenarios Phase)
**Researched**: 2026-02-08

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

The **Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (ДСТУ 2024)** categorizes the cafe/restaurant scenario under both thematic competence and communicative intentions for the A1 level.

> **Каталог Б. Тематичний каталог (3.9. Ресторан, кафе):**
> "страви; напої; посуд і столові прибори." (p. 15)

> **Каталог А. Перелік комунікативних намірів:**
> - "привернути чиюсь увагу, установити контакт: Вибачте. Можна запитати? Скажіть, будь ласка;"
> - "прохання зробити щось: Дайте, будь ласка;"
> - "висловити свої вподобання: Мені подобається. Я люблю." (p. 13)

Additionally, the listening requirements for A1 (§1.1.2.2) explicitly list:
> "Будь ласка, дайте воду / чай / каву / сік. Я хочу їсти / пити. Де їдальня / кафе / ресторан?" (p. 9)

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

### High Frequency (Survival Level)
- **Nouns**: кава (coffee), чай (tea), сік (juice), вода (water), меню (menu), рахунок (bill), цукор (sugar), молоко (milk), офіціант (waiter), стіл/столик (table), замовлення (order), картка (card), готівка (cash).
- **Verbs**: хотіти (to want), пити (to drink), їсти (to eat), коштувати (to cost), платити (to pay).
- **Phrases**: Мені, будь ласка... (For me, please...), Скільки коштує? (How much does it cost?), Разом чи окремо? (Together or separately?), Можна рахунок? (Can I have the bill?).

### Medium Frequency (Topic Expansion)
- **Nouns**: круасан (croissant), тістечко (pastry), серветка (napkin), американо (americano), капучино (cappuccino), льодяна кава (iced coffee).
- **Adjectives**: великий (large), маленький (small), гарячий (hot), холодний (cold).

### Common A1 Collocations
- **Чорна кава** (Black coffee)
- **Кава без цукру** (Coffee without sugar — *Genitive case*)
- **Велика склянка** (A large glass)
- **Оплата карткою** (Payment by card)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**1. The Lviv Coffee Legend (Львівська кава):**
While coffee is popular across Ukraine, Lviv is the undisputed capital of coffee culture. A local legend (often told to tourists) claims that the first cafe in Europe was opened by a Ukrainian, Yuriy Kulchytsky. In modern Ukraine, a *кав'ярня* (coffee shop) is a primary social hub. Unlike the US "grab-and-go" style, Ukrainians often spend hours in a cafe with a single *філіжанка* (small coffee cup) and a laptop or a friend.

**2. The Digital Payment Revolution:**
Ukraine is one of the most digitally integrated countries regarding payments. Even in tiny kiosks or street coffee trucks, contactless payment via Apple Pay/Google Pay or QR codes (often through Monobank) is the standard. Students should be prepared for the waiter to bring a portable terminal automatically when they ask for the bill.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
- **The "Please" Multi-tool**: In English, "please" is for requests. In Ukrainian, **"Будь ласка"** is also used for "You're welcome" and "Here you go" (when the waiter serves the coffee).
- **"I will be..."**: English speakers say "I'll have a coffee." Ukrainians often use the future tense of the verb "to be" for ordering: **"Я буду каву"** (literally: I will be coffee), which is idiomatic and simple for A1 students who just learned the future tense.

### Common Learner Errors
- **Accusative Endings**: Forgetting the **-у** ending for feminine nouns when ordering (e.g., saying *Я хочу кава* instead of *Я хочу каву*).
- **Irregular Verb "їсти"**: English speakers struggle with the unique conjugation of *їсти* (to eat). At A1, it is better to teach it in the 1st person singular (*я їм*) as a fixed chunk for ordering.

### Teaching Sequence Recommendations
1. Review food/drink nouns with their genders.
2. Practice the "Ordering Formula": *Мені, будь ласка, [Accusative].*
3. Integrate the Genitive for preferences: *Кава без молока.*
4. Roleplay the payment: *Карткою чи готівкою?*

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope
- **Accusative Case**: Used for direct objects of ordering (Каву, чай, круасан).
- **Genitive Case**: Used with **без** (without) for preferences (Без цукру) and for expressing absence (Немає соку).
- **Locative Case**: Describing where you are (Я в кафе).
- **Numbers (1-100)**: Handling the bill and prices.
- **Modal Verbs**: Using *хочу* (want) and *можу* (can).
- **Future Tense**: Using *я буду* for "I'll have".

### OUT of Scope
- **Instrumental Case**: Students do **NOT** know the Instrumental case yet. Avoid teaching "Coffee *with* milk" (*з молоком*) as a grammatical structure. If used, treat it as a fixed label.
- **Dative Case**: Do not use "Give *to me*" (*Дайте мені*). Stick to the imperative *Дайте, будь ласка* or *Мені, будь ласка*.
- **Conditional Mood**: Avoid "I would like" (*Я хотів би*). It is too complex for this stage; stick to the present/future indicative.
- **Perfective Aspect**: Do not focus on the difference between *замовляти* and *замовити* yet; use the imperfective as the default.
