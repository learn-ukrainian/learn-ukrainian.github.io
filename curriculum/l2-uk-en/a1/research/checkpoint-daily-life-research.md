## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Core Meal**: In Ukrainian daily life, lunch (`обід`) is traditionally the largest and most substantial meal of the day (often featuring soup like borscht and a main course), unlike the Western European/American emphasis on dinner.
2. **Weather Small Talk**: Discussing the weather (`Яка сьогодні погода?`) is a universal, polite icebreaker in Ukraine, essential for navigating daily social interactions and bridging into future plans.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Past Tense Gender Mismatch** → *Він ходила* instead of *Він ходив*. Why: English speakers forget that past tense verbs in Ukrainian conjugate for the subject's gender (a shared Slavic feature), not just person/number.
2. **Direction vs. Location Confusion** → *Я йду в парку* (Locative) instead of *Я йду в парк* (Accusative). Why: English uses "in the park" for both static location and sometimes motion, masking the rigid morphological boundary in Ukrainian.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonization & Framing**: Present the analytic future tense (`буду` + infinitive) and past tense gender agreement as beautiful, systematic features of the Ukrainian language (shared with other European languages like Polish and Czech), never framing them as variants of Russian. 
- Ensure explicit English scaffolding is used for grammar recap (as per A1 rules, 10-50% immersion), while using predominantly Ukrainian for the integrated tasks.
- Avoid duplicate header keywords in Phase B to prevent `audit_module.py` failures (e.g., ensure H3s don't repetitively use the word "День" or "Погода").
