# Дослідження: Checkpoint: Daily Life

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Indicative mood — present, past, future tenses; conjugation I/II; reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)"
§3: "Thematic Areas - дім, місто, побут, діяльність, природа, купівля, ресторан"
Alignment: This checkpoint consolidates indicative mood tense forms (past and future) and integrates them with thematic vocabulary for daily life routines, shopping scenarios, and weather descriptions.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Notes | Key collocations |
|------|-------|------------------|
| вчора / завтра | Temporal anchors, often placed at the start of sentences to set the tense context. | Вчора я..., Завтра ми... |
| снідати / обідати / вечеряти | Core daily routine verbs (Class I, -ати). Regular conjugation. | снідати кашу, обідати в кафе |
| коштувати | Used primarily in the 3rd person singular/plural for shopping contexts. | Скільки коштує? |
| тепло / холодно | Impersonal adverbs, used without a subject to describe conditions. | Сьогодні тепло. |

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Meal Times:** In Ukraine, "обід" (lunch) is traditionally the largest and most important meal of the day, usually eaten between 1:00 PM and 3:00 PM, and typically includes a warm soup (like борщ or суп).
2. **Small Talk:** Just like in English-speaking cultures, discussing the weather is a universal and polite small-talk topic in Ukraine.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Gender matching in past tense** → *Він читала* (wrong) vs *Він читав* (correct). English speakers often forget that past tense verbs must agree with the subject's gender, not just the person.
2. **Direct translation of weather** → *"Воно є холодно"* (wrong) vs *"Сьогодні холодно"* (correct). Ukrainian uses impersonal constructions for weather without a dummy "it" subject.
3. **Using "бути" for cost** → *"Це є 50 гривень"* (wrong) vs *"Це коштує 50 гривень"* (correct).
