## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ukrainian meal rhythm**: Сніданок (breakfast) is typically light (бутерброд, каша, яйця). Обід is the main meal, often at home or in a їдальня. This contrasts with English "lunch as main meal" and gives learners a real schema for daily routine narration.
2. **Weather proverb**: *Де сонце — там і тепло, де мати — там і добро.* (Where there's sun, there's warmth; where there's a mother, there's goodness.) — connects warmth vocabulary to family warmth culturally.
3. **Time phrases in Ukrainian**: Ukrainians frequently anchor past/future in casual speech: *Вчора зустрілися* (we met yesterday) vs English "we met" — the temporal anchor is often obligatory in Ukrainian colloquial narrative. This makes вчора/завтра natural entry points.

---

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Wrong past tense gender suffix**: *Він ходила* → **Він ходив** — English has no gender agreement, so learners default to the feminine -ла (heard more often) or forget to match subject gender.
2. **Confusing impersonal weather constructions**: *Сьогодні є тепло* → **Сьогодні тепло** — English "it is warm" triggers есть/є insertion; Ukrainian impersonal predicatives drop the copula entirely.
3. **Overusing буде instead of буду**: *Він буду читати* → **Він буде читати** — learners memorize "буду = future" without noting conjugation. The analytical future fully conjugates: я бу́ду, ти бу́деш, він/вона бу́де, ми бу́демо, ви бу́дете, вони бу́дуть.
4. **Mixing up Де?/Куди?/Звідки?**: *Я йду де магазин* → **Я йду до магазину** — the three spatial questions trigger different case/preposition patterns; learners conflate "where" across all three.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show two short diary-style paragraphs — one about вчора, one about завтра. Ask the learner: *What's different about the verbs in each paragraph? Can you spot the pattern?* Let them notice -в/-ла vs буду + infinitive before naming the categories.
- **Best context**: A daily planner or journal page — "Вчора я..." / "Сьогодні я..." / "Завтра я..." — makes all three tenses feel like a unified, practical tool rather than abstract grammar points.
- **Contrastive pairs for common errors**:
  - Gender: *Він пішов / Вона пішла / Дитина пішло* (note neuter)
  - Weather copula: *Сьогодні тепло* vs *Сьогодні сонячний день* (adjective construction allowed for contrast)
  - Куди vs Де: *Де ти? — Я в магазині. / Куди ти йдеш? — Я йду до магазину.*

## Notes for Content Writing

- This is a TTT checkpoint: start with a brief self-assessment task (Test), then teach gaps (Teach), then apply (Test again). The overview section should orient the learner to this structure explicitly.
- The Integration Task section (Вчора/Завтра narratives) is the pedagogical payoff — make the prompts specific enough to scaffold (give a time, a place, a food item) so learners aren't generating from scratch.
- Word count: 1200 target. Sections are tight — keep explanations minimal, lean on example tables and blockquote dialogues. Don't pad; integrate examples into prose.
- Frequency adverbs (зазвичай, часто, іноді, рідко) are in the recommended vocabulary — slot them into the Daily Life section as natural upgrades to bare present tense sentences.
- No subordinate clauses (який, що, бо, якщо) — keep all Ukrainian examples to single-clause sentences max 10 words.
