# Дослідження: My Daily Routine

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Indicative mood — present, past, future tenses; conjugation I/II; reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)"
Alignment: This module directly addresses the §4.2.4.1 requirement by introducing and practicing common daily routine reflexive verbs and their correct conjugation patterns.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Обід (Lunch)**: The Ukrainian lunch (13:00–14:00) is traditionally the main, most substantial meal of the day, often consisting of first (soup) and second courses, in contrast to lighter Western lunches.
2. **Домашній одяг (Home clothes)**: It is a common cultural practice to change into comfortable "home clothes" immediately upon returning home, establishing a clear boundary between the public and private spheres of daily life.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Confusing "вмиватися" and "мити руки"** → Use "вмиватися" for washing oneself (face/body) and "мити" for washing objects/hands. — *English uses "to wash" interchangeably, but Ukrainian separates the reflexive action.*
2. **Using "в" + Accusative for time** → Correct form is Locative "о сьомій годині". — *Learners directly translate the English "at" or confuse it with spatial prepositions.*
3. **Incorrect reflexive endings** → Я дивлюсь (not дивлюся) vs. Ти дивишся. — *Learners forget the phonological rule: -сь after vowels, -ся after consonants.*
