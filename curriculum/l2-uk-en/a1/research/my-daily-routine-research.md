# Дослідження: My Daily Routine

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Indicative mood — present, past, future tenses; conjugation I/II; reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)"
§3 (Thematic): "побут" (daily life), "діяльність" (activities) — both explicitly listed for A1 themes
Alignment: This module operationalizes §4.2.4.1 reflexive verb conjugation in the real-world context of daily routine. The -ся/-сь rule (after vowels: -сь; after consonants: -ся) is the core morphological target.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **"Хто рано встає, тому Бог дає"** — Ukrainian proverb equivalent to "the early bird catches the worm." Highly recognized; opens discussion of morning habits naturally. Can launch the discovery exercise.
2. **Обід as the main meal**: In Ukraine, обід (13:00-14:00) is typically two courses (перша страва = soup, друга страва = main course). This contrasts sharply with the English "light lunch." The term обідня перерва (lunch break) is institutionalized in workplaces and schools.
3. **Домашній одяг** (home clothes): Ukrainians typically change from street clothes immediately upon returning home — a sharp public/private division. This is a concrete, discussable cultural habit that makes the "evening routine" section vivid and memorable.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **-ся/-сь placement** → я дивлюсь ✓ (after vowel) / ти дивишся ✓ (after consonant) — English has no particle, so learners drop it entirely: *"я дивлю"* (wrong). Must be drilled from first exposure.
2. **Time preposition** → *"в сім годин"* (wrong, Accusative) → **о сьомій годині** (correct, Locative). English "at seven" maps naturally to "в," creating systematic interference. Plan explicitly targets this.
3. **вмиватися vs мити** → *"я мию"* (transitive: washing an object) vs **я вмиваюся** (reflexive: washing one's own face/body). Learners extend мити руки to all hygiene contexts.
4. **Motion verb choice** → *"я йду на роботу машиною"* (wrong — йти = on foot) → **я їду на роботу** (by vehicle). Ukrainian splits this where English uses one verb ("go").

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Present three parallel sentences without labels — *"Я мию посуд. Я мию руки. Я вмиваюся."* — and ask: "Two sentences use мити. One uses вмиватися. What's the difference? Look at what follows each verb." Learners notice мити always takes an object; вмиватися never does. Then extend: *"Я одягаюся. Я одягаю пальто."* — learner discovers the reflexive/transitive split through contrast.
- **Best context**: Morning routine timeline. Show clock times (7:00, 7:30, 8:00…) paired with activities. Learners sequence the day, practicing о + time + activity simultaneously with verb conjugation. Connects to a1-23 directly.
- **Contrastive pairs for disambiguation**:
  - вмиватися (self) ↔ мити руки/посуд (object)
  - їхати на роботу (vehicle) ↔ йти на роботу (on foot)
  - спочатку → потім → нарешті (sequence chain)

## Notes for Content Writing
- Module is M38: past tense (M36+) and future tense (M37+) are now available — use sparingly for contrast ("вчора я прокинувся пізно" vs "зазвичай я прокидаюся рано")
- Imperative is BANNED (M47) — no "Встань!" — rephrase as "You can say..." or "A common sentence is..."
- Imperfective aspect only — do not introduce perfective pairs (встати, помитися)
- Keep Ukrainian sentences ≤10 words; single clause
- Дивитися is the §4.2.4.1 conjugation model verb — give full present tense paradigm in a pattern box
- Register: розмовний (colloquial) — informal я дивлюсь (not я дивлюся) is natural here and matches plan register
