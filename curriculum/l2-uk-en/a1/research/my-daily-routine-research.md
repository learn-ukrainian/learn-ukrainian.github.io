## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Ukrainian "Obid"**: Traditionally, lunch (обід) is the main and heaviest meal of the day. In organizations, it typically starts at 12:00, while in rural areas, it might start earlier (11:30–11:45) to accommodate agricultural schedules. Source: cx.ua, cbs.poltava.ua.
2. **Traditional Breakfasts**: While there is no single "standard" breakfast, popular Ukrainian morning dishes include *syrnyky* (cheese pancakes), *kasha* (porridge), and *mlyntsi* (crepes), often served with *smetana* (sour cream) or honey. Source: elle.ua, shuba.life.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Omission of -ся/-сь** → *Я вмиваю* instead of *Я вмиваюся* — Learners often forget the reflexive particle when the action is directed at themselves, changing "I wash (myself)" to "I wash (something)".
2. **Incorrect particle selection** → *Я одягаюся* (correct) vs *Я одягаюсь* (also correct, but learners often struggle with the vowel/consonant rule) — Use -ся after consonants and -сь after vowels (though -ся is often used universally in colloquial speech, -сь is preferred after vowels).
3. **Reflexive vs. Non-Reflexive confusion** → *Мити руки* (to wash hands) vs *Митися* (to wash oneself) — Learners may over-apply the reflexive particle to transitive actions involving body parts.
