## Cultural Hooks
1. **Київ — серце України**: Kyiv is one of the oldest cities in Eastern Europe, founded in the 5th century. Key symbols include the Dnipro River and the chestnut tree (каштан). (Source: Encyclopedia of Ukraine).
2. **Львів — місто кави та книг**: Lviv is famously known as the cultural capital of Ukraine, celebrated for its unique coffee traditions and the oldest printing house in Ukraine. (Source: esu.com.ua).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Case Confusion** (Accusative vs. Locative) → *Я йду в магазин* (Acc) vs. *Я в магазині* (Loc) — Learners often forget that prepositions like *в/на* take Accusative for motion and Locative for location.
2. **Gender Agreement** → *Мій велика машина* instead of *Моя велика машина* — Forgetting that adjectives and possessive pronouns must match the gender of the noun.
3. **Conjugation Mixing** → *Він читає* vs. *Він робить* — Mixing up the endings for Class I (-ати) and Class II (-ити) verbs.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Thematic Integration**: Use the "Trip around Ukraine" narrative to naturally revisit themes like Transport (§3.3), Food (§3.8), and Home (§3.2).
- **Immersion Level**: Aim for 35-40% immersion. Use English for instructions but keep the exam scenarios primarily in Ukrainian.
- **Supportive Tone**: As the "Patient Supportive Tutor," ensure the exam feels like a celebration of progress rather than a high-stress test. Use encouraging phrases like "Ви готові!" or "Чудова робота!".
- **Grammar Scope**: Limit verbs to present, past, and basic future (буду + infinitive) and keep nouns to singular/plural in the 4 taught cases (Nom, Acc, Loc, Voc).
