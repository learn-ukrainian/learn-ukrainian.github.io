## Cultural Hooks

1. **Каштани в Києві** — Chestnut trees line Kyiv's main boulevards; blooming in May is a city celebration. «Київські каштани» is a stock phrase even in elementary textbook dialogues. Concrete: «Навесні каштани цвітуть» — learner can recognize this on a postcard or tourist sign.
2. **Кава у Львові** — Lviv has the densest concentration of coffee houses in Ukraine; the city hosts a Coffee Festival. A tourist asking «Де тут кав'ярня?» is an authentic A1-level real-world scenario and ties directly to the locative drill.
3. **Класний журнал / оцінки** — Ukrainian school grading (1–12 scale, with 12 as excellent) differs from English systems. The word «оцінка» in a classroom self-assessment context resonates with young-adult learners and mirrors the textbook exercises above (шкільний щоденник, самооцінювання boxes in Grade 5/6 textbooks).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Accusative vs. Locative with в/на** → «Я йду *в магазин*» (motion, Acc) vs. «Я *у магазині*» (location, Loc) — English preposition "in" covers both; learners over-apply locative. Grade 5 Litvinova p.205 exercises use messenger-style dialogue to drill question/answer pairs that expose this distinction naturally.
2. **Gender agreement on adjectives** → «*Мій* велика машина» (Error) vs. «*Моя* велика машина» (Correct) — English has no grammatical gender; learners treat adjectives as invariant. Reinforced by the quiz format in the plan's activity_hints.
3. **Class I vs. Class II conjugation** → «Він *читає*» (Class I) vs. «Він *робить*» (Class II) — incorrect endings (-є vs. -ить) in 3rd person singular are the highest-frequency conjugation error at A1 exit.
4. **Писати stem mutation** → «Я *писаю*» (Error) vs. «Я *пишу*» (Correct) — -с-/-ш- alternation not visible in infinitive; learners apply regular Class I pattern blindly.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Open with a short realistic dialogue — a traveller arrives in Kyiv, buys a coffee, asks directions to the metro. Learner reads it and is asked: "Which sentences show WHERE something IS? Which show WHERE someone is GOING?" This surfaces the Acc/Loc contrast before any rule is stated, mirroring the Litvinova Grade 5 messenger-style exercise (p.205) where students discover sentence types by function.
- **Best context**: Travel narrative (transport → café → hotel → market) mirrors real tourist experience; all A1 vocabulary domains appear naturally without forcing.
- **Contrastive pairs for drilling**:
  - «Я в кафé» (Loc) ↔ «Я йду в кафé» (Acc)
  - «Він читає» (Cl.I) ↔ «Він робить» (Cl.II)
  - «Моя сумка» ↔ «Мій рюкзак» (gender agreement)

## Notes for Content Writing
- Tone is celebration-first: the module title is "Final Exam" but the persona is "Patient Supportive Tutor" — frame assessment as a celebration of 64 modules completed, not a test to fear.
- The travel narrative (Kyiv → Lviv) serves as both a cultural hook AND the vehicle for all grammar drills — keep it consistent throughout sections 2 and 3 rather than switching contexts.
- Section 3 ("Cultural Journey") mentions esu.com.ua as a source for Lviv's printing house — use only the factual hook (oldest printing house, Ivan Fedorov, 1574) without extended historical detail; learners need just enough cultural colour to feel the word кава/каштан is real, not invented.
- Activities: 15-item listening quiz, 30-item grammar/vocab quiz, 10-item oral fill-in — high item count means the activities YAML will be large; plan accordingly.
- Do NOT introduce Dative or Instrumental — mention them only as "coming in A2" in the transition section.
