## Cultural Hooks
1. **Ти vs Ви and possessives**: The formal/informal distinction extends to possessives — *твій* (informal "your") vs *Ваш* (formal "your," capitalized in writing). In Ukrainian culture, using *Ваш* with elders, teachers, and strangers is deeply ingrained etiquette, not optional politeness. Switching from *Ваш* to *твій* marks a relationship milestone. (Source: ukrainianlessons.com, cultural usage guides)
2. **Їхній vs Їх — decolonization marker**: The literary standard possessive is the declinable *їхній/їхня/їхнє*. Using the invariable *їх* as a possessive (e.g., *їх будинок*) is a Russianism (Russian uses invariable *их*). Teaching *їхній* reinforces authentic Ukrainian grammar and helps learners avoid surzyk. (Source: Ponomariv "Культура слова", language purity guidelines)
3. **Свій — the untranslatable possessive**: Ukrainian's reflexive possessive *свій* has no direct English equivalent. It signals "one's own" and is one of the first truly Slavic grammar concepts English speakers encounter. Native speakers use it instinctively; learners must consciously build the habit. (Source: e-school.net.ua, SLA research on reflexive acquisition)

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Gender mismatch**: Using *мій* with feminine nouns — *мій книга* → **моя книга**. English has no possessive gender agreement, so learners default to masculine forms.
2. **Свій avoidance**: Saying *Він любить його маму* (He loves his [someone else's] mother) when meaning *Він любить **свою** маму* (He loves his [own] mother). Failure to use *свій* creates ambiguity.
3. **Їхній → їх calque**: Using invariable *їх* instead of *їхній* (e.g., *їх дім* → **їхній дім**). Common among learners exposed to Russian-influenced materials.
4. **Nominative-only usage**: Learners memorize *мій/моя/моє* but freeze when a case change is needed — *Я бачу **мого** друга* (Accusative), not *Я бачу мій друг*. At A1, awareness of case changes in simple sentences is sufficient.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Lead with the *Чий? Чия? Чиє?* question framework — it gives learners a mental anchor before the paradigm tables.
- Present variable possessives (мій/твій/наш/ваш/їхній) first, then contrast with invariable (його/її) — the invariable forms are actually easier but make more sense after seeing the pattern.
- Keep *свій* at awareness level: introduce the concept with 2-3 clear contrastive examples (*свою* vs *його*) but don't demand productive use yet — that's A2.
- Use a "lost and found" scenario for Practice/Production — it naturally generates *Чия це сумка? Це моя сумка.* exchanges.
- Highlight the *їхній* (not *їх*) form explicitly as a language purity point — this connects to the decolonization thread across the curriculum.
- Vocative forms of possessives don't exist, but possessives + Vocative nouns appear naturally: *Мій друже!* — worth a brief note.
