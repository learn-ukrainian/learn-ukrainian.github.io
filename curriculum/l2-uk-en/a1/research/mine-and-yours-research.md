# Дослідження: Mine and Yours

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.2: "утворення родових форм присвійних займенників: мій, моя, моє; твій, твоя, твоє; наш, наша, наше; ваш, ваша, ваше, їхній, їхня, їхнє; ... утворення форм множини присвійних і вказівних займенників: мої, твої, наші, ваші, їхні, ті, ці."
Alignment: This module directly addresses the A1 requirement to form and use possessive pronouns (gender/number agreement) as specified in §4.2.2. It also introduces the reflexive possessive *свій* (required by the plan), which is fully standardized at A2 but essential for basic A1 communication.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Hospitality & Sharing**: Ukrainian culture emphasizes "what is mine is yours" with guests. Offering *свій* (one's own) food/comfort is a key part of hospitality.
2. **Formality in Possession**: The distinction between *твій* (your, informal) and *ваш* (your, formal/plural) mirrors the social distance of *ти/ви*. Using *ваш* with elders or strangers is mandatory respect.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Gender Disagreement**: *Мій мама* instead of *Моя мама*. → Learners often match the pronoun to their own gender or the "default" masculine, rather than the gender of the possessed noun.
2. **Його/Її Declension**: Trying to change *його* to *йога* or *її* to *їїя*. → *Його* and *її* are invariable when used as possessives.
3. **Їх vs Їхній**: Using *Це їх дім* (spoken/Russian influence) instead of *Це їхній дім* (Standard). → *Їх* is "them", *їхній* is "their".
4. **Свій vs Мій/Твій**: Using *Я люблю мою маму* instead of *Я люблю свою маму*. → While *мою* is understood, *свою* is more natural when the subject possesses the object.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Plan Deviation Note**: The plan includes *свій*, which is technically an A2 concept in the State Standard (§4.1.1.4), but the plan explicitly requests it. Introduce it simply as "one's own" for the subject, focusing on high-frequency phrases.
- **Pedagogy**: Use color-coding or visual tables to reinforce gender agreement (M/F/N/Pl).
- **Immersion**: Keep explanations in English (A1), but ensure examples are strictly Ukrainian with translations.
