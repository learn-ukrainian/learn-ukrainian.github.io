# Дослідження: Food, Drinks & Shopping

## State Standard Reference
§3.8 (Купівля): "споживчі товари (овочі, фрукти, м’ясо і молочні продукти, хліб, яйця); одиниці ваги й об’єму; гроші."
§4.2.3.2.1 (Знахідний відмінок): "Без прийменника (об’єкт дії): Віра читає цікаву книжку."
Alignment: This module directly addresses the thematic requirement of §3.8 and the grammatical requirement of Accusative case for direct objects (§4.2.3.2.1). Although Genitive case is strictly an A2 requirement (§4.2.2.2), the A1 thematic requirement for "units of weight and volume" (§3.8) necessitates a formulaic introduction of Genitive for quantities (пляшка води, кілограм яблук).

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Bread Sanctity**: "Хліб — усьому голова" (Bread is the head of everything). It is considered a sin to throw away bread; leftover bread is given to birds or animals.
2. **Hospitality**: Guests are traditionally welcomed with "хліб-сіль" (bread and salt) on an embroidered towel (rushnyk).
3. **Palianytsia**: A type of Ukrainian bread that became a shibboleth during the war due to pronunciation difficulty for non-natives.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Accusative Feminine**: "Я хочу *вода*" → "Я хочу **воду**" — Learners forget to change -а to -у for direct objects.
2. **Genitive Quantities**: "Пляшка *вода*" → "Пляшка **води**" — Learners fail to use Genitive after quantity words.
3. **Soup Verb**: "Пити суп" → "**Їсти** суп" — In Ukrainian, you "eat" soup (with a spoon), you don't "drink" it.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Genitive Handling**: Teach "quantity + noun" as fixed patterns/formulas rather than deep case theory, as Genitive is technically A2. Use visual patterns.
- **Visuals**: Use shopping lists and price tags to make the context authentic.
