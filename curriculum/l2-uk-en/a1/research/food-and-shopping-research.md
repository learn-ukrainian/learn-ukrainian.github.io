# Research Notes: Food, Drinks & Shopping (A1 M18)

**Track**: Core A
**Module**: M18 - Food, Drinks & Shopping
**Level**: A1 (Beginner)
**Researched**: 2026-02-08

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (ДСТУ 2024)**, level A1 (Elementary), the communicative intentions for the public sphere (Buying Goods) and the thematic scope are defined as follows:

> **§4.5. Вираження інтенцій (Комунікативні наміри):**
> "Здатність ініціювати, підтримувати та завершувати спілкування в типових повсякденних ситуаціях... зокрема купівля товарів у магазині, на ринку."
> *(The ability to initiate, maintain, and terminate communication in typical everyday situations... specifically buying goods in a store, at the market.)*

> **Каталог Б. Тематичний каталог (§3.8. Купівля (закупи)):**
> "Магазини і ринки; споживчі товари (овочі, фрукти, м’ясо і молочні продукти, хліб, яйця); засоби гігієни... одиниці ваги й об’єму; гроші."

The module utilizes **Знахідний відмінок (Accusative Case)** for the objects being purchased and **Родовий відмінок (Genitive Case)** for expressing quantity, price, and absence.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

### High Frequency (Crucial for A1)
- **Nouns**: хліб (bread), молоко (milk), вода (water), сир (cheese/curd), яблуко (apple), м’ясо (meat), риба (fish), яйця (eggs), овочі (vegetables), фрукти (fruits), магазин (store), ринок (market), ціна (price), гривня (hryvnia).
- **Verbs**: купувати (to buy), коштувати (to cost), бачити (to see), мати (to have).
- **Adjectives**: свіжий (fresh), смачний (tasty), великий (big), малий (small).
- **Phrases**: Скільки коштує? (How much does it cost?), Дайте, будь ласка... (Give [me], please...), У вас є...? (Do you have...?).

### Lower Frequency (Supplemental)
- **Nouns**: картопля (potato), морква (carrot), пакет (bag/packet), чек (receipt), здача (change), кошик (basket).
- **Adverbs**: багато (many/much), мало (few/little), тут (here), там (there).

### Common A1 Collocations
- **Свіжий хліб** (Fresh bread)
- **Кілограм яблук** (A kilogram of apples - *Genitive plural, usually taught as a fixed chunk at A1*)
- **Десять гривень** (Ten hryvnias)
- **Пакет молока** (A carton/bag of milk)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**The Ukrainian "Rynok" (Market) Culture:**
While supermarkets like *Silpo* or *ATB* are ubiquitous in modern Ukraine, the traditional *ринок* (open-air or covered market) remains the preferred place for many Ukrainians to buy fresh produce, dairy, and meat.
- **Freshness First**: At the market, products are often labeled as "домашній" (homemade/home-grown), which is a high mark of quality. Buying milk or cottage cheese (*домашній сир*) directly from farmers is a common practice.
- **Buying by Weight (На вагу)**: Almost everything, including sweets and cookies, is sold by weight (grams/kilograms). Even in supermarkets, it is standard to weigh your own vegetables and stick a price tag on the bag before reaching the checkout.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
- **The "Syr" Ambiguity**: In Ukrainian, **сир** can mean both "cheese" (hard cheese) and "cottage cheese/curd." To specify, people use *твердий сир* (hard cheese) or *домашній сир* (cottage cheese).
- **Plural Categories**: Words like *овочі* (vegetables) and *фрукти* (fruits) are almost always used in the plural when discussing shopping, whereas English sometimes uses "fruit" as an uncountable noun.
- **Hryvnia Declension**: Counting currency requires careful attention to case endings (1 гривня, 2-4 гривні, 5+ гривень). At A1, focus on the Nominative for 1 and fixed chunks for 2-10.

### Common Learner Errors
- **Accusative Endings**: Forgetting to change the -а to -у for feminine nouns (e.g., "Я купую риба" instead of "Я купую рибу").
- **Stress Shifts**: The word *молоко* (milk) has the stress on the last syllable [molo-KO], but learners often misplace it.
- **Gender Misidentification**: Thinking *м’ясо* (neuter) or *яблуко* (neuter) are feminine because they end in a vowel, leading to incorrect adjective agreement.

### Teaching Sequence
1. Introduce food nouns and categorization (Vegetables vs. Fruits).
2. Practice "У вас є...?" (Do you have...?) with Genitive for absence.
3. Introduce "Я купую..." with Accusative.
4. Integrate Numbers with "Скільки коштує?".

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope
- **Accusative Case**: Direct objects (Купую хліб, воду, рибу).
- **Genitive Case**: Absence (Немає молока) and basic price/quantity (Дві гривні).
- **Locative Case**: Location of shopping (У магазині, на ринку).
- **Present Tense**: Regular verbs (Я купую, він коштує).
- **Numbers**: 1-100 for prices and weights.

### OUT of Scope
- **Instrumental Case**: Paying *with* card/cash (*карткою/готівкою*). If mentioned, use only as fixed adverbial phrases.
- **Dative Case**: "Give *to me*" (*Дайте мені*). Stick to "Дайте, будь ласка" as a formula.
- **Verbal Aspect**: Do not teach the difference between *купувати* (impf.) and *купити* (perf.) yet; focus on the process/habit of shopping using the imperfective.
- **Genitive Plural**: Avoid complex declensions for counting (e.g., *п’ять яблук*). Use 1-4 to stay within simple Genitive singular bounds or teach higher numbers as fixed patterns.
