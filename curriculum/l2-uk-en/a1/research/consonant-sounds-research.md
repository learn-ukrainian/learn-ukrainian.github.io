# Дослідження: Consonant Sounds

## State Standard Reference
§4.1.4: "Vowel and consonant sounds" (lines 577–584)
Alignment: This module directly addresses the A1 phonetics requirement — learners distinguish consonant categories (voiced/voiceless, sonorant, hard/soft) and understand the no-final-devoicing rule unique to Ukrainian.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Скоромовка (tongue twister)**: "Жук жужжить над жабою жаб" — native Ukrainian tongue twister isolating Ж sound; textbooks use скоромовки for Ж/Ш drills (Kravtsova Grade 3 targets Ж/ДЖ/ДЗ pronunciation explicitly).
2. **Хліб is sacred**: In Ukrainian tradition, welcoming guests with хліб і сіль (bread and salt) is the highest symbol of hospitality. Both words appear in this module; pairing them creates a natural cultural anchor for the Х and С sounds.
3. **Вовк in folk tales**: The grey wolf (сірий вовк) appears in nearly every Ukrainian казка — Ivan Tsarevich, Mykyta the Fox. Children's familiarity with вовк across cultures makes it a memorable hook for the sonorant В.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Final devoicing (зуб → \*зуп)** → Correct: зу[б]. English and German devoice final consonants; Ukrainian does NOT. This is the #1 phonological error. Kravtsova Grade 3 explicitly drills "дзвінкі приголосні в кінці слова" — textbook exercises target this exact pattern.
2. **Г pronounced as English hard 'g'** → Correct: Г = voiced glottal/velar fricative (like a voiced 'h'). English speakers hear Г and default to the hard stop [g]. Only Ґ is [g]. Kravtsova Grade 3 has a dedicated section: "Правильна вимова слів зі звуком [ґ]" — showing textbooks treat this as a known error point.
3. **В pronounced as English V (teeth on lip)** → Correct: В in Ukrainian is a bilabial approximant, closer to English W. Learners must keep lips together, no teeth contact. Especially before О/У: вовк, вода sound almost like "wowk", "woda."
4. **Soft consonant confusion (лук vs люк)** → Learners don't hear palatalization as a phonemic distinction. English has no hard/soft consonant contrast. The лук/люк minimal pair forces attention to the feature.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show learners three word pairs on cards: зуб/суп, жук/шапка, гора/хор. Ask: "In each pair, what changed — just one letter. Which is voiced, which is voiceless?" BEFORE explaining the voiced/voiceless system, let learners try the hand-on-throat test themselves. Then reveal the pattern. This mirrors how Kravtsova Grade 3 approaches consonant work: exercises that prompt "дослідження мовних явищ" (language phenomena investigation) before rule delivery.
- **Best context**: The kitchen/home environment works best — хліб, сіль, суп, дім are all high-frequency domestic words that load naturally onto sound demonstrations without needing verbs.
- **Contrastive pairs for disambiguation**:
  - Г vs Ґ: гора (mountain) vs ґанок (porch) — "Ukrainian Г is like a whispered/voiced H, Ґ is the rare imported hard G"
  - В vs English V: вовк — hold a finger in front of lips, feel the air (В) vs feel the vibration on lip (English V)
  - лук vs люк — same letters, different quality of Л; meaning changes completely

## Notes for Content Writing

- Module is in A1.1 (First Contact phase) — ZERO verbs allowed. All examples must be bare nouns, Це + noun, or noun phrases.
- Section headers must be bilingual: `## Сонорні — Sonorant Consonants` (M1-14 rule from A1 quick-ref).
- The hand-on-throat test is a **physical exercise** — describe it in English, let students try with a few examples in a boxed callout.
- The Ґ restoration story (removed 1933, restored 1990) is pedagogically useful but keep it brief — one sentence. Frame it as a fact about the letter's rarity, not as a deep historical lesson.
- Word budget note: the plan allocates only 100 words for the introduction — keep it to a single orienting paragraph bridging from М2 vowels.
- Minimal pair лук/люк is the cleanest hard/soft demonstration — anchor the whole soft-consonant section around it.
