# Research Notes: At the Café (A1 M19)

**Track**: Core A
**Module**: M19 - At the Café
**Level**: A1 (Beginner)
**Researched**: 2026-02-08

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference

According to the **Державний стандарт української мови як іноземної (ДСТУ 2024)**, level A1 (Elementary), the communicative intentions for the public sphere (Public Catering) are defined as follows:

> **§4.5. Вираження інтенцій (Комунікативні наміри):**
> "Здатність ініціювати, підтримувати та завершувати спілкування в типових повсякденних ситуаціях... зокрема замовлення страв і напоїв у кафе, ресторані, їдальні."
> *(The ability to initiate, maintain, and terminate communication in typical everyday situations... specifically ordering dishes and drinks in a cafe, restaurant, or canteen.)*

The standard requires the use of **Знахідний відмінок (Accusative Case)** for objects of desire and **Родовий відмінок (Genitive Case)** for expressing absence or quantity, both of which are within the student's prior knowledge for this module.

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency

### High Frequency (Crucial for A1)
- **Nouns**: кава (coffee), чай (tea), сік (juice), вода (water), меню (menu), рахунок (bill), цукор (sugar), молоко (milk), офіціант (waiter), стіл/столик (table), замовлення (order).
- **Verbs**: хотіти (to want), замовити (to order), пити (to drink), їсти (to eat), коштувати (to cost).
- **Phrases**: Будь ласка (Please/Here you are), Дякую (Thank you), Смачного! (Enjoy your meal), Скільки коштує? (How much does it cost?).

### Lower Frequency (Supplemental)
- **Nouns**: лимон (lemon), вершки (cream), серветка (napkin), тістечко (pastry), чайові (tips), термінал (card terminal).
- **Adjectives**: чорний (black), зелений (green), холодний (cold), гарячий (hot).

### Common A1 Collocations
- **Чорна кава** (Black coffee)
- **Кава без цукру** (Coffee without sugar - *Genitive application*)
- **Склянка води** (A glass of water - *Genitive application*)
- **Чашка чаю** (A cup of tea)

## 3. Cultural Hook

**Lviv Coffee Culture (Львівська культура кави):**
Ukraine, specifically the city of Lviv, has a world-renowned coffee tradition dating back to the 18th century. Unlike the quick "espresso-to-go" culture in some Western countries, a visit to a Ukrainian *кав'ярня* (coffee house) is often a slow, social ritual. 
- **Fact**: In many traditional Lviv cafes, coffee is served in a *філіжанка* (a specific, elegant type of cup) rather than a standard *чашка*.
- **Daily Life**: It is standard practice in Ukraine for the waiter to ask "Разом чи окремо?" (Together or separately?) when bringing the bill to a group, reflecting a culture of splitting checks precisely.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes

### Key Differences from English
- **The "Please" Ambiguity**: In English, "please" is used only for requests. In Ukrainian, **"Будь ласка"** is used for "Please" AND "You're welcome" AND "Here you are" (when handing the menu or money).
- **Ordering Syntax**: English speakers often say "I'll have a coffee." In Ukrainian, using the future tense is too complex for A1. The construction **"Я хочу [Accusative]"** or the polite **"Можна [Accusative]?"** (May I [have]...?) is preferred.

### Common Learner Errors
- **Case Confusion**: Forgetting to change the ending of feminine nouns in the Accusative (e.g., saying "Я хочу кава" instead of "Я хочу каву").
- **Verb Conjugation**: Confusing the Group 1 and Group 2 endings for *пити* (to drink) and *їсти* (to eat). Note: *їсти* is irregular and should be taught as a formulaic chunk at this level.

### Teaching Sequence
1. Review Accusative endings (Feminine -у).
2. Introduce food/drink vocabulary.
3. Model the "Without" construction using Genitive (без цукру, без молока).
4. Practice price dialogues using Numbers.

## 5. Scope Boundaries

### IN Scope
- **Accusative Case**: Direct objects for ordering (Каву, чай, воду).
- **Genitive Case**: Negation/Absence (Немає кави, без цукру) and quantities (Склянка води).
- **Locative Case**: Stating location (Я в кафе, на терасі).
- **Numbers**: Handling the bill (Сто двадцять гривень).
- **Present Tense**: Basic desires (Я хочу, він хоче).

### OUT of Scope
- **Instrumental Case**: Students do **NOT** know the Instrumental case yet. Avoid "Coffee *with* milk" (*кава з молоком*) as a grammatical exercise; if used, teach it only as a fixed, invariable label.
- **Dative Case**: Do not use "Give *to me*" (*Дайте мені*). Use the imperative **"Дайте, будь ласка..."** as a fixed polite formula.
- **Conditional Mood**: Avoid "I would like" (*Я хотів би*). Stick to the present tense "Я хочу" or "Можна".
- **Past Tense**: Talking about what someone ate yesterday is not yet supported by prior knowledge.
