# Дослідження: Travel and Transport

## State Standard Reference
§3 (A1 Thematic Areas, lines 479–556): "подорожі" listed explicitly as one of the 12 required thematic domains at A1.
Alignment: This module directly satisfies the "подорожі" thematic requirement for A1, combining transport vocabulary, station navigation phrases, direction-giving (imperative), and journey description (instrumental + genitive constructions).

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Маршрутка culture**: The маршрутка (fixed-route minibus) is a uniquely post-Soviet Ukrainian institution — you hail it like a bus, pay the driver directly, and shout "Зупиніть, будь ласка!" when you want to get off. This is real daily-life Ukrainian (textbook Grade 6 Golub p.88 references таксометром and city transport as a teachable noun-grammar context). Makes маршрутка culturally sticky.

2. **"Доїхали комфортно? Подякуйте екіпажу!"**: Grade 5 Litvinova p.208 features a real Vinnytsia Transit Company campaign — passengers could thank tram/trolleybus/bus crews by scanning a QR code. Use as a real-world reading hook showing how transport and politeness intersect in Ukrainian public life.

3. **Directional folk pattern**: The paired opposition *наліво / направо* echoes Ukrainian казки (folk tales) — the crossroads choice "Ліворуч підеш — коня втратиш; праворуч підеш — сам загинеш" — makes direction vocabulary culturally resonant for story-minded learners.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Instrumental for transport — forgetting the case** → *Я їду потягом* (correct) vs *Я їду потяг / на потяг* (wrong) — English uses "by train" (preposition), Ukrainian uses instrumental suffix with no preposition. This is the key grammar point; the plan correctly flags it.

2. **Їхати vs летіти** → Learners say *Я їду літаком* instead of *Я лечу літаком* — conflating "to go/travel" with the plane-specific verb. Must pair the verb with context.

3. **метро declension** → Learners write *в метрі, метром* — метро is indeclinable. Only форма метро exists. Contrast with трамвай (declinable).

4. **Directions: Поверніть vs Поверни** → Formal (Поверніть) vs informal (Поверни) imperative distinction confuses beginners. At A1, use Поверніть/Ідіть as the default register (asking strangers = formal). Grade 5 Litvinova's exercise on exclamatory sentences (p.208) models this register.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Show learners a short dialogue at a Kyiv metro station:
  > — Вибачте, як дістатися до вокзалу?
  > — Ідіть прямо, потім поверніть направо. Станція — ліворуч.
  Ask: *How is travel by train expressed differently from travel by metro?* Let learners notice потягом (suffix, no preposition) vs на метро (preposition + indeclinable). Then reveal the pattern.

- **Best context**: City navigation — asking a passerby for directions is the highest-frequency real-life use. Pair it with transport choices: "I need to get to the train station — should I take the metro or a маршрутка?" This mirrors Grade 5 Litvinova p.208's "Доїхали комфортно?" exercise, which frames transport as a social interaction.

- **Contrastive pairs**:
  - *їхати потягом* vs *летіти літаком* (instrumental + correct verb)
  - *на метро* vs *автобусом* (на + indeclinable vs pure instrumental)
  - *Поверніть наліво* vs *Ідіть прямо* (two imperative forms, two direction types)

## Notes for Content Writing

- The plan includes Instrumental case for transport (потягом, автобусом) — this is flagged as **A2 grammar** in the A1 Quick Reference ("Instrumental case (A2)"). However, the plan explicitly lists it in `grammar:` and `vocabulary_hints:`, which constitutes a plan-level exemption. Teach it as a vocabulary pattern ("these words have a special travel form") rather than case grammar instruction. Keep the explanation English, in a pattern box.
- метро is indeclinable — use it in examples but do NOT show declined forms.
- Textbook Grade 6 Golub p.88 mentions таксі (indeclinable, paid-by-meter) — good parallel with метро for the indeclinable noun pattern.
- Keep journey descriptions to *Я їду до Львова потягом* (S+V+destination+means) — maximum 10 Ukrainian words per sentence per A1 constraints.
