# Дослідження: Demonstratives

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.1.4: "Personal pronouns — basic declension" / §4.2.2: "Pronoun gender and number forms"
Alignment: This module covers the demonstrative pronoun subclass (цей/ця/це/ці, той/та/те/ті), which falls under expanded pronoun morphology (§4.2.1.4, lines 1244–1254 in the A2 band — but introduced at A1 level as foundational demonstrative forms). The gender/number agreement aspect maps directly to §4.2.2.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Market/café scenario**: Ukrainians use цей/той constantly when shopping. "Дайте мені цей торт" (proximal, pointing at cake in front) vs "той торт" (pointing at one further away) — authentic, high-frequency real-life usage.
2. **та ambiguity in folk sayings**: "Та дівчина та її подруга пішли на ринок" — the double та (demonstrative + conjunction) appears in spoken Ukrainian naturally and causes confusion for learners. This is a genuine disambiguation challenge.
3. **Classroom pointing game**: Ukrainian teachers use physical demonstration extensively — pointing at objects in the room (цей стіл, та дошка, це вікно) — a natural kinesthetic hook for spatial demonstratives.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **це as only form** → English "this" is invariable, so learners use це for all genders: *це хлопець instead of цей хлопець, *це дівчина instead of ця дівчина. Root cause: L1 transfer.
2. **Copula це confused with demonstrative це** → Learners write "Це стіл гарний" (copula + adjective, ungrammatical) instead of "Цей стіл гарний" (demonstrative + adj) or "Це гарний стіл" (copula sentence). Critical distinction: це alone = "This is…"; це + noun phrase = demonstrative modifying the noun.
3. **та read as conjunction only** → Learners parse "Та книга цікава" as a fragment starting with "and," missing the demonstrative function. Context dependency must be explicitly modeled.
4. **Singular demonstrative with plural noun** → *цей книги instead of ці книги. English has no morphological distinction "this/these," making plural agreement invisible to beginners.

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Show learner three objects on a table (book, pen, city on a map). Ask: "Which word do I use here?" Display: "Це ____ олівець." "Це ____ ручка." "Це ____ місто." — then reveal цей/ця/це with the noun. Learner notices the pattern changes. Then add a second set "over there" to discover той/та/те. The learner infers the rule from paired minimal sentences before reading any explanation.
- **Best context**: Café or market — pointing at items (cakes, books, chairs). Natural spatial reference ("this one here / that one over there") makes proximity contrast feel real rather than abstract.
- **Contrastive pairs**:
  - Це стіл (This is a table — copula) vs Цей стіл великий (This table is big — demonstrative)
  - Та книга (That book) vs …та її подруга (…and her friend) — same form, different function
  - Цей стілець (this chair, near) vs той стілець (that chair, far)

## Notes for Content Writing
- The цей/ця/це paradigm parallels мій/моя/моє already taught in M20 — explicitly signal this analogy, it greatly reduces cognitive load.
- The copula vs demonstrative distinction for це is the hardest concept; give it its own mini-section with a contrast table.
- Keep the та ambiguity section brief but memorable — one clear example sentence is enough; don't overload it.
- All Ukrainian example sentences must be ≤10 words (A1 constraint). The plan examples are fine.
- No imperative verbs in instructions — use English ("Notice that…", "Look at the table…").
- Section headers: Ukrainian with English in parentheses (M21 is M15+, so this applies).
