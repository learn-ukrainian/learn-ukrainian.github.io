# Дослідження: Tomorrow - Future Tense

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.1: "Indicative mood — present, past, future tenses; conjugation I/II; reflexive verbs (-ся/-сь)"
Alignment: This module covers the compound future specifically (буду + infinitive). Fits squarely within the §4.2.4.1 indicative mood scope. Synthetic future (працюватиму) is mentioned as FYI recognition only — consistent with the standard's A1 expectation of basic future formation.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Proverb**: «Не кажи "гоп", поки не перескочиш» — "Don't say 'hop' until you've jumped." Direct warning against over-planning; contrasts nicely with буду (confident future) vs хочу/збираюся (hedged intention). Authentic and very common in everyday speech.
2. **Folk optimism phrase**: «Завтра буде новий день» — culturally resonant especially since 2022 as an expression of resilience. Real-life usage: WhatsApp messages, social media. Demonstrates буду + adjective pattern in a culturally meaningful context.
3. **Weekend planning talk**: Planning "плани на вихідні" is a universal conversation opener in Ukrainian workplaces and friendships. Common question: «Які у тебе плани на вихідні?» — sets up the entire activity layer of the module naturally.

---

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Aspect confusion**: Using буду with perfective infinitive → «буду прочитати» ✗ → «буду читати» ✅. Happens because English "will read" doesn't mark aspect. This is the #1 error and must be pre-empted with explicit rule + paired examples before learners start producing.
2. **Omitting буду**: Learners write «Я читати книгу» calquing English bare infinitive. Reinforce that буду is mandatory — the compound future requires both parts.
3. **Genitive for time expressions**: «наступний тиждень» ✗ → «наступного тижня» ✅. Direct English calque ("next week" stays nominative). Needs a clear pattern box; no explanation of why at A1 — just "these time phrases use -ого/-ого form."
4. **Mixing хочу / збираюся / буду**: Learners collapse all three into "will." Brief disambiguation: буду = confident plan, збираюся = I'm going to (intention), хочу = I want to (desire). Contrastive table beats prose explanation.

## Teaching Strategy

- **Discovery exercise**: Present three speech bubbles with the same verb, different time contexts — "Я **читаю** книгу зараз." / "Я **читав** книгу вчора." / "Я **буду читати** книгу завтра." Ask: "What changes? What stays the same?" Learner notices the pattern before any rule is stated. Works on the tense timeline established in A1-036 (Yesterday).
- **Best context**: Weekend planning conversation. "Які у тебе плани на суботу?" is instantly meaningful and forces all three target structures (буду + infinitive, time adverbs, наступного тижня).
- **Contrastive pairs**: буду читати ✅ vs буду прочитати ✗ (aspect); завтра vs наступного тижня (time expression form change); буду їхати vs збираюся їхати (confidence vs intention).

## Notes for Content Writing
- Synthetic future (працюватиму) appears in Section 2 as FYI only — one brief callout box, no production required. Learners will see it in authentic content (news headlines, song lyrics) and need passive recognition.
- Genitive for time expressions (наступного тижня/року): treat as a memorized chunk pattern, not a case explanation. "These time phrases change their ending" — no case name needed at A1.
- All VESUM lookups passed (16/16). Use stress marks on: завтра, наступного, тижня, скоро.
- The persona is Event Planner — lean into scheduling language (мати план, складати розклад, планувати зустріч) across examples.
