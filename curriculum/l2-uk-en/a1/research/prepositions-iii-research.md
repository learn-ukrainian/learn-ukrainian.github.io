# Research: A1 M30 "Prepositions III"
## Subtitle: Direction, Location & Origin

**Level:** A1 | **Module:** 30
**Researched:** 2026-02-08

## 1. Grammar: State Standard 2024 Reference
The topic of prepositions for spatial orientation is covered across several sections of the *Державний стандарт української мови (2024)* for the A1 level.

*   **Accusative for Direction (§ 4.2.3.2.2):** 
    > "в, у, на (указівка на кінцевий пункт руху або переміщення): Степан заходить у кімнату. Я кладу книжку на твій стіл."
*   **Locative for Location (§ 4.2.3.3):**
    > "у, в, на (позначення місця): Спортивний костюм у великій сумці. Студент працює в новій лабораторії. Гроші лежать на столі."
*   **Phonetic Variants of Prepositions (§ 4.1.7):**
    > "уживання прийменника з і його варіантів із, зі: з Алжиру, він із Сирії, зі школи."
*   **Genitive for Origin (A2 § 4.2.2.2.2 - applied as A1 communicative model):**
    > "з, із, зі (вихідний пункт руху – звідки?): Вони приїхали з Об’єднаних Арабських Еміратів."
    *Note: While origin is often formally categorized at A2, A1 students (having learned Genitive in M16) are expected to use these in "ready-made communicative models" for basic introductions (e.g., "Я з України").*

## 2. Vocabulary Frequency
The focus is on the "spatial triad" of interrogatives and their corresponding prepositions.

| Category | Interrogative | Prepositions | Case | Frequency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Location** | **Де?** (Where?) | в/у, на, біля | Locative | High |
| **Direction** | **Куди?** (Where to?) | в/у, на, до | Accusative / Genitive | High |
| **Origin** | **Звідки?** (Where from?) | з, із, зі | Genitive | High |

**Key Vocabulary Items:**
*   **High Frequency:** де, куди, звідки, в/у, на, до, з, через, тут, там, близько, далеко.
*   **Common Collocations:** 
    *   *на роботі* (at work), *на вокзалі* (at the station), *додому* (home - adverb).
    *   *через парк* (through the park), *біля метро* (near the metro).

## 3. Cultural Hook
*   **Institutional "На":** In Ukrainian culture and language, certain public spaces and institutions strictly take the preposition **"на"** (on/at) instead of **"в"** (in). Common examples essential for A1 students include *на пошті* (at the post office), *на вокзалі* (at the station), and *на роботі* (at work). Using *в роботі* or *в пошті* is a common marker of non-native speech.
*   **Phonetic Harmony (Euphony):** The variation of *з / із / зі* is not just grammatical but deeply cultural in its pursuit of "милозвучність" (euphony). For example, **"зі Львова"** is mandatory to avoid a difficult consonant cluster (*з Лв...*). This reflects the Ukrainian linguistic priority of "melodic" speech.

## 4. Pedagogical Notes
*   **The Accusative/Locative Trap:** English often uses "at" or "in" for both location ("I am at school") and direction ("I am going to school"). Ukrainian students must learn to distinguish by the interrogative: *Де?* (Location -> Locative) vs. *Куди?* (Direction -> Accusative).
*   **"До" vs "В/На":** Students often confuse *до* (towards/to) and *в/на* (into/onto). A good rule of thumb for A1 is that *в/на* implies entering or arriving at a surface, while *до* is more general "to" or "towards a destination/person."
*   **Common Error:** Using the Nominative after prepositions. At M30, students should be reinforced on the specific case endings for Accusative, Locative, and Genitive.

## 5. Scope Boundaries
**In Scope:**
*   **Interrogatives:** *Де, куди, звідки.*
*   **Prepositions:** *в/у, на* (Acc/Loc), *до* (Gen), *з/із/зі* (Gen), *через* (Acc), *біля* (Gen).
*   **Cases:** Accusative (direction), Locative (location), Genitive (origin/destination).
*   **Tenses:** Present, Past, Future (e.g., "Я був у Києві", "Я поїду до Львова").

**Out of Scope:**
*   **Cases:** **Dative** (e.g., motion towards a person *до вчителя* is Genitive, but *йти до когось* vs *дати комусь* is different) and **Instrumental** (e.g., *над, під, перед, за, між* for location).
*   **Complex Prepositions:** *з-за* (from behind), *з-під* (from under), *посеред* (in the middle of).
*   **Abstract Prepositions:** Using these spatial prepositions for abstract concepts (e.g., "in terms of", "from my perspective"). Keep it purely physical and spatial.
