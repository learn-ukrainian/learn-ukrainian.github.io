Alignment: This module directly addresses the A1 requirement for distinguishing location (Locative case) from direction (Accusative case) using the prepositions *в/у* and *на*. It also introduces the concept of origin (*звідки*) as outlined in the phonetics/euphony section of the A1 standard.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Ukrzaliznytsia (Ukrainian Railways)**: Trains are the pulse of Ukraine. The question "Звідки ти приїхав?" (Where did you arrive from?) is a classic icebreaker in sleeper cars (плацкарт/купе), where total strangers share tea and life stories while traveling between cities like Kyiv, Lviv, and Odessa.
2. **The "Slipper" Culture**: In Ukraine, it is mandatory to remove outdoor shoes when entering a home. A guest is immediately asked "Де твої капці?" (Where are your slippers?) or offered a pair. This highlights the static location (вдома/у квартирі) as a clean, sacred space. (Source: common social etiquette verified by travel guides).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Static vs. Dynamic confusion**: Using *Де* for direction. 
   * *Неправильно:* Де ти йдеш? → *Правильно:* Куди ти йдеш? — "Де" is for location, "Куди" for destination.
2. **Preposition selection (в vs. на)**: English speakers often use "in" where Ukrainian requires "on" for functional places.
   * *Неправильно:* в роботі → *Правильно:* на роботі — Concepts like work, post office, or stadium are perceived as "open surfaces" or "functions" rather than just containers.
3. **Case Mismatch after "до"**: Learners often use the Locative after "до" because it means "to".
   * *Неправильно:* до школі → *Правильно:* до школи — "До" strictly requires the Genitive case.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Visual Contrast**: Use a "Decision Table" or Mermaid diagram to show the relationship between the question (Де/Куди/Звідки) and the case/preposition combo.
- **Euphony Rule**: Reinforce the **у/в** rule (у before consonants, в before vowels/after vowels) as students will be using these constantly in this module.
- **Supportive Tone**: A1 students struggle with cases. Frame the "Case change" not as a burden, but as a "signpost" that tells the listener if you are staying or going.
