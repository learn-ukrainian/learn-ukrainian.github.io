# Research: Can, Must, Want — Modals

**Level:** A1 | **Module:** 24
**Researched:** 2026-02-08

## State Standard Reference
- **Grammar Reference (§4.2.4.1):** "дієвідмінювання дієслів у теперішньому часі: ... могти (можу, можеш, може, можемо, можете, можуть)". This section explicitly lists *могти* as a core verb for conjugation at the A1 level.
- **Communicative Intentions (§1.1.2.1.2):** "вказівки, наприклад, щодо напрямку руху... Треба йти прямо. ... Треба читати. Читаємо." This establishes the use of the impersonal modal *треба* for giving directions and instructions.
- **Communicative Intentions (§1.1.2.2):** "дуже прості вказівки про можливість отримати їжу, воду, першу допомогу тощо: Будь ласка, дайте воду / чай / каву / сік. Я хочу їсти / пити. Де їдальня / кафе / ресторан? Мені треба лікаря / ліки / швидку допомогу." This anchors *хочу* and *треба* in survival-level communication.
- **Level Requirement:** A1 students must be able to express ability, desire, and necessity using basic modal constructions followed by an infinitive.

## Vocabulary Frequency
- **High-Frequency Modal Verbs:**
  - **могти** (can/be able to): #1 modal verb. Essential for requests and expressing possibility.
  - **хотіти** (want): High frequency for expressing desires and needs.
  - **треба** (it is necessary / one must): Extremely common impersonal modal. Used more frequently than "must" in English for daily tasks.
  - **мусити** (must/have to): Used for strong personal obligation or external pressure.
  - **вміти** (can/know how to): High frequency for expressing learned skills (e.g., "I can speak Ukrainian").
- **Collocations & Common Phrases:**
  - *Я хочу додому.* (I want to go home.)
  - *Я хочу спати.* (I want to sleep.)
  - *Я можу допомогти.* (I can help.)
  - *Тобі треба йти.* (You must go.)
  - *Мені треба допомога.* (I need help.)
  - *Чи можна...?* (May I...?) — The most frequent way to ask permission.
- **Frequency Note:** *Могти*, *хотіти*, and *треба* are all within the top 200 most frequent words in Ukrainian.

## Cultural Hook (verified)
- **Fact 1: The "Треба" Philosophy.** In Ukrainian daily life, the impersonal *треба* (it is necessary) is often preferred over the personal *я мушу* (I must). This usage subtly shifts the focus from the individual's willpower to a general necessity or social expectation. For example, instead of saying "I must wash the dishes," a Ukrainian is more likely to say "Dishes need washing" (*Треба помити посуд*).
- **Fact 2: Ability vs. Willpower in Proverbs.** A popular Ukrainian motivational phrase is *"Хто хоче, той може"* (He who wants, can). It highlights the cultural belief that desire (*хотіти*) is the primary driver of capability (*могти*).
- **Fact 3: Politeness and Permission.** The word *можна* (one can/it is allowed) is the universal key to politeness in Ukraine. Asking *"Можна?"* when entering a room or *"Чи можна запитати?"* (May I ask?) is the standard A1-level etiquette for respectful interaction.

## Pedagogical Notes
- **Stem Changes (The "Hissing" Shift):**
  - *Могти* → *можу* (г → ж shift).
  - *Хотіти* → *хочу* (т → ч shift).
  - *Мусити* → *мушу* (с → ш shift).
  - Learners often struggle with these shifts and try to conjugate them like regular Group 1 verbs (*хотю*, *могу*).
- **Subject Case Contrast:**
  - **Nominative:** Used with *могти*, *хотіти*, *мусити*, *вміти* (*Я хочу*, *Ти можеш*).
  - **Dative:** Used with *треба*, *можна*, *потрібно* (*Мені треба*, *Тобі можна*). Since Dative is not fully taught yet, these should be introduced as fixed pronominal phrases (*Мені треба*, *Тобі треба*).
- **"Can" vs. "Can":** English uses "can" for both physical ability and learned skills. Ukrainian distinguishes between **могти** (situational/physical possibility: "I can lift this box") and **вміти** (acquired skill: "I can play the piano").
- **Negation:** The particle *не* always precedes the modal (*не хочу*, *не можу*). Note that *не треба* often carries a sense of "don't" (prohibition) in addition to "not necessary."

## Scope Boundaries
- **IN SCOPE:**
  - Present tense conjugation of *могти*, *хотіти*, *мусити*, *вміти*.
  - Use of *треба* and *можна* with infinitives.
  - Basic subject forms for *треба* (мені, тобі, нам, вам).
  - Using modals with previously learned infinitives (читати, робити, їсти, пити, йти).
- **OUT OF SCOPE:**
  - **Conditional Mood:** *Я хотів би* (I would like) is reserved for B1.
  - **Perfective Modals:** *Змогти*, *схотіти* (except perhaps *зможу* as a lexical item for "I will be able to").
  - **Full Dative Case:** Noun subjects with *треба* (*Івану треба*) should be avoided unless taught as a formula.
  - **Instrumental Case:** Forms like *Я повинен бути обережним* (I should be careful) are too complex for A1; stick to *треба* + verb.
  - **Gerunds/Participles:** *Потрібний*, *бажаний* are out of scope.
