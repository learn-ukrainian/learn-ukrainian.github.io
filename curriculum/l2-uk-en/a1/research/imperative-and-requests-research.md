# Дослідження: Imperative and Requests

## State Standard Reference
§4.2.4.2: "Imperative mood — 2nd person only at A1" (lines 721–725)
Alignment: This module directly delivers the §4.2.4.2 requirement. Allowed forms: 2nd person singular (ти-form) and plural (ви-form). Forbidden at A1: 1st person plural (читаймо), 3rd person (хай/нехай) — those belong to A2 §4.2.3.2.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Classroom song refrain** — The textbook excerpt (Grade 7, litvinova p.60) features the ТНМК song "Історія України за 5 хвилин" with imperatives in the refrain: *Подивись наліво, подивись направо* — a living example of ти-form imperative in a famous Ukrainian pop-patriotic song. Learners can hear the form in context.
2. **Folk proverb pattern** — Ukrainian proverbs frequently use bare imperatives: *Не кажи "гоп", поки не перескочиш* ("Don't say 'hop' before you jump" ≈ Don't count your chickens). The negative imperative is embedded in everyday folk wisdom — a natural discovery hook.
3. **Market/service register** — Real-life Ukrainian market interactions use a very specific pattern: vendor says *Беріть, беріть!* (Take it, take it!) to invite customers; customer says *Дайте, будь ласка* (Please give me). This ти/ви contrast in commercial settings is authentic and memorable.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Infinitive instead of imperative** → *Читати!* used as a command instead of *Читай!* — Happens because English uses base form ("Read!") and learners map it to the Ukrainian infinitive. Textbook Вправа 84 (Grade 7, litvinova p.62) targets exactly this: learners must transform bracketed infinitives into imperative forms.
2. **Wrong ви-form suffix** → *\*читайте* correct, but *\*пишайте* instead of *пишіть* — Learners overapply -те to all verbs. Must teach: -айте (Group I stems in -а) vs. -іть (Group II stems in consonant). The suffix depends on the stem type.
3. **Forgetting к→ж mutation in сказати** → *\*скажиte* → *скажіть* is correct, but learners write *\*скажайте* by analogy. Mutation сказати → скажи is irregular and must be memorized.
4. **Будь ласка position confusion** — English speakers place "please" at sentence start ("Please give me"); Ukrainian strongly prefers post-verb position: *Дайте, будь ласка*, not *\*Будь ласка дайте* (though both are grammatical, the post-verb form is more natural).

## Teaching Strategy
- **Discovery exercise**: Show learners 4 signs from a Ukrainian classroom: a poster reading *СЛУХАЙ УВАЖНО*, a board instruction *ЧИТАЙТЕ ТЕКСТ*, a teacher's speech bubble *Скажи мені*, and a "No smoking" sign *Не курити!* — without explanation, ask: "What do all these have in common? What's different about the last one?" Learners notice: the first three are direct personal commands, the last is impersonal (infinitive). This mirrors Вправа 79 from Grade 7 litvinova (p.60), where students identify and analyze imperative forms in the ТНМК song before the rule is explained.
- **Best context**: Classroom commands is the perfect A1 container — learners encounter *Читайте, пишіть, слухайте, дивіться* in every lesson. Market/café context (Дайте, будь ласка) adds a second authentic register.
- **Contrastive pairs**: (1) ти vs. ви form: *Читай* (to a friend/child) vs. *Читайте* (to a group or adult); (2) command vs. request: *Дай!* (bare command) vs. *Дайте, будь ласка* (polite request); (3) personal imperative vs. public prohibition: *Не кури!* vs. *Не курити!* (sign).

## Notes for Content Writing
- The 8 required verbs split into two formation groups: stem-final vowel group (-айте: читай, слухай, показуй) and consonant group (-іть: пиши → пишіть, говори → говоріть). Make this visual — a two-column table helps.
- Стояти → стій is the most irregular; flag it explicitly. Learners who try to derive it will fail.
- The Чи не могли б ви...? pattern (indirect request) is genuinely A2+ conditional, but including it as a "bonus polite formula" at A1 is fine since it's memorized as a chunk, not analyzed. Keep it brief.
- дивитися imperative: the -ся drops. *Дивись* not *\*Дивися*. Worth calling out explicitly.
- Do NOT introduce 1st person plural (читаймо) or хай/нехай — those are A2 (§4.2.3.2).
