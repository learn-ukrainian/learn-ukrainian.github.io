## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Origin of 'Hryvnia'**: The name comes from the Kyivan Rus' "hryvna," a silver or gold ingot or neck ornament (torc) worn around the neck ("hryva" meaning mane/neck). It served as both decoration and a unit of weight/currency.
2. **The ₴ Symbol**: Officially adopted in 2004, the symbol is based on a cursive Cyrillic letter "г" with two horizontal lines symbolizing stability, aligning it visually with the Euro (€) and Yen (¥).
3. **The Proverb**: "Копійка береже гривню" (A kopeck saves a hryvnia) — the Ukrainian equivalent of "a penny saved is a penny earned," emphasizing frugality.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Gender of 1 and 2**: Using "один" or "два" for feminine nouns like "гривня".
   - *Error*: два гривні → *Correct*: дві гривні.
   - *Why*: Numbers 1 and 2 are the only cardinals 1-10 with gendered forms (один/одна/одне, два/дві).
2. **The 1-2-5 Rule (Case Agreement)**: Using the wrong case after numbers.
   - *Error*: п'ять гривні → *Correct*: п'ять гривень.
   - *Why*: Learners struggle with the jump from Nominative Plural (2-4) to Genitive Plural (5+).
3. **Soft Sign in Tens vs Teens**: Confusing where the 'ь' goes.
   - *Error*: п'ятнадцять vs п'ятдесят.
   - *Why*: In 'teens' (11-19), the soft sign is at the end; in 'tens' (50-80), there is no soft sign between the roots or at the end in modern orthography (e.g., п'ятдесят).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Visual Agreement Tables**: Crucial for A1. Show clearly: 1 (Nom.Sg) | 2, 3, 4 (Nom.Pl) | 5-20, 0 (Gen.Pl).
- **Phonetics**: Focus on the pronunciation of 'п'ять' [pjat'] and the shifting stress in 'одина́дцять' vs 'двадця́ть'.
- **Modern Pricing**: Use realistic 2024 prices (e.g., Coffee ~ 40-60 UAH) to keep the lesson practical for travelers.
