## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Origin of the Name**: The word "hryvnia" (гривня) comes from the ancient Slavic "hryvna" (гривна), which was a type of neck jewelry (torc) made of gold or silver. In Kyivan Rus, these pieces of jewelry were used as weight and accounting units before becoming official currency.
2. **The ₴ Symbol**: The official symbol for the Hryvnia (₴) was introduced in 2004. It consists of a cursive letter "г" (representing the first letter of the name) with two horizontal lines symbolizing stability, similar to the Euro (€) or Yen (¥) symbols.
3. **Modern Pricing**: In 2024-2025, a standard cup of coffee in Kyiv costs approximately 35–45 UAH, a loaf of bread is about 35 UAH, and a 0.5L bottle of water is roughly 15–20 UAH.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Number-Noun Agreement (The 1-2-5 Rule)**: Learners often use the same form for all numbers. 
   - Error: *п'ять гривні* → Correct: **п'ять гривень** (Genitive Plural).
   - Rule: 1 = Nominative Singular, 2-4 = Nominative Plural, 5+ = Genitive Plural.
2. **Soft Sign Placement**: Learners often misplace the soft sign in 11-19 vs 50-80.
   - Error: *п'ятьдесят* → Correct: **п'ятдесят** (no soft sign in the middle of tens).
   - Error: *п'ятнадцять* → Correct: **п'ятнадцять** (soft sign stays at the end).
3. **Currency Gender**: Forgetting that "hryvnia" is feminine.
   - Error: *один гривня* → Correct: **одна гривня**.

- Use the "Bazaar Accountant" persona to create a fun, interactive shopping scenario.
- Mention that coins (kopecks) are rarely used in cash transactions now (1, 2, 5, 25 are out of circulation, 10 and 50 are rare), but remain in digital pricing.
