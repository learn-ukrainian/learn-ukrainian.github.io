# Syllables and Word Division

> **Чому це важливо? — Why does this matter?**
>
> Breaking words into syllables is the master key to reading Ukrainian fluently. By learning how to divide words correctly, you will never feel overwhelmed by long, complex words again. Це важливо. (This is important.)

## Що таке склад? — What Is a Syllable?

Ласкаво просимо! Welcome to the next step in reading Ukrainian! Це алфавіт. (This is the alphabet.) Це літера. (This is a letter.) You already know the alphabet, and you can recognize individual letters. Now, we will look at how those letters group together to form words. The building block of every word is the syllable, or **склад** (syllable). Це склад. (This is a syllable.)

When you look at a long word, it can seem intimidating. But if you break it down into smaller, manageable pieces, reading becomes much easier. This is where syllables come in. Це слово. (This is a word.)

In Ukrainian, counting syllables is incredibly straightforward. There is a golden rule you can always rely on: the number of syllables in a word is exactly equal to the number of vowels, or **голосні** (vowels), it contains. Це голосна. (This is a vowel.)

Every single syllable must have one—and only one—vowel. Vowels are the heart and soul of the syllable. Consonants just attach themselves around this vowel core. Це приголосна. (This is a consonant.)

Let us look at a short word:
- **кіт** (cat)

Це кіт. (This is a cat.) This word has exactly one vowel: the letter **і**. Because it has one vowel, it has one syllable. Це склад. (This is a syllable.) You pronounce it all in one breath.

А тепер довге слово. And now a long word:
- **молоко́** (milk)

Це молоко. (This is milk.) Count the vowels. We see the letter **о** three times. Three vowels mean three syllables. When we break it apart, we get **мо-ло-ко**. Це слово. (This is a word.)

А як щодо цього слова? What about this word?
- **Украї́на** (Ukraine)

Це Україна. (This is Ukraine.) Let us count the vowels: **У**, **а**, **ї**, **а**. That makes four vowels. Therefore, the word has exactly four syllables: **У-кра-ї-на**.

> [!warning] Обережно! Careful!
> English speakers often try to group syllables based on how English words sound, which can be tricky. In English, you might have silent vowels. In Ukrainian, every single vowel you see is fully pronounced. Це звук. (This is a sound.) You never skip a vowel, and you never guess the number of syllables. Just count the vowels!

By remembering to look for the vowels first, you will instantly know how many beats the word has. Next time you see a long word, do not panic. Simply count the vowels, and you will know exactly how many parts make up the word. Це просто. (This is simple.)

## Типи складів — Open and Closed Syllables

Now let's talk about two types of syllables: open and closed. This is very important.

An open syllable is one that ends in a vowel. Це правило. (This is a rule.) This is the absolute favorite type of syllable in the Ukrainian language. Українська мова любить відкриті склади. The Ukrainian language loves open syllables. Most of the time, words will try to break in a way that leaves syllables open.

For example, look at these syllables: **ма-**, **но-**, **ти-**, **со-**. They end in vowels, so they are perfectly open. Це правило. (This is a rule.)

Розглянемо слово «вулиця». Let's consider the word for street:
- **ву́лиця** (street)

Це вулиця. (This is a street.) If we break it down, we get **ву-ли-ця**. Every single part ends in a vowel. All three are open syllables! This makes the word sound smooth and melodic.

A closed syllable, on the other hand, ends in a consonant. Це правило. (This is a rule.) You will typically find these at the very end of a word or before a group of consonants. 

For example, syllables like **кін-**, **мак-**, or **бук-** are closed because they end with a consonant.

Look at the word for bus:
- **авто́бус** (bus)

Це автобус. (This is a bus.) Here, the breakdown is **ав-то-бус**. The first two parts are open, but the final part, **-бус**, ends in a consonant. That is a closed syllable. Це правило. (This is a rule.)

> [!culture] Українська мелодія. Ukrainian melody.
> Ukrainian is often described as a highly melodic language. One of the main reasons for this musical quality is its strong preference for open syllables!

The most interesting difference from English happens when you have multiple consonants in a row. How do Ukrainians split them?

The rule is to push the consonants forward to the beginning of the next syllable to keep the current syllable open. This is called the principle of maximal onset. Це принцип. (This is a principle.)

Наприклад, слово «сестра». For example, the word "sister":
- **сестра́** (sister)

Це сестра. (This is a sister.) An English speaker might want to split this as **сес-тра**. But Ukrainian pushes the consonant cluster forward to keep the first part open. The correct split is **се-стра**.

Ще один приклад. Another example:
- **о́стрів** (island)

Це острів. (This is an island.) Instead of breaking it as **ос-трів**, the correct Ukrainian division pushes the consonants to the next syllable, giving us **о-стрів**.

І ще одне слово. And one more word:
- **де́рево** (tree)

Це дерево. (This is a tree.) The syllables are **де-ре-во**. Three vowels, three syllables, and all of them are perfectly open. By pushing the consonants to the start of the next beat, you give your Ukrainian a truly authentic rhythm. Це гарно. (This is beautiful.)

## Правила переносу — Word Division Rules

Іноді слово занадто довге. Sometimes the word is too long. Ви повинні розділити слово. You have to split the word. This process is called **перено́с** (word division or hyphenation). Це перенос. (This is word division.)

In Ukrainian schools, correct word division is an important skill. Гарний почерк дуже важливий. Good handwriting is very important. Це школа. (This is school.) Luckily, the rules for moving parts of a word to a new line are logical and build directly on what you just learned about syllables. Це правило. (This is a rule.)

While you generally divide words by their syllables, there are a few special, strict rules for **перено́с** that you must memorize.

**Правило 1: Rule 1: Never abandon a single letter.**
You cannot leave one single letter by itself at the end of a line, and you cannot carry one single letter over to the new line. This is true even if that single letter forms a complete syllable on its own. Це помилка. (This is an error.)
- Let us look at **Украї́на** (Ukraine) again. The syllables are **У-кра-ї-на**. However, you cannot write **У-** on one line and **країна** on the next. The **У** is too lonely! Please move more letters.

**Правило 2: Rule 2: Keep special character pairings together.**
There are certain character combinations in Ukrainian that act as a team. Do not separate them.
For instance, the soft sign (**ь**) softens the consonant that comes right before it. Це знак. (This is a sign.) It has no sound on its own, so it cannot start a new line. Always keep it attached to its consonant.
- In the word **па́льці** (fingers), you divide it as **паль-ці**. Never write **пал-ьці**. Це помилка. (This is an error.)

The same rule applies to the apostrophe (**'**). Це апостроф. (This is an apostrophe.) It tells you how to pronounce the letters around it, so it sticks with the consonant before it.
- In the word **сім'я́** (family), you split it as **сі-м'я**. Це сім'я. (This is a family.) You cannot split it as **сім-'я**.

Additionally, the letter combinations **дж** and **дз** often represent a single sound. Це звук. (This is a sound.) When they do, you cannot split them apart.
- For the word **ґу́дзик** (button), the correct split is **ґу-дзик** (button). Це ґудзик. (This is a button.) Never write **ґуд-зик** (button).

Also, when two consonants sit next to each other, you can usually split between them, like in **сільськи́й** (rural) becoming **сіль-ський**. Notice how the **ль** stays together because of the soft sign rule!

> [!tip] Практика! Practice!
> A great way to practice is with long words. Look at **бібліоте́ка** (library). Це бібліотека. (This is a library.) It has five vowels, so it splits as **бі-блі-о-те-ка**. The word **університе́т** (university) splits as **у-ні-вер-си-тет**. Це університет. (This is a university.) Write them out and practice drawing lines between the syllables!

## Підсумок — Summary & Self-Check

Let us review the fundamental concepts you have learned about syllables and dividing words. You now have the tools to tackle long, intimidating words with confidence. Це підсумок. (This is a summary.)

First, you learned the golden rule of Ukrainian syllables: count the vowels! Це голосна. (This is a vowel.) Every single vowel creates exactly one syllable. If a word has three vowels, it has three syllables. It is that simple.

Second, you discovered that Ukrainian prefers open syllables. Це правило. (This is a rule.) Whenever possible, consonants are pushed to the beginning of the next syllable to keep the rhythm smooth and melodic. This is why you say **се-стра** instead of breaking it apart like in English.

Third, you learned the practical rules of **перено́с** (word division) for writing. Це перенос. (This is word division.) Remember to never leave a single letter alone on a line, even if it is a full syllable. Also, remember that the soft sign (**ь**) and the apostrophe (**'**) are loyal companions—they must always remain attached to the consonant that comes just before them.

Take a moment to test your understanding with these self-check questions:

1. How do you determine the exact number of syllables in any Ukrainian word?
2. Where should you split a consonant cluster like «ст» in the word **сестра́** (sister)?
3. Can you leave a single letter at the end of a line when you are hyphenating a word for the next line?
4. What happens to the soft sign (**ь**) when you divide a word?

By keeping these simple rules in mind, your reading will become much more fluent, and your writing will look perfectly natural. Keep practicing by counting the vowels in every new word you meet!
