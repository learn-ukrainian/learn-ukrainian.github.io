# The Locative: Where Things Are

## Розминка: Де ми знаходимося?

### Питання «Де?» (The Question "Where?")

Hello, neighbor! We often look for things. Keys, phones, passports — they like to hide. To find them, we use one simple word: **Де?** (Where?).

This word helps us orient ourselves. When you are in Kyiv, you might ask directions. At home, you look for your phone.

We use this word constantly.

_Приклади:_
*   **Де** паспорт? (Where is the passport?)
*   **Де** мама? (Where is mom?)
*   **Де** телефон? (Where is the phone?)
*   **Де** наш дім? (Where is our home?)
*   **Де** таксі? (Where is the taxi?)

### Наш дім (Our Home)

Let's visit my home. For Ukrainians, **дім** [dʲim] (home) is a special place. We rest here.

Inside the home, there is a **кімната** [kʲimˈnɑtɑ] (room). In the room, there is furniture. For example, a **стіл** [sʲtʲil] (table). We eat and work here.

To say where an object is, we need small words. These are **в** / **у** (in) and **на** (on / at).

> [!culture]
> **Гостинність (Hospitality)**
> When you enter a Ukrainian home, the host often offers slippers. Guests leave shoes by the door. Cleanliness is important.

_Приклади:_
*   Кіт **у** кімнаті. (The cat is in the room.)
*   Книга **на** столі. (The book is on the table.)
*   Ми **в**дома. (We are at home.)
*   Мама **на** кухні. (Mom is in the kitchen.)
*   Тато **на** дивані. (Dad is on the sofa.)
*   Студент **у** ліжку. (The student is in bed.)

### Робота та місто (Work and City)

We do not stay at home all day. Life is active in the city!

We go to the **магазин** [mɑhɑˈzɪn] (shop / store). We buy bread there. Children go to a place called **школа** [ˈʃkɔlɑ] (school). Adults have a duty. It is **робота** [rɔˈbɔtɑ] (work).

Each place is different. We speak about them in different ways. Today we will learn how.

_Приклади:_
*   Студент **у** школі. (The student is at school.)
*   Тато **на** роботі. (Dad is at work.)
*   Я **в** магазині. (I am in the shop.)
*   Ми **у** парку. (We are in the park.)
*   Вона **в** аптеці. (She is at the pharmacy.)
*   Він **у** банку. (He is at the bank.)

## Теорія: Місцевий відмінок

So, we know where dad is. Dad is at work. The student is at school. But why do we say «у школ**і**» and not «школа»? Why «в магазин**і**»? This is our new tool: the Locative Case (Місцевий відмінок).

Imagine you are putting things in their places. The book is not just «стіл». It is **на столі** (on the table). The clothes are not just «шафа». They are **у шафі** (in the wardrobe). In Ukrainian, we change the word ending to show location.

### Золоте правило: закінчення -і

For most words, this is very simple. You take the word and change the last letter to **-і**. This works for masculine words (he) and feminine words (she). This is the most popular ending.

Look at how it works:

*   **Телефон** (phone) $\rightarrow$ **у телефоні** [u telefɔnʲi] (in the phone)
*   **Стіл** (table) $\rightarrow$ **на столі** [na stɔlʲi] (on the table)
*   **Магазин** (store) $\rightarrow$ **у магазині** [u maɦazɪnʲi] (in the store)
*   **Кімната** (room) $\rightarrow$ **у кімнаті** [u kʲimnatʲi] (in the room)
*   **Автобус** (bus) $\rightarrow$ **у автобусі** [u aʋtɔbusʲi] (in the bus)
*   **Паспорт** (passport) $\rightarrow$ **у паспорті** [u paspɔrtʲi] (in the passport)

> [!observe]
> **Logic Check**
> In English, you use prepositions like "in" or "at". In Ukrainian, we use prepositions (**в/у**, **на**) AND we change the word.
> "I am **in** the park." $\rightarrow$ Я **у** парк**у**.

### М’який знак і закінчення -ї

Sometimes a word ends with a soft sound or the letter **-я** (like *кухня*). Or **-й** (like *музей*). Then we cannot write **-і**. We need another letter to keep it soft. We use **-ї**.

This rule applies to words ending in **-я** (soft 'a') or **-й** (jot).

*   **Кухня** (kitchen) $\rightarrow$ **на кухні** (But: **їдальня** $\rightarrow$ **в їдальні**, soft sign becomes **і**)
    *   *Note:* If the word ends in **-ія**, we write **-ії**.
*   **Лінія** (line) $\rightarrow$ **на лінії** [na lʲinʲiji] (on the line)
*   **Музей** (museum) $\rightarrow$ **у музеї** [u muzejі] (in the museum)
*   **Трамвай** (tram) $\rightarrow$ **у трамваї** [u tramvajі] (in the tram)

Try to remember this pair: "Museum on the line". Both get **-ї**.

### Магічна зміна звуків (К, Г, Х)

Ukrainian language likes flow. Some sounds are hard to say before **-і**. Try to say "rk-i" or "kh-i". It sounds hard. So Ukrainians change these sounds. This is called alternation.

This happens in the Locative case.

1.  **К $\rightarrow$ Ц**
2.  **Г $\rightarrow$ З**
3.  **Х $\rightarrow$ С**

We call this "Hand-Leg-Ear" rule.

| Word (Nominative) | Locative Case | Change |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Парк** (park) | **У парку** (Exception!) | (No change here) |
| **Аптека** (pharmacy) | **В аптеці** [ʋ aptet͡sʲi] | **К $\rightarrow$ Ц** |
| **Книга** (book) | **У книзі** [u knɪzʲi] | **Г $\rightarrow$ З** |
| **Поверх** (floor) | **На поверсі** [na pɔʋersʲi] | **Х $\rightarrow$ С** |
| **Дорога** (road) | **На дорозі** [na dɔrɔzʲi] | **Г $\rightarrow$ З** |
| **Бібліотека** (library) | **У бібліотеці** [u bʲiblʲiɔtɛt͡sʲi] | **К $\rightarrow$ Ц** |

> [!tip]
> **How to remember?**
> Most words with these changes are feminine (she). Pharmacy, library, road, leg.
> Masculine words (park, bank) often have the ending **-у** (у парку, у банку). We will learn this later. Just remember: **аптека $\rightarrow$ в аптеці**.

### Де ми? Всередині чи на поверхні? (В/У vs НА)

When do we use **В (У)** and when **НА**? In English, this is "in" vs "on".

**Preposition В (У)** means "container". You are inside boundaries. There are walls and a roof.

*   **У школі** (inside the building).
*   **У кімнаті** (inside the space).
*   **У місті** (inside city limits).
*   **У сумці** (inside the bag).
*   **У шафі** (inside the wardrobe).

**Preposition НА** means surface. The object lies on top.

*   **На столі** (on the table surface).
*   **На стіні** (on the wall surface).
*   **На підлозі** (on the floor surface).
*   **На дивані** (on the sofa surface).

### Секретна категорія: Події та Процеси

We also use **НА** for an **event** or **activity**. This is very important.

If you say "I am in the office", you talk about the building. You are physically there.
If you say "I am **at work**", you talk about the process. You are busy.

*   **На роботі** (At work — process).
*   **На уроці** (At the lesson — event).
*   **На концерті** (At the concert — event).

Work is not a box. A concert is not a box. It is something that happens. So we are "on" this process.

### Особливі місця: Куди йдемо?

Some words historically always take **НА**. This is tradition.

1.  **На вулиці** (Outside / On the street). The street is an open space.
2.  **На пошті** (At the post office).
3.  **На кухні** (In the kitchen). Kitchen is a room, but we say "on the kitchen".
4.  **На ринку** / **На базарі** (At the market). The market is an open square.

> [!myth-buster]
> **Ukraine: In or On?**
> In the past, some people said "on Ukraine". This is old. It meant a region without borders.
> Now Ukraine is an independent state. It has clear borders. So we say **в Україні** (in Ukraine). Just like **у Польщі** (in Poland) or **у США** (in the USA).
> Correct: **Я живу в Україні.**

### В чи У? Як обрати?

You saw **В** and **У**. They are the same word. The difference is music. Ukrainian does not like too many consonants together.

The Rule (Euphony):
1.  Between consonants use **У**.
    *   Він **у** **ш**колі. (He is at school.)
2.  Between vowels use **В**.
    *   Вон**а** **в** **а**птеці. (She is at the pharmacy.)
3.  If a word starts with **В** or **Ф**, use **У**.
    *   Ми **у** **В**аршаві. (We are in Warsaw.)

Don't worry if you make a mistake. People will understand you. But with time, you will feel the rhythm.

_Practice:_
*   Книга **на** столі.
*   Кіт **у** кімнаті.
*   Ми **в** офісі.
*   Студент **у** бібліотеці.

## Практика: Вдома та в місті

### Запитання «Де?» (Asking Where)

Now you feel the rhythm. You know the difference between **у** and **в**. But why do we need this grammar? To ask questions!

Imagine a morning. You are in a hurry. You cannot see your phone. You ask a friend.

**Structure:**
1.  Word **Де** (Where).
2.  Object (Nominative case).

_Приклад:_
— **Де телефон?** [de te.leˈfɔn] (Where is the phone?)
— **Він тут.** [ʋin tut] (It is here.)

— **Де мама?** [de ˈma.ma] (Where is mom?)
— **Вона там.** [ʋoˈna tam] (She is there.)

— **Де магазин?** [de ma.ɦaˈzɪn] (Where is the shop?)
— **Він зліва.** [ʋ in ˈzʲlʲi.ʋa] (It is on the left.)

— **Де аптека?** [de apˈtɛka] (Where is the pharmacy?)
— **Вона справа.** [ʋoˈna ˈspra.ʋa] (It is on the right.)

We do not change the word in the question. We just add intonation. Try to ask about things around you.

### У кімнаті (Inside the Room)

Now let's answer. We know the object is inside something. We use **у** (or **в**) and ending **-і**.

Imagine your room. There are many things. Books, clothes. Everything has a place.

> [!tip]
> **Watch the Ending!**
> Most words change the last letter to **-і**.
> **Банк** → **У банку** (exception! ending is -у).
> **Парк** → **У парку** (exception! ending is -у).
> But: **Кімната** → **У кімнаті**. **Школа** → **У школі**.

_Приклад:_
**Студент у школі.** [stuˈdɛnt u ˈʃkɔ.lʲi]
(The student is in the school.)

**Гроші в банку.** [ˈɦrɔ.ʃi ʋ ˈban.ku]
(The money is in the bank.)

**Ми в магазині.** [mɪ ʋ ma.ɦaˈzɪ.nʲi]
(We are in the shop.)

**Кіт у кімнаті.** [kʲit u kʲimˈnatʲi]
(The cat is in the room.)

**Віза у паспорті.** [ˈʋʲiza u ˈpaspɔrtʲi]
(The visa is in the passport.)

**Кава у чашці.** [ˈkaʋa u ˈt͡ʃaʃt͡sʲi]
(The coffee is in the cup.)

**Фото у телефоні.** [ˈfɔtɔ u te.leˈfɔnʲi]
(The photo is in the phone.)

You see the logic? We speak about closed space.

### На поверхні та на роботі (On Surfaces and At Work)

Sometimes we are **на** (on) a surface. Or we do an activity. Here we use **на**. It also likes the ending **-і** (or **-у**).

When we say "at work", we do not think about the building. We think about labor. When we say "on the table", it is a surface.

> [!myth-buster]
> **Office or Work?**
> **В офісі** [ʋ ˈɔ.fi.sʲi] — you are inside the room (location).
> **На роботі** [na rɔˈbɔ.tʲi] — you are working (activity).
> You can be "at work" but "in the park" (with a laptop).

_Приклад:_
**Книга на столі.** [ˈknɪ.ɦa na stoˈlʲi]
(The book is on the table.)

**Тато на роботі.** [ˈta.tɔ na rɔˈbɔ.tʲi]
(Dad is at work.)

**Діти на вулиці.** [ˈdʲi.tɪ na ˈʋu.lɪ.t͡sʲi]
(The children are outside.)

**Ми на концерті.** [mɪ na kɔnˈt͡sɛrtʲi]
(We are at the concert.)

The street is an open space. So we say **на**. The market is also often an open place. So — **на ринку**.

### Читання: Мій день (Reading: My Day)

Read this simple story. Try to understand where everything is.

Привіт! Я студент. Я у Києві. Моя школа у центрі.
Зараз ранок. Я на уроці. Учитель у класі. Книга на столі.
Де мій телефон? Він у рюкзаку. Рюкзак на підлозі.
Увечері я вдома. Мама на кухні. Тато на дивані.
Кіт у кріслі. Ми вдома. Все добре.

Я люблю моє місто. Тут є парк, банк і аптека.
Парк великий. Банк у центрі. Аптека справа.
Мій дім теж тут. Він гарний.
У домі є кімната. У кімнаті є вікно.
Біля вікна стоїть стіл.
Я тут живу. Це мій світ.

### Короткі діалоги (Short Dialogues)

Let's combine everything. In life, we speak fast. Question and answer.

Read these scenes aloud.

**Ситуація 1: Вдома (At home)**
— **Де кіт?** [de kʲit]
— **Він на кріслі.** [ʋ in na ˈkrʲi.sʲlʲi]
— **А де собака?**
— **Собака на вулиці.**

**Ситуація 2: Телефонний дзвінок (Phone call)**
— **Алло! Ти де?** [tɪ de]
— **Я в центрі.** [ja ʋ ˈt͡sɛn.trʲi]
— **Ти на роботі?**
— **Ні, я у парку.**

**Ситуація 3: Пошук речей (Finding things)**
— **Де паспорт?** [de ˈpas.pɔrt]
— **Він у сумці.** [ʋ in u ˈsum.t͡sʲi]
— **А де сумка?**
— **Сумка на стільці.** [na sʲtʲilʲˈt͡sʲi]

**Ситуація 4: На вулиці (On the street)**
— **Де банк?** [de baŋk]
— **Банк там, у центрі.** [baŋk tam u ˈt͡sɛntrʲi]
— **А де аптека?**
— **Аптека на цій вулиці.**

**Ситуація 5: У школі (At school)**
— **Де студент?** [de stuˈdɛnt]
— **Він на уроці.** [ʋ in na uˈrɔt͡sʲi]
— **А де учитель?**
— **Учитель у класі.**

## Підсумок: Мій світ

Now you can find things. You live in a world of objects. To describe your reality, you simply say where things are. This is the final step.

### Опис кімнати (Describing the Room)

Your room is a collection of surfaces and containers. When you describe it, you answer «Де це?» (Where is this?).

In Ukrainian, walls and floors are open spaces. You are always **на підлозі** (on the floor). Even if a poster is on the wall, it is **на стіні** (on the wall).

> [!fact]
> **Surfaces are everywhere**
> In English, a picture is "in" the corner. In Ukrainian, if that corner is volume, it is **у кутку**. But if it is the floor surface, **на** wins.

_Приклади:_
*   **Телефон на столі.** (The phone is on the table.)
*   **Килим на підлозі.** (The carpet is on the floor.)
*   **Книга у сумці.** (The book is in the bag.)
*   **Ми на дивані.** (We are on the sofa.)
*   **Лампа на столі.** (The lamp is on the table.)
*   **Одяг у шафі.** (Clothes are in the wardrobe.)

Sitting furniture often takes **на** (surface). Storage furniture takes **у/в** (container).

### Моє місто (My City)

Your world is big. When you talk about the city, you shift between buildings and concepts.

School, work, and market are things you *do*. That is why we use **на**. But a bank or shop is a container.

_Приклади:_
*   **Магазин на вулиці.** (The store is on the street.)
*   **Діти у школі.** (The children are at school.)
*   **Я у парку.** (I am in the park.)
*   **Він на заводі.** (He is at the factory.)
*   **Ми на ринку.** (We are at the market.)
*   **Вона в аптеці.** (She is at the pharmacy.)

Remember: **вулиця** (street) is always a surface. You are never "in" the street in Ukrainian. Always **на вулиці**.
