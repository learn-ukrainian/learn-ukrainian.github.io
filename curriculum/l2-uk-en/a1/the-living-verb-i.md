# The Living Verb I

> **Чому це важливо?**
>
> Words that name things are great, but words that do things bring your language to life. Verbs are the engines of your sentences. Once you understand how they change, you can start expressing your own thoughts and actions clearly.

## Вступ

ВітА́ємо! СьогО́дні ми вивчА́ємо дієслО́во.
(Welcome! Today we are studying the verb.)

Welcome to the beating heart of the Ukrainian language: the verb (дієслО́во). Up until now, you have learned how to name objects, describe them, and say where they are. Але для спілкувА́ння ми мА́ємо дієслО́ва. (But for communication we have verbs.) You need action. You need to say what you do, what you know, and what you read.

In English, verbs are relatively simple. You say «I read», «you read», «we read». Only when you talk about «he» or «she» do you add an «s» to make it «reads». Ukrainian verbs require more attention, but they follow logical patterns. Once you learn the pattern, you have the key to hundreds of words.

Кожне дієслО́во мА́є бА́зову фО́рму. Ми називА́ємо це інфінітИ́в.
(Every verb has a base form. We call this the infinitive.)

В англІ́йській мО́ві це «to read», «to know». В українській мО́ві це закІ́нчення **-ти**.
(In English it is "to read", "to know". In Ukrainian it is the ending **-ти**.)

Ви завждИ́ мО́жете впІ́знати інфінітИ́в. Він мА́є закІ́нчення **-ти**.
(You can always recognize the infinitive. It has the ending **-ти**.)

Ми бА́чимо дієслО́во. ВонО́ мА́є закІ́нчення **-ти**. Це дУ́же важлИ́во знА́ти.
(We see the verb. It has the ending **-ти**. This is very important to know.)

Ось дужЕ́ важлИ́ві дієслО́ва на кО́жен день:
(Here are very important verbs for every day:)

| Ukrainian | English |
| :--- | :--- |
| **знА́ти** | to know |
| **читА́ти** | to read |
| **дУ́мати** | to think |
| **слУ́хати** | to listen |
| **питА́ти** | to ask |

Take a close look at that list. Do you notice a pattern? Not only do they all end in **-ти**, but they all end in **-ати**. 

Це найбІ́льша грУ́па дієслІ́в.
(This is the largest group of verbs.)
If you can learn how to use just one of these verbs, you will instantly know how to use all of them.

> [!tip] ВажлИ́во (Important)
> Think of the dictionary form as a verb that is asleep. It has the potential for action, but it isn't doing anything yet. To wake it up and put it to work in a sentence, we need to change its ending.
> ІнфінітИ́в не рО́бить дІ́ю. Він тІ́льки називА́є дІ́ю.
> (The infinitive does not do the action. It only names the action.)

## Презентація: The -ати Pattern

ТепЕ́р час дІ́яти! Ми вивчА́ємо прА́вила.
(Now it is time to act! We study the rules.)

Now it is time to wake up these verbs and put them into action.
Хто рО́бить дІ́ю? Це я? Це ти? Це вонИ́?
(Who does the action? Is it me? Is it you? Is it they?)

В українській мО́ві закІ́нчення дієслО́ва змІ́нюється. Це залЕ́жить від осО́би.
(In Ukrainian the ending of the verb changes. It depends on the person.)
This is called conjugation. It might sound intimidating, but it is just a matching game. You match the person to the correct ending.

СпочА́тку ми вивчА́ємо займЕ́нники. Хто рО́бить дІ́ю?
(First we study the pronouns. Who does the action?)
**я** (I)
**ти** (you, informal singular)
**він** (he), **вонА́** (she), **вонО́** (it)
**ми** (we)
**ви** (you, formal or plural)
**вонИ́** (they)

To conjugate an **-ати** verb, you follow two simple steps:
1. Take the dictionary form and chop off the **-ти**. This leaves you with the stem.
2. Add the new ending that matches the person.

ПЕ́рший прИ́клад — дієслО́во **знА́ти**.
(The first example is the verb **знА́ти**.)

Крок один: ПрибирА́ємо **-ти**. ЗалишА́ється корІ́нь: **зна-**.
(Step one: We remove **-ти**. The stem remains: **зна-**.)

Крок два: ДодаЄ́мо закІ́нчення.
(Step two: We add the endings.)

Я знА́ю текст.
(I know the text.)

Ти знА́єш слО́во.
(You know the word.)

Він знА́є прА́вило. ВонА́ знА́є мІ́сто.
(He knows the rule. She knows the city.)

Ми знА́ємо це.
(We know this.)

Ви знА́єте такО́ж.
(You know as well.)

ВонИ́ знА́ють усе.
(They know everything.)

> [!did-you-know] ЦІкА́вий факт (Interesting fact)
> Notice the pattern of the endings: **-ю**, **-єш**, **-є**, **-ємо**, **-єте**, **-ють**. All of these endings start with a soft vowel sound. This makes the **-ати** verb family sound very smooth and melodic when spoken aloud.

ТепЕ́р беремО́ інше дієслО́во. Ми беремО́ дієслО́во **читА́ти** (to read).
(Now we take another verb. We take the verb **читА́ти**.)

ПрибирА́ємо **-ти**, і мА́ємо корІ́нь: **чита-**. ДалІ́ додаЄ́мо закІ́нчення!
(We remove **-ти**, and we have the stem: **чита-**. Next we add the endings!)

Я читА́ю кнИ́гу.
(I read a book.)

Ти читА́єш текст.
(You read the text.)

ВонА́ читА́є журнА́л.
(She reads a magazine.)

Ми читА́ємо менЮ́.
(We read the menu.)

Ви читА́єте дО́бре.
(You read well.)

ВонИ́ читА́ють.
(They read.)

This pattern is incredibly reliable. When you encounter a new verb that ends in **-ати**, you can confidently guess that it follows this exact same set of rules.
Ви вчИ́те систЕ́му мО́ви.
(You are learning the system of the language.)
This means every time you learn one new **-ати** verb, you are actually learning six new ways to express yourself!

ДалІ́ ми беремО́ нО́ві дієслО́ва.
(Next we take new verbs.)

The verb is **дУ́мати** (to think). The stem is **дума-**. The ending for «I» is **-ю**.

Я дУ́маю про текст.
(I think about the text.)

The verb is **питА́ти** (to ask). The stem is **пита-**. The ending for «she» is **-є**.

ВонА́ питА́є викладачА́.
(She asks the teacher.)

The verb is **слУ́хати** (to listen). The stem is **слуха-**. The ending for «we» is **-ємо**.

Ми слУ́хаємо мУ́зику.
(We listen to music.)

Ми читА́ємо діалО́г.
(We read a dialogue.)
Let's see these verbs in context:

— ПривІ́т! Що ти рО́биш?
(Hi! What are you doing?)

— Я читА́ю кнИ́гу. А ти?
(I am reading a book. And you?)

— Я слУ́хаю мУ́зику. Ти знА́єш гурт «ОкеА́н Ельзи»?
(I am listening to music. Do you know the band "Okean Elzy"?)

— Так, знА́ю. Я дУ́маю. ВонИ́ сУ́пер!
(Yes, I know them. I think. They are super!)

— ВонА́ питА́є. Що ми читА́ємо?
(She asks. What are we reading?)

— Ми читА́ємо текст.
(We read the text.)

> [!warning] Обережно (Careful)
> The biggest visual trap for beginners is the letter **є**. It looks like a backwards English «e» or a symbol for the Euro currency, but it sounds like «ye» as in «yellow». Pay close attention to this letter in the **ти**, **він/вона/воно**, **ми**, and **ви** forms!

## Практика та типові помилки

Час для прА́ктики!
(Time for practice!)

Українці чА́сто не кА́жуть займЕ́нник. ЗакІ́нчення дієслО́ва покА́зує осО́бу.
(Ukrainians often do not say the pronoun. The verb ending shows the person.)
So the pronoun (like «I» or «we») is not always necessary.

ЗнА́ю.
(I know.)

ЧитА́ємо.
(We read.)

ДжО́нсон та СмІ́т? ЗнА́ють.
(Johnson and Smith? They know.)

In Ukrainian, the ending **-ю** in **знА́ю** means "It is ME doing the knowing!" For beginners, it is perfectly fine to use the pronouns every time. As you get more comfortable, you will naturally start dropping them.

Ще трІ́шки прА́ктики читА́ння.
(A little more reading practice.)
Read them out loud and translate them in your head:

Я знА́ю. Він читА́є.
(I know. He is reading.)

Ми дУ́маємо. Це дО́бре.
(We think. This is good.)

Ви слУ́хаєте? ВонА́ питА́є.
(Are you listening? She is asking.)

Так, я слУ́хаю. ВонА́ питА́є. Де кафЕ́?
(Yes, I am listening. She is asking. Where is the cafe?)

СтудЕ́нти читА́ють текст.
(The students are reading the text.)

ВикладА́ч питА́є, а студЕ́нти дУ́мають.
(The teacher asks, and the students think.)



> [!culture] КультУ́ра (Culture)
> The distinction between **ти** and **ви** is very important in Ukrainian culture. You use **ти** with family, close friends, and children. You use **ви** with strangers, teachers, colleagues, and anyone you want to show respect to. Always default to **ви** when meeting someone new!

Ми чА́сто рО́бимо помИ́лки. Це нормА́льно.
(We often make mistakes. This is normal.)
We should discuss a few common mistakes that beginners make when learning this pattern.

The first common mistake is forgetting to drop the **-ти** before adding the new ending. You might accidentally say «Я знати-ю». Remember, the dictionary form must be broken apart before it can be used. Always chop off that **-ти** first!

The second common mistake involves the **ви** form. Because **ви** can mean one person (formal) or multiple people (plural), beginners sometimes get confused about which ending to use. The rule is simple: the grammar is always the same. Whether you are talking to one respected professor or a room full of students, you always use the **-єте** ending.

Ви читА́єте кнИ́гу.
(You (Professor) read the book.)

Ви читА́єте текст.
(You (students) read the text.)

Ці закІ́нчення тІ́льки для тепЕ́рішнього чА́су.
(These endings are only for the present tense.)
They describe actions happening right now, or actions that happen regularly. Do not try to use these endings to talk about yesterday or tomorrow. We will learn those rules in a different lesson!

# Підсумок

СьогО́дні ми рО́бимо велИ́кий крок. Ми вивчА́ємо дієслО́ва!
(Today we make a big step. We study verbs!)
Ви мО́лодці! Ви дО́бре знаЄ́те прА́вила!
(You are great! You know the rules well!)
You have taken a massive step forward in your Ukrainian journey today. You have learned how to take a sleeping dictionary verb and wake it up to describe the world around you.

Ось прА́вила ще раз:
(Here are the rules again:)
Here is a final review of the core pattern. For verbs ending in **-ати**, remove the **-ти** and add the following endings to the stem:
*   **я**: add **-ю**
*   **ти**: add **-єш**
*   **він / вонА́ / вонО́**: add **-є**
*   **ми**: add **-ємо**
*   **ви**: add **-єте**
*   **вонИ́**: add **-ють**

Ці закІ́нчення — ключ до розумІ́ння. ТепЕ́р ви мО́жете висловлювати дУ́мки. Ви мО́жете стА́вити питА́ння.
(These endings are the key to understanding. Now you can express your thoughts. You can ask questions.)

**Self-Check Questions:**

1. What are the two letters that most Ukrainian dictionary verbs end with?
2. What happens to those two letters when you want to use the verb with a person?
3. If the stem is **зна-**, what is the correct form for «we»?
4. Why is it okay to say **читА́ю** instead of **я читА́ю**?
5. When should you use the pronoun **ти** instead of **ви**?

Take your time reviewing this pattern. Write out the conjugations for **знА́ти**, **читА́ти**, and **дУ́мати** on a piece of paper. Say them out loud. Pay special attention to the vowels in the endings. Once this pattern feels natural, you will be amazed at how quickly your ability to speak Ukrainian grows. You have unlocked a whole new level of communication. Keep up the excellent work!
