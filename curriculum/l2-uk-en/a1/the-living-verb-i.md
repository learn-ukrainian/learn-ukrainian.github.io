# The Living Verb I

## Розминка: Від слів до дій

### Вітаю! (Welcome!)
You already know many Ukrainian words. You see a **телефон** [tɛlɛˈfɔn] (phone) or a **журнал** [ʒurˈnɑl] (magazine). But naming things is not enough. You want to speak. To speak, we need action. We need the **дієслово** [dijɛˈslɔʋɔ] (verb).

The verb is the engine of the sentence. It turns a static picture into a movie. Without verbs, we just point at objects. With verbs, we truly communicate.

### Ключ до мови (The Master Key)
You might worry about complex grammar. **Не хвилюйтесь!** (Do not worry!) Ukrainian verbs follow a beautiful logic. Today, we discover a "Master Key". This is the **-ати** pattern. Many essential verbs share this pattern. You learn one simple rule, and you unlock hundreds of words immediately. It is like magic. You will confidently say "I read", "I know", or "I listen".

### Ваш день (Your Day)
Think about your daily life. You do many things every day. You work. You listen to news. You ask questions. Now you can say these things in Ukrainian.

*   **Я працюю.** [jɑ prɑˈt͡sʲuju] — I work.
*   **Я слухаю радіо.** [jɑ ˈsluxɑju ˈrɑdʲijo] — I listen to the radio.
*   **Він знає все.** [ʋin ˈznɑje ʋsɛ] — He knows everything.

> [!tip]
> **Де "Я"? (Where is "I"?)**
> In Ukrainian, the verb ending often tells us *who* is doing the action. You can say **Я читаю** [jɑ t͡ʃɪˈtɑju] (I read) or just **Читаю** [t͡ʃɪˈtɑju]. Both are correct! We will see this logic in action very soon.

## Презентація: Перша дієвідміна

### Спляче дієслово (The Sleeping Verb)
Imagine you buy a new mixer for your kitchen. It is shiny and beautiful. But if you do not plug it in, it does nothing. It just sits on the counter. In Ukrainian, verbs in the dictionary are like that unplugged mixer. We call this the **інфінітив** (infinitive).

You can easily spot these "sleeping verbs". They almost always end in **-ти**.
*   **читати** [t͡ʃɪˈtɑtɪ] — to read
*   **знати** [znɑtɪ] — to know
*   **слухати** [ˈsluxɑtɪ] — to listen

These words name the action, but they do not *perform* it. If you say «Я читати» [jɑ t͡ʃɪˈtɑtɪ], you are saying "I to read". It sounds like a robot. To make the verb work, we must "plug it in". We must change the ending to match the person. This is what we learn today.

### Простий алгоритм (Simple Algorithm)
Ось хороша новина. You do not need to memorize every verb individually. Ukrainian is very logical. There is a specific group of verbs that follow one simple pattern. We call them "Type 1" verbs, or the **-ати** group.

Це ваш "Майстер-ключ". If you learn this pattern for one verb, you instantly know how to use hundreds of others.

**Запам'ятайте логіку (Remember the logic):**
1.  Take the infinitive: **читати** [t͡ʃɪˈtɑtɪ].
2.  Remove the **-ти** to find the "stem": **чита-**.
3.  Add the specific ending for the person.

> [!tip]
> **Золоте правило (The Golden Rule)**
> For this group of verbs, the stem **чита-** stays strong. It does not change. You just glue the new endings onto it. It is like Lego bricks.

### Модель: Читати (Model: To Read)
Let us see this pattern in action with our model verb **читати** [t͡ʃɪˈtɑtɪ] (to read). **Подивіться на таблицю (Look at the table)** and watch how the endings change.

| Особа (Person) | Українська (Ukrainian) | Вимова (Pronunciation) | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Я** (I) | **читаю** | [t͡ʃɪˈtɑju] | I read |
| **Ти** (You, sing.) | **читаєш** | [t͡ʃɪˈtɑjɛʃ] | You read |
| **Він / Вона** (He/She) | **читає** | [t͡ʃɪˈtɑjɛ] | He/She reads |
| **Ми** (We) | **читаємо** | [t͡ʃɪˈtɑjɛmɔ] | We read |
| **Ви** (You, pl./formal) | **читаєте** | [t͡ʃɪˈtɑjɛtɛ] | You read |
| **Вони** (They) | **читають** | [t͡ʃɪˈtɑjutʲ] | They read |

Помітьте ритм. **Я** (I) always loves the **-ю** ending. **Ти** (You) ends with a soft hiss **-ш**. **Вони** (They) has the long tail **-ють**.

**Читайте приклади (Read the examples):**
*   **Я читаю журнал.** [jɑ t͡ʃɪˈtɑju ʒurˈnɑl] — I read a magazine.
*   **Ми читаємо тут.** [mɪ t͡ʃɪˈtɑjɛmɔ tut] — We read here.

### Де "Я"? (Where is "I"?)
In English, you must strictly say "I read" or "We read". If you just say "Read", it sounds like a command. But Ukrainian is different. Ukrainian verbs are very informative. The ending **-ю** in **читаю** *already* signals that "I" am doing it. The ending **-ємо** in **читаємо** screams "We"!

Because the verb is so strong, we often drop the pronoun completely. This is very natural. It makes your speech faster and more fluid.

*   **Знаю.** [znɑju] — (I) know.
*   **Слухаєш?** [ˈsluxɑjɛʃ] — (Do you) listen?
*   **Чекаємо.** [t͡ʃɛˈkɑjɛmɔ] — (We) are waiting.

> [!context]
> **Скромність у мові (Modesty in Language)**
> Ukrainians focus on the action, not the ego. Using "Я" (I) in every sentence can sound a bit self-centered or emphatic. If the context is clear, feel free to drop it. "Working" sounds better than "I am working".

### Нові слова (New Words)
Now that you have the key, let us unlock more doors. All these essential verbs follow the exact same **читати** pattern. You just swap the stem.

*   **слухати** [ˈsluxɑtɪ] (to listen) → **Я слухаю** [jɑ ˈsluxɑju]. **Ми слухаємо радіо.**
*   **питати** [pɪˈtɑtɪ] (to ask) → **Ми питаємо** [mɪ pɪˈtɑjɛmɔ]. **Він питає про це.**
*   **грати** [ˈɦrɑtɪ] (to play) → **Вони грають** [ʋɔˈnɪ ˈɦrɑjutʲ]. **Я граю тут.**
*   **знати** [znɑtɪ] (to know) → **Ти знаєш** [tɪ znɑjɛʃ]. **Ви знаєте все.**

> [!note]
> **А як щодо "писати"? (What about "to write"?)**
> The verb **писати** (to write) is very common, but it has a small secret. It changes slightly: **Я пишу** (not *писаю*). We will learn this special group later. For now, focus on the perfect **-ати** verbs above.

Спробуйте послухати музику цих слів.
**Він слухає джаз.** [ʋin ˈsluxɑjɛ d͡ʒɑz] — He listens to jazz.
**Ви знаєте це.** [ʋɪ ˈznɑjɛtɛ t͡sɛ] — You know this.

### Про слово "Працювати" (About "To Work")
There is one very common verb that fits this rhythm perfectly but looks slightly different in the dictionary: **працювати** [prɑt͡sʲuˈʋɑtɪ] (to work).

When we conjugate it, we keep the clear, strong sound **працю-** [prɑt͡sʲu-].
*   **Я працюю.** [jɑ prɑˈt͡sʲuju] — I work.
*   **Ти працюєш.** [tɪ prɑˈt͡sʲujɛʃ] — You work.
*   **Вони працюють.** [ʋɔˈnɪ prɑˈt͡sʲujutʲ] — They work.

It sounds exactly like **читаю**, **читаєш**, **читають**. So do not be afraid of the longer dictionary form. The result is familiar and comfortable. You will use this word every day.
**Я працюю вдома.** [jɑ prɑˈt͡sʲuju ˈʋdɔmɑ] — I work at home.

### Типова помилка (Common Trap)
The biggest mistake beginners make is keeping the verb in the "freezer". They learn the word **знати** (to know) and say:
*   ❌ **Я знати.** (I to know.)

This is understandable, but it is wrong. It stops the flow of the sentence. Always ask yourself: "Who is doing the action?"
*   If it is me → **-ю**. (Я знаю)
*   If it is you → **-єш**. (Ти знаєш)
*   If it is us → **-ємо**. (Ми знаємо)

Make the verb live. Let it breathe and change.
**Ми знаємо все.** [mɪ ˈznɑjɛmɔ ʋsɛ] — We know everything.

## Практика: Життя в русі

Now that the verb is awake and out of the dictionary, let us put it to work. You have the "objects" from our previous lessons—books, radio, letters. Now you have the "engine" to make them move. We will build simple, strong sentences that describe your reality right now.

### Будуємо речення (Building Your First Sentences)
You already know the pattern. Now we simply connect the **Subject** (who), the **Verb** (action), and the **Object** (what). In Ukrainian, this feels very similar to English, but with more rhythm.

Let us look at **слухати** (to listen). This is a perfect Class I verb. It describes what you do with music, news, or a friend.

| Хто? (Who?) | Дія (Action) | Що? (What?) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Я** (I) | **слухаю** | **радіо** (radio) |
| **Ми** (We) | **слухаємо** | **музику** (music) |

Notice the harmony. The ending **-ю** matches **Я**. The ending **-ємо** matches **Ми**.

*   **Я слухаю новини.** [jɑ ˈsluxɑju nɔˈʋɪnɪ] — I listen to the news.
*   **Ти слухаєш джаз.** [tɪ ˈsluxɑjɛʃ d͡ʒɑz] — You listen to jazz.
*   **Студент слухає текст.** [stuˈdɛnt ˈsluxɑjɛ tɛkst] — The student listens to the text.

> [!observe]
> **Приховане "About" (The Hidden "About")**
> In English, you listen **to** music. In Ukrainian, you just "listen music" (**слухати музику**). There is no extra word like "to" in the middle. The verb connects directly to the object. It is faster and more direct.

### Мистецтво знати (The Art of Knowing)
The verb **знати** (to know) is your anchor. You use it to confirm facts or express familiarity with a place. It follows the exact same rhythm as **читати**.

*   **Я знаю це.** [jɑ ˈznɑju t͡sɛ] — I know this.
*   **Ми знаємо телефон.** [mɪ ˈznɑjɛmɔ tɛlɛˈfɔn] — We know the phone number.
*   **Ви знаєте все.** [ʋɪ ˈznɑjɛtɛ ʋsɛ] — You know everything.

Використовуйте це сміливо! It is a strong statement.

### Питати і працювати (Asking and Working)
Let us look at **питати** (to ask) and **працювати** (to work). Imagine you are in an office or a university. These actions happen all around you.

**Питати** works well with the word **про** (about).
*   **Я питаю про текст.** [jɑ pɪˈtɑju prɔ tɛkst] — I ask about the text.
*   **Ти питаєш про це?** [tɪ pɪˈtɑjɛʃ prɔ t͡sɛ] — Do you ask about this?

**Працювати** describes your labor or your job.
*   **Він працює тут.** [ʋin prɑˈt͡sʲujɛ tut] — He works here.
*   **Ми працюємо вдома.** [mɪ prɑˈt͡sʲujɛmɔ ˈʋdɔmɑ] — We work at home.


### Без займенників (Without Pronouns)
You will often hear Ukrainians speak without the words "I", "you", or "we". This is not lazy. It is efficient. Because the ending of the verb **already tells you** who is doing the action, the pronoun is extra information.

If I say **«Читаю»**, the ending **-ю** screams "I am doing it!". We do not need **Я**.

*   **Знаю це.** [ˈznɑju t͡sɛ] — (I) know this.
*   **Слухаємо радіо.** [ˈsluxɑjɛmɔ ˈrɑdʲijo] — (We) listen to the radio.
*   **Чекаєш автобус?** [t͡ʃɛˈkɑjɛʃ ɑʋˈtɔbus] — (Are you) waiting for the bus?

This sounds very natural. It shows you are comfortable with the language. You are focusing on the *action*, not on yourself.

## Культурний контекст: Нація читачів

Historically, the written word has held a sacred place in Ukraine. The first book printed in Ukraine, the "Apostol," appeared in Lviv in 1574. Literacy has always been highly valued, and you will often see people reading books in parks, on the metro, or in cafes.

The verb **читати** [t͡ʃɪˈtɑtɪ] (to read) is one of the most frequently used words in the language. It connects you to the history and the daily life of the country.

*   **Я читаю журнал.** [jɑ t͡ʃɪˈtɑju ʒurˈnɑl] — I read a magazine.
*   **Ми читаємо вдома.** [mɪ t͡ʃɪˈtɑjɛmɔ ˈʋdɔmɑ] — We read at home.
*   **Вони читають швидко.** [ʋɔˈnɪ t͡ʃɪˈtɑjutʲ ˈʃʋɪdkɔ] — They read quickly.

> [!culture]
> **Птах і слово (The Bird and the Word)**
>
> There is a famous Ukrainian saying:
> **«Птицю пізнати по пір'ю, а людину по мові.»**
> [ˈptɪt͡sʲu pʲizˈnɑtɪ pɔ ˈpʲirju ɑ lʲuˈdɪnu pɔ ˈmɔʋi]
> *"You know a bird by its feathers, and a person by their language."*
>
> This reminds us that how we speak — our choice of words and our grammar — shows our character. By learning these verb endings, you are showing respect for the language structure.

# Summary

In this module, you unlocked the "Master Key" to Ukrainian verbs.

*   **Type 1 Verbs**: You learned the **-ати** pattern (читати, знати, слухати).
*   **Conjugation**: You now know how to change the ending for each person:
    *   Я → **-ю**
    *   Ти → **-єш**
    *   Ми → **-ємо**
    *   Вони → **-ють**
*   **Pro-drop**: You learned that you can drop the pronoun ("Читаю" instead of "Я читаю") because the ending is strong enough.
*   **Key Verbs**: You added essential action words to your vocabulary: **читати** (read), **знати** (know), **слухати** (listen), **питати** (ask), **працювати** (work).

**Вітаю!** (Congratulations!) You have moved from naming objects to describing actions. Now go and use your new power! **Ми говоримо українською!** (We speak Ukrainian!)
