# Around the City

## Вступ: Місто навколо нас

### Welcome to the City

Hello, neighbor! **Привіт, сусіде!** Imagine our walk. **Уявіть нашу прогулянку.** We are in a city. **Ми у місті.** It is lively here. **Тут цікаво.** To feel comfortable, we need to know what we see around us.

Look to the right. **Подивіться направо.** You see a wide street. **Ви бачите широку вулицю.** Cars drive there. **Там їдуть машини.** Buses drive there too. **Там також їдуть автобуси.** And over there is a square. **Ось площа.** It is big. **Вона велика.** It is beautiful. **Вона гарна.** It is the heart of our city. **Це серце міста.**

Are you tired? **Ви втомилися?** We can find transport. **Ось транспорт.** Here is a stop. **Тут зупинка.** We wait for the bus here. **Тут ми чекаємо на автобус.** Do you need medicine? **Ви шукаєте ліки?** Here is a pharmacy. **Ось аптека.**

> [!tip]
> **Cultural Note**: Unlike in some countries, distinct "squares" (**площа**) are central hubs in Ukrainian cities. They are not just open spaces but meeting points. For example, *Площа Ринок* in Lviv is the center of social life.

### Key Question: Where vs. Where to?

To avoid getting lost, you must understand the difference between two questions. In English, we often use "Where" for both situations, but in Ukrainian, these are two different worlds.

The first word is **Де** [dɛ] (Where — static location). We use it when we are standing in one place.
*   **Де я?** — [dɛ ja] — Where am I?
*   **Я в аптеці.** — [ja ʋ apˈtɛt͡sʲi] — I am in the pharmacy.

The second word is **Куди** [kuˈdɪ] (Where to — direction/motion). We use it when we are walking or driving.
*   **Куди ви йдете?** — [kuˈdɪ ʋɪ jˈdɛtɛ] — Where are you going?
*   **Я йду на площу.** — [ja jdu na ˈplɔʃt͡ʃu] — I am going to the square.

Remember this difference. **Пам'ятайте цю різницю.** **Де** — is static (location). **Куди** — is motion.

### Our Goal Today

Today I will be your guide. **Сьогодні я ваш гід.** We will learn to navigate. **Ми будемо гуляти.** Instead of just being a tourist with a map, you will be able to ask for the way.

We are going to learn important words: **прямо** [ˈprʲamɔ] (straight), **наліво** [naˈlʲiʋɔ] (to the left), and **направо** [naˈpraʋɔ] (to the right). You will also discover how to find a **пошта** [ˈpɔʃta] (post office) or a supermarket.

This is a practical lesson. **Це практичний урок.** We will walk a lot. **Ми будемо багато гуляти.** We will speak a lot. **Ми будемо багато говорити.** Let's start! **Починаємо!**

## Теорія: Напрямки та інфраструктура

### Basic Directions

So, we are leaving home. **Ми йдемо з дому.** Where are we going? **Куди ми йдемо?** To avoid getting lost, we need words for navigation. These are your main helpers in any city.

The most important words here are adverbs. They answer the question «Куди?» (Where to?).

*   **Прямо** [ˈprʲɑmɔ] — straight. It is simple. **Це просто.** You walk and do not turn. **Ви йдете і не повертаєте.**
*   **Наліво** [nɑˈlʲiʋɔ] — to the left.
*   **Направо** [nɑˈprɑʋɔ] — to the right.
*   **Назад** [nɑˈzɑd] — back / backwards.

> [!tip]
> **Ліворуч** [lʲiˈʋɔrut͡ʃ] and **праворуч** [prɑˈʋɔrut͡ʃ] are slightly more formal synonyms for «on the left» / «to the left» and «on the right» / «to the right». You will see them on signs or hear them from GPS navigators.

Look at the examples. **Подивіться на приклади.** Here we use these words with motion verbs.

*   **Ми йдемо прямо.** (We are going straight.)
*   **Повертайте направо.** (Turn right.)
*   **Автобус їде наліво.** (The bus goes to the left.)
*   **Дивіться назад.** (Look back.)

These words do not change. **Ці слова не змінюються.** It is very convenient! **Це дуже зручно!** You do not need to think about endings. Just remember the direction. **Просто запам'ятайте напрямок.**

### City Infrastructure: What and Where?

Now let's look around. **Подивіться навколо.** What do we see in the city? **Що ми бачимо у місті?** We have buildings, roads, and open spaces.

Here is basic vocabulary for our walk. **Ось слова для прогулянки:**

*   **Вулиця** [ˈʋulɪt͡sʲɑ] — street.
    *   **Це гарна вулиця.** (This is a beautiful street.)
*   **Площа** [ˈplɔʃt͡ʃɑ] — square.
    *   **Тут велика площа.** (Here is a big square.)
*   **Зупинка** [zuˈpɪnkɑ] — stop (bus stop, tram stop).
    *   **Де зупинка?** (Where is the stop?)
*   **Аптека** [ɑpˈtɛkɑ] — pharmacy.
    *   **Ось аптека.** (Here is the pharmacy.)
*   **Пошта** [ˈpɔʃtɑ] — post office.
    *   **Там пошта.** (The post office is there.)
*   **Вокзал** [ʋɔkˈzɑl] — train station.
    *   **Це старий вокзал.** (This is an old station.)

Remember the Locative Case? We use it to say **where** we are. Here it is important to choose the correct preposition: **в** (in) or **на** (on/at).

Use **на** for open spaces:
*   **На вулиці** [nɑ ˈʋulɪt͡sʲi] — on the street.
*   **На площі** [nɑ ˈplɔʃt͡ʃi] — on the square.
*   **На зупинці** [nɑ zuˈpɪnt͡sʲi] — at the stop.

Use **в** (or **у**) for buildings (inside):
*   **В аптеці** [ʋ ɑpˈtɛt͡sʲi] — in the pharmacy.
*   **На пошті** [nɑ ˈpɔʃtʲi] — at the post office. (Exception! We usually think of «function» here, like «at work»).
*   **На вокзалі** [nɑ ʋɔkˈzɑlʲi] — at the station. (Also usually **на**).

> [!context]
> Wait, why **на пошті**? Some words historically take **на**. Examples: **на пошті**, **на вокзалі**, **на ринку** (at the market), **на роботі** (at work). Imagine these as open spaces or general locations, not just boxes you go inside.

Let's practice. **Давайте практикувати.** Where are the people? **Де люди?**

*   **Студент зараз на вулиці.** (The student is on the street now.)
*   **Ми чекаємо на зупинці.** (We are waiting at the stop.)
*   **Бабуся в аптеці.** (Grandma is in the pharmacy.)

### Where vs. Where to?

**Це момент істини.** (This is the moment of truth.) **Ми розрізняємо місце і напрямок.** (We distinguish location and direction.)

1.  **Де?** (Where?) — Static. Nothing moves. Case: **Locative**.
2.  **Куди?** (Where to?) — Dynamic. We are moving there. Case: **Accusative**.

**Подивіться на різницю.** (Look at the difference.) It changes the word ending!

| Question | Case | Example | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Де ти?** | Locative | Я **в парку**. | I am in the park. (Sitting on a bench) |
| **Куди ти йдеш?** | Accusative | Я йду **в парк**. | I am going to the park. (Walking there) |

In the Accusative case for inanimate objects, the word often does not change or changes **-а** to **-у**.

*   **Аптека** (Nominative)
    *   *Where?* — В аптец**і**. (Locative)
    *   *Where to?* — В аптек**у**. (Accusative)
*   **Вулиця** (Nominative)
    *   *Where?* — На вулиц**і**. (Locative)
    *   *Where to?* — На вулиц**ю**. (Accusative)

Let's compare in sentences:

*   **Мама в магазині.** (Mom is in the shop.) — *She is there.*
*   **Мама йде в магазин.** (Mom is going to the shop.) — *She is going there.*
*   **Автобус на площі.** (The bus is on the square.) — *It stands.*
*   **Автобус їде на площу.** (The bus is going to the square.) — *It moves.*
*   **Тато на пошті.** (Dad is at the post office.)
*   **Тато йде на пошту.** (Dad is going to the post office.)

This is logical, right? **Це логічно, так?** If you move — it is **Куди**. If you stand — it is **Де**.

### Landmarks: Near and Opposite

We do not always know the exact address. Often we say: "It is near the bank" or "It is opposite the park".

For this, we need two magic words:
*   **Біля** [ˈbʲilʲɑ] — near / by / at.
*   **Навпроти** [nɑu̯ˈprɔtɪ] — opposite / across from.

Attention! After these words, we use the **Genitive Case**. Don't panic! For now, we learn just one simple rule for singular nouns.

**The Rule:**
*   Words ending in **-а** change to **-и**.
*   Words ending in **-я** change to **-і**.
*   Masculine words (consonant) often add **-а** or **-у**.

Let's look at examples with words ending in **-а** (most popular in the city):

*   **Аптека** → **Біля аптеки**. (Near the pharmacy).
*   **Пошта** → **Навпроти пошти**. (Opposite the post office).
*   **Зупинка** → **Біля зупинки**. (Near the stop).
*   **Школа** → **Навпроти школи**. (Opposite the school).

This is a very popular construction. **Це дуже популярна конструкція.** Ukrainians constantly use landmarks.

*   **Ми живемо біля аптеки.** (We live near the pharmacy.)
*   **Зупинка навпроти пошти.** (The stop is opposite the post office.)
*   **Чекайте біля школи.** (Wait near the school.)

> [!myth-buster]
> **Myth:** You need exact addresses like «15 Khreshchatyk Street».
> **Reality:** Locals use landmarks. «Де ти?» — «Я біля МакДональдза». It is much more common to navigate by objects than by street numbers.

### How to Ask for Directions?

You are a tourist. You are lost. What to do? You need to ask a local. Be polite!

Here is your success formula:
**Вибачте** (Excuse me) + **Підкажіть, будь ласка** (Tell me please / Prompt me please) + **Question**.

Basic questions:
1.  **Де знаходиться...?** [dɛ znɑˈxɔdɪt͡sʲɑ] — Where is located...?
2.  **Де тут...?** [dɛ tut] — Where is ... here? (Simple and casual).

Let's look at a dialogue. **Подивіться на діалог.**

**Tourist:** Вибачте! Підкажіть, будь ласка, де тут метро?
**Local:** Добрий день. Метро прямо, а потім наліво.
**Tourist:** Дякую!
**Local:** Будь ласка.

Or an option with a landmark:

**Tourist:** Вибачте, де знаходиться банк?
**Passerby:** Банк там, навпроти аптеки.
**Tourist:** Ага, бачу. Дякую!

Remember the word **знаходиться** [znɑˈxɔdɪt͡sʲɑ]. It means «is located». It is a reflexive verb, so it ends in **-ся**.
*   **Де знаходиться вокзал?** (Where is the station located?)
*   **Він знаходиться на площі.** (It is located on the square.)

Now you have a full set of tools. You know directions, you know places, and you can ask for the way. **Ви можете запитати дорогу.** Time to practice! **Час практикувати!**

## Практика: Мапа міста

Now that you know how to ask a passerby for help, let's try to be independent. **Ви знаєте слова.** Imagine you are standing in the center of a Ukrainian city with a map in your hands. **Ви в центрі міста. У вас є мапа.** The map does not speak, but it gives you clear instructions. Let's practice reading the city like a book, step by step. **Давайте читати мапу.**

### Finding Your Location

First, you must answer the most important question: **«Де я?»** (Where am I?). On a digital map, you are usually a blue dot. On a paper map, you look for street names. In Ukrainian, saying where you are often requires the preposition **«на»** [na] (on) for streets and squares, because they are open spaces.

When you identify your spot, you state it simply. You do not need complex sentences. You just name the street or the object near you. Remember to use the Locative case for the street name if you can, or just point to the name.

**Grammar note:**
Usually, we use **«на»** + Locative case for streets.
*   **вулиця** [ˈʋulɪt͡sʲɐ] (street) → **на вулиці** [na ˈʋulɪt͡sʲi]
*   **площа** [ˈplɔʃt͡ʃɐ] (square) → **на площі** [na ˈplɔʃt͡ʃi]

**Examples:**
*   **Ви тут.** [ʋɪ tut] — You are here.
*   **Я на вулиці Франка.** [ja na ˈʋulɪt͡sʲi frɐnˈka] — I am on Franko Street.
*   **Ми на площі Ринок.** [mɪ na ˈplɔʃt͡ʃi ˈrɪnɔk] — We are on Rynok Square.

> [!tip]
> **Look for the Names**
> Ukrainian streets are often named after people (Shevchenko, Franko, Lesya Ukrainka). When you see **«вулиця Шевченка»**, the name «Шевченка» is in a special form (Genitive case), meaning "Street *of* Shevchenko". You do not change the name itself when you say "I am on...". You only change the word **«вулиця»** to **«вулиці»**.

### Building a Route

Once you find yourself, find your destination. Now you connect the two points. A route is just a sequence of simple actions. To organize these actions, we use words that order time.

The two most useful words for a navigator are **«спочатку»** (at first / first) and **«потім»** (then / afterwards). These words help you break a long trip into small, manageable steps. You can build a whole paragraph just by alternating these two words with directions.

**Useful words:**
*   **спочатку** [spoˈt͡ʃatku] — first / at first
*   **потім** [ˈpɔtʲim] — then / afterwards
*   **прямо** [ˈprjamɔ] — straight
*   **назад** [naˈzad] — back / backwards

**Examples:**
*   **Спочатку ідіть прямо.** [spoˈt͡ʃatku iˈdʲitʲ ˈprjamɔ] — First, go straight.
*   **Потім повертайте наліво.** [ˈpɔtʲim poʋerˈtajte naˈlʲiʋo] — Then turn left.
*   **Аптека там.** [apˈtɛkɐ tam] — The pharmacy is there.

### Landmarks: Traffic Light and Intersection

Maps often show key objects to help you navigate. These are your anchors. Two very common anchors in any city are the **«світлофор»** (traffic light) and the **«перехрестя»** (intersection).

When you give or follow instructions, these points tell you *when* to turn. In Ukrainian, we use the preposition **«на»** (on/at) with these words too, using the Locative case ending **-і**. This is a very stable pattern.

**New Vocabulary:**
*   **світлофор** [sʲʋitloˈfɔr] — traffic light
*   **перехрестя** [pereˈxrɛsʲtʲɐ] — intersection
*   **тротуар** [trotuˈar] — sidewalk

**Usage:**
*   **світлофор** → **на світлофорі** [na sʲʋitloˈfɔri] (at the traffic light)
*   **перехрестя** → **на перехресті** [na pereˈxrɛsʲtʲi] (at the intersection)

**Examples:**
*   **Повертайте направо на світлофорі.** [poʋerˈtajte naˈpraʋo na sʲʋitloˈfɔri] — Turn right at the traffic light.
*   **Стійте на тротуарі.** [sʲˈtʲijte na trotuˈarʲi] — Stand on the sidewalk.
*   **Аптека на перехресті.** [apˈtɛkɐ na pereˈxrɛsʲtʲi] — The pharmacy is at the intersection.

### Clarification: Near and Opposite

Sometimes "left" or "right" is not precise enough. You need to verify your destination by looking at what is around it. The map shows that the bank is next to the post office, or the hotel is across from the park.

For this, we use the prepositions **«біля»** (near/by) and **«навпроти»** (opposite). These are magical words because they require a specific change in the next word (the Genitive case). But you don't need to memorize the whole table yet. Just remember these common pairs as fixed phrases.

**Key patterns:**
*   **біля** [ˈbʲilʲɐ] + word changes to **-и** or **-а** (mostly).
*   **навпроти** [naʋˈprɔtɪ] + word changes to **-и** or **-а** (mostly).

**Vocabulary:**
*   **станція** [ˈstant͡sʲijɐ] — station
*   **парк** [park] — park
*   **банк** [baŋk] — bank

**Examples:**
*   **Я чекаю біля аптеки.** [ja t͡ʃeˈkaju ˈbʲilʲɐ apˈtɛkɪ] — I am waiting near the pharmacy.
*   **Пошта навпроти парку.** [ˈpɔʃtɐ naʋˈprɔtɪ ˈparku] — The post office is opposite the park.
*   **Метро біля станції.** [meˈtrɔ ˈbʲilʲɐ ˈstant͡sʲiji] — The metro is near the station.

## Ваш вихід: Як пройти...?

### Situation: A Tourist in the City

Imagine a situation on the street. **Уявіть ситуацію на вулиці.** You see a tourist. **Ви бачите туриста.** He looks at a map. **Він дивиться на мапу.** He is looking for the center or the metro. **Він шукає центр або метро.** You can help! **Ви можете допомогти!**

You are a tourist. You are lost. **Ви турист. Ви заблукали.** What to do? **Що робити?** You need to ask a local. **Треба запитати місцевого.** Be polite! **Будьте ввічливі!**

> [!tip]
> **Keep it simple!**
> Ukrainian directions are often very short. You don't need long polite phrases like "If you would be so kind as to turn left...". Just say **«Ідіть прямо»** [iˈdʲitʲ ˈprjamɔ] (Go straight). It is polite and clear.

Here is an example of a dialogue. **Подивіться на діалог.**

**Tourist:** Вибачте! Підкажіть, будь ласка, де тут метро?
**Local:** Добрий день. Метро прямо, а потім наліво.
**Tourist:** Дякую!
**Local:** Будь ласка.

We also use landmarks. This helps the tourist. For example: «Банк біля пошти» or «Метро навпроти парку». This is better than «three hundred meters».

**Useful phrases:**
*   **Ідіть прямо.** [iˈdʲitʲ ˈprjamɔ] — Go straight.
*   **Повертайте направо.** [poʋerˈtajte naˈpraʋɔ] — Turn right.
*   **Це тут.** [t͡sɛ tut] — It is here.
*   **Це там.** [t͡sɛ tam] — It is there.

### Task: Your Route

Now it is your turn. **Тепер ваша черга.** Tell about your route. **Розкажіть про ваш маршрут.** It can be the way from home to the shop. **Це дорога з дому в магазин.** Or from work to the metro. **Або з роботи в метро.** Use the words **вулиця** [ˈʋulɪt͡sʲɐ] (street), **площа** [ˈplɔʃt͡ʃɐ] (square) and **парк** [park] (park).

Try to describe this path. **Опишіть цей шлях.**
1.  **Де ви зараз?** (Where are you now?) — Я на вулиці...
2.  **Куди ви йдете?** (Where are you going?) — Я йду в...
3.  **Де це місце?** (Where is this place?) — Це біля...
4.  **Як туди пройти?** (How to get there?) — Ідіть прямо...

This is a simple exercise. **Це проста вправа.** It trains your skills. You learn to think like a navigator. Do not be afraid to make mistakes. The main thing is to be understood.

Here is a sample answer. Read it aloud.

> [!model-answer]
> **Route Example**
>
> Я зараз удома. Я йду в магазин. Магазин на вулиці Франка. Це недалеко. Я виходжу на вулицю. Я йду прямо через парк. Потім я повертаю направо. Магазин біля аптеки. Він навпроти школи.
>
> *Translation:* I am at home now. I am going to the shop. The shop is on Franko Street. It is not far. I go out onto the street. I go straight through the park. Then I turn right. The shop is near the pharmacy. It is opposite the school.

Try to change the details. Say «пошта» instead of «магазин». Say «наліво» instead of «направо». This way you will remember these words for a long time. Practice is the path to success!

## Культурний контекст: Навігація по-українськи

You have already practiced routes on a map, but a real city has its own rules. To feel like a local, it is not enough just to know the words «ліворуч» or «праворуч». You need to understand the rhythm of a Ukrainian city. Let's talk about transport, landmarks, and the heart of every city.

### Etiquette in a Marshrutka

In Ukraine, **маршрутки** [marʃˈrutkɪ] (minibuses) are very popular. These are small buses of yellow or white color. They are fast and convenient, but have one important rule: the driver does not stop at every stop automatically.

You must tell the driver that you want to get off. Do it loudly and clearly in advance. The standard phrase is very simple, but mandatory. Without it, you will go further!

> [!culture]
> **Voice of the People**
> In a marshrutka, it is customary to pass money for the fare through other passengers. You give money to a neighbor and say: «Передайте, будь ласка, за одного» [pɛrɛˈdai̯tɛ budʲ ˈlaska za ɔdˈnɔɦɔ]. This is normal and polite.

Here is how it sounds in practice:

1. **На зупинці, будь ласка!** [na zuˈpɪnt͡si budʲ ˈlaska] — (At the stop, please!)
2. **Тут, будь ласка!** [tut budʲ ˈlaska] — (Here, please!)
3. **На наступній, будь ласка!** [na nasˈtupnʲij budʲ ˈlaska] — (At the next one, please!)

### Landmarks instead of Meters

Ukrainians rarely use cardinal directions (north, south) in the city. We also rarely say the exact distance in meters. Instead, we use **орієнтири** [orʲijenˈtɪrɪ] (landmarks).

We build a route from one visible object to another. Most often we use the words **біля** [ˈbʲilʲa] (near) and **навпроти** [naˈu̯prɔtɪ] (opposite). Note that the word after them changes slightly (this is the Genitive case, but for now just remember these phrases as whole blocks).

> [!tip]
> **Useful Tip**
> If you ask for directions, look for large objects: a bank, a pharmacy, a post office. These are your main navigation points.

Examples of live instructions:

1. **Аптека біля банку.** [apˈtɛka ˈbʲilʲa ˈbanku] — (The pharmacy is near the bank.)
2. **Зупинка навпроти парку.** [zuˈpɪnka naˈu̯prɔtɪ ˈparku] — (The stop is opposite the park.)
3. **Пошта біля метро.** [ˈpɔʃta ˈbʲilʲa mɛtˈrɔ] — (The post office is near the metro.)

### Heart of the City: Maidan and Square

Every Ukrainian city has a center. Usually, it is a **площа** [ˈplɔʃt͡ʃa] (square) or **майдан** [mai̯ˈdan] (square/plaza). It serves as more than just a place on a map. Locals use it for meetings, concerts, and walks.

The word «майдан» has a special meaning for Ukrainians (remember Maidan Nezalezhnosti in Kyiv). Remember the grammatical rule: open spaces use the preposition **на**.

Here is how we talk about the center:

1. **Ми гуляємо на площі.** [mɪ ɦuˈlʲajemɔ na ˈplɔʃt͡ʃi] — (We are walking in the square.)
2. **Я чекаю на майдані.** [ja t͡ʃɛˈkaju na mai̯ˈdanʲi] — (I am waiting at the maidan.)
3. **Концерт на площі Ринок.** [kɔnˈt͡sɛrt na ˈplɔʃt͡ʃi ˈrɪnɔk] — (The concert is on Rynok Square.)

### Додаткова практика: У центрі міста

**Турист**: Добрий день! Ви знаєте, де площа Ринок?
**Львів'янин**: Добрий день. Так, це центр міста.
**Турист**: Це далеко?
**Львів'янин**: Ні, це близько. Ідіть прямо, а потім наліво.
**Турист**: Дякую!
**Львів'янин**: Будь ласка. Гарної прогулянки!
