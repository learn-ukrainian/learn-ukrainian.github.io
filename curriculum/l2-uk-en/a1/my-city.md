## Діало́ги (Dialogues)

Navigating a new place begins with knowing how to ask for directions and how to understand the answers. When you visit a city in Ukraine, you will often need to find a specific building, shop, or service. 

Read the first dialogue. A tourist is looking for a pharmacy and stops a person on the street to ask for help. Notice how simple and direct the question is.

<div class="dialogue">


**Турист:** Ви́бачте, де тут апте́ка? *(Excuse me, where is a pharmacy here?)*


**Перехожий:** Аптека на ву́лиці Шевче́нка. *(The pharmacy is on Shevchenko street.)*


**Турист:** А бібліоте́ка? *(And the library?)*


**Перехожий:** Бібліотека в це́нтрі, бі́ля парку. *(The library is in the center, near the park.)*


**Турист:** Дякую! *(Thank you!)*


**Перехожий:** Будь ла́ска! *(You're welcome!)*


</div>



In this conversation, the tourist uses the word **тут** (here) to ask about the immediate vicinity. The passerby (**перехожий**) provides clear locations using the prepositions **на** (on) and **в** (in) along with the locative case. They also introduce the word **біля** (near) to describe one location in relation to another.

Now read the second dialogue. Two friends are talking about what buildings and services are located near where they live.

<div class="dialogue">


**Олена:** Що є біля твого́ до́му? *(What is near your house?)*


**Марко:** Біля дому є магази́н і кафе́. *(Near the house there is a shop and a café.)*


**Олена:** А шко́ла? *(And a school?)*


**Марко:** Школа дале́ко, у центрі мі́ста. *(The school is far, in the city center.)*


</div>



This dialogue focuses on describing a neighborhood. The phrase **біля твого дому** (near your house) and its answer **біля дому** (near the house) are extremely common everyday expressions. We also see the verb **є** (there is/are) used to confirm the existence of a place. Finally, the locations are contrasted using distance words like **далеко** (far) and precise locations like **у центрі міста** (in the city center).

## Місця́ в мі́сті (City Places)

Every city (**місто**) is made up of different functional spaces. Whether you are running errands, seeking entertainment, or dealing with travel, you need to know the names of these essential city places. 

Here is your core vocabulary for buildings and services:

*   **аптека** (pharmacy)
*   **бібліотека** (library)
*   **лікарня** (hospital)
*   **магазин** (shop / store)
*   **супермаркет** (supermarket)
*   **ресторан** (restaurant)
*   **кафе** (café)
*   **банк** (bank)
*   **пошта** (post office)
*   **вокзал** (train station)
*   **готель** (hotel)
*   **музей** (museum)
*   **театр** (theater)
*   **кінотеатр** (cinema)
*   **церква** (church)
*   **стадіон** (stadium)
*   **площа** (square)
*   **університет** (university)

Notice that **магазин** is a general word for any kind of shop, while **супермаркет** specifically refers to a large grocery store. A **кафе** is a very popular place in Ukraine to meet friends and drink coffee. 

### Prepositions: В or На?

When you want to say that you are *in* or *at* one of these places, you must use the locative case. You learned the locative case endings in Module 29. As a quick review, most locations take the preposition **в** (or its phonetic twin **у**) because they represent enclosed spaces. However, certain public spaces and institutions traditionally take the preposition **на**.

Here are the places that use **в / у** (inside an enclosed space):

*   **в аптеці** (in the pharmacy)
*   **у бібліотеці** (in the library)
*   **у лікарні** (in the hospital)
*   **в магазині** (in the shop)
*   **у супермаркеті** (in the supermarket)
*   **у ресторані** (in the restaurant)
*   **у кафе** (in the café — note that foreign words ending in -е do not change their ending!)
*   **у банку** (in the bank)
*   **у готелі** (in the hotel)
*   **в музеї** (in the museum)
*   **у театрі** (in the theater)
*   **у кінотеатрі** (in the cinema)
*   **у церкві** (in the church)
*   **в університеті** (in the university)

Here are the places that traditionally use **на** (at a public service or open area):

*   **на пошті** (at the post office)
*   **на вокзалі** (at the train station)
*   **на стадіоні** (at the stadium)
*   **на площі** (at the square)

:::tip
Remember the Ukrainian euphony rule: use **в** after a vowel to keep the language flowing smoothly, and use **у** after a consonant to avoid harsh clusters of sounds.
:::

### What Do You Do There?

Now let's connect these places to actions. By combining verbs you already know with the new city vocabulary, you can describe your daily routine.

*   Я **купую** лі́ки в аптеці. *(I buy medicine in the pharmacy.)*
*   Я **читаю** в бібліотеці. *(I read in the library.)*
*   Я **працюю** в о́фісі. *(I work in the office.)*
*   Я **відпочиваю** в парку. *(I relax in the park.)*
*   Ми **вечеряємо** у ресторані. *(We have dinner in the restaurant.)*
*   Вони́ **дивляться** фільм у кінотеатрі. *(They watch a film in the cinema.)*
*   Ти **п'єш** ка́ву у кафе. *(You drink coffee in the café.)*

## Де це? (Where Is It?)

When someone asks you where a building is, giving the exact street address is not always necessary. Often, people just want to know the general direction or distance. You can provide this information using specific location words.

Here are the most essential location words you need to know:

*   **тут** (here)
*   **там** (there)
*   **далеко** (far)
*   **близько** (near / close)
*   **у центрі** (in the center)
*   **на розі** (on the corner)

The first four — тут, там, далеко, близько — are adverbs that never change their form. The last two — у центрі and на розі — are prepositional phrases (preposition + noun) that work the same way in a sentence. You can use any of them after a noun to state its location.

*   Аптека **тут**. *(The pharmacy is here.)*
*   Вокзал **далеко**. *(The train station is far.)*
*   Магазин **близько**. *(The shop is close.)*
*   Кафе **на розі**. *(The café is on the corner.)*
*   Музей **у центрі**. *(The museum is in the center.)*

### Using "Біля" (Near)

If you want to say that one place is near another specific place, you must use the word **біля**. 

However, **біля** is a demanding preposition. It requires the noun that follows it to take the genitive case. You will learn the rules for the genitive case later. For now, it is best to memorize these common combinations as fixed chunks of vocabulary.

Memorize these useful chunks:

*   **біля парку** (near the park)
*   **біля дому** (near the house)
*   **біля університету** (near the university)
*   **біля ста́нції** (near the station)
*   **біля метро** (near the subway)

You can use these chunks to give very helpful directions:

*   Бібліотека **біля парку**. *(The library is near the park.)*
*   Магазин тут, **біля дому**. *(The shop is here, near the house.)*
*   Ресторан **біля університету**. *(The restaurant is near the university.)*

### Describing Your City with "Є"

When you want to tell someone what exists in your city or neighborhood, use the word **є**. You have seen this word before when talking about possession (У мене́ є...), but it also translates perfectly to the English concept of "there is" or "there are". 

Place **є** right after the location to emphasize what can be found there.

*   У моє́му місті **є** вели́кий парк. *(In my city there is a large park.)*
*   У моєму місті **є** два музеї. *(In my city there are two museums.)*
*   Тут **є** хоро́ший ресторан. *(There is a good restaurant here.)*
*   Біля дому **є** супермаркет. *(Near the house there is a supermarket.)*
*   Там **є** стара́ церква. *(There is an old church there.)*

By combining the word **є** with your new vocabulary, you can give a complete tour of your environment in Ukrainian!
