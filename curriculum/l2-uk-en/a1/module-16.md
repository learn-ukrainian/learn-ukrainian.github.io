# The Genitive I: Absence

> **Note:** The genitive case has many uses. Today we learn its first function: expressing that something **doesn't exist** or is **absent**.

## Warm-up: The Missing Coffee

Imagine this situation: You're in a cafรฉ. You order coffee. The waiter says:

**"ะะธะฑะฐััะต, ะบะฐะฒะธ ะฝะตะผะฐั."** (Sorry, there's no coffee.)

This is your introduction to Ukraine's most versatile case. The genitive has more uses than any other case, but today we focus on just one: expressing that something is absent or lacking.

The genitive is used with:
- **ะฝะตะผะฐั** (there is no)
- **ะฝะตะผะฐ** (informal: there's no)
- **ะฑะตะท** (without)
- **ั ะผะตะฝะต ะฝะตะผะฐั** (I don't have)

Understanding this pattern opens up a whole new range of expression. You can now say what you DON'T have, what ISN'T there, and what you want WITHOUT something!

> ๐ก **Thinking Trick:** Think of it this way: The genitive is the "of" case. "ะะตะผะฐั ะบะฐะฒะธ" literally means "There is no (of) coffee" โ the coffee that would exist is absent.
