## Навичка 1: Часові форми (Skill 1: Tenses)

### Модель: Минулий час

Let's talk about the past. Мину́лий час. (Past tense.) The past tense always ends in an «л» sound, or «в» for men.
Remember that the past tense must match the gender of the person doing the action.

| Person | Ending | Example | Meaning |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|
| Він (He) | -в | **Він чита́в.** | He read. |
| Вона (She)| -ла | **Вона́ чита́ла.**| She read. |
| Воно (It) | -ло | **Мі́сто спа́ло.** | The city slept. |
| Вони (They)| -ли | **Вони́ чита́ли.**| They read. |

Вчо́ра він чита́в. (Yesterday he read.) 
Вчо́ра вона́ чита́ла. (Yesterday she read.)
If you are a man, you say: Я чита́в. (I read.) If you are a woman, you say: Я чита́ла. (I read.)

> [!warning]
> The past tense matches gender. A common mistake is saying «Він чита́ла». Always match the ending to the subject!

### Модель: Майбутній час

Now let's look at the future. Майбу́тній час. (Future tense.) To make plans, we use the verb «бути».
You combine a form of «бути» with the dictionary form of your main verb.

| Бути | Verb | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| я бу́ду | чита́ти | I will read |
| ти бу́деш | чита́ти | you will read |
| він/вона́ бу́де| чита́ти | he/she will read |
| ми бу́демо | чита́ти | we will read |
| ви бу́дете | чита́ти | you will read |
| вони́ бу́дуть| чита́ти | they will read |

За́втра я бу́ду чита́ти. (Tomorrow I will read.) 
За́втра ми бу́демо відпочива́ти. (Tomorrow we will rest.)

Sometimes, for very common actions, we use a shorter word for the future. We will learn more about this later, but you might see it soon.
Я прочита́ю. (I will read.)
Я зроблю́. (I will do.)

### Модель: Куди чи Де

We use different questions for places depending on motion.

When you ask «Куди́?» (Where to?), you are talking about motion towards a destination.
Вчо́ра він ішо́в у магази́н. (Yesterday he was going to the store.) 
Куди́ він ішо́в? (Where was he going?)

When you ask «Де?» (Where at?), you are talking about a static location.
Вчо́ра він був у магази́ні. (Yesterday he was in the store.) 
Де він був? (Where was he?)

### Практика: Часові форми

Read the examples above and try to make your own sentences using the past and future tenses. Practice asking questions with "Куди" and "Де".

- ☐ Can you use "бути" + dictionary form for the future tense?
- ☐ Do you understand the difference between "Куди" and "Де"?

## Навичка 2: Повсякденне життя (Skill 2: Daily Life)

### Модель: Щоденна рутина

Let's talk about your day. Щоде́нна рути́на. (Daily routine.) We can use our past and future tenses to describe what we do from morning to night.

Вчо́ра я вста́в ра́но. (Yesterday I got up early.)
По́тім я сні́дав. (Then I had breakfast.)
Я пив ка́ву та їв ка́шу. (I drank coffee and ate porridge.)
Вде́нь я працюва́в. (In the afternoon I worked.)
Уве́чері я вече́ряв та чита́в. (In the evening I had dinner and read.)
По́тім я ліг спа́ти. (Then I went to bed.)

And what about tomorrow? Let's plan it.

За́втра я бу́ду встава́ти ра́но. (Tomorrow I will get up early.)
Я бу́ду сні́дати. (I will have breakfast.)
По́тім я бу́ду працюва́ти. (Then I will work.)
Я бу́ду вече́ряти вдо́ма. (I will have dinner at home.)

### Модель: Замовлення їжі та покупки

When you go out, you need to buy things. Купува́ти. (To buy.) Let's practice ordering food and asking for prices.
Here is a small conversation in a cafe. Read it carefully.

— До́брий день! (Good afternoon!)
— Приві́т! Я бу́ду ка́ву, будь ла́ска. (Hello! I will have coffee, please.)
— Ви бу́дете торт? (Will you have cake?)
— Ні, дя́кую. Я не хо́чу. Скі́льки кошту́є ка́ва? (No, thank you. I don't want to. How much does the coffee cost?)
— Ка́ва кошту́є 50 гри́вень. (The coffee costs 50 hryvnias.)
— Дя́кую. Ось гро́ші. (Thank you. Here is the money.)

> [!tip]
> In Ukrainian, you do not need the verb "to be" to ask for a price. We do not say "How much is it?". We always ask «Скі́льки кошту́є?» (How much does it cost?).

### Практика: Повсякденне життя

Think about your daily routine. Try to write down what you did yesterday and what you will do tomorrow. Practice the cafe dialogue out loud.

### Самоперевірка

- ☐ Can you describe your daily routine in the past and future?
- ☐ Can you order a coffee and ask for the price?

## Навичка 3: Опис та погода (Skill 3: Description & Weather)

### Модель: Прислівники способу дії

Sometimes you want to describe how someone does something. We use adverbs for this. Adverbs describe the action.
They answer the question «Як?» (How?). They tell us if an action is fast, slow, good, or bad.

Він чита́є до́бре. (He reads well.)
Вона́ чита́є пога́но. (She reads poorly.)
Він іде́ шви́дко. (He walks quickly.)
Ми чита́ємо ти́хо. (We read quietly.)
Вони́ чита́ють го́лосно. (They read loudly.)

These words usually come after the verb, but they can also come before. Both ways are correct.
Я шви́дко сні́дав. (I had breakfast quickly.)
Вона́ до́бре працюва́ла. (She worked well.)

### Модель: Прогноз погоди

Talking about the weather is a great way to start a conversation. Прогно́з пого́ди. (Weather forecast.)
In Ukrainian, we do not use the word "it" for weather. We just state the condition directly. This is very simple and easy to remember.

Сього́дні те́пло. (Today is warm.)
Вчо́ра було́ хо́лодно. (Yesterday was cold.)
За́втра бу́де те́пло. (Tomorrow will be warm.)
Сього́дні со́нячно. (Today is sunny.)

We can also connect the weather to the seasons of the year.
Взи́мку хо́лодно. (In winter it is cold.)
Влі́тку те́пло. (In summer it is warm.)
Навесні́ до́бре. (In spring it is good.)
Восени́ пога́но. (In autumn it is bad.)

> [!culture]
> Talking about the weather is a very normal and polite way to start a small talk conversation in Ukraine. It is safe and everyone understands it. You can always say «Сього́дні те́пло!» to a friend or neighbor.

### Практика: Опис та погода

Use adverbs to describe how you do different activities. Look out the window and describe the weather today.

### Самоперевірка

- ☐ Can you use adverbs like "добре", "погано", "швидко"?
- ☐ Can you talk about the weather and seasons?

## Інтеграційне завдання (Integration Task)

### Вчорашні події та завтрашні плани

Let's read a short story that combines everything. This is a great way to practice. Read the sentences slowly.

Вчо́ра я вста́в о сьо́мій годи́ні. (Yesterday I got up at seven o'clock.)
Було́ хо́лодно та хма́рно. (It was cold and cloudy.)
Я сні́дав удо́ма. (I had breakfast at home.)
Я пив ка́ву. (I drank coffee.)
По́тім я пішо́в на робо́ту. (Then I went to work.)
Вде́нь я обі́дав у кафе́. (In the afternoon I had lunch in a cafe.)
Обі́д коштува́в 150 гри́вень. (Lunch cost 150 hryvnias.)

Now let's look at tomorrow.

За́втра я бу́ду відпочива́ти. (Tomorrow I will rest.)
За́втра бу́де те́пло та со́нячно. (Tomorrow will be warm and sunny.)
Я піду́ в магази́н. (I will go to the store.)
Я бу́ду купува́ти хліб та молоко́. (I will buy bread and milk.)
По́тім я бу́ду вече́ряти. (Then I will have dinner.)
