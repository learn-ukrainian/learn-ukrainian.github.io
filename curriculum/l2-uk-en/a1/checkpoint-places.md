## Що ми зна́ємо? (What Do We Know?)

You have traveled a long way through this module phase. Navigating a new city in a new language is a major milestone. When you step out onto a street in Ukraine, you need to quickly understand your surroundings. By now, you know how to ask where things are, how to explain where you are going, and how to describe where you come from. 

Let's reflect on the tools you have gathered over the past few lessons. First, can you apply the rules of euphony for smooth, natural speech? We use the alternating letters **у** and **в**, **і** and **й**, **з** and **із** or **зі** to make the Ukrainian language flow like a continuous song. Avoiding clashing sounds makes your speech sound much more natural to native ears.

Next, think about space. Can you say where things are located? 
*   **Я в шко́лі.** (I am at school.)
*   **Брат на робо́ті.** (Brother is at work.)
*   **Студент у парку.** (The student is in the park.)

Can you name important places in the city? 
*   **Тут є апте́ка.** (There is a pharmacy here.)
*   **Музей у це́нтрі.** (The museum is in the center.)
*   **Супермаркет на ву́лиці.** (The supermarket is on the street.)

Can you describe where you are going when you move? 
*   **Я йду в магази́н.** (I am going to the store.)
*   **Вона ї́де на вокза́л.** (She is traveling to the station.)
*   **Ми йдемо́ на пло́щу.** (We are going to the square.)

Can you give directions to a lost tourist and talk about transport? 
*   **Ідіть пря́мо, по́тім напра́во.** (Go straight, then to the right.)
*   **Ми ї́демо на метро́.** (We are traveling by metro.)
*   **Аптека налі́во.** (The pharmacy is to the left.)

Finally, can you say where you or your friends are from?
*   **Я з Украї́ни.** (I am from Ukraine.)
*   **Він зі США.** (He is from the USA.)
*   **Вони з Кана́ди.** (They are from Canada.)

These three questions are your navigation tools. The difference between **Де?** (Where at?), **Куди?** (Where to?), and **Звідки?** (Where from?) is the absolute key to unlocking the city.

## Чита́ння (Reading Practice)

Let's see all these pieces working together in a real situation. When you visit a bustling city like Kyiv, you need to combine your knowledge of locations, directions, and transport. 

Read the story of a tourist navigating the capital. Notice how they state their origin, ask for directions to a specific landmark, and use transport. 

> **Турист у Києві** (A tourist in Kyiv)
> 
> Це Марк. Він турист. *(This is Mark. He is a tourist.)*
> Марк з Канади. *(Mark is from Canada.)*
> За́раз він у Києві. *(Now he is in Kyiv.)*
> Марк хо́че зна́ти: де музей? *(Mark wants to know: where is the museum?)*
> Він пита́є: «Де тут музей?» *(He asks: "Where is the museum here?")*
> Лю́ди ка́жуть: «Музей у центрі». *(People say: "The museum is in the center.")*
> Вони кажуть: «Ідіть на метро». *(They say: "Go to the metro.")*
> Марк іде́ на ста́нцію. *(Mark goes to the station.)*
> Він їде на метро. *(He travels by metro.)*
> Марк виходить і йде прямо. *(Mark exits and goes straight.)*
> Потім він іде направо. *(Then he goes to the right.)*
> Ось вели́ка пло́ща. *(Here is a large square.)*
> Музей на пло́щі. *(The museum is on the square.)*
> Марк ду́же ра́дий. *(Mark is very happy.)*
> Потім він іде в кафе́. *(Then he goes to the cafe.)*
> Він хоче пи́ти ка́ву. *(He wants to drink coffee.)*
> Потім Марк їде у Львів. *(Then Mark travels to Lviv.)*
> Він питає: «Де вокзал?» *(He asks: "Where is the station?")*

Look closely at the movement and positioning in the text. When Mark states his origin, he uses the preposition **з** (from): 
*   **Марк з Канади.** (Mark is from Canada.)

When he states his current location, he uses the locative case with **у** or **на**: 
*   **у Києві** (in Kyiv)
*   **у центрі** (in the center)
*   **на площі** (on the square)

When he describes his destination, he uses the accusative case: 
*   **у Львів** (to Lviv)
*   **в кафе** (to the cafe)
*   **на станцію** (to the station)

He navigates using simple directional words and transport phrases: 
*   **прямо** (straight)
*   **направо** (to the right)
*   **на метро** (by metro)

This is the power of combining your vocabulary. You can now build complete narratives about travel, exploration, and movement.

## Грама́тика (Grammar Summary)

It is time to organize everything you know about places and movement. Ukrainian relies on a beautiful, consistent logic. The prepositions you use change depending on your intention: are you describing a static location, a dynamic direction, or an origin?

Let's review the core patterns that form the foundation of urban navigation.

**1. Euphony (Милозву́чність)**
Ukrainian avoids clashing consonants and clashing vowels. This is why we alternate short words to maintain a musical rhythm.
*   **у** / **в** (in, into, at): **Він у Києві** (He is in Kyiv) vs. **Вона в кімнаті** (She is in the room). 
*   **і** / **й** (and): **Брат і сестра** (Brother and sister) vs. **Мама й тато** (Mom and dad).
*   **з** / **із** / **зі** (from): **Я з України** (I am from Ukraine), **Ми зі США** (We are from the USA), **Вона зі школи** (She is from school).

**2. Location: Де? (Where?)**
To say where something is located, we use the locative case with **в** / **у** (inside) or **на** (on/at open spaces and events). 
*   **в школі** (at school)
*   **в аптеці** (at the pharmacy)
*   **в центрі** (in the center)
*   **на роботі** (at work)
*   **на площі** (on the square)
*   **на вокзалі** (at the station)

**3. Direction: Куди? (Where to?)**
To say where you are going, we use the accusative case with **в** / **у** or **на**. The preposition matches the one you would use for the static location.
*   **у школу** (to school)
*   **в аптеку** (to the pharmacy)
*   **у центр** (to the center)
*   **на роботу** (to work)
*   **на площу** (to the square)
*   **на вокзал** (to the station)

**4. Origin: Звідки? (Where from?)**
To say where you come from or where you are returning from, we use **з** / **із** / **зі** plus the genitive case.
*   **з України** (from Ukraine)
*   **з Канади** (from Canada)
*   **з роботи** (from work)
*   **зі школи** (from school)

**5. Transport and Directions**
When navigating the city streets, we use fixed phrases that you can easily memorize.
*   Transport: **на метро** (by metro), **на автобусі** (by bus) or simply **автобусом** (by bus).
*   Directions: **прямо** (straight), **направо** (to the right), **наліво** (to the left), **далеко** (far), **близько** (close).

## Діало́г (Connected Dialogue)

Theory becomes real when you use it with another person. Imagine you are lost in a busy Ukrainian city. You need to ask a local resident for directions. You will need to introduce yourself, state your goal, and understand their instructions clearly.

Read this conversation. Pay close attention to how the tourist and the local exchange information about places, movement, and public transport.

<div class="dialogue">


**Марк:** Ви́бачте, я з Канади. Де тут музей? *(Excuse me, I am from Canada. Where is the museum here?)*


**Жінка:** Музей у центрі. Ідіть на метро до ста́нції Хреща́тик. *(The museum is in the center. Go by metro to the station Khreshchatyk.)*


**Марк:** А як діста́тися від метро? *(And how to get there from the metro?)*


**Жінка:** Ви́йдіть і йдіть направо. Музей на площі. *(Exit and go to the right. The museum is on the square.)*


**Марк:** Дякую! А потім я хо́чу ї́хати у Львів. Де вокзал? *(Thank you! And then I want to travel to Lviv. Where is the station?)*


**Жінка:** Вокзал далеко, ї́дьте на метро до станції Вокза́льна. *(The station is far, travel by metro to the station Vokzalna.)*


</div>



Let's break down the progression in the dialogue. 

Mark starts with the concept of origin to establish context and explain why he needs help.
*   **Я з Канади.** (I am from Canada.)

Then he asks regarding the location of the museum. The woman responds with the locative case.
*   **Музей у центрі.** (The museum is in the center.)

She then gives a command involving transport and direction to a specific destination.
*   **Ідіть на метро.** (Go by metro.)

Mark asks for further directions from the station. 
*   **А як дістатися від метро?** (And how to get there from the metro?)

The woman uses simple directional commands and confirms the final location.
*   **Вийдіть і йдіть направо.** (Exit and go to the right.)
*   **Музей на площі.** (The museum is on the square.)

Finally, Mark states a new destination using the accusative case.
*   **Я хочу їхати у Львів.** (I want to travel to Lviv.)

The woman replies that the station is far away and gives another transport instruction.
*   **Вокзал далеко.** (The station is far.)
*   **Їдьте на метро.** (Travel by metro.)

You are now capable of having this exact conversation on the streets of Ukraine.

## Підсумок — Summary

You have reached a significant checkpoint in your learning journey. You can now navigate Ukrainian cities with confidence and clarity. Let's look at exactly what you have mastered.

You know the euphony rules for natural, flowing speech. You can say **брат і сестра** but **мама й тато**, choosing the right connector every time. You alternate **у** and **в**, **з** and **зі** without thinking twice. Your Ukrainian flows smoothly, without the harsh clashes that mark a beginner.

You can say exactly WHERE something is located using the locative case. When someone asks **Де музей?**, you answer with confidence: **Музей у центрі. Він на площі.** You know that **в** and **на** each have their own places — **в школі** for enclosed spaces, **на вокзалі** for open or public ones.

You can say WHERE you are GOING using the accusative case for direction. The question **Куди?** is now your compass: **Я йду в магазин. Вона їде на вокзал. Ми хо́чемо їхати у Львів.** You understand that the preposition stays the same — only the case ending changes.

You can say WHERE you are FROM using genitive chunks. **Я з України. Він зі США. Вона з Канади.** The question **Звідки?** opens the door to identity — where you come from is always part of the conversation.

You can use different modes of transport — **на метро, автобусом, на автобусі** — and give clear, precise directions: **прямо, направо, наліво**. You have built the necessary tools to explore any city in Ukraine, interact with locals, and find your way to museums, cafes, and train stations.

Next, we move to a new essential survival skill. In the A1.6 phase, we will explore Food and Shopping. You will learn how to order food in a restaurant, buy items in a grocery store, and use the accusative case for direct objects.

Ви гото́ві до мі́ста! You are fully ready to explore the city.
