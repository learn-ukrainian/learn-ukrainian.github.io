# Lesson Content

## warm-up
**The Object of Desire**

Welcome to Phase A1.2. The city awaits.

In Phase 1, you learned to describe *who* you are ("Ğ¯ ÑÑ‚ÑƒĞ´ĞµĞ½Ñ‚") and *where* you are ("Ğ¯ Ğ²Ğ´Ğ¾Ğ¼Ğ°"). The world was static. You were a statue in a museum.
Now, you start to move. You interact. You want things. You take things. You see things.

In English, "I see the **lamp**" and "**The lamp** is here" use the same word: "lamp".
In Ukrainian, this is impossible.
When you *do something* to a word, you change it. You mark it. You tag it as the "target".
This tag is called the **Accusative Case**.

Imagine you have a laser pointer.
- **Subject:** YOU (The Actor).
- **Verb:** The Laser Beam (The Action).
- **Object:** The Target (The Accusative).

When the laser hits a Feminine noun, it melts the ending.

### Section 1: The Invisible Shield (Masculine & Neuter)

If a noun is **Masculine Inanimate** (an object, not a person) or **Neuter**, nothing happens. The laser beam bounces off.

### Deep Dive: The Laser Beam (Transitivity)
