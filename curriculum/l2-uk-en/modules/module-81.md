# Lesson Content

## Warm-up

### Welcome to B1!

You already know a lot: cases, tenses, hundreds of words. But have you noticed how Ukrainian speakers talk about the past? Why sometimes **читав** and sometimes **прочитав**? Why **писав листа годину** (was writing a letter for an hour) but **написав листа** (wrote and finished the letter)?

This isn't just grammar. It's a **way of seeing the world**.

English asks: **When?** — past, present, future.

Ukrainian asks: **How?** — process or result? Ongoing or completed?

> **Did you know?**
>
> Slavic languages have a unique system called **aspect** (in Ukrainian: **вид** /wɪd/) that doesn't exist in English, German, or French. It's not about *when* something happened, but about *how* you see it: as a **process** or as a **result**. Native speakers of Slavic languages literally **perceive time differently** from speakers of Germanic or Romance languages.

Two questions about the same evening:
- **Що ти робив учора?** — What were you doing yesterday? (process)
- **Що ти зробив учора?** — What did you accomplish yesterday? (result)

One word change — different worldview.

## Presentation

### Two Aspects, Two Perspectives

Ukrainian verbs come in pairs. In this module, you'll learn five key terms:

| Ukrainian | Pronunciation | English |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **вид** | /wɪd/ | aspect |
| **недоконаний** | /nɛdoˈkonɑnɪj/ | imperfective |
| **доконаний** | /doˈkonɑnɪj/ | perfective |
| **тривалість** | /trɪˈvɑlʲistʲ/ | duration |
| **завершення** | /zɑvɛrˈʃɛnʲːɑ/ | completion |

**Imperfective aspect (недоконаний вид)** — you're inside the action, seeing the process:
- Duration, repetition
- No focus on completion
- "What was happening?"

**Perfective aspect (доконаний вид)** — you're looking at the action from outside, seeing the result:
- One completed action
- Result achieved
- "What happened? What got done?"

| Imperfective | Perfective | Difference |
|--------------|------------|-----------|
| читати | прочитати | reading vs finished reading |
| писати | написати | writing vs wrote (completed) |
| робити | зробити | doing vs done |
| вчити | вивчити | learning vs learned (mastered) |
| їсти | з'їсти | eating vs ate (finished) |

> **Myth vs Fact**
>
> **Myth:** "Perfective = past tense, imperfective = present."
>
> **Fact:** Aspect and tense are different things! Both aspects exist in the past (читав/прочитав), future (буду читати/прочитаю). Aspect is about perspective, tense is about time.

### Aspect in the Past Tense

**Imperfective = process, duration, repetition:**

- Я **читав** книгу весь вечір. — I was reading a book all evening.
- Вона **працювала** в банку три роки. — She worked at a bank for three years.
- Ми **ходили** в кіно щотижня. — We went to the cinema every week.

**Perfective = one completed action, result:**

- Я **прочитав** книгу. — I read (finished) the book.
- Вона **написала** листа. — She wrote (completed) the letter.
- Ми **подивилися** фільм. — We watched (saw) the film.

> **Time markers hint at aspect**
>
> **Imperfective markers:** весь день (all day), три години (three hours), щодня (every day), завжди (always), часто (often), довго (for a long time)
>
> **Perfective markers:** раптом (suddenly), нарешті (finally), вже (already), одного разу (once), за годину (in an hour)

### Aspect in the Future Tense

**Imperfective (буду + infinitive) = process:**

- Я **буду читати** завтра. — I will be reading tomorrow.
- Вона **буде працювати** весь день. — She will be working all day.
- Ми **будемо чекати** на тебе. — We will be waiting for you.

**Perfective (single word) = completed action:**

- Я **прочитаю** цю книгу. — I will read (finish) this book.
- Вона **напише** листа. — She will write (complete) the letter.
- Ми **подивимося** фільм. — We will watch (see) the film.

| Imperfective Future | Perfective Future |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| буду робити | зроблю |
| буду писати | напишу |
| буду читати | прочитаю |
| буду вчити | вивчу |
| буду готувати | приготую |

### Aspect in the Present Tense

**Only imperfective aspect has a present tense!**

Why? The present describes actions happening now — they're not finished yet.

- Я **читаю** книгу. — I am reading a book. (ongoing)
- Вона **пише** листа. — She is writing a letter. (ongoing)
- Ми **готуємо** вечерю. — We are preparing dinner. (ongoing)

**Perfective in "present" form = future:**

- Я **прочитаю** = I will read (not "I am reading")
- Вона **напише** = She will write (not "She is writing")

> **Did you know?**
>
> This explains why Ukrainian speakers sometimes confuse English tenses! In Ukrainian, "Я прочитаю" looks like a present tense form (я прочита-**ю**, like "я чита-**ю**"), but it means future. The English tense system and the Ukrainian aspect system are **different ways of organizing reality**.

### The Key Decision: Which Aspect to Choose?

**Use imperfective when:**

1. **Process matters:** Я довго шукав ключі. (I was looking for keys for a long time.)
2. **Repeated action:** Він завжди запізнювався. (He was always late.)
3. **Ongoing state:** Вона працювала вчителькою. (She worked as a teacher.)
4. **No focus on result:** Що ти робив? (What were you doing?)

**Use perfective when:**

1. **Result matters:** Я знайшов ключі! (I found the keys!)
2. **Single event:** Він запізнився на зустріч. (He was late to the meeting.)
3. **Completed action:** Вона закінчила університет. (She graduated from university.)
4. **Sequence of events:** Він прийшов, сів і почав читати. (He arrived, sat down, and started reading.)

> **Did you know?**
>
> Narrative in Ukrainian is built on the **perfective aspect** — a sequence of completed actions: "Він **прийшов**, **сів**, **відкрив** книгу і **почав** читати." (He arrived, sat down, opened the book and started reading.) But if you're describing background or atmosphere — that's imperfective: "**Було** тихо. **Світило** сонце. Він **сидів** і **читав**." (It was quiet. The sun was shining. He was sitting and reading.)

### Choose the Correct Aspect

   > [!answer] **читав** (duration)
2. Нарешті я ___ (читав/прочитав) цю книгу!
   > [!answer] **прочитав** (completed)
3. Вона завжди ___ (готувала/приготувала) смачну їжу.
   > [!answer] **готувала** (repetition)
4. Вчора вона ___ (готувала/приготувала) борщ.
   > [!answer] **приготувала** (one completed action)
5. Що ти ___ (робив/зробив) весь день?
   > [!answer] **робив** (process)
6. Що ти ___ (робив/зробив) вчора?
   > [!answer] **зробив** (focus on result)

### Transform the Sentences

Change imperfective to perfective (or vice versa):

1. Він писав листа годину. (He was writing a letter for an hour.)
   > [!answer] Він **написав** листа. (He wrote/finished the letter.)
2. Вона прочитала статтю. (She read/finished the article.)
   > [!answer] Вона **читала** статтю. (She was reading the article.)
3. Ми дивилися фільм весь вечір. (We were watching a movie all evening.)
   > [!answer] Ми **подивилися** фільм. (We watched/finished the movie.)
4. Я вивчив нові слова. (I learned/mastered new words.)
   > [!answer] Я **вчив** нові слова. (I was learning new words.)

### Describe a Sequence

Use perfective aspect for a sequence of completed actions:

**Example:** Він прокинувся, встав, умився, поснідав і пішов на роботу.
(He woke up, got up, washed, had breakfast, and went to work.)

Describe your morning using perfective aspect:
- прокинутися (to wake up)
- встати (to get up)
- умитися (to wash up)
- одягнутися (to get dressed)
- поснідати (to have breakfast)
- вийти (to leave)

## Production

### Dialogues with Aspect Contrast

Create dialogues contrasting both aspects:

**Example:**
- A: Що ти робив учора ввечері? (What were you doing yesterday evening?)
- B: Читав книгу. (I was reading.)
- A: Прочитав? (Did you finish it?)
- B: Ні, ще читаю. (No, still reading.)

### Describe Your Week

Use both aspects:
1. What you accomplished (completed) — perfective
2. How you spent your time (process) — imperfective

**Example:**
Цього тижня я **прочитав** дві статті (completed). Увечері я зазвичай **дивився** серіал (repetition). У середу я **написав** важливого листа (completed). Весь четвер я **працював** над проєктом (duration).

(This week I read two articles. In the evenings I usually watched a series. On Wednesday I wrote an important letter. All Thursday I worked on a project.)

# Summary

## The Aspect System

**Imperfective = HOW the action unfolds:**
- Process, duration
- Repetition, habit
- Ongoing state

**Perfective = WHAT happened:**
- Completed action
- Result achieved
- Single event

## Key Decisions

| Context | Aspect |
|---------|--------|
| Duration (весь день) | Imperfective |
| Repetition (завжди, щодня) | Imperfective |
| One completed action | Perfective |
| Sequence of events | Perfective |
| Focus on result (нарешті!) | Perfective |
| Focus on process | Imperfective |

## Remember

- Present tense → only imperfective
- Perfective in "present" form → future
- Narrative (sequence) → perfective
- Background, atmosphere → imperfective

> **Key Takeaway**
>
> Aspect is not a grammar rule — it's a **way of seeing an action**. Imperfective — you're inside the process. Perfective — you're looking at the result from outside. This is the heart of Slavic thinking about time.
