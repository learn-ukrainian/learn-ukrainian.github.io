## 2. Narrative Arc Analysis

- **HOOK:** Strong. "більше ніж історична постать; це живий архетип". Immediately establishes relevance.
- **TENSION:** The conflict between the "Soft Power" of Olga and "Hard Power" of Sviatoslav drives the early narrative.
- **JOURNEY:** We follow him from the shadow of his father's death, through the Khazar campaign, to the Danube, and finally the tragic trap.
- **CLIMAX:** The siege of Dorostol and the "Do not shame the Rus land" speech.
- **RESOLUTION:** His death and the "skull cup" legacy.
- **CALL TO ACTION:** The "Heritage" section linking him to modern SSO is a powerful bridge to the present.

**Verdict:** Excellent pacing and structural integrity.

## 3. Weak Moment Detection

- **No major weak moments found.** The text avoids "textbook voice" and uses active, engaging language ("кардіограму серця", "пардус").
- **Minor Note:** The metaphor "броунівським рухом" in "Хронологічний огляд правління" is a bit anachronistic for a history text, but acceptable for a modern audience.

## 5. Critique & Issues

1.  **Vocabulary Count:** The module has 15 vocabulary items defined in the sidecar file. The soft target for C1 is ~24 words. While the *text* is rich in vocabulary, the *explicit list* is shorter than ideal. **Action:** Consider adding 5-10 more terms from the text (e.g., *гегемон*, *легітимність*, *експансія*, *катафрактарій*, *християнізація*) in a future update.
2.  **Metaphor Choice:** The use of "броунівським рухом" (Brownian motion) to describe his campaigns implies chaos/randomness, whereas the text later argues his campaigns were strategic ("економічна логіка"). This is a slight contradiction.
3.  **Citation Specificity:** The claim about Russian myths depicting him in a "kosovorotka" is strong but general. It works for the narrative but could be more precise about *which* 19th-century historiography is meant.

## 6. Summary

This is an **excellent** module that meets the high standards of the BIO seminar track. It transforms a standard biography into a deep inquiry about the nature of power, state-building, and identity. The decolonization lens is applied sharply and effectively.

## 7. Verdict

**Status:** **PASS** (with recommendation to expand vocabulary list later).
