## Cultural Hooks
1. **Ivan Franko's "Kamenyari" (The Stonecutters)**: The suffix `-яр` is immortalized in Franko's poem "Kamenyari", symbolizing the relentless spirit of progress and Ukrainian resilience. The phrase «лупайте сю скалу» (chip away at this rock) is iconic.
2. **The Kobzar Tradition**: The suffix `-ар` is central to `кобзар` (kobza player), the spiritual guardian of Ukrainian history and epic poetry. The Kobzar is not just a musician but a symbol of national memory (Shevchenko's "Kobzar").

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Hard vs. Soft Consonants (-ар vs. -яр)**:
   - *Error*: `лікаяр` or `каменар`.
   - *Correction*: `лікар` (after hard 'к'), `каменяр` (after soft 'н').
   - *Why*: Confusion over phonetic rules; `-яр` follows soft consonants, `-ар` follows hard ones (usually).
2. **Suffix Overgeneralization (-ник vs. -ач)**:
   - *Error*: `викладник` instead of `викладач`.
   - *Correction*: `викладач`.
   - *Why*: `-ник` is the most productive suffix, so learners overapply it. `-ач` often implies an active agent derived from a verb (викладати -> викладач).
3. **Russian Interference (Soft Sign)**:
   - *Error*: `лікарь`, `секретарь`.
   - *Correction*: `лікар`, `секретар` (hard 'р' at the end).
   - *Why*: In Russian, these words end with a soft sign (`врач` is different, but `секретарь`, `календарь` match). In Ukrainian, names of professions on `-р` are typically hard in the nominative.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Register**: Distinction between `робітник` (blue-collar worker) and `працівник` (employee/generic) is crucial for B2.
- **Feminatives**: Briefly mention that for these masculine suffixes, specific feminine forms exist (`лікарка`, `викладачка`, `працівниця`), but focus the structural analysis on the masculine derivation bases first.
- **Tone**: Use the "Recruiter" persona to frame this as "Job Titles" or "Defining Roles" to make grammar practical.
