# Дослідження: Другорядні члени речення

## State Standard Reference
§4.3.2: "Просте ускладнене речення: просте речення з однорідними членами... дієприкметниковим зворотом..."
Alignment: The State Standard explicitly covers syntax at the level of simple and complex sentences (§4.3.2, §4.3.4). While "другорядні члени" (secondary members) are not always defined as isolated standalone categories in the B2 catalog, they are the foundational components that form the "просте ускладнене речення" (simple extended sentences with homogeneous members, participial phrases, and appositions) required at this proficiency level.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Poetic Syntax**: The iconic Ukrainian poem "Садок вишневий коло хати" by Taras Shevchenko is a masterpiece of sentence expansion painting a vivid scene ("вишневий" – agreed attribute, "коло хати" – adverbial of place/non-agreed attribute). It demonstrates how secondary members create atmosphere.
2. **Modern Urban Rhythm**: Contemporary Ukrainian literature (e.g., Serhiy Zhadan) relies heavily on complex strings of non-agreed attributes and adverbials to create the staccato, descriptive rhythm characteristic of modern Ukrainian urban realism.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Verbal Government Interference**: "дякувати кого" / "вибачати кого" → **"дякувати кому" / "вибачати кому"** — Russian interference leads learners to use the Accusative case where the internal logic of Ukrainian requires the Dative.
2. **Infinitive Function Confusion**: "Він почав читати" (part of compound predicate) vs "Вона просила його прийти" (infinitive acting as an object) — learners struggle to distinguish when an infinitive completes an auxiliary verb vs when it is a secondary member.
3. **Apposition Hyphenation**: Writing "ріка-Дніпро" instead of "ріка Дніпро" (no hyphen for a generic class word followed by a proper noun) vs "Дніпро-ріка" (hyphen needed when the proper noun comes first).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonization**: Teach unique Ukrainian verb government (дякувати, хворіти на, зраджувати) purely as inherent system rules. Never frame them as "differences from Russian". Treat Ukrainian as the absolute baseline.
- **Pedagogy (TTT)**: Start with a diagnostic sentence breakdown, move to rule discovery, then controlled practice. 
- **Register & Immersion**: 100% Ukrainian immersion is required. Explain complex linguistic terminology using simple, descriptive Ukrainian phrasing.
