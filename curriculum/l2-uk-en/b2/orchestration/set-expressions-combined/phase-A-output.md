## Cultural Hooks
1. **Сакральність короваю**: The expression «На чужий коровай очей не поривай» is rooted deeply in Ukrainian wedding traditions. The *korovai* (ritual bread) symbolized the couple's destiny and shared prosperity. Attempting to claim someone else's korovai was metaphorically akin to stealing their fate, making this idiom a profound cultural injunction against envy, far deeper than simply "don't take someone's food" (Source: Ukrainian ethnographic records of wedding rites).
2. **Аграрний контекст досвіду**: The origin of «собаку з'їв» is linked to grueling agricultural labor. A peasant who worked so hard they "would even eat a dog" (an absolute taboo) signifies someone who has passed through extreme exhaustion and trials. Thus, it evolved to mean a person with undisputed, hard-won expertise—someone who has survived the ultimate test of their craft.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Калькування англійських ідіом** → *«дощ падає котами і собаками»* (it's raining cats and dogs) замість українського відповідника «ллє як з відра» — Direct L1 interference.
2. **Лексична підміна компонентів** → *«продавати усмішки»* замість правильного «продавати витрішки» — Learners often substitute components of fixed expressions with semantically related words, breaking the idiomatic unity.
3. **Контамінація (Contamination)** → *«грати значення»* — A hybrid error mixing «відігравати роль» (to play a role) and «мати значення» (to have meaning).
