## Cultural Hooks
1. **Pioneers of Fact-Checking:** Ukraine has become a global center of excellence for media literacy and fact-checking (e.g., organizations like StopFake, founded in 2014), proactively countering disinformation rather than just passively consuming news.
2. **Debate Culture:** The vibrant university debate culture in Ukraine often utilizes the British Parliamentary format, placing a strong emphasis on the strict etiquette of polite but firm disagreement, reflecting European democratic integration.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Direct Translation of "Make an argument":** Learners say *"робити аргумент"* instead of the correct collocation *"наводити аргумент"*.
2. **Register Mismatch in Disagreement:** Defaulting to informal *"Я не згоден"* in professional settings instead of employing hedging/polite forms like *"Дозвольте не погодитися"* or *"Слушна думка, проте..."*.
3. **Causal Conjunction Mix-up:** Overusing the colloquial *"так як"* instead of the formal *"оскільки"* or *"тому що"* when structuring written analytical reports.

## Cross-References
- **Builds on:** b2-69, b2-70 (Business Writing), b2-73, b2-74 (Media Analysis), b2-75 (Discussion Debate)

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing:** Highlight Ukrainian agency in the media landscape. Ukrainians are active creators of counter-disinformation frameworks and robust civil discourse, not just subjects of information wars. Use examples of modern Ukrainian civic organizations or independent journalism.
- **Immersion:** 100% Ukrainian. Do not use English scaffolding.
- **Tone:** Professional, objective, and analytical. Use modal hedging («ймовірно», «можна припустити») appropriately for B2/C1 threshold writing.
