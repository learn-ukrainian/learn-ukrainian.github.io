## Cultural Hooks
1. **Іван Пулюй (Ivan Puluj)**: A Ukrainian physicist and co-discoverer of X-rays. He constructed the first X-ray tube but lost the priority to Wilhelm Röntgen because he delayed publishing his findings in a major European academic language, focusing instead on translating the Bible into Ukrainian. A perfect cautionary tale about the importance of finalizing and publishing research.
2. **Марина В'язовська (Maryna Viazovska)**: Ukrainian mathematician who won the Fields Medal in 2022 for her breakthrough in solving the sphere-packing problem in 8 dimensions. Her clear formulation of the problem is a modern example of academic excellence.
3. **Агапіт Печерський (Agapetus of Pechersk)**: An 11th-century monk in Kyivan Rus, considered the first physician-researcher, emphasizing empirical observation over purely spiritual healing.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **«Прийти до висновку»** → Correct form: **«Дійти висновку»** (або «зробити висновок»). Why: Calque from Russian "прийти к выводу" which inappropriately uses a verb of physical motion for an abstract concept.
2. **«В якості прикладу»** → Correct form: **«Як приклад»** (або «наприклад»). Why: Russian calque "в качестве" misuses the locative case for role/comparison.
3. **Dangling participles (Дієприслівникові звороти)** → Error: "Читаючи статтю, виникло питання." Correct: "Коли я читав статтю, виникло питання." Why: The subject of the main clause ("питання") must be the agent of the participle ("читаючи").
4. **Passive voice dominance** → Error: "Було зроблено аналіз." Correct: "Ми проаналізували" або "Аналіз зроблено" (безособова форма на -но/-то). Why: Overuse of passive voice is a hallmark of Soviet bureaucratic style; Ukrainian favors active subjects and natural impersonal forms.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Frame the active voice ("Ми дослідили") as a distinct feature of the Ukrainian academic tradition, actively decolonizing from the passive, bureaucratic style ("было исследовано") imposed during the Soviet era.
- 100% Ukrainian immersion is required. Explain all academic terminology in Ukrainian without English scaffolding.
- Emphasize the transition from drafting to editing, ensuring learners understand that good academic writing requires multiple revisions.
