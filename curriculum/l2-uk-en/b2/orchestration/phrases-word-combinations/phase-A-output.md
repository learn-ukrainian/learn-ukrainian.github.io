## Cultural Hooks
1. The theoretical foundation of Ukrainian syntax, including the psychological and semantic analysis of phrase connections, was heavily shaped by the 19th-century linguist Oleksandr Potebnia (Олександр Потебня), who argued that syntax reflects the deep cognitive patterns of the nation.
2. Ukrainian language naturally favors active, verbal constructions (дієслівні словосполучення) over passive, nominal ones, which gives the language its characteristic dynamic and action-oriented tone, contrasting with the heavily nominalized administrative style of imperial Russian.

## Common Learner Errors
1. Calqued government (Russian interference) → Correct Ukrainian: «дякувати кому» (Dative) instead of «дякувати кого» (Accusative). This happens due to L1/L2 structural interference.
2. Case mismatch in learning → Correct Ukrainian: «навчатися мови» (Genitive) instead of «навчатися мові» (Dative).
3. Misidentifying connection types → Treating verb+infinitive constructions as government («змусити працювати»), when in fact the infinitive connects via adjunction (прилягання) because it does not change form.
