## Cultural Hooks
1. **Rhythm of Ukrainian Prose**: Classic Ukrainian literature (e.g., Kotsiubynsky, Nechuy-Levytsky) is renowned for its melodic, expansive syntax, heavily utilizing participial constructions (дієприкметникові та дієприслівникові звороти) that flow naturally, unlike the rigid, bureaucratic sentence structures often imposed during the Soviet era.
2. **Typography in Direct Speech**: Ukrainian typography strictly uses angular quotation marks («...») for direct speech, a tradition rooted in early European printing that distinguishes it aesthetically and structurally from English ("...") or German („...“) standards.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Pronoun Shift in Indirect Speech**: Learner says: *Він сказав, що я піду* instead of *Він сказав, що він піде*. → Requires strict mapping of grammatical persons during transformation.
2. **Missing Punctuation in Complex Sentences**: Forgetting commas before subordinating conjunctions (що, який, тому що). → Ukrainian syntactic punctuation is strictly formal, unlike English where it can often be rhetorical or optional.
3. **Misplaced Gerund Clauses**: Dangling modifiers (дієприслівникові звороти) where the action of the gerund doesn't match the active subject of the main clause.
