## Cultural Hooks
1. **#гуглю_українською**: A powerful sociolinguistic trend and social media hashtag that emerged as a form of digital decolonization. It encourages users to search in Ukrainian to boost Ukrainian-language content algorithms and marginalize Russian results.
2. **"Словотвір" (Slovotvir)**: A popular community-driven platform where Ukrainians collaboratively propose and vote on authentic native alternatives (calques) for new foreign technological terms, reflecting an active, grassroots approach to language purity and development.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Russian-derived borrowings (Русизми)** → Using *получати* instead of *отримувати*, *принімати* instead of *брати*. Why it happens: Historical interference and passive exposure to Russian/Soviet-era vocabulary.
2. **Incorrect Phonetic Adaptation** → Misplacing stress (e.g., *маркЕтинг* instead of *мАркетинг*) or failing to apply the "Rule of Nine" (правило дев'ятки) for 'и/і'. Why it happens: Learners apply native language spelling rules or Russian phonetic habits to Ukrainian borrowings.
3. **Declension of Unadaptable Nouns** → Attempting to decline words like *бюро* or *меню*. Why it happens: Overgeneralization of the Ukrainian noun declension system.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize Ukrainian as an active, vibrant system that effortlessly integrates new concepts (e.g., through productive suffixes like *-ка* in *блогерка*) and creates native calques (e.g., *застосунок*). Avoid any framing that treats Ukrainian vocabulary development as a reaction to or derivative of Russian.
- **Tone**: Maintain 100% Ukrainian immersion. The tone should be academic yet engaging (Neologist persona), treating the learner as a fellow researcher of a living language.
