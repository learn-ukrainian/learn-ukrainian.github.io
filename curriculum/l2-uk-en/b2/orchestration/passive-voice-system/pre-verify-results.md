## Grammar Rules
- **Passive Paradigm**: Ukrainian uses three main passive/impersonal structures: passive participles (-н-, -ен-, -т-), impersonal forms (-но, -то), and reflexive verbs (-ся).
- **-но, -то Impersonal Forms**: Правопис §77 (context from textbooks) — These forms are unchangeable, denote a completed result, and are used in one-member sentences without a subject.
- **Agent Restriction**: In sentences with -но, -то, the agent (doer) cannot be expressed in the instrumental case (e.g., *not* "іспит складено студентом").
- **Preference for Active**: Ukrainian stylistics strongly favor active constructions over passive ones with -ся (e.g., prefer "учні читають" over "рецепт читається учнями").

## Calque Warnings
- **"Бути" + Participle**: Avoid "був зроблений" where the resultative "зроблено" or an active "зробили" is more natural.
- **Passive with -ся**: Often a calque from Russian (e.g., "будується" vs. "будують"). Prefer active voice for naturalness.
- **Instrumental Agent**: Using a doer in the instrumental case with a passive verb is a common stylistic error; the instrumental case should only be used for the instrument (e.g., "написано олівцем").
