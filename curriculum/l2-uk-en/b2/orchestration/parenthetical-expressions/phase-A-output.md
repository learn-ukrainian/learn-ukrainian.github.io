# Дослідження: Вставні слова та вирази

## State Standard Reference
§4.3.2: "просте речення зі вставними словами: Може, то птахи приносять на своїх крилах весну?;"
Alignment: The module addresses B2 syntactical requirements by teaching parenthetical words as discourse and modality markers within simple complicated sentences (просте ускладнене речення).

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Frequency / Source | Key collocations |
|------|-------------------|------------------|
| мабуть | Very High / Spoken & Written | мабуть, так; мабуть, ні |
| на жаль | High / Formal & Informal | на превеликий жаль; на жаль, це так |
| кажуть | High / Conversational | як кажуть; кажуть, що... |
| до речі | Very High / Spoken | а до речі; до речі, про це... |
| отже | High / Academic & Formal | отже, можна зробити висновок; отже, ми бачимо |
| по-перше | Medium / Structured Speech | по-перше... по-друге... |

## Cultural Hooks
1. Polite Uncertainty: Ukrainians frequently employ words like «мабуть» or «напевно» even when expressing certain facts. This functions to soften the statement and avoid sounding categorical, which aligns with cultural communication preferences.
2. Collective Wisdom: The impersonal parenthetical «кажуть» (they say) operates as a marker of collective knowledge or rumor. It appeals to community authority and shared social understanding rather than a single verifiable source.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "На жаль" vs "Нажаль" → «На жаль» is always written as two words. It is a preposition + noun, not an adverb.
2. "По перше" vs "По-перше" → Must be written with a hyphen.
3. Homonymy (Verb vs Parenthetical) → Confusing the modal verb "може" with the parenthetical "може", resulting in punctuation errors (failing to isolate the parenthetical with commas).
4. False Parentheticals → Putting commas around particles like «навіть», «майже», «адже», «принаймні» because they "feel" parenthetical but are actually not.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Treat Ukrainian parenthetical rules, spelling ("на жаль"), and punctuation purely as part of the Ukrainian linguistic system. Do not compare these features to Russian. Ensure no Russianisms (кушати, получати) slip into the Talk Show Host dialogues.
- Maintain 100% Ukrainian immersion as per B2 requirements.
- Use Mermaid flowcharts for the "Decision Tree" on whether a word can be removed without breaking the sentence structure.
- Incorporate the Talk Show Host persona naturally in the final section, focusing on how these expressions control discourse dynamics.
