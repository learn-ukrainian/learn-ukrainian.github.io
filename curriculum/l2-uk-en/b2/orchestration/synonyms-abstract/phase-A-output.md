## Cultural Hooks
1. Hryhoriy Skovoroda’s "Philosophy of the Heart" (Кордоцентризм): Skovoroda viewed "думка" not merely as a cognitive, rational process, but as a spiritual unit born in the heart ("серце") — «шукай справжніх благ». This frames Ukrainian intellectual tradition as deeply rooted in emotional intelligence and internal reflection.
2. Lesya Ukrainka’s "Contra spem spero!": Her metaphor of the word as "hard steel" (твердая криця) demonstrates the materialization of an abstract "ідея" into a physical weapon of resistance. Ukrainian intellectual history frequently treats thoughts and ideas as actionable tools for national survival.

## Common Learner Errors
1. "З моєї думки..." → "На мою думку..." — Translating the English "in my opinion" using the wrong preposition. "На" + Accusative is strictly required.
2. "Мій погляд, що..." → "На мій погляд..." or "Я вважаю, що..." — "Погляд" is a noun phrase (perspective) and cannot directly govern a "що" clause like the English "opinion that".
3. "У мене є теорія..." (when guessing) → "У мене є припущення..." — Using the academic "теорія" for a casual guess instead of "припущення", failing to match the appropriate register.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Decolonized framing: Present the Ukrainian hierarchy of abstract thought as an independent, fully developed academic system. Avoid any comparisons to Russian terminology; instead, focus on the inherent richness of Ukrainian roots (e.g., "уявлення", "припущення").
- Use modal hedging («можливо», «ймовірно», «можна припустити») in the "Scientist's Diary" narrative to accurately model B2-level intellectual uncertainty.
- Ensure the "Sociologist" persona strictly maintains an academic register when discussing "концепція" and "теорія", reserving "думка" for more general observations.
