## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Typography of Ukrainian Independence**: Modern Ukrainian publishing rigorously enforces the use of traditional angular quotes («ялинки») as the primary quotation mark, moving away from colonial or digitized approximations (" "). This typographic choice is a mark of high-quality editing and respect for the Ukrainian graphic tradition.
2. **Quoting the Classics**: Syntactic transformation in Ukrainian schools is traditionally taught using iconic quotes from Taras Shevchenko, Lesia Ukrainka, or Ivan Franko. Transforming a fiery imperative like Shevchenko's «Борітеся — поборете!» into indirect speech (Шевченко закликав, щоб ми боролися) brilliantly demonstrates how modality shifts from direct emotion to historical reporting.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The "If" Question Trap**: Using «якщо» instead of the particle «чи» for yes/no questions (e.g., *Він запитав, якщо я там був* → Correct: *Він запитав, чи я там був*).
2. **Missing Pronoun Shift**: Failing to shift first-person pronouns to third-person during transformation (e.g., *Олена сказала: «Я прийду»* → *Олена сказала, що я прийду* instead of *...що вона прийде*).
3. **Punctuation Confusion**: Putting the period inside the quotation marks at the end of a sentence (a common English typographic rule) instead of outside, or mixing dialogue dashes with quotation marks.
