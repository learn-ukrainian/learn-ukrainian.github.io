## Cultural Hooks
1. **Kyiv-Mohyla Academy & Ostroh Academy**: The cradle of early modern Eastern European scholarship. They fundamentally shaped the academic register and philosophical terminology in the 16th-17th centuries before centuries of Russian imperial suppression.
2. **First Cybernetic Machines in Europe**: The MESM (МЕОМ) was created in Kyiv under Serhii Lebedev in 1950, highlighting Ukraine's foundational role in computer science (електронно-обчислювальна машина, кібернетика), defying the Soviet narrative that entirely centralized technical progress in Moscow.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Calquing Russian abstractions (Russianisms)** -> Direct calques of Russian academic clichés like "приймати участь" instead of the correct "брати участь", or "співпадати" instead of "збігатися". 
2. **Overuse of the Active Subject ("Я")** -> Using "Я досліджував" instead of "нами досліджено" / "у дослідженні проаналізовано". Academic Ukrainian relies heavily on impersonal structures or the pluralis modestiae ("ми").
3. **Improper Compounding** -> Incorrect hyphenation or separated writing of complex terms (e.g., writing *науковотехнічний* instead of *науково-технічний*).

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize that Ukrainian scientific vocabulary possesses strong European (Latin/Greek) roots alongside autonomous native formation patterns that were actively suppressed (during the Executed Renaissance) or Russified during the Soviet era.
- Ensure all vocabulary and grammatical concepts are provided with native Ukrainian collocations. 
- Use angular quotes «» exclusively. Do not use inline IPA transliteration for content prose.
