## Cultural Hooks

1. **Плітки as social glue** — Wikipedia (uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Плітки): *"Плітки, також зводи, чутки — особлива, здебільшого недостовірна інформація, що розповсюджується винятково усною формою."* The same article notes their social function: чутки "підтримує тонус їхнього соціального існування." This validates the lesson's framing of gossip as a social bonding mechanism — not merely a negative phenomenon — giving the cultural hook genuine linguistic grounding.

2. **Canonical idioms verified in GRAC concordance** — *Кажуть* appears in historical Ukrainian literary texts from Котляревський onward (*"...тамъ кажуть росте срібло да золото, усі люде говорять..."*), confirming deep roots in spoken Ukrainian. The anonymizing function of *кажуть* is a centuries-old rhetorical device in Ukrainian oral tradition, not a calque from any neighboring language.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Explicit "вони" insertion** → *Вони кажуть, що він одружується* ✗ → *Кажуть, що він одружується* ✓  
   *Why:* English obligatorily requires a subject ("They say..."), so learners over-transfer the pronoun. The entire communicative function of the structure — anonymous, diffuse agency — is destroyed when "вони" appears, because it implies a specific known group.

2. **Register mismatch: using -но/-то in casual speech** → *Йому було сказано* ✗ (in a WhatsApp exchange) → *Йому сказали* ✓  
   *Why:* The -но/-то passive is bookish/official; learners who have just studied it (b2-03) default to it universally. The lesson must explicitly map context → form choice.

3. **Conflation with безособові речення** → treating *Кажуть* as an impersonal construction parallel to *Холодно* or *Треба*  
   *Why:* Both are одночленні, but неозначено-особові have an implicit human agent doing a volitional action; безособові have no agent at all. Learners who confuse these misanalyze the case governance (e.g., they wrongly expect dative experiencer in *кажуть* constructions).

4. **Subject re-entry after initial correct use** → *Кажуть, що він поїхав, але вони не знають куди* ✗ → *Кажуть, що він поїхав, але ніхто не знає куди* ✓  
   *Why:* Learners correctly open with the неозначено-особова structure, then immediately reintroduce a pronoun in the follow-up clause, breaking the anonymizing frame.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Textbook grounding is Grade 8 (Заболотний 2025, Авраменко 2025):** Both cover неозначено-особові речення in §42-43/§4.3.3.1. The B2 module deepens this with register analysis and passive system contrast — frame explicitly as an expansion of what learners saw in school.
- **неозначено-особовий NOT in VESUM:** Use the term freely in metalinguistic explanation, but avoid listing it as learner-production vocabulary. It is a technical grammatical label.
- **плітка vs чутка distinction is real and frequency-backed:** плітка (0.29 IPM) is markedly informal and specifically personal gossip; чутка (2.41 IPM) is broader (rumors, reports). The lesson's vocabulary section should make this distinction explicit — it is not synonymy.
- **GRAC concordance confirms *кажуть* is deeply embedded in Ukrainian oral tradition** — this is an authentically Ukrainian structure, not a borrowing or calque. Frame it positively as a marker of natural Ukrainian speech fluency.
- **Dative case governance** (*мені сказали*, *йому повідомили*) is the primary case challenge for this structure — ensure the grammatical base section gives this prominent treatment with multiple examples.
- **Register contrast table** for Phase B: a clean 3-column table (Situation | Colloquial 3pl | Official -но/-то) will serve as the core reference chart for this module — plan it in the register comparison section.
