## Cultural Hooks
1. **Mykhailo Kravchuk (1892-1942)**: A world-renowned Ukrainian mathematician whose work on matrices and polynomials contributed to the invention of the first electronic computer (Atanasoff–Berry computer). He co-authored the first three-volume dictionary of Ukrainian mathematical terminology. A perfect figure to anchor a "Numerals & Science" theme.
2. **Kravchuk Polynomials**: Used today in coding theory and image processing. His tragic fate (Gulag victim) and rehabilitation serve as a poignant historical context.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Numeral Agreement (2, 3, 4 vs 5+)**:
   - *Error*: два студента / п'ять студенти
   - *Correct*: **два студенти** (N. Plural) / **п'ять студентів** (Gen. Plural)
   - *Reason*: Confusion between the rule for 2-4 (Nom. Pl.) and 5+ (Gen. Pl.).

2. **Compound Numeral Declension**:
   - *Error*: з п'ятидесяти учнями
   - *Correct*: **з п'ятдесятьма учнями** (or п'ятдесятьома)
   - *Reason*: Incorrectly declining the first part of tens (50-80) like in Russian. In Ukrainian 50, 60, 70, 80 only decline the *second* part (п'ят-десяти).

3. **Time Expressions**:
   - *Error*: у сім годин / без десяти вісім
   - *Correct*: **о сьомій годині** / **за десять восьма** (або десята до восьмої)
   - *Reason*: Direct translation from English/Russian. Ukrainian uses ordinal numbers for hours and specific prepositions.
