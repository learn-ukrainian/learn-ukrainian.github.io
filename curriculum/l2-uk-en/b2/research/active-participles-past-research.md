> "просте речення з дієприкметниковим зворотом: Озеро, оточене густими кущами, було не широке, а довге"

**Alignment**: §4.1.3.1 directly mandates teaching synthetic future alongside compound forms — this validates the plan's Section 5 (синтетичний майбутній час). §4.3.2 validates teaching participial phrase syntax. No B2 State Standard section explicitly mandates teaching the -лий form itself — its treatment is pedagogically framed as error-correction (russism avoidance) rather than active production. The lexicalized adjectives (колишній, минулий, майбутній) are high-frequency vocabulary items covered under B2 cumulative vocabulary targets (~5940 words).

**Textbook confirmation (Grade 11 Glazova, tier 2)** — most authoritative textbook source:

**Textbook confirmation (Grade 7 Litvinova, tier 1)** — explicit колишній / бувший contrast:

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Суржик** (Wikipedia verified): "Су́ржик — елементи двох або кількох мов, об'єднані штучно, без дотримання норм літературної мови." The term originally meant a grain mixture (surzh = mixed wheat-rye grain), then became a metaphor for the Russian-Ukrainian mixed language that developed under Soviet language policy. The -вший/-щий particle forms entered Ukrainian through this Soviet bureaucratic and educational influence. The "Антисуржик" movement (book by Олександра Сербенська, 1990s) is a post-independence linguistic hygiene effort to reclaim normative Ukrainian from these Soviet-era forms.

2. **Milozvanist' (милозвучність) principle** (textbook-confirmed): Ukrainian phonaesthetics systematically favour open syllables and avoid heavy consonant clusters. The grade 10 Karaman textbook explicitly teaches this as a reason why Ukrainian has fewer participial adjective forms than other Slavic languages — the resulting forms (читаючий, сказавший) violate the natural euphonic flow. This is an organic Ukrainian-centric explanation rather than a comparison to any other language.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Who Did" trap** — using *читавший / сказавший / написавший* as active past participles → **correct**: *той, що читав / той, що сказав / той, що написав* or relative clause with *який*. Why it happens: direct calque from Russian morphology where -вший is productive and grammatical.

2. **бувший → колишній confusion** — writing *бувший директор, бувший чоловік* → **correct**: *колишній директор, колишній чоловік*. Why it happens: бувший is a frozen Russian loanform that persists in oral surzyk speech; learners who have heard Ukrainian spoken with Russian influence acquire it as natural.

3. **Overgeneralising -лий to transitive verbs** — attempting *прочитавлий, написавлий* (non-existent) → **correct**: -лий forms exist ONLY from intransitive perfective verbs (пожовтіти → пожовтілий, зів'яти → зів'ялий). Transitive verbs do not form active past participles in normative Ukrainian.

4. **минулий vs колишній confusion** — *минулий президент* (incorrect for 'former president') vs *колишній президент* (correct) — *минулий* = temporal past ('last year'), *колишній* = status/role change ('former').

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Formation rule**: Legitimate -лий forms come from **intransitive perfective verbs** only: пожовтіти → пожовтілий, зів'яти → зів'ялий, посивіти → посивілий, застаріти → застарілий. These describe natural state changes (nature, aging, deterioration) and are almost exclusively used in literary/artistic register.
- **High-value for learners**: The lexicalized adjectives (колишній, минулий, майбутній) at 10–60 ppm are B2 vocabulary essentials. Spending significant content space on them is pedagogically justified beyond the grammar point itself.
- **бувший frequency**: 0.66 ppm — present enough in real text to warrant active correction, but low enough that learners will mostly produce it from L1-adjacent interference rather than from reading authentic Ukrainian.
- **Synthetic future**: State Standard §4.1.3.1 explicitly lists both forms (буду висловлювати / висловлюватиму) as co-registered, confirming the plan's framing of synthetic future as an elegant stylistic choice rather than an archaic or "wrong" form.
- **Grade 7 Avramenko (tier 1)** note: The textbook instructs students to replace active present participles with "more natural" equivalents — this same principle extends to past forms at higher levels and is the pedagogical spine of this module.
- **Колишній collocation note**: GRAC confirms колишній президент is the canonical fixed collocation (президент is top content-word collocate). Use this real-world example prominently.
- **Decolonized framing note**: Frame -вший/-щий as a violation of Ukrainian's organic phonaesthetic principle of милозвучність — not as "different from Russian" but as internally foreign to how Ukrainian sound patterns naturally work. The historical explanation is Soviet-era канцелярит and institutional language interference, not a Russian/Ukrainian comparison.
