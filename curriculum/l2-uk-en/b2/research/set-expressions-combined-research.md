# Дослідження: Сталі вирази: Тіло, тварини, предмети та абстракції

## State Standard Reference

**§4.4.1.2** (lines 3459–3469): «Стилістичні засоби лексики: антоніми… синоніми… омоніми… епітети… **метафори**: ідуть дощі, сонце сідає…»

Phraseological units (фразеологізми) are the institutionalized form of lexical metaphor — frozen figurative expressions. This module addresses §4.4.1.2 directly: learners analyze, classify, and produce idiomatic language as advanced lexical-stylistic competence at B2. Thematic grounding also falls under **§3 (B2 thematic catalogue)**, sub-topic «традиції» (lines 2874–3064), since somatic and zoomorphic idioms encode Ukrainian cultural-historical tradition.

**Alignment**: Module teaches learners to recognize phraseological units as whole-unit metaphors (not compositional), to produce them in appropriate register, and to correct interference errors — all core B2 stylistic competence expectations.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Коровай як весільний символ** (Wikipedia verified): Коровай — «великий круглий пухкий хліб із прикрасами з тіста», випікали «зазвичай на весілля або на якісь інші урочистості та свята, виконує святкові та обрядові функції.» The proverb «На чужий коровай очей не поривай» thus draws on deep bridal-economy symbolism: the korovai represented the couple's fate and community prosperity — coveting it meant coveting another's life-luck.

2. **Козацький побут і «собаку з'їв»**: Ukrainian phraseological dictionaries and ethnographers trace this expression to extreme survival conditions (campaigns, sieges) where eating dog was the marker of someone who had endured — and therefore mastered — the worst. The expression thus indexes *earned* expertise through hardship, not academic knowledge. This distinguishes it from Polish «zjeść zęby» (to wear down one's teeth = gain experience), showing Ukrainian-specific framing.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Calque interference** → *«дощ падає котами і собаками»* (literal translation of *it's raining cats and dogs*). Correct: «ллє як з відра». **Why**: English-speaking learners activate their L1 idiom system and translate component-by-component, not recognizing Ukrainian idioms as opaque wholes.

2. **Contamination (контамінація)** → *«грати значення»* (blend of «грати роль» + «мати значення»). Correct: either «грати роль» or «мати значення» — never mixed. **Why**: Both structures share the semantic field of «importance/influence» in the learner's mental lexicon, causing cross-activation.

3. **Lexical substitution within idiom frame** → *«продавати усмішки»* instead of «продавати витрішки». **Why**: Learners know the verb «продавати» but substitute a semantically plausible but non-idiomatic noun. «Витрішки» has almost no standalone frequency (214 total corpus occurrences), making it opaque to learners who haven't explicitly learned it.

4. **Body-part pronoun mapping error** → translating *pulling one's leg* as *«тягнути когось за ногу»* instead of «морочити голову» or «жартувати». **Why**: somatic mapping is language-specific — English "leg" maps to deception/teasing in English phraseology; Ukrainian uses "head" (голова) for the same semantic domain.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **«Зоофразеологія»** is not a VESUM-valid standalone noun. Use «фразеологізми з назвами тварин» or «зооморфні фразеологічні одиниці» throughout the module.
- The GRAC corpus does not return hits for multi-word idioms like «тримати язик за зубами» or «як кіт наплакав» when queried as phrases — this is a corpus segmentation limitation, not evidence of low frequency. Both expressions are textbook-documented (Zabolotnyi Grade 11 appendix) and can be treated as high-frequency pedagogically.
- Section 2 (Соматизми) should foreground the concept of **внутрішня форма** early — this term is in the plan and is the analytical key that explains why idioms are non-compositional. It is VESUM-valid via its components (внутрішній + форма).
- Decolonized framing note: Ukrainian somatic phraseology has its own typology and cultural logic. When contrasting Ukrainian idioms with other-language equivalents in Section 4, use English or Polish as the comparison languages — NOT Russian. The «make a mountain out of a molehill» English equivalent for «робити з мухи слона» is a clean cross-linguistic comparison without any Russian mediation needed.
- The Avramenko Grade 11 §49 textbook explicitly frames фразеологізми as «виразні стилістичні засоби мови» — align Section 1's introduction with this framing to maintain textbook continuity for learners who have encountered this material in school context.
