## State Standard Reference
§4.2.5 (B2, lines 3356–3357): "Утворення назв осіб за видом діяльності: діяти – діяч, виконувати – виконавець, просити – прохач, танцювати – танцівник."

**Alignment:** The module directly addresses §4.2.5 by systematically covering the three most productive person-forming suffixes (-ар/-яр, -ач, -ник), extending the examples beyond the standard's four illustrations. The module also connects to §4.2.6 (дієслівні іменники on -ння) by contrasting agent nouns with action nouns — deepening register awareness. §4.1.2 (noun declension — advanced forms) is implicitly activated because nouns in -ар/-яр have three distinct declension groups (тверда, м'яка, мішана) whose correct use is B2-appropriate.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Кобзар як культурний архетип** — Verified via Ukrainian Wikipedia: "Кобза́р — український народний співець та музикант. Кобзарі були творцями, хранителями і передавачами епічної традиції у формі історичних пісень, дум, релігійних піснеспівів... Значення їх для українського народу можна порівняти зі значенням давньогрецьких поетів у давній Греції." The word кобзар itself is the suffix -ар attached to кобза (a string instrument), exemplifying the most culturally loaded instance of the suffix. Тарас Шевченко named his 1840 debut collection «Кобзар» — the entire Ukrainian literary canon is indexed under this suffix-bearing noun. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Кобзар

2. **Іван Франко — каменяр-поет** — Verified via Ukrainian Wikipedia: Франко was "видатний український поет, прозаїк, драматург, літературний критик, публіцист, перекладач, науковець, громадський і політичний діяч." His 1878 poem «Каменярі» employs каменяр as a metaphor for revolutionaries breaking through social oppression — the stone-cutter becomes a symbol of collective labour and unyielding national will. The word itself (камінь → каменяр, soft н' → suffix -яр) is a perfect illustration of the phonetic allomorphy rule taught in this module. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Франко_Іван_Якович. **Note:** A Wikipedia article specifically for «Каменярі (поема)» was not found in the cache; the poem details above reflect established literary scholarship and should be verified against a primary source before inclusion in content.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Interference from Russian soft -р forms: *лікарь, *секретарь → лікар, секретар** — Russian has лека́рь, секрета́рь with mandatory ь at word end. Ukrainian профessions in -р never take м'який знак. Error source: phonological transfer. Correction: the final -р in Ukrainian person nouns is categorically hard (тверда основа) regardless of declension group; м'який знак only appears in literary/archaic forms in other word categories.

2. **Suffix overgeneralisation -ник where -ач is required: *викладник, *читальник → викладач, читач** — Learners apply -ник (the more transparent role/group suffix) to agent-of-action contexts where -ач is the productive form. The diagnostic test: if the base verb describes a repeated/characteristic action performed by an individual agent, -ач is correct (читати → читач, слухати → слухач, викладати → викладач). The -ник suffix primarily attaches to nouns/adjectives or to verbs whose action defines group membership (робити → робітник via -ітник).

3. **Phonetic rule omission: *каменар → каменяр** — Learners may not perceive the soft consonant at the base stem's end. The derivation chain камінь → [stem кам'ен-] → кам-ен-яр requires -яр (soft allomorph) because the immediately preceding consonant н' is historically soft. Contrasted with: базар, бібліотекар (hard stems → -ар). Teaching strategy: pair the suffix allomorphy rule with the declension group distinction (мішана group for stressed -яр in occupational nouns, per Litvinova Grade 6 tier-1 textbook).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Textbook grounding:** Grade 11 Avramenko (tier 2) explicitly labels -ар, -ач as "Професійні суфікси" and lists them alongside -ець, -ист, -ик, -тель — use this framing to position the module within a broader suffix taxonomy without teaching the others.
- **Declension caution:** The plan mentions фонетичне правило for -ар/-яр, but the textbook data (Litvinova Grade 6, Avramenko Grade 6, both tier 1) shows that -яр in occupational nouns triggers the **мішана** declension group, not м'яка. Content should not conflate the formation rule (soft stem → -яр suffix) with the declension group label. Present the formation rule cleanly; the declension behaviour is a B1 prerequisite that needs only a brief review note.
- **Register axis** is the main B2 value-add: the pair робітник / працівник (4.95 vs 15.16 IPM) illustrates semantic precision — content should dwell on this pragmatic distinction with authentic collocations.
- **Деколонізаційний фрейм:** The кобзар and каменяр hooks position Ukrainian as a language with its own autonomous literary canon and cultural memory. Content must present these not as curiosities but as evidence of Ukrainian word-formation's expressive power. Frame Шевченко's «Кобзар» and Франко's «Каменярі» as the language's own symbolic vocabulary — not in comparison with Russian equivalents.
- **-ач gender pairs:** The plan mentions викладач/викладачка and читач/читачка — at B2, content should present the -ка feminine suffix as unremarkable default, not as a "new" form, and use both genders naturally throughout.
- **Word budget signal:** The vocabulary frequency data justifies spending more word budget (≈1000 of 4000) on the -ар/-яр section because it carries the most phonetic nuance, the most common errors, and the culturally richest examples.
