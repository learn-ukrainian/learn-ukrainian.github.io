## Cultural Hooks

1. **Верба як сакральний символ України** (Wikipedia verified): "Без верби і калини нема України" is culturally foundational — the willow (верба) appears in over 100 Ukrainian folk songs (пісень) and is a primary symbol of mourning, homeland longing, and waterways. The proverb «Де вода, там і верба» reflects the agroecological observation that willows mark water sources — crucial knowledge for хлібороби navigating the steppe landscape. Willow branches replace palm fronds in Ukrainian Вербна неділя (Palm Sunday), marking the tree's sacred status in the folk-religious calendar.

2. **Суржик як соціолінгвістичний феномен** (Wikipedia verified): "Суржик — елементи двох або кількох мов, об'єднані штучно, без дотримання норм літературної мови. Первісно термін «суржик» вживався переважно щодо українсько-російського суржику." The term originally referred to mixed grain (суміш зерна пшениці й жита) — the food metaphor itself encodes the folk view of language mixing as degraded material. This etymological hook gives the practice section a memorable anchor.

3. **«На городі бузина, а в Києві дядько»** — the cultural hook here is geographic-political: Kyiv is the referent of national relevance, and the proverb uses the capital to dramatize complete logical disconnection. Grade 4 textbooks (Savchenko) already expose learners to proverb classification — B2 learners can be assumed to have met the genre before.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Обережного Бог береже»** instead of **«Береженого Бог береже»** → The plan correctly flags this. The error stems from applying the regular adjective «обережний» (careful person) instead of the passive participle «бережений» (one who is guarded/protected). The proverb uses a passive theological construction — the one who is guarded by precaution, not the cautious actor. Learners must be drilled on the authentic form before the calque-form becomes fossilized.

2. **Confusing «мати значення» (correct) with «відігравати роль» (correct) vs. «відігравати значення» (error)** → This contamination error comes from blending two correct Ukrainian constructions. Grade 10 (Karaman, p.54) covers фразеологічна калька explicitly — content should cite this pattern as a classic B2 interference error.

3. **Translating «It's raining cats and dogs» → «Йде як кішки і собаки»** instead of **«Ллє як з відра»** → L1 transfer through a calque that sounds absurd in Ukrainian. The section 5 exercises address this directly. Learners need to understand that idioms encode culture, not literal meanings — Ukrainian rain imagery is domestic (bucket), not zoological (English).

4. **«Тише їдеш — далі будеш»** (Russicism borrowed from «Тише едешь — дальше будешь»**) instead of **«Хто тихо ходить, той густо місить»** → Direct transfer from the Russian cognate proverb. The Ukrainian version uses a qualitatively different image (kneading dough thoroughly = slow, careful work yields dense results) versus Russian's speed/distance metaphor. The register difference matters — the Ukrainian proverb is more artisanal and domestic.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **«Брід» vs. «брод»**: The plan uses «брод» throughout but VESUM cannot find this form. Ukrainian dictionaries list the word as «брід» (genitive: «броду») — a shallow river crossing. This may be a plan-level inaccuracy in the vocabulary hint. Content should use **«брід» (genitive: «броду»)** and the proverb «Не знаючи броду, не лізь у воду» — verify against SUM (Словник української мови) if the content phase tool permits.

- **Section 2 word budget (1000 words)**: This is the heaviest section. The four proverbs in this section have rich satirical/rhetorical dimensions (personification in «природа як мачуха», the absurdist humor of «бузина–дядько», the informal variant «а осінь пирогами»). Allocate ~250 words per proverb for analysis depth to meet the 1000-word target.

- **Section 5 (Практика)** must contain an explicit essay production task (written artifact, not just fill-in). Grade 10 Karaman (p.112) provides good methodological grounding for the «sources of Ukrainian phraseology» framing — agrarian observation, crafts, military vocabulary.

- **Personification of nature**: The proverb «Природа одному мати, другому — мачуха» involves a grammatically notable zero-copula construction in the second clause («другому — мачуха»). At B2, this can be analyzed as a stylistic ellipsis (§4.4.1.3 — еліпсис).

- **«Куй залізо, поки гаряче»**: This is cognate with English «Strike while the iron is hot» — but the Ukrainian version uses «куй» (imperative of «кувати») which is a B2-level imperative with aspect choice. Imperfective imperative here = general maxim, not a specific command. This aspectual nuance connects to the preceding B1 aspect modules.

- **Decolonization note**: The module's anti-surzhyk section (§5) should frame language purism not as elitism but as sovereignty — maintaining Ukrainian phraseological independence from Russian interference is a cultural-political act, not pedantry. The Soviet language policy deliberately encouraged surzhyk to undermine Ukrainian linguistic autonomy.
