## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Agency" Shift**: Modern Ukrainian linguistic culture actively discourages "bureaucratic passive" (канцелярит) in favor of active voice. This mirrors a societal shift away from Soviet-era passivity ("it was decided") towards personal responsibility ("we decided"). Using active voice is often seen as a marker of a "free person's language."
2. **The -NO/-TO Authority**: Historically, forms ending in -но/-то (e.g., *наказано, зроблено*) carry a nuance of absolute, indisputable fact or completion, often used in hetman decrees and military orders, distinct from the merely descriptive passive participles.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Calquing "is being done" with "-ся"**: *Будинок будується робітниками* → **Робітники будують будинок**. (Reflexive passive with an agent is often unnatural in Ukrainian; active voice is preferred).
2. **Confusing State vs. Action**: *Завдання виконане* (State/Adjective: The task is [a] completed [one]) vs. *Завдання виконано* (Action/Event: The task has been completed). Learners often use the adjectival form when they mean the event.
3. **Russian-influenced Present Passive**: Using forms like *читаємий* (read-able/being read) is a severe error. Ukrainian lacks present passive participles. Correct: *який читають* or active voice.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Stylistic nuance**: Clearly distinguish when passive is *good* (scientific objectivity, unknown agent) vs. when it is *bad* (avoiding responsibility, clunky translation).
- **Terminological precision**: Ensure the distinction between "Passive Voice" (grammatical category) and "Impersonal Sentences" (syntactic category) is clear but not overwhelming.
- **Visual Aid**: A decision tree is essential: "Do you know the agent?" -> Yes -> "Is the agent important?" -> Yes -> USE ACTIVE VOICE.
