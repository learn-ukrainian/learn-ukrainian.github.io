## State Standard Reference

**§4.3.2** (lines 3395–3407): *"Просте ускладнене речення: просте речення з дієприкметниковим зворотом: Озеро, оточене густими кущами, було не широке, а довге."*

**§4.1.3.1** (lines 3297–3317): Indicative mood forms at B2 — mapping description specifies "complex aspect usage, passive voice (participles), pluperfect."

**Alignment:** This module directly addresses both §4.3.2 (дієприкметниковий зворот in simple complex sentences — comma punctuation, post-noun vs pre-noun position) and §4.1.3.1 (passive participle as a verbal form). The plan's framing of §4.3.2 as the syntax mandate for participial phrase construction is confirmed by the exact Standard text. Formation morphology and agreement are the linguistic competence prerequisites enabling this syntactic skill.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **д→дж alternation omitted:** *\*садений* → **посаджений** (садити — stem ends in д, triggers д→дж before -ен-). Same pattern: *\*народений* → **народжений**. Cause: learners apply -ений directly to infinitive without accounting for the final-consonant alternation.

2. **т→ч alternation omitted / wrong suffix:** *\*оплатений* → **оплачений** (оплатити — т→ч before -ений), *\*платений* → **плачений** (платити). Also: *\*захватений* → **захоплений** (захопити — п→пл). Cause: learners conflate -тий (for monosyllabic stems ending in vowel) with -ений for -ти stems ending in т.

3. **-тий vs -ний/ений on monosyllabic stems:** *\*миний* → **митий** (мити), *\*забуний* → **забутий** (забути), *\*покриний* → **покритий** (покрити). Cause: learners default to -ний as the "standard" suffix and apply it to vowel-final monosyllabic stems where -тий is required.

4. **Agreement gender/number mismatch:** *\*написаний книга* → **написана книга** (feminine noun requires feminine form). Cause: treating the participle as invariant (verb-like) rather than fully adjectival.

## Textbook Formation Rules (Grade 7 Litvinova, tier 1; Grade 11 Glazova, tier 2)

| Suffix | Trigger condition | Example |
|--------|------------------|---------|
| **-н-** | Infinitive stem ends in -а- | писати → писаний, читати → читаний |
| **-ен-(-єн-)** | Stem ends in consonant, -и-, -і-, -ї- | побачити → побачений, вигоїти → вигоєний |
| **-т-** | Monosyllabic or vowel-final short stems | мити → митий, налити → налитий, колоти → колотий |

**Consonant alternations before -ен-** (Grade 10 Glazova, tier 2; Grade 10 Karaman, tier 2):
- д → дж: садити → саджений, народити → народжений
- т → ч: крутити → кручений, платити → плачений, оплатити → оплачений
- з → ж: возити → вожений
- с → ш: носити → ношений
- ст → щ: простити → прощений, мостити → мощений

## Notes for Content Writing

- The plan's Клас 1 (hard group) adjective declension frame is pedagogically sound — treat дієприкметники exactly as тверда група прикметників. No special declension table needed; reference the learner's existing adjective paradigm.
- The "Agency Rule" (avoid passive abuse) is the most pragmatically important B2 skill here. Allocate generous drill space for active↔passive transformation.
- Золота пектораль hook: Борис Мозолевський is a culturally significant figure (poet and archaeologist) — the discovery was serendipitous (he was part of a dam construction survey team). This adds narrative texture beyond the bare fact.
- Softia Kyivska: The XVII–XVIII century rebuilding was commissioned by Hetman Ivan Mazepa — this is a decolonized framing point (Ukrainian Baroque as a distinct national aesthetic, not "Muscovite influence").
- For колокації section: відкрите питання (abstract) vs відкрите вікно (physical) demonstrates the participle's dual attributive role — useful for stylistic register discussion.
- The **-тий** mnemonic most effective in textbooks: "односкладовий корінь + голосний + ти → -тий". Examples: мити, бити, пити, крити, шити, лити, колоти, полоти.
