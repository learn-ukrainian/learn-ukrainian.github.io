## Cultural Hooks
1. High Level of Formality: In the Ukrainian corporate sphere, addressing someone with capitalized «Ви» is a mandatory marker of professional respect in written text, contrasting sharply with the egalitarian lower-case "you" in English or informal "ти".
2. The "Доброго дня!" Myth: There is a widespread grammatical misconception that "Доброго дня" is a polite greeting. In Ukrainian tradition, the genitive case is used for parting wishes (бажаю чого? - доброго дня). The authentic, grammatically correct greeting is always in the nominative case: «Добрий день!» or the verbal «Вітаю!».

## Common Learner Errors
1. Soviet-era bureaucratic calques (Surzhyk) → Correct form — Why it happens:
   - "приймати участь" (calc of Russian "принимать участие") → "брати участь"
   - "на протязі" (literal calc of Russian "на протяжении", meaning "in a draft" in Ukrainian) → "протягом"
   - "приймати міри" (calc of Russian "принимать меры") → "вживати заходів"
2. Register mismatch in greetings: Learners often translate casual English phrases directly ("Доброго часу доби" / "Привіт"), which are perceived as unprofessional or jarring in a first-contact formal Ukrainian email.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonization & Authenticity**: Explicitly frame the correction of Surzhyk phrases («приймати участь», «на протязі») not merely as "grammar rules", but as a restoration of authentic Ukrainian thought patterns, replacing rigid Soviet-era bureaucratic structures (канцелярит) with natural, professional Ukrainian.
- **Agency Pass**: Despite the use of nominalization to elevate the register, maintain Ukrainian as an active, forward-looking language. Emphasize positive framing and constructive tone over dry, passive directives.
