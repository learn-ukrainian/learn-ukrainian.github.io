## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Institute of Scientific Language (VUAN) & 'Executed Renaissance'**: In the 1920s, Ukrainian scholars actively forged a distinct, organic scientific terminology (e.g., using «тяжіння» instead of the international/Russian «гравітація», «відсоток» instead of «процент»). This independent development was tragically halted and forcibly russified in the 1930s.
2. **Ivan Puliui's Legacy**: A brilliant physicist who pre-dated Röntgen in X-ray research and simultaneously co-translated the Bible into Ukrainian. He exemplifies the synthesis of high science and linguistic agency, proving Ukrainian was fully capable of the highest academic and spiritual registers long before it was officially permitted.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Passive Voice Abuse (Russian Calque)**: «Робота була зроблена студентом» → **Correct form**: «Студент виконав роботу» (Active) or «Роботу виконано» (Impersonal -но/-то). — *Why it happens*: Learners transfer the heavy use of passive participles from Russian academic style; Ukrainian strongly prefers active constructions or subjectless impersonal forms.
2. **Active Participle Calques**: «домінуючий фактор», «слідуючий приклад» → **Correct form**: «панівний фактор» / «провідний чинник», «наступний приклад». — *Why it happens*: Ukrainian has systematically lost present active participles; Russian relies on them heavily in formal text.
3. **Lexical Calques in Collocations**: «приймати участь», «підводити підсумки» → **Correct form**: «брати участь», «підбивати підсумки».

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Never frame Ukrainian academic style as "trying to be different from Russian." Frame it as an organically developed system that prioritizes clarity, active agency, and elegant impersonal forms over the heavy, convoluted participle chains typical of imperial styles.
- **Tone**: The persona is a Professional Language Coach/Professor. Maintain a rigorous but encouraging academic tone.
- **No English Scaffolding**: 100% Ukrainian immersion required. Concepts must be explained through context, clear Ukrainian definitions, and contrasting examples.
- **IPA Rule**: Do not use inline IPA in the text; reserve phonetics exclusively for the YAML vocabulary file. 
- **Formatting**: Use Mermaid flowcharts to illustrate the structure of an academic paper (Вступ → Основна частина → Висновки).
- **Engagement**: Ensure the cultural notes (VUAN, Puliui) are highlighted using specific engagement callouts like `> [!history-bite]` or `> [!cultural]`.
