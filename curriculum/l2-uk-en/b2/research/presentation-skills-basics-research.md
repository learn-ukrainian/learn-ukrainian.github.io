## Cultural Hooks

1. **Феофан Прокопович and *De arte rhetorica* (1706)** — Прокопович was rector of the Kyiv Academy (1710–1716) and authored the treatise *De arte rhetorica libri X* in 1706, one of the most systematised rhetoric textbooks of its era in Eastern Europe. The Academy trained generations of Ukrainian orators and theologians in classical rhetoric: inventio, dispositio, elocutio. This Ukrainian-origin rhetorical tradition predates and is independent of later Petrine-era borrowing. Source: Wikipedia (uk) — Феофан Прокопович.

2. **Києво-Могилянська академія (founded 1632) as Eastern Europe's premier rhetoric centre** — Founded by Metropolitan Petro Mohyla, the Academy was one of the largest and most distinguished educational centres in Eastern Europe for nearly two centuries. Rhetoric was a required discipline. This provides an authentic Ukrainian pedigree for the module's claim about публічне слово — anchoring it in a living tradition rather than importing Western frameworks. Source: Wikipedia (uk) — Києво-Могилянська академія (1632–1817).

**Decolonized framing note:** The Wikipedia summary for Прокопович leads with "український й російський богослов" due to his later Petrine service. The module should foreground his Kyiv Academy period (1706–1716) and the Ukrainian rhetorical tradition he represents. His court work is a separate political chapter; the rhetoric textbook belongs to Ukrainian intellectual history.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Актуально" (Russian calque) → "власне" / "насправді"** — Ukrainian learners with Russian background or heavy English influence overuse "актуально" as a discourse filler meaning "actually/specifically." The correct Ukrainian equivalents are "власне" (specifically/properly) or "насправді" (in fact). The Russian "актуально" means "timely/relevant" in Ukrainian too, but NOT "actually."

2. **"Так" as a connector (English "so" calque) → "отже" / "тож"** — Learners import English sentence-linking "so" as "так" (Ukrainian for "yes"). The plan correctly identifies this; the module should explicitly contrast "так" (affirmation) vs. "отже/тож" (logical connectors).

3. **Passive constructions instead of active voice** — Bureaucratic/Soviet style produces "було розглянуто," "виконано," "зазначено" in presentations. The module's "Agency Pass" section addresses this directly: "Ми розглянули" / "Я пропоную" restores speaker agency and matches the authentic public register of post-2014 Ukrainian professional speech.

4. **Filler words ("ну", "е-е-е", "як би")** — Universal learner problem for public speaking. Section 5 addresses this; worth noting that "як би" is particularly stigmatised in professional Ukrainian as a russified hedge (cf. Russian "как бы").

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Section 2 (Риторична спадщина)** is the cultural anchor section and should reference *De arte rhetorica* (Прокопович, 1706) and the Kyiv-Mohyla tradition explicitly — this grounds Ukrainian rhetoric as an indigenous tradition, not an import.
- **Section 3 (Signposting)** aligns most precisely with State Standard §4.4.1.3 — the content should systematically present вставні слова as text-structuring devices at B2.
- **Register** is публіцистичний — avoid academic stiffness; professional but accessible.
- **Agency Pass** (section 3) is a pedagogically original feature — ensure at least 4–5 contrast pairs (passive → active) with authentic professional contexts.
- `підсумовуючи` warrants a one-sentence note identifying it as a frozen дієприслівник used as a discourse marker — this prepares learners for B2/C1 formal writing.
