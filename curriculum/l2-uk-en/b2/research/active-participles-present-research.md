# Дослідження: Активні дієприкметники теперішнього часу (-учий/-ючий)

## State Standard Reference

**§4.1.1.2** (B2, lines 3116–3129): "Прикметник: прикметники чоловічого, жіночого, середнього роду із закінченнями -ий (-ій), -а (-я), -е (-є)... відмінювання складних прикметників із компонентом -лиций..." — Describes advanced adjective forms at B2, including participle-adjectives.

**§4.3.2** (B2, lines 3395–3407): "просте речення з дієприкметниковим зворотом: Озеро, оточене густими кущами, було не широке, а довге" — The canonical B2 standard example uses a **past passive** participial phrase, not an active present participle. Active present participles do not appear in B2 productive examples.

**§4.2.3.1** (B1, lines 2269–2291): Дієприкметники and дієприслівники are introduced at B1 in the indicative-verb section. Active present participles are part of the B1 awareness inventory.

**Alignment**: The plan's pedagogical strategy — passive recognition of -учий/-ючий in literary/documentary texts + active avoidance in productive speech — is fully aligned with the State Standard's treatment at B2. The Standard provides one example of a participial phrase at B2 (passive, not active), confirming that productive use of active present participles is not a B2 requirement. The module therefore correctly teaches the form at the level of *literacy awareness*, not output production.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Олександр Пономарів (1935–2021)** — Ukrainian linguist, Doctor of Philology (1991), professor at Institute of Journalism, Kyiv National University. Renowned for popularising language culture via columns and lectures. His characterisation of active present participles as "zombie forms" (-учий/-ючий as Russian-derived calques that drain Ukrainian of its natural verbal energy) is widely cited in language pedagogy. Source: [Wikipedia — Пономарів Олександр Данилович](https://uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Пономарів_Олександр_Данилович)

2. **Закон про забезпечення функціонування української мови як державної (2019)**: Grade 5 and Grade 11 textbooks (Avramenko, NUS-aligned) explicitly open language lessons with reference to this law, establishing that language correctness has real civic and legal stakes. The "чинний vs діючий закон" distinction is a direct test of literacy under this legal framework — educated speakers must use "чинний" in legal/professional contexts. Source: Grade 5 Avramenko 2022, р. 8; Grade 11 Avramenko 2019, §18.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"діючий закон"** → "чинний закон" — calque of Russian *действующий* meaning "in force/valid". Root cause: Russian uses one word for both "actively functioning" and "legally valid"; Ukrainian distinguishes them. **Чинний** = juridical validity only; **діючий** = physical/active functioning (volcano, army, ingredient). Frequency data confirms "чинний" (8.46 IPM) is approximately twice as common as "діючий" (3.90 IPM) in actual Ukrainian text.

2. **"існуючий"** → "наявний / теперішній / даний" — calque of Russian *существующий*. The form "існуючий" appears 2.11 IPM in the corpus — primarily in translated or bureaucratic texts. Natural Ukrainian uses "наявний" for existing resources/problems (2.64 IPM), "теперішній" for current state.

3. **"слідуюча зупинка"** → "наступна зупинка" — calque of Russian *следующая*. The massive frequency of "наступний" (33.67 IPM) vs near-zero for "слідуючий" confirms "наступний" is the overwhelmingly natural Ukrainian form.

4. **"оточуюче середовище"** → "довкілля" — The phrase "оточуюче середовище" has 0 IPM in GRAC (not found in 2 billion token corpus), while "довкілля" has 13.85 IPM. This is the strongest quantitative evidence of the calque's unnaturalness.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **VESUM flag on завідуючий/керуючий**: Both forms are absent from VESUM. Content should present them as occurring in official institutional titles while consistently noting that **завідувач** and **керівник** are the standard Ukrainian forms. The "lexicalized" framing in the plan should not be read as "fully correct" — rather as "tolerated in fixed institutional titles only."

2. **Morphological derivation path**: Textbook Karaman confirms: основа 3-ї особи множини теперішнього часу + суфікс -уч-(-юч-) для I дієвідміни / -ач-(-яч-) для II. Examples: читають → читаючий, говорять → говорячий, мислять → мислячий. The plan's derivation algorithm is textbook-accurate.

3. **Frequency contrast for pedagogical impact**: "оточуюче середовище" = 0 IPM vs "довкілля" = 13.85 IPM is a compelling data point for the lesson. Use it.

4. **Decolonization framing**: The plan's Пономарів "zombie" metaphor is well-supported by textbook evidence — Grade 7 (Litvinova), Grade 11 (Avramenko), and Grade 10 (Karaman) all teach replacement as the primary skill. This is not a prescriptive fringe position — it is the mainstream normative teaching across all modern Ukrainian textbooks.

5. **State Standard note**: The B2 level example of a дієприкметниковий зворот (line 3401) uses a **past passive** form ("оточене"), not an active present form. This supports the plan's framing that active present participles are a recognition-only item at B2.
