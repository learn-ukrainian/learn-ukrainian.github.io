## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Ви" Capitalization Nuance**: In Ukrainian business correspondence, capitalizing "Ви" (and "Вас", "Вам") is a strict marker of respect when addressing a *specific individual*. However, it is a common error to use it when addressing a group (plural "ви" should be lowercase). This distinction is a key shibboleth of literacy.
2. **Response Time Etiquette**: Unspoken rule: "Telegraphic" answers expected within 3 days; full letters within 1-2 days. If a request takes longer, an intermediate "receipt acknowledgment" (лист-підтвердження отримання) is mandatory within 3 days. Silence > 3 days is considered unprofessional.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Tautologies (Pleonasm)**: "спільне співробітництво" (common mistake) → "співробітництво" (correct); "вільна вакансія" → "вакансія".
2. **Calques from English/Russian**: "приймати участь" (calque) → "брати участь" (correct); "вірна відповідь" (calque) → "правильна відповідь".
3. **Subject Line Neglect**: Sending emails with empty or vague subjects ("Hello", "Question"). Ukrainian business culture demands precise, functional subject lines (e.g., "Щодо проєкту Х").

## Notes for Content Writing
- Focus on the "Escalation" scenario: How to be polite but firm when a deadline is missed. This requires specific modal verbs and conditional structures ("Будемо вдячні, якщо...", "Змушені повідомити...").
- Emphasize the "Sandwich Method" for complaints (Positive -> Negative -> Positive/Constructive) as it aligns with Ukrainian tendency towards indirectness in conflict.
