## Cultural Hooks
1. **Звукопис у поезії Шевченка**: Taras Shevchenko's masterful use of alliteration, such as in "Реве та стогне Дніпр широкий / Сердитий вітер завива", where the repetition of the 'р' (r) sound perfectly mimics the roaring of the river and the wind. This is a classic textbook example of Ukrainian sound painting.
2. **Ономатопея в дитячому фольклорі та літературі**: Ukrainian folklore is incredibly rich in specific onomatopoeic verbs that don't always have direct English equivalents, such as "тьохкати" (for a nightingale) or "кукурікати" (for a rooster). Authors like Natalia Zabila utilized these extensively in children's poetry to create engaging, rhythmic texts.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Translating English onomatopoeia directly** → *Using "бум" instead of "гуп" or "бах"*. — English speakers often map their native sound symbolism onto Ukrainian. Ukrainian has its own distinct set of sound-imitating roots (e.g., "нявкати" vs "meow", "хрюкати" vs "oink").
2. **Confusing accidental repetition with stylistic intent** → *Assuming any repeated vowel is assonance*. — Learners at B2 need to distinguish between the natural frequency of certain vowels in Ukrainian (like 'а' or 'о' in endings) and the deliberate, clustered repetition meant to evoke an emotion or soundscape.
3. **Overlooking the euphonic rules of Ukrainian** → *Failing to see how basic alternations (у/в, і/й) serve as foundational euphony before advanced stylistic devices are applied*. — Learners must remember that Ukrainian inherently strives for euphony at a grammatical level.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing**: Emphasize the natural melodiousness (мелодійність) of the Ukrainian language, which has historically been celebrated in independent Ukrainian literary criticism (e.g., the works of Potebnia). Do not compare it to Russian phonetics. If a comparison is needed, contrast the specific sound-symbolism of Ukrainian with English.
- **Tone & Immersion**: Maintain a 100% Ukrainian environment. The tone should be academic yet accessible, treating the learner as a serious student of literature and communication.
- **Word Count Strategy**: To hit the 4000-word target, ensure that the analysis of literary excerpts (Shevchenko, Kostenko, Kotsiubynsky) is deep and descriptive. Don't just list examples; provide full paragraphs analyzing *why* the sounds work and *how* they make the reader feel. Expand the section on "Ономатопея в сучасних медіа" to provide real-world, modern context.
