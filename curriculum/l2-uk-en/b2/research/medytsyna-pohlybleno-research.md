## Cultural Hooks
1. **Mykola Amosov (Микола Амосов)**: World-renowned cardiac surgeon and cybernetician. Famous for his "system of limits and loads" for longevity and his book "Thoughts and Heart". He performed thousands of heart surgeries and advocated for physical discipline as the key to health.
2. **Medical Reform ("Money follows the patient")**: Since 2018, Ukrainians choose their own **сімейний лікар** (family doctor) regardless of residence. This created the *National Health Service of Ukraine (НСЗУ)* and the electronic *eHealth* system (patients book appointments via apps like Helsi).
3. **Oleksandr Bohomolets**: Founder of the Institute of Physiology in Kyiv and pioneer in gerontology (science of aging). Legend says Stalin funded him hoping for an "elixir of youth" (ACS serum).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Лічити vs Лікувати**:
   - Error: *Лікар лічить пацієнта.* (The doctor counts the patient.)
   - Correct: *Лікар лікує пацієнта.* (The doctor treats the patient.)
   - Why: Interference with Russian "лечить" (to treat) vs Ukrainian "лічити" (to count).
2. **Gender of "Біль"**:
   - Error: *Сильна біль.* (Feminine)
   - Correct: *Сильний біль.* (Masculine)
   - Why: "Біль" is masculine in Ukrainian, unlike Russian "боль" (feminine).
3. **Приймати участь vs Приймати ліки**:
   - Error: *Я приймаю участь у конференції.* (Calque from Russian)
   - Correct: *Я беру участь у конференції.* BUT *Я приймаю ліки.*
   - Note: "Приймати" IS correct for medicine/food, but incorrect for "participate".

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Reform Context**: Ensure the dialogue reflects the MODERN system (electronic referrals, family doctor as gatekeeper), not the old Soviet polyclinic system.
- **Pain Descriptions**: B2 level requires specific adjectives (*ріжучий, ниючий, пульсуючий*) beyond just "болить".
- **Tone**: The persona is a Chief Surgeon, so the tone should be professional, precise, but encouraging healthy habits (Amosov style).
