## State Standard Reference

**Finding:** The B2 syntax section (§4.3) in the State Standard begins at line 3375 with `"4.3. Синтаксис."` and proceeds directly to `"4.3.1. Просте речення"` (line 3377). There is **no dedicated B2 subsection** for словосполучення as a standalone syntactic unit. The state-standard-2024-mapping.yaml confirms this: no `phrases` entry exists under `b2 > syntax`.

The plan's reference to "§4.3.1" for the definition of словосполучення is imprecise — in the B2 Standard, §4.3.1 = Просте речення. The словосполучення topic is covered implicitly under B2 §4.3 (Синтаксис) as prerequisite knowledge, and explicitly in the **Grade 8 and Grade 11 school curriculum** textbooks.

**Polysemy alert — керування:** In general Ukrainian corpus usage, "керування" most commonly means "management/driving" (транспортний, впоратися, засіб collocates). The syntactic meaning (government) is a specialised secondary sense. Module should establish this distinction explicitly to avoid confusion.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Дякувати + Д.в. as national identity marker.** The Avramenko Grade 8 textbook (2025, tier 1) and Zabolotnyi Grade 8 (2025, tier 1) both single out "дякую вам" (not "дякую вас") as a defining example of correct Ukrainian government — distinguishing Ukrainian from interference patterns. This grammatical point carries cultural weight as a marker of careful, educated speech.

2. **Наукова мова та Шевченків синтаксис.** Phrase structure analysis (синтаксичний розбір) is central to the Ukrainian school tradition of literary analysis. Shevchenko's poetry is a classic corpus for demonstrating nominal and verbal phrase types in educational contexts — providing a Ukrainian-grounded anchor for what might otherwise feel like abstract grammar terminology.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Керування: дякувати/вибачати + Знахідний (L1 English/L2 Russicism)** → Correct: **дякувати кому? (Д.в.)** — дякую вам, дякую тобі. Error pattern: "дякую вас" (by analogy with "thank you" direct object, or Russian "благодарить кого"). Source: Zabolotnyi Grade 8, 2025 — textbook explicitly labels this the most widespread grammatical error in word combinations.

2. **Вибачати/вибачте + Знахідний** → Correct: **вибачте мені (Д.в.)**, not **вибачте мене (Зн.в.)**. Paired error with дякувати. Supported by Zabolotnyi Grade 5 (2023): "Вибачити (кому?) сестрі".

3. **Керування vs прилягання confusion with infinitive constructions.** Learners often mis-classify "змусити працювати" (pryliahannia — infinitive adjoins without form change) as government, since it resembles "читати книгу" (government — book takes Accusative). The infinitive does not change its form, which is the diagnostic for adjunction. Source: plan's own content_outline point; confirmed in Glazova Grade 11 (2019): syntactic connection table distinguishes these explicitly.

4. **Негація + Родовий (bonus advanced point):** In government with negation, the dependent noun shifts from Accusative to Genitive: "Хлопець викликає довіру" (Зн.в.) → "Хлопець не викликає довіри" (Р.в.). This is a subtle government alternation that catches B2 learners. Source: Zabolotnyi Grade 11 (2019).

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **Polysemy of "керування"** must be addressed in the opening of the government section — the word means both "syntactic government" and "management/driving" in general use. A brief orientation prevents confusion.

2. **State Standard gap:** Since the Standard has no explicit B2 "phrases" section, the module can legitimately frame itself as "systematising what you already know" (TTT approach fits perfectly — Test shows implicit knowledge, Teach makes it explicit, Task deploys it). This framing is pedagogically honest.

3. **Negation + Genitive alternation** (error #4 above) is an excellent advanced point for the Practice section — it ties government directly to information structure and is genuinely surprising to B2 learners.

4. **Decolonized framing is natural here:** Ukrainian government patterns (дякувати кому, вибачати кому) are documented in Grade 5–11 textbooks as distinctively Ukrainian norms — not as "different from Russian" but as the established norm of educated Ukrainian. Frame positively: "Літературна українська мова вимагає..."

5. **Transformation exercises** (шовкова сукня → сукня з шовку) are well-supported by textbook tradition and give a productive, active skill to anchor the agreement vs. government distinction.

6. **The subject–predicate exclusion** (підмет і присудок — НЕ словосполучення) is highlighted in Voron Grade 11 (2019) as a key "Зауважте!" point. Include this to pre-empt a very common analytical error.

7. **Word target 4000:** Scientific-register analysis passages (e.g., analysing a newspaper lead paragraph for all phrase types) will be the density driver for the final Practice section — rich, authentic text with full classification walkthrough.
