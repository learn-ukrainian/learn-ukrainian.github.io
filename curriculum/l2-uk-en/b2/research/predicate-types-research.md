# Дослідження: Типи присудків

## State Standard Reference

**Alignment note**: The B2 State Standard (lines 3375–3418, §4.3) does not contain a discrete subsection explicitly titled "predicate types." The plan's cited §4.3.2 maps to **"Просте ускладнене речення"** (lines 3395–3407) in the CEFR-aligned State Standard — this section covers participial phrases, homogeneous members, and parentheticals, not predicate classification directly.

The closest authoritative alignment is:

**§4.3.1 — Просте речення** (lines 3377–3393): At B2 level, the learner must produce and analyse simple sentences across all communicative types. Predicate-type classification is implicit prerequisite knowledge for §4.3.2 (complex simple sentence) and §4.3.3 (one-member sentences), both of which require the learner to identify the predicate centre.

**§4.3.3 — Просте односкладне речення** (lines 3409–3418): Distinguishes between verb-headed (означено-особові, неозначено-особові, безособові) and noun-headed (номінативні) one-member sentences — directly requires understanding of what constitutes a predicate vs. its absence.

**Direct quote from §4.3.3.1** (line 3414): *"безособові речення: Було приємно дивитися на веселих дітей. Там усе правильно записано."* — the second example (*правильно записано*) is a compound nominal predicate using a passive participle as the nominal part, demonstrating that predicate-type analysis is live at B2 level.

**Conclusion**: The plan's §4.3.2 reference is a cross-reference to the school curriculum standard (not the CEFR State Standard indexed here). Content should cite §4.3.1 + §4.3.3 from the CEFR Standard, and note that the school-curriculum treatment of predicate types is codified in Grade 8 Ukrainian syntax curriculum.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Леся Українка — майстер номінативних речень і нульової зв'язки**: Lesya Ukrainka (1871–1913), Ukrainian writer, co-founder of the literary circle "Pleiad," is famous for syntactically dense prose-poetry. Her dramatic works (e.g., *Лісова пісня*, *Кам'яний господар*) are saturated with compound nominal predicates and null-copula constructions — e.g., *"Земля — свята"*, *"Воля — то найвищий скарб"* — making her work the ideal authentic textual anchor for illustrating the null copula as a distinctly Ukrainian syntactic feature. Source: Wikipedia (verified above).

2. **Науковий стиль і іменний присудок**: Ukrainian academic and journalistic writing heavily favours compound nominal predicates to express classification, definition, and evaluation — a register B2 learners must master for reading analytical texts. The plan's register target (науковий) directly aligns with the frequency of nominal predicates in institutional Ukrainian prose. Example pattern from real Ukrainian academic writing: *"Предикативність є основною категорією речення."* / *"Присудок — центральний член речення."* (null copula variant in non-academic registers).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Аналітична форма майбутнього = складений дієслівний присудок** → **Хибно**. *"Буду читати"* is a simple predicate in Ukrainian — the analytic future form is a single grammaticalized construction equivalent to *"читатиму"*. Learners with English background map "I will read" (auxiliary + main verb) directly onto the compound verbal predicate pattern. Fix: emphasise grammatical indivisibility (неподільність) as the criterion — both components express a single temporal meaning, not phase/modal + action split.

2. **Змішування складеного дієслівного та складеного іменного**: *"Він став працювати"* (compound verbal — infinitive of action) vs. *"Він став лікарем"* (compound nominal — noun in instrumental as nominal part). Learners confuse the two because both use *стати* as auxiliary. Fix: the diagnostic question is — does the second component name a state/quality/role (→ nominal) or describe an action (→ verbal)?

3. **Нульова зв'язка у теперішньому часі**: English speakers default to inserting *є* in present-tense nominal predicates (*"Книга є цікавою"*), which in contemporary Ukrainian registers as Church Slavonic or official bureaucratic style. The neutral norm is null copula: *"Книга — цікава."* The dash (тире) signals the absent copula orthographically. Fix: frame the null copula as the unmarked Ukrainian default, with *є* as a marked stylistic choice.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Zaболотний Grade 8 (2025)** is the primary textbook source; textbook RAG confirms it contains the full taxonomy table with examples. Phase B can draw examples from this source directly.
- **Null copula framing**: Present this as a feature Ukrainian possesses (it has a zero-copula option for stylistic flexibility), not as "Ukrainian drops the copula." The distinction matters for decolonized framing.
- **Instrumental case in nominal predicates**: When the copula is overt (*був, буде, стане*), the nominal part typically takes the instrumental (*"Вона була лікарем"*), but nominative is also attested in literary/elevated register (*"Вона була лікар"*). Phase B should note this stylistic variation.
- **напівзв'язка substitution**: Replace with *"дієслово-зв'язка з фазово-оціночним значенням"* or simply list *стати, залишатися, виявитися, здаватися, вважатися* as "неповні зв'язки" (standard academic term in Ukrainian linguistics).
- **Register alignment**: The plan targets науковий register. Compound nominal predicates dominate Ukrainian scientific and academic prose. This should be highlighted in the practical section — analysis of authentic academic texts will naturally surface nominal predicates.
- **Contrastive note (non-Russian framing)**: English uses "to be" as the only full copula; Portuguese and Polish have similar null-copula tendencies in certain registers — these are useful parallels that don't invoke Russian.
