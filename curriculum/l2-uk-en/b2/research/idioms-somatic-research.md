# Дослідження: Соматичні фразеологізми: Голова, обличчя, руки, ноги, тіло

## State Standard Reference

§4.4.1.2: "Стилістичні засоби лексики: антоніми... синоніми... омоніми... епітети: теплий день, смачний торт; метафори: ідуть дощі, сонце сідає; скорочення (вибірково)..." (lines 3459–3469)

**Important caveat**: §4.4.1.2 does not explicitly list фразеологізми among B2 lexical devices — the section covers synonyms, antonyms, epithets, metaphors, and sound imitation. Somatic idioms fall under the lexical stylistics umbrella as a practical extension of «метафори» and «стилістичні засоби лексики». The direct CEFR bridge appears in the C1 intro (line 3488): *"детальне розуміння її системних зв'язків та можливість... невимушеного використання значного діапазону виражальних засобів, зокрема... найуживаніших ідіом"* — meaning B2 must introduce the idiom corpus that C1 will master.

**Alignment**: This module addresses the B2 stage of lexical stylistics development (§4.4.1.2) and lays the foundation for C1 idiom mastery. Frame somatic phraseology as an instance of lexical metaphor (semantic shift from body-literal to body-figurative), consistent with §4.4.1.2's coverage of метафори.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **«Золоті руки» (1961) — Віталій Коротич**: GRAC confirms the idiom *золоті руки* served as the title of poet Vítaliy Korotych's debut poetry collection (Kyiv, 1961). This grounds the phraseology lesson in 20th-century Ukrainian cultural memory — a worker-craftsman aesthetic rooted in the Sixties generation (*шістдесятники*). Source: GRAC concordance, multiple attested references.

2. **Фразеологія ділової мови (1926) — Підмогильний і Плужник**: During the Ukrainization era, Valerian Pidmohylny and Yevhen Pluzhnyk compiled a landmark Russian–Ukrainian dictionary of business phraseology specifically to establish the *самобутність* (distinctiveness) of Ukrainian idiomatic expression versus Russian. Republished during independence — evidence that Ukrainian phraseological norms are a living, defended tradition, not a derivative of any other language. Source: Wikipedia «Фразеологія ділової мови».

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Кидатися в очі»** → Correct: **«впадати в око»** — "Кидатися в очі" is a Russian calque (*бросаться в глаза*). The GRAC corpus confirms «впадати в око / впадати в очі» as the standard Ukrainian form. This error is extremely common because learners read Russian-language Ukrainian texts (Soviet-era publications) that used the calque freely.

2. **«Сміятися над кимось»** → Correct: **«сміятися з когось»** — GRAC metalinguistic commentary explicitly flags «сміятися над» as an influence from Russian «смеяться над кем-то». The Ukrainian construction uses preposition «з + Gen». Multiple GRAC attestations of «над» date primarily to 19th-century literary texts (archaic influence); modern normative usage is «з».

3. **«Ламати голову» vs «морочити голову»** — Learners confuse these two: «ламати голову (над чимось)» = to rack one's brains (about a problem — action performed by the subject on themselves); «морочити голову (комусь)» = to pester/confuse someone (action directed at another person). The plan correctly flags this distinction.

4. **VESUM gap — «опростоволоситися»**: This verb does not appear in the VESUM morphological dictionary, though it is attested in GRAC. Treat it as a literary/marked form. The plan's etymology note (sacredness of head covering) is linguistically sound and adds cultural depth, but present alongside the neutral synonym «зганьбитися» to avoid leaving learners without a productive form.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Register table is the spine of Section 5**: The plan's register differentiation (книжні / розмовні / нейтральні) is the most pedagogically load-bearing element. Allocate sufficient space for a clear table contrasting «втратити обличчя» (formal/book) vs «водити за носа» (colloquial) vs «мати золоті руки» (neutral). This is the synthesis skill that separates B2 from B1 idiom awareness.
- **Literal vs. figurative disambiguation — Section 2 priority**: «стати на ноги» shows strong literal usage in GRAC (physical standing up) alongside figurative (achieve independence). Content must address this disambiguation explicitly, especially in context sentences.
- **Avoid "Ukrainian has X unlike Russian"**: Frame positively — Ukrainian body-idioms constitute a *system* (голова = інтелект, руки = дія, ноги = рух, серце = емоції) that reflects the worldview of a specific culture. Reference Polish or English parallels where comparison is useful (e.g., English *to have golden hands*, Polish *mieć złote ręce* — but note Ukrainian precedence is independent).
- **«Опростоволоситися»**: Use only in the etymology sidebar for Section 2. Do not include in vocabulary table without VESUM coverage. Use «зганьбитися» in exercises.
- **Case governance sidebar (Section 3)**: «взяти в руки» (Acc) / «тримати в руках» (Loc) / «з рук у руки» (Gen→Acc) — this prepositional governance mini-lesson should be presented as a compact table, not embedded in flowing text, to prevent it from inflating the section at the expense of idiom depth.
- **Word target distribution**: 500+800+800+900+1000 = 4000 words. Section 5 (1000 words) is the heaviest and rightly so — it contains the synthesis activity plus the full classification exercise. Ensure content in Sections 2-4 does not borrow word count from Section 5.
