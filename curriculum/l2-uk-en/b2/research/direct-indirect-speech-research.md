## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ukrainian Wikipedia on Пряма мова** — "Пряма мова — це спосіб передачі чужої мови, при якому мовець або автор тексту повністю зберігає її лексичні, синтаксичні та стилістичні особливості." Useful framing for the вступ: пряма мова preserves the speaker's full linguistic identity (lexical, syntactic, stylistic). This is a distinctly Ukrainian-language resource — not derived from any other language's definition.

2. **Ivan Franko's reported maxim** (plan mentions it): «Кожний є сином свого народу» — usable as an authentic academic цитата model. Franko's essays and Shevchenko's letters are canonical sources for both direct quotation and stylistic analysis in Ukrainian academic writing. These exemplify how Ukrainian literary culture treats the author's voice as inviolable — lапки preserve not just meaning but authorial identity, a value embedded in Ukrainian academic norms.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Лапки у діалозі** → тире на початку репліки — Learners transfer direct-speech punctuation rules (лапки) into dialogue formatting. In Ukrainian, dialogue uses тире, not лапки. The error is widespread because both represent «чуже мовлення» but have entirely different graphical conventions.

2. **Pronoun shift omission** → Я сказав: «Я піду» → ❌ «Він сказав, що я піду» / ✅ «Він сказав, що він піде» — Learners forget to shift 1st-person pronouns to 3rd-person when transforming to indirect speech. Demonstratives are similarly affected: цей → той, тут → там, зараз → тоді.

3. **Наказовий спосіб left intact in indirect speech** → ❌ «Він сказав, щоб іти» / ✅ «Він сказав, щоб я йшов» — The imperative must be rendered as «щоб + минулий час» with the subject explicitly stated. Learners either leave the infinitive (incomplete transformation) or omit the щоб entirely.

4. *(Bonus — common at B2)* **Лапки при переказі** — Learners use quotation marks around non-verbatim reported content, confusing цитування (verbatim + attribution) with переказ (content only, no marks).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Four positional models**: Textbooks use formula notation (А: «П», «П» — а., «П, — а, — п», А: «П», — а.). Phase B should present these as a visual reference table early in section 2, then explain each with examples from Ukrainian literature (Шевченко, Франко, Леся Українка).
- **Dialogue section**: Emphasize тире + no лапки as the defining rule. Use a contrasting pair: a direct speech version and a dialogue version of the same exchange to show the difference concretely.
- **Indirect speech**: The transformation mechanic is the pedagogical core. A parallel-column format (пряма → непряма) works best — show pronoun shift, verb shift, particle/conjunction selection, and lost elements (звертання, вигуки) all in one systematic table.
- **Цитування in academic register**: The scientific/publicistic register framing in section 4 is appropriate for B2. Distinguish: повна цитата (verbatim, лапки, attribution) vs. часткова цитата (embedded in own sentence) vs. переказ (no лапки). Franko and Шевченко examples anchor this in Ukrainian literary culture.
- **Decolonized framing**: Do not frame Ukrainian punctuation rules as departures from any other system. Present the four positional models as inherent to Ukrainian syntactic tradition, rooted in Ukrainian literary style guides (Правопис 2019). The use of «—» (тире) in dialogue is a feature of Ukrainian typographic culture shared with French and other Romance traditions — not derived from any Slavic neighbor.
- **Register note**: The план correctly marks this as «науковий» register. Ensure examples in section 4 (цитування) come from academic/publicistic text, not just literary fiction.
- **Free indirect speech** (невласне пряма мова): Voron Gr. 11 covers it, and it's present in Ukrainian prose. The plan doesn't scope it, but a one-sentence awareness note in the вступ would add B2-level depth without scope creep.
- **Word target**: 4000 words. Section 5 (практика) carries 1000 words — ensure exercises are substantive and include authentic Ukrainian texts for error-correction and stylistic analysis.
