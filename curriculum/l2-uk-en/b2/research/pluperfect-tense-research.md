2. **Акт проголошення незалежності України (24 серпня 1991)** — Wikipedia explicitly notes this official state document contains pluperfect forms, demonstrating that the pluperfect is not purely archaic or literary; it surfaces in official-documentary register when precise anteriority must be marked. ## Key Linguistic Facts (for Phase B writing)

**Formation** (Wikipedia verified):
- Past tense of main verb + auxiliary **бути** in past form: **був / була / було / були**
- Agreement is with the grammatical subject (gender + number), NOT invariant
- Typical word order: main verb first → auxiliary second: «бачив був», «написала була»
- Emphatic/inverted order possible: «був бачив» (foregrounded anteriority)

**Two core meanings** (Wikipedia):
1. **Anteriority** — action that preceded another past action: «Я читав був цю книжку, та забув її зміст»
2. **Interrupted/annulled action** — action begun but discontinued or whose result no longer holds: «знаходить вербову сопілку, що **був кинув**» (Lesya Ukrainka)

**Critical distinction for «було» / «бувало»** (Wikipedia, confirmed in Zabolotnyi textbook RAG):
- Neuter «було» used as discourse particle = **habitual/recurring past** (NOT pluperfect): «А ще, **було**, як намалює що-небудь…» = *used to*
- «бувало» = same habitual meaning, conversational register
- These are NOT pluperfect forms — they are discourse markers. The pluperfect requires full gender/number agreement.

**Historical dimension** (Wikipedia):
- Widely used in Old Ukrainian literary language (XVI–XVIII cc.): «пошолъ билъ, сказалъ билъ»
- V. M. Rusanivsky argues Ukrainian indicative has *four* tenses: present, past, **pluperfect**, future — making Ukrainian typologically richer than often presented
- Parallel to French Plus-que-parfait and Portuguese pretérito mais-que-perfeito (useful for contrastive explanation without defaulting to English "had + past participle")

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Auxiliary agreement collapse** → treating «був» as invariant:
   - *Вона прийшла **був*** ❌ → **Вона прийшла була** ✓
   - *Діти пішли **був*** ❌ → **Діти пішли були** ✓
   - *Why it happens*: Learners pattern-match on conditional «б/би» (which IS invariant) and incorrectly extend that logic to the pluperfect auxiliary.

2. **English over-transfer** → mapping every "had done" onto the Ukrainian pluperfect:
   - Sequential past-in-past narration in Ukrainian routinely uses the **simple past** + temporal adverbs: «Спочатку він написав листа, а потім вийшов» (not *написав був*)
   - *Why it happens*: English past perfect is obligatory in subordinate clauses after "before/after"; Ukrainian is not — the simple past + context is the neutral choice.

3. **Habitual «було» / «бувало» confusion**:
   - «Ходив **було** туди щодня» = *used to go there every day* (habitual particle, NOT pluperfect)
   - «Ходив **був** туди» = *had gone there* (anterior, pluperfect)
   - *Why it happens*: Both involve a past verb + the word «було», but grammatically they are entirely different — one is a syntactic insert, the other is a full auxiliary with agreement.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **No «аннульований» in any examples** — use «анульований результат» (single н, VESUM-verified).
2. The Wikipedia article provides **directly quotable literary examples** from canonical authors (Lesya Ukrainka, Shevchenko, Marko Vovchok) — Phase B should incorporate these verbatim as primary evidence.
3. The «Жив-був» formula is more accurately described as a **legendary/habitual-past formula** rooted in the pluperfect tradition, not technically the full pluperfect paradigm itself — Phase B should make this nuance clear: «жив-був» signals the register and mythic time, even if the form has been grammaticalised into a fixed expression.
4. Avoid framing the pluperfect as "a tense that exists in Ukrainian unlike in Russian" — this is Russocentric framing. Instead: *Ukrainian has four indicative tenses; the pluperfect is one of them.* The comparison to Portuguese and French (both with analytic pluperfects) is more illuminating and decolonised.
5. The **Акт незалежності** anchor is pedagogically powerful: it shows the pluperfect is not a dead relic but an active precision tool in modern Ukrainian across registers.
6. Textbook RAG returned **no dedicated passages on davnomynulyi chas** — this is expected since the form is B2+ scope; the primary sources for Phase B are the Wikipedia article, literary canon, and the State Standard mapping. Phase B should treat this as advanced analytical material.
