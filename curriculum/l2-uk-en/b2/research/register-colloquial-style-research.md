## Common Learner Errors

1. **Суржик contamination in fillers** → Correct Ukrainian equivalents required:
   - *тіпа/тіпу* (Russified form) → **типу** (Ukrainian orthographic standard)
   - *короче* (Russian discourse marker) → **коротше** (Ukrainian comparative adverb)
   - *да* (Russian affirmative) → **так / ну / авжеж** (Ukrainian options)
   - Why it happens: learners absorb these from mixed-language Ukrainian digital content and Russian-origin media.

2. **Дякую тебе** (accusative) vs. correct **Дякую тобі** (dative):
   - Ukrainian *дякувати* governs dative; accusative is a direct Russianism (cf. Russian *благодарить кого*).
   - Plan explicitly includes this as a transformation example — good diagnostic anchor.

3. **Particle orthography errors** — three distinct rules that learners conflate:
   - *бо* after verbs = hyphen: **іди-бо, скажи-бо** (emphatic softener)
   - *же/ж* as separate modifiers: **що ж, як же, але ж** (no hyphen)
   - *таки* after the word it modifies: **він таки прийшов** vs. **таки він прийшов** (position changes meaning slightly)
   - Why it happens: learners over-apply or under-apply hyphenation by analogy with prefix constructions.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Frequency hierarchy for drill intensity**: таки (137 IPM) and власне/типу (66–68 IPM) deserve the most practice; краш/крінж (0.02–0.05 IPM) should be introduced as emerging vocabulary only, not drilled for production.
- **Textbook anchor**: Karaman Grade 10, p. 235 (`10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0416`) explicitly asks students to extract colloquial style markers from text — this is the direct pedagogical model for the module's transformation exercises. Avramenko Grade 11, p. 164 provides the formal/colloquial lexical contrast framework (neutral vs. stylistically marked: *піти* → *поволоктися*, *побрести*).
- **Particle taxonomy to use**: Karaman Grade 10, p. 210 is authoritative — presents підсилювальні (ж, же, таки) and видільні (же, ж, таки) as overlapping categories. Use this taxonomy for the grammar section rather than inventing a new framework.
- **Decolonized framing already embedded in plan**: the plan correctly uses "перехід від суржикових «тіпа», «короче», «да» до природних українських маркерів." Phase B must maintain this framing — name these as surzyk forms, not "Russian borrowings," and emphasize that Ukrainian has its own native equivalents that are richer and more expressive.
- **Ellipsis examples from State Standard** (§4.4.1.3) are ready-to-use: *Смачного!*, *Максим пішов до бібліотеки, Оксана – також збирається* — cite these directly in the grammar section with analysis.
- **Word target note**: At 4000 words with 5 sections (700+850+850+800+800), the tightest risk is sections 4 and 5 at 800 words each. The Praktykum section especially risks coming in short if activities are too condensed. Phase B should aim 10–15% overshoot on those sections.
