**§ Speaking (lines 2727–2733):** Learner can defend own position with arguments and examples, address an audience on topics within professional sphere, speak publicly with fluency that does not cause difficulties for listeners.

Alignment: This module directly tests all four §1 speech activity domains (слухання, читання, письмо, говоріння) specified for B2, plus the stylistic and lexical breadth in §4.4. As a final exam, it serves as the summative assessment of the full B2 competency profile.

## Cultural Hooks

**1. The letter ґ and the 1933 purge (Wikipedia: Ґ)**
The letter ґ was removed from the Ukrainian alphabet in 1933 under Soviet ideological pressure (the Kaganovich–Olinter orthographic reform), as part of the broader Russification campaign that also destroyed the 1928 Kharkiv Orthography. It was restored in the **3rd edition of Ukrainian Pravopys in 1990** — before independence, as part of the cultural renaissance of late Soviet Ukraine. The words ґрунт, ґанок, ґудзик retain it as a marker of authentic Ukrainian phonology (the plosive [g] versus Ukrainian фрикативний [ɦ]).

> **⚠️ Plan correction needed:** The plan states the letter was restored "в 1991 р." — this is imprecise. The correct year is **1990** (3rd edition of the Pravopys). 1991 is Ukraine's independence year. The content should use 1990.

**2. Ukrainian B2/C1 certification and the State Standard 2024**
Ukraine does not yet have a single unified state exam system equivalent to DELF/DELE/TestDaF for Ukrainian as L2, but the State Standard 2024 formally defines competency levels A1–C2 for Ukrainian language instruction. The B2 level is described as the threshold for "professional communication readiness" — making this module's framing as a professional checkpoint directly grounded in the Standard. Learners who demonstrate B2 can navigate official documents, professional emails, and media analysis — all tested in this exam.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«приймати участь» → «брати участь»** — Russian calque (принимать участие). The correct Ukrainian collocation is брати участь. This error is extremely common at all levels among learners with Russian exposure.

2. **«слідуюча зупинка» → «наступна зупинка»** — Russian loan (следующий). Ukrainian uses наступний. The word *слідуючий* as an active participle is non-standard in literary Ukrainian; the adjective наступний covers this meaning.

3. **Register mixing: «привіт» in formal writing** — Learners use informal greeting forms in professional emails/letters. B2 formal register requires «Вітаю» or «Шановний/Шановна + name». The distinction between ти/ви registers is often undermastered even at B2.

4. **«отримав/отримала»** is correct Ukrainian; learners sometimes write **«получив/получила»** (Russian). Correct form: отримати (B2 learners should have this fully mastered by exam time — flag if it appears).

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **Year correction for ґ:** Use 1990 (Pravopys, 3rd edition), not 1991. The cultural hook is strongest as "відновлена в 1990 р. у третьому виданні «Українського правопису» — рік до незалежності, як символ повернення мовної ідентичності."

2. **Exam format framing:** The State Standard does not prescribe a specific exam format for B2. Content should present the exam as a **demonstration of integrated competence**, not a bureaucratic checklist — aligned with the TTT pedagogy and the plan's focus on "readiness for C1."

3. **Milозвучність (euphony):** The plan's §1 mentions милозвучність in the cultural context. This is a genuine Ukrainian phonological concept (правила евфонії: у/в, і/й, з/із/зі alternations), well-grounded in the State Standard (A1: §4.1.7, lines 590–594; B1: §4.1.3, lines 2022–2026). At B2, learners should be applying these automatically — the exam section can present them as self-monitoring criteria.

4. **Synonym richness as a B2 marker:** The plan emphasises lexical richness (use of synonyms) as a key B2→C1 indicator. Concrete examples: обрій / небокрай / видноколо (three synonyms for "horizon") demonstrate active synonym command beyond passive recognition.

5. **Word target is 4,000 words** — this is an integration/checkpoint module, so the content can afford longer worked examples, model texts, and annotated exam tasks. The 5-section split (600+1000+800+800+800 = 4,000) is tight; Phase B should aim for 4,200–4,400 to ensure gate passage.

6. **Decolonized framing:** Present Ukrainian language certification as a **positive marker of European linguistic identity**, not as a response to Russian influence. The letter ґ story should be framed as: "Ця літера — частина живої фонетичної системи української мови, що повернулася до абетки в 1990 році як природний процес мовної нормалізації."
