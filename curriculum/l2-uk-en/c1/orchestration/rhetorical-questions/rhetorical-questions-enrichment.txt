## State Standard Reference
§4.4.4: "Стилістичні засоби синтаксису: ... риторичні запитання: І хто б міг подумати?"
Alignment: This module directly addresses the C1 requirement for mastering stylistic syntax, specifically the use of rhetorical questions as a tool for emphasis and persuasion in various functional styles.

## Vocabulary Frequency
| Word | Frequency / Source | Key collocations |
|------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Хіба** | High (General/Lit) | Хіба це можливо? Хіба ж я знав? Хіба не ясно? |
| **Невже** | High (General/Lit) | Невже це правда? Невже ти не розумієш? Невже так буває? |
| **Чи** | Very High (All styles) | Чи варто говорити? Чи не час нам..? Чи ж ми не єдині? |
| **Як** | Very High | Як можна забути? Як не любити? Як же так? |
| **Доколе** | Low (Archaic/High) | Доколе нам терпіти? (Stylistic marker for C1) |

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Panas Myrnyi**: The novel title *"Хіба ревуть воли, як ясла повні?"* is the quintessential Ukrainian rhetorical question, originating from the Bible (Job 6:5) and becoming a proverb for social unrest caused by injustice.
2. **Taras Shevchenko**: His poetry is built on rhetorical questions to mobilize the nation (e.g., *"Чи ми ще зійдемося знову?"* in *Davydovi psalmy*).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Intonation Mismatch**: Learners often use rising "question" intonation instead of the falling/level intonation required for rhetorical statements.
2. **Particle Confusion**: Using "невже" (surprise) where "хіба" (doubt/negation) is semantically required.
   - *Error*: "Невже ти не бачиш?" (Simple surprise) vs *Correction*: "Хіба ти не бачиш?" (Implying: "You should see it").
3. **Overuse in Formal Writing**: Using too many rhetorical questions in academic essays (prohibited) vs. publicistic style (encouraged).

## Cross-References
- **Builds on**: B2 syntax modules (Complex sentences), B1 particles.
- **Prepares for**: C1.5 Literature (analyzing authorial style), C2 Rhetoric.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Focus on the **function** over form. At C1, it's not just about forming the question, but *why* it is used (irony, despair, mobilization).
- Distinguish between **diatribe** (dialogue with oneself using questions) and isolated rhetorical questions.
