## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Cekh" System**: Kobzari were not random beggars but members of organized professional guilds (cekhs) with strict exams, hierarchy (uchni, bratishchyky, cekhmeister), and a secret language ("lebiyska mova").
2. **The "Executed Congress" Myth & Reality**: The tragedy of the 1930s repressions against kobzari (often associated with the Kharkiv Congress of 1934) symbolizes the Soviet attempt to erase Ukrainian historical memory.
3. **The Instrument Evolution**: The transition from the diatonic "starosvitska" bandura to the chromatic Kyiv/Kharkiv bandura changed the music from intimate accompaniment to orchestral performance.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Terminological Confusion**: Calling any bandura player a "kobzar". *Correction*: "Kobzar" refers specifically to the traditional, usually blind, epic singer; modern players are "bandurysty".
2. **Declension of Surnames**: Incorrect declension of names like "Вересай" (noun-type) vs "Гнатюк" (noun-type). *Rule*: Follow standard masculine noun declension.
3. **Stylistic register**: Using modern slang when discussing archaic epics. *Correction*: Maintain a respectful, slightly elevated or academic register.
