## Cultural Hooks
1. **Ivan Franko as "The Titan of Labor"**: Franko wrote over 6,000 works in his lifetime. He was nominated for the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1916 but died before he could receive it.
2. **"Boryslav Laughs" Precedence**: Often cited as one of the first literary works in the world to depict an organized workers' strike and the conflict between labor and capital in the oil industry ("naftova lykhomanka").
3. **The Tukhlya Rock**: The village of Tukhlya exists in the Lviv region, and locals still point to the rocks associated with the legend of Zakhar Berkut, blending fiction with geography.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Confusing Dialect with Standard**: Learners may use words like *легінь* (lad) or *ґазда* (master) in neutral official contexts.
   *   *Correction*: Mark as dialectal/stylistic. Use *юнак* or *господар* in standard language.
2. **Passive Voice Overuse**: Writing "Було написано Франком" instead of "Франко написав".
   *   *Correction*: Use active voice to reflect agency (Rule: Agency Pass).
3. **Genre Confusion**: Calling "Захар Беркут" a "documentary" instead of a "historical novella" (історична повість) combining history with romantic legend.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Dialect Handling**: When quoting Franko's dialect-heavy passages (especially from *Zakhar Berkut* or *Boryslav*), provide glosses or explanations in standard Ukrainian within the analysis, not English.
- **Narrative Arc**: Frame the module as a journey from the "High Carpathians" (History/Legend) to the "Grime of Boryslav" (Industry/Realism) to the "Depth of the Soul" (Short Stories).
- **Stylistics**: Dedicate specific attention to how Franko uses language to characterize social classes (e.g., the bureaucratic language of officials vs. the lively dialect of peasants).
