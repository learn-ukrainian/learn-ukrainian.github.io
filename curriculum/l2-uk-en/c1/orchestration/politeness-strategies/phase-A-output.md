## Cultural Hooks
1.  **"Pan/Pani" Renaissance**: The restoration of *пан/пані* is not just a translation of Mr/Ms, but a reclamation of European identity and dignity after the Soviet imposed *товариш*. It implies a specific respectful distance.
2.  **Diminutives as Softeners**: Unlike English "please", Ukrainian often uses diminutives (*хвилинка*, *кавуся*, *момент*) to soften commands and show *Positive Politeness* (warmth/intimacy), even in semi-formal contexts.
3.  **The "Ty/Vy" Threshold**: The transition from *Ви* to *ти* is a significant ritual (formerly *bruderschaft*), marking a change in pragmatic rules. Using *Ви* with a capitalized letter in writing is a persistent marker of high respect, despite modern prescriptive trends allowing lowercase.

## Common Learner Errors
1.  **"Вибачаюся" vs "Вибачте"**: Using reflexive *я вибачаюся* ("I excuse myself") is considered illiterate/surzhyk. **Correct**: *Вибачте* or *Прошу вибачення*.
2.  **Direct Imperatives**: Translating "Give me..." as *Дай мені...* without softeners. **Correct**: *Чи не міг би ти дати...* or *Дай, будь ласка...*.
3.  **"Будь ласка" Placement**: Overusing *...будь ласка* at the very end of long sentences (English calque). **Correct**: *Будь ласка, зробіть...* or *Прошу зробити...*.
