# Дослідження: Морфологічні стилістичні засоби

## State Standard Reference

**§4.4.3** (lines 4569–4583): *"Стилістичні засоби лексики: антоніми... синоніми... омоніми... епітети... метафори... зменшувально-пестливі форми: хлопець – хлопчик, дощ – дощик, донька – донечка; згрубілі форми: вітер – вітрище, великий – величезний; складні слова..."*

**§4.4.1.1** (lines 4551–4559): Стилі української мови — розмовний, офіційний, науковий, публіцистичний, художній, релігійний, епістолярний. Module must demonstrate morphological choices across these registers.

**§4.2** (lines 4432–4470): Word formation at C1 — comparison degrees, aspect pairs, agent nouns, deverbal nouns (-нн-, -енн-), abstract nouns (-ість-), denominal adjectives. These are the *formal* word-formation patterns; the module's value-add is framing them as **stylistic resources** across registers.

**§4.1.1.2** (lines 4179–4191): Short-form adjectives with zero ending — *годен, смутен, Галин* — marked as C1 mastery. State Standard explicitly lists these as a separate morphological category.

**§4.1.3.1** (lines 4403–4423): Verb indicative — synthetic future (боротимуся, кричатиме) vs analytic (буде кричати). Both are standard; stylistic choice between them is a C1-level competency.

**§4.1.3.2** (lines 4425–4427): Imperative — *мрій, мріймо; борися, борімося, боріться; хай/нехай бореться* — full paradigm including hortative.

**Alignment:** Module covers §4.4.3 (diminutives/augmentatives as stylistic lexical devices), §4.2 (word formation as stylistic resource), §4.1.1.2 (short adjective forms), §4.1.3.1–3.2 (verb aspect and mood as stylistic tools). Comprehensive C1 coverage of morphological stylistics.

Key point: stylistic possibilities of word formation appear through *зіставлення слів одного кореня й одного значення* (comparing words of same root/meaning). Covers фемінітиви as a contemporary stylistic/social topic.

**Grade 11 Zabolotnyi (p. 185):** Suffix table with stylistic coloring:
- **-ість, -ство, -цтво, -ота, -ання/-ення/-іння/-ття, -ізм** → abstract/generalizing meaning → науковий, офіційно-діловий, публіцистичний styles (examples: *громадськість, людство, збирання, реалізм*)
- **-альн-, -увальн-, -арн-** → book register (not shown in excerpt but referenced)

**Grade 11 Zabolotnyi (p. 188):** Short adjectives — *нестягнені форми: дрібен, зелен, рад, срібен, здоров* — "вживаємо переважно у фольклорі, розмовному мовленні, у поезії." Direct textbook grounding for plan section §3.

**Grade 11 Avramenko (p. 178):** Complex words (*складні слова*) carry different stylistic functions — terms in book styles, emotional-expressive in artistic/publicistic. Reduplication (*ходжу-ходжу, старий-престарий*) intensifies meaning.

## Cultural Hooks

**1. Diminutives as a national-cultural marker of Ukrainian.**
Ukrainian has one of the most developed diminutive systems among Slavic languages, extending to nouns, adjectives, adverbs, and even verb prefixes. This is not mere sentimentality — it is a grammaticalized expression of intimacy, care, and affective positioning in discourse. The diminutive *матінка, батечко, сонечко, донечка* appear even in formal oral address within families, reflecting Ukrainian relational culture. Source: §4.4.3 State Standard; Зализняк, А.А., comparative Slavic diminutive data; Ukrainian Wikipedia на Стилістика: *"шість стилів: розмовно-побутовий, художній, офіційно-діловий, науковий, публіцистичний, сакральний"* — diminutives are markers of the first two.

**2. Short adjective forms as a living link to Old Ukrainian in folk songs.**
Forms like *ясен місяць, красна панна, сивий голубонько* preserved in *народні пісні та думи* represent a direct grammatical continuity with 16th–18th century literary Ukrainian. These are not archaisms in folklore — they are living registers that mark genre. When Ukrainian poets (Шевченко, Франко, Леся Українка) deploy *ясен, красен, смутен*, they signal folkloric register consciously, as a stylistic choice. State Standard §4.1.1.2 explicitly lists short-form adjectives as C1 mastery. Source: Grade 11 Zabolotnyi p. 188, Ukrainian folk song tradition.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Augmentative overextension from folk register into neutral prose.**
Learners see *вітрище, хатище* in textbooks and apply them freely in formal writing. Error pattern: *"У статті йдеться про проблемище"* (sounds humorous/ironic, not emphatic). Correct: augmentatives in -ище are **marked** — they carry irony, expressiveness, or folk flavor; in academic/official register they are out of place. Fix: teach register-appropriateness as a constraint, not just form.

2. **Imperfective/perfective aspect conflation in historical present.**
Learners use *доконаний вид* in historical present narrative, losing the continuous/processual effect: *"Він увійшов у кімнату і бачив книгу"* (mixes aspects incoherently). The imperfective *бачив/дивився* sustains the narrative frame while the perfective *увійшов* marks event boundaries. Fix: frame imperfective = background/state, perfective = foreground/event — in narrative present tense both must be consistent.

3. **Over-nominalizing in spoken/informal register.**
English-influenced learners (and those who read too much official text) use *здійснення, проведення, забезпечення* in conversation. This is a common stylistic fault even among native speakers in bureaucratic registers. Example error: *"Мета нашої зустрічі — здійснення обговорення"* → correct: *"Ми зібралися, щоб обговорити."* The -ання/-ення nominals are powerful in written formal register but marked as stiff/bureaucratic in speech.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Fемінітиви** (лікарка, дослідниця, авторка): Grade 11 Avramenko §50 raises this directly. At C1 this is a live stylistic/social choice, not just a grammar rule. Include as contemporary word-formation note — это is genuinely Ukrainian (not a calque), reflects current normative shift in Ukrainian.
- **Прийменники задля/для, внаслідок/через**: the plan correctly identifies these as register markers. *Задля* = elevated/purposive (literary, official), *через* = conversational causal. This register distinction is well-established and should be anchored with authentic examples.
- **Synthetic future (кричатиме) vs analytic (буде кричати)**: State Standard §4.1.3.1 lists both as normative at C1. Stylistically: synthetic = literary/elevated, analytic = conversational. This nuance is not widely known among L2 learners.
- **Particle taxonomy**: ж/же (emphasis/contrast), бо (causal/explanatory), ось (deictic), таки (concessive/affirmative) — teach as a mini-system; Grade 7 Avramenko and Grade 10 Karaman both cover particles as a service POS that "надає словам або реченням певних смислових чи емоційно-експресивних відтінків."
- **Decolonized framing**: Ukrainian diminutive richness, short adjective forms in folk registers, and particle use are genuinely Ukrainian phenomena with deep roots in Old Ukrainian and folk tradition — frame them as expressions of Ukrainian linguistic culture, not as "emotional" features contrasted with any other language.
