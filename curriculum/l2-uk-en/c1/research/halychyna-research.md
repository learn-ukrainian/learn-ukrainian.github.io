## Cultural Hooks

1. **Юрій-Франц Кульчицький і кавова традиція** (Wikipedia verified): Руський шляхтич (Ruthenian/Ukrainian nobleman), hero of the Battle of Vienna (1683), is credited with opening one of the first Viennese coffeehouses and popularizing coffee in Europe. The "culture of кава" in Galicia thus connects to a historically documented Ukrainian figure — not a generic Habsburg habit. The plan's "культ кави" section gains authentic Ukrainian grounding here.

2. **ЗУНР: 1918–1923** (Wikipedia verified): The ЗУНР existed de facto 1918–1919 (capital: Lviv), de jure until 1923 — it emerged from the collapse of Austria-Hungary after WWI. Proclamation date: 1 November 1918 (Ukrainian Night in Lviv). This state-building tradition anchors the module's "Боротьба за державність" section with precise dates.

3. **Галичина як регіон** (Wikipedia verified): Three competing territorial definitions exist — (a) medieval Galician Principality boundaries, (b) Habsburg Kingdom of Galicia and Lodomeria (XVIII–XX cent.), (c) post-WWII Ukrainian SSR borders (modern Lviv + Ternopil + Ivano-Frankivsk oblasts, ~55,700 km²). This directly supports the plan's pedagogical goal of "подолання узагальнень" — students must learn that "Галичина" ≠ all of Western Ukraine.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Галичина = вся Західна Україна** → Correct framing: Галичина is one of four distinct Western Ukrainian regions (Галичина, Волинь, Буковина, Закарпаття), each with distinct history and traditions. This is the plan's explicit correction point.

2. **Коліжанка / склеп / диван в академічному тексті** → These Galicianisms cause stylistic dissonance in formal writing: `коліжанка` (not in VESUM standard) → use `колега/подруга` in formal context; `склеп` (store in ґвара) → standard meaning is `склеп` (vault/crypt); `диван` (carpet in ґвара) → standard `диван` = couch/sofa. Learners who have lived in/around Lviv or consumed Galician media are prone to transfer these into formal registers.

3. **Файно / файний в нейтральному тексті** → `файно` (GRAC-verified as adv, regional positive evaluative) sounds strongly dialectal to non-Galician Ukrainians. Learners may adopt it as a charming regionalism but deploy it inappropriately. Correct: teach as **стилістично маркована лексика** with explicit register warning.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **коліжанка flag**: The plan lists it as required vocabulary, but VESUM does not contain it. This is expected — it's a genuine Galician dialectalism. However, content must introduce it with an explicit register warning (не в літературній мові). Do not treat it as standard vocabulary.
- **Кульчицький framing**: Wikipedia identifies him as "руський шляхтич" — this is historical "Rusynian" (forerunner of modern Ukrainian identity), not Russian. Content should clarify this decolonizing detail: this is a Ukrainian cultural figure, not a Polish or Austrian one.
- **ЗУНР abbreviation**: Per §4.1.1.1, declined abbreviations are C1 scope. Content can actively model `ЗУНР**у**` (gen.), `у ЗУНР**і**` (loc.) to reinforce this grammar point organically.
- **Three ethnographic groups (Гуцули / Бойки / Лемки)**: Each has geographically distinct home territory within the Carpathian arc. Do not conflate. Гуцули: eastern Carpathians (Ivano-Frankivsk + Zakarpattia); Бойки: central Carpathians; Лемки: western Carpathians (many deported 1944–47). The Lemko tragedy is culturally sensitive and should be mentioned with appropriate gravity.
- **"Бабця Австрія" as ironic trope**: Treat the Habsburg nostalgia critically — it is a cultural myth that performs identity work, not a historical truth. Content should acknowledge both the genuine civic achievements of the period AND the exploitative aspects of imperial rule (forced conscription, Polonisation pressure). Avoid romanticizing colonialism.
- **Textbook RAG result**: Grade 10 Karaman (§11) provides a solid terminological base for the dialect section: "Територіальний діалект — різновид національної мови, який є засобом спілкування людей, об'єднаних спільністю території." This definition can be quoted or paraphrased to anchor the ґвара discussion academically.
- **Word target**: 4000 words. Recommend 1.4× overshoot target during drafting (~5600 words) given the rich multi-section structure.
