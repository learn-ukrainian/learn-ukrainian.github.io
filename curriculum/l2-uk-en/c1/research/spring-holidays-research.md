## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Great Day" Etymology**: *Великдень* literally means "Great Day". It is believed that on this day the sun does not set but "plays" (сонце грає) at sunrise.
2. **The "Willow" Logic**: Ukraine has no palms for Palm Sunday. We use pussy willow (*верба*) because it is the first tree to bloom in spring. The ritual beating with willow branches ("Не я б'ю — верба б'є") signifies transferring the tree's vitality to the person.
3. **Ancestral Connection**: The week after Easter (*Проводи* or *Гробки*) is essentially a "picnic with the dead" at the cemetery, a pre-Christian tradition of sharing the joyful news of resurrection/spring with deceased ancestors.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Паска vs. Пасха**: Learners often use *Пасха* (Russian influence or reference to Jewish Passover/Pesach). Correct: *Великдень* for the holiday, *паска* for the bread.
2. **Писанка vs. Крашанка**: Confusing the raw, wax-written ritual egg (*писанка* - talisman, not eaten) with the boiled, single-colored egg (*крашанка* - eaten).
3. **Date Confusion**: Mixing up "Catholic Easter" and "Orthodox Easter". Ukrainian tradition follows the Julian calendar (old style) for Paschalia, often differing from the Gregorian date.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Tone**: Respectful but analytical. Explain the *why* behind rituals (e.g., egg = universe/life source).
- **Immersion**: Use authentic terms like *плащаниця*, *всеношна*, *клечання* without over-explaining them in English (C1 level).
- **Syncretism**: Highlight how "Green Holidays" (Pentecost) overlaid the pagan "Rosalia" (Rusalyi) week.
