## Cultural Hooks

**1. "Тіні забутих предків" premiere protest — 4 вересня 1965 (Wikipedia, verified)**
At the premiere in Kyiv's cinema "Україна", literary critic Іван Дзюба addressed the audience from the stage; Василь Стус (postgraduate, Institute of Literature of the Academy of Sciences) and journalist В'ячеслав Чорновіл called from the audience for the public to rise in protest against Soviet arrests of Ukrainian intellectuals. 140 attendees signed a letter of protest. All three were immediately fired from their positions. The film became #1 on the list of 100 best films in Ukrainian cinema history; Harvard University added it to mandatory viewing for advanced cinema studies.

**2. "20 днів у Маріуполі" — Oscar 2024 (Wikipedia, verified)**
Documentary directed by Мстислав Чернов, filmed by AP correspondent team during the Russian siege of Mariupol (February–March 2022). Won Best Documentary Feature at the 2024 Academy Awards — the first Oscar win in Ukrainian history. The film was created with virtually no resources, under fire, with no evacuation route.

**3. Олександр Довженко — world cinema classic (Wikipedia, verified)**
Director of "Земля", "Звенигора", "Арсенал", "Україна в огні". Described in Ukrainian Wikipedia as «класик світового кінематографу». The Brussels 1958 World's Fair list of 12 greatest films of all time included "Земля" (1930) — this is a widely cited historical fact but **not directly confirmed by RAG tools in this session**; flag for content writer to verify against secondary sources.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **вражати + accusative (not locative)** → Correct: *фільм вражає глядача* (not: *вражає на глядача*). Confusion with similar verbs that take locative (наголошувати на чомусь). The plan correctly flags this.

2. **грати роль** (acting) vs **гратися** (playing with toys/games) → *Актор грає роль* / *дитина грається*. Learners calque English "play" and use гратися in professional contexts, or drop the noun роль entirely.

3. **знімати фільм** (authentic) vs **робити кіно** (calque of Russian "делать кино") → Production discourse requires знімати / зняти стрічку, а не "робити кіно". Also: **знімальний майданчик** (not *площадка*); **касові збори** (not *зборки* or *виручка*).

4. **Параджанов spelling**: Some learners write "Паражанов" (Russian-influenced) — correct Ukrainian is Параджанов (Сергій Йосипович).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Protest scene accuracy**: The plan attributes the 1965 protest to three figures — confirmed. However, the plan says "В. Стуса, В. Чорновола та І. Дзюби" — Wikipedia specifies Дзюба spoke from the stage, Чорновіл and Стус acted from inside the audience. This distinction is pedagogically interesting for the protest narrative.
- **"Земля" Brussels 1958**: The plan cites this as a cultural hook. This fact is historically well-established but not verified via RAG in this session. Content writer should not present it as the only source of "Земля"'s greatness — the film's artistic legacy stands independently.
- **Sentsov, Lukich, Aliev**: Plan lists О. Сенцов, А. Лукіч, Н. Алієв as contemporary directors. Oleh Sentsov was imprisoned by Russia (2014–2019) for his filmmaking and activism — this is essential decolonized framing context that must appear in the content.
- **Poetic Cinema framing**: Avoid framing Ukrainian poetic cinema as a "branch" of Soviet cinema. It is an independent Ukrainian aesthetic movement. Dovzhenko's agency was consistently undermined by Soviet censorship (the plan correctly notes this) — the content should affirm his Ukrainian identity, not his Soviet-era titles.
- **Register**: The module targets literary/analytical register (літературний). Рецензія writing in Section 5 should model this register explicitly with vocabulary scaffolding.
- **Word target**: 4000 words. Suggested allocation: Intro 500 / Dovzhenko 800 / Parajanov 1000 / Contemporary 900 / Practice 800 — exactly as in plan. No redistribution needed; the plan is well-balanced.
- **Decolonized angle**: The module's three historical moments (1930s Dovzhenko under Soviet censorship → 1965 Parajanov protest → 2022–2024 wartime documentary) form a coherent arc of Ukrainian cultural resistance. This arc should be made explicit in the introduction.
