# Дослідження: Розвиток наукової тези

## State Standard Reference

**Primary alignment — §4.4.6** (line 4600, C1 level):
> "Використання елементів риторики в композиції тексту."

This is the closest State Standard anchor for thesis development — rhetorical elements including argumentative thesis in academic text composition. The thesis is explicitly a rhetorical compositional instrument.

**Secondary alignment — §4.4.1.1** (lines 4551–4559, C1 level):
> "Стилі української мови: розмовний; офіційний; науковий; публіцистичний; художній; релігійний; епістолярний."

The plan references "§4.4.1.1: теза як смисловий центр та інструмент композиції наукового тексту" — however, at C1, §4.4.1.1 defines *styles* (not thesis). The thesis-as-compositional-center definition does not appear verbatim in this section; it describes the scientific style as one of seven functional styles. The thesis definition should be framed as emerging from §4.4.6 (rhetoric) operating within the scientific style (§4.4.1.1), not as a direct §4.4.1.1 quote.

**§4.4.5** (line 4598): "Уміння створити текст певного стилю за планом." — supports the productive output objective.

**Alignment summary**: Module c1-009 addresses C1 §4.4.6 (rhetoric in composition) and §4.4.5 (producing styled text), contextualised within the scientific substyle of §4.4.1.1. Content writers should not attribute the thesis *definition* to §4.4.1.1 verbatim; rather, cite §4.4.6 for the rhetorical thesis concept.

## Cultural Hooks

**1. Києво-Могилянська академія (1632–1817) as rhetorical centre**

Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia — Києво-Могилянська академія (1632–1817)

Founded by Metropolitan Petro Mohyla, it became one of the largest and most prominent educational institutions in Eastern Europe. In the 17th–18th centuries it was explicitly modelled on Western European Jesuit colleges, with *public disputations* (Latin *theses*) as a central academic practice — students defended propositiones/theses before faculty and public audiences. This is the direct etymological and cultural ancestor of the contemporary Ukrainian наукова теза. The institution was Ukrainian, not Russian — it predates the Russian Academy of Sciences (1724) by nearly a century.

**Decolonization frame**: Soviet historiography minimized the Mohyla Academy's role as a Ukrainian European institution, instead framing it as a "common heritage." Decolonized framing restores it as a specifically Ukrainian institution that transmitted Western rhetorical traditions (thesis defence) into Ukrainian intellectual culture independently.

**2. Інститут української наукової мови ВУАН (ІУНМ, 1921–1930)**

Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia — ІУНМ ВУАН

Founded 1921 under the All-Ukrainian Academy of Sciences; led by academician Agathangel Krymsky. At its peak (1928), the Institute had 223 members including 3 academicians, 44 professors, coordinating 34 terminological dictionaries across natural sciences, technology, law, arts. In 1930, the Soviet regime liquidated it; its director Hryhorii Kholodnyi was sentenced in the SVU case and executed in 1938. Multiple editors were imprisoned or shot.

**Decolonization frame**: Ukrainian academic terminology was an independent, deliberate scholarly project — not borrowed from Russian. The ІУНМ created native Ukrainian equivalents for scientific concepts including logical/argumentative vocabulary. Soviet repression of ІУНМ constitutes direct suppression of Ukrainian scientific epistemology. Contemporary decolonization of academic Ukrainian (post-2022 push) is a direct continuation of the 1920s project.

## Common Learner Errors

**1. Теза-анонс vs. змістовна теза**

Pattern: Learners write announcements masquerading as theses — "У цій роботі я розповім про проблеми екології" — instead of an arguable claim. This is the most universal error at C1.

→ Correct form: "Антропогенне забруднення річкових басейнів України вимагає негайного запровадження міжнародних стандартів моніторингу, оскільки наявна система контролю не відповідає масштабам кризи."

Why it happens: Transfer from essay-planning habit in L1; learners confuse *topic statement* with *thesis statement*.

**2. Circular argumentation (тавтологія)**

Pattern: The thesis and the supporting argument repeat the same idea: "Шевченко є великим поетом, тому що його поезія є геніальною."

→ Correct: thesis requires a *different* proposition as evidence, not a synonym of the thesis itself.

Why it happens: Learners conflate descriptive assertion with arguable claim; тавтологія is a cross-linguistic error pattern (common in English-speaking learners too).

**3. Теза vs. Гіпотеза confusion**

Pattern: Learners use гіпотеза when they mean теза, treating both as "an idea we're exploring." A гіпотеза is a working assumption *before* evidence; a теза is a claim *submitted for proof* or already supported by preliminary evidence.

→ Practical test: if you can write "Ми припускаємо, що..." → гіпотеза. If "Ми стверджуємо, що..." → теза.

Why it happens: English "hypothesis" and "thesis" overlap in informal academic English; Ukrainian maintains a stricter disciplinary distinction.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **§ reference fix**: Do not attribute the thesis *definition* to §4.4.1.1 verbatim. Frame it as: thesis is the rhetorical instrument of text composition (§4.4.6) within the scientific style (§4.4.1.1). The plan's exact quote "§4.4.1.1: теза як смисловий центр" is a pedagogical paraphrase, not a direct standard quote — content writers should not reproduce it as a quotation.

2. **ІУНМ hook is strong**: The repression of 1920s Ukrainian scientific terminology is emotionally resonant and pedagogically relevant — shows that Ukrainian academic language has a deliberate, fought-for history, not a Soviet-era borrowing. Use it in the ethics/history section to motivate careful word choice.

3. **"So what?" test in Ukrainian**: Тест "І що з того?" — learners can apply it by asking after each draft thesis: "Якщо це правда — що це змінює? Що з цього випливає?" If the answer is "nothing specific" → thesis is too weak.

4. **Колокації to inject**: висунути тезу / захистити тезу / підтвердити/спростувати гіпотезу / навести аргумент / обґрунтувати твердження / довести тезу / поставити під сумнів. All VESUM-verified.

5. **Деколонізація**: Avoid framing Ukrainian academic style as "derived from" or "similar to" scholarly norms from any other language. Frame it as: Ukrainian has its own scholarly tradition rooted in the Mohyla Academy (17th c.) and systematised by ІУНМ (1920s), with decolonization post-2022 as a third wave of standardisation.

6. **Word budget guidance**: Total target 4000 words. The section "Практичний інструментарій та аналіз помилок" (900 words) is the most actionable — prioritise rich worked examples (теза-анонс → correct transformation). The historical/ethics section (900 words) benefits from concrete ІУНМ narrative rather than abstract principles.
