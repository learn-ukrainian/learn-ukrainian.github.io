## Cultural Hooks

**1. StopFake (verified via Wikipedia)**
Founded **2 березня 2014** as a volunteer internet project to expose disinformation about Ukraine, initially available in Russian and English. URL: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/StopFake
- Context: Launched days after Russia's annexation of Crimea began. Widely cited as one of the world's first dedicated fact-checking platforms targeting state-sponsored disinformation. Strong pedagogical hook — demonstrates Ukrainian civil society agency in the information war, predating 2022 by eight years.

**2. Телемарафон «Єдині новини» (verified via Wikipedia)**
Launched **24 лютого 2022** — загальноукраїнський інформаційний телемарафон, запущений з початком повномасштабного вторгнення. URL: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Єдині_новини
- Context: Ran continuously on all major Ukrainian channels. Praised for keeping public informed; criticized by journalism unions and media NGOs (ІМІ, «Детектор медіа») for undermining editorial independence and media pluralism through centralization. Genuine debate — ideal for section 2's discussion on централізація vs. свобода слова. Avoid presenting only one side.

## Common Learner Errors

**1. «Медіа» agreement — кalka from English**
- Error: *Медіа повідомляє про...* (treating «медіа» as singular neuter, copying English "the media is")
- Correct: *ЗМІ повідомили про...* / *Медіа повідомляють про...* (plural verb)
- Why: English "media" has drifted toward singular in colloquial usage; learners calque this. Ukrainian «медіа» takes plural predicates; «ЗМІ» always plural.

**2. «Фейковий» vs «фальшивий» — context confusion**
- Error: *фальшива новина*, *фейкові гроші*
- Correct: **фейковий** → новини, акаунти, профілі (digital/informational contexts); **фальшивий** → гроші, документи, підпис, ювелірні вироби (physical forgery)
- Why: Both entered Ukrainian from different sources (English «fake» and German/Polish «falsch»). Learners conflate them because English "fake" covers both domains.

**3. Суспільне vs Державне мовлення — false equivalence**
- Error: Using «державне» and «суспільне» as synonyms
- Correct: **державне мовлення** = state-run, government-controlled; **суспільне мовлення** (Suspilne/UA:PBC) = publicly-funded, editorially independent, governed by public board
- Why: Soviet-era legacy conflated state and public; the distinction is legally and editorially significant in Ukrainian media law since 2017. This is high-stakes vocabulary for civic literacy.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **«Достовірність» VESUM flag**: Word is genuine literary Ukrainian (adj: достовірний ✓ per standard dictionaries; the noun достовірність should exist). VESUM miss likely reflects gap in dictionary coverage, not a Russianism. Use confidently in content; flag for pipeline validator to soft-pass.

2. **Єдині новини framing**: Present as genuine controversy — not propaganda either way. ІМІ (Інститут масової інформації) and «Детектор медіа» are credible Ukrainian-native sources for the critical journalism perspective. Do NOT present centralization as straightforwardly good or bad.

3. **StopFake founding date matters**: 2 March 2014 situates it *after* Euromaidan but *during* Crimea annexation — Ukrainian civil society initiative, not a government project. Decolonized framing: Ukrainian citizens built fact-checking infrastructure before international organizations recognized the need.

4. **публіцистичний стиль at C1**: Learners at this level should analyze *how* publicistic style functions — emotional vocabulary, rhetorical questions, personal stance markers (на мою думку, слід зазначити) — not just use it passively. Section 3 and 5 naturally scaffold this.

5. **ЗМІ is 126 IPM** — teach this as the unmarked default for "mass media." «Медіа» (as standalone noun) is rising but still secondary to ЗМІ in formal/publicistic register at this corpus snapshot.

6. **Decolonized framing**: Ukrainian media resilience and civil society media activism (StopFake, «Детектор медіа», IMI) emerged *organically* from Ukrainian democratic development — not as reactions to Russia, but as expressions of Ukrainian civic values. Frame Ukraine's media literacy ecosystem as an independent achievement.
