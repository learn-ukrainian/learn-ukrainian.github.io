## State Standard Reference
§3.20: "Медіа й соціальні мережі: преса; радіо; телебачення; інтернет і соціальні мережі; незалежність медіа та свобода слова; пропаганда, фейкові новини."
Alignment: This module directly covers all points of §3.20, specifically focusing on the independence of media, freedom of speech, and the identification of propaganda/fake news as required by the C1 standard. It also integrates §4.4.1.1 (publicistic style) and §4.4.4 (text structuring elements) for critical analysis.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Marathon**: The "Yedyni Novyny" (United News) telethon phenomenon during the full-scale invasion – a unique example of centralized media in wartime vs. freedom of speech debates.
2. **StopFake**: Ukraine founded one of the world's first major fact-checking organizations (StopFake) in 2014 to combat Russian propaganda, making Ukraine a global leader in this field.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Calque "медіа" usage**: Using "медіа" as singular (like "media is" in English) when it often functions as a collective plural or requires specific agreement patterns in Ukrainian contexts (though "ЗМІ" is strictly plural). Correction: "Медіа повідомляють" vs "ЗМІ повідомляють".
2. **Confusing "фальшивий" vs "фейковий"**: "Фальшивий" is usually for objects (money, documents), while "фейковий" is specifically for news/accounts.
3. **Government vs State**: Misusing "урядовий" (governmental) vs "державний" (state) when describing media ownership structure (e.g., Suspilne is a *public* broadcaster, not state).

## Notes for Content Writing
- Ensure the distinction between "Державні" (State-owned) and "Суспільні" (Public) media is crystal clear, as this is often confused by foreigners.
- Use the "Standard journalistic standards" (balance of opinions, separation of facts from comments) as a framework for the analysis section.
- Incorporate specific markers of manipulation (clickbait headlines, emotional vocabulary) in the "Critical Analysis" section.
