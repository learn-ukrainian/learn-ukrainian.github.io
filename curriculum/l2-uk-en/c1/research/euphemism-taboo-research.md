## Cultural Hooks
1. **Ancient Taboos**: Historically, Ukrainians avoided naming dangerous animals or spirits directly to summon them. The word *ведмідь* (honey-eater) itself is an ancient euphemism replacing the original Indo-European name for "bear". Similarly, *сірий* (the grey one) for wolf.
2. **The "Last" Superstition**: In modern Ukraine, especially among military, aviators, and extreme sports enthusiasts, there is a strong taboo against using the word *останній* (last), replacing it with *крайній* (extreme/edge) to avoid implying "final/death". However, linguists prescribe *останній* for neutral contexts (e.g., *останній день місяця*), creating a tension between prescriptivism and cultural usage.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Over-politeness**: Using formal religious euphemisms (*упокоївся*) in casual conversation, sounding archaic or overly dramatic.
2. **The "Extreme" Mistake**: Using *крайній* (edge/limit) everywhere instead of *останній* (last) in fear of the taboo, even when referring to neutral sequences (e.g., *крайній вагон* instead of *останній вагон*).
3. **Direct Translation**: Translating English idioms literally (e.g., *sleeping together* -> *спати разом* usually implies literal sleep, whereas *переспати* is the sexual euphemism/dysphemism).
