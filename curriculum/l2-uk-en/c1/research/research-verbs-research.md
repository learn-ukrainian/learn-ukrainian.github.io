# Дослідження: Дієслова наукового дослідження

## State Standard Reference

§4.4.1.1 (lines 4551–4559): "Стилі української мови: розмовний; офіційний; **науковий**; публіцистичний; художній; релігійний; епістолярний."

Alignment: This module develops the lexical foundation of the **науковий** style at C1. The plan's reference to §4.4.1.1 is correctly identified — the Standard names the scientific style as one of seven functional styles at this level. The module operationalises this by building a verb taxonomy for academic discourse: investigation, argumentation, presentation, conclusion. Textbook (Grade 10, Karaman) elaborates: "Основна функція — інформативна (повідомлення, пояснення, з'ясування, обґрунтування, роз'яснення, класифікація понять, аргументований доказ)" — all functions directly map to the module's verb categories.

**Pedagogically significant frequency gap**: `вважати` (35.82 IPM) vs `обґрунтовувати` (0.39 IPM) vs `підсумовувати` (0.12 IPM). The low-frequency items are stylistically marked formal verbs — essential in academic writing but rare in general corpus. This supports explicit teaching rather than incidental acquisition.

**Register contrast**: `розслідувати` (2 VESUM entries — imperfective + perfective) is a genuine Ukrainian verb, but belongs to legal/criminal register. The distinction from `досліджувати` (scientific register) is a high-value teaching point confirmed by corpus evidence.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **НТШ (Наукове товариство імені Шевченка)** — founded 1873, became "рушійна сила формування та розвитку української науки кінця XIX — першої половини XX ст." (Wikipedia). Михайло Грушевський served as head 1897–1913 and was the most prolific contributor; Іван Франко and Іван Пулюй (physicist, first developer of the X-ray tube prototype, one of the first full members of НТШ) co-established the society's standards for Ukrainian scientific terminology. The НТШ published the journal *Записки НТШ* — one of the first Ukrainian-language scientific periodicals — which required contributors to use native Ukrainian lexical forms rather than borrowed bureaucratic Russian calques.

2. **Агатангел Кримський** (1871–1942) — "один з організаторів Академії наук України (1918), мовознавець, дослідник сходознавства, поліглот кримськотатарського походження; жертва сталінського терору" (Wikipedia). Кримський is a key figure for this module because his academic prose actively resisted Russified bureaucratisms (канцеляризми) and demonstrated the richness of native Ukrainian synonymic chains. His arrest in 1941 by Stalin's NKVD is directly tied to Soviet suppression of authentic Ukrainian academic language — making him a powerful decolonization anchor for the cultural context section.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **`рахуємо` → `вважаємо`** — Calque from Polish "rachować" / Russian "считать" (in the opinion sense). `Рахувати` in Ukrainian means arithmetical counting only. "Ми рахуємо, що..." is a hypercorrection trap for English speakers who learned from bilingual (Russian-Ukrainian) sources. Corpus frequency: `вважати` 35.82 IPM confirms it as the overwhelming norm.

2. **`вірно` → `правильно` / `слушно`** (in evaluative contexts) — `Вірно` in standard Ukrainian means "faithfully/loyally" (вірний пес, вірний друг). Its use as "correctly" (вірно зроблений висновок) is a Russianism (`верно` = correctly in Russian). The correct academic forms are `правильно зроблений висновок` or `слушний висновок`. Textbook (Grade 9, Avramenko) lists "точність" as a defining feature of the scientific style — this error directly violates that standard.

3. **`розслідувати` → `досліджувати`** (register confusion) — English "investigate" covers both scientific research and criminal investigation, but Ukrainian lexicalises these separately. L2 learners translating from English often reach for `розслідувати` when writing about scientific inquiry. This is a register error (legal → scientific), not a grammar error — making it harder to catch without explicit instruction.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Frequency data pedagogical framing**: Present `вважати` (35.82 IPM) as the default epistemic verb of Ukrainian academic writing — not a simplification, but the genuine norm. Its high frequency makes "Ми вважаємо, що..." the natural, fluent choice.
- **`підсумовувати` and `обґрунтовувати`** are low-frequency but high-value: signal academic register mastery precisely because general speakers rarely use them. Frame as "маркери академічної майстерності."
- **Textbook anchor** (Grade 10, Karaman, p. 231): The definition of scientific style's informative function lists seven sub-functions: повідомлення, пояснення, **з'ясування**, **обґрунтування**, роз'яснення, класифікація понять, аргументований **доказ** — these map directly onto the module's verb groups and can be cited as authoritative source.
- **Decolonization angle**: НТШ and Кримський represent the *organic* development of Ukrainian academic language before Soviet language engineering. Frame the authentic Ukrainian verbs not as "alternatives to Russian forms" but as the historically established norm that was *suppressed* — they are the original, not the replacement.
- **Agency Pass** framing: Grade 11 textbooks confirm the scientific style in Ukrainian is associated with "логічність, доказовість" — active authorial voice fits this. The passive ("було досліджено") is a Soviet-era convention that current mon.gov.ua guidelines recommend replacing.
- **Total word budget**: 4000 words across 5 sections (500 + 700 + 1000 + 900 + 900). The largest section (Verbs of Research, 1000 words) should include a verb classification table with collocations — this adds density without padding.
