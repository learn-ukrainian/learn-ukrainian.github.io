## Cultural Hooks
1. **Коливо (Кутя):** The ritual dish must be eaten first at the memorial meal. Wheat symbolizes resurrection (grain dies to live), and honey symbolizes the sweetness of eternal life/paradise.
2. **The Threshold:** Knocking the coffin three times against the threshold when leaving the house symbolizes the final farewell to the home and the separation of the worlds of the living and the dead.
3. **Provody (Hrobky):** Unlike the somber mood of the funeral, the "Provody" (week after Easter) is a time of "joyful meeting" with ancestors, where families picnic on graves to share the news of Christ's resurrection.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Confusion of "Пам'ятник" vs "Пам'ять":** Learners often use "пам'ятник" (physical monument) when they mean "пам'ять" (memory/remembrance).
   *Error:* "Він залишив по собі хороший пам'ятник у серцях." -> *Correct:* "Він залишив по собі добру пам'ять."
2. **Preposition usage with "Сумувати":** Learners influenced by Russian might say "сумувати по комусь".
   *Standard:* "Сумувати за кимось" (Yearn for/Miss someone).
3. **Number of "Похорон":** Learners may treat "похорон" as plural (like English "funeral").
   *Correction:* "Похорон" is singular masculine. "Похорони" (plural) is also used but refers to the rites collectively or multiple events. "На похороні" (Singular Locative) is the standard usage for attending one ceremony.
