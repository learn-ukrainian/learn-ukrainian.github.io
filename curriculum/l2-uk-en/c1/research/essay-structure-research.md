## Cultural Hooks

**1. Оксана Забужко — Ukrainian intellectual essay tradition**
Wikipedia confirms: Забужко Оксана Стефанівна — "українська письмениця, феміністська літературознавиця, поетеса, есеїстка, публіцистка." Key works in her essayistic bibliography include *Хроніки від Фортінбраса* (1999), *З мапи книг і людей* (2012), *Планета Полин* (2020). Her 2007 monograph *Notre Dame d'Ukraine: Українка в конфлікті міфологій* is the landmark example of Ukrainian critical-intellectual essay form — a book-length thesis-driven argument about Lesya Ukrainka built with rigorous academic architecture. **Pedagogical use**: demonstrates that Ukrainian essay writing at its highest level is not a loose, impressionistic form but a tightly argued, thesis-driven intellectual project.

**2. Євген Маланюк — "вісниківська" essay tradition**
Wikipedia confirms: Маланюк Євген Филимонович — "український письменник, культуролог-енциклопедист, публіцист, літературний критик, сотник Армії УНР." His *Книга спостережень* (Toronto, 1962; expanded 1966) is the canonical example of Ukrainian political-cultural essay: short, aphoristically structured paragraphs, every sentence earning its place, no filler. As a "вісниківець" (associated with Донцов's journal *Вісник*), Malanuk's prose represents exactly the model of the precise, biting, structurally flawless essay this module teaches. **Pedagogical use**: Section 3 (TEEL) uses Malanuk as a historical illustration of disciplined paragraph structure — each of his paragraphs contains a claim, development, and echo of the larger thesis.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Слабке тематичне речення** → presents a fact ("Шевченко народився у 1814 році") instead of an arguable claim ("Ранні поетичні твори Шевченка демонструють напругу між народною традицією та романтичним індивідуалізмом"). Why: learners conflate "introducing a topic" with "making an argument." Fix: every topic sentence must contain a *claim* that requires proof, not a statement that is simply true.

2. **Відсутній "Link" (зв'язок) у TEEL** → paragraph ends after evidence without returning to the thesis. Why: learners treat paragraphs as self-contained units. At C1 the reader expects every paragraph to visibly advance the thesis. Fix: the final 1-2 sentences of each body paragraph must explicitly reconnect to the main thesis using lexical threading (повтор ключових слів) or explicit transitional language (як це підтверджує наша теза...).

3. **Змішування регістрів** → academic body text contains informal insertions: "я думаю," "це дуже цікаво," "загалом, ця тема класна." Why: students write how they think, not how the register demands. Fix: explicit forbidden/permitted register table: forbidden (я думаю → на моє переконання; дуже цікавий → вартий уваги; класно → переконливо) taught as active substitution drills.

4. **Вступ без чіткої тези** → students write vague contextual paragraphs that never arrive at a specific, arguable thesis statement. The Ukrainian academic tradition tolerates more expansive introductions than English — students mistake this breadth for permission to avoid a thesis altogether. Fix: the thesis statement must appear explicitly in the final sentence(s) of the introduction and be detectable as a single, falsifiable claim.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **TEEL model**: This is an English-language pedagogical framework (Australian/UK schools). Present it as a *practical analytical scaffold* adapted for Ukrainian academic writing — not as an imported foreign model. Frame it as naming something Ukrainian essayists already do (Маланюк's paragraphs are natural TEEL). The Ukrainian metalanguage for the components exists: тематичне речення, пояснення, доказ/підтвердження, зв'язок.
- **когерентність vs зв'язність**: These are near-synonyms in everyday use but technically distinct. Content must draw the distinction clearly: когерентність = смислова/логічна єдність тексту (reader can follow the argument); зв'язність = формальні мовні засоби поєднання (pronouns, connectors, lexical chains). Both VESUM-verified.
- **Register table**: The formal/informal substitution pairs in Section 5 (я думаю → на моє переконання; хорошо → добре) need to include only Ukrainian-authentic academic phrases. "На моє переконання" and "вагомим є" are both verified collocations.
- **Decolonized framing**: Ukrainian has a rich, independent essay tradition (Маланюк, Хвильовий, Костецький, Забужко) that predates and does not derive from Soviet Russian academic prose. The module's Section 1 must establish this positively. Do NOT frame Ukrainian academic style as "different from Russian" — frame it as: the Ukrainian tradition developed through the diaspora intelligentsia (Маланюк, Шерех/Шевельов) and the Kyiv renaissance (Забужко), with distinct aesthetic values: intellectual precision, argumentative rigour, and stylistic integrity.
- **Word target 4000**: Plan allocates 600+700+1000+800+900 = 4000 words exactly. No slack — content writer must hit each section minimum. Section 3 (TEEL anatomy at 1000 words) is the densest — allocate generous examples (model paragraph with TEEL labels, before/after rewrites).
- **Держстандарт §4.4.5 reference in content**: The plan explicitly cites §4.4.5 in Section 2 ("вимоги Держстандарту §4.4.5"). This is accurate — §4.4.5 = "Уміння створити текст певного стилю за планом." Content should cite this naturally to anchor the lesson's authority.
