## Cultural Hooks

1. **Суржик as sociolinguistic phenomenon**: The term «суржик» originally referred to a mixture of wheat and rye grain — a metaphor for impure hybrid. Soviet-era language policy suppressed Ukrainian, creating widespread Ukrainian–Russian contact speech. Post-2014 and especially post-2022, research shows significant language shift toward Ukrainian in urban centres (Kyiv, Kharkiv, Odesa), making суржик an active contemporary issue — not merely historical. The 2019 Language Law (Закон про забезпечення функціонування української мови як державної) created the position of Language Ombudsman (Уповноважений із захисту державної мови). Source: Ukrainian academic sociolinguistics, widely documented.

2. **Ukrainian in the Indo-European classification**: Ukrainian belongs to the East Slavic subgroup of the Slavic branch of Indo-European — alongside Belarusian; Polish is West Slavic, Bulgarian is South Slavic. The Halich-Volhynian chronicle (Галицько-Волинський літопис, 13th century) provides early attestation of features uniquely Ukrainian — demonstrating Ukrainian as an independent literary tradition, not a derived form of any other East Slavic language. This fact is pedagogically significant for decolonized framing.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Compound numeral declension — partial inflection**: Learners inflect only the last component of compound numerals (e.g., *до ста сімдесяти **одного*** → correct, but *до **ста** сімдесяти одного* with ста correct), while forgetting to inflect all components. The State Standard (§4.1.1.3) explicitly requires full paradigm knowledge. Common error: «трьохстах» written as «трьохста» (dropping the -х ending in locative).

2. **Paronyme confusion — ефектний / ефективний**: Textbook source (Avramenko grade 11, p.154–155) confirms this is the canonical example. Learners confuse: «ефектний» (impressive, striking in appearance) vs «ефективний» (efficient, producing results). Also documented: «адрес / адреса» — «адрес» is a formal/official dedication (поетичний адрес, вітальний адрес); «адреса» is a physical or email address. «компанія / кампанія» — «компанія» (group of people, company); «кампанія» (organised campaign/effort).

3. **Pronoun orthography — negative vs indefinite**: Learners merge «ніхто» (negative, written together) with «ні з ким» (negative with preposition, split in three). Similarly, «будь-хто» (indefinite, hyphenated) vs «хтозна-хто» (also hyphenated). The alternation rule: prepositions split the prefix-pronoun compound, inserting themselves between components (ні **з** ким, будь **у** кого).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Checkpoint format**: Module must function as a genuine diagnostic assessment, not just review prose. Each section should present tasks with clear metalinguistic instruction — students must *apply* knowledge, not just read about it.
- **Word target 4000**: With 6 sections (600+800+800+700+600+500), the outline sums to 4000 exactly — no buffer. Phase B should aim for 4400–4500 (1.1x overshoot) to ensure final word count clears the gate after minor edits.
- **Integrated task (Section 6)**: The authentic text chosen for analysis should ideally contain: recognisable functional style markers, at least one paronyme usage, pronoun forms, and a numeral in non-nominative case — enabling all C1.3 competencies to be assessed in one passage.
- **Деколонізована перспектива**: Frame Ukrainian's place in Indo-European classification positively and independently. The bilingualism section should frame Ukrainian as the prestige norm being actively reclaimed — not simply one of two equal codes.
- **Суржик treatment**: Present суржик as a sociolinguistic phenomenon with phonetic, lexical, and grammatical dimensions, not as «bad language» — this is the academic sociolinguistic framing and more pedagogically sound.
- **Avoid**: Russianisms in example sentences: use «отримувати» not «получати», «їсти» not «кушати», «вирушати» not «відправлятися».
