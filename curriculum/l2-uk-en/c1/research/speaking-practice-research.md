## Cultural Hooks

**1. Філіжанка vs. Чашка — Lviv's Sociolinguistic Marker**
The word *філіжанка* (coffee cup, from Polish *filiżanka*) is a well-documented regional lexical marker distinguishing Lviv usage from Kyiv usage (*чашка*). This is cited in Ukrainian sociolinguistics as a classic example of territorial dialectal variation at the lexical level — specifically the Galician urban dialect's Polish-influenced vocabulary versus central Ukrainian norms. It functions as a memorable, concrete entry point into the abstract topic of dialects without any Soviet/Russian framing.

**2. Суржик — Ukrainian sociolinguistic definition (Wikipedia/textbook verified)**
Wikipedia: *"Суржик — елементи двох або кількох мов, об'єднані штучно, без дотримання норм літературної мови. Первісно термін «суржик» вживався переважно щодо українсько-російського суржику."* The term originates from the agricultural term for mixed grain (wheat + rye) — a powerful metaphor for impure mixture. Grade 5 Golub textbook treats it under *Екологія мови* with the slogan "З суржиком ми — покруч, будяки!" Grade 10 Karaman covers it in sociolinguistic and psycholinguistic dimensions. Important decolonized framing: surzhyk arose from Soviet-era forced Russification policies, not from any natural linguistic convergence — resistance to surzhyk is an act of linguistic sovereignty.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **стаж → досвід confusion**: English speakers calque "work experience" as *досвід роботи* in formal documents, but professional Ukrainian requires *стаж роботи* for official records of years of service. *Досвід* covers general experience; *стаж* is the bureaucratic/HR term. → Correction: *У резюме зазначте загальний стаж роботи — п'ять років*, not *\*досвід роботи — п'ять років* (in formal HR context).

2. **Register collapse in formal writing**: Learners mix formal and colloquial registers — inserting *ну*, *давай*, *загалом-то*, *якось так* into job interview or business negotiation language. Ukrainian official register is marked by *Ви*-address, modal constructions (*дозвольте*, *маю честь*, *звертаємося до Вас*), and absence of filler particles. → Correction: Replace *Ну, в принципі, я згоден* with *Загалом, я підтримую цю пропозицію*.

3. **здавати екзамен (Russianism) → складати екзамен**: The verb *здавати* in the sense of "to take/sit an exam" is a direct calque from Russian *сдавать экзамен*. Standard Ukrainian uses *складати іспит/екзамен*. → Correction: *Студенти склали підсумковий іспит*, not *\*здали іспит*.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **This is a checkpoint module** (module_type: checkpoint, focus: checkpoint) — primary function is consolidation and self-assessment, not new content introduction. Content should feel like a structured review + active practice session, not a lecture.
- **"Ви" register is mandatory** throughout the professional communication section — model it in every dialogue and formal writing example.
- **Суржик section**: frame positively (overcoming surzhyk = linguistic empowerment, not shame) — avoid moralizing tone. The decolonized angle: Soviet institutional pressure created surzhyk; reclaiming pure Ukrainian is an act of cultural sovereignty.
- **CEFR C1 oral descriptors** referenced in the plan (fluency, accuracy, lexical range) map to: *плавність* (fluency), *точність* (accuracy), *лексичне розмаїття* (range). Use these as rubric headers in the self-assessment section.
- **Textbook grounding**: Grade 8 Zabolotnyi §topic 11 covers офіційно-діловий стиль with the exact two-form table (written/oral). Grade 9 Avramenko introduces *резюме* as a document type. Both are usable as pedagogical anchors.
- **Word budget alert**: 4000-word minimum; plan allocates 400+1000+900+800+900 = 4000 exactly. No slack. Recommend targeting 4400–4500 in Phase B to ensure gate passes after any editing.
- **Avoid**: naming Russian TV or media as contrast examples for Ukrainian media literacy — use only Ukrainian examples (ТСН, Суспільне, Українська правда, etc.).
