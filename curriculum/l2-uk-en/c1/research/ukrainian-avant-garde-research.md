## Cultural Hooks

1. **Олександра Екстер — «амазонка авангарду»**: Ukrainian artist Oleksandra Exter (born Kyiv, 1882) was described by poet Benedikt Livshyts as «амазонка авангарду» — she became an abstractionist when even her friends Picasso, Braque, and Léger had not yet dared to do so. She pioneered European cubism and futurism and transformed 20th-century theatre design, which was later adopted across Europe and America. Ukrainian Wikipedia explicitly identifies her as «українська художниця греко-єврейського походження» with no Russian attribution. *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Екстер_Олександра_Олександрівна)*

2. **Михайло Бойчук — розстріляний разом з учнями**: Boychuk (1882–1937) founded «бойчукізм» — a unique national artistic language fusing Byzantine traditions, European innovations, and Ukrainian folk art. Although his school did not contradict revolutionary ideals, the Soviet regime executed him together with his wife and students in 1937. His ideas survived through his students («бойчукісти»), who became teachers for the next generation. He is explicitly identified as a «представник Розстріляного відродження». *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Бойчук_Михайло_Львович)*

3. **Malevich's Ukrainian identity**: Ukrainian Wikipedia describes Kazimir Malevich as «український художник-авангардист та вчений польського походження, засновник супрематизму» — explicitly positioning him as Ukrainian, not Russian. Born in Kyiv (1879), he spent his formative years in Ukraine and explicitly acknowledged the influence of Ukrainian folk embroidery and pysanky on his suprematist color system. The standard Soviet framing claiming him as a «Russian artist» is a colonial distortion. *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Малевич_Казимир_Северинович)*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Модерн» vs «модернізм»** → Learners conflate these two distinct styles. «Модерн» (also «сецесія») refers specifically to Art Nouveau / Jugendstil (curved organic forms, ~1890–1910); «модернізм» is the broader 20th-century movement encompassing cubism, futurism, suprematism. Error is amplified because English «modern» maps ambiguously across both. Correct usage: *«Модерн — це архітектурний стиль кінця XIX ст., а авангард — це радикальне відгалуження модернізму XX ст.»*

2. **Russian colonial attribution of artists** → Learners who encountered Malevich or Archipenko in English/Russian-language sources will have learned them as «Russian artists.» This must be explicitly corrected with biographical evidence: Malevich born Kyiv, Archipenko born Kyiv (1887), both self-identified with Ukrainian cultural space. Failure to correct this perpetuates colonial misrepresentation.

3. **«Безпредметний» vs «абстрактний»** → These are not synonyms in Ukrainian art discourse. «Безпредметний» (non-objective) refers specifically to art with no reference to the observable world whatsoever (Malevich's suprematism); «абстрактний» can include abstraction that still hints at recognizable forms. The plan correctly foregrounds «безпредметність» — content writers should treat this distinction as a teaching point, not use the words interchangeably.

## Decolonization Notes

- **Do NOT frame Malevich, Archipenko, Tatlin, or Exter as «Russian artists who worked in Ukraine»** — this is the Soviet/Russian framing. The accurate frame: Ukrainian artists whose work emerged from the Ukrainian cultural matrix of Kyiv, Kharkiv, Odesa, and Lviv.
- Давид Бурлюк: Ukrainian Wikipedia identifies him as «лідер вітчизняного футуризму» — «вітчизняний» (native/homeland) explicitly signals Ukrainian ownership of this tradition.
- The Спецфонд narrative (museum workers hiding «formalist» art) is an important counter-narrative to Soviet destruction — frame it as an act of Ukrainian cultural resistance, not merely a bureaucratic incident.
- Розстріляне відродження directly contextualizes the destruction of avant-garde artists: «знищення українських культурних еліт 1920-30-х сталінським Великим терором» — this is deliberate cultural genocide, not collateral damage.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Word budget allocation check**: Plan has 500 + 900 + 1000 + 1000 + 600 = 4000 words total — meets minimum exactly. Writers should target 4400–4500 to build in buffer for audit gate.
- **Intro section**: The «модерн vs модернізм» disambiguation is a high-value C1 teaching moment — allocate ~80 words to it explicitly.
- **Kyiv Roots section (900 words)**: The richest decolonization opportunity. Must name Kyiv-born dates for Malevich (1879), Archipenko (1887). Exter studied at Kyiv Art School. Include 2–3 specific examples of folk-to-avant-garde visual translation (e.g., Malevich's geometric color blocks ← pysanka patterns).
- **Spetsfond section**: Highly engaging narrative — use present-tense historical narrative («музейники ховають полотна») to build drama. This section benefits from C1 participle constructions (відібрані для знищення, збережені для майбутнього).
- **Practice section**: The comparative essay prompt (Ukrainian vs. international avant-garde) aligns perfectly with §4.4.1.1 художній стиль practice. Provide a model paragraph with epithets and metaphors so learners have a structural scaffold.
- **Boychuk pedagogy note**: His execution in 1937 is historically documented (date, place: Kyiv, shot). This fact should be stated plainly, not euphemized.
