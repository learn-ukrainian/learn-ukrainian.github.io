## Cultural Hooks

**1. Агатангел Кримський (1871–1941) — model of Ukrainian academic prose**
- Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (verified)
- Ukrainian historian, philologist, orientalist, polyglot of Crimean Tatar origin; one of the founders of the Academy of Sciences of Ukraine (1918); researcher of Old Ukrainian language; victim of Stalinist terror (arrested 1941, died in NKVD custody)
- Pedagogical angle: Кримський's scholarly writing — published in Ukrainian at a time when the Tsarist empire discouraged it — demonstrates the intellectual vitality of Ukrainian as a full academic language. His 1898 *Малоруська мова і українська-руська література* is a landmark work of Ukrainian academic prose style.
- Hook: He wrote dozens of scholarly volumes across linguistics, history, and Orientalism — all in Ukrainian. His style exemplifies the impersonal, nominalized academic register this module teaches.

**2. Києво-Могилянська академія (1632–1817) — origin of Ukrainian scholarly language**
- Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (verified)
- Founded by Metropolitan Petro Mohyla; one of the greatest educational centers in Eastern Europe; existed in Kyiv 1632–1817
- Pedagogical angle: Ukrainian academic terminology as an indigenous intellectual tradition — lecture language, theological debates, philosophical treatises. The Academy systematically developed a Ukrainian scholarly register centuries before modern standardization.
- Hook: The very grammar structures in this module (impersonal constructions, nominalization) have roots in the academic language forged at Mohyla Academy — Ukrainian scholarly prose is not an import but a centuries-old living tradition.

## Common Learner Errors

**1. First-person intrusion: «Я думаю...» → «На думку дослідників...» / «Вважається, що...»**
— Why it happens: English academic writing now permits first-person; learners transfer «I think / I argue» directly. Ukrainian academic register requires either impersonal constructions or the inclusive we form: «На нашу думку» is acceptable in Ukrainian academic writing, but bare «Я вважаю» marks the text as informal.

**2. Copula calque: «Це є важливим» → «Це важливо»**
— Why it happens: Calque from English "This is important" / influence of constructions like "це є" that appear in certain Ukrainian registers but are stilted in academic prose. Standard: drop «є» entirely. «Це є важливим» reads as hypercorrection.

**3. Wrong passive construction: «Результати були отримані» → «Результати отримано»**
— Why it happens: Learners import the periphrastic passive (be + past participle) from English or similar constructions. Ukrainian academic style strongly prefers the -но/-то short impersonal form. Glazova Grade 11 (textbook, tier 2) states explicitly: «Українська мова уникає пасивних конструкцій [з -ся]. Тому перевагу, де це можливо, слід надавати [активним або -но/-то формам].» The form «результати були отримані» is marked as calque-like.

**4. Colloquial connectors in academic text: «коротше кажучи», «до речі», «взагалі»**
— Why it happens: These are common spoken discourse markers learners absorb from listening. Academic register requires: «узагальнюючи» / «отже» (instead of «коротше кажучи»); «принагідно зауважимо» (instead of «до речі»); «загалом» over «взагалі».

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **хеджінг terminology:** Use «хеджінг» as a section header/concept label only. In all examples and exercises, use native Ukrainian expressions (модальні конструкції, пом'якшення категоричності). Never ask a learner to inflect «хеджінг».

2. **-но/-то vs. periphrastic passive:** The textbooks (Glazova, Avramenko) confirm -но/-то is the natural Ukrainian academic passive. Content must present «результати отримано» as the PRIMARY form and «результати були отримані» as the non-preferred calque. This is a key decolonization point — the -но/-то form is uniquely Ukrainian, not shared with Russian.

3. **Copula «є»:** The plan correctly identifies «Це є важливим» as a learner error. Content should note this is not archaic or wrong per se — it appears in some formal registers — but in modern academic prose, the zero-copula «Це важливо» is standard.

4. **Кримський as cultural anchor:** Frame him as a polyglot scholar who *chose* Ukrainian as his academic language despite political pressure — this reinforces the module's implicit message that Ukrainian academic language is a conscious, dignified intellectual choice.

5. **Decolonization framing for impersonal constructions:** Ukrainian has its own rich tradition of безособові речення across all styles (scientific, artistic, official). Present these as an indigenous Ukrainian grammatical feature — not as a workaround for lacking personal forms.

6. **Word budget check:** Target 4000 words across 6 sections (450+700+750+650+700+750 = 4000). Sections are equal-weight — no single section is oversized. Recommend 1.3–1.5× overshoot in draft (~5200–6000 words) to ensure target is met after editing.
