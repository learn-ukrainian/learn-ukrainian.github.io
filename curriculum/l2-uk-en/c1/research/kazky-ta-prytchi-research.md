## Cultural Hooks

1. **Котигорошко** (Wikipedia verified): "Хлопчик надзвичайної сили, який народився від горошини та визволив власних братів і сестру з полону Змія." — The archetype encodes the Ukrainian folk motif of latent power in the seemingly weak (горошина → богатир): the common person who defeats supernatural evil. His name literally means "Roll-a-Pea." Cross-cultural analogue: Jack (Jack and the Beanstalk), Thumbelina hero-type. Key decolonization note: Soviet folklorists frequently subordinated Котигорошко to Russian bylina heroes — Ukrainian folklore scholarship reclaims him as an independent East Slavic archetype with distinct symbolic coding.

2. **Григорій Сковорода** (Wikipedia verified): Ukrainian philosopher-mystic, theologian, poet, pedagogue — "Сократом" of Ukrainian philosophy. His prose байки (unlike verse fables by Aesop/La Fontaine tradition) called мораль "силою" ("strength/force") — emphasizing the instructive core as the engine of the text. Key fable: "Бджола та Шершень" — the value of working one's "природжене ділі" (innate vocation). 18th-century figure — predates Romantic Ukrainian nationalism, making his work foundational rather than reactive.

3. **Леонід Глібов** (Wikipedia verified): Ukrainian writer, poet, байкар, publisher, civic figure — Ukraine's canonical fabulist in the Aesopian tradition. His фables feature named animal characters (Лисичка, Щука, Бджола) whose actions reveal human social vices. Grade 6 and 8 textbooks (Zabotnyi, Avramenko) teach him as the primary Ukrainian fabulist. Key distinction from Skovoroda: Hlibov uses verse form and direct moral; Skovoroda uses prose and complex allegorical "сила."

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Literal translation of idioms** → "піймати облизня" — L1 English speaker reads "to catch/grab a lick" literally. Correct: idiomatic meaning = "to fail / come away empty-handed / be rebuffed." Happens because learners decode morphology without recognizing the idiom boundary.

2. **Word order in storytelling formulas** → "Жив-був козак" — English speakers produce *"Козак жив-був"* or *"Один козак жив..."* missing the inverted formula жив-був + subject. The inversion signals narrative register entry; it is not optional stylistic variation but a genre marker. Cf. English "Once upon a time" — non-negotiable formula.

3. **Казка vs. байка confusion** → English "fairy tale/fable" does not map cleanly onto Ukrainian: байка = fable (animal allegory + moral, Aesopian tradition); казка = folk tale (magic, adventure, or social — no necessary moral); притча = parable (philosophical, religious — human actors, abstract truth). Learners collapse all three into "fairy tale" because English lacks distinct terms for байка and притча.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Textbook grounding confirmed**: Grade 5 (Avramenko, Zabotnyi) and Grade 7 (Avramenko) cover казка типологія, structural elements (зачин/кінцівка), трикратні повтори. Grade 8–9 (Zabotnyi, Avramenko) cover Сковорода байки and Глібов байки. Content writer can cite these as pedagogical anchor points.
- **Decolonization priority**: Avoid framing Ukrainian казки as "related to Russian сказки" or noting Slavic common origins (common Soviet framing). Frame independently: Ukrainian казки have their own typological system (три групи: чарівні, про тварин, соціально-побутові) per the Ukrainian literary tradition.
- **Пан Коцький**: Ukrainian tale about a cat whose reputation terrifies a fox; wolf and bear bring tribute out of blind fear — the satire targets performative submission to unearned authority. This is a rich decolonization hook: "страх, що має великі очі" as cultural critique of hierarchical submission.
- **Frequency note**: зачин (0.15 IPM) is genuinely low-frequency literary/structural term — flag for vocabulary table as "literary/structural" register, not everyday speech.
- **§4.4.3 coverage**: Phase B content in Sections 2 and 4 must explicitly cover постійні епітети (constant epithets as a structural folk device, not incidental stylistic choice), metaphors in magical space creation, and звуконаслідування (onomatopoeia in oral storytelling tradition). These are State Standard requirements at C1 level.
- **Word target**: 4000 words — recommend 1.4× overshoot approach (aim ~5500–5800 words in draft). Content outline as specified has adequate section weights (600+900+900+800+800 = 4000); writer should expand each section generously.
