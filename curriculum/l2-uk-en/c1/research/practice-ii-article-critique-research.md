## Cultural Hooks

1. **НТШ (Наукове товариство імені Шевченка), founded 1873** — Ukraine's first modern academic institution, preceding any Russian-empire counterpart in Ukrainian-language science. The НТШ established the practice of *рецензування* (peer review) in Ukrainian academic publishing through its *Записки НТШ* series. The hook: Ukrainian peer-review culture has a 150-year tradition — not a borrowed or derivative practice.
   Source: Wikipedia — Наукове товариство імені Шевченка

2. **«Літературно-науковий вістник» (1898–1932)** — founded on the base of «Зоря», with Ivan Franko as de facto first editor, Mykhailo Hrushevsky as initiator. First all-Ukrainian literary-scientific journal. Franko's role as editor institutionalized rigorous critical review of both literary and scientific texts in Ukrainian — making him the founding model of Ukrainian *фахова критика*. The journal set standards for analytical distance, cited evidence, and objective tone that remain relevant today.
   Source: Wikipedia — Літературно-науковий вістник

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Summary trap** ("Автор стверджує, що...") → correct form uses evaluation verbs that interpret rather than simply report: «Автор *аналізує* / *нехтує* / *перебільшує* / *акцентує увагу* на...». Why: L2 learners default to safe summary because it requires only reading comprehension, not analytical judgement. The module's evaluation-verb section (Section 2) must address this directly.

2. **Personal/emotional register in critique** ("Ця стаття погана" / "Я думаю, що автор помилився") → correct academic form uses impersonal constructions and objective criteria: «Дослідження має певні обмеження», «Методологія потребує уточнення», «У роботі зазначено...» Why: Learners transfer informal opinion-giving patterns to formal academic writing; Ukrainian academic style requires third-person impersonal, which differs sharply from conversational register.

3. **Familiarity in academic reference** (using first names, "Іван Франко сказав") → correct form: «Франко зазначає», «як стверджує Іван Франко у своїй праці...» with full surname at first mention. Why: Academic distance conventions in Ukrainian follow formal-register rules — using first names alone signals student or conversational register. The correction is especially important at C1 where learners are targeting near-native academic production.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The **decolonization angle** is strong and natural here: НТШ was Ukrainian peer-review culture operating in parallel with (and often in resistance to) Russian-imperial academic institutions — frame it positively as an *indigenous* Ukrainian tradition of rigorous critique, not as a reaction against something external.
- The **«repressed terminology» point** (1930s) in vocabulary section merits concrete examples: Soviet-era imposition of Russian-calque academic terms (e.g., кратко → стисло, обосновать → обґрунтувати) displaced authentic Ukrainian scholarly vocabulary. Post-independence restoration of these terms should be presented as recovery of authentic academic voice, not "new" vocabulary.
- The **impersonal construction** set (вважається, доведено, встановлено, у роботі зазначено) should be taught as a register *system*, not individual items — learners need to recognize the pattern, not memorize formulae.
- **Section 3** (methodology evaluation) requires concrete example mini-texts — a sample flawed abstract for learners to critique works better than abstract definitions of репрезентативна вибірка.
- **Peer Review** activity in Section 6 is pedagogically sound — aligns with §4.4.6 (rhetoric in text composition) and produces authentic communicative output. Ensure the review templates in Section 5 are positioned BEFORE the Section 6 workshop so learners have tools before the production task.
