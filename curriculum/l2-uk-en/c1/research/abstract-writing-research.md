§4.4.6 (line 4600): "Використання елементів риторики в композиції тексту."

**Alignment:** This module addresses C1 §4.4.1.1 directly — the *науковий стиль* is the dominant register. The ability to produce abstract/referative text falls under §4.4.5 (creating a text in a prescribed style to plan) and §4.4.6 (rhetoric in text composition). No standalone §entry for *анотація* or *реферат* exists at C1; these are treated as genre-level applications of scientific style mastery.

**Note on C2 connection:** C2 §4.3.8 (lines 5738–5739) explicitly names "анотація, реферат, рецензія, конспект" as *secondary texts* (вторинні тексти). This C1 module introduces the competence that C2 deepens. Frame accordingly — C1 = foundational production; C2 = full mastery.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Науковий стиль — textbook definition (Karaman, Grade 10):** "Науковий стиль української мови... Його основна функція — інформативна... діапазон його використання — наукові праці (дисертації, монографії, статті, доповіді, реферати, рецензії, анотації, тези)." This is the exact genre inventory for the module and grounds the lesson in the textbook canon learners already know.

2. **"Вернадська традиція"** — Volodymyr Vernadsky (Вернадський, 1863–1945), Ukrainian-born naturalist and founder of noosphere theory, wrote prolific scientific Ukrainian that became a model for terminologically precise yet logically fluid academic prose. His *Нариси та промови* (1920s Kyiv VUAN publications) demonstrate the "precision + flow" ideal referenced in the plan. This is an authentic Ukrainian scientific tradition — not a Soviet imposition.

3. **МОН вимоги:** Ukrainian academic publishers follow МОН abstract formatting requirements distinguishing *описова* (up to 500 characters) from *розширена/інформативна* (up to 1800 characters) anотація. The structured format (актуальність → мета → методи → результати → висновки) is codified in ДКС Ukraine standards for scientific periodicals.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Я думаю" / "Мені здається" in abstracts** → Correct: *"Автор вважає"*, *"На думку автора"*, *"У статті стверджується"*. Why: English academic writing permits first-person *I argue*, but Ukrainian academic tradition requires impersonality (*безособовість*). L1 transfer from English is the dominant cause.

2. **"Папір"** (calque of English *paper*) → Correct: *стаття*, *праця*, *дослідження*, *робота* (depending on context). Why: Direct loan-translation from English academic English. Extremely common among Ukrainians trained in international contexts. *Папір* means *paper (material)* in Ukrainian, never an academic publication.

3. **Tense conflation** — Using past tense throughout instead of the correct pattern: present for describing article content (*стаття розглядає*, *автор аналізує*) vs. past for research results (*було виявлено*, *встановлено, що*). Why: English uses simple present for both functions; Ukrainian grammar distinguishes them. Also: overusing passive participles (*розглянутий*, *визначений*) instead of -но/-то forms (*розглянуто*, *визначено*) — the textbook (Avramenko Grade 11, p. 82) explicitly notes that *безособові форми на -но, -то* are preferred over passive participles for stating actions.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **-но/-то forms are the grammatical spine.** The corpus confirms *встановлено* at 52.98 IPM — it's among the most frequent passive forms in Ukrainian. Section 3 (Стилістичні норми) should give extensive -но/-то paradigm: *розглянуто, визначено, встановлено, досліджено, проаналізовано, виявлено, запропоновано, обґрунтовано, показано, підтверджено*.

- **Textbook grounding is strong.** Karaman Grade 10 has explicit Тема 17 (Анотація) and Тема 14 (Реферат) — these are legitimate secondary school topics. Frame the C1 module as *mastery* not *introduction*; learners have seen the genre before but now produce it with academic precision.

- **"Сухий стиль" framing is culturally accurate.** Ukrainian academic tradition genuinely prioritizes *лаконічність та інформативність* over rhetorical flourish — this is not a Soviet imposition but a feature of the scientific style that predates 1917 (evident in VUAN publications of the 1920s). Frame positively as a disciplined Ukrainian genre convention.

- **Distinction анотація vs реферат:** Анотація = brief characterization (100–300 words), tells the reader *what the text is about*. Реферат = detailed summary (1000–3000 words), tells the reader *what the text says and argues*. This is the functional distinction to drill.

- **МОН / ДКС vetting note:** The plan cites `mon.gov.ua` as a source — this is correct for фахові видання abstract formatting requirements. Instructors should note the distinction: *коротка анотація* (≤500 chars for indexing) vs. *розширена анотація* (≤1800 chars for international databases like Scopus/Web of Science). The Scopus/WoS connection motivates why the module matters at C1 academic level.

- **No Russian comparison needed or useful.** Ukrainian impersonality with -но/-то is a native Ukrainian feature codified in pre-Soviet Galician and VUAN literature. Portuguese, Polish, and German academic styles also prefer impersonal/passive constructions — use these as comparanda if cross-linguistic framing is needed.
