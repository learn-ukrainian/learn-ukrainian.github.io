# Дослідження: Жанр — Анотація та реферат

## State Standard Reference
§1.3.1 (Письмо): "писати огляди та анотації до текстів із професійної чи загальнокультурної сфери;"
§1.3.2 (Типи текстів): "реферат, доповідь, повідомлення на теми зі сфери професійних чи особистих зацікавлень;"
Alignment: This module directly teaches the skill of writing "анотація" and "реферат" as explicitly required for C1 writing competence.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Vernadsky Tradition"**: Ukrainian academic writing values precision but also logical flow, influenced by the founders of the Academy of Sciences like Volodymyr Vernadsky.
2. **"Dry" vs. "Juicy"**: Unlike some Western abstracts that might use a "hook", traditional Ukrainian abstracts are strictly informative and "dry" (сухий стиль), focusing purely on the contribution.
3. **Impersonal Construction**: The heavy use of "розглянуто", "визначено" reflects a cultural humility in science—the facts speak, not the author (though this is slowly modernizing).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Calque "Paper"**: Using "папір" instead of "стаття" or "робота" in academic context.
2. **Subjectivity**: Using phrases like "Я думаю", "Мені здається" in an abstract (should be "Автор вважає", "На думку автора").
3. **Tense Confusion**: Mixing past ("дослідив") and present ("досліджує") randomly. Abstracts usually describe the paper in present tense ("стаття розглядає") or the completed research in past ("було виявлено").

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Stylistic Balance**: While teaching the "impersonal" academic style required for abstracts, maintain the "Agency Pass" in the instructional voice. E.g., "Ви напишете..." (active) instructing them to write "Було написано..." (passive/impersonal for the abstract).
- **Differentiation**: Clearly distinguish "анотація" (brief, often for databases) vs "реферат" (longer, informative summary of content).
- **Standardization**: Emphasize that Ukrainian journals have strict requirements (DKS standards) for abstract length and structure.
