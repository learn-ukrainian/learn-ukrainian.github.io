## Cultural Hooks

1. **Феофан Прокопович і Києво-Могилянська академія**: Прокопович (1677–1736) was rector of the Kyiv-Mohyla Academy (1710–1716), one of the greatest learning centres in Eastern Europe (founded 1632 by Metropolitan Petro Mohyla). He authored a substantial Latin-language rhetoric course (*De arte rhetorica*) at the Academy — making the KMA a direct historical source of the Ukrainian rhetorical tradition, centuries before modern Ukrainian statehood. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Феофан_Прокопович; uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Києво-Могилянська_академія_(1632—1817).

2. **TEDxKyiv та IT Arena**: TEDxKyiv (active since 2010) and IT Arena (Lviv, annual IT conference) are the two most prominent contemporary Ukrainian platforms modelling internationally-competitive public speaking norms in Ukrainian. Both demonstrate how Ukrainian has organically developed a modern oral academic register — speakers code-switch minimally and use Ukrainian signposting vocabulary naturally. This anchors the cultural relevance for C1 learners bridging academic to professional contexts.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"на слідуючому слайді"** → **"на наступному слайді"** — Russian calque (*на следующем*). «Слідуючий» does not exist as a standard Ukrainian adjective in this sense; «наступний» is the norm. Very frequent in Ukrainian presentations by L1 Russian speakers.

2. **"Я маю питання"** → **"Дозвольте запитати" / "У мене є запитання"** — «Я маю питання» is a direct calque of Russian *у меня есть вопрос* rephrased into pseudo-Ukrainian. The Ukrainian etiquette phrase uses дієслово «дозволити» (permission request) or the existential construction «є запитання». Note also: «питання» (issue/question in a broader sense) vs «запитання» (a question directed at someone) — this distinction is pedagogically valuable.

3. **"презентувати про проект"** → **"презентувати проект"** — The preposition «про» is inserted by analogy with «розповідати про». The verb «презентувати» governs the accusative directly: *презентувати [що?]* — no preposition. Same pattern: «представити проект», «демонструвати результати».

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Register distinction is the core pedagogical payload**: the gap between written «доповідь» (formal, read/delivered from text) and oral «виступ» (spontaneous/prepared speech) must be made explicit early in the module. Ukrainian professional norms treat these differently.
- **Signposting phrases** (Перейдімо до наступної тези; Варто звернути увагу; Як ми бачимо з попереднього розділу) are the main productive output target — Phase B should include a rich set of 15+ graded examples across formality levels.
- **Ораторський синтаксис** section needs concrete contrasts: written Ukrainian allows long subordinate chains (40+ word sentences); oral Ukrainian for presentations favours 15–20 word active-voice sentences. Include side-by-side rewrites.
- **KMA rhetorical tradition framing**: open the module with Прокопович as the historical anchor, pivot to TEDxKyiv/IT Arena as the contemporary continuation — this gives decolonized framing that traces Ukrainian rhetoric as an independent tradition, not a derivative of Soviet/Russian public speaking norms.
- **Decolonized framing reminder**: Soviet-era Ukrainian public speaking was heavily influenced by Russian oratory conventions (heavy nominalization, passive voice, impersonal constructions). Modern Ukrainian oral style consciously moves toward direct, active, shorter sentences — frame this as a positive development of Ukrainian's own oral tradition, not as borrowing from English/Western norms.
- **Graph/chart language** (секторна діаграма, стовпчикова діаграма, динаміка зростає/спадає) should be presented as a sub-register of науковий стиль — link to §4.4.1.1 (стилі мови) which students studied earlier.
- **Word budget note**: §§ on signposting (1000 words) and audience interaction (700 words) carry the heaviest productive load — Phase B content writer should prioritize dialogue-style examples and scripts in those sections.
