**Alignment:** Module c1-012 directly addresses the **науковий** стиль listed under §4.4.1.1 and the production competence under §4.4.5. The IMRaD structure, impersonal constructions ("У статті проаналізовано"), and genre-specific markers (анотація, ключові слова, список літератури) all operationalise the scientific style at C1 production level. The module also touches §4.4.4 (structural markers: вставні слова, еліпсис) as deployed in academic writing.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Іван Пулюй (1845–1918)** — Ukrainian physicist and electrical engineer; first developer of the X-ray tube (prototype of modern X-ray apparatus); professor and rector of the German Technical University in Prague. Established fundamental properties of cathode and X-rays. One of the first full members of the Shevchenko Scientific Society (НТШ). His international scientific correspondence (written in German and French) and published articles in European journals preceded Röntgen's 1895 announcement — making him an authentic example of a Ukrainian scientist who mastered international academic writing conventions. *Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia, Пулюй Іван Павлович.*

2. **Наукові фахові видання України** — In Ukraine, a "наукове фахове видання" is a periodical registered by МОН Ukraine's Department of Personnel Attestation, in which results of doctoral research may be published. As of 2017, the list comprised 1,546 print journals/collections and 90 electronic publications, divided into Категорія А (indexed in international databases) and Категорія Б (national registry). The role of НБУВ ім. Вернадського as the central scientific information hub makes it a recognisable institutional anchor for learners. *Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia, Наукове фахове видання (Україна).*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"абстракт" замість "анотація"** — Calque from English "abstract." Correct Ukrainian: *анотація* (коротке резюме статті). The word "абстракт" exists in Ukrainian but means "abstract art" or a philosophical concept — using it for the article summary is a false-friend error rooted in uncritical borrowing.

2. **"папір" замість "стаття/робота"** — Calque from English "paper" (as in research paper). Learned via English-medium academic environments; learners in scientific fields often write "наукові папери." Correct forms: *наукова стаття*, *наукова робота*, *публікація*.

3. **Я-конструкції в науковому тексті** ("Я вважаю", "Я дослідив") — Transfer from conversational or essay register. Ukrainian academic writing requires impersonal constructions: *У статті проаналізовано*, *Встановлено*, *Доведено*, *Автор зазначає*. Textbooks (Karaman, Grade 10) explicitly mark this as a feature distinguishing науковий стиль from розмовний.

## Textbook Source Notes

**Karaman, Grade 10 (tier 2)** is the primary textbook source for this topic:
- Тема 9: "Науковий стиль, сфери використання і призначення" — defines scientific style function (informative), characteristics (терміни, facts, complex sentences, precision, logic), and compositional-architectural features (documentation of claims via citations and references).
- Тема 10: "Основні жанри наукового стилю" — lists genres (дисертації, монографії, статті, доповіді, реферати, рецензії, анотації, тези) and gives structural guidance for наукові повідомлення: three-part structure (вступ → основна частина → висновок), formulation of the наукова проблема in the introduction.
- **Content writing note:** Karaman provides the Ukrainian-language description of scientific style that serves as an authentic model. Quotes from it should be used as reading excerpts in activities.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **IMRaD framing is international, not Ukrainian-specific** — present it as a global academic convention that Ukrainian науковці use when publishing internationally, while Ukrainian-language journals (especially older "Вісники") may still use the older three-part or five-part article structure. This dual awareness (international IMRaD vs. domestic tradition) is pedagogically authentic.

2. **"Пісочний годинник" metaphor** (hourglass model) is the standard pedagogical device for IMRaD: Introduction starts broad (context) → narrows to specific methods → Results are narrow empirical findings → Discussion and Conclusions open back out to implications. Use this visual metaphor explicitly.

3. **ДСТУ 8302:2015** is the mandatory Ukrainian standard for bibliographic references (confirmed by Wikipedia article on Бібліографічне посилання). It is required for all фахові видання Категорії А та Б. Content should show at least 2–3 formatted examples.

4. **Register contract for безособовий стиль:** The pattern Verb-but-no-subject (past passive participle + -о/-но/-то) is the core morphosyntactic feature: *проаналізовано*, *встановлено*, *виявлено*, *доведено*, *описано*. This should be systematically exemplified across all IMRaD sections.

5. **Decolonization note:** The Soviet academic tradition enforced Russian as the language of science; Ukrainian-language scholarly publishing was suppressed from the 1930s onward (Розстріляне відродження). Frame the current MОН requirements and the НТШ tradition (going back to Franko, Pулюй, Hrushevsky) as the authentic Ukrainian scholarly lineage — not as a recent imposition.
