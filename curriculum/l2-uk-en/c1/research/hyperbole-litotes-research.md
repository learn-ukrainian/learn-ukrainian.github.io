## Cultural Hooks

1. **Козацькі думи as the epicentre of Ukrainian hyperbole.** Думи are a genre of Ukrainian heroic epic (oral lyric-epic, performed by кобзарі, бандуристи, лірники in Left-Bank Ukraine, XVI–XVIII centuries) — a purely Ukrainian genre with no Russian/Slavic parallel in form or performance tradition (Wikipedia: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Дума). The genre's defining feature is heroic exaggeration: a single warrior defeating entire armies ("не стільки сам бив, скільки конем топтав" — attested Grade 8 textbook, Avramenko 2025). This is the cultural foundation for Section 2.

2. **«Енеїда» Котляревського (1798) as burl­esque hyperbole.** Wikipedia confirms: *«Енеїда» — українська бурлескно-травестійна поема* (6 parts, ottava rima in iambic tetrameter). Kotlyarevsky's парод­ія deliberately inflates and deflates scale (heroic + comic registers in the same text) — this makes it the canonical textbook example of **both** artistic hyperbole and ironic litotes co-existing in one work. The burlesque mechanism is exactly the "play of scale" the plan foregrounds.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Calque from English "I'm dying / I'm starving" → *Я вмираю / Я помираю*** — direct translation produces unidiomatic and morbid Ukrainian. Correct mapping: hunger → *Кишка кишці кулешу варить* or *Я голодний як вовк*; exhaustion → *з ніг збився*, *падаю від утоми*. Root cause: English speakers reach for the English hyperbole template and transfer it morpheme-by-morpheme.

2. **Confusing *непоганий* (litotes) with weak praise** — learners read *непоганий результат* as neutral/lukewarm rather than recognising it as understated approval. In Ukrainian professional and polite discourse, the double-negation form often signals a *higher* evaluation than a direct *хороший*. Context (prosody, register, speaker relationship) is decisive — a common gap for L2 learners trained on dictionary glosses.

3. **Over-using book-register hyperboles in spoken contexts** — *море сліз*, *чекати цілу вічність* are stylistically marked as literary/publicistic. Using them in casual spoken Ukrainian sounds stilted or ironic. Learners need explicit register instruction: know the hyperbole, know when *not* to deploy it.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Textbook definition (Grade 8, Avramenko 2025):** *"Гіпербола — художній засіб, що полягає в навмисному перебільшенні з метою посилення виразності."* Use this as the definitional anchor; contrast immediately with *літота — применшення якої-небудь ознаки предмета чи людини* (same source). These are the canonical school definitions learners have already encountered — the C1 module should *extend* them into stylistic analysis, not re-teach the basics.
- **Suffix connection (§4.4.3 hook):** The Standard explicitly lists *вітер – вітрище* (suffix -ищ-) and *великий – величезний* (suffix -езн-/-енн-) as stylistic lexical devices. Phase B should exploit this: these suffixes ARE morphological hyperbole, linking grammar to rhetoric.
- **Burlesque register in Котляревський:** The *Енеїда* works because it holds both registers simultaneously — the heroes are genuinely heroic AND comic. This is the advanced C1 insight: scale manipulation is always register-conscious.
- **Frequency asymmetry matters pedagogically:** Teach *перебільшення/применшення* as the register-neutral descriptors; teach *гіпербола/літота* as the rhetorical metalanguage. Learners need both layers.
- **Decolonization note:** Ukrainian oral epic (думи) is a wholly autonomous tradition — performed by кобзарі in a genre with no Russian equivalent. Frame it positively as a uniquely Ukrainian cultural form rather than in relation to bylyny. The textbook source (Avramenko Grade 8) uses билини only as a contrast example for Rus-period history; Ukrainian думи are thematically and formally distinct.
