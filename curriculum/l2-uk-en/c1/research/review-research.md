## Cultural Hooks

1. **Бізнес-етикет: від радянської до європейської норми звертання.** In Soviet-era Ukraine, formal address universally used ім'я + по батькові (e.g., Іване Петровичу). Since independence — and accelerating sharply after 2014 and 2022 — Ukrainian IT companies (EPAM Ukraine, Grammarly, MacPaw, Ajax Systems) and creative industries widely adopted the European/American norm Пан/Пані + ім'я or simply first-name Ти within flat hierarchies. This generational shift is observable and debated: conservative professional sectors (law, government, academia) retain по батькові, while tech, media, and NGOs have largely moved to first-name or Пан/Пані + ім'я. Source: uk.wikipedia.org + widely reported in Ukrainian business press.

2. **Закон про мову (2019) і ринок праці.** Ukraine's Law No. 2704-VIII ("On Ensuring the Functioning of Ukrainian as the State Language," 2019) mandates Ukrainian as the language of official communications, services, and the workplace. This created practical demand for high-register professional Ukrainian, directly motivating the vocabulary covered in M21–M33 (CV writing, business correspondence, workplace terms). The law is culturally significant as a marker of Ukrainian civic identity and decolonization — not merely a bureaucratic requirement. Source: Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **аплайтися → подаватися на вакансію** — English-Ukrainian calque; аплайтися is unattested in VESUM (non-standard). Learners who work in IT or are exposed to English frequently import this verb. Correct forms: подаватися на вакансію / на посаду; надсилати заявку.

2. **мітинг (у значенні наради) → нарада / зустріч** — мітинг is a legitimate Ukrainian word but means a political rally/demonstration. Using it for a workplace meeting is a semantic calque from English "meeting." Drill distinction: мітинг на площі (political rally) vs. нарада у відділі (staff meeting) vs. ділова зустріч (business meeting).

3. **Паронімічна плутанина: робочий / робітничий** — робочий = pertaining to work-as-activity (робочий час, робочий стіл, робоче місце); робітничий = pertaining to the working class as a social category (робітничий клас, робітничий рух, робітнича солідарність). Learners conflate these because both derive from робота. Минімальні пари drill is appropriate.

4. **Помилки у звертанні: Ви vs Ти + Пан/Пані** — Learners often mix register layers: saying "Пане Іване, ти можеш..." is a violation of register coherence; Пан/Пані requires Ви. Conversely, switching to Ти without invitation in a formal context is a register breach. The module's error-analysis section should include contrastive dialogue pairs.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Word budget priority**: The review structure covers 6 sections with a 4000-word target. The two professional sections (Огляд, Бізнес-етикет/CV) together budget 1400 words — content writer should aim for 1500+ to allow for 1.5× overshoot. The error-analysis section (700 words) can expand with additional minimal pairs and correction drills.
- **Suzhyk framing**: Always frame суржик as a sociolinguistic phenomenon with historical roots in Soviet linguistic policy, not as a personal failing. The Soviet state actively suppressed Ukrainian, creating conditions for mixed speech. Decolonized framing acknowledges structural causes.
- **CEFR self-assessment**: The final section on CEFR C1 criteria should use the official Ukrainian CEFR descriptors. Avoid framing "readiness for C1.2 Checkpoint" as a pass/fail anxiety moment — frame it as a moment of reflection and agency.
- **Паронімічний drill (робочий/робітничий)**: Confirmed both words exist in VESUM and GRAC. робочий has very high corpus frequency (collocations: робоче місце, робоча група, робочий день). робітничий is moderate but clearly distinct (collocations: робітничий клас, робітнича партія, робітнича рада). This drill is well-supported.
- **Calque correction activities**: аплайтися is absent from VESUM — this is valid as a "ghost word" example. мітинг is in VESUM but must be taught with its correct Ukrainian meaning (political rally) vs. нарада for professional meetings.
- **Style §4.4.1.1 anchor**: The sociolinguistics section ties directly to the upcoming C1.3 Movoznavstvo block (styles of Ukrainian). Brief preview of офіційний, науковий, публіцистичний styles is appropriate here as a bridge.
