## Cultural Hooks

1. **Вишиванка як «код нації»** (Wikipedia, uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Вишиванка): The term «вишиванка» is actually a 20th-century neologism — the traditional names were «сорочка», «вишита сорочка», «вишивана сорочка». The embroidery has protective (оберегове) meaning and varies significantly by region. This is an excellent hook: the word students know best («вишиванка») is the newest name, inviting discussion of how naming itself is a cultural act.

2. **Всесвітній день вишиванки** (Wikipedia, uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Всесвітній_день_вишиванки): Held every third Thursday of May; initiated by Ukrainian students in 2006 in Chernivtsi; intentionally unattached to any state or religious holiday — making it a grassroots decolonization symbol. It has spread to Ukrainian diaspora communities worldwide.

3. **Плахта vs. запаска distinction** (Wikipedia, Українське національне вбрання, §39): The плахта was a 4-metre checkered celebratory wrap; the запаска was everyday wear. Married women wore both (front and back); unmarried women wore only the чорна запаска. This layering system reflects social-semiotic coding that maps directly onto the plan's embroidery-as-passport concept.

4. **Регіональні відмінності засвідчені документально**: The 1598 Venetian catalogue by Cesare Vecellio described Ukrainian (Ruthenian) male costume; 19th-century ethnographer descriptions show Polissia, Slobozhanshchyna, Volyn, Podillia, Halychyna, and Hutsul regions had distinctly different silhouettes, color systems, and headdress conventions. This grounds the plan's pedagogical objective on regional variation in primary-source documentation.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Юбка» / «рубашка» замість «спідниця» / «сорочка»** → Correct: спідниця, сорочка. These Russianisms are confirmed absent from VESUM. Learners at C1 level often retain them from earlier Russian exposure; the module's explicit correction section (Section 4) must emphasize these are not stylistic variants — «юбка» and «рубашка» do not exist in literary Ukrainian.

2. **«Вишиванка» = вишита сорочка (confusion of вишиванка / вишивка / вишита сорочка)** → Correct disambiguation: «вишивка» = the embroidery itself (result/process noun); «вишиванка» = the embroidered garment (product noun, modern term); «вишита сорочка» = the traditional name. Using «вишиванка» as a general term for all embroidery is a metonymic error common among L2 learners.

3. **Overgeneralization of «шаровари» to all Ukrainian male peasant clothing** → The plan explicitly targets the «шароварщина» myth. Historically, шаровари were a Cossack status symbol; rural men wore narrow полотняні штани (гачі/порти). Learners (and Soviet-era caricatures) impose шаровари as universal peasant wear — this is historically inaccurate and connects to the folklorization/infantilization of Ukrainian culture under Soviet rule.

4. **«Розпоясатися» as active vocabulary** → This verb is not found in VESUM or GRAC corpus (0 occurrences). Phase B should restructure this to a cultural-etymological discussion: explore «пояс» as the culturally loaded noun (its spiritual symbolism as a boundary between «верх» and «низ») rather than presenting the derived verb as active vocabulary. Consider «знімати пояс», «пояс як оберіг» as safer collocations.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **«Згарди» flag**: VESUM returns no result. This is a real Hutsul term for a multi-strand copper/brass bead ornament, but it may be too dialectal for VESUM coverage. Use it with a brief gloss ("гуцульська прикраса зі скляних або металевих намистин") and do not list it as active production vocabulary.

- **Decolonized framing**: The plan's «шароварщина» section is an explicit decolonization task — Soviet and Russian sources systematically reduced Ukrainian identity to шаровари + вишиванка as caricature. The module should name this directly. Key term «шароварщина» is in VESUM, which validates its use as a critical concept.

- **Покривання ritual**: The Wikipedia source documents that married women were forbidden to show hair publicly (табу). This is a strong ethnographic anchor for the headdress vocabulary chain: вінок → очіпок → намітка. Frame it as a rite of passage, not a restriction.

- **Pояс symbolism**: The belt/sash (пояс, крайка) was not merely decorative — it was the oldest garment element, worn by both men and women, functioning as a magical boundary and social marker. The verb «розпоясатися» doesn't appear in modern corpora, but the cultural concept is powerful and should appear as a cultural note with the etymological connection made explicit, not as active vocabulary.

- **Wikipedia confirmed**: Цезаре Вечелло (1598) gives a documented early external description of Ukrainian dress. This is a useful historical anchor for the introduction section.

- **Word target**: 4,000 words. Section allocations in plan total: 600+1000+900+700+800 = 4,000 words — exactly on target. No headroom for overshoot is built in; Phase B should aim for 4,200–4,500 to pass the gate confidently.
