## State Standard Reference

**§4.3.2** (C1 Syntax, lines 4488–4500): "просте речення зі вставними словами: *Завтра в мене, знаєш, важливі справи*"
— Direct grammar scope: parenthetical words as syntactic complication of simple sentences at C1.

**§4.4.4** (C1 Stylistics, lines 4585–4596): "елементи структурування тексту: вставні слова *по-перше, по-друге, по-третє, отже* та ін."
— Rhetorical/structural dimension: parentheticals as text-organisation devices.

**§4.4.1.1** (C1 Stylistics, lines 4551–4559): Lists науковий стиль as one of seven Ukrainian functional styles — the register frame for the entire module.

**Alignment**: The module sits at the intersection of three Standard requirements. §4.3.2 covers the syntax of вставні слова (comma punctuation, distinction from particles); §4.4.4 covers their rhetorical use as text-structuring elements; §4.4.1.1 anchors them in the науковий стиль register. The plan's focus on epistemic gradation and hedging is the C1 extension of foundational вставні слова work introduced in Grade 5–8 textbooks.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ukrainian modality — Wikipedia (uk.wikipedia.org)**: "Модальність — це функціонально-семантична категорія, яка виражає відношення змісту висловлювання до дійсності, тобто це ставлення мовця до змісту висловлювання." Useful as a precise Ukrainian-language definition to anchor the opening section. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Модальність_(мовознавство).

2. **Folk proverb angle (plan-referenced)**: The plan cites "Може, дощ, а може, сніг" as a cultural hook for the Ukrainian philosophy of epistemic openness. This proverb reflects a genuine Ukrainian pragmatic preference for leaving propositional commitment flexible in informal speech — a cultural pattern that makes hedging feel natural rather than evasive. No Wikipedia article for this specific proverb, but it can be grounded in the contrast: informal "може" (93.99 IPM) vs. formal "ймовірно" (37.29 IPM).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"навряд чи" с комами** → без ком — Learners pattern-match on the rule "вставні слова виділяються комами" and apply it to "навряд чи", which is a modal particle, not a parenthetical. Textbook Grade 8 (Zabolotnyi) distinguishes these categories; the plan correctly flags this as a C1-level point.

2. **"нажаль" (одним словом)** → "на жаль" (завжди окремо) — High-frequency error. "На жаль" is a prepositional phrase functioning as a вставне слово, not a fused adverb. Grade 8 (Zabolotnyi §38) uses "на жаль" explicitly in exercises, making it a recoverable textbook reference.

3. **"я думаю" як академічний маркер** → "на мою думку", "вважаю", "переконаний", "припускаю" — Direct calque from English academic writing ("I think"). At C1 academic register, "я думаю" marks a text as conversational. The expansion of authorial stance phrases is a core skill gap at this level.

4. **"однак" без урахування позиції** → Conjunction at sentence start (no comma before following clause) vs. вставне слово in mid-sentence (set off by commas on both sides). Example: *"Однак результати різняться"* (conjunction) vs. *"Результати, однак, різняться"* (parenthetical). This position-dependent punctuation distinction is a key C1 accuracy point.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Frequency-based pedagogy**: The IPM data supports a natural certainty scale: мабуть (93.99) > очевидно (48.16) > ймовірно (37.29) > навряд (33.01) > безперечно (14.79) > безсумнівно (3.56). Present this as a corpus-grounded scale, not an arbitrary list.
- **Textbook grounding**: Grade 5 (Avramenko §75-76) and Grade 8 (Zabolotnyi §38) are the foundational treatments of вставні слова. C1 content should explicitly build on these: learners already know the comma rule and basic groups — the module extends to epistemic gradation, academic register, and the particle/parenthetical distinction.
- **Register contrast is the core pedagogy**: The module's central move is demonstrating that the same semantic content (uncertainty, probability) is expressed differently in conversational Ukrainian (мабуть/може) vs. academic Ukrainian (ймовірно/припускаємо/є підстави вважати). Ground every example in this contrast.
- **"Голослівність" as persona anchor**: The Аналітик ризиків persona's concern with "голослівність" (unsubstantiated claims) is linguistically precise — the term appears in VESUM and is appropriate for the academic register.
- **Decolonized framing note**: Ukrainian epistemic modality has its own rich system (вставні слова by semantic group, modal particles, conditional mood) — describe it on its own terms. The IPM data from GRAC (Ukrainian corpus, 2B tokens) is sufficient authority. No need to reference any other language system.
