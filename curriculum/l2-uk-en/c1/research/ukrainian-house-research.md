## Cultural Hooks

**1. Сволок як «паспорт хати» (Wikipedia → Українська хата, §Гуцульщина)**
On the Hutsul region, beams (svolok) were decorated with carved details (різьба): "оздоблювали різьбою окремі деталі (галерейки, одвірки, сволоки)." In general practice, the main beam recorded the house's founding date and owner names — functioning as a legal and spiritual registration document. This "passport of the house" concept is a powerful C1 cultural hook with no equivalent in English-language housing culture.

**2. Магічний захист через колір і розпис (Wikipedia → §Типи українського житла)**
> "Вірили, що тільки розмальована хата може бути надійним захистом від злих сил. Вогонь і його відповідник — червоний колір, вважали надійними захисниками від будь-якого лиха. Червоною смугою вище долівки обводили стіни."

A red line painted above the dolіvka level encircled the house walls — a closed circle meant to protect from evil forces. Similarly, colorful wall paintings (настінний розпис) were believed to attract the attention of evil spirits, distracting them from harming the inhabitants. This ritual logic connects architectural aesthetics directly to cosmological protection — ideal material for the "Хата як мікрокосм" opening section.

**3. Ритуал вибору місця (Wikipedia → §Звичаї, вірування)**
Before building, the site was divined: grain was sown — if it grew well, the site was suitable. Bread, salt, grain, and water were left overnight; if undisturbed and the water level rose, the omen was positive. A closed metal or clay vessel with wool was placed on the site: if the wool became damp by morning, the house too would be damp. Quote from Wikipedia: "Закінчуючи будівництво, майстри обов'язково залишали непокритою частину даху над сіньми, вважаючи, що через цей отвір мусить вилетіти все зло."

**4. Regional diversity falsifies the "single uniform hata" myth (Wikipedia §Типи)**
The dominant 19th-century housing type was the three-room hata (хата + сіни + комора). Two major wall-construction systems existed: **зрубний** (log frame — Polissia, Carpathians) and **каркасний/мазанка** (wattle-and-daub framework — forest-steppe, steppe). This structural diversity directly underpins the plan's regional section. Key textbook source (Grade 7, Pometun 2024): "У XII–XIV ст. як у містах, так і в селах основним будівельним матеріалом було дерево. У XV ст. і наступних періодах в Україні будувалися також житла і господарські споруди, плетені з лози й обмазані глиною."

## Common Learner Errors

**1. відкривати → відчиняти** (for hinged/swinging objects)
- Error: *"Він відкрив двері і зайшов"* (calque from English "open" / Russian "открыть")
- Correct: *"Він відчинив двері і зайшов"*
- Why it happens: English "open" and Russian "открыть" cover both functions. Ukrainian lexicalizes the distinction: **відчиняти** = doors, windows, shutters (things on hinges); **відкривати** = bottles, eyes, continents, institutions (abstract/sealed openings). This is a systematic source of C1-level errors.

**2. у кутку → на покуті** (sacred corner vs. ordinary corner)
- Error: *"Ікони стояли у кутку"*
- Correct: *"Ікони стояли на покуті"*
- Why it happens: "кут" is the transparent general word (corner); "покуть" is the liturgical-ethnographic term for the sacred diagonal corner opposite the stove. Learners apply the general word to sacred-space descriptions, flattening the register.

**3. хата / будинок / дім confusion**
- **Хата** = traditional single-story rural dwelling (material/architectural referent); in literary/elevated register, any home
- **Будинок** = physical building, multi-story or urban (будинок культури, жилий будинок)
- **Дім** = home as abstract concept (рідний дім, повернутися додому); also formal "house" (Дім офіцерів)
- Error pattern: learners use "будинок" for traditional rural hata descriptions, or "хата" pejoratively for any old building
- Register note: "хата" in publicistic Ukrainian can be used affectionately for any home, not just rural/traditional

**4. мазати + accusative specificity**
- Error: *"мазати стіну"* (correct in modern general sense)
- Traditional collocations: *"мазати долівку"* (applying clay to earthen floor), *"мазати стіни глиною"* (applying clay render to walls)
- The verb has a specialized register in traditional-house context that differs from contemporary uses (мазати маслом, мазати рану)

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **Open with the cosmological frame** — the spatial diagonal Піч↔Покуть is the conceptual spine of the module. Establish it in §1 and echo it in every subsequent section. This prevents the module from reading as a dry architecture survey.

2. **The proverb bank is rich** — Wikipedia §22 provides authentic proverbs directly usable as comprehension anchors: *"Своя хата — своя правда, своя стріха — своя втіха"*; *"У своїй хаті й кутки помагають"*. Use these as section openers rather than fabricating examples.

3. **Regional section needs precision**: Wikipedia confirms Polissia = зрубний; Podillia/steppe = каркасний/мазанка; Carpathians (Hutsuls) = ґражда enclosed farmstead. Don't conflate — the plan's framing of "спростування стереотипу про однотипність" requires naming specific regions.

4. **Сволок pedagogical hook**: The "паспорт хати" metaphor is verifiable from Wikipedia (carved details on Hutsul beams) and from ethnographic tradition of recording dates/names. This is a strong narrative anchor for §2.

5. **Verb distinction section (§5)**: The відчиняти/відкривати distinction should include a contrastive grid with collocational examples — not just a definition. Include: відчиняти (двері, вікна, ставні, хвіртку) vs відкривати (пляшку, очі, виставку, Америку). This is where learners will need most explicit anchoring.

6. **Decolonized framing note**: Soviet ethnography often described Ukrainian folk architecture as a regional variant of "Eastern Slavic dwelling types." Frame the regional diversity of Ukrainian housing as intrinsically Ukrainian — a function of diverse ecological zones within Ukraine itself (Polissia forests, Carpathian timber, steppe clay), not as a derivative of any external cultural pattern.

7. **Word budget**: target is 4000 words minimum. Sections 2 and 3 (architecture + interior) carry the heaviest burden (900 + 1000 words). Plan for vivid descriptive prose, not lists — the persona is Етно-архітектор and the content is C1 literary register, so full sentences with participial constructions are expected and appropriate.
