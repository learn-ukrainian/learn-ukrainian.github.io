**Key frequency insight**: `доказувати` (0.45 IPM) is 13.5× less frequent than `доводити` (6.11 IPM). The near-homophony creates a persistent interference error at C1. ## Cultural Hooks

1. **Козацька рада — oral democratic tradition** (Wikipedia verified): The Kozak Rada (16th–18th centuries) was a general assembly — an organ of direct democratic governance on the Zaporizhian Sich. All free Cossacks had the right to speak, and persuasive oratory was the primary tool of leadership. Koshovyi Otaman Ivan Sirko (died 1680) was legendary not only as a military commander (65 victories) but as a heroic figure embedded in oral tradition — songs and tales. The Rada institutionalized rhetorical culture: the ability to marshal logos and pathos in open debate was a survival skill. This provides a uniquely Ukrainian indigenous rhetorical tradition that predates modern European rhetorical theory's academic codification.

2. **Іван Дзюба — «Інтернаціоналізм чи русифікація?» (1965)** (Wikipedia verified): Written September–December 1965, this is a landmark of Ukrainian intellectual persuasion — a 200-page samvydav (самвидав) document addressed to Communist Party leadership, using the Party's own Marxist-Leninist logic as its argumentative framework. Дзюба's method is an exemplary deductive rhetorical strategy: he takes the official ideological thesis and demonstrates how Soviet language policy contradicts it. The document is structured thesis → evidence → antithesis → synthesis, making it a live specimen of exactly the argumentation model this module teaches. The text uses measured ethos (appeals to official authority), dense logos (statistics, policy citations), and only restrained pathos — a deliberate rhetorical choice. Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia.

3. **Шістдесятники — civic rhetoric under constraint** (Wikipedia verified): The sixtiers (1950s–1960s) were a generation of Ukrainian intellectuals with a strong civic position who worked within the constraints of Soviet censorship. Their rhetorical mode of veiled persuasion — allegory, irony, historical allusion — represents a specific register of Ukrainian public speech: persuasion under authoritarian conditions. This is highly relevant to the module's focus on ethos and pathos in political speech.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **доказувати → доводити** — English speakers calque "to prove" (one English word) onto `доказувати`, unaware it carries a judicial/forensic meaning (to prove guilt, to prove a physical fact). The academically correct form for arguing a thesis, presenting a position, or demonstrating a logical claim is `доводити`. Textbook confirmation: Grade 11 Voron (tier 2) uses «доведення тези» and «доводиться істинність... тези» consistently. Error frequency in GRAC: `доказувати` appears at only 0.45 IPM vs `доводити` at 6.11 IPM — learners who overuse `доказувати` are flagged as non-native immediately.

2. **«Я думаю, що...» → formal opinion markers** — Direct calque of English "I think that..." is perceived as colloquial/informal in formal Ukrainian. The publicistic and academic registers require: «На мою думку», «Переконаний/переконана, що», «Вважаю за доцільне», «На моє переконання». The plan correctly flags this. Textbook support: Grade 11 Zabolotnyi «Практична риторика» chapter teaches this distinction.

3. **Mixing rhetorical figure terminology** — Learners frequently confuse: (a) **анафора** (repetition at the start of clauses) with **епіфора** (repetition at the end); (b) **антитеза** (structural contrast) with mere use of antonyms; (c) **градація** (ascending/descending sequence of intensity) with simple enumeration. The textbook (Grade 11 Glazova, Grade 11 Voron) provides clear definitions with Ukrainian literary examples (Ліна Костенко, Василь Яворівський, Тарас Шевченко, Григорій Сковорода) that the module should draw on.

4. **переконання vs переконування** — These are genuine lexical doublets that learners conflate. `Переконання` (abstract noun, result: «глибоке переконання», «моє переконання») vs `переконування` (verbal noun, process: «мистецтво переконування», «техніка переконування»). Both verified in VESUM. The plan correctly identifies this as a summary-section item.

## Textbook Findings

**Grade 11, Zabolotnyi (tier 2) — «Практична риторика»** (chunks `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0010`, `s0017`):
- Contains памятка «Як виголосити незабутню промову»: start with unexpected argument or rhetorical question; develop thoughts logically and sequentially; use antithesis, metaphor, comparison; reinforce with repetition and gradation.
- Defines антитеза (from Гр. *antithesis* — протиставлення): a rhetorical figure of deliberate juxtaposition of contrasting concepts for persuasive impact. Example: «Такий красивий ти, краю мій, і безнадійно так далекий ти» (Stus).
- Defines градація (from Lat. *gradatio* — підвищення): ascending and descending forms; ascending example from Kostenko: «Криши, ламай, трощи стереотипи!»

**Grade 11, Voron (tier 2)** (chunks `s0058`, `s0060`, `s0085`):
- Теза must remain unchanged throughout the argument; arguments must be true and sufficient for the thesis independently.
- Defines градація (each subsequent word exceeds the previous in intensity), анафора (repetition at clause start), епіфора (repetition at clause end) with literary examples.
- Exercise format: one learner builds a thesis argument, another constructs the counter-argument — directly maps to the Thesis → Antithesis → Synthesis model in the plan.

**Grade 5, Golub (tier 1)** (chunks `s0247`, `s0248`):
- «Переконливість та аргументованість» — §91; defines аргумент as «судження, міркування, докази, які мовець використовує на підтвердження своєї тези.» Clean foundational definition that can be referenced at C1 level as a baseline that the module elaborates.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Deductive frame**: Ukrainian academic and publicistic registers strongly prefer deductive structure (general thesis → supporting evidence → conclusion). This is textbook-confirmed (Voron: «Теза може бути сформульована у вступі, завершуючи його і водночас розпочинаючи основну частину»). Content should model this explicitly rather than teaching both equally.
- **Authentic Ukrainian literary examples are essential** for all rhetorical figures: Ліна Костенко (gradation), Василь Стус (antithesis), Григорій Сковорода (antithesis), Іван Дзюба (logos-heavy deductive structure). Use these, not invented sentences.
- **Zelenskyy as case study**: His wartime addresses use a distinctive structure — ethos established via personal identification («Я залишаюся тут»), logos through specific facts and timelines, pathos through anaphora and parcellation. The plan calls for analysis of this; content should treat it as a live specimen, not a reference.
- **Parцеляція register note**: «Ми переможемо. Разом.» is the canonical Ukrainian parcellation example. Its emotional economy (maximum pathos via minimal syntax) should be contrasted with the complex, multi-clause analytical structure in the same section.
- **Козацька Рада hook**: Frame as positive tradition of direct democratic oratory — a specifically Ukrainian rhetorical culture rooted in participatory governance, not imported from classical antiquity. This decolonizes the topic from the Western-centric «Aristotle invented rhetoric» narrative.
- **Do NOT compare to Russian rhetorical tradition**. Where cross-linguistic comparison is pedagogically useful, use Polish publicistic tradition (similar Slavic context) or reference classical Greek rhetoric directly.
- **Word budget note**: 4000-word target with 5 content sections + summary. Section allocations in plan are reasonable. The «Ритмомелодика та синтаксис» section (600 words) is relatively short — tricolon and parcellation examples must be dense and illustrative to carry the weight. Suggest 4–5 worked examples per device minimum.
