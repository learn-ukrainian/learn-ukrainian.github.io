## State Standard Reference

**§4.1.3.1** (lines 4403–4423, UKRAINIAN-STATE-STANDARD-2024.txt):

> «Дієвідмінювання дієслів у теперішньому часі: брати (беру, береш, бере, беремо, берете, беруть); дієвідмінювання дієслів дати, їсти в майбутньому і теперішньому часі: дам, даси, дасть, дамо, дасте, дадуть; їм, їси, їсть, їмо, їсте, їдять; дієвідмінювання дієслів недоконаного виду в майбутньому часі — синтетична форма: боротимуся, питимеш, кричатиме, мріятимуть.»

**Alignment:** This module directly addresses the State Standard's explicit requirement for mastery of дати/їсти paradigms, брати vowel alternation, and synthetic future — all named in §4.1.3.1. The module extends beyond the Standard's minimum by adding etymological grounding (давньоруська → морфонологія), suppletive stem analysis (бути/йти), and motion verb directionality.

## Cultural Hooks

**1. «Бути чи не бути» — Hamlet's soliloquy in Ukrainian translation**
- Wikipedia confirms the phrase «Бути чи не бути» is the Ukrainian rendering of Shakespeare's "To be, or not to be" (Hamlet, Act III, Scene 1).
- **Григорій Кочур** (1908–1994): Ukrainian translator, poet, literary scholar, шістдесятник. Spent years in Soviet labor camps; his translation work was an act of cultural resistance.
- **Максим Рильський** (1895–1964): Ukrainian poet, neoclassicist, academician of both УРСР and СРСР academies; known for elegant, literary translations.
- Both translators faced the challenge of rendering the infinitive «бути» in a language where this suppletive verb lacks a standard present-tense form (replaced by «є» in 3sg). The philosophical weight of the infinitive itself is grammatically significant — «бути» as an abstract, timeless concept.
- **Pedagogical use:** Opens the module by showing that «бути» is not just a grammar item but a culturally loaded philosophical word with a history of Ukrainian literary engagement.

**2. Прислів'я «Дають — бери, б'ють — тікай»**
- Classic Ukrainian proverb encoding two irregular imperative forms: **бери** (брати, with а→е alternation in all forms) and **тікай** (regular, but contrasting).
- Functions as mnemonic: the stress pattern in **бери** naturally demonstrates the е form that confuses learners who expect *бра-.
- Source: folk paremiology, public domain.

## Common Learner Errors

**1. Over-regularization of дати and їсти**
- Error: *я **даю** замість я **дам**; *я **їю** замість я **їм***
- Why it happens: дати and їсти are the only Ukrainian verbs that use the archaic atematic paradigm (дам, даси, дасть — no thematic vowel -є-/-и-). Learners default to the productive I-conjugation pattern (-ю).
- Correct forms: дам, даси, дасть, дамо, дасте, дадуть / їм, їси, їсть, їмо, їсте, їдять
- Textbook source: Авраменко 7 клас (2024) §33 «Відмінювання дієслів бути, їсти, дати» — tier-1, priority reference.

**2. Ignoring the fleeting vowel in брати → беру**
- Error: *я **браю** замість я **беру***
- Why it happens: The infinitive stem бра- leads learners to expect *бра- + ю. The а→е alternation (fleeting vowel triggered by open syllable in conjugated forms) is invisible from the infinitive alone.
- Pattern: брати — беру, береш, бере, беремо, берете, беруть (State Standard §4.1.3.1 explicitly lists this paradigm)
- Cascades to compounds: забрати → заберу, відібрати → відберу

**3. Missing the д→дж consonant mutation in ходити → ходжу**
- Error: *я **ходю** замість я **ходжу***
- Why it happens: The mutation [д]→[дж] in 1sg personal forms is a synchronic alternation that learners often treat as irregular rather than systematic.
- Pattern (from Глазова Gr.10, Литвинова Gr.7): [д]→[дж]: водити→воджу, ходити→ходжу; [т]→[ч]: платити→плачу; [з]→[ж]: возити→вожу; [с]→[ш]: носити→ношу.
- Important: the mutation appears ONLY in 1sg (я ходжу) — all other forms are regular (ти ходиш, він ходить). This 1sg-only scope is a key pedagogical point.

**Bonus error: Confusing йтиму vs піду (motion verb future)**
- Error: Learners use *ходитиму* when они означають конкретний рух в одному напрямку (where піду is correct).
- Distinction: **йти/піти** = unidirectional, single trip; **ходити** = multidirectional/habitual. Future: **піду** (perfective = single completed motion); **йтиму** / **буду йти** = ongoing/in-progress future motion.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **Textbook anchor:** Авраменко 7 клас (2024) §33 is the primary school-level treatment of дати/їсти/бути paradigms — cite or echo its framing. Глазова Grade 10 covers consonant alternations systematically — can be used for the alternation section.

2. **Decolonized framing is already built into the plan.** The suppletive analysis of бути/йти is a universal cross-linguistic phenomenon (cf. Portuguese ser/estar, Polish być/iść with suppletive chodził). Do NOT frame Ukrainian irregular verbs as "exceptions from a Russian baseline" — treat Ukrainian morphonological history (давньоруська → сучасна морфонологія) as an independent trajectory.

3. **Frequency hierarchy for teaching order:** бути (669 IPM) → дати (109) → брати (76) → йти (67) → їсти (31) → бігти (8). Plan's sequence (вступ → дати/їсти → чергування → бути → рух) deviates slightly from pure frequency order — this is pedagogically justified because дати/їсти are structurally simpler to present as a pair. Accept the plan's order.

4. **суплетивний at 0.01 IPM** confirms it is a metalanguage term unfamiliar to most users. At C1, this is appropriate — introduce it explicitly with definition, not assumed knowledge.

5. **Prefix productivity of дати:** віддати, подати, продати, передати, здати, роздати, задати, надати — all retain the atematic paradigm (відdam, подам etc.). This is a high-yield teaching point: learn дам once, apply to ~8 common derived verbs.

6. **Cultural hook precision:** Кочур (1908–1994) was a шістдесятник imprisoned by Soviets — his engagement with Hamlet carries political resonance. Рильський represents the neoclassicist tradition of high literary Ukrainian. Both are documented on Ukrainian Wikipedia. The contrast between their translation philosophies (Кочур: faithful, scholarly; Рильський: lyrical, academic) can frame the discussion of «бути» as a philosophically untranslatable infinitive.
