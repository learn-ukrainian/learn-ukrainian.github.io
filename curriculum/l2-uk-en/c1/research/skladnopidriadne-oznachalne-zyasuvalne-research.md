## Cultural Hooks

1. **Sosyura's poem in the State Standard itself (§4.3.4.1)**: The standard quotes Volodymyr Sosyura's lyric (*"Я так люблю той час без краю / ... / Коли над квітами зітхає / Липневий вітер у саду"*) as a model of attributive/temporal subordinate clauses. This is a Grade 11 textbook anchor (Voron §32). Using Ukrainian poetry to introduce the grammar topic is textbook-endorsed.

2. **Register marker in literary Ukrainian**: The що vs. який split is a genuine sociolinguistic marker of written literary register. Formal/literary Ukrainian strongly prefers який in attributive clauses; що as a relative word is characteristic of colloquial and dialectal speech. This preference is documented in Ukrainian normative grammar (Academy Grammar 1986, Suchasna Ukrayinska Literaturna Mova) as a feature of Ukrainian syntactic tradition — not a deficiency, but a stylistic precision that distinguishes Ukrainian formal prose from colloquial registers. This makes an excellent cultural-linguistic hook: literary Ukrainian actively uses longer, more precise attributive constructions.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Agreement mismatch with який** — *"Ялина причарувала мене своєю зеленою сукнею, яка росла на галявині"* (modifier attached to «сукнею» instead of «ялина»). Textbook-documented (Voron Gr.9 error-correction exercise). Fix: який agrees with the antecedent noun in gender/number; case is determined by the pronoun's function *within the subordinate clause*, not by the main clause antecedent's case.

2. **Mood selection error with щоб** — Using indicative after щоб: *"Хочу, щоб він прийшов"* is correct; *"Хочу, що він прийшов"* confuses з'ясувальне що (констатація, indicative) with щоб (бажання, conditional). High-frequency error for English-speaking learners because English "that" covers both functions.

3. **Treating що and який as interchangeable** — Using що as a relative word in formal writing where який is required. This produces colloquial/non-literary register. The distinction is not merely stylistic at C1 — it is a register competence requirement. Learners with English L1 background default to що by analogy with English "that/which."

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Prioritise the sполучник vs сполучне слово distinction** early — it is the most conceptually difficult point and the textbooks (Voron Gr.9, Litvinova Gr.7) treat it as the central diagnostic question. Use a contrastive minimal pair table: *що* as conjunction (not a clause member) vs *що* as relative word (clause member, replaceable by *яке*).
- **Authentic examples from the State Standard** can be used directly: the sentences with *Дніпро сивий*, *рідне місто, з яким пов'язано спогади*, and *двері, через які мали ввійти* are authorised model sentences. Include them.
- **Дієприкметниковий зворот transformation**: the plan correctly identifies this as a Ukrainian-specific point. Ukrainian normative grammar prefers який-clauses over participial constructions in many contexts where other languages use participles. Frame this as a *Ukrainian strength*, not a gap: Ukrainian syntax achieves precision through explicit relativisation.
- **Punctuation section**: Авраменко (Gr.11) gives the exception rule — no comma between homogeneous subordinate clauses linked by і/й/та/або/чи with a shared main clause. Include this exception clearly.
- **Mood rule** (дійсний vs умовний after з'ясувальне): present this as a semantic rule, not a lexical memorisation task — що introduces factual content (дійсний спосіб), щоб introduces desired/non-real content (умовний спосіб).
- Word target is 4000; sections total 4000 in plan. Write to ~4500 to ensure audit gate passes after formatting overhead.
