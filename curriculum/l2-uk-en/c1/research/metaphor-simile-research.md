## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ліна Костенко** (born 1930) — Ukrainian poet-shistdesiatnytsya, dissident, Shevchenko Prize laureate (1987), Légion d'honneur (2022). Her poem "Страшні слова, коли вони мовчать" is a canonical example of personification of abstract concepts (слова мовчать, причаїлись) — directly relevant as the plan's opening literary anchor. Wikipedia-verified.

2. **Григорій Сковорода** (1722–1794) — Ukrainian philosopher-mystic, poet, pedagogue; called "the Ukrainian Socrates." His philosophical fables and dialogues use allegory extensively — the world-as-fountain image is from his treatise "Наркіс" (Narcissus). Skovoroda's concept of "сродна праця" (congenial work) is itself a metaphor of spiritual alignment. Wikipedia-verified. His use of allegory represents the deep Ukrainian tradition of metaphorical thinking that predates Romantic-era Ukrainian literary poetry.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Overuse of «як» for all similes** → стилістично монотонно. Correct approach: functional differentiation — «як» = neutral/logical, «мов/наче/ніби» = poetic/emotive uncertainty. Textbook (Zabolotnyi gr.8): «Сполучниками як, мов, ніби, наче, неначе приєднуємо не лише порівняльний зворот, а й частину складного речення» — learners must also distinguish порівняльний зворот from compound sentence clause.

2. **Literal calquing of English metaphors** → e.g., translating "it's raining cats and dogs" as *ллє котами і собаками* instead of «ллє як з відра». Root cause: L1 transfer. Reinforces need for Ukrainian-specific figurative inventory, not direct mapping from English.

3. **Confusing метафора and уособлення** — learners often label any personification as "metaphor." Distinction: метафора = implicit comparison via identity transfer (Він — лев); уособлення = specifically animating non-human entities with human qualities (Степ дихає). Both are трopes but different mechanisms.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The plan correctly cites §4.4.3 and §4.4.4 — confirmed exact text in Standard. Example sentences from Standard (тепла атмосфера, сонце заходить, холодний як лід, стрибає мов м'яч) should appear verbatim in the module to anchor the Standard reference.
- Skovoroda's world-as-fountain allegory is in "Наркіс" — describe it as a sustained allegorical metaphor, not just a simile. Pedagogically powerful as a Ukrainian-origin example predating European Romantic influence.
- The Kostenko poem title "Страшні слова, коли вони мовчать" is itself an oxymoronic personification — worth foregrounding this irony in the intro section.
- Grade 8 Zabolotnyi textbook explicitly addresses the critical confusion: порівняльний зворот vs. складне речення with same markers (як, мов, наче). C1 learners should master this distinction.
- Decolonized framing note: Ukrainian figurative language has a continuous tradition from Skovoroda (18th c.) through Shevchenko, Franko, to the shistdesiatnyky — a native, unbroken aesthetic lineage. No need to reference any other language tradition as a baseline.
