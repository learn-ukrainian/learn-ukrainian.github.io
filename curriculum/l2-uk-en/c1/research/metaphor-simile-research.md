# Дослідження: Метафора та порівняння: Образна мова

## State Standard Reference
§4.4.3: "Стилістичні засоби лексики: ... метафори: тепла атмосфера, сонце заходить..."
§4.4.4: "Стилістичні засоби синтаксису: ... порівняння: краще, ніж я; краще за мене; кращий від мене; холодний, як лід; стрибає, мов м’яч..."
Alignment: This module directly addresses the C1 requirement for mastering stylistic lexical and syntactic devices, specifically focusing on the distinction and usage of metaphors and similes as outlined in the standard.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Lina Kostenko's "Страшні слова, коли вони мовчать"**: A quintessential example of personification (*слова мовчать, слова причаїлись*) and metaphorical weight in modern Ukrainian poetry.
2. **Hryhoriy Skovoroda's "Fables"**: The concept of "affinity" (*сродність*) and his use of extended allegories (the world as a fountain) provides a historical anchor for deep metaphorical thought in Ukrainian culture.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Calquing English Idioms** → Translating "it's raining cats and dogs" literally instead of using *ллє як з відра*. (Failure to use functional equivalents).
2. **Overuse of "як"** → Using *як* exclusively for similes, ignoring stylistically richer markers like *мов, немов, наче, ніби* which add nuance and poetic flavor.
3. **Confusing Metaphor and Simile** → Misidentifying direct metaphors (*Він — лев*) as similes due to the linking verb implied or present.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Focus on the *generative* power of metaphors in C1 — not just identifying them, but creating them to persuade or evoke emotion.
- Emphasize the subtle differences between simile markers (*мов* vs *як*) — *мов* often implies a softer, more poetic comparison than the logical *як*.
