## Cultural Hooks

1. **Капела «ДУМКА» (1919)**: Full verified name — Національна заслужена академічна капела України «Думка». The acronym stands for «Державна українська мандрівна капела». Founded 1919. Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia search result (confirmed). This is an exact factual detail the plan relies on — verified accurate.

2. **Тріумвірат «Золотого віку»**:
   - Максим Березовський — «класик європейської музики», автор духовних концертів та опери «Демофонт», першої української симфонії. (Wikipedia)
   - Дмитро Бортнянський (1751–1825, Глухів, Гетьманщина) — автор 6 опер, хорових концертів, 10 двохорних концертів. (Wikipedia). Born in Hlukhiv — Ukrainian origin must be foregrounded; Russophone historiography often frames him as a "Russian composer" due to his work in St. Petersburg.
   - Артем Ведель — Ukrainian composer, director, singer, violinist. (Wikipedia)

3. **ДахаБраха**: Ukrainian ethno-ensemble. Artistic director: Владислав Троїцький. Repertoire: Ukrainian, Crimean Tatar, English, German. Self-defines as «етно-хаос». (Wikipedia). NPR Tiny Desk Concert reference in plan is a verified cultural milestone.

4. **ONUKA**: Ukrainian electro-folk group. Defining feature: use of «національних народних мотивів та інструментів в електронній музиці». (Wikipedia). Founded by Nata Zhyzhchenko (granddaughter of folk singer), uses theremin and Soviet-era instruments.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **голос ≠ голосування** — "voice" vs. "vote/voting". False-friend trap for English speakers (both translate to "voice" loosely). Correct: *голос виконавця* (singer's voice) vs. *право голосу* (right to vote). Learners must not use *голосування* when discussing vocal performance.

2. **\*грати партію → виконувати партію** — Calque from Russian *играть партию* / English "play a part". In Ukrainian vocal/operatic context, the verb is *виконувати*, not *грати* (which applies to instruments). Error is reinforced by contact with Russian operatic terminology.

3. **Generic aesthetic evaluators** — Learners default to *гарний*, *добрий*, *красивий* instead of professional descriptors. Target replacements: *насичений* (rich/full), *оксамитовий* (velvety), *проникливий* (soulful/penetrating), *дзвінкий* (ringing/bright), *віртуозний* (virtuosic). This is the stylistic refinement goal of §4.4.3 at C1.

## Decolonization Notes for Content Writing

- **Бортнянський**: Do NOT frame as "Russian composer" despite his career in St. Petersburg. He was born in Hlukhiv (Гетьманщина) and his formation was Ukrainian. Frame: «Дмитро Бортнянський — уродженець Глухова, центру Гетьманщини».
- **Золотий вік хорової музики**: Soviet historiography subordinated this tradition to "all-Russian" sacred music. Frame it as an autonomous Ukrainian sacred polyphonic school of the 18th century.
- **ДахаБраха / ONUKA**: These are specifically Ukrainian responses to global music — not "post-Soviet" acts. Frame their success as *Ukrainian* cultural export.
- **кордоцентризм**: A specifically Ukrainian philosophical concept (Hryhorii Skovoroda tradition) — not generic Slavic. Reference it as an indigenous Ukrainian intellectual framework.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **акапела spelling**: Flag for Phase B — VESUM does not contain "акапела". Use «спів a cappella» (as in standard Ukrainian music journalism) or «акапельний спів» for the adjective form. Do not use «акапела» as a standalone noun without checking Правопис.
- **Word budget**: 4000-word target with 500+900+800+900+900 section plan = 4000 exactly. 1.5× overshoot strategy recommended (write ~4500–5000 for Phase B; validator trims). All sections are of roughly equal weight — no single section dominates.
- **мелізматика / колоратура**: Both are extremely low-frequency terms (8 and 18 GRAC occurrences). Treat as specialist vocabulary requiring contextualised definition on first use. Do not assume prior knowledge.
- **Лисенко / Степовий**: Plan cites Микола Лисенко as founder of the Ukrainian art song (романс) tradition and Яків Степовий as successor — both are well-established historically. No Wikipedia verification needed; standard Ukrainian music history.
- **Essay task (section 5)**: Content-generation trigger — learner writes critical essay about Ukrainian vocal school. The essay task is a language output task (tests writing in художній/публіцистичний register), not a content-knowledge test. Aligns with Rule 10 (activities test language, not content).
