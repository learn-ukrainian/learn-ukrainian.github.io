## Cultural Hooks

**1. НТШ та формування наукової термінології (verified via Wikipedia)**
Наукове товариство імені Шевченка (НТШ) — founded 1873 in Lviv. Reorganized as a full scientific institution on 13 March 1892 with three sections (Istorychno-filosofska, Filolohichna, Matematychno-pryrodopysno-likarska). Under Mykhailo Hrushevsky as head (1897–1913), it became the de facto Ukrainian Academy of Sciences. Ivan Franko was central to the Philological Section. The NTSh consciously built Ukrainian scientific vocabulary from living folk speech rather than borrowing Russian/Church Slavonic forms. This directly motivates the module's point about scientific terminology emerging from народна мова rather than imperial imposition.
→ *Use in "Дієприкметники та наукова термінологія" section.*

**2. Українізація та її ліквідація як мовна деколонізація (verified via Wikipedia)**
Українізація (1920s Ukrainian SSR) expanded Ukrainian in official contexts. Its violent reversal in the 1930s ("Розстріляне відродження") included deliberate pressure to align Ukrainian syntax with Russian — linguists who had systematized distinctly Ukrainian constructions (including preference for active voice over passive) were targeted. Soviet-era grammars artificially inflated passive constructions to narrow the perceived gap between Ukrainian and Russian. The авторські конструкції на -но, -то (e.g., "записано", "збудовано") are authentically Ukrainian — not calques from Russian passives.
→ *Use in "Пасивний стан та деколонізація синтаксису" section.*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **-ся passive with agent** → `Книга читається студентом` ❌ → `Студент читає книгу` ✅  
   *Why*: Direct calque of Russian passive construction. Ukrainian grammatical tradition strongly prefers active voice or безособові -но/-то forms. "Книга читається" without agent is acceptable; with agent it is non-standard.

2. **Active participle calques** → `бажаючі` ❌ → `охочі` ✅; `подорожуючі` ❌ → `подорожні` ✅; `завідуючий` ❌ → `завідувач` ✅  
   *Why*: Ukrainian active participles in -уч-/-юч- existed in Old Ukrainian but were largely displaced by native constructions. Present-day standard Ukrainian rejects them as Russianisms; they are replaced by adjectives, nouns, or relative clauses.

3. **Register mixing / канцеляризми in wrong context** → `здійснити перехід` in casual speech ❌ → `перейти` ✅; conversely, `крутий аргумент` in academic writing ❌ → `переконливий аргумент` ✅  
   *Why*: B2 learners often either over-formalize (importing bureaucratic stamps from official texts) or under-formalize (using colloquial vocabulary in academic registers). This is the key competency gap between B2 and C1.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The plan's example "Там усе правильно записано" appears verbatim in the State Standard §4.3.3.1 — use it. It's both pedagogically canonical and Standard-compliant.
- The 7-style system (adding релігійний + епістолярний to the B2 5-style system) is the concrete, memorable upgrade from B2. Make this explicit as a learner milestone.
- "Agency Pass" as a criterion: frame it as evaluating whether the grammatical subject is actually performing an action, not just occupying a syntactic slot. This is a genuine Ukrainian linguistic principle (діяч-суб'єкт), not just a pedagogical invention.
- The деколонізація синтаксису hook is factually grounded (Ukrainization reversal, 1930s) — but keep it analytical, not polemical. Focus on *what* was changed linguistically and *why* the authentic Ukrainian forms are preferable, not on blame.
- Section word targets: Вступ 500 + Пасив 850 + Дієприкметники 750 + Складнопідрядні 800 + Стилі 650 + Фразеологія 450 = 4000. These are minimums; overshoot to 4400–4500 for safety.
- The plan cites "§4.4.1.1" for "систематизація знань про 7 функціональних стилів" — confirmed correct. Also cite §4.3.3.1 for безособові речення.
