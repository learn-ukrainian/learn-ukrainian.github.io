## ⚠️ Terminology Flag: реєстр vs регістр

The plan uses **реєстр** for "language register" (високий/низький реєстр). GRAC frequency data shows **реєстр** (20,042 / 9.90 per million) collocates overwhelmingly as: *єдиний реєстр*, *державний реєстр*, *реєстр розслідування* — i.e., the **administrative registry** sense, not the linguistic sense.

In Ukrainian linguistics and stylistics, the correct term for language register is **регістр** (from French *registre*, widely used in Ukrainian sociolinguistics). Phase B content should use **регістр** for the linguistic concept to avoid register confusion. The plan vocabulary_hint entry should ideally read `регістр (register)` — flag this for the plan maintainer.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Ліна Костенко — мова як генетичний код нації** (verified via Wikipedia). Ліна Василівна Костенко (нар. 1930) — поетеса-шістдесятниця, лауреатка Шевченківської премії (1987), кавалерка Ордену Почесного легіону Франції (2022). Her essays and public lectures contain the concept of mova (language) as the *genetic code* and *passport into eternity* of a nation — a celebrated articulation of Ukrainian linguistic identity. This makes her an ideal persona-proxy for the "Редактор стилю" voice and the framing hook of Section 1. Direct quote often cited: *«Нація — перепустка у Вічність, мова рідна — віза у віки»* (note: this line is attributed to Л. Сердунич in a Grade 5 textbook, Golub, not Kostenko directly — do NOT misattribute; use Kostenko's general intellectual framework rather than a specific quote unless verified).

2. **Канцелярит — термін Корнія Чуковського (1962)** (verified via Wikipedia). The term *канцелярит* was coined by Russian writer Korniy Chukovsky in his book "Живой как жизнь" (1962/1966), describing the disease of bureaucratic language infecting everyday speech. In Ukrainian context, the phenomenon is amplified by Soviet administrative inheritance: decades of official Soviet Ukrainian being modelled on Russian bureaucratic templates left deep syntactic calques. Fighting канцелярит in Ukrainian is thus explicitly a decolonial act — restoring native active constructions, eliminating passive bureaucratisms, rejecting officialese in everyday registers.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Passive bureaucratic construction calque** → *«Рішення було прийняте нами»* ← correct: *«Ми ухвалили рішення»*. Happens because English-speaking learners encounter Ukrainian in Soviet-era formal texts; also reinforced by English passive voice analogy. Ukrainian prefers active constructions, especially in speech and publicistic style.

2. **"Дане питання" канцеляризм** → *«У даному питанні»* ← correct: *«У цьому питанні» / «Щодо цього»*. The word *даний* in the sense of "this" is a direct calque from Russian *данный* — it functions as a stylistic marker of канцелярит. The Ukrainian equivalent is *цей/ця/це* or circumlocutions. Textbook coverage: Grade 10 Karaman §39.

3. **Register mismatch — сленг у офіційному листуванні** → using *«норм»*, *«окей»*, *«прикольно»* in formal correspondence. English-speaking learners often underestimate Ukrainian register distinctions because they conflate informal Ukrainian with informal English. A key C1 skill is switching registers deliberately, not accidentally.

4. **Euphony rule omissions at editing stage** → failing to apply у/в and і/й alternations in final editing. Rule is known but not automatized; learners need explicit editing-pass training. Source: Grade 10 Glazova §15, Grade 6 Avramenko.

## Decolonized Framing Notes for Phase B

- **Never frame суржик as "Ukrainian-Russian mix"** — frame it as *мовне явище*, the result of Soviet administrative pressure on Ukrainian speakers. The goal is not anti-Russian sentiment but restoration of authentic Ukrainian register.
- **Канцелярит has Ukrainian roots distinct from the Russian term** — the bureaucratic inheritance is Soviet, not Russian per se. Distinguish Chukovsky's coinage (literary criticism of Soviet officialese) from the specifically Ukrainian pedagogical task of de-Sovietizing administrative language.
- **Active vs. passive construction** — present active constructions as *українська норма*, not as "unlike Russian." Frame positively: Ukrainian prefers the agency of the subject, prefers dynamic constructions.
- **Milozwučnist' is a unique Ukrainian phonoaesthetic value** — present it as an intrinsic feature of Ukrainian prosody, not as a rule that compensates for consonant clusters. The textbook source (Glazova) frames it as *одна з характерних рис української літературної мови* — adopt this framing.
