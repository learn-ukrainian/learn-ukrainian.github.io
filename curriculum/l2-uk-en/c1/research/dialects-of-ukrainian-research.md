## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Poltava Base**: Standard Ukrainian was codified based on the Middle Dnieper dialects (South-Eastern group), popularized by Ivan Kotliarevsky's *Eneida* (1798). This makes the SE dialect the "closest" to the textbook standard.
2. **"Shadows of Forgotten Ancestors"**: Kotsiubynsky's masterpiece (and Parajanov's film) is the premier cultural touchstone for the Hutsul dialect (South-Western group), showcasing its distinct rhythm and lexicon (*ґазди*, *полонина*, *трембіта*).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Dialect vs. Surzhyk**: Confusing organic, historical dialectal forms (e.g., *робити* vs *роблю*) with *Surzhyk* (chaotic Russian-Ukrainian interference).
   * *Correction*: Dialect is a structured system; Surzhyk is a chaotic mix.
2. **Stigmatization**: Viewing dialects as "uneducated" or "incorrect" rather than valuable variations.
   * *Correction*: C1 learners must appreciate dialects as reservoirs of linguistic richness.
3. **Hypercorrection**: Avoiding legitimate dialectal words that resemble Russian (e.g., *держати* vs *тримати*) due to fear of Surzhyk, even when the word is standard or dialectally valid.

## Notes for Content Writing (Phase B)
- **Tone**: Academic but appreciative. Treat dialects as "flavors", not "errors".
- **Phonetics**: Use IPA explicitly when contrasting dialect pronunciation (e.g., *[chodyt]* vs *[chodyt']*).
- **Visuals**: Describe the mental map of Ukraine clearly (North = Polissia, SW = Mountains/West, SE = Steppe/Center).
