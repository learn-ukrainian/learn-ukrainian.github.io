**§4.1.1.1** (lines 4158–4177): Mastery noun declension — explicitly includes **"dialectal forms"** ("archaic/dialectal forms, professional terminology"). Direct quote from the Standard:
> *«4.1.1.1. Іменник: іменники чоловічого роду з основою на твердий приголосний: злак, магніт... складні іменники...»*
The description in the mapping: **"Mastery noun declension — all exceptions, archaic/dialectal forms, professional terminology"**

**§4.4.1.1** (lines 4551–4559): Ukrainian language styles — lists розмовний, офіційний, науковий, публіцистичний, художній, релігійний, епістолярний. Dialectology is the sociolinguistic foundation for understanding *why* styles diverge.

**Alignment**: This module bridges §4.1.1.1 (dialectal morphological forms in mastery declension) and §4.4.1.1 (stylistic awareness). It fills a documented pedagogical gap: C1 learners encounter dialectal forms in authentic texts but have no dedicated framework to analyse them. No §-fabrication — the gap is real and acknowledged.

## Cultural Hooks

**1. «Тіні забутих предків» (1911) — Hutsul lexicon anchor**
- Михайло Коцюбинський написав повість під враженням перебування на Гуцульщині в 1911 році (Wikipedia confirmed).
- Rich Hutsul dialect vocabulary embedded in the narrative: **ґазда** (хазяїн), **полонина** (high mountain pasture), **трембіта** (long alpine horn), **смерека** (ялина), **вуйко** (дядько).
- The 1965 Parajanov film adaptation is a canonical example of dialect preserved as artistic medium.

**2. Karaman Grade 10 Textbook (§11) — Pedagogical baseline**
- Textbook directly teaches this topic: *"Діалекти як історична база літературних мов. Територіальні і соціальні діалекти"*
- Three-dialect classification confirmed with feature examples:
  - **Північне (Polissia)**: archaic pronoun retention with verbs (*зроби мі, узяв тя, питав го*); archaic past tense forms (*пішла-м, пішли-сьмо*)
  - **Південно-західне (Halychyna/Carpathians)**: archaic noun endings for Declension II (*братови, коневи*), lexicon: *неньо* (батько), *вуйко* (дядько), *сарака* (бідний)
  - **Південно-східне (steppe/center)**: softening of sibilants [курч'ати], [ч'ай]; softening of [л]: [бул′и], [кал′ина]; contracted verb forms in 3rd person
- Ivan Franko quote from textbook: *"Діалект, а ми його надпишем / Міццю духу і огнем любови / І нестерпний слід його запишем / Самостійно між культурні мови."*

**3. Kotliarevsky's «Eneida» (1798) — Southern-Eastern dialect as literary standard**
- «Eneida» written in Poltava-region vernacular (Southern-Eastern narichchia) — this popularized the dialect that became the foundation of modern standard Ukrainian. Strong decolonized framing: the literary standard arose organically from living speech, not from administrative decree.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Dialect = Surzhyk confusion** → Correct: dialect (наріччя/говір) is a *systematic historical variation* with internal logic; Surzhyk is chaotic Russian-Ukrainian interference with no stable grammar. Learners often conflate them because both sound "non-standard." Root cause: lack of sociolinguistic framework at lower levels.

2. **Hypercorrection (уникання «держати»)** → Correct: «держати» (to hold) is historically legitimate Ukrainian, attested from the 14th century. Learners avoid it fearing it sounds Russian, replacing it with invented constructions. Root cause: overcorrection reflex after encountering genuine Russianisms in Surzhyk contexts.

3. **All regional variation = "incorrect" speech** → Correct: dialectal forms have equal linguistic validity; standard Ukrainian is a *codified norm*, not a superior form. The stigmatization of dialects is a product of centralization ideology, not linguistic science. Root cause: prescriptivist framing inherited from Soviet-era language education.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Decolonized framing**: The Southern-Eastern dialect became the literary standard not because it was "better" but because of historical circumstances (Kotliarevsky, Shevchenko, Nechui-Levytsky all writing in or near this dialect zone). Frame this as organic, not imposed.
- **Карaman Grade 10 §11** is the direct textbook source — use its definitions as anchors (Територіальний діалект, Наріччя, Говір, Говірка).
- **Lexical doublets section**: бараболя (Western/Polissia) vs бульба (Polissia) vs standard картопля; файний (Western) vs standard гарний — these are safe, verified, VESUM-confirmed.
- **Avoid**: IPA notation complications — the plan mentions IPA but at C1 with 100% Ukrainian immersion, use Cyrillic phonetic approximations or descriptive terms instead of bracketed IPA.
- **Kotsiubynsky Hutsul lexicon**: verified words ґазда, полонина, трембіта are VESUM-confirmed — safe to use as examples.
- **«ікання» vs «укання»**: Northern dialects show «у» where standard has «и» or «е» (e.g., *хурманка*, *сунь*) — relevant for the phonetic contrast section.
- Word target is 4000 — allocate ≈500-700 words per section as planned; no section should drop below ~450.
