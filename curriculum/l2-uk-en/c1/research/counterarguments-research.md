Alignment: This module's core — teaching learners to deploy counterargument, concession, and refutation as rhetorical elements in text composition. Directly mandated at C1.

Alignment: Section 4 of the plan ("Урізноманітнення вставних слів") maps exactly to §4.4.4 — moving learners beyond English-calque "однак" toward the full Ukrainian parenthetical repertoire.

Alignment: Section 3 of the plan on допустові конструкції (хоча..., проте...; незважаючи на те що...) maps to the C1 mastery level of complex sentences.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Іван Вишенський (бл. 1550–1621)** — Ukrainian Orthodox monk and polemicist, widely cited as the founding voice of Ukrainian intellectual argumentation. His tracts (*Послання*, *Обличення*) against the 1596 Brest Union deployed sharp rhetorical techniques: reductio ad absurdum, direct exposure of opponent's faulty premises, and refusal to grant false equivalencies. He argued in Ukrainian Church Slavonic with vernacular force, establishing that *спростування* must expose the logical root of error, not merely deny it. Source: Wikipedia (uk) — Іван Вишенський.

2. **Літературна дискусія 1925–1928** — Public debate about the ideological and aesthetic direction of Ukrainian Soviet literature. Mikola Khvylovyi's pamphlets (*Камо грядеши?*, *Думки проти течії*, *Україна чи Малоросія?*) exemplify systematic counterargument: he never simply rejected the Russocentric position but first cited it fully (principle of intellectual charity), then dismantled it with historical and cultural evidence. His slogan "Геть від Москви!" was the conclusion of a structured refutation, not a bare denial. Source: Wikipedia (uk) — Літературна дискусія 1925–1928; Микола Хвильовий.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Голе заперечення" (bare denial)** → Correct form: acknowledge + concede + refute.
   Learner error: *Я не згоден, бо це неправильно.*
   Correct: *Справді, ця позиція має певне обґрунтування; однак, якщо уважніше розглянути факти, стає очевидно, що...*
   Why: English-speaking learners default to adversarial "I disagree because" framing; Ukrainian academic discourse expects concession before refutation.

2. **Однак-overuse (English "however" calque)** → Ukrainian has a wider repertoire: *водночас*, *з іншого боку*, *попри це*, *натомість*, *утім*, *проте*.
   Learner error: Начало кожного контраргументу з *Однак...* (directly calqued from English rhythm).
   Why: The Grade 8 Avramenko textbook (tier 1) explicitly teaches that *однак/проте* at sentence start function as conjunctions, not parenthetical words — learners conflate these roles.

3. **Register mismatch: розмовне vs. академічне** → *брехня* → *хибне твердження* / *маніпуляція* / *невідповідність фактам*; *неправильний* → *хибний* / *спростований*.
   Why: C1 learners know the colloquial term but lack the full academic register stack; the plan's Section 4 directly targets this. Reinforce with word fields.

## Textbook Grounding

- **Grade 11, Voron (2019)** — explicit спростування exercise: "один учень (учениця) має скласти доведення певного твердження, а другий (друга) — його спростування... При спростуванні твердження слід указати на хибність або недостатність доказів опонента, аргументувати свою думку (*Тут говорили, що…, але це не так, адже…*)." — This is the base-level refutation formula; C1 must expand it.
- **Grade 8, Avramenko (2025, tier 1)** — critical metalinguistic point: *однак/проте* at sentence start = conjunctions (= *але*); in mid-sentence = parenthetical words. C1 learners need this distinction to use the parenthetical repertoire correctly.
- **Grade 8, Zabolotnyi (2025, tier 1)** — parenthetical word table: "навпаки, отже, відповідно, значить, до речі, крім того" for sequence-of-thought structuring. Content should reference this framework and expand into higher-register variants.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Decolonized framing**: Ukrainian polemical tradition (Вишенський → Хвильовий) is rich and independent. Do NOT frame logical argumentation as imported from Western rhetoric or as adapted from Russian academic conventions. Ukrainian has its own *полемічна традиція* dating to the 16th century.
- **Однак/проте distinction** (Avramenko Grade 8) is a high-value metalinguistic point unique to Ukrainian orthographic/punctuation rules — use it as a concrete, memorable teaching moment in Section 4.
- **The Khvylovyi example is pedagogically ideal** — his argument structure in the Літературна дискусія is: (1) full citation of opposing view, (2) concession of its partial validity, (3) exposure of its logical flaw (conflation of "Soviet" and "Russian"), (4) counter-proposal. This is exactly the TDCRS algorithm (Теза—Доказ—Контраргумент—Спростування—Резюме) the plan mandates.
- **Word budget signal**: Section 1 (Вступ, 500 words) is tight — Вишенський's full treatment belongs in Section 2 (800 words). The intro should introduce the *principle* of intellectual charity and set stakes; Section 2 delivers the historical depth.
- **Avoid**: Do not use "Ad hominem" as a Latin term without full Ukrainian explanation; give *перехід на особистості* first, then clarify the Latin label is the international term.
