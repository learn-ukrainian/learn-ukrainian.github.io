## State Standard Reference

**§4.3.2** (lines 4488–4500): *"Просте речення зі вставними словами: Завтра в мене, знаєш, важливі справи"*
**§4.4.1.1** (lines 4551–4559): *"Стилі української мови: розмовний; офіційний; науковий; публіцистичний; художній; релігійний; епістолярний."*

**Alignment**: Module addresses §4.3.2 (вставні слова in complex simple sentences), §4.4.1.1 (scientific vs epistolary styles), and §4.4.4 (syntactic stylistic devices including parenthetical structures). The module extends the State Standard by applying вставні слова specifically to epistemic modality and academic hedging — the register-specific functional layer that §4.4.1.1 (науковий стиль) implicitly requires.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **Наукове товариство імені Шевченка (НТШ)**: Founded in Lviv in the late 19th century, the НТШ was *"рушійна сила формування та розвитку української науки кінця XIX — першої половини XX ст."* (Wikipedia). Its scholars — including Михайло Грушевський, who chaired НТШ 1897–1913 and authored over 2,000 scholarly works — established a tradition of Plurālis Auctoris ("ми"-авторське), using "ми" instead of "я" to project scholarly objectivity. This convention was a deliberate choice of the nascent Ukrainian academic community, not an imitation of Soviet norms. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Наукове_товариство_імені_Шевченка

2. **Леся Українка — епістолярна спадщина**: Described as *"письменниця, перекладачка й культурна діячка"* (Wikipedia), Леся Українка left an extensive epistolary corpus (hundreds of surviving letters) notable for its diplomatic, hedged register: constructions like "Чи не могли б Ви" and "Дозволю собі зауважити" are documented examples of high-register вежливий хеджінг. This contrasts sharply with the imperative, bureaucratic tone of Soviet канцелярит, making her letters an ideal primary source for the module's cultural section. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Леся_Українка

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Я думаю/Я відчуваю, що…" (subjective framing calque)** → Correct: "Видається, що…" / "Дані свідчать на користь того, що…" — Happens because English academic English permits "I believe/I think" in hedged argument; Ukrainian scientific style strongly prefers impersonal/objective framing.

2. **"Може бути" as English calque** → Correct: "Можливо" or "Мабуть" — "Може бути" is an overextension of the modal дієслово pattern; in academic Ukrainian it reads as неграмотна калька від "maybe." Both "можливо" і "мабуть" are morphologically вставні слова, not modal predicates.

3. **Overconfident categorical statements (Soviet канцелярит legacy)** → Correct: replace "Це доводить, що X" / "Це точно так" with "Не виключено, що X" / "Цілком імовірно, що X" — Soviet academic writing favoured authoritative declarations; contemporary Ukrainian academic style, following European norms, requires epistemic calibration.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Certainty scale** (шкала впевненості) is the strongest pedagogical spine for this module. Anchor every section to it: from "навряд чи" (~20–25%) through "мабуть/можливо" (~50–60%) to "безперечно/безсумнівно" (~95–100%). This is testable, memorable, and immediately usable.
- **хеджінг as metalanguage**: Use the English loanword in quotation marks with a Ukrainian gloss on first mention, then use native terminology. The term is not in VESUM, so do not present it as standard Ukrainian.
- **Decolonized framing for канцелярит contrast**: Frame Soviet imperative academic style as a foreign imposition on Ukrainian discourse norms — contrast with the НТШ tradition and Леся Українка's diplomatic epistolary style as the authentic Ukrainian baseline.
- **Plurālis Auctoris** ("на нашу думку," "ми розглянемо"): explicitly tie to НТШ founding tradition, not to Soviet academic convention (they are superficially similar but historically distinct in motivation).
- **"Можна припустити" structure**: GRAC shows припускати collocates heavily with `що` and `могти` — the pattern "можна припустити, що" is the canonical academic form. Use this as the anchor collocation.
- **Register warning**: "скоріш за все" is marked as conversational/publicistic — flag it explicitly in the activities as inappropriate for formal academic writing; learners should use "найімовірніше" or "цілком ймовірно" instead.
- Word target is 4000 — the certainty scale section (Підсумок) and Error Analysis section are natural expansion points if needed.
