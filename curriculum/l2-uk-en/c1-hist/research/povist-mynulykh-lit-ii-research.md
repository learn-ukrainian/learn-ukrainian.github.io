# Research Notes: Povist Mynulykh Lit II

## Key Fragments for Analysis

### 1. The Calling of the Varangians (862)

- **Primary Source Text**: "Земля наша велика і щедра, а порядку в ній нема. Приходьте княжити і володіти нами".
- **Historiography**:
  - **Normanist Theory**: Germanic origin of statehood. Relies on the 862 legend.
  - **Anti-Normanist/Modern**: Internal socio-economic prerequisites for statehood existed. "Rus" name likely pre-dates 862 (attributed to southern origins or fused etymologies).
  - **Political Implication**: Used by imperial historians to deny Slavic capacity for self-organization.
- **Analysis**: The "Order vs Chaos" archetype. The "Foreign Ruler" motif as a neutral arbiter in tribal conflicts.

### 2. The Choice of Faiths (988)

- **Primary Source Text**: The "Examination of Religions" (Wisdom of the Philosophers).
- **The Legends**:
  - Islam rejected ("Русі єсть веселіє пити").
  - Judaism rejected (Loss of Jerusalem = sign of God's disfavor).
  - Western Christianity rejected ("Fathers did not accept").
  - Greek Orthodoxy accepted (The beauty of Hagia Sophia).
- **Realpolitik**:
  - Byzantium was the superpower.
  - Marriage to Anna Porphyrogenita required baptism.
  - "Choice without choice" – legitimizing the inevitable.
- **Ideology**: The "Korsun Legend" vs "Kyiv Baptism". PVL emphasizes the deliberate, wise choice of the Prince.

### 3. Olga's Revenge

- **Primary Source Text**: Three revenges (Burial alive, Burning in bathhouse, Drunken massacre) + City burning (pigeons).
- **Folklore Roots**:
  - Parallels with pagan funeral rites (human sacrifice, funeral feast/trizna).
  - Riddles and cunning (viking/slavic trickster archetype).
- **Gender**: Olga as the "Manly Woman" (virago). The only female ruler praised for "manly wisdom".

## Ideological Program of PVL

- **Unity of the Land**: Written during feudal fragmentation (early 12th c.). Calls for unity of Rurikid princes.
- **Kyivocentrism**: Kyiv as the "Mother of Rus Cities" (New Jerusalem).
- **Christian Providentialism**: History as God's plan.

## Terminology

- **Варяги (Varangians)**: Scandinavian warrior-merchants.
- **Етіологічний міф (Etiological myth)**: Explains origins (why we are who we are).
- **Агіографія (Hagiography)**: Lives of saints (stylistic influence).
