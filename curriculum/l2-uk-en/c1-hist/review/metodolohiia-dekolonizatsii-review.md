## Verification Summary

**The "Would I Stay?" Test:**
Yes. This is not a dry theory dump. It challenges the reader personally («Чи здається мені суржик смішним...?»). It invites introspection. I found myself nodding along with the description of "internal colonization".

**Narrative Analysis:**

- **Hook:** The "iceberg" metaphor for decolonization (beyond statue toppling).
- **Tension:** The "Second Rus" theft and the "schizophrenic" situation of the empire.
- **Climax:** The realization of "internalized inferiority" and the call to "kill the internal colonizer".
- **Resolution:** A calm, indifferent attitude towards the former empire.

**Decolonization Check:**
The module tackles the hardest part of decolonization: not just blaming the empire, but taking responsibility for one's own mind («доросла нація бере відповідальність»). The distinction between "anti-colonial" (anger) and "post-colonial" (irony/distance) is a key intellectual upgrade for the learner.

**Citations Check (Specific Evidence):**

- «Деколонізація — це вбивство «внутрішнього колонізатора»...» (Strong metaphor).
- «...модель Саїда ідеально накладається на російсько-українські стосунки.» (Application of theory).
- «Він підірвав російську літературу зсередини, внісши в неї «чужий» елемент.» (Reframing Gogol).

## Issues Found

1.  **Minor Pacing in Theory:** The section on "Theoretical Foundations" lists four major thinkers back-to-back. While necessary, it's a lot of abstract concepts at once. _Self-correction during writing:_ I ensured each has a clear application to Ukraine to keep it grounded.
2.  **Visuals:** Similar to Module 09, a visual comparison of "Imperial" vs "Decolonized" maps or art would be powerful here. (Platform limitation, but noted).
3.  **Risk of Abstraction:** The "Methods" section runs the risk of being too theoretical. However, the "Practical Application" section successfully anchors it with specific textual examples (Pereyaslav Council).
