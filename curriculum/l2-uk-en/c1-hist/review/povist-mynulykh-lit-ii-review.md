## Summary

The module has been heavily rebuilt to include 11 key fragments from the Primary Chronicle, ranging from the Apostle Andrew legend to the Blinding of Vasylko.
The analysis has been deepened with historiographical context (Shakhmatov, Norman Theory, Rybakov) and philological details (etymology of "Rus", "trizna").
However, the module falls short of the ambitious 4500-word seminar target, landing at ~3000 words. The density of analysis is fulfilling for a 90-minute semantic session, but for a "deep reading" seminar, it could be expanded further with more primary source text.

## Critical Analysis (The "Red Team" View)

### 1. Strengths

- **Textual Criticism**: The inclusion of Shakhmatov's hypothesis is a strong C1-level move. It teaches students to question the _author_ of the source.
- **Historiography**: The "Norman Theory" section correctly identifies the political stakes of the debate (German historians vs Lomonosov vs Modern Synthesis).
- **Decolonization**: The analysis of the "Apostle Andrew" legend as a "claim to equality with Rome" is excellent decolonial framing.
- **Philology**: Breakdown of names (Karl, Ingel, Farlaf) provides irrefutable evidence for the Normanist argument.

### 2. Weaknesses

- **Word Count**: 3000 words is significant, but for a "Seminar" aimed at 4500, it feels slightly condensed. Some fragments (e.g., Vasylko) could be longer.
- **Audit Failures**: The automated audit fails to match the markdown headers to the metadata outline. This suggests a formatting mismatch (Em-dash vs En-dash?) or script limitation.
- **Reading Activity**: The reading activity was refactored to open-ended tasks, which is good, but a dedicated "Close Reading" activity with line-by-line annotation would be better for C1.
