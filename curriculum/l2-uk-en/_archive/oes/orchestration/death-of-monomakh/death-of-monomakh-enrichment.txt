## Section-Mapped Research Notes

### Вступ: 1125 рік — Кінець «Золотої Осені»
1125 marks the death of the last undisputed authority of unified Rus. Monomakh's reign (1113-1125) was a period of stabilization, legal reform (Statute), and defense against Polovtsians.

### «Повчання»: Структура та Зміст
Unique genre: Sermon + Autobiography + Letter. Written for his children, not just as future rulers but as Christians. Key themes: humility, protection of the weak (widows, orphans), strict control over administration (don't let officials abuse power).

### «Повчання»: Мовний Аналіз
Language is closer to the vernacular than standard religious texts. Uses 1st person singular active voice. Contains unique lexical items reflecting daily life (hunting, war, travel).

### Автобіографія: «Шляхи і Лови»
Lists 83 major campaigns. Describes dangerous hunts (thrown by a bull, bitten by a bear). Shows the physical reality of a prince's life — constant movement, "sweat and toil".

### Лист до Олега: Дипломатія і Етика
Written to Oleg Sviatoslavych ("Gorislavich") after Oleg killed Monomakh's son Iziaslav in battle. Instead of vengeance, Monomakh offers peace and asks Oleg to return the body. A masterpiece of medieval diplomatic ethics.

### Смерть на Альті та Поховання
Died May 19, 1125. Location: Alta river (site of Boris and Gleb's murder). Buried in St. Sophia, Kyiv. The chronicle entry is solemn, emphasizing his role as a defender ("prosperous in Rus land").

### Міф про «Шапку Мономаха»
Crucial decolonization section. The "Cap" is the physical embodiment of the "Third Rome" ideology. Moscow needed a physical link to Kyiv and Byzantium to justify its imperial claims. Science proves the cap is Central Asian (Tatar) work, likely a gift from Uzbek Khan to Ivan Kalita or Yuri Danilovich (14th c.).

### Спадщина: Мстислав Великий
Mstyslav (1125-1132) continued his father's policies. He was the last Grand Prince to effectively control Novgorod, Polotsk, and Kyiv simultaneously. After his death, the disintegration became irreversible.
