## Section-Mapped Research Notes

### Вступ
Introduce Vasyl-Kostiantyn Ostrozky (1526-1608). Highlights: "Uncrowned King of Rus", Kyiv Voivode, richest magnate. Set the stage: late XVI century, pressure of Reformation and Counter-Reformation.

### Основний матеріал: I — Політична вага та "Домашня війна"
Focus on his secular power. Estates in Volhynia, Kyiv, Podillia. Defense against Tatars ("protection of Christendom"). His role in the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth Senate. Not just a subject, but a partner/rival to the King. Colonization of the steppe (founding cities).

### Основний матеріал: II — Острозька академія: Атени волинські
Founding of the Academy (c. 1576). The concept of "Trilingualism" (Slavonic, Greek, Latin) — a response to Jesuit education. Intellectual circle: Gerasym Smotrytsky, Cyril Lukaris, Jan Liatos. Curriculum: 7 liberal arts. Impact: educated the elite (Hetman Sahaidachny, Meletiy Smotrytsky).

### Основний матеріал: III — Острозька Біблія як меч духа
The strategic need for a Bible (Protestants and Catholics had theirs; Orthodox relied on fragmented manuscripts). Collaboration with Ivan Fedorov. Textological feat: comparing Greek, Latin, and Slavonic manuscripts. 1581 publication. Significance: standardized the canon, weapon in polemics.

### Основний матеріал: IV — Берестейська унія: Від діалогу до опору
His initial stance: favorable to unity to raise the status of the Ruthenian church vs. Latin rite. The shift: realization that bishops were negotiating secretly and subordinating to Rome without autonomy. The 1596 Brest Synod: splitting into two councils (Uniate and Orthodox). Ostrozky as the secular leader of the anti-Union resistance.

### Основний матеріал: V — Культурна спадщина та канонізація
The "Ostroh Renaissance". Assessment by Hrushevsky and Ohienko. The continuity: Ostroh graduates fueled the Kyiv-Mohyla boom. Modern commemoration: National University of Ostroh Academy revival, canonization in 2008.

### Підсумок
Summarize his dual legacy: a medieval prince with Renaissance ambitions. He proved that Ruthenian culture could be high-brow, intellectual, and distinct from both Warsaw and Moscow.
