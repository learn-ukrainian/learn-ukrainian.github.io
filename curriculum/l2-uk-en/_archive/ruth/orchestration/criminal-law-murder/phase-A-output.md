## Хронологія
- 1468: **Судебник Казимира IV** — early codification of criminal liability, introducing differentiation based on intent.
- 1529: **Перший Литовський Статут** — codified "головщина" rates; distinct penalties for murdering nobles vs. peasants.
- 1566: **Другий Литовський Статут** — expansion of criminal articles, introduction of presumption of innocence elements.
- 1576: **Справа князів Четвертинських** — resonance murder case illustrating the "blood feud" transition to state justice.
- 1588: **Третій Литовський Статут** — final systematization; Art. 1, Section XI establishes strict death penalty ("горло") for premeditated murder of nobles.

## Ключові факти та цитати
- **Terminological Fluidity**: Murder was called `мужобойство`, `забойство`, `убивство`. The outcome was often financial settlement (`головщина`) alongside physical punishment.
- **Quote 1 (Statute 1588, modernized Ruthenian)**: «А єсли бы которій шляхтич... чловека простого стану забил, и был на горячей крови пойман... тогды того шляхтича-убийцу горлом карати» (Demonstrates class-based justice but also the severity of "red-handed" capture).
- **Quote 2 (Concept)**: «Головщина» — the "price of a head". For a noble: 100 kop of groschen. For a peasant: 24 kop. A dog could be valued higher than a peasant in earlier codes, though Statutes standardized peasant value.
- **Procedural Term**: «На гарячій крові» (in flagrante delicto) — changed the jurisdiction and immediacy of the sentence.

## Деколонізаційний контекст
- **Imperial Myth**: Russian historiography claims the Statutes as "Russian Law" (`Русское право`) or emphasizes their "feudal cruelty" to justify later Tsarist/Soviet "liberation".
- **Ukrainian Reality**: The Statutes were written in **Ruthenian (Old Ukrainian)**, not Russian. They represented a synthesis of local tradition and **European** (Roman/Magdeburg) law, far more advanced than the Muscovite *Sudebniks* of the same era (which relied more on arbitrary despotic power). The legal culture of the Hetmanate was built on this foundation, not on Muscovite law.
