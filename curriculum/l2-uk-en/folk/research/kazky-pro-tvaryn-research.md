## Cultural Hooks

1. **Березовський Іван Павлович (1923–1991) — landmark Ukrainian animal tale scholarship** (Wikipedia verified): Ukrainian folklorist, Doctor of Philological Sciences, senior researcher at the Institute of Art Studies, Folklore and Ethnography named after M. Rylsky, Ukrainian Academy of Sciences. Published **«Казки про тварин»** (1986) — the definitive Ukrainian animal tale collection, containing the most widely known animal tales with a scholarly preface. He separately documented the structural typology of animal tale plots. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Березовський_Іван_Павлович

2. **Аарне–Томпсон classification: animal tales = types 1–299** (Wikipedia verified): The Aarne–Thompson international folk tale classification system, developed by Antti Aarne (1910) and expanded by Stith Thompson (1928), designates types **1–299** specifically for animal tales — «описують стосунки тварин як людські» (depict animal relationships as human ones). This is the standard comparative framework. Ukrainian animal tales map directly onto this international typological grid. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Класифікація_Аарне_—_Томпсона

3. **Трикстер у світовому та українському фольклорі** (Wikipedia): «Трикстер — поширений архаїчний персонаж міфологій народів світу... характеризується порушенням загальноприйнятих норм, здатністю легко виходити за моральні межі, що в підсумку сприяє відновленню порядку чи виробленню нових правил.» Wikipedia explicitly names **Лисиця** (and **Лис**) as the Ukrainian trickster — alongside French Reynard, Tibetan Tompa, etc. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Трикстер

4. **Статистика образів тварин в українському фольклорі** (Grade 5, Avramenko 2022, tier 1 textbook): *«Фольклористи нараховують 147 прислів'їв і приказок про вовка, 51 — [про лисицю]»* (Grade 5, p. 84). This single stat anchors the lesson: wolf and fox dominate Ukrainian animal lore quantitatively.

5. **Рукавичка як кумулятивна казка** (Grade 2, Kravcova 2019): Direct textbook attestation of the cumulative structure: animals enter one by one — мишка-шкряботушка → жабка-скрекотушка → (and so on). The tale's question-and-answer dialogue pattern (*«А хто-хто в цій рукавичці?»*) is taught as model dialogue in Grade 2. Source: `2-klas-ukrmova-kravcova-2019-2_s0019`.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Conflating байка with казка про тварин** → байка (literary fable) has a named author, verse form, and explicit moral (мораль); казка про тварин is oral/collective, has no author, and embeds moral in narrative action rather than stating it. Glibov's байки ≠ Рукавичка. Confusion is natural because both use animal characters — address explicitly.

2. **Treating Вовк as the predator-villain** → In Ukrainian animal tales, Вовк is frequently the **fool/victim** (Лисичка and Wolf tales: wolf gets punished for being naive). This differs from Central European traditions where wolf = threat. Ukrainian fox outsmarts wolf; wolf is a foil for the fox's cunning, not a predator. Learners from Western cultural backgrounds default to Western wolf symbolism.

3. **"трикстер/трікстер" spelling and scope** → NOT in VESUM dictionary. Two corpus spellings: «трикстер» (Wikipedia) and «трікстер» (plan, 48 GRAC occurrences). Introduce once with parenthetical, then use consistently. More importantly: frame the trickster as a **social corrective** (restores order via chaos), not merely a "cheater" — the Ukrainian fox's cunning is morally ambiguous, serving social critique rather than pure villainy.

## Decolonized Framing Notes

- **Do NOT mention Kolomenka or any "East Slavic" unified tradition.** Ukrainian animal tale corpus has been documented by Ukrainian scholars (Березовський, Грінченко, Чубинський) as a distinct tradition with Podillyan, Polissia, Hutsul, and Galician regional variants.
- The Soviet/Russian scholarly tradition treated Ukrainian and Russian animal tale corpora as one "East Slavic" whole — this is a political-taxonomic maneuver. Treat Ukrainian animal tales as a self-sufficient primary tradition.
- **Колобок**: While appearing in both Ukrainian and Russian tradition, the Ukrainian Колобок has distinct regional variants. Do not frame it as "borrowed from Russian"; treat it as a shared pre-national Slavic narrative crystallized differently in each tradition.
- The Reynard/European fox tradition comparison is legitimate as a **parallel development** (convergent evolution of the trickster figure), not as a source/derivative relationship.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Vocabulary capitalization rule** (textbook-verified): When naming animal characters in tale analysis prose, capitalize: **Лисиця, Вовк, Ведмідь, Коза** (proper-name function). Use lowercase for the animal species in analytical prose: «лисиця як трикстер».
- **"трикстер" spelling**: Use «трикстер» (Wikipedia orthography) and note «трікстер» as variant in first mention. Do NOT use as standalone Ukrainian word without gloss.
- **Section «Текстовий аналіз»**: All three target tales have textbook attestation — Рукавичка (Grade 2), Колобок (Grade 1), Коза-дереза (needs Grade 2-5 search — likely attested). Grade 2 (Kravcova) provides the Рукавичка dialogue pattern directly.
- **Cumulative structure terminology**: Use «кумулятивна казка» (VESUM verified, 0.22 IPM) and «нанизування персонажів» or «ланцюжкова структура» (chain structure) as accessible alternatives.
- **Section «Підсумок» vocabulary table** should include: тотемізм, трикстер, алегорія, байка, кумулятивна казка, лисиця, антропоморфізм + новостворені терміни: ланцюжкова структура, нанизування, соціальна критика.
- **Aarne–Thompson reference**: Types 1–299 = animal tales. Use as analytical framework in «Текстовий аналіз» section — gives learners an internationally recognized tool for comparative analysis.
- **Word target**: 5000 words. Plan sections total: 750 + 850 + 900 + 850 + 850 + 800 = 5000. No overshoot buffer built in — recommend 1.5× overshoot in content draft (write ~6000–6500) and trim in validation.
