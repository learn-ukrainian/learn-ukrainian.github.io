## State Standard Reference

**No FOLK section exists in `state-standard-2024-mapping.yaml`.** The State Standard 2024 covers language-level tracks A1–C2 only. FOLK is a folklore/literary studies track without a corresponding §reference in the linguistic competence framework.

**Closest alignment by theme**: The module supports State Standard thematic area *«традиції»* (present across A1–C2 themes lists, e.g. B2 §3, lines 2874–3064) — oral literature as expression of national ethical norms and cultural identity.

Grade 11 Glazova asks directly: *«Які моральні цінності бережуть українські народні пісні?»* — exact framing relevant to this module.

## Cultural Hooks

1. **«Удова і три сини» — first recorded ~1805, Poltavshchyna**: Vasyl Lomykovskyi began recording duma texts on the Myrhorod region in 1805. The duma depicts a widow who sacrificed everything for her sons; they drove her away to avoid embarrassment before the gentry; she died on foreign ground without forgiving them. Multiple kobzar recordings by Ostap Veresai (published by Mykola Lysenko) and Filaret Kolessa (1910). *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Удова_і_три_сини_(дума))*

2. **«Дума про сестру та брата» — recorded by Lesia Ukrainka in 1908**: Recorded 25+ times, predominantly Poltavshchyna and Kharkivshchyna (1810–1930). Depicts *суспільно-побутові відносини за феодалізму*. Notably, Lesia Ukrainka personally recorded this duma from kobzar Hnat Tykhonovych Honcharenko in Yalta in summer 1908 — a detail that connects literary canon to oral tradition. *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Дума_про_сестру_та_брата)*

3. **Побутові думи as ethical corpus**: *Соціально-побутові пісні* — defined as epic folk works about economic and political conditions of various social groups, their historical role in shaping Ukrainian society, *«у формуванні національних норм етики й моралі»*. This framing exactly matches the module's focus. *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Українські_соціально-побутові_пісні)*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Побутовий» vs. «буденний»** — learners may conflate *побутовий* (relating to domestic/everyday social life, folk genre label) with *буденний* (mundane, ordinary). In duma studies, *побутовий цикл* is a precise genre classification term, not a synonym for "everyday." → Correct use: *соціально-побутова дума*, not *буденна дума*.

2. **«Прокляття» — register confusion** — learners may use *прокляття* in neutral contexts where *осудження* or *докір* is appropriate. In folk tradition, *материнське прокляття* is a formal performative speech act with cosmic force — not mere scolding. The distinction must be explicit.

3. **«Покара» vs. «покарання»** — *покара* is archaic/literary (IPM 0.18); modern Ukrainian prefers *покарання* (IPM much higher). Folk texts use *покара*; modern commentary uses *покарання*. Content should model both forms with explicit register labeling.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Chronological layer**: The plan correctly identifies побутові думи as a **later layer** (17th–18th century) vs. the невільницькі/лицарські cycles. This should be foregrounded in the Вступ — it explains why the moral concerns shift from battlefield to hearth.
- **«Удова і три сини» plot detail**: Sons expelled the mother so she wouldn't shame them *before the gentry (панами)* — this is a crucial class-inflected detail showing feudal social pressure on family ethics, not just personal ingratitude. It makes the moral richer.
- **Lesia Ukrainka connection**: Her 1908 recording of «Дума про сестру та брата» is a rare case of a canonical Ukrainian writer acting as field ethnographer — use this as an engagement hook.
- **Curses as performative speech**: In folk tradition, *материнське прокляття* is irreversible — the widow in «Удова» dies *without forgiving*. This is ethnographically significant: forgiveness is not obligatory; the curse stands. Content should treat this as a feature of folk ethics, not a moral failing.
- **Gender framing**: The module centers women (widow, sister) as moral agents — a distinctive feature of побутові думи vs. the male-dominated heroic cycle. Frame this as a strength of the genre, not a secondary feature.
- **Decolonization**: The побутовий цикл has no parallel in Russian oral tradition. Imperial/Soviet scholarship either ignored these dumas (they don't fit the "Cossack warrior" stereotype) or classified them as "Little Russian domestic songs." They are in fact sophisticated ethical poetry — Грушевський's *Історія української літератури* Vol. 4 is the authoritative source for this framing.
- **Word «покара»**: Gloss explicitly as archaic literary variant of *покарання* on first appearance. Low frequency (IPM 0.18) signals it will be unfamiliar to learners.
