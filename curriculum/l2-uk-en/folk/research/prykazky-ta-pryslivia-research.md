## Cultural Hooks

1. **Матвій Номис (1823–1900)** — the foundational figure of Ukrainian paremiology. His collection *«Українські приказки, прислів'я і таке інше»* (compiled 1861, published St. Petersburg, 1864) contains over **14,500 sayings and riddles** — still the most authoritative anthology of Ukrainian oral micro-genres. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Матвій_Номис and uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Українські_приказки,_прислів'я_і_таке_інше.

2. **Паремія as genre umbrella** — Ukrainian folkloristics uses *паремія* as the technical umbrella term covering both прислів'я, приказки, and related micro-forms. Пареміографія (collection/systematization) and пареміологія (linguistic/structural analysis) are treated as distinct sub-disciplines of folkloristics. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Пареміографія.

3. **Textbook framing** (Grade 5 Авраменко 2022, Grade 5 Заболотний 2022): Both Tier-1 textbooks describe the прислів'я/приказка pair as «золоті зерна народної мудрості» and note the prefix «при-» etymology — these expressions are used *принагідно* (opportunely, contextually). This is the standard Ukrainian school presentation and should anchor the Вступ section.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Interchangeable use of прислів'я / приказка** → Correct distinction: прислів'я = complete sentence expressing a judgment with moral force (*«Хто не працює, той не їсть»*); приказка = incomplete figurative expression lacking an independent predication (*«Гнатися за двома зайцями»*). Why it happens: both terms are often used loosely in popular usage; even Заболотний notes «суттєвої відмінності нема» — learners correctly sense the fluidity and over-generalise.

2. **Conflating паремія with прислів'я only** → Correct: паремія is the scholarly umbrella (includes прислів'я, приказки, загадки, прокльони, тости). Why it happens: прислів'я is the most culturally salient term and learners map паремія onto it.

3. **Misattributing the anti-authority proverb tradition** → A common error is reading Ukrainian folk skepticism toward lords/clergy as a universal "peasant sentiment" shared with other Slavic traditions. In fact, the specifically Ukrainian corpus has a distinctive density of anti-serfdom and anti-imperial proverbs reflecting the Cossack and Haidamaka social memory — this is a Ukrainian-specific ideological register, not a generic Slavic one.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Textbook alignment**: Grade 5 literature textbooks (Авраменко, Заболотний) are the canonical school treatment. The FOLK module should go significantly deeper — genre theory, poemological structure, ideological content, scholarly history — while echoing the «золоті зерна» framing as a familiar entry point.
- **Anti-authority strand**: The plan specifies «скептицизм щодо панів, попів, урядовців» — this is one of the most culturally significant and decolonially rich aspects. Ukrainian proverbs encode centuries of resistance to Polish magnates, Russian tsarism, and Soviet collectivism. Handle with specificity, not vague "folk skepticism."
- **Binary structure X, а Y**: This is the dominant syntactic template (*«Багатий думає про гроші, а бідний — про хліб»*). The content section on поетика should include a worked structural analysis.
- **Nomis collection (1864)** is the anchor for the пареміологія section. Modern corpus: Електронна база паремій (Інститут народознавства НАН України) can be mentioned as a contemporary counterpart.
- **Decolonized framing**: Soviet folkloristics classified Ukrainian proverbs as «малоросійські» variants of Russian folk wisdom. Post-independence scholarship (Дмитренко, Пазяк) re-established the independent Ukrainian paremiological tradition. This should appear in the пареміологія section without framing Ukrainian proverbs relative to Russian ones — discuss the Ukrainian tradition on its own terms.
- **Word target**: 5,000 words — plan allocates 750+900+850+850+850+800. Recommend writing 5,500–6,000 words (1.1–1.2× overshoot) to ensure gate passage after review trimming.
