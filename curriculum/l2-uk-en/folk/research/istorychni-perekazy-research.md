## Cultural Hooks

1. **Олекса Довбуш (~1700–1745), Карпати** — Wikipedia (uk): "найвідоміший опришківський ватажок у Карпатах." Active in the Carpathian mountains, regarded in folk tradition as a defender of the poor against Polish nobility and Hungarian landlords. His life generated an extensive cycle of перекази, songs, and legends. Folk memory considerably elaborated his biography — making him nearly invincible (characteristically superhuman strength, bullet-proof skin). He was killed in 1745, allegedly betrayed by a woman. The contrast between historical record (a regional brigand executed after capture) and the folk heroic image is a perfect case study for "фольклорне оздоблення vs. історичне ядро."

2. **Устим Кармалюк (1787–1835), Поділля** — Wikipedia (uk): "ватажок селянського руху на Поділлі у 1813—1835 роках. В українському фольклорі — шляхетний розбійник та національний герой." Кармалюк led multiple uprisings across Podillia against Polish landlords under Russian imperial rule. He was caught and exiled to Siberia four times and escaped four times — a detail folk tradition amplified into near-mythological resilience. His story became a symbol of peasant resistance against colonial oppression, and numerous перекази about him circulated across Right-Bank Ukraine. He was eventually shot in 1835.

3. **Козацький фольклор** — Wikipedia (uk) makes a methodologically important distinction: "важливо розрізняти власне козацький фольклор і фольклор про козаків." The Cossack перекази in this module belong to the second category — folk memory *about* the Cossacks created by later generations, not texts produced by Cossacks themselves. The Zaporizhian Sich (XVI–XVIII c.) was dismantled by Catherine II in 1775, after which its mythologization began in earnest.

## Textbook Evidence (for content framing)

From **Grade 5 Avramenko (tier 1)**, p. 18 — direct definition of the genre:
> "У народних переказах відтворено мрії українців та українок про справедливість, перемогу добра над злом. Народні перекази бувають як сумні за змістом, так і жартівливі. У них народ наділяв своїх улюбленців богатирською силою, непереможністю."

From **Grade 6 Avramenko (tier 1)**, p. 219 — dictionary distinction:
> "Легенда — розповідь казкового чи фантастичного характеру про незвичайну подію або життя й діяльність якоїсь особи."

(Key implication: *переказ* anchors in a real event or real person; *легенда* may be purely mythological — this distinction is pedagogically exploitable.)

From **Grade 5 Zabolotnyi (tier 1)**, p. 23–25 — actual Cossack переказ ("Як Сірко переміг татар") provides a model text with pedagogical apparatus: comprehension, analysis, comparison questions.

From **Grade 9 Avramenko (tier 2)**, p. 6:
> "регулярно збирати й записувати їх [фольклорні твори] почали лише у ХVІІІ–ХІХ ст." — crucial for the module's framing of folk memory as *reconstructed* oral history.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **переказ ≠ легенда** — Learners (especially those familiar with Western European literary tradition) will conflate the two. Correction: *переказ* claims real events or persons as its anchor; *легенда* need not. A story about Довбуш is a *переказ*; a story about a dragon guarding a mountain is a *легенда*. The distinction is about claimed historicity, not fantastical content (perекази freely include supernatural elements).

2. **Declension of proper names: Довбуш, Кармалюк** — English speakers often leave Ukrainian proper nouns undeclined. Required forms: *Довбуш → Довбуша (Gen.), Довбушеві (Dat.), Довбушем (Instr.)*; *Кармалюк → Кармалюка, Кармалюкові, Кармалюком*. Learners default to nominative in all positions — a pervasive error with Ukrainian proper nouns.

3. **опришок vs. розбійник** — Learners may use *розбійник* (generic brigand) where the culturally loaded *опришок* (Carpathian social bandit with folk-hero status) is required, losing the specific cultural register. Also: the collective *опришківство* as a movement vs. *опришок* as an individual actor — conflation produces awkward sentences.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Decolonized framing is essential here.** Cossack history and figures like Кармалюк have been systematically appropriated or distorted in Russian imperial and Soviet historiography. Frame the Sich as a proto-democratic self-governing institution (*козацька вольниця*) rather than through the Romantic Russian "wild Cossack" stereotype. Кармалюк's uprisings were against Polish landlords *within the Russian imperial system* — the anti-colonial dimension of his resistance is often suppressed.
- The **"folk memory as resistance"** thesis (Підсумок section) should be framed positively: oral tradition preserved historical consciousness that official imperial historiography (both Polish and Russian) tried to erase, not as a deficiency of written culture.
- **Toponymic legends section**: Ukrainian landscape is saturated with memory-markers — могили (burial mounds), Cross-roads crosses, named oak trees. This is distinctly Ukrainian — the landscape as a mnemonic system. Do not frame through Slavic generalities.
- The **Козацькі перекази** section can use the real textbook переказ "Як Сірко переміг татар" (Grade 5 Zabolotnyi) as a sample text for close reading — it is public domain.
- **Word note:** *переказ* in modern Ukrainian also means "retelling/summary" (e.g., переказ тексту = text retelling) and "money transfer" (банківський переказ). Content must disambiguate clearly in the Вступ section.
