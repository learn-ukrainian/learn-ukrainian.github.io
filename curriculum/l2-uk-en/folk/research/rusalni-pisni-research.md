## Cultural Hooks

**1. Зелені свята — origin and span** (Source: Wikipedia, uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Зелені_свята)
> Зелені свята is a pre-Christian cycle of Slavic holidays encompassing several days before and after Trinity Sunday (Трійця). The name derives from the greening of the earth; Ukrainian folk etymology says "God greened all the land." The week before Trinity is called "зелений," "клечальний," or "русальний." The origins trace to Roman Rosalia (Festival of Roses), which passed to the Balkans via Thrace and into Ukrainian territory, fusing with the Christian Pentecost from the 4th century onward.

**2. Русальний тиждень — ritual calendar and taboos** (Source: Wikipedia, uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Русальний_тиждень)
> Rusalka Week (тиждень після Трійці) ran Monday–Thursday. On the first Thursday after Trinity, rusalky celebrated their own Великдень. Core belief: rusalky exit water and wander fields and forests during rye-flowering (кінець травня – початок червня). Canonical taboos: no swimming (rusalky will drown you), no spinning, no laundry hung outdoors. Protection: carry полин (wormwood/євшан-зілля) or любисток. Houses were decorated with fragrant herbs (лепеха, чебрець, м'ята, полин). On Polissia, rusalky were identified with "діди" (ancestral souls) — they were fed bread baked on holy water left in the fields. Ritual expulsion (проводи русалок) occurred the following Monday, returning rusalky to their domain before the first summer rains.

**3. Canonical texts taught in Grade 6 curriculum** (Source: RAG textbook search — Zabolotnyi 2023, Avramenko 2023, tier 1):
Three canonical rusalni pisni are taught in Grade 6 Ukrainian Literature:
- "У ржі на межі" — ambivalent rusalka image (dangerous/sorrowful)
- "Проведу я русалочку до бору" — ritual expulsion song
- "Ой біжить, біжить мала дівчина" — riddle-based rusalka encounter ending in death by tickling (залоскотала)

Textbook framing (Zabolotnyi): Rusalni pisni belong to the **summer calendar-ritual cycle** (календарно-обрядові пісні літнього циклу), structured around two motifs: (1) worship of sun, water, plants as life-giving forces for farmers; (2) preparation for autumn wedding season.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Rusalka = mermaid"** → Rusalka in Ukrainian folk belief is an **unquiet dead** spirit (душа неупокоєна) — drowned maidens, unbaptized children — NOT a benign sea creature. The Western mermaid (морська діва / сирена) is coastal and fundamentally different. English-speaking learners import Disney/Western mermaid imagery. Frame rusalky as spirits of the boundary (life/death, water/land, spring/summer) first, not as aquatic beings.

2. **Проводи = "farewells" (general meaning) vs. ritual expulsion** → Learners may know проводи as generic "send-off" (e.g., проводи в армію). In ritual context, **проводи русалок** means formal expulsion — returning spirits to their realm to end the dangerous liminal period. The ritual restores cosmic order, not just a social goodbye.

3. **Табу as declinable noun** → Табу is a borrowed indeclinable noun in Ukrainian. Learners with Slavic language backgrounds (or even Ukrainian speakers at lower levels) may attempt to decline it: *"порушення табуа"* → **WRONG**. Correct: *порушення табу* (gen. indeclinable). Same pattern: бюро, кіно, шосе.

4. **Троїцький vs. Трійця timeline confusion** → "Troitsky cycle" (Троїцький цикл) spans the week BEFORE Trinity (Зелений тиждень) AND the week AFTER (Русальний тиждень). Learners often assume the ritual action clusters on Трійця Sunday itself — in fact, the most dangerous period is the week AFTER Trinity, not before.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **Decolonized framing critical:** Soviet-era folklore scholarship systematically suppressed Ukrainian animistic traditions, labeling them "superstitions" (забобони) and assimilating Ukrainian rusalky into a generic East Slavic archetype dominated by Russian sources. Frame rusalky as specifically Ukrainian mythological figures with rich regional variation (forest spirits on Polissia ≠ water spirits on the steppe), independent of any comparative Russian framework.

2. **Regional variation is a strength:** Polissia rusalky = ancestral souls (діди); Podillia/steppe rusalky = drowned maidens specifically. This regional complexity should appear in §2 (Русалки в народній демонології) — it shows the living diversity of Ukrainian folk belief.

3. **Three canonical texts are in Grade 6 textbooks** — content writing can gloss and analyze these directly without permission concerns (folk material, public domain). "Ой біжить, біжить мала дівчина" is particularly rich for §4 (Поетика) and §5 (Текстовий аналіз) — the riddle structure (three unanswerable questions → death) encodes the epistemological danger of crossing into the rusalka's realm.

4. **Зелені свята origin note:** The name may derive from Roman Rosalia via Thrace → Ukraine, OR the name alone was borrowed while the holiday developed independently. This is an honest scholarly debate — don't flatten it. Present both hypotheses.

5. **Word target 5000:** The plan allocates 750+900+850+850+850+800. The Підсумок section (800 words) carrying both a literary-reception survey AND a vocabulary table will be tight — recommend allocating ~600 to prose and ~200 to vocabulary table, or expand the prose portion slightly.

6. **Animistic vocabulary cluster to foreground:** анімізм, тотем, культ предків, заложні мерці, неупокоєна душа, мавка/нявка (synonyms for rusalky on Polissia) — these form the semantic core of §5 (Текстовий аналіз).
