## Cultural Hooks

**Hook 1 — The Ukrainian Illia: Ілько Муровець, not "Муромець"**  
Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ілля_Муромець)

The earliest written reference to the hero is from 1574 — a letter by **Filon Kmita**, a Kyiv nobleman (шляхтич), calling him "Ілія Муравленин" (not Муромець). Austrian diplomat Erich Lassota (1594) visited Kyiv and reported the hero's tomb at a Kyiv church, calling him "Morowlin." Ukrainian scholars, including Valery Shevchuk, identify the hero's origin as **Моровійськ** (now village Морівськ, Chernihiv Oblast, Ukraine), not the Russian city of Murom. The hero's relics are preserved in the **Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra** (Далекі печери). Ukrainian name: Ілько Муровець. This is a powerful decolonization anchor — the first written records are Ukrainian.

**Hook 2 — Dobrynia and the Dragon as Baptism Symbol**  
Source: Чижевський — Історія української літератури (1956), RAG chunk `fbf8bdff_c0165`

> "В старинах він: 1) побиває змія, 2) звільняє від змія небогу Володимира Забаву Путятичну… Теми 1, 3 та 5, безумовно, зв'язані з тим, що Добриня брав активну участь в охрищенні новгородців разом з Путятою… Купання — символ охрищення; змій — символ поганства."

Dobrynia's dragon-fight is not merely an adventure — it encodes the historical Christianization of Novgorod. The Zmiy represents paganism; defeating it symbolizes baptism. This gives Phase B a rich interpretive layer beyond the surface combat narrative.

**Hook 3 — Билини зародились у Києві**  
Source: Історія української культури Т.1 (2001), RAG chunk `294b54e7_c0154`

> "Зародились билини наприкінці першого тисячоліття в Києві, Чернігові, Галичі, Новгороді та в інших містах."

Bylyny originated in Kyivan Rus cities — Kyiv, Chernihiv, Halych — not in the Russian north where they were later recorded. The northern preservation (Karelia, White Sea region) is a survival artifact, not an origin point. This fact directly contradicts Russian appropriationist narratives.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Treating bylyny as Russian folklore** → Correct framing: Bylyny are **Kyivan Rus heroic epic**, originating in Kyiv, Chernihiv, Halych. They were later preserved in northern peripheries (Karelia, Arkhangelsk) after being displaced from Ukraine. Ukrainian kobzars preserved distinct Ukrainian variants (e.g., Zenoviy Shtokalko's recordings of Ілько Муровець). Why it happens: Russian cultural appropriation since the 19th century systematically rebranded this shared heritage as exclusively "Russian."

2. **Confusing «дружина» (retinue) with «дружина» (wife)** → In epic context: **дружина** = prince's warrior retinue (masculine collective). Modern meaning = wife/spouse. Learners will import the modern meaning. Context clue: "дружина князя Володимира" in epic always means warriors. Why it happens: polysemy without context flag.

3. **Patronymic: "Нікітич" vs. "Микитич"** → Ukrainian form is **Добриня Микитич** (from Микита). The form "Нікітич" is a calque from Russian "Никитич." Why it happens: learners encounter the Russian form in widespread pop culture (Russian animated films, etc.) and transfer it directly.

## Decolonization Framing Notes for Phase B

- **Do NOT frame bylyny as "Ukrainian and Russian shared folklore equally."** Frame them as: **Kyivan Rus heroic epic** that originated in Ukrainian lands and was later appropriated by the Muscovite tradition during and after the Mongol period.
- Dobrynia's "Russian" reinterpretation was codified in the **Nikon Chronicle (16th century)** — a Muscovite political document created to support Moscow's claims on Kyiv. This is documented (Wikipedia extract, Добриня Микитич article).
- The hero's Ukrainian name **Ілько Муровець** (from Моровійськ, Chernihiv) is supported by earlier written evidence than any Russian claim.
- Women warriors (полениці) — Настасія Микулічна — demonstrate that the epic tradition had strong female agency; this resists both Russian nationalist and Soviet reductions.
- Use "Київська Русь" — not "Давня Русь" or "Русь" without qualifier.

## Textbook Teaching Context

Grade 8 Avramenko (2025, tier 1) — the highest-trust textbook — explicitly teaches Illia Muromets bylyna under the chapter "Прадавня Україна в дзеркалі літератури." Key pedagogical elements noted:
- **Structure analysis**: зачин → основна частина (with triadic repetition) → кінцівка
- **Stylistic devices**: гіпербола, епітет, інверсія, персоніфікація
- **Authorship question**: народна творчість (collective authorship)

These devices map directly to the plan's "Текстовий аналіз" section on epic formulas.

From Грушевський, chunk `5e7696fa_c0361`:
> "Герой ладить зброю, скикає дружину і йде походом… Його військо гримає кіньми як грім на небі, блискає мечем як ясен місяць, пускає стріли як дробен дощик."
