## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Kyiv-Mohyla Rhetoric Tradition**: The 17th-18th century rhetoric courses (e.g., by Teofan Prokopovych) explicitly taught "ornatus" and logical flow as essential for persuasion, grounding these modern concepts in Ukrainian Baroque tradition.
2. **"Sontsepoklonnyk" Flow**: Mykhailo Kotsiubynskyi is often cited as a master of "impressionistic flow," where syntactic cohesion creates a seamless sensory experience (e.g., in "Intermezzo"), contrasting with the dryer academic coherence.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **The "And Then" Trap**: Overusing "і/потім" instead of varied connectors (водночас, натомість, згодом).
   *Correction*: Use precise logical connectors to show *relationship*, not just sequence.
2. **Theme-Rheme Misalignment**: Placing new information (rheme) at the start of a sentence in Ukrainian, which disrupts the "Given-New" flow respected by flexible word order.
   *Correction*: Ensure the sentence starts with known info (topic) and ends with new info (focus).
3. **Paragraph fragmentation**: Treating paragraphs as random breaks rather than logical units with a distinct micro-theme.
   *Correction*: Each paragraph must have a clear topic sentence and a transition.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Focus on the distinction: Cohesion is visible (grammar/words), Coherence is invisible (logic/mental model).
- Use examples of *bad* text (choppy, disjointed) vs *good* text (flowing) to illustrate points effectively.
- Integrating "Given-New" theory is crucial for C2 learners to master Ukrainian word order nuances beyond simple grammar correctnes.
