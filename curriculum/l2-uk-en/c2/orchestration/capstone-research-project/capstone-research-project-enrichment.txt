## Cultural Hooks

1. **ДСТУ 8302:2015** — Ukraine's national standard for bibliographic references, adopted in 2015 and updated in 2016 (ДСТУ 8302:2015:2016). This is distinctly Ukrainian — it replaced Soviet-era ГОСТ standards after independence, reflecting Ukraine's rejection of Russian academic infrastructure. Ukrainian universities now mandate ДСТУ 8302 compliance; it differs from Western styles (APA, MLA, Chicago) in its formatting of author names (surname before initials), Ukrainian-language descriptors (авт., упоряд., пер.), and Cyrillic sorting conventions.

2. **НАЯО (Національне агентство із забезпечення якості вищої освіти)** — established 2019, Ukraine's independent academic quality watchdog. НАЯО published Рекомендації щодо академічної доброчесності in 2020, which are the binding policy framework for all Ukrainian universities. The agency uses Unicheck (a Ukrainian-developed anti-plagiarism system) alongside international tools. This is culturally significant: Ukraine developed its own infrastructure for academic integrity independently of Russian systems.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Посилання ≠ Бібліографія** → Learners conflate посилання (in-text citation) with the бібліографія (reference list). Correct usage: *у тексті вказується посилання [1], а повний запис — у бібліографії*. Happens because English "reference" covers both, but Ukrainian distinguishes them sharply.

2. **Register collapse into розмовний стиль** → Learners write *дослідники знайшли* (too concrete/colloquial) instead of *дослідники встановили / дійшли висновку / виявили*. Similarly *взяти дані* → *отримати/зібрати дані*; *зробити висновки* → *дійти висновків*. The scientific style has a closed lexical set for epistemic actions — learners must internalize it.

3. **Near-verbatim paraphrase classified as "paraphrasing"** → Replacing individual words while preserving sentence structure (word-swap paraphrase) constitutes plagiarism under НАЯО guidelines. Correct паraphrasing requires restructuring the information, not merely substituting synonyms. This is the most common academic integrity error and is explicitly addressed in НАЯО Рекомендації 2020.
