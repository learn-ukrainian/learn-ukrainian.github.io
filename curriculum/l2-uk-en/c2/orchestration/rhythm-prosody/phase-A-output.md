## Cultural Hooks
1. **Mykhailo Kotsiubynsky ("Intermezzo")**: Often cited as the master of "musical prose". His impressionistic style relies heavily on rhythm and sound association rather than just plot.
2. **Vasyl Stefanyk**: Represents the opposite end of the rhythmic spectrum—expressionist, "chopped" prose with short, heavy sentences that mimic the harsh reality of peasant life.
3. **"Милозвучність" (Euphony)**: A core concept in Ukrainian linguistic identity. The "Law of Euphony" (cherhuvannia u/v, i/y) is not just a grammatical rule but a stylistic imperative to avoid "cacophony" (zbih pryholosnykh).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **"The Monotone Drone"**: Learners often produce sentences of uniform length and structure (Subject-Verb-Object), lacking the rhythmic variety of native prose. → *Correction*: Varying sentence length (short for tension, long for description).
2. **Intonation Transfer**: Transferring English sentence stress (stressing every content word) to Ukrainian, which destroys the "wave-like" melodic contour of the Ukrainian phrase. → *Correction*: Focusing on the *phrase* accent and logical stress.
3. **Euphony Neglect**: Ignoring u/v or i/y alternations in writing, creating hard-to-pronounce clusters. → *Correction*: Conscious editing for euphony.
