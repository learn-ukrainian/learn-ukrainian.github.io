## Common Learner Errors

1. **Uncritical transfer of English SLA theory** → Adapting for Ukrainian morphological richness. Why: English-speaking learners of Ukrainian (our likely audience) are familiar with ESL pedagogy, but Ukrainian has seven cases, rich aspect system, and Cyrillic — none of which English SLA literature (built on SVO languages) adequately addresses. Teachers need Ukrainian-specific methodology.

2. **Misaligned correction priorities: over-correcting morphology, under-correcting pragmatics** → Балансувати між граматичною точністю і комунікативною плавністю. Why: Beginning teachers tend to flag every case ending error while missing pragmatic failures (inappropriate register, culturally odd phrasing) — the opposite of what experienced communicative teachers do.

3. **Treating all learner errors as equivalent** → Розрізняти помилки (systematic, accuracy-affecting) від хиб (slips, performance lapses). Why: Error analysis tradition (Corder 1967) distinguishes errors from mistakes; Ukrainian teachers unfamiliar with this distinction apply uniform correction strategies, which is inefficient and demotivating.
