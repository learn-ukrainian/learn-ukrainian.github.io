## Cultural Hooks

1. **Самвидав — від підпілля до Інтернету**: The Ukrainian word *самвидав* (самостійно видавати — to self-publish) predates the internet by decades — it was born in the 1960s as the underground publishing of silenced voices (Стус, Костенко, Сверстюк). Its seamless adoption as the term for online self-publishing is not coincidental: Ukrainian digital culture has consistently framed online independence as continuation of the dissident tradition. This makes the "democratization of publishing" discussion uniquely charged in Ukrainian context.

2. **ЛітАкцент і Читомо**: Ukraine's two most prominent literary criticism portals — *ЛітАкцент* (litakcent.com, founded 2007) and *Читомо* (chytomo.com, founded 2011) — emerged before and just after the Euromaidan, and both became critical nodes for Ukrainian literary identity during the post-2014 cultural surge. Their model (professional critics + community engagement) is a hybrid between traditional journalism and digital crowdsourcing, and offers a concrete anchor for the "critique online" section.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **цифровий** vs. **числовий**: Learners translate "digital" uniformly from English, misusing *числовий* (= relating to numbers, numerical) where *цифровий* (= digital technology/media) is required. → Correct: *цифровий підпис* (digital signature), *числовий ряд* (numerical sequence). Root confusion: both words share the morpheme *числ-/цифр-* but serve distinct semantic domains.

2. **Preposition with платформа**: Learners use "в платформі" by analogy with "в системі." → Correct: **на платформі** (Kindle, Facebook, etc.) — Ukrainian uses *на* for both physical platforms and digital ones. Phrase: *публікувати на платформі*, *читати на пристрої*.

3. **самвидав gender/indeclinability confusion**: Learners treat *самвидав* as indeclinable (by analogy with clipped compound nouns). → It declines normally as a masculine noun: *самвидаву* (Gen.), *у самвидаві* (Loc.). Collocation: *твори самвидаву*, *видано в самвидаві*.
