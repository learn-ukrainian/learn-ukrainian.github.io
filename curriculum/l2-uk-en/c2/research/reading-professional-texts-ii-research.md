## Cultural Hooks
1. **Source Studies Tradition**: Ukrainian academia places immense weight on "джерелознавство" (source studies), a discipline championed by historians like Mykhailo Hrushevsky and Volodymyr Antonovych, emphasizing the critical verification of every document's origin.
2. **Educational Reform**: The concept of "критичне мислення" (critical thinking) is a cornerstone of the modern "New Ukrainian School" (NUS) reform, explicitly contrasting with the Soviet pedagogical tradition of rote memorization and uncritical acceptance of authority.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Semantic Confusion**: Confusing "критика" (negative judgment/blame) with "критичний аналіз" (objective evaluation/dissection).
   * *Correction*: Emphasize that analysis identifies both strengths and weaknesses.
2. **Passive Voice Overuse**: Using passive constructions to describe an author's argument (e.g., "Було стверджено...") instead of active attribution.
   * *Correction*: Use active academic verbs: "Автор стверджує...", "Дослідник наголошує...", "Стаття демонструє...".
3. **Fact vs. Opinion**: Difficulty distinguishing "факт" (verifiable data) from "судження" (interpretative claim) in dense professional texts.
   * *Correction*: Practice identifying markers of subjectivity (modal words, emotive lexis).

## Notes for Content Writing
- The tone should be highly analytical and professional.
- Use examples from diverse fields (history, sociology, economics) to demonstrate universality.
- Ensure "Critical Annotation" is distinct from a simple summary — it must include evaluation.
