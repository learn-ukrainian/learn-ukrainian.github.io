## Cultural Hooks

1. **Михайло Грушевський as founder of critical source studies (джерелознавство)** — Грушевський (1866–1934) was Head of the Central Rada (1917–1918), author of 2,000+ scholarly works, academic at НТШ у Львові (1897–1913), ВУАН (1923). His insistence on document provenance verification and critical source evaluation established Ukrainian academic standards distinct from imperial Russian historiography. Source: Wikipedia — Грушевський Михайло Сергійович.

2. **Нова українська школа (НУШ) and critical thinking** — The НУШ reform (2017/2018) explicitly shifted Ukrainian education from Soviet "knowledge reproduction" toward active competency-based learning, with critical thinking as a core cross-curricular skill. The reform began in 100 pilot schools in 2017–2018 and rolled out to all first-grade classes from 2018–2019. Source: Wikipedia — Нова українська школа.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Критика" ≠ "критичний аналіз"** — Learners conflate негативне судження (criticism as fault-finding) with об'єктивна аналітична оцінка (critical analysis as systematic evaluation). Learners say "я не хочу критикувати" when asked to write a critical annotation. Fix: distinguish explicitly — критичний аналіз includes positive evaluation, identifies methodology, not just flaws.

2. **Passive attribution instead of active** — C2 learners default to "було стверджено", "зазначається", "вважається" instead of authorial attribution. Correct patterns: "автор стверджує", "дослідниця наголошує", "стаття демонструє". This is §4.3.7-adjacent: transforming impersonal passive into active academic register.

3. **"Перший первинний / другий вторинний" confusion** — Learners confuse першоджерело (primary source = original text) with первинне джерело and struggle to define вторинний текст correctly. Fix: первинне джерело = оригінальний текст/документ; вторинний текст = реферат/анотація/рецензія that re-processes it. Anchor to §4.3.8 explicitly.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Decolonized framing**: The discipline of **джерелознавство** originated with Грушевський and Антонович as explicitly Ukrainian scholarly methodology — frame it as an indigenous Ukrainian intellectual tradition, NOT as derivative of Russian academic practice. Soviet historiography suppressed critical source evaluation in favour of ideological conformity; the НУШ reform's emphasis on critical thinking is a conscious restoration of pre-Soviet Ukrainian intellectual values.

- **"Критичний аналіз" as productive phrase**: Since it has zero GRAC bigram frequency, write it as a living productive expression with varied collocations — do not treat it as jargon or freeze it as a set phrase.

- **Active attribution verbs**: Build a mini-paradigm around author citation verbs — стверджує, наголошує, доводить, демонструє, зазначає, застерігає, критикує, пропонує. This directly addresses Common Error #2 and satisfies §4.3.7.

- **§4.3.5 hook**: Rhetoric in text composition (аргумент → контраргумент → висновок) maps onto Section 3; use this structure explicitly to show learners how professional Ukrainian texts are architecturally organised.

- **Secondary texts taxonomy** (§4.3.8): реферат (summary), анотація (critical annotation), рецензія (full review), повідомлення (informational report) — Section 5 should explain the hierarchy and give a worked example of critical annotation specifically.

- **Педагогічна чесність**: The distinction критика ≠ критичний аналіз is semantically non-obvious in Ukrainian (both share the root) — flag this explicitly in Section 3 as a "semantic trap."

- **Word budget note**: Section 3 (Аналіз та деконструкція аргументації, 1000 words) is the cognitive core and should not be compressed. Sections 1 and 6 are the lightest (600 + 800) and can absorb flex if other sections overshoot.
