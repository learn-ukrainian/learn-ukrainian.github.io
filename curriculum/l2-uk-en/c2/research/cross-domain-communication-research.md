## Cultural Hooks

1. **Науково-популярне видання (Wikipedia)**: «Науково-популярне видання — видання відомостей теоретичних та (чи) експериментальних досліджень у галузі науки, культури і техніки, викладених у формі, зрозумілій читачам-нефахівцям.» — This is the definitional anchor for the module's central skill: transforming scientific → popular-science register. Use as the opening pedagogical reference.

2. **«Куншт» (kunsht.com.ua)**: Ukrainian science-and-culture media platform that exemplifies aesthetic + accuracy balance in cross-domain communication. The plan lists it as a source. It represents a generation of Ukrainian-language science popularizers who emerged post-2014 as part of decolonization of the information space — Ukrainian displacing Russian as the default language of public intellectual discourse. Relevant framing: Куншт demonstrates that науково-популярний style can be distinctly Ukrainian, not a translation of Russian-language content.

3. **INSCRIBE/INSCIENCE conferences**: Not found on Ukrainian Wikipedia — do not fabricate a reference. The plan mentions «Science Kids від INSCIENCE» — treat as a credible named initiative but do not cite Wikipedia as source. Use conditionally or remove if unverifiable.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Calque «знаходити спільну мову»** → Correct: **«порозумітися»** — This is a direct calque from Russian «находить общий язык». Ukrainian speakers naturally use «порозумітися» or «дійти порозуміння». Error occurs because the calque is widespread even among native-ish speakers due to Russian-language media saturation.

2. **Chancellery infinitive noun phrase «здійснювати аналіз»** → Correct: **«аналізувати»** — канцеляризм pattern (noun + generic verb) bloats professional texts. The dynamic verb is stylistically superior in all registers except formal legal documents. Same pattern: «проводити дослідження» → «досліджувати»; «надавати допомогу» → «допомагати».

3. **Loan-word overuse: «детермінувати», «імплементація»** in non-academic contexts — Error: importing Latinate internationalism into popularization contexts where simple Ukrainian equivalents exist: «визначати», «впровадження». C2 learners must recognize the register mismatch, not just avoid the words blindly — context matters (academic paper vs. public lecture).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Register framing**: The module operates at the intersection of науковий and публіцистичний styles. Content must model the transformation itself — exemplify the before/after of a scientific sentence rewritten for a general audience.
- **«Вторинний текст»** is a State Standard term (§4.3.8) — use it explicitly in Section 5 so learners encounter the official terminology.
- **Decolonization angle**: Ukrainian science popularization (Куншт, Світогляд) is not derivative — it has its own aesthetic and intellectual voice. Frame it as a Ukrainian cultural development, particularly post-2014.
- **Frequency mismatch warning**: «авдиторія» (0.21 IPM) is the normative Ukrainian form but rare in the corpus because «аудиторія» (the older form) likely dominates corpus usage. Use «авдиторія» consistently and note it as the normative variant — this is itself a decolonization lesson.
- **Textbook grounding**: Авраменко grade 7 covers публіцистичний стиль; Карамань grade 10 has explicit style transformation exercises; Ворон grade 11 has реферат as secondary text type. Phase B can draw on these pedagogical sequences for activity design.
- **Grammar focus**: The plan lists this as `focus: grammar` / `register: академічний`. Section content should be dense with stylistic comparison examples — parallel sentences in scientific vs. popular-science register, preferably on a single topic (e.g., quantum physics or ecology) to show the transformation in action.
- **Word target**: 5000 words minimum. 1.5× overshoot target = ~7500 words for draft, trimmed during validate. Distribute: Вступ 850 / Методи 1250 / Стратегії 1000 / Взаємодія 1000 / Практика 900 = 5000 total. Each section needs substantive analytical content + worked examples.
