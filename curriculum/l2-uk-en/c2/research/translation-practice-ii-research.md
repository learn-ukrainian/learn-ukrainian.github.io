## Cultural Hooks

1. **ДСТУ і ДП «УкрНДНЦ»**: Державні стандарти України (ДСТУ) існують з 1993 року — immediately after independence, Ukraine began building its own standards body, separating from Soviet GOST norms. The Ukrainian National Standardization Body (ДП «УкрНДНЦ») manages terminology standardization, giving the curriculum a direct post-independence decolonization angle: Ukrainian technical terminology is a deliberate sovereign project, not an inherited Soviet vocabulary.
*(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Державні_стандарти_України)*

2. **Acquis communautaire і євроінтеграція**: The EU's *acquis communautaire* — "надбання спільноти" (fr.) — is the entire body of EU law that Ukraine must translate and harmonize as part of its accession process. After the 2022 EU candidate status grant, the Ukrainian Ministry of Justice and the Verkhovna Rada Translation Unit face the task of translating tens of thousands of EU legal documents. The scale makes Ukrainian legal translation one of the largest single-language terminology creation projects in contemporary Europe.
*(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acquis_communautaire)*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Калькування юридичних термінів** → Correct mapping required
   - *civil servant* → ~~«цивільний слуга»~~ → **«державний службовець»**
   - *legal entity* → ~~«легальна організація»~~ → **«юридична особа»**
   - Why: English-Ukrainian false-friend calques; structural borrowing without semantic adjustment. The Ukrainian legal system has established equivalents that do not mirror English word-for-word.

2. **Хибні друзі перекладача** (false friends)
   - *data* → ~~«дата»~~ (= date in Ukrainian) → **«дані»**
   - *academic* → ~~«академічний»~~ only → **«науковий» / «навчальний»** depending on context
   - *accurate* → ~~«акуратний»~~ (= neat/tidy) → **«точний»**
   - Why: Lexical proximity creates systematic substitution errors; learners default to phonological similarity over semantic accuracy.

3. **Зловживання пасивним станом під впливом канцелярської мови**
   - ~~«Переклад було виконано фахівцем»~~ → **«Фахівець виконав переклад»** / **«Переклад виконав фахівець»**
   - ~~«Документ має бути підписано»~~ → **«Документ треба підписати»**
   - Why: Passive constructions with *бути* + past passive participle are a calque from Russian bureaucratic style (*документ был подписан*); Ukrainian prefers active voice or impersonal infinitive constructions. This is both a Russianism and a register error for Ukrainian professional writing.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **CAT tools**: SDL Trados Studio and MemoQ are the two industry-standard CAT tools. Both have Ukrainian interface localizations. The "пам'ять перекладів" (translation memory) concept is central — content should demonstrate how TM consistency ensures terminological unity across large projects (e.g., a 500-page EU directive).
- **MTPE framing**: Post-editing (постредагування) is VESUM-verified but has very low corpus frequency (5 tokens in GRAC) — treat as an emerging professional term appropriate at C2, worth explicit introduction.
- **Decolonization angle for terminology**: Present Ukrainian technical/legal terminology creation as an active, sovereign process — ДСТУ from 1993, Acquis translation project post-2022 — rather than framing it as adaptation of existing Russian/Soviet vocabulary. Ukrainian terminology is built from Latin/Greek roots + Ukrainian word-formation patterns, paralleling Polish, Czech, and other Central European languages.
- **Register enforcement**: The plan correctly identifies канцелярські пасивні конструкції as a learner error. Content should model active and impersonal Ukrainian alternatives explicitly with before/after contrasts.
- **Section word targets**: The plan allocates 650+1000+1150+850+650+700 = 5000 words exactly. Content generation should target ~5500–5700 words (1.1–1.14× overshoot) to ensure the audit gate passes after any editorial trimming.
- **Acquis communautaire**: The term is borrowed from French, not translated — this is standard practice in Ukrainian legal texts. Content should note this explicitly as an example of how Ukrainian legal terminology sometimes retains source-language terms where no functional Ukrainian equivalent exists.
- **Medical translation ethics**: The section on medical translation (850 words) should anchor the ethical dimension in real case patterns — mistranslation of dosage instructions, Latin drug names in Ukrainian pharmacy documents, ICF (International Classification of Functioning) translation — rather than abstract principles.
