## Cultural Hooks
1. **The Shevchenko Canon Wars**: The evolution of Shevchenko criticism from Soviet "revolutionary democrat" framing to George Grabowicz's "The Poet as Mythmaker", which revolutionized Ukrainian literary studies and sparked heated debates.
2. **The "Visnykivtsi" vs. "Sixtiers"**: Contrast between Dmytro Dontsov's "voluntaristic nationalism" (active, aggressive criticism) and the "Sixtiers" (Ivan Dziuba, Yevhen Sverstiuk) who focused on ethical resistance and humanism through literary analysis.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Criticism vs. Fault-finding**: Confusing *літературна критика* (analytic evaluation) with *критиканство* (petty fault-finding).
   * *Correction*: Criticism is about understanding mechanics and meaning, not just listing flaws.
2. **Subjectivity Overload**: Overusing "Мені не сподобалося..." without objective argumentation.
   * *Correction*: Use "Твір характеризується...", "Автор вдається до..." (focus on the text, not the self).
3. **Terminological Imprecision**: Using *сюжет* (plot) and *фабула* (story/chronology) interchangeably.
   * *Correction*: Distinguish between the chronological events (*fabula*) and their artistic arrangement (*syuzhet*).

## Notes for Content Writing
- Ensure the distinction between *літературознавство* (academic science) and *літературна критика* (operative, often media-based response) is clear but nuanced.
- Use excerpts from Dziuba or Grabowicz as high-level reading examples (complex syntax).
