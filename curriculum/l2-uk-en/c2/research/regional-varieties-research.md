## Cultural Hooks

1. **Тіні забутих предків — Коцюбинський / Параджанов dual canon.** The 1911 novella by Mykhailo Kotsiubynsky was written after his stay on Hutsulshchyna and is saturated with Hutsul dialect vocabulary. Serhiy Paradzhanov's 1965 film adaptation is acknowledged as one of the masterpieces of world cinema and a founding document of the Ukrainian poetic cinema movement — both works demonstrate how dialect can be a medium of high art, not a defect. Source: Wikipedia (uk) — «Тіні забутих предків» (повість).

2. **Гуцульський говір as archaic preserver.** Wikipedia (uk) confirms: *"Гуцульський говір — один з архаїчних говорів галицько-буковинської групи південно-західного наріччя."* The Hutsul dialect preserves phonological and lexical features lost in the literary standard — it is living linguistic archaeology, not a corrupted form. This is the core cultural reframe from deficiency to richness.

3. **Котляревський's Eneida and the Poltava basis.** Ivan Kotlyarevsky's *Eneida* (1798), written in the Poltava-Kharkiv vernacular (South-Eastern dialect group), is the landmark that established the polyphonic South-Eastern variety as the basis for the emerging literary standard. This was not an arbitrary choice — it reflected the demographic weight and prestige of the region.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **файний ≠ суржик.** Learners (and native speakers) often label Western dialectisms like *файний* (fine/good, from German *fein*) as surzhyk. Error: *"Він сказав «файний» — це суржик."* Correction: *файний* is an attested word in the South-Western dialect group with a documented German-origin etymology; it appears in VESUM. It is a dialect feature, not language mixing.

2. **Hypercorrection as over-purism.** Learners who become aware of Russianisms sometimes over-correct by rejecting genuine Slavic roots preserved in Ukrainian: e.g., *ждати* (to wait), *держати* (to hold), *хата* (house). These words are natively Ukrainian, documented in dialects, and appear in classical literature. The plan's content outline explicitly addresses this (*"розрізнення реальних русизмів та спільних слов'янських коренів"*).

3. **Misidentifying the literary standard's dialectal source.** Learners often assume the standard is based on Western (Galician) speech, since Galicia had the strongest publishing tradition in the 19th c. In fact, the phonological and morphological base is South-Eastern (Poltava-Dnipro corridor). Galician literary tradition influenced the lexicon and register, but not the core phonology.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **ізоглоса (IPM 0.00)** is near-academic-only. Introduce it with a definitional sentence the first time; do not assume learner exposure. The compound phrase *пучок ізоглос* (bundle of isoglosses) is the standard technical form.

2. **Three-naricchia diagram as anchor.** Phase B content should open with a conceptual map of the three groups with their geographic territories mapped to modern oblasts — this is explicitly in the plan's first section and aligns with §4.3.3's requirement to handle dialectisms stylistically.

3. **Decolonized framing note:** Soviet dialectology systematically subordinated Ukrainian dialect diversity to a Russian-centric framework (portraying Ukrainian as a "dialect continuum" of East Slavic biased toward Russian). C2 content should frame each dialect group autonomously, comparing to Polish (SW borrowings), Romanian/Hungarian (Hutsul lexicon), and Belarusian (North) rather than to Russian when comparative framing is needed.

4. **гіперкорекція / полісизм flag:** Phase B content may use these terms in prose but should not list them as bold vocabulary items since they are not in VESUM. Alternatively, use circumlocution: *явище гіперкорекції* (the phenomenon of hypercorrection), *поліська лексема* (Polissian lexical item).

5. **Surzhyk section requires care:** Frame surzhyk as a product of colonial language policy (Russification, Soviet-era displacement), not as a personal failing of speakers. The C2 learner should understand the sociolinguistic causes — not just the linguistic symptoms.
