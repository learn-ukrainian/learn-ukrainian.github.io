## Cultural Hooks

1. **Хрещатик як урбаністичний символ** (Wikipedia confirmed): Головна вулиця Києва (1.3 км) від Європейської площі через Майдан Незалежності до Бессарабської площі — rebuilt after WWII in Stalinist imperial style, now a living exhibit of contested architectural heritage and public space politics. The same boulevard hosts the Kyiv City Council, symbolizing the inseparability of urban planning and political power.

2. **Декомунізація топоніміки** (Wikipedia confirmed): Процес декомунізації в Україні, що стихійно активізувався під час "Ленінопаду" (Euromaidan 2013–14), is legally anchored in the 2015 decommunization laws. Over 52,000 streets and localities were renamed 2015–2016 — the largest topographic renaming in European peacetime history. This directly connects to §3.17 "місця пам'яті та пам'ятники" and feeds the practical workshop section.

3. **Урбанізація XX ст.** (Wikipedia confirmed): Урбанізаційні процеси в Україні найбільш активно протікали у XX ст. — Soviet industrialization drove mass rural-to-urban migration, creating the massive хрущовки/панельна забудова belt that defines most Ukrainian cities today. Post-1991 suburbanization reversed part of this flow, creating the дачна культура and cotteji (котеджі) boom.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **`оренда` ← Russianism trap `аренда`** → Correct: `оренда`, `орендувати`, `орендар`. The Russian form `аренда` is widely seen in informal Ukrainian text; learners will encounter it and need to recognize it as a Russianism to avoid.

2. **`забудова` vs `будівля`** → `забудова` = the pattern/process of construction/development (collective or ongoing: `житлова забудова`, `незаконна забудова`); `будівля` = a single building (count noun). Learners from English conflate both with "building." Context: `нова забудова мікрорайону` (the development of a micro-district) ≠ `нова будівля` (a new building).

3. **`перепланування` vs `планування`** → The prefix `пере-` signals modification of an existing layout, requiring legal permits (`дозвіл на перепланування`). English speakers often use `планування` for both; in real-estate/legal contexts, using the wrong term changes the legal meaning of a sentence.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Decolonized framing**: Frame Ukrainian urban planning as an independent tradition with its own historiography — Cossack Hetmanate capitals (Бату́рин, Глухів), Greek-founded Black Sea cities (Одеса, Миколаїв), Galician town planning under Austro-Hungarian influence (Lviv grid + Ringstrasse influence). Do NOT use Soviet urban planning as the neutral baseline; frame радянська забудова as one historical layer among many, not as the default.
- **Wartime reconstruction** is now a live policy topic — integrate references to відбудова (reconstruction) as part of the urban planning section; this gives the module genuine contemporary urgency.
- **ОСББ** (Об'єднання співвласників багатоквартирного будинку): This concept has no direct English-speaker analogue (HOA is close but legally different). Give it explicit unpacking — it is both a vocabulary item and a civic governance concept.
- **ревіталізація** (124 ipm, very low): Flag this explicitly — it is an authentic term in current Ukrainian urban discourse (Одеса, Харків, Запоріжжя industrial zone projects) but learners should understand its recent borrowing from Polish/English urban planning discourse. Do not avoid it — it is the correct specialist term.
- **Register spectrum** across sections: Section 2 (housing market) → офіційно-діловий; Section 3 (renovation) → розмовний + публіцистичний; Section 4 (urban planning) → науковий + офіційний; Section 5 (heritage) → культурологічний/публіцистичний; Section 6 (rural/suburban) → соціологічний + розмовний. The Practical Workshop should require production across at least 2 registers.
- **Word count note**: At 5,000-word target with 7 sections averaging ~700 words each, the content generator should target 5,500 words as a safe overshoot buffer to absorb validation trim.
