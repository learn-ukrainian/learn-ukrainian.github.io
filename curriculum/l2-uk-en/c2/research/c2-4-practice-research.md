## Cultural Hooks

1. **Семикутник стилів** — Ukrainian State Standard 2024 codifies seven functional styles (including епістолярний — epistolary — unique among major European state educational standards, reflecting Ukraine's deep literary-letter tradition from Сковорода through Леся Українка). This makes Ukrainian style taxonomy richer than most comparative language curricula, and the register transformation exercise in this module is grounded in a specifically Ukrainian stylistic framework, not a generic European one.
   *Source: §4.3.1.1, lines 5666–5673, UKRAINIAN-STATE-STANDARD-2024.txt*

2. **Дебати як жанр громадянської освіти** — Parliamentary debate (парламентські дебати) re-emerged as a formal educational genre in Ukraine after 1991 and gained significant institutional weight post-Майдан 2014, when civic discourse training became part of school methodology. The Ukrainian debate format draws on both Polish parliamentary traditions and the Anglo-American competitive debate model, creating a hybrid genre that learners at C2 level are expected to navigate. The debate simulation section of this module can anchor itself in this specifically Ukrainian civic-discourse tradition.
   *Source: Академічне письмо (uk.wikipedia.org); general educational methodology context*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Регістрова інтерференція всередині тексту** → Mixing register markers within a single production: e.g., using розмовний lexis (типу, значить, ну) inside a науковий essay, or канцелярит constructions (у зв'язку з вищезазначеним) in a journalistic piece. Happens because learners apply vocabulary at the sentence level rather than monitoring register consistency across the whole text.

2. **Структурні кальки з радянського академічного письма** → Learners producing "academic" Ukrainian that mirrors Soviet Russian essay templates: linear argument stacking without thematic integration, avoidance of першоособового погляду, and formulaic conclusions (таким чином, можна зробити висновок, що…). Ukrainian academic writing post-2014 has developed a more direct, author-present analytical style; the integrated essay section must model this.

3. **Крос-тематична поверховість** → When tasked with integrating multiple thematic domains, learners tend to list references to different topics rather than genuinely synthesizing them: e.g., "Тема 3.1 і тема 3.9 обидві важливі, тому що…" instead of constructing an argument where evidence from migration patterns (3.9) structurally supports a claim about family transformation (3.1). The cross-thematic analysis section must distinguish surface enumeration from genuine analytical integration.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **C2-level activity constraint**: The plan's `activity_hints` include `fill-in` and `group-sort` which the level quick-ref marks as "not used at C2." Phase B must substitute with `critical-analysis`, `comparative-study`, or `essay-response` variants. Flag this for content generation prompt.
- **Six-section structure maps cleanly**: each of the six content sections can be a self-contained pedagogical unit (intro → analysis → transformation → essay → debate → portfolio), giving clear narrative architecture.
- **1.5× overshoot target**: With a 5000-word minimum, content phase should aim for 5500–6000 words to pass gate reliably. Section allocation (700+900+900+900+800+800 = 5000) is tight — each section should skew toward its upper bound.
- **No Russian baseline comparisons**: Register transformation comparisons should use Polish (3 functional styles codified), Portuguese, or French (5 styles) as reference points — never Russian.
- **крос-тематичний introduction**: Since this term is unverified in VESUM, content phase should introduce it formally: *"крос-тематичний аналіз (аналіз явищ на перетині кількох тематичних доменів)"* with explicit definitional anchor on first use.
- **самодіагностика rarity (freq: 27)**: Term is real but unfamiliar. Content phase should briefly gloss it: *"самодіагностика — виявлення власних прогалин у знаннях чи вміннях"* before using idiomatically.
- **Decolonized framing**: The module covers post-2014 Ukrainian civic and academic culture. Frame the debate simulation in terms of Ukrainian parliamentary procedure and civic discourse history, not Soviet-era patterns.
