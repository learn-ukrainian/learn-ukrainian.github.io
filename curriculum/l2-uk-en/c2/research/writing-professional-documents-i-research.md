## Cultural Hooks

1. **ДСТУ 3008:2015** — «Інформація та документація. Звіти у сфері науки і техніки. Структура та правила оформлювання» — the Ukrainian national standard establishing mandatory structural elements and formatting rules for scientific and technical reports. It superseded the 1995 version (ДСТУ 3008-95), which itself was harmonised with ISO 5966:1982. Crucially, dissertation formatting requirements in Ukrainian universities are based on ДСТУ 3008 — making it a real-world document every C2 learner in an academic or professional environment will encounter. *(Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia, uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/ДСТУ_3008:2015)*

2. **Реферат as double-meaning cultural artifact** — Ukrainian Wikipedia confirms the semantic distinction: "якщо в анотації наводиться лише короткий перелік питань, що розглядаються, то в рефераті викладається сутність питань та наводяться найважливіші висновки." In Ukrainian academic culture, "реферат" is simultaneously a student essay genre (literature overview) and a rigorous scholarly genre (informative synthesis of primary sources). This duality — familiar but technically distinct — is a genuine cultural hook that sets up the lesson's core disambiguation work. *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Реферування)*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Згідно інструкції" (Genitive) → "згідно з інструкцією" (Instrumental + з)** — Ukrainian requires the preposition "з" + Instrumental ("згідно з наказом, згідно з вимогами ДСТУ"). The error is a calque from Russian "согласно инструкции" (Genitive, no preposition). Occurs at near-native levels where learners unconsciously import professional register patterns from Russian-language documents they read at work.

2. **Conflating реферат and анотація** → Реферат = "spoiler" (full synthesis: content + conclusions); Анотація = "advertisement" (brief characteristic of the work, not its content). A telling diagnostic: if a learner's "анотація" includes conclusions or data from the text, it has become a реферат.

3. **Subjective constructions in academic register** → "Я вважаю / на мою думку / ми прийшли до висновку" in official/scientific texts. Correct: impersonal passive forms on -но/-то — "встановлено", "досліджено", "зазначено", "вважається", "варто зазначити". This is a stylistic register error, not a grammatical one — it signals insufficient internalisation of the офіційний/науковий style norms.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Textbook grounding**: Grade 7 Litvinova (tier 1) introduces вторинні тексти taxonomy; Grade 10 Karaman (tier 2) has dedicated Topic 17 on анотація and Topic 12 on конспектування — these are the pedagogical precursors this C2 module synthesises and exceeds. Content should explicitly position itself as professionalising what learners encountered at B1-B2.
- **ДСТУ structural elements to name explicitly**: титульний аркуш, зміст, перелік умовних позначень, вступ, основна частина, висновки, список використаних джерел — these are the mandatory elements of ДСТУ 3008:2015 and must appear by name.
- **Decolonised framing**: Do NOT frame impersonal passive (-но/-то forms) as "unlike Russian". Frame positively: Ukrainian scientific style has a distinctive impersonal passive in -но/-то (досліджено, проведено) that signals objective professional register — a feature shared with Polish academic conventions ("zbadano", "stwierdzono"). This is a Ukrainian grammatical resource, not a Russian avoidance strategy.
- **Register table**: Content writing should include a clear contrastive table — Розмовне / Офіційне мовлення — to anchor the практика of de-subjectivisation. E.g., "Я думаю, що…" → "Вважається, що…" / "Встановлено, що…"
- **Frequency note**: "Згідно" (133 IPM) and "відповідно" (167 IPM) are extremely high-frequency items in professional Ukrainian — this grammar point has enormous real-world payoff at C2 level.
- **Annotation length standard**: 500–600 друкованих знаків (characters, not words) is the established Ukrainian convention for анотація length — include this concrete benchmark.
