## Cultural Hooks

**1. Prozorro — народжена з Майдану, визнана світом**

- Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prozorro
- Prozorro (укр. *прозоро* — відкрито/transparently) is Ukraine's electronic public procurement system, born from volunteer activism during Революція Гідності (2014). A volunteer team led by Oleksandr Starodubtsev built the first prototype for $35,000 from six electronic platforms.
- Mandatory for all state bodies from 1 August 2016. Saved **150 billion hryvnias** over four years; 43.5 billion in 2020 alone (43% year-on-year increase).
- Three core principles: *Усі бачать усе / Конкуренція понад усе / Золотий трикутник партнерства* (state + business + civil society).
- International recognition: World Procurement Awards 2016 (best public-sector solution), Open Government Awards 2016 (Paris), ranked #1 in Transparent Public Procurement Rating 2020 (97.05/100). European Bank for Reconstruction and Development named it the recommended model for e-procurement reform.
- **Pedagogical angle**: Prozorro is both a vocabulary-rich cultural hook and a live working context — learners writing тендерна пропозиція are engaging with a globally respected Ukrainian institutional innovation, not a Soviet legacy system.

**2. Дерадянізація ділової мови — офіційний курс**

- The shift from passive bureaucratic constructions («нами було вирішено», «документ підготовлено відділом») to active agency («ми вирішили», «відділ підготував») is a documented policy direction in Ukrainian language reform post-2014.
- The National Agency for Civil Service (НАДС) guidelines and Ukrainian Language Commissioner's materials explicitly promote active constructions in official correspondence as part of the broader normalization/decolonization of Ukrainian business language.
- **Pedagogical angle**: This is not a stylistic preference but an institutional deradicalization initiative — framing it this way gives learners agency and motivation.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«прийняти заходи» → «вжити заходів»** — Russian calque from *принять меры*. In Ukrainian, the correct verb is *вжити* (perfective), not *прийняти*. The noun takes genitive plural: *заходів*. Frequency data: «вжити» = 13.98/M confirms this is the productive Ukrainian form.

2. **«нанести шкоду» → «завдати шкоди»** — Russian calque from *нанести ущерб*. Ukrainian uses *завдати* + genitive (*шкоди*, not accusative *шкоду*). This is a persistent interference error at C1–C2 because both verbs are high-frequency.

3. **«на протязі» → «протягом»** — Russian calque from *на протяжении*. «На протязі» is a Russianism; «протягом» is the correct Ukrainian preposition/adverb. Confirmed in VESUM as both adv and prep forms. Particularly common in formal written Ukrainian — students who have absorbed Soviet-era document templates reproduce this error.

4. **Passive overuse** — «нами було вирішено / документ підготовлено відділом» instead of active constructions. This is a Soviet bureaucratic register feature (канцеляризм) that the module explicitly targets in Section 1. At C2, learners need to understand the difference between *legitimate* канцеляризми («згідно з...», «відповідно до...» — §4.3.3) and *Radyanska* passive overuse that signals institutional deference rather than professional authority.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Prozorro vocabulary density**: Section 3 (Бізнес-планування) and Section 5 (Тендерна документація) should make heavy use of verified Prozorro terminology: *тендерна документація, тендерна пропозиція, тендерний комітет, публічні закупівлі, електронний аукціон, відкриті торги, конкурентний діалог, переговорна процедура, уповноважена особа*. These are all confirmed in the Wikipedia extract as current legal terminology.

- **Pain-Solution-Benefit framing (Section 2)**: The model maps well onto Ukrainian professional culture. Use concrete Ukrainian-language examples, not translated American business templates. Consider a Ukrainian IT-company scenario or an NGO grant proposal as worked examples.

- **Amazon memo vs. slides (Section 4)**: Use the cultural hook that Amazon-style memos (аналітична записка) resemble Ukrainian *доповідні записки* — a native genre already in the learner's repertoire from reading-professional-texts modules. This creates a conceptual bridge.

- **Kantselerizmy nuance**: §4.3.3 of the State Standard explicitly includes «згідно з…; відповідно до…; у зв'язку з…» as C2 lexical competence items. The module must distinguish: these formulaic openers are *legitimate* official style markers — the problem is *passive overuse and vagueness*, not official vocabulary per se. Frame deradянізація as activating agent/agency, not as avoiding formal registers.

- **Word budget note**: At 5000-word target, the five sections average 1000 words each (plan assigns 850/1150/1000/1000/1000). The longest section (Anatomy of a Proposal, 1150) warrants additional grammar paradigm tables or extended annotated examples to hit its target without padding.

- **Decolonized framing**: Ukrainian professional discourse has deep roots in Ukrainian business culture that pre-dates and runs parallel to Soviet bureaucratic practice — notably, the Cossack hetmanate administrative documents (universaly) show Ukrainian formal written tradition. Post-Maidan language reform has actively reclaimed native lexical resources. Content should present Ukrainian business language as a fully autonomous system with its own grammar of authority and persuasion.
