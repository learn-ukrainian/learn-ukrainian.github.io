## Cultural Hooks

**1. Юрій Шевельов (1909–2002) — Ukrainian slavist and linguist**
Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (`Шевельов Юрій Володимирович`)
- Born Georg Shevelov in Kharkiv; later used pseudonym Юрій Шерех
- Professor at Harvard, then Columbia University (Ukrainian and Russian linguistics)
- Foreign member of NAS Ukraine (1991); Shevchenko National Prize laureate (2000)
- His monumental work *A Historical Phonology of the Ukrainian Language* (1979) established Ukrainian phonological history as an independent field — not derived from Church Slavonic or from "Old Russian"
- His concept of «синтетичність» (syntheticity) argues that Ukrainian's strength lies in its capacity to synthesize diverse strata: archaic, folk, literary, scholarly, and modern colloquial — all within a single coherent system. This makes Ukrainian exceptionally rich for stylistic manipulation
- Relevance to module: The C2 learner is, in Shevelov's framework, learning to consciously deploy this synthetic capacity — what he called the mark of a cultivated native speaker

**2. ЗНО / НМТ as a standardisation mechanism**
Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (`Зовнішнє незалежне оцінювання`, `Національний мультипредметний тест`)
- ЗНО (External Independent Evaluation) introduced to eliminate corruption in Ukrainian university admissions — a sovereignty-affirming reform
- НМТ (National Multipurpose Test) introduced in 2022 as a temporary wartime replacement; planned to run 2022–2026
- Ukrainian language is a core ЗНО/НМТ subject — the test codifies and reinforces the literary standard (norm). This makes ЗНО a de facto arbiter of modern Ukrainian language norms, influencing what counts as «правильно»
- Relevance to module: The ЗНО Ukrainian section (морфологія, синтаксис, стилістика) represents the operationalised C1 standard — diagnosing gaps against ЗНО criteria is a culturally meaningful and pedagogically precise framework for learners at the C1→C2 transition

## Common Learner Errors

**1. Passive voice calquing (калькування пасивного стану)**
Pattern: «Було вирішено», «Є потреба» (English: "It was decided", "There is a need")
→ Correct alternatives: «Ми вирішили» (active), «Рішення ухвалено» (impersonal), «Треба» / «Потрібно»
Why: English L1 speakers default to passive constructions; Ukrainian stylistic norms strongly prefer active or impersonal constructions in most registers

**2. Prepositional governance errors (хибне прийменникове керування)**
Pattern: «враження про щось» (calque of English "impression about"), «хворіти грипом» instead of «хворіти на грип»
→ Correct: «враження від» (genitive-governed), «дякувати другові» not «дякувати друга»
Textbook attestation: Glazova Gr.11 §14 — «насміхатися з кого» (not «над ким»), «вибачати кому» (not «кого»)
Why: Ukrainian verb governance patterns diverge significantly from both English and contact varieties; C1 learners who acquired these patterns receptively often produce errors under production pressure

**3. Register mismatch (стилістичний дисонанс)**
Pattern: Mixing colloquial lexemes (класний, супер, по-любому, де-факто unreflectively) into academic or official register text
→ Correct approach: deliberate register awareness — colloquialisms as marked choices in publicistic style, not accidental contamination
Why: Advanced L2 learners often over-rely on colloquial vocabulary acquired through media exposure; the C2 diagnostic must surface this gap explicitly

**4. Дієприслівникові звороти (participial phrase errors)**
Pattern: Dangling participial constructions (copying English gerund-phrase structure) or wrong aspect in gerundive (e.g., using perfective gerund where action is simultaneous)
→ Correct: Perfective gerund (зробивши) = prior action; Imperfective gerund (роблячи) = simultaneous action
Why: English has no grammatical aspect — L2 learners conflate these

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **§4.3.7 is the core deliverable**: every content section should orient toward text transformation competency. The diagnostic sections exist to identify *why* transformation fails (grammar gaps) and *how* (register awareness).
2. **Shevelov's «синтетичність»**: This concept should be presented as a Ukrainian-centric framework — not a comparison to any other Slavic language. Ukrainian's synthetic capacity is intrinsic, not relative.
3. **ЗНО framing**: Use ЗНО/НМТ as a culturally grounded diagnostic benchmark — not as an end goal, but as a shared reference point that contextualises what C1 mastery looks like. C2 moves beyond ЗНО.
4. **Governance table**: The Grade 8 Avramenko and Grade 11 Glazova textbooks provide verified governance pair tables (дякувати другові, вибачати кому, хворіти на грип). These should be used as the source for example pairs in the grammar diagnostics section.
5. **Word target**: 5,000 words minimum. Plan section allocations total 5,000 (500+1000+1250+1000+750+500). Write to 1.2× overshoot (~6,000) to give Phase B buffer.
6. **Decolonized framing**: Shevelov explicitly argued for the autonomy of Ukrainian phonological and morphological development from Church Slavonic and any putative "Eastern Slavic" common ancestor defined through Muscovite norms. Frame all linguistic phenomena positively in Ukrainian terms.
7. **Калькування** section requires extra care: present the error pattern empathetically (it is a natural transfer from English) while clearly demonstrating the Ukrainian-native alternatives. The pedagogical tone at C2 is collegial, not corrective.
