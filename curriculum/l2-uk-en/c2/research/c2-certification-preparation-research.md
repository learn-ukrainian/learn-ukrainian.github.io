## Cultural Hooks

1. **Перший Стандарт «Українська мова як іноземна. Рівні загального володіння А1–С2»** — approved by the Національна комісія зі стандартів державної мови on **31 липня 2024 року**. This is a landmark: Ukraine's first officially codified language proficiency framework aligned with CEFR. The Standard that this curriculum maps to entered force less than two years ago. Source: Wikipedia — *Національна комісія зі стандартів державної мови*.

2. **Обов'язковий іспит з 16 липня 2021 року** — Art. 7 and 11 of the Law "Про забезпечення функціонування української мови як державної" made the language exam mandatory for civil service positions and citizenship acquisition. The state certificate (державний сертифікат) is **безстроковий** (valid indefinitely). Re-examination is permitted no more than once every four months. Results are delivered within 15 calendar days. Exam tasks are approved by the Commission itself. Source: Wikipedia — *Національна комісія зі стандартів державної мови*.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"здавати/брати екзамен" → "складати іспит"** — Lexical interference from Russian ("сдавать экзамен") and English ("take an exam"). The normative Ukrainian verb-noun collocation is "складати іспит" (impf, process) / "скласти іспит" (pf, result). The GRAC corpus confirms these as dominant collocations. "Здавати іспит" occurs in the corpus but carries informal/interference register — unacceptable in official exam writing.

2. **Aspect confusion: "складати" vs "скласти"** — Advanced learners often default to the perfective where imperfective is required (describing the ongoing process of taking an exam) and vice versa. "Він складає іспит зараз" (impf, ongoing) vs "Він успішно склав іспит" (pf, completed with result). This aspectual precision is exactly what the C2 exam evaluates.

3. **Register collapse in written production** — Under exam stress, learners mix colloquial calques, surzhyk, and official register. Typical: writing "по відношенню до" (Russian calque: по отношению к) instead of "щодо"; "приймати участь" instead of "брати участь"; "у відповідності до" instead of "відповідно до". The exam essay section explicitly targets this register control.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **The "складати/скласти іспит" distinction is the linguistic spine of §3 (Мовна компетенція)**. Build this out as a worked contrast with multiple authentic examples from exam instructions rather than invented sentences — this shows the word in its real institutional habitat.
- **The 2024 Standard launch date (31 July 2024)** is a strong opening hook for §1 (Вступ). Learners are preparing for an exam under a framework that is itself brand new — this adds urgency and historical significance.
- **"Поріг проходження"** is an institutional fixed phrase. The GRAC collocations show "поріг" mostly collocates with literal/metaphorical meanings (переступити поріг, стояти на порозі). The "поріг проходження" phrase needs to be presented as a compound term, not treated as compositional. Frequency 0.78 IPM for "сертифікація" signals this is specialist vocabulary — appropriate contextualization needed.
- **Decolonization framing:** Frame Ukrainian certification as an autonomous national system that preceded and is independent of any Russian model. The Комісія was established in 2019 and developed its own criteria aligned with CEFR (Council of Europe), not Soviet-era frameworks. Ukrainian "складати іспит" is native; Russian interference forms should be marked as such without Russian as baseline.
- **The безстроковий certificate** is pedagogically important — it removes retake anxiety and reframes the exam as a one-time milestone, not a recurring obligation. This supports the psychological resilience section (§5).
- **Word target 5000**: Content outline totals 750 + 1000 + 1100 + 1100 + 1050 = 5000 words — exactly at target. Write to 5500+ to ensure post-formatting count clears the gate.
