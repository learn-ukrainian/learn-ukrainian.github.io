## Cultural Hooks

1. **Сергій Жадан** (verified via Ukrainian Wikipedia): Poet, prose writer, frontman of «Жадан і Собаки», author of *Ворошиловград*, *Інтернат*, *Месопотамія*. From March 2024, serving in Brigade «Хартія» of the National Guard of Ukraine. His prose is widely cited by Ukrainian linguists as a masterclass in literary stylisation of authentic spoken Ukrainian — ellipsis-heavy dialogue, emotional parcellation, natural particle use. The plan's reference to his dialogues as a model for living colloquial Ukrainian is pedagogically sound and culturally resonant.

2. **Львівська ґвара** (verified via Ukrainian Wikipedia): Urban dialect/linguistic phenomenon formed in Lviv from mixing southwestern Ukrainian dialects with Polish, with significant German and Jewish lexical elements from the Austrian and Polish periods. Ukrainian Wikipedia identifies it as a «міська говірка, урбаністичний лінгвофеномен». Key examples: *файний* (fine/good), *цьото* (this), *бо* (because, emphatic), *таки* (indeed, emphatic particle). The ґвара exemplifies how urban contact varieties enrich colloquial registers without becoming surzhyk — a productive contrast for C2 analysis.

3. **Суржик** (verified via Ukrainian Wikipedia): «елементи двох або кількох мов, об'єднані штучно, без дотримання норм літературної мови.» The term originated primarily for Ukrainian-Russian contact. Key distinction for the module: суржик is uncontrolled mixing that violates norms; просторіччя is a legitimate normative register. The pair «зрозумів» vs. «поняв» (from the plan) is an ideal minimal pair: «поняв» is a Russianism incompatible with Ukrainian norms; «зрозумів» is the native Ukrainian form.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Conflating суржик with просторіччя** → Correct frame: просторіччя is a normative part of the literary language; суржик is a structurally non-normative mix. Learners who equate all informal speech with «суржик» over-correct and produce stilted, artificially formal register. Error source: metalinguistic confusion, often reinforced by prescriptivist attitudes.

2. **Flat intonation / English intonation transfer** → Ukrainian spoken intonation has a characteristic **висхідно-спадна** (rise-fall) contour on questions and emotional exclamations that differs from English final-rise questions. English L1 speakers impose rising terminals on Ukrainian questions, making speech sound either uncertain or foreign. Example: «Де ти був?» — Ukrainian: strong rise on «ти», sharp fall on «був»; English-influenced: sustained rise through «був».

3. **Over-explicit ellipsis avoidance** → English pragmatics tolerates longer confirmatory responses; Ukrainian colloquial dialogue strongly prefers ellipsis. Learners say «Так, я піду» where native speakers say «Піду» or «Та піду вже». Textbook source (Заболотний, Grade 8, tier 1): «У неповному реченні пропущено один або декілька необхідних членів речення, які можна встановити з тексту або із ситуації спілкування» — learners need training to *produce* not just *recognise* these structures.

4. **Particle function confusion** → «Ну» treated as a pure filler (word parasite) rather than a pragmatic marker with fatic and hedging functions. Similarly, «ж/же» used as Russian-calqued emphasis rather than Ukrainian logical continuity marker. Result: either particle-free speech (formal, unnatural) or overloaded speech with functionless «ну, це, типу».

## Notes for Content Writing

- The **власне / зрештою** frequency data (68 and 32 IPM) strongly supports foregrounding these as the module's core practical takeaways — more frequent than most grammatical terms the learner has studied.
- Grade 8 Заболотний (tier 1) defines неповні речення clearly and provides the dialogue-exchange paradigm («Куди підеш завтра?» – «У басейн»); cite this for the ellipsis section's pedagogical grounding.
- **Decolonized framing note**: Ukrainian colloquial style has its own typological profile (particle system, intonation, ellipsis patterns) that stands on its own — the суржик problem should be framed as a contact linguistics phenomenon, not as "Ukrainian being corrupted by Russian." The positive frame is: Ukrainian has rich colloquial resources that learners must actively acquire.
- Жадан's prose predates his 2024 military service; *Ворошиловград* (2010) and *Інтернат* (2017) are the canonical sources for his dialogue style. Both are set in eastern Ukrainian contexts, making them culturally significant as well as linguistically rich.
- **Register note for Phase B**: The module teaches *about* colloquial style using academic register (as required at C2), while analysing examples drawn from colloquial/literary sources. This meta-register split must be explicit in the introduction.
