**Alignment:** This module addresses §4.3.3 directly. C2 requires *complete* lexical stylistics mastery — all tropes and figures, authorial neologisms, wordplay — at the level of production and analytical commentary, not just recognition. The plan correctly positions paronyms and synonymy as the deepest practical challenge, with §4.3.3 providing the authoritative inventory of devices to cover.

**Note on §4.3.3 vs §4.3.4:** Paronyms are not explicitly itemised in §4.3.3 of the State Standard, but they are thoroughly covered in the NUS Grade 5 (Zabotnyi) and Grade 11 (Avramenko) textbooks as a component of лексична норма / стилістична норма. ## Cultural Hooks

**1. «Репресовані синоніми» 1930-х років (verified pattern, not single Wikipedia article)**

After 1933, the Stalinist language reform reversed Skrypnyk's Ukrainianisation policy. Soviet editorial commissions systematically removed words considered «too Ukrainian» from dictionaries — specifically those that had no Russian analogue and could not be collapsed into a Soviet-Russian norm. Words like **обрій** and **виднокіл** (both = horizon; the former being the native Ukrainian formation, the latter a calque of the native root) were restricted. The 1934 orthographic reform and subsequent dictionary revisions pruned entire synonymic rows, pushing Ukrainian toward Russian lexical structures. This is documentable through the comparison of the 1927 «Правопис» (Харківський) with the 1933–1936 «Правопис» editions. The plan's cultural hook is historically sound and pedagogically powerful.

**2. Борис Грінченко — «Словник» (1907–1909) as the benchmark of Ukrainian lexical richness**

Verified via Wikipedia: Борис Дмитрович Грінченко (1863–1910) — «укладач чотиритомного тлумачного «Словаря української мови»». This four-volume dictionary, compiled under Tsarist censorship, documented thousands of folk lexical items including granular environmental vocabulary (wind types, rain nuances, seasonal terms). It serves as the counterargument to Soviet-era claims of Ukrainian «poverty» — the richness was always there and was actively suppressed, not absent.

*Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia, article «Грінченко Борис Дмитрович»*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Paronymic confusion: воєнний / військовий** → Correct distinction: `воєнний` refers to war as a phenomenon (воєнний стан, воєнний злочин); `військовий` refers to the armed forces as institution (військова служба, військовий підрозділ). Error type: semantic narrowing through interference. Confirmed in Grade 5 Zabolotnyi textbook as a taught pair.

2. **Calque pleonasm: «рахувати за необхідне»** → The verb `рахувати` (= to count, to tally) is used in Russian (`считать`) with epistemic meaning ("to consider"). In Ukrainian, `вважати за необхідне` is correct. This is one of the most persistent calques in educated Ukrainian writers. The module addresses it directly — Phase B must give a full worked example.

3. **Lexical pleonasm with foreign words: «вільна вакансія», «прейскурант цін», «пам'ятний сувенір»** → These are pleonasms because the modifier restates the meaning already encoded in the loanword (vakansiya = vacant position; preyskurant = price list; souvenir = memory object). Error source: Ukrainian public discourse importing Russian bureaucratic/commercial formulae wholesale. These examples are confirmed in Grade 11 Avramenko as taught material.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **литота — unverified form.** Use only if confirmed in SUM-11. If not confirmed, refer to the concept as «художнє применшення» or cite it as «литота (художнє зменшення)».
2. **The paronym section (1300 words) is the anchor.** It must include worked examples with full before/after sentences, not just isolated word pairs. Confirmed by textbook practice (Zabolotnyi, Glazova both use sentence-level examples).
3. **«Репресовані синоніми» hook** is historical/cultural, not linguistic-technical — write it as a 3–4 sentence narrative (story format) before moving into the grammar. This is the emotional entry point for the lesson.
4. **Grінченко's Словник** should be cited with full title: «Словник української мови» (1907–1909), 4 volumes — not as a modern dictionary. Contrast with Soviet dictionaries of the 1930s–1950s to show deliberate narrowing.
5. **C2 activity constraint:** The plan's `activity_hints` include `fill-in`, `match-up`, `error-correction`, `group-sort` — but the C2 quick-ref explicitly prohibits these types. Phase B activities must use `critical-analysis`, `essay-response`, `reading`, `comparative-study`, `quiz` (native-level complexity only). **This discrepancy between `activity_hints` and `activities.types_required` must be resolved in Phase B — follow `activities.types_required`, which lists the correct C2 types.**
6. **Decolonised framing:** The synonymic richness of Ukrainian is an inherent feature of the language, documented by Grínchenko before any Soviet intervention. Frame the 1930s events as *destruction of an existing richness*, not as Ukrainian catching up to any other standard.
