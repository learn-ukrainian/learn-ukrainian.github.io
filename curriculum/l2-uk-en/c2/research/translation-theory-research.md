Alignment: Translation is a fundamental form of secondary text creation, requiring the ability to re-code information from a source language to a target language while maintaining stylistic integrity (§4.3.7 "Уміння трансформувати текст з одного стилю в інший"). This module builds the theoretical foundation for high-level text transformation and cross-linguistic mediation.

## Cultural Hooks
1. **Mykola Lukash ("Mozart of Translation")**: Known for translating "The Decameron" and "Don Quixote" with unparalleled lexical richness, often reviving archaic Ukrainian terms to match the era of the original.
2. **Hryhoriy Kochur**: The "Patriarch" of Ukrainian translation, who continued translating world classics even while imprisoned in the Gulag, mentoring a generation of translators (the "Kochur School").
3. **The "Executed Renaissance"**: Many Ukrainian translators (Pidmohylny, Zerov) were executed in the 1930s specifically because their translations connected Ukraine to Europe, bypassing Russian cultural mediation.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Calquing Syntax**: Mirroring English passive voice (e.g., «Книга була написана ним» instead of «Книгу написав він») — violates Ukrainian stylistic norms.
2. **False Friends**: Mistranslating terms like *pathetic* (жалюгідний, not патетичний) or *sympathy* (співчуття, not симпатія).
3. **Register Mismatch**: Translating English academic hedging ("It suggests that...") too directly/weakly, or using formal vocabulary for English colloquialisms.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Focus on the tension between "faithfulness" to the original and "naturalness" in Ukrainian.
- Use examples from famous Ukrainian translations (e.g., Hamlet by Kochur vs. Andrukhovych) to illustrate different strategies.
- Emphasize that translation is not just word-swapping but cultural mediation.
