## Cultural Hooks

1. **Децентралізація reform (2014–present)** — Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia.
   The decentralization reform began in 2014 with the goal of creating "спроможних територіальних громад" (capable territorial communities). The reform transferred authority and resources from central state organs to local self-government. By 2020, Ukraine consolidated ~11,000 local councils into ~1,470 merged communities (об'єднані територіальні громади). This is one of the most concrete post-Maidan reforms, making it an ideal analytical text anchor.

2. **НАБУ (Національне антикорупційне бюро України)** — Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia.
   НАБУ is defined as "центральний орган виконавчої влади із спеціальним статусом" — it investigates and prosecutes corruption crimes. Established in 2015 alongside НАЗК (National Agency for Corruption Prevention) and САП (Спеціалізована антикорупційна прокуратура, 2015). This anti-corruption architecture formed as a direct EU/IMF conditionality requirement post-2014. The three-agency structure (НАБУ + НАЗК + САП) gives learners a concrete institutional framework for the корупція section.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **гілка vs. галузь** → Correct: *гілка влади* (branch of power) — "галузь влади" is a common Russianism-influenced error (from "отрасль"). In Ukrainian constitutional discourse, only *гілка* is used. Learners often import "галузь" from the economics domain.

2. **законодавство vs. \*законодательство** → Correct: *законодавство* — The Russianism *законодательство* is calqued directly and appears in learner writing at B2+. In Ukrainian, the systematic term is *законодавство* (noun) / *законодавчий* (adj).

3. **Register confusion: публіцистичний vs. академічний** → C2 learners must navigate between these registers. Публіцистичний uses emotional intensifiers (*нагальна потреба, гострі проблеми*) while академічний prefers nominal constructions (*реалізація принципу, забезпечення умов*). Mixing registers in an analytical essay is a diagnostic C2 error.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **безчинство replacement**: Use *свавілля* (arbitrariness, lawlessness) or *зловживання владою* as VESUM-verified alternatives. Or keep безчинство but treat it as legal register term requiring VESUM manual override — investigate first.
2. **ґендерна рівність grounding**: Art. 24 of the Constitution of Ukraine explicitly prohibits discrimination by sex and mandates equality — this is the primary legislative hook. The module can quote it directly.
3. **Wartime framing**: The internal displacement (ВПО) section must be handled with care — use confirmed statistics from УВКБ ООН (UNHCR Ukraine) or Центр Разумкова rather than unverified numbers. Avoid stating specific IDP counts from memory.
4. **НАБУ/НАЗК/САП triad**: Present as a post-2014 institutional achievement, not just a problem list. Ukrainian civil society actively built these institutions — the decolonized framing celebrates this agency.
5. **Громадянський патріотизм vs. nationalism**: The State Standard explicitly includes *патріотизм* (§3.7). Content should distinguish громадянський патріотизм (civic attachment to democratic institutions) from ethnic nationalism — a key discursive distinction in contemporary Ukrainian political science.
6. **Section word budget**: 600+800+800+700+700+700+700 = 5,000 — exactly at target. No buffer. Phase B must write to the upper end of each section's range to reliably exceed the 5,000-word audit gate. Recommend targeting 5,200–5,400 actual words.
