## Cultural Hooks

1. **Диспути Києво-Могилянської академії (1632–1817)**: The Academy — founded by Metropolitan Petro Mohyla, one of the greatest centres of learning in Eastern Europe — used disputatio as its core pedagogical method. Students publicly defended theses against opponents using formal argumentation structures. This is the direct institutional ancestor of structured academic debate in Ukrainian intellectual culture, predating and independent of Russian academic traditions (it drew on Polish Jesuit scholasticism and Byzantine rhetorical heritage). Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Києво-Могилянська_академія_(1632—1817).

2. **Феофан Прокопович і риторичний курс (1705–1709)**: Прокопович delivered his rhetoric course at Kyiv-Mohyla Academy 1705–1709 while rector (1710–1716); the lectures were published in Kyiv in 1979 (3-volume edition). This is the foundational Ukrainian rhetorical treatise cited in the plan. **Decolonization note**: Wikipedia describes Прокопович as "Ukrainian and Russian" — this is Soviet colonial framing. His intellectual formation and rhetorical work are entirely within the Ukrainian Baroque academic tradition. His later service to Peter I represents political circumstance, not his scholarly identity. Frame him as a Ukrainian scholar of the Kyiv-Mohyla Academy. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Феофан_Прокопович.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Підміна тези" ≠ calque from English** → Learners translate "straw man" literally as ~~"солом'яна людина"~~ or ~~"солом'яний аргумент"~~. Correct Ukrainian term: **підміна тези**. Why: direct English calquing; the Ukrainian term is a rhetorical concept name, not a metaphor.

2. **Prepositional rection with "переконати"** → Learners write ~~"переконати когось *про* щось"~~ (Russian calque: убедить *в* чём-то → calqued to English "about"). Correct: **переконати когось *у/в* чомусь** (переконати аудиторію *в* правоті позиції). Why: Russian prepositional pattern intrudes; the Ukrainian governs the locative via *у/в*.

3. **"Диспут" vs "дискусія" vs "дебати"** → Learners conflate these. Key distinctions (confirmed by Grade 10 Glazova textbook): **диспут** = formally prepared public disputation on scientific/social/literary topic (academic conferences, educational institutions); **дебати** = dispute over proposed positions/programmes with opposing teams; **дискусія** = open-ended discussion without fixed opposing sides. The plan's debate formats section must make these distinctions explicit.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Prokopovych dispositio**: The plan maps classical dispositio (exordium → narratio → argumentatio → peroratio) to Ukrainian communication. Textbook Grade 10 Karaman confirms the three-part structure (вступ → основна частина → висновок) in current classroom use. The Latin terms can be introduced as scholarly labels alongside their Ukrainian equivalents — consistent with C2 academic register.

- **Toulmin model labelling**: The plan uses Ukrainian equivalents — теза (claim), підстава (grounds), обґрунтування (warrant), підтримка (backing), застереження (qualifier), спростування (rebuttal). All six Ukrainian terms are VESUM-verified. Content should introduce the model diagrammatically with numbered Ukrainian labels.

- **Frequency register mismatch — красномовство/ентимема**: Both are low-frequency specialist terms (0.31 and 0.01 IPM). They should be flagged as elevated/scholarly vocabulary, not active production targets. Include in vocabulary table as *recommended*, not required.

- **Sofisms section**: Grade 10 Glazova explicitly covers "підміна тези" as a debate technique. The "Різновиди суперечки" section provides classroom-tested Ukrainian terminology for logical fallacies. Content writer should draw on this textbook framing.

- **Decolonization framing**: The Kyiv-Mohyla Academy disputatio tradition should be presented as Ukraine's own indigenous contribution to European academic debate culture (via Polish Baroque scholasticism + Byzantine heritage) — not as a borrowing from or precursor to Russian academic tradition. The civic debate formats (публічні слухання, круглий стіл) should be framed in the context of Ukrainian democratic development post-1991 and the Maidan civic culture.

- **Word target**: 5000 words — recommend writing 5500–5800 to ensure gate passage. Section most likely to undershoot is Практикум (400 target) — expand with detailed worked example of a mini-debate exercise.
