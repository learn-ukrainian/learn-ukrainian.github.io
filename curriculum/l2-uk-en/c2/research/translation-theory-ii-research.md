## Cultural Hooks

**1. «Енеїда» Котляревського (1798) — domestication as cultural act of resistance**
Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Енеїда_(Котляревський)
Котляревський transformed Virgil's Latin epic into a Ukrainian burlesque-travestied poem (бурлескно-травестійна поема), replacing Roman gods and heroes with Ukrainian Cossack archetypes, traditions, and folk humor. Written in чотиристопний ямб. Published in 1798 — widely considered the founding text of modern Ukrainian literary language. This is not a «translation» by academic criteria, but a radical domestication: the source plot becomes a vehicle for Ukrainian cultural self-assertion. Pedagogically valuable: it lets learners debate where domestication ends and transformation begins.

**2. Микола Лукаш (1919–1988) — foreignization as ethical act**
Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Лукаш_Микола_Олексійович
Polyglot (20+ languages), шістдесятник, translator of Goethe's Faust, Boccaccio's Decameron, Cervantes' Don Quixote, Flaubert's Madame Bovary. Lukash's method: preserve the stylistic specificity of the original through archaic Ukrainian vocabulary and dialectalisms — a deliberate foreignization. The Soviet apparatus pressured him to produce smoother, more «Soviet-standard» Russian-leaning translations; Lukash refused, and his work was suppressed. This makes him the Ukrainian paradigm case for the foreignization/domestication debate under conditions of cultural occupation.

**Supporting figure**: Григорій Кочур (1908–1994) — перекладач, поет, літературознавець, шістдесятник. Together with Lukash, formed the core of the Kyiv translation school. Wikipedia article for «Київська школа перекладу» does not exist as a standalone entry — use individual biographical articles.

**Supporting theorist**: Коптілов Віктор Вікторович (1930–2009) — Ukrainian linguist, translation theorist, translator. Laureate of the Lukash Prize (2001) and Rylsky Prize (2000). His textbook «Теорія і практика перекладу» is the canonical Ukrainian-language translation studies text.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Калькування: «приймати участь» → «брати участь»** — Direct structural calque from Russian «принимать участие». This is the most diagnostic false-friend in Ukrainian professional writing. Plan explicitly calls this out as a drill point. Other examples: «відноситись» → «належати/стосуватися», «заключати договір» → «укладати договір».

2. **Стилістичний дисонанс: канцеляризми в художньому тексті** — Learners who have studied formal Ukrainian (bureaucratic register) default to nominalization-heavy, passive constructions when writing prose: «здійснення перекладу» → «переклад», «в цілях забезпечення» → «щоб забезпечити». This registers as Soviet-era bureaucratic Ukrainian, not natural literary language.

3. **Гіперкорекція очуження** — Advanced learners, aware of Russianisms, over-correct by rejecting all words shared between Ukrainian and other Slavic languages. Result: stilted pseudo-archaic or pseudo-dialectal text. The correct principle: use the word that is natural in Ukrainian regardless of whether it also exists in Russian.

4. **False equivalence: еквівалентність vs. адекватність** — Learners conflate the two terms. Correct distinction: еквівалентність = structural/semantic correspondence between source and target; адекватність = fitness of translation for its communicative purpose. A translation can be fully adequate but only partially equivalent (e.g., poetic compression).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **«Невидимість» перекладача (Venuti)**: Frame this concept through Lukash's suppression — the Soviet demand for Venuti's «domesticating invisibility» (translator disappears into natural target-language prose) was weaponized to erase Ukrainian stylistic distinctiveness. The Ukrainian tradition of foreignization (Lukash/Kochur) is therefore an act of cultural resistance, not mere aesthetic choice. This gives the decolonization section an empirically grounded anchor.
- **Nida's terminology**: The plan uses «формальна еквівалентність» / «динамічна еквівалентність». These are Nida's own English terms. In Ukrainian scholarship, «динамічна» is established; note that some Ukrainian sources prefer «функціональна еквівалентність» for Nida's second category. Use «динамічна» as primary (matches plan vocabulary_hints) with a note.
- **Section word targets**: 900+1000+1100+1000+1000 = 5000 — exactly at target. Apply 1.5× overshoot rule: aim for ~5500–6000 words in content generation.
- **Activities gate — no fill-in/match-up at C2**: Plan's `activity_hints` list match-up, fill-in, group-sort — these are NOT used at C2 (per level constraints quick-ref). Activities must be: quiz, essay-response, critical-analysis, comparative-study, reading, true-false. Flag this discrepancy now so Phase B content writer uses correct types.
- **«Приймати участь» drill**: Plan includes this as an explicit error-correction drill under Section 4. It is factually correct as a Russianism — confirmed common error in Ukrainian professional writing.
- **Decolonized framing**: Never frame Ukrainian translation tradition as «responding to» or «departing from» Russian norms. Frame it as an autonomous tradition with its own theoretical lineage: Kotlyarevsky (1798) → Franko (turn-of-century) → Lukash/Kochur (1960s) → contemporary scholars.
