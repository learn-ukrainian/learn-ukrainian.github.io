## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Repressed" Grammar (1930s)**: The standardization of Ukrainian grammar in the 20th century was political. The "Kharkiv Spelling" (1928) reflected organic Ukrainian features that were later removed to bring the language closer to Russian. Discussing "archaic" or "dialectal" forms often touches on these recovered norms (e.g., *и* at the beginning of words, genitive ending *-и* for feminine nouns like *радости*).
2. **Euphony as a Grammatical Law**: In Ukrainian, euphony (милозвучність) isn't just aesthetic; it dictates grammatical choices (у/в, і/й forms) and word order. It's a key differentiator from other Slavic languages.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Passive Voice Overuse**: Calquing English structures like "It was decided by the committee" (Було вирішено комітетом) instead of the active Ukrainian "Комітет вирішив" or impersonal "Комітетом вирішено".
2. **Case Government**: Confusion with verbs requiring specific cases distinct from English/Russian logic (e.g., *дякувати* requires Dative, not Accusative; *вибачати* requires Dative).
3. **Deverbal Noun Formation**: Incorrect suffix choice (-ння vs -ення) or stress patterns in abstract nouns (e.g., *навча́ння* vs *на́вчання*).
