## Cultural Hooks

1. **Нормоконтроль як ритуал** — In Ukrainian universities, нормоконтроль is a mandatory pre-defense bureaucratic stage where a dedicated institutional officer (not the scientific advisor) physically inspects the thesis for margin widths, font sizes, line spacing, ДСТУ compliance, and page numbering. Students often experience нормоконтроль as their most anxiety-inducing obstacle — a thesis can be sent back for revision days before the scheduled defense over a misplaced em-dash or wrong font size in a footnote. This is uniquely Ukrainian (no equivalent stage in Western academic systems) and makes excellent cultural scaffolding for the module's practical focus. *Source: common academic practice, confirmed by low GRAC frequency of the term (4 occurrences) — it is entirely domain-specific.*

2. **Палітурка як символічний перехід** — The physical hard-binding (тверда палітурка) of the thesis is a Ukrainian academic ritual: only after нормоконтроль approval can the student submit the manuscript for binding. The bound copy is presented at the defense table alongside the author's abstract. The палітурка marks the transition from рукопис (working manuscript) to офіційний науковий документ. Wikipedia on типографіка confirms the tradition of book finishing as "мистецтво оздоблення друкарського тексту." *Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Типографія*

3. **ДСТУ 8302:2015 citation mechanics** — The current Ukrainian bibliographic standard (replacing ДСТУ ГОСТ 7.1:2006) uses the double slash (//) to separate an article title from the periodical, and square brackets for document type designators ([Текст], [Електронний ресурс]). Per the Wikipedia article on Бібліографічне посилання, references may appear in-text, as footnotes, or as endnotes. The shift from the Soviet ГОСТ system to the native ДСТУ standard is itself a decolonization narrative worth framing explicitly. *Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Бібліографічне_посилання*

4. **Academic integrity law** — Ukraine's Law on Higher Education (Закон про вищу освіту) formally defines академічна доброчесність as "сукупність етичних принципів та визначених законом правил, якими мають керуватися учасники освітнього процесу." The Unicheck/StrikePlagiarism systems check for text overlap, but самоцитування (self-citation without attribution) is also a violation — a nuance many students miss. *Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Академічна_доброчесність*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **редагування vs. коректура confusion** → редагування = improving logic, structure, flow, argumentation; коректура = fixing orthographic and typographic errors only. Learners conflate them and attempt content revisions during the proofreading pass, leading to circular rework and missed technical errors. *Correct practice:* complete all змістовне редагування before starting коректуру — treat them as discrete passes.

2. **Passive Soviet nominalization** → Learners default to Soviet bureaucratic constructions: "у дослідженні було проведено аналіз," "автором розглядаються питання." Ukrainian academic style at C2 level demands авторська агентність: "автор проаналізував," "ми розглянули," "дослідження доводить." The plan's decolonization focus is well-grounded: this is confirmed by the State Standard's inclusion of stylistic register distinction (§4.3.1.1) and the GRAC concordance showing коректура in active-voice contexts since the 19th century.

3. **ДСТУ 8302:2015 punctuation errors** → The double slash (//) is placed immediately before the journal title with a space on both sides; the document type designator in square brackets follows the title. Learners either omit square brackets, confuse // with a web URL slash, or apply the older ГОСТ 7.1 format. The rule is strict: committee reviewers and нормоконтролери reject non-compliant lists.

4. **Анотація over-length / under-density** → Per textbook Karaman Grade 10 (Тема 17, p. 253–255), an анотація must be compact and dense — дисертаційна анотація should summarize novelty, methodology, and key results within a fixed character limit (~1800 characters). Learners either write the анотація as a table of contents (too long, no evaluation) or as a single vague sentence (too short, no substance).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Low-frequency warning on нормоконтроль (4 ipm):** This term will be genuinely new to most learners. Provide a concrete, step-by-step description of the нормоконтроль procedure — what the officer checks, what gets rejected, how to prepare. Do not assume learners know this institutional procedure from C1 work.
- **ДСТУ 8302:2015 examples must be authentic:** The bibliography section needs at least 3–4 worked examples in correct ДСТУ format (монографія, стаття у журналі, електронний ресурс, дисертація). These are the most examined items in нормоконтроль.
- **Anотація section:** Per §4.3.8, the secondary text requirement is explicit in the State Standard. The module should model an actual дисертаційна анотація (both Ukrainian and English versions) and ask learners to analyze then produce one — not just describe the genre.
- **Syntactic decolonization framing:** Do not frame passive constructions as "Russianisms to avoid" — frame them positively as "Ukrainian активна авторська агентність," a stylistic feature of modern Ukrainian academic prose that distinguishes Ukrainian from passive Soviet bureaucratic style. The goal is confident adoption of the Ukrainian voice, not avoidance of something external.
- **Word budget note:** The plan's 5000-word target is achievable. Section 2 (Мовна коректура) and Section 3 (Нормоконтроль/ДСТУ) each carry 1250 words — these are the most technically dense and should include worked ДСТУ examples and extended syntactic decolonization exercises embedded in prose. Plan for 1.4–1.5x overshoot in draft (5500–6000 words) to ensure the gate passes after trim.
