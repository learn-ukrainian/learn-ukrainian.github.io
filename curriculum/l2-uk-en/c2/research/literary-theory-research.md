## Cultural Hooks

1. **Олександр Потебня (1835–1891)** — Ukrainian philologist, founder of the Kharkiv Linguistic School and the psycholinguistic approach to literary theory. Potebnia's theory that the literary work has the same tripartite structure as the word (зміст / зовнішня форма / внутрішня форма) anticipated structuralist insights by several decades and gave Ukrainian philology a distinctive voice in the European scholarly conversation. His work "Думка та мова" (1862) remains a foundational text. The Institute of Linguistics at the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine bears his name. *(Source: Інститут мовознавства ім. О.О.Потебні; Wikipedia Ukrainian edition)*

2. **Іван Франко як теоретик літератури** — Franko's essay "Із секретів поетичної творчості" (1898) was one of the first systematic Ukrainian works on poetics and literary psychology, synthesizing European theories (Taine's positivism, Spencer's evolutionary aesthetics, psychological approaches) with Ukrainian material. Franko was also the first Ukrainian literary scholar to develop a concept of "national literature" with defined criteria — predating many 20th-century theorists. His scholarly legacy (literary criticism) occupies 18 volumes of his 50-volume collected works. *(Source: md-eksperiment.org; VUE — Велика українська енциклопедія)*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Літературознавство" vs. "літературна критика"**: Learners (especially those coming from academic English contexts) conflate the scholarly discipline (літературознавство — the science) with literary criticism as a practice/genre (критика). In Ukrainian academic usage, these are distinct: літературознавство covers theory + history + criticism; критика is the evaluative/journalistic branch. Correct: *"Формалізм є напрямом у літературознавстві"* — Wrong: *"Формалізм є напрямом літературної критики"*.

2. **Register confusion with "наратив" vs. "оповідь"**: High-register learners sometimes use "наратив" as a catch-all (borrowed from English "narrative") even in contexts where the native Ukrainian "оповідь," "розповідь," or "оповідання" would be more appropriate. At C2, the distinction matters: "наратив" is a theoretical/semiotic concept; "оповідь" refers to the concrete act of telling. Learners should use both correctly in context.

3. **Misattributing formalism to Ukraine's intellectual tradition**: A common framing error is to present the "Russian Formalists" (ОПОЯЗ) as the sole origin point of the formalist tradition without recognizing the broader Slavic context that includes Prague Circle contributions (Mukařovský, the émigré Jakobson) and Potebnia's psycholinguistic precursors. At C2, learners should be able to situate formalism within a pluralized history, not a single national school.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The module is **100% Ukrainian immersion** (C2 requirement). All metalanguage must be in Ukrainian, including explanations of Western theoretical concepts (Derrida, Foucault, Saussure).
- The **Ukrainian literary studies section (§6)** should be the richest section — this is where the course differentiates itself from generic literary theory teaching and grounds the abstract in Ukrainian intellectual heritage.
- **Потебня's theory** can serve as an anchor across the module: introduced in §1 as a preview, developed in §6. This creates an organic Ukrainian-first framing.
- Avoid presenting formalism as "Russian" without qualification — frame it as a **Slavic scholarly movement** with Prague and Kyiv/Kharkiv dimensions alongside Moscow/St. Petersburg.
- The **postcolonial section** should make explicit reference to Ukrainian scholars (Грабович, Забужко, Hundorova) rather than defaulting to Bhabha/Said/Spivak as the only reference points.
- The **Підсумок** section should include a practical decision-making framework (яку методологію обрати для конкретного завдання) — this feeds directly into c2-031 and c2-032.
