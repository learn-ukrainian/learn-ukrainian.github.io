## Cultural Hooks

1. **Олександр Потебня (1835–1891)** — Ukrainian philologist, philosopher, folklorist, ethnographer, literary scholar, pedagogue. Member-correspondent of the St. Petersburg Academy of Sciences from 1875. His Харківська лінгвістична школа predates Russian Formalism (OPOYAZ, 1916) by decades; Shklovsky's «очуднення» concept is traceable to Potebnya's inner/outer form theory. Ukrainian Wikipedia identifies him as «видатний український мовознавець» — framing him as Ukrainian, not "малоросійський". Key work: «Думка та мова» (1862). This gives a decolonised hook: the foundational concepts of 20th-century literary theory have Ukrainian roots.

2. **Теорія літератури** (Wikipedia): «галузь літературознавства, яка науково осмислює, узагальнює закономірності та особливості розвитку художньої творчості, розробляє та систематизує літературні поняття.» — Ukrainian Wikipedia defines the field actively and independently, with no derivative framing. The article distinguishes теорія, історія, та критика літератури — exactly the triad in the plan's introduction section.

3. **Іван Франко**, «Із секретів поетичної творчості» (1898): A landmark Ukrainian contribution to literary theory, written in the positivist era but anticipating psychological and formal approaches. Franko developed his concept of «образного мислення» and «сугестії» — theorising how poetic language operates subconsciously on the reader. This predates much Western psychoanalytic criticism.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«наратив» vs. «оповідь»** → Learners conflate these two terms. **Correct**: «наратив» = the theoretical model/concept (high academic register, borrowed from narratology); «оповідь» = the concrete act of telling a specific story in a text. Error source: both translate to English "narrative," so learners use them interchangeably. The plan explicitly flags this distinction — reinforce with contrastive examples: *«Наратив як теоретична категорія охоплює всі форми оповіді»* vs. *«Оповідь у романі ведеться від першої особи»*.

2. **Latin/Greek scholarly term transliteration** → Learners tend to use transliterated Latin forms in academic writing (e.g., writing *«дискурсус»* or *«парадигма» with Greek declension*). The module must model correct Ukrainian academic usage: **парадигма** declines as a standard feminine noun (парадигми, парадигмою); **дискурс** declines as masculine (дискурсу, дискурсом). The plan's §4 explicitly notes "100% мовна імерсія без використання латинської транслітерації термінів."

3. **«літературознавство» scope confusion** → Learners (especially those with background in humanities in English) conflate літературознавство with «критика» (criticism) or «публіцистика». The plan's introduction section correctly targets this: the scientific discipline (наукова дисципліна) must be distinguished from journalistic literary commentary. Error pattern: using «критик» where «літературознавець» is required, or treating a newspaper book review as «наукова стаття».

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Decolonised framing anchor**: Lead with Потебня's priority over Russian Formalism. The plan mentions Харківська та Київська школи alongside Празький гуртком — this is the correct framing: a pan-Slavic/Central-European intellectual tradition, not a "Russian" movement with Ukrainian participants.
- **Іван Франко as theorist**: The 1898 essay is the plan's core primary source for the classical section. Frame Franko not as a literary practitioner who dabbled in theory, but as a systematic literary theorist working in the European positivist tradition and consciously building a Ukrainian critical vocabulary.
- **Register calibration**: The entire module operates in науковий стиль — sentences should model correct Ukrainian academic prose. Avoid the Russicism «дана» (as in «дана стаття») → use «ця стаття» or «зазначена стаття».
- **§4.3.7 exercise in Section 5**: The plan calls for transforming a postcolonialist academic text into науково-популярний стиль. This is a high-stakes C2 skill gate. The content should model the process, not just describe it — include a before/after excerpt pair.
- **деконструкція frequency warning**: At 0.11 IPM, this term needs more scaffolding than higher-frequency terms. Provide 2-3 authentic usage examples drawn from Ukrainian academic contexts (e.g., Забужко's use of the concept in literary criticism).
