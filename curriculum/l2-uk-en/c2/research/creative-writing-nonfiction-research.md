## Cultural Hooks
1. **The "Samovydets" (Самовидець) Phenomenon:** The contemporary renaissance of Ukrainian literary reportage has been heavily driven by the "Samovydets" competition (run by Tempora publishing), which elevated factual, immersive writing about Ukrainian realities (and the ongoing war) to a high literary art form, establishing an independent, decolonized voice in global journalism.
2. **Khvylovyi's Polemics:** The modern Ukrainian essayistic tradition is deeply rooted in Mykola Khvylovyi's 1920s pamphlets (e.g., "Камо грядеш?"). These texts were not just essays; they were acts of intellectual resistance that defined the European orientation of Ukrainian culture against Russian/Soviet assimilation.

## Common Learner Errors
1. **"Автобіографія" vs. "Мемуари"** → "Мемуари" або "спогади". Learners often use "автобіографія" to refer to memoirs. In Ukrainian, "автобіографія" is typically a formal, chronologically strict account (often an official document), whereas "мемуари" emphasize subjective reflection, selective memory, and literary crafting of lived experience.
2. **Overusing "есе" for all nonfiction** → "Нарис" або "публіцистика". While "essay" translates directly to "есе", the Ukrainian tradition relies heavily on "нарис" for descriptive/travel writing and "публіцистика" for polemical/social commentary. Learners must distinguish these nuances.

## Notes for Content Writing
- **Decolonized Framing:** Frame Ukrainian literary reportage and publicistics as independent traditions. Do not treat them as branches of Soviet journalistic styles. Emphasize the role of documentary prose in preserving authentic Ukrainian memory and agency against imperial erasure.
- Maintain full academic/literary register (100% immersion) appropriate for C2. Introduce advanced concepts like "психогеографія" and "ненадійний оповідач" with clear, elegant Ukrainian definitions.
