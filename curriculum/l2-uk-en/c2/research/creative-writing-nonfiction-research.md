## Cultural Hooks

1. **Хвильовий's pamphlets as a founding gesture**: Микола Хвильовий (1893–1933) published his polemical essays "Камо грядеши?" and "Думки проти течії" (1925–26) under the slogan *"Геть від Москви!"* — a Ukrainian intellectual essay tradition born simultaneously as polemic and political self-definition. He was one of the key figures of the Розстріляне відродження (Executed Renaissance). This is the historical root of the Ukrainian essayistic tradition as a decolonial act. (Source: uk.wikipedia.org — Микола Хвильовий)

2. **Жадан serves as both literary subject and living witness**: Сергій Жадан (b. 1974), author of *Інтернат* (2017) — a war reportage-novel hybrid set in the Donbas conflict — enlisted in the Ukrainian National Guard in March 2024. His trajectory from literary reportage writer to frontline soldier enacts the ethical dilemma of witness and positionality that the module's ethics section addresses directly. *Інтернат* won the Angelus Prize (2018). (Source: uk.wikipedia.org — Жадан Сергій Вікторович)

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Conflating есе with нарис** → These are distinct: нарис (sketch/portrait essay) is rooted in publicistic journalism with a factual subject and documentary function; есе is more philosophical, associative, and argumentative — the Montaigne tradition. Learners use them interchangeably. Error appears in textbook exercises at grade 7–8 (Avramenko), where нарис у публіцистичному стилі is introduced as a structured exercise. Fix: teach the genre taxonomy explicitly before the workshop section.

2. **Russicism in register-marking verbs** → Learners import Russian constructions for "conveying information/testimony": e.g., *розповідати* is used where Ukrainian нonfiction writing prefers *свідчити*, *описувати*, *зображувати*, *фіксувати*. The verb *засвідчувати* (to document/attest) is a key term in nonfiction discourse that learners often omit. Fix: model collocation: *текст засвідчує*, *авторка свідчить*, *репортаж фіксує*.

3. **Missing the memoir/autobiography distinction** → Learners treat мемуари and автобіографія as synonyms. In Ukrainian literary tradition: автобіографія = chronological life narrative; мемуари = selective, episodic, thematic memory — with explicit right to subjectivity and fragmentation. The distinction is structurally pedagogically important for the memoir writing section.

## Textbook Grounding

The RAG corpus confirms that creative nonfiction is taught at school level primarily through:
- **Grade 5** (Zabotnyi, tier 1): Есе — introduced as "невеликий за обсягом твір, який має довільну побудову та в якому автор висловлює власні враження і думки." (Grade 5 level — by C2, learner needs to substantially extend this basic framework.)
- **Grade 7** (Avramenko, tier 1): Нарис у публіцистичному стилі — portait essay, factual basis, journalistic structure.
- **Grade 8** (Avramenko, tier 1): Есе в публіцистичному стилі — "максимально переконливе висловлення авторських вражень, роздумів, емоцій."
- **Grade 11** (Borzenko, tier 2): Документальна проза / щоденник — introduced in the context of Oles Honchar's wartime diary; "зазвичай щоденники люди пишуть для себе."

**Phase B implication**: Learners have encountered these genre definitions at school level; C2 module should go significantly beyond school taxonomy into literary-critical analysis of hybrid genres, ethical frameworks, and authorial voice.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **`автофікція` flag**: Use this term but gloss it as a neologism not yet standardized in VESUM. Prefer native constructions alongside: *автобіографічна проза* (more established) as a parallel/explanation.
- **`психогеографія` flag**: Same status — present as a specialized loanword used in contemporary Ukrainian cultural criticism (especially in Andrukhovych's circle), but not yet VESUM-verified.
- **Decolonization anchor**: Frame the Ukrainian essayistic tradition positively as originating from the chronicle tradition and Cossack-era polemic — not as a response to or variant of Russian literary culture. Khvylovy's essays are a Ukrainian invention; the Montaigne connection is European, not mediated through Russian literature.
- **Word budget note**: The plan's vocabulary section (Практикум) is intentionally lean (400 words). The content-heavy sections (Есеїстична традиція, Мемуарне письмо, Літературний репортаж — 2600 words combined) carry most of the load. To hit 5000 total, each section must be generous with primary text examples and annotated analysis passages. The 1.5× overshoot strategy (target 5500–6000 in draft) is advisable.
- **Witness ethics section**: The "Голос та етика нефікції" section is the most original and sensitive — ground it in Ukrainian war literature specifically (Zhadan, Prokhasko's "Totentanz", field reportage by Anastasiia Magazova etc.) to avoid genericized Anglo-American nonfiction ethics discourse.
- **Dovzhenko diary**: Олександр Довженко's wartime diary (*Щоденник*, 1941–56) — written secretly under Soviet surveillance, published posthumously — is an ideal primary text anchor for the memoir section: demonstrates fragmentation, unreliable/self-censored narrator, temporal displacement under duress.
- **Stus camp notes**: Vasyl Stus's prison camp correspondence and notes (1972–1985) demonstrate memoir-as-existential-testimony in extreme conditions — the ethical stakes of witness at their highest.
