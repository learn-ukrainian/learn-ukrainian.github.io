## Cultural Hooks

1. **Самвидав: Ukrainian term predates Soviet "самиздат"** — The Ukrainian Wikipedia article on Самвидав confirms that the term originated from Ivan Bahrianyi's 1929 self-published poem "Ave Maria," published under the imprint "САМ." The Russian term "самиздат/саміздат" came later as a parody of Soviet publishing house names (Гос*із*дат, Політ*із*дат). Ukrainian scholars and the Wikipedia article frame "самвидав" as the authentic Ukrainian original — this reverses the common misconception that the Ukrainian form is derived from Russian. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Самвидав

2. **Цифровий самвидав as continuity** — The same Wikipedia article explicitly identifies a "цифровий самвидав" concept: the spread of texts as electronic publications on computer media from the late 1980s onward (floppy disks). This provides a direct genealogical bridge from 1960s underground publishing (Стус, Костенко, Сверстюк) through digital distribution to today's internet platforms — exactly the decolonized arc the plan outlines in section 2.

3. **ЛітАкцент** — Ukrainian book web portal about modern Ukrainian and world literature, operated 2007–2022. One of the main sites for professional literary criticism in the digital space. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/ЛітАкцент

4. **Читомо** — Cultural-publishing project, one of the leading internet resources for book publishing in Ukraine (as of 2017). Covers the publishing side of Ukrainian and world literary process. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Читомо

5. **Мережева (електронна) література** — Wikipedia defines it as "різновид літературного мистецтва, який характеризується залежністю тексту від цифрового носія, а це своєю чергою надає можливість створення нелінійних, розгалужених структур і створює підґрунтя для залучення мультимедії в літературний текст." This is the foundational definition for section 1 of the outline.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Саміздат → Самвидав** — Learners (especially those with exposure to Russian) default to the Russified form "саміздат" or "самиздат." Correct Ukrainian form is **самвидав** (м. рід, відмінюваний). Forms: *самвидаву* (gen.), *у самвидаві* (loc.), *твори самвидаву*. The Russian form should be explicitly marked as a Russianism in this context.

2. **В платформі → На платформі** — Calque from English "in a platform" or Russian "в платформе." Ukrainian norm is preposition **на** with digital environments: *на платформі*, *на сайті*, *на пристрої*. Also: *на ЛітАкценті*, *на Читомо*. This is a widespread error in journalism and social media copy.

3. **Цифровий ≠ Числовий** — "Digital" in the technological sense = **цифровий** (цифрова доба, цифровий підпис, цифровий простір). "Numerical/relating to numbers" = **числовий** (числовий ряд, числова вісь). The confusion arises from the ambiguity of the English word "digital" and reinforced by inconsistent translations in early Ukrainian tech vocabulary.

4. **Дигіталізація** — Not in VESUM dictionary. The correct Ukrainian term for "digitization of archival/library materials" is **оцифрування**. For the broader social/technological process, **діджиталізація** appears in press but is also a borrowing. Phase B should use **оцифрування фондів** specifically.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Secondary texts taxonomy for §4.3.8**: Grade 7 Litvinova textbook already establishes the primary/secondary text distinction (план, реферат, конспект, переказ). Phase B must treat this as known — the C2 extension is professional-register рецензія, анотація, довідково-інформаційне повідомлення specifically in digital/online form.

- **Decolonized framing lock**: The самвидав genealogy should be presented as *Ukrainian innovation* that the Soviet system later copied (not vice versa). Ivan Bahrianyi 1929 → шістдесятники 1960s → цифровий самвидав 1989+ → сучасні онлайн-платформи. This is historically accurate and well-sourced from Wikipedia.

- **Frequency warning on гіпертекст / краудсорсинг**: Both terms have very low corpus frequency (0.07 and 0.02 IPM respectively). Phase B should introduce them with explicit definitional scaffolding rather than assuming familiarity, even at C2 level.

- **Replace дигіталізація**: The plan's vocabulary_hints use this form. Phase B must substitute **оцифрування** for the archival/library sense. If дигіталізація appears, it should be in quotes or attributed to a specific source.

- **Preposition на with digital media**: This is listed as a mовна норма point in section 3 of the content outline. Phase B should use multiple natural examples across all sections (not only in the rule-statement), so learners encounter it in context before the explicit note.

- **интерактивний фікшн**: The plan uses "інтерактивна фікшн" — note that the noun фікшн is indeclinable (borrowed). This is acceptable in academic/media register and matches VESUM (not found separately, but блог, подкаст etc. are established precedents for English loanword behavior).
