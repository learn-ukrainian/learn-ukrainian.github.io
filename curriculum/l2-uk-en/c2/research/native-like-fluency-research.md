## Cultural Hooks
1. **Euphony (Милозвучність)**: Not just a phonetic rule (u/v alternation), but a cultural imperative. "Music of the language" is a core value in Ukrainian identity. A native speaker instinctively avoids consonant clusters (збіг приголосних).
2. **The "Pause" Culture**: Unlike English "umm/err", Ukrainian fillers (ну, так, цей, як його) serve different pragmatic functions. Silence is handled differently in Ukrainian discourse (often less awkward than in Anglophone cultures).

## Common Learner Errors
1. **Syntactic Rigidity**: Maintaining SVO order even when emphasis requires OVS (Topicalization). *Correction*: Use inversion to highlight the rheme. *Why*: Transfer from English syntax.
2. **"Bookishness"**: Using full forms where natives use ellipsis or particles. *Correction*: "Ти будеш каву?" instead of "Чи ти будеш пити каву?". *Why*: Over-reliance on textbook grammar.
3. **Calques (Surzhyk/Englishisms)**: Direct translation of idioms ("мати сенс" vs "мати рацію/сенс" distinction, or "приймати участь" vs "брати участь"). *Correction*: Use authentic collocations.

## Notes for Content Writing
- Focus heavily on the concept of **Euphony** as the "litmus test" for native-like flow.
- Emphasize that "Native-like" ≠ "Perfect". Natives make errors, repair speech, and use slang. The goal is *authentic* imperfection vs. *learner* imperfection.
