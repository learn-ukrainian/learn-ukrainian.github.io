## Cultural Hooks

1. **Шевченківська премія (1961)**: Founded in 1961 as the Ukrainian SSR State Prize, it is today Ukraine's highest creative award — *«найвища в Україні творча відзнака за вагомий внесок у розвиток культури й мистецтва»* (Wikipedia). Its 2024 laureates included Dmytro Lazutkin and Yaryna Chornohuz; the documentary film *«20 днів у Маріуполі»* (Mstyslav Chernov) also received the award — a pointed shift from Soviet-era ideological function toward documentary witness of war. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Національна_премія_України_імені_Тараса_Шевченка

2. **«Розстріляне відродження» — term origin**: The plan states "(Єжи Гедройць, 1958)" — **FLAG: probable year error**. The term comes from Yuriy Lavrinenko's 1959 anthology published in Paris by Giedroyć's *Kultura* publishing house. The anthology was *«Розстріляне відродження. Антологія 1917–1933»* (Paris: Kultura, 1959). Giedroyć was the publisher/editor of *Kultura*, not the author of the term. Content should attribute the term to **Lavrinenko (Лавріненко), 1959**, with Giedroyć noted as the publication platform. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Розстріляне_відродження

3. **Коронація слова**: Verified as an international literary competition for novels, plays, screenplays, song lyrics, and children's works. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Коронація_слова

4. **Василь Стус**: Confirmed poet-dissident, member of the Ukrainian Helsinki Group, political prisoner of the USSR. He was nominated for the Nobel Prize in Literature. Posthumously awarded the Shevchenko State Prize and named Hero of Ukraine. Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Стус_Василь_Семенович

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«лауреат премію»** → **«лауреат премії»** — Genitive case required after «лауреат» (it is a noun of qualification, taking Gen. complement like «автор книги»). English speakers tend to use nominative or accusative by analogy with "prize winner."

2. **«получити премію»** → **«отримати/здобути премію»** — «получити» is a Russianism (рос. получить). Standard Ukrainian: отримати, здобути, одержати. The plan explicitly flags this under section 6 vocabulary work.

3. **«відзначено/удостоєно»** case confusion: «відзначено *премією*» (Instr.) vs «удостоєно *премії*» (Gen.) — two correct constructions with different cases. Learners often use the wrong case or mix the two verbs' government. The plan notes this in section 6; Phase B should illustrate both with parallel examples.

4. **«шкільна програма»** collocational error: learners may calque English "curriculum" as «курікулум» or «навчальний план» when «шкільна програма» is the idiomatic Ukrainian term for the canon enforced in schools.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **Year error in plan**: The plan attributes the term «Розстріляне відродження» to «Єжи Гедройць, 1958». The correct attribution is Юрій Лавріненко, 1959 (anthology published by Giedroyć's *Kultura*). Phase B must use 1959 and credit Lavrinenko as the term's author; Giedroyć as publisher/platform.

2. **Low-frequency terms**: «канонотворення» (2 corpus hits) and «деканонізація» (11 hits) are near-terminological coinages. They are VESUM-verified and appropriate for C2, but Phase B should contextualise them as scholarly/critical register, not everyday academic Ukrainian.

3. **«журі» indeclinability**: VESUM returned 17 false matches. The word «журі» is an indeclinable noun (of French origin). Phase B must not decline it: *рішення журі* (not *журею*), *члени журі* (not *журів*).

4. **Decolonised framing for «Розстріляне відродження»**: The plan correctly flags the term-debate (Ярина Цимбал proposes «Червоний ренесанс»). Frame this as Ukrainian scholarly agency — Ukrainians actively naming their own cultural history — not as a debate about Soviet labels.

5. **Vasyl Stus Nobel clarification**: Stus was nominated for the Nobel Prize in 1985 (the year he died in a Soviet camp). The Nobel was not awarded to him posthumously (Nobel rules prohibit posthumous awards). Content should say «висунутий на Нобелівську премію» (nominated), not «претендент» without qualification.

6. **Word target headroom**: Plan sections total 5050 words — very tight against the 5000 minimum. Phase B should target ~5500 to ensure audit passage after any condensation.
