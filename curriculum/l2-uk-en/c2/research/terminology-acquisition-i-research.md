## Cultural Hooks

**Hook 1 — ІУНМ: «Золота доба» 1921–1930**
Source: Wikipedia (uk) — Інститут української наукової мови ВУАН
- Founded 30 May 1921 by merger of VUAN terminological commissions
- Director: Академік Агатангел Кримський
- Peak activity 1925–1929: 34 terminological dictionaries contracted (16 published), over 2 million index cards, 223 non-staff members including 3 academicians and 44 professors
- Sections spanned: botany, geology, mathematics, physics, chemistry, medicine, law, arts, technology
- Explicitly aimed to «очищення мови від невиправданих запозичень»
- 1929–1930: SVU show trial. Director Hryhory Kholodnyi sentenced (8 years, then shot 1938). Editors Vadym Sharko, Kost Osmak arrested. 1937: lexicographer Mykola Stanislavsky shot.

**Hook 2 — Олена Курило: термінолог і жертва**
Source: Wikipedia (uk) — Курило Олена Борисівна
- Ukrainian linguist, dialectologist, author of popular Ukrainian language textbooks, «творчиня української наукової термінології»
- Medical Dictionary (Медичний словник) — her key terminological work of the 1920s
- Arrested as part of the Soviet campaign against «nationalist» Ukrainian linguists in the 1930s; victim of Stalinist terror

**Hook 3 — Іван Пулюй: термінолог-фізик**
Source: Wikipedia (uk) — Пулюй Іван Павлович
- Ukrainian physicist and electrical engineer, inventor, first developer of the X-ray tube (precursor to modern X-ray devices)
- Developed Ukrainian physics terminology — strategy of creating «питомі відповідники» for scientific concepts rather than direct borrowing
- Professor and rector of the Deutsch Technische Hochschule in Prague; state advisor on electrical engineering

## Common Learner Errors

**Error 1**: «домінуючий фактор» → **Correct**: «панівний чинник»
*Why*: Active participle formation on borrowed verb (*домінувати*) + Russicism «фактор» instead of native «чинник». Ukrainian stylistic norm rejects active participles where adjectives exist. GRAC confirms «панівний чинник» in authentic usage across 5 concordance lines. The pattern is systemic: «керуючий» → «керівний», «виконуючий обов'язки» → «виконувач обов'язків».

**Error 2**: «академік» interpreted as "academic person/scholar" → **Correct meaning**: member of a national academy of sciences (institutional title)
*Why*: False friend with English "academic." In Ukrainian, «академік» = specific institutional rank (АН). For "academic" in the general sense, use «науковець», «вчений».

**Error 3**: «комплекція» (build/physique of a person's body) misread as "complexity" (= «складність»)
*Why*: False cognate trap. «Комплекція» derives from Latin *complexio* (constitution, physique). «Складність» or «комплексність» are correct for "complexity." Grade 11 textbooks (Zabolotnyi) treat false cognates but without systematic terminology focus.

**Error 4**: Treating «інтернаціоналізм» as a morphological/phonological copy of the source language form — learners write «психологія» correctly but write «фізіка» (4-syllable Russian/English pattern) or over-adapt by writing «педагогіка» where the ending is already correct Ukrainian.

## Notes for Content Writing

1. **VESUM flags**: «когнат» and «інференція» not in dictionary. Both are legitimate specialized borrowings in academic Ukrainian linguistics (universities use them). Introduce each with explicit definition and note the VESUM status. Do not replace «когнат» — it is the accepted Anglicism in linguistic discourse. For «інференція», consider first-mention pairing: «інференція (умовивід, логічний висновок зі змісту)».

2. **«Питомий» disambiguation**: The word's dominant corpus meaning is «питома вага» (specific gravity — physics/chemistry). When using «питомий відповідник», establish terminological context immediately so learners do not conflate usages. Opportunity for a micro-lesson on polysemy within academic registers.

3. **1933 repression framing**: Soviet circulars of 1933 explicitly mandated bringing Ukrainian terminology closer to Russian norms, reversing the ІУНМ work. This is not "standardization" — it was language policy as cultural suppression. Frame without Russian as baseline: describe what was lost from Ukrainian's own terminological system, not what was "aligned to" Russian.

4. **Active participle rule is systemic**: The «домінуючий → панівний» correction represents a larger pattern that should be tabulated. Other productive pairs: «лідируючий → провідний», «діючий → чинний», «існуючий → наявний/існуючий (поет.)». Grade 11 textbooks (Glazova) treat loanword stylistics but do not address this specific participial error.

5. **Textbook coverage**: Grade 6 textbooks (Litvinova, Golub — tier 1) extensively cover word-formation methods (prefix, suffix, prefix+suffix, zero-suffix, compounding). Grade 11 (Glazova, Zabolotnyi — tier 2) cover stylistic features of loanwords. The C2 module extends this to professional/terminological register — no tier-1 textbook covers terminology acquisition at this depth, so this content is genuinely C2-exclusive.

6. **ІУНМ pedagogical framing**: The 223 members of ІУНМ (3 academicians, 44 professors, composers, artists, architects) demonstrates that terminological work was a broad cultural project, not narrow linguistics. This grounds the module's claim that systematic terminology study is a form of cultural participation.
