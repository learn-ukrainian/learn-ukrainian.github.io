## Common Learner Errors

1. **"Я маю експертизу"** → **"Я є експертом у галузі" / "Маю досвід / компетенції у..."** — direct calque of English "I have expertise." In Ukrainian, `експертиза` primarily means "forensic examination" (судово-медична експертиза); using it for personal professional knowledge is a business-register anglicism. The instrumental-role construction is the native pattern.

2. **"працюю як менеджер"** → **"працюю менеджером"** — the `як` construction is a calque from English "work as a manager." Ukrainian uses the **instrumental case** to express professional role: *є директором, призначений керівником, працює аналітиком*. The `як` form implies a temporary, comparative, or metaphorical reading ("in the manner of a manager"), not an identity statement.

3. **"скіли" / "хард скіли" / "soft skills"** → **"навички" / "фахові навички" / "м'які навички"** — raw English loanwords that appear frequently in Ukrainian business media but are marked as informal/jargon. At C2 level, learners should command the full Ukrainian register and know when the loanword is stylistically appropriate (casual LinkedIn post) vs. inappropriate (CV, formal letter).

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Instrumental case** for role identification (`працюю менеджером`) is a key grammar anchor; the plan flags it in §Стратегічне позиціонування — ensure at least 6 worked examples across the module.
- **Register spectrum** at C2: the module spans офіційний (crisis communication, formal self-presentation) → публіцистичний (expert articles, LinkedIn) → розмовний (elevator pitch, networking). Content should explicitly label these registers at transition points per §4.3.1.1 and §4.3.7.
- **Канцеляризми** (§4.3.3): phrases like "згідно з досвідом", "відповідно до компетенцій" appear in formal CVs and are valid C2 tools — but must be contrasted against over-use (канцелярит), which kills brand voice.
- **"Bravery to be Ukraine"** (referenced in plan): verify sourcing independently — this phrase appears in marketing/media contexts but has no Wikipedia article; do not present as an official campaign without a verifiable source.
- **Word budget alert**: `нетворкінг` and `брендинг` sections risk padding with loanword lists. Keep examples tight — 4–6 per point maximum.
- **Decolonization frame**: Ukrainian professional identity developed independently, with its own trajectory (cooperative movement, pre-Soviet Ukrainian entrepreneurship, post-1991 business culture, post-2014 acceleration). Do not frame Ukrainian business language as "departing from Soviet norms" — frame it as developing its own contemporary register.
