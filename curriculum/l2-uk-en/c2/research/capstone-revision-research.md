## Cultural Hooks

1. **Іван Франко як редактор «Літературно-наукового вісника»**: The journal was founded in 1898 by the Shevchenko Scientific Society (NTSh) on Mykhailo Hrushevsky's initiative. Franko served as editor and is documented to have personally revised manuscripts of young authors, teaching concision and lexical precision. The journal ran until 1932 — its suppression coincides with the Soviet destruction of Ukrainian cultural institutions. This gives the editing topic a decolonization anchor: editorial culture as cultural survival.

2. **Олена Курило та «Уваги до сучасної української літературної мови» (1924)**: Kurilo was a Ukrainian linguist, dialectologist, and creator of Ukrainian scientific terminology — and a victim of Stalinist terror. Her 1924 book addressed stylistics, phraseology, and syntax with the explicit goal of purging Russian-imposed calques from Ukrainian academic writing. Its suppression and her arrest under Soviet rule frames language hygiene directly as a decolonization act, not mere style preference. The book was republished posthumously (2004, Видавництво Соломії Павличко «Основи»), itself a post-independence act of linguistic restoration.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Lexical calques from Russian** → learners write "приймати участь" (Russian: принимать участие) when the correct Ukrainian form is **"брати участь"**; similarly "вірна відповідь" (Russian: верный ответ) → **"правильна відповідь"**. These are deeply habituated interference patterns in L2 learners with Russian exposure.

2. **Passive nominalization overuse** (канцелярський стиль) → learners reproduce Russian bureaucratic structure: "нами було проведено дослідження" → target form: **"ми провели дослідження"**. The impersonal passive construction survives in learner writing as a prestige register marker from Soviet academic conventions.

3. **Invisible pleonasm** → learners do not recognize tautological redundancy in frozen phrases: "вільна вакансія" (вакансія already means "empty post"), "пам'ятний сувенір" (сувенір already means "keepsake"), "спускатися вниз" (спускатися already implies downward direction). Learners treat these as idiomatic rather than erroneous.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The **decolonization frame** is structurally central here, not cosmetic. Kurilo's language hygiene work (1924) was suppressed by Soviet power precisely because it was effective. The module should make explicit that passive constructions, канцеляризми, and calques entered Ukrainian academic writing via Soviet institutional pressure — editing them out is an act of linguistic restoration.
- **§4.3.3 canonical examples** (згідно з…, відповідно до…, у зв'язку з…) should appear verbatim in the module as examples to identify and replace — they are in the State Standard itself.
- **Cognitive challenge for C2**: pleonasm and tautology are hard to spot at speed. Phase B should include exercises requiring slow re-reading and self-questioning ("Does this word add information?").
- **Word target (5000)**: low-frequency specialist terms (когезія, канцеляризм, номіналізація) need ample contextual examples to cement — don't just define, demonstrate in 2–3 authentic-style passages.
- **чекліст** (not чеклист) — this is the verified VESUM spelling; correct the plan vocabulary in Phase B output.
- The **Франко editorial model** (patience, distance before revising, teaching by example) gives the module a humanizing narrative spine — Franko reportedly waited days before returning to a text for final revision, a practice worth anchoring in the temporal-distance recommendation.
