## Cultural Hooks

1. **Книжковий Арсенал (Kyiv)**: Founded 2011 by the Мистецький арсенал, held annually in spring. In its first 9 years, hosted 500+ writers, poets, philosophers, and publishing experts from 50+ countries. Explicitly multidisciplinary — visual art, theatre, and music alongside literature. Verified source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Книжковий_арсенал

2. **Book Forum Lviv**: Operating since 1994 (previously «Форум видавців у Львові», rebranded as «BFL» after 2018), held every September in Lviv. Ukraine's oldest literary book fair, making it 30+ years old in 2024 — a marker of unbroken cultural continuity through independence, Orange Revolution, and full-scale invasion. Verified source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_Forum_Lviv

3. **Сергій Жадан**: Frontman of «Жадан і Собаки» and «Лінія Маннергейма»; novels include «Депеш Мод», «Ворошиловград», «Месопотамія», «Інтернат»; poetry collections «Ефіопія», «Тамплієри», «Список кораблів». Since March 2024 serving in «Хартія» brigade of the National Guard of Ukraine — embodying the writer-as-soldier archetype the plan references. Verified source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Жадан_Сергій_Вікторович

## Common Learner Errors

1. **сучасний vs. модерний** → Distinguish sharply: *сучасний* = contemporary (existing today); *модерний* refers to Modernism (early 20th-century movement) or is used colloquially for "stylish/modern-looking." Saying «сучасна література» (contemporary literature) vs. «модернізм початку XX ст.» (modernism of the early 20th century). English speakers systematically confuse these because "modern" in English covers both semantic spaces.

2. **писатель → письменник** — «Писатель» is a direct Russianism with zero justification in Ukrainian. The correct term is «письменник» (masc.) / «письменниця» (fem.). Frequency data confirms «письменник» is highly active in Ukrainian corpora (22.46 IPM). Occurs in genre labels too: «письменник-фантаст», «письменник-романіст».

3. **цікавитися + орудний відмінок** — Learners often insert an accusative object (*цікавитися сучасну літературу*) by direct transfer from English ("be interested in X"). Correct Ukrainian: *цікавитися сучасною літературою* (instrumental). This is a systematic case governance error, and the plan explicitly targets it in section 5.

## Textbook Grounding

Grade 11 textbooks (Avramenko tier 2; Borzenko tier 2) both contain sections titled «Сучасна українська література» confirming this is taught at upper secondary level in Ukrainian schools. Key textbook framings to leverage:
- Borzenko (p. 218–219): Periodization by decade — шістдесятники → сімдесятники → вісімдесятники → дев'ятдесятники → двотисячники. Useful for the section 1 periodization point.
- Avramenko (p. 240): Genre-style tendencies listed as bullet taxonomy — historical novel, Gothic novella, esseїstyka (Андрухович, Забужко, Пагутяк), "village prose." Confirms the plan's section 3 genre panorama accurately maps school curriculum.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **автофікшн** is not in VESUM — treat as a recent borrowing, mark with лапки on first use or gloss as «автобіографічна проза з елементами вигадки»; don't use as a bare technical term without contextualisation.
- **Андрій Курков** writes primarily in Russian but has gained major international recognition as a "Ukrainian author" — the module's plan correctly identifies the language-choice dilemma as a legitimate cultural discussion point, not a disqualification. Frame decolonially: Kurkov's profile illustrates how international literary markets have historically conflated Ukrainian authorship with Russian-language production, and how this is now being actively contested.
- The plan's §4.3.8 alignment is tight and verified. The "Майстерня книжкового оглядача" section (5) should explicitly name the genre types: *рецензія*, *анотація*, *реферат*, *літературно-критичне есе* — all covered by the Standard's secondary text requirement.
- War as periodization rupture: Wikipedia confirms Ukrainian literary scholars already treat 1991 (independence) as the field's start date. The 2022 full-scale invasion is widely understood as a second major rupture, justifying the plan's framing of war prose as a distinct current within the new generation.
- Шевченківська премія: Ukraine's highest state literary award (National Shevchenko Prize) — anchors the prestige hierarchy in section 4 alongside Книга року ВВС and Angelus.
