## Cultural Hooks

1. **Києво-Могилянська академія (1632–1817)** — Founded by Metropolitan Petro Mohyla as Kyiv-Bratska Collegium; by the late 17th century received academy status and became one of the foremost scholarly centers in Eastern Europe. It established the foundations of Ukrainian academic terminology, rhetoric, and philosophical discourse — centuries before modern Ukrainian universities. Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (verified).

2. **Національна комісія зі стандартів державної мови** — Central executive body (спеціальний статус, coordinates through Cabinet of Ministers) responsible for setting Ukrainian language norms. Since 2019 it has led systematic decolonization of terminology: replacing Soviet calques with organic Ukrainian forms, reviewing professional and scientific registers, and issuing binding recommendations. The commission's member Данута Мазурик signed the 2024 State Standard itself. Source: Ukrainian Wikipedia (verified).

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«приймати участь»** → **«брати участь»** — Calque of Russian _принимать участие_. The verb _приймати_ (to receive/accept) is semantically wrong in this context; Ukrainian uses _брати_ (to take). Found throughout formal writing even among advanced learners. C2 must produce zero instances.

2. **«відноситися до»** → **«належати до»** — Calque of Russian _относиться к_. In Ukrainian classifications and taxonomies, the normative form is _належати до категорії / до групи_. «Відноситися» in the sense of «to pertain to» does not exist in standard Ukrainian.

3. **«вибачатися»** vs **«перепрошувати»** — Functional confusion: _вибачатися_ = to excuse oneself (past wrongdoing); _перепрошувати_ = polite «excuse me» / «I'm sorry» (professional etiquette opener). Advanced learners systematically over-use _вибачатися_ in formal correspondence where _перепрошую_ is required.

4. **«дякувати» + Знах.** → **«дякувати» + Дав.** — Verb government error under Russian influence (_благодарить кого_ [Acc] → Ukrainian _дякувати кому_ [Dat]). Must be explicitly drilled in the oral presentation section (Частина IV).

## Notes for Content Writing

- The checkpoint covers M46–54 (nine modules). Phase B should open with a structured self-diagnostic grid: each section of the checkpoint maps to a subset of those modules, so learners can locate their weak points.
- **Деколонізація термінології** (Частина I) benefits from the Києво-Могилянська академія hook: Ukrainian had a living academic tradition 200+ years before Soviet imposition — the commission is restoring, not inventing.
- **Register mixing** (Частина II): the error pattern of dropping into conversational syntax within written academic texts is endemic at C2. Examples should contrast academic vs. colloquial variants of the same proposition.
- Частина IV oral section: modal markers (_мабуть_, _очевидно_, _варто зазначити_, _слід підкреслити_) are the key C2 differentiator for conference discourse.
- §4.3.8 explicitly lists _реферати_, _повідомлення_, _довідково-інформаційні тексти_ — all three should appear as production task types in the activities.
- C2 vocabulary target is **12,280 cumulative / 2,500 new** (State Standard 2024). Checkpoint activities should reflect specialist/academic register, not repeat B2-level vocabulary.
- **Forbidden patterns**: кот→кіт, хорошо→добре (standard), plus domain-specific: _приймати участь_, _відноситися до_, _вибачатися_ (in etiquette contexts).
- Activity design: C2 spec prohibits fill-in, match-up, error-correction drills. Use `essay-response`, `critical-analysis`, `reading`, `comparative-study`, `quiz` (native-level complexity), `true-false` with nuanced claims.
