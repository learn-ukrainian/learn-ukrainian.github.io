## Cultural Hooks

1. **Закон про мову 2019**: Мовну політику в Україні засновано на Конституції та Законі України «Про забезпечення функціонування української мови як державної» від 16 липня 2019 року. (Wikipedia: Мовна політика в Україні). This law created the office of Уповноважений із захисту державної мови — a posadova osoba whose status is defined by the law itself. First ombudsman: Тарас Кремінь (2019–present). Strong hook for the legislation section — learners can frame this as an institutional sociolinguistic intervention.

2. **Суржик as transitional phenomenon**: Wikipedia defines суржик as «елементи двох або кількох мов, об'єднані штучно, без дотримання норм літературної мови» — «близький до понять арго, жаргон, креол, лінгва франка». The grade 10 Karaman textbook (tier 2) discusses it in «мовознавчому, соціокультурному та психолінгвістичному аспектах». The plan's reframe of суржик as «симптом одужання» (transitional phase, not permanent state) is pedagogically valuable and supported by scholarly framing — cite this contrast explicitly in the practicum section.

3. **Диглосія ≠ Білінгвізм**: Wikipedia gives the precise distinction: диглосія «передбачає свідому оцінку мовцями своїх ідіомів за шкалою «високе — низьке»» — i.e. functional inequality, not just co-existence. This is the key conceptual correction the module must drive home.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **«Я рахую, що...» → «Я вважаю, що...»** — Direct calque from Russian «я считаю», caused by interference during code-switching. Нормативна форма: «я вважаю», «на мою думку», «я переконаний/а». Plan explicitly flags this — anchor it with a real-world example from Ukrainian media discourse.

2. **Білінгвізм = Диглосія (false synonymy)** — Learners (and many native speakers) use these interchangeably. The distinction is functional: білінгвізм describes competence (knowing two languages), диглосія describes power asymmetry (one is "high", one is "low" in social use). Ukrainian historically suffered true diglossia under Russian/Soviet dominance — Russian was the «high» variety for official/scientific discourse, Ukrainian the «low» for domestic/rural use. Framing this as a solved-but-ongoing challenge (not a permanent state) is the decolonized perspective.

3. **Суржик as stigma vs. symptom** — Learners either over-stigmatise surzyk (treating speakers as deficient) or dismiss it as harmless mixing. The correct C2 framing: суржик is a sociolinguistic symptom of historical diglossia — speakers using it are typically transitioning, not failing. The grade 5 Golub textbook (tier 1) already introduces the «mовна екологія» frame: language purity as environmental stewardship, not shame. The module should maintain this ecological framing without moralising.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Decolonization framing is structural, not cosmetic**: The key decolonized move is refusing the framing «українська мова vs. російська мова» as default binary. Instead: frame Ukrainian's sociolinguistic situation comparatively with other post-colonial language recoveries (Catalan, Welsh, Hebrew revival). Russian appears as historical agent of diglossia, not a linguistic comparator.
- **Statistics**: The plan cites 66% favouring removal of Russian from official sphere, 60% Ukrainian-speaking households (2024-2025 data). These should be attributed to Rating Sociological Group surveys. Do not present as absolute — frame as «за даними соціологічного опитування».
- **«Лагідна» → «Стратегічна» українізація**: This conceptual pair is the rhetorical spine of the module. Content should build the contrast progressively: pre-2022 gradualism vs. post-2022 urgency. Frame linguistically — lexical choices, register shifts, institutional language.
- **Section on media and propaganda (§Соціальна варіативність)**: Connect мовний маркер пропаганди to concrete observable features — e.g., use of «Київ» vs. «Киев», passive constructions to obscure agency, specific grammatical calques in troll content.
- **Word target 5000**: The practicum section (650 words) and the Ukrainian language situation section (900 words) are the two largest expandable zones. The practicum should include at minimum 2 extended case studies with Ukrainian-language sociolinguistic analysis — not mere descriptions.
- **All 7 functional styles** (per §4.3.1.1) should be referenced in the social variation section — scientific, official-business, publicistic, colloquial, literary-artistic, religious, epistolary — not just the usual four.
