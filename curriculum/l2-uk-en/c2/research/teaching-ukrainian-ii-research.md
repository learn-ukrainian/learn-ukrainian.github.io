## Cultural Hooks

1. **Василь Сухомлинський (1918–1970)** — видатний український педагог, публіцист, поет, Заслужений вчитель УРСР; «Серце віддаю дітям» is his central humanistic pedagogy treatise, developed at the Pavlysh village school in Kirovohrad region. His approach placed the child's emotional development at the center of curriculum design — a direct philosophical anchor for learner-centered course planning. *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Сухомлинський_Василь_Олександрович)*

2. **Києво-Могилянська академія (1632–1817)** — founded by Metropolitan Petro Mohyla, one of the largest and most distinguished educational centers in Eastern Europe. Its curriculum was structured around rhetoric and poetics as sequential disciplines — a historical precedent for systematic content structuring. Ukraine's own pedagogical tradition of structured, humanistic education predates Western European models and is independent of Russian educational history. *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Києво-Могилянська_академія_(1632—1817))*

3. **Болонський процес** — launched 1999 (Bologna Convention), signed by 29 European ministers of education. Created the European Higher Education Area via the Lisbon Recognition Convention. Ukraine's alignment with Bologna standards grounds the section on modern versus historical curricular structures and the документ hierarchy (силабус vs. робоча програма). *(Source: uk.wikipedia.org/wiki/Болонський_процес)*

## Common Learner Errors

1. **"курикулум" (calque) → "навчальна програма"** — English-speaking learners direct-borrow "curriculum" as *курикулум*. The normative Ukrainian professional term is *навчальна програма* or *зміст навчання*. The module plan explicitly flags this as a correction point.

2. **"оцінка" vs. "оцінювання"** — *оцінка* = the grade/mark (result); *оцінювання* = the process/methodology. Learners conflate these in spoken and written professional registers: ❌ *"проводити оцінку"* → ✅ *"проводити оцінювання"*; ❌ *"оцінювання — це 12 балів"* → ✅ *"оцінка — це 12 балів"*.

3. **Teaching verb triangle: навчати / вивчати / вчити** — *навчати* is transitive and professional (навчати студентів); *вивчати* means to study a subject (вивчати мову); *вчити* is colloquial/ambiguous and should be avoided in academic professional register. Learners default to *вчити* in all contexts.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Decolonization framing**: The Kyiv-Mohyla curriculum tradition (rhetoric → poetics → philosophy) provides a strong Ukrainian-centric historical anchor that does not require any Russian comparison. Frame Ukrainian pedagogical tradition as an autonomous European tradition.
- **силабус vs. робоча програма**: This distinction (public student contract vs. internal administrative document) is high-value for professional readers — treat it as a key terminological clarification, not a footnote.
- **SMART learning outcomes**: Ensure examples use Ukrainian education terminology (компетентність, результат навчання, дескриптор) rather than anglicisms.
- **Word target 5000**: Six sections, averaging ~833 words each. The plan allocates 600/900/1000/1000/900/600 — well-distributed. Sections 3 and 4 (needs analysis, spiral approach) carry the heaviest load; ensure they include concrete worked examples (a sample needs analysis matrix, a sample spiral sequence table) to reach target organically.
- **§4.3.8 integration**: The practice section (Розробка силабусу) is the natural home for secondary-text production — building a syllabus is itself an academic writing task that operationalizes this standard.
- **Sukhomlynsky reference**: Can be introduced as a Ukrainian educator in contrast to Soviet-era standardized curricula — the humanistic tradition vs. command pedagogy model, without invoking Russian comparisons explicitly.
