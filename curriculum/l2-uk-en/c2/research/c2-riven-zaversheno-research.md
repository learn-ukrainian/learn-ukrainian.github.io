- §4.3.5: Elements of rhetoric in text composition

- §4.3.8: Ability to create secondary texts (анотації, реферати, довідково-інформаційні тексти)

## Cultural Hooks

**1. НКСДМ — verified institutional fact (Wikipedia, retrieved 2026-03-13)**
- Національна комісія зі стандартів державної мови — central executive body with special status, coordinates through the Cabinet of Ministers.
- Founded 2019; on **31 липня 2024 року** approved the first Ukrainian language standard: «Українська мова як іноземна. Рівні загального володіння А1–С2».
- Issues **державний сертифікат про рівень володіння державною мовою** (valid indefinitely — безстроково).
- Exam structure: written (test tasks + text production) + oral (conversation with examiner). Results within 15 calendar days. Retake no sooner than 4 months after previous attempt.
- Current Голова: Чернобров Юлія Анатоліївна (from 5 October 2023), Candidate of Philological Sciences.

**2. Language Law professional requirements (Закон «Про забезпечення функціонування української мови як державної»)**
- Article 9 mandates proficiency confirmation for roles defined by law.
- Requirements took effect **16 lipnya 2021 roku** (Articles 7 and 11).
- Civil servants, diplomatic corps, judges, prosecutors, military officers — all must demonstrate certified proficiency. C2 or high-level B2 is expected for senior roles. This is the direct professional motivation for learners at this stage.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **Syntactic calque** («Це саме те, що я хотів сказати» → calqued from "It is exactly what I wanted to say") — Ukrainian prefers emphatic particle constructions and inversion: «Саме це я й мав на увазі» or «Ось що я мав на увазі». The calqued «Це є те, що...» is a direct English/Russian interference pattern, alien to literary Ukrainian.

2. **Register collapse under cognitive load** — At peak difficulty (e.g., improvised academic discussion), learners revert to суржик or informal markers in formal/neutral contexts: *чувак*, *типу*, *ну ось*, *по-любому*. The error is not lexical ignorance but register filter failure when focus shifts to content over form.

3. **Overuse of verbal nouns (відіменники)** — Learners who've mastered nominalization sometimes default to bureaucratic noun-chains: «проведення здійснення встановлення стандарту» instead of the naturalistic «встановити стандарт» or «затвердити стандарт». C2 means knowing when NOT to nominalize, which is the harder skill.

## Notes for Content Writing

- The §4603–4604 descriptor wording should be quoted directly in Section 2 (Стандарт С2) — it is the authoritative, citable source and adds institutional weight.
- НКСДМ facts (founding 2019, first Standard July 2024, exam structure) are verified and concrete — use them in Section 3 to avoid vagueness.
- The "вільного володіння другого ступеня" phrasing is the official Standard term and should appear verbatim in the module, not paraphrased as "найвищий рівень" which dilutes precision.
- Section 4 (stylistic pitfalls) maps directly to §4.3.7 (style transformation skill) — frame pitfalls as the inverse of mastery: knowing what a C2 speaker does NOT do.
- Vocabulary target for C2 is **12,280 cumulative** words (2,500 new at C2) — this fact is pedagogically motivating and belongs in the introduction section.
- Decolonized framing: Ukrainian is a fully independent language system with 7 functional styles codified in its own National Standard — this is not a derivative feature but an autonomous normative tradition grounded in the Academy of Sciences of Ukraine and the 2019 Language Law. Never anchor any explanation to Russian as baseline.
- The "cultural mediator" role (Section 5) gains traction in the context of diaspora communities and post-2022 global Ukrainian language interest (Duolingo data cited in plan is a good hook, but verify exact figures before Phase B — do not fabricate percentages).
