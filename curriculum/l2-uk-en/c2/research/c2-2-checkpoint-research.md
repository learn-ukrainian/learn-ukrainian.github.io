## Cultural Hooks

1. **Інститут літератури ім. Т. Г. Шевченка НАН України** — Ukraine's flagship literary research institution, confirmed on uk.wikipedia.org as «академічна науково-дослідна установа літературознавчого профілю в Україні». Founded 1926, reorganised post-independence. It is the institutional source of canon-formation and methodological standards in Ukrainian literary scholarship — direct hook for Part I of the module.

2. **«Коронація слова»** — confirmed as «міжнародний літературний конкурс романів, п'єс, кіносценаріїв, пісенної лірики та творів для дітей» (uk.wikipedia.org). Launched 2001, it has been a major driver of contemporary popular fiction in Ukrainian, elevating writers like Люко Дашвар (Ірина Чернова) and Ірен Роздобудько — both confirmed active authors. Note: Роздобудько began with Russian-language poetry before switching to Ukrainian; this trajectory is a productive discussion point for Part V on the expanding literary canon.

## Common Learner Errors

1. **критерій → GEN.PL *критеріїв*** — Learners regularise to *«критеріїв»* (3rd declension soft) correctly, but often produce *«критерій» → «критеріїв»* under Russian interference (Rus. «критерий → критериев»). The Ukrainian form **критеріїв** is regular; reinforce by contrast with the pattern *геній → геніїв*, *сценарій → сценаріїв*.

2. **оцінювання vs. оцінка** — Learners conflate the deverbal process noun **оцінювання** (the act/process of assessing) with the result noun **оцінка** (a grade/mark/valuation). In academic discourse these are non-interchangeable: *система оцінювання* (the assessment system) ≠ *висока оцінка* (a high mark). This is explicitly flagged in the plan and is an authentic C2-level distinction.

3. **Anglicism creep in academic register** — At C2 level, learners writing extended academic prose default to calques: **фідбек** (feedback → **зворотний зв'язок**), **бекграунд** (background → **тло** / **контекст**), **аутентичний** for *справжній*. The plan correctly targets this; content should model the pitomі відповідники consistently and offer substitution exercises.

## Notes for Content Writing

- **Naratology caveat**: «наратологія» has a GRAC frequency of only 29 occurrences (0.01 IPM) — it is genuinely specialist. Use it, but gloss it on first mention as «наратологія (теорія оповіді)» and tie directly to recognisable Ukrainian literary examples (e.g., Хвильовий's unreliable narrator in «Я (Романтика)»).
- **§4.3.8 secondary texts**: The plan requires production of рефератів and повідомлень as вторинні тексти. The textbook definition (Litvinova, grade 7) is the correct authoritative grounding. At C2 this should be elevated to academic abstract-writing (наукове реферування), not a recapitulation of school-level summarising.
- **Люко Дашвар / Ірен Роздобудько in mass vs. elite discourse**: Both are legitimate popular fiction writers who gained traction via «Коронація слова». The discussion in Part V about «масова та елітарна культура» should be framed as a question of **literary competence criteria**, not a value judgement on genre — i.e., what linguistic and compositional standards apply across the spectrum.
- **Decolonized framing**: The Інститут літератури ім. Шевченка section should foreground Ukrainian scholarly methodology on its own terms (e.g., the Харківська школа, шістдесятники criticism). Do not frame Ukrainian literary theory as responding to Soviet frameworks — describe it as an independent scholarly tradition with its own schools and debates.
- **Word budget**: 5000-word minimum. With seven content sections totalling 5125 words in the plan outline, there is only a 2.5% buffer. Write at 1.5× overshoot target (~5500–5800 words) to ensure the gate passes comfortably.
