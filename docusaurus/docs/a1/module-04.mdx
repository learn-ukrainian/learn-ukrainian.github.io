## Warm-up

Here's something that feels strange at first: Ukrainian doesn't use "am," "is," or "are" in the present tense!

In English, we say: "I **am** a student. She **is** Ukrainian. This **is** a book."

- Ğ¯ ÑÑ‚ÑƒĞ´ĞµĞ½Ñ‚. (I student.)

Why? Because Ukrainian belongs to the "pro-drop" language family. The verb "to be" (Ğ±ÑƒÑ‚Ğ¸) exists, but in present tense statements, it's invisibleâ€”like an understood handshake. You know it's there, but you don't say it.

> ğŸ’¡ **Did You Know?**
>
