# Food & Drinks

## Warm-up

Food is at the heart of Ukrainian culture. From the world-famous **–±–æ—Ä—â** (borshch) to the beloved **–≤–∞—Ä–µ–Ω–∏–∫–∏** (varenyky), Ukrainian cuisine tells stories of tradition, family, and history. When you learn food vocabulary in Ukrainian, you're not just learning words ‚Äî you're opening the door to Ukrainian hospitality.

> üí° **Did You Know?**
>
> **–ë–æ—Ä—â** (borshch) entered English directly from Ukrainian, not Russian! UNESCO recognized Ukrainian borshch cooking culture as part of Ukraine's intangible cultural heritage in 2022. Each region of Ukraine has its own borshch recipe ‚Äî some add beans, others use mushrooms, but beets (–±—É—Ä—è–∫) are always the star.

### Meals of the Day

Ukrainians typically eat three main meals:

- **–°–Ω—ñ–¥–∞–Ω–æ–∫** (snidanok) ‚Äî Breakfast. From the verb "—Å–Ω—ñ–¥–∞—Ç–∏" (to have breakfast). Literally means "morning meal."
- **–û–±—ñ–¥** (obid) ‚Äî Lunch. The main meal of the day, traditionally eaten around 1-3 PM.
- **–í–µ—á–µ—Ä—è** (vecherya) ‚Äî Dinner/supper. A lighter evening meal, usually around 6-8 PM.

**Example sentences:**

## Cultural Insight

### Food and Ukrainian Identity

Food is deeply tied to Ukrainian identity. During the **Holodomor** (1932-33), Stalin's forced famine killed millions of Ukrainians by confiscating grain and food. Talking about food, cooking traditional dishes, and preserving recipes became acts of cultural survival.

Today, Ukrainian food culture is experiencing a renaissance. Chefs are reviving old recipes, UNESCO has recognized borshch culture, and Ukrainian restaurants are proud symbols of national identity.

> üí° **Did You Know?**
>
> The word **–≥–æ–ª–æ–¥** (holod) means both "hunger" and "famine" in Ukrainian. The **Holodomor** (–ì–æ–ª–æ–¥–æ–º–æ—Ä) literally means "death by hunger" ‚Äî a genocide where food was used as a weapon. This historical tragedy makes food and hospitality even more meaningful in Ukrainian culture. When a Ukrainian offers you food, they're sharing more than a meal ‚Äî they're sharing survival, resilience, and hope.
