# Warm-up

"How are you?"

# Deep Dive: Active vs Passive Emotions

In English, you usually "are" something (I am happy, I am sad). In Ukrainian, you have three choices, and they change the nuance.

## 1. The State (Dative) - "It is happening to me"
**ĞœĞµĞ½Ñ– ÑÑƒĞ¼Ğ½Ğ¾.** (To me [it is] sad.)
This is your internal atmosphere. It just IS. You are the vessel of this feeling.
- *ĞœĞµĞ½Ñ– Ğ¿Ñ€Ğ¸ĞºÑ€Ğ¾.* (I feel pity/regret - It is a pity to me.)

# Myth Buster: The "Cold" Slavs?

:::warning
"Ukrainians are cold and unemotional."
**FALSE.**
**The Truth:** Ukrainians distinguish between "public face" and "private face". On the street, a neutral or serious expression is considered politeâ€”you don't impose your emotions on strangers. This is sometimes called the "peach vs coconut" culture theory. Americans are "peaches" (soft outside, hard inside). Ukrainians are "coconuts" (hard shell, but soft and sweet inside).
Once you are a friend, Ukrainians are incredibly emotional, warm, and open. We share problems, joys, and "pour out the soul" (**Ğ²Ğ¸Ğ»Ğ¸Ğ²Ğ°Ñ‚Ğ¸ Ğ´ÑƒÑˆÑƒ**). If a Ukrainian is "cold" to you, it just means you are still a stranger.
:::
