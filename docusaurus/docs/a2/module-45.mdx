# Warm-up

–õ–∞—Å–∫–∞–≤–æ –ø—Ä–æ—Å–∏–º–æ –¥–æ–¥–æ–º—É! Welcome home! This module covers rooms, furniture, and household vocabulary. Being able to describe your home is essential for daily conversations in Ukrainian.

Whether you are looking for an apartment, describing your living situation, or simply chatting about home life, this vocabulary will help you communicate naturally.

Ukrainian homes often differ from Western ones. Many Ukrainians live in apartments rather than houses. The concept of "–¥–∞—á–∞" (country house) is also very important in Ukrainian culture.

:::note[Ukrainian Apartments]
Many Ukrainians live in apartments rather than houses.
A typical apartment has a living room, bedroom, kitchen, and bathroom.
The balcony is an important space for storage and relaxation.
Apartment buildings from the Soviet era are still common in Ukrainian cities. These buildings usually have five to nine floors. Newer buildings may have elevators, but older ones often do not.
:::
