## Warm-up

"Did you hear what she said?"
Reporting speech is one of the most common social activities. Whether it's news, instructions, or gossip, we constantly repeat what others say.
In English, this is tricky (He said he *was* going...).
In Ukrainian, it's easier! You usually keep the original tense.

**We will cover:**
- **The Connector:** Ğ©Ğ¾
- **The Tense:** Keeping it real (present stays present)
- **The Verbs:** Saying vs Telling vs Asking

> ğŸ’¡ **Think about it**
> Imagine someone says **"I am hungry."**
> In English, you report: "He said (that) he **was** hungry."
> In Ukrainian, you report: "Ğ’Ñ–Ğ½ ÑĞºĞ°Ğ·Ğ°Ğ², Ñ‰Ğ¾ Ğ²Ñ–Ğ½ **Ğ³Ğ¾Ğ»Ğ¾Ğ´Ğ½Ğ¸Ğ¹**" (He said that he [is] hungry).
> Logic: At that moment in history, he *is* hungry.

## Cultural Insight

## The Grandmother News Network

Ukrainians joke about the **"BBC"** (Ğ‘Ğ°Ğ±Ğ° Ğ‘Ğ°Ğ±Ñ– Ğ¡ĞºĞ°Ğ·Ğ°Ğ»Ğ° - "One Grandma Said to Another").
This is a powerful informal network. If you live in an apartment block, the grandmothers on the bench know:
1.  When you came home.
2.  Who you were with.
3.  What you were carrying.

If you respect them and say "Dobryi den", you are a "good child". If not, reports will spread that you are rude or suspicious!

:::note[The Language of Resistance]
During the Soviet era, reported speech was often a tool of propaganda. Official reports would often start with "ĞŸĞ¾Ğ²Ñ–Ğ´Ğ¾Ğ¼Ğ»ÑÑÑ‚ÑŒ, Ñ‰Ğ¾..." (It is reported that...) to distance the source from the information. However, the Ukrainian underground press used reporting verbs to challenge these narratives, often using "Ğ¡Ñ‚Ğ²ĞµÑ€Ğ´Ğ¶ÑƒÑÑ‚ÑŒ" (Claim) to cast doubt on official lies.

Using your own voice to report the truth is a political act in Ukrainian history. When you report what someone said, you are becoming a witness and a transmitter of their story.
:::


Be careful with **"ĞĞ±Ñ–Ñ†ÑÑ‚Ğ¸"** (to promise). In strict contexts, *"ĞĞ±Ñ–Ñ†ÑĞ½ĞºĞ° â€” Ñ†ÑÑ†ÑĞ½ĞºĞ°, Ğ° Ğ´ÑƒÑ€Ğ½ĞµĞ²Ñ– Ñ€Ğ°Ğ´Ñ–ÑÑ‚ÑŒ"* (A promise is a toy, and a fool is happy). This proverb means promises are often empty. Ukrainian culture values action over words.
However, **"Ğ”Ğ°Ñ‚Ğ¸ ÑĞ»Ğ¾Ğ²Ğ¾"** (To give one's word) is serious.
* *Ğ’Ñ–Ğ½ Ğ´Ğ°Ğ² ÑĞ»Ğ¾Ğ²Ğ¾, Ñ‰Ğ¾ Ğ·Ñ€Ğ¾Ğ±Ğ¸Ñ‚ÑŒ Ñ†Ğµ.*

:::tip[Myth: Ukrainian is just a dialect]
