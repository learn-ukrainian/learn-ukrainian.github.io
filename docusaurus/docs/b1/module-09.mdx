### The Core Decision Framework

When choosing aspect, ask yourself these three questions:

| Question | If Yes ‚Üí | If No ‚Üí |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Am I emphasizing a **result** or completion? | Perfective | Imperfective |
| Is this a **one-time, bounded** action? | Perfective | Imperfective |
| Is there a **specific endpoint** or deadline? | Perfective | Imperfective |

### Quick Reference Summary

**Use PERFECTIVE (–¥–æ–∫–æ–Ω–∞–Ω–∏–π) for:**
- Results / completion / achievement
- One-time, bounded actions
- Sequences of events (then... then... then...)
- Beginning or ending of an action (—Ä–∞–ø—Ç–æ–º –∑–∞–≥–æ–≤–æ—Ä–∏–≤)

**Use IMPERFECTIVE (–Ω–µ–¥–æ–∫–æ–Ω–∞–Ω–∏–π) for:**
- Process / duration / ongoing activity
- Repetition / habits
- Background actions in narratives
- General experience questions
- Most prohibitions and invitations

### The Path Forward

From this point on, focus on exposure and production. Read Ukrainian texts, listen to native speakers, and notice how they use aspect. The more you're exposed to natural usage, the more automatic your own aspect selection will become.
