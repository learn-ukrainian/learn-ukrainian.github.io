### The Semantic Distinction Between Time and Content

Understanding when to use –∫–æ–ª–∏ versus —â–æ requires grasping the fundamental difference between temporal reference and content reference. This distinction exists in English too, but less visibly: compare "the day when everything changed" (temporal) versus "the fact that everything changed" (content/factual).

In Ukrainian, this distinction is grammaticalized explicitly. –ö–æ–ª–∏ always implies a time frame ‚Äî a moment, period, or occasion when something occurs. These words name segments of time, and –∫–æ–ª–∏ introduces what happened during that segment.

–©–æ, on the other hand, introduces factual or abstract content. When you say ¬´—Ç–µ, —â–æ –≤—ñ–Ω —Å–∫–∞–∑–∞–≤¬ª (what he said), you're not locating something in time ‚Äî you're identifying a piece of content or information.
