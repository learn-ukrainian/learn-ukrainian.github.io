> üîó **The Logic of Negation + Aspect**
>
> In English, you can say "I didn't read it" regardless of whether you never read it or just haven't finished yet. Ukrainian requires you to be specific:
>
> | Ukrainian | Meaning | Logic |
> |-----------|---------|-------|
> | **–ù–µ —á–∏—Ç–∞–≤** | Never read (in my life) | Denies the process ever existed |
> | **–©–µ –Ω–µ –ø—Ä–æ—á–∏—Ç–∞–≤** | Haven't read yet | Process expected, result pending |
>
> **Why imperfective for general negation?** When you deny something happened, you're denying the *process* existed. No process = no result. Since imperfective describes process, it's the natural choice for "total absence."
>
