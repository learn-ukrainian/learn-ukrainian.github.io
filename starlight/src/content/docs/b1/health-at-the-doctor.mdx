## Здоров'я і самопочуття (~720 words total)

Health is the foundation of a happy life. When we are healthy, we rarely think about visiting doctors or going to hospitals. But when illness strikes, we suddenly realize that physical well-being is our most valuable asset. The Ukrainian word for health is **здоров'я** (health). This fundamental concept belongs to a rich lexical family built around the root "здоров-", which gives us the adjective "здоровий" (healthy) and the perfective verb **одужати** (to recover). When dealing with a serious **хвороба** (disease), you will need to see a professional **лікар** (doctor).

Здоров'я — це найважливіше, що є в людини. Коли ми здорові, ми не думаємо про лікарів чи аптеки. Але коли починається серйозна хвороба, нам потрібен професійний лікар.

> *Health is the most important thing a person has. When we are healthy, we do not think about doctors or pharmacies. But when a serious disease begins, we need a professional doctor.*

Let's look closely at the spelling of the noun "здоров'я". You will notice an apostrophe before the final letter «я». In Ukrainian orthography, the apostrophe acts as a crucial phonetic separator. It appears after labial consonants—like [в]—when they are directly followed by jotted vowels such as «я», «ю», «є», or «ї». The apostrophe tells you to pronounce the [в] hard and clear, and to pronounce the «я» as two distinct sounds: [й] + [а]. This rule preserves the phonetic integrity of the root.

When you need to describe how you feel physically, Ukrainian uses two distinct grammatical patterns. The first pattern is the possessive construction with the preposition «у». We use this specific structure to say that a specific body part hurts. The body part is the grammatical subject (in the Nominative case), and the verb agrees with it.

У мене болить голова. У дитини дуже сильно болить живіт. Якщо болять кілька різних частин тіла, ми використовуємо дієслово у формі множини. Наприклад, після тренування у мене болять ноги і спина.

The second pattern is the Dative experiencer construction. We use this when describing a general physical state or a sensation that happens to us, rather than a specific localized pain. In this structure, the person experiencing the state is in the Dative case, followed by an adverb of state or an impersonal verb. For example, if you suddenly get a high **температура** (temperature), you might use this pattern.

Мені сьогодні дуже погано. Мені холодно, бо у мене несподівано піднялася висока температура. Мене трохи нудить після поїздки в старому автобусі.

Notice the grammatical difference: "my head hurts" takes the Nominative subject "голова" and the possessive "у мене". But "I feel bad" uses the dative "мені" with the adverb "погано". 

The medical vocabulary provides excellent examples of Ukrainian morphophonemic alternations. Let's carefully examine the noun **біль** (pain) and the verb "болить" (it hurts). This is a classic example of the [і] to [о] vowel shift.

В українській мові іменник «біль» належить до чоловічого роду. Ми завжди кажемо «сильний головний біль», а не «сильна». У називному відмінку це слово має закритий склад, тому ми чітко чуємо звук [і]. Але коли ми змінюємо форму слова, цей склад стає відкритим.

> *In the Ukrainian language, the noun "pain" belongs to the masculine gender. We always say "strong headache (masculine)," not "strong (feminine)." In the nominative case, this word has a closed syllable, so we clearly hear the sound [i]. But when we change the form of the word, this syllable becomes open.*

Because the syllable opens up when a vowel ending is added, the [і] naturally shifts back to [о]. This is exactly why the verb is "болить" and the genitive form is "болю". We can also trace this alternation in word formation. If we add the prefix «без-» (without) and the suffix «-н-», we create the adjective "безболісний" (painless). Here, the syllable containing the root vowel is closed once again, so the [і] safely returns.

This vowel alternation is a fundamental pattern of the Ukrainian language. We see the exact same [і] to [о] shift in many names for body parts. The word for nose is «ніс» (closed syllable), but if you want to say you don't have a runny nose, you say «немає носа» (open syllable). It is common to have a strong **нежить** (runny nose) or a deep **кашель** (cough) in winter. A blocked nose is a very unpleasant **симптом** (symptom).

Узимку люди часто мають сильний нежить і глибокий кашель. Коли людина має нежить, вона не може нормально дихати через ніс. Це дуже неприємний симптом.

:::info
**Grammar box**
Remember that both «нежить» and «кашель» are masculine nouns of the second declension in Ukrainian. You will always use them with masculine adjectives, like "сильний нежить".
:::

Consonant alternations are equally important when discussing health. When we put body parts into the Locative case to say where something hurts, the final root consonant often shifts. The word "вухо" (ear) ends in the consonant [х], but in the Locative it becomes «у вусі» (the [х] changes to [с]).

У мене несподівано стріляє у вусі. Лікар дуже уважно дивиться в оці пацієнта. Ці чергування приголосних звуків роблять українську мову такою мелодійною.

Similarly, the word "око" (eye) ends in [к], which reliably shifts to [ц] in the Locative, giving us «в оці». These are the exact same consonant shifts you learned for places, like the noun **аптека** (pharmacy) becoming «в аптеці» (at the pharmacy). Recognizing these phonetic patterns helps you predict how new medical vocabulary will behave.

## У лікаря: діалог (~880 words total)

When you visit a medical clinic in Ukraine, your first point of contact is usually the registry, where you explain your issue and get directed to a general practitioner. The **лікар** (doctor) is the central figure in the healthcare system. They will listen to your symptoms, conduct a primary physical examination, and decide on the next steps. They can either formulate a treatment plan immediately, or they might realize that your condition requires more specialized care.

<div class="dialogue">

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Лікар:</span> Доброго дня! Проходьте, сідайте. На що скаржитесь? *(Good afternoon! Come in, sit down. What are you complaining about?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Добрий день. У мене вже третій день тримається висока температура, є сильний кашель і нежить. *(Good afternoon. I've had a high temperature for the third day now, along with a bad cough and a runny nose.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Лікар:</span> Зрозуміло. Давайте я вас огляну. Відкрийте рота і скажіть «а». *(I see. Let me examine you. Open your mouth and say "a".)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> А-а-а. Мені дуже боляче ковтати. *(A-a-a. It hurts a lot to swallow.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Лікар:</span> Горло дійсно червоне. Тепер я послухаю ваші легені. Зробіть глибокий вдих. *(Your throat is indeed red. Now I will listen to your lungs. Take a deep breath.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Робить вдих. *(Takes a breath.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Лікар:</span> Тепер не дихайте. Добре, можете дихати нормально. У вас звичайна застуда, але потрібен спокій. Я призначу вам ліки. *(Now hold your breath. Good, you can breathe normally. You have a common cold, but you need rest. I will prescribe you medicine.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Чи потрібен мені спеціальний рецепт для цих препаратів? *(Do I need a special prescription for these preparations?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Лікар:</span> Так, я випишу вам рецепт. Ви зможете купити все необхідне в найближчій аптеці. Якщо вам не стане краще через п'ять днів, я випишу вам направлення до профільного спеціаліста. *(Yes, I will write you a prescription. You will be able to buy everything you need at the nearest pharmacy. If you don't get better in five days, I will write you a referral to a specialized specialist.)*</div>

</div>

In this conversation, the specialist uses several key terms that you should memorize. The word **кашель** (cough) and the word **нежить** (runny nose) are both masculine nouns belonging to the second declension soft group. You will always use them with masculine adjectives, such as saying that you have a strong cough. This grammatical trait is essential for making accurate descriptions.

When the medical professional reaches a conclusion, they establish a **діагноз** (diagnosis). To resolve the issue, they will **призначити** (to prescribe) specific medical products. It is important to follow their instructions carefully to ensure a smooth recovery. This ensures that your symptoms clear up as quickly as possible.

Хоча терапевт лікує більшість поширених недуг, стоматологічне здоров'я вимагає зовсім іншого словникового запасу. Візит до стоматолога передбачає використання специфічних анатомічних термінів та швидкий обмін командами. Оскільки руки лікаря зайняті, а ви не можете розмовляти з відкритим ротом, спілкування часто є одностороннім. Стоматолог активно використовує наказовий спосіб, щоб безпечно та ефективно провести вас через процедуру.

<div class="dialogue">

<div class="dialogue-line">*While a general practitioner handles most common ailments, dental health requires a completely different vocabulary set. Visiting a dentist involves specific anatomical terms and a rapid exchange of commands. Because the dentist's hands are busy and you cannot speak while your mouth is open, the communication is often one-sided. The dentist relies heavily on the imperative mood to guide you through the procedure safely and efficiently.*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Стоматолог:</span> Доброго дня! Сідайте в крісло. Що вас турбує? *(Good afternoon! Sit in the chair. What is bothering you?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Добрий день. У мене дуже сильний біль у нижньому правому зубі. *(Good afternoon. I have a very strong pain in my lower right tooth.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Стоматолог:</span> Давайте подивимося. Відкрийте рота ширше. Покажіть, де саме болить. *(Let's take a look. Open your mouth wider. Show me exactly where it hurts.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Ось тут, біля ясен. *(Right here, near the gums.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Стоматолог:</span> Бачу. У вас тут глибокий карієс, і ясна запалені. Потрібна пломба. Я зроблю укол, щоб знеболити. Не рухайтеся, будь ласка. *(I see. You have a deep cavity here, and the gums are inflamed. A filling is needed. I will give you an injection to numb the pain. Don't move, please.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Це буде довго? *(Will this take long?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Стоматолог:</span> Ні, близько двадцяти хвилин. Закрийте очі і розслабтеся. *(No, about twenty minutes. Close your eyes and relax.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Добре, дякую. *(Okay, thank you.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Стоматолог:</span> Усе готово. Я поставив пломбу. Тепер сполосніть рот водою. *(All done. I've placed the filling. Now rinse your mouth with water.)*</div>

</div>

The dental vocabulary introduced here is highly specific but essential for everyday life. The core noun is зуб (tooth), and its plural form is used to describe your ясна (gums). During the dialogue, the patient complains of a severe **біль** (pain) in their lower right tooth. You will hear these words constantly in any dental clinic.

If a tooth is neglected, the decay can spread and lead to a serious **хвороба** (disease) of the gums or the surrounding bone. Throughout this brief consultation, the dentist uses the imperative mood extensively. Commands like «відкрийте» (open), «покажіть» (show), «не рухайтеся» (do not move), and «закрийте» (close) are all standard imperative forms directed at the second person plural. This direct grammar allows the dental professional to work efficiently.

One of the most challenging aspects of medical Ukrainian for learners is mastering the case government of medical verbs. Case government dictates which preposition and grammatical case a verb demands from its object. In English, you might say you are "sick with" something, and in some other Slavic languages, you might use the instrumental case without a preposition. However, Ukrainian has its own strict, unique rules that you must follow to sound natural and fluent. Using the wrong case or preposition immediately marks your speech as foreign or influenced by a different language.

Найпоширенішою синтаксичною помилкою є неправильне керування дієслова «хворіти». В українській мові ми завжди кажемо «хворіти на» та використовуємо іменник у знахідному відмінку. Наприклад, людина хворіє на грип або на тяжку застуду. Ця конструкція є важливою українською нормою, яку слід запам'ятати.

> *The most common syntactic mistake is the incorrect government of the verb "to be sick". In the Ukrainian language, we always say "хворіти на" (to be sick with) and use the noun in the accusative case. For example, a person is sick with the flu or with a severe cold. This construction is an important Ukrainian norm that should be remembered.*

Let us analyze the core medical verbs and the specific cases they govern. The verb **скаржитися** (to complain) requires the preposition "на" and the accusative case. When a patient arrives at the clinic, they might state that they are experiencing a terrible **симптом** (symptom). You would say "Я скаржуся на сильний головний біль" to accurately express your condition.

Another crucial verb is **лікувати** (to treat), which is an imperfective action describing the medical process. If you are describing the process of receiving medical care, you use the reflexive form with the preposition "від" (from) and the genitive case. You might say that your friend is being treated for an infection by saying "Він лікується від інфекції". This preposition clearly marks the source of the ailment.

After completing the prescribed course of treatment, the patient will finally **одужати** (to recover). Because recovery implies moving away from an illness, it logically takes the preposition "від" and the genitive case. The ultimate goal of any medical intervention is to protect your **здоров'я** (health). This successful recovery concludes the entire medical journey.

During a consultation, the doctor might check your **температура** (temperature) to rule out a fever. If everything is clear, they will write a **рецепт** (prescription) for you. This piece of paper is essential for obtaining restricted treatments. It serves as an official authorization from your healthcare provider.

You will then take this document to the nearest **аптека** (pharmacy) to purchase your supplies. You must remember that the word **ліки** (medicine) is a pluralia tantum noun, so all adjectives and verbs agreeing with it must be in the plural form. This means you cannot say that you need "one medicine" in Ukrainian. You must always treat it grammatically as a plural entity.

:::info
**Grammar box**
Always verify the gender of medical nouns to ensure your adjectives agree correctly. Both **кашель** (cough) and **нежить** (runny nose) are masculine nouns in Ukrainian. You must say "сильний кашель" and "сильний нежить". Treating them as feminine is a common learner mistake.
:::

## Спеціалісти та обстеження (~720 words total)

When you arrive at a clinic, your first point of contact is usually a general practitioner. They assess your condition and decide if you need to see a specialist. Depending on your symptoms, they might refer you to a surgeon for injuries, an eye doctor for vision problems, or a pediatrician for a child. Understanding these titles is essential for navigating the healthcare system and maintaining your **здоров'я** (health).

У великій поліклініці працюють різні медичні спеціалісти. Спочатку пацієнт іде до терапевта, який слухає скарги та проводить огляд. Якщо у людини серйозна травма, терапевт направляє її до хірурга. Коли виникають проблеми із зором, допоможе окуліст. Для дітей є окремий лікар — педіатр. Кожен спеціаліст відіграє важливу роль у збереженні здоров'я.

> *In a large polyclinic, various medical specialists work. First, the patient goes to a therapist, who listens to complaints and conducts an examination. If a person has a serious injury, the therapist refers them to a surgeon. When vision problems arise, an eye doctor will help. For children, there is a separate doctor — a pediatrician. Each specialist plays an important role in maintaining health.*

Let us look closely at the word **лікар** (doctor). You might recall that nouns denoting professions often use specific suffixes. Here, we see the agent suffix **-ар**. Because this suffix ends in a historically soft consonant, the word belongs to the soft group of the second declension. This means it behaves exactly like the words for a schoolboy or a baker. When you decline these words, you must use soft endings across all cases.

Before a doctor can make a decision, they usually require clinical data. This brings us to medical investigations, known as **обстеження** (examination). When medical professionals talk about tests and measurements, they rely heavily on the genitive case. The noun that describes what is being tested must answer the question "of what?".

Під час медичного обстеження пацієнт повинен здати аналізи, щоб лікар побачив повну картину стану організму. Найчастіше вам призначать загальний аналіз крові або аналіз сечі. У кабінеті терапевта обов'язковою процедурою є вимірювання тиску та температури. Іноді потрібні більш глибокі дослідження, наприклад, рентген або ультразвукове дослідження. Всі ці процедури допомагають знайти причину хвороби.

> *During a medical examination, a patient must take tests so that the doctor can see the full picture of the body's condition. Most often, you will be prescribed a general blood test or a urine test. In the therapist's office, a mandatory procedure is measuring blood pressure and temperature. Sometimes deeper investigations are needed, for example, an X-ray or an ultrasound examination. All these procedures help find the cause of the disease.*

:::info
**Grammar box**
Notice how the genitive case connects the action to the substance. We say "аналіз крові" (a test of blood) and "вимірювання тиску" (a measurement of pressure). The word "тиск" takes the **-у** ending in the genitive case.
:::

The word **кров** (blood) deserves special attention. It is a feminine noun ending in a consonant, which places it in the third declension. Nouns in this group have a distinct declension pattern. In the genitive case, they take the ending **-і**. Therefore, a blood test is translated using this specific case form. Remembering the third declension rules is vital, since many medical terms and names for a **хвороба** (disease) belong to this group.

Now it is time to practice reading formal medical documents. When you are discharged from a hospital, you receive a medical summary. This document uses a formal register and contains specific technical abbreviations. For instance, you will frequently see УЗД, which stands for an ultrasound examination. Read the following simulated medical summary and identify the patient's main complaints, the final **діагноз** (diagnosis), and the prescribed treatment.

Медична виписка: Пацієнт Іваненко О. П. звернувся до клініки зі скаргами на сильний головний біль, високу температуру та постійний кашель. Під час обстеження лікар зафіксував нежить і загальну слабкість. Було проведено аналіз крові та УЗД внутрішніх органів. На основі результатів встановлено діагноз: гостра респіраторна вірусна інфекція. Лікар призначив пацієнту постільний режим. Призначено такі ліки: таблетки для зниження температури та сироп проти кашлю. Пацієнт повинен приймати ліки двічі на день після їди. Наступний огляд заплановано через тиждень.

> *Medical discharge summary: Patient Ivanenko O. P. applied to the clinic with complaints of a severe headache, high temperature, and a constant cough. During the examination, the doctor recorded a runny nose and general weakness. A blood test and an ultrasound of internal organs were performed. Based on the results, a diagnosis was established: acute respiratory viral infection. The doctor prescribed bed rest for the patient. The following medicines were prescribed: pills for reducing temperature and cough syrup. The patient must take the medicines twice a day after meals. The next examination is scheduled in a week.*

Notice how the text utilizes passive constructions to describe the medical actions. This impersonal style is the hallmark of professional medical records in Ukraine. It removes the focus from the doctor and places all emphasis on the clinical facts. When you read your own medical documents, look for the word **симптом** (symptom) to quickly locate important information. Being able to scan these summaries will give you confidence when you receive a **рецепт** (prescription) or purchase your **ліки** (medicine) at an **аптека** (pharmacy).

## В аптеці (~770 words total)

After a thorough examination at the clinic, your next destination is usually the **аптека** (pharmacy). Navigating a Ukrainian pharmacy requires understanding specific instructions regarding dosage and timing to properly treat your **хвороба** (disease). The pharmacist will often ask for your **рецепт** (prescription) before dispensing serious medications. While some basic remedies for a mild cold can be purchased freely, you must be prepared to specify exactly what the doctor ordered. Let us observe a typical interaction at the counter.

<div class="dialogue">

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Доброго дня. У мене рецепт від лікаря. Чи є у вас ці ліки? *(Good day. I have a prescription from the doctor. Do you have this medicine?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Аптекар:</span> Доброго дня. Покажіть, будь ласка. Так, вони є в наявності. Ось ваші таблетки. *(Good day. Please show it. Yes, they are in stock. Here are your pills.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Підкажіть, скільки разів на день їх треба приймати? *(Tell me, how many times a day should I take them?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Аптекар:</span> Приймайте по одній таблетці тричі на день після їди. *(Take one tablet three times a day after meals.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> А чи є у вас якісь краплі від нежитю? *(And do you have any drops for a runny nose?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Аптекар:</span> Звісно. Я можу запропонувати ці краплі, вони дуже ефективні. *(Of course. I can offer these drops, they are very effective.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Пацієнт:</span> Дякую. Я візьму і ліки, і краплі. *(Thank you. I will take both the medicine and the drops.)*</div>

</div>

В аптеці ви часто чуєте вказівки щодо часу прийому. Фраза «після їди» означає, що препарат не можна пити на порожній шлунок. Якщо фармацевт каже «до їди», вам потрібно випити таблетку за пів години до сніданку чи обіду. Також важливо пам'ятати слово «дозування», яке визначає безпечну кількість препарату для вашого організму.

> *In the pharmacy, you often hear instructions regarding the time of taking the medication. The phrase "після їди" means that the preparation must not be drunk on an empty stomach. If the pharmacist says "до їди", you need to drink the pill half an hour before breakfast or lunch. It is also important to remember the word "дозування", which determines the safe amount of the preparation for your body.*

Now, let us look closely at the grammatical behavior of medical vocabulary, specifically the word for medicine itself. In Ukrainian, **ліки** (medicine) is a classic example of *pluralia tantum* — a noun that only exists in the plural form. You cannot buy "one medicine" as a singular grammatical object; you are always buying medications as a collective concept. Because it is inherently plural, any adjectives, demonstrative pronouns, or verbs describing it must also take plural endings. You will confidently say "ці ліки" (this medicine) or "ефективні ліки" (effective medicine), but never "цей лік". 

Оскільки слово «ліки» завжди має форму множини, воно відмінюється за спеціальними правилами. У називному відмінку ми кажемо «нові ліки». Якщо препаратів немає, ми використовуємо родовий відмінок і кажемо «немає ліків». Коли ми довіряємо певному засобу, ми вживаємо давальний відмінок — «довіряти цим лікам». А процес терапії вимагає орудного відмінка: «лікуватися дорогими ліками».

> *Since the word "ліки" always has a plural form, it is declined according to special rules. In the nominative case, we say "нові ліки" (new medicine). If there are no preparations, we use the genitive case and say "немає ліків". When we trust a certain remedy, we use the dative case — "довіряти цим лікам". And the therapy process requires the instrumental case: "лікуватися дорогими ліками" (to be treated with expensive medicine).*

:::info
**Grammar box**
Remember that *pluralia tantum* nouns trigger plural agreement across the entire sentence. When you talk about quantity, you must use the genitive plural form: "багато ліків" (a lot of medicine). Another common medical word that follows this exact same pattern is **краплі** (drops). You will ask for "краплі від нежитю" (drops for a runny nose) and state that you bought "багато крапель" (many drops).
:::

Ukrainian vocabulary is deeply interconnected through root words. Understanding these connections makes learning new terminology much more intuitive. The medical sphere is a perfect environment to observe these word-formation chains in action. The core concept originates from the verb **лікувати** (to treat), an imperfective verb describing the ongoing process of providing medical care. From this foundational verb, we form the noun for the process itself: **лікування** (treatment). Finally, the physical substances used to execute this process give us the noun **ліки** (medicine). All these words share the same identifying root, linking the action, the process, and the object.

Цей самий корінь формує ще одне дуже важливе слово — «лікар». Лікар — це професіонал, який проводить лікування. Коли ви приходите на прийом, лікар уважно слухає, на що ви вирішили поскаржитися, ставить діагноз і дає корисну пораду. Усе це робиться для того, щоб ви могли швидко одужати і відновити своє здоров'я. Іноді вам потрібна лише порада, але часто вам також необхідний офіційний документ, який лікар має призначити, щоб ви могли піти в аптеку.

> *This same root forms another very important word — "лікар" (doctor). A doctor is a professional who conducts treatment. When you come for an appointment, the doctor listens carefully to what you have decided to complain about, makes a diagnosis, and gives useful advice. All this is done so that you can quickly recover and restore your health. Sometimes you only need advice, but often you also need an official document, which the doctor has to prescribe, so that you can go to the pharmacy.*

You must draw a clear distinction between these types of medical guidance. A "порада" (advice) is a general recommendation, perhaps suggesting you drink more water or get extra rest. However, a **рецепт** (prescription) is a strict, formal authorization required to purchase regulated medications. While a pharmacist might gladly offer you a simple piece of advice for a mild cough, they will absolutely demand a prescription if your illness requires specialized antibiotics.

## Хвороби та симптоми (~770 words total)

Winter and changing seasons bring various illnesses. When you visit a clinic, you must accurately describe your condition using specific medical vocabulary. The most common **хвороба** (disease) during the cold months is usually a simple cold or the seasonal flu. To get the right treatment and avoid misunderstandings, you need to articulate every **симптом** (symptom) clearly. If you need a doctor's help, you will use the verb **скаржитися** (to complain), which requires the preposition «на» followed by the accusative case. However, this is where many learners make a critical grammatical mistake due to interference from other Slavic languages. 

Узимку найчастіша хвороба — це застуда або небезпечний грип. Головний симптом застуди — це закладений ніс та сильний нежить. Пам'ятайте, що слово «нежить» має виключно чоловічий рід, тому ми кажемо «гострий нежить», а не інакше. Також до чоловічого роду належить слово «біль». Коли пацієнт приходить у поліклініку, він скаржиться на сильний головний біль або різкий біль у горлі. Якщо ви скажете лікарю про «сильну біль», він вас зрозуміє, але це буде помітною граматичною помилкою. Зверніть увагу, що ці іменники належать до м'якої групи другої відміни.

> *In winter, the most frequent disease is a cold or the dangerous flu. The main symptom of a cold is a stuffy nose and a severe runny nose. Remember that the word "нежить" (runny nose) has an exclusively masculine gender, so we say "гострий нежить" (acute runny nose), and not otherwise. The word "біль" (pain) also belongs to the masculine gender. When a patient arrives at the clinic, they complain of a severe headache or a sharp pain in the throat. If you tell the doctor about "сильну біль" (using feminine agreement), they will understand you, but it will be a noticeable grammatical error. Note that these nouns belong to the soft group of the second declension.*

The nouns for pain and a runny nose are strictly masculine in Ukrainian. This means all adjectives must agree with the masculine form. You will experience a "сильний біль" (strong pain) and suffer from a "тривалий нежить" (prolonged runny nose). When these words decline, they follow the standard masculine soft group patterns. In the dative case, you treat a runny nose with drops, giving us the form "від нежитю". The noun for a cough, **кашель** (cough), is also a masculine soft-group noun, but it features a fleeting vowel, losing its "е" when declined, becoming "від кашлю".

Beyond naming the specific ailment, you must often describe the physical sensations you are enduring. Ukrainian relies heavily on impersonal constructions to express how the body feels. Instead of saying that your body is doing something, you state that something is happening *to* you.

Окрім болю, хвора людина відчуває інші неприємні стани. Часто ми використовуємо безособові конструкції, щоб описати ці відчуття. Наприклад, пацієнт каже: «Мені боляче ковтати» або «Мені дуже холодно». Якщо у людини серйозні проблеми зі шлунком, вона скаже: «Мене нудить». Ще один типовий симптом інфекції — це висока температура. Коли ми вимірюємо тепло тіла, ми називаємо цифри з десятими частками. Лікар може запитати, яка у вас температура, і ви відповісте: «Тридцять сім і дві» або «Тридцять вісім і п'ять». У мене також часто буває сухий кашель, і я постійно чхаю.

> *Besides pain, a sick person experiences other unpleasant states. Often we use impersonal constructions to describe these sensations. For example, a patient says: "It hurts me to swallow" or "I am very cold". If a person has serious stomach problems, they will say: "I feel nauseous" (it nauseates me). Another typical symptom of an infection is a high temperature. When we measure body heat, we name the numbers with tenths. A doctor might ask what your temperature is, and you will answer: "Thirty-seven and two" or "Thirty-eight and five". I also often have a dry cough, and I constantly sneeze.*

Notice the difference in cases used for these sensations. For general feelings and pain, Ukrainian uses the dative case to mark the experiencer, as seen in "мені боляче" (it is painful to me). However, the verb for nausea is different. To say you are nauseous, you must use the accusative case, resulting in the phrase "мене нудить" (it nauseates me).

:::info
**Grammar box**
When Ukrainians report their **температура** (temperature), they use a very specific decimal format. Instead of saying "thirty-seven point two degrees", they simply say "тридцять сім і дві" (thirty-seven and two [tenths]). The word for degrees is almost always omitted in casual medical conversations. If your temperature is perfectly normal (36.6 °C), you can proudly announce: "тридцять шість і шість".
:::

Medical professionals prefer that you never get sick in the first place. Therefore, a significant portion of medical vocabulary revolves around avoiding illness. This includes building your body's natural defenses and following specific guidelines to stay healthy during the cold season. You will often hear conversations about maintaining immunity and getting vaccinations before the peak of flu season hits the country.

Найкращий спосіб уникнути вірусів — це своєчасна профілактика та міцний імунітет. Лікарі завжди наголошують, що хворобу легше попередити, ніж потім довго її лікувати. Для цього потрібно вести здоровий спосіб життя та правильно харчуватися. Восени також варто зробити щеплення від грипу, щоб захистити свій організм від серйозних ускладнень. Коли лікар дає вам рекомендації, він використовує спеціальні слова. Він скаже: «Вам слід більше відпочивати» або «Тобі варто пити багато теплого чаю». Ці слова завжди вимагають після себе дієслова в неозначеній формі. Якщо ви захворіли, вам необхідно залишатися вдома.

> *The best way to avoid viruses is timely prevention and strong immunity. Doctors always emphasize that a disease is easier to prevent than to treat for a long time later. For this, it is necessary to lead a healthy lifestyle and eat right. In autumn, it is also worth getting a flu vaccination to protect your body from serious complications. When a doctor gives you recommendations, they use special words. They will say: "You should rest more" or "It is worth it for you to drink a lot of warm tea". These words always require a verb in the infinitive form after them. If you get sick, it is necessary for you to stay at home.*

When a doctor or a concerned friend gives you medical advice, they will rely on modal words that express necessity or recommendation. The word "потрібно" indicates a strong necessity, translating directly to "must" or "need to". If the advice is a firm professional suggestion, you will hear "слід" (ought to or should). For friendlier, softer advice about what is a good idea, Ukrainians use "варто" (it is worth doing). All of these modal markers are followed immediately by an infinitive verb, making them incredibly easy to use once you memorize the pattern. You simply state the action that is worth doing to maintain your health and avoid a trip to the pharmacy.

## Підсумок: здоров'я українською

In this module, we have covered the essential vocabulary for discussing your **здоров'я** (health) with medical professionals. You are now fully prepared to visit a **лікар** (doctor) and accurately describe any **хвороба** (disease) you might be facing. Knowing these foundational terms is the first step toward effective communication in any clinical setting.

When you experience severe physical discomfort, such as a sharp **біль** (pain), or when your body reacts with a high **температура** (temperature), you know exactly how to express this. You can also report a persistent **кашель** (cough) without struggling to find the right morphological forms.

If a seasonal cold leaves you with a frustrating **нежить** (runny nose), you can explain your condition clearly using the correct masculine agreements. You also understand that you must eventually obtain specific **ліки** (medicine) to alleviate these issues, often requiring a formal **рецепт** (prescription) from the clinic.

With your paperwork in hand, you can confidently navigate an **аптека** (pharmacy) to purchase your required treatments. The pharmacists there will provide the right dosage based on the official **діагноз** (diagnosis) that was determined during your medical consultation.

You are now equipped to detail every single **симптом** (symptom) when you need to formally **скаржитися** (to complain) about your physical condition to a specialist. This precision allows the medical staff to figure out exactly how to best **лікувати** (to treat) you and manage your recovery process.

Ultimately, following their professional advice and taking the medication on schedule will help you **одужати** (to recover) completely and regain your strength. Remember that only a qualified medical professional can safely **призначити** (to prescribe) the most effective course of action for your specific health needs.

Окрім нової лексики, ми також повторили важливі граматичні правила. Ви побачили, як працює давальний відмінок, коли ми описуємо свій фізичний стан. Конструкції типу «мені холодно» або «мені боляче» є дуже природними для української мови. Також ми звернули увагу на керування дієслів, наприклад, необхідність використовувати знахідний відмінок після прийменника «на», коли ми говоримо про свої скарги.

> *Besides new vocabulary, we also reviewed important grammar rules. You saw how the Dative case works when we describe our physical condition. Constructions like "I am cold" or "I am in pain" are very natural for the Ukrainian language. We also paid attention to verb government, for example, the necessity to use the Accusative case after the preposition "на" when we talk about our complaints.*

Не менш важливою частиною цього модуля було повторення морфологічних явищ. Ви помітили, що багато медичних термінів підпорядковуються правилам чергування голосних і приголосних звуків. Знання цих фонетичних закономірностей допомагає інтуїтивно розуміти, як змінюється слово в різних відмінках. Це робить ваше мовлення не лише граматично правильним, але й фонетично милозвучним, що є характерною рисою справжньої української мови.

> *An equally important part of this module was the review of morphological phenomena. You noticed that many medical terms are subject to the rules of vowel and consonant alternations. Knowing these phonetic patterns helps you intuitively understand how a word changes in different cases. This makes your speech not only grammatically correct but also phonetically melodious, which is a characteristic feature of the authentic Ukrainian language.*

:::tip
**Did you know?** — The way you talk about your symptoms can reveal your level of language mastery. Using the correct gender for tricky nouns like pain and runny nose instantly signals that you have moved beyond beginner translation habits.
:::
