## Професія і фах: базова лексика (~1100 words total)

In the early stages of learning Ukrainian, you probably learned to describe your job using simple phrases like "I am a manager" or "I work in an office". Now that you are at the B1 level, it is time to move beyond these basic structures and develop a nuanced, precise vocabulary to describe your professional life. Work is a central part of social identity in Ukrainian culture. How you speak about your career often reflects your education, ambitions, and place in society. When Ukrainians meet for the first time, asking about work is a standard way to establish common ground. Therefore, having a rich vocabulary to discuss your employment is essential for building meaningful relationships and navigating formal environments.

Для багатьох українців робота — це не просто спосіб заробляти гроші, а важлива частина життя та соціальної самореалізації. У професійному середовищі високо цінують якісну освіту, прагнення до нових знань та вміння розповідати про свої кар'єрні досягнення. Коли ви вмієте точно описати свій професійний шлях, ви показуєте повагу до співрозмовника та демонструєте високий рівень володіння українською мовою.

> *For many Ukrainians, work is not just a way to earn money, but an important part of life and social self-realization. In the professional environment, quality education, a desire for new knowledge, and the ability to talk about your career achievements are highly valued. When you can accurately describe your professional path, you show respect for your interlocutor and demonstrate a high level of Ukrainian language proficiency.*

One of the most common challenges for learners is understanding the subtle semantic distinctions between core concepts related to employment. English often uses the word "job" or "profession" interchangeably, but Ukrainian has specific terms that divide this semantic field. The word **робота** (work/job) is the most general term for employment or any physical or mental effort. However, when talking about your official qualifications and specific roles, you need to use more precise vocabulary.

The word **професія** (profession) refers to a broad vocation or occupation that usually requires special training or formal education, such as a doctor or a teacher. In contrast, **фах** (specialty/field) denotes your specific area of expertise. Closely related to this is **спеціальність** (qualification), which is the exact qualification written on your university diploma.

Finally, your **посада** (position/role) is your specific job title within a given company at this exact moment. Understanding these differences allows you to present yourself accurately. You might have the profession of an engineer, your specialty might be software development, and your current position could be a senior developer. 

Дуже часто люди мають одну спеціальність за дипломом, але працюють зовсім в іншій сфері економіки. Наприклад, на співбесіді людина може сказати: «За фахом я філолог, але зараз моя посада — контент-менеджер у великій компанії». Це абсолютно нормальна ситуація для сучасного українського ринку праці, де гнучкість та вміння вчитися цінуються більше, ніж перша професія.

When describing where you work, you will encounter several essential terms that specify the type of organization or the physical space. A formal, often large-scale industrial or commercial organization is called a **підприємство** (enterprise). If you work in the corporate sector, IT, or services, you will more frequently use the word **компанія** (company).

Within that company, you likely belong to a specific **відділ** (department), such as the marketing department or the human resources department. And, of course, the physical place where administrative work happens is the **офіс** (office).

У кожному офісі чи на підприємстві формується свій унікальний колектив, де люди спілкуються, обмінюються ідеями та вирішують складні робочі завдання. Важливо вміти правильно звертатися до людей, з якими ви проводите значну частину часу.

> *In every office or enterprise, a unique team is formed where people communicate, exchange ideas, and solve complex work tasks. It is important to know how to properly address the people with whom you spend a significant part of your time.*

The people you work with directly are your colleagues. The Ukrainian word for this is **колега** (colleague). Grammatically, this word is fascinating because it belongs to a category called common gender (спільний рід). This means that the word itself always ends in "-а", but it takes masculine or feminine modifiers depending on the biological gender of the person you are referring to.

Якщо ви говорите про чоловіка, ви кажете «мій новий колега». Якщо про жінку — «моя нова колега». У множині форма однакова для всіх: «мої чудові колеги». Ось кілька прикладів: «Мій колега Ігор допоміг мені з проєктом», «Моя колега Олена найкраще знає англійську мову», «Усі колеги зібралися у залі для наради».

The journey of finding and starting a new job, known as **працевлаштування** (employment), follows a predictable lifecycle, and each stage has its own specific vocabulary. This process usually begins when a company announces a **вакансія** (vacancy), meaning an open position is officially available. To apply for this role, you need to submit your **резюме** (CV/resume).

:::info
**Grammar box**
The word **резюме** is an indeclinable noun (невідмінюваний іменник) of neuter gender. This means its ending never changes, regardless of the grammatical case it is in. For example:

**У моєму резюме є помилка.** — *There is a mistake in my resume.* (Locative case)

**Я хочу надіслати резюме.** — *I want to send a resume.* (Accusative case)

**Він прийшов без резюме.** — *He came without a resume.* (Genitive case)
:::

If your resume is successful, you will be invited for a **співбесіда** (job interview). This is a formal meeting with the employer where you discuss your skills and background. Once you pass the interview and get hired, especially if you are young or changing fields, you might start with a **стажування** (internship/training period). Even for experienced professionals, the first few months usually constitute a **випробувальний термін** (probation period) to ensure a good fit between the employee and the company.

Процес працевлаштування може бути довгим і емоційно складним. Кандидати часто хвилюються перед кожною співбесідою, готують відповіді на популярні запитання та уважно вивчають інформацію про компанію. Успішне проходження всіх етапів вимагає впевненості у своїх силах.

Once you are established in your role, you begin to build your professional trajectory. The foundation of your career is your **досвід** (experience). When employers review your background, they look at the duration and quality of your experience in a specific field. In your daily work, you are responsible for a specific set of tasks, which are your duties.

While the singular form is **обов'язок** (duty/responsibility), in professional contexts we almost exclusively focus on the plural form, which is **обов'язки** (duties). You will often see and use these words in common workplace phrases.

For their work, every employee receives financial compensation. In informal or daily communication, people most often use the word **зарплата** (salary). However, in official documents, contracts, and news, you will always see the full formal term, which is **заробітна плата** (formal wage).

За свою працю кожен працівник отримує фінансову винагороду. У неформальному спілкуванні колеги часто обговорюють, якою буде їхня наступна зарплата. Проте підписуючи контракт, ви завжди звертаєте увагу на розмір заробітної плати.

> *For their work, every employee receives financial compensation. In informal communication, colleagues often discuss what their next salary will be. However, when signing a contract, you always pay attention to the size of the formal wage.*

As you gain experience and fulfill your duties effectively, you may receive a **підвищення** (promotion/raise). This signifies upward movement in your career, either through a better position or a higher salary. Conversely, leaving a job, whether voluntarily or not, is called **звільнення** (dismissal/resignation). Your relationship with your **керівник** (manager/supervisor) plays a huge role in both of these outcomes.

A crucial aspect of modern Ukrainian professional vocabulary is the active use of feminine forms for professions, known as фемінітиви. Based on the official 2019 Ukrainian Pravopys (Orthography), using feminine suffixes to denote a woman's profession is the standard, natural, and respectful way to speak and write. This contrasts sharply with the older, Soviet-style preference for masculine forms, which often applied masculine nouns to female professionals to sound "more serious". Today, using masculine forms for women sounds outdated and unnatural in Ukrainian.

Українська мова має багату традицію творення жіночих форм. Найпоширеніший суфікс — це «-к(а)». Наприклад, якщо чоловік — вчитель, то жінка — вчителька. Від слова «лікар» утворюється слово лікарка, від «директор» — директорка, а від «журналіст» — журналістка.

Other productive suffixes include "-ин(я)" and "-иц(я)". For instance, a female choreographer is a **хореографиня** (female choreographer). The word for a male seller is **продавець** (seller), and its feminine counterpart is **продавчиня** (female seller). When a woman writes her resume or introduces herself at an interview, she should confidently use these feminine forms.

Використання фемінітивів є важливою частиною сучасної мовної культури. Вони роблять жінок видимими в усіх професійних сферах і відображають природний розвиток української мови. Тому не бійтеся використовувати ці слова у своєму щоденному спілкуванні.

> *The use of feminine forms is an important part of modern language culture. They make women visible in all professional spheres and reflect the natural development of the Ukrainian language. Therefore, do not be afraid to use these words in your daily communication.*

Finally, we must address one of the most persistent errors made by English speakers when discussing professions. In English, you naturally say "He works as a doctor" or "She works as a manager". If you translate this directly into Ukrainian, you might be tempted to say "Він працює як лікар". This is a grammatical error caused by English influence.

:::tip
**Did you know?**
In Ukrainian, roles, functions, and professions used with the verbs працювати (to work) and бути (to be) require the Instrumental case (Орудний відмінок). You do not need the word «як» (as).
:::

The correct Ukrainian norm is to use the verb and immediately follow it with the profession in the Instrumental case. So, instead of the incorrect phrase using "as", you must use the Instrumental ending.

**Він працює лікарем.** — *He works as a doctor.*

**Вона працює менеджеркою.** — *She works as a manager.*

Це правило працює для будь-якої професії чи посади. Якщо ваш друг змінив роботу і отримав нову посаду, ви скажете: «Він став менеджером». Якщо ваша знайома розповідає про свій минулий досвід, вона скаже: «Я багато років працювала перекладачкою». Запам'ятайте цю конструкцію, адже вона звучить дуже природно і відразу показує ваше добре розуміння української граматики.

By mastering the Instrumental case for professions, using correct feminine forms, and choosing the precise word for your specialty and position, you will be fully prepared to navigate any professional conversation in Ukrainian.

## Вид дієслова у розповіді про роботу (~1100 words total)

Understanding the concept of verbal aspect is essential when discussing your work and career. In Ukrainian, every action can be viewed either as an ongoing process or a completed result. When we talk about our professional experience, this distinction is critical. If you want to emphasize how long you performed a certain role or the continuous nature of your **обов'язки** (duties), you use the imperfective aspect. Conversely, if you want to highlight a specific career milestone, a sudden change, or a concrete achievement, you must use the perfective aspect. Understanding this binary system allows you to accurately convey your professional journey, turning a simple **робота** (job) into a meaningful step within your broader **професія** (profession). For instance, the verb *to work* only exists as an imperfective verb in this context because working is inherently a process. However, finding employment, quitting, or getting promoted are usually singular events that demand perfective verbs.

Коли ми розповідаємо про свою кар'єру, ми постійно робимо вибір між процесом і результатом. Недоконаний вид показує тривалість: «я працювала в компанії», «я вела великий проєкт», «я виконувала свої обов'язки». Доконаний вид фіксує конкретний факт або досягнення: «я влаштувалася на нову роботу», «я змінила професію», «я отримала підвищення». Цей вибір визначає, як слухач сприймає ваш професійний досвід.

> *When we tell about our career, we constantly make a choice between process and result. The imperfective aspect shows duration: "I worked in the company," "I managed a large project," "I fulfilled my duties." The perfective aspect records a specific fact or achievement: "I got a new job," "I changed my profession," "I received a promotion." This choice determines how the listener perceives your professional experience.*

When structuring a career narrative, you will often use a technique called "background versus event." This is a classic storytelling pattern in Ukrainian where the imperfective aspect sets the stage, and the perfective aspect introduces a dynamic shift. Imagine your ongoing **працевлаштування** (employment) as the canvas. You use the imperfective aspect to describe the period when you held a specific **посада** (position) or accumulated professional **досвід** (experience). This ongoing state provides frequent background context. Then, a significant change occurs—perhaps you decide to leave, or you receive an offer from another company. This pivotal moment is an event, and it requires the perfective aspect. By alternating between the two aspects, you create a natural flow that logically explains why you transitioned from one role to another. It clearly separates your day-to-day routine from your career-defining decisions.

Ця структура дуже часто використовується, коли ви описуєте своє попереднє працевлаштування. Недоконаний вид створює фон для вашої історії. Наприклад: «Я працювала в банку вже п'ять років». Це тривала дія, яка описує ваш звичний стан. Потім ви додаєте нову, раптову подію за допомогою доконаного виду: «...доки не отримала нову пропозицію». Поєднання цих двох видів створює повну картину вашого кар'єрного шляху.

> *This structure is very often used when you describe your previous employment. The imperfective aspect creates the background for your story. For example: "I had been working at the bank for five years already." This is a continuous action that describes your habitual state. Then you add a new, sudden event using the perfective aspect: "...until I received a new offer." The combination of these two aspects creates a complete picture of your career path.*

:::tip
**Did you know?**
When preparing your **резюме** (resume), the bullet points describing your daily tasks typically use the imperfective aspect to show ongoing competence, while the summary of your accomplishments often relies on perfective verbs to highlight concrete results.
:::

Another common pattern in professional storytelling is the chronological chain of events. When you want to summarize the steps you took to reach your current status, you rely entirely on a sequence of perfective verbs. Each verb represents a completed action that serves as a prerequisite for the next one. There is no focus on how long each step took; the emphasis is strictly on the successful completion of the sequence. For example, a standard career progression might look like this: first, you graduated from university and acquired a **фах** (specialty). Then, you applied for an open **вакансія** (vacancy). After that, you passed the **співбесіда** (job interview), and finally, you were hired. Because each of these actions is a distinct, finished milestone that moves the narrative forward, the perfective aspect is mandatory. Using an imperfective verb in this sequence would break the chain, suggesting that you got stuck in the middle of the process.

Послідовність завершених подій завжди передається дієсловами доконаного виду. Розглянемо типовий сценарій: спочатку хлопець закінчив університет і здобув свій фах. Потім він подав резюме в міжнародну компанію. Після цього він успішно пройшов співбесіду. І нарешті, він влаштувався на роботу. Кожен крок у цій розповіді є чітким результативним етапом, який відкриває двері до наступного кроку.

> *A sequence of completed events is always conveyed by verbs of the perfective aspect. Let's consider a typical scenario: first, the guy graduated from the university and acquired his specialty. Then he submitted his resume to an international company. After that, he successfully passed the interview. And finally, he got a job. Every step in this narrative is a clear resultant stage that opens the door to the next step.*

To narrate your career effectively, you must memorize the aspect pairs for the most common work-related verbs. Unlike the verb *to work*, which focuses purely on the process, verbs dealing with joining a company, leaving, and receiving compensation have distinct forms for process and result. Let us look at the core vocabulary. The process of applying or getting hired is expressed with the imperfective verb «влаштовуватися», but the moment you actually secure the job, you use the perfective «влаштуватися». Similarly, if you are discussing the difficult process of leaving a company, you might use the imperfective «звільнятися», but the finalized act of quitting is «звільнитися». When talking about compensation, your regular, ongoing **зарплата** (salary) or a recurring bonus is something you receive using the imperfective «отримувати». However, if you are talking about getting a singular, unexpected **підвищення** (promotion), you must switch to the perfective «отримати». Finally, the act of submitting documents, such as an application, uses the pair «подавати» and «подати».

:::info
**Grammar box**
Remember that many perfective verbs in Ukrainian are formed by changing the suffix of the imperfective base, rather than just adding a prefix. Notice how «влаштовуватися» drops the «-ова-» to become «влаштуватися», and «отримувати» drops the «-ува-» to become «отримати».
:::

One of the most nuanced uses of aspect is distinguishing between a habitual action and a single, concrete achievement. In a professional environment, many tasks are repetitive. If you perform a task every day, every week, or every month, it becomes a routine. Habitual actions always require the imperfective aspect, regardless of whether the individual action was completed on those specific days. For instance, if you want to say that you regularly met your targets, you must use the imperfective form because the repetition itself is a process. However, if you are highlighting a unique, one-time success that stands out from your routine, you switch to the perfective aspect. Perhaps your **керівник** (manager) or a **колега** (colleague) praised a specific project, and you want to highlight that exact moment. This distinction completely changes the meaning of a sentence.

Порівняйте дві різні ситуації на роботі. Якщо ви скажете: «Я щомісяця отримувала премію за хороші результати», ви описуєте регулярну звичку в минулому. Слово «щомісяця» вимагає використання недоконаного виду. Але якщо ви скажете: «Я отримала великий бонус за цей складний проєкт», ви наголошуєте на унікальному, одноразовому досягненні. Ваш керівник високо оцінив вашу конкретну роботу, і ця подія більше не повторювалася.

> *Compare two different situations at work. If you say: "I received a premium every month for good results," you are describing a regular habit in the past. The word "every month" requires the use of the imperfective aspect. But if you say: "I received a large bonus for this complex project," you emphasize a unique, one-time achievement. Your manager highly evaluated your specific work, and this event did not repeat.*

English speakers often fall into aspect traps when translating directly from their native language. In English, you might say "I was quitting for three years," meaning the decision and the process of leaving took a long time. If you translate this directly into Ukrainian using the imperfective aspect and a duration of time, it sounds absurd. The sentence «я три роки звільнялася» implies that you stood in the HR office for three years continuously writing a resignation letter. Because an event like **звільнення** (dismissal) is inherently a momentary result, combining its imperfective form with a long duration creates a semantic conflict. Instead, you must separate the background process from the final result. The correct way to express this idea is to use the imperfective aspect for the period of employment, followed by the perfective aspect for the final decision. You should say: «я працювала три роки і нарешті звільнилася». This perfectly aligns with the Ukrainian expectation that certain verbs must convey a clean, finished result.

:::note
**Quick tip**
Whenever a verb implies a sudden change of state (like getting hired, quitting, or graduating), be very careful about attaching time durations like "for a year" or "for three months" to its imperfective form. It is almost always better to describe the background activity instead.
:::

## На співбесіді: ситуативне мовлення

Navigating a Ukrainian **співбесіда** (job interview) and the overall process of **працевлаштування** (employment) requires mastering formal etiquette and structuring your self-presentation clearly and methodically. The interaction typically begins with standard formal greetings that establish a respectful, professional distance between you and the employer. Instead of a casual hello or a simple morning greeting, you should always use the universally accepted «Доброго дня» or «Добрий день». When initiating your formal introduction, the polite phrase «Дозвольте представитися» (Allow me to introduce myself) sets a highly professional, polished tone from the very first second. Following this formal opening, you should smoothly transition into discussing the exact purpose of your visit, usually by explicitly mentioning the specific **вакансія** (vacancy) you applied for. You might confidently say, «Я подавала резюме на посаду...» (I submitted a resume for the position of...). According to standard Ukrainian business communication models, this structured, predictable opening demonstrates immense respect for the interviewer's time and immediately establishes your serious professional demeanor. It clearly separates your workplace persona from your everyday conversational style, signaling to the hiring manager that you deeply understand corporate culture and the etiquette of the professional sphere in Ukraine. A strong, lasting first impression relies heavily on using these exact grammatical formulas, as they show both cultural competence and linguistic precision right from the very first sentence of the interview.

When describing your professional history, **професія** (profession), and career trajectory, it is absolutely crucial to elevate your language from simple, descriptive A2-level statements to much more sophisticated B1-level formal constructions. While saying «Я працювала в школі» (I worked in a school) or «Я робила тексти» (I made texts) is grammatically correct and perfectly fine for a casual chat with a friend, it sounds slightly too informal and simplistic for a serious corporate interview setting. Instead, a strong, competitive candidate will carefully use phrasing that emphasizes the logical relationship between their past work achievements, their **фах** (specialty), and the current opportunity they are interviewing for. You can effectively say «Мій попередній досвід пов'язаний із...» (My previous experience is connected with...) to frame your professional background more abstractly, highlighting the overarching theme of your career rather than just a list of locations. When discussing what you actually did on a daily basis, moving away from the basic, repetitive «Я робила...» (I did...) to the more authoritative and formal «На цій посаді я відповідала за...» (In this position I was responsible for...) shows a significantly higher degree of professional maturity, confidence, and advanced vocabulary command. Using this specific, elevated register demonstrates to the employer that you are not just capable of speaking conversational Ukrainian, but that you can effortlessly navigate the stylistic nuances of the official business style expected in a modern, dynamic workplace environment.

Описуючи свої щоденні робочі завдання та професійні навички, українці дуже часто використовують дієслова, які вимагають орудного відмінка. Це один із найважливіших граматичних маркерів вільного ділового мовлення. Якщо ви хочете сказати, чим саме ви займалися тривалий час, використовуйте дієслово «займатися» плюс відповідний іменник в орудному відмінку. Наприклад, ви можете сказати: «На минулій роботі я займалася маркетингом», або «Мій колега займався графічним дизайном». Коли ви говорите про управління людьми або складними процесами, завжди використовуйте дієслово «керувати». Успішний менеджер може керувати великим відділом, керувати міжнародною командою або керувати цілим фінансовим проє젝트м. Для точного опису постійної взаємодії з людьми чи об'єктами ідеально підходить стійка конструкція «працювати з». Сучасний спеціаліст може щодня працювати з вимогливими клієнтами, працювати з важливими юридичними документами або працювати з великими базами даних.

> *When describing their daily work tasks and professional skills, Ukrainians very often use verbs that require the instrumental case. This is one of the most important grammatical markers of fluent business speech. If you want to say exactly what you were engaged in for a long time, use the verb "займатися" plus the corresponding noun in the instrumental case. For example, you can say: "At my last job I was engaged in marketing", or "My colleague was engaged in graphic design". When you talk about managing people or complex processes, always use the verb "керувати". A successful manager can manage a large department, manage an international team, or manage an entire financial project. To accurately describe constant interaction with people or objects, the fixed construction "працювати з" is perfectly suitable. A modern specialist can work with demanding clients every day, work with important legal documents, or work with large databases.*

There is one major grammatical exception when describing your core **обов'язок** (duty) that frequently confuses and frustrates English-speaking learners. The highly frequent and essential expression «відповідати за» (to be responsible for) does NOT take the instrumental case, despite functioning semantically very similarly to the action-oriented verbs we just discussed. Because this specific phrase relies entirely on the preposition «за», this verb strictly requires the accusative case (Знахідний відмінок). This unique linguistic construction focuses entirely on the final target, the outcome, or the ultimate scope of your responsibility, rather than treating your daily work as a simple tool or an ongoing mechanical process. Therefore, to speak correctly, you must confidently say «відповідати за проєкт» (to be responsible for the project), «відповідати за продажі» (to be responsible for sales), or «відповідати за команду» (to be responsible for the team). Mixing this up and accidentally using the instrumental case here is a very common grammatical mistake among foreigners trying to sound professional, but mastering this specific prepositional exception will instantly make your professional Ukrainian sound significantly more natural, accurate, and authoritative to native speakers listening to your presentation.

:::note
**Quick tip**
When preparing for your interview, mentally separate your action verbs from your responsibility verbs. Always pair «займатися» and «керувати» with the instrumental case, but firmly reserve the accusative case for «відповідати за». Think of the preposition «за» as an arrow pointing directly forward at the specific object or group of people you are ultimately accountable for in the company.
:::

At the very end of the business conversation, your future manager or recruiter will definitely ask directly about your current expectations. ## Підсумок: робота і вид дієслова (~1100 words total)

This module has equipped you with a comprehensive professional vocabulary necessary for discussing your career trajectory, navigating the hiring process, and describing your daily workplace responsibilities. Grouping words by their semantic field makes them significantly easier to memorize and recall during an actual conversation. Let us review the foundational terminology that you will use in almost any professional environment.

In the professional world, your general employment is referred to as **робота** (work). The broader concept of your trained occupation is your **професія** (profession). Your specific area of expertise or field of study is known as your **фах** (specialty).

When discussing your career path, you will often talk about a specific **посада** (position) within a company. The formal process of getting hired is called **працевлаштування** (employment). You usually begin this journey by looking for an open **вакансія** (vacancy).

To apply for a job, you must submit a well-written **резюме** (resume). If the employer is interested, you will be invited for a formal **співбесіда** (job interview). During this meeting, you will discuss your professional **досвід** (experience).

Employers also want to know about every core **обов'язок** (duty) you handled previously. At your new workplace, you will interact daily with your **керівник** (manager). You will also collaborate closely with every other **колега** (colleague) in your department.

Over time, excellent performance often leads to a career **підвищення** (promotion). Naturally, this advancement is accompanied by a higher **зарплата** (salary). Ultimately, any professional journey might also involve a **звільнення** (dismissal) before moving to the next opportunity.

When using this vocabulary in practice, you must pay special attention to two important grammatical irregularities. The word for resume is a foreign borrowing and is entirely indeclinable in the Ukrainian language. This means its ending never changes, regardless of the grammatical case you use it in. You will never add typical noun endings to this word. Additionally, the word for colleague belongs to the special common gender category. It modifies its grammatical adjectives based on the actual biological gender of the person you are referring to. Because of this rule, you will say "мій розумний колега" for a man and "моя розумна колега" for a woman, even though the noun itself remains exactly the same.

У світі професійної діяльності ми часто говоримо про людей, які нас оточують, тому важливо добре знати назви професій. Коли ми детально описуємо місця нашої щоденної праці, ми зазвичай використовуємо слова підприємство, компанія, відділ або офіс. Сам тривалий процес пошуку роботи обов'язково охоплює такі ключові етапи, як подання документів та проходження співбесіди. Кожен успішний кандидат чудово знає, що якісне резюме є першим кроком до омріяної роботи. Якщо ви покажете відмінні результати, компанія з радістю запропонує вам офіційне працевлаштування та стабільну зарплату.

> *In the world of professional activity, we often talk about the people who surround us, so it is important to know the names of professions well. When we detail the places of our daily labor, we usually use the words enterprise, company, department, or office. The lengthy job search process itself necessarily covers such key stages as submitting documents and passing an interview. Every successful candidate knows perfectly well that a high-quality resume is the first step to a dream job. If you show excellent results, the company will gladly offer you official employment and a stable salary.*

Mastering the aspectual pairs of work-related verbs is crucial for accurately narrating your career history. You must consistently choose the imperfective aspect to describe background states, ongoing daily duties, and the general duration of your employment. Conversely, you must strictly use the perfective aspect to report completed actions, successful career transitions, and the final results of your professional decisions. The following list contains the five core verb pairs you will need to describe any career change.

### 1. The pair **працювати** and **попрацювати**

The verb **працювати** (to work) is inherently a process verb, focusing entirely on the continuous duration of employment rather than an achievement. Its perfective counterpart, **попрацювати** (to work for a while), is structurally limited and merely indicates working for a specified period of time before stopping the activity.

Я дуже довго працювала в цій компанії, тому зараз маю великий досвід.

> *I worked in this company for a very long time, so now I have great experience.*

Він попрацював тут лише кілька місяців і швидко знайшов нову цікавішу вакансію.

> *He worked here for only a few months and quickly found a new, more interesting vacancy.*

### 2. The pair **влаштовуватися** and **влаштуватися**

This essential pair describes the complex act of securing employment. You will use the imperfective aspect **влаштовуватися** (to get a job) when discussing the ongoing process of attending interviews and sending applications. You must use the perfective aspect **влаштуватися** to announce the completed moment of officially signing your contract.

Мій колега зараз влаштовується на нову роботу і майже щодня проходить співбесіди.

> *My colleague is currently getting a new job and goes through interviews almost every day.*

Вона успішно влаштувалася на престижну посаду фінансового аналітика минулого тижня.

> *She successfully got a job in the prestigious position of a financial analyst last week.*

### 3. The pair **звільнятися** and **звільнитися**

Whether you are leaving voluntarily or being let go by management, this verb pair handles the termination of your employment. The imperfective form **звільнятися** (to resign) suggests an impending departure that is currently underway, while the perfective form **звільнитися** marks the finalized, irrevocable end of the working relationship.

Багато спеціалістів звільняються з цієї фірми через дуже низьку початкову зарплату.

> *Many specialists are resigning from this firm because of a very low starting salary.*

Після серйозного конфлікту мій керівник вирішив негайно звільнитися за власним бажанням.

> *After a serious conflict, my manager decided to resign immediately of his own free will.*

### 4. The pair **отримувати** and **отримати**

You will frequently use this dynamic pair to talk about salaries, promotions, or professional feedback. The imperfective aspect **отримувати** (to receive) describes regular, periodic receipt, such as your monthly paycheck. The perfective aspect **отримати** highlights a single, completed acquisition, like a one-time financial bonus.

На цій відповідальній посаді я стабільно отримую високу зарплату кожного місяця.

> *In this responsible position, I stably receive a high salary every month.*

Вчора наш відділ нарешті отримав довгоочікуване підвищення після успішного проєкту.

> *Yesterday our department finally received a long-awaited promotion after a successful project.*

### 5. The pair **подавати** and **подати**

This pair is absolutely essential for navigating the modern application process. The imperfective aspect **подавати** (to apply) implies a repeated action of sending out multiple applications over time. The perfective aspect **подати** signifies the successful, completed submission of a specific document to a specific employer.

Вона постійно подавала своє резюме в різні відомі міжнародні технологічні компанії.

> *She constantly submitted her resume to various famous international technological companies.*

Сьогодні вранці я успішно подав усі необхідні документи на нову вакансію в нашому банку.

> *This morning I successfully submitted all the necessary documents for the new vacancy in our bank.*

When you sit down for a formal interview, you must be able to clearly articulate your daily responsibilities. The "Duties Template" we explored relies heavily on your firm mastery of the Instrumental and Accusative cases. Your specific choice of verb dictates exactly which grammatical case your noun must take. Verbs that describe active engagement, management, or collaboration require the Instrumental case, reflecting the idea of working "with" or "by means of" something. The verb indicating direct accountability demands the Accusative case to show the direct object of your responsibility.

Here is a visual synthesis of the structural rules for describing your professional duties:

*   The verb **займатися** (to be engaged in) requires the Instrumental case. It is used to describe the broad field or daily activity you are engaged in. An example is **займатися маркетингом** (to be engaged in marketing).
*   The verb **керувати** (to manage) requires the Instrumental case. It is used to denote leadership, administrative management, or direction. An example is **керувати відділом** (to manage a department).
*   The phrase **працювати з** (to work with) requires the Instrumental case. It is used to indicate the specific people, tools, or materials you interact with. An example is **працювати з клієнтами** (to work with clients).
*   The phrase **відповідати за** (to be responsible for) demands the Accusative case. It is used to specify the exact domain or final outcome for which you are accountable. An example is **відповідати за звіт** (to be responsible for the report).

На моїй попередній роботі я щодня активно займалася стратегічним плануванням та керувала командою з п'яти осіб. Я постійно працювала з найновішими комп'ютерними програмами, тому зараз добре знаю сучасні технології. Водночас я повністю відповідала за загальний річний бюджет нашого великого підприємства.

> *At my previous job, I actively engaged in strategic planning every day and managed a team of five individuals. I constantly worked with the newest computer programs, so now I know modern technologies well. At the same time, I was fully responsible for the overall annual budget of our large enterprise.*

:::tip
**Did you know?**
Modern Ukrainian professional terminology reflects a broader historical process of cultural decolonization. In the Soviet era, the term **робітник** (laborer) was heavily promoted by the state, carrying a strong ideological association with manual labor. Today, the modern Ukrainian business environment overwhelmingly prefers the more neutral and inclusive term **працівник** (employee), which seamlessly applies to everyone from factory staff to corporate office managers. Furthermore, you must pay extremely close attention to how you grammatically state your profession. Due to Russian linguistic influence, some speakers mistakenly use a direct translation of "to work as", which sounds extremely unnatural. The authentic Ukrainian standard strictly requires the Instrumental case without any prepositions whatsoever.

**працювати лікарем** — *to work as a doctor*
**працювати розумним вчителем** — *to work as a smart teacher*

Embracing these authentic grammatical structures actively demonstrates your respect for the natural evolution of the Ukrainian language.
:::
