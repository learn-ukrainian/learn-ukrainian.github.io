## Вступ — Introduction

Welcome to your third step in cracking the Cyrillic alphabet. You have already mastered the vowels and a solid foundation of essential consonants. Now, we are expanding your decodability even further.

In this lesson, we will introduce nine new consonants. This brings your total to twenty-three letters — enough to start reading international words, menus, and essential everyday vocabulary.

A key concept we will explore today is the difference between voiced and voiceless sounds. Ukrainian consonants often come in matched pairs. One sound vibrates in your throat, which is called a voiced consonant. The other sound uses only air and no vibration, which is called a voiceless consonant. Understanding this mechanical difference is the secret to developing a natural and authentic accent.

:::tip[💡 Tip]
**Check your vibration**
Place your hand lightly on your throat. When you make a voiced sound, you will feel a distinct buzz. When you make a voiceless sound, you will only feel air moving. This physical feedback is a powerful tool for improving your pronunciation.
:::

Let's look at the first group of new consonants and practice reading them in common words.

## Приголосні — Consonants Б, Д, П

> — Це **банан**? (Is this a banana?)
> — Так, це **банан**. (Yes, this is a banana.)
> — А це **парк**? (And is this a park?)
> — Ні, це **дім**. (No, this is a home.)
> — Де **парк**? (Where is the park?)
> — **Парк** там. (The park is there.)
> — Ось **парк**. (Here is the park.)

These three consonants are extremely common. Let's look at how to pronounce them.

The letter **Б** is a voiced consonant. Visually, it looks a bit like the number six, but it sounds exactly like the English "B". You can hear it at the beginning of the word **банан** (banana). Watch the pronunciation video below to hear it clearly.

The letter **П** is the voiceless partner to **Б**. Be careful not to confuse it with the letter **Р** (which makes an "R" sound in Ukrainian). The Ukrainian **П** is crisp and unaspirated, meaning there is no puff of air when you say it, like in the word **парк** (park).

### Літера П

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JksSjjxyW5Y" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — П" />

The letter **Д** is a voiced consonant. Its shape resembles a little house. It sounds similar to the English "D", but the tip of your tongue should touch your upper teeth rather than the roof of your mouth. You will see it in the word **дім** (home).

## Приголосні — Consonants З, Г, Х

> — Де **хліб**? (Where is the bread?)
> — **Хліб** тут. (The bread is here.)
> — А **газета**? (And the newspaper?)
> — **Газета** там. (The newspaper is there.)
> — Де **будинок**? (Where is the building?)
> — Цей **будинок** тут. (This building is here.)

Now let's look at three more consonants: a buzzing sound and two sounds formed deeper in your throat.

The letter **З** is a voiced consonant that looks like the number three. It makes a clear buzzing sound, just like the "z" in the English word "zebra." We find it in the word **зуб** (tooth).

### Літера З

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BhASNxitC1A" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — З" />

The letter **Г** is voiced, but it might be the trickiest character in this group for English speakers. It is a soft, throaty sound. It is definitively not a hard sound like in the English word "go." Instead, it sounds like a voiced glottal fricative, almost like a heavy, vibrating sigh. You can practice it with the word **газета**.

### Літера Г

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVnclpSI0DU" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Г" />

The letter **Х** is the voiceless counterpart to the sound above. Visually, it looks exactly like the Latin "X," but it absolutely does not make a "ks" sound. Instead, it sounds like the "ch" in the Scottish word "loch" or the German "Bach." A culturally important word starting with this letter is **хліб**.

:::note[🏺 Culture]
**The Importance of Bread**
The word **хліб** is deeply revered in Ukrainian culture. Bread is a timeless symbol of hospitality and prosperity — which is why welcoming guests with bread and salt is a classic Ukrainian tradition.
:::

## Шиплячі приголосні — Hushers Ж, Ш, Ч

> — Що це? (What is this?)
> — Це **шапка**. (This is a hat.)
> — А це? (And this?)
> — Це **школа**. (This is a school.)
> — Де **школа**? (Where is the school?)
> — Та **школа** там. (That school is there.)

We will now learn three distinctive letters collectively called "hushers." They are great examples of the voiced and voiceless pairs we've been discussing, where your mouth is in the exact same physical position, but one variant uses active vocal cord vibration while the other uses only a stream of air.

The letter **Ж** is voiced. Visually, it looks quite a bit like a six-legged beetle, and appropriately, it makes a rich buzzing sound similar to a flying insect. The sound is very close to the middle consonant in the English word "measure." Ensure your lips are slightly rounded when you produce it.

### Літера Ж

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dIrGVcqPwqM" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ж" />

The letter **Ш** is the unvoiced companion. It looks somewhat like a pitchfork pointing squarely upwards. It produces a strong hissing noise, much like the English "sh" in "ship," but your tongue must be pulled back slightly for a more authentic tone. It appears very frequently, such as in the word **школа**.

### Літера Ш

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1D-6MIw3OXY" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ш" />

The letter **Ч** is also voiceless. It visually resembles an upside-down chair or a handwritten number four. It makes a sharp "ch" sound, similar to the beginning of the English word "church." It appears frequently in basic vocabulary, such as in the word **час** and the universally recognized word **чай**.

### Літера Ч

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsJkbdsY2RA" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Ч" />

Let's summarize the voiced and voiceless consonant pairs we have covered:

- **Б** (voiced) and **П** (voiceless) form a matched pair.
- **Д** (voiced) pairs with **Т** (voiceless).
- **З** (voiced) pairs with **С** (voiceless).
- **Ж** (voiced) pairs with **Ш** (voiceless).
