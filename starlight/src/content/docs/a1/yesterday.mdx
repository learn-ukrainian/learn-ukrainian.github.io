## Dialogues

Every conversation about the past starts with a simple question. The phrase **«Як пройшо́в твій день?»** (How was your day?) is the most natural way to ask someone to share their story.

Read the first dialogue. Pay attention to how Maksym breaks his day into logical parts using time markers.

<div class="dialogue">


**Олег:** Приві́т! Як пройшов твій день? *(Hi! How was your day?)*


**Максим:** До́бре! Зра́нку я проки́нувся о сьо́мій. *(Good! In the morning I woke up at seven.)*


**Олег:** Що ти роби́в зранку? *(What did you do in the morning?)*


**Максим:** Я посні́дав і пішо́в на робо́ту. *(I had breakfast and went to work.)*


**Олег:** А вдень? *(And in the afternoon?)*


**Максим:** Вдень я працюва́в і обі́дав з коле́гою. *(In the afternoon I worked and had lunch with a colleague.)*


**Олег:** А вве́чері? *(And in the evening?)*


**Максим:** Ввечері я диви́вся фільм і ра́но ліг спа́ти. *(In the evening I watched a movie and went to bed early.)*


</div>

Notice that the verb **пройшов** (passed / went by) is in the masculine past tense form because it describes the word **день** (day), which is a masculine noun.

Maksym uses the word **зранку** (in the morning) to start his narrative. Since Maksym is a male speaker, his actions end in **-в** or **-вся**: **прокинувся** (woke up), **поснідав** (had breakfast), **пішов** (went), **працював** (worked), **обідав** (had lunch), **ліг** (lay down). He mentions that he had lunch with a **колега** (colleague).

Now read a conversation between two women. Listen to how Katya links her actions together.

<div class="dialogue">


**Ірина:** Що ти роби́ла у субо́ту? *(What did you do on Saturday?)*


**Катя:** О, я ма́ла чудо́вий день! *(Oh, I had a wonderful day!)*


**Ірина:** Розкажи́! *(Tell me!)*


**Катя:** Зранку я ходи́ла на ри́нок і купи́ла фру́кти. *(In the morning I went to the market and bought fruit.)*


**Ірина:** А по́тім? *(And then?)*


**Катя:** Потім я готува́ла обід. А вдень гуля́ла в парку. *(Then I cooked lunch. And in the afternoon I walked in the park.)*


**Ірина:** А ввечері? *(And in the evening?)*


**Катя:** Ввечері ми з по́другою ходи́ли в рестора́н. *(In the evening my friend and I went to a restaurant.)*


**Ірина:** Як фа́йно! *(How nice!)*


</div>

Iryna asks **«Що ти робила?»** (What did you do?) using the feminine past tense form **робила** because she is talking to a woman. Katya responds with feminine verbs ending in **-ла**: **ходила** (went), **купила** (bought), **готувала** (cooked), **гуляла** (walked).

She also uses the word **потім** (then) to show sequence. When asked **«А потім?»** (And then?), she naturally moves the story forward to the next event.

## Ро́зповідь про день (Narrating a Day)

To tell a compelling story, you need more than just a list of verbs. You need a structure that helps your listener follow along. In Ukrainian, we use time markers and sequencing words to connect our thoughts into a smooth, natural narrative.

### Time Markers

A typical day is divided into four main parts. These words anchor your story and answer the question **«Ко́ли?»** (When?).

* **Зранку** means "in the morning".
* **Вдень** means "in the afternoon" or "during the day".
* **Ввечері** means "in the evening".
* **Вночі** means "at night".

These time markers structure a sequence of events:
* **Зранку я пив ка́ву.** (In the morning I drank coffee.)
* **Вдень я бага́то працював.** (In the afternoon I worked a lot.)
* **Ввечері я чита́в кни́гу.** (In the evening I read a book.)
* **Вночі я добре спав.** (At night I slept well.)

### Sequencing Words

When you want to emphasize the order of your actions, use sequencing words. These turn isolated sentences into a cohesive story.

* **Спочатку** means "first" or "at first".
* **Потім** means "then" or "after that".
* **Після цього́** means "after this".
* **Нарешті** means "finally".

These markers link actions naturally:
* **Спочатку я поснідав.** (First I had breakfast.)
* **Потім я пішов на роботу.** (Then I went to work.)
* **Після цього я обідав.** (After this I had lunch.)
* **Нарешті я пішов додо́му.** (Finally I went home.)

### Daily Routine Verbs

When narrating a day, a few core verbs do most of the heavy lifting. In the past tense, you must remember that the ending changes based on the gender of the speaker. 

Male speakers use endings with **-в** or **-вся**. Female speakers use endings with **-ла** or **-лася**.

The verb **прокинутися** (to wake up) is a reflexive verb, which is why it has the special ending **-ся** or **-лася**.
* **я прокинувся** (I woke up — male speaker)
* **я проки́нулася** (I woke up — female speaker)

The verb **поснідати** (to have breakfast) comes from the noun for breakfast.
* **я поснідав** (I had breakfast — male speaker)
* **я поснідала** (I had breakfast — female speaker)

The verb **обідати** (to have lunch) comes from the noun for lunch.
* **я обідав** (I had lunch — male speaker)
* **я обідала** (I had lunch — female speaker)

The verb **піти** (to go / to set off) is a common action word.
* **я пішов** (I went — male speaker)
* **я пішла** (I went — female speaker)

The verb **повернутися** (to return) is also reflexive.
* **я поверну́вся** (I returned — male speaker)
* **я поверну́лася** (I returned — female speaker)

The phrase **лягти спати** (to go to bed) literally translates as "to lie down to sleep". The word **лягти** (to lie down) has an irregular past tense root.
* **я ліг спати** (I went to bed — male speaker)
* **я лягла́ спати** (I went to bed — female speaker)

Practice ordering these daily events naturally.

- "Ввечері я повернувся і дивився серіа́л."

## Мій учора́шній день (My Yesterday)

Time markers, sequencing words, and verbs come together to form a complete story. Read this narrative told by Anna. Pay close attention to how she structures the timeline, sets the context, and maintains feminine verb endings throughout her story.

### Model Narrative: Anna's Yesterday

> **Учора був звича́йний день. Зранку я прокинулася о пів на сьому. Я поснідала — ї́ла ка́шу і пила́ каву. Потім я пішла на роботу. Вдень я обідала в кафе́ бі́ля о́фісу. Я замо́вила сала́т і сік. Після робо́ти я ходила в магази́н і купила проду́кти. Ввечері я готувала вече́рю і диви́лася серіал. О одина́дцятій я лягла спати.**

*English translation for context:*
*(Yesterday was an ordinary day. In the morning I woke up at half past six. I had breakfast — I ate porridge and drank coffee. Then I went to work. In the afternoon I had lunch in a cafe near the office. I ordered a salad and juice. After work I went to the store and bought groceries. In the evening I cooked dinner and watched a TV series. At eleven o'clock I went to bed.)*

Notice the key elements that make this text flow naturally:

* She sets the scene with **Учора був звичайний день** (Yesterday was an ordinary day). The word **учора** (yesterday) provides the timeframe, while **звичайний** (ordinary) adds descriptive detail.
* The narrative follows a clear chronological path: **Зранку** → **Потім** → **Вдень** → **Після роботи** → **Ввечері**.
* Because Anna is female, every single verb she uses about herself ends in **-ла** or **-лася** (**прокинулася**, **поснідала**, **пішла**, **обідала**, **замовила**, **ходила**, **купила**, **готувала**, **дивилася**, **лягла**).

She uses everyday nouns to add richness to the story. She buys **продукти** (groceries), and she watches a **серіал** (TV series).

### Your Turn: Build Your Own Narrative

You now have all the tools you need to talk about your own day. The best way to practice is to use a consistent template and fill in your own details.

Here is a basic structure you can adapt:
* **Учора...** (Yesterday...)
* **Зранку я...** (In the morning I...)
* **Потім...** (Then...)
* **Вдень...** (In the afternoon...)
* **Ввечері...** (In the evening...)

To make your story richer, combine your past-tense verbs with vocabulary you already know from previous modules.
* **Places:** **кафе** (cafe), **парк** (park), **магазин** (store).
* **Food:** **каша** (porridge), **кава** (coffee), **салат** (salad).
* **People:** **друг** (male friend), **подруга** (female friend), **колега** (colleague).

A male speaker can build a story like this:
* **Учора був га́рний день.** (Yesterday was a good day.)
* **Зранку я прокинувся.** (In the morning I woke up.)
* **Потім я поснідав.** (Then I had breakfast.)
* **Вдень я був у парку.** (In the afternoon I was in the park.)
* **Після цього я обідав з колегою.** (After this I had lunch with a colleague.)

When narrating, you do not need complex sentences. Short, clear statements connected by time markers are the most natural way to speak in everyday conversations. Keep your structure simple and focus on the correct chronological order.

## Summary

A compelling story relies on a strong foundation of vocabulary, grammar, and structural markers. Here is the complete toolkit for narrating a day.

**1. Time Structure**
To give your story a clear timeline, break the day into natural segments. These markers let your listener know exactly when an action happened.
* **зранку** (in the morning)
* **вдень** (in the afternoon)
* **ввечері** (in the evening)
* **вночі** (at night)

**2. Sequencing Words**
To show the order of events and move your story forward smoothly, use connecting words. They build a bridge from one sentence to the next.
* **спочатку** (first / at first)
* **потім** (then)
* **після цього** (after that)
* **нарешті** (finally)

**3. Core Daily Routine Verbs (Past Tense)**
Your essential toolkit for describing daily actions includes these highly frequent verbs.
* **прокинувся / прокинулася** (woke up)
* **поснідав / поснідала** (had breakfast)
* **пішов / пішла** (went)
* **обідав / обідала** (had lunch)
* **повернувся / повернулася** (returned)
* **ліг / лягла спати** (went to bed)

**4. Gender Consistency**
The most important grammatical rule when talking about yourself in the past tense is maintaining gender consistency. Your listener relies on these verb endings.

* **Male speakers:** You must use the **-в** or **-вся** ending for every action you performed. If you woke up, ate, and went to work, you say: **Я прокинувся, я поснідав, я пішов.**
* **Female speakers:** You must use the **-ла** or **-лася** ending for every action you performed. If you woke up, ate, and went to work, you say: **Я прокинулася, я поснідала, я пішла.**

:::tip
Always match your past tense verbs to your own gender when narrating a personal story. This consistency is one of the most important markers of natural Ukrainian speech!
:::

**Self-Check:** Can you tell the story of your yesterday using at least five verbs? Start with **Учора зранку я...** (Yesterday morning I...) and follow your day through to the evening. Be mindful of your verb endings and use time markers to guide the way.
