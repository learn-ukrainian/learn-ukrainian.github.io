## Навичка 1: Читання та Рід (Skill 1: Reading and Gender)

### Модель (Model)

Читання — це дуже важлива навичка. Усі літери вже знайомі. Тепер тільки слова.
Reading is a very important skill. All letters are already familiar. Now only words.

Читання слів. Увага на голосні звуки. Це дуже важливо.
Reading words. Attention to vowel sounds. This is very important.

### Практика (Practice)

First, let's test your Cyrillic reading fluency. We no longer use English letters to show how Ukrainian words sound. You must rely purely on the Cyrillic alphabet to pronounce everything correctly.

A common challenge for beginners is the difference between the letters **И** and **І**. These two vowels look similar to English letters but sound completely different. The letter **І** makes a sharp, high sound, just like the «ee» in «bee». The letter **И** makes a lower, relaxed sound, similar to the «i» in «bit».

Let's look at some examples. Notice the difference in pronunciation when you read them aloud. Pay close attention to the stress marks.

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **ки́ївський** | Kyiv (adjective) |
| **Украї́на** | Ukraine |
| **оліве́ць** | pencil |
| **сир** | cheese |
| **сік** | juice |

:::warning[⚠️ Warning]
Do not mix up **И** and **І** — they change the meaning of words! For example, **кит** (with a deep И) means "whale", but **кіт** (with a sharp І) means "cat".
:::

### Самоперевірка (Self-Check)

Now, let's review noun gender. In Ukrainian, every noun has a gender: masculine, feminine, or neuter. You can usually guess the gender by looking at the last letter of the word.

Here are the standard rules you have learned:
- **Masculine:** Words ending in a consonant (like **студе́нт**, **брат**).
- **Feminine:** Words ending in **-а** or **-я** (like **ма́ма**, **ву́лиця**).
- **Neuter:** Words ending in **-о** or **-е** (like **мі́сто**, **мо́ре**).

However, there are always exceptions to the rules. A very important exception is the word **та́то** (dad). It ends in **-о**, which usually signals a neuter noun. But because a dad is a male person, the word **та́то** is always masculine.

| Word | Gender | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| **та́то** | Masculine | dad |
| **мі́сто** | Neuter | city |
| **ма́ма** | Feminine | mom |

Always check the noun's gender before adding an adjective. If the word is **мі́сто** (neuter), you cannot say **нови́й мі́сто** — it must be **нове́ мі́сто**.

## Навичка 2: Прикметники та Питання (Skill 2: Adjectives, Plurals and Questions)

### Модель (Model)

Час для практики. Це короткі речення. Це дуже цікаво.
Time for practice. These are short sentences. This is very interesting.

Ви вже знаєте багато нових слів. Це слова для щоденної практики. Вони корисні.
You already know many new words. These are words for daily practice. They are useful.

### Практика (Practice)

When you describe objects or people, adjectives must match the noun in gender and number. This is called agreement. If the noun is feminine, the adjective takes a feminine ending. If the noun is plural, the adjective takes a plural ending.

Let's review the plural forms. Usually, you add **-и** or **-і** to the end of the noun to make it plural. The adjective also changes its ending to **-і**.

Look at these basic statements using the structure «Це...» (This is / These are...). Notice how the adjective changes depending on the noun.

| Statement | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Це нови́й стіл.** | This is a new table. (Masculine Singular) |
| **Це нова́ ву́лиця.** | This is a new street. (Feminine Singular) |
| **Це нове́ мі́сто.** | This is a new city. (Neuter Singular) |
| **Це нові́ столи́.** | These are new tables. (Plural) |

### Самоперевірка (Self-Check)

Next, let's practice asking basic questions. For a person, use **Хто це?** (Who is this?). For an object, use **Що це?** (What is this?). For a location, use **Де?** (Where?).

| Question | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Хто це?** | Who is this? |
| **Що це?** | What is this? |
| **Де кафе́?** | Where is the cafe? |

:::tip[💡 Tip]
In Ukrainian, yes/no questions often have the exact same word order as statements. Only your rising intonation shows it's a question: **Це кава.** (statement, pitch drops) vs. **Це кава?** (question, pitch rises).
:::

Finally, let's talk about answering these questions. You can use **Так** (Yes) to confirm, or **Ні** (No) to deny. When you use **Ні**, you must also place the negative word **не** (not) directly before the word you are negating.

Notice how the negative word **не** stands right before the word it negates:

| Dialogue | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| — **Це вели́ке мі́сто?**<br />— **Так, це вели́ке мі́сто.** | — Is this a large city?<br />— Yes, this is a large city. |
| — **Це чо́рна ка́ва?**<br />— **Ні, це не чо́рна ка́ва.** | — Is this black coffee?<br />— No, this is not black coffee. |
| — **Хто це?**<br />— **Це мій брат.** | — Who is this?<br />— This is my brother. |

With **Так**, **Ні**, and basic adjectives, you are ready to answer simple questions and navigate a Ukrainian cafe.

## 🏺 Культурний контекст: Кафе (Cultural Context: Cafe)

Ми йдемо до львівської кав'ярні. Це чудове місце. Там є смачна кава.
We are going to a Lviv coffee shop. This is a wonderful place. There is tasty coffee there.

Ви можете замовити напій. Ви також можете попросити рахунок. Це дуже просто.
You can order a drink. You can also ask for the bill. This is very simple.

Ukraine has a deep and historic connection to coffee culture, especially in the western city of Lviv. The legend goes back to Yuriy Kulchytsky, a Ukrainian noble and hero. After the Battle of Vienna in 1683, Kulchytsky opened one of the very first coffeehouses in Vienna, bringing the rich taste of coffee to Europe. Today, Lviv is famous for its atmospheric cafes, delicious pastries, and fantastic brews.

Let's look at some essential cafe vocabulary using our nominal sentence structures. These phrases will help you navigate any coffee shop.

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Це ка́ва.** | This is coffee. |
| **Це чо́рна ка́ва.** | This is black coffee. |
| **Де раху́нок?** | Where is the bill? |
| **Мо́жна раху́нок?** | Can I have the bill? |

:::note[🏺 Culture]
When food or drinks are served in Ukraine, it is polite to wish everyone a good meal. Always say **Смачно́го!** (Bon appetit!). It is an essential cultural phrase that shows respect and friendliness to the people sharing the table with you.
:::

Whether you are in a bustling Lviv cafe or drinking coffee at home, try using **Смачно́го!** before you eat. It's a small habit that immediately makes your Ukrainian sound more natural.
