> **Why does this matter?**
>
> Learning to read Cyrillic is your most important first step. Because Ukrainian is highly phonetic, mastering these initial seven letters allows you to start reading real words immediately, building your confidence from day one.

## Вступ — Introduction

You are about to unlock a highly phonetic writing system, consisting of exactly thirty-three letters. Unlike English, where spelling often feels like a guessing game, Ukrainian spelling is delightfully consistent. Once you know the sounds, you can read almost anything. What you see is exactly what you get. This structural predictability builds your confidence quickly. Your immediate goal is to master the very first seven letters. We cover three vowels and four consonants. These seven characters form the foundation of your reading journey, allowing you to recognize real words right away.

Before looking at the individual characters, it helps to understand their origin. The Cyrillic script gets its name from Saint Cyril. His brilliant students formalized this alphabet during the era of the First Bulgarian Empire, specifically adapting it to capture the unique sounds of Slavic speech.

:::note[🏺 Culture]
Even before learning all the letters, Ukrainians appreciate when you try to speak! Here are some basic phrases. Since you do not know all these letters yet, we provide the English translation immediately. Listen to how they sound:
«До́брий день!» (Good day!)
«Приві́т!» (Hello!)
«Дя́кую!» (Thank you!)
«Будь ла́ска!» (You're welcome!)
«Так!» (Yes!)
«Ні!» (No!)
:::

To help you hear these sounds perfectly, we rely on a wonderful visual reference. You use the pronunciation video guide by Anna Ohoiko. Her clear demonstrations provide the exact mouth shapes and sounds you need.

## Голосні — Vowels

We begin with the core of any language: vowels (голосні). You produce these sounds entirely with your voice, without any airflow obstruction from your lips or tongue. Vowels are the heart of spoken Ukrainian. They provide the melodic rhythm that makes the language sound so flowing. Whenever you speak, ensure your voice is loud and clear on these vowels. Ukrainian features ten vowels in total, but you only need to focus on three right now: А, О, and У.

Before learning the individual letters, you must understand the Golden Rule of Ukrainian pronunciation. Ukrainian vowels are absolutely never reduced. They remain pure and clear, regardless of whether they are stressed or unstressed. Remember, a lazy vowel can completely change the feeling of a word, so always give them their full value. Keep your mouth engaged and active as you practice these specific sounds. This conscious effort early on prevents bad pronunciation habits from forming.

:::warning[⚠️ Warning]
Native English speakers often unconsciously turn unstressed vowels into a lazy «uh» sound. You must resist this habit! A Ukrainian vowel always keeps its distinct, full sound.
:::

### Літера А

The first letter looks completely familiar. It sounds like the «a» in «father». It stays open and relaxed.

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvB3VpcR3ZE" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — А" />

Notice how clear the sound remains in these examples:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **анана́с** | pineapple |
| **А́фрика** | Africa |
| **Аргенти́на** | Argentina |
| **аеропо́рт** | airport |

### Літера О

Next is another familiar shape. It makes a rounded sound, similar to the «o» in «more».

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QT3c1TiZZgk" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — О" />

A very common learner mistake occurs here. Because of the Golden Rule, you must never reduce an unstressed О to an «a» sound. It stays perfectly rounded everywhere. A good example is the word below:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **о́ко** | eye |
| **оса́** | wasp |
| **о́сінь** | autumn |
| **озе́ро** | lake |

### Літера У

Our third vowel looks quite different from its English counterpart. It resembles a «Y», but it produces the deep «oo» sound found in «moon».

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VB1O6PmtYRU" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — У" />

Push your lips forward to make this sound correctly. You can hear it perfectly in the most important word of all:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Украї́на** | Ukraine |
| **у́рок** | lesson |
| **учи́тель** | teacher |

## Приголосні — Consonants

Now we move to consonants (приголосні). Consonants give words their distinct shape and boundaries. You produce them with some form of airflow obstruction, using your tongue, teeth, or lips. When you articulate them sharply, your spoken Ukrainian becomes instantly easier for native speakers to understand. Unlike vowels, which flow freely, consonants require precise placement. The Ukrainian alphabet contains twenty-two consonants, and you learn four of them today: М, Л, Н, and С. Pay close attention to Anna's mouth movements in the videos to mimic exactly how she produces these sounds.

By mastering these four consonants (приголосні) alongside your three vowels, you possess the building blocks for real communication. You simply need to put them together.

:::tip[💡 Tip]
Some Cyrillic letters look identical to English ones and make the same sound. Others look the same but make a completely different sound. We call the latter **False Friends**. The careful attention you pay to them now saves you from major confusion later!
:::

### Літера М

This one is a true friend. It looks exactly like the English M and makes the exact same humming sound.

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ez95H4ibuJo" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — М" />

You can immediately read your first full Ukrainian word:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **ма́ма** | mom |
| **ма́сло** | butter |
| **ма́ло** | little |

### Літера Л

Here is a brand new shape. It looks a bit like a small tent, or the Greek letter lambda (Λ). It makes the «l» sound.

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v6-3Xg52Buk" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Л" />

Try saying this animal name:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **ла́ма** | llama |
| **ма́ло** | little / few |
| **са́ло** | salo / fat |

### Літера Н

Prepare yourself for your first major **False Friend**. This letter looks exactly like an English H, but it is actually the «n» sound. Your brain tries to trick you, but you must remember it is a nasal consonant.

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vNUfiKHPYaU" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Н" />

Read this word carefully:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **нам** | to us |
| **нас** | us |
| **насо́с** | pump |

### Літера С

Here is your second **False Friend**. It looks exactly like an English C. However, it never makes a hard «k» sound. It is always the soft, hissing «s» sound, just like in «snake».

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7UsFBgSL91E" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — С" />

Combine it with the letters you already know:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **сом** | catfish |
| **сон** | dream |
| **слон** | elephant |
| **сосна́** | pine tree |
| **смола́** | resin |
| **сам** | self / alone |

## Склади і слова — Syllables and Words

You now know seven letters. It is time to build syllables (склади). A syllable is simply a vowel standing alone, or a vowel joined together with consonants. By breaking words down into smaller chunks, reading becomes an easy, logical process. This simple, step-by-step method ensures you never feel overwhelmed. You are not just memorizing random shapes; you are building a reliable system. When you encounter a long word in the future, you instinctively break it down into these manageable, bite-sized syllables.

We categorize them into two main types. Open syllables end directly in a vowel. Try saying these aloud smoothly:
- МА, МО, МУ
- НА, НО, НУ
- ЛА, ЛО, ЛУ
- СА, СО, СУ

Closed syllables end in a consonant. They trap the sound at the end. Practice these combinations:
- АМ, ОМ, УМ
- АН, ОН, УН
- АС, ОС, УС
- АЛ, ОЛ, УЛ

:::note[🏺 Culture]
Ukrainian children learn to read using exactly this method in their first-grade primers. They blend tiny parts together until the whole word emerges naturally!
:::

Let us practice visual blending. You take two short parts and merge them into one fluid word:
- ма + ма = **ма́ма** (mom)
- со + сна = **сосна́** (pine tree)
- а + на + нас = **анана́с** (pineapple)

Single closed syllables read as one whole, solid breath:
- **сом** (catfish)
- **сон** (dream)
- **нам** (to us)
- **нас** (us)
- **сам** (self / alone)

Now for your ultimate reading drill. Every single word below uses only the seven letters you know. Notice how perfectly the system works. Since you know the characters, the pronunciation follows strict logic without any hidden surprises. You are already reading real, meaningful text. Take a deep breath and read them aloud slowly, syllable by syllable. Feel the rhythm of the open and closed syllables working together:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **ма́ма** | mom |
| **сом** | catfish |
| **сон** | dream |
| **оса́** | wasp |
| **ма́сло** | butter |
| **сосна́** | pine tree |
| **анана́с** | pineapple |
| **насо́с** | pump |
| **ла́ма** | llama |
| **смола́** | resin |
| **нам** | to us |
| **нас** | us |
| **сам** | self / alone |
| **ма́ло** | little / few |
| **слон** | elephant |

You can even read full phrases:
«У нас — **анана́с**.» (We have a pineapple.)
«У нас — **сом**.» (We have a catfish.)
«А у нас — **слон**!» (And we have an elephant!)
«Ма́ма, у нас **оса́**!» (Mom, we have a wasp!)
«У нас **ма́ло**.» (We have little.)
«**Анана́с** — нам, **ма́сло** — нам.» (Pineapple for us, butter for us.)
«**Сон**.» (A dream.)
«**Сосна́**.» (A pine tree.)
«**Смола́**.» (Resin.)
«А у вас?» (And what about you?)

:::note[Practice]
Read these out loud for extra practice. You know all these letters!
«Ма́ма, ла́ма, слон!» (Mom, llama, elephant!)
«Нам — ма́сло, нам — анана́с.» (Butter for us, pineapple for us.)
«Сосна́, смола́, насо́с.» (Pine tree, resin, pump.)
«Сам сом, оса́.» (The catfish itself, a wasp.)
«Ма́ло сна, ма́ло ма́сла.» (Little sleep, little butter.)
«Он слон!» (There is an elephant!)
:::

## 📋 Підсумок — Summary

Congratulations on a massive achievement! You successfully mastered seven out of the thirty-three Ukrainian letters. You learned three vowels (голосні) and four consonants (приголосні), and you used them to read your very first authentic words. You now understand the difference between open and closed syllables, and you know how to blend them smoothly. This is the exact same foundation native speakers build upon.

Take a moment for a quick self-check:
1. Can you read the words **ма́сло** and **сом** aloud?
2. Do you remember the two **False Friends** (Н and С), and what sounds they actually make?
3. Do Ukrainian vowels change their sound when unstressed? (No, they are never reduced!)

Your journey continues smoothly in the next step, where you unlock seven more characters.
