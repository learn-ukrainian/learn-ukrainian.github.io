> **Why does this matter?**
>
> Learning a new alphabet is the foundational step in your language journey. By mastering just these first seven letters, you unlock the ability to read real words immediately, proving that the Cyrillic alphabet is logical and highly phonetic.

## Вступ — Introduction

Welcome to the Cyrillic alphabet! Learning a new writing system might seem intimidating, but the Ukrainian alphabet is highly phonetic. There are 33 letters in total, and each letter corresponds to one specific sound. Our pronunciation guide for this journey is Anna Ohoiko from Ukrainian Lessons. You can watch her complete overview here:

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ksXIXj7CXwc" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Alphabet" />

:::note[🏺 Culture]
The Cyrillic script descends from the alphabet created in the 9th century by the students of Saints Cyril and Methodius in the First Bulgarian Empire, designed specifically to capture Slavic sounds.
:::

For our first step, our focus is on just seven letters, chosen to match Ukrainian primer textbooks (like Bolshakova 2018 and Zaharijchuk NUS 2025). We will look closely at three vowels (голосні) and four consonants (приголосні). With these seven building blocks, you will read real words by the end of this page.

Take your time with these initial shapes. Some will look familiar, while others might trick your eyes. Your brain is building new pathways, connecting visual symbols to new sounds. We will take it slow, step by step, ensuring you feel comfortable before moving forward. Let us begin the exciting process of unlocking the Cyrillic code!

## Голосні — Vowels

Ukrainian has 10 vowels (голосні). They are the musical core of the language and are strictly "voice only" with no obstruction. This module introduces our first three vowels: **А**, **О**, and **У**. Unlike English vowels, which can bend and change depending on the word, Ukrainian vowels are pure and stable. They are crisp, clear, and consistently pronounced the exact same way, whether they are stressed or unstressed. This is the Golden Rule of Ukrainian vowels.

The letter **А** is our first true friend. It looks identical to the English capital A, and it represents a very similar sound. It is a wide, open sound, much like the «a» in the English word «father». Whenever you see **А**, open your mouth and produce a clear, bright sound.

- **Літера А**:

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvB3VpcR3ZE" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — А" />

  - Examples: **ананас** (pineapple), **Африка** (Africa), **Аргентіна** (Argentina), **аеропорт** (airport). The sound is never reduced.

:::tip[💡 Tip]
A small accent mark (´) over a vowel indicates the stressed syllable. This is where your voice should be the loudest and strongest. In the word **ма́ма** (mom), the first **А** is stressed.
:::

Next is the letter **О**. This is another familiar shape that looks and functions much like its English counterpart. The sound it makes is round and deep, similar to the «o» in «more» or «horn». Your lips should form a perfect circle when you say it. An important rule in Ukrainian: the vowel **О** never reduces to an /a/ sound in an unstressed position. **О** stays **О** everywhere, maintaining its clear, round shape.

- **Літера О**:

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QT3c1TiZZgk" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — О" />

  - Examples: **око** (eye), **оса** (wasp), **сом** (catfish), **сон** (dream).

Our third vowel is **У**. This letter might look like the English letter Y, but do not let your eyes deceive you. This is our first visual trap. In Ukrainian, **У** makes an «oo» sound, exactly like the «oo» in «boot» or «moon». Whenever you encounter this letter, push your lips forward and make a long, continuous sound.

- **Літера У**:

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VB1O6PmtYRU" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — У" />

  - Examples: **Україна** (Ukraine), **сума** (amount), **мул** (mule), **соус** (sauce).

:::warning[⚠️ Warning]
Visual traps happen because your brain is trying to apply English rules to Cyrillic letters. When you see **У**, consciously remind yourself: «This is not a Y. This sounds like oo.» It takes practice to retrain your eyes!
:::

## Приголосні — Consonants

Now that we have our vowels to provide the melody, we need consonants (приголосні) to provide the structure. Ukrainian has 22 consonants in total. Consonants shape the airflow and give edges to our syllables. We will introduce four consonants today: **М**, **Л**, **Н**, and **С**.

The letter **М** is a helpful starting point for consonants. It looks like the English M and sounds almost exactly like it. It is a warm, humming sound made by pressing your lips together. You will see this letter frequently, especially in essential family words.

- **Літера М**:

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ez95H4ibuJo" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — М" />

  - Examples: **мама** (mom), **масло** (butter), **мало** (little).

Next is the letter **Л**. This shape might be entirely new to you. It resembles a small tent or the Greek letter lambda. It produces an «l» sound, similar to the English «l» in «lamp» or «look». Sometimes it is written with a flattened top, and other times it looks more triangular, but it always represents that steady «l» sound.

- **Літера Л**:

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v6-3Xg52Buk" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Л" />

  - Examples: **лама** (llama), **луна** (echo), **слон** (elephant).

:::note[📝 Note]
The letter **Л** can sometimes appear slightly different in handwriting or certain computer fonts, looking a bit like an upside-down V. Do not worry; as long as it has two legs, it is our friend **Л**.
:::

Now we arrive at our most notorious visual trap: the letter **Н**. It looks exactly like the English capital H. However, in Ukrainian, it never makes a breathing «h» sound. Instead, it makes an «n» sound, just like in the English word «nose» or «net». This will require some mental effort to overcome. Every time you see **Н**, you must consciously tell yourself to produce an «n» sound.

- **Літера Н**:

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vNUfiKHPYaU" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — Н" />

  - Examples: **нам** (to us), **нас** (us), **сон** (dream), **насос** (pump).

Finally, we introduce the letter **С**. Here is another visual trap, though perhaps a gentler one. It looks like the English letter C, but in Ukrainian, it always makes a hissing «s» sound, like a snake. It sounds like the «s» in «sun» or «snake». It is always a soft, continuous «s».

- **Літера С**:

<YouTubeVideo client:load url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7UsFBgSL91E" label="Anna Ohoiko — Ukrainian Lessons — С" />

  - Examples: **сом** (catfish), **сон** (dream), **сосна** (pine tree).

Let us review these four structural pillars. We have the familiar humming **М**, the tent-shaped **Л**, the tricky **Н** that sounds like «n», and the **С** that hisses like «s». Combined with our three vowels, we now have a complete toolkit to start building real, meaningful words.

## Склади і слова — Syllables and Words

We are now going to take the seven individual letters we just learned and combine them to read actual Ukrainian words. The beauty of the Cyrillic alphabet is that reading is simply a matter of linking sounds together. There are no silent letters waiting to confuse you. If you see a letter, you pronounce its sound.

Let us start by blending a consonant and a vowel together to form a syllable. Open syllables end in a vowel. Practice reading these open syllables: **МА МО МУ НА НО НУ ЛА ЛО ЛУ СА СО СУ**. Blending into words: **ма+ма = ма́ма** (two open syllables), **со+сна = сосна́** (two syllables), **а+на+нас = анана́с** (three syllables).

Closed syllables end in a consonant. Practice reading these basic closed syllables to train your tongue: **АМ ОМ УМ АН ОН УН АС ОС УС АЛ ОЛ УЛ**. Once you master these, you can read single-syllable words that follow the same pattern, like **сом** (catfish), **сон** (dream), **нам** (to us), **нас** (us), and **сам** (alone/oneself).

How about the tricky letter **Н**? Remember, it sounds like «n». Let us combine it with **А** and **С**. You get **нас** (us). What if we combine **С**, **А**, and **М**? We get **сам** (oneself).

Let us look at some words featuring the deep, round **О**. The word for catfish uses three letters you know: **С**, **О**, and **М**. Put them together, and you read **сом** (catfish). The word for dream is very similar. We use **С**, **О**, and the tricky **Н**. Together, they form **сон** (dream). The word for wasp is just three letters: **оса́** (wasp).

We can also build slightly longer words. How about the word for butter? It uses the tent-shaped **Л**. Read it letter by letter: **М**, **А**, **С**, **Л**, **О**. Blend them together: **ма́сло** (butter). The word for pine tree is **сосна́** (pine tree). A pump is **насо́с** (pump).

:::note[🏺 Culture]
You are already reading words that connect to nature and everyday life. The word **сосна́** (pine tree) is very common, as pine forests cover large areas of the country's landscape. Other words like **смола́** (resin) share this forest connection.
:::

Let us read a few more animals. A llama is beautifully phonetic: **ла́ма** (llama). An elephant is **слон** (elephant). And what about a delicious tropical fruit? Read this carefully: **анана́с** (pineapple). That is right, it is a pineapple!

Let's read some textbook phrases (from Bolshakova p.22):
- «У нас — ананас.»
- «У нас — сом.»
- «А у вас?»

You have successfully decoded many real words using just seven letters, including: **мама**, **сом**, **сон**, **оса**, **масло**, **сосна**, **ананас**, **насос**, **лама**, **смола**, **нам**, **нас**, **сам**, **мало**, and **слон**. This is a tremendous achievement.

## 📋 Підсумок — Summary

You have taken a massive first step into the world of Cyrillic reading. By focusing on just seven letters, you have built a strong, manageable foundation. You learned the pure vowels **А**, **О**, and **У**, noting that **У** makes an «oo» sound. You mastered the true friend **М** and the new shape **Л**. Most importantly, you conquered the visual traps: **Н** (which sounds like «n») and **С** (which sounds like «s»). You successfully blended these sounds to read words like **ма́ма**, **сон**, and **анана́с**.

Take a moment to review. You have learned 7 of the 33 letters of the alphabet!
- Can you pronounce all seven letters aloud?
- Can you spot the visual traps without hesitating?
- Can you read the words **ма́ма**, **сом**, and **ма́сло**?
- What sound does Н make? What sound does С make?

You are ready to practice! Next module: The Cyrillic Code II adds 7 more letters (К, И, І, Р, В, Т, Е).
