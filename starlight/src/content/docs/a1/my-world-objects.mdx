> **Чому це важливо? — Why does this matter?**
>
> Learning how to point things out and name objects around you is a major milestone. You will learn to say "this table" or "that book", anchoring your Ukrainian in the physical world around you.

## Вступ: Welcome to Your Space

Welcome to your space! In our previous chapters, we discovered that every Ukrainian noun has a grammatical gender — masculine, feminine, or neuter. This wasn't just a linguistic curiosity. Grammatical gender is the essential foundation for matching words together. Today, we put that foundation to practical use by learning how to point to objects in our immediate environment and identify them clearly.

Imagine you are an interior designer in a brand new room. You need to say "this table goes here" or "that lamp looks perfect". To do this in Ukrainian, your pointing words must perfectly match the gender of the object you are pointing at. If you point at a feminine object, you use a feminine pointing word.

There is a wonderful Ukrainian proverb: «В гостя́х до́бре, а вдо́ма кра́ще» (East or West, home is best, literally: As a guest it is good, but at home it is better). Let us make ourselves at home as we learn to name the everyday objects that fill our rooms.

Це дім. Це кімната. Це вікно. Це двері.
Це стіл. Це ліжко. Це шафа. Це лампа.
(This is a house. This is a room. This is a window. This is a door. This is a table. This is a bed. This is a wardrobe. This is a lamp.)

## Презентація: Pointing Near and Far

Now, let us learn our pointing words, also known as demonstrative pronouns. We will start with objects that are close to you, right at your fingertips.

When you want to say "this" (something near), you must choose between **цей**, **ця**, **це**, and **ці**. Your choice depends entirely on the gender of the noun. Notice how beautifully the endings rhyme to help you remember!

| Gender | "This" (Near) | Object | Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Masculine | **цей** | **стіл** (table) | **цей стіл** | this table |
| Feminine | **ця** | **кни́га** (book) | **ця кни́га** | this book |
| Neuter | **це** | **вікно́** (window) | **це вікно́** | this window |
| Plural | **ці** | **ре́чі** (things) | **ці ре́чі** | these things |

Listen to the feminine pair: **ця кни́га**. The ending «-я» sounds very similar to the «-а» ending of the noun. The same happens with neuter: **це вікно́**.

:::tip[💡 Порада]
**The Identification vs. Specification Hurdle**
It is extremely important to understand the difference between **Це стіл** — This is a table, and **Цей стіл** — This table.
When you identify what something is, you always use the fixed word **Це**, no matter the gender. **Це стіл** — This is a table. **Це кни́га** — This is a book.
However, when you specify a particular object, you use the gender-matching demonstrative: **Цей стіл**, **Ця кни́га**. English uses the word "this" for both situations, but Ukrainian makes a clear distinction.
:::

Now, look across the room. For objects that are far away, we use words meaning "that" or "those".

To remember the far demonstratives, use the "T" for "That/There" trick! Every far pointing word starts with the letter **т**.

| Gender | "That" (Far) | Object | Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Masculine | **той** | **стіл** (table) | **той стіл** | that table |
| Feminine | **та** | **кімна́та** (room) | **та кімна́та** | that room |
| Neuter | **те** | **лі́жко** (bed) | **те лі́жко** | that bed |
| Plural | **ті** | **лю́ди** (people) | **ті лю́ди** | those people |

Let us talk about a very special word: **две́рі** (door). In English, a door is a singular object. You have one door, or two doors. But in Ukrainian, **две́рі** is inherently plural. It only exists in the plural form, just like "scissors" or "pants" in English.

Therefore, you can never use a singular pointing word with a door. You must always use the plural form.
If the door is near you, it is **ці две́рі** (these doors / this door). If it is across the room, it is **ті две́рі** (those doors / that door).

:::warning[⚠️ Увага]
Be careful! Because "door" is singular in English, English speakers often want to say **ця две́рі**. This is incorrect. Always remember that the physical door might be one piece of wood, but the Ukrainian word requires plural pointing words: **ці две́рі**.
:::

## Практика: Arranging the Room

It is time to actively arrange our room. We will pair our pointing words with new household vocabulary. This builds muscle memory, helping your brain instantly recognize which gender group a noun belongs to.

Let us look at some more furniture. We have a **ша́фа** — wardrobe, a **ла́мпа** — lamp, and a **стіле́ць** — chair.

First, we determine the gender. **Ша́фа** and **ла́мпа** both end in «-а». They are feminine. **Стіле́ць** ends in a soft sign «-ь» following a consonant, which in this case marks a masculine noun.

Now let us point to them as if they are right next to us:
- **ця ша́фа** (this wardrobe)
- **ця ла́мпа** (this lamp)
- **цей стіле́ць** (this chair)

What if they are across the room? Remember the "T" for "That/There" trick.
- **та ша́фа** (that wardrobe)
- **та ла́мпа** (that lamp)
- **той стіле́ць** (that chair)

:::info[💡 Цікавий факт]
A common mistake for beginners is saying **цей кни́га**. Because English "this" has no gender, learners often default to the masculine **цей** for everything. Always check the ending of the noun! The «-а» in **кни́га** is your signal to use the feminine **ця**.
:::

Let us try a scenario. You are holding your phone, and you are pointing at a table across the room.
Your phone is a **телефо́н** (masculine). It is in your hand, so it is near. You say: **цей телефо́н** (this phone).
The table is a **стіл** (masculine). It is far away. You point and say: **той стіл** (that table).

Now imagine you are sitting on a bed, looking out the window.
The bed is **лі́жко** (neuter). It is near you: **це лі́жко** (this bed).
The window is **вікно́** (neuter). It is far from you: **те вікно́** (that window).

Repeat these pairs aloud. **Цей телефо́н**, **той стіл**. **Це лі́жко**, **те вікно́**. Feeling the difference between "here" and "there" is crucial for natural speech. Keep practicing these associations, and matching gender will become automatic!

Це телефон. Цей телефон. Той телефон.

## 🏺 Культурний контекст: Дім and Хата

When we talk about our living spaces, the words we choose carry deep cultural weight. The general word for "home" or "house" is **дім**. This is the word you use in our proverb: «В гостя́х до́бре, а вдо́ма кра́ще».

However, there is a very special distinction between a modern apartment and a traditional house. A modern, urban apartment is called a **кварти́ра**. A traditional, rural Ukrainian house is a **ха́та**.

In a traditional **ха́та**, the orientation of the room was deeply spiritual. The most important area was the **Поку́ття** (Red Corner). This was usually the corner diagonally opposite the oven. The **Поку́ття** was the spiritual focal point of the home, decorated with icons and traditional embroidered towels (**рушники́**). Even in modern layouts, many families still keep a special place of honor for important cultural and spiritual items. So, whether you live in a modern **кварти́ра** or a traditional **ха́та**, the concept of the home remains a sacred space.

:::note[🏺 Культура]
The word **дім** refers to the physical building and the emotional feeling of home. **Ха́та** is specifically a traditional house, often associated with a village, warmth, and generations of family history.
:::

## 📋 Підсумок: Summary and Self-Check

Let us review what we have built today.
The absolute rule of Ukrainian pointing words is gender agreement. Whether an object is near you (цей, ця, це, ці) or far away (той, та, те, ті), your demonstrative pronoun must match the gender and number of the noun it describes.

Remember the rhyming trick for near objects (**ця кни́га**) and the "T" rule for far objects. Also, never forget the critical difference between pointing out what something is (**Це стіл** — This is a table) and specifying a particular item (**Цей стіл** — This table).

**Self-Check Questions:**
1. How do you translate the identification sentence "This is a table" versus the specification phrase "This table"?
2. Which near demonstrative goes with the feminine word **кни́га**?
3. What demonstrative form must you use with the inherently plural word **две́рі**?
4. What is the difference between a **ха́та** and a **кварти́ра**?

Take a moment to look around your own room. Point to a lamp, a window, or a door, and try to name them in Ukrainian!
