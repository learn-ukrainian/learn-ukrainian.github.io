## Ра́нок (Morning)

Imagine you are in Kyiv. It is 7:00 AM. You open your eyes in a comfortable **готель** (hotel). Your day in Ukraine begins now. We will use everything you have learned in the A1 level to navigate this day. 

Let's start with the morning routine. You can look back at what just happened using the past tense. 

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** Я **прокинувся** о сьо́мій годи́ні. *(I woke up at seven o'clock. — male speaker)*


**Ти:** Я **прокинулася** о сьомій годині. *(I woke up at seven o'clock. — female speaker)*


</div>

You look out the window. What is the weather like today? 

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** До́брого ра́нку! Яка сього́дні пого́да? *(Good morning! What is the weather like today?)*


**Адміністратор:** Сьогодні те́пло і со́нячно. *(Today it is warm and sunny.)*


</div>

It is a beautiful morning, and you are hungry. You walk to a nearby cafe. Remember how to order politely? You use the accusative case for the items you want.

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** Доброго ранку! Будь ла́ска, ка́ву з молоко́м і **круасан**. *(Good morning! Please, a coffee with milk and a croissant.)*


**Бариста:** Ось, будь ласка. *(Here you go.)*


**Ти:** Скі́льки ко́шту́є? *(How much does it cost?)*


**Бариста:** Сто два́дцять гри́вень. *(One hundred twenty hryvnias.)*


**Ти:** Дякую! До поба́чення! *(Thank you! Goodbye!)*


</div>

Now you are energized and ready to explore the city. You want to go to Khreshchatyk, the main street of Kyiv. You need to ask for directions and use public transport. 

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** Ви́бачте, як діста́тися до Хреща́тика? *(Excuse me, how to get to Khreshchatyk?)*


**Перехожий:** Ї́дьте на метро́, ста́нція "Хреща́тик". *(Take the metro, station "Khreshchatyk".)*


</div>

You walk down into the metro station. You need to buy a **квиток** (ticket) or a pass.

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** Оди́н **квиток**, будь ласка. *(One ticket, please.)*


**Касир:** Ві́сім гривень. *(Eight hryvnias.)*


</div>

Inside the metro, you look at the **карта** (map). Kyiv has three metro lines, and you need to know your colors. You see that Khreshchatyk is on the red line, but you are currently on the green one. 

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** Ме́ні потрі́бна зеле́на **лінія**, а по́тім черво́на. *(I need the green line, and then the red one.)*


</div>

You are successfully navigating a major Ukrainian city entirely in Ukrainian!

## День (Daytime)

You step out of the metro station, and you are in the heart of Kyiv. 

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** Я гуля́ю по Хреща́тику. Яка га́рна ву́лиця! *(I am walking on Khreshchatyk. What a beautiful street!)*


</div>

As you walk, you notice a large shop selling traditional Ukrainian items. It is time to buy a **сувенір** (souvenir) for your family. You go inside. You will need your demonstrative pronouns (**цей**, **ця**, **це**) and your numbers to negotiate.

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** До́брий день! Скільки коштує ця вишиванка? *(Good day! How much does this vyshyvanka cost?)*


**Продавець:** Ти́сяча дві́сті гривень. *(One thousand two hundred hryvnias.)*


**Ти:** Ого́, до́рого! А ця? *(Oh, expensive! And this one?)*


**Продавець:** Ця — вісімсо́т. *(This one is eight hundred.)*


**Ти:** До́бре, я беру́! *(Good, I'll take it!)*


</div>

After shopping, you decide to get some lunch. You enter a cozy restaurant and hear someone speaking English. It is a great opportunity to practice introducing yourself and making a new friend. You decide to **зустріти** (to meet) and **познайомитися** (to get acquainted) with them. 

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** Привіт! Ти звідки? *(Hi! Where are you from?)*


**Олена:** Я з Кана́ди. А ти? *(I am from Canada. And you?)*


**Ти:** Я з Аме́рики. Що ти ро́биш тут? *(I am from America. What are you doing here?)*


**Олена:** Я вивча́ю украї́нську! *(I am studying Ukrainian!)*


**Ти:** Як ціка́во! Ході́мо обі́дати! *(How interesting! Let's go have lunch!)*


</div>

Notice how natural it feels to use the imperative **ходімо** (let's go) to make a friendly suggestion. You sit down together to eat. 

<div class="dialogue">


**Офіціант:** Що бу́дете замовля́ти? *(What will you order?)*


**Ти:** Я бу́ду борщ і варе́ники. *(I will have borscht and varenyky.)*


**Олена:** А я буду сала́т. *(And I will have a salad.)*


</div>

While eating, Olena compliments your language skills. 

<div class="dialogue">


**Олена:** Ду́же сма́чно! Ти добре гово́риш украї́нською! *(Very tasty! You speak Ukrainian well!)*


**Ти:** Дякую! *(Thank you!)*


</div>

## Ве́чір (Evening)

The day is winding down, but the evening is just beginning. You want to spend more time with your new friend, so you suggest making plans. You will need to use the future tense and time expressions. 

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** Що будемо роби́ти ввечері? *(What will we do in the evening?)*


**Олена:** Ходімо в кіно! *(Let's go to the cinema!)*


**Ти:** Добре! О котрі́й годині? *(Good! At what time?)*


**Олена:** О сьомій. *(At seven.)*


</div>

You go to the cinema and watch a Ukrainian **фільм** (film). Because you have studied hard, you realize something amazing: you do not understand every single word, but you understand the context, the emotions, and many key phrases! 

<div class="dialogue">


**Ти:** Я не все розумі́ю, але́ розумію бага́то! *(I don't understand everything, but I understand a lot!)*


</div>

After the film, you are both hungry again. You use your linking words and directions to find a nice place. 

<div class="dialogue">


**Олена:** Куди́ йдемо́ пі́сля кіно? *(Where are we going after the cinema?)*


**Ти:** Ходімо в рестора́н. Він пря́мо і налі́во. *(Let's go to a restaurant. It is straight and to the left.)*


</div>

Later that night, you return to your hotel room. You sit on the bed and think about everything that happened. This is where your ability to shift between past, present, and future tenses truly shines. Let's look at your inner monologue:

*   Сьогодні був чудо́вий день! *(Today was a wonderful day!)*
*   Зранку я снідав у кафе. *(In the morning I had breakfast in a cafe. — male speaker)*
*   Потім я гуля́ла по мі́сту і познайо́милася з Оле́ною. *(Then I walked around the city and met Olena. — female speaker)*
*   Ввечері ми ходили в кіно і ресторан. *(In the evening we went to the cinema and a restaurant.)*
*   Мені дуже подо́бається Ки́їв. *(I really like Kyiv.)*
*   Завтра я буду ї́здити по місту. *(Tomorrow I will ride around the city.)*
*   Я хо́чу поба́чити Лавру! *(I want to see the Lavra!)*

You successfully used the past tense to describe completed actions, the present tense to state how you feel right now, and the future tense to plan your next adventure to **Лавра** (the Lavra, a famous monastery in Kyiv).

## Підсумок: ти готовий/гото́ва! (You're Ready!)

Look at what you just did. You woke up, checked the weather, ordered food, navigated the metro, went shopping, met a friend, made evening plans, and reflected on your day. This is what A1 is all about: survival and basic communication. 

Let's review your A1 checklist. If you can do these things, you are fully **готовий** (ready — for males) or **готова** (ready — for females) for the next step.

*   **A1.1:** You can greet people, introduce yourself, and say where you are from. *(Привіт! Ме́не зва́ти... Я з...)*
*   **A1.2:** You can describe people, objects, and your family using adjectives. *(Він висо́кий. Це моя́ ма́ма.)*
*   **A1.3:** You can talk about your daily actions, likes, and habits. *(Я працю́ю. Мені подобається ка́ва.)*
*   **A1.4:** You can tell the time, discuss the weather, and name days and months. *(Зараз п'ята годи́на. Сьогодні хо́лодно.)*
*   **A1.5:** You can navigate a city, ask for directions, and use public transport. *(Де метро? Я ї́ду на авто́бусі.)*
*   **A1.6:** You can order food, go shopping, and handle Ukrainian currency. *(Дайте меню́. Скільки коштує?)*
*   **A1.7:** You can address people politely, give simple instructions, and connect your ideas. *(Будь ласка. Чита́йте!)*
*   **A1.8:** You can talk about the past, make future plans, and handle basic emergencies. *(Я чита́в. Я буду спа́ти. Допоможіть!)*

You have built a massive foundation. You can now survive in a Ukrainian-speaking environment. 

So, what is next? In the A2 level, the language will open up even more. You will learn about the mysterious **відмінки** (cases) in much deeper detail, allowing you to express complex relationships between words. You will discover **доконаний і недоко́наний вид** (perfective and imperfective aspect), which will let you talk about the exact results of your actions. You will learn the synthetic future tense *(я прочита́ю)*, subordinate clauses, and how to tell rich, detailed stories. 

But right now, take a moment to celebrate. 

**Вітаю!** (Congratulations!) 

You have officially finished the A1 level. You have proven that you can learn this beautiful, complex language. You can proudly say that you speak basic Ukrainian. Remember, this is only the **початок** (beginning). The journey ahead is long, but you are now equipped with the tools to **подорожувати** (to travel) further. 

Self-check challenge: Before you move on to A2, take a piece of paper. Can you describe YOUR perfect day in a Ukrainian city in 10 or more sentences? Try it!
