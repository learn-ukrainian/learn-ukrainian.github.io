> **Why does this matter?**
> The Ukrainian alphabet is your key to reading a highly phonetic language. Once you know the letters, you can pronounce almost any word correctly.

## Вступ — Introduction

Let's begin your journey into the Ukrainian language. Ukrainian uses the Cyrillic script. If you have ever felt frustrated by English spelling—where «read» and «bread» can sound completely different—you will love Ukrainian. Cyrillic is highly phonetic, meaning each letter usually maps to just one predictable sound. Once you understand the basic rules, the mystery of the letters will disappear, and you will unlock a beautiful, expressive language.

The Ukrainian alphabet has exactly 33 letters. This might look intimidating at first glance, especially since some letters look completely unfamiliar, but do not worry. You do not need to memorize all of them today. We will take it step by step, mastering small groups of letters in each module. Today, we focus on the structure of the alphabet as a whole and your first ten practice letters.

Here is the complete Ukrainian alphabet for reference. You do not need to memorize it now — think of it as your map. We will explore each group in the modules ahead.

| | | | | | | | |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| А | Б | В | Г | Ґ | Д | Е | Є |
| Ж | З | И | І | Ї | Й | К | Л |
| М | Н | О | П | Р | С | Т | У |
| Ф | Х | Ц | Ч | Ш | Щ | Ь | Ю |
| Я | | | | | | | |

:::note[🏺 **A unique history**]
The Cyrillic alphabet was created in the 9th century by students of Saints Cyril and Methodius. It is not derived from the Latin alphabet you use in English, but rather from the Greek uncial script. The Ukrainian alphabet has its own distinctive letters — **І**, **Є**, **Ї**, and **Ґ** — each representing a sound essential to the Ukrainian language. This alphabet reflects centuries of independent development and is a source of cultural pride.
:::

## Букви і звуки — Letters and Sounds

To learn to read Ukrainian fluently, we must first understand the crucial difference between letters and sounds. Letters (**букви**) are the written symbols you see on the printed page or on a screen. Sounds (**звуки**) are what you actually hear and pronounce when you speak. They are intimately connected, but they are not exactly the same thing.

The Ukrainian language actually has 38 distinct phonemes (sounds), but we only use 33 letters to represent them all. How does that work in practice? Most of the time, it is incredibly straightforward: one letter equals exactly one sound. This reliable phonetic consistency makes Ukrainian much easier to read than English. Once you learn the 33 letters and their corresponding sounds, you will be able to sound out virtually any word you encounter, even if you do not know what it means yet.

However, because there are more sounds than letters, there are a few special cases we will explore later. For example, the iotated vowels — **Я**, **Ю**, **Є**, and **Ї** — can represent two sounds at once when placed at the beginning of a word. There is also a special letter called the soft sign, which has no sound of its own but changes how the previous letter is pronounced. You will master all these fascinating details in future modules.

:::tip[💡 **Read exactly what you see**]
Because Ukrainian spelling is highly phonetic, there are no «silent letters» like the 'k' in the English word «knife» or the 'b' in «comb». You pronounce every single letter in a word.
:::

## Голосні та приголосні — Vowels and Consonants

The 33 letters of the Ukrainian alphabet are logically divided into two main categories: vowels and consonants. Understanding this fundamental division is your very first step toward successfully blending individual letters into syllables and complete words.

Vowels (**голосні**) are sounds you make with an open mouth, using only your voice. Nothing obstructs the flow of air. There are exactly 10 vowel letters in the Ukrainian alphabet: six base vowels (**А О У Е И І**) and four iotated vowels (**Я Ю Є Ї**). You will learn the iotated vowels in Module 2. Vowels are the heart of the word. Every single syllable in a Ukrainian word must contain exactly one vowel. Without a vowel, you cannot have a syllable at all.

Consonants (**приголосні**) are sounds made when the flow of air from your lungs is partially or fully obstructed by your lips, tongue, teeth, or throat. The Ukrainian alphabet contains 22 consonant letters. They provide the necessary structure around the vowels to form recognizable words.

The remaining single letter in the alphabet is the special modifier mentioned earlier. It is categorized as neither a vowel nor a consonant simply because it makes absolutely no sound on its own.

| Category | Letters | Count |
|----------|---------|-------|
| **Голосні** (Vowels) | А О У Е И І + Я Ю Є Ї | 10 |
| **Приголосні** (Consonants) | Б В Г Ґ Д Ж З Й К Л М Н П Р С Т Ф Х Ц Ч Ш Щ | 22 |
| **Modifier** | Ь (soft sign) | 1 |
| **Total** | | **33** |

:::info[💡 **Syllable counting made easy**]
Because every single syllable must have exactly one vowel, you can easily count the syllables in any Ukrainian word just by counting its vowels. For example, the word **молоко́** has exactly three vowels (О, О, О), so you immediately know it has three syllables: мо-ло-ко.
:::

## Перші 10 літер — First 10 Letters

Now, let's learn your first 10 letters. We will start with 4 vowels and 6 consonants. We carefully selected these high-frequency letters because they will allow you to read real, meaningful Ukrainian words immediately. You will be amazed at how much you can read with just these few building blocks.

First, let's look at the vowels.
- The letter **А** sounds like the open 'a' in «father».
- The letter **О** sounds like the rounded 'o' in «more».
- The letter **У** sounds like the 'oo' in «moon».
- The letter **І** sounds like the 'ee' in «see».

Next, let's meet the consonants.
- The letter **М** sounds just like the English M.
- The letter **Т** sounds like the English T.
- The letter **К** sounds like the English K.

Now, beware of the «false friends» — letters that look exactly like English letters but sound completely different!
- **Н** looks like an English H, but it sounds like N.
- **С** looks like an English C, but it sounds like S.
- **Л** sounds like the English L, though your tongue position is slightly different.

## Перші слова — First Words in Context

Now that you can decode several words, let's put them into simple, meaningful sentences. To do this, we need to introduce a few «sight words». These are very common, everyday words that contain letters you have not learned yet. Do not try to decode them letter by letter right now; instead, just learn to recognize their overall shape and meaning.

Here are your first three sight words. You will see them constantly:
- **приві́т** — hello
- **дя́кую** — thank you
- **це** — this is

We also have two very important, fully decodable words used for answering simple questions:
- **так** — yes
- **ні** — no

The little word **це** (this is) acts as a fantastic building block for beginners. By combining **це** with the vocabulary words you can already read, you can form your very first Ukrainian sentences. Notice how we do not need to use a verb like «is» in these simple sentences.

{/**/}
> **Діалог 1 (Dialogue 1)**
> — Привіт! Це мама?
> — Так, це мама. Це тато.
>
> **Діалог 2 (Dialogue 2)**
> — Це місто?
> — Ні, це ліс. Тут ліс, там місто.
>
> **Діалог 3 (Dialogue 3)**
> — Це молоко?
> — Так, це молоко. Це масло.
> — Дякую!

Here are a few more sentences to practice your reading skills. Take your time and sound out the decodable words one by one. Read them aloud to yourself.
- **Мама тут.** — Mom is here.
- **Тато там.** — Dad is there.
- **Це молоко.** — This is milk.
- **Це масло.** — This is butter.
- **Око тут.** — The eye is here.

:::note[🏺 **The power of «це»**]
The word **це** (this is) is one of the most useful words in Ukrainian. You can point at anything around you and say **Це ...** to name it. Try it now with objects near you — it is a technique Ukrainian children use when learning to speak!
:::

:::note[**Mini-challenge**]
Look around the room. Can you find something whose Ukrainian name uses only the 10 letters you learned? Point at it and say **Це ...** out loud. For example, point at a table and say **Це стіл!**
:::

Congratulations! You have already successfully read your first Ukrainian sentences. You are well on your way to reading fluency.
