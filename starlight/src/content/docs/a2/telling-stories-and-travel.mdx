## Сценарій 1: Що вчора трапилось? (~600 words total)

Telling a good story in Ukrainian is like directing a play. First, you must set the stage and describe the background scene. Then, you introduce the action that moves the plot forward. To do this effectively, we use the two aspects of the past tense. The imperfective aspect paints the background picture—what was happening, what the weather was like, or what you were doing. The perfective aspect delivers the main events. These are the sudden actions that interrupted the background process and changed the situation.

Учора був теплий літній вечір. Я сидів на балконі, пив чай і читав цікаву книгу. На вулиці грали діти, а машини їздили дуже повільно. Це був мій ідеальний вечір. Раптом задзвонив телефон. Я взяв його і побачив повідомлення від друга. Він написав, що чекає мене внизу. Я швидко одягнувся і вийшов на вулицю.

> *Yesterday was a warm summer evening. I was sitting on the balcony, drinking tea, and reading an interesting book. Children were playing outside, and cars were driving very slowly. It was my perfect evening. Suddenly the phone rang. I took it and saw a message from a friend. He wrote that he was waiting for me downstairs. I quickly got dressed and went outside.*

To connect these background states and sudden events, you need time connectors. These words act as the logical glue for your narrative, helping the listener follow the sequence of actions. When you want to **розповідати** (to tell — impf.) a story, connectors signal transitions from a calm process to a plot twist, or guide us from the beginning of a story to its end.

Коли ми хочемо розповідати історію, ми часто використовуємо спеціальні слова. Спочатку ми описуємо ситуацію. Потім ми говоримо про першу подію. Якщо стається щось несподіване, ми кажемо «раптом». А в кінці нашої історії ми зазвичай використовуємо слово «нарешті». Ці слова допомагають зробити розповідь логічною та зрозумілою для всіх.

> *When we want to tell a story, we often use special words. At first, we describe the situation. Then we talk about the first event. If something unexpected happens, we say "suddenly". And at the end of our story, we usually use the word "finally". These words help make the narrative logical and understandable for everyone.*

Let's look at a practical example of a travel mishap. When you are at a busy **вокзал** (station), the background is full of ongoing activities. People are waiting for their **потяг** (train) and buying their **квиток** (ticket). But your personal experience consists of a sequence of completed actions. Notice how the imperfective verbs in the following story describe the atmosphere, while the perfective verbs highlight the specific problem the narrator faced.

У п'ятницю я приїхав на вокзал. Там було дуже багато людей. Вони купували квитки, пили каву і чекали на свій потяг. Я стояв біля каси і слухав музику. Раптом я зрозумів, що загубив свій квиток. Я дуже злякався. Я почав шукати його в рюкзаку, але його там не було. Нарешті я знайшов його в кишені куртки. Я зрадів і пішов на платформу. Це була дуже нервова подорож.

> *On Friday I arrived at the train station. There were a lot of people there. They were buying tickets, drinking coffee, and waiting for their train. I was standing near the ticket office and listening to music. Suddenly I realized that I had lost my ticket. I got very scared. I started looking for it in my backpack, but it wasn't there. Finally, I found it in my jacket pocket. I was happy and went to the platform. It was a very nervous trip.*

Let's review how we form these past tense verbs. The past tense requires you to match the verb ending to the gender and number of the subject. For a masculine subject, including a male narrator saying "I", the verb ends in «-в». For a feminine subject, it ends in «-ла». Neuter subjects take «-ло», and plural subjects take «-ли».

:::info
**The stable «-в» suffix**
In Ukrainian, the masculine past tense suffix «-в» is remarkably stable and phonetically distinct. Even for verbs with consonant stems where the suffix might disappear in neighboring Slavic languages, Ukrainian proudly keeps it or changes the root vowel. For example, «могти» (to be able) becomes «міг», «нести» (to carry) becomes «ніс», and «бігти» (to run) becomes «біг».
:::

When you want to ask about an incident during a **подорож** (trip), you need to choose the right question. If you are asking about the background process, you ask «Що відбувалося?» (What was going on?). But if you want to know about the main event, you ask «Що **трапилося**?» (What happened?). Both verbs mean "to happen", but we use the perfective form for sudden plot twists. After the story, you can share your **враження** (impression) of the event.

Коли друзі бачать, що ти нервуєш, вони часто запитують: «Що трапилося?». Ти можеш розповісти їм свою історію і описати свої емоції. Наприклад, ти можеш сказати: «Я злякався, коли не побачив свій телефон». Або: «Я дуже здивувався, коли зустрів там свого брата». Коли ми подорожуємо, кожна нова поїздка залишає сильне враження. Навіть якщо ти просто хочеш безпечно доїхати додому, несподівані події роблять розповідь цікавою. Наші реакції — це важлива частина кожної історії.

> *When friends see that you are nervous, they often ask: "What happened?". You can tell them your story and describe your emotions. For example, you can say: "I got scared when I didn't see my phone". Or: "I was very surprised when I met my brother there". When we travel, every new trip leaves a strong impression. Even if you just want to safely reach home, unexpected events make the narrative interesting. Our reactions are an important part of every story.*

## Сценарій 2: Плануємо подорож (~550 words total)

<div class="dialogue">

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Марко:</span> Куди ми поїдемо у відпустку цього року? *(Where will we go on vacation this year?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Олена:</span> Давай поїдемо в Карпати. Це чудова **подорож**! *(Let's go to the Carpathians. It's a wonderful trip!)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Марко:</span> Гарна ідея. Коли ми виїдемо? *(Good idea. When will we depart?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Олена:</span> Ми виїдемо в п'ятницю ввечері. А де ми будемо жити? *(We will depart on Friday evening. And where will we live?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Марко:</span> Ми **зупинимося** в горах. Я забронюю маленьку хатинку. *(We will stay in the mountains. I will book a small cabin.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Олена:</span> Супер. Як ми туди поїдемо? *(Super. How will we go there?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Марко:</span> Ми купимо **квиток** на нічний **потяг**. *(We will buy a ticket for the night train.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Олена:</span> Чудово. Я буду планувати наші екскурсії. *(Great. I will be planning our excursions.)*</div>

</div>

When we plan a trip, we talk about the future. In Ukrainian, there are two main ways to express future actions. If we want to describe an ongoing process or a continuous state, we use the compound future tense. This is built with the imperfective aspect. You simply take the future forms of the verb «бути» (to be) and add the infinitive of your main verb. For example, you use «я буду», «ти будеш», or «ми будемо».

У Карпатах ми будемо багато гуляти. Ми будемо дихати свіжим повітрям. Олена буде фотографувати гори, а Марко буде читати. Вони не будуть працювати. Вони будуть тільки відпочивати.

> *In the Carpathians, we will walk a lot. We will breathe fresh air. Olena will be photographing the mountains, and Marko will be reading. They will not be working. They will only be resting.*

However, when we focus on a specific result or a one-time completed action, we use the simple future tense. This form uses perfective verbs. Instead of adding a helper verb, perfective verbs take endings that look exactly like present tense endings, but their meaning is entirely future. 

Я обов'язково куплю квитки сьогодні ввечері. Марко знайде хатинку і забронює її. Ми сядемо в потяг і швидко доїдемо до гір. Завтра вранці ми побачимо перші вершини.

> *I will definitely buy the tickets tonight. Marko will find a cabin and book it. We will get on the train and quickly reach the mountains. Tomorrow morning we will see the first peaks.*

:::info
**Result vs. Process**
Compare these ideas: «Я куплю квиток» means "I will buy the ticket" (a completed result). But «Я буду купувати квиток» means "I will be in the process of buying the ticket" (the ongoing activity).
:::

Motion verbs are essential for discussing travel plans. When you talk about the journey itself—moving in one direction towards your destination—you use perfective forms like «поїду» (I will go by vehicle) or «полечу» (I will fly). Once you arrive, you might talk about general movement around the area. For this, you use the imperfective compound form, such as «буду їздити». To connect these verbs to destinations, we use «до» plus the Genitive case for cities, like «до Львова». But we use «на» plus the Accusative case for open areas or events, like «на море» or «на фестиваль».

Коли ви плануєте подорож, вам потрібні нові слова. Ви купуєте квиток на потяг або на літак. Потяг їде довго. Це наша залізниця. Літак летить швидко, і ви прибуваєте в аеропорт. В Україні є багато красивих міст. Ви можете поїхати до Києва, до Львова або до Харкова. Там ви зможете зупинитися в готелі.

> *When you plan a trip, you need new words. You buy a ticket for a train or for an airplane. The train travels for a long time. This is our railway. The airplane flies quickly, and you arrive at the airport. There are many beautiful cities in Ukraine. You can go to Kyiv, to Lviv, or to Kharkiv. There you will be able to stay in a hotel.*

:::tip
**Authentic Ukrainian Names**
Use the authentic word **квиток** (ticket), not the Russian-sounding "білет." Similarly, use **залізниця** (railway). When mentioning Ukrainian cities, always use their proper Ukrainian names: Kyiv (Київ), Lviv (Львів), and Kharkiv (Харків), entirely avoiding old colonial transliterations.
:::

## Сценарій 3: Розкажи про поїздку! (~600 words total)

When a friend returns from a **подорож** (trip), you want to ask about their experience. You can ask «Де ти був?» (Where were you?) to focus on the location itself. To focus on the journey, ask «Куди ти їздив?» (Where did you travel to?). You might also ask how they managed to **доїхати** (reach) their destination, and ask them to **розповісти** (to tell) you the whole story.

<div class="dialogue">

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Марко:</span> Привіт, Олено! Де ти була? Я тебе давно не бачив. *(Hi, Olena! Where have you been? I haven't seen you for a long time.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Олена:</span> Привіт! Я їздила до Одеси на кілька днів. Це була чудова подорож. *(Hi! I went to Odesa for a few days. It was a wonderful trip.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Марко:</span> Ого! Куди ти їздила? На пляж? *(Wow! Where did you travel? To the beach?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Олена:</span> Так, ми ходили на пляж і дивилися на море. *(Yes, we went to the beach and looked at the sea.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Марко:</span> Як ви туди доїхали? *(How did you reach there?)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Олена:</span> Ми купили квиток на потяг і вранці вже були там. *(We bought a ticket for the train and were already there in the morning.)*</div>

<div class="dialogue-line"><span class="speaker">Марко:</span> Тобі треба все мені розповісти! Що там трапилося? *(You need to tell me everything! What happened there?)*</div>

</div>

The verb **їздити** (to travel by vehicle) is an imperfective verb, but it has a special function in the past tense. It describes a completed round-trip. When you say «Я їздив до Одеси» (I traveled to Odesa), it means you went there and you have already returned. 

Використовуйте дієслово «поїхати» для дії без повернення. Наприклад, фраза «Він поїхав до Києва» показує результат. Це означає, що людина зараз у дорозі. Або вона вже там і ще не повернулася. Використовуйте цю форму, щоб показати відсутність людини.

> *Use the verb "поїхати" for an action without return. For example, the phrase "He left for Kyiv" shows a result. This means the person is currently on the way. Or they are already there and have not returned yet. Use this form to show the person's absence.*

This distinction is crucial for storytelling. If your friend is standing right in front of you telling you about their vacation, they will say «Я їздив», because they have successfully returned.

When narrating the sequence of events, you will alternate between the perfective and imperfective aspects. You use perfective verbs for the main events that move the story forward, and imperfective verbs for background processes.

Спочатку ми приїхали до міста. Потім ми знайшли готель і зупинилися там. Вдень ми багато ходили по місту. Ми довго дивилися на красиву архітектуру. А ввечері ми відвідали відому оперу. Ми послухали класичну музику. Ми дуже втомилися, але були щасливі.

> *First we arrived in the city. Then we found a hotel and stayed there. During the day we walked around the city a lot. We looked at the beautiful architecture for a long time. And in the evening we visited a famous opera. We listened to classical music. We were very tired, but we were happy.*

Notice how the actions of arriving («приїхали») and choosing to **зупинитися** (to stay) are perfective results. They are completed points on a timeline, describing what managed to **трапитися** (to happen). But walking around («ходили») is an imperfective process that took up time.

To share your feelings about the journey, use the perfective verb **сподобатися** (to like). The thing you like is the grammatical subject, and you receive the feeling in the Dative case, forming «Мені сподобалося» (It pleased me).

Моя нова подорож була просто чудова! Мені дуже сподобалося це місто. Я отримав багато нових вражень. Там було так цікаво і весело! Я обов'язково хочу поїхати туди ще раз.

You can follow «Мені сподобалось» with an infinitive verb to say you liked doing an action. When talking about your emotions, sharing your **враження** (impression) makes the story much more engaging.

When choosing vocabulary for travel, use authentic Ukrainian words. You buy a **квиток** (ticket) for the **потяг** (train) and depart from the **вокзал** (station). The Russian-sounding «путешествіє» is a colonial calque that you should strictly avoid.

На вокзалі ви можете почути різні слова. Слово «вокзал» є дуже популярним і загальноприйнятим. Але на заході України часто кажуть слово «двірець». Воно має дуже давню і цікаву історію. Також важливо знати різницю між зупинкою та станцією. Автобус прибуває на зупинку, а потяг прибуває на станцію.

> *At the station you can hear different words. The word "вокзал" is very popular and generally accepted. But in the west of Ukraine, they often say the word "двірець". It has a very ancient and interesting history. It is also important to know the difference between a stop and a station. A bus arrives at a stop, and a train arrives at a station.*

:::tip
**Did you know?**
The word «двірець» is widely used in Lviv and other western cities instead of «вокзал». It originates from the Polish word for a manor or court, reflecting the grand architecture of historical train stations in the region.
:::

Using these specific terms correctly not only improves your grammar but also shows respect for the richness and historical depth of the Ukrainian language.

## Мовленнєве завдання: Моя подорож (~450 words total)

Now it is your turn to write your own story. Compose a short narrative of about eight to ten sentences about a recent trip you took. Make sure to use at least three time connectors, such as «спочатку» (at first), «потім» (then), or «нарешті» (finally). You must also include at least two verbs of motion in the past tense. Focus on the clear contrast between the background setting, which uses imperfective verbs, and the main events, which require perfective verbs.

Напишіть коротку розповідь про вашу подорож. Спробуйте розповісти, куди ви їздили та що там трапилося.

> *Write a short story about your trip. Try to tell where you went and what happened there.*

Before you finalize your text, use this detailed checklist to self-correct your grammar. First, check your past tense endings. If you are female, did you use the «-ла» ending for all your actions, like «я їздила» instead of «я їздив»? Second, look at your prepositions of direction. Did you use «до» followed by the Genitive case when going to a city? Finally, check your sequence of events. Did you use words like «потім» or «після цього» to show the order of actions clearly?

Here is a model answer to guide you. Notice how it combines motion verbs, aspect switches, and impressions.

Минулого літа я їздив до Карпат. Ми купили квиток на потяг. Спочатку ми приїхали на вокзал. Потім ми доїхали до міста і поїхали в гори. Погода була чудова, світило сонце. Ми довго йшли лісом. Раптом ми побачили оленя! Нарешті ми піднялися на гору і зупинилися там на ніч. Мені дуже сподобалася ця подорож. Я отримав гарні враження.

> *Last summer I went (impf. — round trip) to the Carpathians. We bought (pf. — event) a train ticket. At first, we arrived (pf. — event) at the station. Then we reached (pf. — event) the city and went (pf. — departed) to the mountains. The weather was (impf. — background) wonderful, the sun was shining (impf. — background). We walked (impf. — process) through the forest for a long time. Suddenly we saw (pf. — event) a deer! Finally, we climbed (pf. — event) the mountain and stayed (pf. — result) there for the night. I really liked (pf. — reaction) this trip. I received (pf. — event) good impressions.*

:::info
**Grammar box**
Always double-check your case endings when talking about transport. You travel **by** a vehicle using the pure Instrumental case without any prepositions.
:::

Let's recap common pitfalls. The most frequent error is forgetting gender agreement. Remember that «я ходив» is for masculine speakers, while female speakers must say «я ходила». Another mistake involves transportation. You do not use the preposition «з» (with) when you travel by a vehicle. You must say «летіти літаком» (to fly by airplane), not «летіти з літаком».

## Підсумок (~150 words)

We have covered all the grammar you need to discuss your next **подорож** (trip, journey). You now know how to build a coherent narrative using imperfective verbs for the background and perfective verbs for the main actions. When /pf.) your story, use time connectors like «спочатку», «потім», «раптом», and «нарешті». Perfective verbs are your best choice for specific completed events.

Always keep the travel logic in mind. Remember that «поїду» is a one-way trip, while «буду їздити» is for habitual travel. In the past tense, «їздив» indicates a completed round trip, whereas «поїхав» means a departure. If something unexpected were to **трапитися** (to happen), you can now explain it clearly. You know how to buy a **квиток** (ticket) for the **потяг** (train).

Pay attention to prepositions: use «до» with the Genitive case for destinations, «з» or «зі» with the Genitive for origins, and «на» with the Accusative for events. You can comfortably navigate the **вокзал** (station). You can also confidently explain how you managed to **доїхати** (to reach by vehicle — pf.) to the city and **зупинитися** (to stay, to stop — pf.) at a hotel.

Finally, you know how to use the verb **сподобатися** (to like — pf.) to talk about your feelings. May your future travels always leave you with the very best **враження** (impression)! Keep practicing your narratives and enjoy your language journey.

Ви чудово попрацювали в цьому модулі. Тепер ви знаєте, як правильно будувати розповідь про минулі події. Не забувайте використовувати дієслова різного виду та правильні прийменники напрямку. Продовжуйте вивчати українську мову з радістю!
