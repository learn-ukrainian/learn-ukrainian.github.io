   2.2 Plural Nouns in –ї and Some Exceptions  

          
  

In this module, you will learn which nouns take the ending –**ї** in plural, become familiar with two groups of nouns that deviate from the regular pattern of plural ending formation, and learn the Ukrainian plural form of pronoun “my.”

## Завда́ння 1

Note how ending –**й **on masculine nouns and ending –**ія **on feminine nouns change in plural.

оди́н трамва́**й**

два трамва́**ї**

одна́ а́кц**ія**

дві а́кці**ї**

## Ціка́во!

Besides the feminine nouns that end in –**ія**, feminine nouns that end in **another vowel + ****я** take the ending –**ї** in plural.

*For example:* галер**е́я** → галер**е́ї, **and ста́т**уя** → ста́т**уї**

In addition, the noun **сім’я́** takes the ending –**ї** in plural: сім’**я́ → **сі́м’**ї**

## Ціка́во!

Nouns that end in –**р** preceded by a different vowel (not –**а **or –**я**) follow the general rule for plural formation and take the ending –**и** in plural.

*For* *example**: *лі́д**ер** → лі́дер**и and **профе́с**ор** → професор**и́**
