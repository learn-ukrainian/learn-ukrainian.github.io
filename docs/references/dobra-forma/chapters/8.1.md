#
   8.1 Accusative Case of Animate Nouns  

          
  

In this module, you will learn the accusative case forms for singular and plural animate nouns and review the accusative case forms for inanimate nouns.

## Завда́ння 1

Note how in the dialogues that have inanimate masculine nouns as direct objects, the accusative case endings on the nouns coincide with the *nominative* case endings – whether singular or plural. However, note how in the dialogues that have **animate** masculine nouns as direct objects, the accusative case endings on the nouns coincide with the *genitive* case endings – whether singular or plural.

– Ти лю́биш **борщ**?

– Я ду́же люблю́ **борщ**!

– Ти лю́биш **Макси́ма**?

– Я коха́ю **Макси́ма**.

– Ти ма́єш на́ші **квитки́**?

– Так, звича́йно! Я ма́ю **квитки́**.

– Ти ма́єш **браті́в**?

– Так, я ма́ю **браті́в** – аж чоти́ри!

## Завда́ння 4

Note how in the first two dialogues, which have feminine **singular** nouns as direct objects, the accusative case endings on the nouns are identical – regardless of whether the noun is animate or inanimate. However, note how in the subsequent dialogues, which have feminine **plural** nouns as direct objects, the accusative form of inanimate nouns coincides with the **nominative** plural endings while the accusative form of animate nouns coincides with the **genitive** plural endings.

– Ти лю́биш **пі́цу**?

– Я ду́же люблю́ **пі́цу**!

– Ти лю́биш **Ні́ну**?

– Я коха́ю **Ні́ну**.

– Ти ма́єш **тарілки́**?

– Так, звича́йно! Я ма́ю і **тарілки́**, і виде́лки, і ло́жки&#8230;

– Ти ма́єш **сесте́р**?
