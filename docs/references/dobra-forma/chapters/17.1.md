#
   17.1 Accusative Case of Adjectives (With Inanimate Nouns)  

          
  

In this module, you will learn the accusative case endings of adjectives for all genders and for plural – when they are used with inanimate nouns. You will practice agreement between adjectives and inanimate nouns in the accusative case, as well as the question word **яки́й**.

## Завда́ння 1

Read the dialog below and note that with inanimate nouns in the accusative case, only the feminine adjective form changes. The rest of the forms are identical to the nominative case. To review contexts in which Ukrainian uses accusative, see [Module 5.1](https://opentext.ku.edu/dobraforma/chapter/5-1/) and [Module 8.2](https://opentext.ku.edu/dobraforma/chapter/8-2/).

– Що ти лю́биш пи́ти?

– Вра́нці я за́вжди п’ю **чо́рну ка́ву**. Я та́кож ча́сто п’ю **зеле́ний чай**. Влі́тку я і́нколи п’ю **холо́дне пи́во**. А ще я люблю́ **газо́вані напо́ї**, але п’ю їх ду́же рі́дко.

Mark all adjectives in the accusative in the sentences below and then answer the questions that follow.

## Цікаво!

As the third sentence in the activity above illustrates, feminine adjectives change in the accusative case even when they modify [exceptional feminine nouns ending in a consonant or a soft sign](https://opentext.ku.edu/dobraforma/chapter/1-2/) that don’t change in the accusative.  For example:

Я знайшла́ **ціка́ву** річ.

Ми ма́ли ду́же **те́плу** о́сінь.

## Важли́во!

With inanimate nouns, only the feminine form of the question word **яки́й **changes in the accusative case. For example:

**Яку́ ка́ву ти лю́биш?**
