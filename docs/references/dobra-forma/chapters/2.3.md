#
   2.3 Plural Nouns with Stem Changes  

          
  

In this module, you will become familiar with three major stem changes that some masculine nouns undergo in the plural, learn several feminine nouns that undergo stem changes in plural, and practice recognizing plural nouns and their meanings by reading authentic Ukrainian business signs.

## Завда́ння 1

Note the stem change that masculine nouns with the suffix –**ець** undergo in plural.

оди́н оліве́**ць**

два олі**вці́**

оди́н хло́п**ець**

два хло́**пці**

Read the texts below and mark all the plural nouns in which a stem change occurred. Then answer the questions that follow.

## Ціка́во!

While most nouns with the suffix –**ець** simply drop the –**е**– in plural, a few nouns replace it with the soft sign. The most common nouns of this kind are:

cтіл**е́ць** – стіл**ь**ці́ (&#8216;chairs')

па́л**ець** – па́л**ь**ці (&#8216;fingers')

–**e**– is also dropped in the noun **день** and its derivatives:

**день **– **дні **(&#8216;days')

ти́ж**день** – ти́**жні **(&#8216;weeks'). *Note that this noun also drops the –**д**– for ease of pronunciation.*

## Завда́ння 6

Note the stem change which some short masculine nouns with –**і**– in the stem undergo in plural.

Це мій к**і**т.

Це мої́ к**о**ти́.

Мадагаска́р – це о́стр**і**в.

Гава́ї – це остр**о**ви́.

Complete the sentences below paying careful attention to context and to what you have learned about the formation of plurals thus far.

## Ціка́во!

Most short nouns that replace –**і**– with –**о**– in the plural are masculine. However, a stem change also occurs in a small number of feminine nouns that have **–****і-** in the stem and end in a consonant. A common one is ніч – но́чі.

A few masculine and feminine nouns replace the stem –**і**– with –**е**-.  These include річ – ре́чі and **папі́р – папе́ри.**
