# Literature Expansion Strategy: "The National Corpus"

**Status**: Proposal (Professor Persona)
**Date**: Feb 10, 2026
**Relation**: Expands `LIT` into `LIT-MAIN` and `LIT-SPEC`. Integrates with `OES` & `RUTH`.

## üèõ The Architectural Philosophy

We reject the "Survey Course" approach (shallow coverage). To defeat colonial narratives, we must demonstrate the **density, continuity, and stylistic diversity** of Ukrainian literature.

We adopt a **Two-Stream Architecture**:

1.  **LIT-MAIN (The Canon)**: The mandatory chronological spine. Cultural Literacy.
2.  **LIT-SPEC (The Library)**: Elective tracks for genre deep-dives and specific reading goals.

This stream is the direct continuation of **RUTH** (which ends with the Cossack Baroque/Skovoroda). It tells the story of the *Modern* Ukrainian nation through its texts.

### Phase 1: Foundations & The Vernacular (1798‚Äì1870s)

*   **The Narrative**: From "Kotliarevsky's laughter" to Shevchenko's rage, to the establishment of Realism.
*   *Current Status*: Covers modules 01-30, but needs ~15 more (Myrny, Nechuy-Levytsky deep dive).

**Total LIT-MAIN Scope**: ~270 Modules.

## üß™ Stream 2: LIT-SPEC (Specialized Tracks)

These tracks run parallel to the Canon. They allow for "binge-reading" in a specific style without needing historical context for every single text.

### üß† LIT-ESSAY (The Intellectuals)
*Priority: High (For C1/C2 Critical Thinking)*
*   **Focus**: Non-fiction, polemics, literary criticism, philosophy.
*   **Authors**: Shevelov, Dontsov, Malaniuk, Hrytsak, Zabuzhko (Essays), Sherekh.
*   **Goal**: Teach the learner how Ukrainians *think* and *argue* about themselves.

### üöÄ LIT-FANTASTIKA (Sci-Fi, Fantasy, Horror)
*   **Focus**: Imagination and Genre Fiction.
*   **Content**:
    *   **Horror**: From Gogol (if claimed) to Kvitka-Osnovianenko's gothic, to moderns.
    *   **Sci-Fi**: Oles Berdnyk (The Ukrainian Strugatsky equivalent), Ihor Rosokhovatsky.
    *   **Fantasy**: The Dyachenkos (Translation issues discussed), Volodymyr Arenev, Dara Korniy (Urban Fantasy/Chimaeras).

### üß∏ LIT-KIDS (Children's Classics)
*   **Focus**: Reading fluency and cultural touchstones.
*   **Content**: Vsevolod Nestayko ("Toreadors"), Ivan Franko ("Fox Mykyta"), Sashko Dermansky.
*   **Note**: Not "baby books" but legitimate literature that every Ukrainian adult knows.

### ‚öîÔ∏è LIT-HIST-FIC (Historical Fiction)
*   **Focus**: Narrative History.
*   **Content**: Pavlo Zahrebelnyi (The monumentalist), Roman Ivanychuk, Vasyl Shklyar ("Black Raven").
*   **Goal**: Immersive history through narrative.
