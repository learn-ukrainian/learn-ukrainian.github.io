## 1. Source Material
*   **The Eyewitness**: *Litopys Samovydtsia* (Chronicle of the Eyewitness).
    *   **Focus**: The Ruin (*Ruyina*), Khmelnytsky Uprising. Factual, dry narrative.
*   **The Panegyrist**: *Hrabianka's Chronicle*.
    *   **Focus**: Glorification of the Cossack Host (*Viysko Zaporozke*). Poetic style.
*   **The Monumentalist**: *Velychko's Chronicle*.
    *   **Focus**: Massive documentation, documents, letters. High Baroque style.
*   **The Lawgiver**: *Constitution of Pylyp Orlyk* (1710).
    *   **Focus**: Social contract, rights of the Host, separation of powers.
*   **The Philosopher**: *Hryhorii Skovoroda*.
    *   **Focus**: *Srodna Pratsia* (Affinity Work), *Fables*, *Garden of Divine Songs*.

---

## 2. Linguistic Focus: The Cossack Baroque
Phase 4 sees the peak of the Old Ukrainian literary language before its transformation into the modern vernacular.

### Narrative Styles
*   **Samovydets**: "Telegram style" - concise, verb-heavy.
*   **Velychko**: "Ornamental style" - rhetorical questions, Latinisms, elaborate metaphors.

### Vocabulary
*   **Military/Administrative**: *Hetman*, *Bulava*, *Koshovyi*, *Oboznyi*, *Universal*.
*   **Philosophical (Skovoroda)**: *Srodnist* (Affinity), *Sertse* (Heart), *Mir* (World/Peace).
