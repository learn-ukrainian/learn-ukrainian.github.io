## 1. Source Material
*   **The Vernacular Milestone**: *Peresopnytsia Gospel* (1556-1561).
    *   **URL**: `http://litopys.org.ua/peresop/peresop.htm` (Text & Analysis).
    *   **Significance**: First major canonical text in Old Ukrainian vernacular. Rich in synonyms and glosses.
*   **The Church Slavonic Standard**: *Ostrih Bible* (1581).
    *   **URL**: `http://litopys.org.ua/ostrob/ostrob.htm` (Approx / PDF refs).
    *   **Significance**: The "Prince of Books". Codification of Church Slavonic to compete with Latin/Polish Catholic texts.
*   **The Polemic Context**: *Polemical Literature* (Vishensky, Smotrytsky).
    *   **Significance**: The debate over *which* language is holy.

---

## 2. Linguistic Focus: The Great Divide
Phase 2 explores the tension between the "living" language (*Prosta Mova*) and the "sacred" language (*Church Slavonic*).

### Vernacular Features (Peresopnytsia)
*   **Glossing**: Using doublets to explain CS terms (e.g., *"arkhirei ili episkop"*).
*   **Dialectisms**: Volhynian/Polissian phonology (*i* < *o* in closed syllables).
*   **Polonisms**: Influence of Protestant Polish bibles (Szymon Budny).

### High Style (Ostrih)
*   **Archaization**: Deliberate return to Old Bulgarian forms to prove antiquity.
*   **Greek Syntax**: Calquing Greek sentence structures.
