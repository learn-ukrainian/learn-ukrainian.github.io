> **Policy**: All research conducted in Ukrainian. No Russian sources.
> **Context**: C1 Biography Track (Seminar Style). Focus on depth, historical accuracy, and authentic terminology.

## 23. Петро Могила (Petro Mohyla)

- **Dates**: 1596 (Sudava) – 1647 (Kyiv).
- **Roles**: Metropolitan of Kyiv, Halych, and All Rus' (1633-1647), Archimandrite of Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra (1627-1633).
- **Key Achievement**: Founder of Kyiv-Mohyla Academy (1632) - "Latin learning with Orthodox spirit".
- **Works**:
  - _Trembnyk_ (1646) - Codified rituals, included Ukrainian folk prayers.
  - _Lithos_ (1644) - Polemic defense of Orthodoxy.
  - _Confessio Orthodoxa_ (1640) - Catchism.
- **Restoration**: St. Sophia Cathedral, Tithe Church excavations (found Volodymyr the Great's relics).
- **Quotes/Context**:
  - "Vested in the dignity of the Metropolitan."
  - Westernization of Orthodoxy ("вчитися у ворога").
  - "Kyiv Athenaeum" (Mohylian Athenaeum) - circle of intellectuals.
  - Canonized 1996.

## 24. Іван Виговський (Ivan Vyhovskyi)

- **Dates**: ~1608 – 1664.
- **Roles**: General Scribe (1650-1657), Hetman (1657-1659).
- **Key Event**: **Battle of Konotop (1659)** - Decisive victory over Muscovite army.
- **Political Achievement**: **Treaty of Hadiach (1658)**.
  - Concept: "Grand Duchy of Rus'" as equal 3rd member of Commonwealth.
  - Rights: Kyiv-Mohyla "Academy" status, freedom of religion (Orthodox rights), cancellation of Union of Brest conditions (in theory).
  - Military: 30k Cossacks + 10k mercenaries.
- **Downfall**: Internal opposition (Pushkar, Barabash), Sirko's raid on Crimea (forced Tatar allies to leave), eventual abdication.

## 25. Іван Сірко (Ivan Sirko)

- **Dates**: ~1610 – 1680.
- **Roles**: Koshovyi Otaman of Zaporizhian Sich (elected ~15 times).
- **Specifics**:
  - Legendary unconquered warrior (65 battles won).
  - Famous Letter to Sultan Mehmed IV (1676) - basis for Repin's painting.
  - 1672: Elected Hetman (rivalry with Samoilovych), exiled to Tobolsk by Tsar. Returned 1673.
  - 1675: Winter raid on Crimea, destroyed Khan's army.
- **Character**: "Urns" (Sorcerer/Kharakternyk) legends. Fierce defender of Orthodoxy but politically unpredictable (fought against Vyhovskyi, against Poland, sometimes against Moscow).
- **Themes**: The Archetypal Zaporozhian, Military Genius, Folklore Hero.

## 26. Юрій Немирич (Yuriy Nemyrych)

- **Dates**: 1612 – 1659.
- **Roles**: Diplomat, Chancellor of the Grand Duchy of Rus', Arian (Protestant) noble turned Orthodox.
- **Education**: Leiden, Amsterdam, Oxford, Cambridge, Sorbonne. Intellectual powerhouse.
- **Ideology**: The "Brain" behind the Treaty of Hadiach. Vision of a federal Commonwealth.
- **Religion**: Originally Arian (Socinian), founded academy in Kysylyn. Converted to Orthodoxy 1657 to serve the Hetmanate.
- **Significance**: Represents the European intellectual elite in the Cossack state. Proof of high political culture.

## 27. Іван Мазепа (Ivan Mazepa)

- **Dates**: 1639 – 1709.
- **Roles**: Hetman (1687-1709) - 22 years (record duration).
- **Cultural Legacy**: **Mazepa Baroque** (architectural style).
  - Built/Restored: St. Sophia walls/bell tower, Lavra churches, Epiphany Cathedral in Podil, Chernihiv Collegium.
  - Donor: "Ktitor" of the Church.
- **Political Turn**: Alliance with Sweden (Charles XII) in 1708.
  - Motivation: Protect liberties of the Host against Peter I's centralization.
- **Battle of Poltava (1709)**: Tragic defeat. "Catastrophe."
- **Baturyn Tragedy (1708)**: Menshikov massacred the capital's population (15,000 dead).
- **Legacy**: Symbol of independence ("Mazepyntsi"). Anathema (political tool of ROC).

## 28. Данило Апостол (Danylo Apostol)

- **Dates**: 1654 – 1734.
- **Roles**: Hetman of Left-Bank Ukraine (1727-1734). "The Last Elective Hetman".
- **Context**: Post-Mazepa repression, abolition of Hetmanate (1st Little Russian Collegium).
- **Achievement**: **"Authoritative Points" (Reshytelni punkty)** - "Constitution" determining Hetmanate's status within Empire.
  - Restored Hetman's office (brief "thaw").
  - Reformed: Finances (budget), Judiciary (Code of Laws), Land ownership (stopped Russian land grabs).
  - Returned Zaporozhians (Oleshky Sich) to Russian citizenship (permission to return).
- **Character**: Veteran (lost an eye), cautious diplomat, focused on internal stability and economy.

## 29. Павло Полуботок (Pavlo Polubotok)

- **Dates**: ~1660 – 1724.
- **Roles**: Acting Hetman (Nakazny Hetman) (1722-1724).
- **Conflict**: Opposed the 1st Little Russian Collegium (Velyaminov). Defended Ukrainian autonomy.
- **Martyrdom**: Summoned to St. Petersburg, imprisoned in Peter and Paul Fortress. Died there.
  - Quote/Legend: Summoned Peter I to God's judgment.
- **Legend**: **The Treasure of Polubotok** (Gold in Bank of England).
  - Myth vs Reality: Symbol of "stolen heritage" and hope for future prosperity.
- **Significance**: Symbol of civic courage and resistance to tyranny without weapons (legal/political resistance).
