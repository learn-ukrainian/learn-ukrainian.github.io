## 2. Linguistic Focus

### A. Phonology (The "Great Shifts")
1. **The Fall of the Yers (ъ, ь):**
   - Weak yers dropping (e.g., *дьнь* -> *день*, *сънъ* -> *сон*).
   - Vocalization of strong yers.
   - Compensation: Lengthening of preceding o/e (leading to modern Ukrainian *i* in closed syllables).
2. **Pleophony (Повноголосся):**
   - The *TorT/TerT* -> *ToroT/TereT* shift (e.g., *градъ* (CS) vs. *городъ* (OES/Vernacular)).
3. **The Yat' (ѣ) Dilemma:**
   - Transition from the Proto-Slavic long [e:] to the Old Ukrainian [i] or [ie] (e.g., *вѣра* -> *віра*).
4. **Epenthetic 'l':**
   - *земля*, *купля* (as opposed to Western Slavic *ziemia*).

### B. Morphology & Syntax
1. **The Vocative Case:** Alive and well in graffiti (e.g., *Андрониче!*, *брате!*).
2. **The Dual Number:** Still active in the XI-XII centuries.
3. **Verb Tenses:** 
   - Survival of the **Aorist** in narratives.
   - Emergence of the **Perfect** (auxiliary *byti* + *-l* participle) as the dominant past tense.
4. **Business/Legal Formulae:** Found in Zvenyhorod letters ("Дай... оли нь водаси" - Give... if you don't give).

## 4. Specific Passages for Grammar Labs

### Lab 1: St. Sophia Graffiti (The Curse of Kuzma)
**Text:** «Коузьма тать. Вкралєси мясо. Асилонь твои кокашь. Аминь.»
- **Grammar Focus:**
  - *Тать* (Old word for thief).
  - *Вкрал єси* (Perfect tense: Participle + Auxiliary).
  - *Кокашь* (Regional/Dialectal word for legs/claws).
- **Activity Idea:** Identify the "insult" and explain the verb tense construction.

### Lab 2: Zvenyhorod Birch Bark Letter #1
**Text:** «от говѣновоє : ко Нѣжьньцю дає 60 дєсят коуно лодиєноую... оли нь водаси то я у конязя поєма отроко прижь приєдю...»
- **Grammar Focus:**
  - *От... ко...* (Prepositional structure for "From... to...").
  - *Лодиєноую* (Adjective derived from *лодія* - boat).
  - *Оли нь водаси* (Conditional "If not give").
- **Activity Idea:** Translate the demand and identify the "threat" (bringing the prince's official).

### Lab 3: Historical Record (1054)
**Text:** «Въ лѣто 6562 мѣсяца февраля 20 успѣ царь нашь...»
- **Grammar Focus:**
  - Dating systems (Years from the Creation).
  - Use of *успѣ* (Aorist).
  - Title *царь* (Applying high titles to Kyivan princes).
