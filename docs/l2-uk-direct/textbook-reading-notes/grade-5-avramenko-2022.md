## Key Finding

**Grade 5 is a consolidation and formalization year.** It reviews everything from Grades 1–4 and reintroduces it with proper linguistic terminology. Key for our curriculum: this is where euphony rules get their fullest treatment, where stress patterns are explicitly taught with word lists, and where syntax becomes formal (словосполучення, підмет/присудок, однорідні члени, складне речення, пряма мова). Maps to **B1 grammar** in our CEFR alignment.

## Stress (§38–39) — CRITICAL FOR PRONUNCIATION

### Stress Properties (p.88–92)

**Рухомий наголос** (movable stress) — Ukrainian stress can shift between forms:
- пíсня → пíсень (stays) BUT пíсня → піснé (shifts in some forms)
- ходжý → хóдиш (1st person vs 2nd person)

**Подвійний наголос** (double stress — both positions acceptable):
- áлфавíт, зáвждí, пóмилóк, дóговíр

**Stress word lists from textbook** (confirmed correct stress positions):
- Дрóва, вирáзно, сантимéтр, листопáд, предмéт, кілóметр
- ходжý, беремó, донестú, булá, живемó
- нóвий, óлень, пóдруга, посерéдині
- чорнóзем, вéрби, óбруч, фáховий, тóнкий

**Логічний наголос** (logical stress) — sentence-level emphasis that changes meaning:
- **Ми** ходили в музей (WE went, not others)
- Ми **ходили** в музей (we WENT, we didn't stay home)
- Ми ходили в **музей** (to the MUSEUM, not the cinema)

**Curriculum implication**: Stress word lists are directly usable for `watch_and_repeat` activities. Logical stress concept important for B1 intonation modules.

## Euphony (§51–52) — CRITICAL FOR A1 MODULE "eufoniia"

### у/в Alternation (p.117–119)

**Core rule**: Alternation prevents consonant clusters from being unpronounceable.

| Position | Use **у** | Use **в** |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Between consonants | хліб **у** полі | — |
| After consonant, before vowel | пішов **у** аптеку | — |
| Between vowels | — | була **в** аптеці |
| After vowel, before consonant | — | сиділа **в** кріслі |
| Start of sentence before consonant | **У** парку росли... | — |
| Start of sentence before vowel | — | **В** Одесі жив... |

**Exceptions/notes**:
- Proper names don't alternate: Україна (always У), Вінниця (always В)
- Some words are fixed: увага (always у-), вчитель (always в-)

### і/й Alternation (p.119–120)

| Position | Use **і** | Use **й** |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Between consonants | хліб **і** масло | — |
| After consonant, before vowel | — | вишні **й** абрикоси |
| Between vowels | — | мова **й** історія |
| Start of sentence | **І** небо, **і** земля | — |

### з/із/зі Preposition Alternation (p.120–121)

| Form | When to use | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **з** | Before vowels (mid-sentence) | вийшов **з** оселі |
| **із** | Between consonants; before з, ц, с, ж, ч, ш | зробив **із** соломи; **із** школи |
| **зі** | Before consonant clusters (зл-, зн-, сп-, шк-) | **зі** школи, **зі** сльозами |
| **зо** | Before два, три | **зо** два рази |

**Curriculum implication**: These euphony rules are the primary content for A1 module `eufoniia` (module 43). Tables can be directly adapted for pattern_drill activities.

## Syntax (§63–81) — SENTENCE STRUCTURE

### Словосполучення (Phrases, §63)

Formal definition: combination of two or more words connected by meaning and grammar.

**Three things that are NOT словосполучення:**
1. Підмет + присудок (subject + predicate) — these are рівноправні (equal)
2. Службове + самостійне слово (preposition + noun) — e.g., "в парку" is NOT a phrase
3. Рівноправні words connected by conjunction — e.g., "хліб і масло"

**Structure**: головне слово (main) → залежне слово (dependent)
- Question goes from main → dependent: дерево (яке?) → високе

### Речення (Sentence, §64–65)

**Grammatical basis** = підмет (ХТО? ЩО?) + присудок (ЩО РОБИТЬ? ЩО ЗРОБИТЬ?)
- Підмет: single underline (——)
- Присудок: double underline (====)

**Sentence types by structure:**
- Просте (simple) — one grammatical basis
- Складне (complex) — two or more grammatical bases, connected by comma or conjunction

### Однорідні члени речення (Homogeneous members, §71–74)

Members that answer the SAME question from the SAME word:
- "Дівчинка читала книжки, журнали **і** газети." (що? що? що?)

**Punctuation rules:**
- No comma before single і/та/й/або
- Comma before а, але, проте, зате, однак (always)
- Comma between items without conjunction

### Пряма мова (Direct speech, §79–81)

Four patterns:
1. А: «П.» — Author says: «Direct speech.»
2. «П,» — а. — «Direct speech,» — author says.
3. «П? — а.» — «Question?» — author says.
4. «П!» — а. — «Exclamation!» — author says.

### Essay (§53) — Есе

Introduced formally in Grade 5:
- 3–10 sentences
- Free form (вільна форма)
- Emotional, personal viewpoint
- Topic-based self-expression

**Curriculum implication**: Essay format validates our `essay` activity type for B1+.

## Implications for l2-uk-direct Curriculum

### B1 Curriculum (Grade 5–6 content)

#### Grammar Sequence for B1
Based on Avramenko Grade 5, the B1 grammar should include:
1. **Euphony rules** (у/в, і/й, з/із/зі) — formalized with tables
2. **Stress patterns** — movable, double, logical stress
3. **Sentence types** — simple vs complex, compound sentences
4. **Direct speech** — quotation patterns
5. **Homogeneous members** — listing, comma rules
6. **Word relationships** — synonyms, antonyms, homonyms, paronyms

#### Key Pedagogical Insights
1. **"Культура слова" boxes** — anti-суржик correction pairs are gold for learner activities
2. **Logical stress** concept — same sentence, different emphasis = different meaning
3. **Словосполучення analysis** — identifying main/dependent words, important for reading comprehension
4. **Essay format** introduced — validates B1 writing activities
5. **Back-matter dictionaries** — model for learner resource design

#### Activity Types Confirmed from Grade 5
1. **Observation exercises** → guided discovery activities
2. **"Культура слова"** → error correction / choose_correct activities
3. **Test (multiple choice)** → quiz activities
4. **Creative exercises** → essay/writing activities
5. **Anti-суржик pairs** → pattern_drill with correct/incorrect alternatives
