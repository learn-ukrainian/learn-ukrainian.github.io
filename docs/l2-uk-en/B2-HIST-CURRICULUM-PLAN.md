# B2-HIST Curriculum Plan: Ukrainian History Track

**Status:** ACTIVE (Expanded January 2026)
**Modules:** 01-140 (140 modules)
**Prerequisite:** B2 Core (M01-70)
**Vocabulary Target:** Content-driven (~24/module)
**Immersion:** 100% Ukrainian

> **Note:** This track was expanded from 61 to 140 modules to align with Ukrainian school curriculum (Issue #423).

---

## Track Overview

This expanded track tells the complete story of Ukraine from prehistoric times through the full-scale Russian invasion of 2022. Following Ukrainian educational standards, it provides learners with comprehensive historical literacy while building advanced vocabulary.

**Learning Goals:**
- Understand Ukrainian history as continuous national development
- Recognize patterns of imperial oppression and resistance
- Analyze primary sources and historical narratives
- Build historical vocabulary (~2,640 new terms across track)
- Understand regional diversity within Ukrainian identity

**Pedagogical Approach:** CBI (Content-Based Instruction) with narrative arcs

## Thematic Strands

Throughout the curriculum, several thematic strands recur:

### 1. Decolonization (throughout)
- M26: "Російські міфи про Україну" - explicit debunking
- Imperial narratives challenged in every phase
- Primary source analysis to counter Russian historiography

### 2. Regional Diversity (Phase 4, 7, 8)
- Bukovyna, Transcarpathia, Slobozhanshchyna coverage
- Southern Ukraine/Crimea history
- Regional identities within Ukrainian nation

### 3. Religious History (Phases 4, 8, 12)
- Orthodox Church splits
- Uniate/Greek-Catholic formation
- Tomos and modern church independence

### 4. Social/Economic History (throughout)
- Peasant life, serfdom
- Industrialization
- Economic transformations

### 5. Women in Ukrainian History (integrated)
- Lesya Ukrainka, Olha of Kyiv
- Rozaliya Zemliachka controversy
- Contemporary women's roles
