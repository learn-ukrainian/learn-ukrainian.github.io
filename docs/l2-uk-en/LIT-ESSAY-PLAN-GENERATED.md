## Track Overview

The intellectual history of Ukraine through essays, pamphlets, and critical thought — from 19th-century polemics to 21st-century decolonization theory. Documents three centuries of Ukrainian intellectual resistance to imperial erasure.

### Vocabulary Focus

- Political/philosophical terminology (суверенітет, самовизначення, ідентичність)
- Literary-critical vocabulary (деконструкція, наратив, дискурс)
- Historical terminology (колоніалізм, імперіалізм, русифікація)
- Academic register (тезис, аргументація, контр-аргумент)

### Grammar Focus

- Complex argumentation structures
- Academic Ukrainian register
- Rhetorical devices in Ukrainian essay tradition
- Archaic forms in 19th-century polemics

### Pedagogical Approach

**Approach:** seminar

**Structure:**
- Primary source excerpt with glossary
- Intellectual context: what question is the author answering?
- Argument analysis in Ukrainian
- Counter-arguments and debate
- Contemporary relevance

**Decolonization:**
- Frame Ukrainian thought as independent intellectual tradition, not 'response to Russia'
- Highlight how each thinker advanced Ukrainian self-understanding
- Show continuity from Kostomarov to Zabuzhko — the unbroken thread
- Critical analysis of ALL ideologies, including Ukrainian nationalism (Dontsov)
