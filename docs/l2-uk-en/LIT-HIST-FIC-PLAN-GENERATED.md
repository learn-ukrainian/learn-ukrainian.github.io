## Track Overview

Epic Ukrainian historical fiction — novels that shaped how Ukrainians understand their own history, from Kozak glory to insurgent resistance. Ukrainian historical fiction was often the ONLY way to tell Ukrainian history when academic history was banned or distorted.

### Vocabulary Focus

- Historical vocabulary (гетьман, козак, яничар, повстанець)
- Archaic forms preserved in historical prose
- Military/political terminology
- Regional/dialectal vocabulary used for authenticity

### Grammar Focus

- Historical narrative register
- Archaic grammatical constructions in historical prose
- Political/military vocabulary in fiction context
- Cross-period stylistic comparison

### Pedagogical Approach

**Approach:** seminar

**Structure:**
- Novel excerpt with glossary and historical context
- Literary analysis: how does fiction shape historical understanding?
- Comparison with actual historical events (cross-reference B2-HIST)
- Discussion: fiction as national memory tool

**Decolonization:**
- Historical fiction was resistance — writing Ukrainian history in Ukrainian when it was forbidden
- Zahrebelny wrote within Soviet system but encoded Ukrainian identity
- Shklyar openly celebrated UPA — post-independence freedom
- Always identify what imperial myth each novel challenges
