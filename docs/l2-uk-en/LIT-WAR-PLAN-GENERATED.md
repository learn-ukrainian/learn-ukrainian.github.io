## Track Overview

Ukrainian war literature from 2014 to present — poetry, prose, testimony, and journalism documenting resistance, displacement, and resilience. These are not 'conflict narratives' — they are testimony of a nation fighting for existence.

### Vocabulary Focus

- Military vocabulary (окоп, обстріл, евакуація, зброя)
- Displacement terminology (переселенець, тимчасова окупація, вимушений переїзд)
- Emotional/trauma vocabulary (ПТСР, стійкість, горювання, надія)
- Journalistic register (свідчення, репортаж, хроніка)

### Grammar Focus

- Documentary prose style
- Fragmented/stream-of-consciousness narrative (trauma writing)
- Poetry of urgency — compressed syntax
- Testimonial voice — first person witness

### Pedagogical Approach

**Approach:** seminar

**Structure:**
- Primary text excerpt (poem, prose fragment, diary entry)
- Historical context: what was happening when this was written?
- Literary analysis: how does form serve the content?
- Biographical context of the author (many are soldiers, volunteers, captives)
- Discussion: what does this text tell us about Ukraine today?

**Decolonization:**
- This is NOT 'conflict literature' — it is RESISTANCE literature
- Authors are not victims — they are agents of their nation's survival
- Never frame as 'tragedy on both sides'
- Fallen authors (Амеліна, Вакуленко, Коцюбайло) treated with dignity, not sentimentality

**Sensitivity:**
- Content includes graphic war descriptions — pedagogical framing required
- Trauma-informed approach: acknowledge emotional weight
- Focus on resilience and agency, not helplessness
