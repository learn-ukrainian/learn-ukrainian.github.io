## Track Overview

Ukrainian children's and young adult literature — from Franko's Fox Mykyta to the modern A-BA-BA-HA-LA-MA-HA revolution. The books that shape how Ukrainian children see their nation. For decades, Ukrainian children read mostly in Russian. The post-independence explosion of Ukrainian children's literature is a cultural miracle — and this track documents it.

### Vocabulary Focus

- Children's vocabulary (казка, пригоди, дружба, сміливість)
- Fantasy/adventure terminology
- School/youth vocabulary
- Folklore vocabulary (лісовик, відьма, козак)

### Grammar Focus

- Simplified literary register (accessible to children but rich for analysis)
- Folklore narrative patterns
- Youth slang and informal register in YA literature
- Pedagogical language analysis

### Pedagogical Approach

**Approach:** seminar

**Structure:**
- Text excerpt with glossary (accessible excerpts)
- Literary analysis: how does this work build Ukrainian identity in children?
- Publishing context: who made these books possible?
- Discussion: childhood reading as nation-building

**Decolonization:**
- For decades, Russian-language books dominated Ukrainian bookstores
- Nestayko, Malkovych, and modern authors are cultural warriors
- A-BA-BA-HA-LA-MA-HA proved Ukrainian children's books can be world-class
- Every Ukrainian child who reads in Ukrainian is a victory

**Practical Value:**
- Learners raising bilingual children will find direct pedagogical resources
- Simplified language makes these texts accessible reading practice
- Cultural fluency: knowing Nestayko is like knowing Roald Dahl for English speakers
