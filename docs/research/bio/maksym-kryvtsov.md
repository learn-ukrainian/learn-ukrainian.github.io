## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Максим Олександрович Кривцов. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Кривцов Максим Олександрович"; T2: Платформа пам'яті «Меморіал»]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** call sign **«Далі»** (from the artist Salvador Dalí — fitting for the poet-soldier; obituaries punned "Не Сальвадор, але митець"). [T3: uk.wikipedia; T5: Новинарня, 8.01.2024]
- **Born:** **22 січня 1990** | місто **Рівне** | Українська РСР, СРСР. Father a press vendor, mother a school librarian. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "22 січня 1990, м. Рівне"]
- **Died:** **7 січня 2024** (aged 33) | **Харківська область** | Україна | **killed in combat** during Russia's full-scale invasion (his red cat, subject of one of his poems, was killed with him). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Життєпис"; T2: «Меморіал»]
- **Family / education key facts:** Studied at Rivne schools № 23 and № 28; graduated a Rivne technical college and the **Kyiv National University of Technologies and Design** (2014, footwear/leather design). Worked at a Rivne non-woven-textile factory, then as photographer and SMM-manager for the «Строкаті єноти» project. Buried **12 January 2024** in Rivne, Alley of Heroes of the «Нове» («Молодіжне») cemetery. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Життєпис"]

**Source agreement note:** birth/death dates are consistent across T1 (Presidential decrees), T2 (Меморіал, PEN Ukraine) and T3 (uk.wiki). His patronymic is **Олександрович** (not "Владиславович," which appeared in loose references). The earlier ghost-wiki omitted both his dates and his unit; this dossier closes that gap.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

A war-dead dossier; the mechanism is **death in Russia's war of aggression**, stated factually.

- **What happened:** killed in action in **Харківська область** as a machine-gunner of the Special Operations Forces (ССО). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **When (specific):** **7 January 2024.** [T1: Орден «За заслуги» III ст., 22.01.2024, posthumous; Хрест Заслуги, 17.01.2024, posthumous; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **By whom (specific RF action):** the **Russian Armed Forces** during the **повномасштабне вторгнення** (24 February 2022 onward). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Mechanism specifics (a decade under that war):** Kryvtsov was a **Revolution of Dignity** participant and, from 2014, a volunteer — in the **5th battalion of the ДУК «Правий сектор»** (2014–2015), then a senior machine-gunner in the **Бригада швидкого реагування НГУ** (2016–2019). After demobilisation he worked at the «ЯРМІЗ» veterans' readaptation centre and as a copywriter at **Veteran Hub**. He rejoined the ЗСУ at the start of the full-scale invasion, serving as a machine-gunner in the ССО across the Kyiv, Kharkiv, Kherson, Luhansk and Donetsk fronts before being killed in January 2024. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Життєпис"]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** his poetry survives and grew in reach after his death — the collection **«Вірші з бійниці»** (2023), its 2025 Portuguese translation (Brazil), posthumous photo-exhibitions («Далі: я поверну собі своє життя», Rivne 2024, Kharkiv 2025), songs set to his verse (Yurcash, Vivienne Mort, Adm:t), the **Уласа-Самчука** literary prize (2024, posthumous), and the **Фонд імені Максима Кривцова**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Творчість," §"Вшанування пам'яті"]

## 3. Major works

A poet, photographer and filmmaker-operator; selected:

- `2015` — camera operator on two short films, **«Перемир'я»** and **«Буду повертатись»**, shot in Піски (Donetsk oblast). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Творчість"]
- `pre-2023` — published in anthologies: *Книга Love 2.0. Любов і війна*; *Там, де вдома: 112 віршів про любов та війну*; *Колискова 21-го століття*. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `2023` — debut collection **«Вірші з бійниці»** (вид. «Наш Формат») — named **one of the best Ukrainian books of 2023 by PEN Ukraine**; translated into Portuguese (Brazil, 2025). [T2: PEN Ukraine; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `2023` — appeared in Roman Liubyi's documentary **«Зошит війни»**, reading his own verse before a firing aperture (бійниця). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `2024` (posthumous) — the photo-exhibition **«Далі: я поверну собі своє життя»** (24 photographs + poems). [T3: uk.wikipedia]

**Note:** the collection's title — *Poems from the firing aperture* (бійниця) — locates the writing literally at the front line; the poems are war-written, not war-recollected.

## 4. Primary-source quotes (≥2 required)

> Kryvtsov **is** a literary author, but too **recent** to be in the project's literary corpus — a `verify_quote` against «Вірші з бійниці» would return no match because the corpus does not yet index it, not because the text is unattested. The poem below is cited to the collection and to its wide reproduction in the Ukrainian press; flagged "за вид. «Вірші з бійниці», 2023; не в корпусі проєкту."

**Quote 1 — his most-shared poem, posted days before his death (the prophetic «бійниця» poem):**

> «Моя голова котиться / від посадки до посадки / як перекотиполе / чи мʼяч / мої руки відірвані / проростуть фіалками навесні / […] / мій прострелений автомат / заржавіє / бідненький / мої змінні речі та екіпу / передадуть новобранцям / та скоріше б уже весна / щоб нарешті / розквітнути / фіалкою»

He posted this poem on social media **a few days before he was killed on 7 January 2024** — a fact widely reported (Главком, 8.01.2024). It is the emblematic text of war poetry in the curriculum: the soldier imagines his own dismembered body returning to the earth and *blooming as a violet*. Pedagogically it pairs unbearable literal detail with botanical rebirth — the core move of the 2022– war-poetry generation. [Вид. «Вірші з бійниці», 2023; transmitted T5: Главком, 8.01.2024 / Збруч «Поезія полеглих»; не в корпусі проєкту]

**Quote 2 — recorded interview speech (Читомо, 19 April 2023):**

> «Я мав жахливу мрію — ходити по Києву з автоматом.»

The bitter paradox of the soldier-civilian: the "terrible dream" of carrying a rifle through a peaceful capital, i.e. of war reaching home. Quoted as a dated, named-outlet interview line. [T5: Читомо — Н. Корнієнко, інтерв'ю з М. Кривцовим, 19.04.2023; recorded speech]

**Honest note (§4 + §10):** the poem is reproduced from its wide, consistent press reproduction (Главком; Збруч; Elle.ua; nashformat e-book listing) rather than from a directly-opened copy of the printed collection; the omitted middle lines are marked «[…]» — a verbatim excerpt, not a paraphrase.

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** **contemporary free-verse** Ukrainian — colloquial, unpunctuated/loosely-punctuated, imagistic, mixing front-line concretes (автомат, бійниця, екіпа, новобранці) with lyric nature imagery (фіалка, калина, перекотиполе) and global pop reference (a "новий червоний Pantone").
- **CEFR readiness:** his *poetry* is paradoxically approachable — high-frequency vocabulary, short lines — but emotionally heavy; **B2** for guided reading, **C1** for full analysis; the *biography* is tellable at B1.
- **Lexicon notes (pre-teach, all VESUM-verified FOUND):** бійниця, перекотиполе, кулеметник (machine-gunner), доброволець, фіалка. Gloss «ССО» (Сили спеціальних операцій), «екіпа», «новобранець», and the call-sign «Далі».
- **Stylistic features:** anaphora («мої руки… / мої ноги… / моя кров… / мої кістки…»), the inventory-of-the-body device, botanical resurrection imagery, deliberate flatness undercut by tenderness («бідненький» of the rusting rifle).

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated:** almost nothing of his *biography* (recent, well-documented). The interpretive question is **literary**: how to read the war-poetry generation — as testimony, as therapy, as canon-in-the-making. PEN Ukraine's 2023 recognition is the institutional argument for canon; the curriculum should present that as a live, ongoing critical conversation rather than a settled verdict.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** the prophetic-poem framing ("he foretold his own death") can **eclipse the craft** — the poems are deliberate art, not omens. Resist the supernatural reading; teach the imagery.
- **Where Russian disinformation intersects:** his early service in the **ДУК «Правий сектор»** is the kind of detail Russian propaganda inflates into "Nazi" framing; name it as propaganda and present his actual record (volunteer 2014, NGU, ССО, ЗСУ).
- **Other-perspective considerations:** Kryvtsov belongs to a **cohort of killed Ukrainian writers** (Володимир Вакуленко, Вікторія Амеліна, Максим Кривцов) whose deaths are themselves a cultural-erasure dimension of the war — present him within that pattern, which is the LIT strand's «war-literature-as-movement» concern.
