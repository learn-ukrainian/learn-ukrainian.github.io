> **FACTUAL CORRECTION (load-bearing).** The project plan recorded Lypa's birthplace as "Poltava region." **This is wrong.** Ivan Lypa was born in **Керч (Kerch), Crimea**, on **24 February (8 March) 1865**. The "Poltava" association is real but belongs to his **mother's lineage** — Ганна Михайлівна Житецька came from **Poltava-region Cossacks who had supported Mazepa** — and to his later years as a zemstvo doctor near Poltava (Мачухи). The plan must be corrected: **born Kerch, not Poltava region.**

## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Іван Львович Липа. [T1: ЕІУ — Лавров Ю. П., "Липа Іван Львович," т. 6, с. 145; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** literary pen-names **Петро Шелест**, **Іван Степовик**. Russified form forbidden in body text (§8): "Иван Львович Липа." [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Born:** **24 лютого (8 березня н. ст.) 1865** | **Керч** | Таврійська губернія, Російська імперія (нині Крим, Україна — тимчасово окупований РФ). Son of a town-міщани family; father **Лев Васильович**, a retired Russian-army soldier; mother **Ганна Михайлівна Житецька**, from **Poltava-region Cossacks who supported Mazepa**. [T1: ЕІУ; T3: uk.wikipedia — "24 лютого (8 березня), Керч"]
- **Died:** **13 листопада 1923** (aged 58) | **Винники** (під Львовом) | Західна Україна (тоді під Польщею) | **stomach cancer** (ill from May 1919); buried in Vynnyky **15 November 1923**. The Orthodox Lypa was buried — with Metropolitan **Андрей Шептицький's** permission — by the Greek-Catholic dean о. Григорій Гірняк. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Family / education key facts:** Church-parish school at the Greek Іоано-Предтеченська church in Kerch, then the **Олександрівська чоловіча гімназія** in Kerch (1878–1887, graduated brilliantly). Read medicine at **Харківський університет** (from 1888); after arrest and expulsion (1893) resumed and graduated at **Казанський університет** (1896–1897). Worked as a zemstvo physician (Липецьке; **Мачухи** near Poltava, 1898–1902; **Дальник** near Odesa, where he built a hospital for the poor, 1904–05). Married the village teacher **Марія Шепель-Шепеленко** (1904). **Father of Юрій Липа**, poet and ideologue (murdered by the NKVD, 1944). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Життєпис"]

**Source agreement note:** ЕІУ, the Кубійович ЕУ, and uk.wiki agree on **Kerch** as birthplace and **24.02 (8.03).1865** as the date — confirming the plan's "Poltava region" is an **error to correct**. Death date **13.11.1923** in **Винники** is consistent across the same sources.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

Lypa is a **statehood-era patriot oppressed first by the Russian Empire, then driven into exile by the Bolshevik conquest of Ukraine** — and harassed in exile by Polish authority. The mechanism is documented across all three.

- **Tsarist political repression (direct, dated):** In **1891** Lypa co-founded the secret **«Братство тарасівців»** (with **Борис Грінченко** and **Микола Міхновський**) to spread Shevchenko's ideas and the cause of Ukrainian national liberation. In **1893** he was **arrested for narodnyk and pro-Ukrainian activity and expelled from Kharkiv University**; he served **13 months' imprisonment** and then lived **three more years under police surveillance in Kerch** before being allowed to resume study (at Kazan). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Життєпис"; T1: ЕІУ]
- **Forced emigration (the Bolshevik conquest):** as a minister of the **УНР** (§3), Lypa shared the fate of the Ukrainian state it served. With the УНР's defeat he went into emigration — minister of health in the **УНР government-in-exile at Tarnów (Poland)** — and in 1922 settled in Galicia (Lviv, then **Винники**). He could not return to Soviet-occupied Naddniprianshchyna; his wife Марія remained in Odesa, his son Юрій studied in Poznań — the family was scattered by the Soviet conquest. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Polish-authority harassment (exile, indirect):** in Vynnyky the Orthodox Ukrainian ex-minister practised medicine **without a permit** and was **persecuted by the Polish police** for it; he earned little and lived very modestly. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Життєпис"]
- **Mechanism specifics (the family arc):** the Soviet mechanism that scattered Ivan Lypa later **killed his son**: **Юрій Липа was murdered by the NKVD in 1944**. The father's exile and the son's execution together trace the Soviet erasure of the УНР generation. [T3: uk.wikipedia — cross-ref bio plan `yurii-lypa`]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** his writings survive (the fairy-tale and parable collections, the novels written in Vynnyky, his letters — edited by **І. Стамбол**, *Листування Івана Липи*, Темпора, 2020); the УНР state he served was destroyed by the Bolsheviks, and the Soviet regime then erased the memory of the УНР ministries he ran.

## 3. Major works

A physician who was also a writer and a statesman; selected, chronological:

- `1891` — co-founder of the **«Братство тарасівців»** (secret society; with Грінченко and Міхновський) — his formative civic act. [T1: ЕІУ; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1905` — published the almanac **«Багаття»** (Odesa, with his wife); founded the **«Одеська літературна спілка»**; published widely in «Діло», «ЛНВ», «Українська Хата», «Зоря». [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1917` — **Ukrainian commissar of Odesa**; founded the publishing house **«Народній Стяг»**; issued the fairy-tale cycle **«Казки про волю»** (Odesa, 1917). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1919` — **міністр віросповідань (культів) УНР**; on **25 January 1919** issued the circular obliging all church record-keeping (especially metrical books) to be conducted **in Ukrainian**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Життєпис"]
- `1920` — member of the commission drafting the **Constitution of the УНР**; minister of health in the УНР government-in-exile (Tarnów); founder of the charitable **«Блакитний Хрест»**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1922–1923` — wrote the novellas **«Кара»** and **«Утома»** in Vynnyky; the parable collection **«Тринадцять притч»** (developed over a quarter-century); posthumous **«Оповіді про смерть, війну і любов»** (1935). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Творчий доробок"; T5: вид. «Каменяр»]
- `posthumous` — his **«Листування Івана Липи 1892–1922»** (ed. І. Стамбол, Темпора, 2020) — the documentary corpus. [T2: Стамбол]

The two below are from documented biographical sources and flagged accordingly.

**Quote 1 — his statehood credo (memoir-transmitted by Іван Огієнко, *Наша культура*, 1937):**

> «Наш державний здвиг невгасимим вогнем запалить усі живі українські душі, і свого часу таки принесе відповідний плід. Помремо ми, але святий вогонь, що його ми сміливо запалили, уже ніколи не погасне. Це те, що переживе нас і створить найрозкішніші легенди в Україні…»

Огієнко, who lived near the lonely Lypa in Vynnyky, recorded this in his memoir. Pedagogically it is the figure's central statement — the УНР's defeat reframed as a fire that outlives its bearers; it pairs the statesman's optimism with the exile's resignation. **Memoir-transmitted** (Огієнко's account), flagged as such, not as an autograph. [T3: uk.wikipedia citing Огієнко, *Наша культура*, 1937; не в корпусі проєкту]

**Quote 2 — his own diary, the premonition of death (his documentary voice):**

> «Чогось мені здається, що тринадцята дата буде щастити мені і що саме в цю дату я маю вмерти.»

A late diary entry; Lypa died on **13 November 1923**, as the entry foretold. A human, intimate counter-weight to the public credo of Quote 1. Transmitted via his щоденник as reproduced in the biographical literature (вид. «Каменяр»; Стамбол). [T5: щоденник Івана Липи, за вид. «Каменяр»; не в корпусі проєкту]

**Documented administrative voice (referenced, not quoted verbatim):** the **circular of 25 January 1919** as minister of cults — obliging Ukrainian-language church record-keeping — is a real, dated document of his ministry; its exact wording was not captured from a Tier 1/2 transcription this pass, so it is described (with date) rather than quoted, to avoid fabricating text. [T3: uk.wikipedia]

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** turn-of-the-century **literary-publicistic** Ukrainian — symbolist-tinged prose (fairy tales, parables, novellas), civic journalism, and the spare administrative Ukrainian of his ministry. His son Юрій described the Vynnyky novellas as woven "ніби блакитним цвітом" (as if from blue blossom).
- **CEFR readiness:** his *fairy tales / parables* are approachable narrative prose at **B1–B2** (the «Казки про волю» were written for a broad readership); his *publicism and the statehood credo* sit at **C1**; the biography is tellable at B1.
- **Lexicon notes (pre-teach, all VESUM-verified FOUND):** тарасівці, віросповідання, притча, новела. Gloss «Братство тарасівців», «Директорія/УНР», «комісар», «блакитний хрест» as proper/period terms; the symbolist «блакитний цвіт» as a stylistic marker.
- **Stylistic features:** allegory and parable (the «Тринадцять притч» turn life-conflicts into moral tales), a symbolist colour-palette, the fusion of the physician's plainness with the patriot's exhortation.

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated:** little of his *biography* is disputed; the genealogical hint that **Григорій Геращенко** (an adopted son) may have been Lypa's biological son is flagged on uk.wiki as a **supposition** («існує припущення»), and is treated here as unproven, not as fact.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** Ivan Lypa is often **eclipsed by his son Юрій** (the better-known ideologue/poet). The corrective is to present the father in his own right — Bratstvo-tarasivtsiv co-founder, twice a UNR minister, physician-philanthropist — not merely as "the father of Yurii Lypa."
- **Where the record was distorted:** the **birthplace error** itself (the plan's "Poltava region") is a small case of how Soviet-era and careless secondary framing displaced a **Crimean-born** Ukrainian patriot from his actual origin — restoring **Kerch** is, in a modest way, a decolonial correction (Crimea as Ukrainian space).
- **Other-perspective considerations:** Lypa's Orthodox-buried-by-a-Greek-Catholic-priest funeral (with Sheptytsky's blessing) is a small emblem of the **cross-confessional Ukrainian national project** in Galicia — worth presenting as the inter-church cooperation it was.
