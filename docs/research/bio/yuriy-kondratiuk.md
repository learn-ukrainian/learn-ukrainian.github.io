## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical — life-name vs. birth-name):** known to history as **Юрій Васильович Кондратюк**; born **Олександр Гнатович Шаргей**. The dual identity is the load-bearing biographical fact (see §2). [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Ю́рій Васи́льович Кондратю́к (справжнє ім'я Шаргей Олександр Гнатович)"; cross-checked against ЕІУ т.5 с.20]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** the assumed legal identity "Юрій Васильович Кондратюк" itself functioned as a life-long alias. Russian-imperial / Russified forms (forbidden in body text, listed only in §8): Юрий Васильевич Кондратюк, Aleksandr Shargei, Yuri Kondratyuk.
- **Born:** 9 (21) June 1897 | Полтава | Російська імперія (Полтавщина). [T3: uk.wikipedia — "нар. 9 (21) червня 1897, Полтава"; ЕІУ т.5 с.20]
- **Died:** **23 February 1942** (aged 44) | near the town **Кіров, Калузька область** (Russia) | died at the front in the Second World War, having volunteered for the народне ополчення (people's militia); buried in a mass grave on the edge of Kirov. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "загинув 23 лютого 1942 року під містом Кіровим Калузької області"; ЕІУ т.5 с.20]
- **Family / education key facts:** mother **Людмила Львівна Шаргей** (née baroness of the **Шліпенбах/Schlippenbach** line — Swedish forebears who stayed in the empire after the Battle of Poltava), a French teacher who spent years in a psychiatric hospital; father **Ігнатій Бенедиктович Шаргей**, a Jewish convert to Catholicism and perpetual student. Raised in Poltava by his grandmother **Катерина Кирилівна** and step-grandfather **Яким Микитович Даценко** (a zemstvo doctor moving in the Ukrainian-intelligentsia circle of Панас Мирний and Володимир Короленко). Graduated the Second Poltava men's gymnasium with a silver medal (1916); briefly at the Petrograd Polytechnic (Sept 1916) before conscription. **Despite Swedish-Jewish ancestry he recorded himself in documents — even in the repressive 1930s — as «українець», native language Ukrainian.** [T3: uk.wikipedia — Життєпис]

**⚠ DATE-CONFLATION CORRECTION (flagged, not smoothed):** the **death year is 1942, not 1941.** The Poltava memorial plaque (and much older literature) gives **1941** (the long-held belief that he fell on **3 October 1941**); but a **signature on a payroll document dated January 1942** proves he was alive after that date, and the now-standard version places his death on **23 February 1942** near Kirov. Two minority versions exist — that he died in 1952 in the USA (John Houbolt's reported claim) or in a camp (argued from a Kondratiuk manuscript notebook reportedly found at Peenemünde) — but the **23 February 1942** front-line death is the mainstream Ukrainian scholarly position. The dossier uses **1942** and records the 1941 plaque as a documented error. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Довгий час уважалося, що… загинув 3 жовтня 1941… його підпис у відомості, датований січнем 1942 року. Тому поширеною стала версія, що вчений загинув 23 лютого 1942 року"]

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section. Kondratiuk's repression is unusual: the threat shaped his **entire identity**, not just a single arrest.

**(a) The assumed identity (1919) — pre-emptive flight from Bolshevik repression.** Олександр Шаргей had been an Imperial-army ensign (прапорщик, commissioned 29 March 1917 from the Tiflis military school), fought against the Ottomans in the South Caucasus (1917–18), and was then swept — by mobilisation, the sources stress, more than by conviction — into the anti-Bolshevik Добровольча армія as a machine-gun platoon commander. When he ended up near Kyiv in 1919, **fearing repression for his former-officer past**, he obtained — with the help of his stepmother **Олена Петрівна Карєєва** — papers in the name of **Юрій Васильович Кондратюк** (a real man who had died), and lived under that name to the end of his life. His genuine scientific career was therefore conducted under a false identity dictated by terror. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Побоюючись репресій за своє офіцерське минуле… роздобув документи на ім'я Юрія Васильовича Кондратюка"]

**(b) The 1930 "шкідництво" conviction and the ОДПУ design bureau.** In **1930** Kondratiuk was **convicted on a fabricated charge of "шкідництво" (wrecking/sabotage)** and made to work as an engineer-designer in a **design bureau of the ОДПУ (OGPU)** until 1932 — the prison-R&D pattern (a "шарашка") that also caught Korolyov. The conviction was false; the labour was coerced. His big 1932–33 Crimean wind-power-station project (12,000 kW) was conducted in this shadow. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "1930 року засуджений за неправдивим звинуваченням у шкідництві, працював у конструкторському бюро ОДПУ"]

**(c) Erasure of authorship.** Kondratiuk self-published his theory at his own expense (Novosibirsk, 1929); his name — both the real one and the assumed one — was, in the words of the §4 narrator, "equally forgotten." His foundational ideas re-surfaced in world history only obliquely: the **lunar-orbit-rendezvous (LOR) trajectory** at the heart of NASA's Apollo programme (1969) came, via John Houbolt's group, from Kondratiuk's 1929 book — yet for decades the Soviet system did not credit the convicted "wrecker" living under a dead man's name. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Внесок у науку]

**What survived:** the 1929 book and its trajectory math survived and shaped the Moon landing; the **man** was buried in a Russian mass grave under a false name, his authorship recovered only late and partially. The decolonial point (see §6) is that both the Soviet and the later Russian "Новосибірський учений" framings detach a **Poltava-born, self-declared-Ukrainian** theorist from Ukraine.

## 3. Major works

- `1916` — completed a Poltava–Petrograd manuscript notebook on the theory of cosmonautics (the earliest draft of his system). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1925` — sent the manuscript **«Про міжпланетні подорожі»** to Holovnauka (Moscow); professor Ветчинкін called it "the most complete study of interplanetary travel written so far." [T3]
- `1929` — **«Завоювання міжпланетних просторів»** (*The Conquest of Interplanetary Spaces*), Novosibirsk, self-published. His major work: derives the basic rocket-flight equation, lays out **multistage-rocket theory** (he was first to formulate it), proposes metal/non-metal + hydrogen-compound propellants, intermediate orbital/interplanetary bases, atmospheric braking on return, solar power for spacecraft, and the use of a body's gravity field — and the **lunar-orbit-rendezvous** scheme later used by Apollo ("траса Кондратюка"). [T3: uk.wikipedia — Внесок у науку]
- `1932–1934` — design of a **12,000-kW Crimean wind power station** (Novosibirsk, then Kharkiv) — far beyond contemporary foreign analogues. [T3]
- `1939` — headed the wind-power design-experimental establishment in Moscow. [T3]

## 6. Contested points

- **Identity, claimed by three states.** Russia frames him as a "Новосибірський учений"; the joint Ukraine–Russia commemorative medal blurs the attribution. The honest Ukrainian position: **Poltava-born, raised in the Ukrainian intelligentsia, self-declared Ukrainian with Ukrainian as native language**, of mixed Swedish-Jewish ancestry. Decolonisation restores the Poltava/Ukrainian facts without erasing the genuinely multi-ethnic heritage. [T3: uk.wikipedia — lead, Ушанування]
- **Date of death (see §1).** 1942 (mainstream) vs. the 1941 plaque (documented error) vs. minority 1952-USA / camp versions. Presented as open at the margins, firm at the centre (1942, front line, mass grave near Kirov).
- **Scale of the Apollo debt.** Popular accounts (the *Life* magazine pieces, the 2020 *Cosmos: Possible Worlds* episode narrated by Neil deGrasse Tyson) credit "траса Кондратюка" as making the Moon landing possible; serious historians agree LOR was decisive and that Houbolt's group drew on Kondratiuk, while cautioning against the romantic "lone-genius handed NASA the answer" simplification. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Внесок у науку, Ушанування]
- **What popular memory simplifies.** The dual identity is often told as adventure; the dossier keeps it as what it was — **a man forced to erase his own name to survive a state that hunted former officers.**
