## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Климент Васильович Квітка. ЕСУ uses this form; ЕІУ gives Климентій Васильович in the article headword. Use Климент Васильович Квітка in curriculum metadata and note `Климентій` as an alias. [T1: ЕСУ; T1: ЕІУ; T1: IEU]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** К., К. К., Кл. Кв., Тиміш Борейко, Юхим Бойко; Климентій Квітка; Klyment Kvitka; Klyment Vasylovych Kvitka. Russified forms are listed only in §8. [T1: ЕСУ]
- **Born:** 4 February 1880 New Style / 23 January 1880 Old Style | село Хмелів, then Роменський повіт, Полтавська губернія | now Хмелів, Роменський район, Сумська область, Україна. ЕСУ and ЕІУ give the Old/New Style date; IEU and УІНП give 4 February 1880. A modern article notes uncertainty around whether Хмелів / Басівка sometimes reflects parish or baptismal registration rather than exact birth site; do not turn that lead into a classroom fact without archival checking. [T1: ЕСУ; T1: ЕІУ; T1: IEU; T2: УІНП; T4: Demydenko]
- **Died:** 19 September 1953 | Москва | Soviet Union; buried at Ваганьківське кладовище according to УІНП. ЕСУ, ЕІУ, IEU, and УІНП agree on the date and city. [T1: ЕСУ; T1: ЕІУ; T1: IEU; T2: УІНП]
- **Family / education key facts:** His father died early; several accounts describe his upbringing in the Karpov household in Kyiv. He studied at the Kyiv music school in 1895-1896 and graduated from the law faculty of Saint Volodymyr University in Kyiv in 1902. He worked in judicial service in Tbilisi, Kyiv, Crimea, the Caucasus, and Chyhyryn before shifting into musical ethnography. He married Лариса Косач / Леся Українка in Kyiv on 7 August / 25 July 1907. [T1: ЕСУ; T1: ЕІУ; T2: УІНП; T4: Demydenko]

**Source disagreement / naming note:** The curriculum should avoid making `Чоловік Лесі Українки` the identity lead. It is a verified relationship, but the dossier's main arc is Kvitka's legal, fieldwork, archival, methodological, and repression history. ЕІУ's `Климентій` is useful for search/aliases; ЕСУ's `Климент` fits the project slug.

## 2. Oppression / pressure mechanism

- **What happened:** Soviet surveillance, arrests/repression, forced professional displacement from Kyiv to Moscow, exile/settlement in Kazakhstan, and long suppression/delay of his Ukrainian ethnomusicological corpus.
- **When:** ЕСУ states that during the Спілка визволення України process he was under NKVS surveillance, that in 1933 he was transferred to Moscow, and that in 1935-1937 he was on settlement in Kazakhstan. IEU summarizes 1933 arrest, forced move to Moscow, and exile to Karaganda and Alma-Ata. УІНП and Demydenko give a more detailed sequence: first arrest in 1933 with roughly one and a half months of imprisonment, a second arrest in early 1934 in the so-called "case of the Slavists," imprisonment in KarLag, and early release in 1936. [T1: ЕСУ; T1: IEU; T2: УІНП; T4: Demydenko]
- **By whom:** Soviet security and judicial-administrative bodies. This pass did not locate a case-file number or a stable archival citation identifying the exact investigative unit. Use "Soviet security organs / NKVS-NKVD apparatus" in lesson prose, not a more specific agency claim unless a case file is verified.
- **Document references:** No classroom-ready archival case number was found. The dossier can cite encyclopedia and institutional-memory accounts of surveillance, arrest, settlement, and KarLag, but must not invent a file number, indictment wording, or rehabilitation document.
- **Mechanism specifics:** The repression was tied to Kvitka's Ukrainian institutional profile: UPR legal service, cooperation with Ukrainian scholarly networks, VUAN musical-ethnography infrastructure, and source work on Ukrainian folk music. Soviet accusation language such as "bourgeois nationalists" appears in secondary accounts as the regime's smear category; it is evidence of Soviet framing, not a valid analytical term. After the pressure cycle of 1933-1936, Kvitka continued work at the Moscow Conservatory and founded/led Soviet institutional structures there, but the move should be taught as constrained survival after repression, not as a neutral career promotion.
- **What survived / what was damaged:** Survived: almost 6,000 songs, 74 scholarly works totaling 3,364 manuscript pages, the `Народні мелодії` / `Українські народні мелодії` corpus, and later printed collections. Damaged: direct Ukrainian institutional continuity. IEU notes that after 1930 he published only two short articles during life, and that substantial publication resumed posthumously in Moscow in 1971-1973 and Kyiv in 1985-1986. [T1: IEU; T1: ЕСУ; T2: NBVU]

## 3. Major works / contributions

- `1906` — `Початки науки про права конституційні` and `Права що до виборів`, early Ukrainian-language legal-popularizing work tied to constitutional rights and elections. [T1: ЕІУ]
- `1912` — `Новітня музична етнографія`, programmatic article in `Рідний край`; an early statement of field-method orientation. [T1: ЕСУ]
- `1917-1918` — `Народні мелодії. З голосу Лесі Українки`, recorded and arranged by Kvitka; NBVU describes part 1 as a 1917 lithographed publication and says the full collection contains 225 songs recorded between 1899 and 1913. [T2: NBVU; T2: NaUKMA local PDF check]
- `1920-1922` — work in VUAN / Ukrainian Scientific Society musical-ethnography structures; ЕСУ places him at VUAN from 1920 and head of the Music-Ethnographic Cabinet from 1921/1922. IEU says he founded the VUAN Cabinet of Musical Ethnography in 1922 and directed it until 1933. [T1: ЕСУ; T1: IEU]
- `1922` — `Українські народні мелодії`, a major collection of 743 songs and melodies, 685 collected/transcribed by Kvitka according to IEU. [T1: IEU; T1: ЕСУ]
- `1923` — `М. Лисенко як збирач народних пісень` and `Ритмічні паралелі в піснях слов'янських народів`; connect Kvitka to the Lysenko-line source-method tradition. [T1: ЕСУ]
- `1924` — `Професіональні народні співці і музиканти на Україні: Програма для досліду їх діяльності й побуту`; a core source-method anchor for kobzar/lirnyk study. [T1: ЕСУ; T4: Lukaniuk]
- `1925` — `Потреби у справі дослідження народної музики на Україні` and `Вступні уваги до музично-етнографічних студій`; use for method and institution-building, not as generic "collector" color. [T1: ЕСУ]
- `1926-1928` — studies including `Первісні тоноряди`, `Віддих у народних співах`, `До вивчення побуту лірників`, and `Пісні українських зимових обрядових свят`; important for rhythm, mode, breath, calendar songs, and professional folk-musician life. [T1: ЕСУ]
- `1930` — `Фольклористична спадщина Миколи Лисенка`, an interpretive bridge between Lysenko and later ethnomusicology. [T1: ЕСУ; T1: IEU]
- `1947` — `Наспіви календарних пісень як джерело вивчення історії музичної культури східних слов'ян`; late Soviet-era publication that needs careful framing because of institutional pressure after repression. [T2: УІНП; T4: Demydenko]
- `1956 / 1971-1973 / 1985-1986 / 2005` — posthumous publication and re-publication cycles: articles on Franko and Lesia Ukrainka's musical-folklore legacy, Moscow `Избранные труды`, Kyiv `Вибрані статті`, and `Українські народні мелодії`. [T1: ЕСУ; T1: IEU; T2: NBVU]

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** mixed legal, musicological, ethnographic, and methodological Ukrainian; older orthography in early publications; later Soviet-era Russian/Ukrainian publication environment after forced displacement.
- **CEFR readiness:** B2 for a short biography and institutional vocabulary; C1 for source-method, notation, rhythm/mode, kobzar-lirnyk social history, and repression chronology.
- **Lexicon notes:** `етномузикологія`, `музична етнографія`, `фонограф`, `нотна транскрипція`, `лад`, `ритмічна група`, `тоноряд`, `кобзар`, `лірник`, `ВУАН`, `Кабінет музичної етнографії`, `правнича термінологія`, `поселення`, `Карлаг`.
- **Stylistic features:** Kvitka is best taught through method words and source chains: performer -> field recording/listening -> notation -> edition -> archive -> scholarly interpretation. Avoid prose that treats "folk song" as decorative memory; his work is technical, comparative, and institution-building.

## 6. Contested points

- **Husband-frame reduction:** Sources legitimately state that he was Lesia Ukrainka's husband, but using that as the identity frame repeats the exact flattening this dossier is meant to correct. Better: "ethnomusicologist, legal scholar, VUAN institution-builder, and Lesia Ukrainka's collaborator/spouse."
- **Collector stereotype:** "Collected 6,000 songs" is true enough as a shorthand across ЕСУ / IEU / УІНП, but it can hide the method: field recording, notation, performer context, genre taxonomy, institutional archive, and comparative analysis.
- **Birthplace detail:** Хмелів is the stable curriculum form because ЕСУ/ЕІУ/IEU use it. Demydenko's note about Хмелів / Басівка / parish registration is a caveat for advanced biography, not a reason to rewrite the slug or location metadata.
- **Repression chronology:** ЕСУ, IEU, УІНП, and Demydenko agree on Soviet pressure and Kazakhstan/KarLag context, but differ in granularity. Do not teach a precise charge, case number, or "national-fascist" accusation as settled unless an archival citation is found. For now: surveillance/arrest/displacement/exile are sourced; case-file specifics remain unverified.
- **Moscow late career:** The Moscow Conservatory role and Soviet institutions are real. They must not erase the Ukrainian VUAN/Kyiv foundation or be narrated as voluntary assimilation. Conversely, avoid a simple martyr story: he survived, worked inside Soviet institutions, and left a complex archive split between Moscow and Kyiv.
- **Recording credit:** The 1908 kobzar/lirnyk phonograph work belongs to a network: Lesia Ukrainka, Klyment Kvitka, Opanas Slastion, Filaret Kolessa, the performers, and institutions. Do not credit Kvitka alone for all dumy preservation, and do not credit Kolessa alone for the whole recording initiative.
- **Gendered flattening:** Do not use Lesia Ukrainka's fame to make Kvitka "the real mind" behind her late work; that only reverses the flattening. The careful frame is collaboration, mutual musical memory, and independent scholarly labor.
