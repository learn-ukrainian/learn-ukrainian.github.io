## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Василь Васильович Овсієнко. [S1] [S2] [S3]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Vasyl Ovsienko; Vasyl Ovsiyenko; Василь Овсієнко. Forbidden Russian-imperial form for body prose: `Василий Васильевич Овсиенко`. [S1] [S2]
- **Born:** 8 April 1949, с. Ставки, Radomyshl district, Zhytomyr oblast, Ukrainian SSR. Ukrainian Wikipedia and KHPG biographical pages agree on the village and family setting; KHPG interview supplies family detail. [S1] [S2] [S3]
- **Died:** 19 July 2023, Kyiv, Ukraine, according to the Encyclopedia of Modern Ukraine. KHPG interview pages predate death and do not provide the death date. [S6]
- **Family / education key facts:** KHPG's memoir/interview page gives parents Vasyl and Frosyna, rural family origins in Stavy, and a family memory shaped by work, hunger, and local village life; Ukrainian Wikipedia reports Kyiv University philology studies from 1967 to 1972. [S1] [S3]

Овсієнко is both a participant in the Ukrainian Helsinki Group and one of the people who preserved the movement's memory through interviews, archives, and KHPG work. His dossier therefore covers both repression and later documentation work. The ESU biography independently corroborates the core chronology, the 2023 death, and his later publishing and rights-documentation roles [S1] [S6].

## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** arrest for samvydav distribution, strict-regime camp sentence, surveillance, second/third prosecutions, Ukrainian Helsinki Group imprisonment, and later rights-archive work after release.
- **When:** spring 1968 copied Dziuba's *Інтернаціоналізм чи русифікація?*; 1972 helped Vasyl Lisovyi and Yevhen Proniuk publish the sixth "Kyiv" issue of *Український вісник* and Lisovyi's open letter; arrested 5 March 1973; sentenced 6 December 1973 to four years strict-regime camp; released 5 March 1977; convicted in 1979 on an alleged resistance charge; threatened by KGB on 9 June 1981 with either repentance or a ten-plus-five sentence; tried 23-26 August 1981 by Zhytomyr court; released by pardon 21 August 1988. [S3]
- **By whom:** Soviet KGB/security apparatus, Kyiv regional court, Zhytomyr court, and Soviet camp administration. [S3]
- **Document references:** Article 62 part 1 is reported for the 1973 arrest/sentence. Exact archival file number: source not found in retrieved sources. [S3]
- **Mechanism specifics:** Ukrainian Wikipedia names the samvydav materials and dates. KHPG documents his own later role as interviewer and archivist, which helps establish why his post-release testimony became a key institutional source for other dissident biographies. [S1] [S3]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** Ovsienko survived imprisonment and became a preserver of other prisoners' voices. Ukrainian Wikipedia reports his 1989 participation in the reburial expedition for Vasyl Stus, Oleksa Tykhy, and Yurii Lytvyn, and his later KHPG archive/interview work. [S3]

The mechanism is not only punitive but archival: the same state that criminalized samvydav created the conditions in which former prisoners later had to rebuild the documentary record from memory, interviews, and surviving copies. [S1] [S3]
