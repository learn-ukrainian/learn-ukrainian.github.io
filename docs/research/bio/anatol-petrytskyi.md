## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Анато́лій Галактіо́нович Петри́цький; signed and universally known as **Анато́ль Петри́цький**.
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Анатоль Петрицький (the form he himself used). FORBIDDEN Russian-imperial transliterations that body text must NOT use: «Анатолий Петрицкий», «Petritsky», «Petritskii» (the Moscow «Советский художник» 1971 edition used the Russified form).
- **Born:** 31 January (12 February New Style) 1895 | Київ | Russian Empire [T1: ЕІУ — Петрицький Анатолій Галактіонович]; [T1: IEU — Petrytsky, Anatol]; [T3: uk.wikipedia]. Son of a railway worker (залізничник).
- **Died:** 6 March 1964 | Київ | Ukrainian SSR (USSR) | natural causes; buried at Байкове кладовище [T1: ЕСУ — Петрицький Анатоль]; [T1: IEU]; [T2: НІБУ — Марченко 2023].
- **Family / education key facts:** Studied at the Київське художнє училище (Kyiv Art School), 1912–17, graduating 1917/18; teachers/mentors included Федір Кричевський and (private lessons) Василь Кричевський, the studio of Олександр Мурашко, the decorative-arts workshop of Олександра Екстер, and briefly Михайло Бойчук. Later trained at the Moscow Higher Artistic-Technical Workshops (ВХУТЕМАС), 1922–24, under О. Древін [T1: IEU; T1: ЕСУ; T2: НІБУ].

> **Note (Петрицький is a "survived" figure, not an executed one).** He was NOT arrested, exiled, or shot. The repression mechanism here is the destruction of his work, the anti-formalist campaign, and forced co-optation — see §2. Do not import arrest/execution language by analogy with the Розстріляне відродження figures he portrayed.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section. Петрицький survived the Terror physically while the regime destroyed the central body of his work and co-opted the rest.

- **What happened:** Professional persecution (anti-formalist campaign) + physical destruction of artworks + co-optation. Not arrest, not exile, not execution.
- **When (specific dates):** Peak from the mid-1930s; the trigger years are 1933 (onset of mass repression of the figures he had portrayed) through the late 1930s. The forced "socialist realism" turn dates from 1932 onward (party resolution «Про перебудову літературно-художніх організацій», 23 April 1932).
- **By whom:** Soviet cultural apparatus — party committees, censorship organs (Головліт), and the security organs (ОДПУ/НКВД) that liquidated the people in his portraits. No individual NKVD case file on Petrytskyi himself is attested (he was not arrested).
- **Mechanism specifics:**
  1. **The destroyed portrait gallery.** From the mid-1920s Petrytskyi built an ambitious "стопортретна" (hundred-portrait) series — «Портрети сучасників» — documenting the leading figures of the Ukrainian cultural renaissance. Of more than a hundred portraits, **no more than a dozen survived**; most vanished during the Stalinist repressions because the sitters had become «вороги народу». Among those portrayed and then repressed were **Лесь Курбас** (shot at Сандармох, 1937), **Михайль Семенко** (shot, 1937), and **Микола Хвильовий** (suicide under persecution, 1933) [T5: SupportYourArt — «Портрет: Анатоль Петрицький»; T1: IEU]. The portraits were exhibited in 1932 at the Харківський будинок літератури ім. В. Блакитного; an album was prepared at the «Рух» publishing house but **never printed** [T5: SupportYourArt; T5: uartlib — «Портрети сучасників»].
  2. **The banned album of theatre costumes.** His 1929 Kharkiv luxury album «Театральні строї Петрицького» (print run 1 500) was suppressed and destroyed — even though a copy reached Picasso's library and Екстер used it to teach composition in Paris [T2: НІБУ — Марченко 2023; T3: uk.wikipedia].
  3. **The formalism campaign.** From the mid-1930s Petrytskyi had to publicly answer for «формалізм». His own recorded defence of Picasso is hedged with the survival-grammar of the era: praising the mastery while disowning the "class worldview" (see §4). His acclaimed painting «Інваліди» — internationally praised at the 1930 Venice Biennale and toured in the USA — was at home relegated to a "спецфонд" after the socialist-realism turn [T2: НІБУ; T3: uk.wikipedia].
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** Destroyed/lost — the bulk of the portrait gallery; the 1929 costume album (banned); early canvases (additional non-political losses: 1918 Kyiv shelling burned early work alongside V. Krychevsky's collection; a 1924 Moscow fire destroyed most of his Moscow canvases). Survived — over 500 theatre designs (now in the Музей театрального, музичного та кіномистецтва України), a handful of the portraits, and easel paintings such as «Півонії».
- **Co-optation (the anti-hagiographic core):** Petrytskyi's talent was useful to the regime, and he was permitted to survive on its terms. He joined the ВКП(б) in 1943, was named Народний художник СРСР (1944), won two Сталінські премії (1949, for «Макар Діброва»; 1951, for «Калиновий гай»), and received the Орден Леніна (1960) [T1: ЕСУ; T3: uk.wikipedia]. He was neither martyr nor dissident — he was a co-opted survivor.

## 3. Major works

- `1917–19` — scenography for Les Kurbas's «Молодий театр»: «Осінь», «Кандіда», «Затоплений дзвін», «Різдвяний вертеп». His debut as a theatre designer (1916) and the start of the avant-garde "формальна революція" in Ukrainian stage design.
- `1920s` — *«Інваліди»* (dated 1924 or 1926 by different sources — see disagreement note below), oil. Shown at the **Venice Biennale 1930**, highly praised, toured the USA; later suppressed domestically.
- `1925` — stage design for *«Вій»*, Kyiv Ukrainian Drama Theatre.
- `mid-1920s–1932` — *«Портрети сучасників»* (the "стопортретна" gallery), the central body of work — mostly destroyed (§2).
- `1927` — co-founder of the futurist organisation **«Нова генерація»** (with Ґео Шкурупій, Вадим Меллер, Дмитро Бузько, Леонід Скрипник, Олекса Влизько and others).
- `1929` — album *«Театральні строї Петрицького»* (Kharkiv) — banned and destroyed; a copy reached Picasso's library.
- `1945` — ballet *«Лілея»* (Київський театр опери та балету).
- `1948–51` — *«Макар Діброва»* and *«Калиновий гай»* set designs (Сталінські премії 1949, 1951) — socialist-realist commissions.
- *«Півонії»* — one of his best-known surviving easel paintings.
- Posthumous albums of his work published in Kyiv, 1968 and 1991.

> **Source disagreement (flagged, not smoothed).** The scale of the portrait series is reported variously: НІБУ documents "понад двадцять" portraits made 1927–30 (Яновський, Рильський, Хвильовий, Курбас, Іван Падалка…); SupportYourArt and uartlib call it a "стопортретна" (~100) series of which "не більше десятку" survived; IEU states "over 150 portraits … most destroyed in the 1930s by the Soviet authorities." The dating of «Інваліди» is given as 1924 (uk.wikipedia) or 1926 (НІБУ). Prefer the ranged, sourced wording in prose; do not invent a single exact figure.

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated in modern UA scholarship:** the exact size and contents of the destroyed portrait gallery (20+ documented vs ~100 planned vs 150+ per IEU); the dating of «Інваліди»; how far his late socialist-realist work (the Stalin-Prize commissions) should be read as cynical survival, sincere belief, or the only space left to a co-opted artist.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** celebratory genius framing can flatten the harder story — that he survived by conforming, joined the party, and accepted Stalin Prizes. Claims that he quietly preserved the avant-garde inside theatre work must be tested against the documented compromises.
- **Where modern Russian disinformation attacks:** the appropriation of Ukrainian avant-garde artists into a Russian/Soviet avant-garde category, erasing the Ukrainian context (Kyiv, Kharkiv, the «Нова генерація», Kurbas's «Молодий театр») in which Petrytskyi worked.
