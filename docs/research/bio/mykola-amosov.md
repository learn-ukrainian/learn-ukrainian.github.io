## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Микола Михайлович Амосов. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Амосов Микола Михайлович"; cross-checked against ЕСУ]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** none. His own scientific/literary works appeared under the Russian form **Николай Михайлович Амосов / Н. М. Амосов** (the language of his publication, not a forbidden imperial relabelling of a Ukrainian — see §6 honesty note); listed in §8.
- **Born:** 6 (19) December 1913 | село **Ольхово**, Череповецький повіт, Новгородська губернія, **Російська імперія** (now Череповецький район, Вологодська область, **Російська Федерація**; the village was flooded by the Rybinsk reservoir). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Died:** 12 December 2002 (aged 88) | **Київ, Україна** | myocardial infarction; buried at the Байкове кладовище. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Origin / identity (honesty up front):** the uk.wikipedia lead itself calls him "**радянський та український лікар російського походження**" — a **Soviet-and-Ukrainian doctor of Russian origin**, born in Russia. He became a Ukrainian figure **by adoption**: he moved to **Київ in 1952 and lived there 49 years**, became an academician of the НАН України (1969), and in the 2008 «Великі українці» poll was voted **the second-greatest Ukrainian after Yaroslav the Wise**. This dossier claims him for Ukraine on the basis of his life and work, **not** by inventing Ukrainian ethnicity (§6). [T3: uk.wikipedia — lead, Біографія, Пам'ять]
- **Family / education key facts:** born to peasants; mother a village midwife. Cherepovets school, then a mechanical technikum; from 1932 a power-station shift chief in Arkhangelsk; **two degrees in parallel** — graduated the **Архангельський медичний інститут with distinction (1939)** and earned an **engineering diploma with distinction (1940)** (diploma project: a large steam-turbine aircraft). WWII chief surgeon of a **field mobile hospital (ППГ-2266)**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Біографія]

## 2. Soviet-system mechanism (read this section's honesty note first)

Amosov was **not** a victim of Soviet repression — he was one of the regime's most **decorated insiders**: **Герой Соціалістичної Праці (1973)**, **Ленінська премія (1961)**, **deputy of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR across five convocations**, academician. Forcing a martyrdom narrative onto him would be a lie. The honest "Soviet treatment" mechanism relevant to this figure has three real strands:

**(a) The Soviet anti-cybernetics legacy.** The field Amosov entered in 1958 — biological cybernetics — had been condemned in the late-Stalin USSR as a "**буржуазна лженаука**" (bourgeois pseudoscience); it was rehabilitated only with the post-Stalin Thaw. Amosov's biocybernetics at the Інститут кібернетики НАН України (which he led, 1959–1990) was thus built **on ground the regime had earlier poisoned** — a genuine instance of Soviet ideology shaping what science was permitted. [framing per source-tier §SPECIAL RULE; T3: uk.wikipedia — Біографія]

**(b) Candor under a triumphalist medicine.** Operating at the lethal frontier of early heart surgery, Amosov wrote with **unusual public honesty about patients who died on his table** (the autobiographical «Думки та серце», 1964) — candor that cut against Soviet medical triumphalism — and quietly **cited the then-unpublished verse of the repressed poet Борис Чичибабін** in his books, a small act of literary solidarity with a persecuted writer. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Факти з життя]

**(c) Russian-language publication as the imperial default.** His ~400 works and 19 monographs were written **in Russian** (the Soviet scientific lingua franca); the Ukrainian editions — e.g. **«Роздуми про здоров'я»** (Київ: Здоров'я, 1990, the Ukrainian edition of the 1977 «Раздумья о здоровье») and Тамара Малашок's translations — came **later**. The Russification of his output is itself the soft mechanism this track documents. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Книги]

**The decolonial turn (what Ukraine did with him):** after 2014/2022, Ukrainian cities renamed streets that had carried **Russian-imperial/Soviet names — Ломоносов, Менделєєв, Луначарський, Корчагінців, Демьян Коротченко — after Amosov instead**. A Russian-born man who chose Ukraine thus became a **vehicle for decolonising Ukrainian toponymy** — the cleanest honest statement of his place in this track.

## 3. Major works

**Medicine / surgery:**
- `1953` — doctoral dissertation **«Резекції легень при туберкульозі»**. [T3]
- `1955` — **first in Ukraine to treat heart defects surgically**; founded the country's first chair of thoracic surgery (and the first anaesthesiology courses/chair). [T3: uk.wikipedia — Біографія]
- `1958` — among the **first in the USSR to introduce the artificial-circulation (heart-lung) method**, with original Soviet heart-lung-machine models of his own design. [T3]
- `1963` — **first in the USSR to perform mitral-valve prosthesis**; `1965` — created and **first in the world introduced antithrombotic heart-valve prostheses**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Біографія]
- `1983` — his clinic reorganised into the **Київський НДІ серцевої хірургії** (now **Інститут серцево-судинної хірургії імені М. М. Амосова**, named 2003), performing ~3000 heart operations a year. [T3]

**Cybernetics / AI:**
- `1959–1990` — headed the department of **biological cybernetics, Інститут кібернетики НАН України**; with colleagues built **autonomous robots controlled by an artificial neural network** (claimed among the first in the world — see §6 NPOV caution); modelled cardiac self-regulation, machine diagnosis of heart disease, and "the internal environment of the organism." [T3: uk.wikipedia — Біографія]

**Books (writer):** «Думки та серце» / «Мысли и сердце» (1964); «Записки з майбутнього» (1965); «ППГ-2266 (Записки полевого хирурга)» (1975); **«Роздуми про здоров'я» / «Раздумья о здоровье»** (1977; UA ed. 1990); «Книга про щастя та нещастя» (1983); «Моя система здоров'я» (1997); «Голоса времён» (1998). [T3: uk.wikipedia — Книги]

## 6. Contested points

- **Russian origin vs Ukrainian adoption (the central honesty).** Amosov was **born in Russia, of Russian origin**, wrote in Russian, and was a **decorated Soviet establishment figure**. Ukraine reclaims him because he chose Kyiv, built Ukrainian cardiac surgery and the Ukrainian Academy's biocybernetics, and was voted second-greatest Ukrainian (2008). The defensible claim is **"Ukrainian by adoption and lifework,"** not "ethnically Ukrainian." Russia can with equal honesty note his Russian birth; the dossier resists any tug toward inflation in either direction. [T3: uk.wikipedia — lead, Пам'ять]
- **Soviet insider, not dissident.** He was a Supreme Soviet deputy and Hero of Socialist Labour. His candor about surgical death and his quotation of Чичибабін show independence of spirit, **but he must not be retrofitted into a dissident** — he worked within, and was honoured by, the Soviet system. (This is the "surface the Soviet-loyalty dimension honestly" requirement.)
- **Priority claims (NPOV caution).** "First in the world" framings (antithrombotic valve prostheses; first neural-network-controlled robots) are partly Soviet-era priority assertions; they should be presented as **claimed/attested in Ukrainian sources**, pending independent corroboration, not as settled global firsts.
- **His health philosophy.** The «Амосов system» (extreme self-experimentation, heavy exercise into old age, a pacemaker) is admired but **medically debated**; popular memory sometimes turns it into a fad rather than a hypothesis he tested on himself.
- **Russian disinformation.** As with other Soviet-era scientists, a "wholly Russian/Soviet" framing erases the Ukrainian half of his life; the post-2014 street-renamings are the counter-assertion. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Назви вулиць]
