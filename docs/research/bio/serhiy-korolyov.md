- **Died:** 14 January 1966 (aged 59) | Москва, СРСР | died on the operating table of acute heart failure during surgery for a rectal sarcoma; the official medical statement was published in *Правда*, 16 Jan 1966 (№ 16). The parents separated; from age three the boy lived in **Ніжин** with his maternal grandparents. His **national identity** is genuinely contested (see §6): the maternal Cossack/Nizhyn roots and his own «українець» self-entry are documented, but his father was Belarusian and a teacher of *Russian*, and Korolyov spent his working life inside the Russian-Soviet defence apparatus. The dossier reports the evidence in both directions and does not flatten it.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section. Korolyov's case has **two distinct Soviet mechanisms**: physical repression (the GULAG), then erasure-by-secrecy.

**(a) Arrest, torture, Kolyma, sharashka (1938–1944).** Korolyov was **arrested on 27 June 1938**. On **27 September 1938** a closed court session sentenced him to **ten years of corrective-labour camps**, five years' loss of political rights, and confiscation of property; the destination was **Колима**. The arrest rested on the interrogation testimony of three already-arrested RNII colleagues — **І. Т. Клейменов, Г. Е. Лангемак і В. П. Глушко** — who named him a participant in an invented "counter-revolutionary Trotskyist organisation" inside the **Реактивний науково-дослідний інститут (РНДІ)**. The case was run by NKVD operatives, lieutenants **Микола Федорович Биков** and **Михайло Миколайович Шестаков**. During interrogation an NKVD investigator **struck Korolyov in the face with a carafe (графин), breaking his lower jaw**; left untreated it healed wrong, permanently limiting how far he could open his mouth — an injury that, the record argues, contributed to his death 28 years later (see §6). [T3: uk.wikipedia — "27 червня 1938 року Корольова було заарештовано… вирок: десять років… Місце покарання — Колима"; IEU: "arrested in 1938, and sent first to a concentration camp and then to a special labor camp for scientists"]

After petitions from his family and from aviators Валентина Гризодубова and Михайло Громов and designer Туполєв, and after **two appeals Korolyov wrote directly to Stalin**, he was returned from Kolyma to Moscow (Бутирська в'язниця, 2 March 1940). On **10 July 1940** the NKVD Special Council under **Лаврентій Берія** re-set his term at eight years; in September 1940 the term was commuted to service inside **ЦКБ-29** — a prison design bureau ("**шарашка**") headed by the still-imprisoned Туполєв, where Korolyov worked on the **Ту-2** bomber. He later secured transfer to a Kazan sharashka developing rocket engines (for the Пе-2), aided by Glushko. He was released early on **27 July 1944** with the conviction struck. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Період ув'язнення]

**(b) Erasure by state secrecy (1944–1966) — the mechanism specific to this figure.** Once Korolyov became chief designer of the Soviet missile and space programme, his very name was made a **state secret**. Publicly he was permitted to be only **«Головний конструктор»** ("the Chief Designer"). He could not be named as the author of Sputnik or of Gagarin's flight; the Nobel committee's reported wish to honour the designer of the first satellite could not be answered because the USSR would not disclose who he was. He gave no interviews under his own name, published technical work under pseudonyms, and was identified only after his death on 14 January 1966 — at which point the regime, having hidden the living man, claimed the dead one as a Soviet glory. [T1: IEU — "During his lifetime his name was kept secret and he was referred to only as the Chief Designer"; T3: uk.wikipedia]

**What survived / what was erased:** the engineering survived and reshaped the 20th century; the *man* was erased twice — first as an "enemy of the people" sent to Kolyma, then as an anonymous "Chief Designer" whose Ukrainian birth, Cossack maternal line and broken jaw were unmentionable. The decolonial point (see §6) is that the achievement was narrated as a **Russian/Soviet** triumph while the person behind it was a Zhytomyr-born, Nizhyn-raised, KPI-trained engineer who once wrote "українець" on his own enrolment papers.

## 3. Major works

A designer, not an author; "works" here are aircraft, rockets and spacecraft programmes, chronological:

- `1924` — **планер К-5**, designed at 17 in Odesa; first project approved for construction. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Винаходи]
- `1929` — glider **«Коктебель»** (with Сергій Іллюшин); `1930` — **«СК-3 „Червона зірка“»**, on which V. Stepanchonok flew the first glider Nesterov loop. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1930` — diploma aircraft **СК-4** (supervisor Туполєв). [T3]
- `1932` — head of the **Група вивчення реактивного руху (ГВРР/ГИРД)**; first Soviet hybrid- and liquid-fuelled rockets tested 1932–33. With Tsander, the group launched the **ГИРД-09** (1933). [T1: IEU — "the Group for the Study of Jet Propulsion, which in 1933 launched the first Soviet liquid-fueled rocket, the GIRD-09"]
- `late 1930s` — first Soviet rocket-plane **СК-9 / РП-318**. [T3]
- `1946–1953` — **Р-1** (a copy of the German А-4/Фау-2), **Р-2**, **Р-11**, **Р-5** and **Р-5М** — the world's first nuclear-armed missile carrier. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Головний конструктор]
- `1957` — **Р-7**, the world's first intercontinental ballistic missile; first successful launch **27 August 1957**; the basis of an entire launch-vehicle family. [T3]
- `4 October 1957` — **перший штучний супутник Землі (Sputnik 1)**. [T1: IEU; T3]
- `12 April 1961` — first crewed spaceflight, **«Восток»**, **Юрій Гагарін**. [T1: IEU — "the first astronauts in earth orbit"; T3]
- `1964–1965` — **«Восход»** (first multi-seat craft) and the first spacewalk (Олексій Леонов, 18 March 1965); programmes **«Луна», «Венера», «Марс», «Зонд»** and the design of **«Союз»**. [T3]

## 6. Contested points

- **National identity (the central debate).** Russia narrates Korolyov as a Russian/Soviet genius; Ukraine reclaims his **Zhytomyr birth, Nizhyn Cossack maternal line, KPI training, and his own «українець» self-entry**. The honest position is neither flat claim: his father was Belarusian and a teacher of Russian, and his career was wholly inside the Russian-Soviet defence state. He is best described as a **Ukrainian-born engineer of mixed heritage who self-identified as Ukrainian on at least one document and held a lifelong cultural attachment to Ukraine**, whose achievement the USSR narrated as Russian. Decolonisation here means *restoring the suppressed Ukrainian facts*, not inventing a tidy nationalist hero. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Життєпис, Цікавинки]
- **What popular memory simplifies.** Soviet and post-Soviet Russian memory presents the space programme as a seamless Russian triumph and omits both the **Kolyma sentence** and the **broken jaw under NKVD interrogation** — i.e. that the state nearly killed its own "Chief Designer" before it used him.
- **Cause of death.** The official line (acute heart failure during sarcoma surgery) is documented, but the uk.wikipedia article and Ukrainian commentators stress the **suppressed detail**: the jaw broken in 1938 prevented intubation, forcing a riskier mask-narcosis on a patient with angina — i.e. the 1938 torture is causally linked to the 1966 death. Presented here as the strongly-argued Ukrainian reading, not as a closed forensic finding. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Смерть]
- **Russian disinformation.** Post-2014/2022, the "Korolyov was Russian, full stop" framing functions as cultural-appropriation of a Ukrainian-born figure — the same erasure pattern the secrecy regime began. Ukraine's 2007 commemorative coin and the 2017 state-level 110th-anniversary marking are the counter-assertion. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Вшанування пам'яті]
