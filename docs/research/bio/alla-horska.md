## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Алла Олександрівна Горська. [T1: ЕІУ — Зарецька Т. І., "Горська Алла Олександрівна," т. 2, с. 175; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** none used professionally; the diaspora honorific «душа українського шістдесятництва» and the 2024 retrospective title «Боривітер» attach to her name. Russified form forbidden in body text (§8): "Алла Александровна Горская."
- **Born:** **18 вересня 1929** | Ялта | Кримська АСРР, РСФРР, СРСР. Daughter of **Олександр Горський**, a Soviet film-industry administrator (director of the Yalta, later the Kyiv, film studio); mother Олена worked as a costume artist. The family moved Yalta → Moscow (1932) → Leningrad (1933), survived two blockade winters, evacuated to Almaty (1943), then settled in **Kyiv (late 1943)**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Родина"]
- **Died:** **28 листопада 1970** (aged 41) | Васильків | Київська область, Українська РСР, СРСР | **murdered** (body officially "discovered" 2 грудня 1970). [T1: ЕІУ; T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Вбивство"]
- **Family / education key facts:** Husband — the painter **Віктор Зарецький** (married 1952). Graduated the Київська художня середня школа ім. Шевченка with a gold medal (1948), then the **Київський художній інститут** (studio of Сергій Григор'єв). Admitted to the Спілка художників 1959. Russian-speaking by upbringing, she **consciously switched to Ukrainian as an adult**, learning the alphabet from the dissident Надія Світлична. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Навчання," §"Суспільно-політична діяльність"]

**Source disagreement note (flagged):** The exact authorship of the murder is **not** archivally closed (§2, §6): the KGB attribution rests on documentary-circumstantial analysis of declassified special reports, not on a recovered order. The cover-story — that her father-in-law **Іван Зарецький** killed her and then took his own life — is recorded as the official 1971 finding and is treated as the regime's fabrication, not as fact.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section. Horská is a **dissident artist assassinated**, and the case is also a study in how the Soviet security state manufactured a cover story.

- **What happened:** On **28 November 1970** Alla Horská was killed at the house of her father-in-law in **Васильків** (Kyiv oblast). The official version: her father-in-law Іван Зарецький murdered her on personal-grudge grounds and, the next day, took his own life. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Вбивство"]
- **When (specific):** killed 28 November 1970; body "officially discovered" by the Vasylkiv militia **2 December 1970**; the investigation opened **3 December 1970** (order of the Vasylkiv district prosecutor) and was **closed 23 January 1971** with the pre-scripted conclusion. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **By whom (attribution):** widely attributed to the **КДБ УРСР (KGB)**. The evidentiary basis is the **declassified Фонд 16 of the ГДА СБУ** (доповідні / спецповідомлення of the KGB chair to the First Secretary of the CC CPU), declassified **2008–2009** and analysed/published by **Олексій Зарецький (2010)**. [T2: ГДА СБУ, Фонд 16; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Document references (the tell, quoted in substance):** between **3 and 18 December 1970** the KGB sent the CC **five special reports** directly concerning Horská. The decisive one of **5 December** stated the **murderer's motive and the overall picture of the crime *before* the investigation itself had reached those conclusions** — and did so before the case was even transferred (7 December) to the higher-level Kyiv-oblast prosecutor, i.e. before the senior investigative team had begun work. The reports of 11 and 18 December tracked the *reactions of her circle* to the death with an operational closeness possible only under continuous KGB surveillance. The analysis concludes the killing and even the burial were prepared in advance. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Вбивство," citing the ГДА СБУ Фонд 16 reports]
- **Mechanism specifics:** the surveillance pre-dated the murder. Horská had been under KGB pressure for years — summoned for interrogations and "зводини" with threats; refused in 1970 to testify in Ivano-Frankivsk in the case of the imprisoned **Валентин Мороз**; days before her death lodged a protest with the УРСР Supreme Court against the "illegality and cruelty" of a dissident sentence. The case-formularів (surveillance files) were **almost all destroyed in 1990**, which the analysis treats as itself significant. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** physically destroyed — the 1964 stained-glass **«Шевченко. Мати»** (smashed on the order of the КДУ rector І. Т. Швець, §3); the surveillance files (1990). Survived — her paintings, graphics and monumental mosaics, now in the НХМУ (Kyiv), the Шептицький National Museum (Lviv), the ЦДАМЛМ, the Музей шістдесятництва, and the **Norton & Nancy Dodge** nonconformist collection at Rutgers.

## 3. Major works

A monumentalist, painter and graphic artist; selected, chronological:

- `1957` — *Пісня про Донбас*; the shaft-worker (шахтарський) cycle that won her admission to the Спілка художників. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1960` — *Автопортрет з сином*; *Портрет батька*. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Вибрані твори"]
- `1962–1963` — *Прип'ять. Паром*; graphic portraits of the шістдесятники. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1963` — portraits of **Василь Симоненко**, **Іван Світличний**, **Євген Сверстюк**; the линогравюра *Скажи, що правда оживе*; *Дума (Т. Шевченко)*. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1964` — the stained-glass **«Шевченко. Мати»** in the vestibule of the Red Building of Kyiv University, co-created with **Опанас Заливаха, Людмила Семикіна, Галина Севрук, Галина Зубченко** for Shevchenko's 150th anniversary — **destroyed** on the rector's order; the commission branded it "ідейно ворожий… чужий принципам соціалістичного реалізму," and Horská and Semykina were (temporarily) expelled from the Спілка. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Мистецька діяльність"]
- `1966` — *Жінка-птах* (панно); monumental work in Donetsk schools. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1967` — the mosaic panel **«Вітер»** (Kyiv); the monumental cycle in Donetsk / Krasnodon (now Сорокине) — including work at the «Молода гвардія» memorial — done with Зарецький, Григорій Синиця and others, fusing бойчукізм, Ukrainian baroque and Mexican muralism. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1960s` — set designs (with director **Лесь Танюк**) for «Ніж у сонці» (Drach), «Отак загинув Гуска» (Куліш), «Правда і кривда» (Стельмах) — **all banned before staging**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]

**Note on destruction/erasure:** much of her monumental work in the Donbas was officially tolerated *because* it carried sanctioned themes — a protective camouflage she used while her easel and graphic work moved underground.

> Horská is a **visual artist**; her documentary voice survives in her **letters** (collected as *Червона тінь калини. Листи, спогади, статті*, 1996; *Душа українського шістдесятництва*, Смолоскип, 2015). The two below are verbatim from her letters to Опанас Заливаха as catalogued on Вікіцитати; the literary `verify_quote` corpus does not index private correspondence, so they are flagged "за вид. листів; не в корпусі проєкту."

**Quote 1 — the artist's revolt against academic norms (letter to Заливаха, 24 September 1969):**

> «Я підняла „повстання на риштованні“: немає образного кольорового рішення голів. У той же день кайдани академізму було скинуто — рішення знайдено.»

Pedagogically central: it shows the *aesthetic* dimension of her dissidence — the break from socialist-realist «академізм» on the scaffolding (риштування) of a monumental commission was, for her, of a piece with the political break. [Вікіцитати — лист до О. Заливахи, 24.09.1969; не в корпусі проєкту]

**Quote 2 — the шістдесятники' cultural temperature, in her own shorthand (letter, January 1968):**

> «Гончар, „Собор“. Собор і комбікорм. Собор в риштованні. Собор і Бітлз.»

A compressed, almost telegraphic reaction to Oles Honchar's just-banned novel *Собор* — the collision of cathedral (соборність / spirit) with «комбікорм» (the regime's productivist materialism) and with «Бітлз» (the forbidden modern world). It captures the generation's idiom. [Вікіцитати — лист до О. Заливахи, січень 1968; не в корпусі проєкту]

**Documented context (not put in her mouth):** at her 7 December 1970 funeral on the Berkovetske cemetery — which turned into a protest rally against the regime — **Василь Стус, Євген Сверстюк** and **Олесь Сергієнко** spoke; Stus's eulogy is part of the documented dissident response and contributed to the pressure that later led to his own persecution. The eulogy text is referenced here as documented event, **not** quoted, because a verbatim, sourced transcription was not captured this pass. [T3: uk.wikipedia; T5: Суспільне — biographical feature]

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** her written voice (letters) is **modernist-colloquial intellectual** Ukrainian — elliptical, allusive, art-critical, switching between aesthetic theory and political shorthand. She wrote it as a **consciously acquired** literary language (she learned Ukrainian as an adult).
- **CEFR readiness:** her *letters* sit at **C1** (art-critical abstraction, cultural allusion); her *biography* is tellable at **B1–B2**; the §2 case-analysis is C1.
- **Lexicon notes (pre-teach, all VESUM-verified FOUND):** монументальний, вітраж, мозаїка, шістдесятники, дисидентка, андеграунд, риштування. Note «риштування» (scaffolding) as the literal-and-metaphorical hinge of Quote 1; gloss «бойчукізм» (the Boichuk monumental school) and «соборність» as proper/derived terms.
- **Stylistic features:** compression, juxtaposition (Собор / комбікорм / Бітлз), the fusion of visual-art vocabulary with moral-political stance.

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated in modern UA scholarship:** the **degree of provability** of direct KGB authorship of the murder. The documentary-circumstantial case (the pre-emptive 5 December special report; the pre-organised burial; the destroyed files) is strong and is the consensus reading among Ukrainian researchers, but **no execution order has been recovered** and the analysis itself concedes such a document likely never existed. The dossier therefore states KGB responsibility as the **well-argued dominant reading**, not as archivally closed.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** Horská is remembered first as a *martyr* and only second as an *artist* — her substantial monumental and graphic œuvre is often eclipsed by the manner of her death. A curriculum treatment must restore the art (the 2024 «Боривітер» retrospective of 100+ works is the corrective).
- **Where Russian/Soviet framing attacks:** the regime's own move was to recast a **political assassination as a domestic crime** («побутове вбивство» by the father-in-law) — the original disinformation. Modern Russian narratives continue to minimise Soviet repression of the шістдесятники; naming the cover-story *as* a cover-story is the decolonial act here.
- **Other-perspective considerations:** Horská's Russian-speaking, Soviet-elite upbringing (a film-administrator's daughter from Yalta) and her *deliberate* turn to Ukrainian make her a key case for the curriculum's treatment of **Russification and the choice of language as resistance** — not a born-nationalist but a converted one.
