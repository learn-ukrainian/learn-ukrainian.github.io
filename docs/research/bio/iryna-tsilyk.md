## 2. Oppression / pressure mechanism

- **Not prison-case biography:** Do not invent arrest, exile, camp term, KGB file, or dissident-sentence biography. Tsilyk's pressure mechanism is wartime witness, war-zone documentary ethics, Russian aggression against Ukraine, public-cultural representation under invasion, and fragile Ukrainian film/literary institutions after post-Soviet collapse.
- **War-zone documentary frame:** SFF summarizes `The Earth Is Blue as an Orange` around daily trauma in a war zone and a family making a film together. DzygaMDB identifies the family as living in the red zone of Donbas and making an amateur film about life under surreal wartime conditions.
- **Ethical pressure of the camera:** Future lessons should ask what changes when people living under shelling film themselves and are also filmed by a director. Avoid treating the family only as passive witnesses.
- **Full-scale invasion pressure:** Book Arsenal 2025 says Tsilyk is working on animated documentary `Червона зона`; HURI's 2024 Ukrainian-language profile describes the planned `Червона зона` as about the first months of Russia's full-scale invasion. This is time-sensitive; re-check development status before module build.
- **Public-witness pressure:** HURI frames Tsilyk as speaking to international audiences and presenting Ukrainian culture during Russia's war. Use dated source labels; do not turn event journalism into timeless biography.
- **Private-life caution:** Several sources mention family ties to writer Artem Chekh and his military service. Include only where directly relevant to a work such as `Я і Фелікс`; avoid private-life detail as filler.

## 3. Major works / contributions

- **`Земля блакитна, ніби апельсин` / `The Earth Is Blue as an Orange` (2020):** documentary feature; SFF 2020 Directing Award: World Cinema Documentary; PRU 2023 Shevchenko Prize. DzygaMDB records Ukraine/Lithuania production, Ukrainian/Russian language, Tsilyk as director and screenwriter, and multiple awards.
- **`Невидимий батальйон` / `Invisible Battalion` (2017):** documentary project about Ukrainian women at war; PEN says Tsilyk directed two documentaries for the film almanac.
- **`Я і Фелікс` / `Rock Paper Grenade` (2022):** feature fiction debut; HURI describes it as an adaptation of Artem Chekh's autobiographical novel `Хто ти такий?`, a coming-of-age story set in 1990s Ukraine.
- **Short films:** PEN/HURI filmographies point to earlier fiction and documentary shorts; keep detailed credits to a later filmography pass rather than overloading BIO dossier.
- **Books / literary work:** PEN lists poetry, prose, and children's books. Publisher and Book Arsenal sources identify works including `Ці`, `Післявчора`, `Родимки`, `Червоні на чорному сліди`, `Таке цікаве життя`, `МІСТОрія однієї дружби`, `Глибина різкості`, and `Тонкий лід` (2025).
- **International cultural mediation:** PEN and HURI both stress translations, festival readings, and international events. Frame this as Ukrainian literary/cinematic presence in world venues, not as validation by foreign institutions alone.

- **Teaching caution:** Do not turn documentary cinema into plot summary only. The learning value is the relation between form and ethics: who holds the camera, who edits, who speaks, who is seen, and who keeps agency.
- **Bridge activity:** Ask learners to compare verbs in film descriptions: `знімає`, `свідчить`, `виживає`, `грає себе`, `монтує`, `пам'ятає`. Which verbs belong to the director and which belong to the filmed family?
