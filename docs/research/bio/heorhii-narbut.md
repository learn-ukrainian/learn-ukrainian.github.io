- **Family / education key facts:** From an impoverished Lithuanian-szlachta line
  settled on the Hlukhiv lands; brother of the poet Володимир Нарбут and father
  of the artist Данило Нарбут. Self-taught at first, then the Hlukhiv gymnasium
  (1896–1906) and Petersburg (1906–1917), studying with Білібін, Добужинський,
  and at the Zvantseva studio, plus Munich (Hollósy school); member of «Світ
  мистецтва» [T3: uk.wikipedia]. Core roles: graphic artist, illustrator,
  designer of the first Ukrainian state notes and stamps, co-founder and rector
  of the Українська академія мистецтв [T1: ЕСУ].

## 2. Oppression mechanism

Not Applicable as a repression-primary biography — Narbut was not arrested,
exiled, or executed; he died of epidemic typhus in 1920 at age 34 during the
revolution. The mechanism to name honestly is the later Soviet political danger
and marginalisation attached to the state symbols he created.

- **What happened:** No personal repression. After his early death, the
  Ukrainian-statehood symbolism he designed — the UNR money and stamps, the
  Cossack-and-trident state seal — became politically dangerous under Soviet rule
  and his legacy was selectively suppressed.
- **When (specific dates):** the symbols date to 1917–1918; the political danger
  attached to them belongs to the subsequent Soviet decades, when the trident and
  UNR currency were treated as nationalist contraband.
- **By whom (specific Russian/Soviet/RF agency):** the Soviet state and its
  censorship/heritage apparatus, which marginalised the UNR-statehood reading of
  his work.
- **Document references:** the 18 July 1918 approval by Hetman Скоропадський of
  Narbut's small State Seal (a Cossack with a musket and the Volodymyr trident)
  and the UNR stamp/banknote designs are the anchors of the statehood legacy
  later treated as dangerous.
- **Mechanism specifics:** the honest frame is that an artist of Ukrainian visual
  statehood died at the moment that statehood fell, after which the empire that
  reconquered Ukraine could not erase the man — he was already dead — but worked
  to erase the meaning of his symbols. Do not invent personal repression.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** his graphic corpus, the «Українська
  абетка», and the UNR money/stamp designs survived and were restored to honour
  after 1991; the unfinished «Енеїда» illustration cycle (only one plate
  completed) was cut short by his death.

## 3. Major works

- `1910`–`1912` — illustrations to Andersen's tales, Krylov's fables, and folk
  tales — his Petersburg book-graphics period [T3].
- `1914` — illustrations for «Малоросійський гербовник» (Лукомський, Модзалевський)
  and «Герби гетьманів Малоросії» (1915) — heraldic mastery of the Cossack
  starshyna tradition [T3].
- `1917` — postage stamps of the Українська Народна Республіка (30, 40, 50
  шагів) — the first Ukrainian state stamps [T3].
- `1917` — «Українська абетка», masterpiece of Ukrainian calligraphy and type
  art [T3] [T1: ЕСУ].
- `1917`–`1920` — UNR banknotes, state charters, and the projects for the State
  Coat of Arms and Seal of the Ukrainian State (small seal approved 18 July 1918)
  [T3].
- `1920` — illustration for Kotliarevsky's «Енеїда» (only one plate completed
  before his death) [T3].

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** visual-graphic (type, heraldry, book design); his verbal register
  in inscriptions and letters is playful, archaising, Cossack-baroque.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** A2/B1 for the «Українська абетка» as a
  visual-text entry point; B2/C1 for discussion of heraldry and statehood design.
- **Lexicon notes:** heraldic and design vocabulary (герб, печатка, тризуб,
  шрифт, графіка); Cossack-baroque archaisms in his self-styling.
- **Stylistic features:** synthesis of Ukrainian baroque graphics, старо­друк
  type, heraldry, and «Світ мистецтва» refinement into a modern Ukrainian
  graphic language.

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated in modern UA scholarship:** scholarship weighs how much of
  Narbut's mature style is «Світ мистецтва» inheritance versus a genuinely
  Ukrainian baroque-graphic synthesis, and credits him (with Vasyl Krychevskyi
  and the Boichuk circle) as a founder of the Ukrainian graphic school of the
  1920s–1930s; the attribution and dating of some state-symbol designs (seal,
  coat of arms, shared work with Модзалевський) is still refined.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** popular memory remembers "the man
  who designed the first Ukrainian money," dropping the Academy rector,
  type-artist, and heraldist. Lead with the maker of Ukrainian visual statehood
  across money, stamps, seal, and alphabet.
- **Where modern Russian disinformation attacks them (if applicable):** Russian
  framing claims him for "Russian" graphic art (his Petersburg «Світ мистецтва»
  years) and minimises the UNR-statehood work; his own 1912 "Mazepist"
  self-inscription and his 1917–1918 state designs refute that claim.
- **Polish / Jewish / other-perspective considerations (if applicable):** his
  family's Lithuanian-szlachta origin is a documented nuance, not a contradiction
  of his Ukrainian self-identification, and should be presented as such.
- **HIST-alignment check (+77 roster requirement):** the 1917–1920 statehood
  frame (UNR, Гетьманат, state symbols) must align with the HIST track rather
  than being invented; record this in the plan metadata.
