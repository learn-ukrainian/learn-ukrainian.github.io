## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Олег Костянтинович Антонов. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Антонов Олег Костянтинович", cross-checked against ЕІУ т.1 с.104]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** none. Russified form (forbidden in body text, listed only in §8): Олег Константинович Антонов, Oleg Antonov.
- **Born:** 25 January (7 February) 1906 | село **Троїці**, Подольський повіт, Московська губернія, **Російська імперія** (now the Троїцький administrative district of Moscow) | into the family of a construction engineer of old gentry descent. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Died:** 4 April 1984 (aged 78) | **Київ**, Українська РСР, СРСР | buried at the Байкове кладовище. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Origin / identity (honesty up front):** the uk.wikipedia lead calls him "**український радянський літакобудівельник**" — a **Ukrainian-Soviet aircraft builder** — though he was **born near Moscow, of Russian-gentry descent**. He became a Ukrainian figure **by lifework**: his design bureau was moved to **Київ in 1952**, he led it there until his death (1984), became an academician of the **АН УРСР (1967)**, and the bureau he built is today the foundation of Ukraine's aerospace industry (§2). The dossier claims him for Ukraine on that basis, not by inventing Ukrainian ethnicity (§6). [T3: uk.wikipedia — lead, Життєпис]
- **Family / education key facts:** built his first glider during studies at the **Saratov industrial technikum** (1924); entered the **Leningrad Polytechnic** machine-building faculty (1925), graduated 1930; one of the **founders of Soviet gliding** (~30 glider types). Married three times, four children; his second wife **Єлизавета Шахатуні** computed aircraft strength in his bureau; daughter **Ганна Антонова** became a professor at the National Aviation University, Kyiv. Member of the ВКП(б) from 1945; **member of the ЦК КПУ 1966–1984**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Життєпис, Особисте життя]

## 2. Soviet-system mechanism (read this section's honesty note first)

Antonov was **not** a victim of Soviet repression — he was a **decorated insider**: **Герой Соціалістичної Праці (1966)**, three Orders of Lenin, **Ленінська премія (1962, for the An-12)**, **member of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of Ukraine (1966–1984)**, deputy of the Supreme Soviet across seven convocations. No martyrdom narrative applies. The honest story has two real strands.

**(a) A 1965 act of civic courage.** In **November 1965 Antonov signed the open letter to the CC CPSU — the "лист 78-ми" (Letter of the 78) — protesting the political repressions against the creative intelligentsia of the Ukrainian SSR and the discrimination against Ukrainian literature.** For a sitting Soviet laureate and (soon) CC CPU member to put his name to a defence of the persecuted **шістдесятники** was a genuine, documented act of Ukrainian-cultural solidarity — the clearest evidence that his Ukrainian allegiance was a choice, not just a workplace. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Громадсько-політична діяльність]

**(b) The bureau as a pillar of Ukrainian sovereignty (the decolonial through-line).** Antonov's **ДКБ — the Antonov Design Bureau — Kyiv-based from 1952** — created the An-series (§3) and grew into the institution that, after independence, became central to Ukraine's identity. Its post-Antonov flagship, the **Ан-225 «Мрія»** (built 1988, **four years after Antonov's death** — attributed to the *bureau* under O. Balabuyev, **not** to Antonov personally), the world's largest aircraft, became a national symbol whose very name means "**Dream**." **Russia destroyed the Мрія on 27 February 2022 at the Battle of Antonov Airport (Hostomel/Гостомель)** — a literal assault on the Ukrainian-sovereignty meaning the bureau had acquired. The bureau's arc — Soviet defence-industry origins → independent-Ukraine flagship → target of Russian aggression — is the decolonial through-line this dossier carries. [T3: uk.wikipedia; the An-225/Hostomel framing per source-tier §SPECIAL RULE]

**The toponymic turn:** like other Kyiv-science figures, Antonov is used to **decolonise Ukrainian street names** — cities have renamed **Туполєва (Kryvyi Rih), Чернишевського (Uman), Ленінградська (Irpin), Аерофлотська (Kharkiv), Баженова (Kramatorsk)** after him.

## 3. Major works

**Gliders (his first love):**
- `1924` — first glider **«Голуб»** (plywood and water-pipes), awarded a diploma for original design. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1933` — chief designer of the Tushino glider factory; created ~30 glider types and many record-setting craft; founder of Soviet gliding. [T3]
- `WWII` — the **А-7 transport-landing glider**: over **600** delivered troops, equipment and supplies to the front and to partisans (Antonov decorated with the medal "Партизанові Вітчизняної війни" 1st degree). [T3: uk.wikipedia — Різне]
- all-metal sport gliders **А-11, А-13** (1958), and the «Славутич» hang-gliders late in life. [T3]

**Aircraft (his main work) — the An-series:**
- `1947/1949` — **Ан-2** (first flight Aug 1947; serial from 1949): a multipurpose biplane with 16 modifications and "40 professions," license-built in Poland — one of the longest-running aircraft in world history; State (Stalin) Prize 1952. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Різне]
- `1950s–1970s` — transport aircraft **Ан-8, Ан-12** (Lenin Prize 1962), **Ан-22 «Антей»**, Ан-26, Ан-32, Ан-72; passenger **Ан-10, Ан-24** (State Prize of the UkrSSR 1976); multipurpose Ан-14, Ан-28. [T3]
- `1982` — **Ан-124 «Руслан»** (NATO "Condor"), the largest serial cargo aircraft in the world; introduced glue-weld joints and composite materials, and a CAD system for transport aircraft. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Життєпис]

**Books (memoir/essay, in Russian):** *На крыльях из дерева и полотна* (1962); *Для всех и для себя* (1965); **«Десять раз сначала»** (1969); *Планеры и самолёты* (Київ, 1990). [T3: uk.wikipedia — Праці]

## 6. Contested points

- **Russian origin vs Ukrainian lifework (the central honesty).** Antonov was **born near Moscow, of Russian-gentry descent**, and was a **Soviet establishment figure and CC CPU member**. Ukraine claims him because his bureau is Kyiv's and Ukraine's; the defensible claim is **"Ukrainian by lifework and institution,"** not "ethnically Ukrainian." Russia can honestly note his Russian birth; the dossier inflates neither side. [T3: uk.wikipedia — lead]
- **Insider who also dissented.** The **1965 Letter of the 78** is real and to his credit — but he remained a decorated CC CPU member and Supreme Soviet deputy. He must be presented as **a loyal Soviet figure who nonetheless took one documented Ukrainian-cultural stand**, not retrofitted into a dissident. (This is the "surface the Soviet-loyalty dimension honestly" requirement.)
- **Attribution discipline — the An-225 and the «Крила України» trap.** The **Ан-225 «Мрія» (1988) postdates Antonov's death (1984)** and belongs to the *bureau*, not the man — the dossier states this explicitly. And per the build brief, the citation **«Крила України» (attrib. В. Корольов) returns zero hits and is NOT cited here.** [build-brief warning; honesty discipline]
- **"World-first/largest" claims.** The An-2's longevity and the An-124/An-225 size records are well attested; nonetheless size/priority superlatives are stated as **attested**, with the bureau (not solely Antonov) credited for post-1984 craft.
- **Russian disinformation.** A "wholly Russian/Soviet aircraft genius" framing erases the Kyiv bureau's Ukrainian meaning; the 2022 destruction of the Мрія and the street-renamings are the counter-assertion. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Вшанування]
