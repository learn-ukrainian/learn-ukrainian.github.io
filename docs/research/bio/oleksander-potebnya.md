## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Олександр Опанасович Потебня
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Oleksandr Potebnia
- **Born:** 22 September (10 September O.S.) 1835 | хутір Манів, поблизу села Гаврилівки, Роменський повіт, Полтавська губернія | Російська імперія (нині Сумська область, Україна)
- **Died:** 11 December (29 November O.S.) 1891 | Харків | Російська імперія | natural causes
- **Family / education key facts:** Born into an impoverished noble family of a former military officer. Educated at the Radom gymnasium (Poland) and then at Kharkiv University (graduated 1856 from the historical-philological faculty). He spent his career at Kharkiv University, becoming a foundational figure of the Kharkiv linguistic school.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** Indirect oppression via systemic Imperial Russian censorship and the suppression of the Ukrainian language (Valuev Circular, 1863; Ems Ukaz, 1876).
- **When (specific dates):** Active career spanning the 1860s–1890s, the height of imperial linguistic bans.
- **By whom (specific Russian/Soviet/RF agency):** Imperial Russian censorship bodies, the Ministry of Internal Affairs.
- **Document references:** N/A for personal arrest. His oppression was cultural/systemic.
- **Mechanism specifics:** Potebnya lived and worked during the era when the Ukrainian language was legally banned from public, educational, and scientific spheres by the Russian Empire. Consequently, he was forced to write his major theoretical works in Russian, even when analyzing Ukrainian phonology and folklore. Despite this, he fiercely defended the distinctiveness of the Ukrainian language against imperial "obshcherussky" (pan-Russian) theories.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** His ideas survived and profoundly influenced the next generation of Ukrainian linguists, though his legacy was later heavily manipulated by Soviet authorities who tried to frame him as a purely "Russian" scholar.

## 3. Major works

- `1860` — *О некоторых символах в славянской народной поэзии*, Kharkiv.
- `1862` — *Мысль и язык* (Думка і мова), Kharkiv. (His magnum opus on the psychology of language).
- `1864` — *О полногласии* (Про повноголосся).
- `1874` — *Из записок по русской грамматике* (Із записок з руської граматики), Vols 1-2. (Despite the title, it deeply analyzed the entire East Slavic language group, demonstrating Ukrainian's independent evolution).
- `1877` — *Малорусская народная песня, по списку XVI века*.

**LINGUISTIC GUARD:** In his phonological work, Potebnya accurately analyzed the development of East Slavic languages. Crucially, words like ворота/золото/болото are examples of the **FIRST** повноголосся (pleophony: TorT/TolT → -оро-/-оло-), NOT "друге повноголосся". Potebnya was instrumental in establishing the scientific basis for East Slavic dialectology.

## 4. Primary-source quotes (≥2 required)

**Quote 1:**
> «Мова є засіб не виражати готову думку, а створювати її… вона відображає не світоспоглядання, яке склалося, а діяльність, яка його складає.»
**Source:** *Мысль и язык* (1862), widely cited in Ukrainian translation in modern linguistics.
**Context:** Explains his core Humboldtian philosophy of language as an active, creative force, not just a passive tool.

**Quote 2:**
> «Ідея національності здатна сприяти людському поступові, якщо вона стверджує взаємоповагу права народів на самостійне існування та розвиток; коли ж через цю ідею стверджують зверхність однієї спільноти над іншою, то вона набуває реакційного смислу.»
**Source:** *Язык и народность* (1895, posthumous publication).
**Context:** A direct intellectual attack on Russian imperial chauvinism and a defense of the inherent value of every national language, including Ukrainian.

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** 19th-century academic prose (written predominantly in Russian due to imperial restrictions), but focusing on Ukrainian folk-dialectal and historical texts.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** C1/C2 (highly abstract linguistic and philosophical terminology).
- **Lexicon notes:** Terms like внутрішня форма слова (inner form of the word), повноголосся (pleophony), етимологія, фольклор.
- **Stylistic features:** Analytical, dense, philosophical. He combined rigorous historical phonology with the psychology of folklore.

## 6. Contested points

- **Debated in scholarship:** The extent of his "Russianness" vs "Ukrainianness." Soviet historiography exclusively labeled him a "Russian philologist," while modern Ukrainian scholarship reclaims him as a foundational Ukrainian thinker who systematically proved the independence of the Ukrainian language.
- **Simplified in memory:** Often reduced to just a grammarian, missing his profound philosophical contributions to aesthetics and the psychology of creativity (the "inner form of the word").
- **Russian disinformation:** Russian academic circles still appropriate him entirely, ignoring his active participation in the Kharkiv Hromada and his explicit defenses of the Ukrainian language's right to exist independently of Russian.
