## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Гео́ргій (Ю́рій) Дани́лович Шкурупі́й, who wrote under the pen-name **Ґео Шкурупій**. The pen-name is built on Георгій → "Geo". [T1: IEU; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Ґео Шкурупій; Гео Шкурупій; self-styled **«Король футуропрерій»** ("King of the Futuro-Prairies"); typographic signature "GEO O GE". Forbidden in body text except here: the Russified transliterations *Geo Shkurupiy / Гео Шкурупий*. [T3: uk.wikipedia; T5: Тиждень — Цимбал]
- **Born:** 20 April 1903 | Тягиня (= Бендери / Bender), Бессарабська губернія | Russian Empire; the town is now in Молдова. Both Tier 1 forms refer to one city: IEU uses "Bendery", the Ukrainian tradition uses the Cossack-era name **Тягиня**. [T1: IEU; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Died:** 8 December 1937 | Ленінград (за іншими даними — Ленінградська область) | shot after resentencing by an NKVD "особлива трійка". [T1: IEU; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Family / education key facts:** Son of a railway worker and a teacher; spent childhood on Поділля. Graduated the Second Kyiv Classical Gymnasium (1920); studied one year at the medical faculty of Kyiv University, then at the Kyiv Institute of Foreign Relations. Worked on the railway, then as editor/screenwriter at film studios and in the newspaper «Більшовик». Wife — Варвара Базас; son — Георгій. [T3: uk.wikipedia; T5: Тиждень — Цимбал]

**Source agreement note:** Birth date (20 April 1903) and death date (8 December 1937, Leningrad) agree across IEU (Tier 1) and the Ukrainian Wikipedia archival summary (Tier 3). The two differ only on framing the birthplace name (Bendery vs Тягиня — same city) and on the precise death locus (Ленінград vs Ленінградська область).

## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** arrest → fabricated "terrorist organisation" case → military-tribunal sentence to 10 years of corrective-labour camps → Solovki → resentencing to death by an NKVD troika → execution by shooting; family punished as relatives of an "enemy of the people".
- **When (specific dates):** arrested in Kyiv **3 December 1934**; two sessions of a military tribunal; sentenced **27 April 1935** to 10 years of corrective-labour camps plus three years' subsequent loss of political rights and confiscation of property; the "особлива трійка" reviewed the case **25 November 1937** and ordered death; shot **8 December 1937**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **By whom (agency):** НКВС (NKVD) of the Ukrainian SSR — investigators named in the file as Гринер/Гринера and Грушевський; a military tribunal for the 1935 sentence; an NKVD "особлива трійка" for the 1937 death sentence.
- **Document references:** the archival-criminal case charges membership in a "Київська терористична організація ОУН" ("Об'єднання українських націоналістів"). His own written complaint to the court (dated in the file 15 January 1935) is preserved in the case materials. [T3: uk.wikipedia, citing the archival case]
- **Mechanism specifics:** The arrest came in the wave that followed the December 1934 Kirov murder, when the regime manufactured "terrorist" conspiracies against the Ukrainian cultural intelligentsia. Shkurupii categorically denied the charges across both tribunal sessions and filed a written protest that his testimony had been falsified under moral pressure and the threat-induced collapse of a sick, ulcer-stricken man. After the first trial the case was sent back for "additional investigation"; the second tribunal still convicted him. He served the camp sentence on **Соловки**. In the autumn-1937 escalation of the Great Terror, his ten-year sentence was converted to execution.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** His books were withdrawn and his name erased from Soviet literary history for two decades; the surviving texts had to be recovered later from periodicals and diaspora reprints. His wife Варвара Базас was classed **«ЖИР» (дружина зрадника батьківщини)** and, with their son Георгій, exiled from Kyiv as family of an "enemy of the people". He was posthumously rehabilitated by the same Soviet regime in **1957**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]

> **Myth-control note (do not write Sandarmokh):** The famous «Соловецький етап» shot at **Сандармох** (Karelia) was executed 27 October – 4 November 1937 (1111 prisoners, incl. Kulish, Kurbas, Zerov, Pidmohylny). Shkurupii's death is dated **8 December 1937 in Leningrad** — a *later, separate* execution. By date alone he cannot belong to the Sandarmokh batch; the dossier and any module must NOT place him at Sandarmokh.

- `1929` — *Для друзів, поетів, сучасників вічності*, summary poetry collection. [T3; T5]
- `1933` — *Зима 1930 року*, fragmentary verse-and-prose poema, Kharkiv–Kyiv. [T3]

Prose / cinema / editing:

- `1925` — *Пригоди машиніста Хорна*, short stories. [T3]

- `1927` — *Зустріч на перехресній станції. Розмова трьох* (with Михайль Семенко and Микола Бажан), panfuturist polemic. [T3; T5]

- `1930` — *Жанна-батальйонерка*, novel. [T1: IEU; T3; T5]
- `1930` — heads the Kyiv branch of «Нова генерація» and edits *Авангард — альманах пролетарських митців Н. Ґ.*, which **first printed Oleksandr Dovzhenko's screenplay «Земля»**. [T3]
- `1932` — *Монгольські оповідання*, short stories. [T3; T5]
- `1934` — *Міс Адрієна*, novel. [T1: IEU; T3]
- `2013` — *Вибрані твори*, Kyiv: Смолоскип — posthumous recovery edition. [T3]

Affiliations: «Комкосмос» (Kyiv, 1921), Аспанфут (1921–1924), АсКК/Комункульт (1924–1925), ВАПЛІТЕ (1926–1927), «Нова генерація» (1927–1931), the futurist group «Флямінго».

## 4. Primary-source quotes (≥2 required)

**Quote 1 — self-definition, cover of *Психетози* (1922):**

> **Король футуропрерій**

His own coined title ("King of the Futuro-Prairies"), printed on his first book. *Футуропрерії* is his neologism (футуро- + прерії). Pedagogically it is the perfect entry point to futurist self-mythologising and to авангард word-creation. [T3: uk.wikipedia; T5: Тиждень — Цимбал]

**Quote 2 — lyric «Барабан печалі», from *Барабан* (1923):**

> **Очі твої підо мною, перса і тіло твоє… / Руки в траві розкинуті, вогкі долоні в долонях моїх.**

Shows that beneath the futurist drum-beat there is intimate lyricism — useful for teaching that "avant-garde" was not only noise and machines. [T5: Тиждень — Цимбал; ukrainian-poetry.com]

**Quote 3 (documentary voice) — written complaint to the tribunal, case file, dated 15 January 1935:**

> **Мої бесіди названі націоналістичними, як і мої настрої. Насправді ж я вказував слідчому, що бесіди були на літературні, історичні і побутові теми… Тому прошу вважати ці мої показання недійсними, оскільки фактично вони не мої, а складені за думкою і бажанням слідчого.**

His own recorded words documenting falsification of testimony under pressure — the load-bearing primary source for the oppression-mechanism section. [T3: uk.wikipedia, quoting the archival case]

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** experimental high-modernist / panfuturist — declarative manifestos, urban-industrial imagery, coined compounds and neologisms, montage prose; alongside surprisingly tender neoromantic lyric.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** C1 for independent reading of the prose and manifestos; B2 with glossary for the lyric.
- **Lexicon notes:** author-coined words (футуропрерії), futurist technical/urban vocabulary, 1920s Kharkiv-orthography forms (including the letter **ґ**, which his pen-name itself uses). Watch the орфографічна history: ґ was abolished by the 1933 reform and only restored in 1990 — hence the doubled tradition Ґео / Гео.
- **Stylistic features:** rhythmic refrain ("Бий, бий, барабан печалі!"), montage and reportage technique, irony and self-mythology, the "experimental / left" novel form.

## 6. Contested points

- **What modern UA scholarship debates:** how seriously to take panfuturist theory vs. treating it as provocation; whether his strongest legacy is the experimental novel *Двері в день* or the early lyric. Yaryna Tsymbal's recent work reframes him as a major, unjustly forgotten 1920s figure, not a footnote to Семенко. [T5: Тиждень — Цимбал; T2/T4: Ільницький]
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** he is often reduced to "a futurist who was shot". The richer truth is a writer who moved through futurism into prose, cinema and reportage, and who actively *resisted* the falsified charges in court.
- **Orthography of the name:** the canonical form is **Ґео** (with ґ) — the figure's own pen-name, restored in modern editions (Смолоскип, 2013) and used as the Ukrainian Wikipedia article title (`Ґео Шкурупій`). The form **Гео** without ґ reflects the Soviet-era abolition of the letter ґ (1933 reform; restored 1990) and is treated here as a historical variant, NOT the canonical name. The slug remains `heo-shkurupii` (a Latin transliteration that does not encode ґ); all Ukrainian-language prose must use **Ґео Шкурупій**.
- **Where Russocentric framing attacks:** Soviet/Russian summaries inherit the security-police vocabulary ("терорист", "буржуазний націоналіст") and the УРЕ silence; these are evidence of the regime's framing, not reliable identity claims (see source-tier special rule).
