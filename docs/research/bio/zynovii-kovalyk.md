## 1. Verified facts

- **Name — four forms, none declared canonical.** ESU: «Ковалик Зеновій» [S1].
  Vatican: «Zynovij Kovalyk» [S4]. Church usage: «Зенон» and «Зиновій» [S2]
  [S3]. Course slug: `zynovii-kovalyk`. The divergence is recorded without
  inferring why the publishers chose different forms.
- **Born:** 18.08.1903, «с. Івачів Горішній, нині Терноп. р-ну Терноп. обл.»
  [S1]; the order's page adds a poor peasant family [S2].
- **Died:** ESU gives «26.06.1941, Львів» [S1] — the only adopted source with an
  exact date. [S2] and [S3] date the killing to 1941 without a day. Vatican
  frame: «(1903-1941)» [S4]. **Not reconciled**; the day is carried with
  attribution to ESU alone.
- **Before the monastery:** «Учителював» [S1]; a junior-class teacher in a
  village school [S2].
- **Redemptorists:** entered 1925 [S1]; vows «у серпні 1926» [S1] / «28 серпня
  1926 року… перші монаші обіти» [S2].
- **Studies:** philosophy and theology in Belgium, 1926–32 [S1] [S2].
- **Office in Lviv:** «від 1939 — економ Львів. монастиря» [S1]; «економа
  монастиря» [S2] — the monastery on вул. Зибликевича (нині Івана Франка) [S2].
- **Beatified:** 27 червня 2001 у Львові [S1]; decree signed 24 квітня 2001,
  proclamation 27 червня [S2]. Two distinct acts — not a divergence.
- **Memorial:** «2003 у рідному селі К. встановлено мемор. дошку» [S1].

### Ordination date — recorded, not resolved

| Source | Date |
| --- | --- |

| Redemptorists [S2] | 9 серпня 1932 |
| UGCC Synod [S3] | «4 вересня 1932 р., рукоположений на священика» |

Two of three give 9 August; the third gives 4 September. No document resolving
this was retrieved, so the packet records the split rather than taking the
majority as fact.

### Agreement worth recording without a provenance inference

ESU [S1] and the order's page [S2] both give the 9 August 1932 ordination and
the office «економ». That agreement is recorded, but separate publishers and
different institutional labels do not by themselves prove independent evidence
chains. The widely circulated claim that Kovalyk was the Lviv monastery's
**superior/rector** is supported by **no** adopted source; it is not used.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** arrest (December 1940), six months of interrogation,
  killing (1941). The order's page [S2] alone supplies the ground «за
  проповіді»; no adopted charge file confirms or excludes that cause.
- **Arrest:** ESU: «20 грудня 1940 заарешт.» [S1]. The order's page: «Вночі з 20
  на 21 грудня 1940 року до монастиря прийшли агенти НКВД» [S2]. The Synod
  agrees with the latter: «Арештовано його у ніч з 20 на 21 грудня 1940 р.»
  [S3]. One source gives a date; two give a span of two dates. Not merged.
- **Reason for arrest as reported by [S2] only:** the order's page says the
  NKVD came for him
  «за проповіді, які саме в ті дні він виголошував у церкві при монастирі на
  вул. Зибликевича під час дев'ятниці на честь Непорочного Зачаття» [S2]
  (paragraph on the night arrest after the Immaculate Conception novena
  preaching). No case file, charge text or interrogation protocol was
  retrieved, so the packet does not treat [S2]'s ground as a settled cause,
  infer other formal allegations, or exclude them.
- **Custody:** «У камері, де він перебував ув'язнення було 32 в'язні (при тому,
  що розмір камери був 4,2 на 3,5 м.)» [S3] — 14.7 m², i.e. roughly 0.46 m² per
  person. «За час своєї шестимісячної неволі він відбув двадцять вісім тяжких
  допитів» [S2] — about one interrogation a week for half a year.
- **Killing:** ESU states: «20 грудня 1940 заарешт., розіпʼятий на стіні тюрем.
  коридору» [S1]. The order's page describes the same: crucified against the
  prison corridor wall in 1941 [S2]; the Synod: «У 1941 р. розп'ятий на стіні»
  [S3]. The agreement is recorded without claiming that their evidence chains
  are independent.
- **What is not available:** no case number, no charge text, no protocols of the
  twenty-eight interrogations were retrieved, and no adopted source reports what
  became of his body afterward. No adopted source names the individual
  perpetrators, so the packet does not either.

## 4. Secondary institutional transcriptions — two carried by [S2], full form

**Evidence class.** Neither passage is adopted as an original memorial card,
handwritten autograph, or direct contemporary witness text. Both are
**secondary institutional transcriptions** on the Redemptorist provincial page
[S2] about its own member. The packet quotes [S2]'s wording with attribution
and paragraph locators; it does not promote them to primary documents.

**Re-grounding correction.** The prior version of this dossier said neither
proposed quotation was carried by the adopted set. That was wrong: **[S2]
carries both**, in fuller and materially different wording than the two
shortened/altered Phase-1 fragments. Both Phase-1 fragments are still
rejected as inexact; the source-verified full wording below replaces them.

- **First-Liturgy memorial-card prayer (secondary transcription on [S2]).**
  Locator: [S2], narrative paragraph after the 9 August 1932 ordination,
  opening «4 вересня 1932 р. він ступив на землю своїх батьків…» and
  introducing «На пам'ятному образкові написав такі слова». Exact [S2]
  wording: «4 вересня 1932 р. він ступив на землю своїх батьків, в рідне
  село Івачів, щоб відправити свою першу Службу Божу. На пам'ятному
  образкові написав такі слова: "О Ісусе, прийми мене зі св. Жертвою Твого
  св. Тіла й Крови. Прийми її за св. Церков, за моє Згромадження та мою
  Вітчизну"». [S2] then narrates the words as prophetic; that is the order's
  interpretation, not an independent finding. The Phase-1 fragment «О Ісусе!
  Прийми мене» truncates the opening clause and invents an exclamation mark
  not in [S2]; it is not used. The full sentence above, as carried by [S2],
  is what the packet quotes.
- **Response to warnings of danger (secondary transcription on [S2]).**
  Locator: [S2], paragraph on open preaching under the first Soviet
  occupation, immediately before the Ternopil 28 August 1940 novena note,
  beginning «У ті часи, коли галичан огортав страх…» and containing «Коли
  його застерігали, попереджуючи про небезпеку…». Exact [S2] wording:
  «Коли його застерігали, попереджуючи про небезпеку, о. Зенон мав лише
  одну відповідь: "Якщо буде така Божа воля, то я радо прийму й смерть, але
  як проповідник не буду ніколи кривити душею"». [S2] places this **before
  the December 1940 arrest**, as reported speech to associates who warned
  him — not as prison testimony and not as a recovered original utterance
  record. The Phase-1 fragment «Якщо така воля Божа, я радо прийму і
  смерть» drops «буде», changes «й» to «і», and cuts «але як проповідник не
  буду ніколи кривити душею». Phase 1 also mis-sourced the fragment as the
  title of a 2021 Redemptorist biography; that attribution is dropped. The
  full sentence above, as carried by [S2], is what the packet quotes, at the
  pre-arrest defiance point only.

Both passages are exact transcriptions of [S2]'s institutional wording, with
paragraph locators on that page. Neither original card nor independent
witness text is adopted; [S2] remains interested order testimony used with
attribution, not independent confirmation.

## 6. Contested points

- **Ordination date** — 9 August vs 4 September 1932; unresolved above.
- **Exact death date** — ESU alone gives 26.06.1941 [S1]; unresolved above.
- **Arrest date vs span** — 20 December [S1] vs the night of 20–21 December [S2]
  [S3].
- **Name form** — four attested forms; none declared canonical (§1). The course
  slug is a file identifier, not a normative ruling.
- **Patronymic — removed.** Phase 1 titled this dossier «Ковалик Зиновій
  Григорович». **No adopted source gives any patronymic**; ESU gives «Ковалик
  Зеновій» with none [S1]. The patronymic is therefore dropped from the title and
  from §8 rather than carried unsourced.
- **Method of killing** — stated because a secular academic reference states it
  [S1], not merely because testimony does. Still, no forensic or archival
  document was retrieved; the packet does not add detail beyond the sources.
- **What popular memory eclipses** — the crucifixion can swallow the formation,
  the preaching and the role among fellow prisoners. The packet keeps the
  chronology and the testimony layer distinct.
- **Lviv prison massacres of June 1941** — a wider context with many victim
  groups. This dossier covers Kovalyk only and does not generalize beyond the
  adopted sources.
