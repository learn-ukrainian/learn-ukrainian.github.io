Vakulenko was a children's writer **abducted on 24 March 2022** from occupied Капитолівка and **murdered** by Russian/proxy forces; his **occupation diary, buried under a cherry tree, was recovered by Victoria Amelina**. ## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is a **documented abduction-and-murder under Russian occupation**, with an unusually complete forensic and investigative chain.

- **What happened:** Russian forces occupied **Капитолівка on 7 March 2022**. On **23 березня** they searched Vakulenko's home, seized books, detained him and his teenage son, **beat him**, and released him after a three-hour interrogation. The **next day, 24 березня 2022**, the same servicemen returned, **abducted him by force** and drove him away in a car marked **«Z»**. According to his ex-wife Ірина Новицька, he was taken **on a denunciation**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Загибель"; T5: BBC News Україна, Нечепоренко, 06.12.2022]
- **When killed / found:** witnesses say the Russians told locals to **bury his body on 12 травня 2022**. After Izyum's liberation, a **mass grave of ≥400 bodies** was found; Vakulenko was **№ 319**. Prosecutors first denied it, but **Суспільне** investigators matched a pre-burial photo (shot-through documents; a recognisable arm tattoo), and **DNA analysis in late November 2022 confirmed** his death. **Two Makarov-pistol bullets** were recovered from the body. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Загибель"]
- **By whom (specific):** the **2025 investigation** concluded the perpetrators were natives of Luhansk oblast serving in the **4th company, 4th battalion, 204th rifle regiment of the mobilisation reserve of the so-called "LNR," 2nd Army Corps, 8th Combined-Arms Army, Southern Military District of the RF Armed Forces**. He was **offered collaboration**, pressed to renounce his pro-Ukrainian views, and — on refusal — **tortured and killed**. Named: a 50-year-old machine-gunner, callsign **«Удод»** (fired the two shots), and the 33-year-old company commander, callsign **«Лев»** (gave the order). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Розслідування вбивства"]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** his books survived; above all, his **occupation diary survived because he buried it under a cherry tree in his garden the day before the abduction**, instructing his father to give it to "our people" when they returned (§4). It was **recovered by Victoria Amelina** (per his father) and edited into the posthumous **«Я перетворююсь… Щоденник окупації»** (Vivat, 2023). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Загибель"; T5: Vivat / Читомо]
- **Posthumous status:** **Герой України** (1 жовтня 2025, посмертно); **орден «За заслуги» III ст.** (2024); **Prix Voltaire** special distinction (2023). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Нагороди"]

## 3. Major works

- `2011` — **«Сонечкова сім'я»** (Нова Дитяча Література) — children's verse and tales (later adapted for visually-impaired children, universal-design edition, 2020). [T3]
- `2012` — **«Ми, Провінція!»** (Крок, Тернопіль) — early nonconformist poetry (1998–2006). [T3]
- `2014` — **«Татусева книга»** (Видавництво Старого Лева) — children's poems (audio edition 2020). [T3]
- `2016` — **«Інфаркт для ґурманів. Щоденник вар'ята»** (Твердиня, Луцьк) — adult prose; English Kindle excerpt *Heart Attack for Gourmets*. [T3]
- `2016` — **«Святославова Сварґа»** (Ліра-К) — poetry. [T3]
- `2019` — **«Лелечині ключі»** (universal-design children's edition). [T3]
- `2021` — **«Три засніжені історії»** (tales); **«Король вірусів Ковід»** (universal-design). [T3]
- `2023` (posthumous) — **«Я перетворююсь… Щоденник окупації. Вибрані вірші»** (Vivat) — his occupation diary plus selected poems. [T3]
- `2024` (posthumous) — **«Маленькі казки великих патріотів / Modest Tales of Worthy Patriots»** (bilingual). [T3]
- editor/compiler of the East-Ukrainian anthology **«Кальміюс»** (Краматорськ, 2017) and the journals «Ізюмська гора» (2007), «Dzerkalo» (2009); ran the **«Зірки на долоньках»** universal-design series for visually-impaired children. [T3]

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** **dual.** His **children's literature** (Сонечкова сім'я, Татусева книга) is warm, simple, rhythmic — among the **most CEFR-accessible** material of any figure in this batch. His **occupation diary and late poems** are spare, dark, documentary; his earlier "nonconformist" / "counter-literary" poetry is denser and more experimental.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** **A2–B1** for the children's verse (a genuine asset for lower-level reading units); **B2** for the occupation diary.
- **Lexicon notes:** children's-register vocabulary (lullaby, family, animals — колискова, сонечко, татусь); occupation-register (окупація, обшук, викрадення, щоденник) in the diary.
- **Stylistic features:** in the children's work, songlike rhyme and tenderness (one diary poem, «Листопадова колискова», was set to music in 2025); in the diary, plain first-person witness.
