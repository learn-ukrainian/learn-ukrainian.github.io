## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is a state-builder driven into lifelong exile; the mechanism is the
Bolshevik destruction of Ukrainian statehood and the subsequent Soviet ban that
erased him at home.

- **What happened:** Forced permanent emigration (1919) after the fall of
  Ukrainian statehood, followed by a Soviet-era publication ban that removed his
  work from Ukraine until 1991.
- **When (specific dates):** Doroshenko went into exile in 1919 and never lived in
  Ukraine again — Prague (1920s–1930s, with a Berlin institute directorship
  1926–1931), then Western Germany (from 1945) and Canada (1947), dying in Munich
  in 1951; his «Нарис історії України» could be republished in Kyiv only in 1992
  [T3: uk.wikipedia].
- **By whom (specific Russian/Soviet/RF agency):** the Bolshevik (Soviet Russian)
  military conquest of Ukraine and the subsequent Soviet censorship/historiography
  apparatus.
- **Document references:** the 1919 emigration, the 1992 first Kyiv reprint of
  «Нарис історії України», and his being co-founder of the Museum of the
  Ukrainian Liberation Struggle in Prague are the documentary anchors.
- **Mechanism specifics:** the empire did not imprison Doroshenko — Bolshevik
  conquest made his country impossible to live in, and Soviet historiography then
  wrote him out as a "bourgeois nationalist" historian of a state it denied ever
  existed. He answered by building Ukrainian scholarship abroad, including the
  first scholarly survey of Ukraine as a process of state development.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** his roughly 1000 works survived in
  emigration and returned after 1991; the Ukrainian state he served as foreign
  minister was destroyed, and his diplomatic archive was scattered.

## 6. Contested points

This is a politically charged state-building biography (Block-G-equivalent), so
this section is mandatory and extended.

- **What's debated in modern UA scholarship:** Doroshenko's politics are the core
  debate. He served as foreign minister of the 1918 Українська Держава under
  Hetman Павло Скоропадський, and after 1919 he co-founded the monarchist/hetman
  -movement Український союз хліборобів-державників with В'ячеслав Липинський.
  Ukrainian historiography debates the Hetmanate-vs-UNR/Directory question: was
  the conservative, state-continuity, hetmanite line he represented a realistic
  alternative to the socialist UNR, or a German-backed dead end? His refusal in
  1917 to form a Central Rada secretariat over disagreements with Hrushevsky also
  marks him as a distinct, non-Hrushevskyan voice. A careful module must present
  the genuine intra-Ukrainian disagreement rather than flatten it.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** popular memory remembers either the
  diplomat-minister or the historian, rarely both, and tends to drop his
  monarchist hetmanite convictions entirely. Lead with the historian who gave
  Ukraine the first state-centred survey of its own past, while naming his
  political line honestly.
- **Where modern Russian disinformation attacks them (if applicable):** Soviet and
  modern Russian framing denies that an independent Ukrainian state existed in
  1917–1921 and brands its officials "bourgeois nationalists"; Doroshenko's
  ministry, his diplomacy, and his scholarship are direct evidence against that
  denial, which is precisely why his work was banned until 1991. Counter the
  denial with the documentary record of the Українська Держава's foreign relations.
- **Polish / Jewish / other-perspective considerations (if applicable):** his
  conservative hetmanite circle and his church-history work intersect with
  Polish, Russian-émigré, and Orthodox-Church debates of the interwar emigration;
  frame these as the contested politics of exile, not as settled positions.
- **HIST-alignment check (+77 roster requirement):** the historical frame —
  Українська революція 1917–1921, the Центральна Рада, the Гетьманат, the
  Берестейський мир, and the emigration — must align with the HIST track rather
  than being invented in the BIO module. Record this alignment requirement in the
  plan metadata.
