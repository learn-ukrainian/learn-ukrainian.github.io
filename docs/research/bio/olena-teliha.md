> **Framing note (read first).** This is an NPOV-severe figure and the truth runs in two directions at once. Teliha was **shot by the Gestapo** at Babyn Yar in 1942 — her physical killer was the Nazi occupation, not the USSR, and this dossier states that plainly (correcting a common popular slippage that files her among "Soviet victims"). The "Russia-oppressed" frame applies through the **second mechanism**: Soviet power drove her family into emigration, erased her from the canon of Soviet Ukraine as a «буржуазна націоналістка», and the movement she served (OUN) was the Soviet state's prime target for four decades. Honour without hagiography: she was a member of the OUN whose 1941 похідні групи entered Ukraine behind the German advance hoping to rebuild Ukrainian statehood, and that hope collapsed into Nazi persecution within months. Both facts are held here.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section, and it is **double**.

- **Primary (physical) — execution by the Nazi occupation.** After the OUN(m) походна група reached Kyiv (Теліга прибула 22 жовтня 1941), she headed the **Спілку українських письменників** and edited **«Літаври»**, the literary supplement to «Українське слово». The German authorities arrested the «Українське слово» editorial board on **12 грудня 1941** and banned «Літаври». On **7 лютого 1942** mass arrests of nationalists began. Warned of a Gestapo ambush at the Spilka premises on вул. Трьохсвятительській, 23, Teliha refused to flee; she was arrested there on **9 лютого 1942** together with her husband Михайло (who was not a Spilka member but refused to leave her). She was **shot by the Gestapo (Sicherheitspolizei/SD)**, with other OUN members, at **Бабин Яр**; the date is commemorated as **21 лютого 1942**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Арешт і загибель"; T4: Попович М. *Нарис історії культури України* — "активісти ОУН(м) Олена Теліга та інші були розстріляні в тому ж Бабиному яру"]
- **By whom (specific agency):** German **гестапо / Sicherheitspolizei und SD** of Reichskommissariat Ukraine. This is **not** a Soviet killing, and the dossier does not pretend otherwise.
- **Secondary (the "Russia-oppressed" mechanism) — Soviet displacement and erasure.** Soviet power was the reason her father's UNR-era institutions were destroyed and the family was driven into emigration (1920–1922); in Soviet Ukraine her name was suppressed for half a century and her work branded «буржуазно-націоналістична», recovered openly only from the late 1980s (Череватенко, «Україна», 1989). The movement she belonged to (OUN) was the Soviet state's principal target of repression into the 1950s. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Вшанування пам'яті"; T1: Політична енциклопедія — Патриляк І. "Теліга Олена"]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** the place and exact date of death are unknown and there is no grave — a symbolic Пам'ятний Хрест was raised at Babyn Yar in 1992 and a stone monument in 2017. Her poetic corpus survived through emigration editions (§3); her letters and memoirs were collected by N. Myronets (2003).

## 3. Major works

Teliha's poetic œuvre is small — **38 known poems** — but canonical for the Празька (Вісниківська) школа. Selected, chronological:

- `1933–1939` — poems and essays in **«Вістник»** (ed. Дмитро Донцов, Львів), the journal that shaped her nationalist worldview. [T3: uk.wikipedia; T4: Просалова В. *Празька літературна школа*]
- `1935` — **«Якими нас прагнете?»** (Вістник, кн. 10) — feminist-polemical essay on the role of the Ukrainian woman in the national struggle. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1937` — **«Сила через радість»** (реферат / essay). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1938` — **«Сліпа вулиця»** (Вістник, кн. 4) — essay. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1941` — publicistic essays **«Розсипаються мури»**, **«Братерство в народі»** (газета «Волинь», ред. У. Самчук, Рівне). [T4: Самчук У. *На білому коні* — ukrlib-samchuk]
- `1941–1942` — editor of **«Літаври»** (Київ). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `posthumous` — collections **«Душа на сторожі»** (1946), **«Прапори духа»** (1947), **«Полум'яні межі»** (1977), **«О краю мій…»** (К., 1999), **«Листи. Спогади»** (упоряд. Н. Миронець, 2003). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Окремі видання"]

Key poems include «Сучасникам», «Поворот», «Лист», «Вечірня пісня», «Мужчинам», «Пломінний день».

**Quote 1 — corpus-verbatim quatrain (her poetry, as transmitted in the corpus):**

> «Залізну силу, що не має меж, / Дихання Боже в сльози перетопить, / І скрутить бич безжалісних пожеж / З маленьких іскор, схованих у попіл.»

Reproduced verbatim by Самчук, who quotes Teliha reciting it at their Kraków meeting; it concentrates her signature imagery — fire forged from buried sparks, force turned through tears into will. [Corpus-attested: ukrlib-samchuk, «На білому коні», chunk `abce6531_c0046`]

**Quote 2 — recorded last words before her arrest (recorded speech):**

> «Коли я не повернусь, то не забувайте про мене. Коли я загину, то знайте, що свій обов'язок сповнила до кінця.»

Said to friends who warned her of the Gestapo ambush at the Спілка premises in February 1942. Pedagogically it is the hinge between her life and her myth — the chosen, foreknown death she also rendered poetically as «Гарячу смерть — не зимне умирання» in the poem «Лист». [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Арешт і загибель"]

**Quote 3 — her essayistic voice, «Якими нас прагнете?» (1935), prose:**

> «Роль української жінки є так само винятковою, як винятковим є положення її краю. Вона мусить бути і його будівничим, допомагаючи мужчинам, і в той же час господинею в житті мужчин.»

The core of her contested gender-politics (see §6). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Життєпис", citing «Якими нас прагнете?», Вістник 1935, кн. 10]

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** **high modernist, passionate-heroic lyric** — what literary scholarship calls the «пасіонарність любовної лірики». Dontsov christened her «поетка вогняних меж». [T4: Просалова; T4: Русанівський В. — wave9 corpus]
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** **B2–C1.** The syntax is compact and the imagery dense (fire/wind/sun symbolism), but the lexicon is largely standard literary Ukrainian — accessible earlier than, say, Olzhych's archaising historiosophy.
- **Lexicon notes:** recurrent fire-and-light field — пожежа, вогонь, іскра, попіл, заграва, пломінний, полум'яні (межі); will-and-steel field — залізна (сила), бич, спис. All standard literary forms.
- **Stylistic features:** antithesis (ніжність ↔ зброя, спокій ↔ буря), prophetic first-person (the poem «Поворот» imagines a return she never made; «Лист» foretells her own death), and an "intimate-heroic" voice that fuses love lyric with martial resolve.

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated in modern scholarship:** (1) **Birth year and birthplace** — older sources give 1907 and sometimes blur Ільїнське/Петербург; Миронець's biographical work corrects to **1906** and to Ільїнське-near-Moscow (raised in Petersburg from age 5), and explicitly catalogues the «неточності, помилки, фальсифікації» around her life. (2) **Her father's rank** — popular accounts inflate Іван Шовгенів into a "minister"; he was **director of a department** of the UNR Ministry of Roads, later академічний ректор — a real but more modest standing. [T2/T4: Миронець 2017]
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** her killing is sometimes folded into the generic "Soviet victim" narrative; in fact she was **executed by the Gestapo**. The flattening erases the specific tragedy — an anti-Soviet émigré returning under Nazi occupation and being murdered by that occupation.
- **Gender politics (genuinely contested):** in «Якими нас прагнете?» Teliha set herself **against Western feminism**, locating the woman's mission in «ніжність» and in sustaining men's fighting spirit. Modern Ukrainian feminist criticism is split — some (Ганна Улюра and others) reclaim her as a strong female voice who lived an entirely unconventional life; others read the essays as subordinating women to a martial-national ideal. The honest position presents both her radical biographical autonomy and the conservative content of her programmatic prose. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Гендерні погляди"; T5: Улюра Г. *Ніч на Венері*, 2020]
- **Ideological proximity to Dontsov:** she was a central «вісниківець» and Dontsov's integral nationalism shaped her worldview; this is not hidden. The OUN(m) походні групи of 1941 entered behind the Wehrmacht hoping to rebuild Ukrainian institutions — a hope that the Nazi regime crushed by December 1941. Naming this initial cooperation-then-persecution is required for NPOV. [T4: Попович М.]
- **Russian/Soviet disinformation:** Soviet historiography branded the OUN milieu «буржуазні націоналісти» and suppressed her name; modern Russian propaganda recycles the "Nazi-collaborator" smear against the whole movement. The corrective is honesty about the Gestapo's role in her death, not denial of the OUN context.
