- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Володимир **Михайлович** Івасюк. [T2: ULP Lesson 239; T3: literary encyclopedia entry in `literary_texts` — «ІВАСЮК Володимир Михайлович (4.III 1949, м. Кіцмань…»)]

- **Died:** **18 травня 1979** (aged 30) | found in **forest near Bryukhovychi** (Lviv area) | official ruling **suicide**; widespread belief in **murder**. Last seen **24 квітня 1979** leaving Lviv apartment for conservatory. [T2: ULP Lesson 239]
- **Family / education key facts:** Parents **Mykhailo and Alexandra**; brother **Mykhailo** (future composer). Medical institute Chernivtsi (expelled after Lenin-monument incident); **Lviv Conservatory**; over **100 songs**, **50 instrumental works** (ULP). [T2: ULP Lesson 239]

**Source disagreement note:** **Cause of death** — Soviet case closed as suicide; ULP presents murder theories (KGB, enemies) with **explicit epistemic humility** («немає доказів… є лише різні теорії»). Do not teach either pole as settled fact without §6 framing.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** Sudden death after state suspicion of Ukrainian-language pop success; **posthumous harassment** of memory (music restrictions, grave arson attempts).
- **When:** disappearance **24–25 April 1979**; body **18 May 1979**; funeral became **unsanctioned mass gathering** in Lviv. [T2: ULP Lesson 239]
- **By whom:** Soviet **KGB/MVD** ecosystem (ULP mentions KGB hypothetically; official investigation forced **suicide-only** narrative).
- **Document references:** **No NKVD/KGB file ID** retrieved in this pass — **do not invent**. ULP cites parents' testimony that authorities **did not want to search** initially.
- **Mechanism specifics:** Ivasiuk was not a classic dissident (ULP: not public anti-Soviet speaker; wrote Ukrainian music; refused Russian-language songs). Success with **«Червона рута»** via **Pisnya-71** and **Rotaru** LP threatened ideological control of youth culture. After death: taxis free on funeral day, musicians refused restaurant gigs — folk mourning ULP describes; state feared **«несанкціонований мітинг»**.
- **What survived / destroyed:** Song corpus widely distributed via tape/LP; official memory suppressed until **perestroika** revival (ghost-wiki chunk references 1987 father memoir — T3 secondary).
