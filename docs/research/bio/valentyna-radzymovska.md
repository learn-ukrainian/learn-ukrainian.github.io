## 2. Oppression mechanism — fabricated SVU arrest + professional persecution → emigration (NOT execution)

This is the load-bearing correction. Specificity:

- **What happened:** **arrested 14 September 1929** at home on the fabricated charge of «участь в українській підпільній контрреволюційній організації — Спілці визволення України» (СВУ). Held **~7 months in an isolated investigation cell**; **released 29 March 1930** without confessing — possibly through the intervention of the educator Іван Соколянський, a member of the special collegium. [T1: ЕІУ — «заарештована у справі "Спілки визволення України" … просиділа 7 місяців в ізольованій слідчій камері. Звільнена 1930»; T3: uk.wikipedia — arrest date 14.09.1929, release 29.03.1930]
- **By whom:** ДПУ/НКВС УСРР, within the СВУ show-trial apparatus (the 1930 Kharkiv process) — the same campaign that targeted Сергій Єфремов, Володимир Дурдуківський and the Ukrainian intelligentsia.
- **Continued persecution (indirect) after release:** barred «з політичних мотивів» from the physiology chair competition at the Kyiv Medical Institute; her Науково-дослідна кафедра педології was **liquidated in 1930**; in 1940 the NKVD **re-opened her 1929–30 file** but did not re-arrest. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **The terror in her family:** her elder brother **Василь Яновський** was arrested 25 November 1937, sentenced by the Особлива нарада при НКВС СРСР on 29 January 1938, and **shot on 25 March 1938**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]. *This is the executed relative the prior plan mistakenly projected onto Valentyna herself.*
- **Exile:** amid the German occupation she worked in Kyiv and Lviv (1941–44), then emigrated — **Bratislava → Munich** (professor of physiology; chair at the UNRRA/UN University in Munich, then the Ukrainian Technical-Husbandry Institute) → **USA, 1950**, living with her son Yevhen. Died abroad 1953. [T1: ЕІУ; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** ~70 works survive; she founded a Ukrainian school of physiologists/biochemists. Late summarising works mostly **remained unpublished**; her career inside Soviet Ukraine was destroyed by the 1929 case and the blacklisting that followed.

> **Decolonial framing (per the task brief).** The honest arc is *persecution → exile → death abroad*, not martyrdom-by-execution. Inflating a released-and-emigrated scholar into a Terror corpse is exactly the fabrication this rebuild removes. The genuine tragedy is real enough: a fabricated case, professional destruction, a brother shot, and a life finished in exile.

## 3. Major works

- `1922` — «О значении реакции среды для тканевых клеток, культивируемых вне организма» — first publication, *Врачебное дело* (Kharkiv; Russian-language journal of the era). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1923` — *Діти часів революції. Фізичний розвиток дітей України* (monograph; child physiology). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1924` — doctoral dissertation (doctor of physiological sciences) on the effect of H-ion concentration (pH) on cells/tissues cultured outside the organism; reviewers Чаговець, Фомін, Ф. Яновський; partial results in *The Journal of Physiology* and *Biochemische Zeitschrift*. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1927` — «Die Alkalireserven des Blutes und die periodische Tätigkeit des Verdauungsapparates» (German journal). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1930s` — tuberculosis biochemistry (blood phosphorus in bone TB), heliotherapy, adolescent fatigue, neuropathology (incl. epilepsy pathogenesis). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- emigration period — *Фізіологія людини та свійських тварин* (2-vol. textbook, Munich); supervised master's/doctoral dissertations there. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- ~70 works total across physiology, biochemistry, pathophysiology, tuberculosis, neuropsychiatry, paediatrics. Member of НТШ and УВАН. [T3: uk.wikipedia]

### Supporting primary-source detail (from her dissertation text)

uk.wikipedia paraphrases her dissertation: she ran **«понад 50 серій дослідів, загалом понад 1200 чашок з культивованими клітинами кролика»**, and — lacking enough Petri (Habrychevsky) dishes — **made them herself from the bottoms of ordinary bottles, hand-grinding the edges**. A vivid, quotable image of improvised Ukrainian lab science under scarcity. [T3: uk.wikipedia, quoting her dissertation]
