## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Дмитро Іванович Коцюбайло. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Коцюбайло Дмитро Іванович"; T1: Герої України — registry entry]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** call sign **«Да Вінчі»** (from his talent for drawing — he trained as an artist). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Born:** **1 листопада 1995** | село **Задністрянське** | Галицький район, Івано-Франківська область, Україна (нині Бурштинська громада, Івано-Франківський район). [T3: uk.wikipedia — "1 листопада 1995, с. Задністрянське"]
- **Died:** **7 березня 2023** (aged 27) | **Бахмут** | Донецька область, Україна | **killed in combat** during Russia's siege of Bakhmut (a shrapnel wound to the neck near Хромове / Часів Яр). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Повномасштабне вторгнення"; T2: Платформа пам'яті «Меморіал»]
- **Family / education key facts:** Born into a large family (a brother, Іван, and four sisters); **great-grandfather served in the UPA**. Trained as a builder (Ivano-Frankivsk professional construction lyceum) and studied to be an artist. A believer of the **Українська греко-католицька церква**. Partner — **Аліна Михайлова**, head of his battalion's medical service. Buried at **Аскольдова могила**, Kyiv. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Сім'я," §"Особисте життя"]

**Source agreement note:** birth and death dates are consistent across T1 (Президент/Герої України registry), T2 (Меморіал) and T3 (uk.wiki). The earlier project ghost-wiki **omitted** both dates; this dossier closes that gap with the converged record. His patronymic is **Іванович** (not "Михайлович," which appeared in some loose references).

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is a war-dead dossier; the "oppression mechanism" is **death in Russia's war of aggression against Ukraine**, stated factually.

- **What happened:** killed in action while defending **Бахмут** against the Russian assault. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **When (specific):** **7 March 2023.** [T1: Хрест бойових заслуг decree, 9 March 2023, posthumous; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **By whom (specific RF action):** the **Russian Armed Forces** during the **повномасштабне вторгнення** (full-scale invasion launched 24 February 2022); the immediate cause was Russian artillery fire near Хромове / Часів Яр on the Bakhmut axis. Per his deputy ("Капуста"), after holding the Хромове road the group returned to Часів Яр under enemy artillery; an explosion wounded Коцюбайло in the neck, a wound incompatible with life despite rapid evacuation. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Повномасштабне вторгнення"]
- **Mechanism specifics (career under fire):** he had been a target of that same war since 2014 — wounded at **Піски** (Donetsk oblast) in 2014 (shrapnel to the face, broken collarbone and ribs), returning to the front after three months; he fought at Карлівка, Піски, Авдіївка, Степанівка, Савур-Могила, Старогнатівка. The 2022–2023 phase ended at Bakhmut. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Російсько-українська війна"]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** the unit he built survives him — the **1-й окремий штурмовий батальйон «Да Вінчі»**, later the **108-й окремий механізований батальйон «Вовки Да Вінчі»**; by the second anniversary of his death the battalion counted **96 fallen побратими**. One of his twin sisters, Олександра, later signed a contract with the battalion her brother had founded. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Сім'я," §"Вшанування пам'яті"]

## 3. Major works

Not an author — his "works" are military-organisational and civic acts, chronological:

- `2014` — volunteer from the start of the war; platoon commander (взвод) of volunteers, then company commander (рота). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `17 March 2016` — appointed commander of the **1-ша окрема штурмова рота ДУК «Правий сектор»** («Вовки Да Вінчі»). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `2020–2023` — member of the leadership (Провід) of the national-liberation movement **«Правий сектор»**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `30 November 2021` — awarded **Герой України** — the **first volunteer to receive the title during his lifetime**. [T1: Указ Президента, 30.11.2021]
- `2022` — listed in the Forbes "30 до 30" ranking; spring 2022 his assault company was reformed into the **1-й окремий штурмовий батальйон «Вовки Да Вінчі»** within the **67-ма ОМБр ЗСУ**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `2021–2023` — a frequent, articulate interviewee on the war (24 канал, lb.ua, novynarnia, censor.net, 5 канал) — a documentary primary corpus (§4). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Інтерв'ю"]

**Posthumous works about him:** the film *«Да Вінчі»* (2024); D. Savchenko, *Вовки Да Вінчі* (2023); the monument at Аскольдова могила (unveiled 21 November 2024).

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated:** little of his *biography* is disputed (recent, well-documented). The contestable terrain is **political affiliation** — his membership in the Провід of **«Правий сектор»**, a movement Russian propaganda routinely inflates into "Nazi" framing. The honest treatment: he was a far-right-adjacent volunteer-movement figure **and** a regular-army battalion commander under the ЗСУ; neither fact cancels the other, and Russian disinformation exploits the first to erase the second.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** the risk is **myth-padding** — turning a real, complicated 27-year-old into a flawless icon. The corrective is to keep him human: a builder-turned-artist-turned-soldier, wounded young, killed at Bakhmut, with a battalion that itself lost 96 people.
- **Where Russian disinformation attacks:** the "Правий сектор = Nazi" line (used to justify the invasion's "denazification" pretext). Naming it as propaganda is required; using his Правий-сектор affiliation to *delegitimise* him reproduces that propaganda.
- **Other-perspective considerations:** his **great-grandfather's UPA service** ties his story to the longer anti-imperial liberation arc (a deliberate self-understanding in the war generation); present it as the continuity the figure himself invoked, not as imposed mythology.
