## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Юрій Володимирович Шевельов (прізвище при народженні — Шнайдер)
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Юрій Шерех (for literature/culture), Гр. Шевчук, George Y. Shevelov
- **Born:** 17 December 1908 | Харків | Харківська губернія, Російська імперія
- **Died:** 12 April 2002 | New York City | USA | natural causes
- **Family / education key facts:** Born to ethnic German parents (Vladimir Schneider and Varvara Meder); the father changed the surname to Shevelov during WWI due to anti-German sentiment. Educated at Kharkiv University under Leonid Bulakhovskyi. Protected his mother and avoided Soviet terror by meticulous maneuvering.

Cite: [T1: ЕІУ — Шевельов Юрій Володимирович]; [T1: IEU — Shevelov, George Yurii].

## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** Forced into permanent exile (emigration) to survive Soviet terror and WWII; subsequently faced a lifelong, state-sponsored campaign of defamation and erasure by the Soviet academic and security apparatus.
- **When (specific dates):** Left Kharkiv in 1943; emigrated to Germany (1944), Sweden (1950), and the USA (1952). The defamation campaign lasted from the late 1940s until the collapse of the USSR.
- **By whom (specific Russian/Soviet/RF agency):** Soviet academic establishments (KGB-directed campaigns), official Soviet linguistics institutions, and later, post-Soviet pro-Russian politicians in Kharkiv (e.g., Mikhail Dobkin, Hennadiy Kernes in 2013).
- **Document references:** KGB files regarding émigré intellectuals; the Kharkiv City Council decision (25 September 2013) ordering the destruction of his memorial plaque.
- **Mechanism specifics:** Having survived the 1930s terror in Kharkiv, Shevelov chose not to evacuate with the retreating Red Army in 1941. He worked in non-political education roles under the German occupation. Fleeing the returning Soviets in 1943, he became a displaced person (DP) in Germany. The Soviet state branded him a "fascist collaborator" and completely banned his name and works from academia. He was forced to publish his monumental historical phonology abroad. The oppression continued posthumously when pro-Russian authorities in Kharkiv violently destroyed his memorial plaque in 2013.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** His academic legacy survived in the West (Harvard, Columbia, Free Ukrainian University) and returned to Ukraine after 1991. The physical plaque in Kharkiv was destroyed but restored in 2022.

## 3. Major works

- `1951` — *Нарис сучасної української літературної мови*, Munich.
- `1954` — *Москва, Маросєйка*, essay (Boston), a seminal political/cultural analysis.
- `1965` — *A Prehistory of Slavic: The Historical Phonology of Common Slavic*. (Dismantled the Russian imperial theory of a unified "Old Russian" proto-language).
- `1979` — *A Historical Phonology of the Ukrainian Language*, Heidelberg. (His magnum opus, proving the independent evolution of Ukrainian).
- `2001` — *Я — мене — мені… (і довкруги)*, 2-volume memoir, Kharkiv-New York.
- `1964` — *Не для дітей*, literary criticism under the pseudonym Yurii Sherekh.

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** Academic, highly polished diaspora Ukrainian (Kharkiv standard preserved and refined), brilliant essayistic style.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** C1/C2.
- **Lexicon notes:** Diaspora orthography elements (Харківський правопис influences), precise linguistic terminology, culturally loaded idioms (Кочубеївщина).
- **Stylistic features:** Irony, razor-sharp logic, lack of sentimentality or provincialism. He consciously wrote to elevate Ukrainian to a world-class academic standard.

## 6. Contested points

- **Debated in scholarship:** The Roman Jakobson conflict at Harvard/Columbia regarding the origins of East Slavic languages. Shevelov's scientific dismantling of the "Common Russian" (общерусский) proto-language deeply angered the Russian-dominated Western Slavic studies establishment.
- **Simplified in memory:** Sometimes remembered only for the 2013 plaque scandal, overshadowing his status as the greatest Ukrainian linguist of the 20th century.
- **Russian disinformation:** The relentless myth that he was a "Nazi collaborator." He worked in the education department under occupation to survive and avoid starvation, strictly writing on language and history, not politics. The 2013 Kharkiv plaque destruction by "titushky" was driven by this exact Soviet-era disinformation.
