## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** Russian occupation arrest, forced/disputed testimony environment, politically motivated prosecution, 17-year sentence, imprisonment, and release through prisoner exchange.
- **When:** Arrested on 2021-09-04/05 after the first Crimea Platform summit period, according to human-rights and official Ukrainian sources. Sentenced by the Russian occupation "Supreme Court of Crimea" on 2022-09-21 to 17 years in a strict-regime colony, per Human Rights House Foundation and other monitors. Released in a prisoner exchange on 2024-06-28, per President of Ukraine and human-rights reporting.
- **By whom:** Russian occupation FSB/security forces and Russian-controlled occupation courts in Crimea.
- **Document references:** Human-rights reports identify the case as the alleged gas-pipeline sabotage case involving Nariman Dzhelyal and the Akhtemov cousins. Exact Russian case number in retrieved sources: source not found.
- **Mechanism specifics:** Dzhelial was the most senior Mejlis representative still living openly in occupied Crimea. After he participated in international attention around Crimea, Russian occupation authorities arrested him and prosecuted him in a case widely described by Ukrainian and international human-rights actors as politically motivated. The mechanism is contemporary colonial hostage-taking: criminal charges are used to remove a nonviolent political voice, intimidate Crimean Tatars, and punish cooperation with Ukrainian and international Crimea platforms. His 2024 release does not erase the original occupation conviction or the risk to others still held.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** His letters, public statements, and post-release testimony survived; the occupation regime destroyed years of family life and sought to silence Mejlis representation inside Crimea.

Because this is an active contemporary case, Phase 2 must re-check status before publication.
