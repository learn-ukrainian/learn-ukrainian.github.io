# Микола Вінграновський — Research Dossier

Canonical slug: `mykola-vinhranovskyi`

## 1. Identity and research scope

Микола Степанович Вінграновський (7 November 1936, Богопіль/Первомайськ, now Mykolaiv oblast — 26 May 2004, Kyiv) was a Ukrainian poet, prose writer, actor, screenwriter, and film director. ESU identifies him first as a poet and records the Shevchenko Prize, Antonovych Foundation prize, Vernadskyi prize, and the title of Honored Arts Worker of Ukraine [S1]. Ukrainian Wikipedia and the MCP Wikipedia extract frame him as a writer-шістдесятник as well as a Dovzhenko-trained cinema figure [S2]. This dossier treats him as a cultural шістдесятник rather than a political-prisoner case: retrieved sources document censorship and ideological constraint, but no arrest, camp sentence, or KGB case file for him was retrieved.

## 2. Chronology

Вінграновський entered the Kyiv theater institute in 1955 and continued at VGIK under Oleksandr Dovzhenko, graduating in 1961 [S1]. While still a student, he played the lead role in the film `Повість полум'яних літ`, from Dovzhenko's script and Yuliia Solntseva's direction [S1]. His literary debut began with magazine poems in 1957-1958, and his 7 April 1961 `Літературна газета` selection brought broad notice [S1, S2].

The first poetry collection, `Атомні прелюди`, appeared in 1962 alongside emblematic early-sixties books by Vasyl Symonenko and Ivan Drach [S2]. `Сто поезій` followed in 1967, though Ukrainian Wikipedia and the MCP corpus note that censorship left ninety-nine poems in the final printed version [S2]. Later books include `Поезії` (1971), `На срібнім березі` (1978), `Київ` (1982), `Губами теплими і оком золотим`, and `Цю жінку я люблю`, with prose for children and adults including `Первінка`, `Сіроманець`, `Літо на Десні`, and `Северин Наливайко` [S1, S2, S3].

In cinema he worked at the Dovzhenko film studio and directed or co-directed feature and documentary films including `Дочка Стратіона`, `Ескадра повертає на захід`, `Берег надії`, `Дума про Британку`, `Тихі береги`, `Климко`, and documentaries about Andrii Malyshko, Dovzhenko's diaries, Chyhyryn, Baturyn, Halych, and Hetman Sahaidachnyi [S1, S2]. He died in Kyiv on 26 May 2004 and is buried at Baikove cemetery [S1, S2].

## 3. Significance for the bio track

Вінграновський is useful for a biography module because his life joins three tracks that learners often see separately: high modern Ukrainian poetry, Dovzhenko-line cinema, and the cultural politics of the thaw. ESU's account stresses a style of lyric freedom, national time-space, and aesthetic transformation rather than a simple protest biography [S1]. The Ukrainian literary encyclopedia chunk retrieved by `mcp__sources.search_sources` similarly places him among the poets who brought philosophical scale, historical imagination, and formal novelty into late-Soviet Ukrainian poetry [S3].

A well-scoped module should not turn him into a camp dissident. His case shows a different Soviet mechanism: editorial constraint, blocked or delayed projects, and ideological criticism of form and content. This makes him a useful companion to political-prisoner dossiers, because students can compare how the regime disciplined culture through both imprisonment and publishability.

The strongest curriculum framing is "freedom inside form." ESU's interpretation of the poems emphasizes national time-space, beauty, and moral meaning rather than direct manifesto [S1]. The textbook source likewise places him in literary шістдесятництво while preserving his own resistance to the label [S4]. A lesson can therefore hold both facts at once: historians need movement categories, but artists often experience those categories as too narrow. That tension is productive for upper-level learners because it teaches source reading, not only biographical recall.

## 4. Persecution, censorship, and source limits

The strongest retrieved censorship fact is the `Сто поезій` episode: the 1967 book title promised one hundred poems, but censorship left ninety-nine in the printed collection [S2]. ESU also records his unproduced screenplay `Світ без війни` (1960), but it does not explicitly attribute the non-production to censorship, so this dossier does not claim that mechanism beyond "source not found" [S1]. A KHPG-style political-prisoner page, arrest warrant, court sentence, or camp record for Вінграновський was not retrieved: source not found.

The primary quote retrieved through Wikipedia/MCP is his refusal of a rigid generation label: "Немає шістдесятників, сімдесятників..." [S2]. That quote should be used pedagogically to complicate the label, not to erase the historical movement around him.

The dossier also requires a negative finding: no retrieved source documents a Vinhranovskyi arrest, trial, exile, or UHG affiliation. That absence matters. It prevents a false equivalence with Zalyvakha, Shabatura, Strokata, Svitlychna, Shukhevych, or Shumuk, whose imprisonment is directly sourced elsewhere in this batch. It also lets the module show a spectrum of pressure: some figures were sentenced; others faced publication filters, ideological reviews, and the narrowing of permissible Ukrainian cultural expression.

## 5. Works and public actions

Core literary works for a lesson include `Атомні прелюди`, `Сто поезій`, `На срібнім березі`, `Київ`, `Губами теплими і оком золотим`, `Цю жінку я люблю`, `Первінка`, `Сіроманець`, and `Северин Наливайко` [S1, S2]. Core cinema references include `Повість полум'яних літ`, `Берег надії`, `Климко`, and the historical documentary cycle on Chyhyryn, Baturyn, Halych, and Sahaidachnyi [S1].

For language pedagogy, his work is especially useful for nature imagery, intensified metaphor, and civic lyric without direct slogan. The 11th-grade literature textbook chunk retrieved by MCP quotes his own statement that he did not accept fixed generation boxes, while also presenting `Атомні прелюди` as a landmark of sixties literature [S4].

Suggested text set: one short lyric from the early 1960s, one nature poem from a later collection, and a prose excerpt from `Сіроманець` or `Первінка`, paired with a short contextual paragraph about censorship of `Сто поезій`. The source pack does not identify a specific rights-cleared poem excerpt, so the curriculum writer must verify the chosen text separately: source not found for a ready excerpt.

## 8. Pedagogical notes

Use a "movement label versus self-label" activity: students read the sourced quote about "шістдесятники" and then list why later historians still group him with the movement. Keep the persecution task precise: censorship of `Сто поезій` is sourced; arrest is source not found.

A second activity can ask learners to sort claims into "biographical fact," "critical interpretation," and "unsourced claim." For example, ESU's birth/death dates are biographical facts [S1]; ESU's account of aesthetic transformation is critical interpretation [S1]; any claim that `Світ без війни` was banned by a named censor is currently unsourced. This is a good dossier for teaching research discipline because not all source gaps are failures; some are guardrails against embellishment.
