## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Григорій Савич Сковорода. [T1: IEU — "Skovoroda, Hryhorii"; T3: uk.wikipedia — "Григо́рій Са́вич Сковорода́"]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** signed himself **Григорій Вар-Сава** / **Григорій Даніил Сковорода**; called "**український Сократ**" by contemporaries. Russian-imperial transliteration (forbidden in body text, §8 only): Григорий Саввич Сковорода / "Grigory Skovoroda."
- **Born:** 22 November (3 December N.S.) 1722 | село **Чорнухи**, Лубенський полк | **Гетьманщина** (the Cossack Hetmanate, under the Russian crown's growing control). [T1: IEU — "b 3 December 1722 in Chornukhy, Lubny regiment"; T3: uk.wikipedia — "22 листопада (3 грудня) 1722"]
- **Died:** 29 October (9 November N.S.) 1794 (aged 71) | село **Пан-Іванівка** (now **Сковородинівка**, Золочівський район, Харківщина) | Слобідська Україна, Російська імперія | of natural causes at the Kovalivsky estate where he spent his last years. [T1: IEU — "d 9 November 1794 in Pan-Ivanivka, Kharkiv vicegerency (now Skovorodynivka near Zolochiv)"; corpus `ukrlib-skovoroda` corroborates "помер 9 листопада 1794 р. у селі Іванівці… (нині Сковородинівка Золочівського району)"]
- **Family / education key facts:** Born to a poor Cossack family. Studied at the **Києво-Могилянська академія, 1734–1753** (with two interruptions; completed only two of the four theology years). Sang in **Empress Elizabeth's court chapel (придворна капела) in St Petersburg, 1741–1744**; then music director at the **Russian imperial mission in Tokaj, Hungary, 1745–1750**, from where he travelled in Central Europe. Taught poetics at the **Переяславський колегіум (1751)** and at the **Харківський колегіум** (poetics 1759–60, syntax/Greek 1762–64, ethics 1768–69), before choosing the life of a **мандрівний філософ** (itinerant philosopher) for his last ~25 years. ## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section. Skovoroda died in 1794, so the mechanism is **not** in-life arrest — it is **(a) imperial/Soviet appropriation of his identity** and, most concretely, **(b) the 2022 Russian missile destruction of his memorial museum**. The second is among the most literal "oppression mechanisms" in this whole track: a state physically bombing a dead philosopher's house-museum.

**(a) Appropriation (the long mechanism).** The Russian imperial and then Soviet canon recast a Hetmanate-born, Mohyla-Academy-formed Ukrainian as a "**Russian philosopher / the first Russian philosopher**," or smoothed him into an undifferentiated "all-Russian Enlightenment." The instruments were standard: УРЕ-style "український і російський мислитель" double-labelling, editions and curricula that foregrounded the Church-Slavonic surface of his language (§5) to argue he "really" belonged to Russian letters, and the erasure of his Cossack origin, his Ukrainian songs/fables, and his Sloboda-Ukraine life-world. His own biography refutes the appropriation: he **refused** every imperial career offered him — the court-chapel track, monastic-clerical advancement in the imperial church, professorial security — choosing instead poverty and freedom (§4 epitaph).

**(b) The 2022 strike (the concrete mechanism, fully documented).** On the **night of 6 May 2022, at ~23:00**, a **Russian missile struck the roof of the Національний літературно-меморіальний музей Г. С. Сковороди in Сковородинівка (Харківська область)**; fire engulfed all rooms, **a museum guard was wounded**, and **280 m² burned out** (outer walls survived). The poet's statue (by Ігор Ястребов) was recovered from the ashes; the gravestone and the Kavaleridze bust, set apart from the building, were unharmed; the most valuable exhibits had been evacuated in advance. The **Russian Ministry of Defence reported the airstrike**, calling the village "Сковородниково" and **falsely claiming a "military command point" was there** — a textbook disinformation cover. On **4 July 2024 the SBU completed its evidence base and charged (оголосила підозру) Ivan Panchenko, commander of the RF 159th fighter aviation regiment (Western Military District)**: on his order a **Su-35S fired a Kh-35 turbojet anti-ship missile** at the memorial complex. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Знищення музею", verbatim dates/agency/munition; ЕІУ — Скрипник П. І., "Сковороди Г. С. меморіальний комплекс," т. 9, с. 609]

**What survived / what was destroyed:** the 18th-c. estate building (the 1972 museum, raised for his 250th anniversary; national status 2008) was gutted; the **statue survived the fire** — a fact Ukrainian commemoration immediately read against his epitaph, «Світ ловив мене, та не впіймав» (the world/Russia tried to seize him and his house, and did not take *him*). The strike fell in **2022, his 300th-anniversary year.**

## 3. Major works

Skovoroda left songs, fables, philosophical dialogues, translations and letters, mostly composed 1750s–1790s and circulating in manuscript in his lifetime (first major printings came later). Selected canon:

- `1753–1785` — **«Сад божественних пісень»** ("Garden of Divine Songs"), 30 spiritual/lyric songs (incl. the famous **«Всякому городу нрав і права»**, §4). [T1: IEU — "30 verses… composed 1753–1785"]
- `1760–1770` — **«Байки Харківські»** (orig. **«Басни Харьковскія»**), 30 prose fables, each with a "Сила" (moral) — incl. **«Бджола та Шершень»**, the core text of *сродна праця* (§4). [T1: IEU — "30 fables… composed 1760–1770"]
- `philosophical dialogues` — **«Наркіс. Разглагол о том: узнай себе»**; **«Розмова п'яти подорожніх про істинне щастя в житті»**; **«Кільце»**; **«Алфавіт, або Буквар миру»** ("Розмова, названа Алфавіт…"); **«Жінка Лотова»**; **«Брань архистратига Михаїла зо Сатаною»** (1783–84); **«Діалог. Ім'я йому — Потоп зміїний»** (completed 1791). [T3: uk.wikipedia — work-list/dates corroborated by corpus context]
- `epitaph` — **«Світ ловив мене, та не впіймав»**, the line he ordered for his own grave (§4).

**Doctrine (his actual philosophy, named):** **дві натури** (two natures — the visible/material and the invisible/eternal in everything); **три світи** (three worlds — макрокосм/всесвіт, мікрокосм/людина, символічний світ/Біблія); **«сродна праця»** (kindred/congenial labour — happiness = living by one's God-given vocation); **«пізнай себе»** (self-knowledge); and the "**philosophy of the heart**" later named **кордоцентризм** by scholars (§6). [T1: IEU — "two natures… congenial work"; doctrine framework per Чижевський/Ушкалов, §6]

Note: Skovoroda wrote in 18th-c. bookish Ukrainian (§5), so the original orthography differs from the modern norm — preserved here as a primary document, not "corrected."

**Quote 1 — «Сад божественних пісень», Пісня 10 («Всякому городу…»):**

> «Всякому городу нрав и права; / Всяка имієт свой ум голова…»

`verify_quote` → **matched, confidence 0.927** (`ukrlib-skovoroda`). Note the authentic Church-Slavonic form «нрав» (not modern «звичай») — a register specimen (§5). The song satirises every estate's self-serving "нрав" while the speaker seeks only a clear conscience — Skovoroda's social-ethical core. [Primary: corpus-verified]

**Quote 2 — his self-composed epitaph:**

> «Світ ловив мене, та не впіймав.»

`verify_quote` → **matched, confidence 1.0** (`ukrlib-skovoroda`). The single most decolonially resonant line he wrote: a life defined by *evading capture* — by the imperial court, by institutional church careers, by wealth and status. After 6 May 2022 (§2b), Ukrainians read it as the answer to Russia's literal attempt to seize his house. [Primary: corpus-verified]

**Quote 3 — «Байки Харківські», fable «Бджола та Шершень» (on сродна праця):**

> «…немає більшої радості, аніж жити за покликанням… Подяка блаженній натурі за те, що потрібне зробила неважким, а важке непотрібним.»

`verify_quote` → **matched, confidence 1.0** (`ukrlib-skovoroda`). **Honesty flag (#M-4):** the closing aphorism is Skovoroda *rendering Epicurus* — the corpus text reads «Про се сказав древній Епікур таке…»; the fable and its framing are Skovoroda's own, but the maxim is his translation of Epicurus, not an original Skovoroda saying. Quote it as his *use* of the idea, not as a personal coinage. [Primary: corpus-verified]

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** **18th-c. книжна українська ("Slaveno-Ruthenian") bookish language** — a learned baroque idiom heavy with **Church Slavonicisms** (нрав, благий, истина, разглагол), Latin and Greek, layered over Ukrainian vernacular and folk elements. This is **not** modern literary Ukrainian and is not freely readable even by educated native speakers without a modernised edition.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** the **original texts are specialist (C2+ / scholarly)** — learners must meet him through **modernised/adapted Ukrainian editions** (the songs and fables adapt well to **B1–B2**; the philosophical dialogues to **C1**).
- **Lexicon notes (pre-teach the *modern* metalanguage):** філософ, просвітитель, мандрівний (філософ), кордоцентризм, сродний (праця), макрокосм, мікрокосм — all VESUM-valid (verified). Separately, flag the *historical* surface forms (нрав, имієт) as baroque specimens to be glossed, not normalised.
- **Stylistic features:** emblematic/allegorical fable; antithesis (visible vs invisible nature); Socratic dialogue; biblical-symbolic exegesis; song-form lyric drawing on Ukrainian baroque and folk song. The pedagogical entry point is one modernised fable («Бджола та Шершень») plus the epitaph — concrete, short, and decolonially loaded.

## 6. Contested points

This section is the dossier's decolonial core; it must be honest, not triumphalist.

- **"Russian" or "Ukrainian" philosopher?** Russian imperial and Soviet scholarship long claimed Skovoroda as a (or *the first*) Russian philosopher. The honest scholarly counter is **not** that he wrote in modern Ukrainian (he did not), but that he was **born in the Cossack Hetmanate, formed at the Kyiv-Mohyla Academy, lived his life in Sloboda Ukraine, and worked inside the Ukrainian baroque tradition** — the appropriation is anachronistic, mapping a 19th-c. imperial "Russia" backward onto an 18th-c. Hetmanate figure. **Дмитро Чижевський** (*Філософія Г. С. Сковороди*, 1934) located him firmly in the Ukrainian/European baroque; **Леонід Ушкалов** is the leading modern Skovorodynist and led the 2006 museum exposition. [T4: Чижевський, 1934; T4: Ушкалов — modern сковородознавство]
- **The bookish-language foothold (handle honestly).** Because his written register is Church-Slavonic-heavy (§5), the simplistic "he wrote in Russian" claim found surface plausibility. The decolonial task is to **explain the actual 18th-c. linguistic situation** (a shared learned bookish language across the Orthodox Slavic world, used by a Hetmanate Ukrainian), not to overstate his vernacular Ukrainianness.
- **Romantic "Ukrainian Socrates" vs rigorous philosopher.** Popular memory loves the barefoot wandering sage with a flute and a knapsack; modern scholarship insists on the **serious theologian-philosopher** behind the icon. Anti-hagiography: keep both, privilege the thought.
- **Religion.** Deeply Christian and biblically saturated, yet he **refused institutional/clerical careers** in the imperial church — neither a regime cleric nor a secular rationalist; a heterodox Christian Platonist.
- **Russian disinformation (live).** Russia continues to claim Skovoroda culturally (statues, "common heritage" rhetoric) **while bombing his museum in 2022** (§2b) — the contradiction is itself the teaching point.
