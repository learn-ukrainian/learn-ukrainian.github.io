> **Stage 2 correction.** The previous plan taught Berta Rapoport as a **«Лікарка і громадська діячка»** (a doctor and feminist public activist) with the patronymic **«Ісаківна»**. Both are **fabricated**: she was neither a doctor nor named Ісаківна. The verified person is **Берта Яківна Рапопорт**, one of the first women sea-captains in the USSR / on the Black Sea. This dossier rebuilds the figure from sources.

## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Рапопорт Берта Яківна (patronymic **Яківна** — father Яків Григорович). [T5: odessa-memory.info — id=87; corroborated by huxley.media, ukrainky.com.ua]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** none attested. Russian-language sources render her as «Берта Яковлевна Рапопорт» (forbidden as a body-text form). The earlier plan's «Берта Ісаківна» is a **fabricated patronymic** and must never reappear.
- **Born:** 15 травня 1914 | Одеса | Russian Empire (Херсонська губернія). A 1944 registry card lists 1911 — flag the disagreement, do not silently resolve. [T5: odessa-memory.info — «15 травня 1914 року»; odesskiye.info — «народилася … 15 травня 1914 року»]
- **Died:** 1967 | Одеса | Ukrainian SSR | cancer. Buried at the Jewish cemetery in Odesa. [T5: odesskiye.info — «померла у 1967 році, внаслідок хвороби – рак»; odessa-memory.info — burial «на Єврейському цвинтарі»]
- **Family / education key facts:** Father Яків Григорович (тесля/carpenter), mother Рашель Аронівна (homemaker); Jewish family of Odesa. Finished school in 1928, then entered the **Одеський морський технікум, відділення судноводіння**; in **1931** received the diploma of **штурман далекого плавання**. Had a daughter, Жанна. [T5: odessa-memory.info; ukrainky.com.ua — «вступила до Одеського морського технікуму на відділення судноводіння»]

Disagreements flagged: (a) **birth year 1914 vs 1911** (1944 card); (b) the steamer of her first posting is rendered both «Батумрада» / «Батум-Совет» (Soviet name; Russian «Батум-Совет» Ukrainianised by some outlets as «Батум-Рада»). Cite the variant attested by the source used.

## 2. Oppression mechanism — Soviet antisemitic marginalization + memorial erasure (indirect, not physical)

This figure was **not** arrested, exiled, or executed. Her oppression is **structural and indirect**, and the honest decolonial arc is: barrier-breaking pioneer → wartime captain → **post-war sidelining for being Jewish and for having been a captive** → near-total erasure from official Soviet maritime memory → recent recovery. Naming this accurately (rather than inflating it into a "terror death") is itself the anti-fabrication discipline this rebuild exists to model.

- **What happened:** removed from sea command after the war; reassigned to shore duties (senior inspector / dispatcher in the Odesa port captain's office); effectively pushed out of the seagoing career she had pioneered.
- **By whom:** Soviet maritime personnel policy of the late-1940s/early-1950s — the period of the state antisemitic "anti-cosmopolitan" campaign — and the standing Soviet suspicion of anyone who had been in enemy captivity.
- **Mechanism specifics:**
  - She was let go / sidelined **«за національною ознакою»** (on the grounds of nationality, i.e. because she was Jewish). [T5: odessa-life.od.ua — post-war dismissal «за національною ознакою»]
  - In **1962** she was nominated for the title **«Почесний працівник морського флоту»**, but the honour **was refused** — sources tie the refusal to her wartime captivity (a former captive was treated as politically suspect). [T5: odessa-memory.info — «запропоновано … 1962 … так і не отримала»; ukrainky.com.ua]
- **What was destroyed / what survived:** no manuscripts or archive were seized; what was "destroyed" was her *place in public memory*. A world-famous interwar pioneer was largely written out of the record for decades. Recovery began only in the 2000s (2006 memorial relief at the Odesa port; later women's-history and local-history publications).

> **Honesty about the Soviet maritime context (per the task brief):** Rapoport served the Soviet merchant and wartime fleet loyally — her wartime medal carries the Soviet war-name, and her recorded defiance of the Spanish fascists invokes the USSR flag. The decolonial point is not to pretend she was an anti-Soviet dissident; it is to show that the *same* regime she served sidelined her for her Jewish identity and her captivity, and that post-Soviet Ukraine reclaims her as part of Odesa's and Ukraine's heritage rather than a generic "Soviet achievement."

## 3. Major works / milestones

A sea-captain leaves voyages, not books. Chronology of milestones (the "works" here):

- `1928` — finishes school; enters the Одеський морський технікум (відділення судноводіння). [T5: ukrainky.com.ua]
- `1931` — diploma, **штурман далекого плавання**. [T5: odesskiye.info]
- `1932` — joins the Black Sea Shipping Company (Чорноморське пароплавство); **четвертий помічник капітана** on the пароплав «Батумрада» («Батум-Совет»). [T5: odessa-memory.info]
- `~1933` — **третій помічник** on the пароплав «Кубань». [T5: odessa-memory.info]
- `жовтень 1934` — **другий помічник** on the пароплав «Катаяма». [T5: odessa-memory.info]
- `~1936` — when «Катаяма» calls at London, a London paper runs **«Перша у світі жінка-моряк»**. [T5: odessa-memory.info; odesskiye.info]
- `лютий 1936` — **старший помічник капітана (старпом)** on «Катаяма». [T5: odessa-memory.info]
- `жовтень 1938` — «Катаяма» seized during the Spanish Civil War; Rapoport and the crew held **~8 months** in a fascist camp/prison. [T5: ukrainky.com.ua; huxley.media]
- `квітень 1941` — **старпом** on the passenger теплохід «Молдавія». [T5: ukrainky.com.ua]
- `листопад 1941` — sent to the **Caspian Shipping Company (Каспійське морське пароплавство)** and appointed **капітан(ка)** of the теплохід **«Туркменістан»**. [T5: odessa-memory.info — «призначили капітаном … "Туркменістан"»; odesskiye.info — «стала капітаном "Туркменістан"»]
- `жовтень 1946` — awarded the medal «За відважну працю у Великій Вітчизняній війні 1941–1945 рр.» (Soviet award title). [T5: odessa-memory.info]
- `1966` — documentary «В одній сім'ї» (dir. Рафаїл Голдін) features her. [T5: huxley.media]
- `травень 2006` — commemorative bas-relief unveiled on the Odesa port building. [T5: odessa-memory.info]

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated / thin in the record:** birth year (1914 vs 1911); the exact name/spelling of her first steamer; the precise wording and even the literal historicity of the "flag-defiance" speech, which reaches us only through later memoir. Treat the speech as memoir-tradition, not minutes.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** the "perfect Soviet heroine" gloss, and equally the opposite over-correction into "anti-Soviet martyr." She is neither — she is a Jewish-Ukrainian pioneer the Soviet system used and then discarded.
- **Where modern Russian framing attacks / absorbs her:** Russocentric retellings file her as a generic "Soviet (Russian) sea-captain," erasing both the Odesa-Jewish-Ukrainian context and the antisemitic post-war sidelining. Reject this; restore the Ukrainian/Odesa and Jewish-heritage frame.
- **Jewish-heritage consideration:** her marginalization is inseparable from Soviet state antisemitism (late-1940s campaigns); name it plainly rather than as vague "they forgot her."
