## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Лариса Петрівна Косач (по чоловікові **Косач-Квітка**); pseudonym **Леся Українка**. [T1: IEU — "Larysa Kosach-Kvitka… under the pseudonym Lesia Ukrainka"; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Леся Українка (the universal name); early childhood form Леся. The pseudonym itself was a programmatic act of national self-naming. Russian-imperial transliteration (forbidden in body text, §8 only): Лариса Петровна Косач / "Lesya Ukrainka" Russified renderings.
- **Born:** 25 February 1871 (13 February O.S.) | Звягель (now **Новоград-Волинський**), Волинська губернія | Російська імперія. [T1: IEU — "b 25 February 1871 in Zviahel (now Novohrad-Volynskyi), Volhynia gubernia"; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Died:** 1 August 1913 (19 July O.S.) (aged 42) | **Сурамі**, Тифліська губернія (now Georgia) | Російська імперія | of **bone-and-joint tuberculosis** (туберкульоз кісток і суглобів). Buried at the **Байкове кладовище**, Kyiv. [T1: IEU — "d 1 August 1913 in Surami, Georgia"; T3: uk.wikipedia — "19 липня (1 серпня) 1913… туберкульоз кісток і суглобів… Байкове кладовище"]
- **Family / education key facts:** Mother **Ольга Косач-Драгоманова — the writer Олена Пчілка**; father Петро Косач, a civil servant of progressive sympathies. Her decisive intellectual influence was her maternal uncle **Михайло Драгоманов**, the émigré political thinker. Self-educated to an extraordinary breadth (read/wrote in ~9 languages, translated Heine, Homer, Dante, Shakespeare, Byron); co-founder of the Kyiv literary circle **«Плеяда»** (mid-1880s) and of the group **Українська соціал-демократія**. [T1: IEU; T3: uk.wikipedia]

**Source / chronology note (flagged, not smoothed):** Her illness chronology is often loosely stated. The verified sequence: she **caught a severe cold at Водохреще on 6 (18) January 1881, aged 10**, which led to tuberculosis of the bones and joints; the condition was **formally diagnosed in summer 1883**. So "ill from childhood, ~age 10 (1881), diagnosed 1883" is the accurate form — she herself called the lifelong fight her «тридцятилітня війна». [T3: uk.wikipedia, verbatim dates]

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section. As with Franko, honesty about *which mechanism* operated is the decolonial discipline: Lesya was not arrested-and-shot; she was **silenced by imperial censorship in life and falsified by the Soviet canon after death**.

**(a) Russian-imperial censorship (named statutes, dated).** Lesya wrote in the Russian Empire under the **Валуєвський циркуляр (1863)** and the **Емський указ (1876)**, which banned Ukrainian-language publishing, theatre and import of Ukrainian books. The direct consequence is documentary and specific: **her work had to be printed in Austrian-ruled Galicia (Lviv/Chernivtsi), not in her own Kyiv** — her first collection **«На крилах пісень» (1893) appeared in Lviv**, and Galician periodicals («Зоря», «Літературно-науковий вістник») carried her, because the empire would not. The «Плеяда» circle operated in the grey zone of what tsarist censorship tolerated. [T1: IEU; context T3: uk.wikipedia]

**(b) Posthumous Soviet sanitisation (named mechanism, post-1930s).** The USSR could not erase so canonical a writer, so it **rebuilt her** into a safe icon: the "**поетеса-революціонерка**," "**дочка Прометея**," foregrounding the civic lyric («Досвітні огні», «Слово, чому ти не твердая криця») as proto-Bolshevik militancy, while **suppressing or flattening**: her **feminism**, her **religious-philosophical** dramas (the Christian-martyr conflict of «Одержима», «У катакомбах»), her **modernist individualism**, and any frank treatment of her relationships and body. The mechanism is **canonisation-by-amputation** — keep the name and a curated 10% of the verse, delete the mind. Modern Ukrainian scholarship (Агеєва, Забужко — §6) is essentially an act of recovering the amputated 90%.

**What survived / what was distorted:** the full corpus survived in print, but for ~60 years was read through a Soviet template that made the most European, feminist and metaphysical dramatist of Ukrainian literature into a one-note revolutionary — the distortion is interpretive, not physical, and meets the section's specificity test (named template, named suppressed themes, post-1930s canon).

## 3. Major works

Chronological selection (poetry, then the mature "drama-poems" she is now most valued for):

- `1890` — **«Contra spem spero!»** — programmatic early poem of defiant hope (§4). [corpus-verified]
- `1893` — **«На крилах пісень»** — debut poetry collection, published **Lviv** (because of imperial censorship, §2a). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1896` — **«Слово, чому ти не твердая криця…»** — the "word-as-weapon" poem (§4). [corpus-verified, dated 26.11.1896 in corpus]
- `1901` — **«Одержима»** — dramatic poem; written **in one night, 18 (31) January 1901, at the deathbed of the dying Сергій Мержинський** in Minsk (he died weeks later, early 1901). [T3: uk.wikipedia — "18 (31) січня 1901 року за одну ніч біля ліжка помираючого Мержинського… вийшла поема «Одержима»"]
- `1907` — **«Кассандра»** — drama; the unheeded truth-teller of Troy (a parable of prophecy and national doom). [T1: IEU]
- `1908–1909` — **«У пущі»**, **«На полі крові»** — philosophical dramas. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1910` — **«Бояриня»** — drama set in Ruin-era Muscovy/Hetmanate; her most explicitly national-historical text, and accordingly the most censored in Soviet times. [T1: IEU]
- `1911` — **«Лісова пісня»** — her masterpiece, a "драма-феєрія" of Volhynian myth, written in ~10–12 days in Kutaisi (§4). [T1: IEU]
- `1912` — **«Камінний господар»** — her Don Juan drama, a feminist-philosophical reworking of the European myth. [T1: IEU]

**Suppression note:** «Бояриня» (anti-Muscovite in theme) was effectively unpublishable/unstaged for much of the Soviet era; the religious dramas were minimised.

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** high literary modernist Ukrainian — **neo-Romantic / neo-classical** diction, philosophically dense, European in reference. «Лісова пісня» layers in **Volhynian folk-dialect** for the forest spirits, contrasted with the villagers' vernacular.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** the short civic lyric («Contra spem spero») can be approached at **B2**; the drama-poems («Кассандра», «Одержима», «Камінний господар») are **C1–C2** (philosophical abstraction, archaic/elevated register, classical allusion).
- **Lexicon notes (pre-teach):** неоромантизм, драматургиня, поетеса, феміністка, генійка — all VESUM-valid (verified, including the feminitive **генійка**). Watch her elevated/archaic verb forms and the Volhynian dialectisms of «Лісова пісня».
- **Stylistic features:** dramatic dialogue in blank verse; mythological/biblical parable as vehicle for contemporary national and gender questions; the "слово-зброя" topos; a deliberately *European* frame (Cassandra, Don Juan, early Christians) chosen partly to evade the parochial cage colonial censorship tried to impose.

## 6. Contested points

- **The de-sexualised "Lesya."** Oksana Zabuzhko's **«Notre Dame d'Ukraine: Українка в конфлікті міфологій» (2007)** argues the Soviet (and partly the populist) tradition stripped Lesya of her body, her European chivalric-gnostic imagination and her status as a major *philosophical* dramatist, leaving a sexless invalid-icon. This re-reading is now central and contested. [T4: Забужко, *Notre Dame d'Ukraine*, 2007]
- **Feminist recovery.** Віра Агеєва's **«Поетеса зламу століть» (1999)** reads Lesya as the key Ukrainian modernist and feminist — against the Soviet "revolutionary democrat" frame. [T4: Агеєва, 1999]
- **The Drahomanov line.** Her uncle Драгоманов shaped her into a European-minded, anti-provincial thinker; popular memory tends to nationalise her while under-reading that cosmopolitan formation. [T1: IEU]
- **Soviet appropriation.** The "дочка Прометея" / revolutionary-poetess cult is a documented selective canon (§2b) — the anti-hagiography task is to read the *whole* writer, including «Бояриня».
- **Russian disinformation.** Russian framings periodically fold her into "common East-Slavic" or "all-Russian Silver Age" literature; her own programmatic pseudonym **Українка** and the anti-Muscovite «Бояриня» refute this directly.
- **The Merzhynsky narrative.** Beware the romantic cliché that «Одержима» was written "after his death": the verified record is that she wrote it **at his dying bed on 18/31 January 1901**, while he was still alive (he died weeks later) — a precision that matters for how the work's anguish is taught. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
