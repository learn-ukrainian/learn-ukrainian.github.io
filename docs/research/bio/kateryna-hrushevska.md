## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Грушевська Катерина Михайлівна.
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Катря Грушевська; Kateryna Hrushevska; K. Hrushevska.
- **Born:** 1900-06-21 | Lviv | Austria-Hungary. ESU gives 21.06.1900, Lviv; Ukrainian Wikipedia via MCP confirms the same date.
- **Died:** 1943-03-30 | Magadan oblast, RSFSR, USSR | camp/deportation death context; precise camp medical cause: source not found. ESU gives the date and Magadan oblast.
- **Family / education key facts:** Daughter and only child of Mykhailo and Mariia-Ivanna Hrushevskyi. Educated at home; ESU states she knew Polish, German, English, and French and studied at Geneva University in 1919-1920. She was acting secretary of the Ukrainian Sociological Institute, VUAN researcher, editor of *Первісне громадянство і його пережитки на Україні*, and a major scholar of Ukrainian epic song.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** NKVD arrest, fabricated anti-Soviet nationalist accusation, military tribunal sentence, imprisonment/exile, death in the Gulag system.
- **When:** Local History states that on 1939-04-15/16 the Military Tribunal of the Kyiv Special Military District sentenced her to eight years. ESU gives death on 1943-03-30 in Magadan oblast. Exact arrest date in retrieved Tier 1/2 sources: source not found; secondary sources identify 1938.
- **By whom:** NKVD and Soviet military tribunal apparatus.
- **Document references:** Local History quotes the charge as participation in an "anti-Soviet nationalist insurgent organization" allegedly preparing armed uprising and a "self-standing bourgeois nationalist state". Archival case number: source not found.
- **Mechanism specifics:** Her repression followed the destruction of the Hrushevskyi historical school. She had continued scholarly work after her father's forced Moscow period and death, then became a target as a carrier of the family's intellectual archive and as an independent woman scholar of Ukrainian oral tradition. The sentence converted folklore research, editorial work, and family memory into supposed counterrevolution. The Magadan death date in ESU should be used, with a note that the retrieved sources did not provide a full medical record or camp file.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** *Українські народні думи* survived in printed volumes and later digitization. Her research program and journals were interrupted; her archive and scholarly network were damaged by the broader Soviet liquidation of Ukrainian humanities.

The dossier should not imply that the charge had evidentiary value. The retrieved Local History account gives the Soviet accusation in the regime's own language; it is evidence of the accusation, not proof of an organization. ESU grounds the identity, dates, and death place, while the digitized *Думи* volumes show the scholarly work the regime interrupted. That source split is important for Phase 2: use the Soviet charge only in quotation or paraphrase as a repressive document, and use Hrushevska's corpus work as the positive pedagogical anchor.

## 3. Major works

- `1923` — *Примітивні оповідання, казки і байки Африки та Америки*, comparative folklore/cultural studies.
- `1924` — *З примітивної культури: Розвідки та доповіді*, cultural studies.
- `1926-1930` — editor, *Первісне громадянство і його пережитки на Україні*, VUAN journal.
- `1927` — *Українські народні думи*, vol. 1, corpus texts 1-13 and introduction.
- `1931` — *Українські народні думи*, vol. 2, corpus texts 14-33 and preface.
- `1930s` — work at VUAN historical and material-culture institutions; much of the trajectory was broken by repression.

## 4. Primary-source quotes

- "Сей другий том корпусу Українських народніх дум виходить" — *Українські народні думи*, vol. 2, "Переднє слово", 1931. This shows the corpus-making ambition and older scholarly orthography.
- "Інтерес до дум... зростає дуже помітно" — same preface. The line gives curriculum writers a concise way to introduce scholarly attention to dumy as a living field rather than a static folklore artifact.

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated in modern UA scholarship:** How to recover her independent intellectual profile from the shadow of Mykhailo Hrushevskyi; how to read early ethnosociological language without flattening it into present-day terminology; how to reconstruct the exact camp record.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** She is often remembered only as Hrushevskyi's daughter. That framing erases her own editorial and corpus work.
- **Where modern Russian disinformation attacks them:** Soviet-style narratives treated Hrushevskyi scholarship as "bourgeois nationalism"; modern Russian-imperial framings continue to delegitimize Ukrainian humanities as separatist ideology.
- **Other-perspective considerations:** Her comparative work includes African and American materials through early twentieth-century scholarly mediation; use with a note on historical terminology and colonial-era anthropology.

A second contested point is disciplinary naming. ESU calls her an ethnosociologist, folklorist, and ethnographer; popular accounts often call her only a historian's daughter or "folklore researcher". The stronger curriculum wording is to name the discipline in full, then explain that Ukrainian folklore studies in the 1920s overlapped with sociology, anthropology, musicology, and source editing. That prevents a false split between "family biography" and "real scholarship".
