## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** **Imperial censorship ban** on performances of Ukrainian-language opera after its success — fear of Ukrainian national awakening.
- **When:** following **1863** Mariinsky premiere success; ban lasting **decades** (ghost-wiki: >20 years; revival **1884** Rostov). [T3: ghost-wiki]
- **By whom:** **Russian imperial censorship** / theatre administration (not a personal arrest narrative).
- **Document references:** Specific decree number **not retrieved** in this pass — cite mechanism qualitatively until archival cite added.
- **Mechanism specifics:** Opera «Запорожець за Дунаєм» triumphed in **Petersburg 1863** but threatened imperial narrative of Little Russian loyalty; ban pushed Ukrainian opera to **traveling troupes** abroad of empire until Staritsky revival.
- **What survived:** Score, libretto tradition, Mariinsky premiere memory; **1966** Horodyshche museum + monument. [T3: ghost-wiki]

## 6. Contested points

- **Libretto:** sole vs Kostomarov co-authorship — **unresolved** here. Ghost-wiki language sample #9 explicitly gives **«за лібретто, написане ним разом з М. Костомаровим»** [T3: ghost-wiki citing encyclopedic tradition], while other lines attribute libretto to Hulak-Artemovsky alone. Phase-2 wiki must photograph the first edition title page before teaching either variant as settled.
- **Composer vs performer:** Serov's harsh review of an early opera **«Весілля»** — «не використав українських весільних мелодій, а просто запозичив куплети з польського водевілю» [T3: ghost-wiki language sample #3] — versus later mastery in **«Запорожець за Дунаєм»**. Pedagogically this is a **creative development** arc, not proof of permanent amateurism.
- **«First Ukrainian opera» claim:** Ukrainian school textbooks and IEU-derived summaries treat **«Запорожець за Дунаєм»** as the first **classical Ukrainian opera** [T3: ghost-wiki §3, language sample #1]. Russian imperial opera histories sometimes subsume it under «all-Russian musical theatre» — decolonized teaching keeps the **Ukrainian libretto + folk melos** criterion explicit.
- **Imperial career vs Ukrainian patriotism:** Decades in Petersburg/Italy and performance in Russian imperial theatres coexist with **Shevchenko friendship**, **Ukrainian-language opera subject**, and post-premiere **censorship** triggered by that subject. Neither hagiography («secret nationalist») nor reduction to «court singer of empire» fits the mixed record.
- **Engineering side career:** Statistical tables (1854) and Petersburg waterworks design (1858) [T3: ghost-wiki] show encyclopedic talent — rarely taught in BIO modules but humanizes the «star bass» caricature.

**Pedagogical pairing:** Assign alongside **Mykola Lysenko** dossier — Hulak-Artemovsky as **bridge generation** between Glinka capella training and fin-de-siècle Ukrainian national opera.
