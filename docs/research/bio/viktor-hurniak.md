## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Гурняк Віктор Петрович
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Гарт, Гартік (Plast pseudonyms), Олігарх (military call sign), Viktor Gurnyak (FORBIDDEN - Russian transliteration)
- **Born:** 1987-06-08 | Horodnytsia, Ternopil Oblast | Ukrainian SSR
- **Died:** 2014-10-19 | near Smila, Luhansk Oblast | Ukraine | Killed in action (mortar strike during medical evacuation)
- **Family / education key facts:** Educated at the Technical College of Ternopil Ivan Puluj National Technical University. Joined the Plast National Scout Organization of Ukraine at age 14, where he became an educator and press secretary. Married to Iryna Hurniak; survived by his wife and daughter Yustyna (born 2012). Co-founder of the LUFA photography agency.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** Killed in action while serving as a volunteer combat medic/driver.
- **When (specific dates):** 2014-10-19.
- **By whom (specific Russian/Soviet/RF agency):** Russian proxy forces ("LPR" militia) and Russian regular artillery units operating in the Luhansk sector.
- **Document references:** Decree of the President of Ukraine No. 450/2021 (awarding the title Hero of Ukraine posthumously); Decree of the President of Ukraine No. 838/2014 (Order for Courage, III class, posthumously).
- **Mechanism specifics:** Following his extensive coverage of the Revolution of Dignity and the early covert invasion of Crimea as a photojournalist (working for Reuters, UNIAN, and INSIDER), Hurniak volunteered to join the Armed Forces of Ukraine in the summer of 2014. He enlisted in the 24th Separate Assault Battalion "Aidar." On October 19, 2014, during the intense and chaotic battles for the 32nd checkpoint along the Bakhmutka highway near the settlement of Smila (Luhansk region), his unit came under heavy enemy mortar fire. Operating a civilian SUV that he had personally supplied as a volunteer, Hurniak attempted to drive into the fire zone to evacuate wounded comrades. An enemy mortar shell struck his vehicle, killing him instantly. This constitutes the physical destruction of a leading Ukrainian cultural documentarian by the Russian military apparatus.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** His life was cut short at 27. However, his extensive and critically important photographic archive of the Euromaidan protests and the early months of the Russo-Ukrainian War survived. His cameras and equipment were recovered, and his legacy is fiercely guarded by the Plast scout organization and the Ukrainian journalistic community.

## 3. Major works

Viktor Hurniak's "works" consist of documentary photojournalism series and posthumous curated exhibitions of his photography:

- `2013-2014` — *Євромайдан* (Euromaidan documentary series), photojournalism, Reuters / INSIDER / UNIAN. Iconic images capturing the violence, hope, and human reality of the Revolution of Dignity.
- `2014` — *Окупація Криму* (Occupation of Crimea), photojournalism. Undercover reporting from the peninsula detailing the initial stages of the Russian military takeover.
- `2014` — *Війна на Сході* (War in the East), frontline photography, LUFA agency. Documentation of the Aidar battalion and the devastation of civilian infrastructure.
- `2014-present` — *Від Майдану до війни* (From Maidan to War), posthumous photo exhibition, Ukrainian PEN / European Parliament. A comprehensive retrospective of his short but profoundly impactful career as a documentarian of Ukraine's struggle for independence.

## 6. Contested points

- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** Popular memory frequently simplifies Hurniak's legacy into the archetype of the "fallen soldier" or purely an "Aidar fighter." While true, this obscures his primary identity as a top-tier photojournalist and a foundational figure in the Ukrainian volunteer and scouting (Plast) movements. He was a documentarian who took up arms only when the existential threat became undeniable.
- **What's debated in modern UA scholarship:** Military historians and analysts continue to debate the chaotic command structure and lack of armored medical evacuation vehicles during the defense of the 32nd checkpoint in October 2014, a systemic failure that forced volunteers like Hurniak to conduct combat evacuations in unarmored civilian SUVs, directly leading to his death.
- **Where modern Russian disinformation attacks them:** Russian state media and proxy channels consistently vilified the Aidar battalion, branding its members—including volunteer medics and journalists like Hurniak—as "punishers" (каратели) and "neo-Nazis." This disinformation was used to retroactively legitimize the shelling of medical evacuation vehicles and the murder of Ukrainian civil society figures.
