## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Маркіян Семенович Шашкевич.
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Маркіян Шашкевич; "Будитель" (epithet); Markiian
  Shashkevych. Russian-imperial transliterations must not appear in body text
  except in the forbidden-forms field in §8.
- **Born:** 6 November 1811 | село Підлисся, Золочівський округ, Королівство
  Галичини та Володимирії | now Золочівський район, Львівська область, Україна
  [T3: uk.wikipedia] [T2: Grade 9 textbook corpus].
- **Died:** 7 June 1843 | село Новосілки, Львівщина | of tuberculosis, blind and
  in poverty; reburied 1893 at the Lychakiv cemetery in Львів [T3: uk.wikipedia].
- **Family / education key facts:** Son of a Greek Catholic priest; educated at
  schools in Білий Камінь and Львів, then the Lviv university and the
  Greek-Catholic theological seminary (1829–1838); briefly expelled for a
  disciplinary lapse; ordained 1838 and served as a village parish priest in
  Гумниська, Нестаничі, and Новосілки [T3: uk.wikipedia]. Core roles: Galician
  "будитель", priest, poet, translator, founder of the new Ukrainian literature
  in Galicia, and leader of the «Руська трійця».

## 2. Oppression mechanism

Not Applicable as a **Russian/Soviet** repression-primary biography — Shashkevych
lived and died under Austrian (Habsburg) rule. The documented mechanism is
Habsburg-Galician pre-publication censorship and police surveillance of the
Ukrainian-vernacular movement, which must be named with the same specificity.

- **What happened:** State censorship blocked the «Руської трійці» publications;
  the 1837 almanac was confiscated and its initiators placed under police
  watch.
- **When (specific dates):** The seminarian collections «Син Руси» (1833) and
  «Зоря» (1834) were stopped by censorship before publication; «Русалка
  Дністровая» was printed in Buda (Hungary) in 1837 to evade Galician
  censorship, after which over a thousand of the print run were confiscated and
  roughly one hundred copies survived; the «Руська трійця» was effectively
  broken up under police supervision [T3: uk.wikipedia] [T2: Grade 9 textbook].
- **By whom (specific agency):** Habsburg Galician censorship authorities and
  the Greek-Catholic consistory censor (the metropolitan ordinariate); the
  censor Венедикт Левицький is named in scholarship as a key obstacle.
- **Document references:** the confiscation of the «Русалка Дністровая» print
  run (1837) and the suppressed «Зоря» manuscript (1834) are the documentary
  anchors; cite the almanac's publication history rather than paraphrase.
- **Mechanism specifics:** the obstacle was not exile or prison but the denial
  of print and pulpit in the vernacular — the seminary rector blocked
  Ukrainian-language preaching, and censors blocked Ukrainian-language print, so
  the movement had to publish abroad. Decolonial note for a separate point:
  during the First World War tsarist Russian occupation of Львів the gendarmerie
  stole his memorial plaque, and Soviet authorities later destroyed a renewed
  one — a Russian-imperial and Soviet attack on his memory distinct from the
  Habsburg-era censorship above.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** «Русалка Дністровая» survived in about
  a hundred copies and became foundational; the suppressed «Зоря» and much
  unpublished material did not.

## 3. Major works

- `1835` — «Голос галичан», the first printed text in the Ukrainian vernacular
  on West Ukrainian lands [T3].
- `1836` — «Азбука і Abecadło», Polish-language scholarly article defending the
  Cyrillic alphabet against a Latin-script project — a founding text of the
  "азбучна війна" [T3].
- `1837` — «Русалка Дністровая» (Buda), the first Ukrainian-vernacular
  folkloric-literary almanac in Galicia; initiated and led by Shashkevych
  [T3] [T2: Grade 9 textbook].
- «Веснівка» («Цвітка дрібная…») — pearl of his landscape lyric, entered school
  anthologies; later set to music by Анатолій Кос-Анатольський [T2: Grade 9
  textbook].
- Patriotic and historical verse: «Руська мова», «Слово до чтителей руського
  язика», «Псалми Русланові», «О Наливайку», «Хмельницького обступлєніє Львова»
  [T3].
- `1842`–`1843` — Ukrainian translations of fragments of «Слово о полку
  Ігоревім» and of the Gospels; the «Читанка» for children published
  posthumously in 1850 [T3].

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** early-Romantic Galician vernacular verse with archaic and
  dialectal features; some Church-Slavonic-tinged translation language.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** B1/B2 for «Веснівка» and short lyric;
  C1 for «Азбука і абецадло», the translations, and the censorship history.
- **Lexicon notes:** Galician dialectisms, nineteenth-century orthography, and
  "руський"/"руська" in the period sense (= Ukrainian, NOT Russian) require an
  explicit Old-East-Slavic-vs-modern clarification for learners.
- **Stylistic features:** folkloric pestliva lexicon, dactylic and feminine
  rhyme, personification and antithesis; programmatic alphabet-defence prose.

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated in modern UA scholarship:** the literary quality and language
  of Shashkevych's Gospel translations is debated — Андрій Даниленко judged them
  "Church Slavonic with some Ukrainian dialectal features," while others stress
  their symbolic primacy as the first vernacular scripture attempts; the exact
  composition and chronology of the «Руська трійця» activity (1833–1836) is also
  refined in recent work.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** popular memory canonises
  Shashkevych as a flawless "будитель" / Galician Shevchenko, flattening the
  short, poor, conflict-ridden life (seminary expulsion, break with his father,
  early death) and the collaborative nature of the «Руська трійця». Lead with the
  movement and the alphabet defence, not a lone-genius myth.
- **Where modern Russian disinformation attacks them (if applicable):** Russian
  framing exploits the period term "руський" to claim Galician Ruthenians as
  "Russian"; the module must teach that "руська мова" here means the Ukrainian
  vernacular and that Shashkevych explicitly built a separate Ukrainian literary
  language, rejecting both Polonisation and the Latin-script project.
- **Polish / Jewish / other-perspective considerations (if applicable):** the
  "азбучна війна" was a Galician Ukrainian response to a Polish-sponsored
  Latin-alphabet project; frame it as a language-sovereignty dispute, not
  ethnic hostility, and note Shashkevych's calls for Ukrainian–Polish equality.
