## 2. Oppression mechanism

The load-bearing section. Handziuk is an **anti-corruption activist assassinated by a contract acid attack**; the prosecution is documented and dated.

- **What happened:** on **31 July 2018, ~08:30**, an assailant doused Handziuk with **sulphuric acid** as she left her home for work in Kherson. The acid struck her back, head, arm and an eye; burns covered **over 40 %** of her body (the death certificate later recorded **39 %**). She was hospitalised in shock, airlifted to Kyiv on 1 August under state protection, endured **eleven operations**, and **died on 4 November 2018**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Убивство Катерини Гандзюк," §"Перебіг подій"]
- **When (specific):** attack **31.07.2018**; death **04.11.2018**; final verdict on the organisers **26 June 2023**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **By whom (court-established):** the **executors** were five former participants of the war in Donbas (5th battalion of the Ukrainian Volunteer Army), coordinated by **Сергій Торбін**; **Микита Грабчук** threw the acid. In June 2019 the executors were sentenced (Торбін 6 yrs 6 mo; Грабчук 6 yrs; Васянович and Вишневський 4 yrs; Горбунов 3 yrs). On **26 June 2023** the Dnipro district court of Kyiv convicted **Владислав Мангер** (head of the Kherson oblast council — the **person who ordered** the attack) and **Олексій Левін / Москаленко** (the **organiser**) to **10 years each**, plus **15 million UAH** compensation to the family. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Покарання для нападників," §"Розслідування"]
- **Document references (article numbers, quoted):** the Генпрокуратура qualified the crime as **«замовлення та організація за попередньою змовою групою осіб умисного тяжкого тілесного ушкодження у спосіб, що має характер особливого мучення та спричинив смерть потерпілої»** (ч. 3 ст. 27, ч. 2 ст. 28, ч. 2 ст. 121 КК України). The case was repeatedly re-qualified — from hooliganism (ч. 4 ст. 296) → grievous bodily harm (ч. 2 ст. 121) → attempted contract murder → murder — a re-qualification trail that itself became evidence of institutional foot-dragging. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Убивство…," §"Розслідування"]
- **Mechanism specifics (the impunity dimension):** Handziuk distrusted the **local Kherson police** (whom she had publicly accused of corruption and of shielding separatist movements) and refused to cooperate with them, giving evidence instead to the Department of Strategic Investigations. The investigation was marked by re-qualifications, a wrongly-detained first suspect (Новіков), softened charges, and protracted delays — prompting the all-Ukrainian **«Хто замовив Катю Гандзюк?»** movement and a Verkhovna Rada **temporary investigative commission (ТСК)** of 18 deputies (created 6 November 2018). [T2: ТСК ВРУ report, adopted 11 July 2019; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** her civic legacy survives — the Ukrainian School of Political Studies' annual **Стипендія ім. Катерини Гандзюк**; her posthumous US State Department **International Women of Courage** award (25 April 2019, received by her father); streets named for her in Kherson (later annulled by the Supreme Court) and Kyiv (the 2022 derussification renaming of вул. Біломорська). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Пам'ять," §"Реакція міжнародної спільноти"]

## 3. Major works

Not an author; her "works" are civic-activist and public-service acts, chronological:

- `2004` — participant in the **Помаранчева революція**; from 2003 head of the regional «Батьківщина молода». [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `2012` — co-founder of the citizen-journalism agency **«МОСТ»** (most.ks.ua), which became the region's main outlet monitoring **public procurement and the spending of public finances**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Громадська та політична діяльність"]
- `2013–2014` — active participant in the Kherson **Євромайдан**; publicly resisted attempts to start a Kherson **«русская весна»**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `2014–` — after the annexation of Crimea and the start of the Donbas war, worked with displaced persons (UNHCR legal-protection assistant); from 2016 part of the **Itchy Trigger Finger Ukrainians** group producing patriotic outdoor advertising aimed at occupied Crimea. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `2017` — publicly accused the head of the regional Economic-Protection police department (**Антощук**) of corruption and extortion (he lost his post); confronted the pro-Russian organiser of "русские пробежки" **Кирило Стремоусов**. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `April 2018` — exposed that ~200 citizens with **forged "press IDs"** had obtained weapons permits from the regional police (2014–2018) — among her last investigations before the attack. [T3: uk.wikipedia]

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated:** by 2023 the **executors and the orderer/organiser are court-established** (Мангер, Левін, Торбін, Грабчук et al.); what remained publicly contested for years was **whether the full chain of beneficiaries was prosecuted** — activists and the family alleged higher-level shielding (naming oblast officials Гордєєв, Рищук) and accused successive prosecutors of sabotage. The dossier states the **convictions as fact** and the wider allegations as **allegations**, not findings.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** the case is sometimes flattened into "corrupt officials silenced an activist," losing the specific, documented **geopolitical edge** — Handziuk fought both local corruption **and** pro-Russian organising in Kherson.
- **Where Russian framing intersects:** her chief local antagonist **Стремоусов** became, in 2022, the Russian occupation administration's deputy head in Kherson (he died in a car crash in November 2022). Her murder was **not** ordered by Moscow, and the dossier does not claim so; but the milieu she resisted overlapped with the forces that later collaborated with the occupation — a documented, not speculative, throughline.
- **Other-perspective considerations:** the case is a touchstone for the safety of **women in Ukrainian civil society** and for the international "Women of Courage" recognition; present it as part of the broader pattern of attacks on Ukrainian activists (≥55 documented acid/violence attacks on activists in 2017–2018, per the campaign), not as an isolated tragedy.
