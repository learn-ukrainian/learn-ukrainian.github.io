(His father's surname is sometimes claimed to have been "Пульгуй," changed later — the wiki notes this has **no documentary support** and likely reflects German transliteration of Slavic names.)
- **Born:** 2 February 1845 | містечко **Гримайлів** (now селище, Чортківський район, Тернопільська область) | then Королівство Галичини та Володимирії, **Австрійська імперія**; into a Greek-Catholic family (father Павло was burgomaster of Hrymailiv 1861–65). - **Died:** 31 January 1918 (aged 72) | **Прага** | then Богемське королівство, Австро-Угорщина; buried at the **Мальвазінки** cemetery. [T1: IEU — "died 31 January 1918 in Prague"; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Family / education key facts:** at the Ternopil gymnasium he co-founded (with the Barvinsky brothers) the secret Ukrainian youth society **«Громада»**; entered the Greek-Catholic seminary in Vienna (1865) and co-founded the Vienna student society **«Січ»** (1868, later praised by Іван Франко); **chose science over the priesthood**, studying philosophy/physics at Vienna University (1869–72); **PhD in physics, Strasbourg University, 1876** (dissertation on the temperature-dependence of the internal friction of gases). **Source disagreement note (flagged):** the **rectorship date differs** — IEU gives **"rector (1899–1900)"**, while uk.wikipedia gives the **1888–1889** academic year as rector of the Prague German Polytechnic (and dean of mechanical engineering 1890). ## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section. Puliui lived under **Austria-Hungary**, not under direct Russian repression; the relevant mechanisms are (a) the **Russian-imperial ban on Ukrainian print** that his Ukrainian Bible was made to defy, and (b) the **long erasure/under-crediting of his scientific priority**. (Indirect mechanisms — censorship, erasure — held to the same specificity rule.)

**(a) The Ems decree and the Ukrainian Bible (resistance to imperial censorship).** Together with **Пантелеймон Куліш** (whom he first met in Vienna in 1869) and **Іван Нечуй-Левицький**, Puluj produced a full translation of the **Holy Scripture into Ukrainian** (he himself translated the **Псалтир**). This was undertaken precisely while the **Емський указ (1876)** outlawed Ukrainian-language publishing in the Russian Empire; the complete Ukrainian Bible could only be printed **abroad — by the British and Foreign Bible Society in 1903** — and carried to Ukrainians whom the empire forbade to read Scripture in their own language. The translation is an act of cultural resistance to imperial censorship, not a neutral scholarly exercise. [T1: IEU — "Translated the Gospels (1871) and New Testament (1880) with Panteleimon Kulish… the Ukrainian Bible published by the British Bible Society in 1903"; T3: uk.wikipedia]

**(b) Erasure / under-crediting of his scientific priority (the X-ray question — framed NPOV).** Puliui designed a cathode-ray tube of his own (the **«Пулюєва трубка»**, from 1881, initially for lighting); his **four cathode-ray papers of 1880–82** (Wiener Berichte) were important enough that the London Physical Society re-published his monograph in English (*Physical Memoirs*, 1889). When X-rays were announced, he very rapidly produced **exceptional X-ray images** — on **2 February 1896** a high-quality image of an entire (child's) body, later published in *The Photogram*; his tube was used for the **first medical X-ray image on the American continent (3 Feb 1896)** — and in two 1896 Wiener-Berichte papers he located where the rays form, showed their ionising action, and **explained their nature**. **The honest priority position:** Puliui **published his X-ray results only *after* Wilhelm Röntgen** (who discovered the rays on 8 Nov 1895 and published first in January 1896, winning the 1901 Nobel Prize); therefore the claim "**Puluj invented/discovered X-rays before Röntgen**" is an **overclaim / contested partisan reading**. What *is* defensible: Puliui was a **foundational, under-credited pioneer** of cathode-ray and X-ray physics whose tube and prior work were instrumental, who advanced the new field with unusual speed and depth, and whom Röntgen — though the two knew each other personally from Kundt's Strasbourg lab — **never once cited**, while Röntgen's own path to the discovery has been called "mysterious" (H. Lindner) and Röntgen ordered his lab notes burned before his death (1923). The erasure of a **Galician-Ukrainian** scientist's contribution from the X-ray story is the decolonial wound; inflating it into "he invented X-rays" is the error this dossier refuses. [T1: IEU — "observed and recorded on film 'mysterious invisible rays' several years prior to W. Roentgen's observation, but he published his results only after Roentgen published his"; T3: uk.wikipedia — Освіта та праця]

**What survived / what was erased:** the science and the Bible survived; the **author's Ukrainian identity and priority were long detached** from both — a Greek-Catholic Habsburg-Ukrainian patriot was an awkward figure for both Soviet historiography (which largely ignored him) and Western physics memory (which credited Röntgen alone). Post-1991 Ukraine reclaims him: **Тернопільський національний технічний університет імені Івана Пулюя**, the NBU coin (2010), the minor planet **(226858) Ivanpuluj**, and the НАН України Puliui Prize.

## 3. Major works

- `1865` — translated a **geometry textbook into Ukrainian** as a first-year student (for gymnasia that did not yet exist). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1871–1903` — **Ukrainian translation of the Holy Scripture** with Куліш and Нечуй-Левицький (Puluj: the Псалтир); complete Ukrainian Bible printed by the British and Foreign Bible Society, **1903**. [T1: IEU; T3]
- `1874` — first physics papers on the internal friction of air vs temperature (Wiener Berichte). [T3]
- `1880–1882` — **four foundational papers on cathode rays**, *Strahlendes Elektrodenmaterial* (Wiener Berichte); re-published in English by the London Physical Society as *Radiant Electrode Matter and the so-Called Fourth State* (*Physical Memoirs*, 1889). [T3: uk.wikipedia — Праці]
- `1881` — the **«Пулюєва трубка»** (Puluj tube), industrially produced in Leipzig; foundational X-ray-capable apparatus. [T3]
- `1896` — **«Über die Entstehung der Röntgen'schen Strahlen und ihre fotografische Wirkung»** (and its *Nachtrag*), Wiener Berichte Bd. 105 — among the first works to call the rays "Röntgen rays." [T3: uk.wikipedia — Праці]
- `1880s–1900s` — pioneering **alternating-current power-station design** in Bohemia (Prague long used electricity from a "Puluj" station); chair of electrotechnics founded 1902. [T3]
- **Political publicistics:** texts arguing for Ukrainian statehood, e.g. **«Самостійність України є ключем для мирного дому Европи»**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Джерела/Посилання]

## 4. Primary-source quotes (≥2 required)

Puliui wrote in Ukrainian, German and English; these Ukrainian-language statements are his own civic voice.

**Quote 1 — his Кредо (life motto):**

> «Нема більшого гонору для інтелігентного чоловіка, як берегти свою і національну честь та без нагороди вірно працювати для добра свого народу, щоб забезпечити йому кращу долю.»

Pedagogically central: a world-class physicist defining honour as **unpaid, faithful work for one's people** — the scientist-patriot ethos that frames the whole figure. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Кредо]

**Quote 2 — his thesis on Ukrainian statehood (essay title/argument):**

> «Самостійність України є ключем для мирного дому Европи.»

Puliui — a Habsburg-era physicist — argued Ukrainian independence as a condition of European peace, a strikingly modern claim; the corollary attributed to him by O. Hryniv is "**Без України Росія не спроможна на світове панування**" ("Without Ukraine Russia is incapable of world domination"). Presented as his publicist thesis (title-level), not a verified sentence-by-sentence quote. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Джерела/Посилання]

**Note:** these are cited from the uk.wikipedia entry (T3); Puluj is not in the literary corpus, so `verify_quote` is not applicable — the credo is widely attributed to him across the Ternopil/НТШ literature.

## 6. Contested points

- **The X-ray priority (the central debate) — see §2b.** The defensible claim is "foundational, under-credited pioneer whose tube and cathode-ray work were instrumental and who advanced X-ray physics with extraordinary speed"; the **overclaim** is "Puluj discovered/invented X-rays before Röntgen." Ukrainian/НТШ scholarship (Гайда, Пляцко) documents the under-crediting and the suspicious gaps in Röntgen's record; serious history still assigns the *discovery* to Röntgen (Nobel 1901). Both must be held together. [T3: uk.wikipedia; T2: Гайда/Пляцко]
- **Surname origin.** The "Пульгуй → Пулюй" change story has **no documentary support** (likely German transliteration variance) — a small example of keeping legend out of the record. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Зауваги]
- **National framing.** A Greek-Catholic Habsburg-Ukrainian who wrote in German and worked in Prague is sometimes claimed by Austrian/Czech and (wrongly) by Russian narratives; he was an explicit **Ukrainian patriot and НТШ member** who advocated Ukrainian independence — the Galician-Ukrainian identity is firm.
- **Russian disinformation / Soviet silence.** Soviet historiography largely **ignored** him (a "bourgeois," religious, émigré-adjacent Ukrainian patriot); the recovery of his name is a post-1991 Ukrainian act. The 2022 renaming of a "Ломоносов" street to "Івана Пулюя" street (Horodok) literalises the decolonial swap. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Вшанування]
