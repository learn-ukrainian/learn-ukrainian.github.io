## 1. Identity / chronology

- **Canonical UA:** `Борис Миколайович Мартос`.
- **Canonical EN:** `Borys Martos`.
- **Core identity:** Ukrainian public and political figure, economist, cooperative organizer and theorist, educator, member of the Ukrainian Central Rada and Little Rada, General Secretary of Land Affairs, Minister of Food Supplies, Minister of Finance, head / premier of the Council of People's Ministers of the Ukrainian People's Republic under the Directory, and later emigration academic.
- **Birth:** ESU gives old/new style 20 May (1 June) 1879, Hradyzk / `Градизьк`, Kremenchuk county, Poltava gubernia, now Poltava oblast. EIU, EIR, and UINP foreground 20 May 1879. IEU gives 1 June 1879 and uses `Horodyshche` in English; learner-facing text should use `20 May / 1 June 1879` only when calendar style is taught, otherwise `1879-1977`.
- **Death / burial:** EIU and ESU give 19 September 1977, Irvington, New Jersey; IEU gives Union, New Jersey. ESU and EIR place burial at Bound Brook / St Andrew cemetery context. Avoid the unsourced 19 October date found in derivative pages and Commons category metadata.
- **Family / social origin:** ESU / UINP / EIR describe him as coming from an old Cossack-officer / starshyna family from Poltava region.
- **Education:** ESU / EIR record Lubny classical gymnasium in 1897 and the mathematics faculty of Kharkiv University in 1908.
- **Student politics:** IEU / EIR describe a secret Ukrainian student hromada at Kharkiv University, participation in the Ukrainian student movement, cooperation with the Revolutionary Ukrainian Party, arrests, and later membership in the Ukrainian Social Democratic Workers' Party.
- **Teaching ban and cooperative path:** EIU / ESU / IEU say political activity cost him the right to teach; he then worked in Volhynia, Kuban, and Poltava cooperative and zemstvo structures before 1917.
- **1917 Central Rada:** EIU / ESU / EIR place him in the Ukrainian Central Rada and Little Rada from the USDRP, in the Peasant Union leadership, and in the first General Secretariat as General Secretary of Land Affairs.
- **Land-policy role:** EIU / ESU / UINP / EIR identify him as a coauthor or developer of Central Rada land legislation and land-committee work.
- **Hetmanate period:** ESU / IEU / UINP place him in cooperative work under the Ukrainian State / Hetmanate: Central Ukrainian Cooperative Committee, Dniprosoiuz, Ukrainbank, Kyiv cooperative education, and the Cooperative Institute.
- **Directory government:** EIU / ESU / UINP / EIR identify him as Minister of Food Supplies, Minister of Finance, and head / premier of the Council of People's Ministers. EIU gives 9 April-27 August 1919 for the finance-minister / head-of-government pairing; EIR notes his 17 August resignation and continued finance work. Use source-labeled dates for exact office chronology.
- **Emigration:** EIU / ESU / IEU / EIR place him in Germany, Czechoslovakia, Podebrady, Prague, Munich, Regensburg, Switzerland, and the United States, with roles in the Ukrainian Husbandry Academy, Ukrainian Technical and Husbandry Institute, Ukrainian Higher School of Economics, Institute for the Study of the USSR, NTSh, and UVAN.
- **Works:** EIU / ESU / IEU list `Теорія кооперації`, `Кооперативна ревізія`, `Гроші української держави`, `Оскілко й Болбочан`, `М. С. Грушевський, яким я його знав`, and the memoir / article collection `Визвольний здвиг України`.

## 2. Political pressure mechanism

- **Imperial policing and student organizing:** Martos' early arrests and teaching ban show how Ukrainian political education was policed before 1917. Student hromadas, RUP / USDRP work, and cooperative education became training grounds for later state service.
- **Cooperation as sovereignty infrastructure:** Credit unions, cooperative courses, Dniprosoiuz, Ukrainbank, and cooperative congresses were not just economics; they built Ukrainian-controlled credit, distribution, education, and professional networks.
- **Land reform pressure:** As General Secretary of Land Affairs, Martos worked inside the Central Rada's most explosive social question: peasant land hunger, landlord property, local land committees, and the search for law before full administrative capacity existed.
- **Food supply under war:** Under the Directory, food supply was a state-survival issue. Army provisioning, civilian scarcity, market disruption, and transport collapse made economics a military and legitimacy problem.
- **Finance and currency pressure:** Finance ministry work and the law on Ukrainian currency connect Martos to fiscal sovereignty. Teach `гривня` here as political-economic infrastructure, not only a modern shopping word.
- **Coalition and front-line pressure:** His April-August 1919 government worked amid Bolshevik, White, Polish, Entente, ZUNR-UNR, and internal socialist pressures. A clean "failed premier" story is too simple.
- **UNR-ZUNR tension:** EIR explicitly notes the program of factual union and a confrontation between Martos' government and ZUNR leadership. Present this as state-integration pressure under war, not as personal quarrel alone.
- **Emigration institution-building:** Ukrainian economic schools and institutes in Podebrady, Prague, Munich, and New York carried state memory, cooperative expertise, and professional vocabulary after territorial defeat.
- **Memory pressure:** Soviet narratives could dismiss UNR finance and cooperation as bourgeois-nationalist; diaspora memory can over-heroize all governments. The useful classroom frame is institutions under impossible constraints.

## 3. Major events / historical footprint

- **1899-1905 student and RUP / USDRP work:** EIR gives secret student-hromada participation, the 1900 Ukrainian student congress in Galicia, arrests, and USDRP entry as the political foundation.
- **1908-1917 cooperative career:** Volhynia, Kuban, and Poltava cooperative work turned a barred teacher into a practical organizer of credit, distribution, cooperative courses, and economic associations.
- **June-August 1917 General Secretary of Land Affairs:** Martos in the first General Secretariat gives a concrete administrative anchor for Central Rada state-building before a stable bureaucracy existed.
- **Central Rada land law / committees:** UINP and EIR identify land-committee organization and the Temporary Land Law of 18 January 1918 as a key policy footprint. Treat authorship as shared, not single-person.
- **1918 cooperative reconstruction:** Under the Hetmanate he shifted to cooperative infrastructure, chaired / helped organize cooperative bodies, worked around Dniprosoiuz and Ukrainbank, and helped build cooperative education in Kyiv.
- **December 1918 food-supply ministry:** His Directory-era food office connects the biography to daily survival, army logistics, and public trust.
- **Finance ministry and government leadership:** EIU / ESU identify him as Minister of Finance and head of the Council of People's Ministers in April-August 1919. EIR's resignation date nuance makes this a source-comparison anchor.
- **12 April 1919 government declaration:** EIR summarizes the government's program: rely on own forces, avoid foreign military force, provision the army and servicemen, and pursue practical union of western and eastern Ukrainian lands.
- **Ukrainian currency:** EIR and UINP link him to the law on independence of Ukrainian currency; use this to connect money, language, state symbols, and practical sovereignty.
- **1922-1938 Podebrady / Prague institutions:** Ukrainian Husbandry Academy and Ukrainian Technical and Husbandry Institute roles make him a bridge from revolutionary government to diaspora professional education.
- **1945-1949 Munich school:** Founder and rector of the Ukrainian Higher School of Economics keeps the cooperative-economics thread alive after the Second World War.
- **1954-1958 Institute for the Study of the USSR:** EIU / ESU / EIR identify leadership roles in the institute; this becomes a Cold War knowledge-production anchor.
- **1977 death and Bound Brook burial:** Late-life US emigration and burial among UPR-era colleagues provide a memory-history endpoint.

## 4. Pedagogical anchors

- **Prime minister without the modern state:** Teach `голова Ради народних міністрів УНР` as a historical office, not simply a modern Ukrainian prime minister title.
- **Land reform in one biography:** Martos helps connect peasant demands, Central Rada law, socialist parties, and administrative limits.
- **Cooperative movement as political language:** `кооперація`, `спілка`, `кредит`, `ревізія`, `банк`, and `земство` become vocabulary for Ukrainian self-organization before and after statehood.
- **Money as sovereignty:** Pair Martos with the OES / basic-language `гривня` surfaces: money is not only a word for prices but a claim to fiscal and symbolic independence.
- **Map anchor:** Hradyzk / Kremenchuk / Poltava region, Lubny, Kharkiv, Liubotyn, Volhynia, Kuban, Kyiv, Rivne, Kamianets-Podilskyi context if needed, Germany, Podebrady, Prague, Munich, Regensburg, Switzerland, New Jersey, Bound Brook.
- **Government under pressure anchor:** April-August 1919 can be taught as a compressed crisis: moving fronts, food, money, army, western-eastern Ukrainian union, and coalition resignation.
- **Emigration school anchor:** Podebrady and Munich let learners see why exile institutions mattered for engineers, economists, and administrators.
- **Comparison anchor:** Pair with Volodymyr Vynnychenko for General Secretariat / socialism, Symon Petliura for Directory leadership, Oleksandr Lototskyi for emigration institutions, and future Mykhailo Tuhan-Baranovskyi for cooperative economics.
- **Anti-hagiography anchor:** His government issued programs and laws but could not stabilize the UNR. Failure under structural pressure is part of the lesson.

## 6. Risks / open questions

- **Birth place / name drift:** Ukrainian sources use `Градизьк`; IEU's English entry says `Horodyshche` and points to a Cherkasy-region disambiguation; older English pages may show `Gradizhsk`. Treat Ukrainian institutional sources as primary for place display and use `Hradyzk` in learner-facing romanization.
- **Birth date style:** ESU explicitly gives 20 May (1 June); EIU / EIR / UINP foreground 20 May; IEU uses 1 June. Learner-facing text should not silently mix old/new style dates.
- **Death place drift:** EIU / ESU say Irvington; IEU says Union, New Jersey; all agree on 19 September 1977. Use New Jersey or source-labeled town if exact place matters.
- **August 1919 chronology:** EIU gives 9 April-27 August 1919 for head-of-government / finance-minister pairing; EIR says he resigned 17 August and remained finance minister later. Do not force one exact date across all roles.
- **Currency overclaim:** UINP calls him one of the "fathers" of the hryvnia; EIR links his work to the law on currency independence. Use careful wording: important in finance / currency policy, not sole creator.
- **Land-law authorship:** EIU / ESU / EIR call him an author or coauthor of land-law projects; legislation was collective and contested. Avoid "he wrote the land reform" as a sole-author claim.
- **Party simplification:** USDRP socialism, cooperative economics, and state-finance work can look contradictory if simplified. Present them as overlapping Ukrainian democratic-socialist and cooperative traditions.
- **ZUNR conflict framing:** EIR mentions confrontation between Martos' government and ZUNR leadership. Use this as wartime institutional tension, not as proof of anti-Galician bias.
- **Image rights:** Commons portrait `Martos Borys.jpg` is public-domain-marked but relies on a German pre-1945 photo rationale and needs US/country-of-origin scrutiny at production time. Prefer clear PD files with file-level checks.
- **Derivative web portraits:** UINP uses a photo credited to `amazing-ukraine.com`; do not use that image source directly unless rights are independently cleared.
