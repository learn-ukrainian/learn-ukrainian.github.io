## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Федір Кіндратович Вовк (also signed Хведір
  Вовк).
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Хведір Вовк; Fedir Vovk. Russian-imperial
  transliterations must not appear in body text except in the forbidden-forms
  field in §8.
- **Born:** 17 March 1847 (5 March O.S.) | село Крячківка, Пирятинський повіт,
  Полтавська губернія | now Полтавська область, Україна [T1: ЕІУ]
  [T3: uk.wikipedia].
- **Died:** 29 June 1918 | Жлобин, Білорусь | died suddenly while travelling
  from Petersburg to Ukraine, answering the UNR government's call for scholars
  to return and build the national state [T3: uk.wikipedia].
- **Family / education key facts:** Studied natural sciences at the Novorossiysk
  (Odesa) and then St Volodymyr (Kyiv) universities — chemistry, botany,
  zoology, comparative anatomy, anthropology; joined the Kyiv hromada alongside
  Drahomanov, Antonovych, Rylskyi, Chubynskyi, and Zhytetskyi [T3: uk.wikipedia].
  Core roles: anthropologist, ethnographer, archaeologist, museologist, and
  author of the first scientifically grounded concept of the Ukrainians as a
  distinct Slavic people [T1: ЕІУ].

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is a repression-by-forced-emigration biography; the mechanism is documented
imperial persecution that drove him abroad for decades.

- **What happened:** Forced into long emigration by Russian-imperial persecution
  of the Ukrainian movement.
- **When (specific dates):** Persecution forced Vovk to leave for abroad in 1879;
  he lived in Switzerland and France from 1879 to 1905, returning to the Russian
  Empire (Petersburg) only in 1906 [T3: uk.wikipedia].
- **By whom (specific Russian/Soviet/RF agency):** the Imperial Russian police
  apparatus acting against the Kyiv hromada and the South-Western Branch of the
  geographic society after the Ems Ukaz climate of 1876.
- **Document references:** the 1879 emigration is the anchor; it followed the
  same wave of suppression that exiled Chubynskyi and Drahomanov. Confirm the
  specific surveillance file against the ЕІУ entry and the Olha Franko
  monograph before module writing.
- **Mechanism specifics:** the empire did not imprison Vovk — it made his work
  impossible at home, so a leading Ukrainian scholar built his career in Paris.
  There he turned exile into advantage, training under the French
  anthropological school (Broca, Topinard, Manouvrier, Mortillet) and publishing
  Ukrainian ethnography to a European audience — a precise example of how
  imperial suppression exported Ukrainian scholarship.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** his corpus of about 628 works
  survived, much of it published abroad; his Kyiv-based institutional base was
  what the persecution denied him for a generation.

## 3. Major works

- `1891`–`1892` — «Шлюбний ритуал та обряди на Україні» (*Rites et usages
  nuptiaux en Ukraine*), first issued in Bulgaria, then Paris — his
  world-recognised comparative-ethnography study [T3].
- `1899` — edited «Матеріали до українсько-руської етнології», т. 1, for the
  NTSh Ethnographic Commission in Lviv [T3].
- `1901` — doctoral thesis «Скелетні видозміни ступні у приматів і людських
  расах» (Paris), awarded the Godard and Russian Academy of Sciences prizes
  [T3].
- `1908` — «Антропометричні досліди українського населення Галичини, Буковини й
  Угорщини» [T3].
- `1916` — «Антропологічні особливості українського народу» and «Етнографічні
  особливості українського народу», in vol. 2 of «Украинскій народъ въ его
  прошломъ и настоящемъ» — the synthesis arguing Ukrainians are a distinct
  people [T3] [T1: ЕІУ].
- `1928` (Prague) / `1995` (Kyiv reprint) — «Студії з української етнографії та
  антропології», the main Ukrainian-language collected studies [T3].
- Pioneer excavator of the Paleolithic Mizyn site (Мізинська стоянка) [T3].

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** academic scientific prose (anthropology, archaeology,
  comparative ethnography); much of the corpus is French.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** C1/C2 for the scholarly texts; B2 for
  carefully glossed excerpts about wedding ritual and folk custom.
- **Lexicon notes:** dense scientific terminology and period anthropological
  vocabulary; many terms are calqued from French and need modern Ukrainian
  equivalents.
- **Stylistic features:** systematic comparative method, classificatory rigour,
  European scholarly apparatus.

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated in modern UA scholarship:** Vovk's physical-anthropology data
  must be handled critically — small samples, period instruments, and the racial
  -typology framework of his school are dated; modern scholarship (Дяченко,
  Наулко and later) superseded the measurements while honouring the pioneering
  method. His ethnography of sexuality («Криптадії», eros/сороміцьке material)
  is a sensitive, recently re-edited part of the corpus (Маєрчик, Боряк, 2018).
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** popular memory turns Vovk's
  "Ukrainians are a distinct people" conclusion into a fixed racial claim; the
  module must present it as a historically situated scientific argument against
  imperial denial, not as anthropometric truth, and must not weaponise his
  physical typology.
- **Where modern Russian disinformation attacks them (if applicable):** Russian
  framing reduced and still reduces Ukraine to "south-western Russia" and denies
  a distinct Ukrainian nation; Vovk's life work directly refuted this, which is
  precisely why his decolonial significance should lead — while the method's
  limits are named honestly.
- **Polish / Jewish / other-perspective considerations (if applicable):** his
  comparative fieldwork spanned Galicia, Bukovyna, and Hungary; period ethnic
  categories must be framed historically, not reproduced as description.
