## 2. Oppression mechanism — Soviet co-optation and surveillance, not physical repression

This is **structurally a co-optation case**, not a Розстріляне-Відродження execution arc. Per the readiness-matrix `SOV` flag, Bilash must **not** be framed as a clean "Soviet success story," nor retro-fitted into a dissident martyrology he did not live. The honest frame is a Ukrainian-language song-canon composer who built an enduring national lyric **inside** the Soviet cultural apparatus, and whose most beloved work was simultaneously promoted and policed.

**The load-bearing documented episode — KGB "Два кольори" (1964).** After public performances of «Два кольори» (music by Bilash, lyrics by Дмитро Павличко, 1964), the authors were summoned to a "розмова" with КДБ officers who accused them of writing a "бандерівський гімн" ("Banderite anthem") because the song's red-and-black colours could be read as the colours of the OUN flag. Pavlychko recalled that he managed to persuade the officers this was not the authors' intent [T3: uk.wikipedia — Два кольори, citing Павличко interview] [T5: Україна молода; wz.lviv.ua — "Пісню «Два кольори» КДБ вважало гімном ОУН"; corroborated across outlets]. The same song was later reported as having been called "тюремною" (a "prison song") in Soviet times [T5: Gazeta.ua, 2017-02-05; corroborate before body use].

**Mechanism specifics.** The pressure here is the censorship-and-suspicion environment around Ukrainian-language popular song during russification: the state decorated Bilash (see §6 honours) while the security service read national-colour symbolism in a hit song as evidence of nationalist subversion. There is **no documented arrest, exile, or imprisonment** of Bilash; do not invent one. His Soviet-theme commissions (§3) are part of the same system and belong in the record alongside the «Два кольори» episode.

**What survived.** «Два кольори» outlived the regime: it was reclaimed as a national-memory song after 2014 (e.g. the гурт «Кому Вниз» performance, 8 May 2015, at the National Museum of the History of Ukraine in WWII) [T3: uk.wikipedia — Два кольори]. <!-- VERIFY exact 2015 framing if quoted in module -->

## 4. Primary-source quotes (≥2 required)

> Provenance note: the verse below is reproduced in the *Україна молода* feature (T5) from Bilash's autobiographical poetry / recollected speech. The attribution is plausible, but the exact wording should be verified against published collections (*Мелодія*, *Мамине крило*) before module use.

### Quote 1 — autobiographical verse on the 1946 rejection

> **В училище не прийняли,**
> **В директора вистачило духу**
> **Сказать, що я не маю слуху.**

*Pedagogical note:* the man told he had "no ear for music" became a People's Artist and the author of «Два кольори». This is a self-deprecating, accessible hook into a discussion of talent, gatekeeping, and persistence [T5: Україна молода — "Казали, що немає слуху"].

### Quote 2 — Bilash to Pavlychko, on finishing «Два кольори»

> **«Дмитре, ми з тобою написали щось таке, що нас переживе.»**

*Pedagogical note:* recollected by Pavlychko; the composer is naming, in the moment, that the song would in fact outlive both authors and the regime [T5: Україна молода; corroborated in Pavlychko interviews].

### Quote 3 — self-description (poet-composer)

> **Так і живу між звуками й словами,**
> **Немов між небом і землею я живу.**

*Pedagogical note:* Bilash on his double life as composer and poet — useful for the language-register section and for showing the figure's own voice beyond his melodies [T5: Україна молода].

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** folk-lyrical (пісенна лірика) — his song idiom rests on Ukrainian народнопісенна tradition; his own verse is plain, confessional, accessible.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** the canonical song texts he set (Pavlychko's «Два кольори», Tkach's «Ясени») are workable at **B1–B2**; his own poetry at **B2**; the opera *Гайдамаки* libretto draws on Shevchenko and sits **C1**.
- **Lexicon notes:** rich in concrete folk imagery (кольори, рушник, ясени, дорога, мати) — high-frequency, low-abstraction vocabulary ideal for affective B1/B2 work; watch dialect/poetic inversions in sung texts.
- **Stylistic features:** strophic song form, refrain-driven memorability, vyshyvanka/colour symbolism (red = love, black = sorrow) that the module can unpack directly.

## 6. Contested points

- **Soviet co-optation vs. national-song hero (the core tension).** Bilash held the full Soviet honours ladder — премія ім. М. Островського (1967), Всесоюзна премія Ленінського комсомолу (1968), Народний артист СРСР (1990) — and wrote Soviet-theme works (*Павло Корчагін*; opera *Прапороносці*; operetta *Дзвони Росії*). Popular memory simplifies him into a pure patriotic-song figure; scholarship must hold **both** the establishment role (he chaired the Kyiv composers' organisation 1976–1994) **and** the national-lyric achievement. Anti-hagiography is mandatory here.
- **«Два кольори» reception arc.** Komsomol-congress origin (1964) → KGB suspicion as an "OUN anthem" → post-2014 reclamation as a war-memory song. The black-and-red symbolism is the contested hinge; present the documented arc, do not flatten it.
- **Modern Russian disinformation.** The general imperial move of absorbing Soviet-era Ukrainian composers into a "Soviet/Russian" cultural product erases the Ukrainian-language song-canon project Bilash served. Reject that framing without flipping to hagiography.
- **HIST-alignment.** Bilash is a music/song-canon figure, **not** a state-building / UNR-ZUNR / dissident / wartime actor — so the HIST-alignment checkpoint is light: align only to the Soviet cultural-policy / russification / шістдесятники context (the frame that makes the KGB episode legible), and do not invent a separate political-history frame inside the biography.
