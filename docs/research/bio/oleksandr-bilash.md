## 2. Why Bilash Belongs

Bilash belongs because he is a composer of the modern Ukrainian song canon.
His importance is not a security-service story. The module spine should be:
Ukrainian language, melody, poetic collaboration, folk-song idiom, and the
survival of his songs in Ukrainian public memory.

The clearest anchor works are:

- «Два кольори» (lyrics: Дмитро Павличко, 1964).
- «Ясени» (lyrics: Михайло Ткач, 1963).
- «Лелеченьки» from the film music to «Сон» about Taras Shevchenko.
- Opera «Гайдамаки» after Shevchenko's poem.

This gives the BIO writer a strong nation-building route without pretending
Bilash was a dissident martyr and without letting Soviet institutional context
erase the Ukrainian cultural meaning of his work.

## 4. Modern Reception

The strongest modern-reception source found so far is the public broadcaster
concert on Суспільне Культура:

- [«Два кольори», «Заповідаю вам любов», «Сніг на зеленому листі» | Концерт
  української ретро-музики](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P6_-jd0nvRU)
  [T2/T3 media].
- Local metadata check: channel `Суспільне Культура`, upload date 2026-06-28,
  concert for the 95th anniversary of Oleksandr Bilash.
- «Два кольори» begins around `1:03:15`.

This source is pedagogically important because it shows Bilash being publicly
commemorated in wartime Ukraine through Ukrainian music, not through a Soviet
or security-service frame.

**Module-writing implication:** open the eventual lesson with a song-memory
question, not an institutional biography. Let students first hear or read why
«Два кольори» and «Ясени» feel legible as Ukrainian lyric memory: colour,
mother, road, ash tree, voice, refrain, and collaboration between composer and
poet. Then move outward to the composer, his education, his large-form work, and
only after that to the Soviet-era institutional context. This order matters
because it makes the Ukrainian cultural object visible before the state system
appears. It also keeps the module aligned with the user's selection criterion:
figures who matter for Ukrainian nation-building, language, and culture. The
hard question for discussion should be "How can a song written inside Soviet
institutions still carry Ukrainian cultural continuity?" rather than "What was
the security-service story?"

## 5. Pavlychko Anecdote Scope Guardrail

There is a documented source trace for the story that Soviet officials read
«Два кольори» suspiciously because of the red-and-black colour symbolism:

- BBC Україна interview with Дмитро Павличко, mirrored at Korrespondent.
- Later *Високий Замок* article/interview with the same Pavlychko recollection.
- Ukrainian Wikipedia «Два кольори» cites this reception history.

**Editorial rule:** this is optional reception context for one song. Do not
frame Bilash through a security-service storyline; do not imply he was an
agent; do not make this the module warning label; do not turn it into a
repression biography. If used, write it as Pavlychko's recollection about how a
song was read by Soviet authorities.
