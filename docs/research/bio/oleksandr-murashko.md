## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Олександр Олександрович Мурашко (born
  Олександр Крачковський).
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** Олександер Мурашко (Galician/diaspora spelling);
  Oleksandr Murashko. Russian-imperial transliterations must not appear in body
  text except in the forbidden-forms field in §8.
- **Born:** 7 September 1875 (26 August O.S.) | Київ | then Russian Empire
  [T1: ЕІУ] [T3: uk.wikipedia].
- **Died:** 14 June 1919 | Київ | killed by a gunshot to the back of the head
  near his home on Лукʼянівка; buried at the Lukianivka cemetery
  [T3: uk.wikipedia].
- **Family / education key facts:** Born out of wedlock as Крачковський; raised
  partly by a peasant grandmother near Борзна who told him of the Cossack past;
  nephew of Микола Мурашко, founder of the Kyiv Drawing School. Studied at the
  Petersburg Academy higher art school (from 1894) in Ілля Рєпін's studio (from
  1896), then Paris and Munich (1901–1903) [T3: uk.wikipedia]. Core roles:
  painter, pedagogue, civic organiser, and co-founder of the Українська академія
  мистецтв [T1: ЕІУ].

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This biography carries a documented assassination whose motive is contested; the
mechanism must be stated plainly and sourced, without fabricating a settled
verdict.

- **What happened:** Murashko was assassinated — shot in the back of the head on
  a June night in 1919 near his Лукʼянівка home, at age 43.
- **When (specific dates):** the night of 14 June 1919, during the violent
  revolutionary period in Kyiv [T3: uk.wikipedia].
- **By whom (specific Russian/Soviet/RF agency) — CONTESTED:** contemporary
  press reported robbery-murder by "bandits"; a version many later scholars
  consider probable is politically motivated killing amid 1919 Kyiv Red Terror
  / early Cheka violence against Ukrainian civic and cultural elites. Do not
  conflate this 1919 context with the later Stalinist Great Terror of the 1930s
  or the executions of figures such as Лесь Курбас and Микола Зеров. Present
  both versions and do not assert the Cheka attribution as established fact.
- **Document references:** the press report of robbery-murder and the
  scholarly-biographical discussion of a political killing (Членова; the ArtHuss
  memoir volumes) are the anchors; cite the dispute, not a single verdict.
- **Mechanism specifics:** the honest frame is an unresolved violent death of a
  leading Ukrainian artist at the moment he was building national art
  institutions — the module should hold the documented murder and the contested
  motive together, modelling source-based caution rather than martyrology.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** his paintings survived in museums in
  Ukraine, Hungary, and elsewhere; the 1922 posthumous exhibition (organised by
  Федір Ернст) gathered over 70 works. The mature career that the 1919 killing
  cut short is the loss.

## 3. Major works

- `1906` — «Карусель», which won a gold medal at the Munich international
  exhibition and was bought for the Budapest Museum of Fine Arts — his
  breakthrough to European recognition [T3].
- `1909` — solo exhibition of 25 works in Berlin, Cologne, and Düsseldorf [T3].
- `1910` — «Портрет Людмили Куксіної» [T3].
- `1911`–`1912` — participation in the Munich «Secession» exhibitions [T3].
- `1916` — «Праля» («The Washerwoman»); «Надвечірʼя» [T3].
- `1917`–`1918` — «Жінка з квітами», «Сонячний рефлекс», family portraits — his
  late, freer manner [T3].
- Earlier portraits of Микола Мурашко («Старий учитель») and genre scenes of
  the Ukrainian народна тема [T3].

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** visual-painterly; his verbal register (letters, memoirs of him)
  is warm, lyrical, life-affirming.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** A2/B1 for guided viewing of the paintings;
  B2/C1 for discussion of impressionism, modern, and the Academy's founding.
- **Lexicon notes:** art-historical vocabulary (живопис, портрет, пленер,
  імпресіонізм, модерн, декоративізм); avoid importing Russian-school art terms
  uncritically.
- **Stylistic features:** synthesis of Repin-school realism, impressionist plein
  air, and art-nouveau decorative organisation into a personal Ukrainian
  colourism.

## 6. Contested points

- **What's debated in modern UA scholarship:** the central dispute is the motive
  of his 1919 murder — robbery versus a politically motivated Cheka killing of
  the Ukrainian intelligentsia — which remains unresolved; scholarship also
  weighs how to read his Repin/Petersburg training against his role as a founder
  of Ukrainian national art, and where to place him between realism,
  impressionism, and art nouveau.
- **What gets simplified in popular memory:** popular memory either claims him as
  a "Russian Repin pupil" or flattens him into a martyr. Lead with the painter
  who raised Ukrainian art to a European level and co-founded the Ukrainian
  Academy of Arts, holding the contested murder as an open, sourced question.
- **Where modern Russian disinformation attacks them (if applicable):** Russian
  framing claims Murashko for Russian art via his Petersburg years; counter with
  the documented facts that a critic noted his works were absent from Russian
  Empire museums while present in Bucharest, England, and America, and that he
  built Ukrainian art institutions in Kyiv.
- **Polish / Jewish / other-perspective considerations (if applicable):** his
  Academy circle included Jewish-Ukrainian artists such as Абрам Маневич; the
  early-twentieth-century Kyiv art world was multi-ethnic and should be framed
  as such, not reduced to a single national story.
