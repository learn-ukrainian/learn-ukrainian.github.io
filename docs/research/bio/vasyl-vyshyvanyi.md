> **Framing note (read first).** This figure invites two opposite distortions and the dossier refuses both. **Against the wiki's thin hagiography** («велична постать… мученицька смерть»): Vyshyvanyi was a real but **politically marginal** émigré whom contemporaries judged «замріяний ідеаліст… аніж дієвий політик», who **never officially claimed the Ukrainian throne**, and who had no plan of state-building. **Against the Soviet/Russian smear**: he was not a foreign "bourgeois puppet" but a Habsburg archduke who learned Ukrainian, Ukrainianised his troops, commanded the USS, served the UNR, refused Nazi cooperation, and **died in Soviet MGB custody in Kyiv in 1948**. One specific anti-myth correction the brief flags: his command was the **USS in southern Ukraine in 1918**, **not** the 1918–19 battles for Lviv or the 1-ша бригада УСС (Jan 1919) — that Galician-Army context was not his.

## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Вільге́льм Франц Йо́зеф Карл фон Га́бсбург-Лотари́нзький (нім. Wilhelm Franz Joseph Karl von Habsburg-Lothringen); known in Ukraine as **Васи́ль Виши́ваний**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "Вільгельм Габсбург"; T2: Snyder, *The Red Prince*]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** **Василь Вишиваний** (the name his Ukrainian soldiers gave him, from the вишиванка he wore); "the Red Prince" (English, after Snyder). Forbidden Russified forms in §8.
- **Born:** **10 лютого 1895** | поблизу **Пули**, Австрійське Примор'я, Австро-Угорщина (нині Істрійська жупанія, Хорватія) | Австро-Угорщина. [T3: uk.wikipedia — "10 лютого 1895… Пула"]
- **Died:** **18 серпня 1948** | **тюремна лікарня Лук'янівської тюрми, Київ** | Українська РСР, СРСР | двосторонній туберкульоз, у радянському ув'язненні (МДБ). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Арешт та смерть"; primary: ГДА СБУ case file]
- **Family / education key facts:** Youngest of six children of Archduke **Карл Стефан** (an Austro-Hungarian admiral, later a Polish-throne claimant) and Archduchess Maria Theresa. Raised at **Живець (Żywiec)** in Galicia; educated at the Vienna Realschule and the **Терезіанська військова академія** (Wiener Neustadt), graduating февраль 1915 as a lieutenant. Chose **Ukrainian** as an elective; read Hrushevsky's «Мала Історія України» (1915), Franko, Fedkovych, Stefanyk, Shevchenko. Never married in Ukraine (later, by uncertain accounts, to Amalія Вюртембергська or the singer Іванна Шмериковська-Прийма); a son, Franz. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Ранні роки"/"Освіта"; T2: Snyder]

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is a **genuine Soviet killing-in-custody**, documented in declassified MGB files.

- **What happened:** arrested by the **Soviet MGB (МДБ СРСР)**, interrogated for half a year, and **died in a Kyiv prison hospital** of untreated bilateral tuberculosis aggravated by detention conditions.
- **When (specific):** arrested **26 серпня 1947** in Soviet-occupied Vienna (together with ОУН émigré Роман Новосад), after a meeting with a French-intelligence officer, **капітан Пеліс'є**. Held first at the **Карлсбадська тюрма** (preliminary investigation), then transferred **наприкінці листопада 1947 до Лук'янівської тюрми в Києві**. Interrogated **almost nightly for ~6 months**. Died **о 3-й годині ночі 18 серпня 1948**. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Арешт та смерть"]
- **By whom (specific agency):** the **Ministry of State Security (МДБ) of the USSR**; the investigation pressed him to confess contacts with the OUN underground and Western intelligence.
- **Document references (primary):** the **declassified ГДА СБУ case file** records the charges (espionage for Western powers, ties to OUN), the interrogation regime, and the **death certificate citing двосторонній туберкульоз** — correcting the prison doctor's misleading "heart disease" entry. His burial place is **unknown** — probably an unmarked spot by the fence of the Лук'янівський cemetery. [Primary: ГДА СБУ — справа Габсбурга-Вишиваного, declassified]
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** the case file and his poetry survived; he was **posthumously rehabilitated 16 січня 1989** by the Soviet rehabilitation commission, after which many documents were declassified and the myth-laden gaps could finally be filled with record. [T3: uk.wikipedia]

## 3. Major works

Vyshyvanyi was primarily a soldier-diplomat; his literary output is small but genuine.

- `1921` — **«Минають дні»** (Відень) — a collection of **23 Ukrainian-language poems**, dedicated to his USS comrades; lyric of arms, memory and the Carpathians. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Літературна діяльність"]
- `1920s` — regular contributions to the Vienna Ukrainian journal **«Соборна Україна»** and other diaspora periodicals. [T3]

His more consequential "works" are **acts**: the Ukrainianisation of his cavalry sotnia (1915), the command of the **USS** in southern Ukraine (1918), his role in the **Берестейський мирний договір** negotiations and its secret Ukrainian-Galicia annex, and his service as колонель / head of the foreign-liaison department of the UNR General Staff (1919).

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** **earnest patriotic-lyric Ukrainian**, written by a **non-native speaker who learned the language as an adult** — direct, declarative, occasionally simple in syntax; a remarkable L2-acquisition case.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** **B1–B2** for his poetry — accessible verse, useful precisely because it was written by a learner; the *historiography* around him (Habsburg politics, Brest-Litovsk, MGB files) is C1.
- **Lexicon notes:** military-patriotic field — стрільці, зброя, воля, до бою; Carpathian/Hutsul imagery — гуцули, хмари, гори. Standard literary forms.
- **Stylistic features:** apostrophe and direct address (до стрільців, до хмар), commemorative dedication, plain rhyme — the diction of a sincere convert rather than a virtuoso.

## 6. Contested points

- **Anti-myth: the "claimant to the throne."** He was floated by some émigré monarchist circles as a candidate for the Ukrainian булава, but **he never officially proclaimed such an intention**, and his own 1947 testimony records that he **refused** the 1918 Zaporizhzhia plot. The "would-be king" framing is largely later legend (and the title of the 2021 Zhadan-libretto opera, below), not his programme. [T3: uk.wikipedia; primary interrogation, §4]
- **Anti-myth: the wrong battlefield.** His military command was the **USS / „Група архікнязя Вільгельма" in southern Ukraine** — he took over the USS **near Kherson on 1 April 1918** and operated around Zaporizhzhia/Oleksandrivsk, refusing to requisition grain or suppress peasant revolts. He was **not** the commander in the **1918 battles for Lviv** nor of the **1-ша бригада УСС (Jan 1919)** of the Galician Army — that context belongs to ZUNR/УГА history, not to him. Conflating the two is a common error and is avoided here. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Дислокація УСС в Україні"]
- **Political weight, honestly.** Skoropadsky saw him as a rival and complained to the Germans, who curtailed his activity; but in emigration he had **no political base** and was viewed as «замріяний ідеаліст». His break with Petliura came over the **1920 Варшавська угода**, which he attacked as an "unnatural" cession of Galicia to Poland — costing him his father's support (Karl Stephan publicly disowned him). [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Співпраця з Директорією"/"Еміграція"]
- **WWII conduct.** Like the Habsburgs generally, he **refused to cooperate with the Nazis**, was under Gestapo surveillance, and was suspected of links to British intelligence — the thread the Soviets later pulled. [T3]
- **Soviet/Russian disinformation.** Soviet historiography cast him as a foreign **«ставленик буржуазних сил»**, a marionette of Austrian politicians; Russian historiography repeats the "puppet" line. Both deny him agency. The declassified files show a man who chose Ukraine and paid for it in a Soviet prison. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Оцінка діяльності"]
- **Modern reception.** The 2021 opera **«Вишиваний. Король України»** (composer Алла Загайкевич, libretto **Сергій Жадан**) and the 2025 НБУ commemorative coin signal his post-2014 recovery — useful as a "how memory is made" case, kept distinct from the documented biography. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Ушанування пам'яті"]
