> **Framing note (read first).** Dontsov is the hardest balance in this batch. He is a genuinely anti-imperial Ukrainian thinker whose central life-axis — Ukraine must orient to Europe and break psychologically and politically from Russia — was prophetic and is vindicated by the present. He is **also** the author of an explicitly anti-democratic, amoral, violence-sanctifying, elitist doctrine («чинний»/integral nationalism) whose darker premises this dossier states plainly, not euphemistically. Both are true at once. The failure modes this figure invites are symmetrical: hagiography that launders the doctrine into harmless patriotism, and the mirror-image Russian/Soviet move that collapses all Ukrainian national thought into "Dontsovism = fascism." Neither is taken here.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

Dontsov is **not** a figure killed by Russia; he died in old age in Canadian emigration in 1973. Honesty requires that the "Russia-oppressed" frame be stated precisely and without inflation. It operates on four documented axes:

- **Tsarist political repression (direct, dated):** As a student social-democrat he was **arrested twice** by the Imperial authorities — **1905 in St. Petersburg** and **1908 in Kyiv**; after the second arrest he served **eight months' imprisonment** before being released on bail through relatives' efforts, whereupon he left for Austrian-ruled Galicia. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Політична діяльність"]
- **Forced emigration (twice over a lifetime):** the 1908 departure to Galicia was flight from Imperial prosecution; the **1939 departure** (Bucharest → Prague → Germany → Paris → USA → Canada) was flight from the advancing Soviet power that would have killed him. He never returned to Soviet Ukraine and could not. [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Soviet ban, erasure and weaponisation (indirect, decades-long):** in the USSR Dontsov's works were banned outright; his name was made into a propaganda synonym for "буржуазний націоналізм" and treason, the bogeyman against whom Soviet anti-nationalist campaigns were run. This is the dominant Soviet mechanism: not a bullet but a seven-decade criminalisation of his ideas and the people who read them.
- **Persecution of his milieu (the human cost):** the «Вісниківство» literary-ideological circle he edited and shaped (Олена Теліга, Олег Ольжич, Євген Маланюк, Юрій Клен, Леонід Мосендз) was decimated — **Теліга and Ольжич were murdered, and Соవిет/Nazi terror scattered the rest** (§7). His influence-network paid in lives even though he did not.
- **Russian aggression, present tense (documented):** after Russia's 2022 occupation of Melitopol the occupiers **demonstratively removed his memorial plaque** in his birth city — a 2022 act recorded on video. The Russian state still treats his name as an enemy символ, which is itself evidence of the through-line. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Вшанування пам'яті" / "Демонтаж меморіальної дошки"]

**What survived / what was destroyed:** his published corpus survived in diaspora editions (his collected «Вибрані твори» were re-edited by Олег Баган, Дрогобич, 2011–2016); the Науково-ідеологічний центр ім. Дмитра Донцова (2007) curates the legacy. What the USSR destroyed was open access to the texts inside Ukraine and the lives of the people who carried his ideas underground.

## 3. Major works

Dontsov was a publicist and editor, not a politician of action. His "works" are books and journals, in chronological order:

- `1913` — *Модерне москвофільство* — early articulation of the anti-Russian orientation; the warning against modelling Ukraine on its "northern neighbour." [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1914–1918` — leadership of the **Союз визволення України (СВУ)**; first head of the organisation (founded 4 August 1914). [T1: ЕІУ]
- `1918` — director of the **Українська телеграфічна агенція (УТА)** under Hetman Skoropadskyi (appointed 24 May 1918). [T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1921` — *Підстави нашої політики* — the "orientation on our own forces / on Europe, against Russia" programme. [T1: ЕСУ]
- `1922–1939` — editor of **«Літературно-науковий вістник»** (from 1922), then **«Вістник»** (Lviv) — the most influential nationalist literary-ideological journal of interwar Galicia, around which «вісниківство» formed. [T1: ЕІУ; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- `1926` — ***Націоналізм*** — his central work; the systematic statement of «чинний» (integral) nationalism. [T1: ЕСУ; T4: Zaitsev]
- `1944` — *Дух нашої давнини* — the traditionalist / "ruling-caste" book; deepened the elite-and-hierarchy doctrine. [T3: uk.wikipedia — §"Еволюція в бік традиціоналізму"]
- `post-war` — *Хрест проти диявола*, *Правда прадідів великих*, *Дві літератури нашої доби*, *Московська отрута* — émigré-period polemics. [T3: uk.wikipedia — bibliography]

**Documentary note:** his archive («Архів Дмитра Донцова») is described by Г. Сварник (Пам'ятки України, 1994); the primary corpus is the diaspora «Вибрані твори», not directly opened this pass (see §10 honest gap).

## 4. Primary-source quotes (≥2 required)

> Dontsov is a **publicist**, not a belletrist; the project's literary `verify_quote` corpus does not index him — `verify_quote(author="Донцов", text="зміцнювати волю до життя…")` returned `matched:false, best_confidence:0.0`, which reflects corpus coverage, not absence of the texts. The passages below are from **«Націоналізм» (1926)** as transmitted in the ЕСУ-/ЕІУ-anchored uk.wikipedia article and must be cited to that edition. They are quoted **because** they are uncomfortable: a hagiographic dossier would hide them.

**Quote 1 — the two founding "вимоги" of «чинний націоналізм» (the doctrine in its own words):**

> «зміцнювати волю до життя, до влади, до експансії», — і — «стремління до боротьби та свідомість її конечности».

These are Dontsov's own first two principles of active nationalism. Pedagogically they are the core teaching object: the doctrine is built on *will, power, expansion and struggle* as ends, not on rights or institutions. [«Націоналізм», 1926; transmitted T3: uk.wikipedia citing ЕСУ; не в корпусі проєкту]

**Quote 2 — the explicit embrace of hierarchy among nations (the doctrine's darkest premise, quoted, not hidden):**

> «Є вищі i менш варті народи, ті, що вміють правити іншими (і собою), і народи, що цього не вміють… Право сильних рас організувати людей i народи для зміцнення існуючої культури i цивілізації».

Quoted **so that learners and writers confront it**, not to endorse it. This is the passage that grounds the "proto-fascist" reading of Dontsov (§6); reproducing it verbatim is the anti-hagiography discipline in action. [«Націоналізм», 1926; transmitted T3: uk.wikipedia; не в корпусі проєкту]

**Honest gap (§4 + §10):** the verbatim passages were captured from the encyclopedic article's transmission of «Націоналізм», **not** from a directly-opened critical edition with page numbers; they are therefore flagged "не в корпусі проєкту; за вид. «Націоналізм», 1926" rather than given a false page citation.

## 5. Language register

- **Register:** high **publicistic-philosophical** Ukrainian — polemical essay, ideological manifesto, literary criticism. Dense, allusive, saturated with Nietzschean/Schopenhauerian vocabulary and Galician interwar lexis.
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** **C2.** Dontsov's prose is among the most demanding non-fiction in the track — abstract political philosophy plus period orthography (he wrote «конечности», «відпорности» etc.). The *biography* can be told at B2–C1; the *texts* are C2; the §6 debate is C1–C2.
- **Lexicon notes (pre-teach, all VESUM-verified FOUND):** націоналізм, волюнтаризм, експансія, інтегральний, ірраціоналізм, еліта, імперіалізм, фанатизм, традиціоналізм. Period spellings (конечність → конечности in gen.) and the loaded term «чинний» (= active/operative) must be glossed as Dontsov's own coinage-in-use.
- **Stylistic features:** aphoristic absolutism, deliberate provocation, "переоцінка вартостей" (Nietzsche's *Umwertung*) as a recurring move; the rhetorical fusion of literary criticism with political command.

## 6. Contested points (Block G — extended, ≥300 words; anti-hagiography)

**This is the load-bearing section for this figure and is deliberately the longest.**

**(a) Is it fascism? The central scholarly dispute.** Dontsov's «чинний націоналізм» openly demands **fanaticism and amorality** ("національна ідея мусила бути аморальною"), the rule of an «ініціативна меншість» through «творче насильство», a hierarchy of "higher and lesser nations," and the rejection of rational, humanist, democratic politics in favour of "воля до життя, до влади, до експансії." Historian **Олександр Зайцев** (*Український інтеґральний націоналізм 1920-х–1930-х років*, Критика, 2013) situates Dontsov within the European "ustashoid"/integral-nationalist family and treats his ideology as a Ukrainian variant of the interwar radical right — while distinguishing the *ideologue* Dontsov from the *organisation* (OUN) that selectively absorbed him. **Myroslav Shkandrij** (*Ukrainian Nationalism: Politics, Ideology, and Literature, 1929–1956*, Yale, 2015) and **Trevor Erlacher** (*Ukrainian Nationalism in the Age of Extremes: An Intellectual Biography of Dmytro Dontsov*, HURI, 2021) document both the proto-fascist content **and** Dontsov's idiosyncrasy (he held no party office, commanded nothing, and quarrelled with almost everyone, including the OUN leadership). The honest statement: the doctrine **contains** authentically fascist-adjacent elements; "Dontsov was a fascist" as a flat identity claim is contested and over-simplifies a polemicist who mutated across decades.

**(b) Antisemitism and the wartime writings.** Dontsov's interwar and wartime publicism includes antisemitic and pro-Axis-orientation passages; his 1930s admiration for the "успіхи" of radical movements in Italy and Germany is on the record (he saw them instrumentally, as models of national will). This must not be sanitised. It must also not be read as identical to the OUN's actions, which he did not direct.

**(c) Relationship to OUN — influence, not command.** Dontsov shaped a generation (the OUN leadership read him; «вісниківство» fed the movement's romanticism), but he **never** headed OUN, never took the OUN oath, and was repeatedly at odds with its actual leaders. Collapsing "Dontsov" into "OUN" — in either a celebratory or a condemnatory direction — is the recurring error. [T4: Zaitsev; T4: Erlacher]

**(d) The democratic critique (from within Ukrainian thought).** Dontsov's integral nationalism was opposed *at the time* by other Ukrainian thinkers — most importantly **В'ячеслав Липинський**, whose conservative, law-and-statehood, territorial-political nationalism is the explicit antithesis of Dontsov's voluntarist ethnic mobilisation (Lisovyi and others frame the Lypynskyi–Dontsov axis as the great interwar Ukrainian debate). Presenting Dontsov without Lypynskyi as his counter-pole is itself a distortion. [T4: В. Лісовий, "Драгоманов і Донцов," Філос. і соц. думка, 1991]

**(e) What popular memory simplifies.** Decommunisation-era street-naming turned Dontsov into an uncomplicated patriot-icon (вулиці in Dnipro, Lviv, Zaporizhzhia, Kherson etc.); the doctrine's anti-democratic, anti-pluralist content is flattened out of that public image. A curriculum that teaches him must teach the texts, not the plaques.

**(f) Russian/Soviet disinformation.** The mirror danger: the USSR, and now the RF, weaponise "Dontsovism" to brand the *entire* Ukrainian national project as fascism — a propaganda move that exploits real passages (§6a–b) to delegitimise Ukrainian statehood wholesale. The correct response is neither denial nor amplification: quote Dontsov accurately, attribute the scholarship, and refuse the Russian syllogism that "Dontsov was extreme → Ukrainian nationhood is illegitimate." [T3: uk.wikipedia; T4: Rudling on the manufacturing of myths]
