## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Зятик Іван. Vatican form: «Ivan Ziatyk» [S4].
- **Born:** 1899-12-26 [S1] [S2], в селі Одрехово «неподалік від Сянока (нині
  територія Польщі)» [S1] [S2], «в сім'ї убогих селян Степана та Марії Зятиків»
  [S1].
- **Died:** 1952-05-17, «у в'язничному шпиталі» [S1] [S2] — prison-hospital
  statement kept separate from the S1/S2 Озерлаг colony wording (no synthesized
  «Озерлаг prison hospital» location). [S5] frames death at Bratsk, Irkutsk
  region, and states «помер від катувань» as encyclopedia wording — not a
  primary MVD file and not a merger with the prison-hospital clause. Vatican
  frame: «(1899-1952)» [S4].
- **Education:** Сяніцька гімназія 1911–1919; Перемиська духовна семінарія from
  1919, «закінчив з відзнакою 30 червня 1923 року»; ordained the same year [S1]
  [S2].
- **Pre-Redemptorist decade:** 1925–1935 prefect of the Greek Catholic seminary
  in Przemyśl; teaching catechetics and special dogmatics; confessor and
  catechist at a girls' gymnasium. The same Church biography is reproduced on
  both live pages [S1] and [S2]; [S2] is a duplicate/reprint for this shared
  claim, not an independent or supplementary source. Count the decade once.
- **Redemptorists:** «15 липня 1935 року о. І. Зятик остаточно вирішив вступити
  до Чину Найсвятішого Ізбавителя» [S1]; novitiate at Holosko; assigned to the
  Lviv monastery in 1937; from 1937 taught dogmatic theology and Scripture at
  the Redemptorist seminary [S2].
- **Office (person-specific, attributed):** two distinct attributed steps in the
  shared Church biography [S1] [S2] (duplicate/reprint; count once), kept
  separate: [S1] states that Metropolitan Josyf Slipyi appointed four vicars,
  including protoihumen Joseph De Vokht, and separately that on departure
  De Vokht transferred succession / powers; [S2] states that De Vokht
  transferred powers to Ziatyk. [S5] independently states that after the 1948
  deportation of protoihumen Joseph de Vokht, Ziatyk received his powers.
  S1/S2 date only the 17 October 1948 Holosko→Univ transfer and then say
  «невдовзі» for De Vokht's deportation (to Belgium on [S1]/[S2] — deportation
  destination, not a nationality claim) — do not back-date that «невдовзі»
  clause from S1/S2 alone. This packet does not infer a
  local-versus-foreigner automatic procedure. Vatican title: «Vicar General of
  the Ukrainian Greek Catholic Church» [S4].
- **Beatified:** 27 червня 2001 — shared beatification frame on the Church
  biography [S2] and the Vatican act [S4]; [S2] is not an independent second
  Church witness for the shared narrative.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** arrest, MGB investigation with hostage pressure on
  relatives, conviction, camp, death.
- **Arrest:** «5 січня 1950 року о. Івана заарештували» [S1] [S2]; the summary
  Synod page agrees: «5 січня 1950 р. радянська влада заарештувала священика»
  [S3].
- **The warrant came after the arrest:** «а 20 січня того ж року Управління
  Міністерства державної безпеки видало ордер на його арешт та обшук» [S1] —
  fifteen days later. The dossier states what the source states; it does not add
  a motive for the investigators, and does not generalize this into "standard
  practice", because no adopted source makes that generalization.
- **Charge (Soviet allegation, not established fact):** «Характер „злочину“ —
  „сприяння учасникам антирадянської націоналістичної організації та
  антирадянська агітація“» (ст. 20-54-1′а», 54–10 ч.2 КК УРСР) [S1]. Verified
  verbatim against the live page by raw-text retrieval 2026-07-17; an earlier
  draft truncated this to agitation alone and dropped the
  anti-Soviet-nationalist-organization clause. The scare quotes around «злочину»
  are the source's own. [S1] also carries an attributed interrogation-protocol
  passage alleging correspondence and material aid — present that text as
  allegation only; it neither proves guilt nor supports a categorical claim that
  “nothing the system called activity” existed. [S2] renders the charge in the
  same terms (assisting anti-Soviet nationalist organizations). Across education,
  office, charge, testimony, the five-diagnosis medical transcription, and the
  beatification narrative, the two live pages reproduce the same Church
  biography: [S2] is a duplicate/reprint for every shared claim, retained under
  separate institutional attribution/URL only; never count a shared claim twice
  (see the cause-of-death table below).
- **Sentence:** «Остаточний вирок було винесено в Києві 21 листопада 1951 року —
  о. Івана Зятика засуджено на 10 років позбавлення волі» [S1] [S2].
- **Camp:** S1/S2 transmit the secondary Church wording «виправно-трудовій
  колонії Озерний Лагер № 7 МВД м. Братська Іркутської області» [S1] [S2].
  This packet retains that phrase only as explicitly attributed S1/S2 wording;
  it does not use it as a complete institutional classification of Озерлаг or
  as an independently verified exact location, and it does not reduce Озерлаг
  to one colony or one point.
- **Succession sequence (attributed, not automatic):** two distinct steps in the
  shared Church biography — [S1] reports Slipyi’s appointment of four vicars
  (including De Vokht) and, separately, De Vokht’s transfer of succession on
  departure; [S2] reports De Vokht transferred powers to Ziatyk — but [S2] is a
  reprint for the shared transfer claim, not a second independent witness. Do
  not collapse appointment and transfer into a single “confirmation of
  powers” claim, and do not invent a local-versus-foreigner rule or an
  automatic procedural succession.

- **Death: record versus testimony** — unresolved above by design.
- **Birthplace naming** — source-form variants, not a free rewrite: [S1] [S2]
  use Одрехово «неподалік від Сянока (нині територія Польщі)»; [S5] uses
  Одрехова (near Sanok, now Poland). Cite each form only with the source that
  spells it that way; do not attach [S1]/[S2] to the [S5] spelling. State

  sentence that reports the deportation itself; they date the Holosko→Univ
  transfer 17 October 1948 and then say only «невдовзі» (verified 2026-07-18
  against both live pages). **[S5] (ESU) does date De Vokht's deportation
  1948** («Після депортації 1948 протоігумена…»). Use 1948 for De Vokht only
  with [S5] attribution; do not invent the year from S1/S2 alone. S1/S2 support
  deportation **to Belgium** as destination; this packet does not treat that as
  a nationality claim.
- **Academic reference now adopted** — live ESU article «Зятик Іван»
  (https://esu.com.ua/article-14092; S. I. Dmytryshyn; vol. 11, 2011, upd.
  2023; Institute of Encyclopedic Research, NASU) is [S5]. An earlier packet
  claim that ESU returned no article was false and is withdrawn. [S5] is

  катувань» collapse the two death levels.
- **What popular memory simplifies** — "Redemptorist martyr" erases the
  professor and the superior. He taught for a decade in Przemyśl [S1] [S2] and
  another period in Lviv [S2] before he was a victim of anything.
- **Chronology (source-bounded, no causal add-on)** — for this case the adopted
  set supports concentration in 1946, Holosko→Univ on 17 October 1948 [S1]
  [S2], De Vokht's deportation year 1948 [S5], transfer of powers after that
  deportation [S1] [S2] [S5], and Ziatyk's arrest in 1950 [S1] [S2] [S3].
  Report those dates without inventing a general reunification rule or a
  causal chain not stated by the sources.
