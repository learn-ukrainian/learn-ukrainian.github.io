## 2. Oppression mechanism

- **What happened:** **Family deportation** under Soviet nationality policies; generational trauma shaping anti-imperial reflex; **student movement** participation (**Revolution on Granite, 1990**).
- **When:** father's deportation **childhood (age 10)** → return **1956**; Taras's activism **1989–1991**.
- **By whom:** **Soviet NKVD/MVD deportation apparatus** (historical); university-era **Soviet ideological control** (refused Soviet philology/journalism paths).
- **Document references:** No personal file number in corpus — family narrative via wikipedia biography (T3); treat deportation as **attested family history**, not speculative.
- **Mechanism specifics:** Chose **biology** over Soviet humanities to avoid ideological capture; discovered contemporary Ukrainian literature **after army** via **Dmytro Stus** (wiki); **Granite Revolution** crystallized civic Ukraine attachment (Stus line heard on Maidan).
- **What survived:** Prose corpus, **FM «Halychyna»** radio cycle, journalism columns (**Halytskyi korrespondent**).

## 6. Contested points

- **«Stanislav phenomenon»** — Borzenko textbook defines it as **1990s postmodernist writers clustered in Ivano-Frankivsk (historical Stanislaviv)**: **Yuriy Andrukhovych, V. Yeshkiliev, Taras Prokhasko, H. Petrosaniak** [T2: Borzenko 11 kl.]. Debate: organic regional scene vs later critical branding — either way, Prokhasko is **canonically listed inside the group** in NUS-aligned material.
- **Biology vs literature:** Wikipedia stresses he **refused Soviet philology/journalism** and graduated **biology (botanist)** [T3]. Critics read his prose as **«plant man»** (ukrlib bio note) — essential to **Neprosti** and **FM Halychyna**, not trivia.
- **Postmodern difficulty vs reader access** — Dovhan preface (Quote 1) jokes that skipping **Neprosti** leaves life «without sound and light» — self-aware difficulty, not elitist contempt for readers.
- **Shevchenko Prize 2020** for **«Tak, ale…»** (essays) vs fiction peak — some readers prefer **1990s novellas** (*Nekropol*, *Essai de deconstruction*). Teach prize work as **mature essayistic voice**, not as «only valid Prokhasko».
- **Family deportation narrative:** Father's **Chita** exile and **1956** return [T3: wikipedia] explain anti-Soviet reflex without turning writer into political martyr biography — oppression mechanism is **inherited trauma + Granite generation**, not personal camp sentence.
- **Politics:** Less parliament-visible than Matios; **Granite Revolution 1990** and columns in **Halytskyi korrespondent** still mark civic presence — avoid «hermit botanist» hagiography.

**Stanislav/Ivano-Frankivsk context:** Born **1968** in Soviet **Stanislav** (renamed **Ivano-Frankivsk 1962**), childhood **Deliatyn** — Carpathian foothill zone linking Hutsul/Boiko cultural layers. **FM «Halychyna»** cycle (2001) encodes local radio memory as literary form; cross-link LIT essay plan for radio-text modules.
