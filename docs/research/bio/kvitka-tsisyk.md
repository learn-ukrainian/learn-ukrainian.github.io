## 2. Oppression mechanism

Kvitka's oppression is **indirect but documentable** — Cold-War cultural policing inside the URSR, not personal arrest.

- **What happened:** Soviet Ukrainian authorities **blocked concert distribution** of her Ukrainian-language albums in the **1980s**; her UPA-related repertoire was treated as ideologically toxic.
- **When:** primarily the **1980s** (ULP dates album releases 1980/1989 against a ban narrative in that decade). [T2: ULP Lesson 240]
- **By whom:** Soviet cultural bureaucracy / concert agencies controlling foreign-artist permissions (ULP names the mechanism; specific file numbers were **not** retrieved in this pass).
- **Mechanism specifics:** As a diaspora artist with an American passport and a repertoire including songs associated with the Ukrainian insurgency, she became unpresentable in official Soviet Ukrainian media even while her voice was ubiquitous in American advertising. ULP Lesson 240 states her work was **"заборонена в радянській Україні"** because **"в її репертуарі були пісні УПА"** — present this as attested pedagogical narrative (T2), not as a quoted KGB document.
- **What survived / what was destroyed:** The albums **`Kvitka: Songs of Ukraine` / «Пісні України» (1980)** and **`Two Colors` / «Два кольори» (1989)** circulated via diaspora networks and later independent Ukraine; Soviet state channels did not integrate them into canonical broadcast.

## 3. Major works

Discography and career landmarks (chronological):

- `1970s` — New York **session / jingle** career: Ford ("Have you driven a Ford lately" — ULP cites **22+ billion** plays), McDonald's, Pepsi, AT&T; backing vocals (e.g., Whitney Houston). [T2: ULP Lesson 240]
- `1978` — First performer of **"You Light Up My Life"** (film title song); **Oscar** and **Golden Globe** for Best Original Song. [T2: grade-6 Zabolotnyi; T2: ULP Lesson 240]
- `1980` — **`Kvitka: Songs of Ukraine` / «Пісні України»** — self-financed Ukrainian folk/pop album (≈40 NYC studio musicians). [T2: Zabolotnyi; T2: ULP]
- `1989` — **`Two Colors` / «Два кольори»** — second Ukrainian album; includes **Dmytro Pavlychko's** poem-song and **Volodymyr Ivasiuk's** «Я піду в далекі гори». [T2: ULP Lesson 240]
- `1990s` — Continued session work; illness **1992–1998**. [T3: ukrainian_wiki]
