## 1. Verified facts

- **Full name (UA, canonical):** Володимир Іванович Вернадський. [T1: IEU — "Vernadsky, Volodymyr"; T3: uk.wikipedia — "Вернадський Володимир Іванович", cross-checked against ЕІУ т.1 с.484]
- **Pseudonyms / aliases:** none. Russian-imperial / Russified forms (forbidden in body text, listed only in §8): Владимир Иванович Вернадский, Vladimir Vernadsky.
- **Born:** 12 March 1863 (28 February O.S.) | Санкт-Петербург | Російська імперія. Childhood years (1868–1875) spent in Ukraine — Poltava and Kharkiv. [T1: IEU — "born 12 March 1863 in Saint Petersburg"; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Died:** 6 January 1945 (aged 81) | Москва | Російська РФСР, СРСР. [T1: IEU — "died 6 January 1945 in Moscow"; T3: uk.wikipedia]
- **Founding of the Ukrainian Academy of Sciences — ⚠ year is 1918, not 1919:** invited to Kyiv after Hetman **Павло Скоропадський**'s **Українська держава** was proclaimed (April 1918), Vernadsky chaired the commission to organise the Academy and the Ukrainian National Library; the commission's funding decision was taken **13 September 1918**, and in **October 1918 he was elected the first president of the Українська академія наук (УАН)**. [T1: IEU — "served as the first president of the All-Ukrainian Academy of Sciences (VUAN)… founding year… 1918"; T3: uk.wikipedia — "13 вересня 1918 року… У жовтні Вернадського обрали першим президентом Української Академії наук (УАН)"]
- **Family / education key facts:** son of the Kyiv-born economist **Іван Васильович Вернадський** (1821–1884) and music teacher Ганна Петрівна; the paternal line had **deep Ukrainian Cossack roots** (the ancestor "Верна" fought on the Cossack side in the 1648–1654 war; a great-grandfather studied at the **Києво-Могилянська академія**). Married **Наталія Старицька** of an old Ukrainian family (1886). Graduated the physics-mathematics faculty of St Petersburg University; doctorate 1897; professor of Moscow University from 1898. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Рід]

**Date / presidency note (flagged):** the **founding year of UAN is firmly 1918** across T1 and T3 — the dossier records this against any "1919" mis-dating. His **presidency end-date** varies in the sources (the wiki lead gives "Президент 1918—1921"; IEU gives 1918–19), because he left active leadership when he went to the Таврійський університет (rector, 1920) and returned to Petrograd in 1921. The dossier states the **founding (1918)** and **first election (October 1918)** as firm, and treats the presidency's terminal date as the looser figure.

## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section. Vernadsky was **not** a GULAG victim; his case is one of (a) imperial censorship that *formed* his Ukrainian consciousness, (b) Bolshevik political persecution, and (c) Soviet erasure of the Ukrainian-statehood context of his greatest institutional achievement. (Indirect mechanisms — censorship, political pursuit, decontextualisation — held to the same specificity rule.)

**(a) Imperial censorship of Ukrainian (the formative wound).** As a boy abroad with his father, Vernadsky read in Petro Lavrov's paper *Вперед* about the **circular banning Ukrainian-language printing** in the Russian Empire (the Валуєвський циркуляр 1863 / Емський указ 1876 regime). At 15 he recorded the ban in his diary as an attack on "**моєю рідною мовою**" ("my native language" — see §4). The empire's suppression of Ukrainian is what radicalised his Ukrainian identity. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Ранні роки]

**(b) Bolshevik persecution.** After the October 1917 coup Vernadsky signed a public appeal declaring that "**зусиллями народу буде покладено кінець пануванню насильників**" ("by the people's effort an end will be put to the rule of the violence-mongers"); the new authorities **ordered the persecution of the signatories**, and Vernadsky fled to **Полтава**. With the Bolsheviks' arrival the young **УАН's position worsened**; Vernadsky himself fell ill with typhus through the winter of 1919–20. He was a Kadet and an anti-Bolshevik liberal — repressed as such, not as a Ukrainian nationalist. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Наукова робота та громадська діяльність]

**(c) Soviet erasure of the 1918 Ukrainian-State context (the decolonial mechanism).** The decisive fact the Soviet narrative buried is that **the Ukrainian Academy of Sciences was founded in 1918 under the independent Ukrainian State (Hetmanate)**, with Vernadsky as its first president and the **National Library** as its companion institution. Soviet historiography reframed the academy as a Soviet creation (ВУАН → АН УРСР), claimed Vernadsky as a "Soviet/Russian" scientist (he has a Українська радянська енциклопедія entry — a forbidden authority here), and detached both man and institution from the 1918 statehood moment. The recovery of that context — and of the National Library's name (now **Національна бібліотека України імені В. І. Вернадського**) — is the live decolonial work. [T3: uk.wikipedia — lead, Вшанування; framing per source-tier §SPECIAL RULE]

**What survived / what was distorted:** the science survived globally; the **Ukrainian-statehood frame of his institution-building was erased**, and his genuinely ambivalent politics (§6) were flattened — Soviet memory made him a tame "Soviet academician," post-1991 Ukraine reclaims him (the 2019 ₴1000 banknote, the **Антарктична станція «Академік Вернадський»**), and honest scholarship must hold both the reclamation and the ambivalence at once.

## 3. Major works

A founder of whole disciplines; selected, chronological:

- `1888–1897` — foundational work in **crystallography and mineralogy** (he reframed mineralogy as "the chemistry of the Earth's crust"). [T3: uk.wikipedia — Творча спадщина]
- `1918` — as first president of **УАН**: organised the academy's statute, the **Ukrainian National Library**, a geodesic institute, and the first **biogeochemical laboratory**. [T3]
- `1924` — **«La Géochimie»** (*Нариси з геохімії*), Paris — founding text of **geochemistry**. [T1: IEU — "La Géochemie (1924)"]
- `1926` — **«Біосфера»** (*La Biosphère*, Paris 1929) — the foundational statement of the **biosphere** concept. [T1: IEU — "La Biosphère (1929)"; T3]
- `1922–1939` — founder and director of the **Радієвий інститут**; pioneer of **radiogeology**. [T3]
- `1940s` — work on **biogeochemistry** and the **noosphere**; his last work, **«Декілька слів про ноосферу»** (1944). [T3: uk.wikipedia — "У 1944 році видав останню свою працю «Декілька слів про ноосферу»"]
- **Ukrainian-question essays:** **«Українське питання і російська громадськість»**, «Про автономію», «Доля Української академії наук», the manuscript «Про Угорську Русь з 1848 р.» — his political-publicist corpus on Ukraine (see §6). [T3: uk.wikipedia — Вернадський і Україна]

## 4. Primary-source quotes (≥2 required)

Vernadsky's diaries were written **in Russian** (the imperial scholarly language); the texts below are the standard Ukrainian renderings — transmission flagged per template.

**Quote 1 — diary, 29 March 1878 (age 15), on the ban on Ukrainian:**

> «Страшенно притісняють українців. Драгоманову навіть в Австрії не дозволили видавати газету українською мовою. У Росії зовсім заборонено друкувати книги моєю рідною мовою. На канікулах я з усією ретельністю візьмуся за неї. В Києві, коли в якомусь домі побачать портрет Шевченка, то його відбирають.»

Pedagogically central: a 15-year-old of mixed imperial milieu calling Ukrainian "**моєю рідною мовою**" and resolving to study it — the censorship producing the consciousness it meant to erase. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Ранні роки; diary, Russian-language original rendered in Ukrainian]

**Quote 2 — pre-death memoir, transferred to the Academy of Sciences of Ukraine:**

> «Я вірю у велике майбуття і України, й Української академії наук…»

The founder's closing faith in the institution he created in 1918 — a direct line to today's НАН України. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Вернадський і Україна]

**Quote 3 (programmatic) — his position in the 1918 academy debate with Hrushevsky:**

> «Важливо створити сильний центр наукових досліджень українського народу, його історії, його мови, природи України.»

This frames the academy's founding purpose; it sits beside the genuine **Vernadsky–Hrushevsky disagreement** (§6) over whether the academy should be modelled on Petersburg and initially staffed by Russians. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Наукова робота]

## 6. Contested points

This section is deliberately substantial — Vernadsky must **not** be romantically inflated.

- **His ambivalent stance on the "Ukrainian question" (the central honesty).** Vernadsky **recognised the Ukrainian people, language and culture** and demanded their recognition against imperial policy — **but he opposed Ukraine's separation from Russia, objected to the Central Rada's rapid Ukrainisation, and initially conceived the УАН as a regional branch of the Petersburg Academy** (only later moving to a position of UAN independence). He was a **Kadet** and a member of the Russian Provisional Government, and also served on a higher-education commission under Skoropadsky's Ukrainian State. He is best described as a **Ukrainian-rooted imperial federalist**, not a national-independence figure. Decolonisation here means telling this truth in both directions — claiming his Ukrainian roots and his founding of the УАН **without** erasing his federalism. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Вернадський і Україна]
- **"Ukrainian and Russian scientist."** The uk.wikipedia lead itself labels him "**український та російський**." Russia claims him fully; Ukraine reclaims his roots and his 1918 academy. The defensible position: a scientist of Ukrainian descent and deep Ukrainian attachment whose career ran through Russian and Soviet institutions and whose politics were imperial-federalist.
- **The 1920 anti-Russian diary outburst.** His diary (30 Aug 1920) contains a bitter denunciation of "the Russian people" and "Russian democracy" — evidence that his federalism coexisted with real disillusion; popular memory tends to drop both the federalism and this rage in favour of a flat icon. [T3: uk.wikipedia — Вернадський і Україна]
- **Religion / philosophy.** His noosphere and his non-confessional religiosity are debated (Мочалов's monographs) — sometimes oversimplified into mysticism, sometimes into pure materialism.
- **Russian disinformation / appropriation.** The "Soviet/Russian academician" framing (and the УРЕ entry) functions to detach the founder of the **Ukrainian** Academy from Ukraine; the 2019 ₴1000 banknote and the Antarctic station are the Ukrainian counter-assertion. Both the appropriation and the genuine complexity must be named. [framing per source-tier §SPECIAL RULE]
