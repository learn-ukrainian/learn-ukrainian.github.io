## 2. Oppression mechanism

Nina Matviyenko's career unfolded inside the **Soviet institutionalization of folk performance** — not prison, but **structured cultural assimilation**.

- **What happened:** Professionalization of village song through the **State Academic Ukrainian Folk Choir named after Hryhoriy Veriovka** — the USSR's flagship ensemble for staged «folk» identity.
- **When:** core soloist decades **late 1960s–1991** (BIO plan chronology; align with Veriovka choir history in grade-10/11 textbooks mentioning Veriovka as canonical institution). [T2: BIO plan; T2: 11 kl. history — Veriovka choir in cultural-life survey]
- **By whom:** Ministry of Culture / Union of Composers ecosystem; concert agencies controlling abroad tours as **Soviet Ukrainian cultural diplomacy**.
- **Mechanism specifics:** Folk material was **edited, harmonized, and choreographed** to satisfy ideological presentation of «brotherly republics» while still carrying Ukrainian melodic DNA. Performers gained **People's Artist** titles and international touring — rewards that also bound them to repertoire filters. BIO plan explicitly frames the **People's Artist / Hero of Ukraine** honors paradox (§plan «Тінь» section).
- **Post-1991:** She co-founded / led the ensemble **«Drevo»** (audit anchor #2535) oriented toward **Polissian and ritual song** — a post-Soviet re-grounding in obрядовий repertuar after the choir era. [**Ensemble name attested in audit brief; detailed chronology not in local corpus**]
- **What survived:** Concert recordings, television roles (e.g., **Marusia Churai**, **Kateryna Bilokur** in tele-theatre — BIO plan points), and autobiography **«Ой, виорю нивку широкую»** (2003, **National Museum of Ivan Honchar** edition). [T2: BIO plan `references` block]
