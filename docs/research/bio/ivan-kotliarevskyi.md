## 2. Oppression mechanism

This is the load-bearing section. Kotliarevsky died in 1838 — the mechanism is **not** in-life repression but **(a) the imperial erasure of the Ukrainian world he was born into**, which his work answers, and **(b) the posthumous Soviet/imperial reduction of that work** to safe categories.

**(a) The erasure his «Енеїда» answers (named instruments, dated).** Kotliarevsky was born in 1769 into a Hetmanate that the Russian Empire was actively dismantling: the office of hetman was abolished (1764), the **Запорозька Січ was destroyed by Catherine II in 1775**, the Hetmanate's regimental order was liquidated, and **serfdom (кріпацтво) was imposed on Left-Bank Ukraine by imperial decree in 1783**, binding the peasantry Kotliarevsky would later write in. In the same period the imperial cultural order demoted Ukrainian to a peasant "**наречие**" unfit for letters. Against exactly this, in **1798** Kotliarevsky published the first three parts of **«Енеїда»** — **the first work of new Ukrainian literature written in the vernacular** (T3: "першого твору нової української літератури, написаного народною мовою") — recasting Virgil's Trojans as **Запорозькі козаки**, i.e. building a literary monument to the very Cossack world the empire had just destroyed. Founding a national literature in a banned-as-unserious language *is* the resistance; the oppression is the cultural-erasure regime it answers.

**(b) Posthumous reduction (the interpretive mechanism).** Soviet criticism domesticated him twice over: it read **«Енеїда»** chiefly as **realist satire of feudal/serf society** (a class reading that muted the national-Cossack memory and the language-founding act), and **«Наталка Полтавка»** as folksy **ethnographic operetta** — while the burlesque-travesty surface was used more broadly to typecast Ukrainian as a comic-peasant register ("Малоросійський" humour), a stereotype later writers had to fight. The first edition (1798) was itself **published without his consent, funded by Максим Парпура** in St Petersburg — a minor irony of how his text first reached print outside his control.

**What survived / what was distorted:** the texts survived and were canonised, but the canon foregrounded the laughter and the "realism" and quieted the **act of national-linguistic founding** that makes Kotliarevsky the hinge of modern Ukrainian culture (§6).

## 3. Major works

- `began 1794; parts 1–3 publ. 1798` — **«Енеїда»** (travesty of Virgil's *Aeneid*; Trojans as Zaporozhian Cossacks). Part 4 — **1809**; parts 5–6 finished **~1820**; **complete work published posthumously, Kharkiv, 1842**. The foundational text of modern Ukrainian literature. ## 5. Language register

- **Register:** **vernacular Poltava-region Ukrainian** in the **бурлеск/травестія** mode — folk lexicon, dense realia (food, dress, customs, demonology), proverbs and humour. «Енеїда» is so lexically rich it is traditionally printed with a glossary. «Наталка Полтавка» juxtaposes that living vernacular with the **satirised Russified chancery-speech** of the Возний (Quote 3).
- **CEFR readiness for full reading:** the vernacular is inviting, but the **archaic/dialectal lexicon and realia push full «Енеїда» reading to B2–C1**; learners do best with **annotated/glossed or lightly modernised editions**. Songs and dialogue from «Наталка Полтавка» work at **B1–B2**.
- **Lexicon notes (pre-teach the metalanguage):** зачинатель, бурлеск, травестія, кріпацтво, вернакулярний — all VESUM-valid (verified). Separately gloss the Возний's mock-bureaucratic Russianisms as *satire targets*, not as norms.
- **Stylistic features:** mock-heroic travesty; encyclopaedic ethnographic detail; song interpolation; the foundational demonstration that Ukrainian vernacular can carry a full literary work — the move every later writer builds on.

## 6. Contested points

- **Burlesque founder or accidental classic?** Scholarship debates whether Kotliarevsky *intended* to found a literature or wrote «Енеїда» as a learned joke that history elevated. The mainstream view (Єфремов, and modern historians of literature) is that, intended or not, **«Енеїда» is the inaugural act of modern Ukrainian literature** in the vernacular. [T4: Сергій Єфремов, *Історія українського письменства* — referenced]
- **The Cossack-Trojan reading.** That the Trojans are Zaporozhian Cossacks (a literary memorial to the Sich destroyed in 1775) is the standard decolonial reading; the Soviet canon preferred the de-nationalised "satire of serfdom" frame (§2b). [scholarly consensus; grounded in §2a]
- **The "comic-Ukrainian" trap.** Because his founding works are comic, the empire could file Ukrainian under "amusing peasant dialect." Recovering Kotliarevsky means showing the **seriousness inside the laughter** — the patriotic couplet (§4, Quote 2) is the counter-evidence.
- **Russian disinformation / shared-heritage claims.** Russia periodically claims Kotliarevsky as part of an "all-Russian" 18th–19th-c. literature; his deliberate choice of the **vernacular** the empire despised, and Наталка's song answering the Russified official (Quote 3), refute this from inside the texts.
- **Honest gap — "freed his serfs."** A common claim that Kotliarevsky freed his household serfs in his will was **not verifiable in this pass** (the fetched wiki text names only his housekeeper-heir Мотрона Веклевичева); it is therefore **left unasserted/flagged**, not stated as fact (#M-4 lesson #2).
