## Why now

1. **PR #2372 / m20 is the rolling proof-of-pipeline** since 2026-05-12. Pt 10's empirical refire (worktree `185032`) under the fully hardened pipeline scored 9.5/10 LLM review + 6/7 step-5 vocab coverage, but failed `word_count` due to *structural-density shift* (writer obeyed `#R-CLEAN-TABLES` and put content in tables/callouts/dialogue boxes instead of prose).
2. **The hardening rules themselves are growing the writer prompt past its ceiling.** PR #2372 caught this: `test_a1_letter_module_writer_prompt_stays_under_ceiling` failed at 130KB; bumped to 132KB tactically.
3. **Codex's brain-pick (audit/2026-05-27-codex-brain-pick-m20/) named the pattern**: "visible compliance tokens" — gates rewarded local knob-hitting rather than integrated teaching. Each hardening rule tightens one knob; the failures move to the next.
4. **The wiki was deliberately built to be the lesson spine** (per `scripts/wiki/compile.py` defaulting to Gemini, with `[S1]…[S9]` chunk citations into `data/sources.db`). m20's wiki has 5 substantive sections × 21 vocab lemmas × 6 L2 error patterns × 3 decolonization bad-form pairs × 5 textbook exercises with chunk IDs — empirically rich enough to be a lesson spine.
5. **Cursor r1's "wiki compile-verify before render" framing + user's "improve wikis if needed"** means **wiki quality is a first-class workstream**, not just an upstream gate. If the wiki is thin or contains laundered hallucinations from compile time, the renderer cannot save it.
6. **User direction**: pivot from "compose lesson" to "render wiki + cited RAG + ULP pattern", as a structural refactor not a new pipeline; wiki improvement is an option to invest in.

| Layer | Role | Source | Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Wiki** | Lesson spine: methodology, sequence (Steps 1-5 at A1), vocab minimum (with frequency stars), L2 error table, decolonization stance with bad-form pairs, textbook example formats, **distractor inventory** (cursor r1 addition: wrong forms for MCQ/error-correction) | `wiki/pedagogy/{level}/{slug}.md` | Curated (Gemini-generated, human/agent-reviewed) — but NOT infallible. Subject to wiki quality pass + completeness gate. |
| **Textbook RAG** | Authority/evidence backing wiki claims; cited via `[S1]…[S9]` markers in wiki | `data/sources.db` chunks fetched via `mcp__sources__get_chunk_context` / `verify_quote` | Source-of-truth for quotes; Grade 1-3 chunks ground examples but do NOT appear as verbatim quotes. |

ADDS NO vocab, examples, citations, dialogue lines, or claims not derivable from wiki + cited RAG. | claude-tools / codex-tools / gemini-tools (cursor-tools deferred — see §"Cursor production readiness") | Bounded by the layered vocab allowlist; structural rules stay load-bearing. |

## Wiki quality pass (user-added workstream, 2026-05-28)

The user flagged: *"we might need to review and improve the wikis. that is an option."* This is the right insight — V7.1's renderer pivot makes the **wiki the load-bearing artifact**. If wikis are thin or laundered, even a perfect renderer ships thin/laundered lessons.

### What to audit per wiki

For each `wiki/pedagogy/{level}/{slug}.md`:

| Check | A1/A2 threshold | B1+ threshold | Seminar threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Methodology section | non-empty | non-empty | non-empty + explicit pedagogical sequence |
| Steps in `## Послідовність введення` | ≥5 | ≥5 | ≥7 |
| L2 errors in `## Типові помилки` table | ≥3 rows | ≥3 rows | ≥3 rows + at least 1 per major grammar category |
| Decolonization pairs | ≥1 (for L1-Russian-substitutable topics) | ≥2 | ≥3 + cultural framing |
| Vocab minimum | ≥20 lemmas | ≥50 lemmas | ≥100 lemmas |
| Textbook exercise formats | ≥3 with chunk_id citations | ≥3 | ≥5 |
| Distractor inventory (cursor r1) | ≥6 wrong-forms across L2 + bad-form pairs | ≥10 | ≥15 |
| Chunk citation resolution | spot-check 3 random | spot-check 5 random | **all chunks verify_quote-validated** |
| External article URL resolution | n/a | n/a | **all URLs HTTP-resolve** |
| Two-source rule on contested claims | n/a | n/a | **enforced at compile time** |

### Wiki improvement process

For wikis that fail the audit:

1. **Identify the gap** via the `wiki_completeness_gate.py` diagnostic.
2. **Re-compile via Gemini** with an improvement-focused prompt: `.venv/bin/python scripts/wiki/compile.py --writer gemini --slug {slug} --improve`. The compile script should accept an `--improve` flag that biases toward expanding thin sections rather than starting from scratch.
3. **Verify the improvement** with the same gate.
4. **Frozen wiki commit** — once a wiki passes, mark it as frozen for downstream rendering (a field like `frozen_at:` in the wiki frontmatter).

### Cost considerations

Wiki improvement is via Gemini default (unmetered per `wiki/compile.py`). Cost is low. Audit + improvement pass on the 1713 modules in the manifest could run as a background batch over days. Pilot first on the m20 wiki.

### Seminar-track special handling (cursor r1)

For HIST/BIO/LIT/etc., the wiki is itself an LLM research artifact. Verification must happen at **wiki compile time**, not writer time. Add a `scripts/wiki/compile_verify.py` step that:
- Runs `mcp__sources__verify_quote` on every quoted citation.
- Runs `mcp__sources__verify_source_attribution` on every authority claim.
- Resolves all external article URLs.
- Applies two-source rule on contested claims (any claim involving dates, causation, biographical facts).
- Marks the wiki `verified_at:` only after all checks pass.

The writer phase then trusts the verified wiki without re-paying tokens to re-verify the same claims every build.

## Vocab gate (new, writer-side) — the layered allowlist

**Hard-reject content lemmas (after VESUM lemmatization) outside this allowlist:**

```
allowed_lemmas = (
    wiki.vocabulary_minimum_lemmas
    ∪ plan.targets.new_vocabulary
    ∪ plan.targets.vocabulary_hints
    ∪ cumulative_learner_state.taught_lemmas   # prior modules in the same level
    ∪ closed_class_function_words              # canonical list (і, та, але, бо, я, ти, у, на, в, з, що, як, де, ...)
    ∪ proper_nouns_in_wiki_examples            # surface-form match required
    ∪ bad_form_markers                         # words inside <!-- bad -->...<!-- /bad --> are exempt
    ∪ quoted_evidence_from_cited_rag_chunks    # words inside `> ` blockquotes citing wiki [S*] anchors
)
```

**Reject condition** (codex r2 pushback; gemini r2 conceded; cursor r1 confirmed):

- Hard-fail on **unsupported content lemmas after normalization** (lemmas not in the allowlist).
- NOT a single-token hard-fail. Allow band-tolerated misses for tokenization edge cases — band thresholds per `scripts/config.py:488,493` precedent (m20 allows up to 80 unsupported UK words; tighten on new lemmas, loosen on function words).
- Single content-lemma miss → fail ONLY if it's pedagogically meaningful (noun/verb/adjective NOT in the allowlist).

**Why NO CEFR runtime permission** (codex r2 → gemini r2 conceded): allowing the writer to "draw from cefr_a1 lemmas via `query_cefr_level`" recreates writer invention through a side door. If a dialogue needs more vocab, **expand the wiki minimum first** (now a first-class workstream), then re-build.

**B1+ scaling** (cursor r1): the gate does NOT become a no-op at higher levels. The allowlist gets larger (~3000 at B2 vs ~200 at A1) but the gate's character shifts from "can you say X?" to **"are you introducing lemmas outside wiki's declared *new this module* set?"** Cumulative covers review; wiki/plan declares new. Failure class moves from "random A1 word" to "author slipped in an undeclared B2 lemma."

**B1+ sequence-drift catch** (cursor r1, codex/gemini-missed risk): at B1+ with a wide allowlist, the writer can produce grammatically fine but pedagogically misaligned prose — using lemmas the learner knows BUT not in the wiki's intended sequence. The vocab gate catches leakage; **`wiki_coverage` row-by-row** catches sequence drift. Both stay load-bearing.

## Rules that stay load-bearing (cursor r1 + codex r2 list)

Keep (~9 of 17):
- `#R-CITE-HONEST` — wiki is LLM-generated; writer must still verify before quoting.
- `#R-VOICE-META` — anti-meta-narration phrase list ("Welcome to…", "In this section…").
- `#R-BAD-FORM-MARKER` — the `<!-- bad -->...<!-- /bad -->` convention.
- `#R-CLEAN-TABLES` — bold ONLY target Ukrainian forms; six-row conjugation tables.
- `#R-AUDIENCE-LANGUAGE-{LEVEL}` — A1 explanations in English, UK as target; B1+ all-UK with EN only in Tab 2.
- `#R-NO-SCAFFOLDING-LEAKS` — no `Крок 5:`, no `[S1]`, no obligation IDs in published prose.
- `#R-NO-CHILDREN-PRIMARY-QUOTES` — Grade 1-3 chunks ground examples but never appear as published blockquotes.
- `#R-GRAMMAR-TERMS-{LEVEL}` — proper grammatical terminology.
- `#R-SINGLE-VOICE-{LEVEL}` — one teacher voice across the module.
- Dialogue count + format rules (`l2_exposure_floor`, `<DialogueBox uk="..." en="...">`).
- **Prose floor rule** (existing `#R-PROSE-FLOOR-A1` from PR #2372). **Cursor r1 blind spot**: renderer pivot doesn't fix word_count vs structural-density; the prose floor rule MUST survive the refactor.
- Artifact emission contract (`<implementation_map>` etc.) — adapted for V7.1's wiki-1:1 obligations.

Trivialize but DO NOT remove (codex r2 pushback): `russianisms_strict`, `paronym_clean`, `surzhyk_clean`, `calques_clean`, `published_quote_for_publishable_refs`, `chunk_context_for_all_refs`, `citations_resolve`, `ai_slop_clean`, `register_consistency`. Fire rate drops to near-zero; gates remain load-bearing because wiki is LLM-generated.

Activity-specific rule cluster (cursor r1 new):
- `#R-ACTIVITY-COMPOSITION` (new, parallel to prose renderer charter):
  - Items composed from wiki vocab minimum ∪ plan targets ∪ cumulative learner state (same as prose allowlist).
  - Distractors sourced from wiki L2 error table + decolonization bad-form pairs ONLY — never invented Russianisms.
  - Schema fields canonical per existing `#R-ACTIVITY-FIELDS`.
  - INLINE/WORKBOOK split per existing `{ACTIVITY_COUNT_TARGET}` rule.

## Voice rewrite — operational tests

**Definition (level-conditional)**:
- A1/A2: rewrite 3rd-person methodological Ukrainian → 2nd-person English teacher voice with UK target forms inline.
- B1+: rewrite methodological UK register → teacher UK register, NO English body text. *(Cursor r1 asymmetry: B1+ is EASIER in one dimension — less creative surface for English — but HARDER in another — register consistency without EN scaffolding.)*

**Tests (in combination)**:
1. **Token-set diff (hard gate)**: every Ukrainian token in rendered module → wiki's Ukrainian token set ∪ allowlist.
2. **Voice anchor lintable negatives**: existing `#R-VOICE-META` forbidden-phrase list as a deterministic post-emit grep.
3. **Voice-golden examples** (gemini r1): 2-3 sample wiki-to-module before/after pairs in the prompt (`Цей розділ вчить...` → `Now, try using...` at A1; `Цей розділ навчає...` → `Тут ти опанує̀ш...` at B1+).
4. **LLM-as-judge** (content-review pass, not build gate): paragraph-by-paragraph "does this rendering preserve the wiki's pedagogical intent without inventing content?"

## Risks / blind spots (cursor r1's contribution)

1. **`word_count` vs structural density (Pt 10 root cause).** Renderer pivot doesn't fix this. V7.1 keeps `#R-PROSE-FLOOR-A1` rule. **Resolved.**
2. **Track-specific prompt forking timeline.** A1→B1 share one prompt with `{LEVEL}` conditionals; seminars will eventually break a single prompt. Plan the fork; defer the file split.
3. **Wiki compile verification ≠ writer verification.** For seminars, verification must happen at wiki compile, not writer time. **Now a first-class workstream (item 11 above).**
4. **Activity distractor supply chain.** Under strict vocab bound, MCQ/error-correction need wrong forms pre-seeded in wiki. **Now part of the wiki completeness gate (distractor inventory threshold).**
5. **cursor-tools production readiness.** Integrated 2026-05-24; one failed m20 attempt (composer-2.5 wrote artifacts to disk instead of stdout fences); deferred behind brain-pick. **Recommend NOT using cursor-tools as the V7.1 writer for m20 pilot.** Use claude-tools or codex-tools.
6. **B1+ asymmetry.** Removing English body composition reduces a major creative surface for prose; register consistency without EN scaffold gets harder. Plan voice-golden examples per level.

From codex/gemini r1+r2 (still applicable):
- Source-registry freshness for wiki citations.
- POS boundary problems → mitigated by band-tolerated misses.
- Stress-mark enforcement from ULP at A1.
- Seminar-track bias requiring claim/evidence graph layer (covered by seminar workstream).
