The hypothesis (claude's framing, surface to colleagues honestly)

Today's bakeoff revealed a structural tension: claude-tools went English-first (25.4% Ukrainian, close to cap), codex-tools went Ukrainian-first (51.77%, 2× over cap). Neither hit the policy sweet spot of ~18-22%. Both look like "compromise outputs" where one concern bled into the other.

The writer prompt is 127 KB. It forces the AI to do two cognitive operations simultaneously: (1) pedagogical-linguistic decisions about Ukrainian content, and (2) L2-EN scaffolding decisions about how an English speaker absorbs that content without overload. These are different operations. Human Ukrainian teachers do them sequentially. AIs may benefit from the same split.

The pitch

PhaseOutputPrompt focusGates
Pass 1l2-uk-direct/a1/my-morning — fully Ukrainian, no English"Be the best Ukrainian teacher for this topic. Linguistic depth, idiomatic flow, decolonized framing. No translation pressure."VESUM, idiom check, Ukrainian style guide, immersion = 100%, dialogue authenticity
Pass 2l2-uk-en/a1/my-morning — English-scaffolded derivation"Given this validated UK module, add A1-appropriate English scaffolding. Don't change UK content. Hold immersion at 18-22%."Word count, citations, formatting, immersion cap, A1 cognitive load (NOT VESUM — already validated in Pass 1)

Bonus structural benefit

Pass 1's artifact is independently shippable to the l2-uk-direct track (L1-agnostic Ukrainian learners — exists in the codebase per CLAUDE.md). NOT throwaway work. Pass 2 just adapts a proven artifact.

Risks claude already named (don't hide these from colleagues)

  • 2× LLM compute per module. If Pass 2 is mostly translation, that's wasteful.
  • Translation loss in Pass 2. Some pedagogical decisions are inherently bilingual: "gloss this word?" depends on whether the English speaker would guess from cognate. "Warn about this false friend?" depends on which L1 the learner has. A UK-only Pass 1 doesn't have that information.
  • "Ukrainian-first" doesn't automatically mean "better Ukrainian." Codex tried this and produced more Ukrainian (52% immersion) but not better Ukrainian (truncation artifact, missing structure, all sections under budget).
  • Reviewer phase also doubles. Pass 1 needs UK-only review (vesum, idiom, decolonization), Pass 2 needs EN-scaffolding review (immersion, gloss handling, A1 cognitive load).
  • AI doesn't think sequentially like a human teacher. A teacher thinks UK-first because UK is their native language. The AI doesn't have a "native" language. Forcing UK-first might not produce richer UK content; it might just produce thinner UK content because the AI lacks an English context to anchor against learner difficulty.
