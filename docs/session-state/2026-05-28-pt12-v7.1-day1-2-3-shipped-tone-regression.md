## The tone failure — UK metalanguage in A1 EN explanations

The 3 evidence quotes from `llm-qg-tone-response.raw.md`:

1. *"The particle **-ся** (або -сь після голосних) simply stays at the end, so context makes the pattern stick."* — `або -сь після голосних` is UK metalanguage embedded inside an English explanation. Should be `(or -сь after vowels)`.

2. *"Verbs that end in **-уватися** or **-юватися** behave slightly differently, наприклад, **користуватися** або **одружуватися**."* — `наприклад` (for example) and `або` (or) are UK conjunctions inside an EN sentence. Should be EN connectors.

3. *"The same trick now works for **дивитися** (наприклад, дивитися в дзеркало під час ранкових зборів): поява вставного «л» — **дивлюся**…"* — A full UK explanatory phrase (`поява вставного «л»` = "appearance of inserted л") inside an EN context. Should be `(appearance of inserted л)`.

All 3 are violations of `#R-AUDIENCE-LANGUAGE-A1` ("A1 explanation prose stays in English; Ukrainian appears only as TARGET"). The rule was in the prompt; the writer ignored it on these 3 spots while otherwise respecting it across the rest of the module.

Pt 10's worktree `185032` (the 9.5/10 baseline) did NOT have this pattern. So **V7.1 introduced a regression** specific to UK-connector leakage in EN explanations.

## Hypothesis — why V7.1 weakened #R-AUDIENCE-LANGUAGE-A1

The V7.1 Renderer Charter I added at L24-40 of `linear-write.md` (PR #2377) explicitly frames the LLM as a renderer of UK wiki content, with this language:

> *Voice rewrite, NOT translation: rewrite 3rd-person methodological Ukrainian wiki prose → 2nd-person teacher voice (English at A1/A2, Ukrainian at B1+). You may shorten, reorder for pedagogical flow, and add transitions. You may NOT add Ukrainian content the wiki did not authorize.*

Three plausible mechanisms for the regression:

1. **"Render the wiki, don't compose" → keep UK fragments inline**: claude may have interpreted "render" as "preserve some UK source markers as anchors" rather than "translate UK explanation prose into EN". The UK-connector pattern (`наприклад`, `або`) looks like UK source words being kept as anchors.

2. **Charter attention displaces A1-specific rule**: #R-AUDIENCE-LANGUAGE-A1 is at ~L304 in the post-V7.1 prompt. Charter sits at L24. When the model balances "render the UK wiki" (top) against "all explanations in EN" (middle), the top wins for ambiguous spots.

3. **"Ukrainian at B1+" mention contaminates A1**: charter says EN at A1/A2 + UK at B1+. The "UK at B1+" phrase may have nudged claude toward more UK-aware voice everywhere.

(1) is most likely; (2) and (3) are amplifiers.
