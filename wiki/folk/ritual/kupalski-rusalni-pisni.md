Купальські й русальні пісні в FOLK-семінарі треба подавати як літній блок української календарно-обрядової пісенності: русальні тексти пов'язані із Зеленими святами, Трійцею, водою, полем, березою, вінком і проводом, а купальські - з Іваном Купала, гуртовим співом, водою, вогнем, вінками, деревцем або Купайлом [S1], [S2], [S3].

Це передлітературний матеріал української літератури: усний текст, записаний варіант, школа, сцена і пізніша літературна рецепція. Модуль має вчити не таємної системи, а читання пісенного тексту: хто говорить, кому, у який календарний момент, з яким повтором, рухом і предметом [S1], [S2].

## Чиста рамка

Correct M05 frame:

- Ukrainian oral verbal art, not a standalone belief-system lesson.
- Christian-era received calendar terms: Трійця, Зелені свята, Івана Купала.
- Older seasonal material, when source-supported, as poetic motif and performance vocabulary.
- Public primary text first; interpretation second.
- No internal source markers in learner pages.

The received names matter. "Івана Купала" should be treated as part of the known cultural calendar, not as a thin label over an alleged hidden system. "Трійця" and "Зелені свята" should likewise remain visible in the rusalky block when sources place the songs there [S1], [S2], [S3].

## Русальний блок

The rusalky block works best if the module treats русалка as a song voice and figure. A clean activity can ask:

1. Where does the text place the figure: birch, water, field, road, village edge?
2. What does the figure ask for or receive?
3. Who is addressed: girls, sisters, community, singer, listener?
4. What action closes the scene: giving, sending away, returning, protecting the field?
5. What does the public source prove, and what does it not prove?

This keeps the text literary and performative. It also lets learners see why water, field, tree, garment and wreath are not decorative nouns: they carry the action of the song.

## Купальський блок

The kupalski block should teach sequence and form. A song may ask where Kupaylo spent winter; another may ask where Kupaylo stood; another may show wreaths moving on water. The teaching question is not "decode the secret object" but "what does the song do with the object?"

Useful sequence:

1. calendar name;
2. performing group;
3. public place;
4. repeated line or refrain;
5. object: wreath, water, fire, tree, flower, garment;
6. movement: circle, carrying, releasing, watching, returning;
7. source limit.

The Osvita literary page that includes "Ой на Івана, ой на Купала" can help future Phase B locate a public occurrence, but it should be treated as reception/context until the primary-text provenance is verified [S4].

## Поетика

M05 poetics are visible at the surface:

- **Рефрен** keeps group rhythm.
- **Анафора** makes repeated action easy to remember.
- **Dialogue** turns the song into a small performed exchange.
- **Address** marks community and role.
- **Object words** make the action concrete: вінок, вода, вогонь, сорочка, берізка, поле.
- **Movement words** keep the song tied to embodied performance.

The module should prefer short, verified excerpts. A full hosted reading page can carry the whole text; the lesson body should use compact quoted lines only after quote verification.

## Міст до письмової і сценічної культури

The bridge to literature, music and theatre must come after a folk text. An authored literary work may use Kupalo or rusalky motifs, but it is reception, not evidence for the whole folk practice. Gogol, Lysenko, theatre, painting or later prose can appear only with checked public source and a clear label: reception. The folk text remains the primary object.

## Методичні питання

1. Which calendar block does the text belong to?
2. What public source supports that classification?
3. Who speaks in the song?
4. What repeated form keeps the group voice together?
5. Which object or movement organizes the action?
6. Where should interpretation stop?
