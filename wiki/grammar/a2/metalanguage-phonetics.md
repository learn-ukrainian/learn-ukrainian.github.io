## Як це пояснюють у школі (How Schools Teach This)
The study of sounds and letters (**Фонетика**, **Графіка**, **Орфоепія**) is a foundational topic in the Ukrainian curriculum, introduced early and revisited with increasing complexity.

**Initial Introduction (Grades 1-2):**
The basic concepts are introduced in the first years of school. Learners differentiate between sounds and letters, identify vowels and consonants, and learn about voiced vs. voiceless sounds (Source 31, 32). Simple exercises involve closing one's ears to feel the "ringing" of voiced consonants like [д] versus the "quiet" of voiceless ones like [т] (Source 30). The alphabet and syllables are also taught (Source 31).

**Systematization (Grade 5):**
Grade 5 marks a deep dive into phonetics. Students learn:
- **Phonetic Transcription:** How to scientifically record the sounds of a word using special symbols ([ ], ´, :, ь) (Source 1, 3, 4, 36).
- **Vowel Pronunciation:** Rules for stressed and unstressed vowels, including the reduction of unstressed [е] -> [еи] and [и] -> [ие], and [о] -> [оу] before a stressed [у] or [і] (Source 21, 27).
- **Consonant Classification:** Consonants are systematically categorized as voiced/voiceless (дзвінкі/глухі), hard/soft (тверді/м'які), and by place of articulation (sibilants - свистячі, шиплячі; labials - губні) (Source 22, 24). Mnemonics are often used, such as "ЦаП ХоЧе ФіСТаШКи" for voiceless consonants (Source 39) and "мавпа Буф" for labials (Source 22).
- **Consonant Assimilation (Уподібнення):** Key rules are introduced, such as a voiceless consonant becoming voiced before a voiced one (`боротьба` -> `[бород´ба]`) and a voiced consonant becoming voiceless (`легко` -> `[лехко]`) (Source 8, 9, 11, 13).
- **Phonetic Analysis:** Students perform complete phonetic breakdowns of words, analyzing each sound (Source 1, 3, 4).

**Review and Mastery (Grades 9-10):**
In higher grades, the focus shifts to mastering **Orthoepic Norms** (Орфоепічна норма) — the standard literary pronunciation. The rules of assimilation are reviewed in more detail, including complex cases involving prefixes and consonant clusters at word boundaries (Source 9, 10, 27, 28). The goal is fluent, accent-free command of the standard language.

## Повна парадигма (Full Paradigm)
Ukrainian has 38 sounds: 6 vowels and 32 consonants (Source 24).

### Голосні звуки (Vowels)
There are 6 vowel sounds represented by 10 letters (Source 21).

| Звук (Sound) | Букви (Letters) | Приклад (Example) |
| :------------ | :---------------- | :---------------- |
| [а]           | а, я              | м**а**м**а**, **я**блуко |
| [о]           | о                 | к**о**л**о**      |
| [у]           | у, ю              | **у**р**о**к, **ю**рба     |
| [е]           | е, є              | ц**е**, **є**нот         |
| [и]           | и                 | т**и**, м**и**          |
| [і]           | і, ї, є (<!-- VERIFY -->) | к**і**т, **ї**жак, [р'ідкіс'н'і] |

### Приголосні звуки (Consonants)
Consonants are classified by several features.

**1. Дзвінкі та Глухі (Voiced and Voiceless)**
Many consonants form voiced/voiceless pairs (Source 39).

| Дзвінкі (Voiced)   | Глухі (Voiceless) |
| :----------------- | :---------------- |
| [б]                | [п]               |
| [д], [д´]          | [т], [т´]         |
| [ґ]                | [к]               |
| [г]                | [х]               |
| [з], [з´]          | [с], [с´]         |
| [ж]                | [ш]               |
| [дж]               | [ч]               |
| [дз], [дз´]        | [ц], [ц´]         |
| —                  | [ф]               |

**2. Сонорні (Sonorants)**
These voiced consonants do not have voiceless pairs. The voice component is stronger than the noise. The mnemonic "МіНеРаЛоВиЙ" helps remember them (Source 22).
- [в], [м], [н], [н´], [л], [л´], [р], [р´], [й]
- Note: Source 30 lists [в] as not having a pair, while Source 39 does not explicitly pair it with [ф], noting [ф] is unpaired. This reflects a key distinction from Russian.

**3. Тверді та М'які (Hard and Soft)**
Many consonants have hard and soft pairs. Softness is a key feature of Ukrainian phonology.
- **Hard only:** [б], [п], [в], [м], [ф] (labials); [ж], [ч], [ш], [дж] (sibilants); [г], [ґ], [к], [х] (velars). Note: these can be *softened* (пом'якшені) before vowels like `і`, but they don't have a distinct soft phoneme pair (Source 27, 34).
