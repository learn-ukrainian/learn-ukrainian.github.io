## Як це пояснюють у школі (How Schools Teach This)
The A2 level culminates in a comprehensive review that integrates previously learned topics into more complex structures and communicative situations. Ukrainian school textbooks for grades 5-8 provide a clear blueprint for this consolidation. The focus shifts from isolated grammar points to their application in sentences and short texts.

**1. Subject-Verb Agreement (Узгодження підмета й присудка):** This is a key review topic, especially with compound or unusual subjects. Textbooks for 8th grade, such as Авраменко (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`), present exercises where students must choose the correct verb form (singular or plural) to agree with subjects involving numerals (`П’ятеро фігуристів (вибуло, вибули)`) or multiple nouns (`Тато з мамою (поїхав, поїхали)`). This reinforces that meaning and grammatical form both play a role.

**2. Sentence Structure and Punctuation (Будова речення і пунктуація):** By the end of A2, learners should be comfortable with sentences containing homogeneous members (однорідні члени речення). Заболотний (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2025_s0227`) uses exercises that require identifying and correctly punctuating lists of nouns, adjectives, or verbs (`Степ і лиман, озеро й море – усе закуталось...`). This also includes the correct use of the vocative case in direct address (`Земле рідна!`, `Пане Юрію...`), a feature emphasized from early grades.

**3. Tenses and Moods (Часи і способи дієслова):** Learners consolidate their understanding of past, present, and future tenses. Літвінова (Source `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0057`) provides exercises for forming all possible future tense forms (`(писати) -> писатиму, буду писати`). The conditional mood (`умовний спосіб`) is also reviewed, emphasizing its formation with the past tense + `б/би` particle (`Я б вам допомогла`, Source `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0068`).

**4. Communicative and Etiquette Formulas (Етикетні формули):** Textbooks across various grade levels (Sources `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0288`, `7-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2024_s0010`, `6-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2023_s0007`) heavily emphasize correct, natural-sounding etiquette phrases. This includes greetings (`Доброго ранку!`, `Добрий день!`), apologies (`Вибачте!`, `Перепрошую!`), and thanks (`Щиро дякую!`). The distinction between correct forms and common errors (`вибачаюсь` vs. `вибачте`) is explicitly taught (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2022_s0042`).

**5. Consolidation through Context:** The final A2 review moves beyond simple sentences. It uses short texts, dialogues, and communicative situations to test integrated knowledge. For example, planning a day (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0166`), giving directions in a city (Source `7-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2024_s0150`), or writing a short, informal post (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2023_s0028`). This approach ensures that grammar is seen as a tool for meaningful communication.

## Повна парадигма (Full Paradigm)

The A2 finale consolidates several core paradigms. The writer should ensure the learner has a solid grasp of these foundational structures.

### 1. Verb Conjugation: Future Tense (Недоконаний вид)
The future tense of imperfective verbs has two forms: a compound form (easier for learners) and a synthetic form (common in speech).

| Особа (Person) | Складена форма (Compound Form) - `БУТИ` + Інфінітив | Проста форма (Simple Form) - Інфінітив + `-му`, `-меш`... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Я | **буду читати** | **читатиму** |
| Ти | **будеш читати** | **читатимеш** |
| Він / Вона / Воно | **буде читати** | **читатиме** |
| Ми | **будемо читати** | **читатимемо** |
| Ви | **будете читати** | **читатимете** |
| Вони | **будуть читати** | **читатимуть** |
*(Джерело: `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0057`)*

### 2. Subject-Verb Agreement with Numerals
The agreement pattern depends on the type of numeral and the focus of the sentence (on the count vs. the action).

| Subject Type | Example Subject | Verb Form (Plural Preferred) | Verb Form (Singular Possible) | Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Numeral 2, 3, 4 + Noun | Три голубки | п'ють | (рідше п'є) | Plural is standard for animate nouns. |
| Collective Numeral (двоє, троє...) + Noun | П’ятеро фігуристів | **вибули** | вибуло | Plural emphasizes individuals; singular emphasizes the group as a unit (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`). |
| Numeral 5+ or "багато" + Gen. Pl. | Багато ялтинців | **не ходять** | не ходить | Plural is more common in modern usage, especially with people. Singular can feel more formal or bookish. (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`) |
| Compound Subject ("X and Y") | Тато з мамою | **поїхали** | (поїхав - *incorrect*) | The verb is always plural. (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`) |
| Compound Subject (list) | Закупи, ресторан, прогулянка | **не входять** | (не входить - *incorrect*) | The verb is always plural. (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`) |

### 3. Vocative Case (Кличний відмінок)
A key feature of Ukrainian, used for direct address. Mastery is essential for politeness.

| Gender & Declension | Nominative (Н.в.) | Vocative (Кл.в.) | Rule | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Masculine, hard group (-о) | друже, батьку | **друже, батьку** | `-е`, `-у` | Привіт, друже! (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0288`) |
| Masculine, soft/mixed | Ігор, Андрій | **Ігорю, Андрію** | `-ю` | Андрію, ходімо. (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2020_s0106`) |
| Masculine name, common | Олег, Павло | **Олеже, Павле** | Suffix change + `-е` | Олеже, ти тут? (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2020_s0106`) |
| Feminine (-а/-я) | Олена, земля | **Олено, земле** | `-о`, `-е` | Земле рідна! (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2025_s0227`) |
| Feminine (names) | Ірино, Анастасіє | **Ірино, Анастасіє** | `-о`, `-є` | Ірино Сергіївно, ви чарівні. (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`) |
| Official Address | пан Президент, пані директорка | **пане Президенте, пані директорко** | `-е`, `-о` | Пане Юрію, вітаємо! (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2025_s0227`) |

## Частотність і пріоритети
At the A2 level, the goal is confident communication in everyday situations. The focus should be on the most frequent and useful structures.

1.  **High-Frequency Verbs:** Master the present, past, and future tenses of the top 50-100 verbs. This includes verbs of motion, communication, and daily routine. The ability to correctly form `я роблю`, `я робив(ла)`, `я буду робити/робитиму` is non-negotiable.

2.  **Core Cases:** While all cases are introduced, A2 mastery should focus on the "big four" for function:
    *   **Nominative:** Who/what is doing the action. The default form.
    *   **Accusative:** The direct object (`бачу + що?`). Crucial for expressing what the action affects. (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0137`)
    *   **Genitive:** Possession (`книга брата`), negation (`немає часу`), and use after numbers 2-4, 5+.
    *   **Locative:** Location (`у місті`, `на столі`). Always used with a preposition.

3.  **Etiquette First:** Correct use of polite formulas is more important than perfect declension. A learner who says `Добрий день` and `Дуже дякую` will be perceived as more fluent and respectful than one who has perfect grammar but is rude. Drills should prioritize these set phrases (`фразеологізми`). (Sources `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0288`, `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0246`)

4.  **Questions and Simple Commands:** The ability to ask for information (`Де ...?`, `Скільки ...?`, `Як ...?`) and give/understand simple commands (`Скажіть, будь ласка...`, `Дайте...`) is fundamental for interaction.

## Типові помилки L2 (Common L2 Errors)
English speakers often make predictable errors based on L1 transfer or oversimplification of Ukrainian rules.

| ❌ Помилково | ✅ Правильно | Чому |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| *Я вибачаюсь.* | **Прошу вибачення! / Вибачте! / Перепрошую!** | The reflexive form `вибачаюсь` implies "I forgive myself." It is grammatically incorrect for apologizing to someone else. Ukrainian textbooks for young learners stress this heavily. (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2022_s0042`) |
| *Доброго дня!* | **Добрий день!** або **Добридень!** | While `Доброго дня!` is widely heard, traditional and prescriptive grammar considers `Добрий день!` to be the correct, established form for a midday greeting. `Доброго ранку!` is correct, however. (Source `7-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2024_s0010`) |
| *Три роки пролетіло.* | **Три роки пролетіли.** | When the noun is in the nominative plural (after 2, 3, 4), the verb should be plural. The singular form `пролетіло` is a common mistake noted for correction in textbooks. (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`, Test 2) |
| *Мадрид і Париж вражає своєю красою.* | **Мадрид і Париж вражають своєю красою.** | A compound subject (X and Y) always takes a plural verb. This is a direct transfer error from English where the singular might feel acceptable depending on phrasing. (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`, Test 2) |
| *Мій брат — комп’ютерщик.* | **Мій брат — комп'ютерник.** | English "-er" suffix for professions does not map to `-щик` in Ukrainian. This is often a Russianism. The correct Ukrainian suffix is often `-ник`. (`гречнева` -> `гречана`) (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-golub-2023_s0037`) |
| *Я бачу журнал, вікно, і газета.* | **Я бачу журнал, вікно й газету.** | The verb `бачити` (to see) requires the direct object to be in the Accusative case. For feminine nouns ending in -а, this changes the ending to -у. Learners often forget to decline the object. (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0137`) |

## Деколонізаційні застереження (Decolonization Notes)
It is critical to teach Ukrainian on its own terms, not as a dialect or variation of Russian.

1.  **Vocative Case (Кличний відмінок):** Vigorously teach and enforce the use of the vocative case for names and nouns in direct address (`Юрію`, `пане`, `земле`). While modern colloquial Russian has largely lost the vocative, it is a vibrant and essential part of standard and spoken Ukrainian. Failing to use it sounds unnatural and can be perceived as disrespectful. (Sources `8-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2025_s0227`, `6-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2020_s0106`)

2.  **Genitive Masculine `-а` vs. `-у`:** Do not use Russian-based logic to explain this. Teach the Ukrainian principles. For example, in sports, the prize (`Кубок`) takes `-а` (`не вибороли Кубка`), while the competition (`Кубок`) takes `-у` (`фіналу Кубку світу`). This distinction is specific to Ukrainian rules. (Source `10-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2018_s0016`)

3.  **Lexical Purity:** Actively correct Russianisms. Use tools like `r2u.org.ua` to show learners the authentic Ukrainian equivalents.
    *   `дякую` not `спасибі` (while `спасибі` exists, `дякую` is more authentically Ukrainian and preferred in formal contexts).
    *   `будь ласка` not `пожалуйста`.
    *   `квиток` (for transport, theatre) vs. `білет` (for exams, lottery) - this distinction is different from Russian. (Source `10-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2018_s0016`)

4.  **Phonetics and Euphony (Милозвучність):** The `і/й/та` and `у/в` alternations are core to the sound of Ukrainian. Emphasize that these are not optional but are rules of literary language. Drills should focus on choosing the correct form based on the preceding/following sounds. (Source `10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0107`)

## Природні приклади (Natural Examples)

Group examples by the concept they illustrate.

**Group 1: Subject-Verb Agreement**
*   `Багато ялтинців не ходять на пляж.` (Plural verb with "багато" + people) (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`)
*   `П’ятеро фігуристів вибули зі змагань.` (Plural verb with collective numeral) (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`)
*   `З моменту нашої останньої зустрічі минуло три місяці.` (Singular neuter verb for time duration) (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`)

**Group 2: Use of Cases**
*   `Степ і лиман, озеро й море – усе закуталось у чарівний тихий світ.` (Nominative list as subject) (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2025_s0227`)
*   `Пане Юрію, щиро вітаємо з днем народження!` (Vocative for address) (Source `8-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2025_s0227`)
*   `На гілках дерева лежить пташине гніздо.` (Locative and Genitive cases) (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0137`)

**Group 3: Tenses and Moods**
*   `Я на добраніч кажу їм: «Люди, будьте щасливі!»` (Present tense + Imperative mood) (Source `10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0107`)
*   `Коли б собака умів розказувати, то розказав би багато інтересних історій.` (Conditional mood) (Source `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0068`)
*   `Ми з батьками часто катаємося в лісопарку по велодоріжці.` (Present tense, daily routine) (Source `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0035`)

**Group 4: Communicative Phrases & Adverbs**
*   `На жаль, не можу. Погано почуваюся, уже два дні з температурою.` (Adverbs of manner and time in a dialogue) (Source `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0153`)
*   `Одного сонячного осіннього дня Оленка запізнилася на перший урок.` (Adverbial phrase of time) (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-golub-2023_s0237`)
*   `Дуже приємно. / Радий з вами познайомитися.` (Set phrases for introductions) (Source `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0212`)

## Рекомендації для вправ (Activity Concepts)

The A2 Finale should be a mix of review and application.

*   **Phase 1: Recognition and Consolidation**
    *   **Transformation Drills:** Provide sentences in one tense and have learners rewrite them in another (e.g., Present -> Past, Imperfective Future -> Perfective Future). (Based on `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0057`)
    *   **Error Correction:** Present sentences with common L2 errors (like those in the table above) and have learners identify and correct them, explaining the rule. (Based on test tasks in `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`)
    *   **"Choose the Right Form":** Multiple-choice exercises focusing on subject-verb agreement, case endings, or correct etiquette formulas. (Based on `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0066`)

*   **Phase 2: Structured Production**
    *   **Sentence Building:** Give students a set of words in the base form and have them construct a grammatically correct sentence. (`Зразок: Пташ..ний — лежить — гніздо — на — гілки — дерево. -> На гілках дерева лежить пташине гніздо.`) (Source `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0137`)
    *   **Guided Dialogue:** Provide a situation and key vocabulary, then have learners write a short dialogue (5-7 lines). Example: "You are calling a friend to invite them to the cinema. They are busy. Rearrange for another day." (Based on `6-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2023_s0007`)

*   **Phase 3: Communicative Application**
    *   **Mini-Essay / Post:** Have learners write a short post (5-10 sentences) about their day, a recent event, or their plans for the weekend, using a variety of tenses and vocabulary. (Based on `5-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2023_s0233` and `6-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2023_s0028`)
    *   **Role-Playing:** Simulate real-world situations: ordering food, asking for directions, introducing a friend, making a phone call. (Based on `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0214`, `7-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2024_s0150`)

## Зв'язки з іншими темами
The A2 Finale is the capstone of the beginner level. It assumes mastery of all A1 topics (Cyrillic alphabet, basic noun genders, present tense of common verbs, personal pronouns).

*   **Prerequisites:** `a1-alphabet`, `a1-noun-genders`, `a1-present-tense`, `a2-past-tense`, `a2-cases-intro`.
*   **Enables:** This module is the gateway to B1. Successful completion means the learner is ready for more complex syntax, including:
    *   `b1-aspect` (the nuanced difference between perfective and imperfective verbs).
    *   `b1-participles-adjectival` (forms like `читаючий`, `прочитаний`).
    *   `b1-verbs-of-motion` (the `іти/ходити` system).
    *   More complex sentence structures with subordinate clauses.
