## Як це пояснюють у школі (How Schools Teach This)
The concept of expressing direction and time is built upon the grammatical category of the adverb (**прислівник**).

In early grades (Source 3), the adverb is introduced as a simple, unchangeable part of speech that answers questions like **де?** (where?), **куди?** (where to?), **звідки?** (from where?), and **коли?** (when?). This establishes the core function of adverbs in providing context for an action. For example: `повіяв (звідки?) здалеку`, `засяяло (де?) угорі` (Source 3: `4-klas-ukrmova-zaharijchuk_s0165`).

By middle school (Source 1, 19), this knowledge is formalized. Textbooks present a more structured list of questions that adverbs answer, explicitly separating location from direction. For instance, a 7th-grade textbook provides the question list: `як? де? куди? звідки? якою мірою? коли? чому? навіщо?` (Source 1: `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0143`).

In higher grades, this is categorized into "Обставинні прислівники" (Adverbials of circumstance), with specific sub-groups for place and time (Source 35: `10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0349`):
*   **Прислівники місця (Adverbs of Place)**: Answer `де?` (location), `куди?` (direction), `звідки?` (origin). Examples include `угорі, ліворуч, вдома, вниз`.
*   **Прислівники часу (Adverbs of Time)**: Answer `коли?` (when?), `відколи?` (since when?), `доки?` (until when?). Examples include `торік, допізна, донині`.

The key pedagogical distinction drilled in Ukrainian schools, which is often a hurdle for English speakers, is the strict separation between static location (`де?`) and directional movement (`куди?`). The question `куди?` signals the use of specific prepositions and cases (primarily Accusative, sometimes Genitive) that differ from the Locative case used for `де?`.

Similarly, for time, the distinction is made between a point in time (`коли?`) and a time limit (`до якого часу?` or `доки?`), with the latter consistently using the preposition `до` followed by the Genitive case.

## Повна парадигма (Full Paradigm)

The concepts of **"Куди?"** and **"До якого часу?"** are expressed through two primary means: dedicated adverbs and prepositional phrases.

### Куди? (Direction)

This question is answered by adverbs of direction or, more commonly, by a preposition + noun in the Accusative or Genitive case.

**1. Adverbs of Direction:** These are single, unchangeable words.

## Частотність і пріоритети (Frequency & Priorities)

For an A2 learner, mastering the core concepts of direction and time is crucial for moving beyond simple statements.

**High Priority (Must Master for A2):**
1.  **The `де?` vs. `куди?` distinction:** This is the single most important concept. Learners must stop using `де` for movement.
2.  **Core Directional Phrases:**
    *   `в / у` + Accusative (for buildings, cities): `Я йду в магазин / у Київ`.
    *   `на` + Accusative (for events, open spaces): `Я йду на роботу / на стадіон`.
    *   `до` + Genitive (for people): `Я йду до лікаря / до друга`.
3.  **Core Time Adverbs:** `сьогодні`, `завтра`, `вчора`, `зараз`, `потім`, `рано`, `пізно`.
4.  **Telling Time:**
    *   `о/об` + (number) + `годині` for "at X o'clock".
    *   `до` + Genitive for deadlines: `до завтра`, `до п'ятої години`.

**Medium Priority (Introduce at A2, Master for B1):**
*   **Wider range of adverbs:** `вниз`, `вгору`, `здалеку`, `звідти`, `допізна`, `влітку`. The extensive list in Source 2 (`7-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2024_s0273`) is an excellent resource for B1 expansion.
*   **The `звідки?` question:** Using `з / із` + Genitive to express origin (`Я з України`, `Я йду з роботи`). This is the natural counterpart to `куди?`. (Source 6).
*   **Compound adverbs:** `щоранку`, `щовечора`.

## Типові помилки L2 (Common L2 Errors)
English speakers often make predictable errors by transferring English grammatical patterns.

| ❌ Помилково (Incorrect) | ✅ Правильно (Correct) | Чому (Why) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `Де ти йдеш?` | `Куди ти йдеш?` | English uses "Where" for both static location and direction. Ukrainian strictly separates them: **де** is for location (Where are you?), **куди** is for direction (Where are you going?). (Source 3, 35) |
| `Я їду в Київ**і**.` (Locative) | `Я їду **в** Київ.` (Accusative) | The preposition `в`/`у` takes the Accusative case for direction (`куди?`), but the Locative case for location (`де?`). The noun form changes accordingly. |
| `Я йду **до** стадіон.` | `Я йду **на** стадіон.` | English "to" is very versatile. Ukrainian `до` is used for people or "towards" a place, while `на` is standard for open spaces, events, and certain types of locations. (Source 5) |
| `Я був **на** школі.` | `Я був **у** школі.` | The choice between `в` (in, inside) and `на` (on, at) is idiomatic. For a school building, `у школі` is correct. `На школі` would imply being on the roof of the school. |
| `Ми працюємо до **п'ятниця**.` (Nominative) | `Ми працюємо до **п'ятниці**.` (Genitive) | The preposition **до** (until, by), when used for time deadlines, always requires the noun to be in the Genitive case. This is a fixed rule. (Pattern from Source 2) |

## Деколонізаційні застереження (Decolonization Notes)
It is critical to teach Ukrainian as a distinct language, not as a dialect or variation of Russian. Relying on parallels can create deeply ingrained errors.

1.  **`в Україні` vs. `на Україні`**: This is a point of major political and grammatical significance. While the form `на Україні` is found in older texts and folk songs (reflecting a time when "Ukraine" was seen as a territory or borderland), the modern, grammatically correct, and politically sovereign standard is **`в Україні`** (in Ukraine). Using `в` emphasizes Ukraine as an independent state, parallel to `в Польщі` (in Poland) or `в Німеччині` (in Germany). The Russian political discourse often insists on `на Украине`, which reflects an imperial perspective of Ukraine as a region, not a nation. This distinction must be taught explicitly.

2.  **Lexical Divergence**: Do not assume adverbs are cognates with identical usage.
    *   Ukrainian **`зараз`** primarily means "now," while Russian `сейчас` can mean "now" or "in a moment." The Ukrainian equivalent for "in a moment" is often still `зараз` or more explicitly `за хвилину`.
    *   Ukrainian **`тиждень`** (week) and Russian `неделя` are false friends. The Russian word means "week", while the Ukrainian word `неділя` means "Sunday".

3.  **Unique Adverbial Constructions**: Ukrainian has a rich inventory of adverbial phrases with no direct Russian equivalent, often formed from older roots. These should be celebrated as unique features of the language. Examples include `з діда-прадіда` (from time immemorial), `віч-на-віч` (face-to-face), `пліч-о-пліч` (shoulder to shoulder), all listed in orthographic dictionaries as distinctly Ukrainian forms (Source 2, 29, 32).

4.  **Avoid Russian-centric Explanations**: Never present a Ukrainian form as "different from the Russian." Ukrainian grammar is the default. Explain its internal logic. For example, explain the `де/куди` distinction as a core feature of Slavic languages that Ukrainian preserves with clarity.

## Природні приклади (Natural Examples)

These examples are drawn from the provided textbook sources to ensure they are natural and pedagogically sound.

**1. Answering "Куди?" (Where to?)**
*   `— Ти куди йдеш? — На тренування.` (— Where are you going? — To training.) (Source 5: `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0042`)
*   `Я теж іду на тренування у школу.` (I'm also going to training at school.) (Source 5: `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0042`)
*   `Подивіться вгору/навкруги/вбік/сюди.` (Look up/around/aside/here.) (Source 1: `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0143`)

**2. Answering "Звідки?" (From where?)**
*   `— Звідки ти йдеш? — Із фотостудії.` (— Where are you coming from? — From the photo studio.) (Source 6: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0018`)
*   `Прийшов (звідки?) здалеку.` (He came from far away.) (Source 1: `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0143`)

**3. Answering "Коли?" (When?)**
*   `Я святкуватиму свій день народження увечері/сьогодні/завтра.` (I will celebrate my birthday in the evening/today/tomorrow.) (Source 1: `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0143`)
*   `Хто рано підводиться, за тим діло водиться.` (He who gets up early gets the work done.) (Source 3: `4-klas-ukrmova-zaharijchuk_s0165`)
*   `Давно ми не подорожували.` (We haven't traveled for a long time.) (Source 21: `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0107`)

**4. Answering "До якого часу?" (Until when?) and other time limits**
*   `До завтра!` (Until tomorrow! / See you tomorrow!) (Source 2: `7-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2024_s0273`)
*   `Фортецю зачинено через реставрацію.` (The fortress is closed due to restoration. - Implies a time period.) (Source 21: `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0107`)
*   `...мені призначено візит до сімейного лікаря об 11:30.` (...an appointment with the family doctor is scheduled for me at 11:30.) (Source 20: `8-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2025_s0104`)
*   `За дві години... повинен написати музичний твір.` (In two hours... [he] must write a musical piece. - A duration/deadline). (Source 28/31: `9-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnij-2017_s0292`)

## Рекомендації для вправ (Activity Concepts)

**Phase 1: Recognition & Core Concepts**
*   **Sorting Drill:** Give students a list of adverbs and prepositional phrases. Have them sort them into columns: `Де?`, `Куди?`, `Коли?`, `Звідки?`.
*   **Question Matching:** Provide sentences with highlighted phrases. Students must write the correct question word (`де`, `куди`, `коли`, etc.).
    *   Example: "Ми йдемо **в парк**." -> Student writes: `Куди?`

**Phase 2: Controlled Practice**
*   **`Де?` -> `Куди?` Transformation:** Provide a sentence describing a location and have the student transform it into a sentence describing movement to that location. This forces the case change.
    *   `Я є в школі.` (Locative) -> `Я йду ...` (`в школу` - Accusative).
    *   `Машина на вулиці.` (Locative) -> `Машина їде ...` (`на вулицю` - Accusative).
*   **Preposition Choice:** Fill-in-the-blanks with the correct preposition (`в`, `на`, or `до`).
    *   `Я йду ___ подруги.` (до)
    *   `Ми їдемо ___ концерт.` (на)
    *   `Вона летить ___ Америку.` (в)

**Phase 3: Production**
*   **Sentence Building:** Give students a verb of motion (йти, їхати), a noun, and a time adverb. Ask them to form a complete sentence.
    *   (їхати, Львів, завтра) -> `Завтра я їду до Львова.`
*   **Personalized Questions:** Ask students questions about their real-life routine, requiring them to use the target grammar.
    *   `Куди ви йдете після уроків?`
    *   `Коли ви зазвичай вечеряєте?`
    *   `До котрої години ви працювали вчора?`

## Зв'язки з іншими темами (Connections)

*   **Prerequisites:** This topic requires a foundational understanding of:
    *   **Noun Cases:** Specifically, the ability to form the **Accusative** and **Genitive** cases.
    *   **Prepositions:** Basic knowledge of prepositions as a concept.
    *   **Verbs of Motion:** Basic verbs like `йти` (to go on foot) and `їхати` (to go by vehicle).

*   **Connects Directly To:**
    *   **Locative Case (`де?`)**: The `куди?` concept is the dynamic counterpart to the static `де?` + Locative. They should be taught in tandem to highlight the difference. See `[Граматика A1: Прийменниковий відмінок (де?)](/grammar/a1/locative-case-place)`.
    *   **Genitive Case (`звідки?`)**: Directional movement (`куди?`) is logically paired with origin (`звідки?`), which uses `з/із` + Genitive. See `[Граматика A2: Родовий відмінок (звідки?)](/grammar/a2/genitive-case-origin)`.

*   **Enables Future Learning:** Mastering directional and temporal phrases is essential for:
    *   **Aspect in Verbs of Motion:** The perfective/imperfective distinction in verbs of motion (e.g., `іти` vs. `піти`) is often taught in the context of specific, directed journeys.
    *   **Narrative Tense:** Describing sequences of events in the past or future (`потім`, `спочатку`, `до того`, `після того`).
    *   **Complex Sentences:** Building subordinate clauses of time and place.
