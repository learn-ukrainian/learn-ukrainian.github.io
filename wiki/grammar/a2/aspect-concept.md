## Як це пояснюють у школі (How Schools Teach This)

The concept of verb aspect (вид дієслова) is a fundamental category of the Ukrainian verb, introduced systematically in middle school. The core distinction is between an action that is ongoing, repeated, or presented as a process versus an action that is completed, viewed as a whole, or has a result.

School textbooks (Джерело: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0200`, `7-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2024_s0056`) introduce the two aspects using simple questions and definitions:

*   **Недоконаний вид (Imperfective):** Represents an incomplete or ongoing action (`незавершена дія`) and answers the question **що робити?** (what to do?). It focuses on the process itself, not its conclusion.
*   **Доконаний вид (Perfective):** Represents a completed or finite action (`завершена дія`) and answers the question **що зробити?** (what to have done?). It emphasizes the result or the completion of the action as a single event.

The pedagogical approach often uses contrasting examples. For instance, the dialogue presented by Авраменко (Джерело: `7-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2024_s0064`) perfectly illustrates the nuance: A mother asks her son to tidy his room. He replies, "Мамо, я **прибирав** у своїй кімнаті" (I *was cleaning* my room), using the imperfective to describe the process. The mother retorts, "**Прибирати** й **прибрати** — різні дії" (*To clean* and *to have cleaned* are different actions), highlighting that the process (прибирав) did not lead to the desired result (прибрав).

This core distinction—process vs. result—is the primary framework used for teaching. Textbooks then build on this by showing how aspectual pairs are formed and how aspect choice impacts tense, particularly in the future tense (Джерело: `10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0322`).

## Повна парадигма (Full Paradigm)

Most Ukrainian verbs form aspectual pairs, where one verb is imperfective and the other is perfective. These pairs are typically formed through several systematic methods.

**1. Prefixes (Префікси)**
The most common way to form a perfective verb is by adding a prefix to an imperfective verb. The meaning can be simply completion, or the prefix can add a specific spatial or temporal nuance.
