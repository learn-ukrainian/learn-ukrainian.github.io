## Як це пояснюють у школі (How Schools Teach This)
The concept of prefixes as word-forming elements is introduced very early in Ukrainian schools. By the 3rd grade, students learn to identify the prefix as the part of the word before the root and understand its role in creating new words (Source 31: `3-klas-ukrainska-mova-kravtsova-2020-1_s0038`). Simple verbs of motion with prefixes (`виїхали`, `поїхали`, `приїхали`, `переїхати`) are presented in context, such as describing a trip (Source 11: `3-klas-ukrainska-mova-ponomarova-2020-1_s0050`).

By the 5th grade, the curriculum formalizes the definition of a prefix (`префікс`) and its function in forming new words or adding shades of meaning (Source 19: `5-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2022_s0064`). Textbooks like Заболотний (Source 32: `5-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2023_s0056`) use visual diagrams to show how a single base verb (e.g., `бігти`) can be combined with numerous prefixes (`пере-`, `про-`, `до-`, `за-`, `ви-` etc.) to create a wide array of new verbs with specific meanings.

The specific meanings of prefixes `пере-` and `про-` are often explained through examples rather than abstract rules.
- **`пере-`** is taught to signify movement **across** something (e.g., `перейти дорогу` - to cross the road), or relocation (e.g., `переїхати в Київ` - to move to Kyiv) (Source 2: `ext-ulp_youtube-222`). Textbooks for older grades explicitly list these meanings, such as "direction of action through something" (Source 36: `6-klas-ukrmova-golub-2023_s0030`).
- **`про-`** is shown to mean movement **past** or **through** a location (`пройти повз нього` - to walk past him) or an action completed over a period of time (`пройшов тиждень` - a week passed) (Source 6: `ext-ulp_youtube-216`).

The pairing of imperfective and perfective verbs (e.g., `переходити` / `перейти`) is a core concept in B1-level grammar. School materials demonstrate how prefixes are a primary tool for creating perfective verbs from simple imperfective ones (Source 35: `7-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2024_s0058`).

## Повна парадигма (Full Paradigm)
The prefixes `пере-` and `про-` combine with basic unidirectional verbs of motion (`йти` - to go by foot, `їхати` - to go by vehicle) to create new perfective verbs. These perfective verbs then have corresponding imperfective forms, typically formed with a suffix and/or stem change.

### `пере-` (across, over, re-)
This prefix primarily indicates crossing a boundary or moving from one location/state to another.

**Verbs of Motion:**
- **`перейти` / `переходити`** (to cross on foot)
- **`переїхати` / `переїжджати`** (to cross by vehicle; to move residence)

## Частотність і пріоритети (Frequency & Priorities)

For a B1 learner, mastering the core meanings and most frequent uses is crucial.

**High-Priority Usages:**
1.  **`перейти / переходити` + `дорогу / вулицю`**: This is the most common and essential use for "to cross the street". The concept of a `пішохідний перехід` (pedestrian crossing) is directly related (Source 14: `5-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2023_s0180`).
2.  **`переїхати / переїжджати` + `в/до [місто/країна]`**: "To move" residence is a fundamental concept for discussing personal history. E.g., `Я переїхав до Києва` (I moved to Kyiv). This is frequently seen in biographical contexts (Source 2, Source 5).
3.  **`пройти` + [час]**: The use of `пройти` to denote the passage of time is extremely common. E.g., `Пройшов тиждень` (A week went by) (Source 6: `ext-ulp_youtube-216`), `Пройшло багато часу` (A lot of time has passed).
4.  **`проїхати` / `пройти` + [відстань]**: To talk about covering distance. E.g., `Ми пройшли десять кілометрів` (We walked 10 km), `Він проїхав 100 км` (He drove 100 km). This is analogous to `пробігти 5 км` (to run 5 km) mentioned in Source 6.
5.  **`переходити на [мову]`**: This is a culturally significant and increasingly common phrase meaning "to switch to [a language]". E.g., `Багато людей переходять на українську мову` (Many people are switching to Ukrainian) (Source 4: `ext-ulp_youtube-36`).

**Secondary Priority:**
- **`пройти повз` + Genitive**: "To go past something". E.g., `пройти повз магазин` (to walk past the store) (Source 6).
- **`пройти крізь` + Accusative**: "To go through something". E.g., `пройти крізь ліс` (to go through the forest), `пройти крізь кригу` (to get through the ice) (Source 3).
- **`переїхати` + `через` + Accusative**: "To cross something by vehicle". E.g. `переїхати через міст` (to drive over the bridge) (Source 11).

## Типові помилки L2 (Common L2 Errors)
English speakers often struggle with the distinctions between motion verbs, aspect, and the specific meanings of prefixes.

| ❌ Помилково | ✅ Правильно | Чому |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Я **перейшов** до Львова минулого року. | Я **переїхав** до Львова минулого року. | The distinction between motion by foot (`йти`) and by vehicle (`їхати`) is crucial and is carried over to prefixed verbs. Moving to another city implies transport, so `переїхати` is required. `Перейти` would imply walking there. (Source 2) |
| Щоб потрапити додому, я **пройшов** міст. | Щоб потрапити додому, я **перейшов** міст. | `Пере-` means to cross *over* or *across* a boundary (the bridge). `Про-` means to go *through* or *past*. You cross *over* a bridge. One could say `я пройшов мостом` (I walked along the bridge), but to express "crossing", `перейти` is correct. |
| Коли я **переходив** вулицю, я побачив друга. | Коли я **переходив** вулицю, я побачив друга. (Correct if the focus is on the process)<br><br>Я **перейшов** вулицю і побачив друга. (Correct if it's a sequence of completed actions) | This is a classic aspectual error. If seeing the friend happened *during the process* of crossing, the imperfective `переходив` is correct. If it happened *after* the crossing was complete, the perfective `перейшов` is needed for the first clause in the sequence. English "when I crossed" is ambiguous. |
| Я **пройшов** іспит. | Я **склав** іспит. | This is a lexical error (verb choice), not a prefix error. While one can `пройти випробування` (go through a trial) (Source 24), the standard verb for passing an academic test or exam is `скласти`. The verb `пройти` here is a calque from English "pass". |
| Він **перейшов** поле на машині. | Він **переїхав** поле на машині. | Same as the first error. Motion `на машині` (by car) requires a verb based on `їхати`. Therefore, `переїхати`. (Source 2: `переїхати через поле`) |
| Будь ласка, **пройдіть** у кімнату. | Будь ласка, **зайдіть** у кімнату. | `Проходити` means to pass *through* or *by*. `Заходити` means to enter *into*. For the instruction "come in", `заходьте` or `зайдіть` is the correct verb. (Source 6: `Заходьте!`) |

## Деколонізаційні застереження (Decolonization Notes)
Ukrainian grammar, particularly verbs of motion, has its own logic and should not be taught as a variation of Russian.

1.  **Distinct Socio-Political Context:** The phrase **`переходити на українську мову`** (Source 4) carries a strong historical and political significance in modern Ukraine. It signifies a conscious act of de-Russification and affirmation of national identity. While the Russian construction `переходить на украинский язык` is grammatically similar, it lacks this powerful contemporary context of societal transformation. Teaching this phrase requires explaining its cultural weight.
2.  **Phonetics:** Prefixes like `роз-` and `без-` are consistently pronounced with a `[з]` sound before voiced consonants and can be pronounced with either `[з]` or `[с]` before voiceless ones in Ukrainian, but are always spelled with `з` (Source 18, 25). In Russian, the spelling changes to `рос-` / `бес-` before voiceless consonants. While `пере-` and `про-` do not have this particular spelling change, it is part of a larger pattern of Ukrainian orthography maintaining morphemic consistency more than Russian, which is more phonetic. This principle should be highlighted.
3.  **Lexical Divergence:** While many core motion verbs overlap, there are differences. For example, the context of "passing an exam" uses `скласти` in Ukrainian, whereas `сдать` is used in Russian. Direct translation or assuming a one-to-one correspondence for prefixed verbs can lead to errors and Russianisms. Always verify collocations with Ukrainian-centric sources (e.g., GRAC corpus, language podcasts like ULP).
4.  **Avoid Russian-centric Terminology:** Use Ukrainian grammatical terms: `префікс` (not `приставка`), `доконаний/недоконаний вид` (perfective/imperfective aspect). The sources consistently use this terminology (Source 16, 19, 35).

## Природні приклади (Natural Examples)
These examples are taken from the provided sources and demonstrate natural usage.

**Pattern 1: `пере-` for Relocation & State Change**
- Я вирішила **переїхати** на один рік в Київ. (I decided to move to Kyiv for one year.) (Source 2: `ext-ulp_youtube-222`)
- Він приїхав до нас щоб допомогти нам **переїжджати** у наш новий будинок. (He came to us to help us move to our new house.) (Source 1: `ext-ulp_youtube-38`)
- Зараз багато є людей, які **переходять** на українську мову з російської. (Now there are many people who are switching to Ukrainian from Russian.) (Source 4: `ext-ulp_youtube-36`)

**Pattern 2: `пере-` for Crossing a Physical Barrier**
- Щоб потрапити до неї, довелось **переїхати** через Замковий міст. (To get to it, we had to cross the Castle Bridge.) (Source 11: `3-klas-ukrainska-mova-ponomarova-2020-1_s0050`)
- Інший спосіб — **перейти** дорогу. (Another way is to cross the road.) (Source 2: `ext-ulp_youtube-222`)
- Мешканці просять побудувати наземний чи підземний пішохідний **перехід**. (Residents are asking to build an above-ground or underground pedestrian crossing.) (Source 14: `5-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2023_s0180`)

**Pattern 3: `про-` for Passage of Time & Duration**
- Ну як **пройшов** ваш перший тиждень? (So, how did your first week go?) (Source 6: `ext-ulp_youtube-216`)
- Ми розговорились і **проговорили** дві години. (We got to talking and talked for two hours.) (Source 6)
- Цей корабель **подолав** той самий маршрут, який 80 років тому... (This ship traveled the same route that 80 years ago...) (Source 3, using a synonym `подолати`, but illustrates the concept of covering a route).

**Pattern 4: `про-` for Movement Past, Through, or Covering Distance**
- Нам пощастило, що ми змогли **пройти** крізь цю кригу. (We were lucky that we were able to get through this ice.) (Source 3: `ext-realna_istoria-100`)
- Інколи над берегом Пруту **проскакував** вершник і зникав. (Sometimes a rider would dash past along the bank of the Prut and disappear.) (Source 9: `8-klas-ukrlit-zabolotnyi-2025_s0165`)
- Запропонуйте детальний маршрут: якою вулицею іти, де **пройти**. (Suggest a detailed route: which street to take, where to pass.) (Source 34: `5-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2023_s0058`, adapted from instruction)

## Рекомендації для вправ (Activity Concepts)

**Phase 1: Recognition & Core Meaning**
1.  **Matching:** Match verbs (`перейти`, `проїхати`, etc.) to pictures (crossing a street, driving past a building, moving trucks).
2.  **Categorization:** Give students a list of sentences. They must categorize the meaning of the prefix: "Crossing a line", "Moving residence", "Passing time", "Covering distance".
3.  **Multiple Choice:** _Щоб потрапити в парк, треба \_\_\_ вулицю._ (а) пройти, (б) перейти, (в) зайти).

**Phase 2: Controlled Practice (Form & Aspect)**
1.  **Fill-in-the-blanks:** Provide sentences with the infinitive in brackets. Students must conjugate the correct verb (`переходити` or `перейти`) in the correct tense/aspect.
    - `Вчора ми ______ (переїхати) в нову квартиру.` -> `переїхали`
    - `Зазвичай я ______ (переходити) дорогу тут.` -> `переходжу`
2.  **Transformation Drills:** Convert imperfective sentences to perfective ones, focusing on past and future.
    - `Я буду переїжджати до Львова.` -> `Я переїду до Львова.`
    - `Він довго переходив дорогу.` -> `Він нарешті перейшов дорогу.`
3.  **`йти` vs. `їхати`:** Choose the correct prefixed verb based on context.
    - `Ми (перейшли / переїхали) кордон на машині.` -> `переїхали`

**Phase 3: Production**
1.  **Route Description:** "Опишіть, як ви добираєтесь з дому на роботу. Використайте дієслова `переходити`, `проїжджати`, `проходити`." (Describe how you get from home to work. Use the verbs...)
2.  **Storytelling:** Give a sequence of pictures (e.g., a person packing boxes, driving, unpacking in a new city). The student must write a short story using `переїхав`, `проїхав`, `пройшов час`.
3.  **Personal Questions:** Engage in a conversation using prompts like:
    - `Ви коли-небудь переїжджали в інше місто чи країну? Розкажіть про це.` (Have you ever moved to another city or country? Tell about it.)
    - `Скільки часу проходить, поки ви доїдете до центру міста?` (How much time passes until you get to the city center?)

## Зв'язки з іншими темами (Connections)

- **Prerequisites:**
    - **A2 Verbs of Motion:** A solid understanding of the basic pair `йти` (unidirectional, foot) vs. `ходити` (multidirectional, foot) and `їхати` vs. `їздити`. The prefixes `пере-` and `про-` almost always attach to the unidirectional stems `йти` and `їхати`.
    - **A2/B1 Aspect:** The concept that prefixes often (but not always) create a perfective verb from an imperfective one (e.g., `їхати` -> `проїхати`).
    - **A2 Cases:** Knowledge of the Accusative case for destination (`в/на Київ`) and `через` (through/across the street), and the Instrumental case for path (`пройти вулицею` - to walk *along* the street).

- **What this enables:**
    - **B1+ Narrative:** Essential for telling stories and describing sequences of events in the past and planned events in the future.
    - **Giving & Understanding Directions:** Moves beyond simple "go straight" to "cross the street", "go past the pharmacy".
    - **Discussing Life Experience:** Crucial for talking about immigration, moving, changing jobs, or personal development (`перейти на новий етап`).
