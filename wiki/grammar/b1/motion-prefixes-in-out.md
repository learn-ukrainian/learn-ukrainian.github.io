## Як це пояснюють у школі (How Schools Teach This)

The concept of directional prefixes is introduced early and reinforced through antonymic pairs. School textbooks focus on the concrete meaning change that a prefix brings to a base verb of motion.

-   **Early Grades (3-4):** The topic starts with simple antonym creation. A Grade 3 textbook gives the exercise: `зайти — …`, `виїхати — …` (Source 23: `3-klas-ukrainska-mova-ponomarova-2020-1_s0053`). The distinction between a prefix (part of a word) and a preposition (separate word) is also a key foundational concept, using examples like `(за)йти (до) друзів` (Source 26: `4-klas-ukrayinska-mova-ponomarova-2021-1_s0025`).

-   **Middle Grades (5-6):** The concept is formalized as a word-formation strategy. A Grade 6 textbook explicitly has students "form antonym pairs using prefixes **в-** and **ви-**" with the given example `Входити — виходити` (Source 13: `6-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2023_s0062`). This demonstrates that **входити/виходити** and **заходити/виходити** are treated as core antonymic motion pairs. The function of prefixes to create new words is a central theme (Source 24: `5-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2023_s0056`).

-   **Aspectual Pairs:** The connection between prefixes and grammatical aspect (perfective/imperfective) is taught in later grades (around Grade 7). Textbooks explain that adding a prefix is a primary way to form a perfective verb from an imperfective one (Source 12: `7-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2024_s0058`). However, with verbs of motion, the unprefixed verbs are already an aspectual pair (e.g., `йти/ходити`). Adding prefixes like `за-` and `ви-` creates *new* aspectual pairs: `заходити` (imperfective) and `зайти` (perfective). The pair `заходити — увійти` is also presented as a valid aspectual pair, showing `увійти` as a synonym for `зайти` (Source 9: `10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0323`).

The core school methodology is to teach these prefixes as meaning-modifiers that indicate direction (in/out) and often create new perfective/imperfective verb pairs.

## Повна парадигма (Full Paradigm)

The prefixes **за-** (in, enter) and **ви-** (out, exit) combine with base verbs of motion to create new aspectual pairs. The imperfective form is usually created by suffix change from the perfective.

### 1. Заходити / Зайти (to enter, go in, drop by)

-   **Imperfective:** заходити (multi-directional base `ходити`)
-   **Perfective:** зайти (uni-directional base `йти`)

**Conjugation Table**

| Tense/Mood    | Imperfective: заходити          | Perfective: зайти                 |
| :------------ | :------------------------------ | :-------------------------------- |
| **Present**   | я заходжу, ти заходиш, він/вона/воно заходить, ми заходимо, ви заходите, вони заходять | *(does not exist)*                |
| **Past**      | заходив / заходила / заходило / заходили | зайшов / зайшла / зайшло / зайшли |
| **Future**    | буду заходити, будеш заходити... | зайду, зайдеш, зайде, зайдемо, зайдете, зайдуть |
| **Imperative**| заходь, заходьте                | зайди, зайдіть                    |

### 2. Виходити / Вийти (to exit, go out)

-   **Imperfective:** виходити (multi-directional base `ходити`)
-   **Perfective:** вийти (uni-directional base `йти`)

**Conjugation Table**

| Tense/Mood    | Imperfective: виходити          | Perfective: вийти                 |
| :------------ | :------------------------------ | :-------------------------------- |
| **Present**   | я виходжу, ти виходиш, він/вона/воно виходить, ми виходимо, ви виходите, вони виходять | *(does not exist)*                |
| **Past**      | виходив / виходила / виходило / виходили | вийшов / вийшла / вийшло / вийшли |
| **Future**    | буду виходити, будеш виходити... | вийду, вийдеш, вийде, вийдемо, вийдете, вийдуть |
| **Imperative**| виходь, виходьте                | вийди, вийдіть                    |

### 3. Заїжджати / Заїхати (to enter by vehicle)

-   **Imperfective:** заїжджати
-   **Perfective:** заїхати

### 4. Виїжджати / Виїхати (to exit by vehicle)

-   **Imperfective:** виїжджати
-   **Perfective:** виїхати

## Частотність і пріоритети

For B1 learners, mastering the fundamental pairs **заходити/зайти** and **виходити/вийти** is the highest priority.

1.  **Core Meaning (Enter/Exit):** The most frequent use is for literal movement into or out of a space (`зайти в дім`, `вийти з кімнати`). The perfective past tense forms (`зайшов`, `вийшов`, `вийшла`) are extremely common in narration.
2.  **"Drop by" / "Call on":** The secondary meaning of `зайти` as "to drop by" or "call on" someone is also very high frequency. It is typically used with `до` + Genitive: `зайти до друга` (to drop by a friend's place). (Source 26).
3.  **Figurative Meanings:** Figurative uses like `вийти сухим з води` (to get away with something) (Source 4) or `вийти на зв'язок` (to get in touch) (Source 6) should be introduced at a B1+ or B2 level, once the literal meanings are solid.
4.  **Stress Distinction:** The stress difference between `вихо́дити` (to exit) and `ви́ходити` (to nurse, to raise) is a crucial point for comprehension and correct pronunciation, though the "nursing" meaning is less frequent (Source 25). It should be highlighted to avoid confusion.

Priority for learners:
- **Phase 1:** Master `зайшов / зайшла` and `вийшов / вийшла` for single past actions.
- **Phase 2:** Master the imperfective present `заходжу / виходжу` for current actions and the imperfective past `заходив / виходив` for repeated or process-oriented past actions.
- **Phase 3:** Introduce the future tense forms (`зайду / вийду`).
- **Phase 4:** Introduce the "drop by" meaning (`зайти до когось`).
