## Як це пояснюють у школі (How Schools Teach This)

The study of stylistic devices in Ukrainian schools begins with the fundamental concept of **пряме і переносне значення слова** (direct and figurative meaning of a word). This concept is introduced as early as the 3rd grade (Source `3-klas-ukrainska-mova-vashulenko-2020-1_s0048`) and is solidified in the 5th grade. Textbooks explain that figurative meanings arise when the name of one object or phenomenon is transferred to another based on some similarity in shape, color, function, or impression (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2023_s0022`, `5-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2022_s0043`). For example, `золота каблучка` (golden ring) is a direct meaning, while `золота осінь` (golden autumn) is figurative, based on color similarity (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2022_s0043`).

In higher grades (10-11), this concept is formalized into the study of **тропи** (tropes), which are defined as words or expressions used in a figurative sense to create imagery and expressiveness (Source `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0004`). School curricula distinguish between tropes and **риторичні фігури** (rhetorical figures), with tropes being the primary tools for lexical stylistics (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0013`).

The use of tropes is primarily associated with the artistic (художній) and publicistic (публіцистичний) styles of language (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0219`). However, textbooks also note the existence of "стерті метафори" (dead or faded metaphors), which have lost their original imagery and are used in scientific or technical language, such as `ніжка стола` (table leg) or `вушко голки` (eye of a needle) (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`).

## Повна парадигма (Full Paradigm)

This topic does not have a "paradigm" in the traditional grammatical sense (like declension or conjugation). Instead, it's a classification of different devices. The main tropes covered in the Ukrainian curriculum are listed below (Source `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0004`, `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0013`, `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`).

| Троп (Trope) | Визначення (Definition) | Приклад (Example) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Метафора** (Metaphor) | Hidden comparison; transferring properties from one object/phenomenon to another based on **similarity**. | `Дні летять.` (Days are flying.) (Source `7-klas-ukrlit-zabolotnyi-2024_s0442`) |
| **Метонімія** (Metonymy) | Replacing a word with another based on **contiguity or association**, not similarity. | `Читаю Франка.` (I'm reading Franko, i.e., Franko's works.) (Source `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0005`) |
| **Синекдоха** (Synecdoche) | A type of metonymy; using a part for the whole, singular for plural, etc. | `Ворог не ступить на нашу землю.` (The enemy [foot] won't step on our land.) (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`) |
| **Епітет** (Epithet) | A figurative, descriptive adjective that highlights a key characteristic. | `сивочолий Київ` (grey-headed Kyiv), `золоті руки` (golden hands) (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`) |
| **Порівняння** (Simile) | An explicit comparison, usually using words like `як`, `мов`, `ніби`, `наче`. | `Вуса, наче пух.` (A mustache like down.) (Source `7-klas-ukrlit-zabolotnyi-2024_s0442`) |
| **Персоніфікація** (Personification) | Attributing human qualities or actions to inanimate objects or abstract ideas. | `Сміється сонце.` (The sun is laughing.) (Source `7-klas-ukrlit-zabolotnyi-2024_s0442`) |
| **Гіпербола** (Hyperbole) | Intentional exaggeration for emphasis or effect. | `море сліз` (a sea of tears), `не бачилися сто років` (we haven't seen each other for a hundred years) (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`) |
| **Оксиморон** (Oxymoron) | Combining two contradictory terms. | `дзвінка тиша` (ringing silence), `живий труп` (living corpse) (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`) |
| **Евфемізм** (Euphemism) | Substituting a mild or indirect expression for one thought to be offensive or harsh. | `незрячий` (non-seeing) for `сліпий` (blind) (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`) |

### Метафора vs. Метонімія

The key distinction taught is that metaphor is based on **similarity**, while metonymy is based on **association/contiguity** (Source `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0005`, `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0059`).

- **Метафора**: `камінний погляд` (stone gaze) – the gaze is *like* a stone in its coldness. (Source `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0005`)
- **Метонімія**: `закипів чайник` (the kettle is boiling) – the water *inside* the kettle is boiling. The connection is container-for-contents. (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0059`)

Types of metonymic relationships are varied (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0059`):
- Author for their work: `читати Ліну Костенко`
- Place for its people: `Львів зустрів гостей`
- Material for the object: `переможець узяв золото` (the winner took gold, i.e., the gold medal)
- Container for its contents: `випити склянку молока`

## Частотність і пріоритети (Frequency & Priorities)

For a B2 learner, the priority shifts from simply recognizing tropes to actively using them to make their speech and writing more expressive, persuasive, and nuanced.

1.  **High Priority (Mastery):**
    *   **Епітет, Порівняння, Метафора:** These are the most fundamental and frequently used tropes in everyday language and literature. A B2 learner should be able to produce them spontaneously. For instance, instead of saying `дуже гарна осінь`, they should be able to say `золота осінь` (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2022_s0043`).
    *   **Персоніфікація:** Very common in descriptive writing and adds significant color. Ex: `осінь... шиє голим полям сорочку з туману` (Source `10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0157`).
    *   **Евфемізм:** Crucial for polite and diplomatic communication. Using `людина похилого віку` instead of `стара людина`, or `незрячий` instead of `сліпий` is a marker of high cultural and linguistic competence (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`).

2.  **Medium Priority (Recognition and Controlled Use):**
    *   **Гіпербола:** Common in conversational language for emphasis (`сто разів тобі казав`), but overuse can sound unnatural.
    *   **Метонімія / Синекдоха:** Understanding these is key to comprehending news headlines (`Київ заявив...`) and literary texts (`читати Шекспіра`). Active use requires a deeper cultural context.

3.  **Lower Priority (Recognition):**
    *   **Оксиморон:** Primarily a literary device. Recognizing it (`солодка мука` - Source `ext-ulp_youtube-14`) is important for text analysis, but active production is less of a priority for general communication.

## Типові помилки L2 (Common L2 Errors)

| ❌ Помилково | ✅ Правильно | Чому |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| *"Йшов дощ котами й собаками."* | *Ллє як з відра.* | This is a literal translation of the English idiom "It's raining cats and dogs." Ukrainian has its own distinct phraseology for heavy rain. The learner must use Ukrainian idioms, not just translate English ones. |
| *"Я прочитав бібліотеку."* | *Я прочитав Франка.* (author for work) <br> *Я прочитав усю свою бібліотеку.* (describing the collection) | The incorrect sentence attempts metonymy but uses the wrong association (place for content). Ukrainian uses author-for-work metonymy (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0059`), but not typically location-for-all-books-within-it in this manner. |
| *"Його характер був як залізо."* | *У нього був залізний характер.* | While the simile (Порівняння) is grammatically correct, using a standard epithet (Епітет) is often more natural and stylistically stronger for common collocations like this (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2022_s0043`). It shows a deeper command of the language. |
| *"Совість була спляча."* | *Совість заснула.* | English often uses adjectives (`a sleeping conscience`), whereas Ukrainian frequently prefers personification with a dynamic verb (`совість заснула` - "conscience fell asleep") to create a more powerful and active image (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2022_s0043`). |
| *"Це була гірка радість."* | *Це була радість крізь сльози.* <br> *З журбою радість обнялась...* (literary) | While "гірка радість" is understandable, it's a less common oxymoron than `радість крізь сльози` (joy through tears). The literary example from Oleksandr Oles' (Source `10-klas-ukrlit-borzenko-2018-prof_s0386`) shows a more poetic, personified way to express mixed feelings. L2 learners often create overly simple or non-idiomatic oxymorons. |

## Деколонізаційні застереження (Decolonization Notes)

1.  **Avoid Russian Calques:** Stylistic norms are a key battleground in language decolonization. Learners must be actively discouraged from using Russian phraseological calques. A notorious example is `приймати участь` (from Russian *принимать участие*). The correct Ukrainian form is **брати участь**. While not a trope, this illustrates the principle of avoiding structural and lexical borrowing from Russian in favor of authentic Ukrainian forms. The style guide of Антоненко-Давидович is a critical resource for identifying such Russianisms (Source `ext-other_blogs-63`).

2.  **Separate Etymological Paths:** Words that look similar in Ukrainian and Russian may have different semantic histories and cultural connotations. The word `поганий` is a prime example. In Old Church Slavonic, it was borrowed from Latin `paganus` (villager, later pagan) and initially meant "pagan" or "infidel," an enemy of the Christian faith of Rus'. Its meaning evolved over centuries from "non-Christian" to "barbaric" to the general "bad." This trajectory is specific to Ukrainian history and its relationship with Christianity, and should not be conflated with the word's evolution in Russian (Source `ext-istoria_movy-16`).

3.  **Ukrainian is the Default:** Never explain a Ukrainian stylistic feature as a deviation from a Russian norm. Ukrainian phraseology, metaphors, and idioms are a self-contained system. For example, when discussing diminutives used for stylistic effect (`котик`, `сонечко`), present them as an integral, productive feature of the Ukrainian language, not as something "more common than in Russian."

4.  **Use Ukrainian Literary Sources:** When seeking examples, prioritize Ukrainian authors. Citing Taras Shevchenko, Lesya Ukrainka, Lina Kostenko, or Ivan Franko reinforces the richness of the native literary tradition. The sources provided heavily rely on Ukrainian poets and writers for their examples (Source `10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0157`, `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0005`, `ext-ulp_youtube-14`).

## Природні приклади (Natural Examples)

Here are 12+ examples of tropes drawn from Ukrainian textbooks and literature, grouped by type.

#### 1. Метафора (Metaphor)
- `Ставить осінь на землю свою золоту жирандоль.` (Autumn places its golden girandole on the earth.) – L. Kostenko (Source `10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0157`)
- `Століття – зморшка на чолі Землі.` (A century is a wrinkle on the forehead of the Earth.) – I. Drach (Source `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0005`)
- `Ідуть-бредуть метафори у фрази, немов чумацькі зоряні вози.` (Metaphors wander into phrases like the Chumaks' starry wagons.) – V. Kykot' (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0219`)
- `В'яне серце моє от щасливих очей.` (My heart withers from [seeing] happy eyes.) – V. Sosiura (Source `ext-ulp_youtube-14`)

#### 2. Метонімія (Metonymy)
- `Переміг Львів.` (Lviv won.) (Meaning: the athletes from Lviv won.) (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`)
- `Читав Коцюбинського.` (He was reading Kotsiubynsky.) (Meaning: he was reading a work by Kotsiubynsky.) (Source `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0005`)
- `Київ радо зустрічає гостей.` (Kyiv happily greets guests.) (Meaning: the people of Kyiv greet guests.) – O. Honchar (Source `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0005`)

#### 3. Епітет (Epithet)
- `Івашка Манжули золоті руки.` (Ivashko Manzhula's golden hands.) (Meaning: skillful hands.) (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2023_s0022`)
- `...і серце твоє не згорить, не замовкне, не згасне.` ([carry words to people] and your heart will not burn, fall silent, or be extinguished.) – M. Rylsky (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0219`)
- `гостре слово` (a sharp word) (Source `5-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2023_s0022`)

#### 4. Персоніфікація (Personification)
- `Річка синіє, зітхає, сміється.` (The river turns blue, sighs, laughs.) (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0024`)
- `З журбою радість обнялась…` (Sorrow embraced joy...) – O. Oles (Source `10-klas-ukrlit-borzenko-2018-prof_s0386`)
- `За сонцем хмаронька пливе.` (A little cloud floats after the sun.) – T. Shevchenko (Source `5-klas-ukrlit-avramenko-2022_s0132`)

#### 5. Оксиморон (Oxymoron)
- `дзвінка тиша` (ringing silence) (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0021`)
- `солодка мука` (sweet torment) (Source `ext-ulp_youtube-14`)

## Рекомендації для вправ (Activity Concepts)

-   **Phase 1 (Recognition & Classification):**
    -   Provide a poem (e.g., "Так ніхто не кохав" by Sosiura, Source `ext-ulp_youtube-14`) and have learners find and label all the tropes (`солодка мука` -> оксиморон; `в'яне серце` -> метафора/персоніфікація).
    -   Create matching exercises: one column with examples (`читати Гоголя`), the other with trope names (`метонімія`) (based on Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-avramenko-2019_s0024`).
    -   "Find the Epithet": Provide sentences and ask learners to identify the figurative adjective (e.g., `У нього залізний характер`).

-   **Phase 2 (Controlled Production & Transformation):**
    -   **Simile to Metaphor:** Give a list of similes (`його очі були як зорі`) and ask learners to rewrite them as metaphors (`зорі його очей`).
    -   **Literal to Figurative:** Provide a simple, literal sentence (`Він дуже сміливий`) and ask learners to rewrite it using a trope (`Він справжній лев` or `У нього левове серце`).
    -   **Euphemism Practice:** Give a list of direct/blunt statements (`Він помер`, `Вона стара`) and ask for polite, euphemistic alternatives (`Він відійшов у вічність`, `Вона поважного віку`).

-   **Phase 3 (Free Production):**
    -   **Creative Prompts:** "Describe a winter morning as if it were a person." (Elicits personification). "Write a short paragraph about your city using at least one example of metonymy and two different epithets."
    -   **Dialogue Writing:** "Write a short, emotional dialogue where one character uses hyperbole to express their frustration, and the other uses metaphors to calm them down."

## Зв'язки з іншими темами (Connections)

-   **Багатозначність (Polysemy):** The concept of tropes is a direct extension of polysemy and figurative meaning, which is a prerequisite from the A2/B1 level. Tropes are the mechanism through which many words acquire new, figurative meanings (Source `11-klas-ukrajinska-mova-glazova-2019_s0219`).
-   **Фразеологія (Phraseology):** Many idioms and set phrases (`фразеологізми`) are essentially "fossilized" or "dead" metaphors, where the original imagery is no longer consciously felt (e.g., `бити байдики`). Understanding tropes helps decode the origins of idioms (Source `ext-other_blogs-65`).
-   **Стилістика (Stylistics):** The choice and density of tropes are defining features of different functional styles (artistic, publicistic, etc.). This topic is a cornerstone of practical stylistics (Source `ext-other_blogs-63`).
-   **Риторика (Rhetoric):** Tropes are powerful tools of persuasion, used to make speeches more memorable, emotional, and impactful. This knowledge is essential for developing rhetorical skills (Source `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0004`).
