## Як це пояснюють у школі (How Schools Teach This)

The concept of synonymy and its stylistic function is a cornerstone of Ukrainian language education, progressing from basic definitions to nuanced application.

**Foundation (Grades 5-6):** The topic begins with the fundamental distinction between stylistically neutral (загальновживані/нейтральні) words, which form the core of all language styles, and stylistically colored (стилістично забарвлені) words, which are specific to certain registers (Source 10). At this stage, students learn to identify words that are inappropriate for formal contexts. For example, in a synonym row like *Дітлахи, діти, мілкота* (Kids, children, small fry), they learn that *діти* is neutral, while *дітлахи* is colloquial and *мілкота* is derogatory (Source 10).

**Intermediate (Grades 7-9):** The focus shifts to understanding the richness synonyms provide. Students analyze how different synonyms for the same concept carry different emotional or evaluative weight. The concept of **стилістичні синоніми** is introduced, emphasizing that words can belong to different styles (Source 24).

**Advanced (Grades 10-11):** By B2 level, students are expected to master the functional application of synonyms across different styles. Textbooks like Заболотний and Глазова explicitly detail these functions (Source 25, 24):

*   **Науковий (Scientific) & Офіційно-діловий (Official-Business):** Synonyms are used cautiously for precision, clarification, and to avoid repetition of terms. The choice is deliberate and functional, not expressive. For example, using *лінгвістика* in a formal paper and then *мовознавство* to vary the text (Source 47). In these styles, synonyms are often used for explaining and detailing thoughts (`пояснення та деталізації думок`) (Source 25).
*   **Публіцистичний (Publicistic) & Художній (Artistic):** These styles leverage the full power of synonymy. Words are chosen for their emotional-expressive shades (`емоційно-експресивні відтінки`) and evaluative connotations (`оцінок`) (Source 25). For instance, choosing between *говорити* (to speak), *ректи* (to utter, archaic/poetic), and *гарчати* (to growl/speak angrily) creates entirely different tones (Source 25).
*   **Розмовний (Conversational):** This style employs a wide range of expressive, sometimes pejorative or diminutive synonyms that are unacceptable in formal writing (Source 10, 24).

Ukrainian pedagogy emphasizes that synonymy is not just about having different words for the same thing; it's about choosing the *right* word for the right context, purpose, and audience (Source 1). This skill is considered a hallmark of a proficient and cultured speaker.

## Типи синонімів та їх стилістичне забарвлення (Types of Synonyms and their Stylistic Nuances)

Ukrainian grammar classifies synonyms based on their interchangeability and stylistic properties. Mastering these distinctions is key to achieving a B2 level of proficiency.

### 1. Абсолютні синоніми (Absolute Synonyms)
These words are considered semantically identical, but often differ in their stylistic register or origin. They are not always interchangeable despite having the same core meaning. (Source 47).

| Нейтральне / Поширене | Книжне / Наукове | Походження / Примітки |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| мовознавство | лінгвістика | `мовознавство` is a native Slavic term, common in all styles; `лінгвістика` is an internationalism, preferred in academic contexts (Source 47). |
| краєвид | ландшафт | `краєвид` is common in artistic and conversational styles; `ландшафт` is a scientific term (geography) (Source 47). |
| посилювати | інтенсифікувати | `посилювати` is a general-purpose word; `інтенсифікувати` is bookish/scientific (Source 47). |
| абетка, алфавіт | — | Both are stylistically neutral and interchangeable. `абетка` is of native origin, `алфавіт` is from Greek. |

### 2. Ідеографічні (семантичні) синоніми (Ideographic/Semantic Synonyms)
These are the most common type of synonyms. They refer to the same concept but differ in shades of meaning, intensity, or the specific aspect they highlight.

| Поняття (Concept) | Синонім | Відтінок значення (Shade of Meaning) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Шлях (Way/Path)** | дорога | A general term for a road, path, or way. (Source 25) |
| | шлях | Often implies a longer journey, a direction, or a metaphorical path in life. (Source 25) |
| | путь | Archaic or poetic term for a path or journey. |
| | гостинець | A wide, well-traveled road; a highway. |
| **Старий (Old)** | давній | Ancient, from a long time ago. |
| | старезний | Very old, decrepit (often with a negative or expressive connotation). |
| | прадавній | Primeval, from the earliest times (bookish/poetic). (Source 37) |
| | ветхий | Old and worn out, dilapidated (often used for objects or buildings). |

### 3. Стилістичні синоніми (Stylistic Synonyms)
These words share a core meaning but have distinct stylistic and emotional connotations, making them suitable for different functional styles.

| Нейтральне | Розмовне | Книжне / Урочисте | Просто/Зневажливе |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **говорити** (to speak) | балакати | ректи, глаголити | базікати, гавкати |
| **обличчя** (face) | мордашка, личко | лик | пика, морда |
| **іти** (to go) | чалапати, чимчикувати | прямувати | плентатись, шкандибати |
| **очі** (eyes) | оченята | зорі | баньки |
| **плакати** (to cry) | — | — | рюмсати, ревіти, скиглити |
| **діти** (children) | дітлахи | — | мілкота |

*Table compiled from examples in Source 24, 25, 10.*

### 4. Контекстуальні синоніми (Contextual Synonyms)
These are words that become synonymous only within a specific context, often used in artistic or publicistic writing for expressive effect. Outside of this context, they are not synonyms.

For example, in the line "Цілу ніч надворі **виє** хуга, **плаче**, **деренчить** в віконнім склі" (All night outside the blizzard **howls**, **cries**, **rattles** on the window pane), the verbs *виє, плаче, деренчить* become synonyms for creating the sound of the storm (Source 48).

## Частотність і пріоритети (Frequency & Priorities)

For a B2 learner, the priority is not simply to memorize long lists of synonyms, but to develop a feel for their appropriate use.

1.  **Master the Neutral Core:** The foundation of communication is the **загальновживана (нейтральна) лексика** (common/neutral vocabulary) (Source 10, 18). Learners must have a firm grasp of the most common, stylistically neutral synonym in a group (e.g., *говорити*, *великий*, *цікавий*) before exploring its more colorful variants.
2.  **Focus on Register Recognition:** At the B2 level, passive recognition is as important as active use. The learner should be able to read a newspaper article and recognize that *здобутки* (achievements) is more formal than *успіхи* (successes), or hear *балакати* in a dialogue and understand it signifies an informal conversation (Source 24).
3.  **Prioritize High-Contrast Pairs:** Focus on synonym pairs with clear stylistic differences that are common in daily communication.
    *   **Нейтральне vs. Розмовне:** *говорити* vs. *балакати*; *їсти* vs. *гамати*.
    *   **Нейтральне vs. Книжне/Офіційне:** *почати* vs. *ініціювати*; *зарплата* vs. *заробітна плата*.
4.  **Understand Functional Use:** The purpose of synonyms varies by style. In scientific and official texts, they provide precision (*симптом* vs. *ознака*) or avoid tautology (Source 25). In artistic and publicistic texts, they create imagery and express emotion (*подивитися* vs. *глянути* vs. *зиркнути*). This functional distinction is a key B2 competency.

## Типові помилки L2 (Common L2 Errors)

English speakers often struggle with the subtle connotations and fixed collocations of Ukrainian synonyms, leading to unnatural or incorrect sentences.

| ❌ Помилково (Incorrect) | ✅ Правильно (Correct) | Чому (Why) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| На зборах директор довго **балакав** про бюджет. | На зборах директор довго **говорив** (or **розповідав**) про бюджет. | **Stylistic Mismatch:** `балакати` is a conversational word, inappropriate for a formal setting like a meeting (`збори`). It implies informal chatting, not official discourse. (Source 24) |
| Я **рахую**, що це чудова ідея. | Я **вважаю**, що це чудова ідея. | **Semantic Calque (from Russian):** This is a direct translation of the Russian "я считаю". In Ukrainian, `рахувати` means "to count". The correct verb for expressing an opinion is `вважати`. |
| Ми повинні **здобути** правильне рішення. | Ми повинні **прийняти** (or **ухвалити**) правильне рішення. | **Incorrect Collocation:** Synonyms are not always interchangeable. You `здобувати` (gain, achieve) things like education or victory. Decisions are `приймати` (accepted) or `ухвалювати` (resolved/passed). |
| Це був дуже **ефективний** учитель. | Це був дуже **ефективний** учитель. **АЛЕ:** Це був **ефектний** виступ. | **Paronym Confusion:** `Ефективний` means "effective" (produces a result). `Ефектний` means "impressive" or "spectacular". English speakers often confuse these due to the single word "effective" covering both senses in some contexts. (Source 20) |
| Дякую, ваша порада була дуже **корисна**. Мені сподобався ваш **корисливий** виступ. | Дякую, ваша порада була дуже **корисна**. Мені сподобався ваш **цікавий/змістовний** виступ. | **False Friends / Negative Connotation:** `Корисний` means "useful". However, its relative `корисливий` means "mercenary" or "self-serving". This is a dangerous pair for learners, who might assume they share a positive root. |

## Деколонізаційні застереження (Decolonization Notes)

It is critical to present Ukrainian synonymy as a rich, self-sufficient system, rather than a variation of Russian. The pressure of historical Russification created many calques (loan translations) and semantic shifts that persist as common errors.

1.  **Actively Reject Russian Calques:** Many learners, especially those with prior exposure to Russian, import Russian phrasing. These must be explicitly corrected.
    *   **"приймати участь" (рос. принимать участие) → ✅ "брати участь"**: The correct Ukrainian expression is `брати участь`. (Source 6)
    *   **"вірне рішення" (рос. верное решение) → ✅ "правильне рішення"**: While `вірний` can mean "loyal" or "true" (e.g., `вірний друг`), using it to mean "correct" is a common Russianism. The proper word is `правильний`.
    *   **"у якості" (рос. в качестве) → ✅ "як"**: For example, "використати як приклад" not "<s>у якості прикладу</s>".

2.  **Highlight Different Stylistic Connotations:** Words that are cognates (look similar) may occupy different stylistic niches.
    *   Ukrainian **`неділя`** means "Sunday". Russian **`неделя`** means "week". The Ukrainian for "week" is **`тиждень`**. This is a classic "false friend".
    *   The word **`жадний`** in Russian primarily means "greedy". In Ukrainian, it can also mean "greedy", but its more common meaning, especially in Western Ukrainian dialects and literature, is "thirsty" or "eager" (*жадний до знань* - thirsty for knowledge). This nuance is purely Ukrainian.

3.  **Emphasize Native Vocabulary:** Promote the use of distinctly Ukrainian synonyms over internationalisms or Slavic words that are more common in Russian.
    *   Instead of always using `процент`, encourage the use of the native Ukrainian synonym `відсоток`.
    *   Alongside `горизонт`, teach the poetic and common Ukrainian word `обрій` (Source 7).

Teaching Ukrainian synonymy is an opportunity to showcase the language's unique character and depth. It requires a conscious effort to present Ukrainian on its own terms, free from the shadow of Russian linguistic norms.

## Природні приклади (Natural Examples)

These examples are sourced from textbooks and demonstrate how synonym choice reflects the style and purpose of the communication.

**Група 1: Науковий та Офіційно-діловий стиль (Precision and Formality)**
*   `Синоніми, які обмежені сферою вживання і належать до конкретних стилів, називають стилістичними.` (Source 25) - *Use of a precise term.*
*   `В офіційно-діловому стилі застосовують для пояснення та деталізації думок.` (Source 25) - *Formal, descriptive language.*
*   `Наказую: відрядити Коваленка Івана Петровича... для супроводу учнів.` (Source 14) - *Official command using formal vocabulary (`відрядити`, `супровід`).*
*   `Абсолютні синоніми... їх уживають у різних стилях мови: лінгвістика (науковий стиль) — мовознавство (публіцистичний та розмовний стилі).` (Source 47) - *Comparing two terms with different stylistic applications.*

**Група 2: Художній стиль (Expressiveness and Imagery)**
*   `І лежать по всьому світі вздовж і вшир дороги биті і неїжджені шляхи.` (Source 25) - *The choice of `шляхи` alongside `дороги` adds a poetic, slightly archaic flavor.*
*   `Розплелись розсипались розпались наче коси вересневі дні.` (Source 12) - *A chain of contextual synonyms creating a powerful, unified image of disintegration.*
*   `Рожевий вечір, як фламінго, над нами крила розпростер.` (Source 17) - *Poetic metaphor where `розпростер крила` is an expressive way to say "spread out".*

**Група 3: Розмовний стиль (Informality and Emotion)**
*   `— Звісно, дуже-дуже хочу! Треба ж інших послухати і себе показати.` (Source 14) - *Informal, enthusiastic speech.*
*   `Чесно кажучи, на першому уроці я думала, що з’їду з глузду.` (Source 33) - *Use of a common idiom (`з’їхати з глузду` - to go crazy) in a personal story.*
*   `Та куди там суші, авокадо і піцам!` (Source 2) - *A colloquial, dismissive phrase.*

**Група 4: Публіцистичний стиль (Evaluation and Persuasion)**
*   `Песиміст у будь-якій ситуації передовсім бачить труднощі; оптиміст завжди бачить можливості.` (Source 19) - *Using antonyms, but the principle of lexical choice for contrast is central to publicistic style.*
*   `Спільна дорога в усіх, та окремий у кожного шлях.` (Source 25) - *A philosophical statement using the slight difference between `дорога` and `шлях` for rhetorical effect.*

## Рекомендації для вправ (Activity Concepts)

**Phase 1: Identification & Analysis**
*   **"Stylystic Sort":** Provide learners with a mixed list of synonyms (e.g., *говорити, ректи, балакати, базікати*). They must sort them into columns: "Neutral," "Bookish/Poetic," "Conversational," "Pejorative." (Based on Source 25).
*   **"Text Analysis":** Give learners short authentic texts from different registers (a news report, a poem, a dialogue from a film). Their task is to highlight synonyms and explain why the author chose that specific word. For example, "Why did the poet use `край` instead of `місце`?"

**Phase 2: Controlled Practice**
*   **"Choose the Right Word":** Multiple-choice questions where learners must pick the most appropriate synonym for a given context.
    *   *На науковій конференції професор _______ про свої останні відкриття.*
        *   a) розповів (neutral, correct)
        *   b) набазікав (pejorative, incorrect)
        *   c) пошептав (wrong meaning)
*   **"Register Transformation":** Provide a sentence in a formal style and ask the learner to rewrite it conversationally, and vice versa. This forces them to change vocabulary.
    *   *Formal:* `Науковці ініціювали проведення нового дослідження.`
    *   *Conversational:* `Вчені почали робити новий експеримент.`

**Phase 3: Production**
*   **"Role-Playing Scenarios":** Assign roles and situations that require different levels of formality. For example, "You are explaining a problem to your boss" vs. "You are complaining about the same problem to a close friend." The learner must adapt their synonym choice accordingly.
*   **"Creative Writing Prompt":** Ask learners to write two short paragraphs describing the same scene (e.g., a sunset). The first must be for a scientific report (objective, neutral language). The second must be for a personal diary (emotional, expressive language). This will highlight the different functions of synonyms like *захід сонця, надвечір'я, присмерк*.

## Зв'язки з іншими темами (Connections)

Understanding synonymy is deeply integrated with other aspects of the Ukrainian language.

*   **Prerequisites:** A solid B1-level understanding of **Функціональні стилі (Functional Styles)** is essential. The learner must first be able to distinguish between a formal and informal context before they can choose the right vocabulary for it.
*   **Corequisite:** This topic is a part of **Лексикологія (Lexicology)** and is often taught alongside **Антоніми (Antonyms)** and **Пароніми (Paronyms)**, as they all deal with semantic relationships between words (Source 42).
*   **Reinforces:** The study of **Словотвір (Word Formation)** is enhanced, as learners see how prefixes and suffixes (e.g., diminutive `-еньк-` or augmentative `-иськ-`) can create stylistically colored synonyms (Source 37). For example, *хлопець* (boy), *хлопчик* (little boy, neutral/tender), *хлопчисько* (urchin, slightly pejorative).
*   **Enables:** Mastery of synonymy is a gateway to C1/C2 level skills, including nuanced **Письмо (Writing)**, understanding literary **Тропи (Tropes)** (Source 12), and appreciating the subtleties of authorial voice and tone in advanced texts.
