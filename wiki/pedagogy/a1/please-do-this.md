## Методичний підхід (Methodological Approach)

Teaching A1 learners how to make requests in Ukrainian is foundational for communicative competence. The Ukrainian pedagogical approach focuses on building a strong link between grammatical form (the imperative mood) and social function (politeness).

The core concept is the **спонукальне речення** (imperative sentence), which serves to express a command, request, summons, or wish (`наказ, вимогу, заклик, побажання`) (Source: `5-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2022_s0208`). From the very beginning, this grammatical structure is tied to real-world communicative acts.

Key principles of the native teaching method are:

1.  **Function First, Form Second:** Even before learners can conjugate verbs, they are exposed to high-frequency "magic words" (`чарівні слова`) like **будь ласка** and **дякую** (Source: `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0187`). These are taught as essential, unanalyzed chunks for polite interaction. First-grade materials introduce collaborative suggestions like `Будемо дружити!` (Let's be friends!), using the future tense before the formal imperative is taught (Source: `1-klas-bukvar-bolshakova-2018-1_s0003`).

2.  **Explicit Instruction of the Imperative Mood (`Наказовий спосіб`):** The imperative is not treated as a footnote but as a core verb mood alongside the indicative and conditional. Textbooks dedicate specific sections to its formation, providing clear conjugation tables for person and number (Source: `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0064`).

3.  **Contextualization and Role-Playing:** Exercises are heavily contextualized. Learners are not just asked to conjugate verbs but to use them in realistic scenarios, such as asking for directions (`Скажіть, будь ласка, як доїхати до...` - Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0083`), ordering in a café, or making a purchase in a shop (Source: `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0187`). This reinforces the practical application of the grammar.

4.  **Emphasis on Politeness and Formality:** The distinction between the informal `ти` (you, singular) and formal/plural `ви` (you, formal/plural) is crucial. Learners are taught to use the `ви` form (`скажіть`, `дайте`, `допоможіть`) as the default for polite interaction with strangers, elders, or in formal settings. This social dimension is as important as the grammatical one.

5.  **Active Production (`Відтворення`):** The goal is active use. Learners are encouraged to move beyond passive recognition to active production through retrieval practice (`retrieval practice`), where they must recall and produce the correct form themselves rather than just selecting it from options (Source: `ext-ulp_youtube-90`).

## Послідовність введення (Introduction Sequence)

The introduction of requests should be carefully scaffolded to build from simple, fixed phrases to more flexible grammatical construction.

-   **Step 1: Foundational Polite Chunks.**
    Before any grammar, introduce `будь ласка` (please) and `дякую` (thank you) as non-negotiable units for any interaction. Pair them with simple nouns: `Воду, будь ласка` (Water, please). This is a pre-grammatical but highly communicative stage.

-   **Step 2: The Informal Imperative (`ти` form).**
    Introduce the 2nd person singular imperative for speaking to friends. Start with the most common, phonetically simplest verbs.
    -   `Читай!` (Read!)
    -   `Пиши!` (Write!)
    -   `Слухай!` (Listen!)
    -   `Дивись!` (Look!)
    -   `Дай!` (Give!)
    At this stage, the goal is recognition and use of the pattern, not a deep dive into stem changes. Pair these commands immediately with `будь ласка` to establish the habit of politeness: `Дай, будь ласка, ручку.` (Source: `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0104`).

-   **Step 3: The Formal/Plural Imperative (`ви` form).**
    Introduce the 2nd person plural/formal imperative by adding the `-іть` or `-те` ending. Emphasize that this form is for (a) speaking to a group of people, or (b) speaking politely to one person.
    -   `Читайте!`
    -   `Пишіть!`
    -   `Слухайте!`
    -   `Скажіть, будь ласка...` (Tell me, please...) (Source: `6-klas-ukrmova-betsa-2023_s0083`)
    This is the most important form for navigating daily life in Ukraine as a foreigner.

-   **Step 4: The Collaborative Imperative (`ми` form).**
    Introduce the 1st person plural "let's..." form for making suggestions.
    -   `Ходімо в кіно!` (Let's go to the cinema!) (Source: `7-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2024_s0093` dialogue)
    -   `Співаймо!` (Let's sing!) (Source: `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0064`)
    -   `Робімо!` (Let's do it!) (Source: `7-klas-ukrmova-litvinova-2024_s0064`)
    This is communicatively very useful for social situations.

-   **Step 5 (A2 Preview): Other Forms of Request.**
    Briefly mention that Ukrainian has more nuanced ways to make requests, which will be covered later. This manages learner expectations. These include:
    -   Infinitive for general commands: `Не говорити!` (No talking!) (Source: `10-klas-ukrmova-karaman-2018_s0069`)
    -   Conditional mood for very polite requests: `Чи не допомогла б ти мені?` (Couldn't you help me?) (Source: `7-klas-ukrmova-avramenko-2024_s0090`)
