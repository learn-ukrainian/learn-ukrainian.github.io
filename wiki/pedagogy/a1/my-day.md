## Методичний підхід (Methodological Approach)

The topic of "My Day" (`Мій день`) is foundational for A1 learners, as it introduces high-frequency vocabulary and basic sentence structures for talking about oneself. The native Ukrainian approach, observed in podcasts for learners and early-grade textbooks, is to build the topic sequentially and contextually.

1.  **Anchor with Meal Times:** The most common entry point is through the three main meals: `сніданок` (breakfast), `обід` (lunch), and `вечеря` (dinner). The pedagogical pattern is to introduce the noun for the meal alongside its corresponding verb: `сніданок` → `снідати`, `обід` → `обідати`, `вечеря` → `вечеряти` (Джерело: `ext-ulp_youtube-253`). This immediately gives learners a functional verbal chunk.

2.  **Introduce Time of Day Adverbs:** Once meal vocabulary is established, it's contextualized with time-of-day adverbs: `вранці` (in the morning), `вдень` (in the afternoon/daytime), `ввечері` (in the evening) (Джерело: `ext-ulp_youtube-253`, `11-klas-ukrmova-zabolotnyi-2019_s0176`). This allows for the creation of simple, meaningful sentences: `Вранці я снідаю` (In the morning, I have breakfast).

3.  **Expand with Core Routine Verbs:** The scope is then widened to include other essential daily actions, particularly reflexive verbs ending in `-ся`, which are common for personal actions. Key verbs are `прокидатися` (to wake up) and `лягати спати` (to go to bed) (Джерело: `ext-ulp_youtube-248`, `4-klas-ukrmova-zaharijchuk_s0150`).

4.  **Integrate Frequency Adverbs:** To add nuance, adverbs of frequency are introduced early. Words like `завжди` (always), `зазвичай` (usually), `часто` (often), and `іноді` (sometimes) allow learners to describe their habits more accurately (Джерело: `ext-ulp_youtube-253`).

5.  **Use Narrative Context:** Instead of isolated vocabulary lists, the concepts are presented within a short narrative or dialogue. A speaker describes their own routine, providing a model for learners to imitate (Джерело: `ext-ulp_youtube-253`, `ext-ulp_youtube-243`). This "listen and repeat" model is central.

## Послідовність введення (Introduction Sequence)

**Step 1: Core Meals & Verbs**
-   Introduce the noun/verb pairs: `сніданок / снідати`, `обід / обідати`, `вечеря / вечеряти`.
-   **Why:** This is the most relatable and immediate daily structure. It provides a skeleton around which the rest of the day can be built (Джерело: `ext-ulp_youtube-253`).

**Step 2: Times of Day (Adverbs)**
-   Introduce `вранці`, `вдень`, `ввечері`, `вночі`.
-   **Why:** These adverbs are simple, require no case changes for the learner, and immediately allow for sentence construction: `(Time) + я + (Verb)`. Example: `Вранці я снідаю`. (Джерело: `5-klas-ukrmova-uhor-2022-1_s0079`).

**Step 3: Foundational Routine Verbs (including Reflexive)**
-   Introduce `прокидатися` (to wake up), `вставати` (to get up), `лягати спати` (to go to bed), `працювати` (to work), `відпочивати` (to rest).
-   **Why:** These verbs frame the beginning and end of the day and introduce the crucial concept of reflexive verbs (`-ся`) in a practical context (Джерело: `ext-ulp_youtube-248`, `ext-article-0`).

**Step 4: Telling Time (Hours)**
-   Introduce the construction `о/об [число] годині`. Focus on full hours first. Example: `о дев'ятій годині` (at 9 o'clock), `об одинадцятій годині` (at 11 o'clock).
-   **Why:** This adds a layer of specificity. The distinction between `о` and `об` (for vowels) is an important phonetic rule to introduce early (Джерело: `ext-ulp_youtube-235`).

**Step 5: Days of the Week & Weekly Rhythm**
-   Introduce the names of the days: `понеділок` through `неділя`.
-   Teach the construction `у [день тижня]` (in the accusative case) to talk about specific days. Example: `У понеділок я працюю.`
