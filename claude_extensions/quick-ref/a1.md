## Grammar Scope

**Allowed:** Nominative, Accusative (from M25), Locative (from M13), Genitive (basics), Vocative.
Present tense verbs (from M15). Past tense (from M36). Future (from M37). Imperative (M47).

**Forbidden (unless plan exempts):** Dative case (A2), Instrumental case (A2), participles,
subordinate clauses (який, що-clause, коли, якщо, тому що, бо, щоб).

**Aspect:** Imperfective only — UNLESS the module's plan explicitly teaches perfective forms
(e.g., imperative module M47 uses perfective verbs like сказати, показати, взяти).

## Immersion Strategy (A1)

A1 immersion is achieved through **structure**, not text dumps:
- Tables (vocabulary groups, paradigms, gender sorting)
- Pattern boxes (читати → читай → читайте)
- Mini-dialogues (2-4 lines, labeled speakers, English gloss per line)
- Bold inline Ukrainian in English prose: "The word **книга** (book) is feminine"
- Example sentences: Ukrainian line + English translation below

**Never** write a block of 5+ Ukrainian sentences followed by a single English translation.
Grammar rules and explanations stay in English at A1.

## Sentence Constraints

- Max 10 words per Ukrainian sentence (STRICT)
- Single clause only (no compound sentences)
- Simple subject-verb-object patterns

## A1-Specific Writing Notes

- No grammar terminology in Ukrainian (іменник, дієслово, голосний) — students don't know these yet
- No IPA, no Latin transliteration — stress marks (´) only
- No Russianisms (кушати→їсти, получати→отримувати)
- Ukrainian section headers with English in parentheses for M15+
- Bilingual headers for M1-14: `## Голосні — Vowels`
