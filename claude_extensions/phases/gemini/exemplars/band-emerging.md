# Exemplar: Emerging Band (A1 M11-20)

> Immersion 25-40%. English explains concepts. Ukrainian in paradigm tables, bulleted example lists (Ukrainian — English gloss), pattern boxes, and inline bold vocabulary. Never write a Ukrainian sentence followed by its English translation in a prose paragraph. Mix container types — don't use tables for everything. Ukrainian sentences max 10 words.

> PEDAGOGY: Discovery before rules. Show Ukrainian examples FIRST, prompt the learner to notice the pattern, THEN state the rule explicitly. Never lead with a grammar rule.

## Example Section: Teaching Noun Gender (discovery approach)

Look at these three groups of Ukrainian words. What do you notice about the endings?

| Group A | Group B | Group C |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **стіл** (table) | **книга** (book) | **вікно** (window) |
| **дім** (house) | **мама** (mom) | **місто** (city) |
| **брат** (brother) | **земля** (land) | **море** (sea) |

Group A words all end in a consonant. Group B ends in **-а** or **-я**. Group C ends in **-о** or **-е**.

These three groups are the three genders of Ukrainian:

| Ending | Gender | Pointing word |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| consonant | Masculine | **цей** (this) |
| **-а** / **-я** | Feminine | **ця** (this) |
| **-о** / **-е** | Neuter | **це** (this) |

The pointing words are your diagnostic tool. If you can say **цей стіл**, it's Masculine. If you can say **ця книга**, it's Feminine.

- **Цей стіл великий.** — This table is big.
- **Ця книга нова.** — This book is new.
- **Це вікно чисте.** — This window is clean.

> [!tip]
> When you meet a new noun, immediately try pairing it with **цей/ця/це**. Whichever one sounds right tells you the gender. Native speakers do this instinctively.

There is one exception to memorize: when a noun refers to a male person, it is always Masculine regardless of ending. The classic example is **тато** (dad) — it ends in **-о**, which normally signals Neuter, but because a dad is a man, it's Masculine:

- **Це тато. Він тут.** — This is dad. He is here.
- **Це дідусь. Він там.** — This is grandpa. He is there.

> [!culture]
> Ukrainian poet Taras Shevchenko often personified Ukraine as a woman — **Україна** is feminine (ending in **-а**), and so is **мова** (language). The sun (**сонце**) is neuter, while the moon (**місяць**) is masculine.

## Example Section: Teaching a Case with Helper Verbs

Before learning the accusative case, notice how these sentences differ:

- **Книга тут.** — The book is here. (book = subject, doing nothing)
- **Я читаю книгу.** — I am reading a book. (book = object, being acted upon)

The word **книга** changed to **книгу**. This is the accusative case — it marks what is being acted upon.

Ukrainian uses helper verbs to identify cases. For the accusative, the helper is **бачу** (I see):

| Question | Helper | Case |
|----------|--------|------|
| хто? що? (who? what?) | **є** (is) | Nominative (subject) |
| кого? що? (whom? what?) | **бачу** (I see) | Accusative (object) |

> [!tip]
> To test if a noun should be in the accusative, try asking "Я бачу... що?" (I see... what?). If the noun answers that question, it needs the accusative form.

Only feminine nouns visibly change in the accusative. Everything else stays the same:

| Gender | Nominative | Accusative | Change |
|--------|-----------|------------|--------|
| Masculine | **стіл** | **стіл** | none |
| Feminine | **книга** | **книгу** | **-а** → **-у** |
| Neuter | **вікно** | **вікно** | none |

Here are trigger verbs that always take an accusative object:

- **Я читаю книгу.** — I am reading a book.
- **Він бачить маму.** — He sees mom.
- **Ми купуємо воду.** — We are buying water.
- **Вона любить музику.** — She loves music.

> [!warning]
> A common English-speaker mistake: using the nominative form as the object. You cannot say ~~Я читаю книга~~ — it must be **Я читаю книгу**. The **-а → -у** change is mandatory.

Now compare these two sentences carefully:

- **Вода холодна.** — The water is cold. (nominative — water is the subject)
- **Дайте воду.** — Give water. (accusative — water is the object)

Same word, same meaning, but different form because of different roles in the sentence. This is the core idea of Ukrainian cases.

> [!did-you-know]
> The Ukrainian proverb **"Хліб усьому голова"** (Bread is the head of everything) uses the nominative — **хліб** is the subject. But when you buy bread at the market, you say **"Дайте хліб"** — and since **хліб** is masculine, it stays the same in the accusative. No change needed!
