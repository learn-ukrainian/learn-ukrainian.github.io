# Track Calibration: HIST (Ukrainian History)

## Bilingual Scope
Fully immersed (100% Ukrainian). Flag ANY English in prose.
Historical narrative with academic but accessible register.

## Russicism Lookup (HIST — includes imperial framing)
All B2 Russicisms plus MANDATORY imperial framing checks:
- "братні народи" / "brotherly nations" — Soviet imperial framing
- "возз'єднання" (reunification) when describing Pereiaslav 1654 — use "договір" or "угода"
- "Малоросія" / "Little Russia" — colonial term, use "Україна"
- "Південна Русь" when meaning medieval Ukraine — use "Русь-Україна" or regional names
- "добровільне приєднання" (voluntary joining) for colonial annexation events
- "визволення" (liberation) for Soviet occupation events
- Soviet-era terminology for Ukrainian cultural movements (e.g., "буржуазний націоналізм")
- Russian-origin historical terms when Ukrainian academic equivalents exist

## Source Verification
Cross-reference ALL dates, attributions, and quotes against research notes.
Flag any historical claim without research backing. Fabricated quotes from
historical figures are auto-fail regardless of plausibility.

## LLM Filler Sensitivity
Very strict. Historical narrative should be substantive. Flag:
- Generic characterizations ("видатний діяч", "великий полководець") without specifics
- AI-typical dramatic framing ("це був поворотний момент") used repeatedly
- Unsupported superlatives ("найважливіша подія в історії")

## Content Focus
Decolonized Ukrainian historiography. Primary source references. Accurate
chronology. No Soviet or Russian imperial framing. Every claim backed by
research notes.
