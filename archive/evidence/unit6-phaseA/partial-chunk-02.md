## Голосні звуки

A 1st-grade textbook (Большакова, page 24) opens with a verse: «Голосні почуєш в пісні, і у темному у лісі, і коли дивуєшся, і коли милуєшся. Легко вимовляються, весело співаються!» — "You hear vowels in a song, in the dark forest, when you marvel, when you admire. Easily pronounced, joyfully sung!" A **голосний** звук (vowel sound) is made with voice alone — air flows freely through the mouth, meeting no obstacles. You can sing it. Try humming [а] on one breath — easy.

:::tip
The test: if you can sing the sound on one held breath, it is a голосний. If lips, teeth, or tongue block the air, it is not.
:::

Now the numbers. Ukrainian has **6 vowel sounds** — [а], [о], [у], [е], [и], [і] — but **10 vowel letters**: А, О, У, Е, И, І, Я, Ю, Є, Ї. The four extras (Я, Ю, Є, Ї) are called **йотовані** (iotated) and can spell two sounds at once. Module 2 unpacks that rule. One structural fact to lock in: every Ukrainian word contains at least one vowel sound. Vowels are the heart of every syllable — without one, no syllable exists.

A tiny notation from Захарійчук, page 13: each голосний звук is marked in sound-models with one dot — `[•]`. Three model words. **«мама»** (mom) splits as ма-ма — two clean [а] sounds. **«молоко»** (milk) splits as мо-ло-ко — three [о] sounds. **«Уля»** (Ulia, a name) — one [у]. Crucially: in **«молоко»** the unstressed «о» stays a clean [о] — it does NOT slide toward [а] as Russian unstressed «о» does.
