## Grammar Rules

- **М'який знак (ь)**: Правопис §26 — «Буквою ь позначаємо на письмі м'якість приголосних звуків.» Applies after д, т, з, с, дз, ц, л, н at end of word/syllable. Canonical examples: кінь, день, палець. Confirms the plan's teaching point that ь carries no sound of its own — it only softens the preceding consonant.

- **Буква Г vs Ґ**: Правопис §5 — Г = глотковий щілинний [ɦ] (fricative, Ukrainian-native); Ґ = hard plosive [g]. Plan correctly teaches this distinction. ґудзик as keyword for Ґ is confirmed as standard.

- **Йотовані літери (Я, Ю, Є, Ї)**: The Правопис does not use the term "йотовані" as a searchable section keyword — this is phonetic/didactic terminology from school grammars (Заболотний, Літвінова), not a formal Правопис category. The plan's claim that these letters can denote two sounds in certain positions is phonetically correct and standard across all Ukrainian school curricula. Acceptable to present as textbook fact without citing a Правопис §number.

- **Приголосні тверді/м'які**: Plan states hard consonants marked [–], soft consonants marked [=] per Захарійчук Gr.1, p.15. This is a didactic notation system (sound-model symbols), not a Правопис rule — no §number applies. Phonetically sound.
