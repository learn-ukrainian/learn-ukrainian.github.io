This isn't a stylistic choice ‚Äî it's a grammatical difference that reflects your perspective on the action. English "will" doesn't convey this difference ‚Äî everything merges into one construction.

Myth vs Fact

Myth: "'–ù–∞–ø–∏—à—É' is present tense because of the -—É/-—é ending."

Fact: For perfective verbs, "present" endings give future meaning! Why? Perfective aspect describes a completed action ‚Äî and what will be completed can only be in the future (the present isn't finished yet). This is the logic of the language, not an exception.
