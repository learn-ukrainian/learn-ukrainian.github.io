üí° Did You Know?

English used to have dative case too! Old English "him" was dative (vs. "hine" accusative). Over time, English merged them. When we say "Give him the book," that "him" is a remnant of the dative case. Ukrainian kept all its cases - which means more precision in who receives what!

üìú History Bite: Why Dative?

The word "dative" comes from Latin "dare" (to give). This case has existed since Proto-Indo-European times - at least 5,000 years! The fundamental human actions of giving, telling, and showing have always needed a way to express the recipient. Ukrainian preserves this ancient grammar beautifully.

üé≠ Culture Corner: Gift-Giving

Ukrainians love giving gifts! "–î–∞—Ä—É–≤–∞—Ç–∏" (to give as a gift) uses dative: "–Ø –¥–∞—Ä—É—é —Ç–æ–±—ñ –∫–≤—ñ—Ç–∏" (I give you flowers). Important customs:

  • Give odd numbers of flowers (even numbers are for funerals)
  • Don't give sharp objects as gifts (superstition)
  • Accept gifts graciously with both hands
