üé≠ Culture Corner: Asking vs Telling

üìù Note: No Perfective Forms

The multidirectional verbs (—Ö–æ–¥–∏—Ç–∏, —ó–∑–¥–∏—Ç–∏) have no perfective forms because they describe repeated or non-specific motion. For completed single journeys, use prefixed forms of the unidirectional verbs:

üìú History Bite: Determinate vs Indeterminate

Slavic motion verbs originally had two stems: "determinate" (one direction, –π—Ç–∏) and "indeterminate" (various directions, —Ö–æ–¥–∏—Ç–∏). This ancient distinction survives in Ukrainian, Russian, Polish, and other Slavic languages. Most other Indo-European languages lost this feature!
