### The Preposition Puzzle

You've learned all 7 cases. Now the challenge: which preposition takes which case?

In English, "in" always means "in." In Ukrainian, the CASE changes the meaning:

  • —É –∫—ñ–º–Ω–∞—Ç—ñ (in the room - locative, location)
  • —É –∫—ñ–º–Ω–∞—Ç—É (into the room - accusative, motion)

Same preposition, different cases, different meanings!

üí° Did You Know?

Ukrainian prepositions are "case governors" - they demand specific cases from the nouns that follow. Some prepositions take only one case; others can take two or three, with the case determining the meaning. Master this, and you've mastered a key part of Ukrainian grammar!

üìú History Bite: Motion vs Rest

This location/motion distinction goes back to Proto-Indo-European! Latin had it (in + ablative vs in + accusative), German still has it (in dem Zimmer vs in das Zimmer). Ukrainian preserves this ancient pattern perfectly.
