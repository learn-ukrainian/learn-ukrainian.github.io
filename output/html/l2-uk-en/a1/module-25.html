Section 8: The Etiquette of Travel

In Ukraine, public transport has unwritten rules.

  1. Give up your seat. If you see an elderly person, a pregnant woman, or a parent with a child, you must stand up. Otherwise, the whole bus will shame you.
  2. Backpack off. If the metro is crowded, take your backpack off and put it on the floor.
  3. Prepare cash. In a Marshrutka, search for 15 hryvnias before you enter. Don't block the door while fishing for coins.
  4. The "Stop" Button. Marshrutkas rarely have buttons. You are the button. Scream.

üó£Ô∏è Deep Dive: The "Passing" Chain
In a packed Marshrutka, you might be far from the driver.
You tap the shoulder of the person in front of you.
You give them 20 UAH and say "Za odnoho" (For one person).
They pass it forward.
Eventually, change comes back. "Zdacha z dvadtsyaty!" (Change from twenty!).
It travels back to you.
It requires trust. And it works.
