### Time to Ask Questions!

You can now make statements in Ukrainian. But conversations need questions! In this module, you'll learn to ask anything - and say "no" properly.

Ukrainian has two types of questions:

  1. Yes/No questions - "Do you speak Ukrainian?"
  2. Wh-questions - "What do you speak?"

And here's a surprise: Ukrainian requires double negatives!

üí° Did You Know?

In English, "I don't know nothing" is considered incorrect (double negative). In Ukrainian, –Ø –Ω—ñ—á–æ–≥–æ –Ω–µ –∑–Ω–∞—é (literally "I nothing not know") is the ONLY correct way to say "I don't know anything"! This is true for all Slavic languages, as well as Spanish, French, and many others.

### Yes/No Questions with –ß–∏

Both are correct! In everyday speech, Ukrainians often skip –ß–∏ and just raise their voice at the end.

üéØ Fun Fact: –ù–µ Never Moves

Unlike English where "not" can appear in different places, Ukrainian –Ω–µ always goes immediately before the verb:

  • "She does not read" = –í–æ–Ω–∞ –Ω–µ —á–∏—Ç–∞—î

Simple rule: find the verb, put –Ω–µ before it!

üîç Myth Buster: "Double Negatives Are Wrong"

English teachers say "don't use double negatives." But this is just an English rule! In Ukrainian (and Russian, Spanish, French, Italian, Greek‚Ä¶), double negatives are required and don't cancel out. Think of them as reinforcing each other, not canceling.

üé≠ Culture Corner: Answering Questions

When answering yes/no questions:

  • –¢–∞–∫ = Yes
  • –ù—ñ = No
  • –¢–∞–∫, –∑–≤–∏—á–∞–π–Ω–æ! = Yes, of course!

  • –ú–∞–±—É—Ç—å = Maybe/Probably
  • –ù–µ –∑–Ω–∞—é = I don't know

Ukrainians often soften "no" with explanations rather than just saying "–Ω—ñ."
