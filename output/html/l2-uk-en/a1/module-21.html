Yesterday, Last Week, Long Ago‚Ä¶

So far, you've only spoken about NOW (present tense). Time to unlock the past!

Ukrainian past tense is beautifully simple - just ONE past tense form (unlike English with its "I did," "I was doing," "I had done," etc.). But there's a twist: the verb must match the GENDER of the subject!

  • –Ø —á–∏—Ç–∞–≤. (I read - male speaker)
  • –Ø —á–∏—Ç–∞–ª–∞. (I read - female speaker)

üí° Did You Know?

Ukrainian past tense comes from an ancient past participle + the verb "to be." Originally, "—è —á–∏—Ç–∞–≤ —î—Å–º—å" (I am having-read) became just "—è —á–∏—Ç–∞–≤." The "to be" disappeared, but the gender agreement from the participle remained. That's why past tense verbs change for gender!
