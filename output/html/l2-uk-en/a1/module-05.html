### Why Words Change

In Module 03, you learned that nouns have gender. Now comes the consequence: words that describe nouns must match their gender!

In English, "this" and "my" never change:

  • this book, this table, this window
  • my book, my table, my window

This is called agreement - and once you understand the pattern, it becomes automatic!

### Agreement Decision Tree

When using a demonstrative or possessive:

  1. Find the noun - What are you describing?
  2. Determine gender - What's the noun's ending?
  3. Apply matching form - Use the correct demonstrative/possessive ending

Example: "this book"

  1. Noun: –∫–Ω–∏–≥–∞ (book)
  2. Gender: feminine (-–∞ ending)
  3. Form: —Ü—è –∫–Ω–∏–≥–∞ (feminine demonstrative)

Example: "my table"

  1. Noun: —Å—Ç—ñ–ª (table)
  2. Gender: masculine (consonant ending)
