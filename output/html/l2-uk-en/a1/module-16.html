üí° Did You Know?

"Genitive" comes from Latin "genitivus" meaning "of birth/origin." It originally indicated where something came from. Over time, it expanded to express possession, absence, and quantity. Ukrainian genitive does all this and more!

üìú History Bite: Why No "Of"?

English uses prepositions ("of the student") or 's ("student's") for possession. Ukrainian evolved differently - the genitive ending itself carries the meaning of "of." This is more efficient: one suffix does what English needs extra words for!
