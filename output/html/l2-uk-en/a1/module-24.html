In Ukraine, the day comes first.

üìÖ Deep Dive: The Old New Year
Ukraine has a unique holiday: Old New Year (Staryi Novyi Rik) on January 14th.
It follows the old Julian calendar.
People don't work, but they celebrate.
It is a second chance to wish "Shchaslyvoho Novoho Roku" (Happy New Year).

üõ†Ô∏è Deep Dive: The Working Saturday
In Ukraine, if a holiday falls on a Tuesday (e.g., Independence Day), the government might create a "Bridge".
Monday becomes a holiday too! (4-day weekend).
But‚Ä¶ you have to pay it back.
The previous or next Saturday becomes a Working Day (Robocha Subota).
People hate it, but they love the 4-day weekend more.
So if someone says "I work on Saturday", ask "Is it a working Saturday?" (Tse robocha subota?).
