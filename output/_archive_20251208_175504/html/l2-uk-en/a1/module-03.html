Masculine, Feminine, and Neuter: How Ukrainian Organizes Nouns

Everything Has a Gender!

In Ukrainian, every noun has a gender: masculine, feminine, or neuter. This isn't about biology - it's about grammar. A table (—Å—Ç—ñ–ª) is masculine, a book (–∫–Ω–∏–≥–∞) is feminine, and a window (–≤—ñ–∫–Ω–æ) is neuter.

Why does this matter? Because adjectives, pronouns, and even verbs change their endings to match the noun's gender. Learning gender now will make everything else easier!

üí° Did You Know?

English used to have grammatical gender too! Words like "sun" were feminine and "moon" was masculine in Old English. Ukrainian kept this feature - and it makes the language more expressive!

The Good News

Unlike German (where you must memorize each word's gender), Ukrainian gender is predictable from the ending in most cases:
